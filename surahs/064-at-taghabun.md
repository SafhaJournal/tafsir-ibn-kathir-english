## 64:1-4 — verses 1-4 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Tafsir of Surat al-Taghabun

It is Medinan, and it is said: Meccan.

Al-Tabarani said: Muhammad ibn Harun ibn Muhammad ibn Bakkar al-Dimashqi narrated to us, al-Abbas ibn al-Walid al-Khallal narrated to us, al-Walid ibn al-Walid narrated to us, Ibn Thawban narrated to us, from Ata' ibn Abi Rabah, from Abdullah ibn Amr, may Allah be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "There is no newborn that is born except that five verses from Surat al-Taghabun are written in the interlacing of its head."

Ibn Asakir mentioned this in the biographical entry of "al-Walid ibn Salih," and it is extremely gharib (singular), rather munkar (rejected).

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful

This surah is the last of the Musabbihat (surahs beginning with glorification of Allah), and discussion has already preceded regarding the glorification of created things for their Creator and Owner. For this reason He said: {His is the dominion and His is the praise}, meaning: He is the One who disposes of all affairs in all the created realms, the One praised for all that He creates and decrees.

His saying: {and He is over all things capable} means: whatever He wills comes to be without any preventer or opposer, and whatever He does not will does not come to be.

His saying: {He it is who created you, yet among you is a disbeliever and among you is a believer} means: He is the One who created you with this description, and He willed that from you, so there must necessarily exist a believer and a disbeliever. He is the All-Seeing regarding who deserves guidance and who deserves misguidance, and He is a Witness over the deeds of His servants, and He will requite them for them with the most complete requital. For this reason He said: {and Allah is All-Seeing of what you do}

Then He said: {He created the heavens and the earth in truth}, meaning: with justice and wisdom, {and He formed you, and made your forms excellent}, meaning: He made your shapes excellent, as in His saying, Exalted is He: {O man, what has deceived you concerning your Lord, the Generous, who created you, then fashioned you, then proportioned you, in whatever form He willed He assembled you} [al-Infitar: 6-8], and as in His saying: {Allah is the One who made for you the earth a settled place and the sky a structure, and formed you and made your forms excellent, and provided you with good things} to the end of the verse [Ghafir: 64]. And His saying: {and to Him is the final return}, meaning: the returning place and the destination.

Then He, Exalted is He, informed of His knowledge encompassing all the created things of the heavens, the earth, and the inner selves, saying: {He knows what is in the heavens and the earth, and He knows what you conceal and what you disclose, and Allah is Knowing of what is within the breasts}

## 64:5-6 — verses 5-6 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, informing about the past nations and the punishment and exemplary chastisement that befell them for opposing the messengers and denying the truth. He says: {Has there not come to you the report of those who disbelieved before} that is, inform them of what happened to them, {and they tasted the evil consequence of their affair} that is, the grievous result of their denial and the wickedness of their deeds, which is what befell them in this world of punishment and disgrace. {And for them is a painful punishment} that is, in the Hereafter, added to this worldly one.

Then He gave the reason for that, saying: {That is because their messengers used to come to them with clear proofs} that is, with arguments, evidences, and proofs, {but they said, "Shall mere mortals guide us?"} that is, they considered it far-fetched that messengership could be given to a human being, and that their guidance should come at the hands of a human like themselves. {So they disbelieved and turned away} that is, they denied the truth and refrained from acting upon it, {and Allah dispensed with them} that is, He had no need of them, {for Allah is Self-Sufficient, Praiseworthy}.

## 64:7-10 — verses 7-10 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Most High says, informing about the polytheists, the disbelievers, and the deniers, that they claim they will not be resurrected: {Say: Yes, by my Lord, you will surely be resurrected, then you will surely be informed of what you did}, meaning: you will certainly be told of all your deeds, the great and the small of them, the little and the big of them. {And that is easy for Allah}, meaning: your resurrection and your recompense.

This is the third verse in which Allah commanded His Messenger ﷺ to swear by his Lord, Mighty and Majestic, concerning the occurrence and reality of the return to life. The first is in Surat Yunus: {And they ask you to inform them, "Is it true?" Say: "Yes, by my Lord, it is surely true, and you cannot escape it"} [Yunus: 53]. The second is in Surat Saba': {And those who disbelieved said: "The Hour will not come to us." Say: "Yes, by my Lord, it will surely come to you"} the verse [Saba': 3]. The third is this one: {Those who disbelieved claim that they will not be resurrected. Say: "Yes, by my Lord, you will surely be resurrected, then you will surely be informed of what you did, and that is easy for Allah"}.

Then the Most High says: {So believe in Allah and His Messenger and the light which We have sent down}, meaning: the Qur'an, {and Allah is well acquainted with what you do}, meaning: none of your deeds are hidden from Him.

His saying: {The Day He will gather you for the Day of Gathering}, this is the Day of Resurrection. It is named thus because the first and the last of creation will be gathered together on that day in one place, where the caller will make himself heard to them and sight will penetrate through them all, as the Most High said: {That is a Day for which mankind will be gathered, and that is a Day to be witnessed} [Hud: 103], and the Most High said: {Say: Indeed, the first and the last will surely be gathered together for the appointed time of a known Day} [al-Waqi'ah: 49-50].

His saying: {That is the Day of al-Taghabun (mutual loss and gain)}. Ibn Abbas said: it is one of the names of the Day of Resurrection. That is because the people of Paradise will gain at the expense of the people of the Fire. Qatadah and Mujahid said the same.

Muqatil ibn Hayyan said: there is no greater loss and gain than that these people are admitted into Paradise while those others are taken away to the Fire.

I say: this has been explained by the saying of the Most High: {And whoever believes in Allah and does righteous deeds, He will remove from him his sins and admit him into gardens beneath which rivers flow, abiding therein forever. That is the great attainment. But those who disbelieved and denied Our signs, those are the companions of the Fire, abiding therein forever, and what a wretched destination}. The explanation of similar verses has already been given more than once.

## 64:11-13 — verses 11-13 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, informing of what He informed of in Surat al-Hadid: {No calamity befalls the earth or yourselves except that it is in a Book before We bring it into being} [al-Hadid: 22]. Likewise He says here: {No calamity befalls except by the permission of Allah}. Ibn Abbas said: by the command of Allah, meaning by His decree and His will.

{And whoever believes in Allah, He guides his heart. And Allah is Knowing of all things}, that is: whoever is struck by a calamity and knows that it is by Allah's decree and predestination, and so he bears it patiently, seeking its reward, and submits to Allah's decree, Allah guides his heart and compensates him, for what he lost of this world, with guidance in his heart and true certainty. And He may sometimes restore to him what had been taken from him, or something better than it.

Ali ibn Abi Talha reported from Ibn Abbas: {And whoever believes in Allah, He guides his heart} means: He guides his heart to certainty, so that he knows that whatever befell him could not have missed him, and whatever missed him could not have befallen him.

Al-A'mash reported from Abu Zibyan, who said: We were with Alqamah when this verse was recited in his presence: {And whoever believes in Allah, He guides his heart}. He was asked about it and said: It is the man who is struck by a calamity, and he knows that it is from Allah, so he accepts it and submits. Narrated by Ibn Jarir and Ibn Abi Hatim.

Sa'id ibn Jubayr and Muqatil ibn Hayyan said: {And whoever believes in Allah, He guides his heart} means: he says the istirja (words of returning to Allah), saying: {Indeed we belong to Allah, and indeed to Him we return} [al-Baqarah: 156].

In the hadith agreed upon [by al-Bukhari and Muslim]: "Amazing is the affair of the believer. Allah does not decree anything for him except that it is good for him. If harm befalls him, he bears it patiently, and that is good for him. And if good fortune befalls him, he gives thanks, and that is good for him. That is for no one except the believer."

Ahmad said: [chain] Ali ibn Rabah heard Junadah ibn Abi Umayyah say: I heard Ubadah ibn al-Samit say that a man came to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and said: "O Messenger of Allah, which deed is most excellent?" He said: "Belief in Allah, affirmation of Him, and struggle in His cause." The man said: "I want something easier than this, O Messenger of Allah." He said: "Do not accuse Allah of wrongdoing in anything He has decreed for you." Neither of the two [al-Bukhari and Muslim] recorded it.

And His saying: {And obey Allah and obey the Messenger} is a command to obey Allah and His Messenger in what He has legislated, to do what He has commanded, and to abstain from what He has forbidden and warned against. Then He says: {But if you turn away, then upon Our Messenger is only the clear conveyance}, meaning: if you turn back from acting, then upon him is only what he was charged with, namely conveyance, and upon you is what you were charged with, namely hearing and obedience.

Al-Zuhri said: From Allah is the message, upon the Messenger is the conveyance, and upon us is submission.

Then the Almighty says, informing that He is the One, the Samad (the Self-Sufficient Master relied upon by all, needing none), besides whom there is no god, saying: {Allah, there is no god but He, and upon Allah let the believers rely}. The first part is a statement about the oneness of Allah (tawhid), and its meaning carries the sense of a command, that is: affirm His oneness in divinity, devote it purely to Him, and rely upon Him, just as the Almighty says: {Lord of the east and the west, there is no god but He, so take Him as a Guardian} [al-Muzzammil: 9].

## 64:14-18 — verses 14-18 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, informing about wives and children: among them are some who are enemies of the spouse and the parent, meaning that one becomes distracted by them from righteous deeds, as in His saying: {O you who believe, let not your wealth and your children distract you from the remembrance of Allah, and whoever does that, they are the losers} [al-Munafiqun: 9]. This is why He says here: {so beware of them}. Ibn Zayd said: meaning, concerning your religion.

Mujahid said regarding {Indeed, among your wives and your children there is an enemy to you}: he said, one of them may push the man to sever ties of kinship or to disobey his Lord, and the man, out of his love for them, cannot help but obey. Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] ... Ibn Abbas, when a man asked him about this verse: {O you who believe, indeed among your wives and your children there is an enemy to you, so beware of them}, said: these were men who had embraced Islam from Mecca and wished to go to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, but their wives and children refused to let them go. When they finally came to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, they saw that the people had become learned in the religion, and they were inclined to punish their families for having held them back, so Allah revealed this verse: {but if you pardon and overlook and forgive, then indeed Allah is Forgiving, Merciful}.

Al-Tirmidhi likewise narrated it from Muhammad ibn Yahya, from al-Firyabi (that is, Muhammad ibn Yusuf), with the same wording, and said it is hasan sahih (sound and authentic). Ibn Jarir and al-Tabarani also narrated it through the hadith of Isra'il, with the same content, and it was also narrated through the route of al-Awfi from Ibn Abbas, similarly, and Ikrimah, his freed slave, said the same.

As for His saying: {Your wealth and your children are only a trial, and with Allah is a great reward}, the Almighty says: wealth and children are only a trial, that is, a test and an ordeal from Allah upon His creation, so that He may know who obeys Him from who disobeys Him.

As for His saying: {and with Allah}, that is, on the Day of Resurrection, {is a great reward}, as He said: {Beautified for people is the love of desires, for women and sons, and heaped-up treasures of gold and silver, and branded horses, and cattle and tilled land. That is the enjoyment of worldly life, but with Allah is the good return} and what follows it [Al Imran: 14, 15].

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] ... Buraydah said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was giving a sermon when al-Hasan and al-Husayn, may Allah be pleased with them both, came forward wearing two red shirts, walking and stumbling. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ came down from the pulpit, picked them up, and placed them in front of him, then said: "Allah and His Messenger spoke the truth: your wealth and your children are only a trial. I looked at these two boys walking and stumbling and I could not be patient, so I cut off my speech and picked them up."

The compilers of the Sunan narrated it likewise from the hadith of Husayn ibn Waqid, with the same content, and al-Tirmidhi said: hasan gharib (a sound, singular report), we know it only through his hadith.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] ... al-Ash'ath ibn Qays said: I came to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ with the delegation of Kindah, and he said to me: "Do you have any children?" I said: a boy was born to me on my journey to you, from the daughter of Jamd, and I wish that in his place the people had been fed to satisfaction instead. He said: "Do not say that, for in them is comfort of the eye, and reward when they are taken away," then he said: "But if you say that, they are indeed a cause of cowardice and grief." Ahmad, may Allah have mercy on him, narrated this alone.

The hadith master Abu Bakr al-Bazzar said: [chain] ... Abu Sa'id said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "The child is the fruit of the hearts, and indeed they are a cause of cowardice, miserliness, and grief." Then he said: it is known only through this chain.

Al-Tabarani said: [chain] ... Abu Malik al-Ash'ari reported that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Your enemy is not the one whom, if you kill him, it is a triumph for you, and if he kills you, you enter Paradise. Rather, the one who may truly be your enemy is your child who came forth from your loins, and then the most hostile enemy to you is your wealth which your right hand possesses."

As for the Almighty's saying: {So fear Allah as much as you are able}, that is, to your utmost effort and capacity, as is established in the two Sahihs from Abu Hurayrah, may Allah be pleased with him, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "When I command you with something, do as much of it as you are able, and whatever I forbid you from, avoid it."

Some of the commentators, as narrated by Malik from Zayd ibn Aslam, said that this great verse abrogates the one in Al Imran, which is His saying: {O you who believe, fear Allah as He should be feared, and do not die except as Muslims} [Al Imran: 102].

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] ... Sa'id ibn Jubayr said regarding His saying {Fear Allah as He should be feared, and do not die except as Muslims}: when this verse was revealed, the deed became difficult for the people, so they stood in prayer until their heels swelled and their foreheads became sore with sores. So Allah sent down, as an easing for the Muslims: {So fear Allah as much as you are able}, and thus the first verse was abrogated.

It has been reported similarly from Abu al-Aliyah, Zayd ibn Aslam, Qatadah, al-Rabi' ibn Anas, al-Suddi, and Muqatil ibn Hayyan.

As for His saying: {and listen and obey}, that is, be submissive to what Allah and His Messenger command you, and do not deviate from it to the right or to the left, and do not put yourselves forward before Allah and His Messenger, and do not fall short of what you have been commanded, and do not undertake what you have been forbidden.

As for the Almighty's saying: {and spend, it is better for yourselves}, that is, give of what Allah has provided you to relatives, the poor, the needy, and those in want, and do good to Allah's creation as He has done good to you. This will be better for you in this world and the next, and if you do not do so, it will be worse for you in this world and the next.

As for His saying: {and whoever is protected from the greed of his own soul, those are the successful ones}, its explanation has already been given in Surat al-Hashr, along with the hadiths reported concerning the meaning of this verse, which suffices us from repeating it here, and to Allah belongs praise and grace.

As for His saying: {If you lend to Allah a goodly loan, He will multiply it for you and forgive you}, that is, whatever you spend of anything, He will replace it, and whatever you give in charity of anything, its reward is upon Him, and this has been made equivalent in status to a loan given to Him, as is established in the authentic hadith that Allah, the Almighty, says: "Who will lend to One who is not unjust nor indigent?" This is why He said: {He will multiply it for you}, as has already been mentioned in Surat al-Baqarah: {so He multiplies it for him manifold} [al-Baqarah: 245].

{and forgive you}, that is, and expiate your sins for you. This is why He said: {and Allah is Most Appreciative}, that is, He rewards abundantly for little, {Forbearing}, that is, He pardons, overlooks, forgives, conceals, and passes over sins, lapses, errors, and misdeeds.

{Knower of the unseen and the witnessed, the Mighty, the Wise}, its explanation has already been given more than once.

وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: ﴿إِنَّ مِنْ أَزْوَاجِكُمْ وَأَوْلادِكُمْ عَدُوًّا لَكُمْ﴾ قَالَ: يحملُ الرَّجُلَ عَلَى قَطِيعَةِ الرَّحِمِ أَوْ مَعْصِيَةِ رَبِّهِ، فَلَا يَسْتَطِيعُ الرَّجُلُ مَعَ حُبِّهِ إِلَّا أَنْ يُطِيعَهُ. وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ خَلَفٍ الْعَسْقَلَانِيُّ حَدَّثَنَا الْفِرْيَابِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا إِسْرَائِيلُ، حَدَّثَنَا سمَاك بْنُ حَرْبٍ، عَنْ عِكْرمة، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ -وَسَأَلَهُ رَجُلٌ عَنْ هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ: ﴿يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِنَّ مِنْ أَزْوَاجِكُمْ وَأَوْلادِكُمْ عَدُوًّا لَكُمْ فَاحْذَرُوهُمْ﴾ -قَالَ: فَهَؤُلَاءِ رِجَالٌ أَسْلَمُوا مِنْ مَكَّةَ، فَأَرَادُوا أَنْ يَأْتُوا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ، فَأَبَى أَزْوَاجُهُمْ وَأَوْلَادُهُمْ أَنْ يَدَعوهم، فَلَمَّا أَتَوْا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ رَأَوُا الناسَ قَدْ فَقِهُوا فِي الدِّينِ، فَهَمُّوا أَنْ يُعَاقِبُوهُمْ، فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ هَذِهِ الْآيَةَ: ﴿وَإِنْ تَعْفُوا وَتَصْفَحُوا وَتَغْفِرُوا فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ﴾

وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ يَحْيَى، عَنِ الْفِرْيَابِيِّ -وَهُوَ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ يُوسُفَ-بِهِ وَقَالَ حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ. وَرَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ وَالطَّبَرَانِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ إِسْرَائِيلَ، بِهِ ورُوي مِنْ طَرِيقِ الْعَوْفِيِّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، نَحْوَهُ، وَهَكَذَا قَالَ عِكْرِمَةُ مَوْلَاهُ سَوَاءً.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنَّمَا أَمْوَالُكُمْ وَأَوْلادُكُمْ فِتْنَةٌ وَاللَّهُ عِنْدَهُ أَجْرٌ عَظِيمٌ﴾ يَقُولُ تَعَالَى: إِنَّمَا الْأَمْوَالُ وَالْأَوْلَادُ فِتْنَةٌ، أَيِ: اخْتِبَارٌ وَابْتِلَاءٌ مِنَ اللَّهِ لِخَلْقِهِ. لِيَعْلَمَ مَنْ يُطِيعُهُ مِمَّنْ يَعْصِيهِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَاللَّهُ عِنْدَهُ﴾ أَيْ: يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ ﴿أَجْرٌ عَظِيمٌ﴾ كَمَا قَالَ: ﴿زُيِّنَ لِلنَّاسِ حُبُّ الشَّهَوَاتِ مِنَ النِّسَاءِ وَالْبَنِينَ وَالْقَنَاطِيرِ الْمُقَنْطَرَةِ مِنَ الذَّهَبِ وَالْفِضَّةِ وَالْخَيْلِ الْمُسَوَّمَةِ وَالأنْعَامِ وَالْحَرْثِ+ [ذَلِكَ مَتَاعُ الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا وَاللَّهُ عِنْدَهُ حُسْنُ الْمَآبِ﴾ وَالَّتِي بَعْدَهَا] [آلِ عِمْرَانَ: ١٤، ١٥] 

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا زَيْدُ بْنُ الْحُبَابِ، حَدَّثَنِي حُسَين بْنُ وَاقَدٍ، حَدَّثَنِي عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ بُرَيدة، سَمِعْتُ أَبِي بُرَيْدَةَ يَقُولُ: كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَخْطُبُ، فَجَاءَ الْحَسَنُ وَالْحُسَيْنُ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا، عَلَيْهِمَا قَمِيصَانِ أَحْمَرَانِ يَمْشِيَانِ وَيَعْثُرَانِ، فَنَزَلَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ مِنَ الْمِنْبَرِ فَحَمَلَهُمَا فَوَضَعَهُمَا بَيْنَ يَدَيْهِ، ثُمَّ قَالَ: "صَدَقَ اللَّهُ وَرَسُولُهُ، إِنَّمَا أَمْوَالُكُمْ وَأَوْلَادُكُمْ فِتْنَةٌ، نَظَرْتُ إِلَى هَذَيْنِ الصَّبِيَّيْنِ يَمْشِيَانِ وَيَعْثُرَانِ فَلَمْ أَصْبِرْ حَتَّى قَطَعْتُ حديثي ورفعتهما".

وَرَوَاهُ أَهْلُ السُّنَنِ مِنْ حَدِيثِ حُسَين بْنِ وَاقِدٍ، بِهِ وَقَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَسَنٌ غَرِيبٌ، إِنَّمَا نَعْرِفُهُ مِنْ حَدِيثِهِ.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا سُرَيج بْنُ النُّعْمَانِ، حَدَّثَنَا هُشَيْم، أَخْبَرَنَا مَجَالِدٌ، عَنِ الشَّعْبِيِّ، حَدَّثَنَا الْأَشْعَثُ بْنُ قَيْسٍ قَالَ: قَدِمْتُ عَلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فِي وَفْدِ كِنْدَةَ، فَقَالَ لِي: "هَلْ لَكَ مِنْ وَلَدٍ؟ " قُلْتُ: غُلَامٌ وُلِدَ لِي فِي مَخرجَي إِلَيْكَ مِنِ ابْنَةِ جَمْدٍ، وَلَوَددْتُ أَنَّ بِمَكَانِهِ: شبَعَ الْقَوْمِ. قَالَ: "لَا تَقُولَنَّ ذَلِكَ، فَإِنَّ فِيهِمْ قُرَّةَ عَيْنٍ، وَأَجْرًا إِذَا قُبِضُوا"، ثُمَّ قَالَ: "وَلَئِنْ قُلْتَ ذَاكَ: إِنَّهُمْ لَمَجْبَنَةٌ مَحْزنة" تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ أَحْمَدُ رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى.

وَقَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو بَكْرٍ الْبَزَّارُ: حَدَّثَنَا مَحْمُودُ بْنُ بَكْرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، عَنْ عِيسَى [بْنِ أَبِي وَائِلٍ] عَنِ ابْنِ أَبِي لَيْلَى، عَنْ عَطِيَّةَ، عَنْ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "الْوَلَدُ ثَمَرَةُ الْقُلُوبِ، وَإِنَّهُمْ مَجبنة مَبخلة مَحْزَنَةٌ" ثُمَّ قَالَ: لَا يُعْرَفُ إِلَّا بِهَذَا الْإِسْنَادِ 

وَقَالَ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا هَاشِمُ بْنُ مَرْثَدٍ حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ إِسْمَاعِيلَ بْنِ عَيَّاشٍ، حَدَّثَنِي أَبِي، حَدَّثَنِي ضَمْضَمُ بنُ زُرْعَةَ، عَنْ شُرَيْحِ بْنِ عُبَيْدٍ، عَنْ أَبِي مَالِكٍ الْأَشْعَرِيِّ؛ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "لَيْسَ عَدُوُّكَ الَّذِي إِنْ قَتَلْتَهُ كَانَ فَوْزًا لَكَ، وَإِنْ قَتَلَكَ دَخَلْتَ الْجَنَّةَ، وَلَكِنَّ الَّذِي لَعَلَّهُ عَدُوٌّ لَكَ وَلَدُكَ الَّذِي خَرَجَ مِنْ صُلْبِكَ، ثُمَّ أَعْدَى عَدُوٍّ لَكَ مالُك الَّذِي مَلَكَتْ يَمِينُكَ" 

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَاتَّقُوا اللهَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُمْ ﴾ أَيْ: جُهْدَكُمْ وَطَاقَتَكُمْ. كَمَا ثَبَتَ فِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى الله عليه وسلم: "إذا أَمَرْتُكُمْ بِأَمْرٍ فَائْتُوا مِنْهُ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُمْ، وَمَا نَهَيْتُكُمْ عَنْهُ فَاجْتَنِبُوهُ" 

وَقَدْ قَالَ بَعْضُ الْمُفَسِّرِينَ -كَمَا رَوَاهُ مَالِكٌ، عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ-إِنَّ هَذِهِ الْآيَةَ الْعَظِيمَةَ نَاسِخَةٌ لِلَّتِي فِي "آلِ عِمْرَانَ" وَهِيَ قَوْلُهُ:} يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اتَّقُوا اللَّهَ حَقَّ تُقَاتِهِ وَلا تَمُوتُنَّ إِلا وَأَنْتُمْ مُسْلِمُونَ} [آلِ عِمْرَانَ: ١٠٢]

قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو زُرْعَة، حَدَّثَنِي يَحْيَى بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ بُكَيْر، حَدَّثَنِي ابْنُ لَهِيعة، حَدَّثَنِي عَطَاءٌ -هُوَ ابْنُ دِينَارٍ-عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿اتَّقُوا اللَّهَ حَقَّ تُقَاتِهِ وَلا تَمُوتُنَّ إِلا وَأَنْتُمْ مُسْلِمُونَ﴾ قَالَ: لَمَّا نَزَلَتِ الْآيَةُ اشْتَدَّ عَلَى الْقَوْمِ الْعَمَلُ، فَقَامُوا حَتَّى وَرِمَتْ عَرَاقِيبُهُمْ وَتَقَرَّحَتْ جِبَاهُهُمْ، فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ تَخْفِيفًا عَلَى الْمُسْلِمِينَ: ﴿فَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُمْ﴾ فنسخت الآية الأولى.

وَرُوِيَ عَنْ أَبِي الْعَالِيَةِ، وَزَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ، وَقَتَادَةَ، وَالرَّبِيعُ بْنُ أَنَسٍ، والسُّدِّيّ، ومُقاتل بْنُ حَيَّان، نَحْوُ ذَلِكَ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَاسْمَعُوا وَأَطِيعُوا﴾ أَيْ: كُونُوا مُنْقَادِينَ لِمَا يَأْمُرُكُمُ اللَّهُ بِهِ وَرَسُولُهُ، وَلَا تَحِيدُوا عَنْهُ يَمْنَةً وَلَا يَسْرَةً، وَلَا تُقَدِّمُوا بَيْنَ يَدَيِ اللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِ، وَلَا تَتَخَلَّفُوا عَمَّا بِهِ أُمِرْتُمْ، وَلَا تَرْكَبُوا مَا عَنْهُ زُجرتم.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَأَنْفِقُوا خَيْرًا لأنْفُسِكُمْ﴾ أَيْ: وَابْذُلُوا مِمَّا رَزَقَكُمُ اللَّهُ عَلَى الْأَقَارِبِ وَالْفُقَرَاءِ وَالْمَسَاكِينِ وَذَوِي الحَاجات، وَأَحْسِنُوا إِلَى خَلْقِ اللَّهِ كَمَا أَحْسَنَ إِلَيْكُمْ، يَكُنْ خَيْرًا لَكُمْ فِي الدُّنْيَا وَالْآخِرَةِ، وَإِنْ لَا تَفْعَلُوا يَكُنْ شَرًّا لَكُمْ فِي الدُّنْيَا وَالْآخِرَةِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَمَنْ يُوقَ شُحَّ نَفْسِهِ فَأُولَئِكَ هُمُ الْمُفْلِحُونَ﴾ تَقَدَّمَ تَفْسِيرُهُ فِي سُورَةِ "الْحَشْرِ" وَذِكْرُ الْأَحَادِيثِ الْوَارِدَةِ فِي مَعْنَى هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ، بِمَا أَغْنَى عَنْ إِعَادَتِهِ هَا هُنَا، وَلِلَّهِ الْحَمْدُ وَالْمِنَّةُ،

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنْ تُقْرِضُوا اللَّهَ قَرْضًا حَسَنًا يُضَاعِفْهُ لَكُمْ وَيَغْفِرْ لَكُمْ﴾ أَيْ: مَهْمَا أَنْفَقْتُمْ مِنْ شَيْءٍ فَهُوَ يُخْلِفُهُ، وَمَهْمَا تَصَدَّقْتُمْ مِنْ شَيْءٍ فَعَلَيْهِ جَزَاؤُهُ، وَنَزَلَ ذَلِكَ مَنْزِلَةَ الْقَرْضِ لَهُ، كَمَا ثَبَتَ فِي الصَّحِيحِ أَنَّ اللَّهَ تَعَالَى يَقُولُ: "مَنْ يُقْرِضُ غَيْرَ ظَلُومٍ وَلَا عَدِيمٍ " وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿يُضَاعِفْهُ لَكُمْ﴾ كَمَا تَقَدَّمَ فِي سُورَةِ الْبَقَرَةِ: ﴿فَيُضَاعِفَهُ لَهُ أَضْعَافًا كَثِيرَةً﴾ [الْبَقَرَةِ: ٢٤٥]

﴿وَيَغْفِرْ لَكُمْ﴾ أَيْ: وَيُكَفِّرْ عَنْكُمُ السَّيِّئَاتِ. وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿وَاللَّهُ شَكُورٌ﴾ أَيْ: يَجْزِي عَلَى الْقَلِيلِ بِالْكَثِيرِ ﴿حَلِيمٌ﴾ أي: [يعفو و] يصفح وَيَغْفِرُ وَيَسْتُرُ، وَيَتَجَاوَزُ عَنِ الذُّنُوبِ وَالزَّلَّاتِ وَالْخَطَايَا وَالسَّيِّئَاتِ.

﴿عَالِمُ الْغَيْبِ وَالشَّهَادَةِ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ﴾ تَقَدَّمَ تفسيره غير مرة.
