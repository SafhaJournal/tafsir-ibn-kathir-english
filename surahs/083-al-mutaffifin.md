## 83:1-6 — verses 1-6 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Tafsir of Surat al-Mutaffifin

It is Medinan.

{In the Name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful}

* *

Al-Nasa'i and Ibn Majah said: [chain] from Ikrimah, from Ibn Abbas, who said: When the Prophet of Allah ﷺ came to Madinah, they were among the most dishonest of people in measure, so Allah revealed: {Woe to al-Mutaffifin (those who deal fraudulently in measure and weight)}, and they gave good measure after that.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] from Hilal ibn Talq, who said: While I was traveling with Ibn Umar, I said, "Who are the best of people in appearance and the most complete of them in measure, the people of Makkah or the people of Madinah?" He said, "It is only right for them. Have you not heard Allah say: {Woe to al-Mutaffifin}?"

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] from Abdullah, who said: A man said to him, "O Abu Abd al-Rahman, the people of Madinah give full measure." He said, "What would prevent them from giving full measure, when Allah, Mighty and Majestic, has said: {Woe to al-Mutaffifin}, up to {The Day when mankind will stand before the Lord of the worlds}?"

What is meant by tatfif (giving short measure) here is: shortchanging in measure and weight, whether by taking more when one collects what is due to him from people, or by giving less when one pays them what is due to them. Therefore the Most High explained al-Mutaffifin, whom He threatened with loss and ruin, that is, with al-wayl (woe), by His saying: {Those who, when they take measure from people}, that is, from people, {take it in full}, that is, they take their due in full and with excess, {but when they measure for them or weigh for them, they give less}, that is, they diminish it. And the better view is that "kalu" (they measured) and "wazanu" (they weighed) are made transitive verbs, with the attached pronoun "hum" in the accusative position, that is, as their direct object; while some hold that it is an emphatic pronoun referring back to the hidden subject pronoun already contained in "kalu" and "wazanu," with the true object omitted because the sense of the speech indicates it, and the two views are close to one another.

Allah, the Exalted, commanded fulfilling the due measure in measure and weight, for He said: {Give full measure when you measure, and weigh with an even balance. That is best and fairest in outcome} [al-Isra: 35], and He said: {Give full measure and weight in justice. We do not burden any soul beyond its capacity} [al-An'am: 152], and He said: {And establish weight in justice, and do not make the balance deficient} [al-Rahman: 9]. And Allah destroyed the people of Shu'ayb (peace be upon him) and annihilated them for their shortchanging people in measure and weight.

Then the Most High said, threatening them: {Do those not think that they will be resurrected for a tremendous Day}? That is, do those people not fear the resurrection and the standing before Him who knows the secrets and the innermost thoughts, on a Day tremendous in its terror, abundant in its dread, momentous in its affair, in which whoever loses is made to enter a scorching fire?

* *

And His saying: {The Day when mankind will stand before the Lord of the worlds}, that is, they will stand barefoot, naked, uncircumcised, in a station that is difficult, distressing, narrow, and constricting for the wrongdoer, and there will overwhelm them, from the matter of Allah, that which strength and senses are powerless before.

Imam Malik said: from Nafi, from Ibn Umar, that the Prophet ﷺ said: "{The Day when mankind will stand before the Lord of the worlds}, until one of them sinks in his sweat up to the middle of his ears."

Al-Bukhari related it, from the hadith of Malik and Abdullah ibn Awn, both of them from Nafi, with it. Muslim also related it through both routes. Likewise it was related by Salih [and Thabit ibn Kaysan], Ayyub ibn Yahya, Abdullah and Ubaydullah the two sons of Umar, and Muhammad ibn Ishaq, from Nafi, from Ibn Umar, with it.

And the wording of Imam Ahmad is: Yazid narrated to us, Ibn Ishaq informed us, from Nafi, from Ibn Umar: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "{The Day when mankind will stand before the Lord of the worlds}, because of the majesty of al-Rahman, Mighty and Majestic, on the Day of Resurrection, until the sweat bridles the men up to the middle of their ears."

Another hadith: Imam Ahmad said: [chain] al-Miqdad, that is, Ibn al-Aswad al-Kindi, said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "When the Day of Resurrection comes, the sun will be brought near to the servants until it is at the distance of a mile or two miles." He said: "Then the sun will melt them, and they will be in sweat according to the measure of their deeds: among them is he whom it reaches to his heels, among them is he whom it reaches to his knees, among them is he whom it reaches to his waist, and among them is he whom it bridles completely."

Muslim related it from al-Hakam ibn Musa, from Yahya ibn Hamzah, and al-Tirmidhi from Suwayd, from Ibn al-Mubarak, both of them from Ibn Jabir, with it.

Another hadith: Imam Ahmad said: [chain] from Abu Umamah: that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "The sun will draw near on the Day of Resurrection to the distance of a mile, and its heat will be increased by so much, such that the vermin will boil from it as pots boil; they will sweat according to the measure of their sins: among them is he who reaches to his ankles, among them is he who reaches to his shins, among them is he who reaches to his waist, and among them is he whom the sweat bridles." Ahmad alone related it.

Another hadith: Imam Ahmad said: [chain] that he heard Uqbah ibn Amir say: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "The sun will draw near to the earth, and the people will sweat: among the people is he whose sweat reaches his heels, among them is he whose sweat reaches halfway up the shin, among them is he whose sweat reaches his knees, among them is he whose sweat reaches the rump, among them is he whose sweat reaches the flank, among them is he whose sweat reaches his shoulders, and among them is he whose sweat reaches the middle of his mouth" (and he gestured with his hand and made it bridle his mouth; I saw the Messenger of Allah ﷺ gesture like this), "and among them is he whom his sweat covers entirely." And he struck with his hand in illustration. Ahmad alone related it.

And in a hadith: that they will stand for seventy years without speaking. And it is said: they will stand for three hundred years. And it is said: they will stand for forty thousand years. And judgment will be passed among them within a period of ten thousand years, as in Sahih Muslim, from Abu Hurayrah, marfu (a report raised in attribution to the Prophet ﷺ): "On a Day whose measure is fifty thousand years."

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] from Abu Hurayrah, who said: The Prophet ﷺ said to Bashir al-Ghifari: "How will you cope on a Day when mankind will stand before the Lord of the worlds for three hundred years of the days of this world, no news coming to them from heaven, and no command being given to them in it?" Bashir said, "It is Allah from whom help is sought." He said, "So when you go to your bed, seek refuge in Allah from the distress of the Day of Resurrection and the evil of the reckoning."

Ibn Jarir related it via the route of Abd al-Salam, with it.

And in Sunan Abi Dawud: that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to seek refuge in Allah from the constriction of the standing place on the Day of Resurrection.

And from Ibn Mas'ud: they will stand for forty years, their heads raised to the sky, no one speaking to them, the sweat having bridled both the righteous among them and the immoral.

And from Ibn Umar: they will stand for a hundred years. Ibn Jarir related both of these.

And in Sunan Abi Dawud, al-Nasa'i, and Ibn Majah, [chain] from A'ishah: that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to open the night prayer by saying "Allahu akbar" ten times, praising Allah ten times, glorifying Him ten times, and seeking His forgiveness ten times, and saying: "O Allah, forgive me and guide me, and provide for me and grant me well being," and he would seek refuge from the constriction of the standing place on the Day of Resurrection.

### Source (Arabic) — review against this

تَفْسِيرُ سُورَةِ الْمُطَفِّفِينَ

وَهِيَ مَدَنِيَّةٌ.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

* *

قَالَ النَّسَائِيُّ وَابْنُ مَاجَهْ: أَخْبَرَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عُقَيْلٍ -زَادَ ابْنُ مَاجَهْ: وَعَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ بِشْرٍ-قَالَا حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ الْحُسَيْنِ بْنِ وَاقِدٍ، حَدَّثَنِي أَبِي، عَنْ يَزِيدَ-هُوَ ابْنُ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ النَّحْوِيِّ، مَوْلَى قُرَيْشٍ-عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: لَمَّا قَدِمَ نَبِيِّ اللَّهِ ﷺ الْمَدِينَةَ كَانُوا مِنْ أَخْبَثِ النَّاسِ كَيْلًا فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ: ﴿وَيْلٌ لِلْمُطَفِّفِينَ﴾ فحسنَّوا الكيلَ بَعْدَ ذَلِكَ .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا جَعْفَرُ بْنُ النَّضْرِ بْنِ حَمَّادٍ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عُبَيْدٍ، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ مُرّة، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ الْحَارِثِ، عَنْ هِلَالِ بْنِ طَلْقٍ قَالَ: بَيْنَا أَنَا أَسِيرُ مَعَ ابْنِ عُمَرَ فَقُلْتُ: مَنْ أَحْسَنُ النَّاسِ هَيْئَةً وَأَوْفَاهُ كَيْلًا؟ أَهْلُ مَكَّةَ أَوِ الْمَدِينَةِ؟ قَالَ: حَقٌّ لَهُمْ، أَمَا سَمِعْتَ اللَّهَ يَقُولُ: ﴿وَيْلٌ لِلْمُطَفِّفِينَ﴾

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو السَّائِبِ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ فُضَيْلٍ، عَنْ ضِرَارٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الْمُكْتِبِ، عَنْ رَجُلٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ قَالَ: قَالَ لَهُ رَجُلٌ: يَا أَبَا عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ، إِنَّ أَهْلَ الْمَدِينَةِ لَيُوَفُّونَ الْكَيْلَ. قَالَ: وَمَا يَمْنَعُهُمْ أَنْ يُوَفُّوا الْكَيْلَ وَقَدْ قَالَ اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ: ﴿وَيْلٌ لِلْمُطَفِّفِينَ﴾ حَتَّى بَلَغَ: ﴿يَوْمَ يَقُومُ النَّاسُ لِرَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ﴾ .

فَالْمُرَادُ بِالتَّطْفِيفِ هَاهُنَا: البَخْس فِي الْمِكْيَالِ وَالْمِيزَانِ، إِمَّا بِالِازْدِيَادِ إِنِ اقْتَضَى مِنَ النَّاسِ، وَإِمَّا بِالنُّقْصَانِ إِنْ قَضَاهم. وَلِهَذَا فَسَّرَ تَعَالَى الْمُطَفِّفِينَ الَّذِينَ وَعَدَهُمْ بالخَسَار والهَلاك وَهُوَ الْوَيْلُ، بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿الَّذِينَ إِذَا اكْتَالُوا عَلَى النَّاسِ﴾ أَيْ: مِنَ النَّاسِ ﴿يَسْتَوْفُونَ﴾ أَيْ: يَأْخُذُونَ حَقَّهُمْ بِالْوَافِي وَالزَّائِدِ، ﴿وَإِذَا كَالُوهُمْ أَوْ وَزَنُوهُمْ يُخْسِرُونَ﴾ أَيْ: يَنْقِصُونَ. وَالْأَحْسَنُ أَنْ يُجْعَلَ "كَالُوا" وَ "وَزَنِوا" مُتَعَدِّيًا، وَيَكُونُ هُمْ فِي مَحَلِّ نَصْبٍ، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَجْعَلُهَا ضَمِيرًا مُؤَكِّدًا لِلْمُسْتَتِرِ فِي قَوْلِهِ: "كَالُوا" وَ "وَزَنُوا"، وَيُحْذَفُ الْمَفْعُولُ لِدَلَالَةِ الْكَلَامِ عليه، وكلاهما متقارب.

وَقَدْ أَمَرَ اللَّهُ-تَعَالَى-بِالْوَفَاءِ فِي الْكَيْلِ وَالْمِيزَانِ، فَقَالَ: ﴿وَأَوْفُوا الْكَيْلَ إِذَا كِلْتُمْ وَزِنُوا بِالْقِسْطَاسِ الْمُسْتَقِيمِ ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌ وَأَحْسَنُ تَأْوِيلا﴾ [الْإِسْرَاءِ: ٣٥] ، وَقَالَ: ﴿وَأَوْفُوا الْكَيْلَ وَالْمِيزَانَ بِالْقِسْطِ لَا نُكَلِّفُ نَفْسًا إِلا وُسْعَهَا﴾ [الْأَنْعَامِ: ١٥٢] ، وَقَالَ: ﴿وَأَقِيمُوا الْوَزْنَ بِالْقِسْطِ وَلا تُخْسِرُوا الْمِيزَانَ﴾ [الرَّحْمَنِ: ٩] . وَأَهْلَكَ اللَّهُ قَوْمَ شُعَيْبٍ ودَمَّرهم عَلَى مَا كَانُوا يَبْخَسُونَ النَّاسَ فِي الْمِكْيَالِ وَالْمِيزَانِ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ تَعَالَى مُتَوَعِّدًا لَهُمْ: ﴿أَلا يَظُنُّ أُولَئِكَ أَنَّهُمْ مَبْعُوثُونَ لِيَوْمٍ عَظِيمٍ﴾ ؟ أَيْ: أَمَا يخافُ أُولَئِكَ مِنَ الْبَعْثِ وَالْقِيَامِ بَيْنَ يَدَي مَنْ يَعْلَمُ السَّرَائِرَ وَالضَّمَائِرَ، فِي يَوْمٍ عَظِيمِ الْهَوْلِ، كَثِيرِ الْفَزَعِ، جَلِيلِ الْخَطْبِ، مَنْ خَسِرَ فِيهِ أُدْخِلَ نَارًا حَامِيَةً؟

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿يَوْمَ يَقُومُ النَّاسُ لِرَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ﴾ أَيْ: يَقُومُونَ حُفَاةً عُرَاةً غُرلا فِي مَوْقِفٍ صَعْبٍ حَرج ضَيِّقٍ ضنَك عَلَى الْمُجْرِمِ، وَيَغْشَاهُمْ مِنْ أَمْرِ اللَّهِ-مَا تَعْجزُ الْقُوَى وَالْحَوَاسُّ عَنْهُ.

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ مَالِكٌ: عَنْ نَافِعٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ أَنَّ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ قَالَ: " ﴿يَوْمَ يَقُومُ النَّاسُ لِرَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ﴾ حَتَّى يَغِيبَ أَحَدُهُمْ فِي رَشْحِهِ إِلَى أَنْصَافِ أُذُنَيْهِ".

رَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ مَالِكٍ وَعَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَوْنٍ، كِلَاهُمَا عَنْ نَافِعٍ، بِهِ . وَرَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ مِنَ الطَّرِيقَيْنِ أَيْضًا. وَكَذَلِكَ رَوَاهُ صَالِحٌ [وَثَابِتُ بْنُ كَيْسَانَ] وَأَيُّوبُ بْنُ يَحْيَى، وَعَبْدُ اللَّهِ وَعُبَيْدُ اللَّهِ ابْنَا عُمَرَ، وَمُحَمَّدُ بْنُ إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ نَافِعٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ، بِهِ .

وَلَفْظُ الْإِمَامِ أَحْمَدَ: حَدَّثَنَا يَزِيدُ، أَخْبَرَنَا ابْنُ إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ نَافِعٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم يَقُولُ::" ﴿يَوْمَ يَقُومُ النَّاسُ لِرَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ﴾ لعظَمة الرَّحْمَنِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ، حَتَّى إِنَّ العرقَ ليُلجِمُ الرجالَ إِلَى أَنْصَافِ آذَانِهِمْ" .

حَدِيثٌ آخَرُ: قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا إِبْرَاهِيمُ بْنُ إِسْحَاقَ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ الْمُبَارَكِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ يَزِيدَ بْنِ جَابِرٍ، حَدَّثَنِي سُلَيْمُ بْنُ عَامِرٍ، حَدَّثَنِي الْمِقْدَادُ-يَعْنِي ابْنَ الْأَسْوَدِ الْكِنْدِيَّ-قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "إِذَا كَانَ يومُ الْقِيَامَةِ أدنِيَت الشَّمْسُ مِنَ الْعِبَادِ، حَتَّى تَكُونَ قيدَ مِيلٍ أَوْ مِيلَيْنِ، قَالَ: فَتُصْهِرُهُمُ الشَّمْسُ، فَيَكُونُونَ فِي العَرق كقَدْر أَعْمَالِهِمْ، مِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَأْخُذُهُ إِلَى عَقِبيه، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَأْخُذُهُ إِلَى رُكْبَتَيْهِ، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَأْخُذُهُ إِلَى حَقْوَيه، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يُلْجِمُهُ إِلْجَامًا".

رَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ، عَنِ الْحَكَمِ بْنِ مُوسَى، عَنْ يَحْيَى بْنِ حَمْزَةَ-وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ، عَنْ سُوَيْدٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ الْمُبَارَكِ-كِلَاهُمَا عَنِ ابْنِ جَابِرٍ، بِهِ .

حَدِيثٌ آخَرُ: قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا الْحَسَنُ بْنُ سَوَّار، حَدَّثَنَا اللَّيْثُ بْنُ سَعْدٍ، عن معاوية ابن صَالِحٍ: أَنَّ أَبَا عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ حَدَّثَهُ، عَنْ أَبِي أُمَامَةَ: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "تَدْنُو الشَّمْسُ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ عَلَى قَدْرِ مِيلٍ، وَيُزَادُ فِي حَرِّهَا كَذَا وَكَذَا، تَغْلِي مِنْهَا الْهَوَامُّ كَمَا تَغْلِي الْقُدُورُ، يُعرَقون فِيهَا عَلَى قَدْرِ خَطَايَاهُمْ، مِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَبْلُغُ إِلَى كَعْبَيْهِ، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَبْلُغُ إِلَى سَاقَيْهِ، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَبْلُغُ إِلَى وَسَطِهِ، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يُلْجِمُهُ الْعَرَقُ". انْفَرَدَ بِهِ أَحْمَدُ .

حَدِيثٌ آخَرُ: قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا حَسَنٌ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ لَهِيعة، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو عُشَّانة حَي بْنُ يُؤمِنُ، أَنَّهُ سَمِعَ عُقْبَةَ بْنَ عَامِرٍ يَقُولُ: سمعتُ رَسُولَ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "تَدْنُو الشَّمْسُ مِنَ الْأَرْضِ فَيَعْرَقُ النَّاسُ، فَمِنَ النَّاسِ مَنْ يَبْلُغُ عَرَقُهُ عَقِبيه، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَبْلُغُ إِلَى نِصْفِ السَّاقِ، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَبْلُغُ إِلَى رُكْبَتَيْهِ، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَبْلُغُ العَجُز، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَبْلُغُ الْخَاصِرَةَ، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَبْلُغُ مَنْكِبَيْهِ، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَبْلُغُ وَسَطَ فِيهِ-وَأَشَارَ بِيَدِهِ فَأَلْجَمَهَا فَاهُ، رَأَيْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يُشِيرُ هَكَذَا-وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يُغَطِّيهِ عَرَقُهُ". وَضَرَبَ بِيَدِهِ إِشَارَةً. انْفَرَدَ بِهِ أَحْمَدُ .

وَفِي حَدِيثٍ: أَنَّهُمْ يَقُومُونَ سَبْعِينَ سَنَةً لَا يَتَكَلَّمُونَ. وَقِيلَ: يَقُومُونَ ثَلَاثُمِائَةِ سَنَةٍ. وَقِيلَ: يَقُومُونَ أَرْبَعِينَ أَلْفَ سَنَةٍ. وَيُقْضَى بَيْنَهُمْ فِي مِقْدَارِ عَشْرَةِ آلَافِ سَنَةٍ، كَمَا فِي صَحِيحِ مُسْلِمٍ عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيرة مَرْفُوعًا: "فِي يَوْمٍ كَانَ مِقْدَارُهُ خَمْسِينَ أَلْفَ سَنَةٍ" .

وَقَدْ قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو عَوْنِ الزِّيَادَيُّ، أَخْبَرَنَا عَبْدُ السَّلَامِ بْنُ عَجْلان، سَمِعْتُ أَبَا يَزِيدَ الْمَدَنِيَّ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ: قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ لِبَشِيرِ الْغِفَارِيِّ: "كَيْفَ أَنْتَ صَانِعٌ فِي يَوْمٍ يَقُومُ النَّاسُ فِيهِ ثَلَاثُمِائَةَ سَنَةٍ لِرَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ، مِنْ أَيَّامِ الدُّنْيَا، لَا يَأْتِيهِمْ فِيهِ خَبَرٌ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ وَلَا يُؤْمَرُ فِيهِ بِأَمْرٍ؟ ". قَالَ بَشِيرٌ: الْمُسْتَعَانُ اللَّهُ. قَالَ: "فَإِذَا أَوَيْتَ إِلَى فِرَاشِكَ فَتَعَوَّذْ بِاللَّهِ مِنْ كَرْب يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ، وَسُوءِ الْحِسَابِ".

وَرَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ مِنْ طَرِيقِ عَبْدِ السَّلَامِ، بِهِ .

وَفِي سُنَنِ أَبِي دَاوُدَ: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم كان يَتَعَوَّذُ بِاللَّهِ مِنْ ضِيقِ الْمَقَامِ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ .

وَعَنِ ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ: يَقُومُونَ أَرْبَعِينَ سَنَةً رَافِعِي رُءُوسِهِمْ إِلَى السَّمَاءِ، لَا يُكَلِّمُهُمْ أَحَدٌ، قَدْ أَلْجَمَ الْعَرَقُ بَرّهم وَفَاجِرَهُمْ.

وَعَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ: يَقُومُونَ مِائَةَ سَنَةٍ. رَوَاهُمَا ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ .

وَفِي سُنَنِ أَبِي دَاوُدَ وَالنَّسَائِيِّ وَابْنِ مَاجَهْ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ زَيْدِ بْنِ الْحُبَابِ، عَنْ مُعَاوِيَةَ بْنِ

صالح، عَنْ أَزْهَرَ بْنِ سَعِيدٍ الْحَوَارِيِّ، عَنْ عَاصِمِ بْنِ حُمَيْدٍ، عَنْ عَائِشَةَ: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ كَانَ يَفْتَتِحُ قِيَامَ اللَّيْلِ: يَكَبِّرُ عَشْرًا، وَيَحْمَدُ عَشْرًا، وَيُسَبِّحُ عَشْرًا، وَيَسْتَغْفِرُ عَشْرًا، وَيَقُولُ: "اللَّهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِي وَاهْدِنِي، وَارْزُقْنِي وَعَافِنِي". وَيَتَعَوَّذُ مِنْ ضِيقِ الْمَقَامِ يَوْمَ القيامة .

## 83:7-17 — verses 7-17 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He, Exalted is He, says, truly: {Indeed the record of the wicked is in Sijjin}, that is: their destiny and their abode are truly in Sijjin, a fi''il (an intensive noun pattern) from al-sajn (imprisonment/confinement), which means narrowness, just as one says fissiq (one exceedingly dissolute), shirriyb (one who drinks excessively), khimmir (one addicted to wine), sikkir (one habitually drunk), and the like. Therefore its matter was magnified, so He said: {And what will make you know what Sijjin is?} that is: it is a tremendous matter, an abiding imprisonment and a painful punishment.

Then some have said: it is beneath the seventh earth. This has already preceded in the hadith of al-Bara' ibn 'Azib, in his long hadith: Allah, Mighty and Majestic, says concerning the soul of the disbeliever: "Write his record in Sijjin."

And Sijjin: it is beneath the seventh earth. And it is said: a green rock beneath the seventh earth. And it is said: a well in Hell.

Ibn Jarir narrated concerning that a gharib (unusual), munkar (rejected/objectionable) hadith that is not sound. He said: [chain] ... from Muhammad ibn Ka'b al-Qurazi, from Abu Hurayrah, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "al-Falaq is a covered pit in Hell, while Sijjin is open."

And the correct view is that "Sijjin" is taken from al-sajn, which is narrowness. For among created things, whatever descends lower becomes narrower, and whatever rises higher becomes wider. The seven celestial spheres, each one of them is wider and higher than the one beneath it, and likewise the earths, each one is wider than the one beneath it, until the absolute lowest point and the narrowest place ends at the center, in the middle of the seventh earth. And since the destiny of the wicked is to Hell, which is the lowest of the low, as Allah, Exalted is He, said: {Then We returned him to the lowest of the low, except those who believe and do righteous deeds} [al-Tin: 5-6]. And He said here: {No indeed! The record of the wicked is truly in Sijjin, and what will make you know what Sijjin is?} which combines narrowness and lowness, just as He said: {And when they are thrown into a narrow place therein, bound together, they will cry out there for destruction} [al-Furqan: 13].

* *

His saying: {A written record} is not an explanation of His saying {And what will make you know what Sijjin is?}. Rather it is an explanation of what has been decreed for them of the destiny to Sijjin, that is: inscribed, written, already settled and finished with, no one is added to it nor is anyone diminished from it. This was said by Muhammad ibn Ka'b al-Qurazi.

Then He said: {Woe that day to the deniers}, that is: when on the Day of Resurrection they come to what Allah has threatened them with of imprisonment and humiliating punishment. The discussion of His saying {woe} has already preceded in a way that makes repeating it unnecessary, and that what is meant by it is destruction and ruin, just as one says "woe to so-and-so." And as has come in al-Musnad and al-Sunan through the narration of Bahz ibn Hakim ibn Mu'awiyah ibn Haydah, [chain] from his father, from his grandfather, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Woe to the one who speaks and lies to make people laugh, woe to him, woe to him."

Then Allah, Exalted is He, said, explaining who these deniers, the wicked disbelievers, are: {Those who deny the Day of Judgment}, that is: they do not believe it will occur, nor do they hold to its reality, and they regard the matter of it as far-fetched. Allah, Exalted is He, said: {And none deny it except every transgressor, sinful one}, that is: a transgressor in his deeds, indulging in what is forbidden and overstepping bounds in his use of what is permitted, and sinful in his words: if he speaks he lies, if he promises he breaks his word, and if he disputes he acts wickedly.

* *

His saying: {When Our verses are recited to him, he says, "Legends of the ancients"}, that is: when he hears the speech of Allah from the Messenger, he denies it and thinks ill of it, believing that it is fabricated, gathered from the books of the ancients, just as Allah, Exalted is He, said: {And when it is said to them, "What has your Lord sent down?" they say, "Legends of the ancients"} [al-Nahl: 24], and He said: {And they say, "Legends of the ancients which he has had written down, and they are dictated to him morning and evening"} [al-Furqan: 5]. Allah, Exalted is He, said: {No indeed! Rather, what they used to earn has covered their hearts with rayn (a rust-like covering that seals the heart)}, that is: the matter is not as they claimed nor as they said, that this Qur'an is legends of the ancients. Rather it is the speech of Allah, His revelation and His sending down upon His Messenger ﷺ. What has veiled their hearts from believing in it is only the rayn that lies upon them, which has clothed their hearts because of the abundance of sins and offenses. That is why Allah, Exalted is He, said: {No indeed! Rather, what they used to earn has covered their hearts with rayn}. And al-rayn afflicts the hearts of the disbelievers, while al-ghaym (a lighter, cloud-like covering) is for the righteous (al-abrar), and al-ghayn (a still fainter covering) is for those brought near (al-muqarrabun).

Ibn Jarir, al-Tirmidhi, al-Nasa'i, and Ibn Majah narrated through several routes, [chain] ... from Abu Hurayrah, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "When the servant commits a sin, a black mark appears on his heart. If he repents from it, his heart is polished, but if he adds more, it increases. That is the saying of Allah: {No indeed! Rather, what they used to earn has covered their hearts with rayn}."

Al-Tirmidhi said: hasan sahih (good and authentic). And the wording of al-Nasa'i is: "When the servant commits an error, a mark is imprinted on his heart. If he desists, seeks forgiveness, and repents, his heart is polished. But if he returns, it is increased in it until it covers his heart entirely, and that is the rayn which Allah mentioned: {No indeed! Rather, what they used to earn has covered their hearts with rayn}."

Ahmad said: [chain] ... from Abu Hurayrah, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "When the believer sins, a black mark appears on his heart. If he repents, desists, and seeks forgiveness, his heart is polished. But if he adds more, it increases until it covers his heart entirely, and that is the rayn which Allah mentioned in the Qur'an: {No indeed! Rather, what they used to earn has covered their hearts with rayn}."

Al-Hasan al-Basri said: it is sin upon sin, until the heart becomes blind, and then it dies. And likewise said Mujahid ibn Jabr, Qatadah, Ibn Zayd, and others.

* *

His saying: {No indeed! Truly, that day they will be veiled from their Lord}, that is: on the Day of Resurrection they will have as their dwelling and abode Sijjin, and then, on the Day of Resurrection, along with that, they will be veiled from seeing their Lord and Creator.

The Imam Abu 'Abdillah al-Shafi'i said: in this verse is a proof that the believers will see Him, Mighty and Majestic, on that day.

And what Imam al-Shafi'i, may Allah have mercy on him, said is of the utmost soundness. It is an inference from the mafhum (implied meaning) of this verse, just as it is indicated by the mantuq (explicit wording) of His saying: {Faces that Day will be radiant, looking at their Lord} [al-Qiyamah: 22-23]. And just as it is indicated by the sound, mutawatir (mass-transmitted) hadiths concerning the believers seeing their Lord, Mighty and Majestic, in the Hereafter, a seeing with the eyes in the open grounds of the Resurrection, and in the splendid gardens of Paradise.

Ibn Jarir [Muhammad ibn 'Ammar al-Razi] said: [chain] ... from al-Hasan, concerning His saying: {No indeed! Truly, that day they will be veiled from their Lord}, he said: the veil will be lifted, so the believers and the disbelievers will look at Him, then the disbelievers will be veiled from Him while the believers continue to look at Him, every day morning and evening, or words to that effect.

His saying: {Then indeed, they will burn in the Blazing Fire}, that is: then they, along with this deprivation of seeing the Most Merciful, are among the people of the fires. {Then it will be said, "This is what you used to deny"}, that is: this will be said to them by way of rebuke and reproach, and belittlement and disparagement.

وَسَجِّينٌ: هِيَ تَحْتَ الْأَرْضِ السَّابِعَةِ. وَقِيلَ: صَخْرَةٌ تَحْتَ السَّابِعَةِ خَضْرَاءُ. وَقِيلَ: بِئْرٌ فِي جَهَنَّمَ.

وَقَدْ رَوَى ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ فِي ذَلِكَ حَدِيثًا غَرِيبًا مُنْكَرًا لَا يَصِحُّ فَقَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا إِسْحَاقُ بْنُ وَهْبٍ الْوَاسِطِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا مسعود بن موسى بن مُشكان الواسطي، حدثنا نَصر بْنُ خُزَيمة الْوَاسِطِيُّ، عَنْ شُعَيْبِ بْنِ صَفْوَانَ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ كَعْبٍ الْقُرَظِيِّ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ قَالَ: "الْفَلَقُ: جُبٌّ فِي جَهَنَّمَ مُغَطَّى، وَأَمَّا سِجِّينٌ فَمَفْتُوحٌ" .

وَالصَّحِيحُ أَنْ "سِجِّينًا" مَأْخُوذٌ مِنَ السَّجن، وَهُوَ الضِّيقُ، فَإِنَّ الْمَخْلُوقَاتِ كُلُّ مَا تَسَافَلَ مِنْهَا ضَاقَ، وَكُلُّ مَا تَعَالَى مِنْهَا اتَّسَعَ، فَإِنَّ الْأَفْلَاكَ السَّبْعَةَ كُلُّ وَاحِدٍ مِنْهَا أَوْسَعُ وَأَعْلَى مِنَ الَّذِي دُونَهُ، وَكَذَلِكَ الْأَرْضُونَ كُلُّ وَاحِدَةٍ أَوْسَعُ مِنَ الَّتِي دُونَهَا، حَتَّى يَنْتَهِيَ السُّفُولُ الْمُطْلَقُ وَالْمَحَلُّ الْأَضْيَقُ إِلَى الْمَرْكَزِ فِي وَسَطِ الْأَرْضِ السَّابِعَةِ. وَلَمَّا كَانَ مَصِيرُ الْفُجَّارِ إِلَى جَهَنَّمَ وَهِيَ أَسْفَلُ السَّافِلِينَ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿ثُمَّ رَدَدْنَاهُ أَسْفَلَ سَافِلِينَ إِلا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ﴾ [التِّينِ] : ٥، ٦] . وَقَالَ هَاهُنَا: ﴿كَلا إِنَّ كِتَابَ الْفُجَّارِ لَفِي سِجِّينٍ وَمَا أَدْرَاكَ مَا سِجِّينٌ﴾ وَهُوَ يَجْمَعُ الضِّيقَ والسفول، كما قال: ﴿وَإِذَا أُلْقُوا مِنْهَا مَكَانًا ضَيِّقًا مُقَرَّنِينَ دَعَوْا هُنَالِكَ ثُبُورًا﴾ [الْفُرْقَانِ: ١٣] .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿كِتَابٌ مَرْقُومٌ﴾ لَيْسَ تَفْسِيرًا لِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَمَا أَدْرَاكَ مَا سِجِّينٌ﴾ وَإِنَّمَا هُوَ تَفْسِيرٌ لِمَا كُتِبَ لَهُمْ مِنَ الْمَصِيرِ إِلَى سِجِّينٍ، أَيْ: مَرْقُومٌ مَكْتُوبٌ مَفْرُوغٌ مِنْهُ، لَا يُزَادُ فِيهِ أَحَدٌ وَلَا يُنْقَصُ مِنْهُ أَحَدٌ؛ قَالَهُ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ كَعْبٍ الْقُرَظِيُّ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿وَيْلٌ يَوْمَئِذٍ لِلْمُكَذِّبِينَ﴾ أَيْ: إِذَا صَارُوا يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ إِلَى مَا أَوْعَدَهُمُ اللَّهُ مِنَ السَّجن وَالْعَذَابِ الْمُهِينِ. وَقَدْ تَقَدَّمَ الْكَلَامُ عَلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَيْلٌ﴾ بِمَا أَغْنَى عَنْ إِعَادَتِهِ، وَأَنَّ الْمُرَادَ مِنْ ذَلِكَ الْهَلَاكُ وَالدَّمَارُ، كَمَا يُقَالُ: وَيْلٌ لِفُلَانٍ. وَكَمَا جَاءَ فِي الْمُسْنَدِ وَالسُّنَنِ مِنْ رِوَايَةِ بَهْز بْنِ حَكِيمِ بْنِ مُعَاوِيَةَ بْنِ حَيَدة، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ جَدِّهِ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "وَيْلٌ لِلَّذِي يُحَدِّث فَيَكْذِبُ، ليضحِكَ النَّاسَ، وَيْلٌ لَهُ، وَيْلٌ لَهُ" .

ثُمَّ قَالَ تَعَالَى مُفَسِّرًا لِلْمُكَذِّبِينَ الْفُجَّارِ الْكَفَرَةِ: ﴿الَّذِينَ يُكَذِّبُونَ بِيَوْمِ الدِّينِ﴾ أَيْ: لَا يُصَدِّقُونَ بِوُقُوعِهِ، وَلَا يَعْتَقِدُونَ كَوْنَهُ، وَيَسْتَبْعِدُونَ أَمْرَهُ. قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَمَا يُكَذِّبُ بِهِ إِلا كُلُّ مُعْتَدٍ أَثِيمٍ﴾ أَيْ: مُعْتَدٍ فِي أَفْعَالِهِ؛ مِنْ تَعَاطِي الْحَرَامِ وَالْمُجَاوَزَةِ فِي تَنَاوُلِ الْمُبَاحِ وَالْأَثِيمِ فِي أَقْوَالِهِ: إِنْ حَدَّثَ كَذَبَ، وَإِنْ وَعَدَ أَخْلَفَ، وَإِنْ خَاصَمَ فَجَرَ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِذَا تُتْلَى عَلَيْهِ آيَاتُنَا قَالَ أَسَاطِيرُ الأوَّلِينَ﴾ أَيْ: إِذَا سَمِعَ كَلَامَ اللَّهِ مِنَ الرَّسُولِ، يُكَذِّبُ بِهِ، وَيَظُنُّ بِهِ ظَنَّ السَّوْءِ، فَيَعْتَقِدُ أَنَّهُ مُفْتَعَلٌ مَجْمُوعٌ مِنْ كُتُبِ الْأَوَائِلِ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَإِذَا قِيلَ لَهُمْ مَاذَا أَنزلَ رَبُّكُمْ قَالُوا أَسَاطِيرُ الأوَّلِينَ﴾ [النَّحْلِ: ٢٤] ، وَقَالَ: ﴿وَقَالُوا أَسَاطِيرُ الأوَّلِينَ اكْتَتَبَهَا فَهِيَ تُمْلَى عَلَيْهِ بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلا﴾ [الْفُرْقَانِ: ٥] ، قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿كَلا بَلْ رَانَ عَلَى قُلُوبِهِمْ مَا كَانُوا يَكْسِبُونَ﴾ أَيْ: لَيْسَ الْأَمْرُ كَمَا زَعَمُوا وَلَا كَمَا قَالُوا، إِنَّ هَذَا الْقُرْآنَ أَسَاطِيرُ الْأَوَّلِينَ، بَلْ هُوَ كَلَامُ اللَّهِ وَوَحْيُهُ وَتَنْزِيلُهُ عَلَى رَسُولِهِ ﷺ، وَإِنَّمَا حَجَبَ قُلُوبَهُمْ عَنِ الْإِيمَانِ بِهِ مَا عَلَيْهَا مِنَ الرَّيْن الَّذِي قَدْ لَبِسَ قُلُوبَهُمْ مِنْ كَثْرَةِ الذُّنُوبِ وَالْخَطَايَا؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿كَلا بَلْ رَانَ عَلَى قُلُوبِهِمْ مَا كَانُوا يَكْسِبُونَ﴾ وَالرَّيْنُ يَعْتَرِي قلوبَ الْكَافِرِينَ، وَالْغَيْمُ لِلْأَبْرَارِ، وَالْغَيْنُ لِلْمُقَرَّبِينَ.

وَقَدْ رَوَى ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ وَالنَّسَائِيُّ وَابْنُ مَاجَهْ مِنْ طُرُقٍ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عَجْلان، عَنِ الْقَعْقَاعِ بْنِ حَكِيمٍ، عَنْ أَبِي صَالِحٍ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ قَالَ: "إِنَّ الْعَبْدَ إِذَا أَذْنَبَ ذَنْبًا كَانَتْ نُكْتَةٌ سَوْدَاءُ فِي قَلْبِهِ، فَإِنْ تَابَ مِنْهَا صُقِلَ قَلْبُهُ، وَإِنْ زَادَ زَادَتْ، فَذَلِكَ قَوْلُ اللَّهِ: ﴿كَلا بَلْ رَانَ عَلَى قُلُوبِهِمْ مَا كَانُوا يَكْسِبُونَ﴾ .

وَقَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ. وَلَفْظُ النَّسَائِيِّ: "إِنَّ الْعَبْدَ إِذَا أَخْطَأَ خَطِيئَةً نُكِت فِي قَلْبِهِ نُكْتَةٌ، فَإِنْ هُوَ نَزَعَ وَاسْتَغْفَرَ وَتَابَ صُقِل قَلْبُهُ، فَإِنْ عَادَ زِيدَ فِيهَا حَتَّى تَعْلُوَ قَلْبَهُ، فَهُوَ الرَّانُ الَّذِي قال الله: ﴿كَلا بَلْ رَانَ عَلَى قُلُوبِهِم مَّا كَانُوا يَكْسِبُونَ﴾

وَقَالَ أَحْمَدُ:حَدَّثَنَا صَفْوَانُ بْنُ عِيسَى، أَخْبَرَنَا ابْنُ عَجْلان، عَنِ الْقَعْقَاعِ بْنِ حَكِيمٍ، عَنْ أَبِي صَالِحٍ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ الْمُؤْمِنَ إِذَا أَذْنَبَ كَانَتْ نُكْتَةٌ سَوْدَاءُ فِي قَلْبِهِ، فَإِنْ تَابَ وَنَزَعَ وَاسْتَغْفَرَ صُقِل قَلْبُهُ، فَإِنْ زَادَ زَادَتْ حَتَّى تَعْلُوَ قَلْبَهُ، وَذَاكَ الرَّانُ الَّذِي ذَكَرَ اللَّهُ فِي الْقُرْآنِ: ﴿كَلا بَلْ رَانَ عَلَى قُلُوبِهِمْ مَا كَانُوا يَكْسِبُونَ﴾ " .

وَقَالَ الْحَسَنُ الْبَصْرِيُّ:هُوَ الذَّنْبُ عَلَى الذَّنْبِ، حَتَّى يَعْمَى الْقَلْبُ، فَيَمُوتُ. وَكَذَا قال مجاهد ابن جَبْرٍ وَقَتَادَةُ، وَابْنُ زَيْدٍ، وَغَيْرُهُمْ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿كَلا إِنَّهُمْ عَنْ رَبِّهِمْ يَوْمَئِذٍ لَمَحْجُوبُونَ﴾ أَيْ: لَهُمْ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ مَنزلٌ وَنُزُلٌ سِجِّينٌ، ثُمَّ هُمْ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ مَعَ ذَلِكَ مَحْجُوبُونَ عَنْ رُؤْيَةِ رَبِّهِمْ وَخَالِقِهِمْ.

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَبُو عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الشَّافِعِيُّ: [فِي] هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ دَلِيلٌ عَلَى أَنَّ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ يَرَوْنَهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ يَوْمَئِذٍ .

وَهَذَا الَّذِي قَالَهُ الْإِمَامُ الشَّافِعِيُّ، رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ، فِي غَايَةِ الْحُسْنِ، وَهُوَ اسْتِدْلَالٌ بِمَفْهُومِ هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ، كَمَا دَلَّ عَلَيْهِ مَنْطُوقُ قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وُجُوهٌ يَوْمَئِذٍ نَاضِرَةٌ إِلَى رَبِّهَا نَاظِرَةٌ﴾ [الْقِيَامَةِ: ٢٢، ٢٣] . وَكَمَا دَلَّتْ عَلَى ذَلِكَ الْأَحَادِيثُ الصِّحَاحُ الْمُتَوَاتِرَةُ فِي رُؤْيَةِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ رَبَّهُمْ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ فِي الدَّارِ الْآخِرَةِ، رُؤْيَةٌ بِالْأَبْصَارِ فِي عَرَصات الْقِيَامَةِ، وَفِي رَوْضَاتِ الْجِنَانِ الْفَاخِرَةِ.

وَقَدْ قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ [مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عَمَّارٍ الرَّازِيُّ] : حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو مَعْمَرٍ المنْقَريّ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الْوَارِثِ ابن سَعِيدٍ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ عُبَيْدٍ، عَنِ الْحَسَنِ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿كَلا إِنَّهُمْ عَنْ رَبِّهِمْ يَوْمَئِذٍ لَمَحْجُوبُونَ﴾ قَالَ: يُكْشَفُ الْحِجَابُ، فَيَنْظُرُ إِلَيْهِ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ وَالْكَافِرُونَ، ثُمَّ يُحْجَبُ عَنْهُ الْكَافِرُونَ وَيَنْظُرُ إِلَيْهِ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ. كُلّ يَوْمٍ غُدْوَةً وَعَشِيَّةً-أَوْ كَلَامًا هَذَا مَعْنَاهُ.

قَوْلُهُ: ﴿ثُمَّ إِنَّهُمْ لَصَالُو الْجَحِيمِ﴾ أَيْ: ثُمَّ هُمْ مَعَ هَذَا الْحِرْمَانِ عَنْ رُؤْيَةِ الرَّحْمَنِ مِنْ أَهْلِ النِّيرَانِ، ﴿ثُمَّ يُقَالُ هَذَا الَّذِي كُنْتُمْ بِهِ تُكَذِّبُونَ﴾ أَيْ: يُقَالُ لَهُمْ ذَلِكَ عَلَى وَجْهِ التَّقْرِيعِ وَالتَّوْبِيخِ، وَالتَّصْغِيرِ وَالتَّحْقِيرِ.

## 83:18-28 — verses 18-28 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Most High says, truly: {Indeed the record of the righteous} - and they are the opposite of the wicked - {is in 'Illiyyun}, meaning: their destination is to 'Illiyyun (a lofty register), which is the opposite of Sijjin (a base register).

Al-A'mash said, from Shamir ibn 'Atiyyah, from Hilal ibn Yasaf, who said: Ibn 'Abbas asked Ka'b, while I was present, about Sijjin. He said: It is the seventh earth, and in it are the spirits of the disbelievers. And he asked him about 'Illiyyun, and he said: It is the seventh heaven, and in it are the spirits of the believers. And likewise more than one person said: it is the seventh heaven.

'Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn 'Abbas, concerning His saying: {No indeed, the record of the righteous is in 'Illiyyun}: meaning Paradise.

And in the narration of al-'Awfi, from him: their deeds are in heaven, with Allah. And likewise said al-Dahhak.

Qatadah said: 'Illiyyun is the right leg of the Throne. And others said: 'Illiyyun is at Sidrat al-Muntaha (the Lote Tree of the Utmost Boundary).

And the apparent meaning is that 'Illiyyun is taken from al-'uluw (height, elevation); and whenever a thing rises and becomes elevated, it becomes greater and more expansive. Therefore, magnifying its affair and exalting its status, He said: {And what will make you know what 'Illiyyun is?} Then He said, confirming what He has recorded for them: {A written record, witnessed by those brought near} - meaning the muqarrabun (those brought near [to Allah]) - and they are the angels, as Qatadah said.

Al-'Awfi said, from Ibn 'Abbas: it is witnessed, from every heaven, by those brought near within it.

Then the Most High said: {Indeed the righteous are in bliss}, meaning: on the Day of Resurrection they will be in lasting bliss and gardens containing comprehensive favor. {Upon couches} - which are thrones beneath the hijal (canopied enclosures) - {gazing}. It was said: its meaning is that they gaze upon their dominion and what Allah has given them of good and favor that never ends and never perishes. And it was said: the meaning of {Upon couches, gazing} is that they gaze at Allah, Mighty and Majestic. This is a counterpart to what those wicked ones were described with: {No indeed, on that Day they will be veiled from their Lord}. So He mentioned about these that they are permitted to look at Allah, Mighty and Majestic, while they are upon their thrones and their couches, as preceded in the hadith of Ibn 'Umar: "Indeed the lowest of the people of Paradise in rank is the one who looks upon his dominion, a journey of two thousand years, seeing its farthest extent as he sees its nearest; and indeed the highest of them is the one who looks at Allah twice a day."

* *

His saying: {You will recognize in their faces the radiance of bliss}, meaning: you will recognize, if you look at them, in their faces the radiance of bliss, that is: the quality of tenderness, dignity, joy, ease, and eminence, from the great bliss they are in.

* *

His saying: {They will be given to drink from a sealed nectar}, meaning: they will be given to drink from wine of Paradise. And al-rahiq (nectar) is one of the names for wine. This was said by Ibn Mas'ud, Ibn 'Abbas, Mujahid, al-Hasan, Qatadah, and Ibn Zayd.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Abu Sa'id al-Khudri - he (the narrator) thought he had raised it to the Prophet ﷺ - said: "Whichever believer gives another believer a drink to quench his thirst, Allah the Most High will give him to drink from the sealed nectar on the Day of Resurrection. And whichever believer feeds another believer who is hungry, Allah will feed him from the fruits of Paradise. And whichever believer clothes another believer who is naked, Allah will clothe him from the green garments (khudr) of Paradise."

Ibn Mas'ud said concerning His saying: {Its seal is musk}: meaning, its mixture is musk.

Al-'Awfi said, from Ibn 'Abbas: Allah perfumed the wine for them, and the last thing placed into it was musk; it was sealed with musk. And likewise said Qatadah and al-Dahhak.

Ibrahim and al-Hasan said: {Its seal is musk}, meaning: its final outcome is musk.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Abu al-Darda' said, concerning {Its seal is musk}: it is a white drink like silver, with which they seal their drink. And if a man from the people of this world were to put his finger into it and then take it out, there would not remain a living soul but would find its fragrance.

Ibn Abi Najih said, from Mujahid: {Its seal is musk}, he said: its fragrance is musk.

* *

His saying: {And for that let the competitors compete}, meaning: and in the like of this state, let those who vie for glory vie, and let them boast and increase and race toward the like of it, those who race. As in His saying: {For the like of this let the workers work} [al-Saffat: 61].

* *

His saying: {And its mixture is from Tasnim}, meaning: the mixture of this described nectar is from Tasnim, that is, from a drink called Tasnim, and it is the noblest and highest drink of the people of Paradise. This was said by Abu Salih and al-Dahhak. Therefore He said: {A spring from which those brought near drink}, meaning: the muqarrabun drink it pure, undiluted, while for the Companions of the Right it is mixed with a mixture. This was said by Ibn Mas'ud, Ibn 'Abbas, Masruq, Qatadah, and others.

## 83:29-36 — verses 29-36 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Allah, exalted is He, informs about the criminals, that they used to laugh at the believers in the worldly abode, meaning: they would mock them and hold them in contempt, and when they passed by the believers they would wink at one another about them, meaning: holding them in contempt. {And when they returned to their own people, they returned delighting} meaning: when these criminals turned back, that is, returned to their dwellings, they returned to them delighting, meaning: whatever they sought, they found. Yet despite this they did not give thanks for Allah's favor upon them; rather they occupied themselves with the believing people, holding them in contempt and envying them. {And when they saw them, they said: Indeed these are truly astray} meaning: because they were not upon their religion. Allah, exalted is He, said: {and they were not sent as keepers over them} meaning: these criminals were not sent as keepers over these believers, recording what proceeded from their deeds and their words, nor were they made responsible for them. So why did they occupy themselves with them and make them the object of their attention, just as Allah, exalted is He, said: {He will say: Remain despised therein and do not speak to Me. Indeed there was a party of My servants who said: Our Lord, we have believed, so forgive us and have mercy upon us, and You are the best of those who show mercy. But you took them in mockery until they made you forget My remembrance, and you used to laugh at them. Indeed I have rewarded them this day for what they patiently endured; indeed it is they who are the successful ones.} [al-Mu'minun: 108-111].

And it is for this that He said here: {So today} meaning: the Day of Resurrection, {those who believed will laugh at the disbelievers} meaning: in return for what those had laughed at them for, {upon couches, gazing} meaning: gazing at Allah, mighty and majestic is He, in return for those who had claimed about them that they were astray. They are not astray; rather they are among the close friends of Allah, the ones brought near, gazing upon their Lord in the abode of His honoring.

* *

His saying: {Have the disbelievers been rewarded for what they used to do?} meaning: were the disbelievers recompensed for the mockery and belittlement with which they used to meet the believers, or not? That is: they have indeed been recompensed with the fullest, most complete, and most perfect recompense.

End of [the tafsir of Surah] "al-Mutaffifin".

يُخْبِرُ تَعَالَى عَنِ الْمُجْرِمِينَ أَنَّهُمْ كَانُوا فِي الدَّارِ الدُّنْيَا يَضْحَكُونَ مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ، أَيْ: يَسْتَهْزِئُونَ بِهِمْ وَيَحْتَقِرُونَهُمْ وَإِذَا مَرُّوا بِالْمُؤْمِنِينَ يَتَغَامَزُونَ عَلَيْهِمْ، أَيْ:مُحْتَقِرِينَ لَهُمْ، ﴿وَإِذَا انْقَلَبُوا إِلَى أَهْلِهِمُ انْقَلَبُوا فَكِهِينَ﴾ أَيْ: إِذَا انْقَلَبَ، أَيْ: رَجَعَ هَؤُلَاءِ الْمُجْرِمُونَ إِلَى مَنَازِلِهِمُ، انْقَلَبُوا إليها فاكهين، أَيْ: مَهْمَا طَلَبُوا وَجَدُوا، وَمَعَ هَذَا مَا شَكَرُوا نِعْمَةَ اللَّهِ عَلَيْهِمْ، بَلِ اشْتَغَلُوا بِالْقَوْمِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ يَحْتَقِرُونَهُمْ وَيَحْسُدُونَهُمْ، ﴿وَإِذَا رَأَوْهُمْ قَالُوا إِنَّ هَؤُلاءِ لَضَالُّون﴾ أَيْ: لِكَوْنِهِمْ عَلَى غَيْرِ دِينِهِمْ، قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَمَا أُرْسِلُوا عَلَيْهِمْ حَافِظِين﴾ أَيْ: وَمَا بُعث هَؤُلَاءِ الْمُجْرِمُونَ حَافِظِينَ عَلَى هَؤُلَاءِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ مَا يَصْدُرُ مِنْ أَعْمَالِهِمْ وَأَقْوَالِهِمْ، وَلَا كُلِّفُوا بِهِمْ؟ فَلِمَ اشْتَغَلُوا بِهِمْ وَجَعَلُوهُمْ نُصْبَ أَعْيُنِهِمْ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿قَالَ اخْسَئُوا فِيهَا وَلا تُكَلِّمُونِ إِنَّهُ كَانَ فَرِيقٌ مِنْ عِبَادِي يَقُولُونَ رَبَّنَا آمَنَّا فَاغْفِرْ لَنَا وَارْحَمْنَا وَأَنْتَ خَيْرُ الرَّاحِمِينَ فَاتَّخَذْتُمُوهُمْ سِخْرِيًّا حَتَّى أَنْسَوْكُمْ ذِكْرِي وَكُنْتُمْ مِنْهُمْ تَضْحَكُونَ إِنِّي جَزَيْتُهُمُ الْيَوْمَ بِمَا صَبَرُوا أَنَّهُمْ هُمُ الْفَائِزُونَ﴾ [الْمُؤْمِنُونَ: ١٠٨ -١١١] .

وَلِهَذَا قَالَ هَاهُنَا: ﴿فَالْيَوْمَ﴾ يَعْنِي: يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ ﴿الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا مِنَ الْكُفَّارِ يَضْحَكُونَ﴾ أَيْ: فِي مُقَابَلَةِ مَا ضَحِكَ بِهِمْ أُولَئِكَ، ﴿عَلَى الأرَائِكِ يَنْظُرُونَ﴾ أَيْ: إِلَى اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، فِي مُقَابَلَةِ مَنْ زَعَمَ فِيهِمْ أَنَّهُمْ ضَالُّونَ، لَيْسُوا بِضَالِّينَ، بَلْ هُمْ مِنْ أَوْلِيَاءِ اللَّهِ الْمُقَرَّبِينَ، يَنْظُرُونَ إِلَى رَبِّهِمْ فِي دَارِ كَرَامَتِهِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿هَلْ ثُوِّبَ الْكُفَّارُ مَا كَانُوا يَفْعَلُونَ﴾ ؟ أَيْ: هَلْ جُوزِيَ الْكُفَّارُ عَلَى مَا كَانُوا يُقَابِلُونَ بِهِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ مِنَ الِاسْتِهْزَاءِ وَالتَّنَقُّصِ أَمْ لَا؟ يَعْنِي: قَدْ جُوزُوا أَوْفَرَ الْجَزَاءِ وَأَتَمَّهُ وَأَكْمَلَهُ.

آخِرُ [تَفْسِيرُ سُورَةِ] "الْمُطَفِّفِينَ".
