### Translation

# Tafsir of Surat "Nun"

It is Medinan.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

The discussion of the disjointed letters has already preceded at the beginning of Surat al-Baqarah, and it was established that His saying {Nun} is like His saying {Sad} and {Qaf} and similar disjointed letters at the beginnings of surahs. A thorough treatment of the matter was given there which makes repeating it unnecessary.

It has been said that what is meant by His saying {Nun} is a great fish upon the vast encircling current of water, which bears the seven earths, as the Imam Abu Ja'far ibn Jarir said:

[chain] Ibn Abbas said: The first thing Allah created was the Pen. He said: "Write." It said: "What shall I write?" He said: "Write the decree (al-qadar)." So it recorded what would occur from that day until the establishment of the Hour. Then He created "the Nun" and raised the vapor of water, and from it the heaven was split open, and the earth was spread over the back of the Nun. The Nun stirred and the earth shook, so it was made firm with the mountains, and indeed they boast in pride over the earth.

Ibn Abi Hatim likewise reported this from Ahmad ibn Sinan, from Abu Mu'awiyah, from al-A'mash, with the same chain. Shu'bah, Muhammad ibn Fudayl, and Waki' also reported it from al-A'mash similarly. Shu'bah added in his narration: "Then he recited: {Nun. By the pen and what they inscribe.}" Sharik also reported it from al-A'mash, from Abu Zabyan (or Mujahid), from Ibn Abbas, mentioning something similar. Ma'mar reported it from al-A'mash: that Ibn Abbas said... and mentioned it, then recited: {Nun. By the pen and what they inscribe.}

Then Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Ibn Abbas said: The first thing my Lord, Mighty and Majestic, created was the Pen. Then He said to it: "Write." So it wrote what would occur until the establishment of the Hour. Then He created "the Nun" above the water, then pressed the earth down upon it.

Al-Tabarani reported this as a marfu' (elevated) report, saying: [chain] Ibn Abbas said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "The first thing Allah created was the Pen and the Fish (al-Hut). He said to the Pen: 'Write.' It said: 'What shall I write?' He said: 'Everything that will occur until the Day of Resurrection.'" Then he recited: {Nun. By the pen and what they inscribe.} So the Nun is the Fish, and the Pen is the pen.

Another hadith on this matter: Ibn Asakir reported from Abu Abdullah, freedman of Banu Umayyah, from Abu Salih, from Abu Hurayrah: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "The first thing Allah created was the Pen. Then He created 'the Nun,' which is the inkwell (al-dawah). Then He said to it: 'Write.' It said: 'What shall I write?' He said: 'Write what will occur,' or 'what is to occur,' 'of deed, provision, trace, or appointed term.'" So it wrote that until the Day of Resurrection. That is His saying: {Nun. By the pen and what they inscribe.} Then the Pen was sealed and it did not speak until the Day of Resurrection. Then He created the intellect (al-'aql) and said: "By My might, I shall perfect you in whomever I love, and I shall diminish you in whomever I despise."

Ibn Abi Najih said: Ibrahim ibn Abi Bakr informed him, from Mujahid, who said: It used to be said: the Nun is the great fish that is beneath the seventh earth.

Al-Baghawi and a group of exegetes mentioned that upon the back of this fish is a rock whose thickness is like the thickness of the heavens and the earth, and upon its back is a bull with forty thousand horns, and upon its back are the seven earths and what is in them and between them. But Allah knows best. What is strange is that some have applied to this meaning the hadith reported by Imam Ahmad:

[chain] Anas reported that Abdullah ibn Salam heard of the arrival of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ in Medina, so he came to him and asked him about certain things. He said: "I am going to ask you about matters which none but a prophet would know." He said: "What is the first sign of the Hour? What is the first food that the people of Paradise will eat? Why does a child resemble its father, and why does a child resemble its mother?" He said: "Gabriel has just informed me of these." Ibn Salam said: "That one is the enemy of the Jews among the angels." He said: "As for the first sign of the Hour, it is a fire that will gather them from the east to the west. As for the first food the people of Paradise will eat, it is the extra lobe of the fish's liver. As for the child, if the man's fluid precedes the woman's fluid, the child resembles him, and if the woman's fluid precedes the man's fluid, it resembles her."

Al-Bukhari reported this through several chains from Humayd, and Muslim also reported it, and he has something similar from the hadith of Thawban, freedman of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. In Sahih Muslim, from the hadith of Abu Asma' al-Rahabi, from Thawban: a rabbi asked the Messenger of Allah ﷺ several questions, among them he said: "What will be their gift?" meaning the people of Paradise when they enter it. He said: "The extra lobe of the fish's liver." He said: "What will be their food after that?" He said: "The bull of Paradise, which grazed at its edges, will be slaughtered for them." He said: "What will be their drink upon it?" He said: "From a spring therein called Salsabil."

It has also been said that what is meant by His saying {Nun} is a Tablet of light.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Mu'awiyah ibn Qurrah, from his father, said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "{Nun. By the pen and what they inscribe} is a Tablet of light and a Pen of light, flowing with what will occur until the Day of Resurrection." This is a mursal (chain missing the Companion) and gharib (singular) report.

Ibn Jurayj said: I was informed that that pen is of light, and its length is a hundred years.

It has also been said that what is meant by His saying {Nun} is an inkwell, and the Pen is the pen. Ibn Jarir said:

[chain] al-Hasan and Qatadah, regarding His saying {Nun}, said: it is the inkwell.

A marfu' hadith, very gharib, has been reported on this. Ibn Abi Hatim said:

[chain] Abu Hurayrah said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "Allah created the Nun, and it is the inkwell."

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Ibn Abbas said: Allah created the Nun, which is the inkwell, and He created the Pen, and said: "Write." It said: "What shall I write?" He said: "Write what will occur until the Day of Resurrection, of every deed done, whether righteous or sinful, and of every apportioned provision, whether lawful or unlawful." Then He attached to everything of that its condition: its entry into this world, how long it would remain in it, and how it would depart from it. Then He appointed over His servants recording angels (hafazah), and for the Book, keepers (khazzan). The recording angels transcribe each day from the keepers the deeds of that day. When the provision is exhausted, the trace is cut off, and the term is completed, the recording angels come to the keepers seeking that day's deeds, and the keepers say to them: "We find nothing of your companion's with us." So the recording angels return and find that they have died.

Ibn Abbas then said: "Are you not an Arab people who hear the recording angels say {Indeed, We used to transcribe what you used to do} [al-Jathiyah: 29]? Can there be a transcription (istinsakh) except from an original?"

As for His saying {By the pen}, apparently it refers to the genus of the pen with which one writes, as in His saying {Read, and your Lord is the Most Generous, who taught by the pen, taught man what he did not know} [al-'Alaq: 3-5]. So it is an oath from Him, Exalted is He, and a reminder to His creation of the favor He bestowed upon them in teaching writing, by which knowledge is attained. Hence He said {and what they inscribe}. Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, and Qatadah said: meaning, and what they write.

Abu al-Duha reported from Ibn Abbas: {and what they inscribe} meaning, and what they do.

Al-Suddi said: {and what they inscribe} refers to the angels and what they write of the deeds of servants.

Others said: rather, what is meant here by the pen is the one which Allah caused to flow with the decree when He wrote the measures of created things fifty thousand years before He created the heavens and the earths.

They cited in this regard the hadiths that have come down concerning the mention of the Pen. Ibn Abi Hatim said:

[chain] Al-Walid ibn Ubadah ibn al-Samit said: My father summoned me when death approached him, and said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say, "The first thing Allah created was the Pen. He said to it: Write. It said: My Lord, what shall I write? He said: Write the decree, what has been and what will be until eternity."

This hadith was narrated by Imam Ahmad through several chains from al-Walid ibn Ubadah, from his father, with this wording. Al-Tirmidhi transmitted it from the hadith of Abu Dawud al-Tayalisi with this wording, and said: hasan sahih gharib (good, sound, singular). Abu Dawud narrated it in the Book of al-Sunnah of his Sunan, from Ja'far ibn Musafir, from Yahya ibn Hassan, from Ibn Rabah, from Ibrahim ibn Abi 'Ablah, from Abu Hafsah, whose name was Hubaysh ibn Shurayh al-Habashi al-Shami, from Ubadah, and he mentioned it.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Ibn Abbas used to relate that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "The first thing Allah created was the Pen. He commanded it, and it wrote everything." This is gharib (singular) from this route, and none of the compilers of the six books recorded it.

Ibn Abi Najih said, from Mujahid, concerning {By the Pen}: it means that with which the Reminder (al-Dhikr) was written.

His saying, {and what they inscribe (yasturun)}, means: what they write, as has already been stated.

His saying, {You are not, by the favor of your Lord, a madman}: meaning, you are not, and praise belongs to Allah, mad, as the ignorant among your people who reject what you have brought them of guidance and manifest truth used to say, attributing madness to you concerning it. {And indeed for you is a reward uninterrupted}: meaning, rather for you is the immense reward and abundant recompense that does not cease and does not perish, for your conveying the message of your Lord to creation and your patience with their harm. The meaning of {uninterrupted (ghayr mamnun)} is: not cut off, as in His saying, {a gift uninterrupted} [Hud: 108], and {for them is a reward uninterrupted} [al-Tin: 6], meaning: not cut off from them. Mujahid said: {ghayr mamnun} means: not counted, and this returns to what we have said.

His saying, {And indeed you are of a great character (khuluq)}: al-Awfi said, from Ibn Abbas: meaning, and indeed you are upon a great religion, which is Islam. Mujahid, Abu Malik, al-Suddi, al-Rabi' ibn Anas, al-Dahhak, and Ibn Zayd said likewise.

Atiyyah said: upon a great discipline (adab). Ma'mar said, from Qatadah: Aishah was asked about the character of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. She said: His character was the Qur'an, meaning it was as it is in the Qur'an.

Sa'id ibn Abi Arubah said, from Qatadah, concerning His saying {And indeed you are of a great character}: it was mentioned to us that Sa'd ibn Hisham asked Aishah about the character of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. She said: Do you not recite the Qur'an? He said: Yes indeed. She said: Then the character of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was the Qur'an.

Abd al-Razzaq said, from Ma'mar, from Qatadah, from Zurarah ibn Awfa, from Sa'd ibn Hisham, who said: I asked Aishah, saying: Tell me, O Mother of the Believers, about the character of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. She said: Do you recite the Qur'an? I said: Yes. She said: His character was the Qur'an.

This is a long hadith. Imam Muslim narrated it in his Sahih, from the hadith of Qatadah, in its full length, and it will come again in Surat al-Muzzammil, Allah willing.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] al-Hasan said: I asked Aishah about the character of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, and she said: His character was the Qur'an.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] a man from Banu Sawad said: I asked Aishah about the character of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. She said: Do you not recite the Qur'an, {And indeed you are of a great character}? He said: I said: Tell me about that. She said: I prepared food for him, and Hafsah prepared food for him. I said to my servant girl: Go, and if she comes with the food and sets it down first, throw the food away! She said: So she came with the food. She said: The servant girl threw it, and the bowl fell and broke, it was a leather mat. She said: The Messenger of Allah gathered it up and said, "Compensate," or "Compensate her," al-Aswad was uncertain, "with a vessel in place of your vessel." She said: He did not say anything more.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Sa'd ibn Hisham said: I came to Aishah, Mother of the Believers, and said to her: Tell me of the character of the Prophet ﷺ. She said: His character was the Qur'an. Do you not recite {And indeed you are of a great character}?

Abu Dawud and al-Nasa'i narrated similarly from the hadith of al-Hasan.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Jubayr ibn Nufayr said: I performed the pilgrimage and entered upon Aishah, may Allah be pleased with her, and asked her about the character of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. She said: The character of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was the Qur'an.

Ahmad narrated it likewise from Abd al-Rahman ibn Mahdi. Al-Nasa'i narrated it in the Tafsir, from Ishaq ibn Mansur, from Abd al-Rahman ibn Mahdi, from Mu'awiyah ibn Salih, with this chain.

The meaning of this is that, upon him be peace, compliance with the Qur'an, in its commands and prohibitions, became for him an ingrained nature and a character he was molded into, and he abandoned his innate natural disposition. Whatever the Qur'an commanded him he did, and whatever it forbade him he left. This is in addition to what Allah had naturally instilled in him of great character: modesty, generosity, courage, forbearance, and clemency, and every beautiful trait. As is established in the two Sahihs from Anas, who said: I served the Messenger of Allah ﷺ for ten years, and he never said to me "Uff," nor did he ever say of anything I did, "Why did you do it?" nor of anything I did not do, "Why did you not do it?" He ﷺ was the best of people in character. I never touched brocade or silk or anything softer than the palm of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, and I never smelled musk or perfume more fragrant than the sweat of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ.

Al-Bukhari said: [chain] I heard al-Bara say: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ was the most handsome of people in face, and the best of people in character (khalq), neither excessively tall nor short.

The hadiths on this subject are numerous, and Abu Isa al-Tirmidhi has a book on this called al-Shama'il.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Aishah said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ never struck a servant of his with his hand, nor a woman, nor did he ever strike anything with his hand at all, except when striving in the way of Allah. Whenever he was given a choice between two matters, he always chose the easier of the two, so long as it was not a sin. If it was a sin, he was the furthest of people from it. He never took revenge for himself for anything done to him, except when the sacred limits of Allah were violated, in which case he would take revenge for the sake of Allah, Mighty and Majestic.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Abu Hurayrah said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "I was only sent to perfect good character." He alone narrated this.

His saying, {So you will see, and they will see, which of you is the afflicted one (al-maftun)}: meaning, you will come to know, O Muhammad, and those who oppose you and deny you will come to know, who among you and them is the deluded, misguided one. This is like His saying, {They will know tomorrow who is the lying insolent one} [al-Qamar: 26], and like His saying, {And indeed, we or you are surely upon guidance or in manifest error} [Saba: 24].

Ibn Jurayj said: Ibn Abbas said concerning this verse: You will know and they will know on the Day of Resurrection.

Al-Awfi said, from Ibn Abbas, concerning {which of you is al-maftun}: meaning, the madness. Mujahid and others said likewise. Qatadah and others said: {which of you is al-maftun} means: more deserving of Satan.

The meaning of al-maftun is clear: it is the one who has been led astray from the truth and has strayed from it. The ba (the letter "bi") entered in His saying {bi-ayyikum al-maftun} to indicate that the verb in His saying {fasatubsiru wa yubsirun} carries an implied meaning, its sense being: "you will come to know and they will come to know," or "you will be informed and they will be informed, which of you is al-maftun."

And Allah knows best.

Then He, the Exalted, said: {Indeed your Lord, He knows best who has strayed from His path, and He knows best the rightly guided} meaning: He, the Exalted, knows which of the two groups, from you and from them, is the rightly guided one, and He knows the party that has strayed from the truth.

تَفْسِيرُ سُورَةِ "ن"

وَهِيَ مَدَنِيَّةٌ.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

* *

قَدْ تَقَدَّمَ الْكَلَامُ عَلَى حُرُوفِ الْهِجَاءِ فِي أَوَّلِ "سُورَةِ الْبَقَرَةِ"، وَأَنَّ قَوْلَهُ: ﴿ن﴾ كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿ص﴾ ﴿ق﴾ وَنَحْوِ ذَلِكَ مِنَ الْحُرُوفِ الْمُقَطَّعَةِ فِي أَوَائِلِ السُّوَرِ، وَتَحْرِيرُ الْقَوْلُ فِي ذَلِكَ بِمَا أَغْنَى عَنْ إِعَادَتِهِ.

وَقِيلَ: الْمُرَادُ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿ن﴾ حُوتٌ عَظِيمٌ عَلَى تَيَّارِ الْمَاءِ الْعَظِيمِ الْمُحِيطِ، وَهُوَ حَامِلٌ لِلْأَرَضِينَ السَّبْعِ، كَمَا قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَبُو جَعْفَرِ بْنُ جَرِيرٍ:

حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ بَشَّارٍ، حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى، حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ -هُوَ الثَّوْرِيُّ-حَدَّثَنَا سُلَيْمَانُ -هُوَ الْأَعْمَشِ-عَنْ أَبِي ظَبْيان، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: أَوَّلُ مَا خَلَقَ اللَّهُ الْقَلَمَ قَالَ: اكْتُبْ. قَالَ: وَمَا أَكْتُبُ؟ قَالَ: اكْتُبِ القَدَر. فَجَرَى بِمَا يَكُونُ مِنْ ذَلِكَ الْيَوْمِ إِلَى يَوْمِ قِيَامِ السَّاعَةِ. ثُمَّ خَلَقَ "النُّونَ" وَرَفَعَ بُخَارَ الْمَاءِ، ففُتِقت مِنْهُ السَّمَاءُ، وَبُسِطَتِ الْأَرْضُ عَلَى ظَهْرِ النُّونِ، فَاضْطَرَبَ النُّونُ فَمَادَتِ الْأَرْضُ، فَأُثْبِتَتْ بِالْجِبَالِ، فَإِنَّهَا لَتَفْخَرُ عَلَى الْأَرْضِ .

وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ عَنْ أَحْمَدَ بْنِ سِنان، عَنْ أَبِي مُعَاوِيَةَ، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، بِهِ. وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ شُعْبَةُ، وَمُحَمَّدُ بْنُ فُضَيل، وَوَكِيع، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، بِهِ. وَزَادَ شُعْبَةُ فِي رِوَايَتِهِ: ثُمَّ قَرَأَ: ﴿ن وَالْقَلَمِ وَمَا يَسْطُرُونَ﴾ وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ شَرِيكٌ، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ أَبِي ظَبْيَانَ -أَوْ مُجَاهِدٍ-عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، فَذَكَرَ نَحْوَهُ. وَرَوَاهُ مَعْمَر، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ: أَنَّ ابْنَ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ ... فَذَكَرَهُ، ثُمَّ قَرَأَ: ﴿ن وَالْقَلَمِ وَمَا يَسْطُرُونَ﴾ ثُمَّ قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ حُمَيْدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا جَرِيرٌ، عَنْ عَطَاءٍ، عَنْ أَبِي الضُّحَى، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: إِنَّ أَوَّلَ شَيْءٍ خَلَقَ رَبِّي، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، الْقَلَمُ، ثُمَّ قَالَ لَهُ: اكْتُبْ. فَكَتَبَ مَا هُوَ كَائِنٌ إِلَى أَنْ تَقُومَ السَّاعَةُ. ثُمَّ خَلَقَ "النُّونَ" فَوْقَ الْمَاءِ، ثُمَّ كَبَسَ الأرض عليه .

وَقَدْ رَوَى الطَّبَرَانِيُّ ذَلِكَ مَرْفُوعًا فَقَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو حَبِيبٍ زَيْدُ بْنُ الْمُهْتَدِي الْمَرُّوذِيُّ حَدَّثَنَا سَعِيدُ بْنُ يَعْقُوبَ الطَّالَقَانِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا مُؤَمَّل بْنُ إِسْمَاعِيلَ، حَدَّثَنَا حَمَّادُ بْنُ زَيْدٍ، عَنْ عَطَاءِ بْنِ السَّائِبِ، عَنْ أَبِي الضُّحَى مُسْلِمِ بْنِ صَبِيح، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ أَوَّلَ مَا خَلَقَ اللَّهُ الْقَلَمُ وَالْحُوتُ، قَالَ لِلْقَلَمِ: اكْتُبْ، قَالَ: مَا أَكْتُبُ، قَالَ: كُلَّ شَيْءٍ كَائِنٍ إِلَى يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ". ثُمَّ قَرَأَ: ﴿ن وَالْقَلَمِ وَمَا يَسْطُرُونَ﴾ فَالنُّونُ: الْحُوتُ. وَالْقَلَمُ: الْقَلَمُ .

حَدِيثٌ آخَرُ فِي ذَلِكَ: رَوَاهُ ابْنُ عَسَاكِرَ عَنْ أَبِي عَبْدِ اللَّهِ مَوْلَى بَنِي أُمَيَّةَ، عَنْ أَبِي صَالِحٍ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيرة: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى الله عليه وسلم يقول: "إِنَّ أَوَّلَ شَيْءٍ خَلَقَهُ اللَّهُ الْقَلَمُ، ثُمَّ خَلَقَ "النُّونَ" وَهِيَ: الدَّوَاةُ. ثُمَّ قَالَ لَهُ: اكْتُبْ. قَالَ وَمَا أَكْتُبُ؟ قَالَ: اكْتُبْ مَا يَكُونُ -أَوْ: مَا هُوَ كَائِنٌ-مِنْ عَمَلٍ أَوْ رِزْقٍ أَوْ أَثَرٍ أَوْ أَجَلٍ. فَكَتَبَ ذَلِكَ إِلَى يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ، فَذَلِكَ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿ن وَالْقَلَمِ وَمَا يَسْطُرُونَ﴾ ثُمَّ خَتَمَ عَلَى الْقَلَمِ فَلَمْ يَتَكَلَّمْ إِلَى يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ، ثُمَّ خَلَقَ الْعَقْلَ وَقَالَ: وَعِزَّتِي لَأُكَمِّلَنَّكَ فِيمَنْ أَحْبَبْتُ، وَلَأَنْقُصَنَّكَ مِمَّنْ أَبْغَضْتُ" .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي نَجِيح: إِنَّ إِبْرَاهِيمَ بْنَ أَبِي بَكْرٍ أَخْبَرَهُ عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ قَالَ: كَانَ يُقَالُ: النُّونُ: الْحُوتُ [الْعَظِيمُ] الَّذِي تَحْتَ الْأَرْضِ السَّابِعَةِ.

وَذَكَرَ الْبَغَوِيُّ وَجَمَاعَةٌ مِنَ الْمُفَسِّرِينَ: إِنَّ عَلَى ظَهْرِ هَذَا الْحُوتِ صَخْرَةً سمكها كغلظ السموات وَالْأَرْضِ، وَعَلَى ظَهْرِهَا ثَوْرٌ لَهُ أَرْبَعُونَ أَلْفَ قَرْنٍ، وَعَلَى مَتْنِهِ الْأَرَضُونَ السَّبْعُ وَمَا فِيهِنَّ وَمَا بَيْنَهُنَّ فَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ. وَمِنَ الْعَجِيبِ أَنَّ بَعْضَهُمْ حَمَلَ عَلَى هَذَا الْمَعْنَى الْحَدِيثَ الَّذِي رَوَاهُ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ:

حَدَّثَنَا إِسْمَاعِيلُ، حَدَّثَنَا حُمَيد، عَنْ أَنَسٍ: أَنَّ عَبْدَ اللَّهِ بْنَ سَلَامٍ بَلَغه مَقْدَم رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ الْمَدِينَةَ، فَأَتَاهُ فَسَأَلَهُ عَنْ أَشْيَاءَ، قَالَ: إِنِّي سَائِلُكَ عَنْ أَشْيَاءَ لَا يَعْلَمُهَا إِلَّا نَبِيٌّ، قَالَ: مَا أَوَّلُ أَشْرَاطِ السَّاعَةِ؟ وَمَا أَوَّلُ طَعَامِ يَأْكُلُهُ أَهْلُ الْجَنَّةِ؟ وَمَا بَالُ الْوَلَدِ يَنْزِعُ إِلَى أَبِيهِ؟ وَالْوَلَدُ يَنْزِعُ إِلَى أُمِّهِ؟ قَالَ: "أَخْبَرَنِي بِهِنَّ جِبْرِيلُ آنِفًا". قَالَ ابْنُ سَلَامٍ: فَذَاكَ عَدُوُّ الْيَهُودِ مِنَ الْمَلَائِكَةِ. قَالَ: "أَمَّا أَوَّلُ أَشْرَاطِ السَّاعَةِ فَنَارٌ تَحشرهم مِنَ الْمَشْرِقِ إِلَى الْمَغْرِبِ. وَأَوَّلُ طَعَامٍ يَأْكُلُهُ أَهْلُ الْجَنَّةِ زيادةُ كَبِدِ حُوتٍ. وَأَمَّا الْوَلَدُ فَإِذَا سَبَقَ مَاءُ الرَّجُلِ مَاءَ الْمَرْأَةِ نَزَعَ الْوَلَدُ، وَإِذَا سَبَقَ مَاءُ الْمَرْأَةِ مَاءَ الرَّجُلِ نَزَعَتْ".

وَرَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ مِنْ طُرُقٍ عَنْ حُمَيد، وَرَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ أَيْضًا وَلَهُ مِنْ حَدِيثِ ثَوْبَانَ -مَوْلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ-نَحْوُ هَذَا. وَفِي صَحِيحِ مُسْلِمٍ مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي أَسْمَاءَ الرَّحْبِيِّ، عَنْ ثَوْبَانَ: أَنَّ حَبْرًا سَأَلَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ عن مَسَائِلَ، فَكَانَ مِنْهَا أَنْ قَالَ: فَمَا تُحْفَتُهُمْ؟ -يَعْنِي أَهْلَ الْجَنَّةِ حِينَ يَدْخُلُونَ الْجَنَّةَ-قَالَ: "زِيَادَةُ كَبِدِ الْحُوتِ". قَالَ: فَمَا غِذَاؤُهُمْ عَلَى أَثَرِهَا؟ قَالَ: "يُنْحَرُ لَهُمْ ثَوْرُ الْجَنَّةِ الَّذِي كَانَ يَأْكُلُ مِنْ أَطْرَافِهَا". قَالَ: فَمَا شَرَابُهُمْ عَلَيْهِ؟ قَالَ: "مِنْ عَيْنٍ فِيهَا تُسَمَّى سَلْسَبِيلًا " .

وَقِيلَ: الْمُرَادُ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿ن﴾ لَوْحٌ مِنْ نُورٍ.

قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا الْحُسَيْنُ بْنُ شَبِيبٍ الْمُكْتِبُ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ زِيَادٍ الْجَزَرِيُّ، عَنْ فُرَاتِ بْنِ أَبِي الْفُرَاتِ، عَنْ مُعَاوِيَةَ بْنِ قُرّة، عَنْ أَبِيهِ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: " ﴿ن وَالْقَلَمِ وَمَا يَسْطُرُونَ﴾ لَوْحٌ مِنْ نُورٍ، وَقَلَمٌ مِنْ نُورٍ، يَجْرِي بِمَا هُوَ كَائِنٌ إِلَى يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ" وَهَذَا مُرْسَلٌ غَرِيبٌ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جُرَيج أُخْبِرْتُ أَنَّ ذَلِكَ الْقَلَمَ مِنْ نُورٍ طُولُهُ مِائَةُ عَامٍ.

وَقِيلَ: الْمُرَادُ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿ن﴾ دَوَاةٌ، وَالْقَلَمُ: الْقَلَمُ. قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ:

حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدِ الْأَعْلَى، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ ثَوْرٍ، عَنْ مَعْمَر، عَنِ الْحَسَنِ وَقَتَادَةَ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿ن﴾ قَالَا هِيَ الدَّوَاةُ.

وَقَدْ رُوِيَ فِي هَذَا حَدِيثٌ مَرْفُوعٌ غَرِيبٌ جِدًّا فَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ:

حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا هِشَامُ بْنُ خَالِدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا الْحَسَنُ بْنُ يَحْيَى، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو عَبْدِ اللَّهِ مَوْلَى بَنِي أُمَيَّةَ، عَنْ أَبِي صَالِحٍ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "خَلَقَ اللَّهُ النُّونَ، وَهِيَ الدَّوَاةُ" .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ حُمَيْدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا يَعْقُوبُ، حَدَّثَنَا أَخِي عِيسَى بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ، حَدَّثَنَا ثَابِتٌ الثُّمَالِيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: إِنَّ اللَّهَ خَلَقَ النُّونَ -وَهِيَ الدَّوَاةُ-وَخَلَقَ الْقَلَمَ، فَقَالَ: اكْتُبْ. قَالَ: وَمَا أَكْتُبُ؟ قَالَ: اكْتُبْ مَا هُوَ كَائِنٌ إِلَى يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ مِنْ عَمَلٍ مَعْمُولٍ، بِهِ بِرٍّ أَوْ فُجُورٍ، أَوْ رِزْقٍ مَقْسُومٍ حَلَالٍ أَوْ حَرَامٍ. ثُمَّ أَلْزَمَ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ مِنْ ذَلِكَ، شَأْنَهُ: دُخُولَهُ فِي الدُّنْيَا، وَمَقَامَهُ فِيهَا كَمْ؟ وَخُرُوجَهُ مِنْهَا كَيْفَ؟ ثُمَّ جَعَلَ عَلَى الْعِبَادِ حَفَظَةً، وَلِلْكِتَابِ خُزَّانًا، فَالْحَفَظَةُ يَنْسَخُونَ كُلَّ يَوْمٍ مِنَ الْخُزَّانِ عَمَلَ ذَلِكَ الْيَوْمِ، فَإِذَا فَنِيَ الرِّزْقُ وَانْقَطَعَ الْأَثَرُ وَانْقَضَى الْأَجَلُ، أَتَتِ الْحَفَظَةُ الْخَزَنَةَ يَطْلُبُونَ عَمَلَ ذَلِكَ الْيَوْمِ، فَتَقُولُ لَهُمُ الْخَزَنَةُ: مَا نَجِدُ لِصَاحِبِكُمْ عِنْدَنَا شَيْئًا فَتَرْجِعُ الْحَفَظَةُ فَيَجِدُونَهُمْ قَدْ مَاتُوا. قَالَ: فَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: أَلَسْتُمْ قَوْمًا عَرَبا تَسْمَعُونَ الْحَفَظَةَ يَقُولُونَ: ﴿إِنَّا كُنَّا نَسْتَنْسِخُ مَا كُنْتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ﴾ [الْجَاثِيَةِ: ٢٩] ؟ وَهَلْ يَكُونُ الاستنساخ إلا من أصل .

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَالْقَلَمِ﴾ الظَّاهِرُ أَنَّهُ جِنْسُ الْقَلَمِ الَّذِي يُكْتَبُ بِهِ كَقَوْلِهِ ﴿اقْرَأْ وَرَبُّكَ الأكْرَمُ الَّذِي عَلَّمَ بِالْقَلَمِ عَلَّمَ الإنْسَانَ مَا لَمْ يَعْلَمْ﴾ [العاق: ٣ -٥] . فَهُوَ قَسَمٌ مِنْهُ تَعَالَى، وَتَنْبِيهٌ لِخَلْقِهِ عَلَى مَا أَنْعَمَ بِهِ عَلَيْهِمْ مِنْ تَعْلِيمِ الْكِتَابَةِ الَّتِي بِهَا تُنَالُ الْعُلُومُ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿وَمَا يَسْطُرُونَ﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وقَتَادَةُ: يَعْنِي: وَمَا يَكْتُبُونَ.

وَقَالَ أَبُو الضُّحى، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿وَمَا يَسْطُرُونَ﴾ أَيْ: وَمَا يَعْمَلُونَ.

وَقَالَ السُّدِّيُّ: ﴿وَمَا يَسْطُرُونَ﴾ يَعْنِي الْمَلَائِكَةَ وَمَا تَكْتُبُ مِنْ أَعْمَالِ الْعِبَادِ.

وَقَالَ آخَرُونَ: بَلِ الْمُرَادُ هاهنا بِالْقَلَمِ الَّذِي أَجْرَاهُ اللَّهُ بِالْقَدَرِ حِينَ كَتَبَ مقادير الخلائق قبل أن يخلق السموات وَالْأَرَضِينَ بِخَمْسِينَ أَلْفَ سَنَةٍ. وَأَوْرَدُوا فِي ذَلِكَ الْأَحَادِيثَ الْوَارِدَةَ فِي ذِكْرِ الْقَلَمِ، فَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ:

حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو سَعِيدِ بْنُ يَحْيَى بْنِ سَعِيدٍ الْقَطَّانُ وَيُونُسُ بْنُ حَبِيبٍ قَالَا حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو دَاوُدَ الطَّيَالِسِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الْوَاحِدِ بْنُ سُليم السُّلَمِيُّ، عَنْ عَطَاءٍ -هُوَ ابْنُ أَبِي رَبَاحٍ-حَدَّثَنِي الْوَلِيدُ بْنُ عُبَادَةَ بْنِ الصَّامِتِ قَالَ: دَعَانِي أَبِي حِينِ حَضَرَهُ الْمَوْتُ فَقَالَ: إِنِّي سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "إِنَّ أَوَّلَ مَا خَلَقَ اللَّهُ الْقَلَمُ، فَقَالَ لَهُ: اكْتُبْ. قَالَ: يَا رَبِّ وَمَا أَكْتُبُ؟ قَالَ: اكْتُبِ الْقَدَرَ [مَا كَانَ] وَمَا هُوَ كَائِنٌ إِلَى الْأَبَدِ".

وَهَذَا الْحَدِيثُ قَدْ رَوَاهُ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ مِنْ طُرُقٍ، عَنِ الْوَلِيدِ بْنِ عُبَادَةَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، بِهِ وَأَخْرَجَهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي دَاوُدَ الطَّيَالِسِيِّ، بِهِ وَقَالَ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ غَرِيبٌ. وَرَوَاهُ أَبُو دَاوُدَ فِي كِتَابِ "السُّنَّةِ" مِنْ سُنَنِهِ، عَنْ جَعْفَرِ بْنِ مُسَافِرٍ، عَنْ يَحْيَى بْنِ حَسَّانَ، عَنِ ابْنِ رَبَاحٍ، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ بْنِ أَبِي عَبْلَةَ عَنْ أَبِي حَفْصَةَ -وَاسْمُهُ حُبَيش بْنُ شُريح الحَبشي الشَّامِيُّ-عَنْ عُبَادَةَ، فَذَكَرَهُ .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الطُّوسِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ الْحَسَنِ بْنِ شَقِيقٍ، أَنْبَأَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ الْمُبَارَكِ، حَدَّثَنَا رَبَاحُ بْنُ زَيْدٍ، عَنْ عُمَرَ بْنِ حَبِيبٍ، عَنِ الْقَاسِمِ بْنِ أَبِي بَزة عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: أَنَّهُ كَانَ يُحَدِّثُ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم قَالَ: "إِنَّ أَوَّلَ شَيْءٍ خَلَقَهُ اللَّهُ الْقَلَمُ فَأَمَرَهُ فَكَتَبَ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ". غَرِيبٌ مِنْ هَذَا الْوَجْهِ، وَلَمْ يُخَرِّجُوهُ 

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي نَجيح، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ: ﴿وَالْقَلَمِ﴾ يَعْنِي: الَّذِي كُتِبَ بِهِ الذِّكْرُ. .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَمَا يَسْطُرُونَ﴾ أَيْ: يَكْتُبُونَ كَمَا تقدم.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿مَا أَنْتَ بِنِعْمَةِ رَبِّكَ بِمَجْنُونٍ﴾ أَيْ: لَسْتَ، وَلِلَّهِ الْحَمْدُ، بِمَجْنُونٍ، كَمَا قَدْ يَقُولُهُ الْجَهَلَةُ مَنْ قَوْمِكَ، الْمُكَذِّبُونَ بِمَا جِئْتَهُمْ بِهِ مِنَ الْهُدَى وَالْحَقِّ الْمُبِينِ، فَنَسَبُوكَ فِيهِ إِلَى الْجُنُونِ، ﴿وَإِنَّ لَكَ لأجْرًا غَيْرَ مَمْنُونٍ﴾ أَيْ: بَلْ لَكَ الْأَجْرُ الْعَظِيمُ، وَالثَّوَابُ الْجَزِيلُ الَّذِي لَا يَنْقَطِعُ وَلَا يَبِيدُ، عَلَى إِبْلَاغِكَ رِسَالَةَ رَبِّكَ إِلَى الْخَلْقِ، وَصَبْرِكَ عَلَى أَذَاهُمْ. وَمَعْنَى ﴿غَيْرُ مَمْنُونٍ﴾ أَيْ: غَيْرُ مَقْطُوعٍ كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿عَطَاءً غَيْرَ مَجْذُوذٍ﴾ [هُودٍ: ١٠٨] ﴿فَلَهُمْ أَجْرٌ غَيْرُ مَمْنُونٍ﴾ [التِّينِ: ٦] أَيْ: غَيْرُ مَقْطُوعٍ عَنْهُمْ. وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: ﴿غَيْرُ مَمْنُونٍ﴾ أَيْ: غَيْرُ مَحْسُوبٍ، وَهُوَ يَرْجِعُ إِلَى مَا قُلْنَاهُ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِنَّكَ لَعَلى خُلُقٍ عَظِيمٍ﴾ قَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: أَيْ: وَإِنَّكَ لَعَلَى دِينٍ عَظِيمٍ، وَهُوَ الْإِسْلَامُ. وَكَذَلِكَ قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَأَبُو مَالِكٍ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ، وَالرَّبِيعُ بْنُ أَنَسٍ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَابْنُ زَيْدٍ.

وَقَالَ عَطِيَّةُ: لَعَلَى أَدَبٍ عَظِيمٍ. وَقَالَ مَعْمَر، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ: سُئلت عائشةُ عَنْ خُلُقِ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ. قَالَتِ: كَانَ خُلُقُهُ الْقُرْآنَ، تَقُولُ كَمَا هُوَ فِي الْقُرْآنِ.

وَقَالَ سَعِيدُ بْنُ أَبِي عَرُوبَة، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِنَّكَ لَعَلى خُلُقٍ عَظِيمٍ﴾ ذُكِرَ لَنَا أَنَّ سَعْدَ بْنَ هِشَامٍ سَأَلَ عَائِشَةَ عَنْ خُلُقِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ. فَقَالَتْ: أَلَسْتَ تَقْرَأُ الْقُرْآنَ؟ قَالَ: بَلَى. قَالَتْ: فَإِنَّ خُلُقِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ كَانَ الْقُرْآنَ.

وَقَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، عَنْ مَعْمَر، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ، عَنْ زُرارة بْنِ أَوْفَى عَنْ سَعْدِ بْنِ هِشَامٍ قَالَ: سَأَلْتُ عَائِشَةَ فَقُلْتُ: أَخْبِرِينِي يَا أُمَّ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ -عَنْ خُلُق رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ. فَقَالَتْ: أَتَقْرَأُ الْقُرْآنَ؟ فقلتُ: نَعَمْ. فَقَالَتْ: كَانَ خُلُقُهُ الْقُرْآنَ .

هَذَا حَدِيثٌ طَوِيلٌ. وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ الْإِمَامُ مُسْلِمٌ فِي صَحِيحِهِ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ قَتَادَةَ بِطُولِهِ وَسَيَأْتِي فِي سُورَةِ "الْمُزَّمِّلِ" إِنْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا إِسْمَاعِيلُ، حَدَّثَنَا يُونُسُ، عَنِ الْحَسَنِ قَالَ: سَأَلْتُ عَائِشَةَ عَنْ خُلُقِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، فَقَالَتْ: كَانَ خُلُقُهُ الْقُرْآنَ .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا أَسْوَدُ، حَدَّثَنَا شَرِيكٌ، عَنْ قَيْسِ بْنِ وَهْبٍ، عَنْ رَجُلٍ مِنْ بَنِي سَوَادٍ قَالَ: سَأَلْتُ عَائِشَةَ عَنْ خُلُقِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ. فَقَالَتْ: أَمَا تَقْرَأُ الْقُرْآنَ: ﴿وَإِنَّكَ لَعَلى خُلُقٍ عَظِيمٍ﴾ ؟ قَالَ: قُلْتُ: حَدِّثِينِي عَنْ ذَاكَ. قَالَتْ: صَنَعْتُ لَهُ طَعَامًا، وَصَنَعَتْ لَهُ حَفْصَةُ طَعَامًا، فَقُلْتُ لِجَارِيَتِي: اذْهَبِي فَإِنْ جَاءَتْ هِيَ بِالطَّعَامِ فَوَضَعَتْهُ قبلُ فَاطْرَحِي الطَّعَامَ! قَالَتْ: فَجَاءَتْ بِالطَّعَامِ. قَالَتْ: فَأَلْقَتِ الْجَارِيَةُ، فَوَقَعَتِ الْقَصْعَةُ فَانْكَسَرَتْ -وَكَانَ نِطْعًا -قالت: فجمعه رسول الله وَقَالَ: "اقْتَضُوا -أَوِ: اقْتَضِي-شَكَّ أَسْوَدُ -ظَرفًا مَكَانَ ظَرفِك". قَالَتْ: فَمَا قَالَ شَيْئًا .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا عُبَيْدُ بْنُ آدَمَ بْنِ أَبِي إِيَاسٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا الْمُبَارَكُ بْنُ فَضَالَةَ، عَنِ الْحَسَنِ، عَنْ سَعْدِ بْنِ هِشَامٍ: قَالَ: أَتَيْتُ عَائِشَةَ أُمَّ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ فَقُلْتُ لَهَا: أَخْبِرِينِي بخلُق النَّبِيِّ صَلَّى لِلَّهِ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ. فَقَالَتْ: كَانَ خُلُقُهُ الْقُرْآنَ. أَمَا تَقْرَأُ: ﴿وَإِنَّكَ لَعَلى خُلُقٍ عَظِيمٍ﴾

وَقَدْ رَوَى أَبُو دَاوُدَ وَالنَّسَائِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ الْحَسَنِ، نَحْوَهُ 

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنِي يُونُسُ، أَنْبَأَنَا ابْنُ وَهْبٍ، وَأَخْبَرَنِي مُعَاوِيَةُ بْنُ صَالِحٍ، عَنْ أَبِي الزَّاهِرِيَّةِ، عَنْ جُبَير بْنِ نُفَيْرٍ قَالَ: حججتُ فدخلتُ عَلَى عَائِشَةَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهَا، فَسَأَلْتُهَا عَنْ خُلُق رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ. فقالت: كَانَ خُلُق رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ الْقُرْآنَ.

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ أَحْمَدُ، عَنْ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ مَهْدِيٍّ. وَرَوَاهُ النَّسَائِيُّ فِي التَّفْسِيرِ، عَنْ إِسْحَاقَ بْنِ مَنْصُورٍ، عَنْ عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ مَهْدِيٍّ، عَنْ مُعَاوِيَةَ بْنِ صَالِحٍ، بِهِ 

وَمَعْنَى هَذَا أَنَّهُ، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ، صَارَ امتثالُ الْقُرْآنِ، أَمْرًا وَنَهْيًا، سَجِيَّةً لَهُ، وَخُلُقًا تَطَبَّعَه، وَتَرَكَ طَبْعَهُ الجِبِلِّي، فَمَهْمَا أَمَرَهُ الْقُرْآنُ فَعَلَهُ، وَمَهْمَا نَهَاهُ عَنْهُ تَرَكَهُ. هَذَا مَعَ مَا جَبَله اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ مِنَ الْخُلُقِ الْعَظِيمِ، مِنَ الْحَيَاءِ وَالْكَرَمِ وَالشَّجَاعَةِ، وَالصَّفْحِ وَالْحِلْمِ، وَكُلِّ خُلُقٍ جَمِيلٍ. كَمَا ثَبَتَ فِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ عَنْ أَنَسٍ قَالَ: خدمتُ رسولَ اللهِ ﷺ عَشْرٌ سِنِينَ فَمَا قَالَ لِي: "أُفٍّ" قَطُّ، وَلَا قَالَ لِشَيْءٍ فَعَلْتُهُ: لِمَ فَعَلْتَهُ؟ وَلَا لِشَيْءٍ لَمْ أَفْعَلْهُ: أَلَا فَعَلْتَهُ؟ وَكَانَ ﷺ أَحْسَنَ النَّاسِ خُلُقًا وَلَا مَسسْتُ خَزًّا وَلَا حَرِيرًا وَلَا شَيْئًا كَانَ أَلْيَنَ مِنْ كَفَّ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ، وَلَا شَمَمْتُ مِسْكًا وَلَا عِطْرًا كَانَ أَطْيَبَ مِنْ عَرَق رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ 

وَقَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ: [حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ سَعِيدٍ أَبُو عَبْدِ اللَّهِ] حَدَّثَنَا إِسْحَاقُ بْنُ مَنْصُورٍ، حَدَّثَنَا إِبْرَاهِيمُ بْنُ يُوسُفَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ الْبَرَاءَ يَقُولُ: كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ أَحْسَنَ النَّاسِ وَجْهًا، وَأَحْسَنَ النَّاسِ خَلْقًا، لَيْسَ بِالطَّوِيلِ الْبَائِنِ، وَلَا بِالْقَصِيرِ 

وَالْأَحَادِيثُ فِي هَذَا كَثِيرَةٌ، وَلِأَبِي عِيسَى التِّرْمِذِيِّ فِي هَذَا كِتَابُ "الشَّمَائِلِ".

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، حَدَّثَنَا مَعْمَر، عَنِ الزُّهْرِيِّ، عَنْ عُرْوَة، عَنْ عَائِشَةَ قَالَتْ: مَا ضَرَبَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ بِيَدِهِ خَادِمًا لَهُ قَطُّ، وَلَا امْرَأَةً، وَلَا ضَرَبَ بِيَدِهِ شيئًا قط، إلا أن يُجَاهِدَ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ. وَلَا خُيِّر بَيْنَ شَيْئَيْنِ قَطُّ إِلَّا كَانَ أَحَبُّهُمَا إِلَيْهِ أَيْسَرَهُمَا حَتَّى يَكُونَ إِثْمًا، فَإِذَا كَانَ إِثْمًا كَانَ أَبْعَدَ النَّاسِ مِنَ الْإِثْمِ، وَلَا انْتَقَمَ لِنَفْسِهِ مِنْ شَيْءٍ يُؤْتَى إِلَيْهِ إِلَّا أَنْ تُنْتَهَكَ حُرُمَاتُ اللَّهِ، فَيَكُونُ هُوَ يَنْتَقِمُ لِلَّهِ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ 

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا سَعِيدُ بْنُ مَنْصُورٍ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الْعَزِيزِ بْنُ مُحَمَّدٍ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عَجْلان، عَنِ الْقَعْقَاعِ بْنِ حَكِيمٍ، عَنْ أَبِي صَالِحٍ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيرة قَالَ: قَالَ رسولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّمَا بُعِثتُ لِأُتَمِّمَ صَالِحَ الْأَخْلَاقِ". تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ

قَالَ ابْنُ جُرَيْجٍ: قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ فِي هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ: سَتَعْلَمُ وَيَعْلَمُونَ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ.

وَقَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنِ ابن عباس: ﴿بِأَيِّيكُمُ الْمَفْتُونُ﴾ أَيِ: الْجُنُونُ. وَكَذَا قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَغَيْرُهُ. وقال قتادة وغيره: ﴿بِأَيِّيكُمُ الْمَفْتُونُ﴾ أَيْ: أَوْلَى بِالشَّيْطَانِ.

وَمَعْنَى الْمَفْتُونِ ظَاهِرٌ، أَيِ: الَّذِي قَدِ افْتُتِنَ عَنِ الْحَقِّ وَضَلَّ عنه، وإنما دخلت الباء في قوله: ﴿بِأَيِّيكُمُ الْمَفْتُونُ﴾ لِتَدُلَّ عَلَى تَضْمِينِ الْفِعْلِ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿فَسَتُبْصِرُ وَيُبْصِرُونَ﴾ وَتَقْدِيرُهُ: فَسَتَعْلَمُ وَيَعْلَمُونَ، أَوْ: فستُخْبَر ويُخْبَرون بِأَيِّكُمُ الْمَفْتُونُ. وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِنَّ رَبَّكَ هُوَ أَعْلَمُ بِمَنْ ضَلَّ عَنْ سَبِيلِهِ وَهُوَ أَعْلَمُ بِالْمُهْتَدِينَ﴾ أَيْ: هُوَ يَعْلَمُ تَعَالَى أَيَّ الْفَرِيقَيْنِ مِنْكُمْ وَمِنْهُمْ هُوَ الْمُهْتَدِي، وَيَعْلَمُ الْحِزْبَ الضَّالَّ عَنِ الْحَقِّ.

## 68:8-16 — verses 8-16 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: just as We bestowed favor upon you and gave you the straight legislation and the magnificent character, {so do not obey the deniers}. {They wish that you would compromise, so they would compromise}. Ibn Abbas said: that you would grant them concessions so that they would grant concessions.

Mujahid said: they wish that you would incline toward their gods and abandon the truth you are upon.

Then the Almighty says: {And do not obey every excessive swearer who is contemptible}. This is because the liar, due to his weakness and contemptibility, guards himself only through his false oaths, which he dares to make using the names of Allah the Almighty, employing them at every moment in other than their proper place.

Ibn Abbas said: al-mahin (the contemptible one) means the liar. Mujahid said: he is the one weak of heart. Al-Hasan said: every excessive swearer who is arrogant, contemptible, and weak.

As for His saying {hammaz (backbiter)}, Ibn Abbas and Qatadah said it means backbiting.

{Mashsha'in bi-namim (one who goes about spreading malicious gossip)} means the one who walks among people, stirring up trouble between them and carrying tales that corrupt relations between them, and that is "the shaver" (al-haliqah, that which shaves away religion or good relations). It is established in the two Sahihs, from the hadith of Mujahid, from Tawus, from Ibn Abbas, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ passed by two graves and said: "They are indeed being punished, and they are not being punished for anything great. As for one of them, he did not guard himself from urine; as for the other, he used to go about with malicious gossip." The rest of the community narrated it in their books, through various chains from Mujahid.

Ahmad said: [chain] Hudhayfah said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "A qattat (habitual gossip-carrier) will not enter Paradise."

The community narrated it, except Ibn Majah, through various chains from Ibrahim.

[chain] Hudhayfah said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "A qattat will not enter Paradise," meaning a namam (malicious gossip-carrier).

[chain] Hammam ibn al-Harith said: A man passed by Hudhayfah, and it was said: this man carries reports to the rulers. So he said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say, or he said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "A qattat will not enter Paradise."

Ahmad said: [chain] Abu Wa'il said: it reached Hudhayfah about a man that he carried gossip, so he said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "A namam will not enter Paradise."

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Asma bint Yazid ibn al-Sakan reported that the Prophet ﷺ said: "Shall I not inform you of the best among you?" They said: Yes indeed, O Messenger of Allah. He said: "Those who, when seen, Allah, Mighty and Majestic, is remembered." Then he said: "Shall I not inform you of the worst among you? Those who go about spreading malicious gossip, who cause corruption between loved ones, and who seek to bring hardship upon the innocent."

Ibn Majah narrated it from Suwayd ibn Sa'id, from Yahya ibn Sulaym, from Ibn Khuthaym, with the same content.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Abd al-Rahman ibn Ghanm reported, tracing it back to the Prophet ﷺ: "The best of Allah's servants are those who, when seen, Allah is remembered; and the worst of Allah's servants are those who go about with malicious gossip, who separate loved ones, and who seek to bring hardship upon the innocent."

As for His saying {manna'in lil-khayr (one who withholds good) mu'tadin (transgressor) athim (sinful)}, that is: he withholds the good that is due from him and available to him. {Mu'tadin} in taking what Allah has made lawful for him, exceeding the prescribed limit in it. {Athim}: that is, he partakes of the forbidden things.

As for His saying {'utullin (coarse, harsh) ba'da dhalika zanim (baseborn/ignoble)}: as for al-'utull, he is the harsh, coarse one, sound of body, who hoards and withholds.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Haritha ibn Wahb said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Shall I not inform you of the people of Paradise? Every weak person considered weak, who, were he to swear an oath upon Allah, Allah would fulfill it for him. Shall I not inform you of the people of the Fire? Every 'utull, jawwaz (gluttonous), arrogant one." Waki' said: "Every jawwaz, ja'zari (coarse and harsh), arrogant one."

The two Sahihs and the rest of the community narrated it, except Abu Dawud, from the hadith of Sufyan al-Thawri and Shu'bah, both from Ma'bad ibn Khalid, with the same content.

Imam Ahmad also said: [chain] Abdullah ibn Amr ibn al-As reported that the Prophet ﷺ said, when mentioning the people of the Fire: "Every ja'zari, jawwaz, arrogant one, who hoards and withholds." Ahmad narrated this alone.

The people of language said: al-ja'zari is the harsh, coarse one, and al-jawwaz is the one who hoards and withholds.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Abd al-Rahman ibn Ghanm said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ was asked about al-'utull al-zanim, and he said: "He is the one of firm, sound physique, the great eater and drinker, who finds abundance of food and drink, oppressive toward people, broad of belly."

With this same chain, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Al-jawwaz, al-ja'zari, al-'utull, al-zanim will not enter Paradise." Several of the Followers also narrated it as a mursal (report with a missing Companion link).

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Zayd ibn Aslam said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "The sky weeps over a servant whose body Allah made sound, whose belly He made spacious, and to whom He gave a mere morsel of this world, yet he was oppressive toward people." He said: "That is al-'utull al-zanim."

Ibn Abi Hatim narrated it likewise through two mursal chains, and several of the Salaf stated this explicitly, among them Mujahid, Ikrimah, al-Hasan, Qatadah, and others: that al-'utull is the one of sound physique, firm and strong in eating, drinking, marital relations, and other such matters.

As for al-zanim, al-Bukhari said: [chain] Ibn Abbas said regarding {'utullin ba'da dhalika zanim}: he is a man from Quraysh who has a zanamah (a hanging appendage) like the zanamah of a sheep.

The meaning of this is that he was notorious for evil, just as a sheep with a hanging appendage is notorious among its fellows. But al-zanim in the language of the Arabs is the illegitimate claimant attached to a people (al-da'i). This was said by Ibn Jarir and more than one of the imams. He said: from this comes the saying of Hassan ibn Thabit, meaning in dispraise of some of the disbelievers of Quraysh:

"And you are a zanim, attached to the family of Hashim, just as the single vessel is attached behind the rider."

And another said:

"A zanim whose father is not known, born of an adulterous mother, of base and ignoble lineage..."

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Ibn Abbas said regarding {zanim}: the illegitimate claimant, the shameless, the ignoble one. Then Ibn Abbas said:

"A zanim whom men falsely claim as an addition, just as extra pieces are added to the width of a hide."

Al-Awfi reported from Ibn Abbas: al-zanim is the illegitimate claimant. It is also said: al-zanim is a man who had a zanamah by which he was recognized. It is also said: he is al-Akhnas ibn Shariq al-Thaqafi, the ally of Banu Zuhrah. Some people of Banu Zuhrah claimed that al-zanim was al-Aswad ibn Abd Yaghuth al-Zuhri, but that is not correct.

Ibn Abi Najih reported from Mujahid, from Ibn Abbas, that he held that al-zanim is one whose lineage is falsely attached.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab said regarding this verse {'utullin ba'da dhalika zanim}: he is the one stuck onto a people, not truly of them.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Ikrimah was asked about al-zanim, and he said: he is the child of fornication.

Al-Hakam ibn Aban reported from Ikrimah regarding the Almighty's saying {'utullin ba'da dhalika zanim}: he said: the believer is distinguished from the disbeliever just as the zanma sheep is distinguished. Al-zanma among sheep is the one that has two hanging pieces of flesh attached at its throat. Al-Thawri reported from Jabir, from al-Hasan, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, who said: al-zanim is the one known for evil just as a sheep is known by its zanamah. And al-zanim is the one stuck on. Ibn Jarir narrated this.

He also narrated, through the route of Dawud ibn Abi Hind, from Ikrimah, from Ibn Abbas, that he said regarding al-zanim: he was described but not identified, until it was said "zanim." He said: he had a zanamah on his neck by which he was recognized. Others said: he was an illegitimate claimant.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] The companions of tafsir said: he is the one who has a zanamah like the zanamah of a sheep.

Al-Dahhak said: he had a small piece of flesh hanging at the root of his ear, and it is also said: he is the base one attached illegitimately to a lineage.

Abu Ishaq said, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, from Ibn Abbas: he is the suspect one who is known for evil.

Mujahid said: al-zanim (the ignoble, marked one) is the one who is known by this description just as a sheep is known [by its distinguishing mark]. Abu Razin said: al-zanim is a sign of disbelief. Ikrimah said: al-zanim is the one known for baseness just as a sheep is known by its zanamah (hanging flesh mark).

The sayings on this are many, and they all return to what we have said, which is that al-zanim is: the one well known for evil, who is recognized by it among people, and he is often illegitimate in birth (da'i) and has fornication [in his origin], for indeed Satan, in general, gains a mastery over such a person that he does not gain over others, as has come in the hadith: "The child of fornication (walad zina) will not enter Paradise," and in another hadith: "The child of fornication is the worst of the three, if he acts according to the deed of his two parents."

Regarding His saying: {Because he is a possessor of wealth and sons, when Our verses are recited to him, he says, 'Tales of the ancients'} the Exalted says: this is his response to what Allah had bestowed upon him of wealth and sons, that he disbelieved in the verses of Allah and turned away from them, and claimed that they were a lie taken from the tales of the ancients, as in His saying: {Leave Me with the one whom I created alone, and to whom I granted extensive wealth, and sons present [with him], and for whom I smoothed the way thoroughly, then he desires that I should add more. No! Indeed he has been stubbornly hostile to Our verses. I will cover him with a mounting difficulty. Indeed he thought and calculated. May he be destroyed, how he calculated! Then may he be destroyed, how he calculated! Then he looked, then he frowned and scowled, then he turned his back and was arrogant, and said, 'This is nothing but sorcery imitated [from others], this is nothing but the speech of a human being'} Allah the Exalted said: {I will cast him into Saqar} [al-Muddathir: 11-26]. The Exalted says here: {We will brand him on the snout}

Ibn Jarir said: We will make his affair clear with an unmistakable clarity, so that they will recognize him and it will not be hidden from them, just as a mark is not hidden on snouts. Similarly Qatadah said: {We will brand him on the snout} means a disgrace that will not leave him, the last thing remaining upon him.

In another report from him: it means a mark upon his nose. Al-Suddi said likewise.

Al-Awfi said, from Ibn Abbas: {We will brand him on the snout} means he will fight on the Day of Badr, and his snout will be struck by the sword in battle. Others said: {We will brand him} means with the mark of the people of the Fire, that is, We will blacken his face on the Day of Resurrection, and the face is expressed here by the word "snout." Abu Ja'far Ibn Jarir related all of this, and inclined toward the view that there is no obstacle to combining all these meanings together, both in this world and in the Hereafter, and this is a sound approach.

Ibn Abi Hatim said, in [his tafsir of] Surat {Amma yatasa'alun}: [chain] ... Abdullah ibn Amr, from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, that he said: "Indeed a servant may be recorded as a believer for ages upon ages, then he dies while Allah is displeased with him. And indeed a servant may be recorded as a disbeliever for ages upon ages, then he dies while Allah is pleased with him. And whoever dies as one who slanders and defames, giving people nicknames, his sign on the Day of Resurrection will be that Allah will brand him on the snout, from both lips."

﴿مَشَّاءٍ بِنَمِيمٍ﴾ يَعْنِي: الَّذِي يَمْشِي بَيْنَ النَّاسِ، وَيُحَرِّشُ بَيْنَهُمْ وَيَنْقُلُ الْحَدِيثَ لِفَسَادِ ذَاتِ الْبَيْنِ وَهِيَ الْحَالِقَةُ، وَقَدْ ثَبَتَ فِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ مِنْ حَدِيثِ مُجَاهِدٍ، عَنْ طَاوُسٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: مَرَّ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ بِقَبْرَيْنِ فَقَالَ: "إِنَّهُمَا لَيُعَذَّبَانِ وَمَا يُعَذَّبَانِ فِي كَبِيرٍ، أَمَّا أَحَدُهُمَا فَكَانَ لَا يَسْتَتِرُ مِنَ الْبَوْلِ، وَأَمَّا الْآخَرُ فَكَانَ يَمْشِي بِالنَّمِيمَةِ" الْحَدِيثَ. وَأَخْرَجَهُ بَقِيَّةُ الْجَمَاعَةِ فِي كُتُبِهِمْ، مِنْ طُرُقٍ عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ، بِهِ .

وَقَالَ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو مُعَاوِيَةَ، حَدَّثَنَا الْأَعْمَشُ، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، عَنْ هَمّام؛ أَنَّ حُذَيفة قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "لَا يَدْخُلُ الْجَنَّةَ قَتَّات".

رَوَاهُ الْجَمَاعَةُ -إِلَّا ابْنُ مَاجَهْ-مِنْ طُرُقٍ، عَنِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، بِهِ .

وَحَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، حَدَّثَنَا الثَّوْرِيُّ، عَنْ مَنْصُورٍ، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، عَنْ هَمَّامٍ، عَنْ حُذَيْفَةَ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "لَا يَدْخُلُ الْجَنَّةَ قَتَّاتٌ" يَعْنِي: نَمَّامًا .

وَحَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى بْنُ سَعِيدٍ الْقَطَّانُ أَبُو سَعِيدٍ الْأَحْوَلُ، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، حَدَّثَنِي إِبْرَاهِيمُ -مُنْذُ نَحْوِ سِتِّينَ سَنَةً-عَنْ هَمَّامِ بْنِ الْحَارِثِ قَالَ: مَرَّ رَجُلٌ عَلَى حُذَيْفَةَ فَقِيلَ: إِنَّ هَذَا يَرْفَعُ الْحَدِيثَ إِلَى الْأُمَرَاءِ. فَقَالَ سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم يَقُولُ -أَوْ: قَالَ-: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "لَا يَدْخُلُ الْجَنَّةَ قَتَّاتٌ" .

وَقَالَ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا هَاشِمٌ، حَدَّثَنَا مَهْدِيٌّ، عَنْ وَاصِلٍ الْأَحْدَبِ، عَنْ أَبِي وَائِلٍ قَالَ: بَلَغَ حُذَيْفَةَ عَنْ رَجُلٍ أَنَّهُ يَنُمُّ الْحَدِيثَ، فَقَالَ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم قَالَ: "لَا يَدْخُلُ الْجَنَّةَ نَمَّامٌ" .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، أَنْبَأَنَا مَعْمَر، عَنِ ابْنِ خُثَيم، عَنْ شَهْر بْنِ حَوْشَب، عَنْ أَسْمَاءَ بِنْتِ يَزِيدَ بْنِ السَّكَنِ؛ أَنَّ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ قَالَ: "أَلَا أُخْبِرُكُمْ بِخِيَارِكُمْ؟ ". قَالُوا: بَلَى يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ. قَالَ: "الذين إذا رُؤوا ذُكر اللَّهُ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ". ثُمَّ قَالَ: "أَلَا أُخْبِرُكُمْ بِشِرَارِكُمْ؟ الْمَشَّاءُونَ بِالنَّمِيمَةِ، الْمُفْسِدُونَ بَيْنَ الْأَحِبَّةِ، وَالْبَاغُونَ لِلْبُرَآءِ العَنَت".

وَرَوَاهُ ابْنُ مَاجَهْ، عَنْ سُوَيْدِ بْنِ سَعِيدٍ، عَنْ يَحْيَى بْنِ سُلَيْمٍ، عن ابن خُثَيم، به .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، عَنِ ابْنِ أَبِي حُسَين، عَنْ شَهْرِ بْنِ حَوْشَبٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ غَنْم -يَبْلُغُ بِهِ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ: "خِيَارُ عِبَادِ اللَّهِ الذين إذا رؤوا ذكر الله، وشرار عباد الله المشاؤون بِالنَّمِيمَةِ، الْمُفَرِّقُونَ بَيْنَ الْأَحِبَّةِ، الْبَاغُونَ لِلْبُرَآءِ الْعَنَتَ" 

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ ﴿مَنَّاعٍ لِلْخَيْرِ مُعْتَدٍ أَثِيمٍ﴾ أَيْ: يَمْنَعُ ما عليه وما لديه من الخير ﴿ِ مُعْتَدٍ﴾ فِي مُتَنَاوَلِ مَا أَحَلَّ اللَّهُ لَهُ، يَتَجَاوَزُ فِيهَا الْحَدَّ الْمَشْرُوعَ ﴿أَثِيمٍ﴾ أَيْ: يَتَنَاوَلُ الْمُحَرَّمَاتِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿عُتُلٍّ بَعْدَ ذَلِكَ زَنِيمٍ﴾ أَمَّا الْعُتُلُّ: فَهُوَ الْفَظُّ الْغَلِيظُ الصَّحِيحُ، الْجَمُوعُ المَنُوعُ.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا وَكِيع وَعَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ، عَنْ مَعْبَد بْنِ خَالِدٍ، عَنْ حَارِثَةَ بْنِ وَهْبٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "أَلَّا أُنَبِّئُكُمْ بِأَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ؟ كُلُّ ضَعِيفٍ مُتَضَعَّف لَوْ أَقْسَمَ عَلَى اللَّهِ لَأَبَرَّهُ، أَلَّا أُنَبِّئُكُمْ بِأَهْلِ النَّارِ؟ كُلُّ عُتل جَوّاظ مُسْتَكْبِرٍ". وَقَالَ وَكِيع: "كُلُّ جَوَّاظ جعظري مستكبر".

أخرجاه في الصحيحين بَقِيَّةُ الْجَمَاعَةِ، إِلَّا أَبَا دَاوُدَ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ سُفْيَانَ الثَّوْرِيِّ وَشُعْبَةَ، كِلَاهُمَا عَنْ مَعْبَدِ بْنِ خَالِدٍ، بِهِ .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ أَيْضًا: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو عبد الرحمن، حدثنا موسى بن علي قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ أَبِي يحدِّث عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرِو بْنِ الْعَاصِ؛ أَنَّ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ قَالَ عِنْدَ ذِكْرِ أَهْلِ النَّارِ: "كُلُّ جَعْظَرِيٍّ جَوَّاظٍ مُسْتَكْبِرٍ جَمَّاعٍ مَنَّاعٍ". تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ أَحْمَدُ .

قَالَ أَهْلُ اللُّغَةِ: الْجَعْظَرِيُّ: الفَظُّ الغَليظ، والجَوّاظ: الجَمُوع المَنُوع.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا وَكِيع، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الْحَمِيدِ، عَنْ شَهْر بْنِ حَوْشب، عَنْ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ غَنْم، قَالَ: سُئل رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ عَنِ العُتلِّ الزَّنِيمِ، فَقَالَ: "هُوَ الشَّدِيدُ الخَلْق الْمُصَحَّحُ، الْأَكُولُ الشَّرُوبُ، الْوَاجِدُ لِلطَّعَامِ وَالشَّرَابِ، الظَّلُومُ لِلنَّاسِ، رَحِيبُ الْجَوْفِ" .

وَبِهَذَا الْإِسْنَادِ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "لَا يَدْخُلُ الْجَنَّةَ الجَواظ الْجَعْظَرِيُّ، الْعُتُلُّ الزَّنِيمُ" وَقَدْ أَرْسَلَهُ أَيْضًا غَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ مِنَ التَّابِعِينَ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ عَبْدِ الْأَعْلَى، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ ثَوْرٍ، عَنْ مَعْمر، عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ قَالَ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "تَبْكِي السَّمَاءُ مِنْ عَبْدٍ أَصَحَّ اللَّهُ جِسْمَهُ، وَأَرْحَبَ جَوْفَهُ، وَأَعْطَاهُ مِنَ الدُّنْيَا مِقضَمًا فَكَانَ لِلنَّاسِ ظَلُومًا. قَالَ: فَذَلِكَ العُتُل الزنيم" .

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ مِنْ طَرِيقَيْنِ مُرْسَلَيْنِ، وَنَصَّ عَلَيْهِ غَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ مِنَ السَّلَفِ، مِنْهُمْ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَعِكْرِمَةُ، وَالْحَسَنُ، وقَتَادَةُ، وَغَيْرُهُمْ: أَنَّ الْعُتُلَّ هُوَ: المُصحَّح الخَلْق، الشَّدِيدُ الْقَوِيُّ فِي الْمَأْكَلِ وَالْمَشْرَبِ وَالْمَنْكَحِ، وَغَيْرِ ذَلِكَ، وَأَمَّا الزَّنِيمُ فَقَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ:

حَدَّثَنَا مَحْمُودٌ، حَدَّثَنَا عُبَيد اللَّهِ، عَنْ إِسْرَائِيلَ، عَنْ أَبِي حَصِين، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿عُتُلٍّ بَعْدَ ذَلِكَ زَنِيمٍ﴾ قَالَ: رجلٌ مِنْ قُرَيْشٍ لَهُ زَنمة مِثْلُ زَنَمة الشَّاةِ.

وَمَعْنَى هَذَا: أَنَّهُ كَانَ مَشْهُورًا بِالشَّرِّ كَشُهْرَةِ الشَّاةِ ذَاتِ الزَّنَمَةِ مِنْ بَيْنِ أَخَوَاتِهَا. وَإِنَّمَا الزَّنِيمُ فِي لُغَةِ الْعَرَبِ: هُوَ الدّعِيُّ فِي الْقَوْمِ. قَالَهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ مِنَ الْأَئِمَّةِ، قَالَ: وَمِنْهُ قَوْلُ حَسَّانَ بْنِ ثَابِتٍ، يَعْنِي يَذُمُّ بَعْضَ كُفَّارِ قُرَيْشٍ:

وأنتَ زَنيم نِيطَ فِي آلِ هَاشِمٍ ... كَمَا نِيطَ خَلْفَ الرّاكِب القَدَحُ الفَرْدُ 

وَقَالَ آخَرُ:

زَنيمٌ لَيْسَ يُعرَفُ مَن أبوهُ ... بَغيُّ الْأُمِّ ذُو حَسَب لَئيم ...

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا عَمَّارُ بْنُ خَالِدٍ الْوَاسِطِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا أَسْبَاطٌ، عَنْ هِشَامٍ، عَنْ عِكْرِمَة، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿زَنِيمٍ﴾ قَالَ: الدعيُّ الْفَاحِشُ اللَّئِيمُ. ثُمَّ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ:

زَنيمٌ تَداعاه الرجالُ زيادَةً ... كَما زيدَ فِي عَرضِ الْأَدِيمِ الأكَارعُ 

وَقَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: الزَّنِيمُ: الدَّعِيُّ. وَيُقَالُ: الزَّنِيمُ: رَجُلٌ كَانَتْ بِهِ زَنَمَةٌ، يُعْرَفُ بِهَا. وَيُقَالُ: هُوَ الْأَخْنَسُ بْنُ شَريق الثَّقَفِيُّ، حَلِيفُ بَنِي زُهْرَةَ. وَزَعَمَ أُنَاسٌ مِنْ بَنِي زُهْرَةَ أَنَّ الزَّنِيمَ الأسودُ بْنُ عَبْدِ يَغُوثَ الزُّهْرِيُّ، وَلَيْسَ بِهِ.

وَقَالَ ابْنِ أَبِي نَجِيح، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: أَنَّهُ زَعَمَ أَنَّ الزَّنِيمَ المُلحَق النَّسَبِ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنِي يُونُسُ حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ وَهْبٍ، حَدَّثَنِي سُلَيْمَانُ بْنُ بِلَالٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ حَرْمَلَةَ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ المُسيَّب، أَنَّهُ سَمِعَهُ يَقُولُ فِي هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ: ﴿عُتُلٍّ بَعْدَ ذَلِكَ زَنِيمٍ﴾ قَالَ سَعِيدٌ: هُوَ الْمُلْصَقُ بِالْقَوْمِ، لَيْسَ مِنْهُمْ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو سَعِيدٍ الْأَشَجُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عُقْبَةُ بْنُ خَالِدٍ، عَنْ عَامِرِ بْنِ قُدَامَةَ قَالَ: سُئِلَ عِكْرِمَةُ عَنِ الزَّنِيمِ، قَالَ: هُوَ وَلَدُ الزِّنَا.

وَقَالَ الْحَكَمِ بْنِ أَبَانَ، عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ فِي قَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿عُتُلٍّ بَعْدَ ذَلِكَ زَنِيمٍ﴾ قَالَ: يُعْرَفُ الْمُؤْمِنُ مِنَ الْكَافِرِ مِثْلُ الشَّاةِ الزَّنْمَاءِ. وَالزَّنْمَاءُ مِنَ الشِّيَاهِ: الَّتِي فِي عُنُقِهَا هَنتان مُعَلَّقَتَانِ فِي حَلْقِهَا. وَقَالَ الثَّوْرِيُّ، عَنْ جَابِرٍ، عَنِ الْحَسَنِ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ قَالَ: الزَّنِيمُ: الَّذِي يُعْرَفُ بِالشَّرِّ كَمَا تُعْرَفُ الشَّاةُ بِزَنَمَتِهَا. وَالزَّنِيمُ: الملصق. رواه ابن جرير.

وَرَوَى أَيْضًا مِنْ طَرِيقِ دَاوُدَ بْنِ أَبِي هِنْدَ عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ أَنَّهُ قَالَ فِي الزَّنِيمِ: قَالَ: نُعِتَ فَلَمْ يُعْرَفْ حَتَّى قِيلَ: زَنِيمٌ. قَالَ: وَكَانَتْ لَهُ زَنَمَةٌ فِي عُنُقِهِ يُعرَف بِهَا. وَقَالَ آخَرُونَ: كَانَ دَعيًا.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو كُرَيْب، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ إِدْرِيسَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ أَصْحَابِ التَّفْسِيرِ قَالُوا هُوَ الَّذِي تَكُونُ لَهُ زَنَمَة مِثْلُ زَنَمَةِ الشَّاةِ.

وَقَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ: كَانَتْ لَهُ زَنَمَة فِي أَصْلِ أُذُنِهِ، وَيُقَالُ: هُوَ اللَّئِيمُ الْمُلْصَقُ فِي النَّسَبِ.

وَقَالَ أَبُو إِسْحَاقَ: عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: هُوَ الْمُرِيبُ الَّذِي يُعْرَفُ بِالشَّرِّ.

وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: الزَّنِيمُ الَّذِي يُعْرَفُ بِهَذَا الْوَصْفِ كَمَا تُعْرَفُ الشَّاةُ. وَقَالَ أَبُو رَزِين: الزَّنِيمُ عَلَامَةُ الْكُفْرِ. وَقَالَ عِكْرِمَةُ: الزَّنِيمُ الَّذِي يُعْرَفُ بِاللُّؤْمِ كَمَا تُعْرَفُ الشَّاةُ بِزَنَمَتِهَا.

وَالْأَقْوَالُ فِي هَذَا كَثِيرَةٌ، وَتَرْجِعُ إِلَى مَا قُلْنَاهُ، وَهُوَ أَنَّ الزَّنِيمَ هُوَ: الْمَشْهُورُ بِالشَّرِّ، الَّذِي يُعْرَفُ بِهِ مِنْ بَيْنِ الناس، وغالبًا يكون دعيًا وله زِنًا، فَإِنَّهُ فِي الْغَالِبِ يَتَسَلَّطُ الشَّيْطَانُ عَلَيْهِ مَا لَا يَتَسَلَّطُ عَلَى غَيْرِهِ، كَمَا جَاءَ فِي الْحَدِيثِ: "لَا يَدْخُلُ الْجَنَّةَ وَلَدُ زِنًا" وَفِي الْحَدِيثِ الْآخَرِ: "وَلَدُ الزِّنَا شَرُّ الثَّلَاثَةِ إِذَا عَمِلَ بِعَمَلِ أَبَوَيْهِ" .

وَقَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿سَنَسِمُهُ عَلَى الْخُرْطُومِ﴾ يُقَاتِلُ يَوْمَ بَدْرٍ، فيُخطم بِالسَّيْفِ فِي الْقِتَالِ. وَقَالَ آخَرُونَ: ﴿سَنَسِمُهُ﴾ سِمَةَ أَهْلِ النَّارِ، يَعْنِي نُسَوِّدُ وَجْهَهُ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ، وَعَبَّرَ عَنِ الْوَجْهِ بِالْخُرْطُومِ. حَكَى ذَلِكَ كُلَّهُ أبو جعفر ابن جَرِيرٍ، وَمَالَ إِلَى أَنَّهُ لَا مَانِعَ مِنَ اجْتِمَاعِ الْجَمِيعِ عَلَيْهِ فِي الدُّنْيَا وَالْآخِرَةِ، وَهُوَ مُتّجه.

وَقَدْ قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ فِي سُورَةِ ﴿عَمَّ يَتَسَاءَلُونَ﴾ حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو صَالِحٍ كَاتِبُ اللَّيْثِ، حَدَّثَنِي اللَّيْثُ حَدَّثَنِي خَالِدٌ عَنْ سَعِيدٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ الْمَلِكِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ، عَنْ عِيسَى بْنِ هِلَالٍ الصَّدَفِيِّ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو، عَنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ أَنَّهُ قَالَ: "إِنَّ الْعَبْدَ يُكْتَبُ مُؤْمِنًا أَحْقَابًا ثُمَّ أَحْقَابًا ثُمَّ يَمُوتُ وَاللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ سَاخِطٌ. وَإِنَّ الْعَبْدَ يُكْتَبُ كَافِرًا أَحْقَابًا ثُمَّ أَحْقَابًا، ثُمَّ يَمُوتُ وَاللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ رَاضٍ. وَمَنْ مَاتَ هَمَّازًا لمَّازًا مُلَقَّبا للناس، كان علامته يوم القيامة أن يسميه اللَّهُ عَلَى الْخُرْطُومِ، مِنْ كِلَا الشَّفَتَيْنِ" .

## 68:17-33 — verses 17-33 (shared commentary)

### Translation

This is a parable which God Almighty struck for the disbelievers of Quraysh regarding the great mercy He had bestowed upon them and the immense blessings He had granted them, namely His sending of Muhammad ﷺ to them. Yet they met this with denial, rejection, and war. Hence He said: {Indeed We tried them} meaning We tested them, {as We tried the companions of the garden} which was an orchard containing various fruits and produce, {when they swore that they would surely cut its fruit in the morning}, meaning they swore among themselves that they would harvest its fruit at night so that no poor person or beggar would know of them, so that its fruit would be abundant for themselves and they would give none of it in charity, {and they did not make any exception}, meaning in what they swore. For this reason God made them break their oaths, saying: {Then a visitation from your Lord came upon it while they were asleep}, meaning a heavenly affliction struck it, {so it became like a blackened, harvested plot}. Ibn Abbas said: that is, like the black night. Al-Thawri and al-Suddi said: like crops when they have been reaped, that is, dried and withered stubble.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: it was mentioned from Ahmad ibn al-Sabbah: [chain] Bishr ibn Zadhan, from Umar ibn Subh, from Layth ibn Abi Sulaym, from Abd al-Rahman ibn Sabit, from Ibn Mas'ud, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said, "Beware of sins, for a servant may commit a sin and thereby be deprived of provision that had already been prepared for him." Then the Messenger of God ﷺ recited: {Then a visitation from your Lord came upon it while they were asleep, so it became like a blackened, harvested plot}. They were deprived of the good of their garden because of their sin.

{Then they called out to one another in the morning}, meaning when morning came, some of them called to others so that they might go to gather the harvest,

{Go out early to your crop if you intend to gather the harvest}, meaning if you wish to reap it. Mujahid said: their crop was grapes.

{So they set off, speaking to one another in secret}, meaning they whispered among themselves in such a way that no one could hear their words. Then God, the Knower of the secret and the whisper, explained what they were whispering about, saying: {So they set off, speaking to one another in secret, saying: Let no poor person enter it upon you today}, meaning some of them said to others: do not let any poor person gain access to it today!

God Almighty said: {And they went early with determined intent}, meaning with strength and resolve. Mujahid said: {and they went early with hard intent} meaning with earnestness. Ikrimah said: with anger. Al-Sha'bi said: {with hard intent} against the poor. Al-Suddi said: {with hard intent} meaning that the name of their village was Hard. Al-Suddi went far astray in this statement of his!

{Assuming they had power over it}, meaning over it, according to what they claimed and sought.

{But when they saw it, they said: Indeed we have lost our way}, meaning when they arrived at it and looked upon it, and it was in the state that God, Mighty and Majestic, described, having changed from its former freshness, bloom, and abundance of fruit into a state of blackened darkness from which nothing could be benefited, they thought they had mistaken the path. For this reason they said: {Indeed we have lost our way}, meaning we must have taken a different path to it and thus lost our way to it. This was said by Ibn Abbas and others. Then they reconsidered and became certain that it was indeed their garden, so they said: {Rather, we have been deprived}, meaning rather this is indeed it, but we have no share and no portion left in it.

{The most moderate of them said}. Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, Ikrimah, Muhammad ibn Ka'b, al-Rabi' ibn Anas, al-Dahhak, and Qatadah said: meaning the most just and best of them: {Did I not say to you, why do you not glorify God}! Mujahid, al-Suddi, and Ibn Jurayj said: {why do you not glorify God} meaning why do you not make an exception (by saying, God willing). Al-Suddi said: in that time, their making such an exception was itself considered a form of glorification (tasbih).

Ibn Jurayj said: it refers to the statement of one who says, "if God wills." And it is also said that its meaning is: {The most moderate of them said: Did I not say to you, why do you not glorify God}, meaning why do you not glorify God and thank Him for what He has given you and blessed you with, {They said: Glory be to our Lord, indeed we have been wrongdoers}. They came with obedience when it no longer benefited them, and they regretted and confessed when it no longer availed them. For this reason they said: {Indeed we have been wrongdoers, then some of them turned to others, blaming one another}, meaning they blamed each other for what they had persisted in, namely withholding from the poor their due share of the harvest. The only response any of them gave to another was an admission of error and sin: {They said: Woe to us, indeed we have been transgressors}, meaning we transgressed, we exceeded bounds, we were tyrannical, and we overstepped the limit until what befell us befell us. {Perhaps our Lord will give us something better in exchange for it. Indeed, to our Lord we turn in hope}. It is said that they hoped He would grant them something better in this world. It is also said that they reckoned its reward in the afterlife. And God knows best.

Some of the early scholars mentioned that these people were from the people of Yemen. Sa'id ibn Jubayr said: they were from a village called Darawan, six miles from Sana'a. It is also said that they were from the people of Abyssinia. Their father had left this garden for them as an inheritance, and they were People of the Book. Their father used to conduct himself in it in a good manner: what he gained from it, he would put back into it what it needed, store for his family their year's provision, and give the surplus in charity. When he died, his sons inherited it and said: our father was foolish to give away some of this to the poor; if we withhold it from them, it will be more abundant for us. When they resolved on this, they were punished with the opposite of what they intended: God caused everything in their hands to vanish entirely, the capital, the profit, and the charity alike, so that nothing remained for them.

God Almighty said: {Such is the punishment}, meaning such is the punishment of one who opposes the command of God, is stingy with what God has given him and blessed him with, withholds the due right of the poor, the needy, and those in want, and exchanges the blessing of God for ingratitude. {But the punishment of the Hereafter is greater, if only they knew}, meaning this is the punishment of this world as you have heard, but the punishment of the Hereafter is far more severe. It has been reported in a hadith narrated by the hafiz al-Bayhaqi through the route of Ja'far ibn Muhammad ibn Ali ibn al-Husayn ibn Ali ibn Abi Talib, from his father, from his grandfather, that the Messenger of God ﷺ forbade harvesting fruit at night and reaping crops at night.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: ذُكِرَ عَنْ أَحْمَدَ بْنِ الصَّبَّاحِ: أَنْبَأَنَا بِشْرُ بْنُ زَاذَانَ، عَنْ عُمَرَ بْنِ صُبْحٍ عَنْ لَيْثِ بْنِ أَبِي سُلَيْمٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ سَابِطٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِيَّاكُمْ وَالْمَعَاصِيَ، إِنَّ الْعَبْدَ لَيُذْنِبُ الذَّنْبَ فَيُحْرَمُ بِهِ رِزْقًا قَدْ كَانَ هُيِّئ لَهُ"، ثُمَّ تَلَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: ﴿فَطَافَ عَلَيْهَا طَائِفٌ مِنْ رَبِّكَ وَهُمْ نَائِمُونَ فَأَصْبَحَتْ كَالصَّرِيمِ﴾ قَدْ حُرِمُوا خَير جَنّتهم بِذَنْبِهِمْ .

﴿فَتَنَادَوْا مُصْبِحِينَ﴾ أَيْ: لَمَّا كَانَ وَقْتُ الصُّبْحِ نَادَى بَعْضُهُمْ بَعْضًا لِيَذْهَبُوا إِلَى الجَذَاذ،

﴿أَنِ اغْدُوا عَلَى حَرْثِكُمْ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ صَارِمِينَ﴾ أَيْ: تُرِيدُونَ الصِرَامَ. قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: كَانَ حَرْثُهُمْ عِنَبًا.

﴿فَانْطَلَقُوا وَهُمْ يَتَخَافَتُونَ﴾ أَيْ: يَتَنَاجَوْنَ فِيمَا بَيْنَهُمْ بِحَيْثُ لَا يُسمعون أَحَدًا كَلَامَهُمْ. ثُمَّ فَسَّرَ اللَّهُ عَالِمُ السِّرِّ وَالنَّجْوَى مَا كَانُوا يَتَخَافَتُونَ بِهِ، فَقَالَ: ﴿فَانْطَلَقُوا وَهُمْ يَتَخَافَتُونَ أَنْ لَا يَدْخُلَنَّهَا الْيَوْمَ عَلَيْكُمْ مِسْكِينٌ﴾ أَيْ: يَقُولُ بَعْضُهُمْ لِبَعْضٍ: لَا تُمَكِّنُوا الْيَوْمَ فَقِيرًا يَدْخُلُهَا عَلَيْكُمْ!

قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَغَدَوْا عَلَى حَرْدٍ﴾ أَيْ: قُوَّةٍ وَشِدَّةٍ. وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: ﴿وَغَدَوْا عَلَى حَرْدٍ﴾ أَيْ: جِدٍّ وَقَالَ عِكْرِمَةُ: غَيْظٍ. وَقَالَ الشَّعْبِيُّ: ﴿عَلَى حَرْدٍ﴾ عَلَى الْمَسَاكِينِ. وَقَالَ السُّدِّيُّ: ﴿عَلَى حَرْدٍ﴾ أي: كان اسم قريتهم حرد. فَأَبْعَدَ السُّدِّيُّ فِي قَوْلِهِ هَذَا!

﴿قَادِرِينَ﴾ أَيْ: عَلَيْهَا فِيمَا يَزْعُمُونَ ويَرومون.

﴿فَلَمَّا رَأَوْهَا قَالُوا إِنَّا لَضَالُّونَ﴾ أَيْ: فَلَمَّا وَصَلُوا إِلَيْهَا وَأَشْرَفُوا عَلَيْهَا، وَهِيَ عَلَى الْحَالَةِ الَّتِي قَالَ اللَّهُ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، قَدِ اسْتَحَالَتْ عَنْ تِلْكَ النَّضَارَةِ وَالزَّهْرَةِ وَكَثْرَةِ الثِّمَارِ إِلَى أَنْ صَارَتْ سَوْدَاءَ مُدْلَهِمَّة، لَا يُنْتَفَعُ بِشَيْءٍ مِنْهَا، فَاعْتَقَدُوا أَنَّهُمْ قَدْ أَخْطَئُوا الطَّرِيقَ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالُوا: ﴿إِنَّا لَضَالُّونَ﴾ أَيْ: قَدْ سَلَكْنَا إِلَيْهَا غَيْرَ الطَّرِيقِ فتُهنا عَنْهَا. قَالَهُ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ وَغَيْرُهُ. ثُمَّ رَجَعُوا عَمَّا كَانُوا فِيهِ، وَتَيَقَّنُوا أَنَّهَا هِيَ فَقَالُوا: ﴿بَلْ نَحْنُ مَحْرُومُونَ﴾ أَيْ: بَلْ هَذِهِ هِيَ، وَلَكِنْ نَحْنُ لَا حَظّ لَنَا وَلَا نَصِيبَ.

﴿قَالَ أَوْسَطُهُمْ﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَسَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ، وَعِكْرِمَةُ، وَمُحَمَّدُ بْنُ كَعْبٍ، وَالرَّبِيعُ بْنُ أَنَسٍ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وقَتَادَةُ: أَيْ: أَعْدَلُهُمْ وَخَيْرُهُمْ: ﴿أَلَمْ أَقُلْ لَكُمْ لَوْلا تُسَبِّحُونَ﴾ ! قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ، وَابْنُ جُرَيْجٍ: ﴿لَوْلا تُسَبِّحُونَ﴾ أَيْ: لَوْلَا تَسْتَثْنُونَ. قَالَ السُّدِّيُّ: وَكَانَ اسْتِثْنَاؤُهُمْ فِي ذَلِكَ الزمان تسبيحًا.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جُرَيْجٍ: هُوَ قَوْلُ الْقَائِلِ: إِنْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ. وَقِيلَ: مَعْنَاهُ: ﴿قَالَ أَوْسَطُهُمْ أَلَمْ أَقُلْ لَكُمْ لَوْلا تُسَبِّحُونَ﴾ أَيْ: هَلَّا تُسَبِّحُونَ اللَّهَ وَتَشْكُرُونَهُ عَلَى مَا أَعْطَاكُمْ وَأَنْعَمَ بِهِ عَلَيْكُمْ، ﴿قَالُوا سُبْحَانَ رَبِّنَا إِنَّا كُنَّا ظَالِمِينَ﴾ أَتَوْا بِالطَّاعَةِ حَيْثُ لَا تَنْفَعُ، وَنَدِمُوا وَاعْتَرَفُوا حَيْثُ لَا يَنْجَعُ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالُوا: ﴿إِنَّا كُنَّا ظَالِمِينَ فَأَقْبَلَ بَعْضُهُمْ عَلَى بَعْضٍ يَتَلاوَمُونَ﴾ أَيْ: يَلُومُ بَعْضُهُمْ بَعْضًا عَلَى مَا كَانُوا أَصَرُّوا عَلَيْهِ مِنْ مَنْعِ الْمَسَاكِينِ مِنْ حَقِّ الْجُذَاذِ، فَمَا كَانَ جَوَابُ بَعْضِهِمْ لِبَعْضٍ إِلَّا الِاعْتِرَافَ بِالْخَطِيئَةِ وَالذَّنْبِ، ﴿قَالُوا يَا وَيْلَنَا إِنَّا كُنَّا طَاغِينَ﴾ أَيِ: اعْتَدَيْنَا وبَغَينا وَطَغَيْنَا وَجَاوَزْنَا الْحَدَّ حَتَّى أَصَابَنَا مَا أَصَابَنَا، ﴿عَسَى رَبُّنَا أَنْ يُبْدِلَنَا خَيْرًا مِنْهَا إِنَّا إِلَى رَبِّنَا رَاغِبُونَ﴾ قِيلَ: رَغِبُوا فِي بَذْلِهَا لَهُمْ فِي الدُّنْيَا. وَقِيلَ: احْتَسَبُوا ثَوَابَهَا فِي الدَّارِ الْآخِرَةِ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

ثُمَّ قَدْ ذَكَرَ بَعْضُ السَّلَفِ أَنَّ هَؤُلَاءِ قَدْ كَانُوا مِنْ أَهْلِ الْيَمَنِ -قَالَ سَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ: كَانُوا مِنْ قَرْيَةٍ يُقَالُ لَهَا ضَرَوَانُ عَلَى سِتَّةِ أَمْيَالٍ مِنْ صَنْعَاءَ. وَقِيلَ: كَانُوا مِنْ أَهْلِ الْحَبَشَةِ-وَكَانَ أَبُوهُمْ قَدْ خَلَّفَ لَهُمْ هَذِهِ الْجَنَّةَ، وَكَانُوا مِنْ أَهْلِ الْكِتَابِ، وَقَدْ كَانَ أَبُوهُمْ يَسِيرُ فِيهَا سِيرَةً حَسَنَةً، فَكَانَ مَا اسْتَغَلَّهُ مِنْهَا يَرُدُّ فِيهَا مَا يَحْتَاجُ إِلَيْهَا وَيَدَّخِرُ لِعِيَالِهِ قُوتَ سَنَتِهِمْ، وَيَتَصَدَّقُ بِالْفَاضِلِ. فَلَمَّا مَاتَ وَرِثَهُ بَنُوهُ، قَالُوا: لَقَدْ كَانَ أَبُونَا أحمقَ إِذْ كَانَ يَصْرِفُ مِنْ هَذِهِ شَيْئًا لِلْفُقَرَاءِ، وَلَوْ أنَّا مَنَعْنَاهُمْ لَتَوَفَّرَ ذَلِكَ عَلَيْنَا. فَلَمَّا عَزَمُوا عَلَى ذَلِكَ عُوقِبوا بِنَقِيضِ قَصْدِهِمْ، فَأَذْهَبَ اللَّهُ مَا بأيديهم بالكلية، رأس المال الربح وَالصَّدَقَةَ، فَلَمْ يَبْقَ لَهُمْ شَيْءٌ.

قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿كَذَلِكَ الْعَذَابُ﴾ أَيْ: هَكَذَا عَذَابُ مَنْ خَالَفَ أَمْرَ اللَّهِ، وَبَخِلَ بِمَا آتَاهُ اللَّهُ وَأَنْعَمَ بِهِ عَلَيْهِ، وَمَنَعَ حَقَّ الْمِسْكِينِ وَالْفُقَرَاءِ وَذَوِي الْحَاجَاتِ، وَبَدَّلَ نِعْمَةَ اللَّهِ كُفْرًا ﴿وَلَعَذَابُ الآخِرَةِ أَكْبَرُ لَوْ كَانُوا يَعْلَمُونَ﴾ أَيْ: هَذِهِ عُقُوبَةُ الدُّنْيَا كَمَا سَمِعْتُمْ، وَعَذَابُ الْآخِرَةِ أَشَقُّ. وَقَدْ وَرَدَ فِي حَدِيثٍ رَوَاهُ الْحَافِظُ الْبَيْهَقِيُّ مِنْ طَرِيقِ جَعْفَرِ بْنِ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عَلِيِّ بْنِ الْحُسَيْنِ بْنِ عَلِيٍّ بْنِ أَبِي طَالِبٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ جَدِّهِ؛ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ نَهَى عَنِ الْجِدَادِ بِاللَّيْلِ، وَالْحَصَادِ بِاللَّيْلِ .

## 68:34-41 — verses 34-41 (shared commentary)

### Translation

When [Allah] Most High mentioned the state of the people of the worldly garden, and what befell them in it of retribution when they disobeyed Allah, Mighty and Majestic is He, and opposed His command, He made clear that for whoever fears Him and obeys Him there are, in the Hereafter, the gardens of bliss which do not perish, nor run out, nor does their bliss ever come to an end.

Then He said: {Shall We then treat the Muslims like the criminals?} That is: shall We make these and those equal in reward? Never, by the Lord of the earth and the heaven. And for this reason He said: {What is the matter with you? How do you judge?} That is: how do you suppose such a thing?

Then He said: {Or do you have a book in which you study? Indeed, in it you shall have whatever you choose} meaning: is there in your hands a book sent down from heaven which you study, memorize, and hand down from the later generations receiving it from the earlier ones, containing a confirmed ruling as you claim? {Indeed, in it you shall have whatever you choose. Or do you have oaths binding upon Us, extending to the Day of Resurrection, that you shall have whatever you decide?} That is: do you have with you covenants from Us and confirmed pledges? {Indeed, you shall have whatever you decide} meaning: that you will indeed obtain what you want and desire. {Ask them, which of them is guarantor of that?} That is: say to them, who is the one who undertakes and vouches for this?

{Or do they have partners?} meaning, from among the idols and rivals [that they associate with Allah], {then let them bring their partners, if they are truthful}.

## 68:42-47 — verses 42-47 (shared commentary)

### Translation

When the Exalted mentioned that the God-fearing have with Him Gardens of Bliss, He clarified when that will occur and take place, saying: {On the Day the leg will be uncovered and they will be called to prostration but will not be able} meaning: the Day of Resurrection and what will occur in it of terrors, tremors, trial, testing, and momentous matters. Al-Bukhari said here:

[chain] Adam told us, al-Layth told us, from Khalid ibn Yazid, from Sa'id ibn Abi Hilal, from Zayd ibn Aslam, from Ata' ibn Yasar, from Abu Sa'id al-Khudri who said: I heard the Prophet ﷺ say: "Our Lord will uncover His leg, and every believing man and believing woman will prostrate to Him, and there will remain those who used to prostrate in the world out of showing off and seeking reputation. Such a one will go to prostrate but his back will return as a single solid piece."

This hadith is recorded in the two Sahihs and elsewhere through various chains, with various wordings, and it is a long, well-known hadith.

Abdullah ibn al-Mubarak said, from Usama ibn Zayd, from Ikrimah, from Ibn Abbas, regarding {On the Day the leg will be uncovered}: he said, it is a day of distress and hardship. Ibn Jarir related this, then said:

[chain] Ibn Humayd told us, Mihran told us, from Sufyan, from al-Mughirah, from Ibrahim, from Ibn Mas'ud, or Ibn Abbas (the doubt being from Ibn Jarir): {On the Day the leg will be uncovered}, he said: concerning a momentous matter, as the poet said: "War arose among us with all its severity (an saq)."

Ibn Abi Najih said, from Mujahid, regarding {On the Day the leg will be uncovered}: he said, the severity of the matter.

Ibn Abbas said: it is the first hour that occurs on the Day of Resurrection.

Ibn Jurayj said, from Mujahid, regarding {On the Day the leg will be uncovered}: he said, the severity and seriousness of the matter.

Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn Abbas concerning His saying {On the Day the leg will be uncovered}: it is the severe and terrifying matter, from the terror of the Day of Resurrection.

Al-Awfi said, from Ibn Abbas concerning His saying {On the Day the leg will be uncovered}: he says, when the matter is uncovered and the deeds become apparent. Its uncovering is the entry into the Hereafter, and the uncovering of the matter regarding it. Al-Dahhak and others likewise related this from Ibn Abbas. Abu Ja'far ibn Jarir related all of this, then said:

[chain] Abu Zayd Umar ibn Shabbah told me, Harun ibn Umar al-Makhzumi told us, al-Walid ibn Muslim told us, Abu Sa'id Rawh ibn Janah told us, from a client (mawla) of Umar ibn Abd al-Aziz, from Abu Burdah ibn Abi Musa, from his father, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said regarding {On the Day the leg will be uncovered}: "concerning a great light, before which they will fall down in prostration."

Abu Ya'la related it, from al-Qasim ibn Yahya, from al-Walid ibn Muslim, with the same chain, but in it there is an unidentified man. God knows best.

His saying {With eyes humbled, disgrace overwhelming them} means: in the Hereafter, on account of their crimes and their arrogance in this world, they will be punished with the opposite of what they were upon. Since they were called to prostration in this world and refused it despite their soundness and health, they will likewise be punished by their inability to do it in the Hereafter, when the Lord, Mighty and Majestic, manifests Himself and the believers prostrate to Him. None of the disbelievers or hypocrites will be able to prostrate; rather, each of their backs will return as a single solid piece. Whenever one of them wishes to prostrate, he will fall backward onto the nape of his neck, the reverse of prostration, just as they were in this world, contrary to what the believers will be upon.

Then the Exalted said: {So leave Me with whoever denies this discourse}, meaning: the Qur'an. This is a severe threat, meaning: leave him and Me to deal with each other; I know best how to draw him on by degrees, extend him in his error, give him respite, then seize him with the seizing of One Mighty and Powerful. Hence He said: {We will draw them on by degrees from where they do not know}, meaning: while they are unaware, indeed they believe that this is a mark of honor from God, while in reality it is a degradation, just as He said: {Do they think that what We extend to them of wealth and children is Us hastening good things to them? Rather, they do not perceive} [al-Mu'minun: 55-56]. And He said: {So when they forgot what they had been reminded of, We opened upon them the gates of everything, until, when they rejoiced in what they were given, We seized them suddenly, and thereupon they were in despair} [al-An'am: 44]. Hence He said here: {And I will grant them respite; indeed My plan is firm}, meaning: I will delay them, give them respite, and extend them, and that is part of My scheme and plot against them. Hence the Exalted said: {Indeed My plan is firm}, meaning: mighty against whoever opposes My command, denies My messengers, and dares to disobey Me.

In the two Sahihs, from the Messenger of God ﷺ, he said: "Indeed God grants respite to the wrongdoer, until when He seizes him, He does not let him escape." Then he recited: {And thus is the seizure of your Lord when He seizes the towns while they are wrongdoing; indeed His seizure is painful and severe} [Hud: 102].

His saying {Or do you ask them for a payment, so that they are burdened with debt? Or do they have the unseen, so they are writing it down?} Their interpretation has already preceded in Surat al-Tur. The meaning here is that you, Muhammad, call them to God, Mighty and Majestic, without any payment which you take from them; rather, you hope for the reward of that with God, Mighty and Majestic. Yet they deny what you have brought them, out of sheer ignorance, disbelief, and obstinacy.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿خَاشِعَةً أَبْصَارُهُمْ تَرْهَقُهُمْ ذِلَّةٌ﴾ أَيْ: فِي الدَّارِ الْآخِرَةِ بِإِجْرَامِهِمْ وَتَكَبُّرِهِمْ فِي الدُّنْيَا، فَعُوقِبُوا بِنَقِيضِ مَا كَانُوا عَلَيْهِ. وَلَمَّا دُعُوا إِلَى السُّجُودِ فِي الدُّنْيَا فَامْتَنَعُوا مِنْهُ مَعَ صِحَّتِهِمْ وَسَلَامَتِهِمْ كَذَلِكَ عُوقِبُوا بِعَدَمِ قُدْرَتِهِمْ عَلَيْهِ فِي الْآخِرَةِ، إِذَا تَجَلَّى الرَّبُّ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، فَيَسْجُدُ لَهُ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ، لَا يَسْتَطِيعُ أَحَدٌ مِنَ الْكَافِرِينَ وَلَا الْمُنَافِقِينَ أَنْ يسجُد، بَلْ يَعُودُ ظَهْرُ أَحَدِهِمْ طَبَقًا وَاحِدًا، كُلَّمَا أَرَادَ أَحَدُهُمْ أَنْ يَسْجُدَ خَرّ لِقَفَاهُ، عَكْسَ السُّجُودِ، كَمَا كَانُوا فِي الدُّنْيَا، بِخِلَافِ مَا عَلَيْهِ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَذَرْنِي وَمَنْ يُكَذِّبُ بِهَذَا الْحَدِيثِ﴾ يَعْنِي: الْقُرْآنَ. وَهَذَا تَهْدِيدٌ شَدِيدٌ، أَيْ: دَعْنِي وَإِيَّاهُ مِنِّي وَمِنْهُ، أَنَا أَعْلَمُ بِهِ كَيْفَ أَسْتَدْرِجُهُ، وَأَمُدُّهُ فِي غَيِّهِ وَأَنْظُرُ ثُمَّ آخُذُهُ أَخْذَ عَزِيزٍ مُقْتَدِرٍ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿سَنَسْتَدْرِجُهُمْ مِنْ حَيْثُ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ﴾ أَيْ: وَهُمْ لَا يَشْعُرُونَ، بَلْ يَعْتَقِدُونَ أَنَّ ذَلِكَ مِنَ اللَّهِ كَرَامَةٌ، وَهُوَ فِي نَفْسِ الْأَمْرِ إِهَانَةٌ، كَمَا قَالَ: ﴿أَيَحْسَبُونَ أَنَّمَا نُمِدُّهُمْ بِهِ مِنْ مَالٍ وَبَنِينَ نُسَارِعُ لَهُمْ فِي الْخَيْرَاتِ بَل لَا يَشْعُرُونَ﴾ [الْمُؤْمِنُونَ: ٥٥، ٥٦] ، وَقَالَ: ﴿فَلَمَّا نَسُوا مَا ذُكِّرُوا بِهِ فَتَحْنَا عَلَيْهِمْ أَبْوَابَ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ حَتَّى إِذَا فَرِحُوا بِمَا أُوتُوا أَخَذْنَاهُمْ بَغْتَةً فَإِذَا هُمْ مُبْلِسُونَ﴾ [الأنعام: ٤٤] . ولهذا قال هاهنا: ﴿وَأُمْلِي لَهُمْ إِنَّ كَيْدِي مَتِينٌ﴾ أَيْ: وَأُؤَخِّرُهُمْ وَأُنْظِرُهُمْ وَأَمُدُّهُمْ وَذَلِكَ مِنْ كَيْدِي وَمَكْرِي بِهِمْ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِنَّ كَيْدِي مَتِينٌ﴾ أَيْ: عَظِيمٌ لِمَنْ خَالَفَ أَمْرِي، وَكَذَّبَ رُسُلِي، وَاجْتَرَأَ عَلَى مَعْصِيَتِي.

وَفِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ عَنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ أَنَّهُ قَالَ: "إِنَّ اللَّهَ تَعَالَى ليُمْلي لِلظَّالِمِ، حَتَّى إِذَا أَخَذَهُ لَمْ يُفْلِتْه". ثُمَّ قَرَأَ: ﴿وَكَذَلِكَ أَخْذُ رَبِّكَ إِذَا أَخَذَ الْقُرَى وَهِيَ ظَالِمَةٌ إِنَّ أَخْذَهُ أَلِيمٌ شَدِيدٌ﴾ [هُودٍ: ١٠٢] .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿أَمْ تَسْأَلُهُمْ أَجْرًا فَهُمْ مِنْ مَغْرَمٍ مُثْقَلُونَ أَمْ عِنْدَهُمُ الْغَيْبُ فَهُمْ يَكْتُبُونَ﴾ تَقَدَّمَ تَفْسِيرُهُمَا فِي سُورَةِ "الطُّورِ" وَالْمَعْنَى فِي ذَلِكَ: أَنَّكَ يَا مُحَمَّدُ تَدْعُوهُمْ إِلَى اللَّهِ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، بِلَا أَجْرٍ تَأْخُذُهُ مِنْهُمْ، بَلْ تَرْجُو ثَوَابَ ذَلِكَ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، وَهُمْ يُكَذِّبُونَ بِمَا جِئْتَهُمْ بِهِ، بِمُجَرَّدِ الْجَهْلِ وَالْكُفْرِ وَالْعِنَادِ.

## 68:48-52 — verses 48-52 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says: {So be patient} O Muhammad over the harm your people cause you and their denial of you, for God will judge in your favor against them, and will make the final outcome yours and that of your followers, in this world and the next. {And do not be like the companion of the fish}, meaning Dhu'l-Nun, who is Yunus ibn Matta (peace be upon him), when he went off in anger at his people. What happened to him happened: his boarding the ship, the fish swallowing him, the fish fleeing with him through the seas and the darknesses of the depths of the ocean, and his hearing the glorification of the sea and what is in it for the Most High, the All-Powerful, whose decree once carried out cannot be turned back. At that point he called out in the darknesses: {There is no god but You, glory be to You, indeed I have been among the wrongdoers} [al-Anbiya: 87]. God said: {So We answered him and delivered him from distress, and thus do We deliver the believers} [al-Anbiya: 88]. And the Exalted said: {Had he not been among those who glorify God, he would have remained in its belly until the Day they are resurrected} [al-Saffat: 143-144]. And He says here: {When he called out while he was makzum (choked with distress)}. Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, and al-Suddi said: meaning full of grief (maghmum). Ata al-Khurasani and Abu Malik said: distressed (makrub).

We have already related in the hadith that when he said, {There is no god but You, glory be to You, indeed I have been among the wrongdoers}, the utterance emerged and circled around the Throne, and the angels said: "O our Lord, this is a faint, familiar voice from strange lands." God said: "Do you not recognize this?" They said: "No." He said: "This is Yunus." They said: "O Lord, Your servant whose righteous deeds and answered supplications have continually been raised up to You?" He said: "Yes." They said: "Will You not have mercy on what he used to do in ease, and so deliver him from this trial?" So God commanded the fish, and it cast him out onto the bare open ground. This is why the Exalted said: {So his Lord chose him and made him among the righteous}.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Abdullah said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "It is not fitting for anyone to say: I am better than Yunus ibn Matta."

Al-Bukhari related it from the hadith of Sufyan al-Thawri, and it is in the two Sahihs from the hadith of Abu Hurayrah.

Regarding His saying: {And indeed those who disbelieve would almost make you slip with their eyes}, Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, and others said: {la-yuzliqunaka} means they would pierce you through with their eyes, that is, they would fix their gazes upon you, meaning they envy you out of their hatred for you, were it not for God's protection and shielding of you from them. In this verse there is evidence that the evil eye, its striking and its effect, is real, by the command of God, Mighty and Majestic, as has come in the hadiths related through numerous and many chains.

The hadith of Anas ibn Malik, may God be pleased with him: Abu Dawud said: [chain] The Prophet ﷺ said: "There is no ruqyah (incantation) except from the evil eye, or from a venomous sting, or from bleeding that will not stop." Al-Abbas did not mention "the evil eye." This is the wording of Sulayman.

The hadith of Buraydah ibn al-Husayb, may God be pleased with him: Abu Abdullah Ibn Majah said: [chain] the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "There is no ruqyah except from the evil eye or from a venomous sting."

This is how Ibn Majah related it. Muslim brought it out in his Sahih from [chain] Buraydah, as a mawquf (halted report), and it contains a story. Shu'bah also related it from Husayn, from al-Sha'bi, from Buraydah, as al-Tirmidhi said. Al-Bukhari related this hadith from the hadith of Muhammad ibn Fudayl, Abu Dawud from the hadith of Malik ibn Mighwal, and al-Tirmidhi from the hadith of Sufyan ibn Uyaynah, all three from Husayn, from Amir, from al-Sha'bi, from Imran ibn Husayn, as a mawquf report.

The hadith of Abu Jundab ibn Junadah: The hafiz Abu Ya'la al-Mawsili, may God have mercy on him, said: [chain] from Abu Dharr, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Indeed the evil eye afflicts a man by God's permission, so he rises up to a great height, then falls down from it." Its chain is gharib (singular), and they did not bring it out.

The hadith of Habis al-Tamimi: Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Hayyah ibn Habis al-Tamimi, that his father informed him that he heard the Messenger of God ﷺ say: "There is nothing to al-hamm (owl-omens), and the evil eye is real, and the most truthful form of divination by omen is al-fa'l (a good omen)."

Al-Tirmidhi related it from [chain], through Yahya ibn Abi Kathir, with it, then said: gharib. He said: Shayban related it from Yahya ibn Abi Kathir, from Hayyah ibn Habis, from his father, from Abu Hurayrah, from the Prophet ﷺ.

I say: Imam Ahmad likewise related it from [chain], from Shayban, from Yahya ibn Abi Kathir, from Hayyah, who related it from his father, from Abu Hurayrah: that the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "There is no harm in al-hamm, and the evil eye is real, and the most truthful form of divination by omen is al-fa'l."

The hadith of Ibn Abbas: Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Ibn Abbas said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "The evil eye is real, the evil eye is real, it brings a man down from a high place." Gharib.

Another route: Muslim said in his Sahih: [chain] from Ibn Abbas, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "The evil eye is real. Were anything to outrun the decree, the evil eye would outrun it. And when you are asked to wash, then wash." Muslim related this alone, without al-Bukhari.

Abd al-Razzaq said, from Sufyan al-Thawri, from Mansur, from al-Minhal ibn Amr, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, from Ibn Abbas, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ used to seek protection for al-Hasan and al-Husayn, saying: "I seek protection for you both by the complete words of God, from every devil and every venomous creature, and from every envious eye that harms." And he would say: "Thus did Ibrahim seek protection for Ishaq and Isma'il (peace be upon them both)."

Al-Bukhari and the compilers of the Sunan brought it out from the hadith of al-Minhal, with it.

The hadith of Abu Umamah As'ad ibn Sahl ibn Hunayf, may God be pleased with him: Ibn Majah said: [chain] Abu Umamah ibn Sahl ibn Hunayf said: Amir ibn Rabi'ah passed by Sahl ibn Hunayf while he was bathing, and said: "I have never seen the like of today, nor the skin of one so concealed." Not long after, Sahl was struck down. He was brought to the Messenger of God ﷺ and it was said to him: "Come quickly, Sahl has collapsed." He said: "Whom do you suspect for this?" They said: "Amir ibn Rabi'ah." He said: "Why does one of you kill his brother? If one of you sees something in his brother that pleases him, let him pray for blessing upon him." Then he called for water, and ordered Amir to perform ablution, washing his face, his hands to the elbows, his knees, and inside his lower garment, and ordered that it be poured over him.

Sufyan said: Ma'mar said, from al-Zuhri: "And he ordered that the vessel be tipped out behind him."

Al-Nasa'i related it from the hadith of Sufyan ibn Uyaynah and Malik ibn Anas, both from al-Zuhri, with it. And from the hadith of Sufyan ibn Uyaynah also, from Ma'mar, from al-Zuhri, from Abu Umamah: "and the vessel is tipped out behind him." And from the hadith of Ibn Abi Dhi'b, from al-Zuhri, from Abu Umamah As'ad ibn Sahl ibn Hunayf, from his father, with it. And from the hadith of Malik also, from Muhammad ibn Abi Umamah ibn Sahl, from his father, with it.

The hadith of Abu Sa'id al-Khudri: Ibn Majah said: [chain] Abu Sa'id said: The Messenger of God ﷺ used to seek protection from the eyes of the jinn and the eyes of mankind. Then when al-Mu'awwidhatan (the two protective surahs) were revealed, he took hold of them and left off everything else.

Al-Tirmidhi and al-Nasa'i related it from the hadith of Sa'id ibn Iyas Abu Mas'ud al-Jurayri, with it, and al-Tirmidhi said: hasan (sound).

Another hadith on his authority: Imam Ahmad said: Abd al-Samad ibn Abd al-Warith told us, my father told me, Abd al-Aziz ibn Suhayb told me, Abu Nadrah told me, from Abu Sa'id: that Jibril (peace be upon him) came to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and said, "Are you suffering, O Muhammad?" He said, "Yes." He said, "In the name of Allah I charm you, from everything that harms you, from the evil of every soul and eye, Allah heals you, in the name of Allah I charm you."

He also related it from Affan, from Abd al-Warith, in the same manner. Muslim and the compilers of the Sunan works, except Abu Dawud, related it from the hadith of Abd al-Warith, with the same chain.

Imam Ahmad also said: Affan told us, Wuhayb told us, Dawud told us, from Abu Nadrah, from Abu Sa'id, or Jabir ibn Abdullah, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was suffering, so Jibril came to him and said, "In the name of Allah I charm you, from everything that harms you, from every envier and eye, and Allah heals you."

He also related it from Muhammad ibn Abd al-Rahman al-Tafawi, from Dawud, from Abu Nadrah, from Abu Sa'id, with the same content.

Abu Zur'ah al-Razi said: Abd al-Samad ibn Abd al-Warith related, from his father, from Abd al-Aziz, from Abu Nadrah, and also from Abd al-Aziz, from Anas, with the same meaning, and both are sound.

The hadith of Abu Hurayrah: Imam Ahmad said: Abd al-Razzaq told us, Ma'mar informed us, from Hammam ibn Munabbih who said: this is what Abu Hurayrah told us from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ: "Indeed, the evil eye is real."

Both Bukhari and Muslim recorded it from the hadith of Abd al-Razzaq.

Ibn Majah said: Abu Bakr ibn Abi Shaybah told us, Isma'il ibn Ulayyah told us, from al-Jurayri, from Mudarib ibn Hazn, from Abu Hurayrah who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "The evil eye is real." He alone related it in this form.

Ahmad also related it, from Isma'il ibn Ulayyah, from Sa'id al-Jurayri, with the same chain.

Imam Ahmad said: Ibn Numayr told us, Thawr, that is ibn Yazid, told us, from Makhul, from Abu Hurayrah who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "The evil eye is real, and Satan attends it, along with the envy of the son of Adam."

Ahmad said: Khalaf ibn al-Walid told us, Abu Ma'shar told us, from Muhammad ibn Qays: Abu Hurayrah was asked, "Did you hear the Messenger of Allah say that bad omens lie in three things: the dwelling, the horse, and the woman?" He said: I said, then I would be saying about the Messenger of Allah ﷺ what he did not say! But I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "The truest form of foreboding is a good omen, and the evil eye is real."

The hadith of Asma bint Umays: Imam Ahmad said: Sufyan told us, from Amr ibn Dinar, from Urwah ibn Amir, from Ubayd ibn Rifa'ah al-Zuraqi who said: Asma said, "O Messenger of Allah, the children of Ja'far are afflicted by the evil eye, shall I have a charm recited for them?" He said, "Yes, for if anything could outrun the divine decree, the evil eye would outrun it."

Al-Tirmidhi and Ibn Majah likewise related it from the hadith of Sufyan ibn Uyaynah, with the same chain. Al-Tirmidhi and al-Nasa'i also related it, from the hadith of Abd al-Razzaq, from Ma'mar, from Ayyub, from Amr ibn Dinar, from Urwah ibn Amir, from Ubayd ibn Rifa'ah, from Asma bint Umays, with the same content. Al-Tirmidhi said: it is hasan sahih (sound and good).

The hadith of Aisha, may Allah be pleased with her: Ibn Majah said: Ali ibn Abi al-Khasib told us, Waki' told us, from Sufyan and Mis'ar, from Ma'bad ibn Khalid, from Abdullah ibn Shaddad, from Aisha, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ ordered her to have a charm recited against the evil eye.

Al-Bukhari related it from Muhammad ibn Kathir, from Sufyan, from Ma'bad ibn Khalid, with the same chain. Muslim recorded it from the hadith of Sufyan and Mis'ar, both from Ma'bad, with the same chain. Then Ibn Majah said:

Muhammad ibn Bashshar told us, Abu Hisham al-Makhzumi told us, Wuhayb told us, from Abu Waqid, from Abu Salamah ibn Abd al-Rahman, from Aisha who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "Seek refuge in Allah, for the evil eye (al-nafs, the self/soul that causes harm) is real." He alone related it in this form.

Abu Dawud said: Uthman ibn Abi Shaybah told us, Jarir told us, al-A'mash told us, from Ibrahim, from al-Aswad, from Aisha who said: the one who cast the evil eye would be ordered to perform ablution, and the afflicted person would be washed with that water.

The hadith of Sahl ibn Hunayf: Imam Ahmad said: Husayn ibn Muhammad told us, Abu Uways told us, al-Zuhri told us, from Abu Umamah ibn Sahl ibn Hunayf: that his father told him that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ set out, and they traveled with him toward Mecca, until when they were at Shi'b al-Kharrar, from al-Juhfah, Sahl ibn Hunayf bathed. He was a man of fair complexion and fine body and skin. Amir ibn Rabi'ah, brother of the Banu Adi ibn Ka'b, looked at him while he was bathing and said, "I have never seen anything like this day, nor skin like a concealed woman's." Sahl then collapsed. He was brought to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and it was said to him, "O Messenger of Allah, can you do something for Sahl? By Allah, he cannot raise his head nor regain consciousness." He said, "Do you suspect anyone regarding him?" They said, "Amir ibn Rabi'ah looked at him." So the Messenger of Allah ﷺ called Amir, became angry with him, and said, "Why does one of you kill his brother? When you see something that pleases you, why do you not invoke a blessing?" Then he said to him, "Wash for him." So he washed his face, his hands, his elbows, his knees, the extremities of his feet, and the inside of his waist-wrap, into a vessel. Then that water was poured over him, a man poured it over his head and back from behind him, then the vessel was turned upside down behind him. This was done, and Sahl went on his way with the people, no longer afflicted.

The hadith of Amir ibn Rabi'ah: Imam Ahmad said in the Musnad entry for Amir: Waki' told us, my father told us, Abdullah ibn Isa told us, from Umayyah ibn Hind ibn Sahl ibn Hunayf, from Abdullah ibn Amir who said: Amir ibn Rabi'ah and Sahl ibn Hunayf set out intending to bathe. He said: they went looking for a sheltered spot. He said: Amir removed a woolen cloak he was wearing, and I looked at him and struck him with my eye, so he went down into the water to bathe. He said: I heard a cracking sound from him in the water, so I went to him and called him three times, but he did not answer me. So I went to the Prophet ﷺ and told him. He said: he came walking and waded into the water, as if I can still see the whiteness of his shins. He said: he struck his chest with his hand, then said, "O Allah, remove from him its heat and its cold and its sickness." He said: then he stood up. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "When one of you sees something in his brother, or in himself, or in his property that pleases him, let him invoke a blessing, for the evil eye is real."

The hadith of Jabir: The Hafiz Abu Bakr al-Bazzar said in his Musnad: Muhammad ibn Ma'mar told us, Abu Dawud told us, Talib ibn Habib ibn Amr ibn Sahl al-Ansari, known as Ibn al-Daji' (son of the bedfellow of Hamzah, may Allah be pleased with him), told us, Abd al-Rahman ibn Jabir ibn Abdullah told me, from his father who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "Most of those who die from my nation, after the decree of Allah and His judgment and His predestination, die because of souls (i.e., the evil eye)."

Al-Bazzar said: meaning the evil eye. He said: we do not know of this hadith being related from the Prophet ﷺ except through this chain.

I say: rather, it has been related through another route from Jabir. The Hafiz Abu Abd al-Rahman Muhammad ibn al-Mundhir al-Harawi, known as Shakkar, said in his book al-Ajaib, which contains valuable and rare benefits: al-Rahawi told us, Ya'qub ibn Muhammad told us, Ali ibn Abi Ali al-Hashimi told us, Muhammad ibn al-Munkadir told us, from Jabir ibn Abdullah, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "The evil eye is real, it may bring a man to the grave and a camel to the cooking pot, and indeed most of the destruction of my nation is due to the evil eye."

Then he related it from Shu'ayb ibn Ayyub, from Mu'awiyah ibn Hisham, from Sufyan, from Muhammad ibn al-Munkadir, from Jabir who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "The evil eye may indeed bring a man into the grave, and bring a camel into the cooking pot."

The hadith of Abdullah ibn Amr: Imam Ahmad said: Qutaybah told us, Rishdin ibn Sa'd told us, from al-Hasan ibn Thawban, from Hisham ibn Abi Ruqayyah, from Abdullah ibn Amr who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "There is no contagion, no bad omen, no owl of ill-fortune, and no envy that transfers on its own, but the evil eye is real." Ahmad alone related it in this form.

A hadith from Ali: The Hafiz Ibn Asakir related, through the route of Khaythamah ibn Sulayman the Hafiz: Ubayd ibn Muhammad al-Kashwari told us, Abdullah ibn Abdullah ibn Abd Rabbihi al-Basri told us, from Abu Raja, from Shu'bah, from Abu Ishaq, from al-Harith, from Ali, that Jibril (peace be upon him) came to the Prophet ﷺ and found him grieved, and said, "O Muhammad, what is this grief I see on your face?" He said, "Al-Hasan and al-Husayn have been afflicted by an evil eye." He said, "He spoke truly regarding the evil eye, for the evil eye is real. Should you not have protected them with these words?" He said, "And what are they, O Jibril?"

He said: Say: "O Allah, Possessor of the mighty dominion, Possessor of the ancient bounty, Possessor of the noble Face, Guardian of the perfect words and the answered supplications, protect al-Hasan and al-Husayn from the souls of the jinn and the eyes of mankind." The Prophet ﷺ said it, and the two of them got up playing before him. Then the Prophet ﷺ said: "Seek protection for yourselves, your wives, and your children with this formula of protection, for none who have ever sought protection have sought protection with anything like it."

Al-Khatib al-Baghdadi said: only Abu Raja' Muhammad ibn 'Ubaydillah al-Hayati, of the people of Tustar, transmitted this report on his own. Ibn 'Asakir mentioned it in the biographical entry on "Tarrad ibn al-Husayn" in his history.

Concerning His saying: {And they say, indeed he is possessed} that is, they scorn him with their eyes and injure him with their tongues, saying: {Indeed he is possessed}, meaning because of his bringing the Qur'an. Allah, exalted is He, said: {And it is nothing but a reminder to the worlds}.

وَقَدْ قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا وَكِيعٌ، حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ أَبِي وَائِلٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "لَا يَنْبَغِي لِأَحَدٍ أَنْ يَقُولَ: أَنَا خَيْرٌ مِنْ يُونُسَ بْنِ مَتَّى".

وَرَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ مِنْ حديث سفيان الثَّوْرِيِّ وَهُوَ فِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي هريرة .

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِنْ يَكَادُ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَيُزْلِقُونَكَ بِأَبْصَارِهِمْ﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَغَيْرُهُمَا: ﴿لَيُزْلِقُونَكَ﴾ لَيُنْفِذُونَكَ بِأَبْصَارِهِمْ، أَيْ: لَيُعِينُونَكَ بِأَبْصَارِهِمْ، بِمَعْنَى: يَحْسُدُونَكَ لِبُغْضِهِمْ إِيَّاكَ لَوْلَا وِقَايَةُ اللَّهِ لَكَ، وَحِمَايَتُهُ إِيَّاكَ مِنْهُمْ. وَفِي هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ دَلِيلٌ عَلَى أَنَّ الْعَيْنَ إِصَابَتُهَا وَتَأْثِيرُهَا حَقٌّ، بِأَمْرِ اللَّهِ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، كَمَا وَرَدَتْ بِذَلِكَ الْأَحَادِيثُ الْمَرْوِيَّةُ مِنْ طُرُقٍ مُتَعَدِّدَةٍ كَثِيرَةٍ.

حَدِيثِ أَنَسِ بْنِ مَالِكٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ: قَالَ أَبُو دَاوُدَ: حَدَّثَنَا سُلَيْمَانُ بْنُ دَاوُدَ العَتَكي، حَدَّثَنَا شَرِيكٌ (ح) ، وَحَدَّثَنَا الْعَبَّاسُ العَنْبَريّ، حَدَّثَنَا يَزِيدُ بْنُ هَارُونَ، أَنْبَأَنَا شَرِيكٌ، عَنْ الْعَبَّاسِ بْنِ ذَرِيح، عَنِ الشَّعْبِيِّ -قَالَ الْعَبَّاسُ: عَنْ أَنَسٍ-قَالَ: قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: "لَا رُقْيَةَ إِلَّا مِنْ عَيْنٍ أَوْ حُمة أَوْ دَمٍ لَا يُرْقَأُ". لَمْ يَذْكُرِ الْعَبَّاسُ الْعَيْنَ. وَهَذَا لَفْظُ سُلَيْمَانَ .

حَدِيثُ بُرَيدة بْنِ الحُصَيب، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ: قَالَ أَبُو عَبْدِ اللَّهِ ابْنُ مَاجَهْ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ نُمَير، حَدَّثَنَا إِسْحَاقُ بْنُ سُلَيْمَانَ، عَنْ أَبِي جَعْفَرٍ الرَّازِيِّ، عَنْ حُصَين، عَنِ الشَّعْبِيِّ، عَنْ بُرَيدة بْنِ الْحُصَيْبِ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "لَا رُقْيَةَ إِلَّا مِنْ عَيْنٍ أَوْ حُمة" .

هَكَذَا رَوَاهُ ابْنُ مَاجَهْ وَقَدْ أَخْرَجَهُ مُسْلِمٌ فِي صَحِيحِهِ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ مَنْصُورٍ، عَنْ هُشَيْمٌ، عَنْ حُصَين بْنِ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ، عَنْ عَامِرٍ الشَّعْبِيِّ، عَنْ بُرَيْدَةَ مَوْقُوفًا، وَفِيهِ قِصَّةٌ وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ شُعْبَةُ، عَنْ حُصَيْنٍ، عَنِ الشَّعْبِيِّ، عَنْ بُرَيْدَةَ. قَالَهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ وَرَوَى هَذَا الْحَدِيثَ الْإِمَامُ الْبُخَارِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ فُضَيْلٍ، وَأَبُو دَاوُدَ مِنْ حَدِيثِ مَالِكِ بْنِ مِغْول، وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ سُفْيَانَ بْنِ عُيَيْنَةَ، ثَلَاثَتُهُمْ عَنْ حُصَيْنٍ، عَنْ عَامِرٍ عَنِ الشَّعْبِيِّ، عَنْ عِمَرَانَ بْنِ حُصَين مَوْقُوفًا .

حَدِيثُ أَبِي جُنْدُبِ بْنِ جُنَادَةَ: قَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو يَعْلَى الْمَوْصِلِيُّ، رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ: حَدَّثَنَا إِبْرَاهِيمُ بْنُ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عَرْعَرَةَ بْنِ البِرِند السَّامِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا دَيْلَمُ بْنُ غَزوان، حَدَّثَنَا وهْب بْنُ أَبِي دُبَيٍّ، عَنْ أَبِي حَرْبٍ عَنْ أَبِي ذَرٍّ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ الْعَيْنَ لَتُولِعُ الرجلَ بِإِذْنِ اللَّهِ، فَيَتَصَاعَدُ حَالِقًا، ثُمَّ يَتَرَدَّى مِنْهُ" إِسْنَادُهُ غَرِيبٌ، وَلَمْ يُخَرِّجُوهُ .

حَدِيثُ حَابِسٍ التَّمِيمِيِّ: قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الصَّمَدِ، حَدَّثَنَا حَرْبٌ، حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى بْنُ أَبِي كَثِيرٍ، حَدَّثَنِي حَيَّة بْنُ حَابِسٍ التَّمِيمِيُّ: أَنَّ أَبَاهُ أَخْبَرَهُ: أَنَّهُ سَمِعَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "لَا شَيْءَ فِي الْهَامِّ، وَالْعَيْنُ حَقٌّ، وَأَصْدَقُ الطيَرَة الفَألُ" .

وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ عَلِيٍّ، عَنْ أَبِي غَسَّانَ يَحْيَى بْنِ كَثِيرٍ، عَنْ عَلِيُّ بْنُ الْمُبَارَكِ، عَنْ يَحْيَى بْنِ أَبِي كَثِيرٍ، بِهِ ثُمَّ قَالَ غَرِيبٌ. قَالَ وَرَوَى شَيْبَانُ، عَنْ يَحْيَى بْنِ أَبِي كَثِيرٍ، عَنْ حَيَّة بْنِ حَابِسٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ.

قُلْتُ: كَذَلِكَ رَوَاهُ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ، عَنْ حَسَنِ بْنِ مُوسَى وحُسَين بن محمد، عن شيبان، يَحْيَى بْنِ أَبِي كَثِيرٍ، عَنْ حَيَّة، حَدَّثَهُ عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ؛ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "لَا بَأْسَ فِي الْهَامِّ، وَالْعَيْنُ حَقٌّ، وَأَصْدَقُ الطِّيرَةِ الْفَأْلُ " .

حَدِيثُ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ الْوَلِيدِ، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ، عَنْ دُوَيد، حَدَّثَنِي إِسْمَاعِيلُ بْنُ ثَوْبَانَ، عَنْ جَابِرِ بْنِ زَيْدٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "العين حَقٌّ، الْعَيْنُ حَقٌّ، تَسْتَنْزِلُ الْحَالِقَ" غَرِيبٌ.

طَرِيقٌ أُخْرَى: قَالَ مُسْلِمٌ فِي صَحِيحِهِ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ الدَّارِمِيُّ، أَخْبَرَنَا مُسْلِمُ بْنُ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، حَدَّثَنَا وُهَيب، عَنِ ابْنِ طَاوُسٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ قَالَ: "الْعَيْنُ حَقٌّ، وَلَوْ كَانَ شَيْءٌ سَابَقَ القَدَرَ سَبَقَت الْعَيْنُ، وَإِذَا اغْتُسلتم فَاغْسِلُوا". انْفَرَدَ بِهِ دُونَ الْبُخَارِيِّ .

وَقَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ الثَّوْرِيِّ، عَنْ مَنْصُورٍ، عَنِ المِنْهال بْنِ عَمْرٍو، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَير، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ يُعَوِّذ الْحَسَنَ وَالْحُسَيْنَ، يَقُولُ: "أُعِيذُكُمَا بِكَلِمَاتِ اللَّهِ التَّامَّةِ، مَنْ كُلِّ شَيْطَانٍ وهَامَّة، وَمَنْ كُلِّ عَيْنٍ لامَّة"، وَيَقُولُ هَكَذَا كَانَ إِبْرَاهِيمُ يُعَوِّذ إِسْحَاقَ وَإِسْمَاعِيلَ، عَلَيْهِمَا السَّلَامُ".

أَخْرَجَهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ وَأَهْلُ السُّنَنِ مِنْ حَدِيثِ الْمِنْهَالِ، بِهِ 

حَدِيثُ أَبِي أُمَامَةَ أَسْعَدَ بْنِ سَهْلِ بْنِ حُنَيْفٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ: "قَالَ ابْنُ مَاجَهْ: حَدَّثَنَا هِشَامُ بْنُ عَمَّارٍ، حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، عَنِ الزُّهْرِيِّ، عَنْ أَبِي أُمَامَةَ ابْنِ سَهْلِ بْنِ حُنَيف قَالَ: مَرَّ عَامِرُ بْنُ رَبِيعَةَ بِسَهْلِ بْنِ حُنَيف، وَهُوَ يَغْتَسِلُ، فَقَالَ: لَمْ أَرَ كَالْيَوْمِ وَلَا جلدَ مُخَبَّأَةٍ. فَمَا لَبِثَ أَنْ لُبِطَ بِهِ، فَأُتِيَ بِهِ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقِيلَ لَهُ: أَدْرِكْ سَهْلًا صَرِيعًا. قَالَ: "مَنْ تَتَّهِمُونَ بِهِ؟ ". قَالُوا: عَامِرَ بْنَ رَبِيعَةَ. قَالَ: "عَلَامَ يَقْتُلُ أَحَدُكُمْ أَخَاهُ؟ إِذَا رَأَى أَحَدَكُمْ مِنْ أَخِيهِ مَا يُعجبه فَلْيَدعُ لَهُ بِالْبَرَكَةِ". ثُم دَعَا بِمَاءٍ فَأَمَرَ عَامِرًا أَنْ يَتَوَضَّأَ فَيَغْسِلُ وَجْهَهُ وَيَدَيْهِ إِلَى الْمِرْفِقَيْنِ، وَرُكْبَتَيْهِ، ودَاخِلة إِزَارِهِ، وَأَمَرَهُ أَنْ يَصُبَّ عَلَيْهِ.

قَالَ سُفْيَانُ: قَالَ مَعْمَر، عَنِ الزُّهْرِيِّ: وَأَمَرَ أَنْ يكفَأ الْإِنَاءُ مِنْ خَلْفِهِ .

وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ النَّسَائِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ سُفْيَانَ بْنِ عُيَيْنَةَ وَمَالِكِ بْنِ أَنَسٍ، كِلَاهُمَا عَنِ الزُّهْرِيِّ، بِهِ. وَمِنْ حَدِيثِ سُفْيَانَ بْنِ عُيَيْنَةَ أَيْضًا عَنْ مَعْمَرٍ، عَنِ الزُّهْرِيِّ، عَنْ أَبِي أُمَامَةَ: ويكفَأ الْإِنَاءُ مِنْ خَلْفِهِ. وَمِنْ حَدِيثِ ابْنِ أَبِي ذِئْبٍ عَنِ الزُّهْرِيِّ، عَنْ أَبِي أُمَامَةَ أَسْعَدَ بْنِ سَهْلِ بْنِ حُنَيف، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، بِهِ. وَمِنْ حَدِيثِ مَالِكٍ أَيْضًا، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ أَبِي أُمَامَةَ بْنِ سَهْلِ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، بِهِ .

حَدِيثُ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ الْخُدْرِيِّ: قَالَ ابْنُ مَاجَهْ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو بَكْرِ بْنُ أَبِي شَيْبَةَ، حَدَّثَنَا سَعِيدُ بْنُ سُلَيْمَانَ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبَّادٌ، عَنْ الْجُرَيْرِيُّ، عَنْ أَبِي نَضْرَةَ، عَنْ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ قَالَ: كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَتَعَوَّذُ مِنْ أَعْيُنِ الْجَانِّ وَأَعْيُنِ الْإِنْسِ. فَلَمَّا نَزَلَ الْمُعَوِّذَتَانِ أَخَذَ بهما وترك ما سوى ذلك.

وَرَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ وَالنَّسَائِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ سَعِيدِ بْنِ إِيَاسٍ أَبِي مَسْعُودٍ الجُرَيري، بِهِ وَقَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَسَنٌ.

حَدِيثٌ آخَرُ عَنْهُ: قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الصَّمَدِ بْنُ عَبْدِ الْوَارِثِ، حَدَّثَنِي أَبِي، حَدَّثَنِي عَبْدُ الْعَزِيزِ بْنُ صُهيب، حَدَّثَنِي أَبُو نَضْرَةَ، عَنْ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ: أَنَّ جِبْرِيلَ أَتَى رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالَ: اشْتَكَيْتَ يَا مُحَمَّدُ؟ قَالَ: "نَعَمْ". قَالَ بِاسْمِ اللَّهِ أَرْقِيكَ، مِنْ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ يُؤْذِيكَ، من شر كل نفس وعين يَشْفِيكَ، بِاسْمِ اللَّهِ أَرْقِيكَ .

وَرَوَاهُ عَنْ عَفَّانَ، عَنْ عَبْدِ الْوَارِثِ، مِثْلَهُ. وَرَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ وَأَهْلُ السُّنَنِ -إِلَّا أَبَا دَاوُدَ-مِنْ حَدِيثِ عَبْدِ الْوَارِثِ، بِهِ .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ أَيْضًا: حَدَّثَنَا عَفَّانُ، حَدَّثَنَا وُهَيْبٌ، حَدَّثَنَا دَاوُدُ، عَنْ أَبِي نَضرة، عَنْ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ -أَوْ: جَابِرِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ؛ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ اشْتَكَى، فَأَتَاهُ جِبْرِيلُ فَقَالَ: بِاسْمِ اللَّهِ أَرْقِيكَ، مِنْ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ يُؤْذِيكَ، مِنْ كل حاسد وعين والله يُشفيك .

وَرَوَاهُ أَيْضًا، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ الطَّفَاوِيِّ، عَنْ دَاوُدَ، عَنْ أَبِي نَضرة، عَنْ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ بِهِ .

قَالَ أَبُو زُرْعَة الرَّازِيُّ: رَوَى عَبْدُ الصَّمَدِ بْنُ عَبْدِ الْوَارِثِ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ الْعَزِيزِ، عَنْ أَبِي نَضْرَةَ، وَعَنْ عَبْدِ الْعَزِيزِ، عَنْ أَنَسٍ، فِي معناه، وكلاهما صحيح.

حديث أبى هُرَيرة: قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، أَنْبَأَنَا مَعْمَر، عَنْ هَمَّام بْنِ مُنَبِّه قَالَ: هَذَا مَا حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو هُرَيرة عَنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ الْعَيْنَ حَقٌّ" .

أَخْرَجَاهُ مِنْ حَدِيثِ عَبْدِ الرَّزَّاقِ .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ مَاجَهْ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو بَكْرِ بْنُ أَبِي شَيْبَةَ، حَدَّثَنَا إِسْمَاعِيلُ بْنُ عُلَيَّة، عَنِ الجُرَيري، عَنْ مُضَارب بْنِ حَزن، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيرة قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "الْعَيْنُ حَقٌّ".تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ.

وَرَوَاهُ أَحْمَدُ، عَنْ إِسْمَاعِيلَ بْنِ عُلَيَّة، عَنْ سَعِيدٍ الْجُرَيْرِيِّ، بِهِ .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ نُمَيْرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا ثَوْرٌ -يَعْنِي ابْنَ يزيد-عن مكحول، عن أبي هُرَيرة قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ "الْعَيْنُ حَقٌّ، ويحضُرها الشيطانُ، وَحَسَدُ ابْنِ آدَمَ" 

وَقَالَ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا خَلَفُ بْنُ الْوَلِيدِ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو مَعْشَرٍ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ قيس: سُئل أبو هُرَيرة: هَلْ سَمِعْتَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ يَقُولُ: الطِّيرَةُ فِي ثَلَاثٍ: فِي الْمَسْكَنِ وَالْفَرَسِ وَالْمَرْأَةِ؟ قَالَ: قُلْتُ: إِذًا أَقُولُ عَلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ مَا لَمْ يَقُلْ! وَلَكِنِّي سَمِعْتُ رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم يقول: "أَصْدَقُ الطِّيرَةِ الفألُ، وَالْعَيْنُ حَقٌّ" .

حَدِيثُ أَسْمَاءَ بِنْتِ عُمَيس: قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ دِينَارٍ، عَنِ عُروةَ بْنِ عَامِرٍ، عَنْ عُبَيد بْنِ رَفَاعَةَ الزُرقي قَالَ: قَالَتْ أَسْمَاءُ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، إِنَّ بَنِي جَعْفَرٍ تُصِيبُهُمُ الْعَيْنُ، أَفَأَسْتَرْقِي لَهُمْ؟ قَالَ: "نَعَمْ، فَلَوْ كَانَ شَيْءٌ يَسْبِقُ القدرَ لَسَبَقَتْهُ الْعَيْنُ".

وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ وَابْنُ مَاجَهْ مِنْ حَدِيثِ سُفْيَانَ بْنِ عُيَيْنَةَ، بِهِ وَرَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ أَيْضًا وَالنَّسَائِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ عَبْدِ الرَّزَّاقِ، عَنْ مَعْمَر، عَنْ أَيُّوبَ، عَنِ عَمْرِو بْنِ دِينَارٍ، عَنْ عُرْوَة بْنِ عَامِرٍ، عَنْ عُبَيد بْنِ رَفَاعَةَ، عَنْ أَسْمَاءَ بِنْتِ عُمَيْسٍ، بِهِ وَقَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ.

حَدِيثُ عَائِشَةَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهَا: قَالَ ابْنُ مَاجَهْ: حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي الخَصِيب، حَدَّثَنَا وَكِيع، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ، ومِسْعَر، عَنْ مَعْبَدِ بْنِ خَالِدٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ شَدَّاد، عَنْ عَائِشَةَ؛ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ أَمَرَهَا أَنْ تَسْتَرِقِيَ مِنَ الْعَيْنِ .

وَرَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ كَثِيرٍ، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ، عَنْ مَعْبَدِ بْنِ خَالِدٍ، بِهِ. وَأَخْرَجَهُ مُسْلِمٌ مِنْ حَدِيثِ سُفيانَ ومِسْعَر، كِلَاهُمَا عَنْ مَعْبَدٍ، بِهِ ثُمَّ قَالَ ابْنُ مَاجَهْ:

حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ بَشَّارٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو هِشَامٍ الْمَخْزُومِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا وُهَيب، عَنْ أَبِي وَاقِدٍ، عَنْ أَبِي سَلَمَةَ بْنِ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ، عَنْ عَائِشَةَ قَالَتْ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "اسْتَعِيذُوا بِاللَّهِ فَإِنَّ النَّفْسَ حَقٌّ". تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ 

وَقَالَ أَبُو دَاوُدَ: حَدَّثَنَا عُثْمَانُ بْنُ أَبِي شَيْبَةَ، حَدَّثَنَا جَرِيرٌ، حَدَّثَنَا الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، عَنِ الْأَسْوَدِ، عَنْ عَائِشَةَ قَالَتْ: كَانَ يُؤْمَرُ الْعَائِنُ فَيَتَوَضَّأُ وَيَغْسِلُ مِنْهُ المَعين .

حَدِيثُ سَهْلِ بْنِ حُنَيف: قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا حُسَين بْنُ مُحَمَّدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو أُوَيْسٍ حَدَّثَنَا الزُّهْرِيِّ، عَنْ أَبِي أُمَامَةَ بْنِ سَهل بْنِ حُنَيف: أَنَّ أَبَاهُ حَدَّثَهُ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ خَرَجَ وَسَارُوا مَعَهُ نَحْوَ مَكَّةَ، حَتَّى إِذَا كَانُوا بِشِعْبِ الخَرَار -مِنَ الْجُحْفَةِ-اغْتَسَلَ سهلُ بْنُ حُنَيف -وَكَانَ رَجُلًا أَبْيَضَ حَسن الْجِسْمِ وَالْجِلْدِ-فَنَظَرَ إِلَيْهِ عَامِرُ بْنُ رَبِيعَةَ، أَخُو بَنِي عَدِيِّ بْنِ كَعْبٍ، وَهُوَ يَغْتَسِلُ، فَقَالَ: مَا رَأَيْتُ كَالْيَوْمِ وَلَا جِلْدَ مُخَبَّأة. فلُبِطَ سَهْلٌ، فَأَتَى رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقِيلَ لَهُ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، هَلْ لَكَ فِي سَهْلٍ. وَاللَّهِ مَا يَرْفَعُ رَأْسَهُ وَلَا يُفيق. قَالَ: "هَلْ تَتَّهِمُونَ فِيهِ مِنْ أَحَدٍ؟ ". قَالُوا: نَظَرَ إِلَيْهِ عَامِرُ بْنُ رَبِيعَةَ. فَدَعَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ عَامِرًا، فَتَغَيَّظَ عَلَيْهِ، وَقَالَ: "عَلَامَ يَقْتُلُ أَحَدُكُمْ أَخَاهُ، هَلَّا إِذَا رَأَيْتَ مَا يُعْجِبُكَ بَركت؟ ". ثُمَّ قَالَ لَهُ: "اغْتَسِلْ لَهُ" -فَغَسَلَ وَجْهَهُ وَيَدَيْهِ وَمَرْفِقَيْهِ وَرُكْبَتَيْهِ وَأَطْرَافَ رِجْلَيْهِ ودَاخلَة إِزَارِهِ فِي قَدح-ثُمَّ صُب ذَلِكَ الْمَاءَ عَلَيْهِ. يَصُبَه رَجُلٌ عَلَى رَأْسِهِ وَظَهْرِهِ مِنْ خَلْفِهِ، ثُمَّ يُكْفَأُ . الْقَدَحُ وَرَاءَهُ. فَفَعَلَ ذَلِكَ، فَرَاحَ سَهْلٌ مَعَ النَّاسِ، لَيْسَ بِهِ بَأْسٌ .

حَدِيثُ عَامِرِ بْنِ رَبِيعَةَ: "قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ فِي مُسْنَدِ عَامِرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا وَكِيع، حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ عِيسَى، عَنْ أمَيّة بْنِ هِنْدِ بْنِ سَهْلِ بْنِ حُنَيْف، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَامِرٍ قَالَ: انْطَلَقَ عَامِرُ بْنُ رَبِيعَةَ وَسَهْلُ بْنُ حَنِيفٍ يُرِيدَانِ الْغُسْلَ، قَالَ: فَانْطَلَقَا يَلْتَمِسَانِ الخمرَ -قَالَ: فَوَضْعَ عَامِرٌ جُبَّة كَانَتْ عَلَيْهِ مِنْ صُوفٍ، فَنَظَرْتُ إِلَيْهِ فَأَصَبْتُهُ بِعَيْنِي فَنَزَلَ الْمَاءَ يَغْتَسِلُ. قَالَ: فَسَمِعْتُ لَهُ فِي الْمَاءِ فَرْقَعَةً، فَأَتَيْتُهُ فَنَادَيْتُهُ ثَلَاثًا فَلَمْ يُجِبْنِي. فَأَتَيْتُ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ فَأَخْبَرْتُهُ. قَالَ: فَجَاءَ يَمْشِي فَخَاضَ الْمَاءَ كَأَنِّي أَنْظُرُ إِلَى بَيَاضِ سَاقَيْهِ، قَالَ: فَضَرَبَ صَدْرَهُ بِيَدِهِ ثُمَّ قَالَ: "اللَّهُمَّ اصْرِفْ عَنْهُ حَرَّهَا وَبَرْدَهَا وَوَصَبَهَا". قَالَ: فَقَامَ. فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِذَا رَأَى أَحَدُكُمْ مِنْ أَخِيهِ، أَوْ مِنْ نَفْسِهِ أَوْ مِنْ مَالِهِ، مَا يُعْجِبُهُ، فَليُبَرِّك، فَإِنَّ الْعَيْنَ حَقٌّ" .

حَدِيثُ جَابِرٍ: قَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو بَكْرٍ الْبَزَّارُ فِي مُسْنَدِهِ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ مَعْمَر، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو دَاوُدَ، حَدَّثَنَا طَالِبُ بْنُ حَبِيبِ بْنِ عَمْرِو بْنِ سَهْلٍ الْأَنْصَارِيُّ -وَيُقَالُ لَهُ: ابْنُ الضَجِيع، ضَجِيعِ حَمْزَةَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ-حَدَّثَنِي عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ جَابِرِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "أَكْثَرُ مَنْ يَمُوتُ مِنْ أُمَّتِي بَعْدَ كِتَابِ اللَّهِ وَقَضَائِهِ وَقَدْرِهِ بِالْأَنْفُسِ" .

قَالَ الْبَزَّارُ: يَعْنِي الْعَيْنَ. قَالَ وَلَا نَعْلَمُ يُرْوَى هَذَا الْحَدِيثِ عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ إِلَّا بِهَذَا الْإِسْنَادِ.

قُلْتُ: بَلْ قَدْ رُوِيَ مِنْ وَجْهٍ آخَرَ عَنْ جَابِرٍ؛ قَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ الْمُنْذِرِ الْهَرَوِيُّ -الْمَعْرُوفُ بشَكَّر-فِي كِتَابِ الْعَجَائِبِ، وَهُوَ مُشْتَمِلٌ عَلَى فَوَائِدَ جَلِيلَةٍ وَغَرِيبَةٍ: حَدَّثَنَا الرَّهَاوِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا يَعْقُوبُ بْنُ مُحَمَّدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي عَلِيٍّ الْهَاشِمِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ المُنكَدر، عَنْ جَابِرِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "الْعَيْنُ حَقٌّ، لتُورِد الرَّجُلَ الْقَبْرَ، وَالْجَمَلَ القِدر، وَإِنَّ أَكْثَرَ هَلَاكِ أمتي في العين" .

ثُمَّ رَوَاهُ عَنْ شُعَيْبِ بْنِ أَيُّوبَ، عَنْ مُعَاوِيَةَ بْنِ هِشَامٍ، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ الْمُنْكَدِرِ، عَنْ جَابِرٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "قَدْ تُدخل الرجلَ العينُ فِي الْقَبْرِ، وَتُدْخِلُ الْجَمَلَ الْقِدْرَ" .

حَدِيثُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو: "قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا قُتَيْبَةُ، حَدَّثَنَا رِشْدِينُ بْنُ سَعْدٍ، عَنِ الْحَسَنِ بْنِ ثَوْبَانَ، عَنْ هِشَامِ بْنِ أَبِي رُقية، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "لَا عَدْوَى وَلَا طيرةَ، وَلَا هَامة وَلَا حَسَد، وَالْعَيْنُ حَقٌّ". تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ أَحْمَدُ .

حَدِيثٌ عَنْ عَلِيٍّ: رَوَى الْحَافِظُ ابْنُ عَسَاكِرَ مِنْ طَرِيقِ خَيْثمة بْنِ سُلَيْمَانَ الْحَافِظِ: حَدَّثَنَا عُبَيْدُ بْنُ مُحَمَّدٍ الكَشَوري، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَبْدِ رَبِّهِ الْبَصْرِيُّ، عَنْ أَبِي رَجَاءٍ، عَنْ شُعْبَةَ، عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ، عَنِ الْحَارِثِ، عَنْ عَلِيٍّ؛ أَنَّ جِبْرِيلَ أَتَى النَّبِيَّ ﷺ فَوَافَقَهُ مُغْتَمًّا، فَقَالَ: يَا مُحَمَّدُ، مَا هَذَا الْغَمُّ الَّذِي أَرَاهُ فِي وَجْهِكَ؟ قَالَ: "الْحَسَنُ وَالْحُسَيْنُ أَصَابَتْهُمَا عَيْنٌ". قَالَ: صَدَق بِالْعَيْنِ، فَإِنَّ الْعَيْنَ حَقٌّ، أَفَلَا عَوَّذْتَهُمَا بِهَؤُلَاءِ الْكَلِمَاتِ؟ قَالَ: "وَمَا هُنَّ يَا جِبْرِيلُ؟ ". قَالَ: قُلِ: اللَّهُمَّ ذَا السُّلْطَانِ الْعَظِيمِ، ذَا الْمَنِّ الْقَدِيمِ، ذَا الْوَجْهِ الْكَرِيمِ، وَلِيَّ الْكَلِمَاتِ التَّامَّاتِ، وَالدَّعَوَاتِ الْمُسْتَجَابَاتِ، عَافِ الْحَسَنَ وَالْحُسَيْنَ مِنْ أَنْفُسِ الْجِنِّ، وَأَعْيُنِ الْإِنْسِ. فَقَالَهَا النَّبِيُّ ﷺ فَقَامَا يَلْعَبَانِ بَيْنَ يَدَيْهِ. فَقَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: "عَوِّذوا أَنْفُسَكُمْ وَنِسَاءَكُمْ وَأَوْلَادَكُمْ بِهَذَا التَّعْوِيذِ، فَإِنَّهُ لَمْ يَتَعَوَّذِ الْمُتَعَوِّذُونَ بِمِثْلِهِ".

قَالَ الْخَطِيبُ الْبَغْدَادِيُّ: تَفَرَّدَ بِرِوَايَتِهِ أَبُو رَجَاءٍ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عُبَيْدِ اللَّهِ الحَيَطي مِنْ أَهْلِ تُسْتَر. ذَكَرَهُ ابْنُ عَسَاكِرَ فِي تَرْجَمَةِ "طَرَّادِ بْنِ الْحُسَيْنِ"، مِنْ تَارِيخِهِ .

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَيَقُولُونَ إِنَّهُ لَمَجْنُونٌ﴾ أَيْ: يَزْدَرُونَهُ بِأَعْيُنِهِمْ وَيُؤْذُونَهُ بِأَلْسِنَتِهِمْ، وَيَقُولُونَ: ﴿إِنَّهُ لَمَجْنُونٌ﴾ أَيْ: لِمَجِيئِهِ بِالْقُرْآنِ، قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى ﴿وَمَا هُوَ إِلا ذِكْرٌ لِلْعَالَمِينَ﴾
