## 73:1-9 — verses 1-9 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Tafsir of Surat al-Muzzammil

It is Meccan.

The hafiz Abu Bakr [Ahmad] ibn Amr ibn Abd al-Khaliq al-Bazzar said: Muhammad ibn Musa al-Qattan al-Wasiti told us, Mu'alla ibn Abd al-Rahman told us, Sharik told us, from Abdullah ibn Muhammad ibn Aqil, from Jabir, who said: The Quraysh gathered in the Dar al-Nadwah and said, "Give this man a name by which people will turn away from him." They said, "A soothsayer (kahin)." They said, "He is not a soothsayer." They said, "A madman." They said, "He is not a madman." They said, "A sorcerer." They said, "He is not a sorcerer." So the polytheists dispersed on that note. This reached the Prophet ﷺ, so he wrapped himself in his garments and covered himself with them. Then Gabriel, peace be upon him, came to him and said: "{O you wrapped one}," "O you covered one."

Then al-Bazzar said: Mu'alla ibn Abd al-Rahman, a group of the people of knowledge have transmitted from him and tolerated his hadith, but he transmitted alone certain hadiths that are not corroborated.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful

The Exalted commands His Messenger ﷺ to abandon wrapping himself, that is, covering himself at night, and to rise up to stand for his Lord, Mighty and Majestic, just as He, the Exalted, said: {Their sides forsake their beds; they call upon their Lord in fear and hope, and they spend out of what We have provided for them} [al-Sajdah: 16]. Likewise the Messenger of Allah ﷺ complied with what Allah the Exalted commanded him regarding the night prayer, and it was obligatory upon him alone, as the Exalted said: {And during the night, pray tahajjud with it as an additional duty for you; it may be that your Lord will raise you to a praised station} [al-Isra: 79]. Here He explained to him the measure of what he should stand for, saying: {O you wrapped one, stand the night, except a little}.

Ibn Abbas, al-Dahhak, and al-Suddi said: {O you wrapped one} means "O you sleeping one." Qatadah said: the wrapped one in his garments. Ibrahim al-Nakha'i said: it was revealed while he was wrapped in a woolen cloak (qatifah).

Shabib ibn Bishr said, from Ikrimah, from Ibn Abbas: {O you wrapped one} he said, meaning: O Muhammad, you have been wrapped up with the Qur'an.

His saying: {half of it} is a substitute for "the night," {or reduce from it a little, or add to it} that is: We commanded you to stand half the night, with a small increase or a small decrease; there is no blame upon you in that.

His saying: {and recite the Qur'an with tartil (measured, careful recitation)} means: recite it at a measured pace, for that aids in understanding the Qur'an and reflecting upon it. Thus did he recite ﷺ. Aisha said: He would recite a surah and give it tartil, so that it would become longer than the longest surah otherwise. In Sahih al-Bukhari, from Anas: he was asked about the recitation of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, and he said: it was drawn out (mad), then he recited {In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful}, drawing out "Bismillah," drawing out "al-Rahman," and drawing out "al-Rahim."

Ibn Jurayj said, from Ibn Abi Mulaykah, from Umm Salamah: she was asked about the recitation of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, and she said: He used to break up his recitation verse by verse: {In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. Praise be to Allah, Lord of the worlds. The Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. Master of the Day of Judgment}. Narrated by Ahmad, Abu Dawud, and al-Tirmidhi.

Imam Ahmad said: Abd al-Rahman told us, from Sufyan, from Asim, from Zirr, from Abdullah ibn Amr, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "It will be said to the companion of the Qur'an: 'Recite and rise, and give tartil as you used to give tartil in the worldly life, for your station will be at the last verse you recite.'"

Abu Dawud, al-Tirmidhi, and al-Nasa'i narrated it from the hadith of Sufyan al-Thawri, with the same chain, and al-Tirmidhi said: hasan sahih.

We have already presented at the beginning of this commentary the hadiths indicating the desirability of tartil and beautifying the voice in recitation, as has come in the hadith: "Beautify the Qur'an with your voices," and "He is not of us who does not chant the Qur'an melodiously," and "This one has been given a flute (mizmar) from among the flutes of the family of David," meaning Abu Musa. Abu Musa said: "Had I known that you were listening to my recitation, I would have beautified it for you most beautifully."

And from Ibn Mas'ud, that he said: Do not scatter it as sand is scattered, and do not rush through it as poetry is rushed through. Pause at its wonders, and move hearts with it, and let none of you be concerned only with reaching the end of the surah. Narrated by al-Baghawi.

Al-Bukhari said: Adam told us, Shu'bah told us, Amr ibn Murrah told us: I heard Abu Wa'il say: A man came to Ibn Mas'ud and said, "I recited the Mufassal [the short surahs] tonight in one prostration cycle (rak'ah)." Ibn Mas'ud said, "Rushing through it like the rushing through of poetry! I have indeed known the pairs which the Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to combine." Then he mentioned twenty surahs from the Mufassal, two surahs per rak'ah.

His saying: {Indeed, We will cast upon you a heavy word} al-Hasan and Qatadah said: meaning, heavy to act upon.

And it was said: it was heavy at the time of its revelation, due to its magnitude. As Zayd ibn Thabit said: It was revealed upon the Messenger of Allah ﷺ while his thigh was upon my thigh, and my thigh nearly was crushed.

Imam Ahmad said: Qutaybah told us, Ibn Lahi'ah told us, from Yazid ibn Abi Habib, from Amr ibn al-Walid, from Abdullah ibn Amr, who said: I asked the Prophet ﷺ, saying, "O Messenger of Allah, do you sense the revelation coming?" The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "I hear ringing sounds, then I fall silent at that; and there is not a single time that revelation comes to me but that I think my soul is about to depart." Ahmad narrated it alone.

At the beginning of Sahih al-Bukhari, from Abdullah ibn Yusuf, from Malik, from Hisham, from his father, from Aisha: that al-Harith ibn Hisham asked the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, "How does revelation come to you?" He said: "Sometimes it comes to me like the ringing of a bell, and that is the hardest upon me, then it lifts from me and I have retained what it said. And sometimes the angel takes the form of a man for me and speaks to me, and I retain what he says." Aisha said: I indeed saw revelation coming down upon him ﷺ on a day of intense cold, and it would lift from him with his forehead streaming with sweat. This is its wording.

Imam Ahmad said: Sulayman ibn Dawud told us, Abd al-Rahman informed us, from Hisham ibn Urwah, from his father, from Aisha, who said: Revelation would come to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ while he was upon his riding mount, and it would strike its lower neck against the ground.

Ibn Jarir said: Ibn Abd al-A'la told us, Ibn Thawr told us, from Ma'mar, from Hisham ibn Urwah, from his father: that the Prophet ﷺ, when revelation came to him while he was on his she-camel, it would place its jiran (the underside of the neck) on the ground, and it would not be able to move until it lifted from him.

This is a mursal (report with a broken chain at the level of the Companion). The jiran is the underside of the neck.

Ibn Jarir preferred that it is heavy in both senses together, as Abd al-Rahman ibn Zayd ibn Aslam said: just as it was heavy in this world, it will be heavy on the Day of Resurrection in the scales.

His saying: {Indeed, the rising of the night is stronger in impact and more upright in speech} Abu Ishaq said, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, from Ibn Abbas: "nasha'a" means: he stood, according to the language of the Abyssinians.

Umar, Ibn Abbas, and Ibn al-Zubayr said: the whole night is "nashi'ah" (a rising/occasion). Likewise said Mujahid and others: it is said "nasha'a" when one rises during the night. And in a report from Mujahid: after the night prayer ('isha). Likewise said Abu Mijlaz, Qatadah, Salim, Abu Hazim, and Muhammad ibn al-Munkadir.

The point is that the "nashi'ah" of the night refers to its hours and times, and every hour of it is called a "nashi'ah," these being its occasions. The intent is that standing in prayer at night involves a greater agreement between heart and tongue, and greater concentration in recitation. Hence He said: {it is stronger in impact and more upright in speech}, meaning: more complete in gathering the mind for performing and understanding the recitation than standing during the day, because daytime is the time when people are dispersed, voices are noisy, and it is the time of livelihood.

The hafiz Abu Ya'la al-Mawsili said: Ibrahim ibn Sa'id al-Jawhari told us, Abu Usamah told us, al-A'mash told us, that Anas ibn Malik recited this verse: "Indeed the rising of the night is stronger in impact and more correct (aswab) in speech," and a man said to him, "We only recite it as {and more upright (aqwam) in speech}." He said to him: "aswab" and "aqwam" and "ahya" and the like of these are all one.

Hence He said: {Indeed, for you in the daytime is prolonged occupation (sabh)}. Ibn Abbas, Ikrimah, and Ata ibn Abi Muslim said: it means free time and sleep.

Abu al-'Aliyah, Mujahid, Ibn Malik, al-Dahhak, al-Hasan, Qatadah, al-Rabi' ibn Anas, and Sufyan al-Thawri said: it means prolonged free time.

Qatadah said: free time, seeking of needs, and returning to occupations.

Al-Suddi said: {prolonged sabh} means much voluntary activity.

Abd al-Rahman ibn Zayd ibn Aslam said regarding His saying, {Indeed, for you in the daytime is prolonged sabh}: it means, for your needs, so devote the night to your religion.

He said: This was at the time when the night prayer was obligatory. Then Allah bestowed His favor upon His servants, so He lightened it and set it aside. He recited: {Stand [in prayer] the night, except for a little} to the end of the verse, then said: {Indeed, your Lord knows, [O Muhammad], that you stand [in prayer] almost two thirds of the night} until he reached: {so recite what is easy from it} [that is, half the night or a third of it. Then a more expansive and spacious command came, lifting the obligation from him ﷺ and from his community], and He said: {And from [part of] the night, pray with it as additional [worship] for you; it is expected that your Lord will resurrect you to a praised station} [al-Isra: 79]. What he said is indeed as he said.

The proof of this is what Imam Ahmad related in his Musnad, where he said: [chain] Yahya narrated to us, Sa'id ibn Abi Arubah narrated to us, from Qatadah, from Zurarah ibn Awfa, from Sa'id ibn Hisham: that he divorced his wife, then traveled to Madinah to sell property he had there and put its proceeds toward horses and weapons, then to fight the Romans (al-Rum) until he died. He met a group of his people, and they told him that six men from his people had intended to do the same in the time of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, and he had said: "Do you not have in me a good example?" So he forbade them from that. He then had them witness his taking his wife back.

He then returned to us and told us that he had gone to Ibn Abbas and asked him about the witr prayer. Ibn Abbas said: Shall I not inform you of the person on earth most knowledgeable about the Messenger of Allah's ﷺ witr? He said: Yes. Ibn Abbas said: Go to Aishah and ask her, then come back to me and tell me her answer.

He said: So I went to Hakim ibn Aflah and asked him to accompany me to her. He said: I will not go near her. I had forbidden her from saying anything about these two factions, but she refused and insisted on doing so. So I insisted upon him, and he came with me. We entered upon her, and she said: Hakim? And she recognized him. He said: Yes. She said: Who is this with you? He said: Sa'id ibn Hisham. She said: Who is Hisham? He said: Ibn Amir. He said: She then invoked mercy upon him and said: Amir was indeed a good man.

I said: O Mother of the Believers, tell me about the character of the Messenger ﷺ. She said: Do you not recite the Qur'an? I said: Yes indeed. She said: The character of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was the Qur'an. I was about to get up, but then it occurred to me to ask about the night prayer of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. I said: O Mother of the Believers, tell me about the night prayer of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. Do you not recite this surah: {O you wrapped in garments}? I said: Yes indeed. She said: Allah made standing in prayer at night obligatory at the beginning of this surah, and so the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and his companions stood in prayer for a year until their feet swelled. Allah withheld the conclusion of this surah in heaven for twelve months, then Allah sent down the lightening of the command at the end of this surah, and so the night prayer became voluntary after having been obligatory.

I was about to get up, but then it occurred to me to ask about the witr of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. I said: O Mother of the Believers, tell me about the witr of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. She said: We used to prepare his tooth-stick (siwak) and water for purification, and Allah would rouse him whenever He willed to rouse him during the night. He would use the siwak, then perform ablution, then pray eight rak'ahs, not sitting in any of them except at the eighth, where he would sit and remember his Lord, Exalted is He, and supplicate [and seek forgiveness, then rise without giving the greeting of peace (taslim). Then he would pray the ninth, then sit and praise his Lord and remember Him and supplicate], then he would give the taslim audibly enough for us to hear. Then he would pray two rak'ahs while sitting, after having given the taslim. That makes eleven rak'ahs, my son.

When the Messenger of Allah ﷺ grew older and gained weight, he prayed witr with seven, then prayed two rak'ahs while sitting after giving the taslim, making that nine, my son. Whenever the Messenger of Allah ﷺ prayed a prayer, he liked to maintain it consistently. When sleep, pain, or illness prevented him from the night prayer, he would pray twelve rak'ahs during the day. I do not know of the Prophet of Allah ﷺ ever reciting the entire Qur'an in one night, nor standing in prayer for an entire night until morning, nor fasting a complete month other than Ramadan.

I then went to Ibn Abbas and told him her account, and he said: She has spoken the truth. Had I been able to visit her, I would have gone to her so she could tell me directly, face to face.

This is how Imam Ahmad related it in full. Muslim also recorded it in his Sahih, from the hadith of Qatadah, in similar wording.

Another chain from Aishah on this same matter: Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Ibn Waki' narrated to us, Zayd ibn al-Hubab narrated to us, and Ibn Humayd narrated to us, Mihran narrated to us, both together, the wording being Ibn Waki's, from Musa ibn Ubaydah, Muhammad ibn Talha narrated to me, from Abu Salamah, from Aishah, who said: I used to make a mat for the Messenger of Allah ﷺ on which he would pray at night. People heard of this and gathered, so he came out as if angry, for he was compassionate toward them and feared that the night prayer would be made obligatory upon them. He said: "O people, take on only those deeds you are able to bear, for Allah does not tire of giving reward until you tire of doing deeds, and the best deeds are those maintained consistently." Then the Qur'an came down: {O you who wraps himself [in clothing], Arise [to pray] the night, except for a little, half of it, or subtract from it a little, or add to it}, until a man would tie a rope and hang from it [to stay awake for prayer]. They remained in that state for eight months. Then Allah saw what they sought of His pleasure, so He had mercy upon them and returned them to the obligation, and left aside standing in prayer at night [as obligatory].

Ibn Abi Hatim also related it through Musa ibn Ubaydah al-Rabadhi, who is weak (da'if). The hadith is found in the Sahih without the addition about the revelation of this surah, and this wording might give the impression that this surah was revealed in Madinah, but that is not so; it is in fact Makkan. His statement in this wording that eight months elapsed between the revelation of its beginning and its end is gharib (odd/singular), for it was mentioned previously in Ahmad's narration that a year elapsed between them.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Abu Sa'id al-Ashajj narrated to us, Abu Usamah narrated to us, from Mis'ar, from Simak al-Hanafi, who said: I heard Ibn Abbas say: The first part of al-Muzzammil was the first thing revealed of it. They used to stand in prayer somewhat like their standing during the month of Ramadan, and nearly a year elapsed between the revelation of its beginning and its end.

Ibn Jarir likewise related it from Abu Kurayb, from Abu Usamah, with the same chain.

Al-Thawri and Muhammad ibn Bishr al-Abdi both said, from Mis'ar, from Simak, from Ibn Abbas: a year elapsed between them. Ibn Jarir also related, from Abu Kurayb, from Waki', from Isra'il, from Simak, from Ikrimah, from Ibn Abbas, the same.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Ibn Humayd narrated to us, Mihran narrated to us, from Sufyan, from Qays ibn Wahb, from Abu Abd al-Rahman, who said: When {O you wrapped in garments} was revealed, they stood in prayer for a year until their feet and shins swelled, until {so recite what is easy from it} was revealed, he said: Then the people found relief.

Al-Hasan al-Basri said the same.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Abu Zur'ah narrated to us, Ubaydullah ibn Umar al-Qawariri narrated to us, Mu'adh ibn Hisham narrated to us, my father narrated to me, from Qatadah, from Zurarah ibn Awfa, from Sa'd ibn Hisham, who said: I said, meaning to Aishah: Tell us about the night prayer of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. She said: Do you not recite {O you wrapped in garments}? I said: Yes indeed. She said: It was indeed the standing in prayer of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and his companions, until their feet swelled, and the last part of it was withheld in heaven for sixteen months, then it was sent down.

Ma'mar said, from Qatadah, regarding {Stand [in prayer] the night, except for a little}: They stood in prayer for a year or two years, until their shins and feet swelled, and Allah then sent down the lightening of it at the end of the surah.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Ibn Humayd narrated to us, Ya'qub al-Qummi narrated to us, from Ja'far, from Sa'id, that is, Ibn Jubayr, who said: When Allah, Exalted is He, revealed to His Prophet ﷺ {O you wrapped in garments}, the Prophet ﷺ remained in this condition for ten years, standing in prayer at night as he had been commanded, and a group of his companions used to stand with him. Then Allah revealed to him, after ten years: {Indeed, your Lord knows that you stand [in prayer] almost two thirds of the night, or half of it, or a third of it, as do a group of those with you} up to His saying: {and establish prayer}, so Allah, Exalted is He, lightened it upon them after ten years.

Ibn Abi Hatim also related it, from his father, from Amr ibn Rafi', from Ya'qub al-Qummi, with the same chain.

Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn Abbas, regarding His saying: {O you who wraps himself [in clothing], Arise [to pray] the night, except for a little, half of it, or subtract from it a little, [or add to it, and recite the Qur'an with measured recitation}: Allah commanded His Prophet and the believers to stand in prayer at night except for a little], and this was difficult for the believers. Then Allah lightened it upon them and had mercy on them, and sent down after this: {He knows that there will be among you those who are ill and others traveling throughout the land} up to His saying: {so recite what is easy from it}. So Allah expanded the matter, and to Him belongs all praise, and did not make it constrictive.

His saying: {And remember the name of your Lord and devote yourself to Him with complete devotion} means: increase your remembrance of Him, cut yourself off unto Him, and free yourself for His worship once you have finished with your occupations and what you need of the affairs of your worldly life, just as He said: {So when you have finished, then stand up} [al-Sharh: 7], meaning: when you have finished your important tasks, then exert yourself in obedience and worship of Him, so that your mind may be free. This is what Ibn Zayd said, or something close in meaning to it.

Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, Abu Salih, Atiyyah, al-Dahhak, and al-Suddi said: {and devote yourself to Him with complete devotion} means: make your worship sincerely for Him alone.

Al-Hasan said: strive earnestly and devote your very self to Him.

Ibn Jarir said: the worshipper is called mutabattil (one who devotes himself completely), and from this comes the narrated hadith that he ﷺ forbade al-tabattul, meaning: cutting oneself off entirely for worship and abandoning marriage.

His saying: {Lord of the east and the west, there is no god but He, so take Him as a Guardian} means: He is the Owner who has full disposal over the easts and the wests, there is no god but He, and just as you have singled Him out alone for worship, so single Him out alone for reliance. {So take Him as a Guardian}, just as He said in another verse: {So worship Him and rely upon Him} [Hud: 123], and as in His saying: {You alone we worship and You alone we ask for help}. There are many verses bearing this meaning, containing the command to devote worship and obedience to God alone, and to single Him out for reliance upon Him.

تَفْسِيرُ سُورَةِ الْمُزَّمِّلِ

وَهِيَ مَكِّيَّةٌ

قَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو بَكْرٍ [أَحْمَدُ] بْنُ عَمْرٍو بْنِ عَبْدِ الْخَالِقِ الْبَزَّارُ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ مُوسَى الْقَطَّانُ الْوَاسِطِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا مُعَلَّى بْنُ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ، حَدَّثَنَا شَرِيكٌ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عَقِيلٍ، عَنْ جَابِرٍ قَالَ: اجْتَمَعَتْ قُرَيْشٌ فِي دَارِ النَّدْوَةِ فَقَالُوا: سَمُّوا هَذَا الرَّجُلَ اسْمًا تَصْدُرُ النَّاسُ عَنْهُ. فَقَالُوا: كَاهِنٌ. قَالُوا: لَيْسَ بِكَاهِنٍ. قَالُوا: مَجْنُونٌ قَالُوا: لَيْسَ بِمَجْنُونٍ. قَالُوا: سَاحِرٌ. قَالُوا: لَيْسَ بِسَاحِرٍ. فَتَفَرَّقَ الْمُشْرِكُونَ عَلَى ذَلِكَ، فَبَلَغَ ذَلِكَ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ، فَتَزَمَّلَ فِي ثِيَابِهِ وَتَدَثَّرَ فِيهَا. فَأَتَاهُ جِبْرِيلُ، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ، فَقَالَ: " يَا أَيُّهَا الْمُزَّمِّلُ " " يَا أَيُّهَا الْمُدَّثِّرُ ".

ثُمَّ قَالَ الْبَزَّارُ: مُعَلَّى بْنُ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ: قَدْ حَدَّثَ عَنْهُ جَمَاعَةٌ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْعِلْمِ، وَاحْتَمَلُوا حَدِيثَهُ، لَكِنْ تَفَرَّدَ بِأَحَادِيثَ لَا يُتَابَعُ عَلَيْهَا. 

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

يَأْمُرُ تَعَالَى رَسُولَهُ ﷺ أَنْ يَتْرُكَ التَّزَمُّلَ، وَهُوَ: التَّغَطِّي فِي اللَّيْلِ، وَيَنْهَضَ إِلَى الْقِيَامِ لِرَبِّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿تَتَجَافَى جُنُوبُهُمْ عَنِ الْمَضَاجِعِ يَدْعُونَ رَبَّهُمْ خَوْفًا وَطَمَعًا وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَاهُمْ يُنْفِقُونَ﴾ [السَّجْدَةِ: ١٦] وَكَذَلِكَ كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ مُمْتَثِلًا مَا أَمَرَهُ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى بِهِ مِنْ قِيَامِ اللَّيْلِ، وَقَدْ كَانَ وَاجِبًا عَلَيْهِ وَحْدَهُ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَمِنَ اللَّيْلِ فَتَهَجَّدْ بِهِ نَافِلَةً لَكَ عَسَى أَنْ يَبْعَثَكَ رَبُّكَ مَقَامًا مَحْمُودًا﴾ [الْإِسْرَاءِ: ٧٩] وَهَاهُنَا بَيَّنَ لَهُ مِقْدَارَ مَا يَقُومُ، فَقَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿يَا أَيُّهَا الْمُزَّمِّلُ قُمِ اللَّيْلَ إِلا قَلِيلا﴾ ء

قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ: ﴿يَا أَيُّهَا الْمُزَّمِّلُ﴾ يَعْنِي: يَا أَيُّهَا النَّائِمُ. وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: الْمُزَّمِّلُ فِي ثِيَابِهِ، وَقَالَ إِبْرَاهِيمُ النَّخَعِيُّ: نَزَلَتْ وَهُوَ مُتَزَمِّلٌ بِقَطِيفَةٍ.

وَقَالَ شَبِيبُ بْنُ بِشر، عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿يَا أَيُّهَا الْمُزَّمِّلُ﴾ قال: يا محمد، زُمّلتَ الْقُرْآنَ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿نِصْفَهُ﴾ بَدَلٌ مِنَ اللَّيْلِ ﴿أَوِ انْقُصْ مِنْهُ قَلِيلا. أَوْ زِدْ عَلَيْهِ﴾ أَيْ: أَمَرْنَاكَ أَنْ تَقُومَ نِصْفَ اللَّيْلِ بِزِيَادَةٍ قَلِيلَةٍ أَوْ نُقْصَانٍ قَلِيلٍ، لَا حَرَجَ عَلَيْكَ فِي ذَلِكَ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَرَتِّلِ الْقُرْآنَ تَرْتِيلا﴾ أَيِ: اقْرَأْهُ عَلَى تَمَهُّلٍ، فَإِنَّهُ يَكُونُ عَوْنًا عَلَى فَهْمِ الْقُرْآنِ وَتَدَبُّرِهِ. وَكَذَلِكَ كَانَ يَقْرَأُ صَلَوَاتُ اللَّهِ وَسَلَامُهُ عَلَيْهِ، قَالَتْ عَائِشَةُ: كَانَ يَقْرَأُ السُّورَةَ فَيُرَتِّلُهَا، حَتَّى تَكُونَ أَطْوَلَ مِنْ أَطْوَلِ مِنْهَا. وَفِي صَحِيحِ الْبُخَارِيِّ، عَنْ أَنَسٍ: أَنَّهُ سُئِلَ عَنْ قِرَاءَةِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، فَقَالَ: كَانَتْ مَدًّا، ثُمَّ قَرَأَ ﴿بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ﴾ يَمُدُّ بِسْمِ اللَّهِ، وَيَمُدُّ الرَّحْمَنِ، وَيَمُدُّ الرَّحِيمِ. 

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جُرَيج، عَنِ ابْنِ أَبِي مُلَيكة عَنِ أُمِّ سَلَمَةَ: أَنَّهَا سُئلت عَنْ قِرَاءَةِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، فَقَالَتْ: كَانَ يُقَطِّعُ قِرَاءَتَهُ آيَةً آيَةً، ﴿بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ﴾ رَوَاهُ أَحْمَدُ، وَأَبُو دَاوُدَ، وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ. 

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ، عَنْ عَاصِمٍ، عَنْ زِرٍّ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ قَالَ: "يُقَالُ لِصَاحِبِ الْقُرْآنِ: اقْرَأْ وارْقَ، ورَتِّل كَمَا كُنْتَ تُرَتِّلُ فِي الدُّنْيَا، فَإِنَّ مَنْزِلَتَكَ عِنْدَ آخِرِ آيَةٍ تَقْرَؤُهَا".

وَرَوَاهُ أَبُو دَاوُدَ، وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ وَالنَّسَائِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ سُفْيَانَ الثَّوْرِيِّ، بِهِ وَقَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ.

وَقَدْ قَدَّمْنَا فِي أَوَّلِ التَّفْسِيرِ الْأَحَادِيثَ الدَّالَّةَ عَلَى اسْتِحْبَابِ التَّرْتِيلِ وَتَحْسِينِ الصَّوْتِ بِالْقِرَاءَةِ، كَمَا جَاءَ فِي الْحَدِيثِ: "زَيِّنوا الْقُرْآنَ بِأَصْوَاتِكُمْ"، وَ "لَيْسَ مِنَّا مَنْ لَمْ يَتَغَنَّ بِالْقُرْآنِ"، وَ "لَقَدْ أُوتِيَ هذا مزمار مِنْ مَزَامِيرِ آلِ دَاوُدَ" يَعْنِي: أَبَا مُوسَى، فَقَالَ أَبُو مُوسَى: لَوْ كُنْتُ أَعْلَمُ أَنَّكَ كُنْتَ تَسْمَعُ قِرَاءَتِي لحبَّرْته لَكَ تَحْبِيرًا. 

وَعَنِ ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ أَنَّهُ قَالَ: لَا تَنْثُرُوهُ نَثْرَ الرَّمْلِ وَلَا تَهُذُّوهُ هَذَّ الشِّعْرِ، قِفُوا عِنْدَ عَجَائِبِهِ، وَحَرِّكُوا بِهِ الْقُلُوبَ، وَلَا يَكُنْ هَمُّ أَحَدِكُمْ آخِرَ السُّورَةِ. رَوَاهُ الْبَغَوِيُّ. 

وَقَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا آدَمُ، حَدَّثَنَا شُعْبَةُ، حَدَّثَنَا عَمْرُو بْنُ مُرَّةَ: سَمِعْتُ أَبَا وَائِلٍ قَالَ: جَاءَ رَجُلٌ إِلَى ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ فَقَالَ: قَرَأْتُ الْمُفَصَّلَ اللَّيْلَةَ فِي رَكْعَةٍ. فَقَالَ: هَذًّا كَهَذِّ الشِّعْرِ. لَقَدْ عرفت النَّظَائِرَ الَّتِي كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقْرِنُ بَيْنَهُنَّ. فَذَكَرَ عِشْرِينَ سُورَةً مِنَ المُفَصّل سُورَتَيْنِ فِي رَكْعَةٍ. 

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنَّا سَنُلْقِي عَلَيْكَ قَوْلا ثَقِيلا﴾ قَالَ الْحَسَنُ، وَقَتَادَةُ: أَيِ الْعَمَلُ بِهِ.

وَقِيلَ: ثقيلٌ وَقْتَ نُزُولِهِ؛ مِنْ عَظَمَتِهِ. كَمَا قَالَ زَيْدُ بْنُ ثَابِتٍ: أُنْزِلَ عَلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ وَفَخِذُهُ عَلَى فَخِذِي، فَكَادَتْ تُرض فَخذي. 

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا قُتَيْبَةُ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ لَهِيعَةَ، عَنْ يَزِيدَ بْنِ أَبِي حَبِيبٍ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ الْوَلِيدِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو قَالَ: سَأَلْتُ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ فَقُلْتُ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، هَلْ تُحِسُّ بِالْوَحْيِ؟ فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "أسمعُ صَلاصيل، ثُمَّ أسكتُ عِنْدَ ذَلِكَ، فَمَا مِنْ مَرَّةٍ يُوحَى إِلَيَّ إِلَّا ظَنَنْتُ أَنَّ نَفْسِي تَفِيضُ"، تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ أَحْمَدُ. 

وَفِي أَوَّلِ صَحِيحِ الْبُخَارِيِّ عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ يُوسُفَ، عَنْ مَالِكٍ، عَنْ هِشَامٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ عَائِشَةَ: أَنَّ الْحَارِثَ بْنَ هِشَامٍ سَأَلَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ: كيف يَأْتِيكَ الْوَحْيُ؟ فَقَالَ: "أَحْيَانًا يَأْتِينِي فِي مِثْلَ صَلْصَلَةِ الْجَرَسِ، وَهُوَ أَشَدُّهُ عَلَيّ، فَيَفْصِمُ عَنِّي وَقَدْ وَعَيت عَنْهُ مَا قَالَ، وَأَحْيَانًا يَتَمَثَّلُ لِيَ الْمَلَكُ رَجُلًا فَيُكَلِّمُنِي فَأَعِي مَا يَقُولُ". قَالَتْ عَائِشَةُ: وَلَقَدْ رَأَيْتُهُ يَنْزِلُ عَلَيْهِ الْوَحْيُ ﷺ فِي الْيَوْمِ الشَّدِيدِ الْبَرْدِ، فَيَفْصِمُ عَنْهُ وَإِنَّ جَبِينَهُ لَيَتَفَصَّدُ عَرَقًا هَذَا لَفْظُهُ. 

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا سُلَيْمَانُ بْنُ دَاوُدَ، أَخْبَرَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ، عَنْ هِشَامِ بْنِ عُرْوَة، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ عَائِشَةَ قَالَتْ: إِنْ كَانَ ليوحَى إِلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ وَهُوَ عَلَى رَاحِلَتِهِ، فَتَضْرِبُ بِجرَانها. 

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ عَبْدِ الْأَعْلَى، حَدَّثَنَا ابن ثور، عن مَعْمَر، عَنْ هِشَامِ بْنِ عُرْوَةَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ؛ أَنَّ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ كَانَ إِذَا أُوحِيَ إِلَيْهِ وَهُوَ عَلَى نَاقَتِهِ، وَضَعَتْ جِرَانَهَا، فَمَا تَسْتَطِيعُ أَنْ تَحَرَّكَ حَتَّى يُسَرّى عَنْهُ. 

وَهَذَا مُرْسَلٌ. الْجِرَانُ: هُوَ بَاطِنُ الْعُنُقِ.

وَاخْتَارَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ أَنَّهُ ثَقِيلٌ مِنَ الْوَجْهَيْنِ مَعًا، كَمَا قَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ: كَمَا ثَقُلَ فِي الدُّنْيَا ثَقُلَ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ فِي الْمَوَازِينِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنَّ نَاشِئَةَ اللَّيْلِ هِيَ أَشَدُّ وَطْئًا وَأَقْوَمُ قِيلا﴾ قَالَ أَبُو إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: نَشَأَ: قَامَ بِالْحَبَشَةِ.

وَقَالَ عُمَرُ، وَابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَابْنُ الزُّبَيْرِ: اللَّيْلُ كُلُّهُ نَاشِئَةٌ. وَكَذَا قَالَ مجاهد، وغير واحد، يُقَالُ: نَشَأَ: إِذَا قَامَ مِنَ اللَّيْلِ. وَفِي رِوَايَةٍ عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ: بَعْدَ الْعِشَاءِ. وَكَذَا قَالَ أَبُو مِجْلَز، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَسَالِمٌ وَأَبُو حَازِمٍ، وَمُحَمَّدُ بْنُ الْمُنْكَدِرُ.

وَالْغَرَضُ أَنَّ نَاشِئَةَ اللَّيْلِ هِيَ: سَاعَاتُهُ وَأَوْقَاتُهُ، وَكُلُّ سَاعَةٍ مِنْهُ تُسَمَّى نَاشِئَةً، وَهِيَ الْآنَّاتُ. وَالْمَقْصُودُ أَنَّ قِيَامَ اللَّيْلِ هُوَ أَشَدُّ مُوَاطَأَةً بَيْنَ الْقَلْبِ وَاللِّسَانِ، وَأَجْمَعُ عَلَى التِّلَاوَةِ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿هِيَ أَشَدُّ وَطْئًا وَأَقْوَمُ قِيلا﴾ أَيْ: أَجْمَعُ لِلْخَاطِرِ فِي أَدَاءِ الْقِرَاءَةِ وَتَفَهُّمِهَا مِنْ قِيَامِ النَّهَارِ؛ لِأَنَّهُ وَقْتُ انْتِشَارِ النَّاسِ ولَغَط الْأَصْوَاتِ وَأَوْقَاتُ الْمَعَاشِ.

[وَقَدْ] قَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو يَعْلَى الْمَوْصِلِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا إِبْرَاهِيمُ بْنُ سَعِيدٍ الْجَوْهَرِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو أُسَامَةَ، حَدَّثَنَا الْأَعْمَشُ، أَنَّ أَنَسَ بْنَ مَالِكٍ قَرَأَ هَذِهِ الْآيَةَ: "إِنْ نَاشِئَةَ اللَّيْلِ هِيَ أَشَدُّ وَطْئًا وَأَصْوَبُ قِيلًا" فَقَالَ لَهُ رَجُلٌ: إِنَّمَا نَقْرَؤُهَا ﴿وَأَقْوَمُ قِيلا﴾ فَقَالَ لَهُ: إِنَّ أَصْوَبَ وَأَقْوَمَ وَأَهْيَأَ وَأَشْبَاهَ هَذَا وَاحِدٌ. 

وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿إِنَّ لَكَ فِي اَلنَّهَارِ سَبْحًا طَوِيلا﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَعِكْرِمَةُ، وَعَطَاءُ بْنُ أَبِي مُسْلِمٍ: الْفَرَاغُ وَالنَّوْمُ.

وَقَالَ أَبُو الْعَالِيَةِ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَابْنُ مَالِكٍ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَالْحَسَنُ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالرَّبِيعُ بْنُ أَنَسٍ، وَسُفْيَانُ الثَّوْرِيُّ: فَرَاغًا طَوِيلًا.

وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: فَرَاغًا وَبُغْيَةً وَمُنْقَلَبًا.

وَقَالَ السُّدِّيُّ: ﴿سَبْحًا طَوِيلا﴾ تَطَوُّعًا كَثِيرًا.

وَقَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿ [إِنَّ لَكَ فِي اَلنَّهَارِ] سَبْحًا طَوِيلا﴾ قَالَ: لِحَوَائِجِكَ، فَأفْرغ لِدِينِكَ اللَّيْلَ. قَالَ: وَهَذَا حِينَ كَانَتْ صَلَاةُ اللَّيْلِ فَرِيضَةً، ثُمَّ إِنَّ اللَّهَ مَنَّ عَلَى الْعِبَادِ فَخَفَّفَهَا وَوَضَعَهَا، وَقَرَأَ: ﴿قُمِ اللَّيْلَ إِلا قَلِيلا﴾ إِلَى آخِرِ الْآيَةِ، ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿إِنَّ رَبَّكَ يَعْلَمُ أَنَّكَ تَقُومُ أَدْنَى مِنْ ثُلُثَيِ اللَّيْلِ﴾ حَتَّى بَلَغَ: ﴿فَاقْرَءُوا مَا تَيَسَّرَ مِنْهُ﴾ [اللَّيْلَ نِصْفَهُ أَوْ ثُلْثَهُ. ثُمَّ جَاءَ أَمْرٌ أُوْسَعُ وَأَفْسَحُ وَضَعَ الْفَرِيضَةَ عَنْهُ وَعَنْ أُمَّتِهِ] فَقَالَ: قَالَ: ﴿وَمِنَ اللَّيْلِ فَتَهَجَّدْ بِهِ نَافِلَةً لَكَ عَسَى أَنْ يَبْعَثَكَ رَبُّكَ مَقَامًا مَحْمُودًا﴾ [الْإِسْرَاءِ: ٤٩] وَهَذَا الَّذِي قَالَهُ كَمَا قَالَهُ.

وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَيْهِ مَا رَوَاهُ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ فِي مُسْنَدِهِ حَيْثُ قَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى، حدثنا سعيد ابن أَبِي عَرُوبة، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ، عَنْ زُرارة بْنِ أَوْفَى، عَنِ سَعِيدِ بْنِ هِشَامٍ: أَنَّهُ طَلَّقَ امْرَأَتَهُ ثُمَّ ارْتَحَلَ إِلَى الْمَدِينَةِ لِيَبِيعَ عَقَارًا لَهُ بِهَا وَيَجْعَلَهُ فِي الكُرَاع وَالسِّلَاحِ، ثُمَّ يُجَاهِدُ الرُّومَ حَتَّى يَمُوتَ. فَلَقِيَ رَهْطًا مِنْ قَوْمِهِ فَحَدَّثُوهُ أَنَّ رَهْطًا مِنْ قَوْمِهِ سِتَّةً أَرَادُوا ذَلِكَ عَلَى عَهْدِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالَ: "أَلَيْسَ لَكُمْ فِيَّ أسوة ؟ " فَنَهَاهُمْ عَنْ ذَلِكَ، فَأَشْهَدَهُمْ عَلَى رَجعتها، ثُمَّ رَجَعَ إِلَيْنَا فَأَخْبَرَنَا أَنَّهُ أَتَى ابْنَ عَبَّاسٍ فَسَأَلَهُ عَنِ الْوِتْرِ فَقَالَ: أَلَّا أُنَبِّئُكَ بِأَعْلَمِ أَهْلِ الْأَرْضِ بِوِتْرِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ؟ قَالَ: نَعَمْ قَالَ: ائْتِ عَائِشَةَ فَاسْأَلْهَا ثُمَّ ارْجِعْ إِلَيَّ فَأَخْبِرْنِي بِرَدِّهَا عَلَيْكَ. قَالَ: فَأَتَيْتُ عَلَى حَكِيمِ بْنِ أفلحَ فاستلحقتُه إِلَيْهَا، فَقَالَ: مَا أَنَا بِقَارِبِهَا؛ إِنِّي نَهَيْتُهَا أَنْ تَقُولَ فِي هَاتَيْنِ الشِّيعَتَيْنِ شَيْئًا، فَأَبَتْ فِيهِمَا إِلَّا مُضِيًا. فأقسمتُ عَلَيْهِ، فَجَاءَ مَعِي، فَدَخَلْنَا عَلَيْهَا فَقَالَتْ: حَكِيمٌ؟ وَعَرَفَتْهُ، قَالَ: نَعَمْ. قَالَتْ: مَنْ هَذَا مَعَكَ؟ قَالَ: سَعِيدُ بْنُ هِشَامٍ. قَالَتْ: مَنْ هِشَامٌ؟ قَالَ: ابْنُ عَامِرٍ. قَالَ: فَتَرَحَّمَتْ عَلَيْهِ وَقَالَتْ: نِعْمَ الْمَرْءُ كَانَ عَامِرٌ. قُلْتُ: يَا أُمَّ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ، أَنْبِئِينِي عَنْ خلق رسول ﷺ؟ قَالَتْ: أَلَسْتَ تَقْرَأُ الْقُرْآنَ؟ قُلْتُ: بَلَى قَالَتْ: فَإِنَّ خُلُقِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ كَانَ الْقُرْآنَ. فَهممت أَنْ أَقُومَ، ثُمَّ بَدَا لِي قيامُ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ، قُلْتُ: يَا أُمَّ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ، أَنْبِئِينِي عَنْ قِيَامِ رَسُولِ الله صلى الله عليه وسلم. أَلَسْتَ تَقْرَأُ هَذِهِ السُّورَةَ: ﴿يَا أَيُّهَا الْمُزَّمِّلُ﴾ ؟ قُلْتُ: بَلَى. قَالَتْ: فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ افْتَرَضَ قِيَامَ اللَّيْلِ فِي أَوَّلِ هَذِهِ السُّورَةِ، فَقَامَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ وَأَصْحَابُهُ حَوْلًا حَتَّى انْتَفَخَتْ أَقْدَامُهُمْ، وَأَمْسَكَ اللَّهُ خَاتِمَتَهَا فِي السَّمَاءِ اثْنَيْ عَشَرَ شَهْرًا، ثُمَّ أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ التَّخْفِيفَ فِي آخِرِ هَذِهِ السُّورَةِ، فَصَارَ قِيَامُ اللَّيْلِ تَطَوُّعًا مِنْ بَعْدِ فَرِيضَةٍ. فَهَمَمْتُ أَنْ أَقْوَمَ، ثُمَّ بَدَا لِي وِتْرُ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ، قُلْتُ: يَا أُمَّ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ، أَنْبِئِينِي عَنْ وِتْرِ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ. قَالَتِ: كُنَّا نَعُدُّ لَهُ سِواكه وطَهُوره، فَيَبْعَثُهُ اللَّهُ لِمَا شَاءَ أَنْ يَبْعَثَهُ مِنَ اللَّيْلِ، فَيَتَسَوَّكُ ثُمَّ يَتَوَضَّأُ ثُمَّ يُصَلِّي ثَمَانِيَ رَكَعَاتٍ لَا يَجْلِسُ فِيهِنَّ إِلَّا عِنْدَ الثَّامِنَةِ، فَيَجْلِسُ وَيَذْكُرُ رَبَّهُ تَعَالَى وَيَدْعُو [وَيَسْتَغْفِرُ ثُمَّ يَنْهَضُ وَمَا يُسَلِّمُ. ثُمَّ يُصَلِّي التَّاسِعَةَ فَيَقْعُدُ فَيَحْمَدُ رَبَّهُ وَيَذْكُرُهُ وَيَدْعُو] ثُمَّ يُسَلِّمُ تَسْلِيمًا يُسْمِعُنَا، ثُمَّ يُصَلِّي رَكْعَتَيْنِ وَهُوَ جَالِسٌ بَعْدَ مَا يُسَلِّمُ. فَتِلْكَ إِحْدَى عَشْرَةَ رَكْعَةً يَا بُنَيَّ. فَلَمَّا أَسَنَّ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ وَأَخَذَهُ اللَّحْمُ، أَوْتَرَ بسبع، ثم صلى ركعتين وهو جالس بعدما يُسَلِّمُ، فَتِلْكَ تِسْعٌ يَا بُنَيَّ. وَكَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ إِذَا صَلَّى صَلَاةً أَحَبَّ أَنْ يُدَاوِمَ عَلَيْهَا، وَكَانَ إِذَا شَغَله عَنْ قِيَامِ اللَّيْلِ نَوْمٌ أَوْ وَجَع أَوْ مَرَضٌ، صَلَّى مِنَ النَّهَارِ ثِنْتَيْ عَشْرَةَ رَكْعَةً، وَلَا أَعْلَمُ نَبِيَّ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَرَأَ الْقُرْآنَ كُلَّهُ فِي لَيْلَةٍ، وَلَا قَامَ لَيْلَةً حَتَّى أَصْبَحَ، وَلَا صَامَ شَهْرًا كَامِلًا غَيْرَ رَمَضَانَ.

فَأَتَيْتُ ابْنَ عَبَّاسٍ فَحَدَّثْتُهُ بِحَدِيثِهَا، فَقَالَ: صَدَقَتْ، أَمَا لَوْ كُنْتُ أَدْخُلُ عَلَيْهَا لَأَتَيْتُهَا حَتَّى تُشَافِهَنِي مُشَافَهَةً.

هَكَذَا رَوَاهُ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ بِتَمَامِهِ. وَقَدْ أَخْرَجَهُ مُسْلِمٌ فِي صَحِيحِهِ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ قَتَادَةَ، بِنَحْوِهِ. 

طَرِيقٌ أُخْرَى عَنْ عَائِشَةَ فِي هَذَا الْمَعْنَى: قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ وَكِيع، حَدَّثَنَا زَيْدُ بْنُ الحُبَاب -وَحَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ حُمَيْدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا مِهْران قَالَا جَمِيعًا، وَاللَّفْظُ لِابْنِ وَكِيعٍ: عَنْ مُوسَى بْنِ عُبَيدة، حَدَّثَنِي مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ طَحْلاء، عَنْ أَبِي سَلَمَةَ، عَنْ عَائِشَةَ قَالَتْ: كُنْتُ أَجْعَلُ لِرَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ حَصِيرًا يُصَلي عَلَيْهِ مِنَ اللَّيْلِ، فَتَسَامَعَ النَّاسُ بِهِ فَاجْتَمَعُوا، فَخَرَجَ كَالْمُغْضَبِ -وَكَانَ بِهِمْ رَحِيمًا، فَخَشِيَ أن يكتب عَلَيْهِمْ قِيَامُ اللَّيْلِ-فَقَالَ: "أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ، اكلَفُوا مِنَ الْأَعْمَالِ مَا تُطِيقُونَ، فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يَمَلّ مِنَ الثَّوَابِ حَتَّى تَمَلُّوا مِنَ الْعَمَلِ، وَخَيْرُ الْأَعْمَالِ مَا ديمَ عَلَيْهِ". وَنَزَلَ الْقُرْآنُ: ﴿يَا أَيُّهَا الْمُزَّمِّلُ قُمِ اللَّيْلَ إِلا قَلِيلا نِصْفَهُ أَوِ انْقُصْ مِنْهُ قَلِيلا أَوْ زِدْ عَلَيْهِ﴾ حَتَّى كَانَ الرَّجُلُ يَرْبِطُ الْحَبْلَ وَيَتَعَلَّقُ، فَمَكَثُوا بِذَلِكَ ثَمَانِيَةَ أَشْهُرٍ، فَرَأَى اللَّهُ مَا يَبْتَغُونَ مِنْ رِضْوَانِهِ، فَرَحِمَهُمْ فَرَدَّهُمْ إِلَى الْفَرِيضَةِ، وَتَرَكَ قِيَامَ اللَّيْلِ. 

وَرَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ مِنْ طَرِيقِ مُوسَى بْنِ عُبَيْدَةَ الرَّبَذِيِّ، وَهُوَ ضَعِيفٌ. وَالْحَدِيثُ فِي الصَّحِيحِ بِدُونِ زِيَادَةِ نُزُولِ هَذِهِ السُّورَةِ، وَهَذَا السِّيَاقُ قَدْ يُوهم أَنَّ نُزُولَ هَذِهِ السُّورَةِ بِالْمَدِينَةِ، وَلَيْسَ كَذَلِكَ، وَإِنَّمَا هِيَ مَكِّيَّةٌ. وَقَوْلُهُ فِي هَذَا السِّيَاقِ: إِنَّ بَيْنَ نُزُولِ أَوَّلِهَا وَآخِرِهَا ثَمَانِيَةَ أَشْهُرٍ -غَرِيبٌ؛ فَقَدْ تَقَدَّمَ فِي رِوَايَةِ أَحْمَدَ أَنَّهُ كَانَ بَيْنَهُمَا سَنَةٌ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو سَعِيدٍ الْأَشَجُّ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو أُسَامَةَ، عَنْ مِسْعَر، عَنْ سِماك الْحَنَفِيِّ، سَمِعْتُ ابْنَ عَبَّاسٍ يَقُولُ: أَوَّلُ مَا نَزَلَ: أَوَّلُ الْمُزَّمِّلِ، كَانُوا يَقُومُونَ نَحْوًا مِنْ قِيَامِهِمْ فِي شَهْرِ رَمَضَانَ، وَكَانَ بَيْنَ أَوَّلِهَا وَآخِرِهَا قَرِيبٌ مِنْ سَنَةٍ.

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ عَنْ أَبِي كُرَيْب، عَنْ أَبِي أُسَامَةَ، بِهِ. 

وَقَالَ الثَّوْرِيُّ وَمُحَمَّدُ بْنُ بِشْرٍ العَبدي، كِلَاهُمَا عَنْ مِسْعَرٍ، عَنْ سِمَاكٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: كَانَ بَيْنَهُمَا سَنَةٌ. وَرَوَى ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، عَنْ أَبِي كُرَيْبٍ، عَنْ وَكِيعٍ، عَنْ إِسْرَائِيلُ، عَنْ سِمَاكٍ، عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، مِثْلَهُ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ حُمَيْدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا مِهْرَان، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ، عَنْ قَيْسِ بْنِ وَهْبٍ، عَنْ أَبِي عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ قَالَ: لَمَّا نَزَلَتْ: ﴿يَا أَيُّهَا الْمُزَّمِّلُ﴾ قَامُوا حَوْلًا حَتَّى وَرِمَتْ أَقْدَامُهُمْ وسُوقُهم، حَتَّى نَزَلَتْ: ﴿فَاقْرَءُوا مَا تَيَسَّرَ مِنْهُ﴾ قَالَ: فَاسْتَرَاحَ النَّاسُ. 

وَكَذَا قَالَ الْحَسَنُ الْبَصْرِيُّ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: [حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو زُرْعَة، حَدَّثَنَا عُبَيد اللَّهِ بْنُ عُمَرَ الْقَوَارِيرِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا مُعَاذُ بْنُ هِشَامٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي] عَنْ قَتَادَةَ، عَنْ زُرارة بْنِ أَوْفَى، عَنْ سَعْدِ بْنِ هِشَامٍ قَالَ: فَقُلْتُ -يَعْنِي لِعَائِشَةَ-: أَخْبِرِينَا عَنْ قِيَامِ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ. قَالَتِ: أَلَسْتَ تَقْرَأُ: ﴿يَا أَيُّهَا الْمُزَّمِّلُ﴾ ؟ قُلْتُ: بَلَى. قَالَتْ: فَإِنَّهَا كَانَتْ قِيَامَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ وَأَصْحَابِهِ، حَتَّى انْتَفَخَتْ أَقْدَامُهُمْ، وحُبس آخِرُهَا فِي السَّمَاءِ سِتَّةَ عَشَرَ شَهْرًا، ثُمَّ نَزَلَ.

وَقَالَ مَعْمَر، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ: ﴿قُمِ اللَّيْلَ إِلا قَلِيلا﴾ قَامُوا حَوْلًا أَوْ حَوْلَيْنِ، حَتَّى انتفخت سوقُهم وَأَقْدَامُهُمْ فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ تَخْفِيفَهَا بَعْدُ فِي آخِرِ السُّورَةِ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ حُميد، حَدَّثَنَا يعقوب القمي عن جعفر، عن سعيد -هُوَ ابْنُ جُبَيْرٍ-قَالَ: لَمَّا أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى عَلَى نَبِيِّهِ ﷺ: ﴿يَا أَيُّهَا الْمُزَّمِّلُ﴾ قَالَ: مَكَثَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ عَلَى هَذِهِ الْحَالِ عَشْرَ سِنِينَ يَقُومُ اللَّيْلَ، كَمَا أَمَرَهُ، وَكَانَتْ طَائِفَةٌ مِنْ أَصْحَابِهِ يَقُومُونَ مَعَهُ، فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ بَعْدَ عَشْرِ سِنِينَ: ﴿إِنَّ رَبَّكَ يَعْلَمُ أَنَّكَ تَقُومُ أَدْنَى مِنْ ثُلُثَيِ اللَّيْلِ وَنِصْفَهُ وَثُلُثَهُ وَطَائِفَةٌ مِنَ الَّذِينَ مَعَكَ﴾ إِلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَأَقِيمُوا الصَّلاةَ﴾ فَخَفَّفَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى عَنْهُمْ بَعْدَ عَشْرِ سِنِينَ. 

وَرَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ رَافِعٍ، عَنْ يَعْقُوبَ الْقُمِّيِّ بِهِ.

وَقَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿يَا أَيُّهَا الْمُزَّمِّلُ قُمِ اللَّيْلَ إِلا قَلِيلا نِصْفَهُ أَوِ انْقُصْ مِنْهُ قَلِيلا [أَوْ زِدْ عَلَيْهِ وَرَتِّلِ الْقُرْآنَ تَرْتِيلا﴾ فَأَمَرَ اللَّهُ نَبِيَّهُ وَالْمُؤْمِنِينَ بِقِيَامِ اللَّيْلِ إِلَّا قَلِيلًا] فَشَقَّ ذَلِكَ عَلَى الْمُؤْمِنِينَ، ثُمَّ خَفَّفَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ وَرَحِمَهُمْ، فَأَنْزَلَ بَعْدَ هَذَا: ﴿عَلِمَ أَنْ سَيَكُونُ مِنْكُمْ مَرْضَى وَآخَرُونَ يَضْرِبُونَ فِي الأرْضِ﴾ إِلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿فَاقْرَءُوا مَا تَيَسَّرَ مِنْهُ﴾ فَوَسَّعَ اللَّهُ -وَلَهُ الْحَمْدُ-وَلَمْ يُضَيِّقْ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَاذْكُرِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ وَتَبَتَّلْ إِلَيْهِ تَبْتِيلا﴾ أَيْ: أَكْثِرْ مِنْ ذِكْرِهِ، وَانْقَطِعْ إِلَيْهِ، وَتَفَرَّغْ لِعِبَادَتِهِ إِذَا فَرَغْتَ مِنْ أَشْغَالِكَ، وَمَا تَحْتَاجُ إِلَيْهِ مِنْ أُمُورِ دُنْيَاكَ، كَمَا قَالَ: ﴿فَإِذَا فَرَغْتَ فَانْصَبْ﴾ [الشَّرْحِ:٧] أَيْ: إِذَا فَرَغْتَ مِنْ مَهَامِّكَ فَانْصَبْ فِي طَاعَتِهِ وعبَادَته، لِتَكُونَ فَارِغَ الْبَالِ. قَالَهُ ابْنُ زَيْدٍ بِمَعْنَاهُ أَوْ قَرِيبٍ مِنْهُ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَأَبُو صَالِحٍ، وَعَطِيَّةُ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ: ﴿وَتَبَتَّلْ إِلَيْهِ تَبْتِيلا﴾ أَيْ: أَخْلِصْ لَهُ الْعِبَادَةَ.

وَقَالَ الْحَسَنُ: اجْتَهِدْ وَبَتِّلْ إِلَيْهِ نَفْسَكَ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: يُقَالُ لِلْعَابِدِ: مُتَبَتِّلٌ، وَمِنْهُ الْحَدِيثُ الْمَرْوِيُّ: أَنَّهُ نَهَى عَنِ التَّبتُّل، يَعْنِي: الِانْقِطَاعَ إِلَى الْعِبَادَةِ وَتَرْكَ التَّزَوُّجِ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿رَبُّ الْمَشْرِقِ وَالْمَغْرِبِ لَا إِلَهَ إِلا هُوَ فَاتَّخِذْهُ وَكِيلا﴾ أَيْ: هُوَ الْمَالِكُ الْمُتَصَرِّفُ فِي الْمَشَارِقِ وَالْمَغَارِبِ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ، وَكَمَا أَفْرَدْتَهُ بِالْعِبَادَةِ فَأَفْرِدْهُ بِالتَّوَكُّلِ، ﴿فَاتَّخِذْهُ وَكِيلا﴾ كَمَا قَالَ فِي الْآيَةِ الْأُخْرَى: ﴿فَاعْبُدْهُ وَتَوَكَّلْ عَلَيْهِ﴾ [هُودٍ:١٢٣] وَكَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ﴾ وَآيَاتٌ كَثِيرَةٌ فِي هَذَا الْمَعْنَى، فِيهَا الْأَمْرُ بِإِفْرَادِ الْعِبَادَةِ وَالطَّاعَةِ لِلَّهِ، وَتَخْصِيصِهِ بِالتَّوَكُّلِ عَلَيْهِ.

## 73:10-18 — verses 10-18 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, commanding His Messenger ﷺ to be patient with what those foolish deniers among his people say, and to abandon them in a gracious withdrawal, that is, one without reproach accompanying it. Then He says to him, warning and threatening the disbelievers of his people, He being the Great One whom nothing can withstand when He is angered:

{And leave Me with the deniers who possess ease and comfort} meaning: leave Me with the deniers who live in luxury, the people of wealth, for they are more capable of obedience than others, and they are held accountable for rights and duties beyond what others are held to. {And give them respite a little} that is, a short while, as He says: {We give them enjoyment for a little, then We shall compel them toward a harsh punishment} [Luqman: 24]. And therefore He says here: {Indeed, with Us are ankal} which are fetters, as said by Ibn Abbas, Ikrimah, Tawus, Muhammad ibn Ka'b, Abdullah ibn Buraydah, Abu Imran al-Jawni, Abu Mijlaz, al-Dahhak, Hammad ibn Abi Salman, Qatadah, al-Suddi, Ibn al-Mubarak, al-Thawri, and others. {And jahim} which is the blazing, flaming fire.

{And food that chokes} Ibn Abbas said: it catches in the throat so that it can neither go down nor come back up.

{And a painful punishment on the Day when the earth and the mountains shake} that is, are shaken violently, {and the mountains become as a heap of loose sand} meaning: they become like sand dunes after having been solid, hard rock, then they are scattered utterly so that nothing of them remains, until the earth becomes a flat, level plain in which you see no crookedness, that is, no valley, and no unevenness, that is, no elevation. Its meaning is that nothing is lowered and nothing is raised.

Then He says, addressing the disbelievers of Quraysh, though what is meant is all mankind: {Indeed, We have sent to you a messenger as a witness over you} that is, over your deeds, {just as We sent to Pharaoh a messenger, but Pharaoh disobeyed the messenger, so We seized him with a ruinous seizure}. Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, Qatadah, al-Suddi, and al-Thawri said: {a ruinous seizure} means a severe one. That is: so beware, you, of denying this Messenger, lest what befell Pharaoh befall you, when Allah seized him with the seizing of One who is Mighty and All-Powerful, as the Exalted says: {So Allah seized him with the punishment of the Hereafter and of the first life} [al-Naziat: 25]. And you are more deserving of destruction and ruin if you deny, for your Messenger is more noble and greater than Musa ibn Imran (peace be upon him). This is also related from Ibn Abbas and Mujahid.

Regarding His saying: {So how will you guard yourselves, if you disbelieve, on a Day that will make the children grey-haired?} it is possible that {a Day} is the object governed by "you guard yourselves," as Ibn Jarir relates concerning the reading of Ibn Mas'ud: "So how will you fear, O people, a Day that will make the children grey-haired, if you disbelieve in Allah and do not affirm Him?" And it is also possible that it is governed by "you disbelieve." On the first reading: how can you attain safety from this Day of tremendous terror if you disbelieve? On the second: how can you attain God-consciousness if you disbelieve in the Day of Resurrection and deny it? Both are sound meanings, but the first is preferable, and Allah knows best.

The meaning of His saying, {a Day that will make the children grey-haired}, is: from the intensity of its terrors, its tremors, and its turmoils. That is when Allah says to Adam (peace be upon him): "Send forth the party bound for the Fire." He says: "How many, from how many?" and He says: "From every thousand, nine hundred and ninety-nine to the Fire, and one to Paradise."

Al-Tabarani said: [chain] Yahya ibn Ayyub al-Allaf, told us Sa'id ibn Abi Maryam, told us Nafi' ibn Yazid, told us Uthman ibn Ata al-Khurasani, from his father, from Ikrimah, from Ibn Abbas, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ recited: {a Day that will make the children grey-haired} and said: "That is the Day of Resurrection, and that is the Day when Allah says to Adam: 'Rise and send forth from your descendants a party to the Fire.' He says: 'How many, O Lord?' He says: 'From every thousand, nine hundred and ninety-nine, and one is saved.'" This weighed heavily upon the Muslims, and the Messenger of Allah ﷺ perceived that, and when he saw it in their faces he said: "Indeed the children of Adam are many, and indeed Ya'juj and Ma'juj (Gog and Magog) are among the offspring of Adam, and no man among them dies until a thousand men from his loins inherit from him. So in them, and in those like them, lies a shield for you."

This is a hadith gharib (singular report), and mention of these hadiths has already come at the beginning of Surat al-Hajj.

Regarding His saying: {The sky is split apart thereby} al-Hasan and Qatadah said: that is, on account of it, due to its severity and terror. Some make the pronoun refer to Allah, Mighty and Majestic, and this is related from Ibn Abbas and Mujahid, but it is not a strong view, because no prior mention of Him has occurred here to which the pronoun could refer.

Regarding the saying of the Exalted: {His promise is bound to be fulfilled} that is, the promise of this Day is bound to be fulfilled, meaning it will certainly occur, and it is a certainty from which there is no escape.

﴿وَذَرْنِي وَالْمُكَذِّبِينَ أُولِي النَّعْمَةِ﴾ أَيْ: دَعْنِي وَالْمُكَذِّبِينَ الْمُتْرَفِينَ أصحابَ الْأَمْوَالِ، فَإِنَّهُمْ عَلَى الطَّاعَةِ أَقْدَرُ مِنْ غَيْرِهِمْ وَهُمْ يُطَالِبُونَ مِنَ الْحُقُوقِ بِمَا لَيْسَ عِنْدَ غَيْرِهِمْ، ﴿وَمَهِّلْهُمْ قَلِيلا﴾ أَيْ: رُوَيْدًا، كَمَا قَالَ: ﴿نُمَتِّعُهُمْ قَلِيلا ثُمَّ نَضْطَرُّهُمْ إِلَى عَذَابٍ غَلِيظٍ﴾ [لُقْمَانَ: ٢٤] ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ هَاهُنَا: ﴿إِنَّ لَدَيْنَا أَنْكَالا﴾ وَهِيَ: الْقُيُودُ. قَالَهُ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَعِكْرِمَةُ، وَطَاوُسٌ، وَمُحَمَّدُ بْنُ كَعْبٍ، وَعَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ بُرَيْدَةَ، وَأَبُو عِمْرَانَ الْجَوْنِيُّ، وَأَبُو مِجلَز، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَحَمَّادُ بْنُ أَبِي سَلْمَانَ، وَقَتَادَةُ وَالسُّدِّيُّ، وَابْنُ الْمُبَارَكِ وَالثَّوْرِيُّ، وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ، ﴿وَجَحِيمًا﴾ وَهِيَ السَّعِيرُ الْمُضْطَرِمَةُ.

﴿وَطَعَامًا ذَا غُصَّةٍ﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: يَنْشَبُ فِي الْحَلْقِ فَلَا يَدْخُلُ وَلَا يَخْرُجُ.

﴿وَعَذَابًا أَلِيمًا يَوْمَ تَرْجُفُ الأرْضُ وَالْجِبَالُ﴾ أَيْ: تُزَلْزَلُ، ﴿وَكَانَتِ الْجِبَالُ كَثِيبًا مَهِيلا﴾ أَيْ: تَصِيرُ كَكُثْبَانِ الرَّمْلِ بَعْدَ مَا كَانَتْ حِجَارَةً صَمَّاءَ، ثُمَّ إِنَّهَا تُنْسَفُ نَسْفًا فَلَا يَبْقَى مِنْهَا شَيْءٌ إِلَّا ذَهَبَ، حَتَّى تَصِيرَ الْأَرْضُ قَاعًا صَفْصَفًا، لَا تَرَى فِيهَا عِوَجًا، أَيْ: وَادِيًا، وَلَا أَمْتًا، أَيْ: رَابِيَةً، وَمَعْنَاهُ: لَا شَيْءَ يَنْخَفِضُ وَلَا شَيْءَ يَرْتَفِعُ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ مُخَاطِبًا لِكُفَّارِ قُرَيْشٍ، وَالْمُرَادُ سَائِرُ النَّاسِ: ﴿إِنَّا أَرْسَلْنَا إِلَيْكُمْ رَسُولا شَاهِدًا عَلَيْكُمْ﴾ أَيْ: بِأَعْمَالِكُمْ، ﴿كَمَا أَرْسَلْنَا إِلَى فِرْعَوْنَ رَسُولا فَعَصَى فِرْعَوْنُ الرَّسُولَ فَأَخَذْنَاهُ أَخْذًا وَبِيلا﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ، وَالثَّوْرِيُّ: ﴿أَخْذًا وَبِيلا﴾ أَيْ: شَدِيدًا، أَيْ فَاحْذَرُوا أَنْتُمْ أَنْ تُكَذِّبُوا هَذَا الرَّسُولَ، فَيُصِيبَكُمْ مَا أَصَابَ فِرْعَوْنَ، حَيْثُ أَخَذَهُ اللَّهُ أَخْذَ عَزِيزٍ مُقْتَدِرٍ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَأَخَذَهُ اللَّهُ نَكَالَ الآخِرَةِ وَالأولَى﴾ [النَّازِعَاتِ: ٢٥] وَأَنْتُمْ أَوْلَى بِالْهَلَاكِ وَالدَّمَارِ إِنْ كَذَّبْتُمْ؛ لِأَنَّ رَسُولَكُمْ أَشْرَفُ وَأَعْظَمُ مِنْ مُوسَى بْنِ عِمْرَانَ. ويُروَى عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ وَمُجَاهِدٍ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَكَيْفَ تَتَّقُونَ إِنْ كَفَرْتُمْ يَوْمًا يَجْعَلُ الْوِلْدَانَ شِيبًا﴾ يُحْتَمَلُ أَنْ يَكُونَ ﴿يَوْمًا﴾ مَعْمُولًا لِتَتَّقُونَ، كَمَا حَكَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ عَنْ قِرَاءَةِ ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ: "فَكَيْفَ تَخَافُونَ أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ يَوْمًا يَجْعَلُ الْوَلَدَانَ شِيبًا إِنْ كَفَرْتُمْ بِاللَّهِ وَلَمْ تُصَدِّقُوا بِهِ"؟ وَيُحْتَمَلُ أَنْ يَكُونَ مَعْمُولًا لِكَفَرْتُمْ، فَعَلَى الْأَوَّلِ: كَيْفَ يحصلُ لَكُمْ أَمَانٌ مِنْ يَوْمِ هَذَا الْفَزَعِ الْعَظِيمِ إِنْ كَفَرْتُمْ؟ وَعَلَى الثَّانِي: كَيْفَ يَحْصُلُ لَكُمْ تَقْوَى إِنْ كَفَرْتُمْ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ وَجَحَدْتُمُوهُ؟ وَكِلَاهُمَا مَعْنًى حَسَنٌ، وَلَكِنَّ الْأَوَّلَ أَوْلَى، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

وَمَعْنَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿يَوْمًا يَجْعَلُ الْوِلْدَانَ شِيبًا﴾ أَيْ: مِنْ شَدَّةِ أَهْوَالِهِ وَزَلَازِلِهِ وَبَلَابِلِهِ، وَذَلِكَ حِينَ يَقُولُ اللَّهُ لِآدَمَ: ابْعَثْ بَعْثَ النَّارِ. فَيَقُولُ مِن كَمْ؟ فَيَقُولُ: مِنْ كُلِّ أَلْفٍ تِسْعُمِائَةٌ وَتِسْعَةٌ وَتِسْعُونَ إِلَى النَّارِ، وَوَاحِدٌ إِلَى الْجَنَّةِ.

قَالَ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى بْنُ أَيُّوبَ الْعَلَّافُ، حَدَّثَنَا سَعِيدُ بْنُ أَبِي مَرْيَمَ، حَدَّثَنَا نَافِعُ بْنُ يَزِيدَ، حَدَّثَنَا عُثْمَانُ بْنُ عَطَاءٍ الْخُرَاسَانِيُّ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَرَأَ: ﴿يَوْمًا يَجْعَلُ الْوِلْدَانَ شِيبًا﴾ قَالَ: "ذَلِكَ يَوْمُ الْقِيَامَةِ، وَذَلِكَ يَوْمَ يَقُولُ اللَّهُ لِآدَمَ: قُمْ فَابْعَثْ مِنْ ذُرِّيَّتِكَ بَعَثًا إِلَى النَّارِ. قَالَ: مِنْ كَمْ يَا رَبِّ؟ قَالَ: مِنْ كُلِّ أَلْفٍ تِسْعُمِائَةٍ وَتِسْعَةٌ وَتِسْعُونَ، وَيَنْجُو وَاحِدٌ". فَاشْتَدَّ ذَلِكَ عَلَى الْمُسْلِمِينَ، وَعَرَفَ ذَلِكَ رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم ثم قَالَ حِينَ أَبْصَرَ ذَلِكَ فِي وُجُوهِهِمْ: "إِنَّ بَنِي آدَمَ كَثِيرٌ، وَإِنَّ يَأْجُوجَ وَمَأْجُوجَ مِنْ وَلَدِ آدَمَ، وَإِنَّهُ لَا يَمُوتُ مِنْهُمْ رَجُلٌ حَتَّى يَرِثَهُ لِصُلْبِهِ أَلْفُ رَجُلٍ. فَفِيهِمْ وَفِي أَشْبَاهِهِمْ جُنَّةٌ لَكُمْ". 

هَذَا حَدِيثٌ غَرِيبٌ، وَقَدْ تَقَدَّمَ فِي أَوَّلِ سُورَةِ الْحَجِّ ذِكْرُ هَذِهِ الْأَحَادِيثِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿السَّمَاءُ مُنْفَطِرٌ بِهِ﴾ قَالَ الْحَسَنُ، وَقَتَادَةُ: أَيْ بِسَبَبِهِ مِنْ شِدَّتِهِ وَهَوْلِهِ. وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يُعِيدُ الضَّمِيرَ عَلَى اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ. وَرُوِيَ عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ وَمُجَاهِدٍ، وَلَيْسَ بِقَوِيٍّ؛ لِأَنَّهُ لَمْ يَجْرِ لَهُ ذِكْرٌ هَاهُنَا.

وَقَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿كَانَ وَعْدُهُ مَفْعُولا﴾ أَيْ: كَانَ وَعْدُ هَذَا الْيَوْمِ مَفْعُولًا أَيْ وَاقِعًا لَا مَحَالَةَ، وَكَائِنًا لَا مَحِيدَ عَنْهُ.

## 73:19-20 — verses 19-20 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: {Indeed this} that is, this surah, {is a reminder} that is, those possessed of understanding take heed by it. Therefore He said: {so whoever wills, let him take a path to his Lord} that is, from among those whom Allah wills to guide, as He qualified it in another surah: {And you do not will except that Allah wills. Indeed, Allah is ever Knowing and Wise} [al-Insan: 30].

Then He said: {Indeed your Lord knows that you stand less than two-thirds of the night, and half of it, and a third of it, and a group of those with you} that is, sometimes this and sometimes that, all of that without deliberate intention on your part, but rather because you are not able to persist continuously upon what He commanded you of night prayer, since it is difficult for you. Therefore He said: {And Allah determines the night and the day} that is, sometimes they are equal, and sometimes one takes from the other, or the other from this one. {He knows that you will not be able to count it} that is, the obligation which He imposed upon you, {so recite what is easy from the Qur'an} that is, without any fixed time limit, meaning: rather stand in the night with what is easy for you. He expressed the prayer by the term "recitation," just as He said in Surat Subhan (al-Isra): {And do not recite your prayer aloud} that is, your recitation, {and do not be silent with it}.

The companions of Imam Abu Hanifah, may Allah have mercy on him, cited this verse as evidence, namely His saying: {so recite what is easy from the Qur'an}, for the position that recitation of al-Fatiha is not specifically required in prayer. Rather, if one recites it or something else from the Qur'an, even a single verse, it suffices. They supported this with the hadith of the man who prayed poorly, found in the two Sahihs: "Then recite what is easy for you from the Qur'an."

The majority responded to them with the hadith of Ubadah ibn al-Samit, also found in the two Sahihs: that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "There is no prayer for one who does not recite al-Fatiha (the Opening chapter)." And in Sahih Muslim, from Abu Hurayrah, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Every prayer in which Umm al-Kitab (the Mother of the Book) is not recited is khidaj (deficient), is khidaj, is khidaj, incomplete." And in Sahih Ibn Khuzaymah, from Abu Hurayrah, marfu' (elevated to the Prophet ﷺ): "The prayer of one who does not recite Umm al-Qur'an does not suffice."

His saying: {He knows that there will be among you sick people, and others traveling through the land seeking of Allah's bounty, and others fighting in the way of Allah} that is, He knew that there would be among this nation those with valid excuses for leaving night prayer: the sick who are unable to do it, travelers in the land seeking of Allah's bounty through earnings and trade, and others occupied with what is more important in their case, namely fighting in the way of Allah. This verse, indeed the whole surah, is Meccan, and fighting had not yet been legislated. So this is among the greatest proofs of prophethood, since it belongs to the category of informing about unseen future matters. Therefore He said: {so recite what is easy from it} that is, perform what is easy for you of it.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Abu Raja' Muhammad said: I said to al-Hasan, "O Abu Sa'id, what do you say about a man who has memorized the entire Qur'an by heart but does not stand in prayer with it, rather only praying the obligatory prayers?" He said: "He uses the Qur'an as a pillow, may Allah curse that one." Allah the Almighty said of the righteous servant: {And indeed, he is a possessor of knowledge because We taught him} [Yusuf: 68], {and you have been taught what neither you nor your fathers knew}. I said: "O Abu Sa'id, Allah said: {so recite what is easy from the Qur'an}?" He said: "Yes, even if five verses."

This clearly reflects the position of al-Hasan al-Basri: that he considered it an obligatory right upon the bearers of the Qur'an that they should stand in prayer with at least something of it during the night. Therefore it is reported in the hadith that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was asked about a man who slept until morning, and he said: "That is a man in whose ear Satan has urinated." It was said that its meaning is: he slept through the obligatory prayer. And it was said: through the night prayer. And in the Sunan: "Perform the Witr prayer, O people of the Qur'an." And in another hadith: "Whoever does not perform Witr is not of us."

Even more strange than this is what is related from Abu Bakr Abd al-Aziz, of the Hanbalis, that he held night prayer during the month of Ramadan to be obligatory. Allah knows best.

Al-Tabarani said: [chain] from Ibn Abbas, from the Prophet ﷺ, regarding {so recite what is easy from it}, he said: "A hundred verses."

This is a hadith that is gharib jiddan (extremely singular in transmission); I have not seen it except in the Mu'jam of al-Tabarani, may Allah have mercy on him.

His saying: {And establish prayer and give zakah} that is, establish your obligatory prayer, and give the obligatory zakah. This is evidence for those who said that the obligation of zakah was revealed in Mecca, though the specific measures of the minimum thresholds (nisab) and the amount to be paid were not clarified until Medina. Allah knows best.

Ibn Abbas, Ikrimah, Mujahid, al-Hasan, Qatadah, and more than one of the Salaf said: this verse abrogated what Allah had first made obligatory upon the Muslims regarding night prayer. They differed concerning the period of time between the two rulings, with various opinions as mentioned previously. It is established in the two Sahihs that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said to that man: "Five prayers in the day and night." He said: "Is there anything else required of me?" He said: "No, except what you volunteer."

His saying, the Almighty: {and loan Allah a goodly loan} means: from charitable giving, for Allah repays for that with the best and most abundant reward, as He said: {Who is it that will loan Allah a goodly loan so that He may multiply it for him many times over} [al-Baqarah: 245].

His saying: {And whatever good you send forth for yourselves, you will find it with Allah, it being better and greater in reward} that is, everything you send forth ahead of you is good that is secured for you, and it is better than what you leave behind for yourselves in this world.

Al-Hafiz Abu Ya'la al-Mawsili said: [chain] al-Harith ibn Suwayd said: Abdullah said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Which of you loves his own wealth more than the wealth of his heir?" They said: "O Messenger of Allah, there is not one of us but that his own wealth is dearer to him than the wealth of his heir." He said: "Know what you are saying." They said: "We know nothing other than that, O Messenger of Allah." He said: "Rather, the wealth of one of you is what he has sent forth, and the wealth of his heir is what he leaves behind."

Al-Bukhari related it from the hadith of Hafs ibn Ghiyath, and al-Nasa'i from the hadith of Abu Mu'awiyah, both from al-A'mash, with this wording.

Then the Almighty said: {And seek forgiveness of Allah. Indeed, Allah is Forgiving and Merciful} that is, increase in remembrance of Him and seeking His forgiveness in all your affairs, for He is Forgiving and Merciful toward whoever seeks His forgiveness.

The end of the tafsir of Surat al-Muzzammil. All praise belongs to Allah.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿إِنَّ رَبَّكَ يَعْلَمُ أَنَّكَ تَقُومُ أَدْنَى مِنْ ثُلُثَيِ اللَّيْلِ وَنِصْفَهُ وَثُلُثَهُ وَطَائِفَةٌ مِنَ الَّذِينَ مَعَكَ﴾ أَيْ: تَارَةً هَكَذَا، وَتَارَةً هَكَذَا، وَذَلِكَ كُلُّهُ مِنْ غَيْرِ قَصْدٍ مِنْكُمْ، وَلَكِنْ لَا تَقْدِرُونَ عَلَى الْمُوَاظَبَةِ عَلَى مَا أَمَرَكُمْ بِهِ مِنْ قِيَامِ اللَّيْلِ؛ لِأَنَّهُ يَشُقُّ عَلَيْكُمْ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿وَاللَّهُ يُقَدِّرُ اللَّيْلَ وَالنَّهَارَ﴾ أَيْ: تَارَةً يَعْتَدِلَانِ، وَتَارَةً يَأْخُذُ هَذَا مِنْ هَذَا، أَوْ هَذَا مِنْ هَذَا، ﴿عَلِمَ أَنْ لَنْ تُحْصُوهُ﴾ أَيِ: الْفَرْضَ الَّذِي أَوْجَبَهُ عَلَيْكُمْ ﴿فَاقْرَءُوا مَا تَيَسَّرَ مِنَ الْقُرْآنِ﴾ أَيْ: مِنْ غَيْرِ تَحْدِيدٍ بِوَقْتٍ، أَيْ: وَلَكِنْ قُومُوا مِنَ اللَّيْلِ مَا تَيَسَّرَ. وَعَبَّرَ عَنِ الصَّلَاةِ بِالْقِرَاءَةِ، كَمَا قَالَ فِي سُورَةِ سُبْحَانَ: ﴿وَلا تَجْهَرْ بِصَلاتِكَ﴾ أَيْ: بِقِرَاءَتِكَ، ﴿وَلا تُخَافِتْ بِهَا﴾

وَقَدِ اسْتَدَلَّ أَصْحَابُ الْإِمَامِ أَبِي حَنِيفَةَ، رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ، بِهَذِهِ الْآيَةِ، وَهِيَ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَاقْرَءُوا مَا تَيَسَّرَ مِنَ الْقُرْآنِ﴾ عَلَى أَنَّهُ لَا يَتَعَيَّنُ قِرَاءَةُ الْفَاتِحَةِ فِي الصَّلَاةِ، بَلْ لَوْ قَرَأَ بِهَا أَوْ بِغَيْرِهَا مِنَ الْقُرْآنِ، وَلَوْ بِآيَةٍ، أَجْزَأَهُ؛ وَاعْتَضَدُوا بِحَدِيثِ الْمُسِيءِ صَلَاتَهُ الَّذِي فِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ: "ثُمَّ اقْرَأْ مَا تَيَسَّرَ مَعَكَ مِنَ الْقُرْآنِ".

وَقَدْ أَجَابَهُمُ الْجُمْهُورُ بِحَدِيثِ عُبَادَةَ بْنِ الصَّامِتِ، وَهُوَ فِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ أَيْضًا: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "لَا صَلَاةَ لِمَنْ لَمْ يَقْرَأْ بِفَاتِحَةِ الْكِتَابِ" وَفِي صَحِيحِ مُسْلِمٌ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "كُلُّ صَلَاةٍ لَا يُقْرَأُ فِيهَا بِأُمِّ الْكِتَابِ فَهِيَ خِدَاج، فَهِيَ خِدَاج، فَهِيَ خِدَاج، غَيْرُ تَمَامٍ". وَفِي صَحِيحِ ابن خزيمة عن أبي هريرة مَرْفُوعًا: "لَا تُجْزِئُ صَلَاةُ مَنْ لَمْ يَقْرَأْ بِأُمِّ الْقُرْآنِ". 

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿عَلِمَ أَنْ سَيَكُونُ مِنْكُمْ مَرْضَى وَآخَرُونَ يَضْرِبُونَ فِي الأرْضِ يَبْتَغُونَ مِنْ فَضْلِ اللَّهِ وَآخَرُونَ يُقَاتِلُونَ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ﴾ أَيْ: عَلِمَ أَنْ سَيَكُونُ مِنْ هَذِهِ الْأُمَّةِ ذَوُو أَعْذَارٍ فِي تَرْكِ قِيَامِ اللَّيْلِ، مِنْ مَرْضَى لَا يَسْتَطِيعُونَ ذَلِكَ، وَمُسَافِرِينَ فِي الْأَرْضِ يَبْتَغُونَ مِنْ فَضْلِ اللَّهِ فِي الْمَكَاسِبِ وَالْمَتَاجِرِ، وَآخَرِينَ مَشْغُولِينَ بِمَا هُوَ الْأَهَمُّ فِي حَقِّهِمْ مِنَ الْغَزْوِ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ وَهَذِهِ الْآيَةُ -بَلِ السُّورَةُ كُلُّهَا-مَكِّيَّةٌ، وَلَمْ يَكُنِ الْقِتَالُ شُرع بَعْدُ، فَهِيَ مِنْ أَكْبَرِ دَلَائِلِ النُّبُوَّةِ، لِأَنَّهُ مِنْ بَابِ الْإِخْبَارِ بِالْمُغَيَّبَاتِ الْمُسْتَقْبِلَةِ. وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿فَاقْرَءُوا مَا تَيَسَّرَ مِنْهُ﴾ أَيْ: قُومُوا بِمَا تَيَسَّرَ عَلَيْكُمْ مِنْهُ.

قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا يَعْقُوبُ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ عُلَيَّة، عَنْ أَبِي رَجَاءٍ مُحَمَّدٍ، قَالَ: قُلْتُ لِلْحَسَنِ: يَا أَبَا سَعِيدٍ، مَا تَقُولُ فِي رَجُلٍ قَدِ اسْتَظْهَرَ الْقُرْآنَ كُلَّهُ عَنْ ظَهْرِ قَلْبِهِ، وَلَا يَقُومُ بِهِ، إِنَّمَا يُصَلِّي الْمَكْتُوبَةَ؟ قَالَ: يتوسَّدُ الْقُرْآنَ، لَعَنَ اللَّهُ ذَاكَ، قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى لِلْعَبْدِ الصَّالِحِ: ﴿وَإِنَّهُ لَذُو عِلْمٍ لِمَا عَلَّمْنَاهُ﴾ [يُوسُفَ: ٦٨] ﴿وَعُلِّمْتُمْ مَا لَمْ تَعْلَمُوا أَنْتُمْ وَلا آبَاؤُكُمْ﴾ قُلْتُ: يَا أَبَا سَعِيدٍ، قَالَ اللَّهُ: ﴿فَاقْرَءُوا مَا تَيَسَّرَ مِنَ الْقُرْآنِ﴾ ؟ قَالَ: نَعَمْ، وَلَوْ خَمْسَ آيَاتٍ.

وَهَذَا ظَاهِرٌ مِنْ مَذْهَبِ الْحَسَنِ الْبَصْرِيِّ: أَنَّهُ كَانَ يَرَى حَقًّا وَاجِبًا عَلَى حَمَلة الْقُرْآنِ أَنْ يَقُومُوا وَلَوْ بِشَيْءٍ مِنْهُ فِي اللَّيْلِ؛ وَلِهَذَا جَاءَ فِي الْحَدِيثِ: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ سُئِلَ عَنْ رَجُلٍ نَامَ حَتَّى أَصْبَحَ، فَقَالَ: "ذَاكَ رَجُلٌ بَالَ الشَّيْطَانُ فِي أُذُنِهِ". فَقِيلَ مَعْنَاهُ: نَامَ عَنِ الْمَكْتُوبَةِ. وَقِيلَ: عَنْ قِيَامِ اللَّيْلِ. وَفِي السُّنَنِ: "أوتِرُوا يَا أَهْلَ الْقُرْآنِ." وَفِي الْحَدِيثِ الْآخَرِ: "مَنْ لَمْ يُوتِرْ فَلَيْسَ مِنَّا". 

وَأَغْرَبُ مِنْ هَذَا مَا حُكِيَ عَنْ أَبِي بَكْرٍ عَبْدِ الْعَزِيزِ، مِنَ الْحَنَابِلَةِ، مِنْ إِيجَابِهِ قِيَامَ شَهْرِ رَمَضَانَ، فَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

وَقَالَ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ سَعِيدِ بْنِ فَرَقَدٍ الجُدّي، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو [حُمَةَ] مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ يُوسُفَ الزُّبَيْدِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ، [عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ] بْنِ طَاوُسٍ -مِنْ وَلَدِ طَاوُسٍ-عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ طَاوُسٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ: ﴿فَاقْرَءُوا مَا تَيَسَّرَ مِنْهُ﴾ قَالَ: "مِائَةُ آيَةٍ". 

وَهَذَا حَدِيثٌ غَرِيبٌ جِدًّا لَمْ أَرَهُ إِلَّا فِي مُعْجَمِ الطَّبَرَانِيِّ، رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَأَقِيمُوا الصَّلاةَ وَآتُوا الزَّكَاةَ﴾ أَيْ: أَقِيمُوا صَلَاتَكُمُ الْوَاجِبَةَ عَلَيْكُمْ، وَآتُوا الزَّكَاةَ الْمَفْرُوضَةَ. وَهَذَا يَدُلُّ لِمَنْ قَالَ: إِنَّ فَرْضَ الزَّكَاةِ نَزَلَ بِمَكَّةَ، لَكِنَّ مَقَادِيرَ النُّصُبِ والمَخْرَج لَمْ تُبَين إِلَّا بِالْمَدِينَةِ. وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

وَقَدْ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَعِكْرِمَةُ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَالْحَسَنُ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ مِنَ السَّلَفِ: إِنَّ هَذِهِ الْآيَةَ نَسَخت الَّذِي كَانَ اللَّهُ قَدْ أَوْجَبَهُ عَلَى الْمُسْلِمِينَ أَوَّلًا مِنْ قِيَامِ اللَّيْلِ. وَاخْتَلَفُوا فِي الْمُدَّةِ الَّتِي بَيْنَهُمَا عَلَى أَقْوَالٍ كَمَا تَقَدَّمَ. وَقَدْ ثَبَتَ فِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ لِذَلِكَ الرَّجُلِ: "خَمْسُ صَلَوَاتٍ فِي الْيَوْمِ وَاللَّيْلَةِ". قَالَ: هَلْ عَلَيَّ غَيْرُهَا؟ قَالَ: "لَا إِلَّا أَنَّ تَطوّع". 

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَأَقْرَضُوا اللَّهَ قَرْضًا حَسَنًا﴾ يَعْنِي: مِنَ الصَّدَقَاتِ، فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ يُجَازِي عَلَى ذَلِكَ أَحْسَنَ الْجَزَاءِ وَأَوْفَرَهُ، كَمَا قَالَ: ﴿مَنْ ذَا الَّذِي يُقْرِضُ اللَّهَ قَرْضًا حَسَنًا فَيُضَاعِفَهُ لَهُ أَضْعَافًا كَثِيرَةً﴾ [البقرة: ٢٤٥] .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَمَا تُقَدِّمُوا لأنْفُسِكُمْ مِنْ خَيْرٍ تَجِدُوهُ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ هُوَ خَيْرًا وَأَعْظَمَ أَجْرًا﴾ أَيْ: جَمِيعُ مَا تُقَدِّمُوهُ بَيْنَ أَيْدِيكُمْ فَهُوَ [خَيْرٌ] لَكُمْ حَاصِلٌ، وَهُوَ خَيْرٌ مِمَّا أَبْقَيْتُمُوهُ لِأَنْفُسِكُمْ فِي الدُّنْيَا.

وَقَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو يَعْلَى الْمَوْصِلِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو خَيْثَمة، حَدَّثَنَا جَرِيرٌ، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، عَنْ الْحَارِثِ بْنِ سُوَيد قَالَ: قَالَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "أَيُّكُمْ مَالُهُ أَحَبُّ إِلَيْهِ مَنْ مَالِ وَارِثِهِ؟ ". قَالُوا: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، مَا مِنَّا مِنْ أَحَدٌ إِلَّا مَالُهُ أَحَبُّ إِلَيْهِ مِنْ مَالِ وَارِثِهِ. قَالَ: "اعْلَمُوا مَا تَقُولُونَ". قَالُوا: مَا نَعْلَمُ إِلَّا ذَلِكَ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ؟ قَالَ: "إِنَّمَا مَالُ أَحَدِكُمْ مَا قَدّم وَمَالُ وَارِثِهِ مَا أَخَّرَ".

وَرَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ حَفْصِ بْنِ غِيَاثٍ، وَالنَّسَائِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي مُعَاوِيَةَ، كِلَاهُمَا عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، بِهِ. 

ثُمَّ قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَاسْتَغْفِرُوا اللَّهَ إِنَّ اللَّهَ غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ﴾ أَيْ: أَكْثِرُوا مِنْ ذِكْرِهِ وَاسْتِغْفَارِهِ فِي أُمُورِكُمْ كُلِّهَا؛ فَإِنَّهُ غَفُورٌ رَحِيمٌ لِمَنِ اسْتَغْفَرَهُ.

آخِرُ تَفْسِيرِ سُورَةِ "الْمُزَّمِّلِ" وَلِلَّهِ الْحَمْدُ.
