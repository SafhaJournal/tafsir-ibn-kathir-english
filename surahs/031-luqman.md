## 31:1-5 — verses 1-5 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Tafsir of Surat Luqman

It is Meccan.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

Most of what pertains to the opening of this surah has already been discussed at the beginning of Surat al-Baqarah, namely that He, Exalted is He, made this Qur'an a guidance, a healing, and a mercy for al-muhsinin (those who do good), who are those who did good in following the sacred law. They established the obligatory prayer within its limits and its times, along with what follows it of regular and non-regular voluntary prayers. They gave the obligatory zakat to those entitled to it, they maintained ties with their relatives and kin, and they had certain faith in the reward of the Hereafter, so they sought from Allah the reward for that, not showing off with it nor seeking any repayment or thanks from people. Whoever does so is among those of whom Allah, Exalted is He, said: {those are upon guidance from their Lord}, meaning upon insight, clear proof, and a plain and manifest path, {and those are the successful ones}, meaning in this world and the Hereafter.

## 31:6-7 — verses 6-7 (shared commentary)

### Translation

When the Exalted mentioned the state of the fortunate ones, those who are guided by the Book of God and benefit from hearing it, as [God] the Exalted said: {God has sent down the best of speech, a Book consistent, oft-repeated, whereat shiver the skins of those who fear their Lord, then their skins and their hearts soften to the remembrance of God. That is the guidance of God by which He guides whom He wills, and whoever God sends astray, for him there is no guide} [al-Zumar: 23], He followed it by mentioning the state of the wretched ones, those who turn away from benefiting by hearing the speech of God and turn instead to listening to pipes and singing set to melodies and instruments of merriment. This is as Ibn Mas'ud said regarding His statement: {And among the people is he who purchases idle talk}. He said: "It is, by God, singing."

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Abu al-Sahba' al-Bakri heard Abdullah ibn Mas'ud being asked about this verse: {And among the people is he who purchases idle talk to lead astray from the path of God}. Abdullah said: "Singing, by God, besides whom there is no other god," repeating it three times.

[chain] Abu al-Sahba' asked Ibn Mas'ud about God's statement: {And among the people is he who purchases idle talk}, he said: "Singing."

Ibn Abbas, Jabir, Ikrimah, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, Mujahid, Makhul, Amr ibn Shu'ayb, and Ali ibn Badhimah said likewise.

Al-Hasan al-Basri said: This verse, {And among the people is he who purchases idle talk to lead astray from the path of God without knowledge}, was revealed concerning singing and pipes.

Qatadah said, regarding His statement: {And among the people is he who purchases idle talk to lead astray from the path of God without knowledge}: "By God, perhaps he does not spend money on it, but his purchase of it is his taking it as something desirable. It is enough misguidance for a person that he chooses false speech over true speech, and what harms him over what benefits him."

It was also said that by His statement {he purchases idle talk} is meant the purchasing of singing slave girls.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] from Abu Umamah, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "It is not lawful to sell singing girls nor to buy them, and consuming their price is unlawful, and it was concerning them that God, Mighty and Majestic, revealed to me: {And among the people is he who purchases idle talk}."

Al-Tirmidhi and Ibn Jarir likewise related it from the hadith of Ubaydullah ibn Zahr in similar wording. Al-Tirmidhi then said: "This is a gharib (singular) hadith," and he weakened the aforementioned Ali ibn Yazid.

I say: Ali, his teacher, and the narrator from him are all weak. And God knows best.

Al-Dahhak said regarding His statement, {And among the people is he who purchases idle talk}: "It means shirk (associating partners with God)." Abd al-Rahman ibn Zayd ibn Aslam said likewise. Ibn Jarir preferred that it refers to every speech that turns one away from the signs of God and from following His path.

As for His statement, {to lead astray from the path of God}: that is, he only does this out of opposition to Islam and its people.

According to the reading with fatha on the ya' (yudilla), the lam would be the lam of consequence, or an explanation of the decreed matter, meaning: they were destined for that so that they would become so.

As for His statement, {and to take it in mockery}: Mujahid said: "He takes the path of God in mockery, ridiculing it."

Qatadah said: "It means he takes the signs of God in mockery." But Mujahid's statement is more fitting.

As for the statement of the Exalted, {those, for them is a humiliating punishment}: that is, just as they held the signs of God and His path in contempt, they will be humiliated on the Day of Resurrection in the everlasting, continuous punishment.

Then the Exalted said: {And when Our verses are recited to him, he turns away in arrogance as if he had not heard them, as if there were in his ears deafness}, meaning: this one who turns toward idle amusement, play, and merriment, when the Qur'anic verses are recited to him, turns away from them, is averse, turns his back, and feigns deafness though there is nothing wrong with his hearing, as if he does not hear them, because he is pained by hearing them, since he derives no benefit from them and has no desire for them. {So give him tidings of a painful punishment}, meaning: on the Day of Resurrection it will pain him, just as he was pained by hearing the Book of God and His verses.

## 31:8-9 — verses 8-9 (shared commentary)

### Translation

This is a mention of the destination of the righteous among the felicitous ones in the Hereafter, those who believed in Allah and affirmed the messengers as true, and did righteous deeds in accordance with the law of Allah: {For them are gardens of bliss}, meaning they enjoy therein every kind of pleasure and delight, of foods and drinks, clothing and dwellings, mounts and women, radiance and hearing such as has never occurred to anyone's mind. They remain therein permanently, they do not depart from it, and they do not desire any change from it.

His saying: {A true promise of Allah}, means this is bound to occur without doubt, because it is from the promise of Allah, and Allah does not break His promise, because He is the Generous, the Bestower, the One who does whatever He wills, capable of all things. {And He is the Mighty}, the One who has subdued everything and to whom everything submits, {the Wise}, in His words and His deeds, the One who made the Qur'an a guidance for the believers: {Say, it is for those who believe a guidance and a healing, and those who do not believe, in their ears is deafness, and it is upon them blindness} [Fussilat: 44], {And We send down of the Qur'an that which is a healing and a mercy for the believers, but it increases the wrongdoers in nothing but loss} [al-Isra: 82].

## 31:10-11 — verses 10-11 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He, glory be to Him, clarifies through this His tremendous power over the creation of the heavens and the earth, and what is in them and what is between them, saying: {He created the heavens without pillars}. Al-Hasan and Qatadah said: they have no pillars, neither visible nor invisible.

Ibn 'Abbas, 'Ikrimah, and Mujahid said: they do have pillars, but you do not see them. The discussion of this matter has already been established at the beginning of Surat "al-Ra'd" in a manner sufficient enough that it need not be repeated here.

{And He cast into the earth firmly set mountains}, meaning: the mountains anchored the earth and weighed it down so that it would not shake with its inhabitants upon the surface of the water. This is why He said: {lest it should shake with you}, meaning: lest it shake with you.

His statement: {and dispersed throughout it every kind of moving creature}, meaning: He scattered throughout it various kinds of animals whose number of forms and colors none knows except the One who created them.

Having established that He is the Creator, He then draws attention to the fact that He is also the Provider, by His saying, Exalted is He: {And We sent down water from the sky and caused to grow therein every noble kind}, meaning: every noble kind of plant, that is, pleasing in appearance.

Al-Sha'bi said: people too are among the growth of the earth. Whoever enters Paradise is noble (karim), and whoever enters the Fire is base (la'im).

His statement: {This is the creation of Allah}, meaning: this that He, Exalted is He, has mentioned of the creation of the heavens and the earth and what is between them, proceeds from the act of Allah, His creation and His decree, alone, with no partner in that. This is why He said: {So show Me what those besides Him have created}, meaning: from among what you worship and call upon, the idols and rivals. {Rather, the wrongdoers}, meaning: those who associate partners with Allah, worshipping others besides Him, {are in manifest error}, meaning: ignorance and blindness, {clear}, meaning: plain and evident, with no concealment in it.

## 31:12 — verse 12

### Translation

The early generations (salaf) differed concerning Luqman, peace be upon him: was he a prophet, or a righteous servant without prophethood? There are two opinions on this, and the majority hold the second view.

Sufyan al-Thawri said, from al-Ash'ath, from Ikrimah, from Ibn Abbas, who said: Luqman was an Abyssinian slave, a carpenter.

Qatadah said, from Abdullah ibn al-Zubayr: I said to Jabir ibn Abdullah, "What has come down to you concerning the matter of Luqman?" He said: "He was short, with a flat nose, from the Nubians."

Yahya ibn Sa'id al-Ansari said, from Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab, who said: Luqman was from the black people of Egypt, thick-lipped. God gave him wisdom but withheld prophethood from him.

Al-Awza'i, may God have mercy on him, said: Abdul-Rahman ibn Harmalah related to me, saying: A black man came to Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab to ask him something, so Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab said to him: "Do not grieve because you are black, for among the best of people were three black men: Bilal, Mihja' the freedman of Umar ibn al-Khattab, and Luqman the Wise, who was a black Nubian, thick-lipped."

Ibn Jarir said: Ibn Wakee' told us, my father told us, from Abu al-Ashhab, from Khalid al-Rab'i, who said: Luqman was an Abyssinian slave, a carpenter. His master said to him: "Slaughter this sheep for us." He slaughtered it, and his master said: "Bring out the two best morsels of flesh in it." So he brought out the tongue and the heart. Some time passed as God willed, then his master said: "Slaughter this sheep for us." He slaughtered it, and his master said: "Bring out the two worst morsels of flesh in it." So he brought out the tongue and the heart. His master said to him: "I ordered you to bring out the two best morsels in it, and you brought them out, and I ordered you to bring out the two worst morsels in it, and you brought them out." Luqman said: "There is nothing better than these two when they are good, and nothing worse than these two when they are bad."

Shu'bah said, from al-Hakam, from Mujahid: Luqman was a righteous servant, not a prophet.

Al-A'mash said: Mujahid said: Luqman was a black slave with large lips and cracked feet.

Hakkam ibn Salm said, from Sa'id al-Zubaydi, from Mujahid: Luqman the Wise was an Abyssinian slave with thick lips and flat feet, and he was a judge over the Children of Israel.

Someone else mentioned that he was a judge over the Children of Israel in the time of David, peace be upon him.

Ibn Jarir said: Ibn Humayd told us, al-Hakam told us, Amr ibn Qays told us, who said: Luqman, peace be upon him, was a black slave with thick lips and flat feet. A man came to him while he was sitting in a gathering of people, speaking to them, and said to him: "Are you not the one who used to herd sheep with me in such-and-such a place?" He said: "Yes." The man said: "What has brought you to this state that I see?" He said: "Truthfulness in speech, and remaining silent about what does not concern me."

Ibn Abi Hatim said: Abu Zur'ah told us, Safwan told us, al-Walid told us, Abdul-Rahman ibn Yazid told us, from Jabir, who said: God raised Luqman the Wise through his wisdom, and a man who had known him before saw him and said to him: "Are you not the slave of the sons of so-and-so, who used to herd sheep just yesterday?" He said: "Indeed." The man said: "What has brought you to this state that I see?" He said: "The decree of God, fulfilling trust, truthfulness in speech, and my leaving what does not concern me."

These reports include some that explicitly deny that he was a prophet, and others that merely suggest this, because his being a servant who had experienced enslavement is incompatible with his being a prophet, since messengers were sent from among the noble lineages of their peoples. For this reason the majority of the salaf held that he was not a prophet. His being a prophet is only transmitted from Ikrimah, if the chain to him is sound, for Ibn Jarir and Ibn Abi Hatim narrated it through the hadith of Wakee', from Isra'il, from Jabir, from Ikrimah, who said: Luqman was a prophet. This Jabir is Ibn Yazid al-Ju'fi, and he is weak. God knows best.

Abdullah ibn Wahb said: Abdullah ibn Ayyash al-Qatabani informed me, from Umar the freedman of Ghufrah, who said: A man stopped before Luqman the Wise and said: "Are you Luqman? Are you the slave of the sons of al-Hishas?" He said: "Yes." He said: "Are you the shepherd of sheep?" He said: "Yes." He said: "Are you the black one?" He said: "As for my blackness, it is evident, so what is it about my affair that amazes you?" He said: "People treading your carpet, crowding at your door, and being satisfied with your speech." He said: "O son of my brother, if you incline toward what I tell you, you would be like this too." Luqman said: "Lowering my gaze, restraining my tongue, the purity of my food, guarding my private parts, my speaking truthfully, my fulfilling my covenant, my honoring my guest, my protecting my neighbor, and my leaving what does not concern me: that is what brought me to what you see."

Ibn Abi Hatim said: My father told us, Ibn Nufayl told us, Amr ibn Waqid told us, from Abdah ibn Rabah, from Rabi'ah, from Abu al-Darda, may God be pleased with him, that he said one day, when Luqman the Wise was mentioned: "What he was given was not given to him because of family, wealth, lineage, or particular traits, but he was a man of few words, deeply reserved, given to long reflection, and profound in insight. He never slept during the day, and no one ever saw him spit, clear his throat, urinate, defecate, wash himself indecently, engage in idle play, or laugh. He never repeated a statement he had made, except when he spoke a piece of wisdom that someone asked him to repeat. He had married and had children, and when they died he did not weep over them. He used to attend upon rulers and go to judges, in order to observe, reflect, and take heed. It was through this that he was given what he was given."

There is a gharib (singular) report from Qatadah, narrated by Ibn Abi Hatim, who said:

My father told us, al-Abbas ibn al-Walid told us, Zayd ibn Yahya ibn Ubayd al-Khuza'i told us, Sa'id ibn Bashir told us, from Qatadah, who said: God gave Luqman the Wise a choice between prophethood and wisdom, and he chose wisdom over prophethood. He said: Gabriel came to him while he was asleep and sprinkled wisdom upon him, or scattered wisdom upon him. He said: And in the morning he was speaking it.

Sa'id said: I heard it said, from Qatadah, that it was said to Luqman: "How did you choose wisdom over prophethood, when your Lord gave you the choice?" He said: "Had He sent prophethood to me as a binding command, I would have hoped for success in it, and I would have hoped to fulfill it properly. But He gave me the choice, and I feared that I would be too weak for prophethood, so wisdom was more beloved to me."

This is from the narration of Sa'id ibn Bashir, and there is weakness in it that scholars have discussed because of him. God knows best.

What Sa'id ibn Abi Arubah narrated, from Qatadah, concerning His statement, {And We had certainly given Luqman wisdom}, is that it means understanding in the religion, and that he was not a prophet and no revelation was sent to him.

His statement, {And We had certainly given Luqman wisdom}, means understanding, knowledge, and eloquent expression. {That he should be grateful to God}: meaning We commanded him to give thanks to God, Mighty and Majestic, for what God had given him, granted him, and bestowed upon him of favor, with which He singled him out above others of his kind and the people of his time.

Then He, Exalted, said: {And whoever is grateful is grateful only for the benefit of his own soul}, meaning that the benefit of that and its reward returns only to those who are grateful, as He, Exalted, said, {And whoever does righteousness is preparing a way for himself} [al-Rum: 44].

His statement, {And whoever disbelieves, indeed God is Free of need and Praiseworthy}, means He is free of need of His servants, and is not harmed by that even if all the people of the earth were to disbelieve altogether, for He is free of need of anyone besides Him. There is no god but God, and we worship none but Him.

وَقَالَ شُعْبَةُ، عَنِ الْحَكَمِ، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ: كَانَ لُقْمَانُ عَبْدًا صَالِحًا، وَلَمْ يَكُنْ نَبِيًّا.

وَقَالَ الْأَعْمَشُ: قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: كَانَ لُقْمَانُ عَبْدًا أَسْوَدَ عَظِيمَ الشَّفَتَيْنِ، مُشَقَّقَ الْقَدَمَيْنِ.

وَقَالَ حَكَّام بْنُ سَلْم، عَنْ سَعِيدِ الزُّبَيْدِيِّ، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ: كَانَ لُقْمَانُ الْحَكِيمُ عَبْدًا حَبَشِيًّا غَلِيظَ الشَّفَتَيْنِ، مُصَفح الْقَدَمَيْنِ، قَاضِيًا عَلَى بَنِي إِسْرَائِيلَ.

وَذَكَرَ غَيْرُهُ: أَنَّهُ كَانَ قَاضِيًا عَلَى بَنِي إِسْرَائِيلَ فِي زَمَنِ دَاوُدَ، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ حُمَيد، حَدَّثَنَا الْحَكَمُ، حَدَّثَنَا عَمْرُو بْنُ قَيْسٍ قَالَ: كَانَ لُقْمَانُ، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ، عَبْدًا أَسْوَدَ غَلِيظَ الشَّفَتَيْنِ، مُصَفَّح الْقَدَمَيْنِ، فَأَتَاهُ رَجُلٌ وَهُوَ فِي مَجْلِسٍ أُنَاسٍ يُحَدِّثُهُمْ، فَقَالَ لَهُ: أَلَسْتَ الَّذِي كُنْتَ تَرْعَى مَعِي الْغَنَمَ فِي مَكَانِ كَذَا وَكَذَا، قَالَ: نَعَمْ. فَقَالَ: فَمَا بَلَغَ بِكَ مَا أَرَى؟ قَالَ: صِدْقُ الْحَدِيثِ، وَالصَّمْتُ عَمَّا لَا يَعْنِينِي .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو زُرْعَة، حَدَّثَنَا صَفْوَانُ، حَدَّثَنَا الْوَلِيدُ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ يَزِيدَ عَنْ جَابِرٍ قَالَ: إِنَّ اللَّهَ رَفَعَ لُقْمَانَ الْحَكِيمَ بِحِكْمَتِهِ، فَرَآهُ رَجُلٌ كَانَ يَعْرِفُهُ قَبْلَ ذَلِكَ، فَقَالَ لَهُ: أَلَسْتَ عَبْدَ بَنِي فُلَانٍ الَّذِي كُنْتَ تَرْعَى بِالْأَمْسِ؟ قَالَ: بَلَى. قَالَ: فَمَا بَلَغَ بِكَ مَا أَرَى؟ قَالَ: قَدَرُ اللَّهِ، وَأَدَاءُ الْأَمَانَةِ، وَصِدْقُ الْحَدِيثِ، وَتَرْكِي مَا لَا يَعْنِينِي.

فَهَذِهِ الْآثَارُ مِنْهَا مَا هُوَ مُصرَّح فِيهِ بِنَفْيِ كَوْنِهِ نَبِيًّا، وَمِنْهَا مَا هُوَ مُشْعِرٌ بِذَلِكَ؛ لِأَنَّ كَوْنَهُ عَبْدًا قَدْ مَسَّه الرِّقُّ يُنَافِي كَوْنَهُ نَبِيًّا؛ لِأَنَّ الرُّسُلَ كَانَتْ تُبْعَثُ فِي أَحْسَابِ قَوْمِهَا؛ وَلِهَذَا كَانَ جُمْهُورُ السَّلَفِ عَلَى أَنَّهُ لَمْ يَكُنْ نَبِيًّا، وَإِنَّمَا يُنْقَلُ كَوْنُهُ نَبِيًّا عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ -إِنْ صَحَّ السَّنَدُ إِلَيْهِ، فَإِنَّهُ رَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، وَابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ مِنْ حَدِيثِ وَكِيع عَنْ إِسْرَائِيلَ، عَنْ جَابِرٍ، عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ فَقَالَ: كَانَ لُقْمَانُ نَبِيًّا. وَجَابِرٌ هَذَا هُوَ ابْنُ يَزِيدَ الْجُعْفِيُّ، وَهُوَ ضَعِيفٌ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

وَقَالَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ وَهْبٍ: أَخْبَرَنِي عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ عَيَّاشٍ القتْبَاني، عَنْ عُمَر مَوْلَى غُفرَة قَالَ: وَقَفَ رَجُلٌ عَلَى لُقْمَانَ الْحَكِيمَ فَقَالَ: أَنْتَ لُقْمَانُ، أَنْتَ عَبْدُ بَنِي الْحِسْحَاسِ؟ قَالَ: نَعَمْ. قَالَ: أَنْتَ رَاعِي الْغَنَمِ؟ قَالَ: نَعَمْ. قَالَ: أَنْتَ الْأَسْوَدُ؟ قَالَ: أَمَا سَوَادِي فَظَاهِرٌ، فَمَا الَّذِي يُعْجِبُكَ مِنْ أَمْرِي؟ قَالَ: وَطءْ النَّاسِ بسَاطك، وغَشْيُهم بَابَكَ، وَرِضَاهُمْ بِقَوْلِكَ. قَالَ: يَا بْنَ أَخِي إِنْ صَغَيتَ إِلَى مَا أَقُولُ لَكَ كُنْتَ كَذَلِكَ. قَالَ لُقْمَانُ: غَضِّي بَصَرِي، وَكَفِّي لِسَانِي، وَعِفَّةُ طُعْمَتِي، وَحِفْظِي فَرَجِي، وَقَوْلِي بِصِدْقٍ، وَوَفَائِي بِعَهْدِي، وَتَكْرِمَتِي ضَيْفِي، وَحِفْظِي جَارِي، وَتَرْكِي مَا لَا يَعْنِينِي، فَذَاكَ الَّذِي صَيَّرَنِي إِلَى مَا تَرَى.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ نُفَيل، حَدَّثَنَا عَمْرُو بْنُ وَاقِدٍ، عَنْ عَبْدَةَ بْنِ رَبَاح، عَنْ رَبِيعَةَ، عَنْ أَبِي الدَّرْدَاءِ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، أَنَّهُ قَالَ يَوْمًا -وذُكرَ لُقْمَانُ الْحَكِيمُ -فَقَالَ: مَا أُوتِيَ مَا أُوتِيَ عَنْ أَهْلٍ وَلَا مَالٍ، وَلَا حَسَبٍ وَلَا خِصَالٍ، وَلَكِنَّهُ كَانَ رِجْلًا صَمْصَامة سِكِّيتًا، طَوِيلَ التَّفَكُّرِ، عَمِيقَ النَّظَرِ، لَمْ يَنَمْ نَهَارًا قَطُّ، وَلَمْ يَرَهُ أَحَدٌ قَطُّ يَبْزُقُ وَلَا يتنخَّع، وَلَا يَبُولُ وَلَا يَتَغَوَّطُ، وَلَا يَغْتَسِلُ، وَلَا يَعْبَثُ وَلَا يَضْحَكُ، وَكَانَ لَا يُعِيدُ مَنْطِقًا نَطَقَهُ إِلَّا أَنْ يَقُولَ حِكْمَةً يَسْتَعِيدُهَا إِيَّاهُ أَحَدٌ، وَكَانَ قَدْ تَزَوَّجَ وَوُلِدَ لَهُ أَوْلَادٌ، فَمَاتُوا فَلَمْ يَبْكِ عَلَيْهِمْ. وَكَانَ يَغْشَى السُّلْطَانَ، وَيَأْتِي الْحُكَّامَ، لِيَنْظُرَ وَيَتَفَكَّرَ وَيَعْتَبِرَ ،فَبِذَلِكَ أُوتِيَ مَا أُوتِيَ.

وَقَدْ وَرَدَ أَثَرٌ غَرِيبٌ عَنْ قَتَادَةَ، رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، فَقَالَ:

حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا الْعَبَّاسُ بْنُ الْوَلِيدِ، حَدَّثَنَا زَيْدُ بْنُ يَحْيَى بْنِ عُبَيْدٍ الْخُزَاعِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا سَعِيدُ بْنُ بَشِيرٍ، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ قَالَ: خَيّر اللَّهُ لُقْمَانَ الْحَكِيمِ بَيْنَ النُّبُوَّةِ وَالْحِكْمَةِ، فَاخْتَارَ الْحِكْمَةَ عَلَى النُّبُوَّةِ. قَالَ: فَأَتَاهُ جِبْرِيلُ وَهُوَ نَائِمٌ فَذرَّ عَلَيْهِ الْحِكْمَةَ -أَوْ: رَشَّ عَلَيْهِ الْحِكْمَةَ -قَالَ: فَأَصْبَحَ يَنْطِقُ بِهَا.

قَالَ سَعِيدٌ: فَسَمِعْتُ عَنْ قَتَادَةَ يَقُولُ: قِيلَ لِلُقْمَانَ: كَيْفَ اخْتَرْتَ الْحِكْمَةَ عَلَى النُّبُوَّةِ وَقَدْ خَيَّرك رَبُّكَ؟ فَقَالَ: إِنَّهُ لَوْ أَرْسَلَ إليَّ بِالنُّبُوَّةِ عَزْمَة لَرَجَوْتُ فِيهِ الْفَوْزَ مِنْهُ، وَلَكُنْتُ أَرْجُو أَنْ أَقُومَ بِهَا، وَلَكِنَّهُ خَيّرني فَخِفْتُ أَنْ أَضْعُفَ عَنِ النُّبُوَّةِ، فَكَانَتِ الْحِكْمَةُ أَحَبَّ إليَّ.

فَهَذَا مِنْ رِوَايَةِ سَعِيدِ بْنِ بَشِيرٍ، وَفِيهِ ضَعْفٌ قَدْ تَكَلَّمُوا فِيهِ بِسَبَبِهِ، فَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

وَالَّذِي رَوَاهُ سَعِيدُ بْنُ أَبِي عَرُوبة، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ، فِي قَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَلَقَدْ آتَيْنَا لُقْمَانَ الْحِكْمَةَ﴾ أَيِ: الْفِقْهُ فِي الْإِسْلَامِ، وَلَمْ يَكُنْ نَبِيًّا، وَلَمْ يُوحَ إِلَيْهِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَلَقَدْ آتَيْنَا لُقْمَانَ الْحِكْمَةَ﴾ أَيِ: الْفَهْمَ وَالْعِلْمَ وَالتَّعْبِيرَ، ﴿أَنِ اشْكُرْ لِلَّهِ﴾ أَيْ: أَمَرْنَاهُ أَنْ يَشْكُرَ اللَّهَ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، عَلَى مَا أَتَاهُ اللَّهُ وَمَنَحَهُ وَوَهَبَهُ مِنَ الْفَضْلِ، الَّذِي خصَّه بِهِ عَمَّنْ سِوَاهُ مِنْ أَبْنَاءِ جِنْسِهِ وَأَهْلِ زَمَانِهِ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَمَنْ يَشْكُرْ فَإِنَّمَا يَشْكُرُ لِنَفْسِهِ﴾ أَيْ: إِنَّمَا يَعُودُ نَفْعُ ذَلِكَ وَثَوَابُهُ عَلَى الشَّاكِرِينَ لِقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَمَنْ عَمِلَ صَالِحًا فَلأنْفُسِهِمْ يَمْهَدُونَ﴾ [الرُّومِ: ٤٤] .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَمَنْ كَفَرَ فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ غَنِيٌّ حَمِيدٌ﴾ أَيْ: غَنِيٌّ عَنِ الْعِبَادِ، لَا يَتَضَرَّرُ بِذَلِكَ، وَلَوْ كَفَرَ أَهْلُ الْأَرْضِ كُلُّهُمْ جَمِيعًا، فَإِنَّهُ الْغَنِيُّ عَمَّنْ سِوَاهُ؛ فَلَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا الله، ولا نعبد إلا إياه.

## 31:13-15 — verses 13-15 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, informing of Luqman's counsel to his son. He is Luqman ibn Anqa' ibn Sadun, and his son's name was Tharan, according to a report related by al-Suhayli. God, exalted is He, mentioned him with the best of mentions, for He gave him wisdom, and here he is counseling his son, who is the person most tender toward him and most beloved to him, so it is fitting that he should grant him the best of what is known. For this reason he first counseled him to worship God alone and not associate anything with Him, then said, warning him: {Indeed, associating partners with God is a tremendous wrong}, meaning: it is the greatest of wrongs.

Al-Bukhari said: Qutaybah related to us, Jarir related to us, from al-A'mash, from Ibrahim, from Alqamah, from Abdullah, may God be pleased with him, who said: When {Those who believe and do not mix their belief with wrongdoing} [al-An'am: 82] was revealed, this was distressing to the companions of the Messenger of God ﷺ, and they said, "Which of us has not mixed his belief with wrongdoing?" So the Messenger of God ﷺ said, "That is not what is meant. Have you not heard the saying of Luqman: {O my son, do not associate partners with God; indeed, associating partners with Him is a tremendous wrong}?"

Muslim also related it, from the hadith of al-A'mash, with the same chain.

Then He joined, with His counsel that he worship God alone, kindness to one's parents, just as He, exalted is He, said: {And your Lord has decreed that you worship none but Him, and that you show kindness to your parents} [al-Isra: 23]. He often joins these two things together in the Qur'an.

Here He said: {And We have enjoined upon man concerning his parents, his mother carried him in weakness upon weakness}. Mujahid said: this means the hardship of the weakness of childbearing.

Qatadah said: it means exertion upon exertion.

Ata' al-Khurasani said: it means weakness upon weakness.

His saying: {and his weaning is in two years}, meaning: his upbringing and nursing after his birth take place over two years, just as He, exalted is He, said: {Mothers shall nurse their children for two full years, for whoever wishes to complete the nursing period} [al-Baqarah: 233].

From this, Ibn Abbas and other leading scholars deduced that the minimum term of pregnancy is six months, because He, exalted is He, said in another verse: {And his bearing and his weaning are thirty months} [al-Ahqaf: 15].

God, exalted is He, mentions the mother's upbringing, her toil, and her hardship in her wakefulness by night and day, only in order to remind the child of her prior kindness toward him, just as He, exalted is He, said: {And say: My Lord, have mercy upon them both as they raised me when I was small} [al-Isra: 24]. For this reason He said: {Be grateful to Me and to your parents; to Me is the final return}, meaning: I shall reward you for that with the fullest reward.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: Abu Zur'ah related to us, Abdullah ibn Abi Shaybah and Mahmud ibn Ghaylan related to us, they said, Ubaydullah related to us, Isra'il informed us, from Abu Ishaq, from Sa'id ibn Wahb, who said: Mu'adh ibn Jabal came to us, having been sent by the Prophet ﷺ, and he stood up, praised God and extolled Him, then said: "I am the messenger of the Messenger of God ﷺ to you, calling you to worship God and not associate anything with Him, and to obey me, for I will not fall short of good for you, and that the final return is to God, and to Paradise or to the Fire: an abode with no departure, and an eternity with no death."

His saying: {But if they both strive against you to make you associate with Me that of which you have no knowledge, do not obey them}, meaning: if they exert every effort upon you to make you follow them in their religion, do not accept that from them, but let that not prevent you from accompanying them in this world with kindness, meaning: treating them well. {And follow the way of those who turn to Me}, meaning: the believers, {then to Me is your return, and I will inform you of what you used to do}.

Al-Tabarani said, in the Book of Companionship: Abu Abd al-Rahman Abdullah ibn Ahmad ibn Hanbal related to us, Ahmad ibn Ayyub ibn Rashid related to us, Maslamah ibn Alqamah related to us, from Dawud ibn Abi Hind, from Abu Uthman al-Nahdi: that Sa'd ibn Malik said: This verse was revealed concerning me: {But if they both strive against you to make you associate with Me that of which you have no knowledge, do not obey them}, the verse. He said: I was a man devoted to my mother, but when I embraced Islam, she said, "O Sa'd, what is this new thing I see you have done? You will surely leave this religion of yours, or I will not eat or drink until I die, and you will be disgraced because of me, and it will be said, 'O killer of his mother.'" I said, "Do not do that, mother, for I will not abandon this religion of mine for anything." So she remained a day and a night without eating, and in the morning she was exhausted. Then she remained another day and another night without eating, and in the morning her exhaustion had intensified. When I saw that, I said, "O mother, know, by God, that if you had a hundred souls and they departed one by one, I would not leave this religion of mine for anything. So if you wish, eat, and if you wish, do not eat." So she ate.

## 31:16-19 — verses 16-19 (shared commentary)

### Translation

These are beneficial counsels which God Most High has related from Luqman the Wise, so that people might follow them and take them as examples. He said: {O my son, if it should be the weight of a grain of mustard seed} that is, if the wrong committed or the sin were the weight of a grain of mustard seed. Some have allowed that the pronoun in the phrase {innaha (indeed it)} is the pronoun of circumstance and narrative (damir al-sha'n wa'l-qissa), and on this basis they have permitted the nominative reading of {mithqal}, but the first view is preferable.

As for His saying, {God will bring it forth}, that is, God will produce it on the Day of Resurrection when He sets up the scales of justice, and He will recompense for it: if good, then good; if evil, then evil. As He Most High said: {And We shall set up the scales of justice for the Day of Resurrection, so that no soul will be wronged in anything. Even if it be the weight of a grain of mustard seed, We will bring it forth. And sufficient are We as reckoners} [al-Anbiya: 47]. And He Most High said: {So whoever does an atom's weight of good will see it, and whoever does an atom's weight of evil will see it} [al-Zalzalah: 7-8]. Even if that atom were locked away and hidden inside a solid, impenetrable rock, or vanished and lost somewhere in the reaches of the heavens or the earth, God will bring it forth, for nothing hidden is hidden from Him, and not even an atom's weight in the heavens or on the earth escapes Him. Hence He said: {Indeed, God is Latif (subtle in perception), Khabir (fully aware)}, that is, subtle in knowledge, so that nothing, however fine, subtle, or minute, is hidden from Him, {Khabir}, fully aware even of the creeping of an ant on a pitch black night.

Some have claimed that what is meant by His saying, {and it be in a rock}, is a rock beneath the seven earths. Al-Suddi mentioned this with his well-traveled chain of transmission (matruq) going back to Ibn Abbas, Ibn Mas'ud, and a group of the Companions, if that is indeed authentic. It is also related from Atiyyah al-Awfi, Abu Malik, al-Thawri, al-Minhal ibn Amr, and others. This, and God knows best, appears to be something taken from the Isra'iliyyat (Israelite narrations), which are neither confirmed as true nor rejected as false. What appears most likely, and God knows best, is that what is meant is that this grain, small as it is, even if it were inside a rock, God would bring it out and reveal it through His subtle knowledge, as Imam Ahmad related:

[chain] Abu Sa'id al-Khudri, may God be pleased with him, from the Messenger of God ﷺ, who said: "Even if one of you were to do a deed inside a solid rock that has no door and no opening, his deed would come out to people, whatever it may be."

Then He said: {O my son, establish prayer}, that is, with its limits, its obligatory elements, and its appointed times, {and enjoin what is right and forbid what is wrong}, that is, according to your capacity and effort, {and be patient over what befalls you}. He knew that one who enjoins what is right and forbids what is wrong is bound to receive harm from people, so he commanded him to be patient.

As for His saying, {Indeed that is among the matters requiring firm resolve}, that is, patience in the face of harm from people is indeed among the matters requiring firm resolve.

As for His saying, {And do not turn your cheek away from people}, He is saying: do not turn your face away from people when you speak to them or they speak to you, out of contempt for them and arrogance toward them, but rather be gentle and open your face to them, as has come in the hadith: "Even if it be that you meet your brother with your face open toward him. And beware of letting your garment drag below the ankles, for that is from vanity (al-mikhilah), and vanity is something God does not love."

Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, on the authority of Ibn Abbas, regarding His saying {And do not turn your cheek away from people}: He is saying, do not be arrogant so as to belittle the servants of God, and do not turn your face away from them when they speak to you. Al-Awfi and Ikrimah related the same from him.

Malik said, on the authority of Zayd ibn Aslam, regarding {And do not turn your cheek away from people}: do not speak while turning away. The same has been related from Mujahid, Ikrimah, Yazid ibn al-Asamm, Abu al-Jawza', Sa'id ibn Jubayr, al-Dahhak, Ibn Yazid, and others.

Ibrahim al-Nakha'i said this means twisting the mouth while speaking.

The correct view is the first one.

Ibn Jarir said: the origin of al-sa'ar is a disease that afflicts camels in their necks or heads, so that their necks are twisted away from their heads. The arrogant man is likened to this. This is the source of the saying of Amr ibn Hunayy al-Taghlibi:

"And when the tyrant turned his cheek in arrogance, we set him straight from his crookedness, and he stood upright."

And Abu Talib said in his poetry:

"And we, of old, did not let wrongdoing stand; when they bent their proud heads, we set them right."

As for His saying, {And do not walk upon the earth exultantly}, that is, joyfully arrogant, tyrannical, and stubbornly defiant. Do not do that, for God will detest you for it. Hence He said: {Indeed, God does not love every self-admiring boaster}, that is, one who is conceited and pleased with himself, and a boaster, that is, over others. And He Most High said: {And do not walk upon the earth exultantly; indeed you will never tear the earth apart, nor will you ever reach the mountains in height} [al-Isra: 37]. This has already been discussed in its place.

Al-Hafiz Abu al-Qasim al-Tabarani said: [chain] Thabit ibn Qays ibn Shammas said: Arrogance was mentioned in the presence of the Messenger of God ﷺ, and he spoke sternly against it, saying: "Indeed, God does not love every self-admiring boaster." A man among the people said: By God, O Messenger of God, I wash my clothes and I am pleased by their whiteness, and I am pleased by the strap of my sandal and the strap of my whip. He said: "That is not arrogance. Arrogance is to belittle the truth and to hold people in contempt."

He also related it through another chain with similar wording, and it contains a long account, including the killing of Thabit and his testament after his death.

As for His saying, {And be moderate in your walk}, that is, walk with moderation, neither slow and sluggish nor fast and excessive, but justly balanced, in between the two extremes.

As for His saying, {And lower your voice}, that is, do not be excessive in speech, and do not raise your voice in that which serves no benefit. Hence He Most High said: {Indeed, the most repulsive of voices is the braying of donkeys}. Mujahid and others said: the ugliest of voices is the braying of donkeys, meaning that the utmost that one who raises his voice can be likened to is the donkey in its loudness and raising of voice, and despite this it is detestable to God Most High. This likening to donkeys in this matter necessitates that it be forbidden and blameworthy in the extreme, for the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "There is no evil example for us. One who takes back his gift is like a dog that vomits and then returns to its vomit."

Al-Nasa'i said in his commentary on this verse: [chain] Abu Hurayrah, from the Prophet ﷺ, that he said: "When you hear the crowing of roosters, ask God from His bounty, and when you hear the braying of donkeys, seek refuge in God from Satan, for it has seen a devil."

The rest of the group besides Ibn Majah have transmitted this through various routes from Ja'far ibn Rabi'ah with the same content, and in some wordings it says "at night," and God knows best.

These, then, are exceedingly beneficial counsels, and they are among the accounts of the great Qur'an concerning Luqman the Wise. Many things have been related from him in the way of wisdom sayings and admonitions, so let us mention a sample and a guiding model of these.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Ibn Umar, may God be pleased with him, said: The Messenger of God ﷺ informed us, saying: "Indeed Luqman the Wise used to say: If God is entrusted with something, He preserves it."

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] from Abu Musa al-Ash'ari, that the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Luqman said to his son, while admonishing him: O my son, beware of covering your face, for it is a cause of fear at night and of blame in the day."

He also said: [chain] al-Sari ibn Yahya said: Luqman said to his son: O my son, wisdom has seated the poor in the seats of kings.

He also said: [chain] Awn ibn Abdullah said: Luqman said to his son: O my son, when you come to the gathering place of a people, greet them with the arrow of Islam, meaning the greeting of peace, then sit at their edge, and do not speak until you see that they have spoken. If they engage in the remembrance of God, then let your arrow join theirs, but if they engage in something else, then move away from them to others.

Our father narrated to us, saying: Amr ibn Uthman ibn Sa'id ibn Kathir ibn Dinar narrated to us, Damrah narrated to us, from Hafs ibn Umar, may Allah be pleased with him, who said: Luqman placed a sack of mustard seeds beside him, and he would counsel his son with an admonition and take out one mustard seed, until the mustard seeds were exhausted. Then he said: My son, I have counseled you with an admonition that if it were counseled to a mountain, it would split apart. He said: So his son was overcome and split apart.

Abu al-Qasim al-Tabarani said: Yahya ibn Abd al-Baqi al-Missisi narrated to us, Ahmad ibn Abd al-Rahman al-Harrani narrated to us, Uthman ibn Abd al-Rahman al-Tara'ifi narrated to us, Abyan ibn Sufyan al-Maqdisi narrated to us, from Khalifah ibn Salam, from Ata' ibn Abi Rabah, from Ibn Abbas, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Take the Sudanese, for three among them are among the masters of the people of Paradise: Luqman the Wise, the Negus, and Bilal the caller to prayer."

Abu al-Qasim al-Tabarani said: He meant the Abyssinians.

A section on obscurity and humility: this is connected to the counsel of Luqman (peace be upon him) to his son. The Hafiz Abu Bakr ibn Abi al-Dunya compiled a separate book on this subject, and we shall mention its main points from it. He said: Ibrahim ibn al-Mundhir narrated to us, Abd Allah ibn Musa al-Madani narrated to us, from Usamah ibn Zayd, from Hafs ibn Ubayd Allah ibn Anas ibn Malik: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "How many a disheveled man with two worn garments, turned away from people's doors, yet if he swore an oath by Allah, Allah would fulfill it for him."

Then he related it from the hadith of Ja'far ibn Sulayman, from Thabit and Ali ibn Zayd, from Anas, from the Prophet ﷺ, mentioning it, with an addition: "among them was al-Bara' ibn Malik."

[It is also related from Anas, may Allah be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Glad tidings for the pious, the wealthy, who when present are not recognized, and when absent are not missed. Those are lamps, cleared of every dusty, disfiguring trial."]

Abu Bakr ibn Sahl al-Tamimi said: Ibn Abi Maryam narrated to us, Nafi' ibn Yazid narrated to us, from Ayyash ibn Abbas, from Isa ibn Abd al-Rahman, from Zayd ibn Aslam, from his father, from Umar, may Allah be pleased with him, that he entered the mosque and found Mu'adh ibn Jabal weeping at the grave of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. He said to him: What makes you weep, O Mu'adh? He said: A hadith I heard you narrate from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. I heard him say: "A small amount of showing off is polytheism, and Allah loves the pious, the hidden, the wealthy, those who when absent are not missed, and when present are not recognized. Their hearts are lamps of guidance; they are saved from every dark, dusty trial."

Al-Walid ibn Shuja' narrated to us, Aththam ibn Ali narrated to us, from Humayd ibn Ata' al-A'raj, from Abd Allah ibn al-Harith, from Abd Allah ibn Mas'ud, may Allah be pleased with him, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "How many a man with two worn garments, given no regard, yet if he swore an oath by Allah, Allah would fulfill it for him. If he said, 'O Allah, I ask You for Paradise,' He would grant him Paradise, and yet would not give him anything of this world."

He also said: Ishaq ibn Ibrahim narrated to us, Abu Mu'awiyah narrated to us, from al-A'mash, from Salim ibn Abi al-Ja'd, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Among my nation is one who, if he came to the door of one of you asking for a dinar, a dirham, or even a small coin, would not be given it, yet if he asked Allah for Paradise, He would give it to him. And if he asked Him for this world, He would not give it to him, not withholding it from him out of contempt for him: a man with two worn garments, given no regard, yet if he swore an oath by Allah, Allah would fulfill it for him."

This is mursal (a report missing its Companion link) from this route.

He also said: Ishaq ibn Ibrahim narrated to us, Ja'far ibn Sulayman informed us, Awf narrated to us, saying: Abu Hurayrah said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Among the kings of Paradise is one who is disheveled, dusty, wearing two worn garments, given no regard: those who, when they seek permission to see rulers, are not admitted, and when they propose marriage to women, are not wed, and when they speak, are not listened to. The needs of one of them churn within his chest. If his light were divided among the people on the Day of Resurrection, it would suffice them all."

He said: Umar ibn Shabbah recited to me, from Ibn A'ishah, who said: Abd Allah ibn al-Mubarak said:

Indeed, how many a man with two worn garments will tomorrow have a dwelling
whose carpets are spread out and whose cushions are laid,
whose rivers flow steadily around his palace,
and which shines, its gardens intertwined around it.

It is also related from the hadith of Ubayd Allah ibn Zahr, from Ali ibn Zayd, from al-Qasim, from Abu Umamah, as a marfu' (elevated) report: "Allah said: Among the most enviable of My friends in My sight is a believer of light means, possessed of a portion of prayer, who has excelled in worshipping his Lord and obeyed Him in secret, and who was obscure among people, not pointed at with fingers, if he bears that with patience." He said: Then the Messenger of Allah snapped his fingers and said: "His death comes swiftly, his inheritance is little, and few weep for him."

And from Abd Allah ibn Amr, who said: The most beloved of Allah's servants to Allah are the strangers. It was said: Who are the strangers? He said: Those who flee with their religion; they will be gathered on the Day of Resurrection with Isa ibn Maryam (peace be upon him).

Al-Fudayl ibn Iyad said: It has reached me that Allah, exalted is He, will say to the servant on the Day of Resurrection: "Did I not bless you? Did I not give you? Did I not conceal you? Did I not...? Did I not...? Did I not keep your name obscure?" Then al-Fudayl said: If you can manage not to be known, then do so. What harm is there to you if you are not praised, and what harm is there to you if you are blamed by people while being praised by Allah?

Ibn Muhayriz used to say: O Allah, I ask You for an obscure remembrance.

Al-Khalil ibn Ahmad used to say: O Allah, make me, in Your sight, among the highest of Your creation, and make me, in my own eyes, among the lowest of Your creation, and in the eyes of people, among the most ordinary of Your creation.

Then he said: Chapter on what has come regarding notoriety (shuhrah)

Ahmad ibn Isa al-Misri narrated to us, Ibn Wahb narrated to us, from Umar ibn al-Harith and Ibn Lahi'ah, from Yazid ibn Abi Habib, from Sinan ibn Sa'd, from Anas, from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, that he said: "It is sufficient evil for a man, except one whom Allah protects, that people point their fingers at him regarding his religion and his worldly affairs. Indeed, Allah does not look at your outward forms, but at your hearts and your deeds."

A similar report is related from Ishaq ibn al-Buhlul, from Ibn Abi Fudayk, from Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahid al-Akhnasi, from Abd al-Wahid ibn Abi Kathir, from Jabir ibn Abd Allah, as a marfu' report, similarly.

A similar report is related from al-Hasan as a mursal report. It was said to al-Hasan: But people point their fingers at you! He said: What is meant is only one who is pointed at for innovation in his religion and for immorality in his worldly life.

And from Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, who said: Do not begin seeking notoriety, and do not elevate yourself in order to be mentioned. Learn and conceal, and remain silent and you will be safe; you will please the righteous and enrage the wicked.

Ibrahim ibn Adham, may Allah have mercy on him, said: He who loves notoriety has not been true to Allah.

Ayyub said: A servant has not been truthful with Allah unless he is pleased that his whereabouts are not known.

Muhammad ibn al-Ala' said: Whoever loves Allah loves that people not know him.

Simak ibn Salamah said: Beware of having too many close friends.

Aban ibn Uthman said: If you wish your religion to remain safe for you, then keep your acquaintances few. Abu al-Aliyah, whenever more than three people sat with him, would rise and leave them.

He said: Ali ibn al-Ja'd narrated to us, Shu'bah informed us, from Awf, from Abu Raja', who said: Talhah saw people walking with him, and he said: Flies of greed, and moths of the Fire.

Ibn Idris said, from Harun ibn Antarah, from Sulaym ibn Hanzalah, who said: While we were around Ubayy, Umar ibn al-Khattab struck him with the whip and said: It is a humiliation for the follower and a trial for the one followed.

Ibn Awn said, from al-Hasan: Ibn Mas'ud went out and some people followed him, so he said: By Allah, if you knew what I shut behind my door, not even two men among you would follow me.

Hammad ibn Zayd said: Whenever we passed by a gathering, with Ayyub among us, and he greeted them, they would return the greeting with great enthusiasm, and that would distress him.

Abd al-Razzaq said, from Ma'mar: Ayyub used to lengthen his shirt, and he was asked about that, so he said: In the past, notoriety lay in lengthening the shirt, but today it lies in shortening it. He once had two sandals made after the pattern of the sandals of the Prophet ﷺ, and he wore them for some days, then took them off, saying: I did not see people wearing them.

Ibrahim al-Nakha'i said: Do not wear clothing that would make you notorious among jurists, nor that which would cause the foolish to hold you in contempt.

Al-Thawri said: They used to dislike fine garments by which one becomes conspicuous and toward which people raise their eyes to look at him, and also dislike shabby garments in which one is held in contempt and one's religion is disparaged.

Khalid ibn Khidash reported to us: Hammad reported to us, from Abu Hasanah, companion of al-Ziyadi, who said: We were with Abu Qilabah when a man wearing coarse cloaks entered upon him, so he said: Beware of this braying donkey.

Al-Hasan, may God have mercy on him, said: Some people place pride in their hearts and humility in their garments, so the wearer of the cloak is more amazed by his cloak than the wearer of the fine striped garment is by his fine garment. What is the matter with them, may they be bereaved of one another.

In one report it is mentioned that Musa (peace be upon him) said to the Children of Israel: What is the matter with you, that you come to me wearing the garments of monks while your hearts are the hearts of wolves? Wear the garments of kings, and soften your hearts with fear of God.

Section on good character

Abu al-Tayyah said, from Anas, may God be pleased with him: The Messenger of God ﷺ was among the people of best character.

From Ata, from Ibn Umar: It was said, O Messenger of God, which of the believers is most excellent? He said: "The one best in character."

From Nuh ibn Abbad, from Thabit, from Anas, as a marfu (elevated report): "Indeed the servant reaches, through his good character, the ranks of the Hereafter and the nobility of stations, even though he is weak in worship. And indeed he reaches, through his bad character, the depths of Hell, even though he is a worshipper." From Sinan ibn Harun, from Humayd, from Anas, marfu: "Good character has taken away the good of this world and the next." From Aishah, marfu: "Indeed the servant reaches, through his good character, the rank of one who prays at night and fasts during the day."

Ibn Abi al-Dunya said: Abu Muslim Abd al-Rahman ibn Yunus reported to me: Abdullah ibn Idris reported to us: my father and my uncle informed me, from my grandfather, from Abu Hurayrah, may God be pleased with him: The Messenger of God ﷺ was asked about what most brings people into Paradise, and he said: "Fear of God and good character." And he was asked about what most brings people into the Fire, and he said: "The two hollow ones: the mouth and the private parts."

Usamah ibn Sharik said: I was with the Messenger of God ﷺ, and the Bedouins came to him from every direction, saying: O Messenger of God, what is the best thing given to a person? He said: "Good character."

Ya'la ibn Mamlak said, from Umm al-Darda, from Abu al-Darda, attributing it: "There is nothing heavier in the balance than good character." And likewise it was narrated by Ata, from Umm al-Darda, with the same wording.

From Masruq, from Abdullah ibn Amr, marfu: "Indeed among the best of you are those best in character."

Abdullah ibn Abi Badr reported to us: Muhammad ibn Ubayd reported to us, from Muhammad ibn Abi Sarah, from al-Hasan ibn Ali, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Indeed God gives the servant reward for good character just as He gives the one who strives in the way of God, the reward comes to him morning and evening."

From Makhul, from Abu Tha'labah, marfu: "Indeed the most beloved of you to me and the closest of you to me in sitting are those best in character, and the most hateful of you to me and the farthest of you from me in station in Paradise are those equal to you in character, the excessively talkative, the ones who twist their mouths in speech, and the ones who speak affectedly and pompously."

From Abu Uways, from Muhammad ibn al-Munkadir, from Jabir, marfu: "Shall I not inform you of the most complete of you in faith? Those best in character, the ones easy and gentle in disposition, those who form bonds of affection and are befriended."

Al-Layth said, from Yazid ibn Abdullah ibn Usamah, from Bakr ibn Abi al-Furat, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "God has not made a man's outward form and character beautiful only to feed him to the Fire."

From Abdullah ibn Ghalib al-Huddani, from Abu Sa'id, marfu: "Two traits do not combine in a believer: miserliness and bad character." And Maymun ibn Mihran said, from the Messenger of God ﷺ: "There is no sin greater with God than bad character, for the one who has it does not emerge from one sin except that he falls into another."

Ali ibn al-Ja'd reported to us: Abu al-Mughirah al-Ahmasi reported to us: Abd al-Rahman ibn Ishaq reported to us, from a man of Quraysh, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "There is no sin greater with God than bad character. Indeed good character melts sins away just as the sun melts ice, and indeed bad character corrupts deeds just as vinegar corrupts honey."

Abdullah ibn Idris said, from his father, from his grandfather, from Abu Hurayrah, marfu: "Indeed you cannot encompass people with your wealth, but you can encompass them with a cheerful face and good character."

Muhammad ibn Sirin said: Good character is an aid to religion.

Section on the condemnation of pride

Alqamah said, from Ibn Mas'ud, raising it: "No one in whose heart is the weight of a mustard seed of pride will enter Paradise, and no one in whose heart is the weight of a mustard seed of faith will enter the Fire."

Ibrahim ibn Abi Ablah said, from Abu Salamah, from Abdullah ibn Amr, marfu: "Whoever has in his heart the weight of an atom of pride, God will cast him down upon his face into the Fire."

Ishaq ibn Isma'il reported to us: Abu Mu'awiyah reported to us, from Umar ibn Rashid, from Iyas ibn Salamah, from his father, marfu: "A man continues to exalt himself until he is recorded with God among the tyrants, and what befell them of punishment befalls him."

Malik ibn Dinar said: Sulayman ibn Dawud (peace be upon them both) once rode the carpet with two hundred thousand of the jinn and two hundred thousand of mankind, and it was raised until he heard the glorification of the angels in the heaven, then they lowered it until his foot touched the water of the sea, and they heard a voice saying: If there were in the heart of your companion the weight of an atom of pride, he would be swallowed into the earth farther than he was raised.

Abu Khaythamah reported to us: Yazid ibn Harun reported to us, from Hammad ibn Salamah, from Thabit, from Anas, who said: Abu Bakr used to preach to us and mention the beginning of the creation of man, until one of us would feel disgust at himself, as he would say: He came out from the passage of urine twice.

Al-Sha'bi said: Whoever kills two people is a tyrant, then he recited: {Do you intend to kill me as you killed a soul yesterday? You only intend to be a tyrant in the land} [al-Qasas: 19]. And al-Hasan said: How strange is the son of Adam, he washes filth with his own hand twice a day, then he grows proud, setting himself against the Tyrant of the heavens. He said: Khalid ibn Khidash reported to us: Hammad ibn Zayd reported to us, from Ali ibn al-Hasan, from al-Dahhak ibn Sufyan, and he mentioned the hadith. He struck an example of this world by what comes out of the son of Adam.

Al-Hasan said, from Yahya, from Ubayy, who said: Indeed the food of the son of Adam is an example struck for this world, even if it is seasoned and salted.

Muhammad ibn al-Husayn ibn Ali, of the descendants of Ali, may God be pleased with him, said: Nothing of pride enters a man's heart except that it diminishes his intellect by that same measure.

Yunus ibn Ubayd said: There is no pride together with prostration, and no hypocrisy together with the profession of God's oneness.

Tawus looked at Umar ibn Abd al-Aziz walking with a swaggering gait, and this was before he became caliph, so Tawus poked him in the side with his finger and said: Is this fitting for one who has filth in his belly? He said to him, as if excusing himself: O uncle, every limb of mine was struck until I learned this manner of walking.

Abu Bakr ibn Abi al-Dunya said: The Umayyads used to strike their children until they learned this manner of walking.

Section on conceited strutting

From Abu Layla, from Ibn Buraydah, from his father, marfu: "Whoever drags his garment out of pride, God will not look at him."

And it was narrated from Ishaq ibn Isma'il, from Sufyan, from Zayd ibn Aslam, from Ibn Umar, marfu, likewise. Muhammad ibn Bakkar reported to us: Abd al-Rahman ibn Abi al-Zinad reported to us, from his father, from al-A'raj, from Abu Hurayrah, marfu: "God will not look on the Day of Resurrection at one who drags his lower garment." And: "While a man was strutting in his two garments, pleased with himself, God caused the earth to swallow him, and he continues sinking into it until the Day of Resurrection."

Al-Zuhri narrated from Salim, from his father: "While a man..." to the end of it.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿يَأْتِ بِهَا اللَّهُ﴾ أَيْ: أَحْضَرَهَا اللَّهُ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ حِينَ يَضَعُ الْمَوَازِينَ الْقِسْطَ، وَجَازَى عَلَيْهَا إِنْ خَيْرًا فَخَيْرٌ، وَإِنْ شَرًّا فَشَرٌّ. كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَنَضَعُ الْمَوَازِينَ الْقِسْطَ لِيَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ فَلا تُظْلَمُ نَفْسٌ شَيْئًا وَإِنْ كَانَ مِثْقَالَ حَبَّةٍ مِنْ خَرْدَلٍ أَتَيْنَا بِهَا وَكَفَى بِنَا حَاسِبِينَ﴾ [الْأَنْبِيَاءِ: ٤٧] ، وَقَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَمَنْ يَعْمَلْ مِثْقَالَ ذَرَّةٍ خَيْرًا يَرَهُ * وَمَنْ يَعْمَلْ مِثْقَالَ ذَرَّةٍ شَرًّا يَرَهُ﴾ [الزَّلْزَلَةِ:٧، ٨] ، وَلَوْ كَانَتْ تِلْكَ الذَّرَّةُ مُحَصَّنَةً مُحَجَّبَةً فِي دَاخِلِ صَخْرَةٍ صَمَّاء، أَوْ غَائِبَةٍ ذَاهِبَةٍ فِي أرجاء السموات أَوِ الْأَرْضِ فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ يَأْتِي بِهَا؛ لِأَنَّهُ لَا تَخْفَى عَلَيْهِ خَافِيَةٌ، وَلَا يَعْزُبُ عَنْهُ مِثْقَالُ ذَرَّةٍ في السموات وَلَا فِي الْأَرْضِ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَطِيفٌ خَبِيرٌ﴾ أَيْ: لِطَيْفُ الْعَلَمِ، فَلَا تَخْفَى عَلَيْهِ الْأَشْيَاءُ وَإِنْ دَقت وَلَطُفَتْ وَتَضَاءَلَتْ ﴿خَبِيرٌ﴾ بِدَبِيبِ النَّمْلِ فِي اللَّيْلِ الْبَهِيمِ.

وَقَدْ زَعَمَ بَعْضُهُمْ أَنَّ الْمُرَادَ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿فَتَكُنْ فِي صَخْرَةٍ﴾ أَنَّهَا صَخْرَةٌ تَحْتَ الْأَرْضِينَ السَّبْعِ، ذَكَرَهُ السُّدِّي بِإِسْنَادِهِ ذَلِكَ الْمَطْرُوقِ عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ وَابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ وَجَمَاعَةٍ مِنَ الصَّحَابَةِ إِنْ صَحَّ ذَلِكَ، وَيُرْوَى هَذَا عَنْ عَطِيَّةَ الْعَوْفِيِّ، وَأَبِي مَالِكٍ، وَالثَّوْرِيِّ، والمِنهال بْنِ عَمْرٍو، وَغَيْرِهِمْ. وَهَذَا وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ، كَأَنَّهُ مُتَلَقًّى مِنَ الْإِسْرَائِيلِيَّاتِ الَّتِي لَا تُصَدَّقُ، وَلَا تَكْذَّبُ، وَالظَّاهِرُ -وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ -أَنَّ الْمُرَادَ: أَنَّ هَذِهِ الْحَبَّةَ فِي حَقَارَتِهَا لَوْ كَانَتْ دَاخِلَ صَخْرَةٍ، فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ سَيُبْدِيهَا وَيُظْهِرُهَا بِلَطِيفِ عِلْمِهِ، كَمَا قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ:

حَدَّثَنَا حَسَنُ بْنُ مُوسَى، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ لَهِيعة، حَدَّثَنَا دَراج، عَنْ أَبِي الْهَيْثَمِ، عَنْ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ الْخُدْرِيِّ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، عَنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: " لَوْ أَنَّ أَحَدَكُمْ يَعْمَلُ فِي صَخْرَةٍ صَمَّاء، لَيْسَ لَهَا بَابٌ وَلَا كوَّة، لَخَرَجَ عَمَلُهُ لِلنَّاسِ كَائِنًا مَا كَانَ" .

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿يَا بُنَيَّ أَقِمِ الصَّلاةَ﴾ أَيْ: بِحُدُودِهَا وَفُرُوضِهَا وَأَوْقَاتِهَا، ﴿وَأْمُرْ بِالْمَعْرُوفِ وَانْهَ عَنِ الْمُنْكَرِ﴾ أَيْ: بِحَسَبِ طَاقَتِكِ وَجُهْدِكَ، ﴿وَاصْبِرْ عَلَى مَا أَصَابَكَ﴾ ،عَلِمَ أَنَّ الْآمِرَ بِالْمَعْرُوفِ وَالنَّاهِيَ عَنِ الْمُنْكَرِ، لَا بُدَّ أَنْ يَنَالَهُ مِنَ النَّاسِ أَذًى، فَأَمَرَهُ بِالصَّبْرِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنَّ ذَلِكَ مِنْ عَزْمِ الأمُورِ﴾ أَيْ: إِنَّ الصَّبْرَ عَلَى أَذَى النَّاسِ لَمِنْ عَزَمِ الْأُمُورِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَلا تُصَعِّرْ خَدَّكَ لِلنَّاسِ﴾ يَقُولُ: لَا تُعرِضْ بِوَجْهِكَ عَنِ النَّاسِ إِذَا كَلَّمْتَهُمْ أَوْ كَلَّمُوكَ، احْتِقَارًا مِنْكَ لَهُمْ، وَاسْتِكْبَارًا عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَكِنْ ألِنْ جَانِبَكَ، وَابْسُطْ وَجْهَكَ إِلَيْهِمْ، كَمَا جَاءَ فِي الْحَدِيثِ: "وَلَوْ أَنْ تَلْقَى أَخَاكَ وَوَجْهُكَ إِلَيْهِ مُنْبَسِط، وَإِيَّاكَ وَإِسْبَالَ الْإِزَارِ فَإِنَّهَا مِنَ المِخيلَة، وَالْمَخِيلَةُ لَا يُحِبُّهَا اللَّهَ".

قَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَلا تُصَعِّرْ خَدَّكَ لِلنَّاسِ﴾ يَقُولُ: لَا تَتَكَبَّرُ فتحقِرَ عبادَ اللَّهِ، وَتُعْرِضَ عَنْهُمْ بِوَجْهِكَ إِذَا كَلَّمُوكَ. وَكَذَا رَوَى الْعَوْفِيُّ وَعِكْرِمَةُ عَنْهُ.

وَقَالَ مَالِكٌ، عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ: ﴿وَلا تُصَعِّرْ خَدَّكَ لِلنَّاسِ﴾ : لَا تكَلَّم وَأَنْتَ مُعْرِضٌ. وَكَذَا رُوي عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ، وعِكْرِمة، وَيَزِيدَ بْنِ الْأَصَمِّ، وَأَبِي الْجَوْزَاءِ، وَسَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْر، وَالضَّحَّاكِ، وَابْنِ يَزِيدَ، وَغَيْرِهِمْ.

وَقَالَ إِبْرَاهِيمُ النَّخعِي: يَعْنِي بذلك: التشديق في الكلام.

وَالصَّوَابُ الْقَوْلُ الْأَوَّلُ.

قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: وَأَصْلُ الصَّعَر: دَاءٌ يَأْخُذُ الْإِبِلَ فِي أَعْنَاقِهَا أَوْ رُؤُوسِهَا، حَتَّى تُلفَتَ أعناقُها عَنْ رُؤُوسِهَا، فَشُبِّهَ بِهِ الرَّجُلُ الْمُتَكَبِّرُ، وَمِنْهُ قَوْلُ عَمْرِو بْنِ حُني التَّغْلبي:

وَكُنَّا إذَا الجَبَّارُ صَعّر خَدّه ... أقَمْنَا لَه مِنْ مَيْلِه فَتَقَوّمَا 

وَقَالَ أَبُو طَالِبٍ فِي شِعْرِهِ:

وَكُنَّا قَديمًا لَا نقرُّ ظُلامَة ... إِذَا مَا ثَنوا صُعْر الرُّؤُوسِ نُقِيمها 

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَلا تَمْشِ فِي الأرْضِ مَرَحًا﴾ أَيْ: جَذْلًا مُتَكَبِّرًا جَبَّارًا عَنِيدًا، لَا تَفْعَلُ ذَلِكَ يُبْغِضُكَ اللَّهُ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يُحِبُّ كُلَّ مُخْتَالٍ فَخُورٍ﴾ أَيْ: مُخْتَالٍ مُعْجَبٍ فِي نَفْسِهِ، فَخُورٌ: أَيْ عَلَى غَيْرِهِ، وَقَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَلا تَمْشِ فِي الأرْضِ مَرَحًا إِنَّكَ لَنْ تَخْرِقَ الأرْضَ وَلَنْ تَبْلُغَ الْجِبَالَ طُولا﴾ [الْإِسْرَاءِ: ٣٧] ، وَقَدْ تَقَدَّمَ الْكَلَامُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ فِي مَوْضِعِهِ.

وَقَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو الْقَاسِمِ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الْحَضْرَمِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عِمْرَانَ بْنِ أَبِي لَيْلَى، حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، عَنِ ابْنِ أَبِي لَيْلَى، عَنْ عِيسَى، عَنْ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ أَبِي لَيْلَى عَنْ ثَابِتِ بْنِ قَيْسِ بْنِ شَمَّاس قَالَ: ذُكِرَ الْكِبْرُ عِنْدَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَشَدَّدَ فِيهِ، فَقَالَ: "إِنِ اللَّهَ لَا يُحِبُّ كُلَّ مُخْتَالٍ فَخُورٍ". فَقَالَ رَجُلٌ مِنَ الْقَوْمِ: وَاللَّهِ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ إِنِّي لِأَغْسِلُ ثِيَابِي فَيُعْجِبُنِي بَيَاضُهَا، وَيُعْجِبُنِي شِراك نَعْلِي، وعِلاقة سَوْطي، فَقَالَ: "لَيْسَ ذَلِكَ الْكِبْرُ، إِنَّمَا الْكِبْرُ أَنْ تَسْفه الْحَقَّ وتَغْمِط النَّاسَ" .

وَرَوَاهُ مِنْ طَرِيقٍ أُخْرَى بِمِثْلِهِ، وَفِيهِ قِصَّةٌ طَوِيلَةٌ، وَمَقْتَلُ ثَابِتٍ وَوَصِيَّتُهُ بَعْدَ مَوْتِهِ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَاقْصِدْ فِي مَشْيِكَ﴾ أَيِ: امْشِ مَشْيًا مُقْتَصِدًا لَيْسَ بِالْبَطِيءِ الْمُتَثَبِّطِ، وَلَا بِالسَّرِيعِ الْمُفْرِطِ، بَلْ عَدْلًا وَسَطًا بَيْنَ بَيْنَ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَاغْضُضْ مِنْ صَوْتِكَ﴾ أَيْ: لَا تُبَالِغْ فِي الْكَلَامِ، وَلَا تَرْفَعْ صَوْتَكَ فِيمَا لَا فَائِدَةَ فِيهِ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِنَّ أَنْكَرَ الأصْوَاتِ لَصَوْتُ الْحَمِيرِ﴾ قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ: إِنَّ أَقْبَحَ الْأَصْوَاتِ لَصَوْتُ الْحَمِيرِ، أَيْ: غَايَةُ مَنْ رَفَعَ صَوْتَهُ أَنَّهُ يُشَبه بِالْحَمِيرِ فِي عُلُوِّهِ وَرَفْعِهِ، وَمَعَ هَذَا هُوَ بَغِيضٌ إِلَى اللَّهِ تَعَالَى. وَهَذَا التَّشْبِيهُ فِي هَذَا بِالْحَمِيرِ يَقْتَضِي تَحْرِيمَهُ وَذَمَّهُ غَايَةَ الذَّمِّ؛ لِأَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "لَيْسَ لَنَا مَثَلُ السَّوْءِ، الْعَائِدُ فِي هِبَتِهِ كَالْكَلْبِ يَقِيءُ ثُمَّ يَعُودُ فِي قَيْئِهِ".

وَقَالَ النَّسَائِيُّ عِنْدَ تَفْسِيرِ هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ: حَدَّثَنَا قُتَيْبَةُ بْنُ سَعِيدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا اللَّيْثُ، عَنْ جَعْفَرِ بْنِ رَبِيعَةَ، عَنِ الْأَعْرَجِ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ [أَنَّهُ] قَالَ: "إِذَا سمعتم صياح الديكة فَاسْأَلُوا اللَّهَ مِنْ فَضْلِهِ، وَإِذَا سَمِعْتُمْ نَهِيقَ الْحَمِيرِ فَتَعَوَّذُوا بِاللَّهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ، فَإِنَّهَا رَأَتْ شَيْطَانًا".

وَقَدْ أَخْرَجَهُ بَقِيَّةُ الْجَمَاعَةُ سِوَى ابْنِ مَاجَهْ، مِنْ طُرُقٍ، عَنْ جَعْفَرِ بْنِ رَبِيعَةَ بِهِ، وَفِي بَعْضِ الْأَلْفَاظِ: "بِاللَّيْلِ"، فَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

فَهَذِهِ وَصَايَا نَافِعَةٌ جِدًّا، وَهِيَ مِنْ قَصص الْقُرْآنِ الْعَظِيمِ عَنْ لُقْمَانَ الْحَكِيمِ. وَقَدْ رُوِيَ عَنْهُ مِنَ الْحِكَمِ وَالْمَوَاعِظِ أَشْيَاءُ كَثِيرَةٌ، فَلْنَذْكُرْ مِنْهَا أُنْمُوذَجًا وَدُسْتُورًا إِلَى ذَلِكَ.

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ إِسْحَاقَ، أَخْبَرَنَا ابْنُ الْمُبَارَكِ، أَخْبَرَنَا سُفْيَانُ، أَخْبَرَنِي نَهْشَل بْنُ مُجَمِّع الضَّبِّيُّ عَنْ قَزْعَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ قَالَ: أَخْبَرَنَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "إِنَّ لُقْمَانَ الْحَكِيمَ كَانَ يَقُولُ: إِنَّ اللَّهَ إِذَا اسْتُودِعَ شَيْئًا حَفِظَهُ" .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو سَعِيدٍ الْأَشَجُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عِيسَى بْنُ يُونُسَ، عَنِ الْأَوْزَاعِيُّ، عَنْ مُوسَى بْنِ سُلَيْمَانَ، عَنِ الْقَاسِمِ [بْنِ مُخَيْمِرة يُحَدِّثُ عَنْ أَبِي مُوسَى الْأَشْعَرِيِّ] أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم قال: " قَالَ لُقْمَانُ لِابْنِهِ وَهُوَ يَعِظُهُ: يَا بُنَيَّ، إِيَّاكَ وَالتَّقَنُّعَ فَإِنَّهُ مَخْوَفَةٌ بِاللَّيْلِ، مَذَمَّةٌ بِالنَّهَارِ" .

وَقَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا عَمْرُو بْنُ عُثْمَانَ، عَنْ ضَمْرَة، حَدَّثَنَا السَّريّ بْنُ يَحْيَى قَالَ: قَالَ لُقْمَانُ لِابْنِهِ: يَا بُنَيَّ، إِنَّ الْحِكْمَةَ أَجْلَسَتِ الْمَسَاكِينَ مَجَالِسَ الْمُلُوكِ.

وَقَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدَةُ بْنُ سُلَيْمَانَ، أَخْبَرَنَا ابْنُ الْمُبَارَكِ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ الْمَسْعُودِيِّ ، عَنْ عَوْن بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ قَالَ: قَالَ لُقْمَانُ لِابْنِهِ: يَا بُنَيَّ، إِذَا أَتَيْتَ نَادِيَ قَوْمٍ فَارْمِهِمْ بِسَهْمِ الْإِسْلَامِ -يَعْنِي السَّلَامَ -ثُمَّ اجْلِسْ فِي نَاحِيَتِهِمْ، فَلَا تَنْطِقْ حَتَّى تَرَاهُمْ قَدْ نَطَقُوا، فَإِنْ أَفَاضُوا فِي ذِكْرِ اللَّهِ فَأجِلْ سَهْمَكَ مَعَهُمْ، وَإِنْ أَفَاضُوا فِي غَيْرِ ذَلِكَ فَتَحَوَّلْ عَنْهُمْ إِلَى غَيْرِهِمْ.

وَحَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا عَمْرُو بْنُ عُثْمَانُ بْنُ سَعِيدِ بْنِ كَثِيرِ بْنِ دِينَارٍ، حَدَّثَنَا ضَمْرَةُ ، عَنْ حَفْصِ بْنِ عُمَرَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، قَالَ: وَضَعَ لُقْمَانُ جِرَابًا مِنْ خَرْدَلٍ إِلَى جَانِبِهِ، وَجَعَلَ يَعِظُ ابْنَهُ وَعْظَةً وَيَخْرُجُ خَرْدَلَةً، حَتَّى نَفَذَ الْخَرْدَلُ، فَقَالَ: يَا بُنَيَّ، لَقَدْ وَعَظْتُكَ مَوْعِظَةً لَوْ وُعِظَها جَبَلٌ لَتَفَطَّرَ. قَالَ: فَتَفَطَّرَ ابْنُهُ.

وَقَالَ أَبُو الْقَاسِمِ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى بْنُ عَبْدِ الْبَاقِي المِصِّيصي، حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ الْحَرَّانِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عُثْمَانُ بْنُ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ الطَّرَائِفِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبْيَنُ بْنُ سُفْيَانَ الْمَقْدِسِيُّ، عَنْ خَلِيفَةَ بْنِ سَلَامٍ، عَنْ عَطَاءِ بْنِ أَبِي رَبَاحٍ ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عليه وسلم: "اتخذوا السُّودَانَ فَإِنَّ ثَلَاثَةً مِنْهُمْ مَنْ سَادَاتِ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ: لُقْمَانُ الْحَكِيمُ، وَالنَّجَاشِيُّ، وَبِلَالٌ الْمُؤَذِّنُ" .

قَالَ أَبُو الْقَاسِمِ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ: أَرَادَ الْحَبَشَ.

فَصْلٌ فِي الْخُمُولِ وَالتَّوَاضُعِ: وَذَلِكَ مُتَعَلِّقٌ بِوَصِيَّةِ لُقْمَانَ، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ، لِابْنِهِ، وَقَدْ جَمَعَ فِي ذَلِكَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو بَكْرِ بْنُ أَبِي الدُّنْيَا كِتَابًا مُفْرَدًا [وَ] نَحْنُ، نَذْكُرُ مِنْهُ مَقَاصِدَهُ، قَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا إِبْرَاهِيمُ بْنُ الْمُنْذِرِ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ مُوسَى الْمَدَنِيُّ، عَنْ أُسَامَةَ بْنِ زَيْدٍ، عَنْ حَفْصِ بْنِ عُبَيْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ أَنَسِ بْنِ مَالِكٍ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "رُبَّ أشعثَ ذِي طِمْرَين يُصْفَح عَنْ أَبْوَابِ النَّاسِ، إِذَا أَقْسَمَ عَلَى اللَّهِ لَأَبَرَّهُ" .

ثُمَّ رَوَاهُ مِنْ حَدِيثِ جَعْفَرِ بْنِ سُلَيْمَانَ، عَنْ ثَابِتٍ وَعَلِيِّ بْنِ زَيْدٍ، عَنْ أَنَسٍ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ فَذَكَرَهُ، وَزَادَ، مِنْهُمُ الْبَرَاءَ بْنَ مَالِكٍ .

[وَرُوِيَ أَيْضًا عَنْ أَنَسٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم: "طُوبَى لِلْأَتْقِيَاءِ الْأَثْرِيَاءِ الَّذِينَ إِذَا حَضَرُوا لَمْ يُعْرَفُوا، وَإِذَا غَابُوا لَمْ يُفْتَقَدُوا، أُولَئِكَ مَصَابِيحُ مُجَرَّدُونَ مَنْ كُلِّ فِتْنَةٍ غَبْرَاءَ مُشِينَةٍ"] . وَقَالَ أَبُو بَكْرِ بْنُ سَهْلٍ التَّمِيمِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ أَبِي مَرْيَمَ، حَدَّثَنَا نَافِعُ بْنُ يَزِيدَ، عَنْ عَيَّاشِ بْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، عَنْ عِيسَى بْنِ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ، عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ عُمَرَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، أَنَّهُ دَخَلَ الْمَسْجِدَ فَإِذَا هُوَ بِمُعَاذِ بْنِ جَبَلٍ يَبْكِي عِنْدَ قَبْرِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، فَقَالَ لَهُ: مَا يُبْكِيكَ يَا مُعَاذُ؟ قَالَ: حَدِيثٌ سَمِعْتَهُ مِنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، سَمِعْتُهُ يَقُولُ: "إِنَّ الْيَسِيرَ مِنَ الرِّيَاءِ شِرْكٌ، وَإِنَّ اللَّهَ يُحِبُّ الْأَتْقِيَاءَ الْأَخْفِيَاءَ الْأَثْرِيَاءَ، الَّذِينَ إِذَا غَابُوا لَمْ يُفْتَقَدُوا، وَإِذَا حَضَرُوا لَمْ يُعْرَفُوا، قُلُوبُهُمْ مَصَابِيحُ الْهُدَى، يَنْجُونَ مِنْ كُلِّ غَبْرَاءَ مُظْلِمَةٍ" .

حَدَّثَنَا الْوَلِيدُ بْنُ شُجَاعٍ، حَدَّثَنَا عَثَّام بْنُ عَلِيٍّ، عَنْ حُمَيْدِ بْنِ عَطَاءٍ الْأَعْرَجِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ الْحَارِثِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ مَسْعُودٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ قَالَ: "رُبَّ ذِي طِمْرَيْنِ لَا يُؤبَه لَهُ، لَوْ أَقْسَمَ عَلَى اللَّهِ لَأَبَرَّهُ، لَوْ قَالَ: اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ الْجَنَّةَ لَأَعْطَاهُ الْجَنَّةَ، وَلَمْ يُعْطِهُ مِنَ الدُّنْيَا شَيْئًا" .

وَقَالَ أَيْضًا: حَدَّثَنَا إِسْحَاقُ بْنُ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو مُعَاوِيَةَ، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ سَالِمِ بْنِ أَبِي الْجَعْدِ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ مِنْ أُمَّتِي مَنْ لَوْ أَتَى بَابَ أَحَدِكُمْ يَسْأَلُهُ دِينَارًا أَوْ دِرْهَمًا أو فِلْسًا لَمْ يُعْطَهُ، وَلَوْ سَأَلَ اللَّهَ الْجَنَّةَ لَأَعْطَاهُ إِيَّاهَا، وَلَوْ سَأَلَهُ الدُّنْيَا لَمْ يُعْطِهِ إِيَّاهَا، وَلَمْ يَمْنَعْهَا إِيَّاهُ لِهَوَانِهِ عَلَيْهِ، ذُو طِمْرَيْنِ لَا يُؤْبَهُ لَهُ، لَوْ أَقْسَمَ عَلَى اللَّهِ لَأَبَرَّهُ" .

وَهَذَا مُرْسَلٌ مِنْ هَذَا الْوَجْهِ.

وَقَالَ أَيْضًا: حَدَّثَنَا إِسْحَاقُ بْنُ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، أَخْبَرَنَا جَعْفَرُ بْنُ سُلَيْمَانَ، حَدَّثَنَا عَوْف قَالَ: قَالَ أَبُو هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ مِنْ مُلُوكِ الْجَنَّةِ مَنْ هُوَ أَشْعَثُ أَغْبَرُ ذُو طِمْرَيْنِ لَا يُؤبَه لَهُ، الَّذِينَ إِذَا اسْتَأْذَنُوا عَلَى الْأُمَرَاءِ لَمْ يُؤْذَنْ لَهُمْ، وَإِذَا خَطَبُوا النِّسَاءَ لَمْ يُنْكَحُوا، وَإِذَا قَالُوا لَمْ يُنصَت لَهُمْ، حَوَائِجُ أَحَدِهِمْ تَتَجَلْجَلُ فِي صَدْرِهِ، لَوْ قُسِّمَ نُورُهُ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ بَيْنَ النَّاسِ لَوَسِعَهُمْ" . قَالَ: وَأَنْشَدَنِي عُمَرُ بْنُ شَبَّةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَائِشَةَ قَالَ: قَالَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ الْمُبَارَكِ:

أَلَا رُبّ ذِي طمْرَين فِي مَنزل غَدًا ... زَرَابِيه مَبْثُوثةً ونَمَارقُه ...

قَد اطَّرَدَتْ أَنْهَارُهُ حَْوَل قَصْره ... وَأشرَقَ والتفَّتْ عَلَيه حَدَائقُه 

وَرُوِيَ -أَيْضًا -مِنْ حَدِيثِ عُبَيد اللَّهِ بْنِ زَحْر، عَنْ عَلِيِّ بْنِ زَيْدٍ، عَنِ الْقَاسِمِ، عَنْ أَبِي أُمَامَةَ مَرْفُوعًا: "قَالَ اللَّهُ: مِنْ أَغْبَطِ أَوْلِيَائِي عِنْدِي: مُؤْمِنٌ خَفِيفُ الْحَاذِ، ذُو حَظٍّ مِنْ صَلَاةٍ، أَحْسَنَ عِبَادَةَ رَبِّهِ، وَأَطَاعَهُ فِي السِّرِّ، وَكَانَ غَامِضًا فِي النَّاسِ، لَا يُشَارُ إِلَيْهِ بِالْأَصَابِعِ. إِنْ صَبَرَ عَلَى ذَلِكَ". قَالَ: ثُمَّ نَقَد رَسُولُ اللَّهِ بِيَدِهِ وَقَالَ: "عُجّلت مَنِيَّتُهُ، وَقَلَّ تُرَاثَهُ، وَقَلَّتْ بَوَاكِيهِ" .

وَعَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو قَالَ: أَحَبُّ عِبَادِ اللَّهِ إِلَى اللَّهِ الْغُرَبَاءُ. قِيلَ: ومَنْ الْغُرَبَاءُ؟ قَالَ: الْفَرَّارُونَ بِدِينِهِمْ، يُجْمَعُونَ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ إِلَى عِيسَى بْنِ مَرْيَمَ .

وَقَالَ الْفُضَيْلُ بْنُ عِيَاضٍ: بَلَغَنِي أَنَّ اللَّهَ تَعَالَى يَقُولُ لِلْعَبْدِ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ: أَلَمْ أُنْعِمْ عَلَيْكَ؟ أَلَمْ أُعْطِكَ؟ أَلَمْ أَسْتُرْكَ؟ أَلَمْ..؟ أَلَمْ..؟ أَلَمْ أَخْمِلْ ذِكْرَكَ؟ ثُمَّ قَالَ الْفُضَيْلُ: إِنِ اسْتَطَعْتَ أَلَّا تُعرَف فَافْعَلْ، وَمَا عَلَيْكَ أَلَّا يُثنى عَلَيْكَ، وَمَا عَلَيْكَ أَنْ تَكُونَ مَذْمُومًا عِنْدَ النَّاسِ مَحْمُودًا عِنْدَ اللَّهِ.

وَكَانَ ابْنُ مُحَيْرِيز يَقُولُ: اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ ذِكْرًا خَامِلًا.

وَكَانَ الْخَلِيلُ بْنُ أَحْمَدَ يَقُولُ: اللَّهُمَّ اجْعَلْنِي عِنْدَكَ مَنْ أَرْفَعِ خَلْقِكَ، وَاجْعَلْنِي فِي نَفْسِي مَنْ أَوْضَعِ خَلْقِكَ، وَعِنْدَ النَّاسِ مِنْ أَوْسَطِ خَلْقِكَ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ : بَابُ مَا جَاءَ فِي الشُّهْرَةِ

حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ عِيسَى الْمِصْرِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ وَهْبٍ، عَنْ عُمَرَ بْنِ الْحَارِثِ وَابْنِ لَهِيعة، عَنْ يَزِيدَ بْنِ أَبِي حَبِيبٍ، عَنْ سِنَان بْنِ سَعْدٍ، عَنْ أَنَسٍ، عَنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ أَنَّهُ قَالَ: "حَسْبُ امرئ من الشَّرِّ -إِلَّا مَنْ عَصَمَ اللَّهُ -أَنْ يُشِيرَ النَّاسُ إِلَيْهِ بِالْأَصَابِعِ فِي دِينِهِ وَدُنْيَاهُ، وَإِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يَنْظُرُ إِلَى صُوَرِكُمْ وَلَكِنْ إِلَى قُلُوبِكُمْ وَأَعْمَالِكُمْ" .

وَرُوِيَ مِثْلُهُ عَنْ إِسْحَاقَ بْنِ الْبُهْلُولِ، عَنِ ابْنِ أَبِي فُدَيْك، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عَبْدِ الْوَاحِدِ الأخْنَسِيّ، عَنْ عَبْدِ الْوَاحِدِ بْنِ أَبِي كَثِيرٍ، عَنْ جَابِرِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ مَرْفُوعًا، مِثْلُهُ .

وَرُوِيَ عَنِ الْحَسَنِ مُرْسَلًا نَحْوُهُ ، فَقِيلَ لِلْحَسَنِ: فَإِنَّهُ يُشَارُ إِلَيْكَ بِالْأَصَابِعِ؟ فَقَالَ: إِنَّمَا الْمُرَادُ مَنْ يُشَارُ إِلَيْهِ فِي دِينِهِ بِالْبِدْعَةِ وَفِي دُنْيَاهُ بِالْفِسْقِ .

وَعَنْ عَلِيٍّ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، قَالَ: لَا تَبْدَأْ لِأَنْ تَشْتَهِرَ، وَلَا تَرْفَعْ شَخْصَكَ لِتُذْكَرَ، وَتَعَلَّمْ وَاكْتُمْ،

وَاصْمُتْ تَسْلَمْ، تَسُر الْأَبْرَارَ، وَتَغِيظُ الْفُجَّارَ.

وَقَالَ إِبْرَاهِيمُ بْنُ أَدْهَمَ، رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ: مَا صَدَقَ اللَّهُ مَنْ أَحَبَّ الشُّهْرَةَ.

وَقَالَ أَيُّوبُ: مَا صَدَقَ اللَّهَ عَبْدُهُ إِلَّا سَرَّهُ أَلَّا يُشْعَرَ بِمَكَانِهِ.

وَقَالَ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ الْعَلَاءِ: مَنْ أَحَبَّ اللَّهَ أَحَبَّ أَلَّا يَعْرِفَهُ النَّاسُ.

وَقَالَ سِمَاك بْنُ سَلَمَةَ: إِيَّاكَ وَكَثْرَةَ الْأَخِلَّاءِ.

وَقَالَ أبَان بْنُ عُثْمَانَ: إِنْ أَحْبَبْتَ أَنْ يَسْلَمَ لَكَ دِينُكَ فَأَقِلَّ مِنَ الْمَعَارِفِ؛ كَانَ أَبُو الْعَالِيَةِ إِذَا جَلَسَ إِلَيْهِ أَكْثَرُ مِنْ ثَلَاثَةٍ نَهَضَ وَتَرَكَهُمْ.

وَقَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ الجَعْد، أَخْبَرَنَا شُعْبَةُ، عَنْ عَوْف، عَنْ أَبِي رَجَاء قَالَ: رَأَى طَلْحَةُ قَوْمًا يَمْشُونَ مَعَهُ، فَقَالَ: ذُبَابُ طَمَعٍ، وَفَرَاشُ النَّارِ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ إِدْرِيسَ، عَنْ هَارُونَ بْنِ عَنْتَرَةَ ، عَنْ سُلَيْمِ بْنِ حَنْظَلَةَ قَالَ: بَيْنَا نَحْنُ حَوْلَ أَبِي إِذْ عَلَاهُ عُمَرُ بْنُ الْخَطَّابِ بِالدِّرَّةِ وَقَالَ: إِنَّهَا مَذَلَّةٌ لِلتَّابِعِ، وَفِتْنَةٌ لِلْمَتْبُوعِ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ عَوْنٍ، عَنِ الْحَسَنِ: خَرَجَ ابْنُ مَسْعُودٍ فَاتَّبَعَهُ أُنَاسٌ، فَقَالَ: وَاللَّهِ لَوْ تَعْلَمُونَ مَا أغلِقُ عَلَيْهِ بَابِي، مَا اتَّبَعَنِي مِنْكُمْ رَجُلَانِ.

وَقَالَ حَمَّادُ بْنُ زَيْدٍ: كُنَّا إِذَا مَرَرْنَا عَلَى الْمَجْلِسِ، وَمَعَنَا أَيُّوبُ، فَسَلَّمَ، رَدُّوا رَدًّا شَدِيدًا، فَكَانَ ذَلِكَ يَغُمه.

وَقَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، عَنْ مَعْمَر: كَانَ أَيُّوبُ يُطِيلُ قَمِيصَهُ، فَقِيلَ لَهُ فِي ذَلِكَ، فَقَالَ: إِنِ الشُّهْرَةَ فِيمَا مَضَى كَانَتْ فِي طُولِ الْقَمِيصِ، وَالْيَوْمَ فِي تَشْمِيرِهِ. وَاصْطَنَعَ مَرَّةً نَعْلَيْنِ عَلَى حَذْوِ نَعْلَيِّ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ، فَلَبِسَهُمَا أَيَّامًا ثُمَّ خَلَعَهُمَا، وَقَالَ: لَمْ أَرَ النَّاسَ يَلْبَسُونَهُمَا.

وَقَالَ إِبْرَاهِيمُ النَّخَعي: لَا تَلْبَسْ مِنَ الثِّيَابِ مَا يُشهر فِي الْفُقَهَاءِ، وَلَا مَا يَزْدَرِيكَ السُّفَهَاءُ.

وَقَالَ الثَّوْرِيُّ: كَانُوا يَكْرَهُونَ مِنَ الثِّيَابِ الْجِيَادَ، الَّتِي يُشتَهر بِهَا، وَيَرْفَعُ النَّاسُ إِلَيْهِ فِيهَا أَبْصَارَهُمْ. وَالثِّيَابَ الرديئة التي يحتقر فيها، ويستذل دينه.

وَحَدَّثَنَا خَالِدُ بْنُ خِدَاش: حَدَّثَنَا حَمَّادٌ، عَنْ أَبِي حَسَنَةَ -صَاحِبِ الزِّيَادِيِّ -قَالَ: كُنَّا عِنْدَ أَبِي قِلابة إِذْ دَخَلَ عَلَيْهِ رَجُلٌ عَلَيْهِ أَكْسِيَةٌ، فَقَالَ: إِيَّاكُمْ وَهَذَا الْحِمَارَ النَّهَّاقَ.

وَقَالَ الْحَسَنُ، رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ: إِنْ قَوْمًا جَعَلُوا الْكِبْرَ فِي قُلُوبِهِمْ، وَالتَّوَاضُعَ فِي ثِيَابِهِمْ، فَصَاحِبُ الْكِسَاءِ بِكِسَائِهِ أَعْجَبُ مِنْ صَاحِبِ الْمِطْرَفِ بِمِطْرَفِهِ ، مَا لَهُمْ تَفَاقَدُوا.

وَفِي بَعْضِ الْأَخْبَارِ أَنَّ مُوسَى، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ، قَالَ لِبَنِي إِسْرَائِيلَ: مَا لَكُمْ تَأْتُونِي عَلَيْكُمْ ثِيَابُ الرُّهْبَانِ، وَقُلُوبُكُمْ قُلُوبُ الذِّئَابِ، الْبَسُوا ثِيَابَ الْمُلُوكِ، وَأَلِينُوا قُلُوبَكُمْ بِالْخَشْيَةِ.

فَصْلٌ فِي حُسْنِ الْخُلُقِ

قَالَ أَبُو الْتِّيَّاحِ: عَنْ أَنَسٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ: كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ مِنْ أَحْسَنِ النَّاسِ خُلُقًا .

وَعَنْ عَطَاءٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ: قِيلَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، أَيُّ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ أَفْضَلُ؟ قَالَ: "أَحْسَنُهُمْ خُلُقًا" .

وَعَنْ نُوحِ بْنِ عَبَّادٍ، عَنْ ثَابِتٍ، عَنْ أَنَسٍ مَرْفُوعًا: "إِنَّ الْعَبْدَ لَيَبْلُغُ بِحُسْنِ خُلُقِهِ دَرَجَاتِ الْآخِرَةِ وَشَرَفَ الْمَنَازِلِ، وَإِنَّهُ لَضَعِيفُ الْعِبَادَةِ. وَإِنَّهُ لَيَبْلُغُ بِسُوءِ خُلُقِهِ دَرَك جَهَنَّمَ وَهُوَ عَابِدٌ" . وَعَنْ سِنَان بْنِ هَارُونَ، عَنْ حُمَيْدٍ، عَنْ أَنَسٍ مَرْفُوعًا: "ذَهَبَ حُسْنُ الْخُلُقِ بِخَيْرِ الدُّنْيَا وَالْآخِرَةِ" . وَعَنْ عَائِشَةَ مَرْفُوعًا: "إِنَّ الْعَبْدَ لَيَبْلُغُ بِحُسْنِ خُلُقِهِ دَرَجَةَ قَائِمِ اللَّيْلِ وَصَائِمِ النَّهَارِ" .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي الدُّنْيَا: حَدَّثَنِي أَبُو مُسْلِمٍ عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ يُونُسَ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهُ بْنُ إِدْرِيسَ، أَخْبَرَنِي أَبِي وَعَمِّي، عَنْ جَدِّي، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ: سُئل رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ عَنْ أَكْثَرِ مَا يُدخلُ الناسَ الْجَنَّةَ، فَقَالَ: "تَقْوَى اللَّهِ وَحُسْنُ الْخُلُقِ". وَسُئِلَ عَنْ أَكْثَرِ مَا يُدْخِلُ النَّاسُ النَّارَ، فَقَالَ: "الْأَجْوَفَانِ: الْفَمُ وَالْفَرْجُ" .

وَقَالَ أُسَامَةُ بْنُ شَرِيك: كُنْتُ عِنْدَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، فَجَاءَتْهُ الْأَعْرَابُ مِنْ كُلِّ مَكَانٍ، فَقَالُوا: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، مَا خَيْرُ مَا أُعْطِيَ الْإِنْسَانُ؟ قَالَ: "حُسْنُ الْخُلُقِ" .

وَقَالَ يَعْلَى بْنُ مَمْلَكٍ : عَنْ أُمِّ الدَّرْدَاءِ، عَنْ أَبِي الدَّرْدَاءِ -يُبَلِّغُ بِهِ -قَالَ: "مَا [مِنْ] شَيْءٍ أَثْقَلُ فِي الْمِيزَانِ مِنْ حُسْنِ الْخُلُقِ" ، وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ عَطَاءٌ، عَنْ أُمِّ الدَّرْدَاءِ، بِهِ .

وَعَنْ مَسْرُوقٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو مَرْفُوعًا: "إِنَّ مِنْ خِيَارِكُمْ أَحَاسِنُكُمْ أَخْلَاقًا" .

حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ أَبِي بَدْرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عُبَيْدٍ ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ أَبِي سَارَةَ، عَنِ الْحَسَنِ بْنُ عَلِيٍّ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَيُعْطِي الْعَبْدَ مِنَ الثَّوَابِ عَلَى حُسْنِ الْخُلُقِ، كَمَا يُعْطِي الْمُجَاهِدَ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ، يَغْدُو عَلَيْهِ الْأَجْرُ وَيَرُوحُ" .

وَعَنْ مَكْحُولٍ، عَنْ أَبِي ثَعْلَبَةَ مَرْفُوعًا: "إِنَّ أَحَبَّكُمْ إليَّ وَأَقْرَبَكُمْ مِنِّي مَجْلِسًا، أَحَاسِنَكُمْ أَخْلَاقًا، وَإِنَّ أَبْغَضَكُمْ إليَّ وَأَبْعَدَكُمْ مِنِّي مَنْزِلًا فِي الْجَنَّةِ مُسَاوِيكُمْ أَخْلَاقًا، الثَّرْثَارُونَ الْمُتَشَدِّقُونَ الْمُتَفَيْهِقُونَ" .

وَعَنْ أَبِي أُوَيْسٍ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ المنْكَدِر، عَنْ جَابِرٍ مَرْفُوعًا: "أَلَا أُخْبِرُكُمْ بِأَكْمَلِكُمْ إِيمَانًا، أحاسنكم أخلاقا، الموطؤون أَكْنَافًا، الَّذِينَ يُؤْلفون ويَأْلفون" .

وَقَالَ اللَّيْثِ، عَنْ يَزِيدَ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ أُسَامَةَ، عَنْ بَكْرِ بْنِ أَبِي الْفُرَاتِ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "مَا حَسَّن اللَّهُ خَلْق رَجُلٍ وخُلُقه فَتَطْعَمَه النَّارُ" .

وَعَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ غَالِبٍ الحُدَّاني، عَنْ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ مَرْفُوعًا: "خَصْلَتَانِ لَا يَجْتَمِعَانِ فِي مُؤْمِنٍ: الْبُخْلُ، وَسُوءُ الْخُلُقِ" ، وَقَالَ مَيْمُونُ بْنُ مِهْرَان، عَنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "مَا مِنْ ذَنْبٍ أَعْظَمُ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ مِنْ سُوءِ الْخُلُقِ؛ وَذَلِكَ أَنَّ صَاحِبَهُ لَا يَخْرُجُ مِنْ ذَنْبٍ إِلَّا وَقَعَ فِي آخَرَ" .

حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ الْجَعْدِ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو الْمُغِيرَةِ الأحْمَسِيّ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ رَجُلٍ مِنْ قُرَيْشٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "مَا مِنْ ذَنْبٍ أَعْظَمُ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ مِنْ سُوءِ الْخُلُقِ؛ إِنَّ الْخُلُقَ الْحَسَنَ لَيُذِيبَ الذُّنُوبَ كَمَا تُذِيبُ الشَّمْسُ الْجَلِيدَ، وَإِنَّ الْخُلُقَ السَّيِّئَ لَيُفْسِدُ الْعَمَلَ كَمَا يُفْسِدُ الْخَلُّ الْعَسَلَ" .

وَقَالَ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ إِدْرِيسَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ جَدِّهِ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ مَرْفُوعًا: " إِنَّكُمْ لَا تَسَعُون النَّاسَ بِأَمْوَالِكُمْ، وَلَكِنْ يَسَعُهم مِنْكُمْ بَسْطُ وُجُوهٍ وَحُسْنُ خُلُقٍ" .

وَقَالَ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ سِيرِينَ: حُسْنُ الْخُلُقِ عَوْنٌ عَلَى الدِّينِ.

فَصْلٌ فِي ذَمِّ الْكِبْرِ

قَالَ عَلْقَمَةُ، عَنِ ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ -رَفَعَهُ -: "لَا يَدْخُلُ الْجَنَّةَ مَنْ كَانَ فِي قَلْبِهِ مِثْقَالُ حَبَّةٍ مِنْ كِبْر، وَلَا يُدْخِلُ النَّارَ مَنْ فِي قَلْبِهِ مِثْقَالُ حَبَّةٍ مِنْ إِيمَانٍ" .

وَقَالَ إِبْرَاهِيمُ بْنُ أَبِي عَبْلَة، عَنْ أَبِي سَلَمَةَ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو مَرْفُوعًا: "مَنْ كَانَ فِي قَلْبِهِ مِثْقَالُ ذَرَّةٍ مِنْ كِبْرٍ، أَكَبَّهُ اللَّهُ عَلَى وَجْهِهِ فِي النَّارِ" .

حَدَّثَنَا إِسْحَاقُ بْنُ إِسْمَاعِيلَ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو مُعَاوِيَةَ، عَنْ عُمَرَ بْنِ رَاشِدٍ، عَنْ إِيَاسِ بْنِ سَلَمَةَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ مَرْفُوعًا: "لَا يَزَالُ الرَّجُلُ يَذْهَبُ بِنَفْسِهِ حَتَّى يُكْتَبَ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ مِنَ الْجَبَّارِينَ، فَيُصِيبُهُ مَا أَصَابَهُمْ مِنَ الْعَذَابِ" .

وَقَالَ مَالِكُ بْنُ دِينَارٍ: رَكِبَ سُلَيْمَانُ بْنُ دَاوُدَ، عَلَيْهِمَا السَّلَامُ، ذَاتَ يوم البساط في مائتي أَلْفٍ مِنَ الْإِنْسِ، وَمِائَتَيْ أَلْفٍ مِنَ الْجِنِّ، فَرُفع حَتَّى سَمِعَ تَسْبِيحَ الْمَلَائِكَةِ فِي السَّمَاءِ، ثُمَّ خَفَضُوهُ حَتَّى مَسَّتْ قَدُمُهُ مَاءَ الْبَحْرِ، فَسَمِعُوا صَوْتًا لَوْ كَانَ فِي قَلْبِ صَاحِبِكُمْ مِثْقَالُ ذَرَّةٍ مِنْ كِبْرٍ لَخُسِفَ بِهِ أَبْعَدَ مِمَّا رُفِعَ.

حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو خَيثَمة، حَدَّثَنَا يَزِيدُ بْنُ هَارُونَ، عَنْ حَمَّادِ بْنِ سَلَمَةَ، عَنْ ثَابِتٍ، عَنْ أَنَسٍ قَالَ: كَانَ أَبُو بَكْرٍ يَخْطُبُنَا فَيَذْكُرُ بَدْءَ خَلْقِ الْإِنْسَانِ، حَتَّى إِنَّ أَحَدَنَا ليُقذر نَفْسَهُ، يَقُولُ: خَرَجَ مِنْ مَجْرَى الْبَوْلِ مَرَّتَيْنِ .

وَقَالَ الشَّعْبِيُّ: مَنْ قَتَلَ اثْنَيْنِ فَهُوَ جَبَّارٌ، ثُمَّ تَلَا ﴿أَتُرِيدُ أَنْ تَقْتُلَنِي كَمَا قَتَلْتَ نَفْسًا بِالأمْسِ إِنْ تُرِيدُ إِلا أَنْ تَكُونَ جَبَّارًا فِي الأرْضِ﴾ [الْقِصَصِ: ١٩] وَقَالَ الْحَسَنُ: عَجَبًا لِابْنِ آدَمَ، يَغْسِلُ الْخَرْءَ بِيَدِهِ فِي الْيَوْمِ مَرَّتَيْنِ ثُمَّ يَتَكَبَّرُ! يُعَارِضُ جَبَّارَ السموات، قَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا خَالِدُ بْنُ خِدَاش، حَدَّثَنَا حَمَّادُ بْنُ زَيْدٍ، عَنْ عَلِيِّ بْنِ الْحَسَنِ، عَنِ الضَّحَّاكِ بْنِ سُفْيَانَ، فَذَكَرَ الْحَدِيثَ. ضَرَبَ مَثَلَ الدنيَا بِمَا يَخْرُجُ مِنَ ابْنِ آدَمَ .

وَقَالَ الْحَسَنُ، عَنْ يَحْيَى، عَنْ أَبِي قَالَ: إِنَّ مطعم بن آدَمَ ضَرْبُ مَثَلٍ لِلدُّنْيَا وَإِنْ قَزّحَه ومَلَّحه.

وَقَالَ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ الْحُسَيْنِ بْنِ عَلِيٍّ -مِنْ وَلَدِ عَلِيٍّ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ -: مَا دَخَلَ قلبَ رَجُلٍ شَيْءٌ مِنَ الْكِبْرِ إِلَّا نَقَصَ مِنْ عَقْلِهِ بِقَدْرِ ذَلِكَ.

وَقَالَ يُونُسُ بْنُ عُبَيْدٍ: لَيْسَ مَعَ السُّجُودِ كِبْرٌ، وَلَا مَعَ التَّوْحِيدِ نِفَاقٌ.

وَنَظَرَ طَاوُسٌ إِلَى عُمَرَ بْنِ عَبْدِ الْعَزِيزِ وَهُوَ يَخْتَالُ فِي مِشْيَتِهِ، وَذَلِكَ قَبْلَ أَنْ يُسْتَخْلَفَ، فَطَعَنَهُ طَاوُسٌ فِي جَنْبِهِ بِأُصْبُعِهِ، وَقَالَ: لَيْسَ هَذَا شَأْنُ مَنْ فِي بَطْنِهِ خَرْءٌ؟ فَقَالَ لَهُ كَالْمُعْتَذِرِ إِلَيْهِ: يَا عَمِّ، لَقَدْ ضُرِبَ كُلُّ عُضْوٍ مِنِّي عَلَى هَذِهِ الْمِشْيَةِ حَتَّى تَعَلَّمْتُهَا.

قَالَ أَبُو بَكْرِ بْنُ أَبِي الدُّنْيَا: كَانَتْ بَنُو أُمَيَّةَ يَضْرِبُونَ أَوْلَادَهُمْ حَتَّى يَتَعَلَّمُوا هَذِهِ الْمِشْيَةَ.

فَصْلٌ فِي الِاخْتِيَالِ

عَنْ أَبِي لَيْلَى، عَنِ ابْنِ بُرَيْدة، عَنْ أَبِيهِ مَرْفُوعًا: "مَنْ جَرَّ ثَوْبَهُ خُيَلَاءَ لَمْ يَنْظُرِ اللَّهُ إِلَيْهِ" .

وَرَوَاهُ عَنْ إِسْحَاقَ بْنِ إِسْمَاعِيلَ، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ، عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ مَرْفُوعًا مِثْلَهُ . وَحَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ بَكَّار، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ أَبِي الزِّنَادِ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنِ الْأَعْرَجِ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ مَرْفُوعًا: "لَا يَنْظُرُ اللَّهُ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ إِلَى مَنْ جَرَّ إِزَارَهُ" . وَ"بَيْنَمَا رَجُلٌ يَتَبَخْتَرُ فِي بُرْدَيْهِ، أَعْجَبَتْهُ نَفْسُهُ، خَسَفَ اللَّهُ بِهِ الْأَرْضَ، فَهُوَ يَتَجَلْجَلُ فِيهَا إِلَى يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ" .

وَرَوَى الزُّهْرِيُّ عَنْ سَالِمٍ، عَنْ أبيه: "بينما رجل ... " إلى آخره .

## 31:20-21 — verses 20-21 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, alerting His creation to His favors upon them in this world and the next: that He has subjected for them what is in the heavens of stars by which they seek light in their night and day, and what is created therein of clouds, rains, snow, and hail, and His making the heavens for them a protected roof, and what He has created for them on earth of a stable dwelling place, rivers, trees, crops, and fruits. And He has perfected upon them His outward and inward favors, of sending messengers and revealing books, and removing doubts and pretexts. Then despite all of this, not all people believed; rather, among them are those who dispute concerning Allah, that is, concerning His oneness and the sending of messengers. And their disputation in that matter is without knowledge, without any support of sound proof, and without any authentic transmitted book. Therefore the Almighty said: {And among the people is he who disputes concerning Allah without knowledge, without guidance, and without an illuminating book}, meaning, a clear, radiant one.

{And when it is said to them}, that is, to these disputers concerning the oneness of Allah: {Follow what Allah has sent down}, meaning, upon His Messenger of the purified laws, {they say, "Rather, we follow that upon which we found our fathers"}, meaning, they had no argument except following their most ancient forefathers. Allah said: {Even though their fathers understood nothing and were not guided?} [al-Baqarah: 170], meaning, what do you think, O you who argue by the deeds of your fathers, that they were upon misguidance while you are successors to them in what they were upon? Therefore He said: {Even though Satan was calling them to the punishment of the Blazing Fire?}

## 31:22-24 — verses 22-24 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, informing about the one who submits his face to Allah, meaning: he devotes his deeds sincerely to Him, submits to His command, and follows His legislated law. That is why He said, {and he is a doer of good} meaning: in his deeds, by following what he was commanded and abandoning what he was forbidden, {then he has grasped the most trustworthy handhold} meaning: he has taken a firm, binding pledge from Allah that He will not punish him. {And to Allah is the outcome of all affairs. And whoever disbelieves, let not his disbelief grieve you} meaning: do not grieve, O Muhammad ﷺ, over them for their disbelief in Allah and in what you brought, for Allah's decree is carried out concerning them, and to Allah is their return, and He will inform them of what they did, meaning: He will repay them for it. {Indeed, Allah is Knowing of what is in the breasts}, so nothing hidden is concealed from Him.

Then He said: {We give them enjoyment a little} meaning: in this world, {then We will drive them} meaning: We will compel them {to a harsh punishment} meaning: a dreadful, difficult punishment, hard upon souls, just as the Almighty said: {Indeed, those who invent lies about Allah will not succeed. ## 31:25-26 — verses 25-26 (shared commentary)

### Translation

God Most High says, informing about these polytheists who ascribe partners to Him: they know that God is the Creator of the heavens and the earth, alone, without partner, and yet despite this they worship along with Him partners which they themselves acknowledge to be His creation and His possession. Therefore He said: {And if you ask them who created the heavens and the earth, they will surely say, "God." Say: Praise be to God} [that is, since the proof has been established against you by your own admission], {but most of them do not know}.

Then He said: {To God belongs whatever is in the heavens and the earth}, meaning: it is His creation and His possession. {Indeed, God is al-Ghani (the Self-Sufficient), al-Hamid (the Praiseworthy)}, meaning: the Self-Sufficient, free of need of anything besides Him, while everything else is in need of Him; the Praiseworthy in all that He has created. To Him belongs all praise in the heavens and the earth for what He has created and legislated, and He is the One deserving of praise in all matters.

## 31:27-28 — verses 27-28 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted speaks informing of His greatness, His majesty and His glory, His most beautiful names and His most sublime attributes, and His complete words which no one can encompass, nor can any human being attain knowledge of their true reality and enumerate them, just as the master of humankind and seal of the messengers said: "I cannot enumerate praise of You, You are as You have praised Yourself." So the Exalted said: {And if whatever trees are on the earth were pens, and the sea, with seven more seas added to it, [were ink], the words of Allah would not be exhausted} [that is: if all the trees of the earth were made into pens, and the sea were made into ink with seven seas added to it], and the words of Allah indicating His greatness, His attributes and His majesty were written with them, the pens would break and the water of the sea would run out, even if the like of it came as additional supply.

"Seven" is mentioned only as a form of emphatic exaggeration, not to indicate a strict limit, nor that there actually exist seven seas surrounding the world, as is said by those who took this from the sayings of the Israelites, which are neither to be believed nor disbelieved. Rather, it is as the Exalted said in the other verse: {Say: If the sea were ink for the words of my Lord, the sea would be exhausted before the words of my Lord were exhausted, even if We brought the like of it as additional supply} [al-Kahf: 109]. What is meant by His saying {the like of it} is not merely one more sea, but the like of it, then the like of it, then the like of it, and so on continuously, because there is no limit to the signs of Allah and His words.

Al-Hasan al-Basri said: If the trees of the earth were made into pens, and the sea were made into ink, and Allah said, "Of My affair is such and such, and of My affair is such and such," the water of the seas would run out and the pens would break.

Qatadah said: The polytheists said, "This is merely speech that is about to run out." So Allah the Exalted said: {And if whatever trees are on the earth were pens}, meaning: if the trees of the earth were pens, and along with the sea there were seven more seas, the wonders of my Lord, His wisdom, His creation, and His knowledge would not be exhausted.

Al-Rabi' ibn Anas said: The likeness of the knowledge of all servants combined, in relation to the knowledge of Allah, is like a drop from the water of all the seas. And Allah has revealed concerning that: {And if whatever trees are on the earth were pens} the verse.

He is saying: if the sea were ink for the words of Allah and all the trees were pens, the pens would break and the water of the sea would be exhausted, while the words of Allah would remain established, nothing being able to exhaust them, because no one is able to measure His true measure, nor to praise Him as He deserves, until He Himself is the One who praises Himself. Indeed our Lord is as He says, and above what we say.

It has been reported that this verse was revealed as an answer to the Jews. Ibn Ishaq said: [chain] from Sa'id ibn Jubayr or Ikrimah, from Ibn 'Abbas: that the rabbis of the Jews said to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ in Medina, "O Muhammad, tell us about your saying: {And you have not been given of knowledge except a little} [al-Isra: 85]. Do you mean us, or your own people?" The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "No indeed." They said, "Do you not recite, among what has come to you, that we have been given the Torah, in which is a clarification of all things?" The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "It is little in the knowledge of Allah, though with you there is of it what suffices you." And Allah revealed, concerning what they had asked him about: {And if whatever trees are on the earth were pens} the verse.

The same has been reported from Ikrimah and 'Ata' ibn Yasar. This would require that this verse be Medinan rather than Meccan, though the well known view is that it is Meccan. And Allah knows best.

As for His saying: {Indeed Allah is Mighty, Wise}, that is: Mighty, He has overpowered everything, subdued it and prevailed over it, so there is nothing to prevent what He wills, nothing to oppose Him, and nothing to override His judgment. {Wise} in His creation and His command, His sayings and His actions, His legislation and all His affairs.

As for His saying: {Your creation and your resurrection are not but as that of a single soul}, that is: the creation of all people and their resurrection on the Day of Return, in relation to His power, is only like the relation of the creation of a single soul. All of it is easy for Him, and {His command, when He wills a thing, is only that He says to it, "Be," and it is} [Ya-Sin: 82], {and Our command is but one, like a glance of the eye} [al-Qamar: 50], meaning: He commands a thing only once, and that thing comes about without needing repetition or reinforcement. {It will be but one shout, and behold, they are on the surface [of the earth, alive]} [al-Nazi'at: 13, 14].

As for His saying: {Indeed Allah is All-Hearing, All-Seeing}, that is: just as He is All-Hearing of their words and All-Seeing of their deeds in the same manner as His hearing and seeing in relation to a single soul, so too His power over them is like His power over a single soul. For this reason He said: {Your creation and your resurrection are not but as that of a single soul. [Indeed Allah is All-Hearing, All-Seeing]}.

## 31:29-30 — verses 29-30 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty informs that He {makes the night pass into the day}, meaning: He takes from it into the day, so this lengthens and that shortens. This happens in the season of summer, when the day lengthens to its utmost extent, then it quickly begins to decrease, so the night lengthens and the day shortens. This happens in the season of winter. {And He has subjected the sun and the moon, each running to a specified term}. It is said: to a determined limit. And it is said: to the Day of Resurrection. Both meanings are correct, and the first opinion is supported by the hadith of Abu Dharr, may Allah be pleased with him, which is in the two Sahihs: that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "O Abu Dharr, do you know where this sun goes?" I said, "Allah and His Messenger know best." He said, "It goes and prostrates beneath the Throne, then it seeks permission from its Lord, and it is about to be said to it: 'Return from whence you came.'"

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] from Ibn Abbas that he said: The sun is like a waterwheel, it runs by day in the sky in its orbit, and when it sets it runs by night in its orbit beneath the earth until it rises from its point of rising in the east. He said: And likewise the moon. Its chain of transmission is sound.

As for His saying, {And that Allah is aware of what you do}, it is like His saying: {Do you not know that Allah knows what is in the heaven and the earth?} [al-Hajj: 70]. The meaning of this is that He, the Almighty, is the Creator, the Knower of all things, as in His saying: {Allah is He who created seven heavens and of the earth their like, the command descends between them, that you may know that Allah has power over all things and that Allah has encompassed all things in knowledge} [al-Talaq: 12].

As for His saying, {That is because Allah is the Truth, and what they call upon besides Him is falsehood}, it means: He only shows you His signs so that you may infer from them that He is the Truth, that is, the truly Existent, the true Deity, and that everything besides Him is falsehood, for He is the Self-Sufficient of all besides Him, while everything else is in need of Him. For everything in the heavens and the earth, all of it, is His creation and His servants; none of them has power to move even a speck of dust except by His permission. And were all the people of the earth to gather together to create a fly, they would be incapable of it. Therefore He said: {That is because Allah is the Truth, and what they call upon besides Him is falsehood, and that Allah is the Most High, the Most Great}, meaning: the Most High, above whom there is nothing higher; the Most Great, who is greater than everything, so that everything is submissive and insignificant in relation to Him.

## 31:31-32 — verses 31-32 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty informs that He is the One who subjected the sea so that ships might run upon it by His command, meaning by His kindness and His subjecting of it. For had He not placed in the water a power by which it carries ships, they would not run. This is why He said: {that He may show you of His signs}, meaning of His power, {indeed in that are signs for every one who is greatly patient and greatly thankful}, meaning greatly patient in adversity, greatly thankful in ease.

Then He said: {And when waves cover them like shadows} meaning like mountains and clouds, {they call upon Allah, sincere in devotion to Him}, just as the Almighty said: {And when affliction touches you at sea, lost are those you call upon except Him} [al-Isra: 67], and He said: {And when they embark on ships, they call upon Allah, sincere in devotion to Him} [al-Ankabut: 65].

Then He said: {But when He has delivered them to the land, among them is one who is moderate}. Mujahid said: meaning a disbeliever. It is as if he interpreted "muqtasid" (moderate one) here as meaning the denier, just as the Almighty said: {But when He delivers them to the land, at once they associate partners with Him} [al-Ankabut: 65].

Ibn Zayd said: it means one who is moderate in deeds.

What Ibn Zayd said is what is meant in His statement: {Among them is one who wrongs himself, and among them is one who is moderate, and among them is one who is foremost in good deeds} [Fatir: 32]. Here "muqtasid" means one who is moderate in deeds. It is also possible that this is what is meant here as well, and that it falls under the category of censuring one who has witnessed those terrors, those tremendous events, and those dazzling signs at sea, and then, after being blessed with deliverance, ought to have met that with complete devotion, diligence in worship, and hastening toward good deeds. So whoever remained merely moderate after that would be falling short, given the circumstance. And Allah knows best.

His statement: {And none rejects Our signs except every treacherous, deeply ungrateful one}: al-khattar (the treacherous one) means the deeply treacherous, faithless one. This was said by Mujahid, al-Hasan, Qatadah, and Malik on the authority of Zayd ibn Aslam, meaning one who breaks his covenant every time he makes one. Al-khatr is the utmost and most complete form of treachery. Amr ibn Ma'dikarib said:

"And indeed, if you had seen Abu Umayr, your two hands would be filled with treachery and betrayal."

His statement: {kafur} (deeply ungrateful) means one who denies blessings and does not give thanks for them, but rather forgets them and does not remember them.

### Translation

The Almighty says, warning people about the Day of Return and commanding them to have taqwa (fear and mindfulness of God) toward Him, and to fear Him, and to dread the Day of Resurrection, when {no father shall avail his child}, meaning: even if he wished to ransom him with himself, it would not be accepted from him. Likewise, if the child wished to ransom his father with himself, it would not be accepted from him.

Then He returns to admonishing them by His saying: {So let not the worldly life delude you} [meaning: let it not distract you, through the ease and comfort you find in it, from the abode of the Hereafter]. {And let not the deluder delude you concerning God}, meaning Satan. This was said by Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, al-Dahhak, and Qatadah. For he deludes the son of Adam, makes promises to him, and fills him with false hopes, though none of it is real. Rather, as the Almighty says: {He promises them and fills them with false hopes, but Satan promises them nothing except delusion} [al-Nisa: 120].

Wahb ibn Munabbih said: Uzayr, peace be upon him, said: When I saw the affliction of my people, my grief intensified, my worry increased, and my sleep grew troubled. So I humbled myself before my Lord, prayed, and fasted. While I was in that state, weeping and humbling myself, the angel came to me. I said to him: Tell me, do the souls of the truthful intercede for the wrongdoers, or do fathers intercede for their sons? He said: As for the Day of Resurrection, in it is the decisive judgment and manifest dominion. There is no leniency in it. No one speaks in it except by permission of the Most Merciful. In it, no father is taken in place of his child, nor child in place of his father, nor brother in place of his brother, nor slave in place of his master. No one concerns himself with another, nor grieves for another's grief, nor does anyone show mercy to another. Everyone is anxious only for himself. No person is held accountable in place of another. Each one is preoccupied with his own concern, weeps over his own calamity, and carries his own burden of sin, and no one else carries that burden along with him.

Related by Ibn Abi Hatim.

## 31:34 — verse 34

### Translation

These are the keys of the unseen which God, exalted is He, has reserved knowledge of to Himself alone, so that none knows them except after He informs them. The knowledge of the time of the Hour is not known by any sent prophet nor any angel brought near, {None can disclose its time except He} [al-A'raf: 187]. Likewise, the sending down of rain is known by none but God, but when He commands it, the angels charged with that matter come to know it, along with whomever among His creation God wills. Likewise, none knows what is in the wombs among what God, exalted is He, wills to create, except Him, but when He decrees it to be male or female, wretched or fortunate, the angels charged with that come to know it, along with whomever among His creation God wills. Likewise, no soul knows what it will earn tomorrow in its worldly life or in the hereafter, {and no soul knows in what land it will die}, whether in its own land or elsewhere, in whichever of God's lands it may be; no one has knowledge of that. This resembles His saying, exalted is He: {With Him are the keys of the unseen; none knows them but He} [al-An'am: 59]. The Sunnah has come naming these five things "the keys of the unseen."

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] I heard my father, Buraydah, say: I heard the Messenger of God ﷺ say: "Five things known by none but God, mighty and majestic is He: {Indeed, with God is the knowledge of the Hour, and He sends down the rain, and He knows what is in the wombs. No soul knows what it will earn tomorrow, and no soul knows in what land it will die. Indeed, God is All-Knowing, All-Aware}."

This is a hadith with a sound chain, and they (the two Shaykhs) did not include it.

The hadith of Ibn Umar: Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Waki', from Sufyan, from Abdullah ibn Dinar, from Ibn Umar, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "The keys of the unseen are five, known by none but God: {Indeed, with God is the knowledge of the Hour, and He sends down the rain, and He knows what is in the wombs. No soul knows what it will earn tomorrow, and no soul knows in what land it will die. Indeed, God is All-Knowing, All-Aware}."

Al-Bukhari alone transmitted it, narrating it in the Book of Seeking Rain (al-Istisqa') of his Sahih, from Muhammad ibn Yusuf al-Firyabi, from Sufyan ibn Sa'id al-Thawri, with this chain. He also narrated it in the Tafsir by another route, saying:

[chain] Umar ibn Muhammad ibn Zayd ibn Abdullah ibn Umar: that his father told him that Abdullah ibn Umar said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "The keys of the unseen are five." Then he recited: {Indeed, with God is the knowledge of the Hour, and He sends down the rain, and He knows what is in the wombs}. He alone transmitted this as well.

Imam Ahmad also narrated it from Ghundar, from Shu'bah, from Umar ibn Muhammad, that he heard his father narrate, from Ibn Umar, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "I have been given the keys of everything except the five: {Indeed, with God is the knowledge of the Hour, and He sends down the rain, and He knows what is in the wombs. No soul knows what it will earn tomorrow, and no soul knows in what land it will die. Indeed, God is All-Knowing, All-Aware}."

[The hadith of Ibn Mas'ud, may God be pleased with him: Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Yahya, from Shu'bah, from Amr ibn Murrah, from Abdullah ibn Salamah, who said: Abdullah said: "Your Prophet ﷺ was given the keys of everything except five: {Indeed, with God is the knowledge of the Hour, and He sends down the rain, and He knows what is in the wombs. No soul knows what it will earn tomorrow, and no soul knows in what land it will die. Indeed, God is All-Knowing, All-Aware}."]

It was likewise narrated from Muhammad ibn Ja'far, from Shu'bah, from Amr ibn Murrah, with the same chain, adding at the end: He said: I said to him, "Did you hear this from Abdullah yourself?" He said, "Yes, more than fifty times."

It was also narrated from Waki', from Mis'ar, from Amr ibn Murrah, with the same chain.

This is a chain of good (hasan) standing, meeting the criteria of the compilers of the Sunan works, though they did not include it.

The hadith of Abu Hurayrah: Al-Bukhari said, in his commentary on this verse: [chain] Ishaq, from Jarir, from Abu Hayyan, from Abu Zur'ah, from Abu Hurayrah, may God be pleased with him: that the Messenger of God ﷺ was one day sitting exposed before the people, when a man came walking up to him and said, "O Messenger of God, what is iman (faith)?" He said: "Iman is that you believe in God, His angels, His books, His messengers, and the meeting with Him, and that you believe in the later resurrection." He said, "O Messenger of God, what is islam (submission)?" He said: "Islam is that you worship God and associate no partner with Him, that you establish prayer, give the obligatory zakat, and fast Ramadan." He said, "O Messenger of God, what is ihsan (excellence)?" He said: "Ihsan is that you worship God as though you see Him, and if you do not see Him, then indeed He sees you." He said, "O Messenger of God, when is the Hour?" He said: "The one asked about it is no more knowledgeable than the one asking, but I will tell you of its signs: when the slave girl gives birth to her mistress, that is one of its signs; and when the barefoot, naked ones become the heads of the people, that is one of its signs, among five things known by none but God. {Indeed, with God is the knowledge of the Hour, and He sends down the rain, and He knows what is in the wombs}." Then the man turned to leave, and the Prophet ﷺ said: "Bring him back to me." They went to bring him back but saw nothing, so he said: "That was Gabriel, who came to teach the people their religion."

Al-Bukhari also narrated it in the Book of Faith, and Muslim narrated it through several routes, from Abu Hayyan, with the same chain. We have discussed it at the beginning of our commentary on al-Bukhari, and mentioned there the hadith of the Commander of the Faithful, Umar ibn al-Khattab, on this matter at length, which is among the reports unique to Muslim.

The hadith of Ibn Abbas: Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Abu al-Nadr, from Abdul Hamid, from Shahr, from Abdullah ibn Abbas, may God be pleased with both of them, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ was sitting in one of his gatherings, when Gabriel came to him and sat before the Messenger of God ﷺ, placing his hands on the knees of the Prophet ﷺ, and said, "O Messenger of God, tell me, what is Islam?" The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Islam is that you submit your face to God, mighty and majestic is He, and testify that there is no god but God alone, with no partner, and that Muhammad is His servant and messenger." He said, "If I do that, have I submitted?" He said: "If you do that, you have submitted." He said, "O Messenger of God, tell me, what is iman?" He said: "Iman is that you believe in God, the Last Day, the angels, the Book, the prophets, and that you believe in death, in life after death, in Paradise and the Fire, in the Reckoning and the Balance, and that you believe in destiny in its entirety, its good and its bad." He said, "If I do that, have I believed?" He said: "If you do that, you have believed." He said, "O Messenger of God, tell me, what is ihsan?" The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Ihsan is that you act for God as though you see Him, and if you do not see Him, then indeed He sees you." He said, "O Messenger of God, tell me, when is the Hour?" The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Glory be to God! Among five things known by none but Him: {Indeed, with God is the knowledge of the Hour, and He sends down the rain, and He knows what is in the wombs. No soul knows what it will earn tomorrow, and no soul knows in what land it will die. Indeed, God is All-Knowing, All-Aware}. But if you wish, I will tell you of some signs of it short of that." He said, "Yes indeed, O Messenger of God, tell me." The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "When you see the slave girl give birth to her mistress, or her master, and you see the shepherds of sheep competing in building tall structures, and you see the barefoot, hungry, destitute ones [become the heads of the people, that is among the signs and portents of the Hour." He said, "O Messenger of God, who are the shepherds of sheep and the barefoot, hungry, destitute ones?" He said: "The Arabs."]

This is a gharib (singular) hadith, and they (the two Shaykhs) did not include it.

The hadith of a man from Banu Amir: Imam Ahmad narrated: [chain] Muhammad ibn Ja'far, from Shu'bah, from Mansur, from Rib'i ibn Hirash, from a man of Banu Amir, that he sought permission to enter upon the Prophet ﷺ, saying, "May I come in?" The Prophet ﷺ said to his servant girl: "Go out to him, for he does not know how to ask permission properly; tell him to say, 'Peace be upon you, may I enter?'" He said: I heard him say that, so I said, "Peace be upon you, may I enter?" He gave permission, so I entered, and I said, "What have you brought us?" He said: "I have brought you nothing but good. I have come to you so that you may worship God alone, with no partner, and abandon al-Lat and al-Uzza, and that you pray five prayers by night and day, and that you fast one month of the year, and that you make pilgrimage to the House, and that you take zakat from the wealth of your rich and return it to your poor."

He said: Then he asked, "Is there any knowledge remaining that you do not know?" He said, "God, mighty and majestic is He, has known good, and indeed among knowledge there is that which none knows except God, mighty and majestic is He: the five: {Indeed, God, with Him is knowledge of the Hour, and He sends down the rain, and He knows what is in the wombs, and no soul knows what it will earn tomorrow, and no soul knows in what land it will die. Indeed, God is Knowing, Aware.} This is a sound chain of transmission.

Ibn Abi Najih said, from Mujahid: A man from the people of the desert came and said, "My wife is pregnant, so tell me what she will bear. Our land is barren, so tell me when the rain will fall. I know when I was born, so tell me when I will die." So God, mighty and majestic is He, sent down: {Indeed, God, with Him is knowledge of the Hour, and He sends down the rain}, up to His saying: {Indeed, God is Knowing, Aware}. Mujahid said: These are the keys of the unseen which God, exalted is He, spoke of: {And with Him are the keys of the unseen; none knows them except Him} [al-An'am: 59]. Narrated by Ibn Abi Hatim and Ibn Jarir.

Al-Sha'bi said, from Masruq, from A'isha, may God be pleased with her, that she said: Whoever tells you that he knows what will happen tomorrow has lied. Then she recited: {And no soul knows what it will earn tomorrow.}

Concerning His saying: {And no soul knows in what land it will die}: Qatadah said: These are matters which God has kept exclusively to Himself, not disclosing them to any angel brought near nor to any messenger-prophet. {Indeed, God, with Him is knowledge of the Hour}, so no one among the people knows when the Hour will come, in which year or in which month, or by night or by day. {And He sends down the rain}, so no one knows when the rain will fall, by night or by day. {And He knows what is in the wombs}, so no one knows what is in the wombs, whether male or female, red or black, or what it is. {And no soul knows what it will earn tomorrow}, whether good or evil, and you, son of Adam, do not know when you will die. Perhaps you will be the one who dies tomorrow, perhaps you will be the one afflicted tomorrow. {And no soul knows in what land it will die}, no one among the people knows where his resting place in the earth will be, whether at sea or on land, in a plain or on a mountain.

It has come in a hadith: "When God intends to seize a servant in a land, He gives him a need that draws him toward it." The Hafiz Abu al-Qasim al-Tabarani said in his al-Mu'jam al-Kabir, in the musnad of Usama ibn Zayd:

[chain] Ishaq ibn Ibrahim told us, Abd al-Razzaq informed us, Ma'mar informed us, from Ayyub, from Abu al-Malih, from Usama ibn Zayd who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "God never decreed the death of a servant in a land except that He gave him a need there."

Abd Allah, son of the Imam Ahmad, said: [chain] Abu Bakr ibn Abi Shaybah told us, Abu Dawud al-Hafari told us, from Sufyan, from Abu Ishaq, from Matar ibn 'Ukamis, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "When God decrees the death of a servant in a land, He gives him a need that draws him toward it."

Al-Tirmidhi likewise narrated it in "al-Qadar," from the hadith of Sufyan al-Thawri, with the same chain. Then he said: "Hasan gharib (a good, singular report), and nothing is known from Matar from the Prophet ﷺ other than this hadith." Abu Dawud narrated it in "al-Marasil." God knows best.

The Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Isma'il told us, Ayyub told us, from Abu al-Malih ibn Usama, from Abu 'Azzah, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "When God intends to seize the soul of a servant in a land, He gives him a need there, or he said: with it."

This Abu 'Azzah is Yasar ibn Abd Allah, and it is also said: ibn Abd al-Hudhali.

Al-Tirmidhi extracted it from the hadith of Isma'il ibn Ibrahim, who is Ibn 'Ulayyah, and said: Sahih (sound).

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Ahmad ibn 'Isam al-Asfahani told us, al-Mu'ammal ibn Isma'il told us, Ubayd Allah ibn Abi Humayd told us, from Abu al-Malih, from Abu 'Azzah al-Hudhali, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "When God intends to seize a servant in a land, He gives him a need that draws him toward it, and he will not stop until he arrives there." Then the Messenger of God ﷺ recited: {Indeed, God, with Him is knowledge of the Hour, and He sends down the rain, and He knows what is in the wombs, and no soul knows what it will earn tomorrow, and no soul knows in what land it will die. Indeed, God is Knowing, Aware.}

Another hadith: The Hafiz Abu Bakr al-Bazzar said: [chain] Ahmad ibn Thabit al-Jahdari and Muhammad ibn Yahya al-Qutu'i told us, they said, 'Umar ibn 'Ali told us, Isma'il told us, from Qays, from Abd Allah, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "When God intends to seize a servant in a land, He gives him a need that draws him toward it." Then al-Bazzar said: We do not know anyone who narrates this hadith as a marfu' (raised to the Prophet) report except 'Umar ibn 'Ali al-Muqaddami. Ibn Abi al-Dunya said: Sulayman ibn Abi Masih told me, saying: Muhammad ibn al-Hakam recited to me, by A'sha Hamdan:

He took no provision from what he had gathered, except embalming spices on the morning of parting, along with a shroud,

and nothing but a whiff of burning wood kindled for him, and how little that is as provision for one setting forth!

Do not grieve over anything, for every young man is driven toward his appointed death, led by the neck.

And everyone who supposes that death will miss him is deceived by follies born of foolishness.

In whatever land his death is decreed, if he is not led there willingly, he will be driven.

The Hafiz Ibn 'Asakir, may God have mercy on him, recorded this in the biographical entry of Abd al-Rahman ibn Abd Allah ibn al-Harith, who is A'sha Hamdan. Al-Sha'bi was the husband of his sister, and he himself was married to al-Sha'bi's sister as well. He had been among those who sought knowledge and studied jurisprudence, then turned to the craft of poetry and became known for it.

Ibn Majah narrated it from Ahmad ibn Thabit and 'Umar ibn Shabbah, both of them from 'Umar ibn 'Ali, as a marfu' report: "When one of you has his appointed term in a land, a need arises that impels him toward it, and when he reaches the furthest point traced out for him, God, mighty and majestic is He, seizes him. Then the earth will say on the Day of Resurrection: My Lord, this is what You entrusted to me."

Al-Tabarani said: [chain] Ishaq ibn Ibrahim told us, Abd al-Razzaq told us, Ma'mar told us, from Ayyub, from Abu al-Malih, from Usama, that the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "God never decreed the death of a servant in a land except that He gave him a need that drew him toward it."

هَذِهِ مَفَاتِيحُ الْغَيْبِ الَّتِي اسْتَأْثَرَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى بِعِلْمِهَا، فَلَا يَعْلَمُهَا أَحَدٌ إِلَّا بَعْدَ إِعْلَامِهِ تَعَالَى بِهَا؛ فَعِلْمُ وَقْتِ السَّاعَةِ لَا يَعْلَمُهُ نَبِيٌّ مُرْسَلٌ وَلَا مَلَكٌ مُقَرَّبٌ، ﴿لَا يُجَلِّيهَا لِوَقْتِهَا إِلا هُوَ﴾ [الْأَعْرَافِ: ١٨٧] ، وَكَذَلِكَ إِنْزَالُ الْغَيْثِ لَا يَعْلَمُهُ إِلَّا اللَّهُ، وَلَكِنْ إِذَا أَمَرَ بِهِ عَلِمَتْهُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ الْمُوَكَّلُونَ بِذَلِكَ ومَنْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ مَنْ خَلْقِهِ. وَكَذَلِكَ لَا يَعْلَمُ مَا فِي الْأَرْحَامِ مِمَّا يُرِيدُ أَنْ يَخْلُقَهُ [اللَّهُ] تَعَالَى سِوَاهُ، وَلَكِنْ إِذَا أَمَرَ بِكَوْنِهِ ذِكْرًا أَوْ أُنْثَى، أَوْ شَقِيًّا أَوْ سَعِيدًا عَلِمَ الْمَلَائِكَةُ الْمُوَكَّلُونَ بِذَلِكَ، ومَنْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ مِنْ خَلْقِهِ. وَكَذَلِكَ لَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ مَاذَا تَكْسِبُ غَدًا فِي دُنْيَاهَا وَأُخْرَاهَا، ﴿وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ بِأَيِّ أَرْضٍ تَمُوتُ﴾ فِي بَلَدِهَا أَوْ غَيْرِهِ مِنْ أَيِّ بِلَادِ اللَّهِ كَانَ، لَا عِلْمَ لِأَحَدٍ بِذَلِكَ. وَهَذِهِ شَبِيهَةٌ بِقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَعِنْدَهُ مَفَاتِحُ الْغَيْبِ لَا يَعْلَمُهَا إِلا هُوَ﴾ الْآيَةَ [الْأَنْعَامِ: ٥٩] . وَقَدْ وَرَدَتِ السُّنَّةُ بِتَسْمِيَةِ هَذِهِ الْخَمْسِ: مَفَاتِيحُ الْغَيْبِ.

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا زَيْدُ بْنُ الْحُبَابِ، حَدَّثَنِي حُسَيْنُ بْنُ وَاقَدٍ، حَدَّثَنِي عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ بُرَيدة، سَمِعْتُ أَبِي -بُرَيدة - يَقُولُ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "خَمْسٌ لَا يَعْلَمُهُنَّ إِلَّا اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ: ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ عِنْدَهُ عِلْمُ السَّاعَةِ وَيُنزلُ الْغَيْثَ وَيَعْلَمُ مَا فِي الأرْحَامِ وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ مَاذَا تَكْسِبُ غَدًا وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ بِأَيِّ أَرْضٍ تَمُوتُ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلِيمٌ خَبِيرٌ﴾ .

هَذَا حَدِيثٌ صَحِيحُ الْإِسْنَادِ، وَلَمْ يُخَرِّجُوهُ.

حَدِيثُ ابْنِ عُمَرَ: قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا وَكِيع، حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ دِينَارٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ مَفَاتِيحُ الْغَيْبِ خَمْسٌ لَا يَعْلَمُهُنَّ إِلَّا اللَّهُ: ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ عِنْدَهُ عِلْمُ السَّاعَةِ وَيُنزلُ الْغَيْثَ وَيَعْلَمُ مَا فِي الأرْحَامِ وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ مَاذَا تَكْسِبُ غَدًا وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ بِأَيِّ أَرْضٍ تَمُوتُ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلِيمٌ خَبِيرٌ﴾ .

انْفَرَدَ بِإِخْرَاجِهِ الْبُخَارِيُّ، فَرَوَاهُ فِي "كِتَابِ الِاسْتِسْقَاءِ" مِنْ صَحِيحِهِ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ يُوسُفَ الْفِرْيَابِيِّ، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ بْنِ سَعِيدٍ الثَّوْرِيِّ، بِهِ . وَرَوَاهُ فِي التَّفْسِيرِ مِنْ وَجْهٍ آخَرَ فَقَالَ:

حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى بْنُ سُلَيْمَانَ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ وَهْبٍ، حَدَّثَنِي عُمَرَ بْنِ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ زَيْدِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عُمَرَ: أَنَّ أَبَاهُ حَدَّثَهُ أَنَّ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عُمَرَ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "مَفَاتِيحُ الْغَيْبِ خَمْسٌ". ثُمَّ قَرَأَ: ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ عِنْدَهُ عِلْمُ السَّاعَةِ وَيُنزلُ الْغَيْثَ وَيَعْلَمُ مَا فِي الأرْحَامِ﴾ انْفَرَدَ بِهِ أَيْضًا .

وَرَوَاهُ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ عَنْ غُنْدَر، عَنْ شُعْبَةَ، عَنْ عُمَرَ بْنِ مُحَمَّدٍ؛ أَنَّهُ سَمِعَ أَبَاهُ يُحَدِّثُ، عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ قَالَ: "أُوتِيتُ مَفَاتِيحَ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ إِلَّا الْخَمْسَ: ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ عِنْدَهُ عِلْمُ السَّاعَةِ وَيُنزلُ الْغَيْثَ وَيَعْلَمُ مَا فِي الأرْحَامِ وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ مَاذَا تَكْسِبُ غَدًا وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ بِأَيِّ أَرْضٍ تَمُوتُ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلِيمٌ خَبِيرٌ﴾ .

[حَدِيثُ ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ: قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى، عَنْ شُعْبَةَ، حَدَّثَنِي عَمْرِو بْنِ مُرَّة، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ سَلَمَةَ قَالَ: قَالَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ : أُوتِيَ نَبِيُّكُمْ ﷺ مَفَاتِيحَ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ غَيْرَ خَمْسٍ: ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ عِنْدَهُ عِلْمُ السَّاعَةِ وَيُنزلُ الْغَيْثَ وَيَعْلَمُ مَا فِي الأرْحَامِ وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ مَاذَا تَكْسِبُ غَدًا وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ بِأَيِّ أَرْضٍ تَمُوتُ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلِيمٌ خَبِيرٌ﴾ ] .

وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ جَعْفَرٍ، عَنْ شُعْبَةَ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ مُرَّةَ، بِهِ. وَزَادَ فِي آخِرِهِ: قَالَ: قُلْتُ لَهُ: أَنْتَ سَمِعْتَهُ مِنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ؟ قَالَ: نَعَمْ. أَكْثَرَ مِنْ خَمْسِينَ مَرَّةً .

وَرَوَاهُ أَيْضًا عَنْ وَكِيع، عَنْ مِسْعَر، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ مُرَّةَ بِهِ .

وَهَذَا إِسْنَادٌ حَسَنٌ عَلَى شَرْطِ أَصْحَابِ السُّنَنِ وَلَمْ يُخَرِّجُوهُ.

حَدِيثُ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ: قَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ عِنْدَ تَفْسِيرِ هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ: حَدَّثَنَا إِسْحَاقُ، عَنْ جَرِيرٍ، عَنْ أَبِي حَيَّانَ، عَنْ أَبِي زُرْعَةَ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ ؛ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ كَانَ يَوْمًا بَارِزًا لِلنَّاسِ، إِذْ أَتَاهُ رَجُلٌ يَمْشِي، فَقَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، مَا الْإِيمَانُ؟ قَالَ: "الْإِيمَانُ: أَنْ تُؤْمِنَ بِاللَّهِ وَمَلَائِكَتِهِ وَكُتُبِهِ وَرُسُلِهِ وَلِقَائِهِ، وَتُؤْمِنُ بِالْبَعْثِ الْآخَرِ". قَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، مَا الْإِسْلَامُ؟ قَالَ: "الْإِسْلَامُ: أَنْ تَعْبُدَ اللَّهَ وَلَا تشرك به شيئا، وتقيم الصلاة، وتؤتي الزكاة الْمَفْرُوضَةَ، وَتَصُومَ رَمَضَانَ". فَقَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، مَا الْإِحْسَانُ؟ قَالَ: "الْإِحْسَانُ: أَنْ تَعْبُدَ اللَّهَ كَأَنَّكَ تَرَاهُ، فَإِنْ لَمْ تَكُنْ تَرَاهُ فَإِنَّهُ يَرَاكَ". قَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، مَتَى السَّاعَةُ؟ قَالَ: "مَا الْمَسْؤُولُ عَنْهَا بِأَعْلَمَ مِنَ السَّائِلِ، وَلَكِنْ سَأُحَدِّثُكَ عَنْ أَشْرَاطِهَا: إِذَا وَلَدَتِ الأمَةُ رَبَّتَهَا، فَذَاكَ مِنْ أَشْرَاطِهَا. وَإِذَا كَانَ الْحُفَاةُ العُرَاة رُؤُوسَ النَّاسِ، فَذَاكَ مِنْ أَشْرَاطِهَا، فِي خمس لا يعلمهن إِلَّا اللَّهُ. ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ عِنْدَهُ عِلْمُ السَّاعَةِ وَيُنزلُ الْغَيْثَ وَيَعْلَمُ مَا فِي الأرْحَامِ﴾ ، ثُمَّ انْصَرَفَ الرَّجُلُ فَقَالَ: "ردُّوه عَلَيَّ". فَأَخَذُوا لِيَرُدُّوهُ، فَلَمْ يَرَوْا شَيْئًا، فَقَالَ: "هَذَا جِبْرِيلُ، جَاءَ لِيُعَلِّمَ النَّاسَ دِينَهُمْ" .

وَرَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ أَيْضًا فِي "كِتَابِ الْإِيمَانِ"، وَمُسْلِمٌ مِنْ طُرُقٍ، عَنْ أَبِي حَيَّانَ، بِهِ . وَقَدْ تَكَلَّمْنَا عَلَيْهِ فِي أَوَّلِ شَرْحِ الْبُخَارِيِّ. وَذَكَرْنَا ثَمَّ حَدِيثَ أَمِيرِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ عُمَرَ بْنِ الْخَطَّابِ فِي ذَلِكَ بِطُولِهِ، وَهُوَ مِنْ أَفْرَادِ مُسْلِمٍ .

حَدِيثُ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو النَّضْرِ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الْحَمِيدِ، حَدَّثَنَا شَهْر، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا، قَالَ: جَلَسَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ مَجْلِسًا لَهُ، فَأَتَاهُ جِبْرِيلُ فَجَلَسَ بَيْنَ يَدَيْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ وَاضِعًا كَفَّيْهِ عَلَى رُكْبَتِي النَّبِيِّ ﷺ، فَقَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، [حَدِّثْنِي] مَا الْإِسْلَامُ؟ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: الْإِسْلَامُ أَنْ تُسْلِمَ وَجْهَكَ لِلَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، وَتَشْهَدَ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ، وَأَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ". قَالَ: فَإِذَا فَعَلْتُ ذَلِكَ فَقَدْ أَسْلَمْتُ؟ قَالَ: "إِذَا فَعَلْتَ ذَلِكَ فَقَدْ أَسْلَمْتَ". قَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، فحدِّثني مَا الْإِيمَانُ؟ قَالَ: "الْإِيمَانُ: أَنْ تُؤْمِنَ بِاللَّهِ، وَالْيَوْمِ الْآخِرِ، وَالْمَلَائِكَةِ، وَالْكِتَابِ، وَالنَّبِيِّينَ، وَتُؤْمِنَ بِالْمَوْتِ، وَبِالْحَيَاةِ بَعْدَ الْمَوْتِ، وَتُؤْمِنَ بِالْجَنَّةِ وَالنَّارِ، وَالْحِسَابِ وَالْمِيزَانِ، وَتُؤْمِنَ بِالْقَدَرِ كُلِّهِ خَيْرِهِ وَشَرِّهِ". قَالَ: فَإِذَا فَعَلْتُ ذَلِكَ فَقَدْ آمَنْتُ؟ قَالَ: "إِذَا فَعَلْتَ ذَلِكَ فَقَدْ آمَنْتَ". قَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، حَدِّثْنِي مَا الْإِحْسَانُ؟ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "الْإِحْسَانُ أَنْ تَعْمَلَ لِلَّهِ كَأَنَّكَ تَرَاهُ، فَإِنْ كُنْتَ لَا تَرَاهُ فَإِنَّهُ يَرَاكَ". قَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، فَحَدِّثْنِي مَتَى السَّاعَةُ؟ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ. فِي خَمْسٍ لَا يَعْلَمُهُنَّ إِلَّا هُوَ: ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ عِنْدَهُ عِلْمُ السَّاعَةِ وَيُنزلُ الْغَيْثَ وَيَعْلَمُ مَا فِي الأرْحَامِ وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ مَاذَا تَكْسِبُ غَدًا وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ بِأَيِّ أَرْضٍ تَمُوتُ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلِيمٌ خَبِيرٌ﴾ ، وَلَكِنْ إِنْ شِئْتَ حَدَّثْتُكَ بِمَعَالِمَ لَهَا دُونَ ذَلِكَ؟ ". قَالَ: أَجَلْ، يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، فَحَدِّثْنِي. قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِذَا رَأَيْتَ الأمَة وَلَدَتْ رَبَّتَها -أَوْ: رَبَّهَا -وَرَأَيْتَ أَصْحَابَ الشَّاءِ يَتَطَاوَلُونَ فِي الْبُنْيَانِ، وَرَأَيْتَ الْحُفَاةَ الْجِيَاعَ الْعَالَةَ [كَانُوا رُؤُوسَ النَّاسِ، فَذَلِكَ مِنْ مَعَالِمِ السَّاعَةِ وَأَشْرَاطِهَا". قَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، ومَنْ أَصْحَابُ الشَّاءِ وَالْحُفَاةِ الْجِيَاعِ الْعَالَةِ؟ قَالَ: "الْعَرَبُ"] .

حَدِيثٌ غَرِيبٌ، وَلَمْ يُخْرِّجُوهُ.

حَدِيثُ رَجُلٍ مِنْ بَنِي عَامِرٍ: رَوَى الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ جَعْفَرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا شعبة، عن مَنْصُورٍ، عَنْ رِبْعي بْنِ حِرَاش، عَنْ رَجُلٍ مِنْ بَنِي عَامِرٍ؛ أَنَّهُ اسْتَأْذَنَ عَلَى النَّبِيِّ ﷺ فَقَالَ: أَأَلِجُ؟ فَقَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ لِخَادِمِهِ: "اخرُجي إِلَيْهِ، فَإِنَّهُ لَا يُحْسِنُ الِاسْتِئْذَانَ فَقُولِي لَهُ: فَلْيَقُلْ: "السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ، أَأَدْخُلُ؟ " قَالَ: فسَمعتهُ يَقُولُ ذَلِكَ، فَقُلْتُ: السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ، أَأَدْخُلُ؟ فَأَذِنَ، فَدَخَلْتُ، فَقُلْتُ: بِمَ أَتَيْتَنَا بِهِ؟ قَالَ: "لَمْ آتِكُمْ إِلَّا بِخَيْرٍ، أَتَيْتُكُمْ أَنْ تَعْبُدُوا اللَّهَ وَحْدَهُ لا شريك له، وأن تَدَعوا اللَّاتَ وَالْعُزَّى، وَأَنْ تُصَلُّوا بِاللَّيْلِ وَالنَّهَارِ خَمْسَ صَلَوَاتٍ؛ وَأَنْ تَصُومُوا مِنَ السَّنَةِ شَهْرًا، وَأَنْ تَحُجُّوا الْبَيْتَ، وَأَنْ تَأْخُذُوا الزَّكَاةَ مِنْ مَالِ أَغْنِيَائِكُمْ فَتَرُدُّوهَا عَلَى فُقَرَائِكُمْ". قَالَ: فَقَالَ: فَهَلْ بَقِيَ مِنَ الْعِلْمِ شَيْءٌ لَا تَعْلَمُهُ؟ قَالَ: "قَدْ عَلم اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ خَيْرًا، وَإِنَّ مِنَ الْعِلْمِ مَا لَا يَعْلَمُهُ إِلَّا اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ: الْخَمْسُ: ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ عِنْدَهُ عِلْمُ السَّاعَةِ وَيُنزلُ الْغَيْثَ وَيَعْلَمُ مَا فِي الأرْحَامِ وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ مَاذَا تَكْسِبُ غَدًا وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ بِأَيِّ أَرْضٍ تَمُوتُ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلِيمٌ خَبِيرٌ﴾ . وَهَذَا إِسْنَادٌ صَحِيحٌ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي نَجِيِح، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ: جَاءَ رَجُلٌ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْبَادِيَةِ فَقَالَ: إِنِ امْرَأَتَيْ حُبْلَى، فَأَخْبِرْنِي مَا تَلِدُ؟ وَبِلَادُنَا جَدبَةٌ، فَأَخْبِرْنِي مَتَّى يَنْزِلُ الْغَيْثُ؟ وَقَدْ عَلمتُ مَتَى وُلدتُ فَأَخْبَرَنِي مَتَى أَمُوتُ؟ فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ: ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ عِنْدَهُ عِلْمُ السَّاعَةِ [وَيُنزلُ الْغَيْثَ] ﴾ ، إِلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلِيمٌ خَبِيرٌ﴾ . قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: وَهِيَ مَفَاتِيحُ الْغَيْبِ الَّتِي قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَعِنْدَهُ مَفَاتِحُ الْغَيْبِ لَا يَعْلَمُهَا إِلا هُوَ﴾ [الْأَنْعَامِ: ٥٩] . رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، وَابْنُ جَرِيرٍ.

وَقَالَ الشَّعْبِيُّ، عَنْ مَسْرُوقٍ، عَنْ عَائِشَةَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهَا، أَنَّهَا قَالَتْ: مَنْ حَدَّثَكَ أَنَّهُ يَعْلَمُ مَا فِي غَدٍ فَقَدْ كَذَبَ، ثُمَّ قَرَأَتْ: ﴿وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ مَاذَا تَكْسِبُ غَدًا﴾ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ بِأَيِّ أَرْضٍ تَمُوتُ﴾ : قَالَ قَتَادَةُ: أَشْيَاءُ اسْتَأْثَرَ اللَّهُ بِهِنَّ، فَلَمْ يُطلع عَلَيْهِنَّ مَلَكا مُقَرَّبًا، وَلَا نَبِيًّا مُرْسَلًا ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ عِنْدَهُ عِلْمُ السَّاعَةِ﴾ ، فَلَا يَدْرِي أَحَدٌ مِنَ النَّاسِ مَتَى تَقُومُ السَّاعَةُ، فِي أَيِّ سَنَةٍ أَوْ فِي أَيِّ شَهْرٍ، أَوْ لَيْلٍ أَوْ نَهَارٍ، ﴿وَيُنزلُ الْغَيْثَ﴾ ، فَلَا يَعْلَمُ أَحَدٌ مَتَّى يَنْزِلُ الْغَيْثُ، لَيْلًا أَوْ نَهَارًا، ﴿وَيَعْلَمُ مَا فِي الأرْحَامِ﴾ ، فَلَا يَعْلَمُ أَحَدٌ مَا فِي الْأَرْحَامِ، أَذَكُرٌ أَمْ أُنْثَى، أَحْمَرُ أَوْ أَسْوَدُ، وَمَا هُوَ، ﴿وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ مَاذَا تَكْسِبُ غَدًا﴾ ، أَخَيْرٌ أَمْ شَرٌّ، وَلَا تَدْرِي يَا ابْنَ آدَمَ مَتَى تَمُوتُ؟ لَعَلَّكَ الْمَيِّتُ غَدًا، لَعَلَّكَ الْمُصَابُ غَدًا، ﴿وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ بِأَيِّ أَرْضٍ تَمُوتُ﴾ لَيْسَ أَحَدٌ مِنَ النَّاسِ يَدْرِي أَيْنَ مَضْجَعُهُ مِنَ الْأَرْضِ، أَفِي بَحْرٍ أَمْ بَرٍّ، أَوْ سَهْلٍ أَوْ جَبَلٍ؟

وَقَدْ جَاءَ فِي الْحَدِيثِ: "إِذَا أَرَادَ اللَّهُ قبض عبد بأرض، جعل له إليها حاجة"، فَقَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو الْقَاسِمِ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ فِي مُعْجَمِهِ الْكَبِيرِ، فِي مُسْنَدِ أُسَامَةَ بْنِ زَيْدٍ:

حَدَّثَنَا إِسْحَاقُ بْنُ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، أَخْبَرَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، أَخْبَرَنَا مَعْمَر، عَنْ أَيُّوبَ، عَنْ أَبِي الْمَلِيحِ، عَنْ أُسَامَةَ بْنِ زَيْدٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "مَا جَعَلَ اللَّهُ ميتَة عَبْدٍ بِأَرْضٍ إِلَّا جَعَلَ لَهُ فِيهَا حَاجَةً" .

وَقَالَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ الْإِمَامِ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو بَكْرِ بْنُ أَبِي شَيْبَةَ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو داود الحَفَريّ، عن سُفْيَانَ، عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ مَطَر بْنِ عُكَامِس قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ : إِذَا قَضَى اللَّهُ مَيْتَةَ عَبْدٍ بِأَرْضٍ، جَعَلَ لَهُ إِلَيْهَا حَاجَةً".

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ فِي "الْقَدَرِ"، مِنْ حَدِيثِ سُفْيَانَ الثَّوْرِيِّ، بِهِ . ثُمَّ قَالَ: "حَسَنٌ غَرِيبٌ، وَلَا يُعْرَفُ لِمَطَرٍ عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ غَيْرُ هَذَا الْحَدِيثِ. وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ أَبُو دَاوُدَ فِي "الْمَرَاسِيلِ"، فَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا إِسْمَاعِيلُ، حَدَّثَنَا أَيُّوبُ، عَنْ أَبِي الْمَلِيحِ بْنِ أُسَامَةَ عَنْ أَبِي عَزَّةَ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم: "إذا أَرَادَ اللَّهُ قَبْضَ رُوحِ عَبْدٍ بِأَرْضٍ جَعَلَ لَهُ فِيهَا -أَوْ قَالَ: بِهَا -حَاجَةً".

وَأَبُو عَزَّةَ هَذَا هُوَ: يَسَار بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ، وَيُقَالُ: ابْنُ عَبْدٍ الهُذَلي.

وَأَخْرَجَهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ إِسْمَاعِيلَ [بْنِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ -وَهُوَ ابْنُ عُلَيّة ، وَقَالَ: صَحِيحٌ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ عِصَامٍ الْأَصْفَهَانِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا الْمُؤَمَّلُ بْنُ إِسْمَاعِيلَ] ، حَدَّثَنَا عُبَيْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ أَبِي حُمَيْدٍ، عَنْ أَبِي الْمَلِيحِ، عَنْ أَبِي عَزَّةَ الْهُذَلِيِّ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم: "إذا أراد الله قبض عبد بأرض، جَعَلَ لَهُ إِلَيْهَا حَاجَةً، فَلَمْ يَنْتَهِ حَتَّى يَقْدُمَهَا". ثُمَّ قَرَأَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ عِنْدَهُ عِلْمُ السَّاعَةِ وَيُنزلُ الْغَيْثَ وَيَعْلَمُ مَا فِي الأرْحَامِ وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ مَاذَا تَكْسِبُ غَدًا وَمَا تَدْرِي نَفْسٌ بِأَيِّ أَرْضٍ تَمُوتُ إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلِيمٌ خَبِيرٌ﴾ .

حَدِيثٌ آخَرُ: قَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو بَكْرٍ الْبَزَّارُ: حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ ثَابِتٍ الجَحْدَري وَمُحَمَّدُ بْنُ يَحْيَى القُطَعي قَالَا حَدَّثَنَا عُمَر بْنُ عَلِيٍّ، حَدَّثَنَا إِسْمَاعِيلُ، عَنْ قَيْسٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عليه وسلم: "إذا أراد الله قبض عبد بِأَرْضٍ جَعَلَ لَهُ إِلَيْهَا حَاجَةً". ثُمَّ قَالَ الْبَزَّارُ: وَهَذَا الْحَدِيثُ لَا نَعْلَمُ أَحَدًا يَرْفَعُهُ إِلَّا عُمَر بْنَ عَلِيٍّ المُقَدميّ. وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي الدُّنْيَا: حَدَّثَنِي سُلَيْمَانُ بْنُ أَبِي مَسِيحٍ قَالَ: أَنْشَدَنِي مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ الْحَكَمِ لِأَعْشَى هَمْدان:

فَمَا تَزَوّدَ ممَّا كَانَ يَجْمَعُه ... سِوَى حَنُوط غَدَاةَ البَيْن مَع خرَق ...

وَغَيْرِ نَفْحة أعْوَاد تُشَبّ لَُه ... وَقلّ ذَلكَ مِنْ زَاد لمُنْطَلق! ...

لَا تَأسَيَنّ عَلَى شَيْء فَكُلّ فَتَى ... إلَى مَنيّته سَيَّارُ فِي عَنَق 

وَكُلّ مَنْ ظَنّ أَنَّ الموتَ يُخْطِئهُ ... مُعَلَّلٌ بأعَاليل مِنَ الحَمق ...

بأيّمَا بَلْدَة تُقْدَر مَنِيَّتُهُ ... إنْ لَا يُسَيَّرْ إلَيها طَائعًا يُسَق ...

أَوْرَدَهُ الْحَافِظُ ابْنُ عَسَاكِرَ، رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ، فِي تَرْجَمَةِ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ الْحَارِثِ، وَهُوَ أَعْشَى هَمْدَان، وَكَانَ الشَّعْبِيُّ زوجَ أُخْتِهِ، وَهُوَ مُزَوّج بِأُخْتِ الشَّعْبِيِّ أَيْضًا، وَقَدْ كَانَ مِمَّنْ طَلَبَ الْعِلْمَ وتَفَقَّه، ثُمَّ عَدَلَ إِلَى صِنَاعَةِ الشِّعْرِ فعُرف بِهِ.

وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ ابْنُ مَاجَهْ عَنْ أَحْمَدَ بْنِ ثَابِتٍ وعُمَر بْنِ شَبَّة، كِلَاهُمَا عَنْ عُمَرَ بْنِ عَلِيٍّ مَرْفُوعًا: "إِذَا كَانَ أَجَلُ أَحَدِكُمْ بِأَرْضٍ أوثَبَتْه إِلَيْهَا حَاجَةٌ، فَإِذَا بَلَغَ أَقْصَى أَثَرَهُ ، قَبَضَهُ اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، فَتَقُولُ الْأَرْضُ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ: رَبِّ، هَذَا مَا أَوْدَعْتَنِي" .

قَالَ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا إِسْحَاقُ بْنُ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، حَدَّثَنَا مَعْمَرٌ، عَنْ أَيُّوبَ، عَنْ أَبِي الْمَلِيحِ، عَنْ أُسَامَةَ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "مَا جَعَلَ اللَّهُ مَنِيَّةَ عَبْدٍ بِأَرْضٍ، إِلَّا جَعَلَ لَهُ إِلَيْهَا حَاجَةً" .
