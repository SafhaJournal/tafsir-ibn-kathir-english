## 71:1-4 — verses 1-4 (shared commentary)

### Translation

# Tafsir of Surat Nuh

It is Meccan.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

The Exalted relates concerning Nuh (peace be upon him) that He sent him to his people, commanding him to warn them of Allah's punishment before it descended upon them, so that if they repented and turned back to Him, it would be lifted from them. Hence He said: {That you warn your people before there comes to them a painful punishment. He said: O my people, indeed I am to you a clear warner} meaning, one whose warning is plain, whose matter is evident and clear.

{That you worship Allah and fear Him} meaning, abandon what He has forbidden and avoid what leads to sin, {and obey me} in what I command you and what I forbid you.

{He will forgive you some of your sins} meaning, if you do what I command you and believe in what I have been sent to you with, Allah will forgive you your sins.

Regarding "min" (some/of) here, it has been said that it is extra (zaidah, a grammatically redundant particle). But the view that it is extra in an affirmative statement is rare. An example of this usage is the saying of some of the Arabs: "There has indeed been some rain" (qad kana min matarin). It has also been said that it carries the meaning of "'an" (concerning), so that the sense would be: He overlooks your sins for you. Ibn Jarir preferred this view. It has also been said that it indicates partitiveness (tab'id), meaning that He forgives you the grave sins which He had promised to take vengeance upon you for committing.

{And He will delay you until a specified term} meaning, He will extend your lifespans and avert from you the punishment which, if you do not restrain yourselves from what He has forbidden you, He will bring down upon you.

Some who hold that obedience, righteousness, and maintaining ties of kinship actually increase one's lifespan cite this verse as evidence, just as it has come in the hadith: "Maintaining ties of kinship increases the lifespan."

Regarding His saying: {Indeed, the appointed time of Allah, when it comes, cannot be delayed, if only you knew} meaning, hasten to obedience before the descent of retribution, for when Allah, the Exalted, decrees that something shall be, it cannot be turned back or resisted. For He is the Mighty One who has subdued all things, the Almighty to whose might all created things have submitted in humility.

## 71:5-20 — verses 5-20 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty informs us about His servant and messenger Nuh (peace be upon him), that he complained to his Lord, mighty and exalted, of what he had encountered from his people, and of how he had endured them through that long span, which was a thousand years less fifty, and of how he had made things clear to his people, explained matters to them, and called them to right guidance and the most upright path. So he said: {My Lord, I have called my people night and day} that is, I did not cease calling them by night or by day, in compliance with Your command and seeking Your obedience. {But my calling has only increased them in flight} that is, every time I called them to draw near to the truth, they fled from it and turned away from it. {And every time I called them so that You might forgive them, they put their fingers in their ears and wrapped themselves in their garments} that is, they blocked their ears so as not to hear what I was calling them to. This is like what the Almighty relates about the disbelievers of Quraysh: {And those who disbelieved said: Do not listen to this Qur'an, and make noise during its recitation, so that perhaps you may overcome} [Fussilat: 26].

Regarding {and wrapped themselves in their garments}, Ibn Jurayj said, from Ibn Abbas: they disguised themselves so that he would not recognize them. Sa'id ibn Jubayr and al-Suddi said: they covered their heads so as not to hear what he was saying.

{And they persisted} that is, they continued in the state they were in, of shirk (polytheism) and immense, monstrous disbelief. {And they were arrogant with great arrogance} that is, they scorned to follow the truth and submit to it.

{Then I called them openly} that is, publicly, among the people.

{Then I proclaimed to them} that is, with plain speech and a raised voice, {and I spoke to them secretly} that is, between myself and them privately, directing the call to them so that it would be more effective upon them.

{So I said: Seek forgiveness from your Lord, for He is ever forgiving} that is, return to Him, turn away from the state you are in, and repent to Him promptly, for whoever repents to Him, He accepts his repentance, even if his sins, whatever they may be, were disbelief and polytheism. This is why he said: {So I said: Seek forgiveness from your Lord, for He is ever forgiving. He will send rain upon you in abundance} that is, continuous rains. This is why the recitation of this surah is recommended in the prayer for rain (istisqa), because of this verse. It is likewise reported of the Commander of the Faithful, Umar ibn al-Khattab, that he ascended the pulpit to pray for rain, and did nothing more than seek forgiveness, reciting the verses concerning seeking forgiveness. Among them was this verse: {So I said: Seek forgiveness from your Lord, for He is ever forgiving. He will send rain upon you in abundance}. Then he said: I have sought rain by means of the stars of heaven by which rain will be sent down.

Ibn Abbas and others said: it means one part following another.

Regarding His saying: {And He will aid you with wealth and children, and make for you gardens, and make for you rivers} that is, if you repent to Allah, seek His forgiveness, and obey Him, your provision will increase, He will water you from the blessings of the sky, and cause to grow for you from the blessings of the earth. He will make crops grow for you, cause milk to flow abundantly for you, and aid you with wealth and children, meaning He will give you wealth and offspring, and make for you gardens containing various kinds of fruit, interspersed with rivers flowing through them.

This is the station of calling by means of enticement. Then he turned to calling them by means of warning, saying: {What is the matter with you, that you do not hope for majesty from Allah?} that is, greatness. This was said by Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, and al-Dahhak. Ibn Abbas said: you do not glorify Allah with the glorification due to Him, that is, you do not fear His might and His vengeance.

{And He has created you in stages} it was said that this means: from a sperm-drop, then from a clot, then from a lump of flesh. This was said by Ibn Abbas, Ikrimah, Qatadah, Yahya ibn Rafi', al-Suddi, and Ibn Zayd.

Regarding His saying: {Do you not see how Allah created seven heavens in layers?} that is, one above another. Is this something received only by way of hearing, or is it among matters perceptible by the senses, known from the movement of the heavenly bodies and eclipses? For the seven moving celestial bodies eclipse one another. The nearest of them is the moon, in the lowest heaven, and it eclipses what is above it. Mercury is in the second, Venus in the third, the sun in the fourth, Mars in the fifth, Jupiter in the sixth, and Saturn in the seventh. As for the remaining stars, the fixed ones, they are in an eighth sphere which they call the sphere of the fixed stars. Those among them inclined to religious terminology say it is the Throne (al-Kursi), and the ninth sphere is the outermost one (al-Atlas). The ether, according to them, is that whose motion is contrary to the motion of the rest of the spheres, for its motion is the origin of all motions, moving from west to east, while the rest of the spheres move in the opposite direction, from east to west, and the rest of the stars revolve with them accordingly. But the moving bodies have a motion contrary to the motion of their spheres, for they move from west to east. Each one traverses its sphere according to its own measure. The moon traverses its sphere once every month, the sun once every year, and Saturn once every thirty years, this being in accordance with the vastness of their spheres, even though the overall speed of motion is proportional. This is a summary of what they say on this subject, though they differ among themselves on many points which we are not here to explain. The point intended is only that Allah, glorified is He: {Allah created seven heavens in layers and made the moon therein a light, and made the sun a lamp} that is, He made a difference between them in luminosity, making each of them a distinct pattern, so that night and day would be known by the rising and setting of the sun. He ordained for the moon stations and phases, and varied its light, so that at times it increases until it reaches its fullness, then begins to decrease until it disappears, so as to indicate the passing of months and years, as He said: {It is He who made the sun a radiance and the moon a light, and ordained for it phases, that you may know the number of years and the reckoning. Allah did not create that except in truth. He explains the signs in detail for a people who know} [Yunus: 5].

Regarding His saying: {And Allah caused you to grow from the earth, a growth}, this is a verbal noun (ism masdar), and its use here is most fitting. {Then He will return you into it} that is, when you die, {and bring you forth a bringing forth} that is, on the Day of Resurrection He will bring you back just as He originated you the first time.

{And Allah made for you the earth a spread-out expanse} that is, He spread it out, leveled it, established it firmly, and fixed it with towering, lofty mountains.

{That you may traverse from it various paths} that is, He created it for you so that you might settle upon it and travel through it wherever you wish, through its regions, its expanses, and its quarters. All of this is what Nuh (peace be upon him) used to draw their attention to, regarding Allah's power and majesty in creating the heavens and the earth, and His favors upon them in the benefits He made available to them from the heavens and the earth. For He is the Creator, the Provider. He made the sky a structure and the earth a bed, and He expanded His provision for His creation. He is the One who must be worshipped and affirmed as one, and none should be associated with Him, for He has no likeness, no equal, no rival, no peer, no consort, no offspring, no minister, and no counselor. Rather, He is the Most High, the Most Great.

## 71:21-24 — verses 21-24 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted informs, speaking of Nuh (peace be upon him), that he reported to Him, He being the All-Knowing from whom nothing is hidden, that despite the aforementioned exposition and his varied call which combined encouragement at times and warning at others, his people disobeyed him, denied him, and opposed him, and followed the children of this world, those who were heedless of the command of God and were granted enjoyment of wealth and children, which in reality was a gradual leading on and a granting of respite, not an honoring. Hence He said: {And they followed him whose wealth and children only increased him in loss}. It is read both as "wa waladuhu" and "wa wuldu" (with damma and with fatha), and the two meanings are close to one another.

His saying, {And they schemed a mighty scheme}: Mujahid said that "kubbaran" means great. Ibn Zayd said "kubbaran" means large. The Arabs say: an amazing matter is "ajib," "ujab," and "ujjab"; a handsome man is "husan" and "hussan"; and beauty is "jumal" and "jummal," with the light and doubled forms carrying one meaning.

The meaning of His saying, {And they schemed a mighty scheme}, is that by their followers being deceived into believing that they were upon truth and guidance, just as it will be said to them on the Day of Resurrection: {Rather, it was your scheming by night and day, when you ordered us to disbelieve in God and set up rivals to Him} [Saba: 33]. Hence He said here: {And they schemed a mighty scheme, and said, "Do not leave your gods, and do not leave Wadd, nor Suwa', nor Yaghuth, nor Ya'uq, nor Nasr"}. These are the names of their idols which they used to worship besides God.

Al-Bukhari said: [chain] Ata, from Ibn Abbas: The idols that had been among the people of Nuh later passed to the Arabs. As for Wadd, it belonged to Kalb at Dumat al-Jandal; as for Suwa', it belonged to Hudhayl; as for Yaghuth, it belonged to Murad, and then to Banu Ghutayf at al-Juruf near Saba; as for Ya'uq, it belonged to Hamdan; and as for Nasr, it belonged to Himyar, to the family of Dhu Kala'. These were the names of righteous men from the people of Nuh (peace be upon him). When they died, Satan inspired their people to set up statues at the places where they used to sit and to name them after them. They did so, and the idols were not worshiped until, after those people had died and the knowledge had faded, they came to be worshiped.

Similarly it has been narrated from Ikrimah, al-Dahhak, Qatadah, and Ibn Ishaq, to the same effect.

Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn Abbas: These were idols that were worshiped in the time of Nuh.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Muhammad ibn Qays, concerning {Yaghuth, Ya'uq, and Nasr}, said: They were righteous people who lived between Adam and Nuh, and they had followers who took them as examples. When they died, their companions who used to follow their example said, "If we made images of them, it would increase our longing for worship when we remember them." So they made images of them. When those people died and others came after them, Iblis crept up to them and said, "They only used to worship them, and by them rain was granted them," so they worshiped them.

Al-Hafiz Ibn Asakir narrated, in the biography of Shith (peace be upon him), via Ishaq ibn Bishr, who said: Juwaybir and Muqatil informed me, from al-Dahhak, from Ibn Abbas, that he said: Forty children were born to Adam (peace be upon him), twenty boys and twenty girls. Among those who lived were Habil, Qabil, Salih, Abd al-Rahman (who had been named Abd al-Harith), and Wadd. Wadd was called "Shith" and also called "Hibatullah" (Gift of God), and his brothers had made him their chief. To him were born Suwa', Yaghuth, Ya'uq, and Nasr.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Urwah ibn al-Zubayr said: Adam (peace be upon him) fell ill, and with him were his sons Wadd, Yaghuth, Ya'uq, Suwa', and Nasr. He said that Wadd was the eldest of them and the most devoted to him.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Abu al-Mutahhir said: They mentioned Yazid ibn al-Muhallab in the presence of Abu Ja'far while he was standing in prayer. When he finished his prayer, he said: You mentioned Yazid ibn al-Muhallab; indeed he was killed in the first land where something other than God was worshiped. Then he mentioned Wadd, saying: Wadd was a Muslim man, beloved among his people. When he died, they camped around his grave in the land of Babylon and grieved over him greatly. When Iblis saw their grief over him, he took the form of a man and said, "I see your grief over this man; would you like me to fashion for you an image like him, so that it may be present in your gathering place and you may remember him?" They said, "Yes." So an image of him was made for them, and they placed it in their gathering place and began to remember him by it. When he saw how attached they had become to remembering him, he said, "Would you like me to place in each of your homes a statue like him, so that each of you may have him in his house and remember him?" They said, "Yes." So he made for every household a statue like him, and they began to remember him through it. Their children grew up seeing what they did with it, and generations passed and the memory of why they remembered him faded, until the descendants of their descendants took it as a god, worshiping it besides God. Thus the first thing worshiped besides God was the idol which they named Wadd.

His saying, {And they have led many astray}, refers to the idols that they took, by which they led many people astray, for their worship continued through the generations down to our own time, among Arabs and non-Arabs and all the various kinds of the children of Adam. Al-Khalil (Ibrahim, peace be upon him) said in his supplication: {And keep me and my sons away from worshiping idols. My Lord, indeed they have led astray many among mankind} [Ibrahim: 35-36].

His saying, {And do not increase the wrongdoers except in error}, is a supplication of his against his people because of their rebellion, disbelief, and obstinacy, just as Musa supplicated against Pharaoh and his likes in His saying: {Our Lord, obliterate their wealth and harden their hearts so that they will not believe until they see the painful punishment} [Yunus: 88]. God answered each of the two prophets regarding his people, and drowned his nation because of their denial of what he had brought them.

وَالْمَعْنَى فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَمَكَرُوا مَكْرًا كُبَّارًا﴾ أَيْ: بِاتِّبَاعِهِمْ فِي تَسْوِيلِهِمْ لَهُمْ بِأَنَّهُمْ عَلَى الْحَقِّ وَالْهُدَى، كَمَا يَقُولُونَ لَهُمْ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ: ﴿بَلْ مَكْرُ اللَّيْلِ وَالنَّهَارِ إِذْ تَأْمُرُونَنَا أَنْ نَكْفُرَ بِاللَّهِ وَنَجْعَلَ لَهُ أَنْدَادًا﴾ [سَبَأٍ:٣٣] وَلِهَذَا قَالَ هَاهُنَا: ﴿وَمَكَرُوا مَكْرًا كُبَّارًا وَقَالُوا لَا تَذَرُنَّ آلِهَتَكُمْ وَلا تَذَرُنَّ وَدًّا وَلا سُوَاعًا وَلا يَغُوثَ وَيَعُوقَ وَنَسْرًا﴾ وَهَذِهِ أَسْمَاءُ أَصْنَامِهِمُ الَّتِي كَانُوا يَعْبُدُونَهَا مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ.

قَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا إِبْرَاهِيمُ، حَدَّثَنَا هِشَامٌ، عَنِ ابْنِ جُرَيْجٍ، وَقَالَ عَطَاءٌ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: صَارَتِ الْأَوْثَانُ الَّتِي كَانَتْ فِي قَوْمِ نُوحٍ فِي الْعَرَبِ بَعْدُ: أَمَّا وَد: فَكَانَتْ لكلب بدومة الجندل؛ وأما سُوَاعٌ: فَكَانَتْ لِهُذَيْلٍ، وَأَمَّا يَغُوثُ فَكَانَتْ لِمُرَادَ، ثُمَّ لِبَنِي غُطَيف بالجُرُف عِنْدَ سَبَأٍ، أَمَّا يُعوقُ: فَكَانَتْ لهَمْدان، وَأَمَّا نَسْرٌ: فَكَانَتْ لِحَمِيرَ لِآلِ ذِي كَلاع، وَهِيَ أَسْمَاءُ رِجَالٍ صَالِحِينَ مِنْ قَوْمِ نُوحٍ، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ، فَلَمَّا هَلَكُوا أَوْحَى الشَّيْطَانُ إِلَى قَوْمِهِمْ أَنِ انْصِبُوا إِلَى مَجَالِسِهِمُ الَّتِي كَانُوا يَجْلِسُونَ فِيهَا أَنْصَابًا وَسَمُّوهَا بِأَسْمَائِهِمْ. فَفَعَلُوا، فَلَمْ تُعْبَدْ حَتَّى إِذَا هَلَكَ أُولَئِكَ وتَنَسَّخَ الْعِلْمُ عُبِدت 

وَكَذَا رُوي عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ، وَالضَّحَّاكِ، وَقَتَادَةَ، وَابْنِ إِسْحَاقَ، نَحْوُ هَذَا.

وَقَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: هَذِهِ أَصْنَامٌ كَانَتْ تُعْبَدُ فِي زَمَنِ نُوحٍ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ حُمَيْدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا مِهْرَانُ، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ، عَنْ مُوسَى، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ قَيْسٍ ﴿ [يَغُوثَ] وَيَعُوقَ وَنَسْرًا﴾ قَالَ: كَانُوا قَوْمًا صَالِحِينَ بَيْنَ آدَمَ وَنُوحٍ، وَكَانَ لَهُمْ أَتْبَاعٌ يَقْتَدُونَ بِهِمْ، فَلَمَّا مَاتُوا قَالَ أَصْحَابُهُمُ الَّذِينَ كَانُوا يَقْتَدُونَ بِهِمْ: لَوْ صَوَرناهم كَانَ أَشْوَقَ لَنَا إِلَى الْعِبَادَةِ إِذَا ذَكَرْنَاهُمْ. فَصَوَّرُوهُمْ، فَلَمَّا مَاتُوا وَجَاءَ آخَرُونَ دَبَّ إِلَيْهِمْ إِبْلِيسُ فَقَالَ: إِنَّمَا كَانُوا يَعْبُدُونَهُمْ وَبِهِمْ يُسْقَوْنَ الْمَطَرَ، فَعَبَدُوهُمْ. 

وَرَوَى الْحَافِظُ ابْنُ عَسَاكِرَ فِي تَرْجَمَةِ شِيثَ، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ، مِنْ طَرِيقِ إِسْحَاقَ بْنِ بِشْرٍ قَالَ: وَأَخْبَرَنِي جُويبر وَمُقَاتِلٌ، عَنِ الضَّحَّاكِ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ أَنَّهُ قَالَ: وُلِدَ لِآدَمَ عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ، أَرْبَعُونَ وَلَدًا، عِشْرُونَ غُلَامًا وَعِشْرُونَ جَارِيَةً، فَكَانَ مِمَّنْ عَاشَ مِنْهُمْ: هَابِيلُ، وَقَابِيلُ، وَصَالِحٌ، وَعَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ -وَالَّذِي كَانَ سَمَّاهُ عَبْدَ الْحَارِثِ-ووَدّ، وَكَانَ وَدّ يُقَالُ لَهُ "شِيثَ" وَيُقَالُ لَهُ: "هِبَةُ اللَّهِ" وَكَانَ إِخْوَتُهُ قَدْ سَوّدوه، وَوُلِدَ لَهُ سَوَاع وَيَغُوثُ وَيَعُوقُ وَنَسْرٌ. 

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو عُمَر الدّوريُّ، حَدَّثَنِي أَبُو إِسْمَاعِيلَ الْمُؤَدَّبُ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ مُسْلِمِ بْنِ هُرمز عَنْ أَبِي حزْرَة، عَنْ عُرْوَةَ بْنِ الزُّبَيْرِ قَالَ: اشْتَكَى آدَمُ، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ، وَعِنْدَهُ بَنُوهُ: وَدٌّ، وَيَغُوثُ، [وَيَعُوقُ] وَسُوَاعٌ، وَنَسْرٌ -قَالَ وَكَانَ وَدّ أكبَرهم وَأَبَرَّهُمْ بِهِ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ مَنْصُورٍ، حَدَّثَنَا الْحَسَنُ بْنُ مُوسَى، حَدَّثَنَا يَعْقُوبُ، عَنْ أَبِي الْمُطَهِّرِ قَالَ: ذَكَرُوا عِنْدَ أَبِي جَعْفَرٍ -وَهُوَ قَائِمٌ يُصَلِّي-يَزِيدَ بْنَ الْمُهَلَّبِ، قَالَ: فَلَمَّا انْفَتَلَ مِنْ صِلَاتِهِ قَالَ: ذَكَرْتُمْ يزيدَ بْنَ الْمُهَلَّبِ، أَمَا إِنَّهُ قُتِلَ فِي أَوَّلِ أَرْضٍ عُبد فِيهَا غيرُ اللَّهِ. قَالَ: ثُمَّ ذَكَرَ وَدًّا -قَالَ: وَكَانَ وَدٌّ رَجُلًا مُسْلِمًا وَكَانَ مُحَبَّبًا فِي قَوْمِهِ، فَلَمَّا مَاتَ عَسْكَرُوا حَوْلَ قَبْرِهِ فِي أَرْضِ بَابِلَ وَجَزِعُوا عَلَيْهِ، فَلَمَّا رَأَى إِبْلِيسُ جَزَعهم عَلَيْهِ، تَشَبَّهَ فِي صُورَةِ إِنْسَانٍ، ثُمَّ قَالَ: إِنِّي أَرَى جَزَعَكُمْ عَلَى هَذَا الرَّجُلِ، فَهَلْ لَكَمَ أَنْ أُصَوِّرَ لَكُمْ مثله، فيكونَ في ناديكم فتذكرونه؟ قالوا: نَعَمْ. فصُوِّر لَهُمْ مَثَلُهُ، قَالَ: وَوَضَعُوهُ فِي نَادِيهِمْ وَجَعَلُوا يَذْكُرُونَهُ. فَلَمَّا رَأَى مَا بِهِمْ مِنْ ذِكْرِهِ قَالَ: هَلْ لَكُمْ أَنْ أَجْعَلَ فِي مَنْزِلِ كُلِّ وَاحِدٍ مِنْكُمْ تِمْثَالًا مِثْلَهُ، فَيَكُونَ لَهُ فِي بَيْتِهِ فَتَذْكُرُونَهُ؟ قَالُوا: نَعَمْ. قَالَ: فَمَثَّلَ لِكُلِّ أَهْلِ بَيْتٍ تِمْثَالًا مِثْلَهُ، فَأَقْبَلُوا فَجَعَلُوا يَذْكُرُونَهُ بِهِ، قَالَ: وَأَدْرَكَ أَبْنَاؤُهُمْ فَجَعَلُوا يَرَوْنَ مَا يَصْنَعُونَ بِهِ، قَالَ وَتَنَاسَلُوا ودَرَس أَمْرُ ذِكْرِهِمْ إِيَّاهُ، حَتَّى اتَّخَذُوهُ إِلَهًا يَعْبُدُونَهُ مَنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ أَوْلَادُ أَوْلَادِهِمْ، فَكَانَ أَوَّلَ مَا عُبِدَ مِنْ غَيْرِ اللَّهِ: الصَّنَمُ الَّذِي سَمَّوْهُ وَدّا.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَقَدْ أَضَلُّوا كَثِيرًا﴾ يَعْنِي: الْأَصْنَامَ الَّتِي اتَّخَذُوهَا أَضَلُّوا بِهَا خَلْقًا كَثِيرًا، فَإِنَّهُ اسْتَمَرَّتْ عِبَادَتُهَا فِي الْقُرُونِ إِلَى زَمَانِنَا هَذَا فِي الْعَرَبِ وَالْعَجَمِ وَسَائِرِ صُنُوفِ بَنِي آدَمَ. وَقَدْ قَالَ الْخَلِيلُ، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ، فِي دُعَائِهِ: ﴿وَاجْنُبْنِي وَبَنِيَّ أَنْ نَعْبُدَ الأصْنَامَ رَبِّ إِنَّهُنَّ أَضْلَلْنَ كَثِيرًا مِنَ النَّاسِ﴾ [إِبْرَاهِيمَ: ٣٥، ٣٦] .

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَلا تَزِدِ الظَّالِمِينَ إِلا ضَلالا﴾ دُعَاءٌ مِنْهُ عَلَى قَوْمِهِ لِتَمَرُّدِهِمْ وَكُفْرِهِمْ وَعِنَادِهِمْ، كَمَا دَعَا مُوسَى عَلَى فِرْعَوْنَ وَمَثَلِهِ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿رَبَّنَا اطْمِسْ عَلَى أَمْوَالِهِمْ وَاشْدُدْ عَلَى قُلُوبِهِمْ فَلا يُؤْمِنُوا حَتَّى يَرَوُا الْعَذَابَ الألِيمَ﴾ [يُونُسَ: ٨٨] وَقَدِ اسْتَجَابَ اللَّهُ لِكُلٍّ مِنَ النَّبِيَّيْنِ فِي قَوْمِهِ، وَأَغْرَقَ أُمَّتَهُ بِتَكْذِيبِهِمْ لِمَا جَاءَهُمْ بِهِ.

## 71:25-28 — verses 25-28 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He, Exalted, says: {because of their sins} (mimma khataayahum), and it was also recited as {khati'atihim}, {they were drowned}, meaning: because of the abundance of their sins, their insolence, and their persistence in their disbelief and their opposition to their messenger, {they were drowned and made to enter a fire}, meaning: they were transferred from the surge of the seas to the heat of the fire, {and they found none to help them besides Allah}, meaning: they had no helper, no rescuer, and no protector to save them from Allah's punishment, just as He says: {He said, "There is no protector today from the command of Allah except for whom He has mercy"} [Hud: 43].

{And Nuh said, "My Lord, do not leave upon the earth from among the disbelievers any dayyar (dweller)"}, meaning: do not leave upon the face of the earth any one of them, not even a single dweller. This is one of the forms of emphatic negation.

Al-Dahhak said: {dayyar} means "a single one." Al-Suddi said: al-dayyar is one who dwells in a house.

So Allah answered his prayer and destroyed all the disbelievers who were upon the face of the earth, even the son of Nuh from his own loins, who separated himself from his father and said: {"I will take refuge on a mountain that will protect me from the water." He said, "There is no protector today from the command of Allah except for whom He has mercy." And the wave came between them, and he was among those drowned} [Hud: 43].

Ibn Abi Hatim said: it was read before Yunus ibn Abd al-A'la, [he said] Ibn Wahb informed us, Shabib ibn Sa'd informed me, from Abu al-Jawza', from Ibn Abbas, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "If Allah had shown mercy to anyone from the people of Nuh, He would have shown mercy to a woman who, when she saw the water, carried her child and climbed the mountain. When the water reached her, she raised him to her shoulder. When the water reached her shoulder, she placed her child on her head. When the water reached her head, she raised her child up with her hand. So if Allah had shown mercy to anyone among them, He would have shown mercy to this woman."

This is a hadith gharib (singular report), and its narrators are trustworthy. Allah saved the people of the ship who believed with Nuh, peace be upon him, and they were the ones whom Allah commanded him to carry with him.

His saying: {Indeed, if You leave them, they will mislead Your servants}, meaning: indeed, if You leave any of them remaining, they will mislead Your servants, meaning those whom You create after them, {and they will not beget except a wicked disbeliever}, meaning: wicked in deeds and disbelieving in heart. This was because of his knowledge of them and his having dwelt among them for a thousand years less fifty years.

Then he said: {My Lord, forgive me and my parents and whoever enters my house as a believer}. Al-Dahhak said: meaning, my mosque. But there is nothing preventing the verse from being taken according to its apparent meaning, which is that he prayed for everyone who entered his dwelling while being a believer. Imam Ahmad said:

[chain] al-Walid ibn Qays al-Tujibi informed him: that he heard Abu Sa'id al-Khudri, or from Abu al-Haytham, from Abu Sa'id, that he heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "Do not keep company except with a believer, and let no one eat your food except one who is God-fearing."

Abu Dawud and al-Tirmidhi both narrated it, from the hadith of Abdullah ibn al-Mubarak, from Haywah ibn Shurayh, with the same wording. Then al-Tirmidhi said: we only know it from this route.

His saying: {and for the believing men and believing women} is a prayer for all believing men and believing women, and this encompasses both the living among them and the dead. For this reason it is recommended to make a similar supplication, following the example of Nuh, peace be upon him, and in accordance with what has come in the reports and the well-known prescribed supplications.

His saying: {and do not increase the wrongdoers except in destruction} (tabar). Al-Suddi said: meaning, destruction. Mujahid said: meaning, loss, that is, in this world and the next.

The end of the tafsir of Surat Nuh, peace be upon him, and to Allah belongs all praise and favor.

﴿وَقَالَ نُوحٌ رَبِّ لَا تَذَرْ عَلَى الأرْضِ مِنَ الْكَافِرِينَ دَيَّارًا﴾ أَيْ: لَا تَتْرُكْ عَلَى [وَجْهِ] الْأَرْضِ مِنْهُمْ أَحَدًا وَلَا تُومُريَّا وَهَذِهِ مِنْ صِيَغِ تَأْكِيدِ النَّفْيِ.

قَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ: ﴿دَيَّارًا﴾ وَاحِدًا. وَقَالَ السُّدِّي: الدَّيَّارُ: الَّذِي يَسْكُنُ الدَّارَ.

فَاسْتَجَابَ اللَّهُ لَهُ، فَأَهْلَكَ جَمِيعَ مَنْ عَلَى وَجْهِ الْأَرْضِ مِنَ الْكَافِرِينَ حَتَّى وَلَدَ نُوحٍ لِصُلْبِهِ الَّذِي اعْتَزَلَ عَنْ أَبِيهِ، وَقَالَ: ﴿سَآوِي إِلَى جَبَلٍ يَعْصِمُنِي مِنَ الْمَاءِ قَالَ لَا عَاصِمَ الْيَوْمَ مِنْ أَمْرِ اللَّهِ إِلا مَنْ رَحِمَ وَحَالَ بَيْنَهُمَا الْمَوْجُ فَكَانَ مِنَ الْمُغْرَقِينَ﴾ [هود: ٤٣] .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: قُرِئَ عَلَى يُونُسَ بْنُ عَبْدِ الْأَعْلَى، أَخْبَرَنَا ابْنُ وَهْبٍ، أَخْبَرَنِي شَبيب بْنُ سَعْدٍ، عَنِ أَبِي الْجَوْزَاءِ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "لَوْ رَحِمَ اللَّهُ مِنْ قَوْمِ نُوحٍ أَحَدًا، لَرَحِمَ امْرَأَةً، لَمَّا رَأَتِ الْمَاءَ حَمَلَتْ وَلَدَهَا ثُمَّ صَعِدَتِ الْجَبَلَ، فَلَمَّا بَلَغَهَا الْمَاءُ صَعِدَتْ بِهِ مَنْكِبَهَا، فَلَمَّا بَلَغَ الْمَاءُ مَنْكِبَهَا وَضَعَتْ وَلَدَهَا عَلَى رَأْسِهَا، فَلَمَّا بَلَغَ الْمَاءُ رَأْسَهَا رَفَعَتْ وَلَدَهَا بِيَدِهَا. فَلَوْ رَحِمَ اللَّهُ مِنْهُمْ أَحَدًا لِرَحِمِ هَذِهِ الْمَرْأَةَ". 

هَذَا حَدِيثٌ غَرِيبٌ، وَرِجَالُهُ ثِقَاتٌ. وَنَجَّى اللَّهُ أَصْحَابَ السَّفِينَةِ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا مَعَ نُوحٍ، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ، وَهُمُ الَّذِينَ أَمَرَهُ اللَّهُ بِحَمْلِهِمْ مَعَهُ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنَّكَ إِنْ تَذَرْهُمْ يُضِلُّوا عِبَادَكَ﴾ أَيْ: إِنَّكَ إِنْ أَبْقَيْتَ مِنْهُمْ أَحَدًا أَضَلُّوا عِبَادَكَ، أَيِ: الَّذِينَ تَخْلُقُهُمْ بَعْدَهُمْ ﴿وَلا يَلِدُوا إِلا فَاجِرًا كَفَّارًا﴾ أَيْ: فَاجِرًا فِي الْأَعْمَالِ كَافِرَ الْقَلْبِ، وَذَلِكَ لِخِبْرَتِهِ بِهِمْ وَمُكْثِهِ بَيْنَ أَظْهُرِهِمْ أَلْفَ سَنَةٍ إِلَّا خَمْسِينَ عَامًا.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿رَبِّ اغْفِرْ لِي وَلِوَالِدَيَّ وَلِمَنْ دَخَلَ بَيْتِيَ مُؤْمِنًا﴾ قَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ: يَعْنِي: مَسْجِدِي، وَلَا مَانِعَ مِنْ حَمْلِ الْآيَةِ عَلَى ظَاهِرِهَا، وَهُوَ أَنَّهُ دَعَا لِكُلِّ مَنْ دَخَلَ مَنْزِلَهُ وَهُوَ مُؤْمِنٌ، وَقَدْ قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ:

حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ، حَدَّثَنَا حَيْوَة، أَنْبَأَنَا سَالِمُ بْنُ غَيْلَانَ: أَنَّ الْوَلِيدَ بْنَ قَيْسٍ التُّجِيبِيّ أَخْبَرَهُ: أَنَّهُ سَمِعَ أَبَا سَعِيدٍ الْخُدْرِيَّ -أَوْ: عَنْ أَبِي الْهَيْثَمِ، عَنْ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ:-أَنَّهُ سَمِعَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "لَا تَصْحَبْ إِلَّا مُؤْمِنًا، وَلَا يَأْكُلْ طَعَامَكَ إِلَّا تَقِيٌّ".

وَرَوَاهُ أَبُو دَاوُدَ وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ الْمُبَارَكِ، عَنْ حَيْوَةَ بْنِ شُرَيْحٍ، بِهِ ثُمَّ قَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: إِنَّمَا نَعْرِفُهُ مِنْ هَذَا الْوَجْهِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَلِلْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَالْمُؤْمِنَاتِ﴾ دُعَاءٌ لِجَمِيعِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَالْمُؤْمِنَاتِ، وَذَلِكَ يَعُم الأحياءَ مِنْهُمْ وَالْأَمْوَاتِ؛ وَلِهَذَا يُسْتَحَبُّ مِثْلُ هَذَا الدُّعَاءِ، اقْتِدَاءً بِنُوحٍ، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ، وَبِمَا جَاءَ فِي الْآثَارِ، وَالْأَدْعِيَةِ [الْمَشْهُورَةِ] الْمَشْرُوعَةِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَلا تَزِدِ الظَّالِمِينَ إِلا تَبَارًا﴾ قَالَ السُّدِّيُّ: إِلَّا هَلَاكًا. وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: إِلَّا خَسَارًا، أَيْ: فِي الدُّنْيَا وَالْآخِرَةِ.

آخِرُ تَفْسِيرِ سُورَةِ "نُوحٍ" [عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ وَلِلَّهِ الحمد والمنة] .
