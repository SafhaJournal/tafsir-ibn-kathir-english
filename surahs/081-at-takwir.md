## 81:1-14 — verses 1-14 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Tafsir of Surat At-Takwir

And it is Meccan.

Imam Ahmad said: Abd al-Razzaq narrated to us, Abdullah ibn Bahir al-Qass informed us: that Abd al-Rahman ibn Yazid al-San'ani informed him: that he heard Ibn Umar say: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Whoever would be pleased to look upon the Day of Resurrection as if he were seeing it with his own eye, let him recite: {When the sun is wrapped up}, and {And when the sky is split open}, and {When the sky is torn asunder}."

And likewise al-Tirmidhi narrated it, from al-Abbas ibn Abd al-Azim al-Anbari, from Abd al-Razzaq, with it.

{In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.}

* *

Ali ibn Abi Talha said, from Ibn Abbas: {When the sun is wrapped up} means: it grew dark. And al-Awfi said, from him: it departed. And Mujahid said: it vanished and departed. And likewise said al-Dahhak.

And Qatada said: its light departed. And Sa'id ibn Jubayr said: {wrapped up} means it sank away.

And al-Rabi' ibn Khuthaym said: {wrapped up} means it was cast down.

And Abu Salih said: {wrapped up} means it was flung down. And from him also: it was turned upside down. And Zayd ibn Aslam said: it falls to the earth.

Ibn Jarir said: The correct view with us in that regard is that al-takwir (the wrapping up) is the gathering of a thing, part of it to another, and from this comes the wrapping of the turban [which is the winding of it around the head, and like the bundling done by one who dislikes a burden, which is] the gathering of garments, part of them to another. So the meaning of His saying, {wrapped up}, is that it was gathered part into another, then it was rolled up and cast away, and when that is done to it, its light departs.

And Ibn Abi Hatim said: Abu Sa'id al-Ashajj and Amr ibn Abdullah al-Awdi narrated to us, Abu Usama narrated to us, from Mujalid, from a shaykh from Bajila, from Ibn Abbas: {When the sun is wrapped up}, he said: Allah will wrap up the sun, the moon, and the stars on the Day of Resurrection into the sea, and Allah will send a dabur wind (a violent westerly wind) that will kindle them into fire. And likewise said Amir al-Sha'bi. Then Ibn Abi Hatim said:

My father narrated to us, Abu Salih narrated to us, Muawiya ibn Salih narrated to me, from Ibn Yazid ibn Abi Maryam, from his father: that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said concerning the word of Allah, {When the sun is wrapped up}: "It is wrapped up into Hell (Jahannam)."

And al-Hafiz Abu Ya'la said in his Musnad: Musa ibn Muhammad ibn Hayyan narrated to us, Durust ibn Ziyad narrated to us, Yazid al-Raqashi narrated to us, Anas narrated to us, he said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "The sun and the moon are two hamstrung bulls in the Fire."

This is a da'if (weak) hadith, because Yazid al-Raqashi is weak, and what al-Bukhari narrated in the Sahih is without this addition. Then al-Bukhari said:

Musaddad narrated to us, Abd al-Aziz ibn al-Mukhtar narrated to us, Abdullah al-Danaj narrated to us, Abu Salama ibn Abd al-Rahman narrated to me, from Abu Hurayra, from the Prophet ﷺ: "The sun and the moon will be wrapped up on the Day of Resurrection."

Al-Bukhari alone narrated it, and this is his wording, but he only recorded it in the Book of the Beginning of Creation, and it would have been fitting for him to mention it here as well, or to repeat it, as is his custom in similar cases! And al-Bazzar narrated it, and gave it a sound chain of transmission, saying:

Ibrahim ibn Ziyad al-Baghdadi narrated to us, Yunus ibn Muhammad narrated to us, Abd al-Aziz ibn al-Mukhtar narrated to us, from Abdullah al-Danaj, he said: I heard Abu Salama ibn Abd al-Rahman ibn Khalid ibn Abdullah al-Qasri in this mosque, the mosque of Kufa, and al-Hasan came and sat with him, and he narrated, saying: Abu Hurayra narrated to us that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Indeed the sun and the moon are two lights in the Fire on the Day of Resurrection." Al-Hasan said: What sin did the two of them commit? He said: I am narrating to you from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, and you say - I think he said - "What sin did the two of them commit?"

Then he said: it is not narrated from Abu Hurayra except through this route, and Abdullah al-Danaj did not narrate from Abu Salama anything besides this hadith.

* *

And His saying, {And when the stars fall, scattering}, means: they scatter, as Allah Most High said: {And when the planets are scattered} [al-Infitar: 2]. And the root meaning of al-inkidar (the falling away) is: pouring down.

Al-Rabi' ibn Anas said, from Abu al-'Aliya, from Ubayy ibn Ka'b, who said: There are six signs before the Day of Resurrection: while the people are in their marketplaces, the light of the sun will suddenly depart. And while they are like that, the stars will scatter. And while they are like that, the mountains will fall upon the face of the earth, so it will move, shake, and become disordered, and the jinn will flee in fright to mankind, and mankind to the jinn, and the beasts, birds, and wild animals will become mingled together, surging one into another: {And when the wild beasts are gathered}, he said: they become mingled together; {And when the pregnant she-camels (al-'ishar) are left untended}, he said: their owners neglect them; {And when the seas are set ablaze}, he said: the jinn will say, "We will bring you the news." He said: so they set off to the sea, and behold, it was a raging fire. He said: and while they are like that, the earth will split with a single split, down to the seventh earth below and up to the seventh heaven above. He said: and while they are like that, the wind will come upon them and cause them to die.

Ibn Jarir narrated it, and this is his wording, and Ibn Abi Hatim narrated part of it, and likewise said Mujahid, al-Rabi' ibn Khuthaym, al-Hasan al-Basri, Abu Salih, Hammad ibn Abi Sulayman, and al-Dahhak concerning His saying, {And when the stars fall, scattering}, that is: they scatter.

And Ali ibn Abi Talha said, from Ibn Abbas: {And when the stars fall, scattering}, that is: they change.

And Yazid ibn Abi Maryam said, from the Prophet ﷺ, concerning {And when the stars fall, scattering}, he said: "They fall away into Hell, and everyone who was worshipped besides Allah is in Hell, except in the case of Isa (peace be upon him) and his mother, for had the two of them been pleased to be worshipped, they would have entered it too." Ibn Abi Hatim narrated it with the aforementioned chain of transmission.

* *

And His saying, {And when the mountains are set moving}, that is: they are removed from their places and blown away, leaving the earth a flat, level plain.

* *

And His saying, {And when the pregnant she-camels are left untended}: Ikrima and Mujahid said: the 'ishar of the camels. Mujahid said: {left untended} means they were left and turned loose.

And Ubayy ibn Ka'b and al-Dahhak said: their owners neglected them. And al-Rabi' ibn Khuthaym said: they are neither milked nor have their udders bound up; their owners have abandoned them.

And al-Dahhak said: they are left with no herder for them.

And the meaning in all of this is close. The point is that al-'ishar are among the camels, namely the choicest of them, the pregnant ones among them that have reached the tenth month of their pregnancy, the singular of which is 'ushara', and it retains that name until it gives birth. People will become preoccupied away from them, from tending them, and from benefiting from them, after having been the most desirous of anything for them, because of what will have overwhelmed them of the great, terrible, and awesome matter, which is the matter of the Resurrection, the coming together of its causes, and the occurrence of its preliminaries.

And it has been said: rather, that will occur on the Day of Resurrection, their owners will see them in that state and have no way to reach them. And it has been said concerning al-'ishar: they are the clouds that are halted from moving between the sky and the earth, due to the ruin of the world. And it has [also] been said: they are the land that is tithed. And it has been said: they are the dwellings that used to be inhabited, left untended due to the departure of their people. The Imam Abu Abdullah al-Qurtubi related all of these sayings in his book al-Tadhkira, and he preferred that it means the camels, attributing that to the majority of people.

I say: rather, nothing else is known from the Salaf (the early generations) and the leading scholars besides this. And Allah knows best.

* *

And His saying, {And when the wild beasts are gathered}, that is: they are gathered together, as Allah Most High said: {And there is no creature on the earth, nor a bird that flies with its two wings, but they are communities like you. We have not neglected anything in the Book. Then to their Lord they will be gathered.} [al-An'am: 38].

Ibn Abbas said: everything will be gathered, even the fly. Ibn Abi Hatim narrated it. And likewise said al-Rabi' ibn Khuthaym and al-Suddi, and more than one other. And likewise Qatada said, in explaining this verse: that these creatures will arrive, and Allah will decree concerning them whatever He wills.

And Ikrima said: their being gathered is their death.

And Ibn Jarir said: Ali ibn Muslim al-Tusi narrated to me, Abbad ibn al-Awwam narrated to us, Husayn informed us, from Ikrima, from Ibn Abbas concerning His saying, {And when the wild beasts are gathered}, he said: the gathering of the animals is their death, and the gathering of everything is death, except for the jinn and mankind, for the two of them will be made to stand on the Day of Resurrection.

Abu Kurayb narrated to us, Waki' narrated to us, from Sufyan, from his father, from Abu Ya'la, from al-Rabi' ibn Khuthaym: {And when the wild beasts are gathered}, he said: the command of Allah came upon them. Sufyan said: my father said: I mentioned it to Ikrima, and he said: Ibn Abbas said: their gathering is their death.

And it has already preceded from Ubayy ibn Ka'b that he said: {And when the wild beasts are gathered}, they become mingled together.

Ibn Jarir said: And the sounder view is the saying of the one who said: {gathered}

were gathered together. Allah, the Exalted, says: {And the birds gathered} [Sad: 19], meaning: assembled.

* *

And His saying: {And when the seas are made to boil over (sujjirat, glossed variously as kindled into fire, made to overflow, or burst open)}. Ibn Jarir said: [chain] from Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab, who said: 'Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, said to a man from the Jews: "Where is Hell?" He said: "The sea." So 'Ali said: "I see him as nothing but truthful." {And the sea kept filled with fire} [al-Tur: 6], {And when the seas are made to boil over} [in the lightened reading, without the doubled letter].

Ibn 'Abbas and more than one other said: Allah sends upon it al-dabur (the destructive west wind), and it sets it ablaze, and it becomes a raging fire. The discussion on this has already preceded at His saying: {And the sea kept filled with fire}.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] 'Abd al-Jabbar ibn Sulayman, Abu Sulayman al-Naffat, a righteous shaykh who resembled Malik ibn Anas, from Mu'awiyah ibn Sa'id, who said: This sea, meaning the sea of Rum (the Mediterranean), lies in the middle of the earth, and all the rivers pour into it, and the great sea pours into it, and at its bottom are wells lined with copper. So when the Day of Resurrection comes, it will be set ablaze.

This is a gharib (rare), remarkable athar (report). And in the Sunan of Abu Dawud: "No one should sail the sea except a pilgrim performing Hajj, or one performing 'Umrah, or a ghazi (one fighting in the cause of Allah), for beneath the sea is a fire, and beneath the fire is a sea," the hadith in full. The discussion on this has already preceded in Surat Fatir.

Mujahid and al-Hasan ibn Muslim said: {sujjirat} means it was kindled. Al-Hasan said: it dried up. Al-Dahhak and Qatadah said: its water sank away and vanished, leaving not a single drop in it. Al-Dahhak also said: {sujjirat} means it burst open. Al-Suddi said: it was opened and set flowing. Al-Rabi' ibn Khuthaym said: {sujjirat} means it overflowed.

* *

And His saying: {And when the souls are paired (zuwwijat)}, meaning: every kind was joined to its like, as in His saying: {Gather those who did wrong, and their counterparts} [al-Saffat: 22].

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] from al-Nu'man ibn Bashir, that he said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, regarding {And when the souls are paired}: "They are the peers (al-duraba'), every man with every people who used to do his deeds." That is because Allah, Mighty and Majestic, says: {And you will be three kinds. So those on the right, what of those on the right? And those on the left, what of those on the left? And the foremost, the foremost} [al-Waqi'ah: 7-10]. He said: They are the peers.

Then Ibn Abi Hatim also narrated it through other chains, from Simak ibn Harb, from al-Nu'man ibn Bashir, that 'Umar addressed the people and recited: {And when the souls are paired}, and said: "Their being paired is that every faction is joined to its own faction." And in another report: "They are the two men who do the [same] deed, so it brings them both into Paradise, or into the Fire."

And in a report from al-Nu'man he said: 'Umar was asked about His saying, the Exalted: {And when the souls are paired}, and he said: "The righteous man is paired with the righteous man, and the wicked man is paired with the wicked man in the Fire. That is the pairing of the souls."

And in a report from al-Nu'man, that 'Umar said to the people: "What do you say about the meaning of this verse: {And when the souls are paired}?" They were silent. He said: "Rather, it is the man who is paired with his counterpart among the people of Paradise, and the man who is paired with his counterpart among the people of the Fire." Then he recited: {Gather those who did wrong, and their counterparts}.

Al-'Awfi said, from Ibn 'Abbas, regarding His saying {And when the souls are paired}: he said: That is when the people will become three kinds.

Ibn Abi Najih said, from Mujahid, regarding {And when the souls are paired}: he said: like kinds among the people were brought together. Al-Rabi' ibn Khuthaym, al-Hasan, and Qatadah said likewise. Ibn Jarir chose this view, and it is the correct one.

Another view on His saying {And when the souls are paired}. Ibn Abi Hatim said:

[chain] from Ibn 'Abbas, who said: A valley will flow from the base of the Throne with water, in the interval between the two blasts of the Trumpet, and the span between them is forty years. From it will sprout every created being that had decayed, whether human, bird, or beast, such that if a passerby who had known them before were to pass by, he would recognize them upon the earth once they had sprouted. Then the spirits will be sent forth, and they will be paired with the bodies. That is the meaning of Allah's saying, the Exalted: {And when the souls are paired}.

Likewise said Abu al-'Aliyah, 'Ikrimah, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, al-Sha'bi, and al-Hasan al-Basri also, regarding His saying: {And when the souls are paired}, that is: they were paired with the bodies. And it was said: the believers were paired with the wide-eyed hur (the maidens of Paradise), and the disbelievers were paired with the shayatin (devils). Al-Qurtubi related this in al-Tadhkirah.

* *

And His saying: {And when the female infant buried alive (al-maw'udah) is asked for what sin she was killed}. This is how the majority read it: {su'ilat} (was asked, in the passive). And al-maw'udah is the girl whom the people of the Jahiliyyah (pre-Islamic ignorance) used to bury in the ground out of aversion to daughters. So on the Day of Resurrection, the buried girl will be asked for what sin she was killed, so that this may be a threat to her killer. For if the wronged one is questioned, what then must the wrongdoer expect?

'Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn 'Abbas, regarding {And when the buried girl is asked}: meaning, {sa'alat} (she asked, in the active). Abu al-Duha likewise said: "She asked," meaning she demanded [justice for] her blood. And the like of this was reported from al-Suddi and Qatadah.

Hadiths have been reported concerning the buried girl. Imam Ahmad said:

[chain] from Judamah bint Wahb, sister of 'Ukkashah, who said: I was present with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ among a group of people, and he was saying: "I had resolved to forbid al-ghilah (a nursing mother having intercourse while breastfeeding), but then I considered the Byzantines and the Persians and found that they practice al-ghilah with their children, and it does their children no harm at all." Then they asked him about al-'azl (coitus interruptus), and the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "That is the hidden burying alive, and it is the meaning of: the buried girl will be asked."

Muslim narrated it through the report of Abu 'Abd al-Rahman al-Muqri, that is 'Abdullah ibn Yazid, from Sa'id ibn Abi Ayyub. Ibn Majah also narrated it, from Abu Bakr ibn Abi Shaybah, from Yahya ibn Ishaq al-Saylahini, from Yahya ibn Ayyub. Muslim also narrated it, as did Abu Dawud, al-Tirmidhi, and al-Nasa'i, through the report of Malik ibn Anas, all three of them from Abu al-Aswad, narrating the same report.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] from Salamah ibn Yazid al-Ju'fi, who said: My brother and I went to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and said: "O Messenger of Allah, our mother Mulaykah used to maintain the ties of kinship and honor guests, and she used to do such and such, but she died in the Jahiliyyah. Will any of that benefit her at all?" He said: "No." We said: "She had buried alive a sister of ours in the Jahiliyyah. Will that benefit her at all?" He said: "The one who buries alive (al-wa'idah) and the one who is buried alive (al-maw'udah) are both in the Fire, unless the one who buried her lives to reach Islam, in which case Allah will pardon her."

Al-Nasa'i narrated it through the report of Dawud ibn Abi Hind, narrating the same.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] from Ibn Mas'ud, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "The one who buries alive and the one buried alive are both in the Fire."

Ahmad also said: [chain] Hasna' bint Mu'awiyah al-Surraymiyyah narrated to me, from her paternal uncle, who said: I said: "O Messenger of Allah, who is in Paradise?" He said: "The Prophet is in Paradise, the martyr is in Paradise, the newborn child is in Paradise, and the buried girl is in Paradise."

Ibn Abi Hatim said: my father narrated to us, Muslim ibn Ibrahim narrated to us, Qurrah narrated to us, who said: I heard al-Hasan say: It was said: "O Messenger of Allah, who is in Paradise?" He said: "The buried girl is in Paradise."

This is a mursal hadith (one whose chain omits the Companion), one of the mursal reports of al-Hasan, and some scholars accepted it.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] from 'Ikrimah, who said: Ibn 'Abbas said: "The children of the polytheists are in Paradise, and whoever claims that they are in the Fire has lied. Allah, Mighty and Majestic, says: {And when the buried girl is asked for what sin she was killed}." Ibn 'Abbas said: She is the one who was buried [alive].

Abd al-Razzaq said: Israil informed us, from Simak ibn Harb, from al-Nu'man ibn Bashir, from Umar ibn al-Khattab, concerning His saying, {And when the girl buried alive is asked, for what sin was she killed}, he said: Qais ibn Asim came to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and said, "O Messenger of Allah, I buried daughters of mine alive during the Jahiliyyah (the pre-Islamic age of ignorance)." He said, "Free a slave for each one of them." He said, "O Messenger of Allah, I am an owner of camels." He said, "Then sacrifice a badanah (a camel or cow offered as a sacrificial animal) for each one of them."

The hafiz Abu Bakr al-Bazzar said: Abd al-Razzaq was contradicted regarding it, and we did not record it except from al-Husayn ibn Mahdi, from him.

Ibn Abi Hatim also related it, saying: Abu Abd Allah al-Zahrani informed us, in what he wrote to me, saying: Abd al-Razzaq narrated to us, and he mentioned it with the same chain of transmission, the same as it, except that he said, "I buried eight daughters of mine alive during the Jahiliyyah." And he said at the end of it, "Then offer a badanah, if you wish, for each one of them." Then he said:

[chain] Khalifa ibn Husayn said: Qais ibn Asim came to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and said, "O Messenger of Allah, I buried alive twelve daughters of mine during the Jahiliyyah, or thirteen." He said, "Free a number of souls (nasam, living persons, i.e. slaves) equal to them." He said: So he freed a number of souls equal to them. Then when the following year came, he brought a hundred she-camels and said, "O Messenger of Allah, this is the charity of my people, following what I did to the Muslims." Ali ibn Abi Talib said: We used to let them out to pasture, and we used to call them al-Qaysiyyah.

* *

And His saying, {And when the scrolls are spread out}: al-Dahhak said: Every person is given his record in his right hand or in his left hand. And Qatadah said: [Your record], O son of Adam, is dictated into, then it is folded up, then it is spread out before you on the Day of Resurrection. So let a man look at what he dictates into his record.

* *

And His saying, {And when the sky is stripped away}: Mujahid said: It is pulled off. Al-Suddi said: It is uncovered. Al-Dahhak said: It peels away and departs.

* *

And His saying, {And when the Hellfire is set ablaze}: al-Suddi said: It is made intensely hot. Qatadah said: It is kindled. He said: It is only kindled by the wrath of Allah and the sins of the children of Adam.

* *

And His saying, {And when Paradise is brought near}: al-Dahhak, Abu Malik, Qatadah, and al-Rabi' ibn Khuthaym said: That is, it drew near to its people.

* *

And His saying, {a soul will know what it has brought}: This is the jawab (the apodosis, the answer to the preceding conditional clauses), meaning: when these matters occur, then every soul will know what it did, and that will be presented to it, just as the Almighty said, {On the Day when every soul will find what it has done of good brought forward, and what it has done of evil, it will wish that there were a great distance between it and that} [Aal Imran: 30]. And the Almighty said, {Man will be informed that Day of what he put forward and what he left behind} [al-Qiyamah: 13].

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Zayd ibn Aslam, from his father, said: When {When the sun is wrapped up} was revealed, Umar said, when he reached {a soul will know what it has brought}, he said, "It is for this that the report was set flowing."

### Source (Arabic) — review against this

تَفْسِيرُ سُورَةِ التَّكْوِيرِ

وَهِيَ مَكِّيَّةٌ.

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، أَخْبَرَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ بَحِيرٍ الْقَاصُّ: أَنَّ عَبْدَ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنَ يَزِيدَ الصَّنْعَانِيَّ أَخْبَرَهُ: أَنَّهُ سَمِعَ ابْنَ عُمَرَ يَقُولُ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "مَنْ سَرَّه أَنْ يَنْظُرَ إِلَى يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ كَأَنَّهُ رأيُ عَيْنٍ فَلْيَقْرَأْ: " إِذَا الشَّمْسُ كُوِّرَتْ "، وَ " وإذَا السَّمَاءُ انفَطَرَتْ "، وَ " إِذَا السَّمَاءُ انْشَقَّتْ ".

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ، عَنِ الْعَبَّاسِ بْنِ عَبْدِ الْعَظِيمِ الْعَنْبَرِيِّ، عَنْ عَبْدِ الرَّزَّاقِ، بِهِ .

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

* *

قَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿إِذَا الشَّمْسُ كُوِّرَتْ﴾ يَعْنِي: أَظْلَمَتْ. وَقَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنْهُ: ذَهَبَتْ، وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: اضمحَلّت وذَهَبت. وَكَذَا قَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ.

وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: ذَهَبَ ضَوْءُهَا. وَقَالَ سَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ: ﴿كُوِّرَتْ﴾ غُوّرت.

وَقَالَ الرَّبِيعُ بْنُ خُثَيم: ﴿كُوِّرَتْ﴾ يَعْنِي: رُمِيَ بِهَا.

وَقَالَ أَبُو صَالِحٍ: ﴿كُوِّرَتْ﴾ أُلْقِيَتْ. وَعَنْهُ أَيْضًا: نُكِّسَتْ. وَقَالَ زَيْدُ بْنُ أَسْلَمَ: تَقَعُ فِي الْأَرْضِ.

قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: وَالصَّوَابُ مِنَ الْقَوْلِ عِنْدَنَا فِي ذَلِكَ أَنَّ التَّكْوِيرَ جَمعُ الشَّيْءِ بَعْضِهِ إِلَى بَعْضٍ، وَمِنْهُ تَكْوِيرُ الْعِمَامَةِ [وَهُوَ لَفُّهَا عَلَى الرَّأْسِ، وَكَتَكْوِيرِ الْكَارِهِ، وَهِيَ] جَمْعُ الثِّيَابِ بَعْضُهَا إِلَى بَعْضٍ، فَمَعْنَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿كُوَّرَتْ﴾ جَمْعُ بَعْضِهَا إِلَى بَعْضٍ، ثُمَّ لُفَّتْ فَرَمَى بِهَا، وإذا فعل بها ذلك ذهب ضوءها .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو سَعِيدٍ الْأَشَجُّ وَعَمْرُو بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الْأَوْدِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو أُسَامَةَ، عَنْ مُجَالِدٍ، عَنْ شَيْخٍ مِنْ بَجِيلة، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿إِذَا الشَّمْسُ كُوِّرَتْ﴾ قَالَ: يُكَوِّرُ اللَّهُ الشَّمْسَ وَالْقَمَرَ وَالنُّجُومَ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ فِي الْبَحْرِ، وَيَبْعَثُ اللَّهُ رِيحًا دَبُّورًا فَتُضْرِمُهَا نَارًا. وَكَذَا قَالَ عَامِرُ الشَّعْبِيُّ. ثُمَّ قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ:

حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو صَالِحٍ، حَدَّثَنِي مُعَاوِيَةُ بْنُ صَالِحٍ، عَنْ ابْنِ يَزِيدَ بْنِ أَبِي مَرْيَمَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عليه وسلم قَالَ فِي قَوْلِ اللَّهِ: ﴿إِذَا الشَّمْسُ كُوِّرَتْ﴾ قَالَ: "كُوِّرَتْ فِي جَهَنَّمَ" .

وَقَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو يَعْلَى فِي مُسْنَدِهِ: حَدَّثَنَا مُوسَى بْنُ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ حَيَّان، حَدَّثَنَا دُرُسْتُ بْنُ زِيَادٍ، حَدَّثَنَا يَزِيدُ الرَّقَاشِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا أَنَسٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "الشَّمْسُ وَالْقَمَرُ ثَوْرَانِ عَقِيرَانِ فِي النَّارِ" .

هَذَا حَدِيثٌ ضَعِيفٌ؛ لِأَنَّ يَزِيدَ الرَّقَاشِيَّ ضَعِيفٌ، وَالَّذِي رَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ فِي الصَّحِيحِ بِدُونِ هَذِهِ الزِّيَادَةِ، ثُمَّ قَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ:

حَدَّثَنَا مُسَدَّد، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الْعَزِيزِ بْنُ الْمُخْتَارِ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ الداناجُ، حَدَّثَنِي أَبُو سَلَمَةَ بْنُ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ: "الشَّمْسُ وَالْقَمَرُ يُكَوَّرَانِ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ" .

انْفَرَدَ بِهِ الْبُخَارِيُّ وَهَذَا لَفْظُهُ، وَإِنَّمَا أَخْرَجَهُ فِي كِتَابِ "بِدْءِ الْخَلْقِ"، وَكَانَ جَدِيرًا أَنْ يَذْكُرَهُ هَاهُنَا أَوْ يُكَرِّرَهُ، كَمَا هِيَ عَادَتُهُ فِي أَمْثَالِهِ! وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ الْبَزَّارُ فَجَوّد إِيرَادَهُ فَقَالَ:

حَدَّثَنَا إِبْرَاهِيمُ بْنُ زِيَادٍ الْبَغْدَادِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا يُونُسُ بْنُ مُحَمَّدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الْعَزِيزِ بْنُ الْمُخْتَارِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الدَّانَاجِ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ أَبَا سَلَمَةَ بْنَ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ خَالِدِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الْقَسْرِيَّ فِي هَذَا الْمَسْجِدِ-مَسْجِدِ الْكُوفَةِ، وَجَاءَ الْحَسَنُ فَجَلَسَ إِلَيْهِ فَحدّث قَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو هُرَيْرَةَ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "إِنَّ الشَّمْسَ وَالْقَمَرَ نُورَانِ فِي النَّارِ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ". فَقَالَ الْحَسَنُ: وَمَا ذَنْبُهُمَا؟ فَقَالَ: أُحَدِّثُكَ عَنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ وَتَقُولُ: أَحْسَبُهُ قَالَ: وَمَا ذَنْبُهُمَا.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: لَا يُرْوَى عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيرة إِلَّا مِنْ هَذَا الْوَجْهِ، وَلَمْ يَرْوِ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ الدَّانَاجُ عَنْ أَبِي سَلَمَةَ سِوَى هَذَا الْحَدِيثِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِذَا النُّجُومُ انْكَدَرَتْ﴾ أَيِ: انْتَثَرَتْ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَإِذَا الْكَوَاكِبُ انْتَثَرَتْ﴾ [الِانْفِطَارِ: ٢] ، وَأَصْلُ الِانْكِدَارِ: الِانْصِبَابُ.

قَالَ الرَّبِيعِ بْنِ أَنَسٍ، عَنْ أَبِي الْعَالِيَةِ، عَنْ أُبَيِّ بْنِ كَعْبٍ قَالَ: سِتُّ آيَاتٍ قَبْلَ يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ، بَيْنَا النَّاسُ فِي أَسْوَاقِهِمْ إِذْ ذَهَبَ ضَوْءُ الشَّمْسِ، فَبَيْنَمَا هُمْ كَذَلِكَ إِذْ تَنَاثَرَتِ النُّجُومُ، فَبَيْنَمَا هم كذلك إذ وَقَعَتِ الْجِبَالُ عَلَى وَجْهِ الْأَرْضِ فَتَحَرَّكَتْ وَاضْطَرَبَتْ وَاخْتَلَطَتْ، فَفَزِعَتِ الْجِنُّ إِلَى الْإِنْسِ وَالْإِنْسُ إِلَى الْجِنِّ، وَاخْتَلَطَتِ الدَّوَابُّ وَالطَّيْرُ وَالْوُحُوشُ، فَمَاجُوا بَعْضُهُمْ فِي بَعْضٍ: ﴿وَإِذَا الْوُحُوشُ حُشِرَتْ﴾ قَالَ: اخْتَلَطَتْ، ﴿وَإِذَا الْعِشَارُ عُطِّلَتْ﴾ قَالَ: أَهْمَلَهَا أَهْلُهَا، ﴿وَإِذَا الْبِحَارُ سُجِّرَتْ﴾ قَالَ: قَالَتِ الْجِنُّ: نَحْنُ نَأْتِيكُمْ بِالْخَبَرِ. قَالَ: فَانْطَلَقُوا إِلَى الْبَحْرِ فَإِذَا هُوَ نَارٌ تَأَجَّجُ، قَالَ: فَبَيْنَمَا هُمْ كَذَلِكَ إِذْ تَصَدَّعَتِ الْأَرْضُ صَدْعَةً وَاحِدَةً إِلَى الْأَرْضِ السَّابِعَةِ السُّفْلَى وَإِلَى السَّمَاءِ السَّابِعَةِ الْعُلْيَا، قَالَ فَبَيْنَمَا هُمْ كَذَلِكَ إِذْ جَاءَتْهُمُ الرِّيحُ فَأَمَاتَتْهُمْ.

رَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ -وَهَذَا لَفْظُهُ-وَابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، بِبَعْضِهِ، وَهَكَذَا قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ وَالرَّبِيعُ بْنُ خُثَيم ، وَالْحَسَنُ الْبَصْرِيُّ، وَأَبُو صَالِحٍ، وَحَمَّادُ بْنُ أَبِي سُلَيْمَانَ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَإِذَا النُّجُومُ انْكَدَرَتْ﴾ ي: تَنَاثَرَتْ.

وَقَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿وَإِذَا النُّجُومُ انْكَدَرَتْ﴾ أَيْ: تَغَيَّرَتْ. وَقَالَ يَزِيدُ بْنُ أَبِي مَرْيَمَ عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ: ﴿وَإِذَا النُّجُومُ انْكَدَرَتْ﴾ قَالَ: "انْكَدَرَتْ فِي جَهَنَّمَ، وَكُلُّ مَنْ عَبَدَ مَنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ فَهُوَ فِي جَهَنَّمَ، إِلَّا مَا كَانَ مِنْ عِيسَى وَأُمِّهِ، وَلَوْ رَضِيَا أَنْ يُعبَدا لَدَخَلَاهَا". رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ بِالْإِسْنَادِ الْمُتَقَدِّمِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِذَا الْجِبَالُ سُيِّرَتْ﴾ أَيْ: زَالَتْ عَنْ أَمَاكِنِهَا ونُسِفت، فَتَرَكَتِ الْأَرْضَ قَاعًا صَفْصَفًا.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِذَا الْعِشَارُ عُطِّلَتْ﴾ قَالَ عِكْرِمَةُ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ: عِشَارُ الْإِبِلِ. قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: ﴿عُطِّلَت﴾ تُرِكَتْ وسُيّبت.

وَقَالَ أُبَيُّ بْنُ كَعْبٍ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ: أَهْمَلَهَا أَهْلُهَا: وَقَالَ الرَّبِيعُ بْنُ خُثَيم لَمْ تَحْلِبْ وَلَمْ تُصَرّ، تَخَلَّى مِنْهَا أَرْبَابُهَا.

وَقَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ: تُرِكَتْ لَا رَاعِيَ لَهَا.

وَالْمَعْنَى فِي هَذَا كُلِّهِ مُتَقَارِبٌ. وَالْمَقْصُودُ أَنَّ الْعِشَارَ مِنَ الْإِبِلِ-وَهِيَ: خِيَارُهَا وَالْحَوَامِلُ مِنْهَا الَّتِي قَدْ وَصَلت فِي حَمْلِهَا إِلَى الشَّهْرِ الْعَاشِرِ-وَاحِدُهَا عُشَراء، وَلَا يَزَالُ ذَلِكَ اسْمُهَا حَتَّى تَضَعَ-قَدِ اشْتَغَلَ النَّاسُ عَنْهَا وَعَنْ كَفَالَتِهَا وَالِانْتِفَاعِ بِهَا، بَعْدَ مَا كَانُوا أَرْغَبَ شَيْءٍ فِيهَا، بِمَا دَهَمهم مِنَ الْأَمْرِ الْعَظِيمِ المُفظع الْهَائِلِ، وَهُوَ أَمْرُ الْقِيَامَةِ وَانْعِقَادُ أَسْبَابِهَا، وَوُقُوعِ مُقَدِّمَاتِهَا.

وَقِيلَ: بَلْ يَكُونُ ذَلِكَ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ، يَرَاهَا أَصْحَابُهَا كَذَلِكَ وَلَا سَبِيلَ لَهُمْ إِلَيْهَا. وَقَدْ قِيلَ فِي الْعِشَارِ: إِنَّهَا السَّحَابُ يُعطَّل عَنِ الْمَسِيرِ بَيْنَ السَّمَاءِ وَالْأَرْضِ، لِخَرَابِ الدُّنْيَا. وَ [قَدْ] قِيلَ: إِنَّهَا الْأَرْضُ الَّتِي تُعشَّر. وَقِيلَ: إِنَّهَا الدِّيَارُ الَّتِي كَانَتْ تَسْكُنُ تُعَطَّل لِذَهَابِ أَهْلِهَا. حَكَى هَذِهِ الْأَقْوَالَ كُلَّهَا الْإِمَامُ أَبُو عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الْقُرْطُبِيُّ فِي كِتَابِهِ "التَّذْكِرَةُ"، وَرَجَّحَ أَنَّهَا الإبل، وعزاه إلى أكثر الناس .

قُلْتُ: بَلْ لَا يُعْرَفُ عَنِ السَّلَفِ وَالْأَئِمَّةِ سِوَاهُ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِذَا الْوُحُوشُ حُشِرَتْ﴾ أَيْ: جُمِعَتْ. كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَمَا مِنْ دَابَّةٍ فِي الأرْضِ وَلا طَائِرٍ يَطِيرُ بِجَنَاحَيْهِ إِلا أُمَمٌ أَمْثَالُكُمْ مَا فَرَّطْنَا فِي الْكِتَابِ مِنْ شَيْءٍ ثُمَّ إِلَى رَبِّهِمْ يُحْشَرُونَ﴾ [الْأَنْعَامِ: ٣٨] . قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: يُحْشَرُ كُلُّ شَيْءٍ حَتَّى الذُّبَابُ. رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ. وَكَذَا قَالَ الرَّبِيعُ بْنُ خُثَيم وَالسُّدِّيُّ، وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ. وَكَذَا قَالَ قَتَادَةُ فِي تَفْسِيرِ هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ: إِنَّ هَذِهِ الْخَلَائِقَ [مُوَافِيَةً] فَيَقْضِي اللَّهُ فِيهَا مَا يَشَاءُ.

وَقَالَ عِكْرِمَةُ: حَشْرُهَا: مَوْتُهَا.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنِي عَلِيُّ بْنُ مُسْلِمٍ الطُّوسِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبَّادُ بْنُ الْعَوَّامِ، أَخْبَرَنَا حُصَين، عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَإِذَا الْوُحُوشُ حُشِرَتْ﴾ قَالَ: حَشرُ الْبَهَائِمِ: مَوْتُهَا، وَحَشْرُ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ الْمَوْتُ غَيْرُهُ الْجِنُّ وَالْإِنْسُ، فَإِنَّهُمَا يُوقَفَانِ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ.

حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو كُرَيْب، حَدَّثَنَا وَكِيع، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ أَبِي يَعْلَى، عَنِ الرَّبِيعِ بْنِ خُثَيم : ﴿وَإِذَا الْوُحُوشُ حُشِرَتْ﴾ قَالَ: أَتَى عَلَيْهَا أَمْرُ اللَّهِ. قَالَ سُفْيَانُ: قَالَ أَبِي: فَذَكَرْتُهُ لِعِكْرِمَةَ، فَقَالَ: قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: حَشْرُهَا: مَوْتُهَا.

وَقَدْ تَقَدَّمَ عَنْ أُبَيِّ بْنِ كَعْبٍ أَنَّهُ قَالَ: ﴿وَإِذَا الْوُحُوشُ حُشِرَتْ﴾ اخْتَلَطَتْ.

قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: وَالْأُولَى قَولُ مَنْ قَالَ: ﴿حُشِرَت﴾ جُمعت، قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَالطَّيْرَ مَحْشُورَةً﴾ [ص: ١٩] ، أَيْ: مَجْمُوعَةً.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِذَا الْبِحَارُ سُجِّرَتْ﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا يَعْقُوبُ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ عُلَية، عَنْ دَاوُدَ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ الْمُسَيَّبِ قَالَ: قَالَ عَلِيٌّ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، لِرَجُلٍ مِنَ الْيَهُودِ: أَيْنَ جَهَنَّمُ؟ قَالَ: الْبَحْرُ. فَقَالَ: مَا أَرَاهُ إِلَّا صَادِقًا. ﴿وَالْبَحْرِ الْمَسْجُورِ﴾ [الطَّوْرِ: ٦] ، ﴿وَإِذَا الْبِحَارُ سُجِّرَتْ﴾ [مُخَفَّفَةٌ] .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ: يُرْسِلُ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهَا الدَّبُّورِ فَتُسَعِّرُهَا، وَتَصِيرُ نَارًا تَأَجَّجُ، وَقَدْ تَقَدَّمَ الْكَلَامَ عَلَى ذَلِكَ عِنْدَ قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَالْبَحْرِ الْمَسْجُورِ﴾

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ الْحُسَيْنِ بْنِ الْجُنَيْدِ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو طَاهِرٍ، حَدَّثَنِي عَبْدُ الْجَبَّارِ بْنُ سُلَيْمَانَ أَبُو سُلَيْمَانَ النَّفَّاطُ-شيخٌ صَالِح يُشبهُ مالكَ بْنَ أَنَسٍ-عَنْ مُعَاوِيَةَ بْنِ سَعِيدٍ قَالَ: إِنَّ هَذَا الْبَحْرَ بَرَكَةٌ-يَعْنِي بَحْرَ الرُّوم-وَسَطَ الْأَرْضِ، وَالْأَنْهَارُ كُلُّهَا تَصُبُّ فِيهِ، وَالْبَحْرُ الْكَبِيرُ يَصُبُّ فِيهِ، وَأَسْفَلُهُ آبَارٌ مُطَبَّقَةٌ بِالنُّحَاسِ، فَإِذَا كَانَ يَوْمُ الْقِيَامَةِ أسجر.

وَهَذَا أَثَرٌ غَرِيبٌ عَجِيبٌ. وَفِي سُنَنِ أَبِي دَاوُدَ: "لَا يَرْكَبُ الْبَحْرَ إِلَّا حَاجٌّ أَوْ مُعْتَمِرٌ أَوْ غَازٍ، فَإِنَّ تَحْتَ الْبَحْرِ نَارًا، وَتَحْتَ النَّارِ بَحْرًا" الْحَدِيثَ، وَقَدْ تَقَدَّمَ الْكَلَامُ عَلَيْهِ فِي سُورَةِ "فَاطِرٍ" .

وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَالْحَسَنُ بْنُ مُسْلِمٍ: ﴿سُجِّرَت﴾ أُوقِدَتْ. وَقَالَ الْحَسَنُ: يَبِسَتْ. وَقَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ، وَقَتَادَةُ: غَاضَ مَاؤُهَا فَذَهَبَ وَلَمْ يُبْقِ فِيهَا قَطْرَةً. وَقَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ أَيْضًا: ﴿سُجِّرَتْ﴾ فُجِّرَتْ. وَقَالَ السُّدِّيُّ: فُتِحَتْ وَسُيِّرَتْ. وَقَالَ الرَّبِيعُ بْنُ خُثَيم ﴿سُجِّرَتْ﴾ فَاضَتْ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِذَا النُّفُوسُ زُوِّجَتْ﴾ أَيْ: جُمِعَ كُلُّ شَكْلٍ إِلَى نَظِيرِهِ، كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿احْشُرُوا الَّذِينَ ظَلَمُوا وَأَزْوَاجَهُمْ﴾ [الصَّافَّاتِ: ٢٢] .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ الصَّبَّاحِ الْبَزَّارُ، حَدَّثَنَا الْوَلِيدُ بْنُ أَبِي ثَوْرٍ، عَنْ سمَاك، عَنِ النُّعْمَانِ بْنِ بَشِيرٍ أَنَّهُ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: ﴿وَإِذَا النُّفُوسُ زُوِّجَتْ﴾ قَالَ: الضُّرَبَاءُ، كُلُّ رَجُلٍ مَعَ كُلِّ قَوْمٍ كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ عَمَلَهُ"، وَذَلِكَ بِأَنَّ اللَّهَ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ يَقُولُ: ﴿وَكُنْتُمْ أَزْوَاجًا ثَلاثَةً فَأَصْحَابُ الْمَيْمَنَةِ مَا أَصْحَابُ الْمَيْمَنَةِ وَأَصْحَابُ الْمَشْأَمَةِ مَا أَصْحَابُ الْمَشْأَمَةِ وَالسَّابِقُونَ السَّابِقُونَ﴾ [الْوَاقِعَةِ: ٧ -١٠] ، قَالَ: هُمُ الضُّرَبَاءُ .

ثُمَّ رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ مِنْ طُرُقٍ أُخَرَ، عَنْ سِمَاكُ بْنُ حَرْبٍ، عَنِ النُّعْمَانِ بْنِ بَشِيرٍ أَنَّ عُمَر خَطَبَ النَّاسَ فَقَرَأَ: ﴿وَإِذَا النُّفُوسُ زُوِّجَتْ﴾ فَقَالَ: تَزَوّجها: أَنْ تُؤَلَّفَ كُلُّ شِيعَةٍ إِلَى شِيعَتِهِمْ. وَفِي رِوَايَةٍ: هُمَا الرَّجُلَانِ يَعْمَلَانِ الْعَمَلَ فَيَدْخُلَانِ بِهِ الْجَنَّةَ أَوِ النَّارَ .

وَفِي رِوَايَةٍ عَنِ النُّعْمَانِ قَالَ: سُئِلَ عُمَرُ عَنْ قَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَإِذَا النُّفُوسُ زُوِّجَتْ﴾ فَقَالَ: يُقْرَنُ بَيْنَ الرَّجُلِ الصَّالِحِ مَعَ الرَّجُلِ الصَّالِحِ، وَيُقْرَنُ بَيْنَ الرَّجُلِ السُّوءِ مَعَ الرَّجُلِ السُّوءِ فِي النَّارِ، فَذَلِكَ تَزْوِيجُ الْأَنْفُسِ.

وَفِي رِوَايَةٍ عَنِ النُّعْمَانِ أَنَّ عُمَرَ قَالَ لِلنَّاسِ: مَا تَقُولُونَ فِي تَفْسِيرِ هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ: ﴿وَإِذَا النُّفُوسُ زُوِّجَتْ﴾ ؟ فَسَكَتُوا. قَالَ: وَلَكِنْ هُوَ الرَّجُلُ يُزَوَّجُ نَظِيرَهُ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ، وَالرَّجُلُ يُزَوَّجُ نَظِيرَهُ مِنْ أَهْلِ النَّارِ، ثُمَّ قَرَأَ: ﴿احْشُرُوا الَّذِينَ ظَلَمُوا وَأَزْوَاجَهُمْ﴾

وَقَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَإِذَا النُّفُوسُ زُوِّجَتْ﴾ قَالَ: ذَلِكَ حِينَ يَكُونُ النَّاسُ أَزْوَاجًا ثَلَاثَةً.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي نَجيح، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ: ﴿وَإِذَا النُّفُوسُ زُوِّجَتْ﴾ قَالَ: الأمثال من الناس جمع بَيْنَهُمْ. وَكَذَا قَالَ الرَّبِيعُ بْنُ خُثَيم وَالْحَسَنُ، وَقَتَادَةُ. وَاخْتَارَهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، وَهُوَ الصَّحِيحُ.

قَوْلٌ آخَرُ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَإِذَا النُّفُوسُ زُوِّجَتْ﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ:

حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ الْحُسَيْنِ بْنِ الْجُنَيْدِ، حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ، حَدَّثَنِي أَبِي، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ أَشْعَثَ [بْنِ سَوَّارٍ] ، عَنْ جَعْفَرٍ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: يَسِيلُ وَادٍ مَنْ أَصْلِ الْعَرْشِ مِنْ مَاءٍ فِيمَا بَيْنَ الصَّيْحَتَيْنِ، وَمِقْدَارُ مَا بَيْنَهُمَا أَرْبَعُونَ عَامًا، فَيَنْبُتُ مِنْهُ كُلُّ خَلْقٍ بَلِيَ، مِنَ الْإِنْسَانِ أَوْ طَيْرٍ أَوْ دَابَّةٍ، وَلَوْ مَرَّ عَلَيْهِمْ مَارٌّ قَدْ عَرَفَهُمْ قَبْلَ ذَلِكَ لَعَرَفَهُمْ عَلَى الْأَرْضِ. قَدْ نَبَتُوا، ثُمَّ تُرسَل الْأَرْوَاحُ فَتَزَوَّجُ الْأَجْسَادُ، فَذَلِكَ قَوْلُ اللَّهِ تَعَالَى : ﴿وَإِذَا النُّفُوسُ زُوِّجَتْ﴾

وَكَذَا قَالَ أَبُو الْعَالِيَةِ، وَعِكْرِمَةُ، وَسَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ، وَالشَّعْبِيُّ، وَالْحَسَنُ الْبَصْرِيُّ أَيْضًا فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَإِذَا النُّفُوسُ زُوِّجَتْ﴾ أَيْ: زُوِّجَتْ بِالْأَبْدَانِ. وَقِيلَ: زُوِّجَ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ بِالْحُورِ الْعِينِ، وَزُوِّجَ الْكَافِرُونَ بِالشَّيَاطِينِ. حَكَاهُ الْقُرْطُبِيُّ فِي "التَّذْكِرَةِ" .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِذَا الْمَوْءُودَةُ سُئِلَتْ بِأَيِّ ذَنْبٍ قُتِلَتْ﴾ هَكَذَا قِرَاءَةُ الْجُمْهُورِ: ﴿سُئلَت﴾ وَالْمَوْءُودَةُ هِيَ الَّتِي كَانَ أَهْلُ الْجَاهِلِيَّةِ يَدُسُّونَهَا فِي التُّرَابِ كَرَاهِيَةَ الْبَنَاتِ، فَيَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ تُسْأَلُ الْمَوْءُودَةُ عَلَى أَيِّ ذَنْبٍ قُتِلَتْ، لِيَكُونَ ذَلِكَ تَهْدِيدًا لِقَاتِلِهَا، فَإِذَا سُئِلَ الْمَظْلُومُ فَمَا ظَنُّ الظَّالِمِ إِذًا؟!

وَقَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿وَإِذَا الْمَوْءُودَةُ سُئِلَتْ﴾ أَيْ: سَأَلَتْ. وَكَذَا قَالَ أَبُو الضُّحَى: "سَأَلَتْ" أَيْ: طَالَبَتْ بِدَمِهَا. وَعَنِ السُّدِّيِّ، وَقَتَادَةَ، مِثْلُهُ .

وَقَدْ وَرَدَتْ أَحَادِيثُ تَتَعَلَّقُ بِالْمَوْءُودَةِ، فَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ:

حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ يَزِيدَ، حَدَّثَنَا سَعِيدٌ بْنُ أَبِي أَيُّوبَ، حَدَّثَنِي أَبُو الْأَسْوَدِ-وَهُوَ: مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ نَوْفَلٍ-عَنْ عُرْوَةَ، عَنْ عَائِشَةَ، عَنْ جُدَامة بَنْتِ وَهْبٍ-أُخْتِ عُكَّاشَةَ-قَالَتْ حضرتُ رسولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ فِي نَاسٍ وَهُوَ يَقُولُ: " لَقَدْ هَمَمْتُ أَنْ أَنْهَى عَنِ الغيلَة، فَنَظَرْتُ فِي الرُّومِ وَفَارِسَ فَإِذَا هُمْ يُغيلُونَ أَوْلَادَهُمْ، وَلَا يَضُرُّ أَوْلَادَهُمْ ذَلِكَ شَيْئًا". ثُمَّ سَأَلُوهُ عَنِ الْعَزْلِ، فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: " ذَلِكَ الْوَأْدُ الْخَفِيُّ، وَهُوَ الْمَوْءُودَةُ سُئِلَتْ".

وَرَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ الْمُقْرِئِ-وَهُوَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ يَزِيدَ-عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ أَبِي أَيُّوبَ وَرَوَاهُ أَيْضًا ابْنُ مَاجَهْ، عَنْ أَبِي بَكْرِ بْنِ أَبِي شَيْبَةَ، عَنْ يَحْيَى بْنِ إِسْحَاقَ السَّيْلَحِينِيِّ، عَنْ يَحْيَى بْنِ أَيُّوبَ وَرَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ أَيْضًا وَأَبُو دَاوُدَ وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ، وَالنَّسَائِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ مَالِكِ بْنِ أَنَسٍ، ثَلَاثَتُهُمْ عَنْ أَبِي الْأَسْوَدِ، بِهِ .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ أَبِي عَدي، عَنْ دَاوُدَ بْنِ أَبِي هِنْدٍ، عَنِ الشَّعْبِيِّ، عَنْ عَلْقَمَةَ، عَنْ سَلَمَةَ بْنِ يَزِيدَ الجُعْفي قَالَ: انطلقتُ أَنَا وَأَخِي إِلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقُلْنَا: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، إِنَّ أُمَّنَا مُلَيْكَةَ كَانَتْ تَصل الرَّحِمَ وَتُقِرِّي الضَّيْفَ، وَتَفْعَلُ [وَتَفْعَلُ] هَلَكَتْ فِي الْجَاهِلِيَّةِ، فَهَلْ ذَلِكَ نَافِعُهَا شَيْئًا؟ قَالَ: "لَا". قُلْنَا: فَإِنَّهَا كَانَتْ وَأَدَتْ أُخْتًا لَنَا فِي الْجَاهِلِيَّةِ، فَهَلْ ذَلِكَ نافعُها شَيْئًا؟ قَالَ: "الوائدةُ والموءودةُ فِي النَّارِ، إِلَّا أَنْ يدركَ الوائدةَ الإسلامُ، فَيَعْفُوَ اللَّهُ عَنْهَا".

وَرَوَاهُ النَّسَائِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ دَاوُدَ بْنِ أَبِي هِنْدٍ، بِهِ .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ سِنَانٍ الْوَاسِطِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو أَحْمَدَ الزُّبَيْرِيُّ ، حَدَّثَنَا إِسْرَائِيلُ، عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ عَلْقَمَةَ وَأَبِي الْأَحْوَصِ، عَنِ ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "الْوَائِدَةُ وَالْمَوْءُودَةُ فِي النَّارِ" .

وَقَالَ أَحْمَدُ أَيْضًا: حَدَّثَنَا إِسْحَاقُ الْأَزْرَقُ، أَخْبَرَنَا عَوْفٌ، حَدَّثَتْنِي حَسْنَاءُ ابْنَةُ مُعَاوِيَةَ الصُّرَيمية، عَنْ عَمِّهَا قَالَ: قُلْتُ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، مَنْ فِي الْجَنَّةِ؟ قَالَ: "النَّبِيُّ فِي الْجَنَّةِ وَالشَّهِيدُ فِي الْجَنَّةِ وَالْمَوْلُودُ فِي الْجَنَّةِ وَالْمَوْءُودَةُ فِي الْجَنَّةِ" .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا مُسْلِمُ بْنُ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، حَدَّثَنَا قُرَّةُ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ الْحَسَنَ يَقُولُ: قِيلَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، مَنْ فِي الْجَنَّةِ؟ قَالَ: "الْمَوْءُودَةُ فِي الْجَنَّةِ".

هَذَا حَدِيثٌ مُرْسَلٌ مِنْ مَرَاسِيلِ الْحَسَنِ، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ قَبِلَهُ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنِي أَبُو عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الظَّهْرَانِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا حَفْصُ بْنُ عُمَرَ الْعَدَنِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا الْحَكَمُ بْنُ أَبَانٍ، عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ قَالَ: قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: أَطْفَالُ الْمُشْرِكِينَ فِي الْجَنَّةِ، فَمَنْ زَعَمَ أَنَّهُمْ فِي النَّارِ فَقَدْ كَذَبَ، يَقُولُ اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ ﴿وَإِذَا الْمَوْءُودَةُ سُئِلَتْ بِأَيِّ ذَنْبٍ قُتِلَتْ﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: هِيَ الْمَدْفُونَةُ.

وَقَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ: أَخْبَرَنَا إِسْرَائِيلُ، عَنْ سمَاك بْنُ حَرْبٍ، عَنِ النُّعْمَانِ بْنِ بَشِيرٍ، عَنْ عُمَرَ بْنِ الْخَطَّابِ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَإِذَا الْمَوْءُودَةُ سُئِلَتْ [بأَيّ ذَنْبٍ قُتِلَتْ] ﴾ ، قَالَ: جَاءَ قَيْسُ بْنُ عَاصِمٍ إِلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، إِنِّي وَأَدْتُ بَنَاتٍ لِي فِي الْجَاهِلِيَّةِ، فَقَالَ: "أَعْتِقْ عَنْ كُلِّ وَاحِدَةٍ مِنْهُنَّ رَقَبَةً". قَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، إِنِّي صَاحِبُ إِبِلٍ؟ قَالَ: "فَانْحَرْ عَنْ كُلِّ وَاحِدَةٍ مِنْهُنَّ بَدَنَةً".

قَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو بَكْرٍ الْبَزَّارُ: خُولِفَ فِيهِ عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، وَلَمْ نَكْتُبْهُ إِلَّا عَنِ الْحُسَيْنِ بْنِ مَهْدِيٍّ، عَنْهُ .

وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ فَقَالَ: أَخْبَرَنَا أَبُو عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الظَّهْرَانِيُّ -فِيمَا كَتَبَ إِلَيَّ-قَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ فَذَكَرَهُ بِإِسْنَادِهِ مِثْلَهُ، إِلَّا أَنَّهُ قَالَ: "وَأَدْتُ ثَمَانِ بَنَاتٍ لِي فِي الْجَاهِلِيَّةِ". وَقَالَ فِي آخِرِهِ: "فَأَهْدِ إِنْ شِئْتَ عَنْ كُلِّ وَاحِدَةٍ بَدَنَةً". ثُمَّ قَالَ:

حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ رَجَاءٍ، حَدَّثَنَا قَيْسُ بْنُ الرَّبِيعِ، عَنِ الْأَغَرِّ بْنِ الصَّبَّاحِ، عَنْ خَلِيفَةَ بْنِ حُصَين قَالَ: قَدِمَ قَيْسُ بْنُ عَاصِمٍ عَلَى رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم فقال: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، إِنِّي وأدتُ اثْنَتَيْ عَشْرَةَ ابْنَةً لِي فِي الْجَاهِلِيَّةِ-أَوْ: ثَلَاثَ عَشْرَةَ-قَالَ :" أَعْتِقْ عَدَدَهُنَّ نَسِما". قَالَ: فَأَعْتَقَ عَدَدَهُنَّ نَسَمًا، فَلَمَّا كَانَ فِي الْعَامِ الْمُقْبِلِ جَاءَ بِمِائَةِ نَاقَةٍ، فَقَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، هَذِهِ صَدَقَةُ قُومِي عَلَى أَثَرِ مَا صَنَعْتُ بِالْمُسْلِمِينَ. قَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَالِبٍ: فَكُنَّا نُرِيحُهَا، وَنُسَمِّيهَا الْقَيْسِيَّةُ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِذَا الصُّحُفُ نُشِرَتْ﴾ قَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ: أُعْطِيَ كُلُّ إِنْسَانٍ صَحِيفَتَهُ بِيَمِينِهِ أَوْ بِشِمَالِهِ. وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: [صَحِيفَتُكَ] يَا ابْنَ آدَمَ، تُملى فِيهَا، ثُمَّ تُطْوَى، ثُمَّ تُنْشَرُ عَلَيْكَ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ، فَلْيَنْظُرْ رَجُلٌ مَاذَا يُمْلِي فِي صَحِيفَتِهِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِذَا السَّمَاءُ كُشِطَتْ﴾ قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: اجْتُذِبَتْ. وَقَالَ السُّدِّيُّ: كُشِفَتْ. وَقَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ: تَنْكَشِطُ فَتَذْهَبُ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِذَا الْجَحِيمُ سُعِّرَتْ﴾ قَالَ السُّدِّيُّ: أُحْمِيَتْ. وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: أُوقَدَتْ. قَالَ: وَإِنَّمَا يُسَعِّرُهَا غَضَبُ اللَّهِ وَخَطَايَا بَنِي آدَمَ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِذَا الْجَنَّةُ أُزْلِفَتْ﴾ قَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ، وَأَبُو مَالِكٍ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالرَّبِيعُ بْنُ خُثَيم أَيْ: قَرُبَتْ إِلَى أَهْلِهَا.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿عَلِمَتْ نَفْسٌ مَا أَحْضَرَتْ﴾ هَذَا هُوَ الْجَوَابُ، أَيْ: إِذَا وَقَعَتْ هَذِهِ الْأُمُورُ حِينَئِذٍ تَعْلَمُ كُلُّ نَفْسٍ مَا عَمِلَتْ وَأُحْضِرَ ذَلِكَ لَهَا، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿يَوْمَ تَجِدُ كُلُّ نَفْسٍ مَا عَمِلَتْ مِنْ خَيْرٍ مُحْضَرًا وَمَا عَمِلَتْ مِنْ سُوءٍ تَوَدُّ لَوْ أَنَّ بَيْنَهَا وَبَيْنَهُ أَمَدًا بَعِيدًا﴾ [آلِ عِمْرَانَ: ٣٠] . وَقَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿يُنَبَّأُ الإنْسَانُ يَوْمَئِذٍ بِمَا قَدَّمَ وَأَخَّرَ﴾ [الْقِيَامَةِ: ١٣] .

قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدَةُ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ الْمُبَارَكِ، أَخْبَرَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ مُطَرّف، عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ قَالَ: لَمَّا نَزَلَتْ: ﴿إِذَا الشَّمْسُ كُوِّرَتْ﴾ قَالَ عُمَرُ: لَمَّا بَلَغَ ﴿عَلِمَتْ نَفْسٌ مَا أَحْضَرَتْ﴾ قال: لهذا أجرىَ الحديثُ.

## 81:15-29 — verses 15-29 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Muslim narrated in his Sahih, and al-Nasa'i in his Tafsir on this verse, from the hadith of Mis'ar ibn Kidam, from al-Walid ibn Sari', from 'Amr ibn Hurayth, who said: I prayed the dawn prayer behind the Prophet ﷺ, and I heard him recite: {So I swear by al-khunnas (the retreating ones), al-jawari al-kunnas (those that run their courses and hide away), and by the night when it 'as'asa (a word used for both its approach and its withdrawal), and the dawn when it breathes}.

Al-Nasa'i also narrated it from Bundar, from Ghundar, from Shu'bah, from al-Hajjaj ibn 'Asim, from Abu al-Aswad, from 'Amr ibn Hurayth, with similar wording.

Ibn Abi Hatim and Ibn Jarir said, by the route of al-Thawri, from Abu Ishaq, from a man of Murad, from 'Ali: {al-khunnas al-jawari al-kunnas}, he said: They are the stars, they recede by day and appear at night.

Ibn Jarir said: Ibn al-Muthanna narrated to us, Muhammad ibn Ja'far narrated to us, he said: Shu'bah narrated to us, from Simak ibn Harb, I heard Khalid ibn 'Ar'arah say, I heard 'Ali, when he was asked about {al-khunnas al-jawari al-kunnas}, say: They are the stars, they recede by day and hide away at night.

And [Ibn Jarir] narrated to us: Abu Kurayb narrated to us, Waki' narrated to us, from Isra'il, from Simak, from Khalid, from 'Ali, who said: They are the stars.

This is a good, sound chain of transmission up to Khalid ibn 'Ar'arah, who is al-Sahmi al-Kufi. Abu Hatim al-Razi said: He narrated from 'Ali, and Simak and al-Qasim ibn 'Awf al-Shaybani narrated from him, and he did not mention any jarh (discrediting) or ta'dil (validating) regarding him. And Allah knows best.

Yunus narrated, from Abu Ishaq, from al-Harith, from 'Ali: that they are the stars. Ibn Abi Hatim narrated it, and likewise it was narrated from Ibn 'Abbas, Mujahid, al-Hasan, Qatadah, al-Suddi, and others: that they are the stars.

Ibn Jarir said: Muhammad ibn Bashshar narrated to us, Hawdhah ibn Khalifah narrated to us, 'Awf narrated to us, from Bakr ibn 'Abdullah, concerning His saying: {al-khunnas al-jawari al-kunnas}, he said: They are the shining stars (al-darari) that run their courses, heading toward the east.

Some of the imams said: the stars are only called "al-khunnas" in the state of their rising, then they are "jawari" (running) in their orbit, and in the state of their setting they are called "kunnas" (hiding), from the saying of the Arabs: "the gazelle took refuge in its kinasah (den, lair)," meaning when it disappeared into it.

Al-A'mash said, from Ibrahim, who said: 'Abdullah said, [regarding] {So I swear by al-khunnas}: he said: the wild cattle. And likewise al-Thawri said, from Abu Ishaq, from Abu Maysarah, from 'Abdullah: {So I swear by al-khunnas, al-jawari al-kunnas}. What are they, O 'Amr? I said: The cattle. He said: And that is what I think too.

And likewise Yunus narrated from Abu Ishaq, from his father.

Abu Dawud al-Tayalisi said, from 'Amr, from his father, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, from Ibn 'Abbas: {al-jawari al-kunnas}, he said: The wild cattle, they retreat into the shade. And Sa'id ibn Jubayr said likewise.

Al-'Awfi said, from Ibn 'Abbas: They are the gazelles. And Sa'id also said likewise, as did Mujahid and al-Dahhak.

Abu al-Sha'tha' Jabir ibn Zayd said: They are the gazelles and the cattle.

Ibn Jarir said: Ya'qub narrated to us, Hushaym narrated to us, Mughirah informed us, from Ibrahim and Mujahid: that the two of them discussed this verse: {al-khunnas al-jawari al-kunnas}, and Ibrahim said to Mujahid: Say about it what you have heard. He said: Mujahid said: We used to hear something about it, and some people say: They are the stars. He said: Ibrahim said: Say about it what you have heard. He said: Mujahid said: We used to hear that they are the wild cattle, when they retreat into their resting place. He said: Ibrahim said: They are lying about 'Ali; this is like what they narrated from 'Ali, that he made the lower liable for the upper, and the upper liable for the lower.

Ibn Jarir withheld judgment concerning His saying: {al-khunnas al-jawari al-kunnas}: is it the stars, or the gazelles and wild cattle? He said: It is possible that all of them are intended.

* *

And His saying: {and the night when it 'as'asa} contains two views:

The first: its approach with its darkness. Mujahid said: it grew dark. Sa'id ibn Jubayr said: when it arises. Al-Hasan al-Basri said: when it covers the people. And 'Atiyyah al-'Awfi said likewise.

'Ali ibn Abi Talhah and al-'Awfi said, from Ibn 'Abbas: {idha 'as'asa} means when it withdraws. Mujahid, Qatadah, and al-Dahhak said likewise, and likewise Zayd ibn Aslam and his son 'Abd al-Rahman said: {idha 'as'asa}, that is: when it goes and turns away.

Abu Dawud al-Tayalisi said: Shu'bah narrated to us, from 'Amr ibn Murrah, from Abu al-Bakhtari, [who] heard Abu 'Abd al-Rahman al-Sulami say: 'Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, came out to us when the caller gave the tathwib (the second call before dawn prayer), and said: Where are those who ask about the witr prayer: {and the night when it 'as'asa, and the dawn when it breathes}? This is when it withdraws. Hasan (a sound report).

Ibn Jarir chose [the view] that what is meant by His saying {idha 'as'asa} is when it withdraws. He said this because of His saying {and the dawn when it breathes}, that is, it grows light, and he also cited the saying of the poet as evidence:

"Until the dawn breathed for it, and its night was swept away from it, and it withdrew ('as'asa)..."

that is, it withdrew. In my view, what is meant by His saying {'as'asa} is when it approaches, even though it is valid to use it for withdrawal too, but approach is more fitting here. It is as though He, exalted, swore by the night and its darkness when it approaches, and by the dawn and its light when it shines, just as He said: {And the night when it covers, and the day when it appears in its brightness} [al-Layl: 1-2], and He said: {By the forenoon brightness, and the night when it grows still} [al-Duha: 1-2], and He said: {[He is] the Cleaver of daybreak, and He has made the night for rest} [al-An'am: 96], and other verses besides these.

Many of the scholars of usul (legal theory) said: the word "'as'asa" is used for both approach and withdrawal, being mushtarak (a word of shared meaning), so on this basis it is valid that either of them may be intended. And Allah knows best.

Ibn Jarir said: Some of those knowledgeable in the speech of the Arabs claimed that "'as'asa" means: it drew near from its beginning and grew dark. Al-Farra' said: Abu al-Bilad al-Nahwi used to recite a line of poetry:

"It 'as'asa until, if it wished to draw near, it would have had a miqbas (taper) from its light..."

meaning: "if it wished, when it drew near," the dhal [of idh] was assimilated into the dal [of dana]. Al-Farra' said: and they used to think that this line of poetry was fabricated.

* *

And His saying: {and the dawn when it breathes}, al-Dahhak said: when it rises. Qatadah said: when it grows light and approaches. Sa'id ibn Jubayr said: when it arises. And this is what is narrated from 'Ali, may Allah be pleased with him.

Ibn Jarir said: meaning: the light of day, when it approaches and becomes clear.

* *

And His saying: {Indeed, it is the word of a noble messenger}, meaning: that this Qur'an is the conveyance of a noble messenger, that is, a noble angel of good character and radiant appearance, and he is Jibril (peace be upon him). This was said by Ibn 'Abbas, al-Sha'bi, Maymun ibn Mihran, al-Hasan, Qatadah, al-Rabi' ibn Anas, and others.

{Possessed of strength}, like His saying {taught to him by one of great power, possessed of soundness, [who then rose up]} [al-Najm: 5-6], that is: strong in constitution, powerful in force and action. {Having a secure position with the Possessor of the Throne}, that is: he has a standing with Allah, Mighty and Majestic, and an elevated rank.

Abu Salih said concerning His saying: {having a secure position with the Possessor of the Throne}: he said: Jibril enters through seventy veils of light without [needing] permission. {Obeyed there}, that is: he has prestige, and his word is heeded, he is obeyed among the highest assembly.

Qatadah said: {obeyed there}, that is: in the heavens, meaning: he is not among the rank and file of the angels, rather he is among the chiefs and nobles, given special attention, chosen for this magnificent mission.

* *

And His saying: {trustworthy} is a description of Jibril with trustworthiness. And this is something very great indeed: that the Lord, Mighty and Majestic, vouches for the integrity of His servant and angelic messenger Jibril, just as He vouched for the integrity of His servant and human messenger Muhammad ﷺ by His saying: {and your companion is not mad}.

Al-Sha'bi, Maymun ibn Mihran, Abu Salih, and those mentioned before said: what is meant by His saying {and your companion is not mad} is Muhammad ﷺ.

* *

And His saying, exalted is He: {And he certainly saw him on the clear horizon}, meaning: Muhammad certainly saw Jibril, who brings him the message from Allah, Mighty and Majestic, in the form upon which Allah created him, having six hundred wings, {on the clear horizon}, that is: the manifest one. This is the first sighting, which took place at al-Batha', and it is what is mentioned in His saying: {taught to him by one of great power, possessed of soundness, who then rose up, while he was on the highest horizon, then he drew near and descended, until he was at the distance of two bow-lengths or nearer, then He revealed to His servant what He revealed} [al-Najm: 5-10], as the explanation and establishment of that has already preceded. And the evidence [shows] that what is meant by that is Jibril (peace be upon him).

What appears, and Allah knows best, is that this surah was revealed before the night of al-Isra' (the Night Journey), because it mentions only this vision, which is the first one. As for the second vision, which is mentioned in His saying: {And he certainly saw him in another descent, at the Lote Tree of the utmost boundary, near which is the Garden of Refuge, when there covered the Lote Tree that which covered it} [an-Najm: 13-16], that one is mentioned only in Surat an-Najm, which was revealed after [Surat] al-Isra'.

* *

His saying: {And he is not, concerning the unseen, withholding (bi-danin)} means: Muhammad ﷺ is not, concerning what Allah revealed to him, one accused (bi-zanin), that is, suspected. Some of them recite it with the dad, meaning: miserly, rather he gives it freely to everyone.

Sufyan ibn 'Uyaynah said: zanin and danin are the same, meaning: he is not a liar and he is not immoral. Al-zanin is the accused one, and al-danin is the miser.

Qatadah said: The Qur'an was unseen (ghayb), so Allah revealed it to Muhammad, and he did not withhold it from the people, rather he conveyed it, spread it, and gave it freely to everyone who wanted it. 'Ikrimah, Ibn Zayd, and more than one other said likewise. Ibn Jarir chose the reading with the dad.

I say: both readings are mutawatir (mass-transmitted), and the meaning of each is sound, as has preceded.

* *

His saying: {And it is not the word of an accursed devil} means: this Qur'an is not the word of an accursed devil, that is, he is not able to bear it, nor does he desire it, nor is it fitting for him. As He said: {And the devils did not bring it down; it is not fitting for them, nor are they able; indeed, they are removed from hearing} [ash-Shu'ara': 210-212].

* *

His saying: {So where are you going} means: so where are your minds going, in your denial of this Qur'an, despite its manifestness and clarity, and the evidence that it came from Allah, Mighty and Majestic. As al-Siddiq (Abu Bakr), may Allah be pleased with him, said to the delegation of Banu Hanifah when they came as Muslims: he commanded them, and they recited to him something of the "Qur'an" of Musaylimah, which was in the utmost of raving nonsense and feebleness of style, so he said: "Woe to you, where are your minds being taken? By Allah, this speech did not come out of an ill," that is, of a deity.

Qatadah said: {So where are you going} means: away from the Book of Allah and from obedience to Him.

* *

His saying: {It is not but a reminder to the worlds} means: this Qur'an is a reminder for all people; they remember by it and take heed. {For whoever among you wills to be upright} means: whoever wants guidance, let him hold fast to this Qur'an, for it is a means of salvation for him and guidance, and there is no guidance in anything other than it. {And you do not will except that Allah, Lord of the worlds, wills} means: the will is not entrusted to you; whoever wills is guided and whoever wills goes astray, rather all of that follows the will of Allah, Mighty and Majestic, Lord of the worlds.

Sufyan al-Thawri said, from Sa'id ibn 'Abd al-'Aziz, from Sulayman ibn Musa: when this verse was revealed: {For whoever among you wills to be upright}, Abu Jahl said: "The matter is up to us; if we wish, we go straight, and if we wish, we do not go straight." So Allah revealed: {And you do not will except that Allah, Lord of the worlds, wills}.

This is the end of the tafsir of Surat al-Takwir, and to Allah is all praise [and grace].

رَوَى مُسْلِمٌ فِي صَحِيحِهِ، وَالنَّسَائِيُّ فِي تَفْسِيرِهِ عِنْدَ هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ مِسْعر بْنِ كِدَام، عَنِ الْوَلِيدِ بْنِ سَرِيع، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ حُرَيث قَالَ: صَلَّيْتُ خَلْفَ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ الصُّبْحَ، فَسَمِعْتُهُ يَقْرَأُ: ﴿فَلا أُقْسِمُ بِالْخُنَّسِ الْجَوَارِي الْكُنَّسِ وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا عَسْعَسَ وَالصُّبْحِ إِذَا تَنَفَّسَ﴾ .

وَرَوَاهُ النَّسَائِيُّ عَنْ بُنْدَارٍ، عَنْ غُنْدَر، عَنْ شُعْبَةَ، عَنْ الْحَجَّاجِ بْنِ عَاصِمٍ، عَنْ أَبِي الْأَسْوَدِ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ حُرَيث، بِهِ نَحْوَهُ .

قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ وَابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، مِنْ طَرِيقِ الثَّوْرِيِّ، عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ رَجُلٍ مِنْ مُرَادٍ، عَنْ عَلِيٍّ: ﴿فَلا أُقْسِمُ بِالْخُنَّسِ الْجَوَارِي الْكُنَّسِ﴾ قَالَ: هِيَ النُّجُومُ تَخْنِسُ بِالنَّهَارِ، وَتَظْهَرُ بِاللَّيْلِ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ الْمُثَنَّى، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ جَعْفَرٍ قَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا شُعْبَةُ، عَنْ سِمَاكِ بْنِ حَرْبٍ، سَمِعْتُ خَالِدَ بْنَ عَرْعَرَةَ، سَمِعْتُ عَلِيًّا وَسُئِلَ عَنْ: ﴿فَلا أُقْسِمُ بِالْخُنَّسِ الْجَوَارِي الْكُنَّسِ﴾ فَقَالَ: هِيَ النُّجُومُ، تخنِس بِالنَّهَارِ وَتَكْنُسُ بِاللَّيْلِ .

وَحَدَّثَنَا أَبُو كُرَيْب، حَدَّثَنَا وَكِيع، عَنْ إِسْرَائِيلَ، عَنْ سِماك، عَنْ خَالِدٍ، عَنْ عَلِيٍّ قَالَ: هِيَ النُّجُومُ.

وَهَذَا إِسْنَادٌ جَيِّدٌ صَحِيحٌ إِلَى خَالِدِ بْنِ عَرْعَرَةَ، وَهُوَ السَّهْمِيُّ الْكُوفِيُّ، قَالَ أَبُو حَاتِمٍ الرَّازِيُّ: رَوَى عَنْ عَلِيٍّ، وَرَوَى عَنْهُ سِمَاكٌ وَالْقَاسِمُ بْنُ عَوْفٍ الشَّيْبَانِيُّ وَلَمْ يَذْكُرْ فِيهِ جَرْحًا وَلَا تَعْدِيلًا وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

وَرَوَى يُونُسُ، عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ، عَنِ الْحَارِثِ، عَنْ عَلِيٍّ: أَنَّهَا النُّجُومُ. رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ وَكَذَا رُوي عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٍ، وَالْحَسَنِ، وَقَتَادَةَ، وَالسُّدِّيِّ، وَغَيْرِهِمْ: أَنَّهَا النُّجُومُ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ بَشَّارٍ، حَدَّثَنَا هَوذة بْنُ خَلِيفَةَ، حَدَّثَنَا عَوْفٌ، عَنْ بَكْرِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿فَلا أُقْسِمُ بِالْخُنَّسِ الْجَوَارِي الْكُنَّسِ﴾ قَالَ: هِيَ النُّجُومُ الدَّرَارِيُّ، الَّتِي تَجْرِي تستقبل المشرق.

وَقَالَ بَعْضُ الْأَئِمَّةِ: إِنَّمَا قِيلَ لِلنُّجُومِ: "الْخُنَّسُ"، أَيْ: فِي حَالِ طُلُوعِهَا، ثُمَّ هِيَ جِوَارٌ فِي فَلَكِهَا، وَفِي حَالِ غَيْبُوبَتِهَا يُقَالُ لَهَا: "كُنَّس" مِنْ قَوْلِ الْعَرَبِ: أَوَى الظَّبْيُ إِلَى كنَاسة: إِذَا تَغَيَّبَ فِيهِ.

وَقَالَ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ قَالَ: قَالَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ: ﴿فَلا أُقْسِمُ بِالْخُنَّسِ﴾ قَالَ: بَقَرُ الْوَحْشِ. وَكَذَا قَالَ الثَّوْرِيِّ، عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ أَبِي مَيْسَرَةَ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ: ﴿فَلا أُقْسِمُ بِالْخُنَّسِ الْجَوَارِي الْكُنَّسِ﴾ مَا هِيَ يَا عَمْرُو؟ قُلْتُ: الْبَقَرُ. قَالَ: وَأَنَا أَرَى ذَلِكَ.

وَكَذَا رَوَى يُونُسُ عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ.

وَقَالَ أَبُو دَاوُدَ الطَّيَالِسِيُّ، عَنْ عَمْرٍو، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿الْجَوَارِي الْكُنَّسِ﴾ قَالَ: الْبَقَرُ [الْوَحْشُ] تَكْنُسُ إِلَى الظِّلِّ. وَكَذَا قَالَ سَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ.

وَقَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: هِيَ الظِّبَاءُ. وَكَذَا قَالَ سَعِيدٌ أَيْضًا، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ.

وَقَالَ أَبُو الشَّعْثَاءِ جَابِرُ بْنُ زَيْدٍ: هِيَ الظِّبَاءُ وَالْبَقَرُ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا يَعْقُوبُ، حَدَّثَنَا هُشَيْم، أَخْبَرَنَا مُغِيرَةُ عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَمُجَاهِدٍ: أَنَّهُمَا تَذَاكَرَا هَذِهِ الْآيَةَ: ﴿فَلا أُقْسِمُ بِالْخُنَّسِ الْجَوَارِي الْكُنَّسِ﴾ فَقَالَ إِبْرَاهِيمُ لِمُجَاهِدٍ: قُلْ فِيهَا بِمَا سَمِعْتَ. قَالَ: فَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: كُنَّا نَسْمَعُ فِيهَا شَيْئًا، وَنَاسٌ يَقُولُونَ: إِنَّهَا النُّجُومُ. قَالَ: فَقَالَ إِبْرَاهِيمُ: قُلْ فِيهَا بِمَا سَمِعْتَ. قَالَ: فَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: كُنَّا نَسْمَعُ أَنَّهَا بَقَرُ الْوَحْشِ حِينَ تَكْنُسُ فِي حُجْرتها. قَالَ: فَقَالَ إِبْرَاهِيمُ: إِنَّهُمْ يَكْذِبُونَ عَلَى عَلَيٍّ، هَذَا كَمَا رَوَوْا عَنْ عَلِيٍّ أَنَّهُ ضَمَّنَ الْأَسْفَلُ الْأَعْلَى، وَالْأَعْلَى الْأَسْفَلَ.

وَتَوَقَّفَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿الْخُنَّسِ الْجَوَارِي الْكُنَّسِ﴾ هَلْ هُوَ النُّجُومُ، أَوِ الظِّبَاءُ وَبَقَرُ الْوَحْشِ؟ قَالَ: وَيُحْتَمَلُ أَنْ يَكُونَ الْجَمِيعُ مُرَادًا.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا عَسْعَسَ﴾ فِيهِ قَوْلَانِ:

أَحَدُهُمَا: إِقْبَالُهُ بِظَلَامِهِ. قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: أَظْلَمَ. وَقَالَ سَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ: إِذَا نَشَأَ. وَقَالَ الْحَسَنُ الْبَصْرِيُّ: إِذَا غَشى النَّاسَ. وَكَذَا قَالَ عَطِيَّةُ الْعَوْفِيُّ.

وَقَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، وَالْعَوْفِيُّ عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿إِذَا عَسْعَسَ﴾ إِذَا أَدْبَرَ. وَكَذَا قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَكَذَا قَالَ زَيْدُ بْنُ أَسْلَمَ، وَابْنُهُ عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ: ﴿إِذَا عَسْعَسَ﴾ أَيْ: إِذَا ذَهَبَ فَتَوَلَّى.

وَقَالَ أَبُو دَاوُدَ الطَّيَالِسِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا شُعْبَةُ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ مُرَّةَ، عَنْ أَبِي البَخْتَري، سَمِعَ أَبَا عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ السُّلَمِيَّ قَالَ: خَرَجَ عَلَيْنَا عَلِيٌّ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، حِينَ ثَوّب الْمُثَوِّبُ بِصَلَاةِ الصُّبْحِ فَقَالَ: أَيْنَ السَّائِلُونَ عَنِ الْوِتْرِ: ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا عَسْعَسَ وَالصُّبْحِ إِذَا تَنَفَّسَ﴾ ؟ هذا حين أدبر حسن.

وَقَدِ اخْتَارَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ أَنَّ الْمُرَادَ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿إِذَا عَسْعَسَ﴾ إِذَا أَدْبَرَ. قَالَ لِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَالصُّبْحِ إِذَا تَنَفَّسَ﴾ أَيْ: أَضَاءَ، وَاسْتَشْهَدَ بِقَوْلِ الشَّاعِرِ أَيْضًا:

حَتّى إِذَا الصُّبحُ لَهُ تَنَفَّسا ... وانجابَ عَنها لَيلُها وعَسعَسَا ...

أَيْ: أَدْبَرَ. وَعِنْدِي أَنَّ الْمُرَادَ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿عَسْعَسَ﴾ إِذَا أَقْبَلَ، وَإِنْ كَانَ يَصِحُّ اسْتِعْمَالُهُ فِي الْإِدْبَارِ، لَكِنَّ الْإِقْبَالَ هَاهُنَا أَنْسَبُ؛ كَأَنَّهُ أَقْسَمَ تَعَالَى بِاللَّيْلِ وَظَلَامِهِ إِذَا أَقْبَلَ، وَبِالْفَجْرِ وَضِيَائِهِ إِذَا أَشْرَقَ، كَمَا قَالَ: ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَغْشَى وَالنَّهَارِ إِذَا تَجَلَّى﴾ [اللَّيْلِ: ١، ٢] ، وَقَالَ: ﴿وَالضُّحَى وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا سَجَى﴾ [الضُّحَى: ١، ٢] ، وَقَالَ ﴿فَالِقُ الإصْبَاحِ وَجَعَلَ اللَّيْلَ سَكَنًا﴾ [الْأَنْعَامِ: ٩٦] ، وَغَيْرُ ذَلِكَ مِنَ الْآيَاتِ.

وَقَالَ كَثِيرٌ مِنْ عُلَمَاءِ الْأُصُولِ: إِنَّ لَفْظَةَ "عَسْعَسَ" تُسْتَعْمَلُ فِي الْإِقْبَالِ وَالْإِدْبَارِ عَلَى وَجْهِ الِاشْتِرَاكِ، فَعَلَى هَذَا يَصِحُّ أَنْ يُرَادَ كُلٌّ مِنْهُمَا، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: وَكَانَ بَعْضُ أَهْلِ الْمَعْرِفَةِ بِكَلَامِ الْعَرَبِ يَزْعُمُ أَنَّ "عَسْعَسَ": دَنَا مِنْ أَوَّلِهِ وَأَظْلَمَ. وَقَالَ الْفَرَّاءُ: كَانَ أَبُو الْبِلَادِ النَّحْوِيُّ يُنشد بَيْتًا:

عَسعَس حَتَّى لَوْ يَشَاءُ ادَّنَا ... كانَ لَهُ مِنِ ضَوئه مَقبس ...

يُرِيدُ: لَوْ يَشَاءُ إِذْ دَنَا، أُدْغِمَ الذَّالَ فِي الدَّالِ. وَقَالَ الْفَرَّاءُ: وَكَانُوا يَرَون أَنَّ هَذَا الْبَيْتَ مَصْنُوعٌ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَالصُّبْحِ إِذَا تَنَفَّسَ﴾ قَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ: إِذَا طَلَعَ. وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: إِذَا أَضَاءَ وَأَقْبَلَ. وَقَالَ سَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ: إِذَا نَشَأَ. وَهُوَ الْمَرْوِيُّ عَنْ عَلِيٍّ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: يَعْنِي: وَضَوءُ النَّهَارِ إِذَا أَقْبَلَ وَتَبَيَّن.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنَّهُ لَقَوْلُ رَسُولٍ كَرِيمٍ﴾ يَعْنِي: أَنَّ هَذَا الْقُرْآنَ لتبليغُ رَسُولٍ كَرِيمٍ، أَيْ: مَلَكٌ شَرِيفٌ حَسَن الْخَلْقِ، بَهِيُّ الْمَنْظَرِ، وَهُوَ جِبْرِيلُ، عَلَيْهِ الصَّلَاةُ وَالسَّلَامُ. قَالَهُ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَالشَّعْبِيُّ، وَمَيْمُونُ بْنُ مِهْران، وَالْحَسَنُ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالرَّبِيعُ بْنُ أَنَسٍ، وَغَيْرُهُمْ.

﴿ذِي قُوَّةٍ﴾ كَقَوْلِهِ ﴿عَلَّمَهُ شَدِيدُ الْقُوَى ذُو مِرَّةٍ [فَاسْتَوَى] ﴾ [النَّجْمِ: ٥، ٦] ، أَيْ: شَدِيدُ الخَلْق، شَدِيدُ الْبَطْشِ وَالْفِعْلِ، ﴿عِنْدَ ذِي الْعَرْشِ مَكِينٍ﴾ أَيْ: لَهُ مَكَانَةٌ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ وَمَنْزِلَةٌ رَفِيعَةٌ.

قَالَ أَبُو صَالِحٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿عِنْدَ ذِي الْعَرْشِ مَكِينٍ﴾ قَالَ: جبريل يدخل في سبعين حجابا من نُورٍ بِغَيْرِ إِذْنٍ، ﴿مُطَاعٍ ثَمَّ﴾ أَيْ: لَهُ وَجَاهَةٌ، وَهُوَ مَسْمُوعُ الْقَوْلِ مُطَاعٌ فِي الْمَلَأِ الْأَعْلَى.

قَالَ قَتَادَةُ: ﴿مُطَاعٍ ثَمَّ﴾ أَيْ: فِي السَّمَوَاتِ، يَعْنِي: لَيْسَ هُوَ مِنْ أَفْنَادِ الْمَلَائِكَةِ، بَلْ هُوَ مِنَ السَّادَةِ وَالْأَشْرَافِ، مُعتَنى بِهِ، انْتُخِبَ لِهَذِهِ الرِّسَالَةِ الْعَظِيمَةِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿أَمِينٍ﴾ صِفَةٌ لِجِبْرِيلَ بِالْأَمَانَةِ، وَهَذَا عَظِيمٌ جِدًّا أَنَّ الرَّبَّ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ يُزَكِّي عَبْدَهُ وَرَسُولَهُ الْمَلَكِيَّ جِبْرِيلَ كَمَا زَكَّى عَبْدَهُ وَرَسُولَهُ الْبَشَرِيَّ مُحَمَّدًا ﷺ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَمَا صَاحِبُكُمْ بِمَجْنُونٍ﴾

قَالَ الشَّعْبِيُّ، وَمَيْمُونُ بْنُ مِهْرَانَ، وَأَبُو صَالِحٍ، وَمَنْ تَقَدَّمَ ذِكْرُهُمْ: الْمُرَادُ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَمَا صَاحِبُكُمْ بِمَجْنُونٍ﴾ يَعْنِي: مُحَمَّدًا ﷺ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَلَقَدْ رَآهُ بِالأفُقِ الْمُبِينِ﴾ يَعْنِي: وَلَقَدْ رَأَى محمدٌ جِبْرِيلَ الَّذِي يَأْتِيهِ بِالرِّسَالَةِ عَنِ اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ عَلَى الصُّورَةِ الَّتِي خَلَقَهُ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهَا لَهُ سِتُّمِائَةُ جَنَاحٍ ﴿بِالأفُقِ الْمُبِينِ﴾ أَيِ: الْبَيِّنُ، وَهِيَ الرُّؤْيَةُ الْأَوْلَى الَّتِي كَانَتْ بِالْبَطْحَاءِ، وَهِيَ الْمَذْكُورَةُ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿عَلَّمَهُ شَدِيدُ الْقُوَى ذُو مِرَّةٍ فَاسْتَوَى وَهُوَ بِالأفُقِ الأعْلَى ثُمَّ دَنَا فَتَدَلَّى فَكَانَ قَابَ قَوْسَيْنِ أَوْ أَدْنَى فَأَوْحَى إَلَى عَبْدِهِ مَا أَوْحَى﴾ [النَّجْمِ: ٥ -١٠] ، كَمَا تَقَدَّمَ تفسيرُ ذَلِكَ وَتَقْرِيرُهُ. والدليلُ أَنَّ المرادَ بِذَلِكَ جِبْرِيلُ، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ. وَالظَّاهِرُ-وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ-أَنَّ هَذِهِ السُّورَةَ نَزَلَتْ قَبْلَ لَيْلَةَ الْإِسْرَاءِ؛ لِأَنَّهُ لَمْ يَذْكُرْ فِيهَا إِلَّا هَذِهِ الرُّؤْيَةَ وَهِيَ الْأُولَى، وَأَمَّا الثَّانِيَةُ وَهِيَ الْمَذْكُورَةُ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَلَقَدْ رَآهُ نزلَةً أُخْرَى عِنْدَ سِدْرَةِ الْمُنْتَهَى عِنْدَهَا جَنَّةُ الْمَأْوَى إِذْ يَغْشَى السِّدْرَةَ مَا يَغْشَى﴾ [النَّجْمِ: ١٣ -١٦] ، فَتِلْكَ إِنَّمَا ذُكِرَتْ فِي سُورَةِ "النَّجْمِ"، وَقَدْ نَزَلَتْ بَعْدَ [سُورَةِ] الْإِسْرَاءِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَمَا هُوَ عَلَى الْغَيْبِ بِضَنِينٍ﴾ أَيْ: وَمَا مُحَمَّدٌ عَلَى مَا أَنْزَلَهُ اللَّهُ إِلَيْهِ بِظَنِينٍ، أَيْ: بِمُتَّهَمٍ. وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ قَرَأَ ذَلِكَ بِالضَّادِ، أَيْ: بِبَخِيلٍ، بَلْ يَبْذُلُهُ لِكُلِّ أَحَدٍ.

قَالَ سُفْيَانُ بْنُ عُيَينة: ظَنِينٌ وَضَنِينٌ سَوَاءٌ، أَيْ: مَا هُوَ بِكَاذِبٍ، وَمَا هُوَ بِفَاجِرٍ. وَالظَّنِينُ: الْمُتَّهَمُ، وَالضَّنِينُ: الْبَخِيلُ.

وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: كَانَ الْقُرْآنُ غَيْبًا، فَأَنْزَلَهُ اللَّهُ عَلَى مُحَمَّدٍ، فَمَا ضَنّ بِهِ عَلَى النَّاسِ، بَلْ بَلَّغه وَنَشَرَهُ وَبَذَلَهُ لِكُلِّ مَنْ أَرَادَهُ. وَكَذَا قَالَ عِكْرِمَةُ، وَابْنُ زَيْدٍ، وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ. وَاخْتَارَ ابنُ جَرِيرٍ قِرَاءَةَ الضَّادِ .

قُلْتُ: وَكِلَاهُمَا مُتَوَاتِرٌ، وَمَعْنَاهُ صَحِيحٌ كَمَا تَقَدَّمَ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَمَا هُوَ بِقَوْلِ شَيْطَانٍ رَجِيمٍ﴾ أَيْ: وَمَا هَذَا الْقُرْآنُ بِقَوْلِ شَيْطَانٍ رَجِيمٍ، أَيْ: لَا يَقْدِرُ عَلَى حَمْلِهِ، وَلَا يُرِيدُهُ، وَلَا يَنْبَغِي لَهُ. كَمَا قَالَ: ﴿وَمَا تَنزلَتْ بِهِ الشَّيَاطِينُ وَمَا يَنْبَغِي لَهُمْ وَمَا يَسْتَطِيعُونَ إِنَّهُمْ عَنِ السَّمْعِ لَمَعْزُولُونَ﴾ [الشعراء: ٢١٠ -٢١٢] .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَأَيْنَ تَذْهَبُونَ﴾ ؟ أَيْ: فَأَيْنَ تَذْهَبُ عُقُولُكُمْ فِي تَكْذِيبِكُمْ بِهَذَا الْقُرْآنِ، مَعَ ظُهُورِهِ وَوُضُوحِهِ، وَبَيَانِ كَوْنِهِ جَاءَ مِنْ عِنْدِ اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، كَمَا قَالَ الصِّدِّيقُ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، لِوَفْدِ بَنِي حَنِيفَةَ حِينَ قَدِمُوا مُسْلِمِينَ، وَأَمَرَهُمْ فَتَلَوْا عَلَيْهِ شَيْئًا مِنْ قُرْآنِ مُسَيْلِمَةَ الَّذِي هُوَ فِي غَايَةِ الْهَذَيَانِ وَالرَّكَاكَةِ، فَقَالَ: وَيْحَكُمْ، أَيْنَ يُذهَب بِعُقُولِكُمْ ؟ وَاللَّهِ إِنَّ هَذَا الْكَلَامَ لَمْ يَخْرُجْ مِنْ إلٍّ، أَيْ: مِنْ إِلَهٍ.

وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: ﴿فَأَيْنَ تَذْهَبُونَ﴾ أَيْ: عَنْ كِتَابِ اللَّهِ وَعَنْ طَاعَتِهِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنْ هُوَ إِلا ذِكْرٌ لِلْعَالَمِينَ﴾ أَيْ: هَذَا الْقُرْآنُ ذِكْرٌ لِجَمِيعِ النَّاسِ، يَتَذَكَّرُونَ بِهِ وَيَتَّعِظُونَ، ﴿لِمَنْ شَاءَ مِنْكُمْ أَنْ يَسْتَقِيمَ﴾ أَيْ: مَنْ أَرَادَ الْهِدَايَةَ فَعَلَيْهِ بِهَذَا الْقُرْآنِ، فَإِنَّهُ منجاةٌ لَهُ وَهِدَايَةٌ، وَلَا هِدَايَةَ فِيمَا سِوَاهُ، ﴿وَمَا تَشَاءُونَ إِلا أَنْ يَشَاءَ اللَّهُ رَبُّ الْعَالَمِينَ﴾ أَيْ: لَيْسَتِ الْمَشِيئَةُ مَوْكُولَةً إِلَيْكُمْ، فَمَنْ شَاءَ اهْتَدَى وَمَنْ شَاءَ ضَلَّ، بَلْ ذَلِكَ كُلُّهُ تَابِعٌ لِمَشِيئَةِ اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ.

قَالَ سُفْيَانُ الثَّوْرِيُّ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ عَبْدِ الْعَزِيزِ، عَنْ سُلَيْمَانَ بْنِ مُوسَى: لَمَّا نَزَلَتْ هَذِهِ الْآيَةُ: ﴿لِمَنْ شَاءَ مِنْكُمْ أَنْ يَسْتَقِيمَ﴾ قَالَ أَبُو جَهْلٍ: الْأَمْرُ إِلَيْنَا، إِنْ شِئْنَا اسْتَقَمْنَا، وَإِنْ شِئْنَا لَمْ نَسْتَقِمْ. فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ: ﴿وَمَا تَشَاءُونَ إِلا أَنْ يَشَاءَ اللَّهُ رَبُّ الْعَالَمِينَ ﴾ .

آخِرُ تَفْسِيرِ سورة "التكوير" ولله الحمد [والمنة] .
