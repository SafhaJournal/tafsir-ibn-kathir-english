## 77:1-15 — verses 1-15 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Tafsir of Surat al-Mursalat

It is Meccan.

Al-Bukhari said: We were told by Umar ibn Hafs ibn Ghiyath, [who said: We were told by my father], who said: We were told by al-A'mash, who said: I was told by Ibrahim, from al-Aswad, from Abdullah, that is, Ibn Mas'ud, who said: While we were with the Prophet ﷺ in a cave at Mina, {And by those sent forth} was revealed to him. He was reciting it and I was receiving it directly from his mouth, and his mouth was still moist with it, when a snake leapt upon us. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Kill it." We rushed toward it but it got away, so the Prophet ﷺ said: "It was spared your harm just as you were spared its harm."

Muslim also transmitted it, by way of al-A'mash.

Imam Ahmad said: We were told by Sufyan ibn Uyaynah, from al-Zuhri, from Ubaydullah, from Ibn Abbas, from his mother, that she heard the Prophet ﷺ recite in the Maghrib prayer {By those sent forth in gusts}.

In the version of Malik, from al-Zuhri, from Ubaydullah, from Ibn Abbas: that Umm al-Fadl heard him reciting {And by those sent forth in gusts}, and she said: "O my son, your recitation of this surah has reminded me that it was the last thing I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ recite in the Maghrib prayer."

Both of them (al-Bukhari and Muslim) transmitted it in their two Sahihs, by way of Malik, with this wording.

In the name of Allah, the Most Merciful, the Most Compassionate

Ibn Abi Hatim said: We were told by my father, who said: We were told by Zakariyya ibn Sahl al-Marwazi, who said: We were told by Ali ibn al-Hasan ibn Shaqiq, who said: We were informed by al-Husayn ibn Waqid, who said: We were told by al-A'mash, from Abu Salih, from Abu Hurayrah, regarding {By those sent forth in gusts}: he said, "The angels."

He said: A similar statement has been reported from Masruq, Abu al-Duha, Mujahid (in one of the reports from him), al-Suddi, and al-Rabi' ibn Anas.

It has also been reported from Abu Salih that he said: "They are the messengers." And in another report from him: that they are the angels. Abu Salih likewise said, regarding "al-'asifat" (those blowing violently), "al-nashirat" (those spreading), ["al-fariqat" (those distinguishing)], and "al-mulqiyat" (those casting): that they are the angels.

Al-Thawri said, from Salamah ibn Kuhayl, from Muslim al-Batin, from Abu al-Ubaydayn, who said: I asked Ibn Mas'ud about {By those sent forth in gusts}, and he said: "The wind." He said likewise regarding {By the violent gales, and the spreaders in spreading}: that they are the wind. Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, Qatadah, and Abu Salih (in one report from him) said the same. Ibn Jarir remained undecided about {By those sent forth in gusts}, as to whether they are the angels when they are sent forth with goodness ('urf), or like the mane ('urf) of a horse, one following another, or whether they are the winds when they blow gradually, one after another. But he affirmed decisively that "al-'asifat 'asfan" refers to the winds, as Ibn Mas'ud and those who followed him said. Among those who also said this about al-'asifat were Ali ibn Abi Talib and al-Suddi. He remained undecided about {And the spreaders in spreading}, as to whether it means the angels or the wind, as mentioned before. And it is reported from Abu Salih that "al-nashirat nashran" means the rain.

The more evident view is that "al-mursalat" refers to the winds, as Allah says: {And We send the fertilizing winds} [al-Hijr: 22], and as He says: {And it is He who sends the winds as good tidings before His mercy} [al-A'raf: 57]. Likewise "al-'asifat" refers to the winds; it is said "the wind blew violently ('asafat)" when it blows with a roaring sound. Similarly "al-nashirat" refers to the winds that spread the clouds across the horizons of the sky, as the Lord, Mighty and Majestic, wills.

Regarding His words: {And by those who separate with distinction, and those who deliver a reminder, as an excuse or a warning} meaning: the angels. This is what Ibn Mas'ud, Ibn Abbas, Masruq, Mujahid, Qatadah, al-Rabi' ibn Anas, al-Suddi, and al-Thawri said. There is no disagreement here, for the angels descend by Allah's command upon the messengers, distinguishing between truth and falsehood, guidance and error, the lawful and the unlawful, and delivering to the messengers a revelation containing an excuse offered to creation and a warning to them of Allah's punishment should they oppose His command.

Regarding His words: {Indeed, what you are promised will surely occur}, this is the matter sworn to by these oaths, that is: what you have been promised regarding the establishment of the Hour, the blowing of the trumpet, the resurrection of bodies, the gathering of the first and the last on one plain, and the recompense of every worker according to his deed, good for good and evil for evil, all of this {will surely occur}, meaning: it will certainly come to pass, without doubt.

Then He says: {So when the stars are obliterated}, meaning: their light will vanish, as in His words: {And when the stars fall, dispersing} [al-Takwir: 2], and as in His words: {And when the planets are scattered} [al-Infitar: 2].

{And when the sky is opened}, meaning: it splits and is rent asunder, its edges sagging and its sides giving way.

{And when the mountains are blown away}, meaning: they are carried off entirely, so that no trace or vestige of them remains, as in His words: {And they ask you about the mountains, so say: My Lord will blow them away completely, leaving the earth a level plain in which you will see no depression or elevation} [Taha: 105-107], and as He says: {And on the Day We shall set the mountains in motion, and you will see the earth exposed, and We shall gather them, leaving out none of them} [al-Kahf: 47].

Regarding His words: {And when the messengers are set a time}, al-Awfi said, from Ibn Abbas: "gathered." Ibn Zayd said: this is like His words: {On the Day when Allah will gather the messengers} [al-Ma'idah: 109]. Mujahid said: "uqqitat" means "given a deadline."

Al-Thawri said, from Mansur, from Ibrahim: "uqqitat" means "given a promise/warning." It seems he takes it as similar to His words: {And the earth will shine with the light of its Lord, and the Record will be laid open, and the prophets and witnesses will be brought, and it will be judged between them in truth, and they will not be wronged} [al-Zumar: 69].

Then He says: {For what day are they deferred? For the Day of Judgment. And what can make you know what the Day of Judgment is? Woe that Day to the deniers}. Allah, exalted is He, says: for what day have the messengers been given a deadline, and their affair postponed, until the Hour comes? as He says: {So do not think that Allah is unmindful of His promise to His messengers. Indeed, Allah is Mighty, Possessor of retribution. On the Day when the earth will be replaced by another earth, and the heavens as well, and they will come forth before Allah, the One, the All-Compelling} [Ibrahim: 47-48]. And that is the Day of Judgment (Yawm al-Fasl), as He says {For the Day of Judgment}.

Then, magnifying its importance, He says: {And what can make you know what the Day of Judgment is? Woe that Day to the deniers}, meaning: woe to them from the punishment of Allah on the morrow. We have already mentioned in the hadith that "wayl" (woe) is a valley in Hell, but this is not sound.

تَفْسِيرُ سُورَةِ الْمُرْسَلَاتِ

وَهِيَ مَكِّيَّةٌ.

قَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا عُمَرُ بْنُ حَفْصِ بْنِ غِيَاثٍ، [حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي] ، حَدَّثَنَا الْأَعْمَشُ، حَدَّثَنِي إِبْرَاهِيمُ، عَنِ الْأَسْوَدِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ -هُوَ ابْنُ مَسْعُودٍ-قَالَ: بَيْنَمَا نَحْنُ مَعَ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ، فِي غَارٍ بِمِنًى، إِذْ نَزَلَتْ عَلَيْهِ: " وَالْمُرْسَلاتِ " فَإِنَّهُ لَيَتْلُوهَا وَإِنِّي لِأَتَلَقَّاهَا مِنْ فِيهِ، وَإِنَّ فَاهُ لَرَطْبٌ بِهَا، إِذْ وَثَبت عَلَيْنَا حَيَّة، فَقَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: "اقْتُلُوهَا". فَابْتَدَرْنَاهَا فَذَهَبَتْ، فَقَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: "وُقِيَتْ شَرَّكُمْ كَمَا وُقِيتُم شرّهَا".

وَأَخْرَجَهُ مُسْلِمٌ أَيْضًا، مِنْ طَرِيقِ الْأَعْمَشِ .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ بْنُ عُيَيْنَة، عَنِ الزّهْري، عَنْ عُبَيد اللَّهِ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، عَنْ أُمِّهِ: أَنَّهَا سَمعَت النَّبِيَّ ﷺ يَقْرَأُ فِي الْمَغْرِبِ بِالْمُرْسَلَاتِ عُرفًا .

وَفِي رِوَايَةِ مَالِكٍ، عَنِ الزُّهْرِيِّ، عَنْ عُبَيد اللَّهِ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: أَنَّ أُمَّ الْفَضْلِ سَمِعَتْهُ يَقْرَأُ: " وَالْمُرْسَلاتِ عُرْفًا "، فَقَالَتْ: يَا بُنَيَّ، ذكَّرتني بِقِرَاءَتِكَ هَذِهِ السُّورَةَ، أَنَّهَا لَآخِرُ مَا سمعتُ مِنْ رسولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقْرَأُ بِهَا فِي الْمَغْرِبِ.

أَخْرَجَاهُ فِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ، مِنْ طَرِيقِ مَالِكٍ، بِهِ .

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

* *

قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا زَكَرِيَّا بْنُ سَهْلٍ الْمَرْوَزِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ الْحَسَنِ بْنِ شَقِيقٍ، أَخْبَرَنَا الْحُسَيْنُ بْنُ وَاقَدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا الْأَعْمَشُ، عَنْ أَبِي صَالِحٍ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيرة: ﴿وَالْمُرْسَلاتِ عُرْفًا﴾ قال: الملائكة.

قَالَ: ورُوي عَنْ مَسْرُوقٍ، وَأَبِي الضُّحَى، وَمُجَاهِدٍ -فِي إِحْدَى الرِّوَايَاتِ-وَالسُّدِّيُّ، وَالرَّبِيعُ بْنُ أَنَسٍ، مثلُ ذَلِكَ.

ورُويَ عَنْ أَبِي صَالِحٍ أَنَّهُ قَالَ: هِيَ الرُّسُلُ. وَفِي رِوَايَةٍ عَنْهُ: أَنَّهَا الْمَلَائِكَةُ. وَهَكَذَا قَالَ أَبُو صَالِحٍ فِي " الْعَاصِفَاتِ" وَ " النَّاشِرَات " [وَ " الْفَارِقَاتِ "] وَ " الْمُلْقِيَاتِ": أَنَّهَا الْمَلَائِكَةُ.

وَقَالَ الثَّوْرِيُّ، عَنْ سَلَمَةَ بْنِ كُهَيل، عَنْ مُسلم البَطين، عَنْ أَبِي العُبَيدَين قَالَ: سَأَلْتُ ابنَ مَسْعُودٍ عَنْ ﴿وَالْمُرْسَلاتِ عُرْفًا﴾ قَالَ: الرِّيحُ. وَكَذَا قَالَ فِي: ﴿فَالْعَاصِفَاتِ عَصْفًا وَالنَّاشِرَاتِ نَشْرًا﴾ إِنَّهَا الرِّيحُ. وَكَذَا قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَأَبُو صَالِحٍ -فِي رِوَايَةٍ عَنْهُ-وَتَوَقَّفَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ فِي ﴿وَالْمُرْسَلاتِ عُرْفًا﴾ هَلْ هِيَ الْمَلَائِكَةُ إِذَا أُرْسِلَتْ بالعُرْف، أَوْ كعُرْف الفَرَس يَتْبَعُ بَعْضُهُمْ بَعْضًا؟ أَوْ: هِيَ الرِّيَاحُ إِذَا هَبَّت شَيْئًا فَشَيْئًا؟ وَقَطَعَ بِأَنَّ الْعَاصِفَاتِ عَصْفًا هِيَ الرِّيَاحُ، كَمَا قَالَهُ ابْنُ مَسْعُودٍ وَمَنْ تَابَعَهُ. وَمِمَّنْ قَالَ ذَلِكَ فِي الْعَاصِفَاتِ أَيْضًا: عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَالِبٍ ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ، وَتَوَقَّفَ فِي ﴿وَالنَّاشِرَاتِ نَشْرًا﴾ هَلْ هِيَ الْمَلَائِكَةُ أَوِ الرِّيحُ؟ كَمَا تَقَدَّمَ. وَعَنْ أَبِي صَالِحٍ: أَنَّ النَّاشِرَاتِ نَشْرًا: الْمَطَرُ.

وَالْأَظْهَرُ أَنَّ: "الْمُرْسَلات" هِيَ الرِّيَاحُ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَأَرْسَلْنَا الرِّيَاحَ لَوَاقِحَ﴾ [الْحِجْرِ: ٢٢] ، وَقَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَهُوَ الَّذِي يُرْسِلُ الرِّيَاحَ بُشْرًا بَيْنَ يَدَيْ رَحْمَتِهِ﴾ [الْأَعْرَافِ: ٥٧] وَهَكَذَا الْعَاصِفَاتُ هِيَ: الرِّيَاحُ، يُقَالُ: عَصَفَتِ الرِّيحُ إِذَا هَبَّت بِتَصْوِيتٍ، وَكَذَا النَّاشِرَاتُ هِيَ: الرِّيَاحُ الَّتِي تَنْشُرُ السَّحَابَ فِي آفَاقِ السَّمَاءِ، كَمَا يَشَاءُ الرَّبُّ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَالْفَارِقَاتِ فَرْقًا فَالْمُلْقِيَاتِ ذِكْرًا عُذْرًا أَوْ نُذْرًا﴾ يَعْنِي: الْمَلَائِكَةَ قَالَهُ ابْنُ مَسْعُودٍ، وَابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمَسْرُوقٌ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالرَّبِيعُ بْنُ أَنَسٍ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ، وَالثَّوْرِيُّ. وَلَا خِلَافَ هَاهُنَا؛ فَإِنَّهَا تَنْزِلَ بِأَمْرِ اللَّهِ عَلَى الرُّسُلِ، تُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ الْحَقِّ وَالْبَاطِلِ، وَالْهُدَى وَالْغَيِّ، وَالْحَلَالِ وَالْحَرَامِ، وَتُلْقِي إِلَى الرُّسُلِ وَحْيًا فِيهِ إِعْذَارٌ إِلَى الْخَلْقِ، وإنذارٌ لَهُمْ عقابَ اللَّهِ إِنْ خَالَفُوا أَمْرَهُ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنَّمَا تُوعَدُونَ لَوَاقِعٌ﴾ هَذَا هُوَ الْمُقْسَمُ عَلَيْهِ بِهَذِهِ الْأَقْسَامِ، أَيْ: مَا وُعِدْتُمْ بِهِ مِنْ قِيَامِ السَّاعَةِ، وَالنَّفْخِ فِي الصُّوَرِ، وَبَعْثِ الْأَجْسَادِ وَجَمْعِ الْأَوَّلِينَ وَالْآخَرِينَ فِي صَعِيدٍ وَاحِدٍ، وَمُجَازَاةِ كُلَّ عَامِلٍ بِعَمَلِهِ، إِنْ خَيْرًا فَخَيْرٌ وَإِنْ شَرًّا فَشَرٌّ، إِنَّ هَذَا كُلَّهُ ﴿لَوَاقِع﴾ أَيْ: لَكَائِنٌ لَا مَحَالَةَ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿فَإِذَا النُّجُومُ طُمِسَتْ﴾ أَيْ: ذَهَبَ ضَوْؤُهَا، كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَإِذَا النُّجُومُ انْكَدَرَتْ﴾ [التَّكْوِيرِ: ٢] وَكَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَإِذَا الْكَوَاكِبُ انْتَثَرَتْ﴾ [الِانْفِطَارِ: ٢] .

﴿وَإِذَا السَّمَاءُ فُرِجَتْ﴾ أَيِ: انْفَطَرَتْ وَانْشَقَّتْ، وَتَدَلَّتْ أَرْجَاؤُهَا، وَوَهَت أَطْرَافُهَا.

﴿وَإِذَا الْجِبَالُ نُسِفَتْ﴾ أَيْ: ذُهِب بِهَا، فَلَا يَبْقَى لَهَا عَيْنٌ وَلَا أَثَرٌ، كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَيَسْأَلُونَكَ عَنِ الْجِبَالِ فَقُلْ يَنْسِفُهَا رَبِّي نَسْفًا فَيَذَرُهَا قَاعًا صَفْصَفًا لَا تَرَى فِيهَا عِوَجًا وَلا أَمْتًا﴾ [طه: ١٠٥ -١٠٧] وَقَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَيَوْمَ نُسَيِّرُ الْجِبَالَ وَتَرَى الأرْضَ بَارِزَةً وَحَشَرْنَاهُمْ فَلَمْ نُغَادِرْ مِنْهُمْ أَحَدًا﴾ [الْكَهْفِ: ٤٧]

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِذَا الرُّسُلُ أُقِّتَتْ﴾ قَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: جُمِعَتْ. وَقَالَ ابْنُ زَيْدٍ: وَهَذِهِ كَقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿يَوْمَ يَجْمَعُ اللَّهُ الرُّسُلَ﴾ [الْمَائِدَةِ: ١٠٩] . وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: ﴿أُقِّتَت﴾ أُجِّلَتْ.

## 77:16-28 — verses 16-28 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: {Did We not destroy the former peoples?} That is, those among them who disbelieved the messengers and opposed what they brought them.

{Then We caused the later ones to follow them}, meaning those who resembled them. This is why He said: {Thus do We deal with the criminals. Woe that Day to the deniers}, as Ibn Jarir said.

Then He says, conferring a favor upon His creation and establishing proof of the resurrection by means of the original creation: {Did We not create you from a despised water?} That is, weak and insignificant in relation to the power of the Creator, Mighty and Majestic, as was mentioned previously in Surat Ya-Sin in the hadith of Busr ibn Jihash: "O son of Adam, how can you defy Me when I created you from something like this?"

{And We placed it in a secure repository}, meaning We gathered it in the womb, which is the resting place of the water from the man and the woman, and the womb is prepared for that, preserving what has been deposited in it.

His saying: {for a known measure of time} means for a specified period of six months or nine months. This is why He said: {So We determined, and how excellent is Our determining. Woe that Day to the deniers}.

Then He says: {Have We not made the earth a receptacle, for the living and the dead?} Ibn Abbas said: {kifatan} means a shelter. Mujahid said: the dead person is gathered into it so that nothing of him is seen. Al-Sha'bi said: its interior is for your dead, and its surface is for your living. Mujahid and Qatadah said likewise.

{And We placed therein lofty firm mountains}, meaning the mountains, by which He anchored the earth so that it would not shake and tremble.

{And We gave you to drink sweet water}, fresh and pure, from the clouds, or from what God caused to spring forth from the springs of the earth.

{Woe that Day to the deniers}, meaning woe to whoever reflects upon these created things that point to the greatness of their Creator, and then, after this, persists in his denial and disbelief.

## 77:29-40 — verses 29-40 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, addressing the disbelievers who deny the return, the reckoning, the Garden and the Fire, that it will be said to them on the Day of Resurrection: {Proceed to that which you used to deny. Proceed to a shadow having three columns}, meaning the flame of the Fire when it rises and smoke ascends with it, for from its intensity and force it has three columns. {Not shading, and not availing against the flame}, that is, the shadow of the smoke facing the flame is not itself shading, nor does it avail against the flame, meaning it does not protect them from the heat of the flame.

Concerning His saying {It throws off sparks like al-qasr}, that is, sparks fly off from its flame like al-qasr. Ibn Mas'ud said: like fortresses. Ibn 'Abbas, Qatadah, Mujahid, and Malik reporting from Zayd ibn Aslam, and others, said: it means the trunks of trees.

{As if they were yellow camels}, that is, like black camels. This was said by Mujahid, al-Hasan, Qatadah, and al-Dahhak, and Ibn Jarir chose this view.

From Ibn 'Abbas, Mujahid, and Sa'id ibn Jubayr: {yellow camels} means the ropes of ships. And from him, meaning Ibn 'Abbas: {yellow camels} means pieces of copper.

Al-Bukhari said: [chain] 'Abd al-Rahman ibn 'Abis said: I heard Ibn 'Abbas say concerning {It throws off sparks like al-qasr}: We used to take a piece of wood three cubits long or more, and raise it up for the winter, and we would call it al-qasar. {As if they were yellow camels}: the ropes of ships, gathered together until they become like the waists of men. {Woe that Day to the deniers}.

Then the Almighty says: {This is the Day when they will not speak}, that is, they will not talk.

{Nor will they be permitted to make excuses}, that is, they are not able to speak, nor are they permitted to do so in order to make excuses. Rather, the proof has been established against them, and the sentence has fallen upon them for the wrong they did, so they do not speak. The stations of the Resurrection are various states, and the Lord, the Almighty, informs of this state at one time and of that state at another, to indicate the severity of the terrors and the tremors on that Day. For this reason He says after each section of this discourse: {Woe that Day to the deniers}.

Concerning His saying: {This is the Day of Judgment. We have gathered you and the former peoples. So if you have a plan, then plot it against Me}, this is an address from the Creator to His servants in which He says to them: {This is the Day of Judgment. We have gathered you and the former peoples}, meaning that He has gathered them by His power upon a single plain, where the caller makes them hear and the sight penetrates through all of them.

Concerning His saying: {So if you have a plan, then plot it against Me}, this is a severe threat and a firm warning, that is: if you are able to escape from My grasp and be saved from My judgment, then do so, for indeed you are not able to do that. As the Almighty says: {O company of jinn and mankind, if you are able to pass beyond the regions of the heavens and the earth, then pass beyond them. You will not pass beyond except by authority} [al-Rahman: 33]. And the Almighty says: {And you will not harm Him at all} [Hud: 57]. And in the hadith: "O My servants, you will never reach a benefit to Me so as to benefit Me, and you will never reach a harm to Me so as to harm Me."

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Abu 'Abdullah al-Jadali said: I came to Bayt al-Maqdis (Jerusalem), and there were 'Ubadah ibn al-Samit, 'Abdullah ibn 'Amr, and Ka'b al-Ahbar conversing in Bayt al-Maqdis. 'Ubadah said: When the Day of Resurrection comes, Allah will gather the former peoples and the later ones upon a single plain, where the sight penetrates through them and the caller makes them hear, and Allah will say: {This is the Day of Judgment. We have gathered you and the former peoples. So if you have a plan, then plot it against Me}. Today no defiant tyrant will escape from Me, nor any rebellious devil. 'Abdullah ibn 'Amr said: We are told that on that Day a neck will come forth from the Fire and will move forward until, when it is between the ranks of the people, it will call out: O people, I have been sent against three whom I know better than a father knows his son and a brother knows his brother. No refuge can hide them from me, and no hidden thing can conceal them from me: he who set up another god alongside Allah, and every defiant tyrant, and every rebellious devil. Then it will coil around them and hurl them into the Fire forty years before the reckoning.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَإِنْ كَانَ لَكُمْ كَيْدٌ فَكِيدُونِ﴾ تَهْدِيدٌ شَدِيدٌ وَوَعِيدٌ أَكِيدٌ، أَيْ: إِنْ قَدَرْتُمْ عَلَى أَنْ تَتَخَلَّصُوا مِنْ قَبْضَتِي، وتَنجُوا مِنْ حُكْمِي فَافْعَلُوا، فَإِنَّكُمْ لَا تَقْدِرُونَ عَلَى ذَلِكَ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى ﴿يَا مَعْشَرَ الْجِنِّ وَالإنْسِ إِنِ اسْتَطَعْتُمْ أَنْ تَنْفُذُوا مِنْ أَقْطَارِ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالأرْضِ فَانْفُذُوا لَا تَنْفُذُونَ إِلا بِسُلْطَانٍ﴾ [الرَّحْمَنِ: ٣٣] ، وَقَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَلا تَضُرُّونَهُ شَيْئًا﴾ [هُودٍ: ٥٧] وَفِي الْحَدِيثِ: "يَا عِبَادِي، إِنَّكُمْ لَنْ تَبلُغوا نَفْعِي فَتَنْفَعُونِي، وَلَنْ تَبْلُغُوا ضُرِّي فَتَضُرُّونِي".

وَقَدْ قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ الْمُنْذِرِ الطَّرِيقِيُّ الْأَوْدِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ فُضَيْلٍ، حَدَّثَنَا حُصيَن بْنُ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ، عَنْ حَسَّانَ بْنِ أَبِي الْمُخَارِقِ، عَنْ أَبِي عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الجَدَلي قَالَ: أَتَيْتُ بَيْتَ الْمَقْدِسِ، فَإِذَا عُبادة بْنُ الصَّامِتِ، وَعَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ عَمْرٍو، وَكَعْبُ الْأَحْبَارِ يَتَحَدَّثُونَ فِي بَيْتِ الْمَقْدِسِ، فَقَالَ عُبَادَةُ: إِذَا كَانَ يَوْمُ الْقِيَامَةِ جَمَعَ اللَّهُ الْأَوَّلِينَ وَالْآخَرِينَ بِصَعِيدٍ وَاحِدٍ، يَنْفُذُهُمُ الْبَصَرُ ويُسمعهم الدَّاعِي، وَيَقُولُ اللَّهُ: ﴿هَذَا يَوْمُ الْفَصْلِ جَمَعْنَاكُمْ وَالأوَّلِينَ فَإِنْ كَانَ لَكُمْ كَيْدٌ فَكِيدُونِ﴾ الْيَوْمَ لَا يَنْجُو مِنِّي جَبَّارٌ عَنِيدٌ، وَلَا شَيْطَانٌ مَرِيدٌ. فَقَالَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ عَمْرٍو : فَإِنَّا نُحَدَّثُ يَوْمَئِذٍ أَنَّهُ يَخْرُجُ عُنُق مِنَ النَّارِ فَتَنْطَلِقُ حَتَّى إِذَا كَانَتْ بَيْنَ ظَهَرَانَيِ النَّاسِ نَادَتْ: أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ، إِنِّي بُعثتُ إِلَى ثَلاثَة أَنَا أَعْرَفُ بِهِمْ مِنَ الْأَبِ بِوَلَدِهِ وَمِنَ الْأَخِ بِأَخِيهِ، لَا يُغَيّبهم عَنِّي وَزَر، وَلَا تُخفِيهم عَنِّي خَافِيَةٌ: الَّذِي جَعَلَ مَعَ اللَّهِ إِلَهًا آخَرَ، وَكُلُّ جَبَّارٍ عَنِيدٍ، وَكُلُّ شَيْطَانٍ مَرِيدٍ. فَتَنْطَوِي عَلَيْهِمْ فَتَقْذِفُ بِهِمْ فِي النار قبل الحساب بأربعين سنة .

## 77:41-50 — verses 41-50 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, informing about His God fearing servants who worshipped Him by performing the obligatory duties and abandoning the forbidden things: that on the Day of Resurrection they will be amid gardens and springs, meaning in contrast to what those wretched ones will be in, namely the shade of al-yahmum, which is the black, foul smelling smoke.

{And fruits from whatever they desire}, meaning from all types of fruit, whatever they seek they find. {Eat and drink, well being to you, for what you used to do}, meaning this is said to them as a form of kindness and honor toward them.

Then the Almighty says, giving a fresh statement: {Indeed thus do We reward the doers of good}, meaning this is Our recompense for whoever does good deeds. {Woe that Day to the deniers.}

His statement, {Eat and enjoy yourselves a little; indeed you are criminals}, is an address to those who deny the Day of Judgment, and He commands them with a command of threat and warning, saying: {Eat and enjoy yourselves a little}, meaning for a brief, near, short period. {Indeed you are criminals}, meaning then you will be driven to the fire of Hell that was mentioned previously. {Woe that Day to the deniers}, as the Almighty says: {We let them enjoy themselves a little, then We will force them to a harsh punishment} [Luqman: 24], and He says: {Indeed those who fabricate lies against Allah will not succeed. A brief enjoyment in this world, then to Us is their return, then We will make them taste the severe punishment for what they used to disbelieve} [Yunus: 69-70].

His statement, {And when it is said to them, bow down, they do not bow down}, means when these ignorant disbelievers are commanded to be among those who pray with the congregation, they refuse to do so and act arrogantly toward it. That is why He says: {Woe that Day to the deniers.}

Then He says: {Then in what statement after this will they believe?} meaning, if they do not believe in this Qur'an, then in what speech will they believe? This is like His statement: {Then in what statement after Allah and His signs will they believe?} [al-Jathiyah: 6].

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Isma'il ibn Umayyah said: I heard a Bedouin man say: I heard Abu Hurayrah narrate that when he recited {By the winds sent forth in succession}, and reached {Then in what statement after this will they believe?}, he would say: I believe in Allah and in what He has revealed.

This hadith has already been mentioned in Surat al-Qiyamah.

End of the tafsir of Surat "al-Mursalat." [To Allah belongs all praise and grace.]
