## 91:1-10 — verses 1-10 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Tafsir of Surah {And by the sun and its morning brightness}

And it is Meccan.

The hadith of Jabir, which is found in the two Sahihs, has already preceded: that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said to Mu'adh, "Why did you not pray with {Glorify the name of your Lord, the Most High}, {And by the sun and its morning brightness}, {And by the night when it covers}?"

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

* *

Mujahid said: {And by the sun and its morning brightness} means: and its light. Qatadah said: {and its morning brightness} means the entire day.

Ibn Jarir said: The correct view is to say that Allah swore by the sun and its daylight, for the visible light of the sun is the day.

{And by the moon when it follows it}: Mujahid said: it follows it. Al-'Awfi, from Ibn 'Abbas, said regarding {And by the moon when it follows it}: it follows the day. Qatadah said: {when it follows it} refers to the night of the new crescent, for when the sun sets the crescent is seen.

Ibn Zayd said: It (the moon) follows it (the sun) during the first half of the month; then it (the sun) follows it (the moon); and it (the moon) precedes it (the sun) during the latter half of the month.

Malik said, from Zayd ibn Aslam: {when it follows it} refers to the Night of Decree (Laylat al-Qadr).

* *

His saying, {And by the day when it reveals it}: Mujahid said: it illuminates it. Qatadah said regarding {And by the day when it reveals it}: when the day covers it.

Ibn Jarir said: Some grammarians interpreted that to mean: "And the day, when it dispels the darkness," because the wording of the passage points to it.

I say: had this speaker interpreted [that] to mean {And by the day when it reveals it}, that is, reveals the flat expanse of the earth, that would have been more fitting, and his interpretation would then also hold correctly for Allah's saying {And by the night when it covers it}, and it would be better and stronger, and Allah knows best. For this reason Mujahid said: {And by the day when it reveals it} is like His saying, {And by the day when it appears in brightness} [al-Layl: 2].

As for Ibn Jarir, he chose that the pronoun throughout all of that refers back to the sun, because of its having already been mentioned. And they said regarding His saying, {And by the night when it covers it}: meaning, when it covers the sun as it sets, so the horizons grow dark.

Baqiyyah ibn al-Walid said, from Safwan: Yazid ibn Dhi Hamamah narrated to me, saying: When night comes, the Lord, Glorious is His Majesty, says: "My great creation has covered My servants; the night stands in awe of Him, yet the One who created it is more deserving of being held in awe." Narrated by Ibn Abi Hatim.

* *

His saying, {And by the heaven and what built it}: it is possible that "ma" here is a masdariyyah (a particle turning what follows into a verbal noun), meaning: "And by the heaven and its construction." This is the view of Qatadah. And it is possible that it carries the meaning of "man" (who), meaning: "And by the heaven and the One who built it." This is the view of Mujahid. Both meanings are inseparable from one another, and "construction" here means raising up, as in His saying, {And the heaven We constructed it with strength}, that is, with power, {and indeed We are expanding it. And the earth We spread it out, and how excellent are the spreaders} [al-Dhariyat: 47-48].

Likewise His saying, {And by the earth and what spread it out}: Mujahid said: {spread it out} means: he stretched it out. Al-'Awfi, from Ibn 'Abbas, said regarding {and what spread it out}: meaning: he created within it.

'Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn 'Abbas: {spread it out} means: he divided it.

Mujahid, Qatadah, al-Dahhak, al-Suddi, al-Thawri, Abu Salih, and Ibn Zayd said: {spread it out} means: he flattened it out.

This is the most well-known of the sayings, and most of the exegetes hold it, and it is what is recognized among the people of language. Al-Jawhari said: "tahawtuhu" is like "dahawtuhu," meaning: I spread it out.

* *

His saying, {And by the soul and what proportioned it}: meaning, He created it well-formed and upright upon the sound fitrah (innate nature), as Allah, Exalted is He, said: {So direct your face toward the religion, inclining truly, the fitrah of Allah upon which He created mankind. There is no changing the creation of Allah} [al-Rum: 30]. And the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Every newborn is born upon the fitrah, then his parents make him a Jew, a Christian, or a Magian, just as an animal is born whole and intact. Do you perceive in it any deficiency?"

The two of them (al-Bukhari and Muslim) reported it from the narration of Abu Hurayrah.

And in Sahih Muslim, from the narration of 'Iyad ibn Himar al-Mujashi'i, from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, who said: "Allah, Mighty and Sublime, says: I created My servants as people of pure inclination (hunafa'), then the devils came to them and turned them away from their religion."

* *

His saying, {Then He inspired it with its wickedness and its righteousness}: meaning, He guided it to its wickedness and its righteousness, that is, He made that clear to it, and guided it to what had been decreed for it.

Ibn 'Abbas said: {Then He inspired it with its wickedness and its righteousness} means: He made clear to it good and evil. Mujahid, Qatadah, al-Dahhak, and al-Thawri said likewise.

Sa'id ibn Jubayr said: He inspired it with good and evil. Ibn Zayd said: He placed within it its wickedness and its righteousness.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] ... Abu al-Aswad al-Dili said: 'Imran ibn Husayn said to me: "Tell me, what people do and toil at, is it something that has been decreed for them and already settled by a decree that has preceded, or is it something yet to come which their Prophet ﷺ brought to them, and by which the proof was established against them?" I said: "Rather, it is something that has been decreed for them." He said: "Then could that be injustice?" I was struck with severe alarm at that. I said to him: "There is nothing but that it is His creation and in the grip of His hand; He is not asked about what He does, while they are asked." He said: "May Allah make you sound in judgment; I only asked so as to test your intellect. A man from Muzaynah, or Juhaynah, came to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and said: O Messenger of Allah, tell me, what people do and toil at, is it something that has been decreed for them and already settled by a decree that has preceded, or is it something yet to come which their Prophet brought to them, and by which the proof was established against them? He said: Rather, it is something that has already been decreed for them. He said: Then what do we work for? He said: Whomever Allah has created for one of the two abodes, He prepares him for it. And the confirmation of that is in the Book of Allah: {And by the soul and what proportioned it, then He inspired it with its wickedness and its righteousness}."

Narrated by Ahmad and Muslim, from the hadith of 'Azrah ibn Thabit, with this chain.

* *

His saying, {He has certainly succeeded who purifies it, and he has certainly failed who corrupts it}: it is possible that the meaning is: he has succeeded who purifies his own soul, that is, through obedience to Allah, as Qatadah said, and cleanses it of base character traits and vices. Something similar is reported from Mujahid, 'Ikrimah, and Sa'id ibn Jubayr. And it is like His saying, {He has certainly succeeded who purifies himself, and remembers the name of his Lord, and prays} [al-A'la: 14-15].

{And he has certainly failed who corrupts it}: that is, buries it away, meaning he obscures it and debases it by abandoning it and depriving it of guidance, until he commits sins and forsakes obedience to Allah, Mighty and Sublime.

It is also possible that the meaning is: he has succeeded whose soul Allah has purified, and he has failed whose soul Allah has corrupted, as al-'Awfi and 'Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn 'Abbas.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] ... Ibn 'Abbas said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say concerning Allah's saying, {He has certainly succeeded who purifies it}, the Prophet ﷺ said: "A soul has succeeded whom Allah has purified."

Ibn Abi Hatim narrated it from the hadith of Abu Malik, with this chain. This Juwaybir is Ibn Sa'id, whose hadith is discarded (matruk, abandoned as unreliable), and al-Dahhak never met Ibn 'Abbas.

Al-Tabarani said: [chain] ... Ibn 'Abbas said: Whenever the Messenger of Allah ﷺ passed by this verse, {And by the soul and what proportioned it, then He inspired it with its wickedness and its righteousness}, he would pause, then say: "O Allah, grant my soul its righteousness; You are its Guardian and Protector, and the best of those who purify it."

Another hadith: Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] ... Abu Hurayrah said: I heard the Prophet ﷺ recite {then He inspired it with its wickedness and its righteousness}, then say: "O Allah, grant my soul its righteousness, and purify it, You are the best of those who purify it, You are its Guardian and Protector." They did not report it by this route.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] ... 'A'ishah reported that she missed the Prophet ﷺ from his sleeping place, so she felt for him with her hand, and found him prostrating, saying: "My Lord, grant my soul its righteousness, and purify it, You are the best of those who purify it, You are its Guardian and Protector." He alone related it.

Another hadith: Imam Ahmad said: [chain] ... Zayd ibn Arqam said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to say: "O Allah, I seek refuge in You from incapacity, laziness, decrepit old age, cowardice, miserliness, and the punishment of the grave. O Allah, grant my soul its righteousness, and purify it, You are the best of those who purify it, You are its Guardian and Protector. O Allah, I seek refuge in You from a heart that does not humble itself, a soul that is not satisfied, knowledge that does not benefit, and a supplication that is not answered." Zayd said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to teach them to us, and we teach them to you.

Narrated by Muslim from the hadith of Abu Mu'awiyah, from 'Asim al-Ahwal, from 'Abdullah ibn al-Harith and Abu 'Uthman al-Nahdi, from Zayd ibn Arqam, with this chain.

### Source (Arabic) — review against this

تَفْسِيرُ سُورَةِ وَالشَّمْسِ وَضُحَاهَا

وَهِيَ مَكِّيَّةٌ.

تَقَدَّمَ حَدِيثُ جَابِرٍ الَّذِي فِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ لِمُعَاذٍ: "هَلَّا صَلَّيْتَ بِـ ﴿سَبِّحِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ الأعْلَى﴾ ﴿وَالشَّمْسِ وَضُحَاهَا﴾ ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَغْشَى﴾ ؟

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

* *

قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: ﴿وَالشَّمْسِ وَضُحَاهَا﴾ أَيْ: وَضَوْئِهَا. وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: ﴿وَضُحَاهَا﴾ النَّهَارُ كُلُّهُ.

قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: وَالصَّوَابُ أَنْ يُقَالَ: أَقْسَمَ اللَّهُ بِالشَّمْسِ وَنَهَارِهَا؛ لِأَنَّ ضَوْءَ الشَّمْسِ الظَّاهِرَ هُوَ النَّهَارُ .

﴿وَالْقَمَرِ إِذَا تَلاهَا﴾ قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: تَبِعَهَا. وَقَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿وَالْقَمَرِ إِذَا تَلاهَا﴾ قَالَ: يَتْلُو النَّهَارَ. وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: ﴿إِذَا تَلاهَا﴾ لَيْلَةَ الْهِلَالِ، إِذَا سَقَطَتِ الشَّمْسُ رُؤِيَ الْهِلَالُ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ زَيْدٍ: هُوَ يَتْلُوهَا فِي النِّصْفِ الْأَوَّلِ مِنَ الشَّهْرِ، ثُمَّ هِيَ تَتْلُوهُ. وَهُوَ يَتَقَدَّمُهَا فِي النِّصْفِ الْأَخِيرِ مِنَ الشَّهْرِ.

وَقَالَ مَالِكٌ، عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ: إِذَا تَلَاهَا لَيْلَةَ الْقَدْرِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَالنَّهَارِ إِذَا جَلاهَا﴾ قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: أَضَاءَ. وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: ﴿وَالنَّهَارِ إِذَا جَلاهَا﴾ إِذَا غَشِيَهَا النَّهَارُ.

قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: وَكَانَ بَعْضُ أَهْلِ الْعَرَبِيَّةِ يَتَأَوَّلُ ذَلِكَ بِمَعْنَى: وَالنَّهَارُ إِذَا جَلَّا الظُّلْمَةِ، لِدَلَالَةِ الْكَلَامِ عَلَيْهَا.

قُلْتُ: وَلَوْ أَنَّ هَذَا الْقَائِلَ تَأَوَّلَ [ذَلِكَ] بِمَعْنَى ﴿وَالنَّهَارِ إِذَا جَلاهَا﴾ أَيِ: الْبَسِيطَةُ، لَكَانَ أَوْلَى، وَلَصَحَّ [تَأْوِيلُهُ فِي] قَوْلِ اللَّهِ ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَغْشَاهَا﴾ فَكَانَ أَجْوَدْ وَأَقْوَى، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ. وَلِهَذَا قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: ﴿وَالنَّهَارِ إِذَا جَلاهَا﴾ إِنَّهُ كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَالنَّهَارِ إِذَا تَجَلَّى﴾ [الليل: ٢] .

وَأَمَّا ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ فَاخْتَارَ عَوْدَ الضَّمِيرِ فِي ذَلِكَ كُلِّهِ عَلَى الشَّمْسِ، لِجَرَيَانِ ذِكْرِهَا. وَقَالُوا فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَغْشَاهَا﴾ يَعْنِي: إِذَا يَغْشَى الشَّمْسَ حِينَ تَغِيبُ، فَتُظْلِمُ الْآفَاقُ.

وَقَالَ بَقِيَّة بْنُ الْوَلِيدِ، عَنْ صَفْوَانَ، حَدَّثَنِي يَزِيدُ بْنُ ذِي حَمَامَةَ قَالَ: إِذَا جَاءَ اللَّيْلُ قَالَ الرَّبُّ جَلَّ جَلَالُهُ: غَشِيَ عِبَادِي خَلْقِي الْعَظِيمُ، فَاللَّيْلُ يَهَابُهُ، وَالَّذِي خَلَقَهُ أَحَقُّ أَنْ يَهَابَ. رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَالسَّمَاءِ وَمَا بَنَاهَا﴾ يُحْتَمَلُ أَنْ تَكُونَ "مَا" هَاهُنَا مَصْدَرِيَّةٌ، بِمَعْنَى: وَالسَّمَاءِ وَبِنَائِهَا. وَهُوَ قَوْلُ قَتَادَةَ، وَيُحْتَمَلُ أَنْ تَكُونَ بِمَعْنَى "مَن" يَعْنِي: وَالسَّمَاءِ وَبَانِيهَا. وَهُوَ قَوْلُ مُجَاهِدٍ، وَكِلَاهُمَا مُتَلَازِمٌ، وَالْبِنَاءُ هُوَ الرَّفْعُ، كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَالسَّمَاءَ بَنَيْنَاهَا بِأَيْدٍ﴾ أَيْ: بِقُوَّةٍ ﴿وَإِنَّا لَمُوسِعُونَ وَالأرْضَ فَرَشْنَاهَا فَنِعْمَ الْمَاهِدُونَ﴾ [الذَّارِيَاتِ: ٤٧، ٤٨] .

وَهَكَذَا قَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَالأرْضِ وَمَا طَحَاهَا﴾ قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: ﴿طَحَاهَا﴾ دَحَاهَا. وَقَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿وَمَا طَحَاهَا﴾ أَيْ: خَلَقَ فِيهَا.

وَقَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿طَحَاهَا﴾ قَسَمَهَا.

وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَقَتَادَةُ وَالضَّحَّاكُ، والسُّدِّي، وَالثَّوْرِيُّ، وَأَبُو صَالِحٍ، وَابْنُ زَيْدٍ: ﴿طَحَاهَا﴾ بَسَطَهَا.

وَهَذَا أَشْهَرُ الْأَقْوَالِ، وَعَلَيْهِ الْأَكْثَرُ مِنَ الْمُفَسِّرِينَ، وَهُوَ الْمَعْرُوفُ عِنْدَ أَهْلِ اللُّغَةِ، قَالَ الْجَوْهَرِيُّ: طَحَوْتُهُ مِثْلُ دَحَوْتُهُ، أَيْ: بَسَطْتُهُ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَنَفْسٍ وَمَا سَوَّاهَا﴾ أَيْ: خَلَقَهَا سَوِيَّةً مُسْتَقِيمَةً عَلَى الْفِطْرَةِ الْقَوِيمَةِ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَأَقِمْ وَجْهَكَ لِلدِّينِ حَنِيفًا فِطْرَةَ اللَّهِ الَّتِي فَطَرَ النَّاسَ عَلَيْهَا لَا تَبْدِيلَ لِخَلْقِ اللَّهِ﴾ [الرُّومِ: ٣٠] وَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "كُلُّ مَوْلُودٍ يُولَدُ عَلَى الْفِطْرَةِ، فَأَبَوَاهُ يُهَوِّدانه أَوْ يُنَصِّرانه أَوْ يُمَجِّسانه، كَمَا تُولَدُ الْبَهِيمَةُ بَهِيمَةً جَمْعَاء هَلْ تُحِسُّونَ فِيهَا مِنْ جَدْعَاءَ؟ ".

أَخْرَجَاهُ مِنْ رِوَايَةِ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ 

وَفِي صَحِيحِ مُسْلِمٍ مِنْ رِوَايَةِ عِيَاضِ بْنِ حِمَارٍ الْمُجَاشِعِيِّ، عَنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "يَقُولُ اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ: إِنِّي خَلَقْتُ عِبَادِي حُنَفاء فَجَاءَتْهُمُ الشَّيَاطِينُ فَاجْتَالَتْهُمْ عَنْ دِينِهِمْ" .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَأَلْهَمَهَا فُجُورَهَا وَتَقْوَاهَا﴾ أَيْ: فَأَرْشَدَهَا إِلَى فُجُورِهَا وَتَقْوَاهَا، أَيْ: بَيَّنَ لَهَا ذَلِكَ، وَهَدَاهَا إِلَى مَا قُدِّرَ لَهَا.

قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿فَأَلْهَمَهَا فُجُورَهَا وَتَقْوَاهَا﴾ بَيَّنَ لَهَا الْخَيْرَ وَالشَّرَّ. وَكَذَا قال مجاهد، وقتادة، والضحاك، والثوري.

وَقَالَ سَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ: أَلْهَمَهَا الْخَيْرَ وَالشَّرَّ. وَقَالَ ابْنُ زَيْدٍ: جَعَلَ فِيهَا فُجُورَهَا وَتَقْوَاهَا.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ بَشَّارٍ، حَدَّثَنَا صَفْوَانُ بْنُ عِيسَى وَأَبُو عَاصِمٍ النَّبِيلُ قَالَا حَدَّثَنَا عَزْرَة بْنُ ثَابِتٍ، حَدَّثَنِي يَحْيَى بْنُ عَقِيلٍ، عَنْ يَحْيَى بْنِ يَعْمَر، عَنْ أَبِي الْأَسْوَدِ الدِّيلِيِّ قَالَ: قَالَ لِي عِمْرَانُ بْنُ حُصَيْنٍ: أَرَأَيْتَ مَا يَعْمَلُ فِيهِ النَّاسُ وَيَتَكَادَحُونَ فِيهِ، أَشَيْءٌ قُضِيَ عَلَيْهِمْ وَمَضَى عَلَيْهِمْ مِنْ قَدَر قَدْ سَبَقَ، أَوْ فِيمَا يُستَقبَلُون مِمَّا أَتَاهُمْ بِهِ نَبِيُّهُمْ ﷺ، وَأَكَّدَتْ عَلَيْهِمُ الْحُجَّةُ؟ قُلْتُ: بَلْ شَيْءٌ قُضِيَ عَلَيْهِمْ. قَالَ: فَهَلْ يَكُونُ ذَلِكَ ظُلْمًا؟ قَالَ: فَفَزِعْتُ مِنْهُ فَزَعًا شَدِيدًا، قَالَ: قُلْتُ لَهُ: لَيْسَ شَيْءٌ إِلَّا وَهُوَ خَلقُه وملْك يَده، لَا يسألُ عَمَّا يَفْعَلُ وَهُمْ يُسْأَلُونَ. قَالَ: سَدَّدَكَ اللَّهُ، إِنَّمَا سَأَلْتُ لِأَخْبَرَ عَقْلَكَ، إِنَّ رَجُلًا مِنْ مُزَينة -أَوْ جُهَيْنَةَ-أَتَى رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، أَرَأَيْتَ مَا يَعْمَلُ النَّاسُ فِيهِ وَيَتَكَادَحُونَ، أَشَيْءٌ قُضِيَ عَلَيْهِمْ وَمَضَى عَلَيْهِمْ مِنْ قَدر قَدْ سَبَقَ، أَمْ شَيْءٌ مِمَّا يَسْتَقْبِلُونَ مِمَّا أَتَاهُمْ بِهِ نَبِيُّهُمْ، وَأَكَّدَتْ بِهِ عَلَيْهِمُ الْحُجَّةُ؟ قَالَ: "بَلْ شَيْءٌ قَدْ قُضِيَ عَلَيْهِمْ". قَالَ: فَفِيمَ نَعْمَلُ؟ قَالَ: "مَنْ كَانَ اللَّهُ خَلَقَهُ لِإِحْدَى الْمَنْزِلَتَيْنِ يُهَيِّئه لَهَا، وَتَصْدِيقُ ذَلِكَ فِي كِتَابِ اللَّهِ: ﴿وَنَفْسٍ وَمَا سَوَّاهَا فَأَلْهَمَهَا فُجُورَهَا وَتَقْوَاهَا﴾

رَوَاهُ أَحْمَدُ وَمُسْلِمٌ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ عَزْرَة بْنِ ثَابِتٍ بِهِ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿قَدْ أَفْلَحَ مَنْ زَكَّاهَا وَقَدْ خَابَ مَنْ دَسَّاهَا﴾ يُحْتَمَلُ أَنْ يَكُونَ الْمَعْنَى: قَدْ أَفْلَحَ مَنْ زَكَّى نَفْسَهُ، أَيْ: بِطَاعَةِ اللَّهِ -كَمَا قَالَ قَتَادَةُ-وَطَهَّرَهَا مِنَ الْأَخْلَاقِ الدَّنِيئَةِ وَالرَّذَائِلِ. ويُروَى نَحْوُهُ عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ، وَعِكْرِمَةَ، وَسَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ. وَكَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿قَدْ أَفْلَحَ مَنْ تَزَكَّى وَذَكَرَ اسْمَ رَبِّهِ فَصَلَّى﴾ [الْأَعْلَى: ١٤، ١٥] .

﴿وَقَدْ خَابَ مَنْ دَسَّاهَا﴾ أَيْ: دَسَّسَهَا، أَيْ: أَخْمَلَهَا وَوَضَعَ مِنْهَا بِخِذْلَانِهِ إِيَّاهَا عَنِ الهُدَى، حَتَّى رَكِبَ الْمَعَاصِيَ وَتَرَكَ طَاعَةَ اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ.

وَقَدْ يُحْتَمَلُ أَنْ يَكُونَ الْمَعْنَى: قَدْ أَفْلَحَ مَنْ زَكَّى اللَّهُ نَفْسَهُ، وَقَدْ خَابَ مَنْ دَسَّى اللَّهُ نَفْسَهُ، كَمَا قَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ وَعَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عباس.

وقال ابن أبي حاتم: حدثنا أَبِي وَأَبُو زُرْعَة قَالَا حَدَّثَنَا سَهْلُ بْنُ عُثْمَانَ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو مَالِكٍ -يَعْنِي عَمْرُو بْنُ هِشَامٍ-عَنْ جُوَيبر، عَنِ الضَّحَّاكِ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ فِي قَوْلِ اللَّهِ: ﴿قَدْ أَفْلَحَ مَنْ زَكَّاهَا﴾ قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: "أَفْلَحَتْ نَفْسٌ زَكَّاهَا اللَّهُ" .

وَرَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي مَالِكٍ، بِهِ. وَجُوَيْبِرٌ [هَذَا] هُوَ ابْنُ سَعِيدٍ، مَتْرُوكُ الحديث، والضحاك لم يلق ابن عباس.

وَقَالَ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى بْنُ عُثْمَانَ بْنِ صَالِحٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ لَهِيعة، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ دِينَارٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ إِذَا مَرَّ بِهَذِهِ الْآيَةِ: ﴿وَنَفْسٍ وَمَا سَوَّاهَا فَأَلْهَمَهَا فُجُورَهَا وَتَقْوَاهَا﴾ وَقَفَ، ثُمَّ قَالَ: "اللَّهُمَّ آتِ نَفْسِي تَقْوَاهَا، أَنْتَ وَلِيُّهَا وَمَوْلَاهَا، وَخَيْرُ مَنْ زَكَّاهَا" .

حَدِيثٌ آخَرُ: قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو زُرْعَة، حَدَّثَنَا يَعْقُوبُ بْنُ حُمَيْدٍ الْمَدَنِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الْأُمَوِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا مَعْن بْنُ مُحَمَّدٍ الْغِفَارِيُّ، عَنْ حَنْظَلَةَ بْنِ عَلِيٍّ الْأَسْلَمِيِّ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ يَقْرَأُ: ﴿فَأَلْهَمَهَا فُجُورَهَا وَتَقْوَاهَا﴾ قَالَ: "اللَّهُمَّ آتِ نَفْسِي تَقْوَاهَا، وَزَكِّهَا أَنْتَ خَيْرُ مَنْ زَكَّاهَا، أَنْتَ وَلِيُّهَا وَمَوْلَاهَا" لَمْ يُخَرِّجُوهُ مِنْ هَذَا الْوَجْهِ.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا وَكِيع، عَنْ نَافِعٍ -عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ-عَنْ صَالِحِ بْنِ سُعَيد، عَنْ عَائِشَةَ: أَنَّهَا فَقَدت النَّبِيَّ ﷺ مِنْ مَضْجَعِهِ، فَلَمَسَتْهُ بِيَدِهَا، فَوَقَعَتْ عَلَيْهِ وَهُوَ سَاجِدٌ، وَهُوَ يَقُولُ: "رَبِّ، أَعْطِ نَفْسِي تَقْوَاهَا، وَزَكِّهَا أَنْتَ خَيْرُ مَنْ زَكَّاهَا، أَنْتَ وَلِيُّهَا وَمَوْلَاهَا" تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ.

حَدِيثٌ آخَرُ: قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَفَّانُ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الْوَاحِدِ بْنُ زِيَادٍ، حَدَّثَنَا عَاصِمٌ الْأَحْوَلُ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنُ الْحَارِثِ، عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَرْقَمَ قَالَ: كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "اللَّهُمَّ، إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الْعَجْزِ والكَسَل وَالْهَرَمِ، والجُبن وَالْبُخْلِ وَعَذَابِ الْقَبْرِ. اللَّهُمَّ، آتِ نَفْسِي تَقْوَاهَا، وَزَكِّهَا أَنْتَ خَيْرُ مَنْ زَكَّاهَا، أَنْتَ وَلِيُّهَا وَمَوْلَاهَا. اللَّهُمَّ، إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ قَلْب لَا يَخْشَعُ، وَمِنْ نَفْس لَا تَشْبَعُ، وعِلْم لَا يَنْفَعُ، وَدَعْوَةٍ لَا يُسْتَجَابُ لَهَا". قَالَ زَيْدٌ: كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ يُعَلِّمُنَاهُنَّ وَنَحْنُ نُعَلِّمُكُوهُنَّ.

رَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي مُعَاوِيَةَ، عَنْ عَاصِمٍ الْأَحْوَلِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ الْحَارِثِ -وَأَبِي عُثْمَانَ النَّهْدِيِّ، عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَرْقَمَ، بِهِ .

## 91:11-15 — verses 11-15 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted informs about Thamud, that they denied their messenger, because of the tughyan (transgression, overstepping bounds) and injustice they were engaged in.

Muhammad ibn Ka'b said: {by its transgression} means: by all of them together.

The first view is preferable, and it was said by Mujahid, Qatadah, and others besides them. That resulted for them in denial in their hearts of what their messenger brought them of guidance and certainty.

{When the most wretched of them was sent forth} means: the most wretched of the tribe. He was Qudar ibn Salif, the hamstringer of the she-camel, and he is Uhaymir Thamud (the little red one of Thamud), and he is the one about whom the Exalted said: {So they called their companion, and he took it up, and hamstrung her} [al-Qamar: 29]. This man was mighty among them, noble in his people, well-lineaged, a chief, obeyed, as Imam Ahmad said:

Ibn Numayr told us, Hisham told us, from his father, from 'Abdullah ibn Zam'ah, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ delivered a sermon, and he mentioned the she-camel and mentioned the one who hamstrung her, and he said: "{When the most wretched of them was sent forth}, there was sent forth against her a fierce man, mighty, formidable among his kinsfolk, like Abu Zam'ah."

Al-Bukhari related it in the Tafsir, Muslim in the Description of the Fire, al-Tirmidhi and al-Nasa'i in the Tafsir sections of their two Sunan works, and likewise Ibn Jarir and Ibn Abi Hatim [through several routes] from Hisham ibn 'Urwah, with it.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] ... 'Ammar ibn Yasir said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said to 'Ali: "Shall I not tell you of the most wretched of people?" He said: "Yes." He said: "Two men: the little red one of Thamud who hamstrung the she-camel, and the one who will strike you, O 'Ali, upon this," meaning his head, "until this becomes drenched with it," meaning his beard.

* *

His saying: {Then the messenger of Allah said to them} means: Salih (peace be upon him): {The she-camel of Allah} means: beware, do not touch the she-camel of Allah with any harm, {and her turn to drink} means: do not transgress against her regarding her turn to drink, for she has the drink of a day and you have the drink of a known day. Allah said: {But they denied him and hamstrung her} means: they denied him in what he brought them, and that resulted for them in their hamstringing the she-camel which Allah had brought forth from the rock as a sign for them and a proof against them. {So their Lord thundered down upon them for their sin} means: He became angry with them and destroyed them. {And leveled it} means: He made the punishment fall upon them equally, upon all of them alike.

Qatadah said: It has reached us that Uhaymir Thamud did not hamstring the she-camel until their young and their old, their males and their females, all followed him in it. So when the people shared together in hamstringing her, Allah thundered down upon them for their sins, and leveled it upon them.

* *

His saying: {And He does not fear its consequence} - and it was also recited: "fa-la yakhafu 'uqbaha" (so He does not fear its consequence).

Ibn 'Abbas said: Allah does not fear any consequence from anyone. Mujahid, al-Hasan, Bakr ibn 'Abdullah al-Muzani, and others besides them said likewise.

Al-Dahhak and al-Suddi said: {And He does not fear its consequence} means: the one who hamstrung her did not fear the consequence of what he did. But the first view is preferable, on account of the context indicating it, and Allah knows best.

End of the tafsir of {By the sun and its morning brightness}.

حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ نُمَيْرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا هِشَامٌ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ زَمْعَةَ قَالَ: خَطَبَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ، فَذَكَرَ النَّاقَةَ، وَذَكَرَ الَّذِي عَقَرَهَا، فَقَالَ: " ﴿إِذِ انْبَعَثَ أَشْقَاهَا﴾ انْبَعَثَ لَهَا رَجُلٌ عَارِمٌ عَزِيزٌ مَنِيعٌ فِي رَهْطِهِ، مِثْلُ أَبِي زَمْعَةَ".

وَرَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ فِي التَّفْسِيرِ، وَمُسْلِمٌ فِي صِفَةِ النَّارِ، وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ وَالنَّسَائِيُّ فِي التَّفْسِيرِ مِنْ سُنَنِهِمَا وَكَذَا ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ وَابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ [مِنْ طُرُقٍ] عَنْ هِشَامِ بْنِ عُرْوَةَ، بِهِ .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو زُرْعَة، حَدَّثَنَا إِبْرَاهِيمُ بْنُ مُوسَى، حَدَّثَنَا عِيسَى بْنُ يُونُسَ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ إِسْحَاقَ، حَدَّثَنِي يَزِيدُ بْنُ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ خُثَيم عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ كَعْبٍ الْقُرَظِيِّ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ خُثَيم أَبِي يَزِيدَ عَنْ عَمَّارِ بْنِ يَاسِرٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ لَعَلِيٍّ: "أَلَا أُحَدِّثُكَ بِأَشْقَى النَّاسِ؟ ". قَالَ: بَلَى: قَالَ: "رَجُلَانِ؛ أُحَيْمِرُ ثَمُودَ الَّذِي عَقَر النَّاقَةَ، وَالَّذِي يَضْرِبُكَ يَا عَلِيُّ عَلَى هَذَا -يَعْنِي قَرنه-حَتَّى تَبْتَلَّ مِنْهُ هَذِهِ" يَعْنِي: لِحْيَتَهُ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَقَالَ لَهُمْ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ﴾ يَعْنِي: صَالِحًا، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ: ﴿نَاقَةُ اللَّهِ﴾ أَيِ: احْذَرُوا نَاقَةَ اللَّهِ أَنْ تَمَسُّوهَا بِسُوءٍ، ﴿وَسُقْيَاهَا﴾ أَيْ: لَا تَعْتَدُوا عَلَيْهَا فِي سُقْيَاهَا، فَإِنَّ لَهَا شِرْبُ يَوْمٍ وَلَكُمْ شِرْبُ يَوْمٍ مَعْلُومٍ. قَالَ اللَّهُ: ﴿فَكَذَّبُوهُ فَعَقَرُوهَا﴾ أَيْ: كَذَّبُوهُ فِيمَا جَاءَهُمْ بِهِ فَأَعْقَبَهُمْ ذَلِكَ أَنْ عَقَرُوا النَّاقَةَ الَّتِي أَخْرَجَهَا اللَّهُ مِنَ الصَّخْرَةِ آيَةً لَهُمْ وَحُجَّةً عَلَيْهِمْ، ﴿فَدَمْدَمَ عَلَيْهِمْ رَبُّهُمْ بِذَنْبِهِمْ﴾ أَيْ: غَضِبَ عَلَيْهِمْ، فَدَمَّرَ عَلَيْهِمْ، ﴿فَسَوَّاهَا﴾ أَيْ: فَجَعَلَ الْعُقُوبَةَ نَازِلَةً عَلَيْهِمْ عَلَى السَّوَاءِ.

قَالَ قَتَادَةُ: بَلَغَنَا أَنَّ أُحَيْمِرَ ثَمُودَ لَمْ يَعْقِرِ النَّاقَةَ حَتَّى تَابَعَهُ صَغِيرُهُمْ وَكَبِيرُهُمْ، وَذَكَرُهُمْ وَأُنْثَاهُمْ، فَلَمَّا اشْتَرَكَ الْقَوْمُ فِي عَقْرِهَا دَمْدَمَ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِمْ بِذُنُوبِهِمْ فَسَوَّاهَا.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَلا يَخَافُ عُقْبَاهَا﴾ وقرئ: "فلا يخاف عقباها".

قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: لَا يَخَافُ اللَّهُ مِنْ أَحَدٍ تَبِعَةً. وَكَذَا قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَالْحَسَنُ، وَبَكْرُ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الْمُزَنِيُّ، وَغَيْرُهُمْ.

وَقَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ وَالسُّدِّيُّ: ﴿وَلا يَخَافُ عُقْبَاهَا﴾ أَيْ: لَمْ يَخَفِ الَّذِي عَقَرَهَا عَاقِبَةَ مَا صَنَعَ. وَالْقَوْلُ الْأَوَّلُ أَوْلَى؛ لِدَلَالَةِ السِّيَاقِ عَلَيْهِ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

آخِرُ تفسير "والشمس وضحاها".
