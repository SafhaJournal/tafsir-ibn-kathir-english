## 80:1-16 — verses 1-16 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Tafsir of Surat Abasa (He Frowned)

And it is Meccan.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

* *

More than one of the commentators mentioned that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was one day addressing one of the great men of Quraysh, hoping eagerly for his acceptance of Islam. While he was addressing him and conversing privately with him, Ibn Umm Maktum came forward, and he was among those who had accepted Islam early on, and he began to ask the Messenger of Allah ﷺ about something and press him persistently. The Prophet ﷺ wished that he would hold off at that moment so that he could be free to address that man, out of eagerness and desire for his guidance. So he frowned in the face of Ibn Umm Maktum and turned away from him, and attended to the other man. So Allah, Mighty and Majestic, revealed: {He frowned and turned away, because the blind man came to him. And what would make you know, perhaps he would purify himself}, that is, purity and cleanliness would come about for him in himself. {Or be reminded, so that the reminder would benefit him}, that is, admonition and restraint from forbidden things would come about for him. {But as for him who deems himself self-sufficient, to him you attend}, that is, as for the wealthy man, you expose yourself to him, hoping that he might be guided. {And no blame is upon you if he does not purify himself}, that is, you are not held accountable for it if purity does not come about for him. {But as for him who comes to you striving, while he fears}, that is, he seeks you out and aims for you so that he may be guided by what you say to him, {yet from him you are diverted}, that is, you occupy yourself with something else. From this Allah, Mighty and Majestic, commanded His Messenger ﷺ not to single out anyone with the warning, but rather to be equal in it between the noble and the lowly, the poor and the rich, the masters and the slaves, the men and the women, the young and the old. Then it is Allah who guides whom He wills to a straight path, and to Him belongs the far-reaching wisdom and the decisive proof.

Al-Hafiz Abu Ya'la said in his Musnad: [chain: Muhammad, that is Ibn Mahdi, from Abd al-Razzaq, from Ma'mar, from Qatadah] from Anas, concerning His saying {He frowned and turned away}: Ibn Umm Maktum came to the Prophet ﷺ while he was speaking with Ubayy ibn Khalaf, so he turned away from him. Then Allah revealed {He frowned and turned away, because the blind man came to him}. After that the Prophet ﷺ used to honor him.

Qatadah said: Anas ibn Malik informed me, saying: I saw him on the day of al-Qadisiyyah wearing a coat of mail and carrying a black banner, meaning Ibn Umm Maktum.

Abu Ya'la and Ibn Jarir said: [chain: Sa'id ibn Yahya al-Umawi, from his father, from Hisham ibn Urwah, from what he had read back to him] from Urwah, from Aishah, who said: {He frowned and turned away} was revealed concerning Ibn Umm Maktum, the blind man. He came to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and began saying, "Guide me." She said: And with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ were some of the great men of the polytheists. She said: So the Prophet ﷺ began turning away from him and attending to the other man, saying, "Do you see any harm in what I am saying?" And he would say, "No." So it was concerning this that {He frowned and turned away} was revealed.

Al-Tirmidhi related this hadith from Sa'id ibn Yahya al-Umawi, with his chain of transmission, similarly, then said: Some of them related it from Hisham ibn Urwah, from his father, who said: {He frowned and turned away} was revealed concerning Ibn Umm Maktum, without mentioning in it "from Aishah."

I say: it is likewise in al-Muwatta'.

Then Ibn Jarir and Ibn Abi Hatim also related, through the route of al-Awfi, from Ibn Abbas, concerning His saying {He frowned and turned away, because the blind man came to him}, that he said: While the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was conversing privately with Utbah ibn Rabi'ah, Abu Jahl ibn Hisham, and al-Abbas ibn Abd al-Muttalib, and he used to expose himself to them a great deal and was eager for them to believe, a blind man came toward him, called Abdullah ibn Umm Maktum, walking while he was conversing with them. Abdullah began asking the Prophet ﷺ to recite a verse of the Qur'an to him, and said, "O Messenger of Allah, teach me from what Allah has taught you." So the Messenger of Allah ﷺ turned away from him, and frowned in his face, and turned away and disliked his speech, and attended to the others. When the Messenger of Allah ﷺ finished his private conversation and set out to return to his family, Allah withheld part of his sight, then he nodded his head, then Allah revealed: {He frowned and turned away, because the blind man came to him. And what would make you know, perhaps he would purify himself, or be reminded, so that the reminder would benefit him}. When what was revealed concerning him was revealed, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ honored him and spoke with him, and the Prophet ﷺ would say to him, "What do you need? Do you want anything?" And when he left his presence he would say, "Do you have a need for anything?" That was because of what Allah, Exalted is He, had revealed: {But as for him who deems himself self-sufficient, to him you attend, and no blame is upon you if he does not purify himself}.

There is gharabah (strangeness) and nakarah (an objectionable quality) in it, and its chain of transmission has been criticized.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain: Ahmad ibn Mansur al-Ramadi, from Abdullah ibn Salih, from al-Layth, from Yunus, from Ibn Shihab] who said: Salim ibn Abdullah said, from Abdullah ibn Umar: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say, "Bilal calls the adhan while it is still night, so eat and drink until you hear the adhan of Ibn Umm Maktum." And he is the blind man concerning whom Allah revealed {He frowned and turned away, because the blind man came to him}, and he used to call the adhan along with Bilal. Salim said: He was a man impaired in sight, and he would not call the adhan until the people said to him, when they saw the break of dawn, "Call the adhan."

And likewise Urwah ibn al-Zubayr, Mujahid, Abu Malik, Qatadah, al-Dahhak, Ibn Zayd, and more than one of the earlier and later scholars mentioned that it was revealed concerning Ibn Umm Maktum. The well known view is that his name was Abdullah, though it is said: Amr. And Allah knows best.

* *

His saying {No indeed, it is a reminder}, that is, this surah, or the enjoining of equality among people in conveying knowledge, whether they be noble or lowly among them.

Qatadah and al-Suddi said: {No indeed, it is a reminder} means the Qur'an, {so whoever wills, remembers it}, that is, whoever wills remembers Allah in all his affairs. And it is possible that the pronoun refers back to the revelation, on account of the speech indicating it.

* *

His saying {in honored pages, exalted, purified}, that is, this surah, or the admonition, and the two are inseparable; rather, the entire Qur'an is {in honored pages}, that is, magnified and revered, {exalted}, that is, of elevated rank, {purified}, that is, from defilement and from addition and deficiency.

* *

His saying {by the hands of safarah (envoys, scribes)}: Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, al-Dahhak, and Ibn Zayd said: they are the angels. Wahb ibn Munabbih said: they are the companions of Muhammad ﷺ. Qatadah said: they are the reciters (qurra'). Ibn Jurayj said, from Ibn Abbas: al-safarah in the Nabataean language means the reciters.

Ibn Jarir said: The correct view is that the safarah are the angels, and safarah means those who mediate between Allah and His creation. From this it is said: the safir (envoy) is the one who strives among people toward reconciliation and good, as the poet said:

I do not abandon mediation among my people,
and I do not go about with deceit, if I go about.

Al-Bukhari said: Safarah means the angels. Safarat means she brought about reconciliation between them. And the angels, when they descend with Allah's revelation and convey it, were made like the envoy who brings about reconciliation among a people.

* *

His saying {kiram bararah (noble and dutifully righteous)}, that is, their nature is noble, good, honorable, and their character and their deeds are dutiful, pure, complete. From this it is fitting that the bearer of the Qur'an be, in his deeds and his words, upon rectitude and right guidance.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain: Isma'il, from Hisham, from Qatadah, from Zurarah ibn Awfa, from Sa'd ibn Hisham] from Aishah, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "The one who recites the Qur'an and is skilled in it is with the safarah, the kiram, the bararah, and the one who recites it while it is difficult for him has two rewards." It was related by al-jama'ah (the compilers of the major hadith collections) from the route of Qatadah, with it.

### Source (Arabic) — review against this

تَفْسِيرُ سُورَةِ عَبَسَ

وَهِيَ مَكِّيَّةٌ.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

* *

ذَكَرَ غيرُ وَاحِدٍ مِنَ الْمُفَسِّرِينَ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ كَانَ يَوْمًا يخاطبُ بَعْضَ عُظَمَاءِ قُرَيْشٍ، وَقَدْ طَمع فِي إِسْلَامِهِ، فَبَيْنَمَا هُوَ يُخَاطِبُهُ وَيُنَاجِيهُ إِذْ أَقْبَلَ ابنُ أُمِّ مَكْتُومٍ-وَكَانَ مِمَّنْ أَسْلَمَ قَدِيمًا-فَجَعَلَ يَسْأَلُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ عَنْ شَيْءٍ وَيُلِحُّ عَلَيْهِ، وودَّ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ أَنْ لَوْ كَفَّ سَاعَتَهُ تِلْكَ لِيَتَمَكَّنَ مِنْ مُخَاطَبَةِ ذَلِكَ الرَّجُلِ؛ طَمَعًا وَرَغْبَةً فِي هِدَايَتِهِ. وعَبَس فِي وَجْهِ ابْنِ أُمِّ مَكْتُومٍ وَأَعْرَضَ عَنْهُ، وَأَقْبَلَ عَلَى الْآخَرِ، فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ: (عَبَسَ وَتَوَلَّى * أَنْ جَاءَهُ الأعْمَى * وَمَا يُدْرِيكَ لَعَلَّهُ يَزَّكَّى) ؟ أَيْ: يَحْصُلُ لَهُ زَكَاةٌ وَطَهَارَةٌ فِي نَفْسِهِ. (أَوْ يَذَّكَّرُ فَتَنْفَعَهُ الذِّكْرَى) أَيْ: يَحْصُلُ لَهُ اتِّعَاظٌ وَانْزِجَارٌ عَنِ الْمَحَارِمِ، (أَمَّا مَنِ اسْتَغْنَى * فَأَنْتَ لَهُ تَصَدَّى) أَيْ: أَمَّا الْغَنِيُّ فَأَنْتَ تَتَعَرَّضُ لَهُ لَعَلَّهُ يَهْتَدِي، (وَمَا عَلَيْكَ أَلا يَزَّكَّى) ؟ أَيْ: مَا أَنْتَ بِمُطَالَبٍ بِهِ إِذَا لَمْ يَحْصُلْ لَهُ زَكَاةٌ. (وَأَمَّا مَنْ جَاءَكَ يَسْعَى * وَهُوَ يَخْشَى) أَيْ: يَقْصِدُكَ وَيَؤُمُّكَ لِيَهْتَدِيَ بِمَا تَقُولُ لَهُ، (فَأَنْتَ عَنْهُ تَلَهَّى) أَيْ: تَتَشَاغَلُ. وَمِنْ هَاهُنَا أَمَرَ اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ رَسُولَهُ ﷺ أَلَّا يَخُصَّ بِالْإِنْذَارِ أَحَدًا، بَلْ يُسَاوِي فِيهِ بَيْنَ الشَّرِيفِ وَالضَّعِيفِ، وَالْفَقِيرِ وَالْغَنِيِّ، وَالسَّادَةِ وَالْعَبِيدِ، وَالرِّجَالِ وَالنِّسَاءِ، وَالصِّغَارِ وَالْكِبَارِ. ثُمَّ اللَّهُ يَهْدِي مَنْ يَشَاءُ إِلَى صِرَاطٍ مُسْتَقِيمٍ، وَلَهُ الْحِكْمَةُ الْبَالِغَةُ وَالْحُجَّةُ الدَّامِغَةُ.

قَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو يَعْلَى فِي مُسْنَدِهِ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ-هُوَ ابْنُ مَهْدِيٍّ-حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، أَخْبَرَنَا مَعْمَر، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ [عَنْ أَنَسٍ] فِي قَوْلِهِ: (عَبَسَ وَتَوَلَّى) جَاءَ ابْنُ أُمِّ مَكْتُومٍ إِلَى النَّبِيِّ ﷺ وَهُوَ يُكَلِّمُ أُبَيَّ بْنَ خَلَفٍ، فَأَعْرَضَ عَنْهُ، فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ: (عَبَسَ وَتَوَلَّى * أَنْ جَاءَهُ الأعْمَى) فَكَانَ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ بَعْدَ ذَلِكَ يُكْرِمُهُ.

قَالَ قَتَادَةُ: وَأَخْبَرَنِي أَنَسُ بْنُ مَالِكٍ قَالَ: رَأَيْتُهُ يَوْمَ الْقَادِسِيَّةِ وَعَلَيْهِ دِرْعٌ ومعه راية سوداء- يَعْنِي ابْنَ أُمِّ مَكْتُومٍ .

وَقَالَ أَبُو يَعْلَى وَابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا سَعِيدُ بْنُ يَحْيَى الْأُمَوِيُّ، حَدَّثَنِي أَبِي، عَنْ هِشَامِ بْنِ عُرْوَةَ مِمَّا عَرَضَهُ عَلَيْهِ عَنْ عُرْوَة، عَنْ عَائِشَةَ قَالَتْ: أُنْزِلَتْ: (عَبَسَ وَتَوَلَّى) فِي ابْنِ أُمِّ مَكْتُومٍ الْأَعْمَى، أَتَى إِلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَجَعَلَ يَقُولُ: أَرْشِدْنِي. قَالَتْ: وَعِنْدَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ من عُظَمَاءِ الْمُشْرِكِينَ. قَالَتْ: فَجَعَلَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يُعرض عَنْهُ وَيُقْبِلُ عَلَى الْآخَرِ، وَيَقُولُ: "أَتَرَى بِمَا أَقُولُ بَأْسًا؟ ". فَيَقُولُ: لَا. فَفِي هَذَا أُنْزِلَتْ: (عَبَسَ وَتَوَلَّى) .

وَقَدْ رَوَى التِّرْمِذِيُّ هَذَا الْحَدِيثَ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ يَحْيَى الْأُمَوِيُّ، بِإِسْنَادِهِ، مِثْلَهُ، ثُمَّ قَالَ: وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ بَعْضُهُمْ عَنْ هِشَامِ بْنِ عُرْوَةَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ قَالَ: أنزلَت (عَبَسَ وَتَوَلَّى) فِي ابْنِ أُمِّ مَكْتُومٍ، وَلَمْ يَذْكُرْ فِيهِ عَنْ عَائِشَةَ .

قُلْتُ: كَذَلِكَ هُوَ فِي الْمُوَطَّأِ .

ثُمَّ رَوَى ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ وَابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ أَيْضًا مِنْ طَرِيقِ الْعَوْفِيِّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَوْلَهُ: (عَبَسَ وَتَوَلَّى * أَنْ جَاءَهُ الأعْمَى) قَالَ: بَيْنَا رسولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ يُنَاجِي عتبةَ بْنَ رَبِيعَةَ، وَأَبَا جَهْلِ بنَ هِشَامٍ، وَالْعَبَّاسَ بْنَ عَبْدِ الْمُطَّلِبِ-وَكَانَ يَتَصَدَّى لَهُمْ كَثِيرًا، وَيَحْرِصُ عَلَيْهِمْ أَنْ يُؤْمِنُوا-فَأَقْبَلَ إِلَيْهِ رَجُلٌ أَعْمَى-يُقَالُ لَهُ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ أُمِّ مَكْتُومٍ-يَمْشِي وَهُوَ يُنَاجِيهِمْ، فَجَعَلَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ يَسْتَقْرِئُ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ آيَةً مِنَ الْقُرْآنِ، وَقَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، عَلِّمْنِي مِمَّا عَلَّمَكَ اللَّهُ. فَأَعْرَضَ عَنْهُ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ، وَعَبَسَ فِي وَجْهِهِ، وَتَوَلَّى وكَرهَ كَلامَه، وَأَقْبَلَ عَلَى الْآخَرِينَ، فَلَمَّا قَضَى رسولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ نَجْوَاهُ، وَأَخَذَ يَنْقَلِبُ إِلَى أَهْلِهِ، أَمَسَكَ اللَّهُ بَعْضَ بَصَرِهِ، ثُمَّ خَفَق بِرَأْسِهِ، ثُمَّ أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ: (عَبَسَ وَتَوَلَّى * أَنْ جَاءَهُ الأعْمَى * وَمَا يُدْرِيكَ لَعَلَّهُ يَزَّكَّى * أَوْ يَذَّكَّرُ فَتَنْفَعَهُ الذِّكْرَى) فَلَمَّا نَزَلَ فِيهِ مَا نَزَلَ، أَكْرَمَهُ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ وَكَلَّمَهُ وَقَالَ لَهُ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: "مَا حَاجَتُكَ؟ هَلْ تُرِيدُ مِنْ شَيْءٍ؟ " وَإِذَا ذَهَبَ مِنْ عِنْدِهِ قَالَ: "هَلْ لَكَ حَاجَةٌ فِي شَيْءٍ؟ ". وَذَلِكَ لَمَّا أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: (أَمَّا مَنِ اسْتَغْنَى * فَأَنْتَ لَهُ تَصَدَّى * وَمَا عَلَيْكَ أَلا يَزَّكَّى) .

فِيهِ غَرَابَةٌ وَنَكَارَةٌ، وَقَدْ تُكُلّم فِي إِسْنَادِهِ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ مَنْصُورٍ الرَّمَادِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ صَالِحٍ، حَدَّثَنَا اللَّيْثُ، حَدَّثَنَا يُونُسُ، عَنِ ابْنِ شِهَابٍ قَالَ: قَالَ سَالِمِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عمر: سمعت رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم يَقُولُ: "إِنَّ بِلَالًا يُؤَذِّنُ بِلَيْلٍ، فَكُلُوا وَاشْرَبُوا حَتَّى تَسْمَعُوا أَذَانَ ابْنِ أُمِّ مَكْتُومٍ". وَهُوَ الْأَعْمَى الَّذِي أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ فِيهِ: (عَبَسَ وَتَوَلَّى * أَنْ جَاءَهُ الأعْمَى) وَكَانَ يُؤَذِّنُ مَعَ بِلَالٍ. قَالَ سَالِمٌ: وَكَانَ رَجُلا ضريرَ الْبَصَرِ، فَلَمْ يَكْ يُؤَذِّنُ حَتَّى يَقُولَ لَهُ النَّاسُ-حِينَ يَنْظُرُونَ إِلَى بُزُوغِ الْفَجْرِ-: أذَّن .

وَهَكَذَا ذَكَرَ عُرْوَةُ بْنُ الزُّبَيْرِ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَأَبُو مَالِكٍ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَابْنُ زَيْدٍ، وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ مِنَ السَّلَفِ وَالْخَلَفِ: أَنَّهَا نَزَلَتْ فِي ابْنِ أُمِّ مَكْتُومٍ. وَالْمَشْهُورُ أَنَّ اسْمَهُ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ، وَيُقَالُ: عَمْرٌو. وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ وَالسُّدِّيُّ: (كَلا إِنَّهَا تَذْكِرَةٌ) يَعْنِي: الْقُرْآنَ، (فَمَنْ شَاءَ ذَكَرَهُ) أَيْ: فَمَنْ شَاءَ ذَكَرَ اللَّهَ فِي جَمِيعِ أُمُورِهِ. وَيُحْتَمَلُ عَوْدُ الضَّمِيرِ عَلَى الْوَحْيِ؛ لِدَلَالَةِ الْكَلَامِ عَلَيْهِ.

## 80:17-32 — verses 17-32 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Most High says, censuring whoever among the children of Adam denies the resurrection and the rising: {May man be killed, how disbelieving he is!} Al-Dahhak said, from Ibn Abbas: "{May man be killed}" means man is cursed. Abu Malik said the same. This applies to the type of man who disbelieves, because of the frequency of his denial without any basis, but merely out of deeming it far-fetched and out of ignorance.

Ibn Jarir said: "{how disbelieving he is}" means how intense is his disbelief! Ibn Jarir also said: it is possible that what is meant is: what thing made him a disbeliever? That is: what carried him to denying al-ma'ad (the return, i.e. the resurrection).

Qatadah said, and al-Baghawi related this from Muqatil and al-Kalbi: "{how disbelieving he is}" means how cursed he is.

Then the Most High explained to him how He created him from a lowly thing, and that He is able to bring him back as He originated him, so He said: {From what thing did He create him? From a drop of fluid He created him, then determined him.} That is: He determined his lifespan, his provision, his deeds, and whether he is wretched or fortunate. {Then He made the way easy for him.} Al-Awfi said, from Ibn Abbas: then He made his coming out from his mother's womb easy for him. Ikrimah, al-Dahhak, Abu Salih, Qatadah, and al-Suddi said likewise, and Ibn Jarir preferred this.

Mujahid said: this is like His saying: {Indeed, We guided him to the way, whether he be grateful or ungrateful} [al-Insan: 3], meaning: We made it clear to him, We explained it to him, and We made his deed easy for him. Al-Hasan and Ibn Zayd said likewise. This is the more correct view, and Allah knows best.

* *

His saying: {Then He caused him to die and made him to be buried} means: after creating him, He caused him to die and made him to be buried, that is, He made him one who has a grave. The Arabs say: "qabartu al-rajula" (I buried the man) when he himself undertook the burial of him, and "aqbarahu Allah" (Allah made him have a grave). And: "'adabtu qarn al-thawr" (I cut off the horn of the bull) and "a'dabahu Allah," and "batartu dhanab al-ba'ir" (I cut off the tail of the camel) and "abtarahu Allah," and "taradtu 'anni fulanan" (I drove so-and-so away from me) and "atradahu Allah," meaning He made him one who is driven away. Al-A'sha said:

"Had she rested a dead man against her breast, he would have lived, and he would not have been carried off to a qabir (one who buries)."

And His saying: {Then when He wills, He will raise him} means: He will resurrect him after his death, and from this the terms al-ba'th and al-nushur (the resurrection and the rising) are derived: {And among His signs is that He created you from dust, then, behold, you are human beings dispersing} [al-Rum: 20], {And look at the bones, how We raise them up, then We clothe them with flesh} [al-Baqarah: 259].

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] from Abu Sa'id, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "The earth eats up everything of man except the 'ajb of his tail (the coccyx)." It was said: "And what is that, O Messenger of Allah?" He said: "Like a mustard seed, from it they will be recreated."

This hadith is established in the Sahih through the narration of al-A'mash, from Abu Salih, from Abu Hurayrah, without this addition, and its wording is: "Every son of Adam decays except the 'ajb of the tail; from it he was created and upon it he will be reassembled."

* *

His saying: {No indeed, he has not yet fulfilled what He commanded him} Ibn Jarir said: this means: no, the matter is not as this disbelieving man says, that he has discharged Allah's due upon him in himself and his wealth. "{He has not yet fulfilled what He commanded him}" means: he has not performed what was made obligatory upon him of the duties owed to his Lord, Mighty and Majestic.

Then he, and Ibn Abi Hatim, narrated through the route of Ibn Abi Najih, from Mujahid, his saying: "{No indeed, he has not yet fulfilled what He commanded him}" he said: no one will ever fulfill all of what was made obligatory upon him. Al-Baghawi related something similar to this from al-Hasan al-Basri. I have not found among the earlier scholars any statement on it other than this. What occurs to me as its meaning, and Allah knows best, is that the meaning is: "{Then when He wills, He will raise him}" meaning He will resurrect him, "{No indeed, he has not yet fulfilled what He commanded him}," that is, He will not do it now until the term is completed and the decree is finished with respect to the children of Adam, those among them whom the Most High has decreed will come into existence and be brought out into this world. He has commanded this, in existence and in decree. So when that reaches its end with Allah, Allah will raise up all creatures and bring them back as He originated them.

Ibn Abi Hatim narrated from Wahb ibn Munabbih, who said: Uzayr (peace be upon him) said: the angel who came to me said: the graves are the belly of the earth, and the earth is the mother of creation. So when Allah has created what He wills to create, and these graves that Allah has extended for them are complete, this world will come to an end, and whoever is upon it will die, and the earth will cast out what is in its interior, and the graves will bring forth what is in them. This resembles what we have said as to the meaning of the verse, and Allah, glorified and exalted, knows best what is correct.

He said: {So let man look at his food.} In this is a reminder of a favor, and in it is an argument from the reviving of plants from the dead earth for the reviving of bodies after they had been decayed bones and scattered dust. {That We poured down water in abundance,} meaning: We sent it down from the sky upon the earth. {Then We split the earth open, splitting it,} meaning: We settled it (the water) within it, so it entered into its depths and permeated through the parts of the seed deposited within it, and so it sprouted, rose up, and appeared on the surface of the earth. {And caused to grow therein grain, and grapes and qadb.} As for al-habb (grain), it is everything that is spoken of among the grains, and al-'inab (grapes) is well known, and al-qadb is al-fasfasah (alfalfa), which the animals eat while it is moist. It is also called al-qatt. Ibn Abbas, Qatadah, al-Dahhak, and al-Suddi said this.

Al-Hasan al-Basri said: al-qadb is fodder.

{And olives.} It is well known. It is an udm (condiment), and its pressed juice is also an udm. Lamps are lit with it, and the body is anointed with it. {And date palms.} Eaten as balah (unripe green dates), busr (ripening dates), rutab (fresh ripe dates), and tamr (dried dates), raw and cooked, and from it rubb (date syrup) and vinegar are pressed. {And thick gardens.} That is: orchards. Al-Hasan and Qatadah said: "{ghulb}" means thick, fine date palms. Ibn Abbas and Mujahid said: "al-hada'iq" (gardens) means everything that is intertwined and clustered together. Ibn Abbas also said: "{ghulb}" means the trees whose shade one takes shelter under. Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn Abbas: "{And thick gardens}" means tall. Ikrimah said: "{ghulb}" means thick in the middle. And in another report: thick in the neck. Have you not seen that when a man is thick in the neck, it is said: "By Allah, he is indeed aghlab"? Ibn Abi Hatim narrated this, and Ibn Jarir cited for al-Farazdaq:

"He howled, and so stirred up a thick-necked lion; so woe to Ibn al-Maraghah for what he has stirred up!"

* *

His saying: {And fruit and abb.} As for fruit, it is what is enjoyed for pleasure among produce. Ibn Abbas said: fruit is everything eaten fresh. And al-abb is what the earth grows, of what the animals eat and people do not eat. And in a report from him: it is grass fodder for the livestock. Mujahid, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, and Abu Malik said: al-abb is pasturage. And from Mujahid, al-Hasan, Qatadah, and Ibn Zayd: al-abb is for the livestock as fruit is for the children of Adam. From 'Ata': everything that grows on the surface of the earth is abb. Al-Dahhak said: everything the earth grows besides fruit is abb.

Ibn Idris said, from 'Asim ibn Kulayb, from his father, from Ibn Abbas: al-abb is the plant growth of the earth that the animals eat and people do not eat. Ibn Jarir narrated it through three routes from Ibn Idris, then said: Abu Kurayb and Abu al-Sa'ib narrated to us, they said: Ibn Idris narrated to us, 'Abd al-Malik narrated to us, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, who said: Ibn Abbas counted and said: al-abb is what the earth grows for the an'am (livestock). This is the wording of Abu Kurayb, and Abu al-Sa'ib said: what the earth grows of what people eat and the livestock eat.

Al-Awfi said, from Ibn Abbas: al-abb is pasturage and grazing land. Mujahid, al-Hasan, Qatadah, Ibn Zayd, and more than one other said likewise.

Abu 'Ubayd al-Qasim ibn Sallam said: Muhammad ibn Yazid narrated to us, al-'Awwam ibn Hawshab narrated to us, from Ibrahim al-Taymi, who said: Abu Bakr al-Siddiq, may Allah be pleased with him, was asked about the saying of the Most High: {And fruit and abb}, and he said: "What sky would shade me, and what earth would carry me, if I said concerning the Book of Allah what I do not know?"

This is munqati' (a report with a broken chain) between Ibrahim al-Taymi and al-Siddiq. As for what Ibn Jarir narrated, where he said: Ibn Bashshar narrated to us, Ibn Abi 'Adi narrated to us, Humayd narrated to us, from Anas, who said: 'Umar ibn al-Khattab recited {He frowned and turned away}, and when he came to this verse: {And fruit and abb}, he said: "We know what fruit is, but what is al-abb?" Then he said: "By your life, O son of al-Khattab, this indeed is takalluf (undue exertion, going beyond what is required)!"

This is a sound (sahih) chain of transmission, and more than one narrator related it from Anas with the same content. This is to be understood as meaning that he wanted to know its shape, its kind, and its exact identity, otherwise he, and everyone who reads this verse, knows that it is among the plant growth of the earth, from His saying: {And We caused to grow therein grain, and grapes and qadb, and olives and date palms, and thick gardens, and fruit and abb.}

* *

His saying: {As provision for you and for your livestock.} That is: sustenance for you and for your livestock in this abode until the Day of Resurrection.

وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: هَذِهِ كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿إِنَّا هَدَيْنَاهُ السَّبِيلَ إِمَّا شَاكِرًا وَإِمَّا كَفُورًا﴾ [الْإِنْسَانِ: ٣] أَيْ: بَيَّنَّا لَهُ وَوَضَّحْنَاهُ وَسَهَّلْنَا عَلَيْهِ عَمَلَهُ وَهَكَذَا قَالَ الْحَسَنُ، وَابْنُ زَيْدٍ. وَهَذَا هُوَ الْأَرْجَحُ وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: (ثُمَّ أَمَاتَهُ فَأَقْبَرَهُ) أَيْ: إِنَّهُ بَعْدَ خَلْقِهِ لَهُ (أَمَاتَهُ فَأَقْبَرَهُ) أَيْ: جَعَلَهُ ذَا قَبْرٍ. وَالْعَرَبُ تَقُولُ: "قَبَرْتُ الرَّجُلَ": إِذَا وَلِيَ ذَلِكَ مِنْهُ، وَأَقْبَرَهُ اللَّهُ. وَعَضَبْتُ قَرْنَ الثَّوْرِ،

وَأَعْضَبَهُ اللَّهُ، وَبَتَرْتُ ذَنَبَ الْبَعِيرِ وَأَبْتَرَهُ اللَّهُ. وَطَرَدْتُ عَنِّي فُلَانًا، وَأَطْرَدَهُ اللَّهُ، أَيْ: جَعَلَهُ طَرِيدًا قَالَ الْأَعْشَى:

لَو أسْنَدَتْ مَيتًا إِلَى نَحْرها عَاش، وَلم يُنقَل إلى قَابِر وَقَوْلُهُ: (ثُمَّ إِذَا شَاءَ أَنْشَرَهُ) أَيْ: بَعَثَهُ بَعْدَ مَوْتِهِ، وَمِنْهُ يُقَالُ: الْبَعْثُ وَالنُّشُورُ، ﴿وَمِنْ آيَاتِهِ أَنْ خَلَقَكُمْ مِنْ تُرَابٍ ثُمَّ إِذَا أَنْتُمْ بَشَرٌ تَنْتَشِرُونَ﴾ [الرُّومِ: ٢٠] ، ﴿وَانْظُرْ إِلَى الْعِظَامِ كَيْفَ نُنْشِزُهَا ثُمَّ نَكْسُوهَا لَحْمًا﴾ [الْبَقَرَةِ: ٢٥٩] .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا أصبغُ بنُ الفَرج، أَخْبَرَنَا ابْنُ وَهْبٍ، أَخْبَرَنِي عَمْرُو بْنُ الْحَارِثِ: أَنْ دَرَّاجًا أَبَا السَّمْحِ أَخْبَرَهُ، عَنْ أَبِي الْهَيْثَمِ، عَنْ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ، عَنْ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ قَالَ: "يَأْكُلُ الترابُ كلَّ شَيْءٍ مِنَ الْإِنْسَانِ إِلَّا عَجْبُ ذَنَبه قِيلَ: وَمَا هُوَ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ؟ قَالَ: "مِثْلُ حَبَّةِ خَرْدَلٍ مِنْهُ يُنْشَئُونَ" .

وَهَذَا الْحَدِيثُ ثَابِتٌ فِي الصَّحِيحِ مِنْ رِوَايَةِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ أَبِي صَالِحٍ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، بِدُونِ هَذِهِ الزِّيَادَةِ، وَلَفْظُهُ: "كُلُّ ابْنِ آدَمَ يَبْلى إِلَّا عَجْبُ الذَّنَب، مِنْهُ خُلِقَ وَفِيهِ يُركَّب" .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: (كَلا لَمَّا يَقْضِ مَا أَمَرَهُ) قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: يَقُولُ: كَلَّا لَيْسَ الْأَمْرُ كَمَا يَقُولُ هَذَا الْإِنْسَانُ الْكَافِرُ؛ مِنْ أَنَّهُ قَدْ أَدَّى حَقَّ اللَّهِ عَلَيْهِ فِي نَفْسِهِ وَمَالِهِ، (لَمَّا يَقْضِ مَا أَمَرَهُ) يَقُولُ: لَمْ يُؤد مَا فُرض عَلَيْهِ مِنَ الْفَرَائِضِ لِرَبِّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ.

ثُمَّ رَوَى-هُوَ وَابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ-مِنْ طَرِيقِ ابْنِ أَبِي نَجِيح، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ قَوْلَهُ: (كَلا لَمَّا يَقْضِ مَا أَمَرَهُ) قَالَ: لَا يَقْضِي أَحَدٌ أَبَدًا كُلَّ مَا افتُرض عَلَيْهِ. وَحَكَاهُ الْبَغَوِيُّ، عَنِ الْحَسَنِ الْبَصْرِيِّ، بِنَحْوٍ مِنْ هَذَا. وَلَمْ أَجِدْ لِلْمُتَقَدِّمِينَ فِيهِ كَلامًا سِوَى هَذَا. وَالَّذِي يَقَعُ لِي فِي مَعْنَى ذَلِكَ-وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ-أَنَّ الْمَعْنَى: (ثُمَّ إِذَا شَاءَ أَنْشَرَهُ) أَيْ: بَعَثَهُ، (كَلا لَمَّا يَقْضِ مَا أَمَرَهُ) [أَيْ] لَا يَفْعَلُهُ الْآنَ حَتَّى تَنْقَضِيَ الْمُدَّةُ، وَيَفْرَغَ الْقَدَرُ مِنْ بَنِي آدَمَ مِمَّنْ كَتَبَ تَعَالَى لَهُ أَنْ سيُوجَدُ مِنْهُمْ، وَيُخْرَجُ إِلَى الدُّنْيَا، وَقَدْ أَمَرَ بِهِ تَعَالَى كَوْنًا وَقَدَرًا، فَإِذَا تَنَاهَى ذَلِكَ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ أَنْشَرَ اللَّهُ الْخَلَائِقَ وَأَعَادَهُمْ كَمَا بَدَأَهُمْ.

وَقَدْ رَوَى ابنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، عَنْ وَهْبِ بْنِ مُنَبّه قَالَ: قَالَ عُزَير، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ: قَالَ الْمَلَكُ الَّذِي جَاءَنِي: فَإِنَّ الْقُبُورَ هِيَ بطنُ الْأَرْضِ، وَإِنَّ الْأَرْضَ هِيَ أُمُّ الْخَلْقِ، فَإِذَا خَلَقَ اللَّهُ مَا أَرَادَ أَنْ يَخْلُقَ وَتَمَّتْ هَذِهِ القبورُ الَّتِي مَدّ اللَّهُ لَهَا، انْقَطَعَتِ الدُّنْيَا وَمَاتَ مَنْ عَلَيْهَا، وَلَفَظَتِ الْأَرْضُ مَا فِي جَوْفِهَا، وَأَخْرَجَتِ القبورُ مَا فِيهَا، وَهَذَا شَبِيهٌ بِمَا قُلْنَا مِنْ مَعْنَى الْآيَةِ، وَاللَّهُ -سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَى أَعْلَمُ بِالصَّوَابِ.

وَقَالَ: (فَلْيَنْظُرِ الإنْسَانُ إِلَى طَعَامِهِ) فِيهِ امْتِنَانٌ، وَفِيهِ اسْتِدْلَالٌ بِإِحْيَاءِ النَّبَاتِ مِنَ الْأَرْضِ الْهَامِدَةِ عَلَى إِحْيَاءِ الْأَجْسَامِ بَعْدَمَا كَانَتْ عَظَاما بَالِيَةً وَتُرَابًا مُتَمَزِّقًا، (أَنَّا صَبَبْنَا الْمَاءَ صَبًّا) أَيْ: أَنْزَلْنَاهُ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ عَلَى الْأَرْضِ، (ثُمَّ شَقَقْنَا الأرْضَ شَقًّا) أَيْ: أَسْكَنَاهُ فِيهَا فَدَخَلَ فِي تُخُومها وتَخَلَّل فِي أَجْزَاءِ الْحَبِّ المودعَ فِيهَا فَنَبَتَ وَارْتَفَعَ وَظَهَرَ عَلَى وَجْهِ الْأَرْضِ، (فَأَنْبَتْنَا فِيهَا حَبًّا * وَعِنَبًا وَقَضْبًا) فَالْحَبُّ: كُلُّ مَا يُذْكَرُ مِنَ الْحُبُوبِ، وَالْعِنَبُ مَعْرُوفٌ وَالْقَضْبُ هُوَ: الْفَصْفَصَةُ الَّتِي تَأْكُلُهَا الدَّوَابُّ رَطْبَةٌ. وَيُقَالُ لَهَا: القَتّ أَيْضًا قَالَ ذَلِكَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ. وَالسُّدِّيُّ.

وَقَالَ الْحَسَنُ الْبَصْرِيُّ: الْقَضْبُ الْعَلَفُ.

(وَزَيْتُونًا) وَهُوَ مَعْرُوفٌ، وَهُوَ أدْمٌ وَعَصِيرُهُ أُدْمٌ، وَيُسْتَصْبَحُ بِهِ، وَيُدَّهَنُ بِهِ. (وَنَخْلا) يُؤْكَلُ بَلَحًا بُسْرًا، وَرُطَبًا، وَتَمْرًا، وَنِيئًا، وَمَطْبُوخًا، وَيُعْتَصَرُ مِنْهُ رُبٌّ وَخَلٌّ. (وَحَدَائِقَ غُلْبًا) أي: بساتين. قال الحسن، وقتادة: (َ غُلْبًا) نَخْلٌ غِلَاظٌ كِرَامٌ. وَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ: "الْحَدَائِقُ": كُلُّ مَا الْتَفَّ وَاجْتَمَعَ. وَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ أَيْضًا: (غُلْبًا) الشَّجَرُ الَّذِي يُسْتَظَلُّ بِهِ. وَقَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: (وَحَدَائِقَ غُلْبًا) أَيْ: طِوَالٌ. وَقَالَ عِكْرِمَةُ: (غُلْبًا) أَيْ: غِلَاظُ الْأَوْسَاطِ. وَفِي رِوَايَةٍ: غِلَاظُ الرِّقَابِ ، أَلَمْ تَرَ إِلَى الرَّجُلِ إِذَا كَانَ غَلِيظَ الرَّقَبَةِ قِيلَ: وَاللَّهِ إِنَّهُ لَأَغْلَبُ. رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، وَأَنْشَدَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ لِلْفَرَزْدَقِ:

عَوَى فَأثارَ أغلبَ ضَيْغَمِيًا ... فَويلَ ابْنَ المَراغَة مَا استَثَارا 

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: (وَفَاكِهَةً وَأَبًّا) أَمَّا الْفَاكِهَةُ فَهُوَ مَا يَتَفَكَّهُ بِهِ مِنَ الثِّمَارِ. قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: الْفَاكِهَةُ: كُلُّ مَا أُكِلَ رَطْبًا. وَالْأَبُّ مَا أَنْبَتَتِ الْأَرْضُ، مِمَّا تَأْكُلُهُ الدَّوَابُّ وَلَا يَأْكُلُهُ النَّاسُ-وَفِي رِوَايَةِ عَنْهُ: هُوَ الْحَشِيشُ لِلْبَهَائِمِ. وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَسَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ، وَأَبُو مَالِكٍ: الْأَبُّ: الْكَلَأُ. وَعَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ، وَالْحَسَنِ، وَقَتَادَةَ، وَابْنِ زَيْدٍ: الْأَبُّ لِلْبَهَائِمِ كَالْفَاكِهَةِ لِبَنِي آدَمَ. وَعَنْ عَطَاءٍ: كُلُّ شَيْءٍ نَبَتَ عَلَى وَجْهِ الْأَرْضِ فَهُوَ أبٌّ. وَقَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ: كُلُّ شَيْءٍ أَنْبَتَتْهُ الْأَرْضُ سِوَى الْفَاكِهَةِ فَهُوَ أبٌّ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ إِدْرِيسَ، عَنْ عَاصِمِ بْنِ كُلَيْبٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: الْأَبُّ: نَبْتُ الْأَرْضِ مِمَّا تَأْكُلُهُ الدَّوَابُّ وَلَا يَأْكُلُهُ النَّاسُ. وَرَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ مِنْ ثَلَاثِ طُرُقٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ إِدْرِيسَ، ثُمَّ قَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو كُرَيْب وَأَبُو السَّائِبِ قَالَا حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ إِدْرِيسَ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الْمَلِكِ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ قَالَ: عَدَّ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ وَقَالَ: الْأَبُّ: مَا أَنْبَتَتِ الْأَرْضُ لِلْأَنْعَامِ. هَذَا لَفْظُ أَبِي كُرَيْبٍ، وَقَالَ أَبُو السَّائِبِ: مَا أَنْبَتَتِ الْأَرْضُ مِمَّا يَأْكُلُ النَّاسُ وَتَأْكُلُ الْأَنْعَامُ.

وَقَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: الْأَبُّ: الْكَلَأُ وَالْمَرْعَى. وَكَذَا قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَالْحَسَنُ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَابْنُ زَيْدٍ، وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ.

وَقَالَ أَبُو عُبَيْدٍ الْقَاسِمُ بْنُ سَلَّامٍ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ يَزِيدَ، حَدَّثَنَا الْعَوَّامُ بْنُ حَوشَب، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ التَّيمي قَالَ: سُئِلَ أَبُو بَكْرٍ الصِّدِّيقُ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، عَنْ قَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: (وَفَاكِهَةً وَأَبًّا) فَقَالَ: أَيُّ سَمَاءٍ تُظِلُّنِي، وَأَيُّ أَرْضٍ تُقِلُّنِي إِنْ قلتُ فِي كِتَابِ اللَّهِ مَا لَا أَعْلَمُ .

وَهَذَا مُنْقَطِعٌ بَيْنَ إِبْرَاهِيمَ التَّيْمِيِّ وَالصِّدِّيقِ. فَأَمَّا مَا رَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ حَيْثُ قَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ بَشَّارٍ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ أَبِي عَدِيٍّ، حَدَّثَنَا حُمَيد، عَنْ أَنَسٍ قَالَ: قَرَأَ عُمَرُ بْنُ الْخَطَّابِ (عَبَسَ وَتَوَلَّى) فَلَمَّا أَتَيَ عَلَى هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ: (وَفَاكِهَةً وَأَبًّا) قَالَ: عَرَفْنَا مَا الْفَاكِهَةُ، فَمَا الْأَبُّ؟ فَقَالَ: لَعَمْرُكَ يَا ابْنَ الْخَطَّابِ إِنَّ هَذَا لَهُوَ التَّكَلُّفُ .

فَهُوَ إِسْنَادٌ صَحِيحٌ، وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ غَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ عَنِ أَنَسٍ، بِهِ. وَهَذَا مَحْمُولٌ عَلَى أَنَّهُ أَرَادَ أَنْ يَعْرِفَ شَكْلَهُ وَجِنْسَهُ وَعَيْنَهُ، وَإِلَّا فَهُوَ وَكُلُّ مَنْ قَرَأَ هَذِهِ الْآيَةَ يَعْلَمُ أَنَّهُ مِنْ نَبَاتِ الْأَرْضِ، لِقَوْلِهِ: (فَأَنْبَتْنَا فِيهَا حَبًّا * وَعِنَبًا وَقَضْبًا * وَزَيْتُونًا وَنَخْلا * وَحَدَائِقَ غُلْبًا * وَفَاكِهَةً وَأَبًّا)

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: (مَتَاعًا لَكُمْ وَلأنْعَامِكُمْ) أَيْ: عِيشَةً لَكُمْ وَلِأَنْعَامِكُمْ فِي هَذِهِ الدَّارِ إِلَى يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ.

## 80:33-42 — verses 33-42 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Ibn 'Abbas said: {the Deafening Blast} is one of the names of the Day of Resurrection; Allah magnified it and warned His servants of it. Ibn Jarir said: it is perhaps a name for the blowing of the Trumpet. Al-Baghawi said: {the Deafening Blast} means the Cry of the Resurrection; it was named that because it deafens the hearing, meaning it makes itself heard with such intensity that it nearly renders them deaf.

{The Day when a man will flee from his brother, and his mother and his father, and his wife and his children} means: he sees them, and flees from them, and moves away from them, because the terror is immense and the matter is momentous.

Ikrimah said: the man will meet his wife and say to her: O you, what kind of husband was I to you? She will say: You were an excellent husband! and she will praise him with the best she is able. Then he will say to her: I ask of you today a single good deed that you give to me, so that perhaps I may be saved from what you see. She will say to him: How easy is what you have asked, but I am not able to give you anything, for I fear the same as what you fear. He said: And the man will meet his son and cling to him, and say: O my son, what kind of father was I to you? And he will praise him well. Then he will say to him: O my son, I am in need of the weight of a single atom of your good deeds, so that perhaps I may be saved by it from what you see. His son will say: O my father, how easy is what you have asked, but I fear the same as what you fear, so I cannot give you anything. Allah, the Exalted, says: {The Day when a man will flee from his brother, and his mother and his father, and his wife and his children}.

And in the sound (sahih) hadith, concerning the matter of intercession: when each of the ulu al-'azm (those of firm resolve, the greatest of the messengers) is asked to intercede with Allah for creation, he says: myself, myself, I ask Him for nothing today except for myself, until even 'Isa the son of Maryam (peace be upon him) says: I ask Him for nothing today except for myself, I do not ask Him for Maryam who bore me. And it is for this that Allah, the Exalted, said: {The Day when a man will flee from his brother, and his mother and his father, and his wife and his children}.

Qatadah said: he flees first from the dearest of them, then the next dearest, and from the nearest, then the next nearest, on account of the horror of that day.

* *

And His saying: {Every man among them, that Day, will have a matter that will occupy him} means: he will be in a preoccupation that distracts him from all others.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] ... from Ibn 'Abbas, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "You will be gathered barefoot, naked, walking on foot, uncircumcised." He said: His wife then said: O Messenger of Allah, will some of us see the nakedness of others? He said: {Every man among them, that Day, will have a matter that will occupy him}, or he said: "What will preoccupy him from looking."

Al-Nasa'i narrated it alone, [chain] ... Thabit ibn Yazid, who is Abu Zayd al-Ahwal al-Basri, one of the trustworthy narrators, ... from Ibn 'Abbas, with the same wording. And al-Tirmidhi narrated it, [chain] ... from Ibn 'Abbas, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "You will be gathered barefoot, naked, uncircumcised." A woman then said: Will some of us see, or perceive, the nakedness of others? He said: "O Fulanah (so-and-so), {every man among them, that Day, will have a matter that will occupy him}." Then al-Tirmidhi said: This is a hasan sahih (good and authentic) hadith, and it has been narrated by more than one route from Ibn 'Abbas, may Allah be pleased with him.

Al-Nasa'i said: [chain] ... from 'A'ishah, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "People will be resurrected on the Day of Resurrection barefoot, naked, uncircumcised." 'A'ishah then said: O Messenger of Allah, what then of the private parts? He said: {Every man among them, that Day, will have a matter that will occupy him}.

Al-Nasa'i narrated it alone by this route.

Then Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] ... from Anas ibn Malik, who said: 'A'ishah, may Allah be pleased with her, asked the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, saying: O Messenger of Allah, may my father and mother be sacrificed for you, I am going to ask you about a matter, so inform me of it. He said: "If I have knowledge of it." She said: O Prophet of Allah, how will the men be gathered? He said: "Barefoot, naked." Then she waited a while and said: O Prophet of Allah, how will the women be gathered? He said: "Likewise, barefoot, naked." She said: What shame, from the Day of Resurrection! He said: "And about what are you asking? A verse has been sent down to me: it will not harm you whether you had clothes on or not."

She said: Which verse is it, O Prophet of Allah? He said: {Every man among them, that Day, will have a matter that will occupy him}.

Al-Baghawi said in his Tafsir: [chain] ... from Sawdah, the wife of the Prophet ﷺ, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "People will be resurrected barefoot, naked, uncircumcised, their sweat having risen up to bridle them, and reaching the lobes of their ears." I said: O Messenger of Allah, what shame, will some of us look at others? He said: "The people will be preoccupied, {every man among them, that Day, will have a matter that will occupy him}."

This is a very gharib (singular) hadith by this route, and Ibn Jarir likewise narrated it, [chain] ... from al-Fadl ibn Musa, with the same wording. But Abu Hatim al-Razi said: 'A'idh ibn Shurayh is da'if (weak); there is weakness in his hadith.

* *

And His saying: {Faces, that Day, will be bright, laughing, rejoicing} means: the people there will be two groups: {Faces, that Day, will be bright}, meaning radiant with light, {laughing, rejoicing}, meaning joyful and glad from the joy of their hearts, with cheerfulness having appeared upon their faces; and these are the people of Paradise.

{And faces, that Day, will have upon them dust, overcast by gloom} means: gloom, that is, blackness, will rise over them and cover them.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] ... from his grandfather, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Sweat will bridle the disbeliever, then dust will settle upon their faces." He said: This is [the meaning of] His saying: {And faces, that Day, will have upon them dust}.

Ibn 'Abbas said: {overcast by gloom} means: the blackness of the faces will cover them.

* *

And His saying: {Those are the disbelievers, the wicked} means: disbelievers in their hearts, wicked in their deeds, as Allah, the Exalted, said: {And they will not beget except a wicked disbeliever} [Nuh: 27].

This is the end of the tafsir of Surah 'Abasa, and to Allah belongs all praise and grace.

قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: (الصَّاخَّةُ) اسْمٌ مِنْ أَسْمَاءِ يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ، عَظَّمَهُ اللَّهُ، وحَذّره عِبَادَهُ. قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: لَعَلَّهُ اسْمٌ لِلنَّفْخَةِ فِي الصُّورِ. وَقَالَ البَغَويّ: (الصَّاخَّةُ) يَعْنِي صَيْحَةَ الْقِيَامَةِ؛ سُمِّيَتْ بِذَلِكَ لِأَنَّهَا تَصُخّ الْأَسْمَاعَ، أَيْ: تُبَالِغُ فِي إِسْمَاعِهَا حَتَّى تَكَادَ تُصمّها .

(يَوْمَ يَفِرُّ الْمَرْءُ مِنْ أَخِيهِ * وَأُمِّهِ وَأَبِيهِ * وَصَاحِبَتِهِ وَبَنِيهِ) أَيْ: يَرَاهُمْ، وَيَفِرُّ مِنْهُمْ، وَيَبْتَعِدُ عَنْهُمْ؛ لِأَنَّ الْهَوْلَ عَظِيمٌ، وَالْخَطْبَ جَلِيلٌ.

قَالَ عِكْرِمَةُ: يَلْقَى الرَّجُلُ زَوْجَتَهُ فَيَقُولُ لَهَا: يَا هَذِهِ، أَيُّ بَعْلٍ كنتُ لَكِ؟ فَتَقُولُ: نِعْمَ الْبَعْلُ كنتَ! وَتُثْنِي بِخَيْرٍ مَا اسْتَطَاعَتْ، فَيَقُولُ لَهَا: فَإِنِّي أطلبُ إِلَيْكِ اليومَ حَسَنَةً وَاحِدَةً تَهَبِينَهَا لِي لَعَلِّي أَنْجُو مِمَّا تَرَيْنَ. فَتَقُولُ لَهُ: مَا أَيْسَرَ مَا طلبتَ، وَلَكِنِّي لَا أُطِيقُ أَنْ أُعْطِيَكَ شَيْئًا أَتَخَوَّفُ مِثْلَ الَّذِي تَخَافُ. قَالَ: وَإِنَّ الرَّجُلَ لَيَلْقَى ابْنَهُ فَيَتَعَلَّقُ بِهِ فَيَقُولُ: يَا بُنَيَّ، أَيُّ وَالِدٍ كنتُ لَكَ؟ فَيُثْنِي بِخَيْرٍ. فيقولُ لَهُ: يَا بُنَيَّ، إِنِّي احْتَجْتُ إِلَى مِثْقَالِ ذَرَّةٍ مِنْ حَسَنَاتِكَ لَعَلِّي أَنْجُو بِهَا مِمَّا تَرَى. فَيَقُولُ وَلَدُهُ: يَا أَبَتِ، مَا أَيْسَرَ مَا طَلَبْتَ، وَلَكِنِّي أَتَخَوَّفُ مِثْلَ الَّذِي تَتَخَوَّفُ، فَلَا أَسْتَطِيعُ أَنْ أُعْطِيَكَ شَيْئًا. يَقُولُ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى (يَوْمَ يَفِرُّ الْمَرْءُ مِنْ أَخِيهِ * وَأُمِّهِ وَأَبِيهِ * وَصَاحِبَتِهِ وَبَنِيهِ)

وَفِي الْحَدِيثِ الصَّحِيحِ-فِي أَمْرِ الشَّفَاعَةِ-: أَنَّهُ إِذَا طُلِبَ إِلَى كُلٍّ مِنْ أُولِي الْعَزْمِ أَنْ يَشْفَعَ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ فِي الْخَلَائِقِ، يَقُولُ: نَفْسِي نَفْسِي، لَا أَسْأَلُهُ اليومَ إِلَّا نَفْسِي، حَتَّى إِنَّ عِيسَى ابْنَ مَرْيَمَ يَقُولُ: لَا أَسْأَلُهُ الْيَوْمَ إِلَّا نَفْسِي، لَا أَسْأَلُهُ مَرْيَمَ الَّتِي وَلَدَتْنِي. وَلِهَذَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: (يَوْمَ يَفِرُّ الْمَرْءُ مِنْ أَخِيهِ * وَأُمِّهِ وَأَبِيهِ * وَصَاحِبَتِهِ وَبَنِيهِ) .

قَالَ قَتَادَةُ: الْأَحَبُّ فالأحبَ، وَالْأَقْرَبُ فالأقربَ، مِنْ هَوْلِ ذَلِكَ الْيَوْمِ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: (لِكُلِّ امْرِئٍ مِنْهُمْ يَوْمَئِذٍ شَأْنٌ يُغْنِيهِ) أَيْ: هُوَ فِي شُغُل شَاغِلٍ عَنْ غَيْرِهِ.

قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عَمَّارِ بْنِ الْحَارِثِ، حَدَّثَنَا الْوَلِيدُ بْنُ صَالِحٍ، حَدَّثَنَا ثَابِتٌ أَبُو زَيْدٍ الْعَبَّادَانِيُّ، عَنْ هِلَالِ بْنِ خَبَّاب، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: " تُحْشَرُونَ حُفَاةً عُرَاةً مُشَاةً غُرلا " قَالَ: فَقَالَتْ زوجته: يا رسول الله، أوَ يرى بَعْضُنَا عَوْرَةَ بَعْضٍ؟ قَالَ: " (لِكُلِّ امْرِئٍ مِنْهُمْ يَوْمَئِذٍ شَأْنٌ يُغْنِيهِ) أَوْ قَالَ: "مَا أَشْغَلَهُ عَنِ النَّظَرِ".

وَقَالَ النَّسَائِيُّ: أَخْبَرَنِي عَمْرُو بْنُ عُثْمَانَ، حَدَّثَنَا بَقِيَّة، حَدَّثَنَا الزُّبَيْدِيُّ، أَخْبَرَنِي الزُّهْرِيُّ، عَنْ عُرْوَةَ، عَنْ عَائِشَةَ، أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "يُبْعَثُ النَّاسُ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ حُفَاةً عُرَاةً غُرلا". فَقَالَتْ عَائِشَةُ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، فَكَيْفَ بِالْعَوْرَاتِ؟ فَقَالَ: " (لِكُلِّ امْرِئٍ مِنْهُمْ يَوْمَئِذٍ شَأْنٌ يُغْنِيهِ) .

انْفَرَدَ بِهِ النَّسَائِيُّ مِنْ هَذَا الْوَجْهِ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا أَزْهَرُ بْنُ حَاتِمٍ، حَدَّثَنَا الْفَضْلُ بْنُ مُوسَى، عَنْ عائد ابن شُرَيح، عَنْ أَنَسِ بْنِ مَالِكٍ قَالَ: سَأَلَتْ عَائِشَةُ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهَا، رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالَتْ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، بِأَبِي أَنْتَ وَأُمِّي، إِنِّي سَائِلَتُكَ عَنْ حَدِيثٍ فَتُخْبِرُنِي أنتَ بِهِ. فَقَالَ: "إِنْ كَانَ عِنْدِي مِنْهُ عِلْمٌ". قَالَتْ: يَا نَبِيَّ اللَّهِ، كَيْفَ يُحشر الرِّجَالُ؟ قَالَ: "حُفَاةً عُرَاةً". ثُمَّ انتظَرتْ سَاعَةً فَقَالَتْ: يَا نَبِيَّ اللَّهِ، كَيْفَ يُحْشَرُ النِّسَاءُ؟ قَالَ: "كَذَلِكَ حُفَاةً عُرَاةً". قَالَتْ: وَاسَوْأَتَاهُ مِنْ يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ! قَالَ: "وَعَنْ أَيِّ ذَلِكَ تَسْأَلِينَ؟ إِنَّهُ قَدْ نَزَلَ عَلَيَّ آيَةٌ لَا يَضُرُّكِ كَانَ عَلَيْكِ ثِيَابٌ أَوْ لَا يَكُونُ".

قَالَتْ: أيةُ آيَةٍ هِيَ يَا نَبِيَّ اللَّهِ؟ قَالَ: " (لِكُلِّ امْرِئٍ مِنْهُمْ يَوْمَئِذٍ شَأْنٌ يُغْنِيهِ) .

وَقَالَ الْبَغَوِيُّ فِي تَفْسِيرِهِ: أَخْبَرَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ إِبْرَاهِيمَ الشُّرَيْحِيُّ، أَخْبَرَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ الثَّعْلَبِيُّ، أَخْبَرَنِي الْحُسَيْنُ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ الْعَزِيزِ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ أَبِي أُوَيْسٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ أَبِي عَيَّاشٍ، عَنْ عَطَاءِ بْنِ يَسَارٍ، عَنْ سَوْدَةَ زَوْجَ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ قَالَتْ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "يُبْعَثُ النَّاسُ حُفَاةً عُرَاةً غُرلا قَدْ أَلْجَمَهُمُ الْعَرَقُ، وَبَلَغَ شُحُومَ الْآذَانِ". فَقُلْتُ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، وَاسَوْأَتَاهُ يَنْظُرُ بَعْضُنَا إِلَى بَعْضٍ؟ فَقَالَ: "قَدْ شُغل النَّاسُ، (لِكُلِّ امْرِئٍ مِنْهُمْ يَوْمَئِذٍ شَأْنٌ يُغْنِيهِ) .

هَذَا حَدِيثٌ غَرِيبٌ مِنْ هَذَا الْوَجْهِ جِدًّا، وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ عَنْ أَبِي عَمَّارٍ الْحُسَيْنِ بْنِ حُرَيْثٍ الْمَرْوَزِيِّ، عَنِ الْفَضْلِ بْنِ مُوسَى، بِهِ . وَلَكِنْ قَالَ أَبُو حَاتِمٍ الرَّازِيُّ: عَائِذُ بْنُ شُرَيْحٍ ضَعِيفٌ، فِي حَدِيثِهِ ضَعْفٌ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: (وُجُوهٌ يَوْمَئِذٍ مُسْفِرَةٌ * ضَاحِكَةٌ مُسْتَبْشِرَةٌ) أَيْ: يَكُونُ النَّاسُ هُنَالِكَ فَرِيقَيْنِ: (وُجُوهٌ يَوْمَئِذٍ مُسْفِرَةٌ) أَيْ: مُسْتَنِيرَةٌ، (ضَاحِكَةٌ مُسْتَبْشِرَةٌ) أَيْ: مَسْرُورَةٌ فَرِحَةٌ مِنْ سُرُورِ قُلُوبِهِمْ، قَدْ ظَهَرَ الْبِشْرُ عَلَى وُجُوهِهِمْ، وَهَؤُلَاءِ أَهْلُ الْجَنَّةِ.

(وَوُجُوهٌ يَوْمَئِذٍ عَلَيْهَا غَبَرَةٌ * تَرْهَقُهَا قَتَرَةٌ) أَيْ: يَعْلُوهَا وَيَغْشَاهَا قَتَرَةٌ، أَيْ: سَوَادٌ.

قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا سَهْلِ بْنِ عُثْمَانَ الْعَسْكَرِيِّ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو عَلِيٍّ مُحَمَّدٌ مَوْلَى جَعْفَرِ بْنِ مُحَمَّدٍ، عَنْ جَعْفَرِ بْنِ مُحَمَّدٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ جَدِّهِ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "يُلْجِمُ الكافرَ العرقُ ثُمَّ تَقَعُ الغُبْرة عَلَى وُجُوهِهِمْ". قَالَ: فَهُوَ قَوْلُهُ: (وَوُجُوهٌ يَوْمَئِذٍ عَلَيْهَا غَبَرَةٌ) .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: (تَرْهَقُهَا قَتَرَةٌ) أَيْ: يَغْشَاهَا سَوَادُ الْوُجُوهِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: (أُولَئِكَ هُمُ الْكَفَرَةُ الْفَجَرَةُ) أَيِ: الْكَفَرَةُ قُلُوبُهُمْ، الْفَجَرَةُ فِي أَعْمَالِهِمْ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَلا يَلِدُوا إِلا فَاجِرًا كَفَّارًا﴾ [نُوحٍ: ٢٧] .

آخِرُ تفسير سورة "عبس" ولله الحمد والمنة.
