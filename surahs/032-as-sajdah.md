## 32:1-3 — verses 1-3 (shared commentary)

### Translation

# Tafsir of Surat al-Sajdah

It is Meccan.

Al-Bukhari said in "Kitab al-Jumu'ah": [chain] Abu Hurayrah, may Allah be pleased with him, said: The Prophet ﷺ used to recite in the Fajr prayer on Friday: {Alif Lam Mim. Tanzil} (the Sajdah), and {Hal ata 'ala al-insan}.

Muslim also narrated it from the hadith of Sufyan al-Thawri, with the same chain.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Jabir said: The Prophet ﷺ would not sleep until he had recited {Alif Lam Mim. Tanzil} (the Sajdah) and {Tabaraka alladhi bi-yadihi al-mulk}. Ahmad alone narrated this.

The discussion of the disjointed letters has already preceded at the beginning of Surat al-Baqarah in a manner that makes repeating it here unnecessary.

As for His saying, {Tanzil al-Kitabi la rayba fih} (The revelation of the Book, in which there is no doubt), that is, there is no doubt or suspicion that it has been sent down, {from the Lord of the worlds}.

Then He said, informing of the polytheists: {Am yaqoolouna aftarah} (Or do they say he fabricated it), rather they say: {he fabricated it}, meaning he invented it from himself. {Rather it is the truth from your Lord, so that you may warn a people to whom no warner has come before you, that perhaps they may be guided}, meaning that they may follow the truth.

## 32:4-6 — verses 4-6 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty informs that He is the Creator of all things, having created the heavens and the earth and what is between them in six days, then risen over the Throne (istawa). The discussion of this has already preceded.

{You have no protector or intercessor besides Him} means: rather He is the Master who holds the reins of all affairs, the Creator of everything, the Manager of everything, the One capable over everything. So His creation has no protector besides Him, and no intercessor except after His permission.

{Will you not then remember} meaning: O you who worship other than Him, who place your trust in someone other than Him, He, Exalted, Blessed, and Transcendent, is far above having any equal, partner, rival, minister, or counterpart. There is no god but He and no lord besides Him.

Al-Nasa'i has related here a hadith, saying: [chain] ... Abu Hurayrah reported that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ took my hand and said: "Indeed Allah created the heavens and the earth and what is between them in six days, then rose over the Throne on the seventh day. He created the soil on Saturday, the mountains on Sunday, the trees on Monday, that which is disliked on Tuesday, the light on Wednesday, the moving creatures on Thursday, and Adam on Friday in the last hour of the day after 'Asr. He created him from the surface soil of the earth, its red and its black, its good and its foul. For that reason Allah made among the children of Adam the good and the foul."

Thus he related this hadith with its chain and text. Muslim and al-Nasa'i also transmitted it, from the hadith of al-Hajjaj ibn Muhammad al-A'war, from Ibn Jurayj, from Isma'il ibn Umayyah, from Ayyub ibn Khalid, from Abd Allah ibn Rafi', from Abu Hurayrah, from the Prophet ﷺ, in a similar wording to this account.

Al-Bukhari identified a defect in it in his book "al-Tarikh al-Kabir," saying: "Some of them said: Abu Hurayrah from Ka'b al-Ahbar, and this is more correct." Likewise more than one of the hadith masters identified this defect in it. And Allah knows best.

His saying, {He directs the affair from the heaven to the earth, then it ascends to Him}, means: His command descends from the highest of the heavens to the farthest depths of the seventh earth, as Allah the Almighty said: {Allah is He who created seven heavens and of the earth their like. The command descends between them so that you may know that Allah has power over all things and that Allah encompasses all things in knowledge} [al-Talaq: 12].

Deeds are raised up to their register above the heaven of this world, and the distance between it and the earth is a journey of five hundred years, and the thickness of the heaven is five hundred years.

Mujahid, Qatadah, and al-Dahhak said: the descent of the angel takes a journey of five hundred years, and his ascent takes a journey of five hundred years, but he traverses it in the blink of an eye. This is why the Almighty said: {In a Day whose measure is a thousand years of what you count}.

{That is the Knower of the unseen and the witnessed} means: the Manager of these affairs, who is a witness over the deeds of His servants, to whom their weighty and their trivial matters are raised, their small and their great. He is {the Mighty}, who has made everything subject to His might, subduing it and overpowering it, and to whom servants and necks submit in humility, {the Merciful} to His believing servants. So He is mighty in His mercy, merciful in His might. This is perfection: might joined with mercy, and mercy joined with might, so He is merciful without weakness.

## 32:7-9 — verses 7-9 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: He is the One who made the creation of things good, perfected it, and made it precise.

Malik said, on the authority of Zayd ibn Aslam, regarding {The One who made excellent everything He created}: he said, He made the creation of everything excellent, as though he took it to mean the earlier and the later part reversed in order.

Then, having mentioned the creation of the heavens and the earth, He began to mention the creation of man, saying: {And He began the creation of man from clay}, meaning: He created the father of mankind, Adam, from clay.

{Then He made his progeny from an extract of despised fluid}, meaning: they reproduce in this way from a sperm-drop that comes out from between the loins of the man and the ribs of the woman.

{Then He fashioned him}, meaning Adam: when He created him from dust, He created him well-formed and upright. {And breathed into him of His spirit, and made for you hearing, sight, and hearts}, meaning: intellects, {little thanks do you give}, meaning: for these faculties which Allah, Mighty and Majestic, has bestowed upon you as provision. The truly fortunate one is he who employs them in obedience to his Lord, Mighty and Majestic.

## 32:10-11 — verses 10-11 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, informing about the polytheists in their considering the return improbable, when they said: {Are we, when we are lost in the earth} meaning: our bodies are torn apart and scattered among the parts of the earth and gone, {shall we indeed be in a new creation}? meaning: shall we indeed return after that state?! They consider this improbable, but this is only improbable in relation to their own powerless capacity, not in relation to the capacity of the One who originated them and created them from nothingness, whose affair, when He wills a thing, is only to say to it "Be," and it is. For this reason He said: {Nay, they are disbelievers in the meeting with their Lord}.

Then He said: {Say: the angel of death who has been charged over you will take you} The apparent meaning of this verse is that the angel of death is a specific individual among the angels, as is directly understood from the hadith of al-Bara' mentioned previously in Surat Ibrahim. He has been named in some reports Izra'il, and this is the well-known view, stated by Qatadah and more than one other. He has helpers. It has likewise come in the hadith that his helpers pull the souls out from the rest of the body, until when it reaches the throat, the angel of death himself takes hold of it.

Mujahid said: the earth was gathered together for him and made like a basin for him, from which he takes wherever he wishes. Zuhayr ibn Muhammad related this from the Prophet ﷺ in similar wording, as a mursal (report with a gap before the Companion). Ibn Abbas, may Allah be pleased with both of them, said the same.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Ja'far ibn Muhammad said: I heard my father say: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ looked at the angel of death at the head of a man from the Ansar, and the Prophet ﷺ said to him: "O angel of death, be gentle with my companion, for he is a believer." The angel of death said: "O Muhammad, be at ease in your soul and let your eye be comforted, for I am gentle with every believer. And know that there is no house of clay or of hair cloth, on land or on sea, but that I go over it in inspection five times every day, so much so that I know their young and their old among themselves better than they know themselves. By Allah, O Muhammad, if I wished to seize the soul of a mosquito, I would not be able to do so until Allah Himself is the One who commands its seizure."

Ja'far said: it has reached me that he only inspects them at the times of prayer. So when he comes to them at the time of death, if the person was one who maintained the prayer, the angel draws near to him, repels the devil from him, and the angel prompts him: "There is no god but Allah, Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah," in that momentous state.

Abd al-Razzaq said: [chain] Ibrahim ibn Maysarah said: I heard Mujahid say: there is no house of hair cloth or of clay upon the face of the earth but that the angel of death circles around it twice every day.

Ka'b al-Ahbar said: By Allah, there is no house in which there is anyone of the people of this world but that the angel of death stands at its door seven times every day, looking to see whether there is anyone in it whom he has been commanded to take. Ibn Abi Hatim related this.

As for His saying: {Then to your Lord you will be returned}, meaning: on the day of your return and your rising from your graves for your reckoning.

## 32:12-14 — verses 12-14 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty informs of the condition of the polytheists on the Day of Resurrection, and their state when they witness the resurrection and stand before God, humiliated and abased, hanging their heads down, that is, out of shame and disgrace, saying: {Our Lord, we have seen and heard} that is, we now hear Your word and obey Your command, just as the Almighty said: {How well they will hear and see on the Day they come to Us} [Maryam: 38]. And likewise they will turn upon themselves in blame when they enter the Fire, saying: {If only we had listened or reasoned, we would not have been among the companions of the Blaze} [al-Mulk: 10]. And so these too say: {Our Lord, we have seen and heard, so return us} that is, to the worldly abode, {we will do righteous deeds, indeed we are certain now} that is, we have become certain and convinced that Your promise is true and Your meeting is true.

The Lord, Almighty, already knew of them that if He returned them to the worldly abode they would be just as they were in it, disbelievers who denied the signs of God and opposed His messengers, just as He said: {If only you could see when they are made to stand before the Fire and they say: Would that we were returned, and we would not deny the signs of our Lord, and we would be among the believers. Rather, what they used to conceal before has become apparent to them, and if they were returned they would go back to that which they were forbidden, and indeed they are liars. And they said: There is nothing but our worldly life, and we will not be resurrected} [al-An'am: 27-29].

And He said here: {Had We willed, We would have given every soul its guidance}, just as the Almighty said: {Had your Lord willed, everyone on earth would have believed, all of them together} [Yunus: 99].

{But the word from Me has come into effect: I will surely fill Hell with jinn and men altogether} that is, from the two kinds. So their abode is the Fire, they have no way to turn away from it and no escape from it. We seek refuge with God and His perfect words from that.

{So taste, because you forgot the meeting of this Day of yours} that is, it will be said to the people of the Fire by way of reprimand and rebuke: taste this punishment because of your denial of it, your considering its occurrence to be far off, and your feigning forgetfulness of it, since you dealt with it as one who is truly forgetful of it deals with it. {Indeed We have forgotten you} that is, We will treat you with the treatment of one who forgets, for the Almighty does not forget anything and nothing is lost from Him. Rather, this is from the way of reciprocal treatment, just as the Almighty said: {Today We forget you just as you forgot the meeting of this Day of yours} [al-Jathiyah: 34].

And His saying: {And taste the punishment of eternity for what you used to do} that is, because of your disbelief and your denial, just as He said in another verse: {They taste therein neither coolness nor drink, except boiling water and pus, a fitting recompense. Indeed they did not expect a reckoning, and they denied Our signs, greatly denying. And everything We have recorded in a book. So taste, for We will not increase you except in punishment} [al-Naba: 24-30].

﴿فَذُوقُوا بِمَا نَسِيتُمْ لِقَاءَ يَوْمِكُمْ هَذَا﴾ أَيْ: يُقَالُ لِأَهْلِ النَّارِ عَلَى سَبِيلِ التَّقْرِيعِ وَالتَّوْبِيخِ: ذُوقُوا [هَذَا] الْعَذَابَ بِسَبَبِ تَكْذِيبِكُمْ بِهِ، وَاسْتِبْعَادِكُمْ وُقُوعَهُ، وَتَنَاسِيكُمْ لَهُ؛ إِذْ عَامَلْتُمُوهُ مُعَامَلَةَ مَنْ هُوَ نَاسٍ لَهُ، ﴿إِنَّا نَسِينَاكُمْ﴾ أَيْ: [إِنَّا] سَنُعَامِلُكُمْ مُعَامَلَةَ النَّاسِي؛ لِأَنَّهُ تَعَالَى لَا يَنْسَى شَيْئًا وَلَا يَضِلُّ عَنْهُ شَيْءٌ، بَلْ مِنْ بَابِ الْمُقَابَلَةِ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿الْيَوْمَ نَنْسَاكُمْ كَمَا نَسِيتُمْ لِقَاءَ يَوْمِكُمْ هَذَا﴾ [الْجَاثِيَةِ: ٣٤] .

## 32:15-17 — verses 15-17 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: {Only those believe in Our signs} meaning: only those affirm them as true {who, when reminded of them, fall down in prostration} meaning: they listen to them and obey them in word and deed, {and glorify their Lord with praise, and are not arrogant} that is, they do not show arrogance toward following them and submitting to them, as the ignorant among the wicked disbelievers do. God, the Exalted, has said: {Indeed, those who are too arrogant to worship Me will enter Hell in humiliation} [Ghafir: 60].

Then He, the Exalted, says: {Their sides forsake their beds} meaning by this: standing in prayer at night, and forsaking sleep and lying on soft bedding. Mujahid and al-Hasan said concerning His statement: {Their sides forsake their beds}, this means: standing in prayer at night.

From Anas, Ikrimah, Muhammad ibn al-Munkadir, Abu Hazim, and Qatadah: it is the prayer performed between the two night prayers (Maghrib and Isha). And from Anas also: it is waiting for the night prayer (al-Atamah). Ibn Jarir related this with a good chain.

Al-Dahhak said: it is the night prayer performed in congregation, and the dawn prayer performed in congregation.

{They call upon their Lord in fear and hope} meaning: in fear of the severity of His punishment, and in hope of the abundance of His reward, {and they spend from what We have provided them}. Thus they combine performing the acts of devotion that pertain to themselves alone with those that benefit others as well. The foremost of these people, their leader and their pride in this world and the next, is the Messenger of God ﷺ, as Abdullah ibn Rawahah, may God be pleased with him, said:

"Among us is the Messenger of God reciting His Book, when a clear brightness of dawn breaks forth...

He showed us guidance after blindness, so our hearts are certain that what he says will come to pass...

He spends the night forsaking his side from his bed, when the beds grow heavy with the polytheists' sleep..."

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Ibn Mas'ud, from the Prophet ﷺ, said: "Our Lord marvels at two men: a man who rose from his mat and his blanket, and from among his family and his people, to his prayer, [so our Lord says: O My angels, look at My servant, he rose from his bed and his mat, and from among his people and his family, to his prayer] out of desire for what is with Me, and fear of what is with Me. And a man who fought in the path of God, the Mighty and Majestic, and they were defeated, and he knew what awaited him if he fled, and what awaited him if he returned, so he returned until his blood was shed, out of desire for what is with Me and fear of what is with Me. So God, the Mighty and Majestic, says to the angels: Look at My servant, he returned out of desire for what is with Me, and dread of what is with Me, until his blood was shed."

Abu Dawud related this likewise in "al-Jihad," from Musa ibn Isma'il, from Hammad ibn Salamah, with similar wording.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] from Mu'adh ibn Jabal, who said: I was with the Prophet ﷺ on a journey, and one morning I found myself near him while we were traveling, so I said: O Prophet of God, tell me of a deed that will admit me to Paradise and keep me far from the Fire. He said: "You have asked about a great matter, yet it is easy for one for whom God makes it easy. You worship God and do not associate anything with Him, you establish the prayer, you give the zakat, you fast Ramadan, and you perform the pilgrimage to the House." Then he said: "Shall I not point you to the gates of good? Fasting is a shield, and charity extinguishes sin, and a man's prayer in the depths of the night." Then he recited: {Their sides forsake their beds}, until he reached {they used to do}. Then he said: "Shall I not tell you of the head of the matter, its pillar, and the peak of its hump?" I said: Yes indeed, O Messenger of God. He said: "The head of the matter is Islam, its pillar is prayer, and the peak of its hump is jihad in the path of God." Then he said: "Shall I not tell you of the master of all of that?" I said: Yes indeed, O Prophet of God. So he took hold of his own tongue and said: "Restrain this from yourself." I said: O Messenger of God, will we indeed be held accountable for what we speak? He said: "May your mother be bereft of you, O Mu'adh! Is there anything that topples people into the Fire on their faces, or he said, on their nostrils, except the harvests of their tongues?"

Al-Tirmidhi, al-Nasa'i, and Ibn Majah related this in their Sunan collections, through various chains from Ma'mar. Al-Tirmidhi said: hasan sahih (good and authentic). Ibn Jarir related it from the hadith of Shu'bah, from al-Hakam, who said: I heard Urwah ibn al-Nazzal narrating from Mu'adh ibn Jabal, that the Messenger of God ﷺ said to him: "Shall I not point you to the gates of good: fasting is a shield, charity expiates sin, and a servant's standing in prayer in the depths of the night," and he recited this verse: {Their sides forsake their beds, they call upon their Lord in fear and hope, and they spend from what We have provided them}.

He also related it from the hadith of al-Thawri, from Mansur ibn al-Mu'tamir, from al-Hakam, from Maymun ibn Abi Shabib, from Mu'adh, from the Prophet ﷺ with similar wording, and from the hadith of al-A'mash, from Habib ibn Abi Thabit, and al-Hakam, from Maymun ibn Abi Shabib, from Mu'adh, as a marfu (raised) report with similar wording. And from the hadith of Hammad ibn Salamah, from Asim ibn Abi al-Najud, from Shahr, from Mu'adh ibn Jabal, from the Prophet ﷺ, concerning His statement: {Their sides forsake their beds}, he said: "It is a servant's standing in prayer at night."

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] from Mu'adh ibn Jabal, who said: I was with the Prophet ﷺ during the expedition of Tabuk, and he said: "If you wish, I will inform you of the gates of good: fasting is a shield, charity extinguishes sin, and a man's standing in prayer in the depths of the night," then the Messenger of God ﷺ recited: {Their sides forsake their beds, they call upon their Lord in fear and hope, and they spend from what We have provided them}.

Then he said: [chain] from Asma bint Yazid, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "When God gathers the first and the last on the Day of Resurrection, a caller will come and call out in a voice that all creation will hear: The people of the gathering will know today who is most worthy of honor. Then he will return and call out: Let those whose {sides used to forsake their beds}, the verse, stand up. So they will stand, and they will be few."

Al-Bazzar said: [chain] from Zayd ibn Aslam, from his father, who said: Bilal said, when this verse was revealed: {Their sides forsake their beds}, the verse: We used to sit in gatherings, and some of the Companions of the Messenger of God ﷺ would pray after Maghrib until Isha, so this verse was revealed: {Their sides forsake their beds}.

Then he said: We do not know of Aslam narrating from Bilal anything other than this, and there is no other chain for it from Bilal besides this one.

And His statement: {No soul knows what has been hidden for them of comfort for the eyes, as reward for what they used to do} means: no one knows the magnitude of what God has hidden for them in the Gardens of everlasting bliss and pleasures the like of which no one has ever glimpsed. Since they hid their deeds, God hid for them their reward, a fitting recompense, for the reward corresponds in kind to the deed.

Al-Hasan [al-Basri] said: a people hid their deeds, so God hid for them what no eye has seen, and what has never occurred to the heart of any human being. Ibn Abi Hatim related this.

Al-Bukhari said, concerning His statement: {No soul knows what has been hidden for them of comfort for the eyes}, the verse: [chain] from Abu Hurayrah, may God be pleased with him, from the Messenger of God ﷺ, who said: "God, the Exalted, has said: I have prepared for My righteous servants what no eye has seen, no ear has heard, and what has never occurred to the heart of any human being." Abu Hurayrah said: recite, if you wish: {No soul knows what has been hidden for them of comfort for the eyes}.

He said: and [chain] from Abu Hurayrah, who said: God said the like of it. It was said to Sufyan: is this a marfu report? He said: what else would it be?

Muslim and al-Tirmidhi related it from the hadith of Sufyan ibn Uyaynah, with this chain. Al-Tirmidhi said: hasan sahih (good and authentic).

Then al-Bukhari said: Ishaq ibn Nasr told us, Abu Usamah told us, from al-A'mash, Abu Salih told us, from Abu Hurayrah, from the Prophet ﷺ: "God the Exalted says: I have prepared for My righteous servants what no eye has seen, no ear has heard, and what has never occurred to the heart of any human being, as a treasure beyond what I have shown you." Then he recited: {No soul knows what has been hidden for them of comfort for the eyes, as reward for what they used to do}.

Abu Mu'awiyah said, from al-A'mash, from Abu Salih: Abu Hurayrah recited "qurrat a'yun" (the plural form). Al-Bukhari alone transmitted this from this route.

Imam Ahmad said: Abd al-Razzaq told us, Ma'mar told us, from Hammam ibn Munabbih, who said: This is what Abu Hurayrah told us from the Messenger of God ﷺ: "God the Exalted said: I have prepared for My righteous servants what no eye has seen, no ear has heard, and what has never occurred to the heart of any human being."

Both [Bukhari and Muslim] narrated it in their Sahihs from the report of Abd al-Razzaq. Al-Tirmidhi narrated it in his Tafsir, and Ibn Jarir, from the hadith of Abd al-Rahim ibn Sulayman, from Muhammad ibn Amr, from Abu Salamah, from Abu Hurayrah, may God be pleased with him, from the Messenger of God ﷺ with the same wording. Then al-Tirmidhi said: This is a hasan sahih (good, sound) hadith.

Hammad ibn Salamah said, from Thabit, from Abu Rafi', from Abu Hurayrah, may God be pleased with him. Hammad said: I think it is from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "Whoever enters Paradise lives in bliss and is never in hardship; his clothes never wear out and his youth never fades. In Paradise is what no eye has seen, no ear has heard, and what has never occurred to the heart of any human being."

Muslim narrated it from the hadith of Hammad ibn Salamah, with this chain.

Imam Ahmad narrated: Harun told us, Ibn Wahb told us, Abu Sakhr told me, that Abu Hazim told him, saying: I heard Sahl ibn Sa'd al-Sa'idi, may God be pleased with him, say: I attended a gathering of the Messenger of God ﷺ in which he described Paradise until he finished, then said at the end of his account: "In it is what no eye has seen, no ear has heard, and what has never occurred to the heart of any human being." Then he recited this verse: {Their sides forsake their beds, [calling upon their Lord in fear and hope]} up to His saying: {they used to do}.

Muslim also narrated it in his Sahih from Harun ibn Ma'ruf and Harun ibn Sa'id, both from Ibn Wahb, with this chain.

Ibn Jarir said: Al-Abbas ibn Abi Talib told me, Mu'alla ibn Asad told us, Sallam ibn Abi Muti' told us, from Qatadah, from Uqbah ibn Abd al-Ghafir, from Abu Sa'id al-Khudri, from the Messenger of God ﷺ, reporting from his Lord, Mighty and Majestic, who said: "I have prepared for My righteous servants what no eye has seen, no ear has heard, and what has never occurred to the heart of any human being." Neither [Bukhari nor Muslim] narrated this.

Muslim also said in his Sahih: Ibn Abi Umar and others told us, Sufyan told us, Mutarrif ibn Tarif and Abd al-Malik ibn Sa'id told us, both heard al-Sha'bi report from al-Mughirah ibn Shu'bah, who said: I heard him on the pulpit, tracing it back to the Prophet ﷺ, saying: "Moses, peace be upon him, asked his Lord, Mighty and Majestic: What is the lowest rank of the people of Paradise? He said: It is a man who comes after the people of Paradise have already been admitted to Paradise, and it is said to him: Enter Paradise. He says: O Lord, how, when the people have already settled into their dwellings and taken their portions? It is said to him: Would you be satisfied to have the like of the dominion of one of the kings of the world? He says: I am satisfied, Lord. He says: That is for you, and its like, and its like, and its like, and its like. He said in the fifth instance: I am satisfied, Lord. He says: This is for you and ten times its like, and for you is whatever your soul desires and your eye delights in. He says: I am satisfied, Lord. He said: Lord, what of those of highest rank? He said: Those are the ones I have willed. I planted their honor with My own hand and sealed it, so that no eye has seen it, no ear has heard it, and it has never occurred to the heart of any human being." He said: Its confirmation in the Book of God is: {No soul knows what has been hidden for them of comfort for the eyes, as reward for what they used to do}.

Al-Tirmidhi narrated it from Ibn Abi Umar, and said: hasan sahih. He said: Some narrated it from al-Sha'bi, from al-Mughirah, without raising it to the Prophet, but the raised version (marfu') is more sound. Ibn Abi Hatim said: Ja'far ibn Munir al-Mada'ini told us, Abu Badr Shuja' ibn al-Walid told us, Ziyad ibn Khaythamah told us, from Muhammad ibn Juhadah, from Amir ibn Abd al-Wahid, who said: It has reached me that a man among the people of Paradise remains in his place for seventy years, then turns and finds a woman more beautiful than what he had. She says to him: Has the time not come for us to have a share of you? He says: Who are you? She says: I am from al-mazid (the increase). He remains with her seventy years, then turns and finds a woman more beautiful than what he had. She says to him: Has the time not come for us to have a share of you? He says: Who are you? She says: I am the one about whom God said: {No soul knows what has been hidden for them of comfort for the eyes}.

Ibn Lahi'ah said: Ata' ibn Dinar told me, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, who said: The angels come to them three times in the measure of each day of the days of this world, bringing with them gifts from God from the Gardens of Eden that are not in their own gardens. That is His saying: {No soul knows what has been hidden for them of comfort for the eyes}. And they are informed that God is pleased with them.

Ibn Jarir said: Sahl ibn Musa al-Razi told us, al-Walid ibn Muslim told us, from Safwan ibn Amr, from Abu al-Yaman al-Hawzani, or someone else, who said: Paradise has a hundred levels. The first is a level of silver, its ground is silver, its dwellings are silver, [its vessels are silver], and its soil is musk. The second is gold, its ground is gold, its dwellings are gold, its vessels are gold, and its soil is musk. The third is pearl, its ground is pearl, its dwellings are pearl, its vessels are pearl, and its soil is musk. And ninety-seven beyond that, containing what no eye has seen, no ear has heard, and what has never occurred to the heart of any human being. Then he recited this verse: {No soul knows what has been hidden for them of comfort for the eyes, as reward for what they used to do}.

Ibn Jarir said: Ya'qub ibn Ibrahim told me, Mu'tamir ibn Sulayman told us, from al-Hakam ibn Aban, from al-Ghitrif, from Jabir ibn Zayd, from Ibn Abbas, from the Prophet ﷺ, from Gabriel, the Trustworthy Spirit, who said: "The good deeds and bad deeds of a servant are brought, and some are cancelled against others. If a single good deed remains, God will make room for him in Paradise on account of it." He said: I then went to see "Yazdad," and he related a similar hadith. I said: Where then does the good deed go? He said: {Those are the ones from whom We accept the best of what they did, and We overlook their bad deeds, among the companions of Paradise. That is the true promise which they were promised} [al-Ahqaf: 16]. I said: Regarding His saying: {No soul knows what has been hidden for them of comfort for the eyes}, he said: It is the servant who does something in secret which he confides only to God, which no one else knows of, so God confides to him on the Day of Resurrection a comfort of the eyes in return.

[أرَانَا الهُدَى بَعْدَ العَمَى فَقُلُوبُنَا ... بِهِ مُوقِنَاتٌ أنَّ مَا قَال وَاقِعُ] ...

يَبيتُ يُجَافِي جَنْبَهُ عَنْ فِرَاشِه ... إِذَا اسْتَثْقَلَتْ بالْمُشْرِكِين المَضَاجِعُ ...

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا رَوْحٌ وَعَفَّانُ قَالَا حَدَّثَنَا حَمَّادُ بْنُ سَلَمَةَ، أَخْبَرَنَا عَطَاءِ بْنِ السَّائِبِ، عَنْ مُرَّة الْهَمْدَانِيِّ، عَنْ ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ قَالَ: "عَجِبَ رَبُّنَا مِنْ رَجُلَيْنِ: رَجُلٌ ثَارَ مِنْ وِطَائه وَلِحَافِهِ، وَمِنْ بَيْنِ أَهْلِهِ وَحَيِّه إِلَى صَلَاتِهِ، [فَيَقُولُ رَبُّنَا: أَيَا مَلَائِكَتِي، انْظُرُوا إِلَى عَبْدِي، ثَارَ مِنْ فِرَاشِهِ وَوِطَائِهِ، وَمِنْ بَيْنِ حَيِّهِ وَأَهْلِهِ إِلَى صَلَاتِهِ] رَغْبَةً فِيمَا عِنْدِي، وَشَفَقَةً مِمَّا عِنْدِي. وَرَجُلٌ غَزَا فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، فَانْهَزَمُوا، فَعَلِمَ مَا عَلَيْهِ مِنَ الْفِرَارِ، وَمَا لَهُ فِي الرُّجُوعِ، فَرَجَعَ حَتَّى أُهْرِيقَ دَمُهُ، رَغْبَةً فِيمَا عِنْدِي وَشَفَقَةً مِمَّا عِنْدِي. فَيَقُولُ اللَّهُ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ لِلْمَلَائِكَةِ: انْظُرُوا إِلَى عَبْدِي رَجَعَ رَغْبَةً فِيمَا عِنْدِي، وَرَهْبَةً مِمَّا عِنْدِي، حَتَّى أُهْرِيقَ دَمُهُ".

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ أَبُو دَاوُدَ فِي "الْجِهَادِ"، عَنْ مُوسَى بْنِ إِسْمَاعِيلَ، عَنْ حَمَّادِ بْنِ سَلَمَةَ، بِهِ بِنَحْوِهِ. 

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، أَخْبَرَنَا مَعْمَر، عَنْ عَاصِمِ بْنِ أَبِي النَّجُود، عَنْ أَبِي وَائِلٍ، عَنْ مُعَاذِ بْنِ جَبَلٍ قَالَ: كُنْتُ مَعَ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ فِي سَفَرٍ، فَأَصْبَحْتُ يَوْمًا قَرِيبًا مِنْهُ، وَنَحْنُ نَسِيرُ، فَقُلْتُ: يَا نَبِيَّ اللَّهِ، أَخْبِرْنِي بِعَمَلٍ يُدْخِلُنِي الْجَنَّةَ وَيُبَاعِدُنِي مِنَ النَّارِ. قَالَ: "لَقَدْ سَأَلْتَ عَنْ عَظِيمٍ، وَإِنَّهُ لَيَسِيرٌ عَلَى مَنْ يَسَّرَهُ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ، تَعْبُدُ الله ولا تشرك به شيئا، وَتُقِيمُ الصَّلَاةَ، وَتُؤْتِي الزَّكَاةَ، وَتَصُومُ رَمَضَانَ، وَتَحُجُّ الْبَيْتَ". ثُمَّ قَالَ: "أَلَا أَدُلَّكَ عَلَى أَبْوَابِ الْخَيْرِ؟ الصَّوْمُ جُنَّةٌ، وَالصَّدَقَةُ تُطْفِئُ الْخَطِيئَةَ، وَصَلَاةُ الرَّجُلِ فِي جَوْفِ اللَّيْلِ". ثُمَّ قَرَأَ: ﴿تَتَجَافَى جُنُوبُهُمْ عَنِ الْمَضَاجِعِ﴾ ، حَتَّى بَلَغَ ﴿يَعْمَلُون﴾ . ثُمَّ قَالَ: "أَلَا أُخْبِرُكَ بِرَأْسِ الْأَمْرِ وَعَمُودِهِ وَذُرْوَةِ سَنَامِهِ؟ " فَقُلْتُ: بَلَى، يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ. فَقَالَ: "رَأْسُ الْأَمْرِ الْإِسْلَامُ، وَعَمُودُهُ الصَّلَاةُ، وَذُرْوَةُ سَنَامه الْجِهَادُ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ". ثُمَّ قَالَ: "أَلَّا أُخْبِرُكَ بِمَلَاكِ ذَلِكَ كُلِّهِ؟ " فَقُلْتُ: بَلَى، يَا نَبِيَّ اللَّهِ. فَأَخَذَ بِلِسَانِهِ ثُمَّ قَالَ: "كُفّ عَلَيْكَ هَذَا". فَقُلْتُ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، وَإِنَّا لَمُؤَاخَذُونَ بِمَا نَتَكَلَّمُ بِهِ. فَقَالَ: ثَكِلَتْكَ أُمُّكَ يَا مُعَاذُ، وَهَلْ يَكُب النَّاسَ فِي النَّارِ عَلَى وُجُوهِهِمْ -أَوْ قَالَ: عَلَى مَنَاخِرِهِمْ -إِلَّا حَصَائِدُ أَلْسِنَتِهِمْ".

رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ وَالنِّسَائِيُّ وَابْنُ مَاجَهْ فِي سُنَنِهِمْ، مِنْ طُرُقٍ عَنْ مَعْمَرٍ، بِهِ. وَقَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ. وَرَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ مِنْ حَدِيثِ شُعْبَةَ، عَنِ الْحَكَمِ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ عُرْوَة بْنَ النَّزَّالِ يُحَدِّثُ عَنْ مُعَاذِ بْنِ جَبَلٍ؛ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم قال لَهُ: "أَلَا أَدُلَّكَ عَلَى أَبْوَابِ الْخَيْرِ: الصَّوْمُ جُنَّةٌ، وَالصَّدَقَةُ تُكَفِّرُ الْخَطِيئَةَ، وَقِيَامُ الْعَبْدِ فِي جَوْفِ اللَّيْلِ"، وَتَلَا هَذِهِ الْآيَةَ: ﴿تَتَجَافَى جُنُوبُهُمْ عَنِ الْمَضَاجِعِ يَدْعُونَ رَبَّهُمْ خَوْفًا وَطَمَعًا وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَاهُمْ يُنْفِقُونَ﴾ .

وَرَوَاهُ أَيْضًا مِنْ حَدِيثِ الثَّوْرِيِّ، عَنْ مَنْصُورِ بْنِ الْمُعْتَمِرِ، عَنِ الْحَكَمِ، عَنْ مَيْمُونِ بْنِ أَبِي شَبِيبٍ، عَنْ مُعَاذٍ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ بِنَحْوِهِ، وَمِنْ حَدِيثِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ حَبِيبِ بْنِ أَبِي ثَابِتٍ، وَالْحَكَمِ عَنْ مَيْمُونِ بْنِ أَبِي شَبِيبٍ، عَنْ مُعَاذٍ مَرْفُوعًا بِنَحْوِهِ. وَمِنْ حَدِيثِ حَمَّادُ بْنُ سَلَمَةَ، عَنْ عَاصِمِ بْنِ أَبِي النَّجُود، عن شَهْرٍ، عَنْ مُعَاذِ بْنِ جَبَلٍ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ، فِي قَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿تَتَجَافَى جُنُوبُهُمْ عَنِ الْمَضَاجِعِ﴾

قَالَ: "قِيَامُ الْعَبْدِ مِنَ اللَّيْلِ". 

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ سَنَان الْوَاسِطِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا يَزِيدُ بْنُ هَارُونَ، حَدَّثَنَا فِطْر بْنُ خَلِيفَةَ، عَنْ حَبِيبِ بْنِ أَبِي ثَابِتٍ، وَالْحَكَمِ، وَحَكِيمِ بْنِ جُبَيْر، عَنْ مَيْمُونِ بْنِ أَبِي شَبِيبٍ، عَنْ مُعَاذٍ بْنِ جَبَلٍ قَالَ: كُنْتُ مَعَ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ فِي غَزْوَةِ تَبُوكَ فَقَالَ: "إِنْ شِئْتَ أَنْبَأَتُكَ بِأَبْوَابِ الْخَيْرِ: الصَّوْمُ جُنَّةٌ، وَالصَّدَقَةُ تُطْفِئُ الْخَطِيئَةَ، وَقِيَامُ الرَّجُلِ فِي جَوْفِ اللَّيْلِ"، ثُمَّ تَلَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: ﴿تَتَجَافَى جُنُوبُهُمْ عَنِ الْمَضَاجِعِ يَدْعُونَ رَبَّهُمْ خَوْفًا وَطَمَعًا وَمِمَّا رَزَقْنَاهُمْ يُنْفِقُونَ﴾ .

ثُمَّ قَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا سُوَيْدُ بْنُ سَعِيدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ مُسْهِر، عَنْ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ شَهْر بْنِ حَوْشَب، عَنْ أَسْمَاءَ بِنْتِ يَزِيدَ قَالَتْ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِذَا جَمَعَ اللَّهُ الْأَوَّلِينَ وَالْآخَرِينَ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ، جَاءَ مُنَادٍ فَنَادَى بِصَوْتٍ يُسمعُ الْخَلَائِقَ: سَيَعْلَمُ أَهْلُ الْجَمْعِ الْيَوْمَ مَن أَوْلَى بِالْكَرَمِ. ثُمَّ يَرْجِعُ فَيُنَادِي: لِيَقُمِ الَّذِينَ كَانَتْ ﴿تَتَجَافَى جُنُوبُهُمْ عَنِ الْمَضَاجِعِ﴾ الْآيَةَ، فَيَقُومُونَ وَهُمْ قَلِيلٌ". 

وَقَالَ الْبَزَّارُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ شَبِيب، حَدَّثَنَا الْوَلِيدُ بْنُ عَطَاءِ بْنِ الْأَغَرِّ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الْحَمِيدِ بْنُ سُلَيْمَانَ، حَدَّثَنِي مُصْعَبٌ، عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ قَالَ: قَالَ بِلَالٌ لَمَّا نَزَلَتْ هَذِهِ الْآيَةُ: ﴿تَتَجَافَى جُنُوبُهُمْ عَنِ الْمَضَاجِعِ﴾ [الْآيَةَ] ، كُنَّا نَجْلِسُ فِي الْمَجْلِسِ، وَنَاسٌ مِنْ أَصْحَابِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ يُصَلُّونَ بَعْدَ الْمَغْرِبِ إِلَى الْعَشَاءِ، فَنَزَلَتْ هَذِهِ الْآيَةُ: ﴿تَتَجَافَى جُنُوبُهُمْ عَنِ الْمَضَاجِعِ﴾ .

ثُمَّ قَالَ: لَا نَعْلَمُ رَوَى أَسْلَمُ عَنْ بِلَالٍ سِوَاهُ، وَلَيْسَ لَهُ طَرِيقٌ عَنْ بِلَالٍ غَيْرَ هَذِهِ الطَّرِيقِ .

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَلا تَعْلَمُ نَفْسٌ مَا أُخْفِيَ لَهُمْ مِنْ قُرَّةِ أَعْيُنٍ جَزَاءً بِمَا كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ﴾ أَيْ: فَلَا يَعْلَمُ أَحَدٌ عَظَمَةَ مَا أَخْفَى اللَّهُ لَهُمْ فِي الْجَنَّاتِ مِنَ النَّعِيمِ الْمُقِيمِ، وَاللَّذَّاتِ الَّتِي لَمْ يَطَّلِعُ عَلَى مِثْلِهَا أَحَدٌ، لَمَّا أَخْفَوْا أَعْمَالَهُمْ أَخْفَى اللَّهُ لَهُمْ مِنَ الثَّوَابِ، جَزَاءً وِفَاقًا؛ فَإِنَّ الْجَزَاءَ مِنْ جِنْسِ الْعَمَلِ.

قَالَ الْحَسَنُ [الْبَصْرِيُّ] : أَخْفَى قَوْمٌ عَمَلَهُمْ فَأَخْفَى اللَّهُ لَهُمْ مَا لَمْ تَرَ عَيْنٌ، وَلَمْ يَخْطُرْ عَلَى قَلْبِ بَشَرٍ. رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ.

قَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ: قَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَلا تَعْلَمُ نَفْسٌ مَا أُخْفِيَ لَهُمْ مِنْ قُرَّةِ أَعْيُنٍ﴾ الآية: حدثنا علي بن عَبْدِ اللَّهِ، حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، عَنْ أَبِي الزِّنَاد، عَنِ الْأَعْرَجِ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، عَنِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: أَعْدَدْتُ لِعِبَادِي الصَّالِحِينَ مَا لَا عَيْنٌ رَأَتْ، وَلَا أُذُنٌ سَمِعَتْ، وَلَا خَطَرَ عَلَى قَلْبِ بَشَرٍ". قَالَ أبو هريرة: فاقرؤوا إِنْ شِئْتُمْ: ﴿فَلا تَعْلَمُ نَفْسٌ مَا أُخْفِيَ لَهُمْ مِنْ قُرَّةِ أَعْيُنٍ﴾ .

قَالَ: وَحَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو الزِّنَادِ، عَنِ الْأَعْرَجِ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ: قَالَ اللَّهُ مِثْلَهُ . قِيلَ لِسُفْيَانَ: رِوَايَةً؟ قَالَ: فَأيُّ شَيْءٍ؟.

وَرَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ سُفْيَانَ بْنِ عُيَيْنَةَ، بِهِ . وَقَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا إِسْحَاقُ بْنُ نَصْرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو أُسَامَةَ، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو صَالِحٍ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ: "يَقُولُ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: أَعْدَدْتُ لِعِبَادِي الصَّالِحِينَ مَا لَا عَيْنٌ رَأَتْ، وَلَا أُذُنٌ سَمِعَتْ، وَلَا خطر عَلَى قَلْبِ بَشَرٍ، ذُخْرًا منْ بَله مَا أطلعْتم عَلَيْهِ"، ثُمَّ قَرَأَ: ﴿فَلا تَعْلَمُ نَفْسٌ مَا أُخْفِيَ لَهُمْ مِنْ قُرَّةِ أَعْيُنٍ جَزَاءً بِمَا كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ﴾ .

قَالَ أَبُو مُعَاوِيَةَ، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ أَبِي صَالِحٍ، قَرَأَ أَبُو هُرَيْرَةَ: "قُرَّات أَعْيُنٍ". انْفَرَدَ بِهِ الْبُخَارِيُّ مِنْ هَذَا الْوَجْهِ. 

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، حَدَّثَنَا مَعْمَر، عَنْ هَمَّامِ بْنِ مُنَبِّه قَالَ: هَذَا مَا حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو هُرَيْرَةَ عَنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إن الله تَعَالَى قَالَ: أَعْدَدْتُ لِعِبَادِي الصَّالِحِينَ مَا لَا عَيْنٌ رَأَتْ، وَلَا أُذُنٌ سَمِعَتْ، وَلَا خطر عَلَى قَلْبِ بَشَرٍ".

أَخْرَجَاهُ فِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ مِنْ رِوَايَةِ عَبْدِ الرَّزَّاقِ . وَرَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ فِي التَّفْسِيرِ، وَابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ عَبْدِ الرَّحِيمِ بْنُ سُلَيْمَانَ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو، عَنْ أَبِي سَلَمَةَ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، عَنِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ بِمِثْلِهِ. ثُمَّ قَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: هَذَا حَدِيثٌ حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ. 

وَقَالَ حَمَّادُ بْنُ سَلَمَةَ، عَنْ ثَابِتٍ، عَنْ أَبِي رَافِعٍ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، قَالَ حَمَّادٌ: أَحْسَبُهُ عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ قَالَ: "مَنْ يَدْخُلِ الْجَنَّةَ يَنْعَمُ لَا يَبْأَسُ، لَا تَبْلَى ثِيَابُهُ، وَلَا يَفْنَى شَبَابُهُ، فِي الْجَنَّةِ مَا لَا عَيْنٌ رَأَتْ، وَلَا أُذُنٌ سَمِعَتْ، وَلَا خطر على قلب بشر".

رَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ مِنْ حَدِيثِ حَمَّادِ بْنِ سَلَمَةَ، بِهِ. 

وَرَوَى الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا هَارُونُ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ وَهْبٍ، حَدَّثَنِي أَبُو صَخْرٍ، أَنَّ أَبَا حَازِمٍ حدَّثه قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ سَهْلِ بْنِ سَعْدٍ السَّاعِدِيِّ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، يَقُولُ: شَهِدْتُ مِنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ مجلسا وَصَفَ فِيهِ الْجَنَّةَ، حَتَّى انْتَهَى، ثُمَّ قَالَ فِي آخِرِ حَدِيثِهِ: "فِيهَا مَا لَا عَيْنٌ رَأَتْ، وَلَا أُذُنٌ سَمِعَتْ، وَلَا خَطَرَ عَلَى قَلْبِ بَشَرٍ"، ثُمَّ قَرَأَ هَذِهِ الْآيَةَ: ﴿تَتَجَافَى جُنُوبُهُمْ عَنِ الْمَضَاجِعِ [يَدْعُونَ رَبَّهُمْ خَوْفًا وَطَمَعًا] ﴾ ، إِلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿يَعْمَلُون﴾ .

وَأَخْرَجَهُ مُسْلِمٌ فِي صَحِيحِهِ عَنْ هَارُونَ بْنِ مَعْرُوفٍ، وَهَارُونَ بْنِ سَعِيدٍ، كِلَاهُمَا عَنِ ابْنِ وَهْبٍ، بِهِ. 

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنِي الْعَبَّاسُ بْنُ أَبِي طَالِبٍ، حَدَّثَنَا مُعَلَّى بْنُ أَسَدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا سَلَّامُ بْنُ أَبِي مُطِيعٍ، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ، عَنْ عُقْبَةَ بْنِ عَبْدِ الْغَافِرِ، عَنْ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ الْخُدْرِيِّ، عَنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، يَرْوِي عَنْ رَبِّهِ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، قَالَ: "أَعْدَدْتُ لِعِبَادِي الصَّالِحِينَ مَا لَا عَيْنٌ رَأَتْ، وَلَا أُذُنٌ سَمِعَتْ، وَلَا خَطَرَ عَلَى قَلْبِ بَشَرٍ". لَمْ يُخَرِّجُوهُ. 

وَقَالَ مُسْلِمٌ أَيْضًا فِي صَحِيحِهِ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ أَبِي عُمَرَ وَغَيْرُهُ، حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، حَدَّثَنَا مُطَرّف بْنُ طَريف وَعَبْدُ الْمَلِكِ بْنُ سَعِيدٍ، سَمِعَا الشَّعْبِيَّ يُخْبِرُ عَنِ الْمُغِيرَةِ بْنِ شُعْبَةَ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُهُ عَلَى الْمِنْبَرِ -يَرْفَعُهُ إِلَى النَّبِيِّ ﷺ -قَالَ: "سَأَلَ مُوسَى، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ رَبَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ: مَا أَدْنَى أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ مَنْزِلَةً؟ قَالَ: هُوَ رَجُلٌ يَجِيءُ بَعْدَمَا أُدْخِلَ أَهْلُ الْجَنَّةِ الْجَنَّةَ، فَيُقَالُ لَهُ: ادْخُلِ الْجَنَّةَ. فَيَقُولُ: أَيْ رَبِّ، كَيْفَ وَقَدْ نَزَلَ النَّاسُ مَنَازِلَهُمْ، وَأَخَذُوا أَخَذَاتِهِمْ؟ فَيُقَالُ لَهُ: أَتَرْضَى أَنْ يَكُونَ لَكَ مِثْلَ مُلك مَلِكٍ مَنْ مُلُوكِ الدُّنْيَا؟ فَيَقُولُ: رَضِيتُ رَبِّ. فَيَقُولُ: لَكَ ذَلِكَ، وَمِثْلُهُ، وَمِثْلُهُ، وَمِثْلُهُ، وَمِثْلُهُ، فَقَال فِي الْخَامِسَةِ: رَضِيتُ رَبِّ. فَيَقُولُ: هَذَا لَكَ وَعَشْرَةُ أَمْثَالِهِ وَلَكَ مَا اشْتَهَتْ نَفْسُكَ ولَذَّت عَيْنُكَ. فَيَقُولُ: رَضِيتُ رَبِّ. قَالَ: رَبِّ، فَأَعْلَاهُمْ مَنْزِلَةً؟ قَالَ: أُولَئِكَ الَّذِينَ أرَدتُ، غَرَسْتُ كَرَامَتَهُمْ بِيَدِي، وَخَتَمْتُ عَلَيْهَا، فَلَمْ تَرَ عَيْنٌ، وَلَمْ تَسْمَعْ أُذُنٌ، وَلَمْ يَخْطُرْ عَلَى قَلْبِ بَشَرٍ"، قَالَ: وَمِصْدَاقُهُ مِنْ كِتَابِ اللَّهِ: ﴿فَلا تَعْلَمُ نَفْسٌ مَا أُخْفِيَ لَهُمْ مِنْ قُرَّةِ أَعْيُنٍ جَزَاءً بِمَا كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ﴾ .

وَرَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ عَنِ ابْنِ أَبِي عُمَرَ، وَقَالَ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ، قَالَ: وَرَوَاهُ بَعْضُهُمْ عَنِ الشَّعْبِيِّ، عَنِ الْمُغِيرَةِ وَلَمْ يَرْفَعْهُ، والمرفوع أصح. قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا جَعْفَرُ بْنُ مُنِيرٍ الْمَدَائِنِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو بَدْرٍ شجاعُ بْنُ الْوَلِيدِ، حَدَّثَنَا زِيَادُ بْنُ خَيْثَمة، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ جُحَادة، عَنْ عَامِرِ بْنِ عَبْدِ الْوَاحِدِ قَالَ: بَلَغَنِي أَنَّ الرَّجُلَ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ يَمْكُثُ فِي مَكَانِهِ سَبْعِينَ سَنَةً، ثُمَّ يَلْتَفِتُ فَإِذَا هُوَ بِامْرَأَةٍ أَحْسَنَ مِمَّا كَانَ فِيهِ، فَتَقُولُ لَهُ: قَدْ أنَى لَكَ أَنْ يَكُونَ لَنَا مِنْكَ نَصِيبٌ؟ فَيَقُولُ: مَنْ أَنْتِ؟ فَتَقُولُ: أَنَا مِنَ الْمَزِيدِ. فَيَمْكُثُ مَعَهَا سَبْعِينَ سَنَةً، ثُمَّ يَلْتَفِتُ فَإِذَا هُوَ بِامْرَأَةٍ أَحْسَنَ مِمَّا كَانَ فِيهِ، فَتَقُولُ لَهُ: قَدْ أَنَى لَكَ أَنْ يَكُونَ لَنَا مِنْكَ نَصِيبٌ، فَيَقُولُ: مَنْ أَنْتِ؟ فَتَقُولُ: أَنَا الَّتِي قَالَ اللَّهُ: ﴿فَلا تَعْلَمُ نَفْسٌ مَا أُخْفِيَ لَهُمْ مِنْ قُرَّةِ أَعْيُنٍ﴾ .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ لَهِيعَة: حَدَّثَنِي عَطَاءُ بْنُ دِينَارٍ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْر قَالَ: تَدْخُلُ عَلَيْهِمُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ فِي مِقْدَارِ كَلِّ يَوْمٍ مِنْ أَيَّامِ الدُّنْيَا ثَلَاثَ مَرَّاتٍ، مَعَهُمُ التُّحَفُ مِنَ اللَّهِ مِنْ جَنَّاتِ عَدْنٍ مَا لَيْسَ فِي جَنَّاتِهِمْ، وَذَلِكَ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَلا تَعْلَمُ نَفْسٌ مَا أُخْفِيَ لَهُمْ مِنْ قُرَّةِ أَعْيُنٍ﴾ ، ويُخْبَرون أَنَّ اللَّهَ عَنْهُمْ رَاضٍ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا سَهْلُ بْنُ مُوسَى الرَّازِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا الْوَلِيدُ بْنُ مُسْلِمٍ، عَنْ صَفْوَانَ بْنِ عَمْرٍو، عَنْ أَبِي الْيَمَانِ الْهَوْزَنِيِّ -أَوْ غَيْرِهِ -قَالَ: الْجَنَّةُ مِائَةُ دَرَجَةٍ، أوَّلها دَرَجَةُ فِضَّةٍ وَأَرْضُهَا فِضَّةٌ، وَمَسَاكِنُهَا فِضَّةٌ، [وَآنِيَتُهَا فِضَّةٌ] وَتُرَابُهَا الْمِسْكُ. وَالثَّانِيَةُ ذَهَبٌ، وَأَرْضُهَا ذَهَبٌ، وَمَسَاكِنُهَا ذَهَبٌ، وَآنِيَتُهَا ذَهَبٌ، وَتُرَابُهَا الْمِسْكُ. وَالثَّالِثَةُ لُؤْلُؤٌ، وَأَرْضُهَا لُؤْلُؤٌ، وَمَسَاكِنُهَا اللُّؤْلُؤُ، وَآنِيَتُهَا اللُّؤْلُؤُ، وَتُرَابُهَا الْمِسْكُ. وَسَبْعٌ وَتِسْعُونَ بَعْدَ ذَلِكَ، مَا لَا عَيْنٌ رَأَتْ، وَلَا أُذُنٌ سَمِعَتْ، وَلَا خَطَرَ عَلَى قَلْبِ بَشَرٍ. ثُمَّ تَلَا هَذِهِ الْآيَةَ: ﴿فَلا تَعْلَمُ نَفْسٌ مَا أُخْفِيَ لَهُمْ مِنْ قُرَّةِ أَعْيُنٍ جَزَاءً بِمَا كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ﴾ 

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنِي يَعْقُوبُ بْنُ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، حَدَّثَنَا مُعْتَمِرُ بْنُ سُلَيْمَانَ، عَنِ الْحَكَمِ بْنِ أَبَانَ، عن الغِطْرِيف، عَنْ جَابِرِ بْنِ زَيْدٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ، عَنِ الرُّوحِ الْأَمِينِ قَالَ: "يُؤْتَى بِحَسَنَاتِ الْعَبْدِ وَسَيِّئَاتِهِ، يُنْقَصُ بَعْضُهَا مِنْ بَعْضٍ، فَإِنْ بَقِيَتْ حَسَنَةٌ [وَاحِدَةٌ] وَسَّعَ اللَّهُ لَهُ فِي الْجَنَّةِ"، قَالَ: فَدَخَلْتُ عَلَى "يَزْدَادَ" فَحَدَّث بِمِثْلِ هَذَا الْحَدِيثِ، قَالَ: فَقُلْتُ: فَأَيْنَ ذَهَبَتِ الْحَسَنَةُ؟ قَالَ: ﴿أُولَئِكَ الَّذِينَ نَتَقَبَّلُ عَنْهُمْ أَحْسَنَ مَا عَمِلُوا وَنَتَجاوَزُ عَنْ سَيِّئَاتِهِمْ فِي أَصْحَابِ الْجَنَّةِ وَعْدَ الصِّدْقِ الَّذِي كَانُوا يُوعَدُونَ﴾ [الْأَحْقَافِ: ١٦] . قُلْتُ: قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَلا تَعْلَمُ نَفْسٌ مَا أُخْفِيَ لَهُمْ مِنْ قُرَّةِ أَعْيُنٍ﴾ ، قَالَ: الْعَبْدُ يَعْمَلُ سِرًّا أسرَّه إِلَى اللَّهِ، لَمْ يُعلم بِهِ النَّاسُ، فأسَرَّ اللَّهُ لَهُ يوم القيامة قُرَّة أعين.

## 32:18-22 — verses 18-22 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty informs us of His justice [and His generosity], that He will not treat as equal, in His judgment on the Day of Resurrection, one who believed in His signs and followed His messengers, with one who was a sinner, that is, one who went outside obedience to his Lord and denied His messengers sent to him. As the Almighty said: {Or do those who have committed evil deeds think that We will make them like those who have believed and done righteous deeds, equal in their life and their death? Evil is what they judge} [al-Jathiyah: 21], and the Almighty said: {Or shall We make those who believe and do righteous deeds like those who cause corruption in the earth? Or shall We make the righteous like the wicked?} [Sad: 28], and the Almighty said: {Not equal are the companions of the Fire and the companions of Paradise. The companions of Paradise, they are the successful} [al-Hashr: 20]. This is why the Almighty says here: {Is he who was a believer like he who was a sinner? They are not equal}, that is, in the sight of Allah on the Day of Resurrection.

Ata' ibn Yasar, al-Suddi, and others mentioned that this was revealed concerning Ali ibn Abi Talib and Uqbah ibn Abi Mu'ayt. This is why He detailed their respective rulings, saying: {As for those who believed and did righteous deeds}, that is, whose hearts affirmed the truth of Allah's signs and who acted according to what they required, and these are the righteous deeds, {for them are Gardens of Refuge}, that is, containing dwellings, homes, and lofty chambers, {as a hospitality}, that is, as a welcome and an honor, {for what they used to do. And as for those who were sinners}, that is, who went outside obedience, {their refuge is the Fire. Every time they wish to come out of it, they will be returned into it}, like His saying: {Every time they wish to come out of it from anguish, they are returned into it} [al-Hajj: 22].

Al-Fudayl ibn Iyad said: By Allah, hands will indeed be bound, feet will indeed be shackled, the flame will lift them up, and the angels will strike them back down.

{And it will be said to them, "Taste the punishment of the Fire which you used to deny"}, that is, this will be said to them as a rebuke and a reproach.

Concerning His saying: {And We will surely let them taste the nearer punishment before the greater punishment, that perhaps they may return}, Ibn Abbas said: by the nearer punishment He means the calamities of this world, its sicknesses and afflictions, and whatever befalls its people by which Allah tests His servants so that they may turn back to Him in repentance. Similar has been reported from Ubayy ibn Ka'b, Abu al-Aliyah, al-Hasan, Ibrahim al-Nakha'i, al-Dahhak, Alqamah, Atiyyah, Mujahid, Qatadah, Abd al-Karim al-Jazari, and Khusayf.

Ibn Abbas said, in another report from him: he means by it the establishment of the prescribed legal punishments (hudud) against them.

Al-Bara' ibn Azib, Mujahid, and Abu Ubaydah said: he means by it the punishment of the grave.

Al-Nasa'i said: [chain] from Abu al-Ahwas and Abu Ubaydah, from Abdullah, concerning {And We will surely let them taste the nearer punishment before the greater punishment}, he said: years of drought that afflicted them.

Abdullah ibn al-Imam Ahmad said: [chain] from Ibn Abi Layla, from Ubayy ibn Ka'b, concerning this verse: {And We will surely let them taste the nearer punishment before the greater punishment}, he said: the calamities and the smoke have already passed, as have the seizing blow and the persistent affliction.

Muslim narrated it from the hadith of Shu'bah, with the same chain, as a mawquf (halted report) of similar wording. Al-Bukhari has something similar from Ibn Mas'ud.

Abdullah ibn Mas'ud also said, in another report from him: the nearer punishment is what befell them of killing and captivity on the day of Badr. Malik said likewise, from Zayd ibn Aslam.

Al-Suddi and others said: there remained no house in Makkah that grief did not enter, over someone of theirs killed or taken captive. Some of them were struck by loss or fined, and some had both combined upon them.

His saying: {And who is more unjust than one who is reminded of the signs of his Lord and then turns away from them}, that is, no one is more unjust than one whom Allah has reminded of His signs, and made them clear and plain to him, and who then afterward abandoned them, denied them, turned away from them, and pretended to forget them, as if he did not know them.

Qatadah, may Allah have mercy on him, said: beware of turning away from the remembrance of Allah, for whoever turns away from His remembrance has been deluded with the greatest delusion, has been reduced to the most severe want, and has committed one of the gravest of sins.

This is why the Almighty says, as a threat to whoever does that: {Indeed, We are Avengers against the criminals}, that is, I will take vengeance against whoever does that, the harshest vengeance.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] from Junadah ibn Abi Umayyah, from Mu'adh ibn Jabal, who said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "Three things, whoever does them has committed a crime: whoever raises a banner without right, or is undutiful to his two parents, or walks alongside a wrongdoer to support him, has committed a crime. Allah the Almighty says: {Indeed, We are Avengers against the criminals}." Ibn Abi Hatim also narrated it, from the hadith of Isma'il ibn Ayyash, with the same chain, and this is a hadith that is extremely gharib (singular, rare in its transmission).

## 32:23-25 — verses 23-25 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, informing about His servant and messenger Moses, peace be upon him, that He gave him the Book, which is the Torah.

Concerning His saying, {So be not in doubt of meeting him}: Qatadah said, this refers to the Night Journey. Then it is related from Abu al-Aliyah al-Riyahi, who said: The cousin of your Prophet, meaning Ibn Abbas, told me that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "On the night I was taken on the Night Journey, I was shown Moses son of Imran, a tawny, tall man with curly hair, as if he were one of the men of Shanu'ah. And I saw Jesus, a man of medium build, with a reddish and white complexion and smooth hair. And I saw Malik, the keeper of the Fire, and the Dajjal, among the signs that Allah showed him." {So be not in doubt of meeting him}, meaning that he had indeed seen Moses and met Moses on the night of the Night Journey.

Al-Tabarani said: [chain] Muhammad ibn Uthman ibn Abi Shaybah told us, al-Hasan ibn Ali al-Hulwani told us, Rawh ibn Ubadah told us, Sa'id ibn Abi Arubah told us, from Qatadah, from Abu al-Aliyah, from Ibn Abbas, from the Prophet ﷺ, concerning His saying: {And We made him a guide for the Children of Israel}, he said: Moses was made a guide for the Children of Israel. And concerning His saying: {So be not in doubt of meeting him}, he said: of Moses's meeting with his Lord, Mighty and Majestic.

Concerning His saying, {And We made him}, that is, the Book which We gave him, {a guide for the Children of Israel}, as the Exalted said in Surat al-Isra: {And We gave Moses the Book and made it a guide for the Children of Israel, saying: Take not besides Me any guardian} [al-Isra: 2].

Concerning His saying: {And We made from among them leaders who guided by Our command when they were patient and were certain of Our signs}, that is, because they were patient in adhering to Allah's commands, abandoning what He forbade and warned against, believing in His messengers, and following them in what they brought, there arose among them leaders who guided to the truth by Allah's command, called to good, enjoined what is right, and forbade what is wrong. Then, when they altered, distorted, and reinterpreted the scripture, that station was stripped from them, and their hearts became hardened, distorting words from their proper places. So there remained neither righteous deed nor sound belief among them. This is why He said: {And We made from among them leaders who guided by Our command when they were patient}. Qatadah and Sufyan said: when they were patient in restraining themselves from the world. Al-Hasan ibn Salih said the same.

Sufyan said: This is how these people were, and it is not fitting for a man to be a leader whom others follow until he has guarded himself against worldly attachment.

Waki' said: Sufyan said: Religion cannot do without knowledge, just as the body cannot do without bread.

Ibn Bint al-Shafi'i said: My father read to my uncle, or my uncle to my father, that Sufyan was asked about the saying of Ali, may Allah be pleased with him: "Patience is to faith as the head is to the body." Have you not heard His saying: {And We made from among them leaders who guided by Our command when they were patient}? He said: When they took hold of the head of the matter, they became heads (leaders). Some of the scholars said: Through patience and certainty, leadership in religion is attained.

This is why the Exalted said: {And We certainly gave the Children of Israel the Scripture, judgment, and prophethood, and provided them with good things, and favored them over the worlds. And We gave them clear proofs of the matter, but they differed not except after knowledge had come to them} [al-Jathiyah: 16, 17], just as He said here: {Indeed, your Lord will judge between them on the Day of Resurrection concerning that over which they used to differ}, that is, in matters of belief and deeds.

## 32:26-27 — verses 26-27 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: Has it not been shown to those who deny the messengers what Allah destroyed before them of the past nations, because of their denying the messengers and their opposition to them in what they brought them of the straight paths, so that not a remnant, nor a trace, nor a mark remained of them? {Do you perceive any one of them, or hear from them a whisper?} [Maryam: 98]. This is why He said: {They walk among their dwellings}, meaning: these deniers walk among the dwellings of those former deniers and see no one there of those who used to inhabit and cultivate them. They have gone from there, {as though they had never lived there} [al-A'raf: 92], as He said: {So those were their houses, fallen into ruin because of the wrong they did} [al-Naml: 52], and He said: {How many a city have We destroyed while it was doing wrong, so it lies in ruins, and how many an abandoned well and lofty palace. Have they not traveled through the land so that they might have hearts by which to understand or ears by which to hear? Indeed, it is not the eyes that are blind, but blind are the hearts which are in the breasts} [al-Hajj: 45-46]. This is why He said here: {Indeed in that are signs}, meaning: in the passing away of those people and their destruction and what befell them because of their denial of the messengers, and in the deliverance of those who believed in them, there are signs, lessons, admonitions, and manifest proofs.

{Will they not then listen?} meaning: to the accounts of those who came before, how their affair turned out.

His saying: {Have they not seen that We drive the water to the barren land?} Allah the Almighty explains His kindness to His creation and His favor upon them in sending down water, either from the sky or by surface flow, that is, what the rivers carry and what descends from the mountains to the lands that need it at their appointed times. This is why He said: {to the barren land}, which is [the land] in which there is no vegetation, as the Almighty said: {And indeed We will make what is upon it a barren waste} [al-Kahf: 8], meaning: dry, growing nothing. And what is meant by His saying: {to the barren land} is not the land of Egypt alone, rather it is part of what is intended. Even though many exegetes have used it as an example, it is not meant exclusively, but it is certainly included within this verse, for it is in itself a soft, dense land that needs so much water that if rain were to fall upon it, its buildings would collapse. So Allah drives to it the Nile with what it carries of the surplus resulting from the rains of the lands of Abyssinia, in which there is red silt, and it covers the land of Egypt, which is a salty, sandy land in need of that water, and also of that silt, so that crops may grow in it. Thus they profit every year from fresh water rained down in lands other than their own, and fresh silt from a land other than their own. Glory be to the Wise, the Generous, the Bestower, praised from the very beginning.

Ibn Lahi'ah said, from Qays ibn Hajjaj, from someone who related to him, that he said: When Egypt was conquered, its people came to Amr ibn al-'As, who was governor there, when the month of Bu'unah, one of the foreign months, arrived, and they said: O Commander, our Nile has a custom without which it will not flow. He said: What is that? They said: When twelve nights of this month have passed, we take a virgin girl from between her parents, satisfy her parents, adorn her with the finest of jewelry and garments, and then cast her into this Nile. Amr said to them: This cannot be in Islam, for Islam destroys what came before it. So they waited through Bu'unah and the Nile did not flow, until they were on the verge of leaving the land. Amr wrote to Umar ibn al-Khattab about this, and Umar wrote back to him: You have done rightly in what you did. I have sent you a note inside this letter of mine, so cast it into the Nile. When his letter arrived, Amr took the note and opened it, and in it was written: From the servant of Allah, Umar, Commander of the Believers, to the Nile of the people of Egypt. To proceed: If you flow of your own accord, then do not flow, but if it is Allah, the One, the Overpowering, who makes you flow, then we ask Allah to make you flow. He said: So he cast the note into the Nile, and in the morning of Saturday, Allah had made the Nile rise sixteen cubits in a single night, and Allah cut off that custom from the people of Egypt from that day onward. This was narrated by the hafiz Abu al-Qasim al-Lalika'i al-Tabari in his book "al-Sunnah."

This is why the Almighty said: {Have they not seen that We drive the water to the barren land, and We bring forth thereby crops from which their livestock and they themselves eat? Will they not then see?}, as the Almighty said: {So let man look at his food: how We poured down the rain in abundance, then split the earth in fissures, and caused to grow therein grain, and grapes and fodder plants, and olives and palm trees, and gardens dense with foliage, and fruit and pasture, [as provision for you and your livestock]} [Abasa: 24-32]. This is why He said here: {Will they not then see?} Ibn Abi Najih said, from a man, from Ibn Abbas, regarding His saying: {to the barren land}: it is that which receives no rain except a rain that avails it nothing, except what comes to it by way of floods.

And from Ibn Abbas and Mujahid: it is a land in Yemen.

Al-Hasan, may Allah have mercy on him, said: they are villages between Yemen and Syria.

Ikrimah, al-Dahhak, Qatadah, al-Suddi, and Ibn Zayd said: the barren land is that in which there is no vegetation, and it is dust colored.

I say: this is like His saying: {And a sign for them is the dead land which We give life, and We bring forth from it grain of which they eat. And We placed therein gardens of palm trees and grapevines, and We caused springs to gush forth in it, that they might eat of its fruit, though their hands did not make it. Will they not then be grateful?} [Ya-Sin: 33-35].

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿أَوَلَمْ يَرَوْا أَنَّا نَسُوقُ الْمَاءَ إِلَى الأرْضِ الْجُرُزِ﴾ : يُبَيِّنُ تَعَالَى لُطْفَهُ بِخَلْقِهِ، وَإِحْسَانَهُ إِلَيْهِمْ فِي إِرْسَالِهِ الْمَاءَ إِمَّا مِنَ السَّمَاءِ أَوْ مِنَ السَّيْحِ، وَهُوَ: مَا تَحْمِلُهُ الْأَنْهَارُ وَيَنْحَدِرُ مِنَ الْجِبَالِ إِلَى الْأَرَاضِي الْمُحْتَاجَةِ إِلَيْهِ فِي أَوْقَاتِهِ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿إِلَى الأرْضِ الْجُرُزِ﴾ ، وَهِيَ [الْأَرْضُ] الَّتِي لَا نَبَاتَ فِيهَا، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَإِنَّا لَجَاعِلُونَ مَا عَلَيْهَا صَعِيدًا جُرُزًا﴾ [الْكَهْفِ: ٨] ، أي: يَبَسًا لا تنبت شيئًا وَلَيْسَ الْمُرَادُ مِنْ قَوْلِهِ: ﴿إِلَى الأرْضِ الْجُرُزِ﴾ أَرْضَ مِصْرَ فَقَطْ، بَلْ هِيَ بَعْضُ الْمَقْصُودِ، وَإِنْ مَثَّلَ بِهَا كَثِيرٌ مِنَ الْمُفَسِّرِينَ فَلَيْسَتْ [هِيَ] الْمَقْصُودَةُ وَحْدَهَا، وَلَكِنَّهَا مُرَادَّةٌ قَطْعًا مِنْ هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ، فَإِنَّهَا فِي نَفْسِهَا أَرْضٌ رَخْوَةٌ غَلِيظَةٌ تَحْتَاجُ مِنَ الْمَاءِ مَا لَوْ نَزَّلَ عَلَيْهَا مَطَرًا لَتَهَدَّمَتْ أَبْنِيَتُهَا، فَيَسُوقُ اللَّهُ إِلَيْهَا النَّيْلَ بِمَا يَتَحَمَّلُهُ مِنَ الزِّيَادَةِ الْحَاصِلَةِ مِنْ أَمْطَارِ بِلَادِ الْحَبَشَةِ، وَفِيهِ طِينٌ أَحْمَرُ، فَيَغْشَى أَرْضَ مِصْرَ، وَهِيَ أَرْضٌ سَبَخَةٌ مُرْمِلَةٌ مُحْتَاجَةٌ إِلَى ذَلِكَ الْمَاءِ، وَذَلِكَ الطِّينِ أَيْضًا لينبُتَ الزَّرْعُ فِيهِ، فَيَسْتَغِلُّونَ كُلَّ سَنَةٍ عَلَى مَاءٍ جَدِيدٍ مَمْطُورٍ فِي غَيْرِ بِلَادِهِمْ، وَطِينٍ جَدِيدٍ مِنْ غَيْرِ أَرْضِهِمْ، فَسُبْحَانَ الْحَكِيمِ الْكَرِيمِ الْمَنَّانِ الْمَحْمُودِ ابْتِدَاءً.

قَالَ ابْنُ لَهِيعَة، عَنْ قَيْسِ بْنِ حَجَّاجٍ، عَمَّنْ حَدَّثَهُ قَالَ: لَمَّا فُتِحَت مِصْرُ، أَتَى أَهْلُهَا عَمْرَو بْنَ الْعَاصِ -[وَكَانَ أميرًا بها] -حين دخل بؤونة مِنْ أَشْهُرِ الْعَجَمِ، فَقَالُوا: أَيُّهَا الْأَمِيرُ، إِنَّ لِنِيلِنَا سُنَّة لَا يَجْرِي إِلَّا بِهَا. قَالَ: وَمَا ذَاكَ؟ قَالُوا: إِذَا كَانَتْ ثِنْتَا عَشْرَةَ لَيْلَةً خَلَتْ مِنْ هَذَا الشَّهْرِ عَمَدنا إِلَى جَارِيَةٍ بِكْر بَيْنَ أَبَوَيْهَا، فَأَرْضَيْنَا أَبَوَيْهَا، وَجَعَلْنَا عَلَيْهَا مِنَ الْحُلِيِّ وَالثِّيَابِ أَفْضَلَ مَا يَكُونُ، ثُمَّ أَلْقَيْنَاهَا فِي هَذَا النِّيلِ. فَقَالَ لَهُمْ عَمْرٌو: إِنَّ هَذَا لَا يَكُونُ فِي الْإِسْلَامِ، إِنَّ الْإِسْلَامَ يَهْدِمُ مَا كَانَ قَبْلَهُ. فَأَقَامُوا بؤونة وَالنِّيلُ لَا يَجْرِي، حَتَّى هَمُّوا بِالْجَلَاءِ، فَكَتَبَ عَمْرٌو إِلَى عُمَرَ بْنِ الْخَطَّابِ بِذَلِكَ، فَكَتَبَ إِلَيْهِ: إِنَّكَ قَدْ أَصَبْتَ بِالَّذِي فَعَلْتَ، وَقَدْ بَعَثْتُ إِلَيْكَ بِبِطَاقَةٍ دَاخِلَ كِتَابِي هَذَا، فَأَلْقِهَا فِي النِّيلِ. فَلَمَّا قَدِمَ كِتَابُهُ أَخَذَ عَمْرٌو الْبِطَاقَةَ فَفَتَحَهَا فَإِذَا فِيهَا: مِنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ عُمَرَ أَمِيرِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ إِلَى نِيلِ أَهْلِ مِصْرَ، أَمَّا بَعْدُ ... فَإِنَّكَ إِنْ كُنْتَ إِنَّمَا تَجْرِي مِنْ قِبَلِكَ فَلَا تَجْرِ، وَإِنْ كَانَ اللَّهُ الْوَاحِدُ الْقَهَّارُ هُوَ الَّذِي يُجْرِيكَ فَنَسْأَلُ اللَّهَ أَنْ يُجْرِيَكَ. قَالَ: فَأَلْقَى الْبِطَاقَةَ فِي النِّيلِ، وَأَصْبَحُوا يَوْمَ السَّبْتِ وَقَدْ أَجْرَى اللَّهُ النِّيلَ سِتَّةَ عَشَرَ ذِرَاعًا فِي لَيْلَةٍ وَاحِدَةٍ، وَقِطَعَ اللَّهُ تِلْكَ السُّنَّة عَنْ أَهْلِ مِصْرَ إِلَى الْيَوْمِ. رَوَاهُ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو الْقَاسِمِ اللَّالِكَائِيُّ الطَّبَرَيُّ فِي كِتَابِ "السُّنَةِ" لَهُ. 

وَلِهَذَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿أَوَلَمْ يَرَوْا أَنَّا نَسُوقُ الْمَاءَ إِلَى الأرْضِ الْجُرُزِ فَنُخْرِجُ بِهِ زَرْعًا تَأْكُلُ مِنْهُ أَنْعَامُهُمْ وَأَنْفُسُهُمْ أَفَلا يُبْصِرُونَ﴾ ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَلْيَنْظُرِ الإنْسَانُ إِلَى طَعَامِهِ. أَنَّا صَبَبْنَا الْمَاءَ صَبًّا. ثُمَّ شَقَقْنَا الأرْضَ شَقًّا. فَأَنْبَتْنَا فِيهَا حَبًّا. وَعِنَبًا وَقَضْبًا. وَزَيْتُونًا وَنَخْلا. وَحَدَائِقَ غُلْبًا. وَفَاكِهَةً وَأَبًّا. [مَتَاعًا لَكُمْ وَلأنْعَامِكُمْ] ﴾ [عَبَسَ: ٢٤-٣٢] ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ هَاهُنَا: ﴿أَفَلا يُبْصِرُونَ﴾ . وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي نَجِيح، عَنْ رَجُلٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿إِلَى الأرْضِ الْجُرُزِ﴾ قَالَ: هِيَ الَّتِي لَا تُمطر إِلَّا مَطَرًا لَا يُغْنِي عَنْهَا شَيْئًا، إِلَّا مَا يَأْتِيهَا مِنَ السُّيُولِ.

وَعَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٍ: هِيَ أَرْضٌ بِالْيَمَنِ.

وَقَالَ الْحَسَنُ، رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ: هِيَ قُرَى فِيمَا بَيْنَ الْيَمَنِ وَالشَّامِ.

وَقَالَ عِكْرِمة، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَقَتَادَةُ، والسُّدِّيّ، وَابْنُ زَيْدٍ: الْأَرْضُ الْجُرُزُ: الَّتِي لَا نَبَاتَ فِيهَا وَهِيَ مُغْبَرَّةٌ.

قُلْتُ: وَهَذَا كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَآيَةٌ لَهُمُ الأرْضُ الْمَيْتَةُ أَحْيَيْنَاهَا وَأَخْرَجْنَا مِنْهَا حَبًّا فَمِنْهُ يَأْكُلُونَ. وَجَعَلْنَا فِيهَا جَنَّاتٍ مِنْ نَخِيلٍ وَأَعْنَابٍ وَفَجَّرْنَا فِيهَا مِنَ الْعُيُونِ. لِيَأْكُلُوا مِنْ ثَمَرِهِ وَمَا عَمِلَتْهُ أَيْدِيهِمْ أَفَلا يَشْكُرُونَ﴾ [يس: ٣٣-٣٥] .

## 32:28-30 — verses 28-30 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, informing of the disbelievers' impatience for God's might to befall them and for His wrath and vengeance to descend upon them, out of denial that it could happen, disbelief, and stubbornness: {And they say, when will this fath (opening/victory/judgment) be?} That is: when will you be granted victory over us, O Muhammad, as you claim that there will come a time when you will prevail over us and be avenged upon us? So when will this be? We see nothing of you and your companions except that you are hiding, fearful, and abased!

God Almighty said: {Say: on the Day of the Fath} that is, when God's might, displeasure, and wrath descend upon you in this world and the next, {those who disbelieved will not benefit from their faith, nor will they be granted respite}, just as the Almighty said: {But when their messengers came to them with clear proofs, they exulted in what they had of knowledge, and what they used to mock overtook them. Then when they saw Our might, they said: we believe in God alone and we disbelieve in what we used to associate with Him. But their faith did not benefit them when they saw Our might, this being the way of God which has already passed among His servants, and there the disbelievers were lost} [Ghafir: 83-85]. Whoever claims that what is meant by this "fath" is the conquest of Mecca has strayed far from the truth and has erred grievously, for on the day of the conquest of Mecca the Messenger of God ﷺ accepted the Islam of the tulaqa' (those freed after the conquest), who numbered nearly two thousand. Had the conquest of Mecca been intended, he would not have accepted their Islam, on account of His saying: {Say: on the Day of the Fath, those who disbelieved will not benefit from their faith, nor will they be granted respite}. Rather, what is meant is the fath that is the judgment and the decisive separation, as in His saying: {So render judgment (fath) between me and them, and save me and those believers who are with me} [al-Shu'ara: 118], and as in His saying: {Say: our Lord will bring us together, then He will judge (yaftah) between us in truth, and He is the Judge (al-Fattah), the All-Knowing} [Saba: 26]. The Almighty also said: {And they sought judgment (istaftahu), and every obstinate tyrant was disappointed} [Ibrahim: 15], and He said: {And before that they used to seek victory/judgment (yastaftihuna) against those who disbelieved} [al-Baqarah: 89], and He said: {If you seek judgment (tastaftihu), the judgment (fath) has already come to you} [al-Anfal: 19].

Then He said: {So turn away from them and wait; indeed, they are waiting} that is: turn away from these polytheists and convey what has been revealed to you from your Lord, as in His saying: {Follow what has been revealed to you from your Lord; there is no god but He; and turn away from the polytheists} [al-An'am: 106]. And wait, for God will surely fulfill for you what He has promised you, and He will surely grant you victory over whoever opposes you. Indeed, He does not break His promise.

As for His saying, {indeed, they are waiting}, it means: you are waiting, and they are waiting, and they anticipate misfortunes befalling you all, as in {Or do they say, a poet, we await for him the calamities of time} [al-Tur: 30]. You will see the outcome of your patience with them and in delivering God's message, in the form of His support and backing for you. And they will find, after what they have been waiting for concerning you and your companions, the grievous punishment of God descending upon them and His chastisement befalling them. God is sufficient for us, and He is the best Guardian. [And God knows best.]

[End of the tafsir of Surat Alif Lam Mim al-Sajdah]

يَقُولُ تَعَالَى مُخْبِرًا عَنِ اسْتِعْجَالِ الْكَفَّارِ وقوعَ بَأْسِ اللَّهِ بِهِمْ، وَحُلُولِ غَضَبِهِ وَنِقْمَتِهِ عَلَيْهِمْ، اسْتِبْعَادًا وَتَكْذِيبًا وَعِنَادًا: ﴿وَيَقُولُونَ مَتَى هَذَا الْفَتْحُ﴾ ؟ أَيْ: مَتَى تُنْصَرُ عَلَيْنَا يَا مُحَمَّدُ؟ كَمَا تَزْعُمُ أَنَّ لَكَ وَقْتًا تُدَال عَلَيْنَا، ويُنْتَقم لَكَ مِنَّا، فَمَتَى يَكُونُ هَذَا؟ مَا نَرَاكَ أَنْتَ وَأَصْحَابَكَ إِلَّا مُخْتَفِينَ خَائِفِينَ ذَلِيلِينَ!

قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿قُلْ يَوْمَ الْفَتْحِ﴾ أَيْ: إِذَا حَلَّ بِكُمْ بَأْسُ اللَّهِ وسَخَطه وَغَضَبُهُ فِي الدُّنْيَا وَفِي الْأُخْرَى، ﴿لَا يَنْفَعُ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا إِيمَانُهُمْ وَلا هُمْ يُنْظَرُونَ﴾ ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَلَمَّا جَاءَتْهُمْ رُسُلُهُمْ بِالْبَيِّنَاتِ فَرِحُوا بِمَا عِنْدَهُمْ مِنَ الْعِلْمِ وَحَاقَ بِهِمْ مَا كَانُوا بِهِ يَسْتَهْزِئُونَ. فَلَمَّا رَأَوْا بَأْسَنَا قَالُوا آمَنَّا بِاللَّهِ وَحْدَهُ وَكَفَرْنَا بِمَا كُنَّا بِهِ مُشْرِكِينَ. فَلَمْ يَكُ يَنْفَعُهُمْ إِيمَانُهُمْ لَمَّا رَأَوْا بَأْسَنَا سُنَّةَ اللَّهِ الَّتِي قَدْ خَلَتْ فِي عِبَادِهِ وَخَسِرَ هُنَالِكَ الْكَافِرُونَ﴾ [غَافِرٍ: ٨٣-٨٥] ، ومَنْ زَعَمَ أَنَّ الْمُرَادَ مِنْ هَذَا الْفَتْحِ فتحُ مَكَّةَ فَقَدْ أَبْعَدَ النَّجْعة، وَأَخْطَأَ فَأَفْحَشَ، فَإِنَّ يَوْمَ الْفَتْحِ قَدْ قَبِل رسولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ إِسْلَامَ الطُّلَقَاءِ، وَقَدْ كَانُوا قَرِيبًا مِنْ أَلْفَيْنِ، وَلَوْ كَانَ الْمُرَادُ فَتْحَ مَكَّةَ لَمَا قَبِلَ إِسْلَامَهُمْ؛ لِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿قُلْ يَوْمَ الْفَتْحِ لَا يَنْفَعُ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا إِيمَانُهُمْ وَلا هُمْ يُنْظَرُونَ﴾ ، وَإِنَّمَا الْمُرَادُ الْفَتْحُ الَّذِي هُوَ الْقَضَاءُ وَالْفَصْلُ، كَقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَافْتَحْ بَيْنِي وَبَيْنَهُمْ فَتْحًا وَنَجِّنِي وَمَنْ مَعِيَ مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ﴾ [الشُّعَرَاءِ: ١١٨] ، وَكَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿قُلْ يَجْمَعُ بَيْنَنَا رَبُّنَا ثُمَّ يَفْتَحُ بَيْنَنَا بِالْحَقِّ وَهُوَ الْفَتَّاحُ الْعَلِيمُ﴾ [سَبَأٍ: ٢٦] ، وَقَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَاسْتَفْتَحُوا وَخَابَ كُلُّ جَبَّارٍ عَنِيدٍ﴾ [إِبْرَاهِيمَ: ١٥] ، وَقَالَ: ﴿وَكَانُوا مِنْ قَبْلُ يَسْتَفْتِحُونَ عَلَى الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا﴾ [الْبَقَرَةِ: ٨٩] ، وَقَالَ: ﴿إِنْ تَسْتَفْتِحُوا فَقَدْ جَاءَكُمُ الْفَتْحُ﴾ [الْأَنْفَالِ: ١٩] .

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿فَأَعْرِضْ عَنْهُمْ وَانْتَظِرْ إِنَّهُمْ مُنْتَظِرُونَ﴾ أَيْ: أَعْرِضْ عَنْ هَؤُلَاءِ الْمُشْرِكِينَ وَبَلِّغْ مَا أُنْزِلَ إِلَيْكَ مِنْ رَبِّكَ، كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿اتَّبِعْ مَا أُوحِيَ إِلَيْكَ مِنْ رَبِّكَ لَا إِلَهَ إِلا هُوَ وَأَعْرِضْ عَنِ الْمُشْرِكِينَ﴾ [الْأَنْعَامِ: ١٠٦] ، وَانْتَظِرْ فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ سَيُنْجِزُ لَكَ مَا وَعَدَكَ، وَسَيَنْصُرُكَ عَلَى مَنْ خَالَفَكَ، إِنَّهُ لَا يُخْلِفُ الْمِيعَادَ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنَّهُمْ مُنْتَظِرُونَ﴾ أَيْ: أَنْتَ مُنْتَظِرٌ، وَهُمْ مُنْتَظِرُونَ، وَيَتَرَبَّصُونَ بِكُمُ الدَّوَائِرَ، ﴿أَمْ يَقُولُونَ شَاعِرٌ نَتَرَبَّصُ بِهِ رَيْبَ الْمَنُونِ﴾ [الطُّورِ:٣٠] ، وَسَتَرَى أَنْتَ عَاقِبَةَ صَبْرِكَ عَلَيْهِمْ وَعَلَى أَدَاءِ رِسَالَةِ اللَّهِ، فِي نُصْرَتِكَ وَتَأْيِيدِكَ، وَسَيَجِدُونَ غِبَّ مَا يَنْتَظِرُونَهُ فِيكَ وَفِي أَصْحَابِكَ، مِنْ وَبِيلِ عِقَابِ اللَّهِ لَهُمْ، وَحُلُولِ عَذَابِهِ بِهِمْ، وَحَسْبُنَا اللَّهُ وَنِعْمَ الْوَكِيلُ، [وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ] .
