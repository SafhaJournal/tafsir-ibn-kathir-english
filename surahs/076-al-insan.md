## 76:1-3 — verses 1-3 (shared commentary)

### Translation

# Tafsir of Surat al-Insan

It is Meccan.

It has already been established in Sahih Muslim, from Ibn Abbas: that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to recite in the Fajr prayer on Friday {Alif Lam Mim, Tanzil} of al-Sajdah, and {Hal ata ala al-insan}.

Abdullah ibn Wahb said: Ibn Zayd informed us that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ recited this surah, {Hal ata ala al-insan hinun min al-dahr}, and it had been revealed to him while a black man was present with him. When he reached the description of the gardens (of Paradise), the man let out a deep sigh and his soul departed. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Longing for Paradise has taken out the soul of your companion, or he said, your brother." This is a mursal (chain missing a Companion) and gharib (singular) report.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful

The Exalted informs us concerning man that He brought him into existence after he had been nothing worth mentioning, on account of his insignificance and weakness. He says: {Has there come upon man a period of time when he was nothing mentioned?}

Then He clarifies this, saying: {Indeed, We created man from a nutfah (drop) of amshaj (mixtures)}, that is, mixed substances. Al-mashj and al-mashij mean something mixed, one part within another.

Ibn Abbas said concerning His statement, {min nutfatin amshaj}: it means the fluid of the man and the fluid of the woman when they come together and mix, and then he subsequently moves from one stage to another, from one condition to another, and from one color to another. Ikrimah, Mujahid, al-Hasan, and al-Rabi' ibn Anas said likewise: al-amshaj is the mixing of the man's fluid with the woman's fluid.

His statement, {nabtalihi}, means: We test him, as in His statement {that He may test you as to which of you is best in deed} [al-Mulk: 2]. {fa ja'alnahu sami'an basiran} means: We gave him hearing and sight, by means of which he is enabled to obey or disobey.

His statement, {Indeed, We guided him to the way}, means: We made it clear to him, explained it, and gave him insight into it, as in His statement {And as for Thamud, We guided them, but they preferred blindness over guidance} [Fussilat: 17], and as in His statement {and We guided him to the two paths} [al-Balad: 10], meaning: We made clear to him the path of good and the path of evil. This is the view of Ikrimah, Atiyyah, Ibn Zayd, and Mujahid (in the well-known report from him), and of the majority.

It has been reported from Mujahid, Abu Salih, al-Dahhak, and al-Suddi that they said concerning His statement, {Indeed, We guided him to the way}: it means his coming out from the womb. This is a gharib (unusual) view, and the correct, well-known one is the first.

His statement, {imma shakiran wa imma kafuran} is in the accusative as a circumstantial clause (hal) referring to the pronoun "hu" in His statement {We guided him to the way}. Its meaning is: he is, in that regard, either wretched or blessed, as it comes in the hadith reported by Muslim from Abu Malik al-Ash'ari, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Every person goes out in the morning, and sells himself, either destroying it or freeing it." This has already been discussed in Surat al-Rum concerning His statement, {The nature instilled by Allah in which He created mankind} [al-Rum: 30], from the narration of Jabir ibn Abdullah, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Every newborn is born upon the fitrah (natural disposition), until his tongue expresses on his behalf. When his tongue expresses on his behalf, he becomes either grateful or ungrateful."

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Abu Amir told us, Abdullah ibn Ja'far told us, from Uthman ibn Muhammad, from al-Maqburi, from Abu Hurayrah, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "There is no one who goes out but that at his door there are two banners: a banner in the hand of an angel, and a banner in the hand of a devil. If he goes out for what Allah loves, the angel follows him with his banner, and he remains under the angel's banner until he returns to his house. And if he goes out for what angers Allah, the devil follows him with his banner, and he remains under the devil's banner until he returns to his house."

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Abd al-Razzaq told us, Ma'mar told us, from Ibn Khuthaym, from Abd al-Rahman ibn Sabit, from Jabir ibn Abdullah: that the Prophet ﷺ said to Ka'b ibn Ujrah: "May Allah protect you from the rule of fools." He said: What is the rule of fools? He said: "Rulers who will come after me, who do not follow my guidance and do not follow my sunnah. Whoever affirms their lies as true and helps them in their wrongdoing, those are not from me and I am not from them, and they will not come to my Hawd (basin). And whoever does not affirm their lies as true and does not help them in their wrongdoing, those are from me and I am from them, and they will come to my Hawd. O Ka'b ibn Ujrah, fasting is a shield, and charity extinguishes sin, and prayer is an offering, or he said: a proof. O Ka'b ibn Ujrah, no flesh that has grown from unlawful earnings will enter Paradise, the Fire is more fitting for it. O Ka'b, people go out in the morning in two groups: one who buys himself and thus frees it, and one who sells himself and thus destroys it."

This was also reported from Affan, from Wuhayb, from Abdullah ibn Uthman ibn Khuthaym, with the same chain.

## 76:4-12 — verses 4-12 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Allah, exalted is He, informs of what He has prepared for the disbelievers among His creation: the chains, the shackles, and the sa'ir (blazing fire), which is the flame and the burning in the fire of Hell, as He said: {When the shackles are around their necks and the chains, they are dragged through the boiling water, then in the fire they are burned} [Ghafir: 71, 72].

When He mentioned what He has prepared of the sa'ir for these wretched ones, He said afterward: {Indeed the righteous will drink from a cup whose mixture is kafur (camphor)}. It is known what camphor has of cooling and pleasant scent, along with what is added to that of delight in Paradise.

Al-Hasan said: the coolness of camphor is combined with the fragrance of ginger. Hence He said: {A spring from which the servants of Allah drink, making it gush forth in springs}, meaning: this that has been mixed for these righteous ones from camphor is a spring from which the ones brought near among the servants of Allah drink undiluted, without mixture, and they quench their thirst with it. Hence "yashrabu" (they drink) has been made to carry the meaning of "yarwa" (they are satiated), to the point that it is made transitive with the preposition "bi", and {'aynan} (a spring) is put in the accusative as a specifier.

Some of them said: this drink is like camphor in its fragrance. Others said: it is from a spring called Kafur. Others said: it is permissible that it be in the accusative governed by {yashrabu}. Ibn Jarir related these three opinions.

His saying {making it gush forth in springs} means: they direct it wherever they wish and to wherever they wish, among their palaces, their dwellings, their gathering places, and their locations.

Tafjir means causing to gush out, as Allah, exalted is He, said: {And they said, "We will never believe in you until you make a spring gush forth for us from the earth"} [al-Isra: 90]. And He said: {And We caused a river to gush forth between them} [al-Kahf: 33].

Mujahid said: {making it gush forth in springs} means they direct it wherever they wish. Ikrimah and Qatadah said likewise. Al-Thawri said: they channel it wherever they wish.

His saying {They fulfill their vows and fear a day whose evil is widespread} means: they worship Allah in what He has made obligatory upon them of the acts of obedience required by the foundation of the sacred law, and what they have made obligatory upon themselves by way of vows.

Imam Malik said, [chain] from A'ishah, may Allah be pleased with her, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Whoever vows to obey Allah, let him obey Him, and whoever vows to disobey Allah, let him not disobey Him." Al-Bukhari related it from the hadith of Malik.

And they abandon the forbidden things that He has prohibited them from, out of fear of the evil reckoning on the Day of Return, which is the day whose evil is widespread, meaning: spread out, general, affecting the people except whomever Allah has mercy upon.

Ibn 'Abbas said: widely spread. Qatadah said: the evil of that day spreads, by Allah, until it fills the heavens and the earth.

Ibn Jarir said: from this comes their saying "the crack in the glass spread and lengthened" (istatara, istatala). And from it is the saying of al-A'sha:

"So she departed, having left in the heart a crack, spread wide, despite her distance."

meaning: extended and widespread.

His saying {And they feed food, despite their love for it} was said to mean: out of love for Allah, exalted is He, taking the pronoun to refer back to Allah, glorious and exalted, on account of what the context indicates. But the clearer view is that the pronoun refers back to the food, meaning: they feed food in a state of their loving it and desiring it, which is what Mujahid and Muqatil said, and Ibn Jarir preferred it, similar to His saying, exalted is He: {And gives wealth, despite his love for it} [al-Baqarah: 177], and similar to His saying, exalted is He: {You will never attain righteousness until you spend from what you love} [Al 'Imran: 92].

Al-Bayhaqi related, through al-A'mash, from Nafi', who said: Ibn 'Umar fell ill and craved grapes, when grapes first appeared in season. Safiyyah, meaning his wife, sent someone who bought a bunch of grapes for a dirham. A beggar followed the messenger, and when he entered with it the beggar said: "The beggar!" Ibn 'Umar said: "Give it to him." So they gave it to him. Then she sent another dirham and bought another bunch, and a beggar followed the messenger again, and when he entered, the beggar said: "The beggar!" Ibn 'Umar said: "Give it to him." So they gave it to him. Then Safiyyah sent word to the beggar and said: "By Allah, if you return you will never get any good from it again." Then she sent another dirham and bought with it.

And in the authentic collection: "The best charity is that you give charity while you are healthy, tightfisted [with your wealth out of love for it], hoping for wealth and fearing poverty," meaning: in the state of your love for wealth, your eagerness for it, and your need of it. Hence Allah, exalted is He, said: {And they feed food, despite their love for it, to the poor, the orphan, and the captive}. As for the poor person and the orphan, their description has already been given previously. As for the captive: Sa'id ibn Jubayr, al-Hasan, and al-Dahhak said: the captive is one of the people of the qiblah (the Muslims). Ibn 'Abbas said: their captives at that time were polytheists. What supports this is that the Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, commanded his companions on the day of Badr to treat the captives with honor, so they would give them precedence over themselves at meal times. Sa'id ibn Jubayr, 'Ata, al-Hasan, and Qatadah said likewise.

The Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, enjoined kindness toward slaves in more than one hadith, to the point that among the last things he enjoined, he kept saying: "Prayer, and those whom your right hands possess."

Ikrimah said: they are the slaves, and Ibn Jarir preferred this, because of the generality of the verse covering both the Muslim and the polytheist.

Mujahid said: it is the imprisoned person, meaning: they feed these people food while they themselves desire it and love it, saying by the language of their state: {We only feed you for the sake of Allah}, meaning: hoping for the reward of Allah and His pleasure, {We do not desire from you any repayment or thanks}, meaning: we do not seek from you any recompense with which you would repay us, nor that you thank us before the people.

Mujahid and Sa'id ibn Jubayr said: by Allah, they did not say this with their tongues, but Allah knew it from their hearts, and so He praised them for it, so that whoever desires such a thing might be moved to aspire to it.

{Indeed we fear from our Lord a day, scowling and distressing}, meaning: we only do this hoping that Allah will have mercy on us and meet us with His kindness, on the scowling, distressing day.

'Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn 'Abbas: {'abusan} (scowling) means constricted, {qamtariran} (distressing) means long.

Ikrimah and others reported from him, regarding His saying {a day, scowling and distressing}, meaning: the disbeliever scowls on that day until sweat like tar flows from between his eyes.

Mujahid said: {'abusan} means one whose lips are drawn in scowling, and regarding {qamtariran} he said: the contraction of the face in a frown.

Sa'id ibn Jubayr and Qatadah said: faces scowl on it from the terror, and {qamtariran} means the shrinking of the forehead and the area between the eyes, from the terror.

Ibn Zayd said: al-'ubus is evil, and al-qamtarir is severe.

The clearest, most illuminating, sweetest, highest, and most fitting of these expressions is the saying of Ibn 'Abbas, may Allah be pleased with him.

Ibn Jarir said: al-qamtarir means severe. It is said "a qamtarir day" and "a qumatir day," and "a day 'asib and 'asabsab (harsh)." It is said "the day iqmatarra, yaqmatirru, iqmitraran," and that is the most severe and longest of days in tribulation and hardship. From this comes the saying of one of them:

"O sons of our uncle, do you remember our affliction upon you, when there came a day qumatir?"

Allah, exalted is He, said: {So Allah protected them from the evil of that day, and gave them radiance and joy}. This is of the category of eloquent paronomasia (tajanus balighah). {So Allah protected them from the evil of that day} means: He kept them safe from what they feared, {and gave them radiance} meaning: in their faces, {and joy} meaning: in their hearts. This was said by al-Hasan al-Basri, Qatadah, Abu al-'Aliyah, and al-Rabi' ibn Anas. This is similar to His saying, exalted is He: {Faces that day, radiant, laughing, joyful} [Abasa: 38, 39]. That is because when the heart is joyful, the face shines with light. Ka'b ibn Malik said in his lengthy hadith: "and the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, when he was pleased, his face would shine with light until it was as if it were a piece of the moon." And A'ishah said: "The Messenger of Allah ﷺ came in to me pleased, the lines of his face gleaming with light..." the hadith continues.

His saying {And He rewarded them for their patient endurance} means: on account of their patience He gave them, granted them, and settled them in {a garden and silk}, meaning: a spacious dwelling, a comfortable life, and beautiful clothing.

Al-Hafiz Ibn 'Asakir related, in the biographical entry of Hisham ibn Sulayman al-Darani, that Surat "Hal ata 'ala al-insan" was recited to Abu Sulayman al-Darani, and when the reciter reached His saying {And He rewarded them for their patient endurance with a garden and silk}, he said: for their patience in forsaking desires in this world. Then he recited:

"How many slain by desire, and how many taken captive! Fie upon one who desires other than the beautiful. The desires of a person bequeath to him humiliation, and cast him into prolonged affliction."

فَبَانَتْ وَقَد أسْأرت فِي الفُؤا ... دِ صَدْعًا، عَلَى نَأيها مُستَطيرًا 

يَعْنِي: مُمْتَدًّا فَاشِيًا.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَيُطْعِمُونَ الطَّعَامَ عَلَى حُبِّهِ﴾ قِيلَ: عَلَى حُبِّ اللَّهِ تَعَالَى. وَجَعَلُوا الضَّمِيرَ عَائِدًا إِلَى اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ لِدَلَالَةِ السِّيَاقِ عَلَيْهِ. وَالْأَظْهَرُ أَنَّ الضَّمِيرَ عَائِدٌ عَلَى الطَّعَامِ، أَيْ: وَيُطْعِمُونَ الطَّعَامَ فِي حَالِ مَحَبَّتِهِمْ وَشَهْوَتِهِمْ لَهُ، قَالَهُ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَمُقَاتِلٌ، وَاخْتَارَهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، كَقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَآتَى الْمَالَ عَلَى حُبِّهِ﴾ [الْبَقَرَةِ: ١٧٧] ، وَكَقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿لَنْ تَنَالُوا الْبِرَّ حَتَّى تُنْفِقُوا مِمَّا تُحِبُّونَ﴾ [آلِ عِمْرَانَ: ٩٢] .

وَرَوَى الْبَيْهَقِيُّ، مِنْ طَرِيقِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ نَافِعٍ قَالَ: مَرِضَ ابْنُ عُمَرَ فَاشْتَهَى عِنَبًا -أَوَّلَ مَا جَاءَ الْعِنَبُ-فَأَرْسَلَتْ صَفِيَّةُ -يَعْنِي امْرَأَتَهُ-فَاشْتَرَتْ عُنْقُودًا بِدِرْهَمٍ، فَاتَّبَعَ الرسولَ السَّائِلُ، فَلَمَّا دَخَلَ بِهِ قَالَ السَّائِلُ: السَّائِلَ. فَقَالَ ابْنُ عُمَرَ: أَعْطُوهُ إِيَّاهُ. فَأَعْطَوْهُ إِيَّاهُ. ثُمَّ أَرْسَلَتْ بِدِرْهَمٍ آخَرَ فَاشْتَرَتْ عُنْقُودًا فَاتَّبَعَ الرسولَ السائلُ، فَلَمَّا دَخَلَ قَالَ السَّائِلُ: السَّائِلَ. فَقَالَ ابْنُ عُمَرَ: أَعْطُوهُ إِيَّاهُ. فَأَعْطَوْهُ إِيَّاهُ. فَأَرْسَلَتْ صَفِيَّةُ إِلَى السَّائِلِ فَقَالَتْ: وَاللَّهِ إِنْ عُدتَ لَا تصيبُ مِنْهُ خَيْرًا أَبَدًا. ثُمَّ أَرْسَلَتْ بِدِرْهَمٍ آخَرَ فَاشْتَرَتْ بِهِ .

وَفِي الصَّحِيحِ: "أَفْضَلُ الصَّدَقَةِ أَنْ تَصَدّقَ وَأَنْتَ صَحِيحٌ، شَحِيحٌ، تَأْمَلُ الْغِنَى، وَتَخْشَى الْفَقْرَ" أَيْ: فِي حَالِ مَحَبَّتِكَ لِلْمَالِ وَحِرْصِكَ عَلَيْهِ وَحَاجَتِكَ إِلَيْهِ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَيُطْعِمُونَ الطَّعَامَ عَلَى حُبِّهِ مِسْكِينًا وَيَتِيمًا وَأَسِيرًا﴾ أَمَّا الْمِسْكِينُ وَالْيَتِيمُ، فَقَدْ تَقَدَّمَ بَيَانُهُمَا وَصِفَتُهُمَا. وَأَمَّا الْأَسِيرُ: فَقَالَ سَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ، وَالْحَسَنُ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ: الْأَسِيرُ: مِنْ أَهْلِ الْقِبْلَةِ. وَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: كَانَ أُسَرَاؤُهُمْ يَوْمَئِذٍ مُشْرِكِينَ. وَيَشْهَدُ لِهَذَا أن رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم أَمَرَ أَصْحَابَهُ يَوْمَ بَدْرٍ أَنْ يُكْرِمُوا الْأُسَارَى، فَكَانُوا يُقَدِّمُونَهُمْ عَلَى أَنْفُسِهِمْ عِنْدَ الْغَدَاءِ، وَهَكَذَا قال سعيد بن جبير، وعطاء، والحس، وقتادة.

وَقَدْ وَصَّى رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم بالإحسان إلى الأرقاء في غير ما حَدِيثٍ، حَتَّى إِنَّهُ كَانَ آخِرَ مَا أَوْصَى أَنْ جَعَلَ يَقُولُ: "الصلاةَ وَمَا مَلَكَتْ أَيْمَانُكُمْ" .

وَقَالَ عِكْرِمَةُ: هُمُ الْعَبِيدُ -وَاخْتَارَهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ-لِعُمُومِ الْآيَةِ لِلْمُسْلِمِ وَالْمُشْرِكِ.

قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: هُوَ الْمَحْبُوسُ، أَيْ: يُطْعِمُونَ لِهَؤُلَاءِ الطَّعَامَ وَهُمْ يَشْتَهُونَهُ وَيُحِبُّونَهُ، قَائِلِينَ بِلِسَانِ الْحَالِ: ﴿إِنَّمَا نُطْعِمُكُمْ لِوَجْهِ اللَّهِ﴾ أَيْ: رجاءَ ثَوَابِ اللَّهِ وَرِضَاهُ ﴿لَا نُرِيدُ مِنْكُمْ جَزَاءً وَلا شُكُورًا﴾ أَيْ: لَا نَطْلُبُ مِنْكُمْ مُجَازَاةً تُكَافِئُونَا بِهَا وَلَا أَنْ تَشْكُرُونَا عِنْدَ النَّاسِ.

قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ وَسَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ: أَمَا وَاللَّهِ مَا قَالُوهُ بِأَلْسِنَتِهِمْ، وَلَكِنْ عَلِمَ اللَّهُ بِهِ مِنْ قُلُوبِهِمْ، فَأَثْنَى عَلَيْهِمْ بِهِ لِيَرْغَبَ فِي ذَلِكَ رَاغِبٌ.

﴿إِنَّا نَخَافُ مِنْ رَبِّنَا يَوْمًا عَبُوسًا قَمْطَرِيرًا﴾ أَيْ: إِنَّمَا نَفْعَلُ هَذَا لَعَلَّ اللَّهَ أَنْ يَرْحَمَنَا وَيَتَلَقَّانَا بِلُطْفِهِ، فِي الْيَوْمِ الْعَبُوسِ الْقَمْطَرِيرِ.

قَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ ﴿عَبُوسًا﴾ ضَيِّقًا، ﴿قَمْطَرِيرًا﴾ طَوِيلًا.

## 76:13-22 — verses 13-22 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted informs us about the people of Paradise and the lasting bliss they are in, and the encompassing favor bestowed upon them, saying: {Reclining therein upon couches}. The discussion of this has already been given in Surat al-Saffat, along with the mention of the differing views on "reclining": whether it means lying down, resting comfortably, sitting cross-legged, or simply being settled in a seated position. And the "couches" (araik) are the raised beds set within canopies.

His saying: {They will not see therein any sun or bitter cold} means: with them there is no distressing heat and no painful cold, rather it is one continuous, everlasting, unchanging climate. {They will not desire any change from it} [al-Kahf: 108].

{And its shades will be close over them}, meaning its branches are near to them. {And its clusters of fruit will be brought low, subdued} meaning: whenever one reaches for it, the cluster draws near to him and hangs down from the top of its branch, as though it were listening and obedient, just as the Exalted said in another verse: {And the fruit of the two gardens will be near} [al-Rahman: 54], and He said: {Their clusters hanging low} [al-Haqqah: 23].

Mujahid said regarding {And its clusters of fruit will be brought low, subdued}: if he stands, it rises to his height; if he sits, it hangs down to him until he can reach it; and if he lies down, it hangs down to him until he can reach it. That is the meaning of His saying {subdued}.

Qatadah said: no thorn and no distance will hold back their hands from it.

Mujahid said: the ground of Paradise is of silver leaf, its soil is musk, the trunks of its trees are of gold and silver, its branches are of moist pearl, chrysolite, and ruby, with leaves and fruit in between. Whoever eats from it standing is not harmed by it, whoever eats from it sitting is not harmed by it, and whoever eats from it lying down is not harmed by it.

His saying: {And they will be served round with vessels of silver and cups} means: the servants will circulate among them with vessels of food, which are of silver, and cups for drink, which are goblets that have neither handles nor spouts.

His saying: {Vessels of crystal, of crystal made from silver}: the first is in the accusative as the predicate of "kana" (i.e., "they were crystal vessels"), and the second is in the accusative either as a substitution or as a specifier, since it is explained by His saying {crystal made of silver}.

Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, al-Hasan al-Basri, and others said: it has the whiteness of silver combined with the clarity of glass, whereas crystal vessels (qawarir) are ordinarily only made of glass. So these cups are of silver, yet are nonetheless transparent, such that what is inside them can be seen from the outside, and this has no counterpart in this world.

Ibn al-Mubarak said, from Isma'il, from a man, from Ibn Abbas: "There is nothing in Paradise except that you have been given something resembling it in this world, except for vessels of crystal made from silver." Reported by Ibn Abi Hatim.

His saying: {They have determined its measure precisely} means: according to the exact measure of their thirst, neither more nor less, rather it is prepared for that purpose, measured according to what will satisfy the one drinking it. This is the meaning given by Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, Abu Salih, Qatadah, Ibn Abza, Abdullah ibn Ubaydullah ibn Umayr, Qatadah, al-Sha'bi, and Ibn Zayd, and it was likewise stated by Ibn Jarir and others. This carries a greater sense of care, honor, and dignity.

Al-Awfi reported from Ibn Abbas: {They have determined its measure precisely} means it was measured to fit the hand comfortably. Al-Rabi ibn Anas said the same. Al-Dahhak said: according to the measure of the servants' hands. This does not contradict the first view, for it is measured both in quantity and in what satisfies thirst.

His saying: {And they will be given to drink therein a cup whose mixture is ginger} means: they will be given to drink, that is, the righteous ones again, in these cups {a cup}, meaning wine, {whose mixture is ginger}. At times their drink is mixed with camphor, which is cooling, and at times with ginger, which is warming, so that the matter is balanced; and these people have their drink mixed sometimes with one and sometimes with the other. As for those brought near (al-muqarrabun), they drink each of the two undiluted, as stated by Qatadah and others. It was already mentioned: {A spring from which the servants of Allah drink}, and here He says: {A spring therein called Salsabil}, meaning: the ginger comes from a spring in Paradise called Salsabil.

Ikrimah said: it is the name of a spring in Paradise. Mujahid said: it was so named because of the smoothness of its flow and the swiftness of its course.

Qatadah said regarding {A spring therein called Salsabil}: it is a spring whose water flows smoothly and is easily drawn.

Ibn Jarir related from some of them that it was so named because of its smoothness going down the throat. He himself preferred that it encompasses all of these meanings together, and that is correct, as he said.

The saying of the Exalted: {And there will circulate among them youths made eternal; when you see them, you would think them scattered pearls} means: circulating among the people of Paradise in service are youths from among the youths of Paradise, {made eternal}, meaning: remaining in one unchanging state forever, not altering from it, their ages never increasing beyond that stage of youth. As for those who interpret them as boys wearing earrings in their ears, this is simply describing the underlying meaning in that way, because it is the young who are suited to such adornment, not the old.

His saying: {When you see them, you would think them scattered pearls} means: when you see them spreading out to fulfill the needs of their masters, and their great number, and the radiance of their faces, and the beauty of their complexions, garments, and adornments, you would think them scattered pearls. There is no comparison more excellent than this, nor any sight more beautiful than pearls scattered upon a beautiful setting.

Qatadah said, from Abu Ayyub, from Abdullah ibn Amr: "There is not one among the people of Paradise but a thousand servants attend to him, each servant performing a task different from what his fellow performs."

His saying: {And when you see} means: and when you see, O Muhammad, {there}, meaning: there, that is, in Paradise, amid its bliss, its vastness, its elevation, and the joy and delight within it, {you will see bliss and a great dominion}, meaning: a magnificent kingdom belonging to Allah there, and an overwhelming sovereignty.

It is established in the authentic hadith that Allah, the Exalted, says to the last of the people of the Fire to come out of it, and the last of the people of Paradise to enter it: "You shall have the equivalent of this world and ten times more."

We have already mentioned, in the hadith related through the path of Thuwayr ibn Abi Fakhitah, from Ibn Umar, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Indeed the lowest in rank among the people of Paradise is one who, looking upon his dominion extending a distance of two thousand years' journey, sees its farthest reaches just as he sees its nearest." If this is what Allah, the Exalted, gives to the lowest ranking person in Paradise, then what do you suppose is given to one of higher rank and greater favor with Him, the Exalted?

Al-Tabarani related here a very unusual (gharib) hadith. He said: [chain] ...from Ibn Umar, who said: A man from Abyssinia came to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, and the Messenger of Allah said to him: "Ask and inquire." He said: O Messenger of Allah, you have been favored over us in appearance, complexion, and prophethood; tell me, if I believe in what you believe in and act according to what you act upon, will I truly be with you in Paradise? He said: "Yes, by the One in whose hand is my soul, indeed the whiteness of the black man will be seen in Paradise from a distance of a thousand years' journey." Then the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Whoever says 'there is no god but Allah' will have a covenant with Allah because of it, and whoever says 'Glory be to Allah and praise be to Him' will have a hundred thousand and twenty-four thousand good deeds recorded for him." A man then said: How then can we perish after this, O Messenger of Allah? The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Indeed a man will come on the Day of Resurrection with a deed which, if placed upon a mountain, would weigh it down, yet the blessing, or the favors of Allah, will arise and will nearly exhaust all of it, except that Allah covers him with His mercy." And this surah was revealed: {Has there come upon man a period of time} down to His saying {and a great dominion}. The Abyssinian said: Will my own eye truly see what your eyes will see in Paradise? He said: "Yes." The man then wept until his soul departed. Ibn Umar said: I saw the Messenger of Allah ﷺ lowering him into his grave with his own hands.

His saying: {Upon them will be garments of green fine silk and heavy brocade} means: the clothing of the people of Paradise there is silk, among which is sundus, which is fine, light silk like shirts and similar garments that lie against their bodies, while istabraq is that which has sheen and luster, and is worn as the outer layer, as is customary in dress. {And they will be adorned with bracelets of silver}: this is the description of the righteous (al-abrar), while as for those brought near (al-muqarrabun), it is as He said: {They will be adorned therein with bracelets of gold and pearl, and their garments therein will be of silk} [al-Hajj: 23].

And when the Exalted mentioned the outward adornment of silk and jewelry, He said afterward: {And their Lord will give them to drink a purifying drink}, meaning: He purified their inward selves from envy, malice, rancor, harm, and all base character traits, as we have related from the Commander of the Faithful, Ali ibn Abi Talib, who said: When the people of Paradise arrive at the gate of Paradise, they find there two springs, and it is as though they are inspired to know this, so they drink from one of them, and Allah removes whatever harm was in their insides, then they wash themselves from the other, and the radiance of bliss flows over them.

His saying: {Indeed this is a reward for you, and your effort has been accepted with gratitude} means: this is said to them in honor of them and as kindness toward them, just as He said: {Eat and drink with satisfaction for what you sent forth in the days gone by} [al-Haqqah: 24], and just as He said: {And they will be called out to: This is the Paradise which you have inherited for what you used to do} [al-A'raf: 43].

His saying: {And your effort has been accepted with gratitude} means: Allah rewarded you abundantly for the little that you did.

وَقَوْلُهُ : ﴿قَوَارِيرَ قَوَارِيرَ مِنْ فِضَّةٍ﴾ فَالْأَوَّلُ مَنْصُوبٌ بِخَبَرِ "كَانَ" أَيْ: كَانَتْ قَوَارِيرَ. وَالثَّانِي مَنْصُوبٌ إِمَّا عَلَى الْبَدَلِيَّةِ أَوْ تَمْيِيزٌ؛ لِأَنَّهُ بَيَّنَهُ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿قَوَارِيرَ مِنْ فِضَّةٍ﴾

قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَالْحَسَنُ الْبَصْرِيُّ، وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ: بَيَاضُ الْفِضَّةِ فِي صَفَاءِ الزُّجَاجِ، وَالْقَوَارِيرُ لَا تَكُونُ إِلَّا مِنْ زُجَاجٍ. فَهَذِهِ الْأَكْوَابُ هِيَ مِنْ فِضَّةٍ، وَهِيَ مَعَ هَذَا شَفَّافَةٌ يُرَى مَا فِي بَاطِنِهَا مِنْ ظَاهِرِهَا، وَهَذَا مِمَّا لَا نَظِيرَ لَهُ فِي الدُّنْيَا.

قَالَ ابْنُ الْمُبَارَكِ، عَنْ إِسْمَاعِيلَ، عَنْ رَجُلٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: لَيْسَ فِي الْجَنَّةِ شَيْءٌ إِلَّا قَدْ أُعْطِيتُمْ فِي الدُّنْيَا شَبَهُهُ إِلَّا قَوَارِيرَ مِنْ فِضَّةٍ. رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿قَدَّرُوهَا تَقْدِيرًا﴾ أَيْ: عَلَى قَدْرِ رِيِّهِمْ، لَا تَزِيدُ عَنْهُ وَلَا تَنْقُصُ، بَلْ هِيَ مُعَدّة لِذَلِكَ، مُقَدَّرَةٌ بِحَسْبِ رِيِّ صَاحِبِهَا. هَذَا مَعْنَى قَوْلِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٍ، وَسَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ، وَأَبِي صَالِحٍ، وَقَتَادَةَ، وَابْنِ أَبْزَى، وَعَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عُبَيد اللَّهِ بْنِ عُمَيْرٍ، وَقَتَادَةَ، وَالشَّعْبِيِّ، وَابْنِ زَيْدٍ. وَقَالَهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ. وَهَذَا أَبْلَغُ فِي الِاعْتِنَاءِ وَالشَّرَفِ وَالْكَرَامَةِ.

وَقَالَ العَوفي، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿قَدَّرُوهَا تَقْدِيرًا﴾ قُدِّرَتْ لِلْكَفِّ. وَهَكَذَا قَالَ الرَّبِيعُ بْنُ أَنَسٍ. وَقَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ: عَلَى قَدْرِ أكُفّ الخُدّام. وَهَذَا لَا يُنَافِي الْقَوْلَ الْأَوَّلَ، فَإِنَّهَا مُقَدَّرَةٌ فِي القَدْر وَالرِّيِّ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَيُسْقَوْنَ فِيهَا كَأْسًا كَانَ مِزَاجُهَا زَنْجَبِيلا﴾ أَيْ: وَيُسْقَوْنَ -يَعْنِي الْأَبْرَارَ أَيْضًا-فِي هَذِهِ الْأَكْوَابِ ﴿كَأْسًا﴾ أَيْ: خَمْرًا، ﴿كَانَ مِزَاجُهَا زَنْجَبِيلا﴾ فَتَارَةً يُمزَج لَهُمُ الشَّرَابُ بالكافور وَهُوَ بَارِدٌ، وَتَارَةً بِالزَّنْجَبِيلِ وَهُوَ حَارٌّ، لِيَعْتَدِلَ الْأَمْرُ، وَهَؤُلَاءِ يُمْزَجُ لَهُمْ مِنْ هَذَا تَارَةً وَمِنْ هَذَا تَارَةً. وَأَمَّا الْمُقَرَّبُونَ فَإِنَّهُمْ يَشْرَبُونَ مِنْ كُلٍّ مِنْهُمَا صِرْفًا، كَمَا قَالَهُ قَتَادَةُ وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ. وَقَدْ تَقَدَّمَ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿عَيْنًا يَشْرَبُ بِهَا عِبَادُ اللَّهِ﴾ وَقَالَ هَاهُنَا: ﴿عَيْنًا فِيهَا تُسَمَّى سَلْسَبِيلا﴾ أَيِ: الزَّنْجَبِيلُ عَيْنٌ فِي الْجَنَّةِ تُسَمَّى سَلْسَبِيلًا.

قَالَ عِكْرِمَةُ: اسْمُ عَيْنٍ فِي الْجَنَّةِ. وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: سُمِّيَتْ بِذَلِكَ لِسَلَاسَةِ سَيْلِهَا وحِدّة جَريها.

وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: ﴿عَيْنًا فِيهَا تُسَمَّى سَلْسَبِيلا﴾ عَيْنٌ سَلِسَة مُستَقِيد مَاؤُهَا.

وَحَكَى ابنُ جَرِيرٍ عَنْ بَعْضِهِمْ أَنَّهَا سُمِّيَتْ بِذَلِكَ لِسَلَاسَتِهَا فِي الحَلْق. وَاخْتَارَ هُوَ أَنَّهَا تَعُمّ ذَلِكَ كلَّه، وَهُوَ كَمَا قَالَ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَيَطُوفُ عَلَيْهِمْ وِلْدَانٌ مُخَلَّدُونَ إِذَا رَأَيْتَهُمْ حَسِبْتَهُمْ لُؤْلُؤًا مَنْثُورًا﴾ أَيْ: يَطُوفُ عَلَى أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ للخدْمَة ولدانٌ مِنْ وِلْدَانِ الْجَنَّةِ ﴿مُخَلَّدُونَ﴾ أَيْ: عَلَى حَالَةٍ وَاحِدَةٍ مُخَلَّدُونَ عَلَيْهَا، لَا يَتَغَيَّرُونَ عَنْهَا، لَا تَزِيدُ أَعْمَارُهُمْ عَنْ تِلْكَ السِّنِّ. وَمَنْ فَسَرَّهُمْ بِأَنَّهُمْ مُخَرّصُونَ فِي آذَانِهِمُ الْأَقْرِطَةَ، فَإِنَّمَا عَبَّرَ عَنِ الْمَعْنَى بِذَلِكَ؛ لِأَنَّ الصَّغِيرَ هُوَ الَّذِي يَلِيقُ لَهُ ذَلِكَ دُونَ الْكَبِيرِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِذَا رَأَيْتَهُمْ حَسِبْتَهُمْ لُؤْلُؤًا مَنْثُورًا﴾ أَيْ: إِذَا رَأَيْتَهُمْ فِي انْتِشَارِهِمْ فِي قَضَاءِ حَوَائِجِ السَّادَةِ، وَكَثْرَتِهِمْ، وَصَبَاحَةِ وُجُوهِهِمْ، وَحُسْنِ أَلْوَانِهِمْ وَثِيَابِهِمْ وَحُلِيِّهِمْ، حَسِبْتَهُمْ لُؤْلُؤًا مَنْثُورًا. وَلَا يَكُونُ فِي التَّشْبِيهِ أَحْسَنُ مِنْ هَذَا، وَلَا فِي الْمَنْظَرِ أَحْسَنُ مِنَ اللُّؤْلُؤِ الْمَنْثُورِ عَلَى الْمَكَانِ الْحَسَنِ.

قَالَ قَتَادَةَ، عَنْ أَبِي أَيُّوبَ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو: مَا مِنْ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ مِنْ أَحَدٍ إِلَّا يَسْعَى عَلَيْهِ أَلْفُ خَادِمٍ، كُلُّ خَادِمٍ عَلَى عَمَلٍ مَا عَلَيْهِ صَاحِبُهُ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِذَا رَأَيْتَ﴾ أَيْ: وَإِذَا رَأَيْتَ يَا مُحَمَّدُ، ﴿ثَمَّ﴾ أَيْ: هُنَاكَ ، يَعْنِي فِي الْجَنَّةِ وَنَعِيمِهَا وسَعَتها وَارْتِفَاعِهَا وَمَا فِيهَا مِنَ الحَبْرَة وَالسُّرُورِ، ﴿رَأَيْتَ نَعِيمًا وَمُلْكًا كَبِيرًا﴾ أَيْ: مَمْلَكَةً لِلَّهِ هُناك عَظِيمَةً وَسُلْطَانًا بَاهِرًا.

وَثَبَتَ فِي الصَّحِيحِ أَنَّ اللَّهَ تَعَالَى يَقُولُ لِآخَرِ أَهْلِ النَّارِ خُرُوجًا مِنْهَا، وَآخَرِ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ دُخُولًا إِلَيْهَا: إِنَّ لَكَ مثلَ الدُّنْيَا وَعَشْرَةَ أَمْثَالِهَا.

وَقَدْ قَدّمنا فِي الْحَدِيثِ المَرويّ مِنْ طَرِيقِ ثُوَير بْنِ أَبِي فَاخِتَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ أَدْنَى أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ مَنْزِلَةً لَمَنْ يَنْظُرُ فِي مُلْكِهِ مَسِيرَةَ أَلْفَيْ سَنَةٍ يَنْظُرُ إِلَى أَقْصَاهُ كَمَا يَنْظُرُ إِلَى أَدْنَاهُ". فَإِذَا كَانَ هَذَا عَطَاؤُهُ تَعَالَى لِأَدْنَى مَنْ يَكُونُ فِي الْجَنَّةِ، فَمَا ظَنُّكَ بِمَا هُوَ أَعْلَى مَنْزِلَةً، وَأَحْظَى عِنْدَهُ تَعَالَى.

وَقَدْ رَوَى الطَّبَرَانِيُّ هَاهُنَا حَدِيثًا غَرِيبًا جِدًّا فَقَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ عَبْدِ الْعَزِيزِ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بن عَمَّارٍ الْمَوْصِلِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عُفَيْفُ بْنُ سَالِمٍ، عَنْ أَيُّوبَ بْنِ عُتْبَةَ، عَنْ عَطَاءٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ قَالَ: جَاءَ رَجُلٌ مِنَ الْحَبَشَةِ إِلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ: فَقَالَ لَهُ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ: "سَلْ وَاسْتَفْهِمْ". فَقَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، فُضّلْتُم عَلَيْنَا بِالصُّوَرِ وَالْأَلْوَانِ وَالنُّبُوَّةِ، أفرأيتَ إِنْ آمنتُ بِمَا آمنتَ بِهِ وعملتُ بِمِثْلِ مَا عملتَ بِهِ، إِنِّي لَكَائِنٌ معكَ فِي الْجَنَّةِ؟ قَالَ: "نَعَمْ، وَالَّذِي نَفْسِي بِيَدِهِ، إِنَّهُ لَيُرَى بَيَاضُ الْأَسْوَدِ فِي الْجَنَّةِ مِنْ مَسِيرَةِ أَلْفِ عَامٍّ". ثُمَّ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "مَنْ قَالَ: لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ، كَانَ لَهُ بِهَا عَهدٌ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ، وَمَنْ قَالَ: سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ وَبِحَمْدِهِ، كُتِبَ لَهُ مِائَةُ أَلْفِ حَسَنَةٍ، وَأَرْبَعَةٌ وَعِشْرُونَ أَلْفَ حَسَنَةٍ". فَقَالَ رَجُلٌ: كَيْفَ نَهْلَكُ بَعْدَ هَذَا يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ؟ فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ الرَّجُلَ لَيَأْتِيَ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ بِالْعَمَلِ لَوْ وُضِعَ عَلَى جَبَلٍ لَأَثْقَلَهُ، فَتَقُومُ النِّعْمَةُ -أَوْ: نعَم اللَّهِ-فَتَكَادُ تَسْتَنْفِذُ ذَلِكَ كُلَّهُ، إِلَّا أَنْ يَتَغَمّده اللَّهُ بِرَحْمَتِهِ". وَنَزَلَتْ هَذِهِ السُّورَةُ: ﴿هَلْ أَتَى عَلَى الإنْسَانِ حِينٌ مِنَ الدَّهْرِ﴾ إِلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَمُلْكًا كَبِيرًا﴾ فَقَالَ الْحَبَشِيُّ: وَإِنَّ عَيْنِي لَتَرَى مَا تَرَى عَيْنَاكَ فِي الْجَنَّةِ؟ قَالَ: "نَعَمْ". فَاسْتَبْكَى حَتَّى فَاضَتْ نَفْسُهُ. قَالَ ابْنُ عُمَرَ: فَلَقَدْ رَأَيْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يُدليه فِي حُفرَته بِيَدِهِ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿عَالِيَهُمْ ثِيَابُ سُنْدُسٍ خُضْرٌ وَإِسْتَبْرَقٌ﴾ أَيْ: لِبَاسُ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ فِيهَا الْحَرِيرُ، وَمِنْهُ سُنْدُسٌ، وَهُوَ رَفِيعُ الْحَرِيرِ كَالْقُمْصَانِ وَنَحْوِهَا مِمَّا يَلِي أَبْدَانَهُمْ، وَالْإِسْتَبْرَقُ مِنْهُ مَا فِيهِ بَرِيقٌ وَلَمَعَانٌ، وَهُوَ مِمَّا يَلِي الظَّاهِرَ، كَمَا هُوَ الْمَعْهُودُ فِي اللِّبَاسِ ﴿وَحُلُّوا أَسَاوِرَ مِنْ فِضَّةٍ﴾ وَهَذِهِ صِفَةُ الْأَبْرَارِ، وَأَمَّا الْمُقَرَّبُونَ فَكَمَا قَالَ: ﴿يُحَلَّوْنَ فِيهَا مِنْ أَسَاوِرَ مِنْ ذَهَبٍ وَلُؤْلُؤًا وَلِبَاسُهُمْ فِيهَا حَرِيرٌ﴾ [الْحَجِّ: ٢٣]

وَلَمَّا ذَكَرَ تَعَالَى زِينَةَ الظَّاهِرِ بِالْحَرِيرِ وَالْحُلِيِّ قَالَ بَعْدَهُ: ﴿وَسَقَاهُمْ رَبُّهُمْ شَرَابًا طَهُورًا﴾ أَيْ: طَهَّرَ بَوَاطِنَهُمْ مِنَ الحَسَد وَالْحِقْدِ وَالْغِلِّ وَالْأَذَى وَسَائِرِ الْأَخْلَاقِ الرّديَّة، كَمَا رَوَيْنَا عَنْ أَمِيرِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ عَلِيِّ بْنِ أَبِي طَالِبٍ أَنَّهُ قَالَ: إِذَا انْتَهَى أهلُ الْجَنَّةِ إِلَى بَابِ الْجَنَّةِ وَجَدوا هُنَالِكَ عَيْنَيْنِ فَكَأَنَّمَا أُلْهِمُوا ذَلِكَ فَشَرِبُوا مِنْ إِحْدَاهُمَا [فَأَذْهَبَ اللَّهُ] مَا فِي بُطُونِهِمْ مِنْ أَذًى، ثُمَّ اغْتَسَلُوا مِنَ الْأُخْرَى فَجَرت عَلَيْهِمْ نضرةُ النَّعِيمِ.

## 76:23-31 — verses 23-31 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, bestowing favor upon His Messenger ﷺ for what He revealed to him of the Mighty Qur'an as a revelation: {So be patient for the judgment of your Lord}, meaning: just as I have honored you with what I have revealed to you, so be patient with His decree and His predestination, and know that He will manage your affairs with the best management. {and do not obey among them a sinner or a disbeliever}, meaning: do not obey the disbelievers and the hypocrites if they wish to turn you away from what has been revealed to you. Rather, convey what has been revealed to you from your Lord, and place your trust in Allah, for Allah will protect you from the people. So al-athim (the sinner) is the one who is corrupt in his deeds, and al-kafur (the disbeliever) is the one who disbelieves in his heart.

{And remember the name of your Lord morning and evening}, meaning: the beginning of the day and its end.

{And during a part of the night prostrate to Him, and glorify Him a long part of the night}, like His saying: {And during a part of the night, pray with it as additional worship for you; it is expected that your Lord will raise you to a praised station} [al-Isra: 79], and like His saying: {O you wrapped in garments, stand the night, except a little, half of it, or subtract a little from it, or add to it, and recite the Qur'an with measured recitation} [al-Muzzammil: 1-4].

Then He said, denouncing the disbelievers and those who resemble them in their love of this world, their eager pursuit of it, and their devotion to it, while casting the Hereafter behind their backs: {Indeed, these people love the immediate life and leave behind them a heavy day}, meaning: the Day of Resurrection.

Then He said: {We have created them and strengthened their joints}. Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, and more than one other said: it means their creation. {and if We wish, We can replace them with their likes, a complete replacement}, meaning: if We wish, We will resurrect them on the Day of Resurrection, and We will replace them, bringing them back as a new creation. This is an argument from the original creation for the possibility of the return.

Ibn Zayd and Ibn Jarir said: {and if We wish, We can replace them with their likes, a complete replacement} means: if We wish, We will bring another people in their place, like His saying: {If He wills, He can do away with you, O people, and bring others, and Allah is over that ever competent} [al-Nisa: 133], and like His saying: {If He wills, He can do away with you and bring a new creation, and that is not difficult for Allah} [Ibrahim: 19-20, and Fatir: 16-17].

Then the Exalted said: {Indeed, this}, meaning: this surah, {is a reminder, so whoever wills may take to his Lord a way}, meaning a path and a route, that is: whoever wills may be guided by the Qur'an, like His saying: {And what would it harm them if they believed in Allah and the Last Day and spent from what Allah provided them, and Allah is ever Knowing of them} [al-Nisa: 39].

Then He said: {And you do not will except that Allah wills}, meaning: no one has the power to guide himself, nor to enter into faith, nor to bring about benefit for himself, {except that Allah wills; indeed Allah is ever Knowing, Wise}, meaning: He is Knowing of who deserves guidance, so He makes it easy for him and provides him with its means, and of who deserves misguidance, so He turns him away from guidance. His is the far-reaching wisdom and the overwhelming proof. Hence the Exalted said: {indeed Allah is ever Knowing, Wise}.

Then He said: {He admits whom He wills into His mercy, and the wrongdoers, He has prepared for them a painful punishment}, meaning: He guides whom He wills and misguides whom He wills. Whoever He guides, none can misguide him, and whoever He misguides, none can guide him.

[End of Surat al-Insan] [And Allah knows best]

﴿وَاذْكُرِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلا﴾ أَيْ: أولَ النَّهَارِ وَآخِرَهُ.

﴿وَمِنَ اللَّيْلِ فَاسْجُدْ لَهُ وَسَبِّحْهُ لَيْلا طَوِيلا﴾ كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَمِنَ اللَّيْلِ فَتَهَجَّدْ بِهِ نَافِلَةً لَكَ عَسَى أَنْ يَبْعَثَكَ رَبُّكَ مَقَامًا مَحْمُودًا﴾ [الْإِسْرَاءِ: ٧٩] وَكَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿يَا أَيُّهَا الْمُزَّمِّلُ قُمِ اللَّيْلَ إِلا قَلِيلا نِصْفَهُ أَوِ انْقُصْ مِنْهُ قَلِيلا أَوْ زِدْ عَلَيْهِ وَرَتِّلِ الْقُرْآنَ تَرْتِيلا﴾ [الْمُزَّمِّلِ: ١-٤] .

ثُمَّ قَالَ: مُنْكِرًا عَلَى الْكُفَّارِ وَمَنْ أَشْبَهَهُمْ فِي حُبّ الدُّنْيَا وَالْإِقْبَالِ عَلَيْهَا وَالِانْصِبَابِ إِلَيْهَا، وَتَرْكِ الدَّارِ الْآخِرَةِ وَرَاءَ ظُهُورِهِمْ: ﴿إِنَّ هَؤُلاءِ يُحِبُّونَ الْعَاجِلَةَ وَيَذَرُونَ وَرَاءَهُمْ يَوْمًا ثَقِيلا﴾ يَعْنِي: يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿نَحْنُ خَلَقْنَاهُمْ وَشَدَدْنَا أَسْرَهُمْ﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ: يَعْنِي خَلْقَهم. ﴿وَإِذَا شِئْنَا بَدَّلْنَا أَمْثَالَهُمْ تَبْدِيلا﴾ أَيْ: وَإِذَا شِئْنَا بَعَثْنَاهُمْ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ، وبَدلناهم فَأَعَدْنَاهُمْ خَلْقًا جَدِيدًا. وَهَذَا اسْتِدْلَالٌ بِالْبُدَاءَةِ عَلَى الرَّجْعَةِ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ زَيْدٍ، وَابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: ﴿وَإِذَا شِئْنَا بَدَّلْنَا أَمْثَالَهُمْ تَبْدِيلا﴾ [أَيْ] : وَإِذَا شِئْنَا أَتَيْنَا بِقَوْمٍ آخَرِينَ غَيْرِهِمْ، كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿إِنْ يَشَأْ يُذْهِبْكُمْ أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ وَيَأْتِ بِآخَرِينَ وَكَانَ اللَّهُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ قَدِيرًا﴾ [النِّسَاءِ: ١٣٣] وَكَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿إِنْ يَشَأْ يُذْهِبْكُمْ وَيَأْتِ بِخَلْقٍ جَدِيدٍ وَمَا ذَلِكَ عَلَى اللَّهِ بِعَزِيزٍ﴾ [إِبْرَاهِيمَ: ١٩، ٢٠، وَفَاطِرٍ ١٦، ١٧] .

ثُمَّ قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِنَّ هَذِهِ﴾ يَعْنِي: هَذِهِ السُّورَةَ ﴿تَذْكِرَةٌ فَمَنْ شَاءَ اتَّخَذَ إِلَى رَبِّهِ سَبِيلا﴾ أَيْ طَرِيقًا وَمَسْلَكًا، أَيْ: مَنْ شَاءَ اهْتَدَى بِالْقُرْآنِ، كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَمَاذَا عَلَيْهِمْ لَوْ آمَنُوا بِاللَّهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الآخِرِ وَأَنْفَقُوا مِمَّا رَزَقَهُمُ اللَّهُ وَكَانَ اللَّهُ بِهِمْ عَلِيمًا﴾ [النساء: ٣٩] .

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿وَمَا تَشَاءُونَ إِلا أَنْ يَشَاءَ اللَّهُ﴾ أَيْ: لَا يَقْدِرُ أَحَدٌ أَنْ يَهدي نَفْسَهُ، وَلَا يَدْخُلُ فِي الْإِيمَانِ وَلَا يُجْرِ لِنَفْسِهِ نَفْعًا، ﴿إِلا أَنْ يَشَاءَ اللَّهُ إِنَّ اللَّهَ كَانَ عَلِيمًا حَكِيمًا﴾ أَيْ: عَلِيمٌ بِمَنْ يَسْتَحِقُّ الْهِدَايَةَ فَيُيَسّرها لَهُ، وَيُقَيِّضُ لَهُ أَسْبَابَهَا، وَمَنْ يَسْتَحِقُّ الْغَوَايَةَ فَيَصْرِفُهُ عَنِ الْهُدَى، وَلَهُ الْحِكْمَةُ الْبَالِغَةُ، وَالْحُجَّةُ الدَّامِغَةُ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ كَانَ عَلِيمًا حَكِيمًا﴾

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿يُدْخِلُ مَنْ يَشَاءُ فِي رَحْمَتِهِ وَالظَّالِمِينَ أَعَدَّ لَهُمْ عَذَابًا أَلِيمًا﴾ أَيْ: يَهْدِي مَنْ يَشَاءُ وَيُضِلُّ مَنْ يَشَاءُ، وَمَنْ يَهْدِهِ فَلَا مُضِلَّ لَهُ، وَمَنْ يُضْلِلْ فَلَا هَادِيَ لَهُ.
