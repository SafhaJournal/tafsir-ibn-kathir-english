### Translation

# Tafsir of Surat al-Rahman

It is Meccan.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] a man said to Ibn Mas'ud: "How do you recognize this reading: {ma'in ghayri yasinin} or {asinin}?" He said: "Have you read the entire Qur'an?" The man said: "I recite the mufassal (short final surahs), combining it all in a single rak'ah." Ibn Mas'ud said: "Do you recite it in a rushed manner like poetry, may you have no father? I know the pairings of the Prophet ﷺ, in which he used to pair two surahs together from the beginning of the mufassal." And the first surah of the mufassal for Ibn Mas'ud was {al-Rahman}.

Abu 'Isa al-Tirmidhi said: [chain] Jabir said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ came out to his companions and recited to them Surat al-Rahman from beginning to end, and they remained silent. So he said: "I recited it to the jinn on the night of the jinn, and they gave a better response than you. Every time I reached His saying, {So which of the favors of your Lord do you both deny?}, they said: 'We deny none of Your favors, our Lord, so to You belongs all praise.'"

Then he said: This is a gharib (singular) hadith; we know it only through the report of al-Walid ibn Muslim from Zuhayr ibn Muhammad. He then related from Imam Ahmad that he did not recognize it, and that he rejected the transmission of the people of Syria from this Zuhayr ibn Muhammad.

Al-Hafiz Abu Bakr al-Bazzar narrated it from 'Amr ibn Malik, from al-Walid ibn Muslim, and also from 'Abdullah ibn Ahmad ibn Shabbawayh, from Hisham ibn 'Ammar, both of them from al-Walid ibn Muslim, with the same chain. He then said: We do not know it to be narrated except through this route.

Abu Ja'far ibn Jarir said: [chain] Ibn 'Umar reported that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ recited Surat al-Rahman, or it was recited in his presence, and he said: "What is it with me, that I hear the jinn giving a better response to their Lord than you?" They said: "What is that, O Messenger of Allah?" He said: "Every time I reached the saying of Allah, {So which of the favors of your Lord do you both deny?}, the jinn would say: 'We deny none of the favors of our Lord.'"

Al-Hafiz al-Bazzar narrated it from 'Amr ibn Malik with the same chain, then said: We do not know it to be narrated from the Prophet ﷺ except through this route with this chain.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful

The Exalted informs of His bounty and mercy toward His creation: that He sent down the Qur'an upon His servants and made its memorization and understanding easy for whomever He has shown mercy. He said: {Al-Rahman. He taught the Qur'an. He created man. He taught him al-bayan (clear expression)}. Al-Hasan said: it means speech. Al-Dahhak, Qatadah, and others said: it means good and evil. Al-Hasan's statement here is better and stronger, because the context concerns His teaching of the Qur'an, which is the performance of its recitation, and this comes about only through making speech easy for creation and facilitating the emergence of the letters from their places of articulation in the throat, tongue, and lips, according to their varying points and types of articulation.

His saying, {The sun and the moon move by precise calculation}, means: they run in succession according to a fixed reckoning that does not vary or become disordered, {It is not for the sun to overtake the moon, nor does the night outstrip the day; each floats in an orbit} [Ya-Sin: 40]. And the Exalted said: {[He is] the Cleaver of the daybreak, and He has made the night for rest, and the sun and the moon for calculation. That is the ordaining of the Almighty, the All-Knowing} [al-An'am: 96].

It is reported from 'Ikrimah that he said: If Allah were to place the light of the sight of all humans, jinn, animals, and birds combined into the eyes of a single servant, then lifted just one veil out of seventy veils in front of the sun, that servant would not be able to look at it. The light of the sun is one part out of seventy parts of the light of the Kursi (Footstool), and the light of the Kursi is one part out of seventy parts of the light of the Throne, and the light of the Throne is one part out of seventy parts of the light of the Veil (al-Sitr). So consider what Allah will grant His servant of light in his eyes at the moment he looks directly upon the face of his Generous Lord. Narrated by Ibn Abi Hatim.

His saying, {And the star and the tree both prostrate}: Ibn Jarir said: The commentators differed regarding the meaning of {al-najm} after agreeing that "the tree" (al-shajar) refers to what stands on a stalk. 'Ali ibn Abi Talhah reported from Ibn 'Abbas that he said: al-najm is what spreads flat upon the surface of the earth, meaning plants without stalks. Sa'id ibn Jubayr, al-Suddi, and Sufyan al-Thawri said likewise. Ibn Jarir, may Allah have mercy on him, preferred this view.

Mujahid said: al-najm refers to the star in the sky. Al-Hasan and Qatadah said likewise. This view is the more apparent one, and Allah knows best, because of His saying: {Do you not see that to Allah prostrates whoever is in the heavens and whoever is on the earth, and the sun, the moon, the stars, the mountains, the trees, the moving creatures, and many of the people...} [al-Hajj: 18].

His saying, {And the heaven He raised, and He set up the balance}, meaning: justice, as He said: {We have certainly sent Our messengers with clear proofs and sent down with them the Book and the balance so that people may maintain justice} [al-Hadid: 25]. Likewise He says here: {so that you do not transgress in the balance}, meaning: He created the heavens and the earth with truth and justice, so that all things would be conducted with truth and justice. For this reason He said: {And establish weight in justice and do not make deficient the balance}, meaning: do not shortchange the weight, but rather weigh with truth and justice, as He said: {And weigh with an even balance} [al-Shu'ara: 182].

His saying, {And the earth He laid out for the creatures}, meaning: just as He raised the heaven, He laid out the earth, spread it, and anchored it with towering, firmly-set mountains, so that it would be stable for the creatures (al-anam) upon its surface, who are the various created beings differing in their types, forms, colors, and languages, throughout all its regions and expanses. Ibn 'Abbas, Mujahid, Qatadah, and Ibn Zayd said: al-anam means creation.

{Therein is fruit}, meaning: of varying colors, tastes, and fragrances. {And the palm trees having sheaths (akmam)}: He singled this out for mention due to its nobility and its benefit, both fresh and dried. Regarding al-akmam, Ibn Jurayj reported from Ibn 'Abbas: they are the coverings of the spathe. Several other commentators said likewise: it is that from which the cluster (al-qinw) emerges, then it splits open to reveal the bunch, which is at first unripe dates, then fresh dates, then it ripens fully and reaches its perfect ripeness and maturity.

Ibn Abi Hatim mentioned from 'Amr ibn 'Ali al-Sayrafi: [chain] al-Sha'bi said: Caesar wrote to 'Umar ibn al-Khattab: "I inform you that my messengers came to me from your land, and they claimed that among you there is a tree not fit for any good, that emerges like the ears of donkeys, then splits open like pearls, then turns green so that it becomes like green emerald, then turns red so that it becomes like red ruby, then ripens and matures so that it becomes like the finest faludhaj (a sweet dessert) ever eaten, then dries so that it becomes provision for the resident and travel-food for the traveler. If my messengers have told me the truth, I consider this tree to be none other than one of the trees of Paradise." 'Umar ibn al-Khattab wrote back to him: "From 'Umar, Commander of the Believers, to Caesar, King of the Romans: your messengers have told you the truth. This tree is with us, and it is the tree that Allah caused to grow for Mary when she went into labor with her son Jesus. So fear Allah and do not take Jesus as a god besides Allah, for {the likeness of Jesus in the sight of Allah is like the likeness of Adam. He created him from dust, then said to him, 'Be,' and he was. The truth is from your Lord, so do not be among the doubters}" [Al 'Imran: 59-60].

It is also said: al-akmam refers to its fibrous covering (rufatuha), which is the fiber (al-lif) found on the neck of the palm tree. This is the view of al-Hasan and Qatadah.

{And the grain having husks, and fragrant plants}: 'Ali ibn Abi Talhah reported from Ibn 'Abbas: {And the grain having al-'asf} means straw.

Al-'Awfi reported from Ibn 'Abbas: al-'asf refers to the green leaf of the crop after its heads have been cut off; it is called al-'asf once it dries. Qatadah, al-Dahhak, and Abu Malik said likewise: its 'asf is its straw.

Ibn 'Abbas, Mujahid, and several others said: al-rayhan means the leaf.

Al-Hasan said: it is this fragrant plant of yours.

'Ali ibn Abi Talhah reported from Ibn 'Abbas: al-rayhan is the green shoot of the crop.

The meaning of this, and Allah knows best, is that grain such as wheat, barley, and the like has, while it is growing, an 'asf, which is what is on the ear of grain, and a rayhan, which is the leaf that wraps around its stalk.

It is also said: al-'asf is the leaf when the crop first sprouts as a green shoot, while al-rayhan is the leaf once it has become established and the grain has formed within it, as Zayd ibn 'Amr ibn Nufayl said in his famous poem:

"Say to him: Who causes the grain to sprout from the moist earth, so that from it the green shoot rises up swaying? And brings forth from it its grain in its heads? In that are signs indeed for one who is mindful."

His saying, {So which of the favors of your Lord do you both deny?}, meaning: which of the favors, O company of the two weighty ones, humans and jinn, do you deny? This was said by Mujahid and others, and the context following it supports this: that is, the favors are manifest upon you, and you are immersed in them, unable to deny or reject them. So we say as the believing jinn said: "O Allah, we deny none of Your favors, our Lord, so to You belongs all praise." Ibn 'Abbas used to say: "Not by any of them, O Lord," meaning: we do not deny any of them.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Asma' bint Abi Bakr said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ reciting while he was praying near the Corner, before he was commanded to proclaim openly what he had been ordered, while the polytheists were listening, {So which of the favors of your Lord do you both deny?}

تَفْسِيرُ سُورَةِ الرَّحْمَنِ

وَهِيَ مَكِّيَّةٌ.

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَفَّانُ، حَدَّثَنَا حَمَّادُ، عَنْ عَاصِمٍ، عَنْ زِرٍّ، أَنَّ رَجُلًا قَالَ لِابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ: كَيْفَ تَعْرِفُ هَذَا الْحَرْفَ: "مَاءٍ غَيْرِ يَاسِنٍ أَوْ آسِنٍ"؟ فَقَالَ: كُلَّ الْقُرْآنِ قَدْ قَرَأْتَ. قَالَ: إِنِّي لَأَقْرَأُ الْمُفَصَّلَ؛ أَجْمَعَ فِي رَكْعَةٍ وَاحِدَةٍ. فَقَالَ: أَهَذًّا كهذِّ الشِّعْرِ، لَا أَبَا لَكَ؟ قَدْ عَلِمْتُ قَرَائِنَ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ الَّتِي كَانَ يَقْرِنُ قَرِينَتَيْنِ قَرِينَتَيْنِ مِنْ أَوَّلِ الْمُفَصَّلِ، وَكَانَ أَوَّلَ مُفَصَّلِ ابْنِ مَسْعُودِ: ﴿الرَّحْمَنُ﴾ .

وَقَالَ أَبُو عِيسَى التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ وَاقِدٍ أَبُو مُسْلِمٍ، حَدَّثَنَا الوليد بن مسلم، عن زهير بن محمد، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ المُنْكَدِر، عَنْ جَابِرٍ قَالَ: خَرَجَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ عَلَى أَصْحَابِهِ فَقَرَأَ عَلَيْهِمْ سُورَةَ "الرَّحْمَنِ" مِنْ أَوَّلِهَا إِلَى آخِرِهَا، فَسَكَتُوا فَقَالَ: "لَقَدْ قَرَأْتُهَا عَلَى الْجِنِّ لَيْلَةَ الْجِنِّ، فَكَانُوا أَحْسَنَ مَرْدُودًا مِنْكُمْ، كُنْتُ كُلَّمَا أَتَيْتُ عَلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿فَبِأَيِّ آلاءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ﴾ ، قَالُوا: لَا بِشَيْءٍ مِنْ نِعَمِكَ -رَبَّنَا-نُكَذِّبُ، فَلَكَ الْحَمْدُ" .

ثُمَّ قَالَ: هَذَا حَدِيثٌ غَرِيبٌ، لَا نَعْرِفُهُ إِلَّا مِنْ حَدِيثِ الْوَلِيدِ بْنِ مُسْلِمٍ، عَنْ زُهَيْرِ بْنِ مُحَمَّدٍ. ثُمَّ حَكَى عَنِ الْإِمَامِ أَحْمَدَ أَنَّهُ كَانَ لَا يَعْرِفُهُ، يُنْكِرُ رِوَايَةَ أَهْلِ الشَّامِ عَنْ زُهَيْرِ بْنِ مُحَمَّدٍ هَذَا.

وَرَوَاهُ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو بَكْرٍ الْبَزَّارُ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ مَالِكٍ، عَنِ الْوَلِيدِ بْنِ مُسْلِمٍ. وَعَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ أَحْمَدَ بْنِ شَبَّوَيْهِ، عَنْ هِشَامِ بْنِ عَمَّارٍ، كِلَاهُمَا عَنِ الْوَلِيدِ بْنِ مُسْلِمٍ، بِهِ. ثُمَّ قَالَ: لَا نَعْرِفُهُ يُرْوَى إِلَّا مِنْ هَذَا الْوَجْهِ .

وَقَالَ أَبُو جَعْفَرِ بْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدٌ بْنُ عَبَّادِ بْنِ مُوسَى، وَعَمْرُو بْنُ مَالِكٍ الْبَصْرِيُّ قَالَا حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى بْنُ سُلَيْمٍ، عَنْ إِسْمَاعِيلَ بْنِ أُمَيَّةَ، عَنْ نَافِعٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ؛ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَرَأَ سُورَةَ "الرَّحْمَنِ" -أَوْ: قُرِئَت عِنْدَهُ-فَقَالَ: "مَا لِي أَسْمَعُ الْجِنَّ أَحْسَنَ جَوَابًا لِرَبِّهَا مِنْكُمْ؟ " قَالُوا: وَمَا ذَاكَ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ؟ قَالَ: "مَا أَتَيْتُ عَلَى قَوْلِ اللَّهِ: ﴿فَبِأَيِّ آلاءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ﴾ إِلَّا قَالَتِ الْجِنُّ: لَا بِشَيْءٍ مِنْ نِعْمَةِ رَبِّنَا نُكَذِّبُ".

وَرَوَاهُ الْحَافِظُ الْبَزَّارُ عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ مَالِكٍ، بِهِ . ثُمَّ قَالَ: لَا نَعْلَمُهُ يُرْوَى عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ إِلَّا مِنْ هَذَا الوجه بهذا الإسناد.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

* *

يُخْبِرُ تَعَالَى عَنْ فَضْلِهِ وَرَحْمَتِهِ بِخَلْقِهِ: أَنَّهُ أَنْزَلَ عَلَى عِبَادِهِ الْقُرْآنَ وَيَسَّرَ حِفْظَهُ وَفَهْمَهُ عَلَى مَنْ رَحِمَهُ، فَقَالَ: ﴿الرَّحْمَنُ. عَلَّمَ الْقُرْآنَ. خَلَقَ الإنْسَانَ. عَلَّمَهُ الْبَيَانَ﴾ قَالَ الْحَسَنُ: يَعْنِي: النُّطْقَ . وَقَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَغَيْرُهُمَا: يَعْنِي الْخَيْرَ وَالشَّرَّ. وَقَوْلُ الحسن ها هنا أَحْسَنُ وَأَقْوَى؛ لِأَنَّ السِّيَاقَ فِي تَعْلِيمِهِ تَعَالَى الْقُرْآنَ، وَهُوَ أَدَاءُ تِلَاوَتِهِ، وَإِنَّمَا يَكُونُ ذَلِكَ بِتَيْسِيرِ النُّطْقِ عَلَى الْخَلْقِ وَتَسْهِيلِ خُرُوجِ الْحُرُوفِ مِنْ مَوَاضِعِهَا مِنَ الْحَلْقِ وَاللِّسَانِ وَالشَّفَتَيْنِ، عَلَى اخْتِلَافِ مَخَارِجِهَا وَأَنْوَاعِهَا.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿الشَّمْسُ وَالْقَمَرُ بِحُسْبَانٍ﴾ أَيْ: يَجْرِيَانِ مُتَعَاقِبَيْنَ بِحِسَابٍ مُقَنَّن لَا يَخْتَلِفُ وَلَا يَضْطَرِبُ، ﴿لَا الشَّمْسُ يَنْبَغِي لَهَا أَنْ تُدْرِكَ الْقَمَرَ وَلا اللَّيْلُ سَابِقُ النَّهَارِ وَكُلٌّ فِي فَلَكٍ يَسْبَحُونَ﴾ [يس: ٤٠] ، وَقَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَالِقُ الإصْبَاحِ وَجَعَلَ اللَّيْلَ سَكَنًا وَالشَّمْسَ وَالْقَمَرَ حُسْبَانًا ذَلِكَ تَقْدِيرُ الْعَزِيزِ الْعَلِيمِ﴾ [الْأَنْعَامِ: ٩٦] .

وَعَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ أَنَّهُ قَالَ: لَوْ جَعَلَ اللَّهُ نُورَ جَمِيعِ أَبْصَارِ الْإِنْسِ وَالْجِنِّ وَالدَّوَابِّ وَالطَّيْرِ فِي عَيْنَيْ عَبْدٍ، ثُمَّ كَشَفَ حِجَابًا وَاحِدًا مِنْ سَبْعِينَ حِجَابًا دُونَ الشَّمْسِ، لَمَا اسْتَطَاعَ أَنْ يَنْظُرَ إِلَيْهَا. وَنُورُ الشَّمْسِ جُزْءٌ مِنْ سَبْعِينَ جُزْءًا مِنْ نُورِ الْكُرْسِيِّ، وَنُورُ الْكُرْسِيِّ جُزْءٌ مِنْ سَبْعِينَ جُزْءًا مِنْ نُورِ الْعَرْشِ، وَنُورُ الْعَرْشِ جُزْءٌ مِنْ سَبْعِينَ جُزْءًا مِنْ نُورِ السِّتْرِ. فَانْظُرْ مَاذَا أَعْطَى اللَّهُ عَبْدَهُ مِنَ النُّورِ فِي عَيْنَيْهِ وَقْتَ النَّظَرِ إِلَى وَجْهِ رَبِّهِ الْكَرِيمِ عِيَانًا. رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَالنَّجْمُ وَالشَّجَرُ يَسْجُدَانِ﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: اخْتَلَفَ الْمُفَسِّرُونَ فِي مَعْنَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَالنَّجْمُ﴾ بَعْدَ إِجْمَاعِهِمْ عَلَى أَنَّ الشَّجَرَ مَا قَامَ عَلَى سَاقٍ، فَرَوَى عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: النَّجْمُ مَا انْبَسَطَ عَلَى وَجْهِ الْأَرْضِ -يَعْنِي مِنَ النَّبَاتِ. وَكَذَا قَالَ سَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ، وَسُفْيَانُ الثَّوْرِيُّ. وَقَدِ اخْتَارَهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ.

وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: النَّجْمُ الَّذِي فِي السَّمَاءِ. وَكَذَا قَالَ الْحَسَنُ وقَتَادَةُ. وَهَذَا الْقَوْلُ هُوَ الْأَظْهَرُ وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ؛ لِقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿أَلَمْ تَرَ أَنَّ اللَّهَ يَسْجُدُ لَهُ مَنْ فِي السَّمَوَاتِ وَمَنْ فِي الأرْضِ وَالشَّمْسُ وَالْقَمَرُ وَالنُّجُومُ وَالْجِبَالُ وَالشَّجَرُ وَالدَّوَابُّ وَكَثِيرٌ مِنَ النَّاسِ﴾ الْآيَةَ [الْحَجِّ: ١٨] .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَالسَّمَاءَ رَفَعَهَا وَوَضَعَ الْمِيزَانَ﴾ يَعْنِي: الْعَدْلَ، كَمَا قَالَ: ﴿لَقَدْ أَرْسَلْنَا رُسُلَنَا بِالْبَيِّنَاتِ وَأَنزلْنَا مَعَهُمُ الْكِتَابَ وَالْمِيزَانَ لِيَقُومَ النَّاسُ بِالْقِسْطِ﴾ [الْحَدِيدِ: ٢٥] ، وَهَكَذَا قَالَ هَاهُنَا: ﴿أَلا تَطْغَوْا فِي الْمِيزَانِ﴾ أي: خلق السموات وَالْأَرْضَ بِالْحَقِّ وَالْعَدْلِ، لِتَكُونَ الْأَشْيَاءُ كُلُّهَا بِالْحَقِّ وَالْعَدْلِ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿وَأَقِيمُوا الْوَزْنَ بِالْقِسْطِ وَلا تُخْسِرُوا الْمِيزَانَ﴾ أَيْ: لَا تَبْخَسُوا الْوَزْنَ، بَلْ زِنوا بِالْحَقِّ وَالْقِسْطِ، كَمَا قَالَ [تَعَالَى] ﴿وَزِنُوا بِالْقِسْطَاسِ الْمُسْتَقِيمِ﴾ [الشُّعَرَاءِ: ١٨٢] .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَالأرْضَ وَضَعَهَا لِلأنَامِ﴾ أَيْ: كَمَا رَفَعَ السَّمَاءَ وَضَعَ الْأَرْضَ وَمَهَّدَهَا، وَأَرْسَاهَا بِالْجِبَالِ الرَّاسِيَاتِ الشَّامِخَاتِ، لِتَسْتَقِرَّ لِمَا عَلَى وَجْهِهَا مِنَ الْأَنَامِ، وَهُمُ: الْخَلَائِقُ الْمُخْتَلِفَةُ أَنْوَاعُهُمْ وَأَشْكَالُهُمْ وَأَلْوَانُهُمْ وَأَلْسِنَتُهُمْ، فِي سَائِرِ أَقْطَارِهَا وَأَرْجَائِهَا. قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ وَمُجَاهِدٌ وَقَتَادَةُ وَابْنُ زَيْدٍ: الْأَنَامُ: الْخَلْقُ.

﴿فِيهَا فَاكِهَةٌ﴾ أَيْ: مُخْتَلِفَةُ الْأَلْوَانِ وَالطُّعُومِ وَالرَّوَائِحِ، ﴿وَالنَّخْلُ ذَاتُ الأكْمَامِ﴾ أَفْرَدَهُ بِالذِّكْرِ لِشَرَفِهِ وَنَفْعِهِ، رَطْبًا وَيَابِسًا. وَالْأَكْمَامُ -قَالَ ابْنُ جُرَيْج عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: هِيَ أَوْعِيَةُ الطَّلْعِ. وَهَكَذَا قَالَ غَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ مِنَ الْمُفَسِّرِينَ، وَهُوَ الَّذِي يَطْلُعُ فِيهِ الْقِنْوُ ثُمَّ يَنْشَقُّ عَنِ الْعُنْقُودِ، فَيَكُونُ بُسْرًا ثُمَّ رُطَبًا، ثُمَّ يَنْضَجُ وَيَتَنَاهَى يَنْعُه وَاسْتِوَاؤُهُ.

قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ ذُكِرَ عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ عَلِيٍّ الصَّيْرَفِيِّ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو قُتَيْبَةَ، حَدَّثَنَا يُونُسُ بْنُ الْحَارِثِ الطَّائِفِيُّ، عَنِ الشَّعْبِيِّ قَالَ: كَتَبَ قَيْصَرُ إِلَى عُمَرَ بْنِ الْخَطَّابِ: أُخْبِرُكَ أَنَّ رُسُلِي أَتَتْنِي مِنْ قِبَلِكَ، فَزَعَمَتْ أَنَّ قِبَلَكُمْ شَجَرَةً لَيْسَتْ بِخَلِيقَةٍ لِشَيْءٍ مِنَ الْخَيْرِ، تَخْرُجُ مِثْلَ آذَانِ الْحَمِيرِ، ثُمَّ تَشَقَّقُ مِثْلَ اللُّؤْلُؤِ، ثُمَّ تَخْضَرُّ فَتَكُونُ مِثْلَ الزُّمُرُّدِ الْأَخْضَرِ، ثُمَّ تَحْمَرُّ فَتَكُونُ كَالْيَاقُوتِ الْأَحْمَرِ، ثُمَّ تَيْنَع وَتَنْضَجُ فَتَكُونُ كَأَطْيَبِ فَالَوْذَجَ أُكِل، ثُمَّ تَيْبَسُ فَتَكُونُ عِصْمَةً لِلْمُقِيمِ وَزَادًا لِلْمُسَافِرِ، فَإِنْ تَكُنْ رُسُلِي صَدَقَتْنِي فَلَا أَرَى هَذِهِ الشَّجَرَةَ إِلَّا مِنْ شَجَرِ الْجَنَّةِ. فَكَتَبَ إِلَيْهِ عُمَرُ بْنُ الْخَطَّابِ مِنْ عُمَرَ أَمِيرِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ إِلَى قَيْصَرَ مِلْكِ الرُّومِ، إِنَّ رُسُلَكَ قَدْ صَدَقُوكَ ، هَذِهِ الشَّجَرَةُ عِنْدَنَا، وَهِيَ الشَّجَرَةُ الَّتِي أَنْبَتَهَا اللَّهُ عَلَى مَرْيَمَ حِينَ نُفِسَتْ بِعِيسَى ابْنِهَا، فَاتَّقِ اللَّهَ وَلَا تَتَّخِذْ عِيسَى إِلَهًا مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ، فَإِنَّ ﴿مَثَلَ عِيسَى عِنْدَ اللَّهِ كَمَثَلِ آدَمَ خَلَقَهُ مِنْ تُرَابٍ ثُمَّ قَالَ لَهُ كُنْ فَيَكُونُ. الْحَقُّ مِنْ رَبِّكَ فَلا تَكُنْ مِنَ الْمُمْتَرِينَ﴾ [آلِ عِمْرَانَ: ٥٩، ٦٠] .

وَقِيلَ: الْأَكْمَامُ رُفَاتُهَا، وَهُوَ: اللِّيفُ الَّذِي عَلَى عُنُقِ النَّخْلَةِ. وَهُوَ قَوْلُ الْحَسَنِ وقَتَادَةَ.

﴿وَالْحَبُّ ذُو الْعَصْفِ وَالرَّيْحَانُ﴾ قَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿وَالْحَبُّ ذُو الْعَصْفِ﴾ يعني: التبن.

وَقَالَ العَوْفي، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿الْعَصْفِ﴾ وَرَقُ الزرع الأخضر الذي قطع رؤوسه، فَهُوَ يُسَمَّى الْعَصْفَ إِذَا يَبِسَ. وَكَذَا قَالَ قَتَادَةُ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَأَبُو مَالِكٍ: عَصْفُهُ: تِبْنُهُ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ: ﴿وَالرَّيْحَانُ﴾ يَعْنِي: الْوَرَقَ.

وَقَالَ الْحَسَنُ: هُوَ رَيْحَانُكُمْ هَذَا.

وَقَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عباس: ﴿وَالرَّيْحَانُ﴾ خَضِرُ الزَّرْعِ.

وَمَعْنَى هَذَا -وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ-أَنَّ الْحَبَّ كَالْقَمْحِ وَالشَّعِيرِ وَنَحْوِهِمَا لَهُ فِي حَالِ نَبَاتِهِ عَصْفٌ، وَهُوَ: مَا عَلَى السُّنْبُلَةِ، وَرَيْحَانٌ، وَهُوَ: الْوَرَقُ الْمُلْتَفُّ عَلَى سَاقِهَا.

وَقِيلَ: الْعَصْفُ: الْوَرَقُ أَوَّلَ مَا يُنْبِتُ الزَّرْعُ بَقْلًا. وَالرَّيْحَانُ: الْوَرَقُ، يَعْنِي: إِذَا أَدْجَنَ وَانْعَقَدَ فِيهِ الْحَبُّ. كَمَا قَالَ زَيْدُ بْنُ عَمْرِو بْنِ نُفَيْلٍ فِي قَصِيدَتِهِ الْمَشْهُورَةِ.

وَقُولا لَهُ: مَنْ يُنْبِتُ الحَبَّ فِي الثَّرى ... فَيُصْبِحَ مِنْهُ البقلُ يَهْتَزُّ رابيا؟ ...

وَيُخْرجَ منْه حَبَّه في رُؤوسه؟ ... فَفي ذَاكَ آياتٌ لِمَنْ كَانَ وَاعِيَا 

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَبِأَيِّ آلاءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ﴾ أَيْ: فَبِأَيِّ الْآلَاءِ -يَا مَعْشَرَ الثَّقَلَيْنِ، مِنَ الْإِنْسِ وَالْجِنِّ-تُكَذِّبَانِ؟ قَالَهُ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ. وَيَدُلُّ عَلَيْهِ السِّيَاقُ بَعْدَهُ، أَيِ: النِّعَمُ ظَاهِرَةٌ عَلَيْكُمْ وَأَنْتُمْ مَغْمُورُونَ بِهَا، لَا تَسْتَطِيعُونَ إِنْكَارَهَا وَلَا جُحُودَهَا ، فَنَحْنُ نَقُولُ كَمَا قَالَتِ الْجِنُّ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ: "اللَّهُمَّ، وَلَا بِشَيْءٍ مِنْ آلَائِكَ رَبَّنَا نكذِّب، فَلَكَ الْحَمْدُ". وَكَانَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ يَقُولُ: "لَا بأيِّها يَا رَبِّ". أَيْ: لَا نُكَذِّبُ بِشَيْءٍ مِنْهَا.

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى بْنُ إِسْحَاقَ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ لَهِيعَةَ، عَنْ أَبِي الْأَسْوَدِ، عَنْ عُرْوَة، عَنْ أَسْمَاءَ بِنْتِ أَبِي بَكْرٍ قَالَتْ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم وهو يَقْرَأُ، وَهُوَ يُصَلِّي نَحْوَ الرُّكْنِ قَبْلَ أَنْ يُصْدَعَ بِمَا يُؤْمَرُ، وَالْمُشْرِكُونَ يَسْتَمِعُونَ ﴿فَبِأَيِّ آلاءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ﴾ .

## 55:14-25 — verses 14-25 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He, Exalted, mentions His creation of man from clay resembling pottery, and His creation of the jinn from a smokeless flame of fire, which is: the tip of its blaze. This was said by al-Dahhak, from Ibn Abbas. And Ikrimah, Mujahid, al-Hasan, and Ibn Zayd say the same.

Al-Awfi said, from Ibn Abbas: {min marijin min nar} means from the blaze of the fire, from the finest part of it.

Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn Abbas: {min marijin min nar} means from the purest part of the fire. Ikrimah, Mujahid, al-Dahhak and others said likewise.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Aishah said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "The angels were created from light, the jinn were created from a smokeless flame of fire, and Adam was created from what has been described to you."

Muslim also related it, from Muhammad ibn Rafi' and Abd ibn Humayd, both from Abd al-Razzaq, with the same chain.

His saying: {So which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?} has already been explained.

{Lord of the two easts and Lord of the two wests} means: the east of summer and the east of winter, and the west of summer and the west of winter. In another verse He said: {So I swear by the Lord of the risings and settings} [al-Ma'arij: 40], and that refers to the different points where the sun rises and its movement each day, and its appearance from that point to the people. In yet another verse He said: {Lord of the east and the west, there is no god but He, so take Him as a Guardian} [al-Muzzammil: 9]. What is meant there is the entire category of easts and wests. And since there are benefits for creation, both jinn and men, in the differing of these easts and wests, He said: {So which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?}

His saying: {He released the two seas, meeting together} Ibn Abbas said: meaning, He sent them forth.

Concerning His saying: {meeting together}, Ibn Zayd said: meaning, He prevented them from meeting, by means of the barzakh (barrier) He placed between them, the dividing barrier separating the two.

What is meant by His saying, {the two seas}, is the salty and the sweet. The sweet refers to these rivers that flow freely among people. We have already discussed this in Surat al-Furqan, at His saying: {And it is He who released the two seas, this one sweet and fresh and this one salty and bitter, and He placed between them a barzakh and a forbidding barrier} [al-Furqan: 53]. Ibn Jarir preferred here that what is meant by "the two seas" is the sea of the sky and the sea of the earth, and this is reported from Mujahid, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, Atiyyah, and Ibn Abza.

Ibn Jarir said: because pearls are generated from the water of the sky and the shells of the sea of the earth. Even if this is so, that is not what is meant here, for the wording does not support it. He, Exalted, said: {Between them is a barzakh which they do not transgress}, meaning, He placed between them a barzakh, which is a barrier of land, so that neither of them would transgress against the other, for each would otherwise corrupt the other and remove it from the quality that is intended of it. And what is between the sky and the earth is not called a barzakh or a forbidding barrier.

His saying: {From both of them emerge pearls and coral} means: from the combination of the two, and if that is found in only one of them, it suffices, as He, Exalted, said: {O company of jinn and men, did there not come to you messengers from among yourselves?} [al-An'am: 130], while messengers came only from among men, not from jinn, yet this general phrasing is sound. Pearls (lu'lu') are well known, but as for coral (marjan), it is said: it refers to small pearls. This was said by Mujahid, Qatadah, Abu Razin, and al-Dahhak, and it is reported from Ali.

It is also said: it refers to the large and fine pearls. Ibn Jarir related this from some of the early scholars. Ibn Abi Hatim related it from al-Rabi' ibn Anas, and related it from al-Suddi, from someone who told him, from Ibn Abbas. Something similar is reported from Ali, Mujahid also, and Murrah al-Hamdani.

It is also said: it is a type of red-colored gem. Al-Suddi said, from Abu Malik, from Masruq, from Abdullah, who said: coral is the red bead. Al-Suddi said: it is called "al-bussadh" in Persian.

As for His saying: {And from each you eat tender meat and extract ornaments which you wear} [Fatir: 12], the meat comes from both the bitter and the sweet, but the ornaments come only from the salty, not from the sweet.

Ibn Abbas said: no drop ever falls from the sky into the sea and lands in a shell without becoming a pearl from it. Ikrimah said the same, and added: if it does not land in a shell, ambergris grows from it. Something similar is reported through other chains from Ibn Abbas.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Ibn Abbas said: When the sky rains, the shells in the sea open their mouths, and whatever falls into them, meaning of the raindrops, becomes pearls.

Its chain is sound. And since the acquisition of this ornament is a blessing upon the people of the earth, He reminded them of it by saying: {So which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?}

His saying: {And His are the ships raised up} means: the ships that sail upon the sea. Mujahid said: whatever ship has its sail raised is "munsha'ah" (raised up), and whatever has not had its sail raised is not "munsha'ah." Qatadah said: {al-munsha'at} means the created things. Others said: al-munshi'at, with a kasrah on the shin, means those that initiate movement.

{Like mountains} means: like mountains in their great size, and what they carry of goods and profits transported from one region to another and from one land to another, containing benefit for people in bringing what they need of various kinds of merchandise. For this reason He said: {So which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?}

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Amirah ibn Sa'd said: I was with Ali ibn Abi Talib, may Allah be pleased with him, on the bank of the Euphrates, when a ship appeared with its sail raised. He spread out his hands and said: Allah, Mighty and Majestic, says: {And His are the ships raised up upon the sea like mountains}. By the One who created them, sailing in the sea by His power, I did not kill Uthman, nor did I conspire in his killing.

## 55:26-30 — verses 26-30 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted informs us that all the inhabitants of the earth will pass away and all of them will die, and likewise the inhabitants of the heavens, except whom Allah wills, and no one will remain except His noble Face. For the Lord, exalted and sanctified is He, does not die. Rather He is the Living One who never dies at all.

Qatadah said: He informed of what He created, then He informed that all of that would perish.

In a transmitted supplication: "O Living One, O Sustainer, O Originator of the heavens and the earth, O Possessor of Majesty and Honor, there is no god but You, by Your mercy we seek help, set right for us all our affairs, and do not leave us to ourselves for the blink of an eye, nor to any of Your creation."

Al-Sha'bi said: When you recite {Everyone upon it will perish}, do not fall silent until you recite {and there will remain the Face of your Lord, Possessor of Majesty and Honor}.

This verse is like His saying, Exalted is He: {Everything is destined to perish except His Face} [al-Qasas: 88]. And the Exalted described His noble Face in this noble verse as being {Possessor of Majesty and Honor}, meaning He is worthy of being held in majesty so that He is not disobeyed, and of being obeyed so that He is not opposed. This is like His saying: {And be patient with yourself among those who call upon their Lord morning and evening, desiring His Face} [al-Kahf: 28], and like His saying, reporting of those who give charity: {We only feed you for the sake of Allah's Face} [al-Insan: 9].

Ibn Abbas said: {Possessor of Majesty and Honor} means Possessor of Greatness and Pride.

And when He informed of the equal fate of all the inhabitants of the earth in death, and that they will proceed to the abode of the Hereafter, where the Possessor of Majesty and Honor will judge them with His just judgment, He said: {So which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?}

His saying: {Whoever is in the heavens and the earth asks of Him; every day He is in a matter (sha'n)}. This is a report about His self-sufficiency from all else, and the dependence of all creatures upon Him at every moment, and that they ask Him with the language of their state and their speech, and that every day He is engaged in some matter.

Al-A'mash reported from Mujahid, from Ubayd ibn Umayr: regarding {every day He is in a matter}, he said: Among His matters is that He answers one who calls upon Him, or gives to one who asks, or frees one who is captive, or heals one who is sick.

Ibn Abi Najih reported from Mujahid, who said: Every day He answers one who calls, removes distress, answers one in dire need, and forgives a sin.

Qatadah said: The inhabitants of the heavens and the earth cannot do without Him. He gives life to the living, and brings death to the dying, He raises up the young, and frees the captive. He is the ultimate resort of the needs of the righteous and their cry for help, and the ultimate resort of their complaints.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Suwayd ibn Jabalah, that is al-Fazari, said: Indeed your Lord, every day He is in a matter. He frees necks (from bondage), He grants desires, and He casts down in punishment.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Munib ibn Abdullah ibn Munib al-Azdi, from his father, said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ recited this verse: {every day He is in a matter}, so we said: O Messenger of Allah, what is that matter? He said: "That He forgives a sin, relieves a distress, raises up some people, and puts down others."

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] I heard Yunus ibn Maysarah ibn Halbas narrating from Umm al-Darda, from Abu al-Darda, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "Allah, Mighty and Majestic, said: {every day He is in a matter}." He said: "Among His matters is that He forgives a sin, relieves a distress, raises up some people, and puts down others."

Ibn Asakir narrated it through multiple chains from Hisham ibn Ammar, with the same wording. Then he traced it from the hadith of Abu Hammam al-Walid ibn Shuja, from al-Wazir ibn Sabih, who said: al-Walid ibn Muslim guided us to it, from Mutarrif, from al-Sha'bi, from Umm al-Darda, from Abu al-Darda, from the Prophet ﷺ, and he mentioned it. He said: The correct one is the first, meaning its first chain of transmission.

I say: it has also been reported as mawquf (halted report), as al-Bukhari attached it in the form of a definite assertion, making it from the speech of Abu al-Darda. And Allah knows best.

Al-Bazzar said: [chain] Muhammad ibn Abd al-Rahman ibn al-Baylamani, from his father, from Ibn Umar, from the Prophet ﷺ: {every day He is in a matter}, he said: "He forgives a sin, and He removes a distress."

Then Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Abu Hamzah al-Thumali, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, from Ibn Abbas, that Allah created a Preserved Tablet (al-Lawh al-Mahfuz) of white pearl, its two covers of red ruby, its pen of light, and its writing of light, its width being that between heaven and earth. He looks into it every day three hundred and sixty times, creating with every glance, giving life and causing death, granting might and bringing low, and doing what He wills.

ثُمَّ قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: وَحَدَّثَنَا أَبُو كُرَيْب، حَدَّثَنَا عُبَيْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ مُوسَى، عَنْ أَبِي حَمْزَةَ الثُّمَالي، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْر، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، أَنَّ اللَّهَ خَلَقَ لَوْحًا مَحْفُوظًا مِنْ دُرَّةٍ بَيْضَاءَ، دَفَّتَاهُ يَاقُوتَةٌ حَمْرَاءُ، قَلَمُهُ نُورٌ، وَكِتَابُهُ نُورٌ، عَرْضُهُ مَا بَيْنَ السَّمَاءِ وَالْأَرْضِ، يَنْظُرُ فِيهِ كُلَّ يوم ثلثمائة وَسِتِّينَ نَظْرَةً، يَخْلُقُ فِي كُلِّ نَظْرَةٍ، وَيُحْيِي وَيُمِيتُ، وَيُعِزُّ وَيُذِلُّ، وَيَفْعَلُ مَا يَشَاءُ .

## 55:31-36 — verses 31-36 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn Abbas, concerning His saying: {We shall attend to you, O you two heavy burdens} that he said: this is a threat from Allah to His servants, and it is not that Allah has any preoccupation and is then free. Al-Dahhak said likewise: this is a threat. Qatadah said: a time of Allah's attending to His creation has drawn near. Ibn Juraij said: {We shall attend to you} means, We shall settle your affairs.

Al-Bukhari said: it means, We shall call you to account; nothing preoccupies Him from anything else. This is a well-known usage in the speech of the Arabs; one says, "I shall certainly attend to you," when there is no preoccupation with him at all, meaning, "I shall certainly seize you when you are off guard."

As for His saying, {O you two heavy burdens}: the two heavy burdens are mankind and the jinn, as it has come in the authentic hadith: "Every thing hears it except the two heavy burdens," and in another version: "except the jinn and mankind." And in the hadith of the Trumpet: "the two heavy burdens are mankind and the jinn." {So which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?}

Then He said: {O company of jinn and mankind, if you are able to pass beyond the regions of the heavens and the earth, then pass beyond them; you will not pass beyond them except by authority}, meaning: you are not able to flee from Allah's command and decree; rather, He encompasses you, and you have no power to escape His judgment, nor to pass beyond His ruling over you. Wherever you go, you are encompassed. This is at the standing place of the gathering, where the angels surround the creatures in seven rows on every side, so that no one is able to go anywhere {except by authority}, meaning, except by Allah's command. {The man will say on that day, "Where is the escape?" No indeed, there is no refuge. To your Lord on that day is the final place of rest} [al-Qiyamah: 10-12]. And He, exalted, said: {And for those who have earned evil deeds, the recompense of an evil deed is the like of it, and abasement will cover them; they will have no protector from Allah. It is as if their faces were covered with pieces of the darkest night. Those are the companions of the Fire; they will abide therein forever} [Yunus: 27]. It is for this reason that He said: {There will be sent upon you both a flame of fire and molten copper, and you will not be helped}.

Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn Abbas: the shuwaz is the flame of the fire.

Sa'id ibn Jubayr said, from Ibn Abbas: the shuwaz is smoke.

Mujahid said: it is the green flame that breaks off. Abu Salih said: the shuwaz is the flame that is above the fire and below the smoke. Al-Dahhak said: {a shuwaz of fire} is a flowing stream of fire.

As for His saying, {and copper}, Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn Abbas: {and copper} is the smoke of the fire. The like of this has been narrated from Abu Salih, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, and Abu Sinan.

Ibn Jarir said: the Arabs call smoke nuhas, with either damma or kasra on the nun, and the reciters are agreed on the damma. An example of nuhas meaning smoke is the saying of Nabighah of Ja'dah:

"It shines like the light of a lamp of sesame oil, in which Allah has placed no nuhas,"

meaning smoke, so he said.

Al-Tabarani related, through the route of Juwaybir, from al-Dahhak, that Nafi' ibn al-Azraq asked Ibn Abbas about the shuwaz, and he said: it is the flame that has no smoke with it. He then asked him for a witness to that from the language, so he recited to him the saying of Umayyah ibn Abi al-Salt about Hassan:

"Is there one to convey to Hassan from me a message that creeps stealthily to Ukaz,

was not your father among us a blacksmith, base among the blacksmiths in defending honor,

a Yemeni who kept working the bellows and blowing steadily the flame of the shuwaz?"

He said: you have spoken truly. Then what is nuhas? He said: it is the smoke that has no flame. He said: do the Arabs know this usage? He said: yes, have you not heard the Nabighah of the Banu Dhubyan say:

"It shines like the light of a lamp of sesame oil, in which Allah has placed no nuhas."

Mujahid said: nuhas is brass, which is melted and poured upon their heads. Qatadah said likewise. Al-Dahhak said: {and copper} is a flowing stream of copper.

The meaning, according to every view, is: if you were to flee on the Day of Resurrection, the angels and the guardians of Hell would turn you back by sending flame from the fire and molten copper upon you, so that you would return. It is for this that He said: {and you will not be helped. So which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?}

## 55:37-45 — verses 37-45 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He, exalted, says: {And when the heaven splits apart} on the Day of Resurrection, as this verse indicates, along with similar verses that came bearing the same meaning, such as His saying: {And the heaven will split, and that Day it will be frail} [al-Haqqah: 16], and His saying: {And the Day the heaven will be split open with clouds and the angels will be sent down in successive descent} [al-Furqan: 25], and His saying: {When the heaven splits apart, and has listened to its Lord and was obligated to} [al-Inshiqaq: 1-2].

As for His saying: {and it will become rose-colored like oil}, meaning it will melt as dregs and silver melt when smelted, and it will change color as dyes used for coloring change, sometimes red, sometimes yellow, sometimes blue, sometimes green. That is because of the severity of the matter and the terror of the great Day of Resurrection. Imam Ahmad said:

[chain] ... Anas ibn Malik said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "People will be resurrected on the Day of Resurrection while the sky rains lightly upon them."

Al-Jawhari said: al-tashsh is light rain.

Al-Dahhak reported from Ibn Abbas concerning His saying {rose-colored like oil}: he said it is red tanned leather. Abu Kudaynah reported from Qabus, from his father, from Ibn Abbas: {and it will become rose-colored like oil}: like a rose-colored (ward) horse. Al-Awfi reported from Ibn Abbas: its color will change. Abu Salih said: like a rose-colored packhorse, then afterward it becomes like oil.

Al-Baghawi and others have related that the rose-colored horse is yellow in spring and red in winter, and when the cold intensifies its color becomes dusty gray.

Al-Hasan al-Basri said: it will take on various colors. Al-Suddi said: it will be like the color of a rose-colored mule, and it will be like molten metal, like the dregs of oil. Mujahid said: {like oil}: like the colors of dyes. Ata al-Khurasani said: like the color of rose oil in its yellowness. Qatadah said: today it is green, but on that Day its color will tend toward redness, a day of various colors. Abu al-Jawza said: in the clarity of oil. [Abu Salih] Ibn Jurayj said: the sky will become like melted oil, and that is when the heat of Hell strikes it.

As for His saying: {So on that Day no human or jinn will be asked about his sin}, this is like His saying: {This is a Day in which they will not speak, nor will they be permitted to make excuses} [al-Mursalat: 35-36]. This describes one state, while there is another state in which creatures will be questioned about all their deeds, as Allah, exalted, said: {So by your Lord, We will surely question them all together, about what they used to do} [al-Hijr: 92-93]. For this reason, Qatadah said concerning {So on that Day no human or jinn will be asked about his sin}: there was indeed a questioning, then the mouths of the people were sealed, and their hands and feet spoke of what they used to do.

Ali ibn Abi Talhah reported from Ibn Abbas: He will not ask them, "Did you do such and such?" because He knows that better than they do, but rather He will say, "Why did you do such and such?" This is a second interpretation.

Mujahid said concerning this verse: the angels will not ask the criminal, for they will be recognized by their marks.

This is a third interpretation. It seems this occurs after the order has been given for them to be taken to the Fire; at that time they will not be asked about their sins but will simply be driven to it and cast into it, as Allah, exalted, said: {The criminals will be known by their marks}, meaning by signs that appear on them.

Al-Hasan and Qatadah said: they will be recognized by the blackness of their faces and the blueness of their eyes.

I say: this is comparable to how the believers will be recognized by the radiant markings (ghurrah and tahjil) that are traces of ablution (wudu).

As for His saying: {and they will be seized by the forelocks and the feet}, meaning the guardian angels (zabaniyah) will gather each one's forelock together with his feet, and cast him into the Fire in that condition.

Al-Amash reported from Ibn Abbas: he will be seized by his forelock and his foot, and broken as firewood is broken to fit into an oven.

Al-Dahhak said: his forelock and his feet will be joined together with a chain behind his back.

Al-Suddi said: the forelock of the disbeliever will be joined with his feet, his forelock bound to his foot, and his back twisted.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] ... A man from Kindah said: I came to Aisha and entered upon her, with a curtain between us, and I said: Did the Messenger of Allah ﷺ tell you that there will come upon him a moment in which he will have no power to intercede for anyone? She said: Yes, I asked him about this while he and I were under one covering. He said: "Yes, when the Bridge (Sirat) is set up, I will have no power to intercede for anyone until I know where I myself will be led. And on the Day when some faces turn white and some faces turn black, until I see what will be done with me," or he said, "what will be revealed to me, and at the Bridge, when it becomes sharp and becomes hot." She said: What does 'becomes sharp' and 'becomes hot' mean? He said: "It becomes sharp until it is like the edge of a sword, and it becomes hot until it is like a burning ember. As for the believer, he crosses over it and it does not harm him, but as for the hypocrite, he clings to it until, when he reaches its middle, he falls headlong from his footing, his hand plunging down toward his feet, and the guardian angels strike him with a hook in his forelock and his foot and hurl him into Hell, where he falls for a distance of fifty years." I said: What is the weight of the man? She said: The weight of ten fat pregnant she-camels. So on that Day the criminals will be recognized by their marks, and they will be seized by the forelocks and the feet.

This is a gharib (singular) hadith, indeed extremely gharib, and it contains wordings whose attribution to the Prophet ﷺ is objectionable (munkar). In its chain there is someone unnamed, and a report like this cannot be used as evidence. And Allah knows best.

As for His saying: {This is the Hell which the criminals used to deny}, meaning: this is the Fire whose existence you used to deny; here it is, present, and you behold it with your own eyes. This will be said to them as a rebuke, a reproach, a belittlement, and a humiliation.

As for His saying: {They will circulate between it and scalding water, heated to the utmost}, meaning: at one time they will be tormented in the blazing Fire, and at another time they will be given to drink from the scalding water, which is a drink like molten copper that cuts through the intestines and inner organs. This is like His saying, exalted: {When the shackles will be around their necks and the chains, they will be dragged, in the scalding water, then in the Fire they will be burned} [Ghafir: 71-72].

As for His saying {heated to the utmost}: meaning hot, having reached the extreme limit of heat, unbearable due to its intensity.

Ibn Abbas said concerning His saying: {They will circulate between it and scalding water, heated to the utmost}: its boiling has reached its peak and its heat has intensified. Mujahid, Said ibn Jubayr, al-Dahhak, al-Hasan, al-Thawri, and al-Suddi said the same.

Qatadah said: its cooking has reached completion since Allah created the heavens and the earth. Muhammad ibn Kab al-Qurazi said: the servant will be seized and his forelock moved about in that scalding water until the flesh melts away and only the bone and the two eyes remain in the head. This is like what Allah, exalted, says: {in the scalding water, then in the Fire they will be burned}. And "al-hamim al-an" means the hot one. From al-Qurazi there is another narration: {scalding water, heated to the utmost} means present. This is also the view of Ibn Zayd, and "present" does not contradict what was first reported from al-Qurazi, that it means hot, as in His saying, exalted: {They will be given to drink from a boiling spring} [al-Ghashiyah: 5], meaning hot, extremely hot, unbearable. And like His saying: {without awaiting its readiness} [al-Ahzab: 53], meaning its being cooked and ripened. So His saying {scalding water, heated to the utmost} means scalding water, extremely hot.

Since the punishment of the sinful transgressors and the blessing of the God-fearing are both part of His favor, mercy, justice, and kindness toward His creation, and since His warning to them of His punishment and might is among the things that restrain them from the shirk, sins, and other things in which they are engaged, He said, reminding His creation of this favor: {So which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?}

حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ الْمَلِكِ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ أَبِي الصَّهْبَاءِ، حَدَّثَنَا نَافِعٌ أَبُو غَالِبٍ الْبَاهِلِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا أَنَسِ بْنِ مَالِكٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم: "يُبْعَثُ النَّاسُ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ وَالسَّمَاءُ تَطِش عَلَيْهِمْ" .

قَالَ الْجَوْهَرِيُّ: الطَّشُّ: الْمَطَرُ الضَّعِيفُ.

وَقَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَرْدَةً كَالدِّهَانِ﴾ ، قَالَ: هُوَ الْأَدِيمُ الْأَحْمَرُ. وَقَالَ أَبُو كُدَيْنة، عَنْ قَابُوسٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿فَكَانَتْ وَرْدَةً كَالدِّهَانِ﴾ : كَالْفَرَسِ الْوَرِدِ. وَقَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: تَغَيَّرَ لَوْنُهَا. وَقَالَ أَبُو صَالِحٍ: كالبِرْذَون الْوَرِدِ، ثُمَّ كَانَتْ بَعْدُ كَالدِّهَانِ.

وَحَكَى البَغَوي وَغَيْرُهُ: أَنَّ الْفَرَسَ الْوَرِدَ تَكُونُ فِي الرَّبِيعِ صَفْرَاءَ، وَفِي الشِّتَاءِ حَمْرَاءَ، فَإِذَا اشْتَدَّ الْبَرْدُ اغْبَرَّ لَوْنُهَا.

وَقَالَ الْحَسَنُ الْبَصْرِيُّ: تَكُونُ أَلْوَانًا. وَقَالَ السُّدِّيُّ. تَكُونُ كَلَوْنِ الْبَغْلَةِ الْوَرِدَةِ، وَتَكُونُ كَالْمُهْلِ كَدُرْدِيِّ الزَّيْتَ. وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: ﴿كَالدِّهَان﴾ : كَأَلْوَانِ الدِّهَانِ. وَقَالَ عَطَاءٌ الْخُرَاسَانِيُّ: كَلَوْنِ دُهْن الوَرْد فِي الصُّفْرَةِ. وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: هِيَ الْيَوْمَ خَضْرَاءُ، وَيَوْمَئِذٍ لَوْنُهَا إِلَى الْحُمْرَةِ يَوْمَ ذي ألوان. وقال أبو الجوزاء: في صفاء الدهن. وقال [أبو صالح] بن جُرَيْجٍ: تَصِيرُ السَّمَاءُ كَالدُّهْنِ الذَّائِبِ، وَذَلِكَ حِينَ يُصيبها حَرُّ جَهَنَّمَ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَيَوْمَئِذٍ لَا يُسْأَلُ عَنْ ذَنْبِهِ إِنْسٌ وَلا جَانٌّ﴾ ، وَهَذِهِ كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿هَذَا يَوْمُ لَا يَنْطِقُونَ. وَلا يُؤْذَنُ لَهُمْ فَيَعْتَذِرُونَ﴾ [الْمُرْسَلَاتِ:٣٥، ٣٦] ، فَهَذَا فِي حَالٍ، وثَمّ حَالٌ يُسْأَلُ الْخَلَائِقُ فِيهَا عَنْ جَمِيعِ أَعْمَالِهِمْ، قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَوَرَبِّكَ لَنَسْأَلَنَّهُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ. عَمَّا كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ﴾ [الْحِجْرِ:٩٢، ٩٣] ؛ وَلِهَذِهِ قَالَ قَتَادَةُ: ﴿فَيَوْمَئِذٍ لَا يُسْأَلُ عَنْ ذَنْبِهِ إِنْسٌ وَلا جَانٌّ﴾ ، قَالَ: قَدْ كَانَتْ مَسْأَلَةٌ، ثُمَّ خُتِمَ عَلَى أَفْوَاهِ الْقَوْمِ، وَتَكَلَّمَتْ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَأَرْجُلُهُمْ بِمَا كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ.

قَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: لَا يَسْأَلُهُمْ: هَلْ عَمِلْتُمْ كَذَا وَكَذَا؟ لِأَنَّهُ أَعْلَمُ بِذَلِكَ مِنْهُمْ، وَلَكِنْ يَقُولُ: لِمَ عَمِلْتُمْ كَذَا وَكَذَا؟ فَهُوَ قَوْلٌ ثَانٍ.

وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ فِي هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ: لَا يَسْأَلُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ عَنِ الْمُجْرِمِ، يُعْرَفُون بِسِيمَاهُمْ.

وَهَذَا قَوْلٌ ثَالِثٌ. وَكَأَنَّ هَذَا بَعْدَ مَا يُؤْمَرُ بِهِمْ إِلَى النَّارِ، فَذَلِكَ الْوَقْتُ لَا يُسْأَلُونَ عَنْ ذُنُوبِهِمْ، بَلْ يُقَادُونَ إِلَيْهَا وَيُلْقَوْنَ فِيهَا، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿يُعْرَفُ الْمُجْرِمُونَ بِسِيمَاهُمْ﴾ أَيْ: بِعَلَامَاتٍ تَظْهَرُ عَلَيْهِمْ.

وَقَالَ الْحَسَنُ وقَتَادَةُ: يَعْرِفُونَهُمْ بِاسْوِدَادِ الْوُجُوهِ وَزُرْقَةِ الْعُيُونِ.

قُلْتُ: وَهَذَا كَمَا يُعْرَفُ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ بِالْغُرَّةِ وَالتَّحْجِيلِ مِنْ آثَارِ الْوُضُوءِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَيُؤْخَذُ بِالنَّوَاصِي وَالأقْدَامِ﴾ أَيْ: تَجْمَعُ الزَّبَانِيَةُ نَاصِيَتَهُ مَعَ قَدَمَيْهِ، وَيُلْقُونَهُ فِي النَّارِ كَذَلِكَ.

وَقَالَ الْأَعْمَشُ عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: يُؤْخَذُ بِنَاصِيَتِهِ وَقَدَمِهِ ، فَيُكْسَرُ كَمَا يُكْسَرُ الْحَطَبُ فِي التَّنُّورِ.

وَقَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ: يُجْمَعُ بَيْنَ نَاصِيَتِهِ وَقَدَمَيْهِ فِي سِلْسِلَةٍ مِنْ وَرَاءِ ظَهْرِهِ.

وَقَالَ السُّدِّيُّ: يُجْمَعُ بَيْنَ نَاصِيَةِ الْكَافِرِ وَقَدَمَيْهِ، فَتُرْبَطُ نَاصِيَتُهُ بِقَدَمِهِ، ويُفتل ظَهْرُهُ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو تَوْبَةَ الرَّبِيعُ بْنُ نَافِعٍ، حَدَّثَنَا مُعَاوِيَةَ بْنِ سَلَّامٍ، عَنْ أَخِيهِ زَيْدِ بْنِ سَلَّامٍ أَنَّهُ سَمِعَ أَبَا سَلَّامٍ -يَعْنِي جَدَّهُ-أَخْبَرَنِي عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ، حَدَّثَنِي رَجُلٌ مِنْ كِنْدَةَ قَالَ: أَتَيْتُ عَائِشَةَ فَدَخَلْتُ عَلَيْهَا، وَبَيْنِي وَبَيْنَهَا حِجَابٌ، فَقُلْتُ: حَدَّثَكِ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ أَنَّهُ يَأْتِي عَلَيْهِ سَاعَةٌ لَا يَمْلِكُ لِأَحَدٍ فِيهَا شَفَاعَةً؟ قَالَتْ: نَعَمْ، لَقَدْ سَأَلْتُهُ عَنْ هَذَا وَأَنَا وَهُوَ فِي شِعَار وَاحِدٍ، قَالَ: "نَعَمْ حِينَ يُوضَعُ الصِّرَاطُ، وَلَا أَمَلِكُ لِأَحَدٍ فِيهَا شَفَاعَةً، حَتَّى أَعْلَمَ أَيْنَ يُسْلَكُ بِي؟ وَيَوْمَ تَبْيَضُّ وُجُوهٌ وَتَسْوَدُّ وُجُوهٌ، حَتَّى أَنْظُرَ مَاذَا يُفْعَلُ بِي -أَوْ قَالَ: يُوحَى-وَعِنْدَ الْجِسْرِ حِينَ يَسْتَحِدُّ وَيَسْتَحِرُّ" فَقَالَتْ: وَمَا يَسْتَحِدُّ وَمَا يَسْتَحِرُّ؟ قَالَ: "يَسْتَحِدُّ حَتَّى يَكُونَ مثل شفرة السيف، ويستحر حتى يكون مِثْلَ الْجَمْرَةِ، فَأَمَّا الْمُؤْمِنُ فَيُجِيزُهُ لَا يَضُرُّهُ، وَأَمَّا الْمُنَافِقُ فَيَتَعَلَّقُ حَتَّى إِذَا بَلَغَ أَوْسَطَهُ خَرَّ مِنْ قَدِمِهِ فَيَهْوِي بِيَدِهِ إِلَى قَدَمَيْهِ، فَتَضْرِبُهُ الزَّبَانِيَةُ بِخُطَّافٍ فِي نَاصِيَتِهِ وَقَدَمِهِ، فَتَقْذِفُهُ فِي جَهَنَّمَ، فَيَهْوِي فِيهَا مِقْدَارَ خَمْسِينَ عَامًا". قُلْتُ: مَا ثِقَلُ الرَّجُلِ؟ قَالَتْ: ثِقَلُ عَشْرِ خَلِفَاتٍ سِمَانٍ، فَيَوْمَئِذٍ يُعْرَفُ الْمُجْرِمُونَ بِسِيمَاهُمْ فَيُؤْخَذُ بِالنَّوَاصِي وَالْأَقْدَامِ.

هَذَا حَدِيثٌ غَرِيبٌ [جِدًّا] ، وَفِيهِ أَلْفَاظٌ مُنْكَرٌ رَفْعُهَا، وَفِي الْإِسْنَادِ مَنْ لَمْ يُسَمّ، وَمِثْلُهُ لَا يُحْتَجُّ بِهِ ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿هَذِهِ جَهَنَّمُ الَّتِي يُكَذِّبُ بِهَا الْمُجْرِمُونَ﴾ أَيْ: هَذِهِ النَّارُ الَّتِي كُنْتُمْ تُكَذِّبُونَ بِوُجُودِهَا هَا هِيَ حَاضِرَةٌ تُشَاهِدُونَهَا عِيَانًا، يُقَالُ لَهُمْ ذَلِكَ تَقْرِيعًا وَتَوْبِيخًا وَتَصْغِيرًا وَتَحْقِيرًا.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿يَطُوفُونَ بَيْنَهَا وَبَيْنَ حَمِيمٍ آنٍ﴾ أَيْ: تَارَةً يُعَذَّبُونَ فِي الْجَحِيمِ، وَتَارَةً يُسْقَوْنَ مِنَ الْحَمِيمِ، وَهُوَ الشَّرَابُ الَّذِي هُوَ كَالنُّحَاسِ الْمُذَابِ، يُقَطِّعُ الْأَمْعَاءَ وَالْأَحْشَاءَ، وَهَذِهِ كَقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِذِ الأغْلالُ فِي أَعْنَاقِهِمْ وَالسَّلاسِلُ يُسْحَبُونَ. فِي الْحَمِيمِ ثُمَّ فِي النَّارِ يُسْجَرُونَ﴾ [غَافِرٍ: ٧١، ٧٢] .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿آنٍ﴾ أَيْ: حَارٌّ وَقَدْ بَلَغَ الْغَايَةَ فِي الْحَرَارَةِ، لَا يُسْتَطَاعُ مِنْ شِدَّةِ ذَلِكَ.

قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿يَطُوفُونَ بَيْنَهَا وَبَيْنَ حَمِيمٍ آنٍ﴾ قَدِ انْتَهَى غلْيه، وَاشْتَدَّ حَرُّهُ. وَكَذَا قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَسَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَالْحَسَنُ، وَالثَّوْرِيُّ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ.

وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: قَدْ أنَى طَبْخُهُ مُنْذُ خَلَقَ الله السموات وَالْأَرْضَ. وَقَالَ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ كَعْبٍ الْقُرَظِيُّ: يُؤْخَذُ الْعَبْدُ فيحرّكُ بِنَاصِيَتِهِ فِي ذَلِكَ الْحَمِيمِ، حَتَّى يَذُوبَ اللَّحْمُ وَيَبْقَى الْعَظْمُ وَالْعَيْنَانِ فِي الرَّأْسِ. وَهِيَ كَالَّتِي يَقُولُ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿فِي الْحَمِيمِ ثُمَّ فِي النَّارِ يُسْجَرُونَ﴾ . والحميم الآن: يَعْنِي الْحَارَّ. وَعَنِ الْقُرَظِيِّ رِوَايَةٌ أُخْرَى: ﴿حَمِيمٍ آنٍ﴾ أَيْ: حَاضِرٌ. وَهُوَ قَوْلُ ابْنِ زَيْدٍ أَيْضًا، وَالْحَاضِرُ، لَا يُنَافِي مَا رُوِيَ عَنِ الْقُرَظِيِّ أَوَّلًا أَنَّهُ الْحَارُّ، كَقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿تُسْقَى مِنْ عَيْنٍ آنِيَةٍ﴾ [الْغَاشِيَةِ: ٥] أَيْ حَارَّةٍ شَدِيدَةِ الْحَرِّ لَا تُسْتَطَاعُ. وَكَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿غَيْرَ نَاظِرِينَ إِنَاهُ﴾ [الْأَحْزَابِ:٥٣] يَعْنِي: اسْتِوَاءَهُ وَنُضْجَهُ. فَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿حَمِيمٍ آنٍ﴾ أَيْ: حَمِيمٌ حَارٌّ جِدًّا. وَلَمَّا كَانَ مُعَاقَبَةُ الْعُصَاةِ الْمُجْرِمِينَ وَتَنْعِيمُ الْمُتَّقِينَ مِنْ فَضْلِهِ وَرَحْمَتِهِ وَعَدْلِهِ وَلُطْفِهِ بِخُلُقِهِ، وَكَانَ إِنْذَارُهُ لَهُمْ عَذَابَهُ وَبَأْسَهُ مِمَّا يَزْجُرُهُمْ عَمَّا هُمْ فِيهِ مِنَ الشِّرْكِ وَالْمَعَاصِي وَغَيْرِ ذَلِكَ، قَالَ مُمْتَنًّا بِذَلِكَ عَلَى بَرِيَّتِهِ: ﴿فَبِأَيِّ آلاءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ﴾ .

## 55:46-53 — verses 46-53 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Ibn Shawdhab and 'Ata' al-Khurasani said: this verse, {And for him who fears the standing before his Lord there are two gardens}, was revealed concerning Abu Bakr al-Siddiq.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: my father narrated to us, Muhammad ibn Musaffa narrated to us, Baqiyyah narrated to us, from Abu Bakr ibn Abi Maryam, from 'Atiyyah ibn Qays, concerning His saying: {And for him who fears the standing before his Lord there are two gardens}: it was revealed about the one who said, "Burn me with fire, perhaps I may lead God astray." He said: he repented for a day and a night after saying this, so God accepted his repentance from him and admitted him to Paradise.

The correct view is that this verse is general, as Ibn 'Abbas and others have said. The Exalted says: and for whoever fears his standing before God, Mighty and Majestic, on the Day of Resurrection, {and restrained the soul from desire} [al-Nazi'at: 40], and did not transgress, and did not prefer the worldly life, and knew that the Hereafter is better and more lasting, and so fulfilled the obligations of God and avoided His prohibitions, for him on the Day of Resurrection, with his Lord, there are two gardens, as al-Bukhari, may God have mercy on him, said.

'Abdullah ibn Abi al-Aswad narrated to us, 'Abd al-'Aziz ibn 'Abd al-Samad al-'Ammi narrated to us, Abu 'Imran al-Jawni narrated to us, from Abu Bakr ibn 'Abdullah ibn Qays, from his father, that the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Two gardens of silver, their vessels and what is in them, and two gardens of gold, their vessels and what is in them, and there is nothing between the people and their looking upon their Lord, Mighty and Majestic, except the cloak of grandeur upon His face, in the Garden of 'Adn."

The rest of the group (of hadith compilers) besides Abu Dawud transmitted it from the hadith of 'Abd al-'Aziz, with this chain.

Hammad ibn Salamah said, from Thabit, from Abu Bakr ibn Abi Musa, from his father, Hammad said: I do not know it except that he attributed it (to the Prophet ﷺ), concerning His saying: {And for him who fears the standing before his Lord there are two gardens}, and concerning His saying: {And besides these two, there are two other gardens} [he said]: two gardens of gold for those brought near (al-muqarrabin), and two gardens of silver for the companions of the right hand (ashab al-yamin).

Ibn Jarir said: Zakariyya ibn Yahya ibn Aban al-Misri narrated to us, Ibn Abi Maryam narrated to us, Muhammad ibn Ja'far informed us, from Muhammad ibn Abi Harmalah, from 'Ata' ibn Yasar, Abu al-Darda' informed me, that the Messenger of God ﷺ recited one day this verse: {And for him who fears the standing before his Lord there are two gardens}, so I said: even if he committed fornication or theft? He said: {And for him who fears the standing before his Lord there are two gardens}. I said: even if he committed fornication and theft? He said: {And for him who fears the standing before his Lord there are two gardens}. I said: even if he committed fornication and theft, O Messenger of God? He said: "Even if the nose of Abu al-Darda' is rubbed in the dust."

Al-Nasa'i transmitted it from the hadith of Muhammad ibn Abi Harmalah, with this chain. Al-Nasa'i also transmitted it from Mu'ammal ibn Hisham, from Isma'il, from al-Jurayri, from Musa, from Muhammad ibn Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas, from Abu al-Darda', with this chain. It has also been narrated as mawquf (halted report) upon Abu al-Darda'. And it was narrated from him that he said: whoever fears the standing before his Lord will not commit fornication and will not steal.

This verse is general, applying to both mankind and the jinn, and it is among the clearest proofs that the jinn will enter Paradise if they believe and are God-fearing. For this reason God, Exalted is He, conferred this favor upon the two weighty kinds (mankind and jinn) by saying: {And for him who fears the standing before his Lord there are two gardens. Then which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?}.

Then He described these two gardens, saying: {Having ample, spreading branches (afnan)}, meaning fresh, beautiful branches, bearing every kind of ripe, excellent fruit. {Then which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?}. Thus said 'Ata' al-Khurasani and a group (of scholars): that the afnan are the branches of trees touching one another.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: my father narrated to us, 'Amr ibn 'Ali narrated to us, Muslim ibn Qutaybah narrated to us, 'Abdullah ibn al-Nu'man narrated to us, I heard 'Ikrimah saying: {Having afnan}, he says: the shade of the branches over the walls. Have you not heard the saying of the poet where he says:

"What stirred your longing was the cooing of a dove, calling among the branches of the boughs, calling as a mother of two chicks, having encountered a hungry one, one with two talons among the falcons, a falcon..."

Al-Baghawi related from Mujahid, 'Ikrimah, al-Dahhak, and al-Kalbi: that it means the straight branch [tall ones].

He said: Abu Sa'id al-Ashajj narrated to us, 'Abd al-Salam ibn Harb narrated to us, 'Ata' ibn al-Sa'ib narrated to us, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, from Ibn 'Abbas: {Having afnan}: having colors (alwan).

He said: and it has been narrated from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, al-Hasan, al-Suddi, Khusayf, al-Nadr ibn 'Arabi, and Abu Sinan, the same as that. The meaning of this view is that in them are varieties (funun) of pleasures, and Ibn Jarir preferred this.

'Ata' said: every branch gathers varieties of fruit. Al-Rabi' ibn Anas said: {Having afnan}: having wide courtyards (fina').

All of these sayings are correct, and there is no contradiction between them, and God knows best. Qatadah said: {Having afnan} indicates their vastness, their excellence, and their superiority over all else.

Muhammad ibn Ishaq said, from Yahya ibn 'Abbad ibn 'Abdullah ibn al-Zubayr, from his father, from Asma', she said: I heard the Messenger of God ﷺ, and he mentioned the Lote Tree of the Utmost Boundary (Sidrat al-Muntaha), and said: "A rider travels in the shade of one of its branches for a hundred years, or he said: a hundred riders take shade in the shade of one of its branches. In it are cushions of gold, as if its fruit were water jars."

Al-Tirmidhi transmitted it from the hadith of Yunus ibn Bukayr, with this chain.

{In them are two springs, flowing}, meaning: they flow to water those trees and branches, so they bear fruit of every color. {Then which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?}. Al-Hasan al-Basri said: one of them is called "Tasnim," and the other "al-Salsabil."

'Atiyyah said: one of them is of water that does not stagnate, and the other is of wine, delightful to those who drink it.

For this reason He said after this: {In both of them are two kinds of every fruit}, meaning: of all types of fruits, both what they know and better than what they know, and what no eye has seen, nor ear heard, nor has occurred to the heart of any human being. {Then which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?}.

Ibrahim ibn al-Hakam ibn Aban said, from his father, from 'Ikrimah, from Ibn 'Abbas: there is no fruit in this world, sweet or bitter, but that it exists in Paradise, even the bitter colocynth.

Ibn 'Abbas said: nothing of what is in the Hereafter exists in this world except in name, meaning that there is a vast gulf between them, and a clear difference in degree of excellence.

وَهَذِهِ الْآيَةُ عَامَّةٌ فِي الْإِنْسِ وَالْجِنِّ، فَهِيَ مِنْ أَدَلِّ دَلِيلٍ عَلَى أَنَّ الْجِنَّ يَدْخُلُونَ الْجَنَّةَ إِذَا آمَنُوا وَاتَّقَوْا؛ وَلِهَذَا امْتَنَّ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى عَلَى الثَّقَلَيْنِ بِهَذَا الْجَزَاءِ فَقَالَ: ﴿وَلِمَنْ خَافَ مَقَامَ رَبِّهِ جَنَّتَانِ. فَبِأَيِّ آلاءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ﴾ .

ثُمَّ نَعَتَ هَاتَيْنِ الْجَنَّتَيْنِ فَقَالَ: ﴿ذَوَاتَا أَفْنَانٍ﴾ أَيْ: أَغْصَانٍ نَضِرَة حَسَنَةٍ، تَحْمِلُ مِنْ كُلِّ ثَمَرَةٍ نَضِيجَةٍ فَائِقَةٍ، ﴿فَبِأَيِّ آلاءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ﴾ . هَكَذَا قَالَ عَطَاءٌ الْخُرَاسَانِيُّ وَجَمَاعَةٌ: إِنَّ الْأَفْنَانَ أَغْصَانُ الشجَر يَمَسُّ بعضُها بَعْضًا.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا عَمْرُو بْنُ عَلِيٍّ، حَدَّثَنَا مُسْلِمُ بْنُ قُتَيْبَةَ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ النُّعْمَانِ، سَمِعْتُ عِكْرِمَةَ يَقُولُ: ﴿ذَوَاتَا أَفْنَانٍ﴾ ، يَقُولُ: ظِل الْأَغْصَانِ عَلَى الْحِيطَانِ، أَلَمْ تَسْمَعْ قَوْلَ الشَّاعِرِ حَيْثُ يَقُولُ:

مَا هاجَ شَوقَكَ مِنْ هَديل حَمَامَةٍ ... تَدْعُو عَلَى فَنَن الغُصُون حَمَاما ...

تَدْعُو أَبَا فَرْخَين صَادَفَ طَاوِيًا ... ذَا مِخْلَبَيْنِ مِنَ الصُّقُورِ قَطاما 

وَحَكَى الْبَغَوِيُّ، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ، وَعِكْرِمَةَ، وَالضَّحَّاكِ، وَالْكَلْبِيِّ: أَنَّهُ الْغُصْنُ الْمُسْتَقِيمُ [طِوَالًا] .

قَالَ: وَحَدَّثَنَا أَبُو سَعِيدٍ الْأَشَجُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ السَّلَامِ بْنُ حَرْبٍ، حَدَّثَنَا عَطَاءُ بْنُ السَّائِبِ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿ذَوَاتَا أَفْنَانٍ﴾ : ذَوَاتَا أَلْوَانٍ.

قَالَ: وَ [قَدْ] رُوِيَ عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ، وَالْحَسَنِ، وَالسُّدِّيِّ، وخُصَيف، وَالنَّضْرِ بْنِ عَرَبِيٍّ ، وَأَبِي سِنَان مِثْلُ ذَلِكَ. وَمَعْنَى هَذَا الْقَوْلِ أَنَّ فِيهِمَا فُنُونًا مِنَ الْمَلَاذِّ، وَاخْتَارَهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ.

وَقَالَ عَطَاءٌ: كُلُّ غُصْنٍ يَجْمَعُ فُنُونًا مِنَ الْفَاكِهَةِ، وَقَالَ الرَّبِيعُ بْنُ أَنَسٍ: ﴿ذَوَاتَا أَفْنَانٍ﴾ : وَاسِعَتَا الْفِنَاءِ.

وَكُلُّ هَذِهِ الْأَقْوَالِ صَحِيحَةٌ، وَلَا مُنَافَاةَ بَيْنَهَا، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ. وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: ﴿ذَوَاتَا أَفْنَانٍ﴾ يُنْبِئُ بِسِعَتِهَا وَفَضْلِهَا وَمَزِيَّتِهَا عَلَى مَا سِوَاهَا.

وَقَالَ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ يَحْيَى بْنِ عَبَّادِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ الزُّبَيْرِ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ أَسْمَاءَ قَالَتْ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ -وَذَكَرَ سِدْرَةَ الْمُنْتَهَى-فَقَالَ: "يَسِيرُ فِي ظِلِّ الفَنَن مِنْهَا الرَّاكِبُ مِائَةَ سَنَةٍ-أَوْ قَالَ: يَسْتَظِلُّ فِي ظِلِّ الفَنَن مِنْهَا مِائَةُ رَاكِبٍ-فِيهَا فِرَاشُ الذَّهَبِ، كَأَنَّ ثَمَرَهَا القِلال".

رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ يُونُسَ بْنِ بُكَيْرٍ، بِهِ .

﴿فِيهِمَا عَيْنَانِ تَجْرِيَانِ﴾ أَيْ: تَسْرَحَانِ لِسَقْيِ تِلْكَ الْأَشْجَارِ وَالْأَغْصَانِ فَتُثْمِرُ مِنْ جَمِيعِ الْأَلْوَانِ، ﴿فَبِأَيِّ آلاءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ﴾ قَالَ الْحَسَنُ الْبَصْرِيُّ: إِحْدَاهُمَا يُقَالُ لَهَا: "تَسْنِيمٌ"، وَالْأُخْرَى "السَّلْسَبِيلُ".

وَقَالَ عَطِيَّةُ: إِحْدَاهُمَا مِنْ مَاءٍ غَيْرِ آسِنٍ، وَالْأُخْرَى مِنْ خَمْرٍ لَذَّةٍ لِلشَّارِبِينَ.

وَلِهَذَا قَالَ بَعْدَ هَذَا: ﴿فِيهِمَا مِنْ كُلِّ فَاكِهَةٍ زَوْجَانِ﴾ أَيْ: مِنْ جَمِيعِ أَنْوَاعِ الثِّمَارِ مِمَّا يَعْلَمُونَ وَخَيْرٍ مِمَّا يَعْلَمُونَ، وَمِمَّا لَا عَيْنٌ رَأَتْ، وَلَا أُذُنٌ سَمِعَتْ، وَلَا خطر على قلب بشر، ﴿فَبِأَيِّ آلاءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ﴾ .

قَالَ إِبْرَاهِيمُ بْنُ الْحَكَمِ بْنِ أَبَانٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: مَا فِي الدُّنْيَا ثَمَرَةٌ حُلْوَةٌ وَلَا مُرَّةٌ إِلَّا وَهِيَ فِي الْجَنَّةِ حَتَّى الْحَنْظَلَةُ .

## 55:54-61 — verses 54-61 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: {Reclining} meaning the people of Paradise. What is meant by reclining here is lying down, though it is also said to mean sitting cross-legged. {Upon couches whose linings are of brocade}, this being the thick type of silk brocade. So said Ikrimah, al-Dahhak, and Qatadah.

Abu Imran al-Jawni said: it is silk brocade interwoven with gold. Thus attention is drawn to the nobility of the outer covering by way of the nobility of the lining, and this is an instance of pointing to the higher by means of the lower.

Abu Ishaq reported, from Hubayrah ibn Yarim, from Abdullah ibn Mas'ud, who said: "These are the linings, so how would it be if you were to see the outer coverings?"

Malik ibn Dinar said: "Their linings are of brocade, and their outer coverings are of light."

Sufyan al-Thawri, or Sharik, said: "Their linings are of brocade, and their outer coverings are of solid light."

Al-Qasim ibn Muhammad said: "Their linings are of brocade, and their outer coverings are of mercy."

Ibn Shawdhab reported, from Abu Abdillah al-Shami: "God mentioned the linings and did not mention the outer coverings, and over the outer coverings are veils (mahabis), and none knows what is beneath the veils except God." All of this was mentioned by the Imam Ibn Abi Hatim.

{And the fruit of the two gardens is near at hand}, meaning: their fruit is close to them, and whenever they wish they may take of it, in whatever position they find themselves, just as He said: {Their clusters of fruit hang low} [al-Haqqah: 23], and He said: {And their shades will be close over them, and their fruits made easy to reach} [al-Insan: 14], meaning: no one who reaches for it is prevented from it; rather it descends toward him from its branches. {Then which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?}

Having mentioned the couches and their magnificence, He then said: {In them}, meaning in the couches, {are maidens restraining their glances}, meaning they are modest, restraining their eyes from any but their spouses, so that they see nothing in Paradise more beautiful than their own spouses. So said Ibn Abbas, Qatadah, Ata' al-Khurasani, and Ibn Zayd.

It has been related that one of them says to her husband: "By God, I see nothing in Paradise more beautiful than you, nor anything in Paradise dearer to me than you. So praise be to God who made you for me and made me for you."

{Untouched before them by any man or jinn}, meaning: rather they are virgins, well matched with their spouses in age, whom none before their husbands, whether human or jinn, has touched. This is also among the proofs for the entry of believing jinn into Paradise.

Artah ibn al-Mundhir said: Damrah ibn Habib was asked, "Do the jinn enter Paradise?" He said: "Yes, and they marry; for the jinn there are jinn women, and for humans there are human women. That is the meaning of His saying: {Untouched before them by any man or jinn. Then which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?}"

Then He describes them for those who would seek them: {As if they were rubies and coral}. Mujahid, al-Hasan, al-Suddi, Ibn Zayd, and others said: this refers to the clarity of ruby and the whiteness of coral, taking "coral" here to mean pearl.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: my father narrated to us, Muhammad ibn Hatim narrated to us, Ubaydah ibn Humayd narrated to us, from Ata' ibn al-Sa'ib, from Amr ibn Maymun al-Awdi, from Abdullah ibn Mas'ud, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "Indeed, one of the women of the people of Paradise, the whiteness of her calf can be seen through seventy garments of silk, until her marrow is seen. That is because the Almighty says: {As if they were rubies and coral}. As for the ruby, it is a stone such that if you were to insert a thread into it and then purify it, you would see the thread through it."

Thus it was related by al-Tirmidhi from the report of Ubaydah ibn Humayd and Abu al-Ahwas, from Ata' ibn al-Sa'ib, with the same wording. He also related it as mawquf (halted report), then said: "and this is more sound."

The Imam Ahmad said: Affan narrated to us, Hammad ibn Salamah narrated to us, Yunus informed us, from Muhammad ibn Sirin, from Abu Hurayrah, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "For every man among the people of Paradise there are two wives from the wide eyed maidens (hur al-'in); upon each of them are seventy garments, and the marrow of her calf can be seen through the garments."

The Imam Ahmad related this alone by this route. Muslim related it from the report of Isma'il ibn Ulayyah, from Ayyub, from Muhammad ibn Sirin, who said: they either boasted or recalled among themselves whether men are more numerous in Paradise or women. Abu Hurayrah said: "Did not Abu al-Qasim ﷺ say: 'The first group to enter Paradise will be in the form of the moon on the night it is full, and the group that follows them will be like the brightest shining star in the sky. Each man among them will have two wives, and the marrow of their calves will be seen through the flesh, and there is no unmarried person in Paradise.'"

This hadith is recorded in the two Sahihs, from the report of Hammam ibn Munabbih and Abu Zur'ah, from Abu Hurayrah, may God be pleased with him.

The Imam Ahmad said: Abu al-Nadr narrated to us, Muhammad ibn Talhah narrated to us, from Humayd, from Anas, that the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "A morning or an evening journey in the path of God is better than the world and all that is in it, and the space of one of your bows, or the place of his sandal strap, meaning his whip, in Paradise is better than the world and all that is in it. And if a woman of the people of Paradise were to look down upon the earth, she would fill all that is between them with fragrance, and all that is between them would become sweet, and her headscarf alone is better than the world and all that is in it."

Al-Bukhari related it from the report of Abu Ishaq, from Humayd, from Anas, in similar wording.

As for His saying: {Is the reward of good except good?}, meaning: there is for whoever does good deeds in this world nothing but good in reward in the Hereafter. As the Almighty said: {For those who do good is the best reward, and more} [Yunus: 26].

Al-Baghawi said: Abu Sa'id al-Sharihi informed us, Abu Ishaq al-Tha'labi narrated to us, Ibn Fanjuwayh informed me, Ibn Shaybah narrated to us, Ishaq ibn Ibrahim ibn Bahram narrated to us, al-Hajjaj ibn Yusuf al-Muktib narrated to us, Bishr ibn al-Husayn narrated to us, from al-Zubayr ibn Adi, from Anas ibn Malik, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ recited {Is the reward of good except good?} and said: "Do you know what your Lord has said?" They said: "God and His Messenger know best." He said: "He says: is the reward of one upon whom I bestowed the blessing of monotheism anything but Paradise?"

Since what has been mentioned contains such tremendous blessings that no deed could ever match, being rather sheer favor and grace, He said after all of this: {Then which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?}

Related to His saying, {And for whoever fears the standing before his Lord there are two gardens}, is what al-Tirmidhi and al-Baghawi related, from the report of Abu al-Nadr Hashim ibn al-Qasim, from Abu Aqil al-Thaqafi, from Abu Farwah Yazid ibn Sinan al-Ruhawi, from Bukayr ibn Fayruz, from Abu Hurayrah, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Whoever fears travels by night, and whoever travels by night reaches his destination. Indeed, God's merchandise is precious. Indeed, God's merchandise is Paradise."

Al-Tirmidhi then said: "Gharib (singular), we do not know it except from the report of Abu al-Nadr."

Al-Baghawi related, from the report of Ali ibn Hujr, from Isma'il ibn Ja'far, from Muhammad ibn Abi Harmalah, freedman of Huwaytib ibn Abd al-Uzza, from Ata' ibn Yasar, from Abu al-Darda', that he heard the Messenger of God ﷺ narrating from the pulpit and saying: {And for whoever fears the standing before his Lord there are two gardens}. I said: "Even if he committed fornication and even if he stole, O Messenger of God?" The Messenger of God ﷺ said: {And for whoever fears the standing before his Lord there are two gardens}. I said it a second time: "Even if he committed fornication and even if he stole, O Messenger of God?" He said [the Messenger of God ﷺ]: {And for whoever fears the standing before his Lord there are two gardens}. I said it a third time: "Even if he committed fornication and even if he stole, O Messenger of God?" He said: "Even so, though the nose of Abu al-Darda' be rubbed in the dust."

﴿وَجَنَى الْجَنَّتَيْنِ دَانٍ﴾ أَيْ: ثَمَرُهَا قَرِيبٌ إِلَيْهِمْ، مَتَى شَاءُوا تَنَاوَلُوهُ عَلَى أَيِّ صِفَةٍ كَانُوا، كَمَا قَالَ: ﴿قُطُوفُهَا دَانِيَةٌ﴾ [الْحَاقَّةِ:٢٣] ، وَقَالَ: ﴿وَدَانِيَةً عَلَيْهِمْ ظِلالُهَا وَذُلِّلَتْ قُطُوفُهَا تَذْلِيلا﴾ [الْإِنْسَانِ:١٤] أَيْ: لَا تُمْنَعُ مِمَّنْ تَنَاوَلَهَا، بَلْ تَنْحَطُّ إِلَيْهِ مِنْ أَغْصَانِهَا، ﴿فَبِأَيِّ آلاءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ﴾ .

وَلَمَّا ذَكَرَ الْفُرُشَ وَعَظَمَتَهَا قَالَ بَعْدَ ذَلِكَ: ﴿فِيهِنَّ﴾ أَيْ: فِي الْفُرُشِ ﴿قَاصِرَاتُ الطَّرْفِ﴾ أَيْ غَضِيضَاتٌ عَنْ غَيْرِ أَزْوَاجِهِنَّ، فَلَا يَرَيْنَ شَيْئًا أَحْسَنَ فِي الْجَنَّةِ مِنْ أَزْوَاجِهِنَّ. قَالَهُ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَعَطَاءٌ الْخُرَاسَانِيُّ، وَابْنُ زَيْدٍ.

وَقَدْ وَرَدَ أَنَّ الْوَاحِدَةَ مِنْهُنَّ تَقُولُ لِبَعْلِهَا: وَاللَّهِ مَا أَرَى فِي الْجَنَّةِ شَيْئًا أَحْسَنَ منك، ولا في الجنة شيئ أَحَبَّ إِلَيَّ مِنْكَ، فَالْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ الَّذِي جَعَلَكَ لِي وَجَعَلَنِي لَكَ.

﴿لَمْ يَطْمِثْهُنَّ إِنْسٌ قَبْلَهُمْ وَلا جَانٌّ﴾ أَيْ: بَلْ هُنَّ أَبْكَارٌ عُرُبٌ أَتْرَابٌ، لَمْ يَطَأْهُنَّ أَحَدٌ قَبْلَ أَزْوَاجِهِنَّ مِنَ الْإِنْسِ وَالْجِنِّ. وَهَذِهِ أَيْضًا مِنَ الْأَدِلَّةِ عَلَى دُخُولِ مُؤْمِنِي الْجِنِّ الْجَنَّةَ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ يَنْعَتُهُنَّ لِلْخُطَّابِ: ﴿كَأَنَّهُنَّ الْيَاقُوتُ وَالْمَرْجَانُ﴾ ، قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَالْحَسَنُ، [وَالسُّدِّيُّ] ، وَابْنُ زَيْدٍ، وَغَيْرُهُمْ: فِي صَفَاءِ الْيَاقُوتِ وَبَيَاضِ الْمَرْجَانِ، فَجَعَلُوا الْمَرْجَانَ هَاهُنَا اللُّؤْلُؤَ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ حَاتِمٍ، حَدَّثَنَا عُبِيدة بْنُ حُمَيْد، عَنْ عَطَاءِ بْنِ السَّائِبِ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ مَيْمُونٍ الْأَوْدِيِّ ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ مَسْعُودٍ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ قَالَ: "إِنَّ الْمَرْأَةَ مِنْ نِسَاءِ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ ليُرى بَيَاضُ سَاقِهَا مِنْ وَرَاءِ سَبْعِينَ حُلَّةً مِنَ الْحَرِيرِ ، حَتَّى يُرَى مُخُّهَا، وَذَلِكَ أَنَّ اللَّهَ تَعَالَى يَقُولُ: ﴿كَأَنَّهُنَّ الْيَاقُوتُ وَالْمَرْجَانُ﴾ ، فَأَمَّا الْيَاقُوتُ فَإِنَّهُ حَجَرٌ لَوْ أَدْخَلْتَ فِيهِ سِلْكًا ثُمَّ اسْتَصْفَيْتَهُ لَرَأَيْتَهُ مِنْ وَرَائِهِ".

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ عُبَيْدَة بْنِ حُمَيْدٍ وَأَبِي الْأَحْوَصِ، عَنْ عَطَاءِ بْنِ السَّائِبِ، بِهِ . ورواه موقوفا، ثم قال: وهو أصح .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَفَّانُ، حَدَّثَنَا حَمَّادُ بْنُ سَلَمَةَ، أَخْبَرَنَا يُونُسُ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ سِيرِين، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ قَالَ: "لِلرَّجُلِ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ زَوْجَتَانِ مِنَ الْحُورِ الْعِينِ، عَلَى كُلِّ وَاحِدَةٍ سَبْعُونَ حُلَّةً، يُرى مُخُّ سَاقِهَا مِنْ وَرَاءِ الثِّيَابِ".

تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ مِنْ هَذَا الْوَجْهِ . وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ مِنْ حَدِيثِ إِسْمَاعِيلَ بْنِ عُلَيَّة، عَنْ أَيُّوبَ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ سِيرِينَ قَالَ: إِمَّا تَفَاخَرُوا وَإِمَّا تَذَكَّرُوا، الرِّجَالُ أَكْثَرُ فِي الْجَنَّةِ أَمِ النِّسَاءُ؟ فَقَالَ أَبُو هُرَيْرَةَ: أو لم يَقُلْ أَبُو الْقَاسِمِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ أَوَّلَ زُمْرَةٍ تَدْخُلُ الْجَنَّةَ عَلَى صُورَةِ الْقَمَرِ لَيْلَةَ الْبَدْرِ، وَالَّتِي تَلِيهَا عَلَى أضْوَأ كَوْكَبٍ دُرّي فِي السَّمَاءِ، لِكُلِّ امْرِئٍ مِنْهُمْ زَوْجَتَانِ اثْنَتَانِ، يُرَى مُخُّ سُوقِهِمَا مِنْ وَرَاءِ اللَّحْمِ، وَمَا فِي الْجَنَّةِ أَعْزَبُ" .

وَهَذَا الْحَدِيثُ مُخَرّجٌ فِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ هَمّام بْنِ مُنَبّه وَأَبِي زُرْعَة، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو النَّضْرِ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ طَلْحَةَ، عَنْ حُمَيْدٍ عَنْ أَنَسٍ؛ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "لَغَدْوَةٌ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ أَوْ رَوْحَةٌ خَيْرٌ مِنَ الدُّنْيَا وَمَا فِيهَا، وَلَقَابُ قَوْسِ أَحَدِكُمْ -أَوْ مَوْضِعُ قَيْدِهِ -يَعْنِي: سَوْطَهُ-مِنَ الْجَنَّةِ خَيْرٌ مِنَ الدُّنْيَا وَمَا فِيهَا، وَلَوِ اطَّلَعَتِ امْرَأَةٌ مِنْ نِسَاءِ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ إِلَى الْأَرْضِ لَمَلَأَتْ مَا بَيْنَهُمَا رِيحًا، وَلَطَابَ مَا بَيْنَهُمَا، ولنَصِيفها عَلَى رَأْسِهَا خَيْرٌ مِنَ الدُّنْيَا وَمَا فِيهَا".

وَرَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ حُمَيْدٍ، عَنْ أَنَسٍ بِنَحْوِهِ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿هَلْ جَزَاءُ الإحْسَانِ إِلا الإحْسَانُ﴾ أَيْ: مَا لِمَنْ أَحْسَنَ فِي الدُّنْيَا الْعَمَلَ إِلَّا الْإِحْسَانُ إِلَيْهِ فِي الدَّارِ الْآخِرَةِ. كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿لِلَّذِينَ أَحْسَنُوا الْحُسْنَى وَزِيَادَةٌ﴾ [يُونُسَ:٢٦] .

وَقَالَ الْبَغَوِيُّ: أَخْبَرَنَا أَبُو سَعِيدٍ الشَّريحِي، حَدَّثَنَا أبو إسحاق الثعلبي، أخبرني ابن فَنجُوَيه، حدثنا ابْنُ شَيْبَةَ، حَدَّثَنَا إِسْحَاقُ بْنُ إِبْرَاهِيمَ بْنِ بَهْرَام، حَدَّثَنَا الْحَجَّاجُ بْنُ يُوسُفَ المُكْتَب، حَدَّثَنَا بِشْر بْنُ الْحُسَيْنِ، عَنِ الزُّبَيْرِ بْنِ عَدِيّ، عَنْ أَنَسِ بْنِ مَالِكٍ قَالَ: قَرَأَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: ﴿هَلْ جَزَاءُ الإحْسَانِ إِلا الإحْسَانُ﴾ ، قَالَ: "هَلْ تَدْرُونَ مَا قَالَ رَبُّكُمْ؟ " قَالُوا: اللَّهُ وَرَسُولُهُ أَعْلَمُ. قَالَ: "يَقُولُ هَلْ جَزَاءُ مَا أَنْعَمْتُ عَلَيْهِ بِالتَّوْحِيدِ إِلَّا الْجَنَّةُ" .

وَلَمَّا كَانَ فِي الَّذِي ذُكِرَ نِعَمٌ عَظِيمَةٌ لَا يُقَاوِمُهَا عَمَلٌ، بَلْ مُجَرَّدُ تَفَضُّلٍ وَامْتِنَانٍ، قَالَ بَعْدَ ذَلِكَ كُلِّهِ: ﴿فَبِأَيِّ آلاءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ﴾ .

وَمِمَّا يَتَعَلَّقُ بِقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَلِمَنْ خَافَ مَقَامَ رَبِّهِ جَنَّتَانِ﴾ ، مَا رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ وَالْبَغَوِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي النَّضْرِ هَاشِمِ بْنِ الْقَاسِمِ، عَنْ أَبَى عَقِيلٍ الثَّقَفِيِّ، عَنْ أَبِي فَرْوَةَ يَزِيدَ بن سِنان الرّهاوي، عن بُكَيْر ابن فَيْرُوزَ ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "مَنْ خَافَ أَدْلَجَ، وَمَنْ أَدْلَجَ بَلَغَ الْمَنْزِلَ، أَلَا إِنَّ سِلْعَةَ اللَّهِ غَالِيَةٌ، أَلَا إِنَّ سِلْعَةَ اللَّهِ الْجَنَّةُ".

ثُمَّ قَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: غَرِيبٌ، لَا نَعْرِفُهُ إِلَّا مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي النَّضْرِ .

وَرَوَى الْبَغَوِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ عَلِيِّ بْنِ حُجْر، عَنْ إِسْمَاعِيلَ بْنِ جَعْفَرٍ، عن محمد بن أبي حَرْمَلَة -مَوْلَى حُوَيْطِبِ بْنِ عَبْدِ الْعُزَّى-عَنْ عَطَاءِ بْنِ يَسَار، عَنْ أَبِي الدَّرْدَاءِ؛ أَنَّهُ سَمِعَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُصُّ عَلَى الْمِنْبَرِ وَهُوَ يَقُولُ: ﴿وَلِمَنْ خَافَ مَقَامَ رَبِّهِ جَنَّتَانِ﴾ ، قُلْتُ: وَإِنْ زَنَى وَإِنْ سَرَقَ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ؟ فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: ﴿وَلِمَنْ خَافَ مَقَامَ رَبِّهِ جَنَّتَانِ﴾ . فَقُلْتُ الثَّانِيَةَ: وَإِنْ زَنَى وَإِنْ سَرَقَ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ؟ فَقَالَ [رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ] ﴿وَلِمَنْ خَافَ مَقَامَ رَبِّهِ جَنَّتَانِ﴾ . فَقُلْتُ الثَّالِثَةَ: وَإِنْ زَنَى وَإِنْ سَرَقَ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ؟ فَقَالَ: "وَإِنْ، رَغِمَ أَنْفُ أَبِي الدَّرْدَاءِ" .

## 55:62-78 — verses 62-78 (shared commentary)

### Translation

These two gardens are lower in rank, excellence, and station than the two before them, by the explicit wording of the Qur'an. Allah Most High said: {And besides these two, there are two other gardens}.

It has already been mentioned in the hadith: "Two gardens of gold, their vessels and what is in them, and two gardens of silver, their vessels and what is in them. The first two are for the muqarrabun (those brought near), and the other two are for the ashab al-yamin (companions of the right)."

Abu Musa said: two gardens of gold are for the muqarrabun, and two gardens of silver are for the ashab al-yamin.

Ibn Abbas said regarding {And besides these two, there are two other gardens}: below them in rank. Ibn Zayd said: below them in excellence.

The evidence for the superiority of the first two over the other two consists of several points. First, He described the first pair before these, and precedence indicates special regard. Then He said: {And besides these two, there are two other gardens}, and this clearly shows the nobility of what came first and its elevation over the second.

There He said: {possessing branches (afnan)}, meaning boughs, or varieties of pleasures, while here He said: {dark green, deepened in hue (mudhammatan)}, meaning blackish from the intensity of their being watered.

Ibn Abbas said concerning His words {mudhammatan}: they have become blackish out of greenness, from the intensity of being watered with water.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Ibn Fudayl, from Ata' ibn al-Sa'ib, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, from Ibn Abbas: {mudhammatan}, he said: two green ones. And it has been related from Abu Ayyub al-Ansari, Abdullah ibn al-Zubayr, Abdullah ibn Abi Awfa, Ikrimah, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, Mujahid (in one of the reports), Ata', Atiyyah al-Awfi, al-Hasan al-Basri, Yahya ibn Rafi', and Sufyan al-Thawri, something similar.

Muhammad ibn Ka'b said: {mudhammatan}: filled with greenness. Qatadah said: green from being watered, soft and lush. There is no doubt about the freshness of the branches upon the trees interwoven with one another.

There He said: {In them are two springs flowing}, and here He said: {gushing forth abundantly (nadhdhakhatan)}. Ali ibn Abi Talhah, from Ibn Abbas, said: meaning overflowing. Flowing (jary) is stronger than gushing (nadhkh).

Al-Dahhak said: {nadhdhakhatan} meaning full, never ceasing.

There He said: {In both of them are of every fruit, two kinds}, and here He said: {In them are fruit, date palms, and pomegranates}. There is no doubt that the first is more general and greater in individual items and variety concerning fruit, since it is an indefinite noun in an affirmative context and thus does not signify comprehensiveness in itself. That is why the statement {date palms and pomegranates} was explained as a case of joining the specific to the general, as al-Bukhari and others established. The date palm and pomegranate were mentioned specifically because of their superiority over other fruits.

Abd ibn Humayd said: [chain] Yahya ibn Abd al-Hamid, Husayn ibn Umar, Mukharraq, from Tariq ibn Shihab, from Umar ibn al-Khattab, who said: some Jews came to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and said, "O Muhammad, is there fruit in Paradise?" He said: "Yes, in it are fruit, date palms, and pomegranates." They said, "Do they eat as they eat in this world?" He said: "Yes, and many times more." They said, "Then do they relieve themselves?" He said: "No, but they sweat and perspire, and Allah removes whatever impurity is in their stomachs."

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] my father, al-Fadl ibn Dukayn, Sufyan, from Hammad, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, from Ibn Abbas, who said: the date palms of Paradise, their fronds are garments for the people of Paradise, from them come their tunics and their fine robes, their stalks are red gold, their trunks are green emerald, their fruit is sweeter than honey and softer than butter, and it has no pit.

And my father told us: [chain] Musa ibn Isma'il, Hammad ibn Salamah, from Abu Harun, from Abu Sa'id al-Khudri, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "I looked at Paradise, and there was a pomegranate among its pomegranates like a saddled camel."

Then He said: {In them are khayrat (good ones), beautiful ones (hisan)}. It has been said that the meaning is many good and beautiful things in Paradise, this was said by Qatadah. And it has been said that khayrat is the plural of khayyirah, meaning a righteous woman, good in character and beautiful in face, and this is the view of the majority. It has been related as a marfu' (elevated) report from Umm Salamah. In another hadith, which we will present in Surat al-Waqi'ah, the wide-eyed houris sing: "We are the khayrat, the beautiful ones, created for noble husbands." That is why some read it as "khayyirat" with doubling of the letter, {hisan. So which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?}

Then He said: {houris secluded in tents}, whereas there He said: {In them are maidens restraining their glances}. There is no doubt that a woman who restrains her own glance by herself is more excellent than one who is restrained by others, though all of them are secluded and protected.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Amr ibn Abdullah al-Awdi, Waki', from Sufyan, from Jabir, from al-Qasim ibn Abi Bazzah, from Abu Ubaydah, from Masruq, from Abdullah, who said: every Muslim has a khayrah (a chosen wife), and every khayrah has a tent, and every tent has four doors. Every day a gift, an honor, and a present that had not been there before is brought in to her. They are not boisterous, nor overbearing, nor foul-smelling, nor malodorous, but wide-eyed houris, as though they were sheltered eggs.

Regarding His words {in tents}, al-Bukhari said:

[chain] Muhammad ibn al-Muthanna, Abd al-Aziz ibn Abd al-Samad, Abu Imran al-Jawni, from Abu Bakr ibn Abdullah ibn Qays, from his father, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "In Paradise there is a tent of a single hollow pearl, sixty miles wide, in every corner of which is a family who cannot see the others; the believers move about among them."

He also related it through the hadith of Abu Imran, with the same chain, saying "thirty miles." Muslim also brought it out through the hadith of Abu Imran, with the same chain, and its wording is: "The believer will have in Paradise a tent of a single hollow pearl, sixty miles in length, in which the believer will have a family whom the believer visits, and they will not see one another."

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] al-Hasan ibn Abi al-Rabi', Abd al-Razzaq, Ma'mar, from Qatadah, who was informed by Khulayd al-Asari, from Abu al-Darda', who said: the tent is a single pearl, in it are seventy doors of pearl.

And my father told us: [chain] Isa ibn Abi Fatimah, Jarir, from Hisham, from Muhammad ibn al-Muthanna, from Ibn Abbas concerning His words {houris secluded in tents}, he said: [in] tents of pearl. And in Paradise there is a single tent of pearl, four farsakhs by four farsakhs, upon which are four thousand golden door-panels.

Abdullah ibn Wahb said: Amr informed us that Darraj Abu al-Samh related to him, from Abu al-Haytham, from Abu Sa'id, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "The lowest in rank among the people of Paradise is the one who has eighty thousand servants and seventy-two wives, and a dome is set up for him of pearl, chrysolite, and ruby, as vast as the distance between al-Jabiyah and Sana'a."

Al-Tirmidhi related it through the hadith of Amr ibn al-Harith, with the same chain.

Regarding His words {whom no man has touched before them, nor any jinn}: the same has already been mentioned previously, except that here He adds in describing the first pair His words: {as though they were rubies and coral. So which of the favors of your Lord will you both deny?}

Regarding His words {reclining upon green cushions (rafraf) and beautiful fine carpets (abqari)}: Ali ibn Abi Talhah, from Ibn Abbas, said: rafraf means cushions for reclining. Mujahid, Ikrimah, al-Hasan, Qatadah, al-Dahhak, and others said likewise, that they are cushions for reclining. Al-Ala' ibn Badr said: the rafraf is upon the couch, like hanging cushions.

Asim al-Jahdari said: {reclining upon green rafraf} means pillows. This is also the view of al-Hasan al-Basri in one report from him.

Abu Dawud al-Tayalisi said, from Shu'bah, from Abu Bishr, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, concerning His words {reclining upon green rafraf}: he said: the rafraf are the gardens of Paradise.

Regarding His words {and beautiful abqari}: Ibn Abbas, Qatadah, al-Dahhak, and al-Suddi said: abqari means ornamented carpets. Sa'id ibn Jubayr said: they are the finest of the ornamented carpets, meaning the best of them.

Mujahid said: abqari means brocade.

Al-Hasan al-Basri was asked about His words {and beautiful abqari}, and he said: they are the spreads of the people of Paradise, no father to you all, so seek after them. Another report from al-Hasan [al-Basri] states that they are cushions for reclining. Zayd ibn Aslam said: abqari is red, yellow, and green. Al-Ala' ibn Zayd was asked about abqari, and he said: they are spreads beneath that.

Abu Hazrah Ya'qub ibn Mujahid said: al-Abqari refers to garments of the people of Paradise that no one is familiar with. Abu al-'Aliyah said: al-Abqari refers to velvet cushions, no matter how fine they are. Al-Qutaybi said: among the Arabs, every embroidered garment is called abqari. Abu 'Ubaydah said: it is derived from the name of a land where such embroidery was made. Al-Khalil ibn Ahmad said: among the Arabs, anything that impresses, whether concerning men or otherwise, is called abqari. From this comes the Prophet's ﷺ saying about 'Umar: "I have not seen an abqari (extraordinary man) accomplish such a feat."

In any case, the description of the comforts of the people of the first two gardens is higher and loftier than this description, for He said there: {Reclining upon cushions whose linings are of brocade}, describing the linings of their cushions and remaining silent about their outer coverings, this being sufficient given that what He praised regarding the linings implies something even greater and more fitting for the outer coverings. The completion of the closing point is that after the preceding descriptions He said: {Is the reward of good except good?}, thus describing the people of that garden with ihsan (excellence, doing good), which is the highest of ranks and ultimate ends, as in the hadith of Jibril (peace be upon him) when he asked about Islam and then about Iman. These are several angles establishing the superiority of the first two gardens over these latter two, and we ask Allah, the Generous, the Bestower, to make us among the people of the first two.

Then He said: {Blessed is the name of your Lord, Possessor of Majesty and Honor}, meaning: He is deserving that He be held in majesty and not be disobeyed, that He be honored and thus worshipped, that He be thanked and not disbelieved in, and that He be remembered and not forgotten.

Ibn 'Abbas said: {Possessor of Majesty and Honor} means Possessor of grandeur and greatness.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Abu al-Darda said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Exalt Allah, and He will forgive you."

In another hadith: "Part of holding Allah in majesty is honoring the elderly Muslim, the one in authority, and the bearer of the Qur'an, so long as he is not extreme in it nor negligent of it."

Al-Hafiz Abu Ya'la said: [chain] from Anas that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Persist in saying: O Possessor of Majesty and Honor."

Al-Tirmidhi likewise narrated it [chain] through Hammad ibn Salamah, with the same wording.

Then he said: Mu'ammal erred in this, and it is gharib (a singular report) and not well preserved; rather, this is narrated from Hammad ibn Salamah, from Humayd, from al-Hasan, from the Prophet ﷺ.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Rabi'ah ibn 'Amir said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "Persist in calling upon the Possessor of Majesty and Honor."

Al-Nasa'i narrated it from the hadith of 'Abdullah ibn al-Mubarak, with the same chain.

Al-Jawhari said: "So-and-so alazza with so-and-so" means he clung persistently to him.

As for the saying attributed to Ibn Mas'ud: "Alizzu (persist) in saying: O Possessor of Majesty and Honor," it means: adhere to it constantly. It is also said that al-ilzaz means persistent supplication.

I say: both meanings are close to one another, and Allah knows best, it amounts to constancy, adherence, and persistence. In Sahih Muslim and the four Sunan collections, from the hadith of 'Abdullah ibn al-Harith, from 'A'ishah, she said: When the Messenger of Allah ﷺ gave the salam, he would not remain seated, that is, after the prayer, except for the time it takes to say: "O Allah, You are Peace and from You comes peace. Blessed are You, O Possessor of Majesty and Honor."

This is the end of the commentary on Surat al-Rahman, and to Allah belongs all praise [and gratitude].

هَاتَانِ الْجَنَّتَانِ دُونَ اللَّتَيْنِ قَبْلَهُمَا فِي الْمَرْتَبَةِ وَالْفَضِيلَةِ وَالْمَنْزِلَةِ بِنَصِّ الْقُرْآنِ، قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَمِنْ دُونِهِمَا جَنَّتَانِ﴾ .

وَقَدْ تَقَدَّمَ فِي الْحَدِيثِ: "جَنَّتَانِ مِنْ ذَهَبٍ آنِيَتُهُمَا وَمَا فِيهِمَا، وَجَنَّتَانِ مِنْ فِضَّةٍ آنِيَتُهُمَا وَمَا فِيهِمَا، فَالْأُولَيَانِ لِلْمُقَرَّبِينَ، والأخريان لأصحاب اليمين".

وَقَالَ أَبُو مُوسَى: جَنَّتَانِ مِنْ ذَهَبٍ لِلْمُقَرَّبِينَ، وَجَنَّتَانِ مِنْ فِضَّةٍ لِأَصْحَابِ الْيَمِينِ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿وَمِنْ دُونِهِمَا جَنَّتَانِ﴾ مِنْ دُونِهِمَا فِي الدَّرَجِ. وَقَالَ ابْنُ زَيْدٍ: مِنْ دُونِهِمَا فِي الْفَضْلِ.

وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى شَرَفِ الْأُولَيَيْنِ عَلَى الْأُخْرَيَيْنِ وجوه: أحدها: أنه نعت الأولين قَبْلَ هَاتَيْنِ، وَالتَّقْدِيمُ يَدُلُّ عَلَى الِاعْتِنَاءِ ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿وَمِنْ دُونِهِمَا جَنَّتَانِ﴾ . وَهَذَا ظَاهِرٌ فِي شَرَفِ التَّقَدُّمِ وَعُلُوِّهِ عَلَى الثَّانِي.

وَقَالَ هُنَاكَ: ﴿ذَوَاتَا أَفْنَانٍ﴾ : وَهِيَ الْأَغْصَانُ أَوِ الْفُنُونُ فِي الْمَلَاذِّ، وَقَالَ هَاهُنَا: ﴿مُدْهَامَّتَان﴾ أَيْ سَوْدَاوَانِ مِنْ شِدَّةِ الرِّيِّ.

قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿مُدْهَامَّتَان﴾ قَدِ اسْوَدَّتَا مِنَ الْخُضْرَةِ، مِنْ شِدَّةِ الرِّيِّ مِنَ الْمَاءِ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو سَعِيدٍ الْأَشَجُّ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ فُضَيْل، حَدَّثَنَا عَطَاءُ بْنُ السَّائِبِ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿مُدْهَامَّتَان﴾ : قَالَ: خَضْرَاوَانِ. ورُوي عَنْ أَبِي أَيُّوبَ الْأَنْصَارِيِّ، وَعَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ الزُّبَيْرِ، وَعَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ أَبِي أَوْفَى، وَعِكْرِمَةَ، وَسَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَير، وَمُجَاهِدٍ -فِي إِحْدَى الرِّوَايَاتِ-وَعَطَاءٍ، وَعَطِيَّةَ العَوْفي، وَالْحَسَنِ الْبَصْرِيِّ، وَيَحْيَى بْنِ رَافِعٍ، وَسُفْيَانَ الثَّوْرِيِّ، نَحْوُ ذَلِكَ.

وَقَالَ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ كَعْبٍ: ﴿مُدْهَامَّتَان﴾ : مُمْتَلِئَتَانِ مِنَ الْخُضْرَةِ. وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: خَضْرَاوَانِ مِنَ الرِّيِّ نَاعِمَتَانِ. وَلَا شَكَّ فِي نَضَارَةِ الْأَغْصَانِ عَلَى الْأَشْجَارِ الْمُشَبَّكَةِ بَعْضُهَا فِي بَعْضٍ. وَقَالَ هُنَاكَ: ﴿فِيهِمَا عَيْنَانِ تَجْرِيَانِ﴾ ، وَقَالَ هَاهُنَا: ﴿نَضَّاخَتَان﴾ ، وَقَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: أَيْ فَيَّاضَتَانِ. وَالْجَرْيُ أَقْوَى مِنَ النَّضْخِ.

وَقَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ: ﴿نَضَّاخَتَان﴾ أَيْ مُمْتَلِئَتَانِ لَا تَنْقَطِعَانِ.

وَقَالَ هُنَاكَ: ﴿فِيهِمَا مِنْ كُلِّ فَاكِهَةٍ زَوْجَانِ﴾ ، وَقَالَ هَاهُنَا: ﴿فِيهِمَا فَاكِهَةٌ وَنَخْلٌ وَرُمَّانٌ﴾ ، وَلَا شَكَّ أَنَّ الْأُولَى أَعَمُّ وَأَكْثَرُ فِي الْأَفْرَادِ وَالتَّنْوِيعِ عَلَى فَاكِهَةٍ، وَهِيَ نَكِرَةٌ فِي سِيَاقِ الْإِثْبَاتِ لَا تَعُمُّ؛ وَلِهَذَا فُسِّرَ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَنَخْلٌ وَرُمَّانٌ﴾ مِنْ بَابِ عَطْفِ الْخَاصِّ عَلَى الْعَامِّ، كَمَا قَرَّرَهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ وَغَيْرُهُ، وَإِنَّمَا أُفْرِدَ النَّخْلُ وَالرُّمَّانُ بِالذِّكْرِ لِشَرَفِهِمَا عَلَى غَيْرِهِمَا.

قَالَ عَبْدُ بْنُ حُمَيْدٍ: حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى بْنُ عَبْدِ الْحَمِيدِ، حَدَّثَنَا حُصَيْنُ بْنُ عُمَرَ، حَدَّثَنَا مُخَارِقٌ، عَنْ طَارِقِ بْنِ شِهَابٍ، عَنْ عُمَرَ بْنِ الْخَطَّابِ قَالَ: جَاءَ أُنَاسٌ مِنَ الْيَهُودِ إِلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالُوا: يَا مُحَمَّدُ، أَفِي الْجَنَّةِ فَاكِهَةٌ؟ قَالَ: "نَعَمْ، فِيهَا فَاكِهَةٌ وَنَخْلٌ وَرُمَّانٌ". قَالُوا: أَفَيَأْكَلُونَ كَمَا يَأْكُلُونَ فِي الدُّنْيَا؟ قَالَ: "نَعَمْ وَأَضْعَافٌ". قَالُوا: فَيَقْضُونَ الْحَوَائِجَ؟ قَالَ: "لَا وَلَكِنَّهُمْ يَعْرَقُونَ وَيَرْشَحُونَ، فَيُذْهِبُ اللَّهُ مَا فِي بطونهم من أذى" .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي حَدَّثَنَا الفَضْل بْنُ دُكَيْن، حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، عَنْ حَمَّادٍ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَير، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: نَخْلُ الْجَنَّةِ سَعَفُهَا كُسْوَةٌ لِأَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ، مِنْهَا مُقَطَّعَاتهم، وَمِنْهَا حُلَلهم، وكَرَبُها ذَهَبٌ أَحْمَرُ، وَجُذُوعُهَا زُمُرُّدٌ أَخْضَرُ، وَثَمَرُهَا أَحْلَى مِنَ الْعَسَلِ، وَأَلْيَنُ مِنَ الزُّبْدِ، وَلَيْسَ لَهُ عَجَمٌ.

وَحَدَّثَنَا أَبِي: حَدَّثَنَا مُوسَى بْنُ إِسْمَاعِيلَ، حَدَّثَنَا حَمَّادُ -هُوَ ابْنُ سَلَمَةَ-عَنْ أَبِي هَارُونَ، عَنْ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ الْخُدْرِيِّ، أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "نَظَرْتُ إِلَى الْجَنَّةِ فَإِذَا الرُّمَّانَةُ مِنْ رُمَّانِهَا كَمِثْلِ الْبَعِيرِ المُقْتَب" .

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿فِيهِنَّ خَيْرَاتٌ حِسَانٌ﴾ قِيلَ: الْمُرَادُ خَيْرَاتٌ كَثِيرَةٌ حَسَنَةٌ فِي الْجَنَّةِ، قَالَهُ قَتَادَةُ. وَقِيلَ: خَيْرَاتٌ جَمْعُ خَيِّرَةٍ، وَهِيَ الْمَرْأَةُ الصَّالِحَةُ الْحَسَنَةُ الخُلُق الْحَسَنَةُ الْوَجْهِ، قَالَهُ الْجُمْهُورُ. وَرُوِيَ مَرْفُوعًا عَنْ أُمِّ سَلَمَةَ . وَفِي الْحَدِيثِ الْآخَرِ الَّذِي سَنُورِدُهُ فِي سُورَةِ "الْوَاقِعَةِ" : أَنَّ الْحُورَ الْعِينَ يُغَنِّينَ: نَحْنُ الْخَيْرَاتُ الْحِسَانُ، خُلِقْنَا لِأَزْوَاجٍ كِرَامٍ. وَلِهَذَا قَرَأَ بَعْضُهُمْ: "فِيهِنَّ خَيّرات"، بِالتَّشْدِيدِ ﴿حِسَانٌ. فَبِأَيِّ آلاءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ﴾ .

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿حُورٌ مَقْصُورَاتٌ فِي الْخِيَامِ﴾ ، وَهُنَاكَ قَالَ: ﴿فِيهِنَّ قَاصِرَاتُ الطَّرْفِ﴾ ، وَلَا شَكَّ أَنَّ الَّتِي قَدْ قَصَرَت طَرْفَهَا بِنَفْسِهَا أَفْضَلُ مِمَّنْ قُصرت، وَإِنْ كَانَ الْجَمِيعُ مُخَدَّرَاتٍ.

قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا عَمْرُو بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الْأَوْدِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا وَكِيعٌ، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ، عَنْ جَابِرٍ، عَنِ الْقَاسِمِ بْنِ أَبِي بزَّة، عَنْ أَبِي عُبَيْدَةَ، عَنْ مَسْرُوقٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ قَالَ: إِنَّ لِكُلِّ مُسْلِمٍ خَيرة، وَلِكُلِّ خَيرة خَيْمَةٌ، وَلِكُلِّ خَيْمَةٍ أَرْبَعَةُ أَبْوَابٍ، يَدْخُلُ عَلَيْهَا كُلَّ يَوْمٍ تُحْفَةٌ وَكَرَامَةٌ وَهَدِيَّةٌ لَمْ تَكُنْ قَبْلَ ذَلِكَ، لَا مَرّاحات وَلَا طَمّاحات، وَلَا بَخِرَاتٍ وَلَا ذَفِرَاتٍ، حُورٌ عِينٌ، كَأَنَّهُنَّ بَيْضٌ مَكْنُونٌ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فِي الْخِيَامِ﴾ ، قَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ:

حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ الْمُثَنَّى، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الْعَزِيزِ بْنُ عَبْدِ الصَّمَدِ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو عِمْرَانَ الْجَوْنِيُّ، عَنْ أَبِي بَكْرِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ قَيْسٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ؛ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عليه وسلم قال: "إِنَّ فِي الْجَنَّةِ خَيْمَةً مِنْ لُؤْلُؤَةٍ مُجَوَّفَةٍ، عَرْضُهَا سِتُّونَ مِيلًا فِي كُلِّ زَاوِيَةٍ مِنْهَا أهلٌ مَا يَرون الْآخَرِينَ، يَطُوفُ عَلَيْهِمُ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ".

وَرَوَاهُ أَيْضًا مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي عِمْرَانَ، بِهِ . وَقَالَ: "ثَلَاثُونَ مِيلًا". وَأَخْرَجَهُ مُسْلِمٌ مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي عِمْرَانَ، بِهِ. وَلَفْظُهُ: "إِنَّ لِلْمُؤْمِنِ فِي الْجَنَّةِ لَخَيْمَةً مِنْ لُؤْلُؤَةٍ وَاحِدَةٍ مُجَوَّفَةٍ، طُولُهَا ستون ميلا لِلْمُؤْمِنِ فِيهَا أَهْلٌ يَطُوفُ عَلَيْهِمُ الْمُؤْمِنُ، فَلَا يَرَى بَعْضُهُمْ بَعْضًا" .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا الْحَسَنُ بْنُ أَبِي الرَّبِيعِ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرزَّاق، أَخْبَرَنَا مَعْمَرٌ، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ، أَخْبَرَنِي خُلَيْد العَصَري، عَنْ أَبِي الدَّرْدَاءِ قَالَ: الْخَيْمَةُ لُؤْلُؤَةٌ وَاحِدَةٌ، فِيهَا سَبْعُونَ بَابًا مِنْ دُرٍّ.

وَحَدَّثَنَا أَبِي حَدَّثَنَا عِيسَى بْنُ أَبِي فَاطِمَةَ، حَدَّثَنَا جَرِيرٌ، عَنْ هِشَامٍ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ الْمُثَنَّى، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿حُورٌ مَقْصُورَاتٌ فِي الْخِيَامِ﴾ ، وَقَالَ: [فِي] خِيَامِ اللُّؤْلُؤِ، وَفِي الجنة خيمة واحدة من لؤلؤة، أربع فَرَاسِخَ فِي أَرْبَعَةِ فَرَاسِخَ، عَلَيْهَا أَرْبَعَةُ آلَافِ مِصْرَاعٍ مِنَ الذَّهَبِ.

وَقَالَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ وَهْبٍ: أَخْبَرَنَا عَمْرٌو أَنَّ دَرَّاجا أَبَا السَّمح حَدَّثَهُ، عَنْ أَبِي الْهَيْثَمِ، عَنْ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ، قَالَ: "أَدْنَى أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ مَنْزِلَةً الَّذِي لَهُ ثَمَانُونَ أَلْفَ خَادِمٍ، وَاثْنَتَانِ وَسَبْعُونَ زَوْجَةً، وَتُنْصَبُ لَهُ قُبَّةٌ مِنْ لُؤْلُؤٍ وَزَبَرْجَدٍ وَيَاقُوتٍ، كَمَا بَيْنَ الْجَابِيَةِ وَصَنْعَاءَ".

وَرَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ عَمْرِو بْنِ الْحَارِثِ، بِهِ .

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿لَمْ يَطْمِثْهُنَّ إِنْسٌ قَبْلَهُمْ وَلا جَانٌّ﴾ : [قَدْ] تَقَدَّمَ مِثْلُهُ سَوَاءٌ، إِلَّا أَنَّهُ زَادَ فِي وَصْفِ الْأَوَائِلِ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿كَأَنَّهُنَّ الْيَاقُوتُ وَالْمَرْجَانُ. فَبِأَيِّ آلاءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ﴾ .

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿مُتَّكِئِينَ عَلَى رَفْرَفٍ خُضْرٍ وَعَبْقَرِيٍّ حِسَانٍ﴾ : قَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: الرَّفْرَفُ: الْمَحَابِسُ. وَكَذَا قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَعِكْرِمَةُ، وَالْحَسَنُ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَغَيْرُهُمَا: هِيَ الْمَحَابِسُ. وَقَالَ الْعَلَاءُ بْنُ بَدْرٍ الرَّفْرَفُ عَلَى السَّرِيرِ، كَهَيْئَةِ الْمَحَابِسِ الْمُتَدَلِّي.

وَقَالَ عَاصِمٌ الْجَحْدَرِيُّ: ﴿مُتَّكِئِينَ عَلَى رَفْرَفٍ خُضْرٍ﴾ يَعْنِي: الْوَسَائِدَ. وَهُوَ قَوْلُ الْحَسَنِ الْبَصْرِيِّ فِي رِوَايَةٍ عَنْهُ.

وَقَالَ أَبُو دَاوُدَ الطَّيَالِسِيُّ، عَنْ شُعْبَةَ، عَنْ أَبِي بِشْرٍ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿مُتَّكِئِينَ عَلَى رَفْرَفٍ خُضْرٍ﴾ قَالَ: الرَّفْرَفُ: رِيَاضُ الْجَنَّةِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَعَبْقَرِيٍّ حِسَانٍ﴾ : قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ: الْعَبْقَرِيُّ: الزَّرَابِيُّ. وَقَالَ سَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ: هِيَ عِتَاقُ الزَّرَابِيِّ، يَعْنِي: جِيَادَهَا.

وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: الْعَبْقَرِيُّ: الدِّيبَاجُ.

وَسُئِلَ الْحَسَنُ الْبَصْرِيُّ عَنْ قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَعَبْقَرِيٍّ حِسَانٍ﴾ فَقَالَ: هِيَ بُسُطُ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ -لا أبا لكم- فَاطْلُبُوهَا. وَعَنِ الْحَسَنِ [الْبَصْرِيِّ] رِوَايَةٌ: أَنَّهَا الْمَرَافِقُ. وَقَالَ زَيْدُ بْنُ أَسْلَمَ: الْعَبْقَرِيُّ: أَحْمَرُ وَأَصْفَرُ وَأَخْضَرُ. وَسُئِلَ الْعَلَاءُ بْنُ زَيْدٍ عَنِ الْعَبْقَرِيِّ، فَقَالَ: الْبُسُطُ أَسْفَلَ مِنْ ذَلِكَ. وَقَالَ أَبُو حَزْرَة يَعْقُوبُ بْنُ مُجَاهِدٍ: الْعَبْقَرِيُّ: مِنْ ثِيَابِ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ، لَا يَعْرِفُهُ أَحَدٌ. وَقَالَ أَبُو الْعَالِيَةِ: الْعَبْقَرِيُّ: الطَّنَافِسُ المخْمَلة، إِلَى الرِّقَّةِ مَا هِيَ. وَقَالَ الْقُتَيْبِيُّ: كُلُّ ثَوْبٍ مُوَشى عِنْدَ الْعَرَبِ عَبْقَرِيٌّ. وَقَالَ أَبُو عُبَيْدَةَ: هُوَ مَنْسُوبٌ إِلَى أَرْضٍ يُعْمَلُ بِهَا الْوَشْيُ. وَقَالَ الْخَلِيلُ بْنُ أَحْمَدَ: كُلُّ شَيْءٍ يَسُرُّ مِنَ الرِّجَالِ وَغَيْرِ ذَلِكَ يُسَمَّى عِنْدَ الْعَرَبِ عَبْقَرِيًّا. وَمِنْهُ قَوْلُ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ فِي عُمَرَ: "فَلَمْ أَرَ عَبْقَرِيًّا يَفْرِي فَرِيَّهُ" .

وَعَلَى كُلِّ تَقْدِيرٍ فَصِفَةُ مَرَافِقِ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّتَيْنِ الْأُولَيَيْنِ أَرْفَعُ وَأَعْلَى مِنْ هَذِهِ الصِّفَةِ؛ فَإِنَّهُ قَدْ قَالَ هُنَاكَ: ﴿مُتَّكِئِينَ عَلَى فُرُشٍ بَطَائِنُهَا مِنْ إِسْتَبْرَقٍ﴾ ، فَنَعَتَ بَطَائِنَ فُرُشِهِمْ وَسَكَتَ عَنْ ظَهَائِرِهَا ، اكْتِفَاءً بِمَا مَدَحَ بِهِ الْبَطَائِنَ بِطَرِيقِ الْأَوْلَى وَالْأَحْرَى. وَتَمَامُ الْخَاتِمَةِ أَنَّهُ قَالَ بَعْدَ الصِّفَاتِ الْمُتَقَدِّمَةِ: ﴿هَلْ جَزَاءُ الإحْسَانِ إِلا الإحْسَانُ﴾ فَوَصَفَ أَهْلَهَا بِالْإِحْسَانِ وَهُوَ أَعْلَى الْمَرَاتِبِ وَالنِّهَايَاتِ، كَمَا فِي حَدِيثِ جِبْرِيلَ لَمَّا سَأَلَ عَنِ الْإِسْلَامِ، ثُمَّ الْإِيمَانِ. فَهَذِهِ وُجُوهٌ عَدِيدَةٌ فِي تَفْضِيلِ الجنتين الأوليين على هاتين الأخيريين ، وَنَسْأَلُ اللَّهَ الْكَرِيمَ الْوَهَّابَ أَنْ يَجْعَلَنَا مِنْ أَهْلِ الْأُولَيَيْنِ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿تَبَارَكَ اسْمُ رَبِّكَ ذِي الْجَلالِ وَالإكْرَامِ﴾ أَيْ: هُوَ أَهْلٌ أَنْ يُجَلَّ فَلَا يُعْصَى، وَأَنْ يُكْرَمَ فَيُعْبَدَ، وَيُشْكَرَ فَلَا يُكْفَرَ، وَأَنْ يُذْكَرَ فَلَا يُنْسَى.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿ذِي الْجَلالِ وَالإكْرَامِ﴾ ذِي الْعَظَمَةِ وَالْكِبْرِيَاءِ.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا مُوسَى بْنُ دَاوُدَ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ ثَابِتِ بْنِ ثَوْبَانَ، عَنْ عُمَيْرِ بْنِ هَانِئٍ، عَنْ أَبِي الْعَذْرَاءِ، عَنْ أَبِي الدَّرْدَاءِ، قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "أجِدّوا اللَّهَ يَغْفِرْ لَكُمْ" .

وَفِي الْحَدِيثِ الْآخَرِ: "إِنَّ مِنْ إِجْلَالِ اللَّهِ إِكْرَامَ ذِي الشَّيْبَةِ الْمُسْلِمِ، وَذِي السُّلْطَانِ، وَحَامِلِ الْقُرْآنِ غَيْرِ الْغَالِي فِيهِ وَلَا الْجَافِي عَنْهُ" .

وَقَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو يَعْلَى: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو يُوسُفَ الْجِيزِيُّ ، حَدَّثَنَا مُؤَمَّلُ بْنُ إِسْمَاعِيلَ، حَدَّثَنَا حَمَّادٌ، حَدَّثَنَا حُمَيْدٌ الطَّوِيلُ، عَنْ أَنَسٍ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عليه وسلم قَالَ: "أَلِظُّوا بِيَا ذَا الْجَلَالِ وَالْإِكْرَامِ".

وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ، عَنْ مَحْمُودِ بْنِ غَيْلَانَ، عَنْ مُؤَمَّلِ بْنِ إِسْمَاعِيلَ، عَنْ حَمَّادِ بْنِ سَلَمَةَ، بِهِ .

ثُمَّ قَالَ: غَلَطَ الْمُؤَمَّلُ فِيهِ، وَهُوَ غَرِيبٌ وَلَيْسَ بِمَحْفُوظٍ، وَإِنَّمَا يُرْوَى هَذَا عَنْ حَمَّادِ بْنِ سَلَمَةَ، عَنْ حُمَيْدٍ، عَنِ الْحَسَنِ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا إِبْرَاهِيمُ بْنُ إِسْحَاقَ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ الْمُبَارَكِ، عَنْ يَحْيَى بْنِ حَسَّانَ الْمَقْدِسِيِّ، عَنْ رَبِيعَةَ بْنِ عَامِرٍ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم يقول: "أَلِظُّوا بِذِي الْجَلَالِ وَالْإِكْرَامِ".

وَرَوَاهُ النَّسَائِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ الْمُبَارَكِ، بِهِ .

وَقَالَ الْجَوْهَرِيُّ: أَلَظَّ فُلَانٌ بِفُلَانٍ: إِذَا لَزِمَهُ .

وَقَوْلُ ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ: "أَلِظُّوا بِيَا ذَا الْجَلَالِ وَالْإِكْرَامِ" أَيِ: الْزَمُوا. وَيُقَالُ: الْإِلْظَاظُ هُوَ الْإِلْحَاحُ.

قُلْتُ: وَكِلَاهُمَا قَرِيبٌ مِنَ الْآخَرِ -وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ-وَهُوَ الْمُدَاوَمَةُ وَاللُّزُومُ وَالْإِلْحَاحُ. وَفِي صَحِيحِ مُسْلِمٍ وَالسُّنَنِ الْأَرْبَعَةِ مِنْ حَدِيثِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ الْحَارِثِ، عَنْ عَائِشَةَ قَالَتْ: كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ إِذَا سَلَّمَ لَا يَقْعُدُ -يَعْنِي: بَعْدَ الصَّلَاةِ-إِلَّا قَدْرَ مَا يَقُولُ: "اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ السَّلَامُ وَمِنْكَ السَّلَامُ، تَبَارَكْتَ ذَا الْجَلَالِ وَالْإِكْرَامِ" .

آخِرُ تَفْسِيرِ سُورَةِ الرَّحْمَنِ، وَلِلَّهِ الحمد [والمنة]
