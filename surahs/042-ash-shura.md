### Translation

# Tafsir of Surat al-Shura

It is Meccan

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful

The discussion of the disconnected letters has already preceded. Ibn Jarir has related here a strange, extraordinary, and rejected (munkar) report. He said:

Ahmad ibn Zuhayr told us, Abd al-Wahhab ibn Najdah al-Hawti told us, Abu al-Mughirah Abd al-Quddus ibn al-Hajjaj told us, from Artah ibn al-Mundhir, who said: A man came to Ibn Abbas, while Hudhayfah ibn al-Yaman was present with him, and said to him: "Tell me about the interpretation of Allah's saying {Ha Meem Ain Seen Qaf}." He said: Ibn Abbas lowered his head, then turned away from him. The man repeated his question, and Ibn Abbas turned away again, giving him no answer, disliking the question. The man repeated it a third time, and still Ibn Abbas gave him no reply. Then Hudhayfah said: "I will inform you of it. I know why he disliked it. It was revealed concerning a man from among the People of his House called Abd al-Ilah, or Abd Allah, who dwells upon a river among the rivers of the East, upon which two cities are built, the river cutting a channel between them. When Allah permits the removal of their dominion and the cutting off of their state and their term, Allah sends a fire upon one of them at night, so that it becomes black and dark by morning, having been burned, as though it had never been in its place. Its sister city awakens astonished, saying: 'How did it escape?' But no sooner has the whiteness of that day passed than every stubborn tyrant among them gathers within it, and then Allah causes it and them all to be swallowed up by the earth. That is the meaning of His saying {Ha Meem Ain Seen Qaf}: Ha Meem means a resolve from Allah, Most High, a trial and a decree; Ain means justice from Him; Seen means it will be; Qaf means it will befall these two cities."

Even stranger than this is what the hafiz Abu Ya'la al-Mawsili related in the second part of the Musnad of Ibn Abbas, and from Abu Dharr from the Prophet ﷺ concerning this matter, but its chain is very weak and disconnected. He said:

Abu Talib Abd al-Jabbar ibn Asim told us, Abu Abd al-Malik al-Hasan ibn Yahya al-Khushani al-Dimashqi told us, from Abu Mu'awiyah, who said: Umar ibn al-Khattab ascended the pulpit and said: "O people, has any of you heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ explain {Ha Meem Ain Seen Qaf}?" Ibn Abbas sprang up and said: "I have." He said: "Ha Meem is one of the names of Allah, Most High." Umar said: "And Ain?" He said: "Those who fled witnessed the punishment of the Day of Badr." Umar said: "And Seen?" He said: "{Those who have done wrong will come to know to what a turning they will be turned}." Umar said: "And Qaf?" He fell silent. Then Abu Dharr stood up and gave the same explanation as Ibn Abbas, may Allah be pleased with them both, and said: "Qaf: a calamity from the sky that will overwhelm the people."

Concerning His saying {Thus does He reveal to you and to those before you, Allah the Almighty, the All-Wise}: that is, just as He sent down this Qur'an to you, so too did He send down the Books and Scriptures to the prophets before you. His saying {Allah the Almighty}: that is, in His vengeance, {the All-Wise}: in His words and His deeds.

The Imam Malik, may Allah have mercy on him, related from Hisham ibn Urwah, from his father, from A'ishah: that al-Harith ibn Hisham asked the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, saying: "O Messenger of Allah, how does the revelation come to you?" The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Sometimes it comes to me like the ringing of a bell, and that is the hardest upon me, then it leaves me after I have retained what it said. And sometimes the angel comes to me in the form of a man and speaks to me, and I retain what he says." A'ishah said: I saw him when the revelation descended upon him on a bitterly cold day, and when it left him his forehead was streaming with sweat.

Both of them (al-Bukhari and Muslim) related it in the two Sahihs, and the wording is al-Bukhari's.

Al-Tabarani related it from Abd Allah ibn al-Imam Ahmad, from his father, from Amir ibn Salih, from Hisham ibn Urwah, from his father, from A'ishah, from al-Harith ibn Hisham: that he asked the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, "How does the revelation descend upon you?" He said: "Like the ringing of a bell, then it leaves me after I have retained what it said." He said: "And that is the hardest upon me." He said: "And sometimes the angel comes to me and appears to me in a form, and speaks to me, and I retain what he says."

The Imam Ahmad said: Qutaybah told us, Ibn Lahi'ah told us, from Yazid ibn Abi Habib, from Amr ibn al-Walid, from Abd Allah ibn Amr, may Allah be pleased with them both, who said: I asked the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, saying: "O Messenger of Allah, do you sense the revelation?" The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "I hear ringing sounds, then I fall silent at that. There is not a single time revelation comes to me but that I think my soul is being taken." Ahmad alone related this.

We have already mentioned the manner in which revelation came to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, at the beginning of our commentary on al-Bukhari, in a way that makes repeating it here unnecessary. To Allah belongs all praise and grace.

Concerning His saying, Most High, {To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is in the earth}: that is, all of it are His servants and His possession, under His subjugation and His disposal. {And He is the Most High, the Most Great}, as in His saying, Most High, {the Great, the Most High} [al-Ra'd: 9] and {and He is the Most High, the Great} [Saba: 23]. The verses on this theme are numerous.

Concerning His saying {The heavens almost break apart from above them}: Ibn Abbas, al-Dahhak, Qatadah, al-Suddi, and Ka'b al-Ahbar said: that is, out of awe, from His Majesty. {And the angels exalt their Lord with praise and ask forgiveness for those on earth}, as in His saying: {Those who carry the Throne and those around it exalt their Lord with praise and believe in Him and ask forgiveness for those who have believed: Our Lord, You encompass all things in mercy and knowledge} [Ghafir: 7].

Concerning His saying {Unquestionably, Allah is the Forgiving, the Merciful}: this is an announcement of that fact and an exaltation of it.

Concerning His saying {And those who have taken protectors besides Him}: meaning the polytheists, {Allah is watchful over them}: that is, He is a witness over their deeds, recording them and counting them precisely, and He will requite them for them with the fullest recompense. {And you are not a manager over them}: that is, you are only a warner, and Allah is a manager over all things.

تَفْسِيرُ سُورَةِ الشُّورَى

وَهِيَ مَكِّيَّةٌ

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

قَدْ تَقَدَّمَ الْكَلَامُ عَلَى الْحُرُوفِ الْمُقَطَّعَةِ. وَقَدْ رَوَى ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ هَاهُنَا أَثَرًا غَرِيبًا عَجِيبًا مُنْكَرًا، فَقَالَ:

حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ زُهَير، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الْوَهَّابِ بْنُ نَجْدَةَ الحَوْطي، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو الْمُغِيرَةِ عَبْدُ الْقُدُّوسِ بْنُ الْحَجَّاجِ، عَنْ أَرْطَاةَ بْنِ الْمُنْذِرِ قَالَ: جَاءَ رَجُلٌ إِلَى ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ فَقَالَ لَهُ -وَعِنْدَهُ حُذيفة بْنُ الْيَمَانِ-: أَخْبِرْنِي عَنْ تَفْسِيرِ قَوْلِ اللَّهِ: ﴿حم عسق﴾ قَالَ: فَأَطْرَقَ ثُمَّ أَعْرَضَ عَنْهُ، ثُمَّ كَرَّرَ مَقَالَتَهُ فَأَعْرَضَ عَنْهُ، فَلَمْ يُجِبْهُ بِشَيْءٍ وَكَرِهَ مَقَالَتَهُ، ثُمَّ كَرَّرَهَا الثَّالِثَةَ فَلَمْ يُحِرْ إِلَيْهِ شَيْئًا. فَقَالَ حُذَيْفَةُ : أَنَا أُنْبِئُكَ بِهَا، قَدْ عَرَفْتُ لِمَ كَرِهَهَا؟ نَزَلَتْ فِي رَجُلٍ مِنْ أَهْلِ بَيْتِهِ يُقَالُ لَهُ "عَبْدُ الْإِلَهِ" -أَوْ: عَبْدُ اللَّهِ-يَنْزِلُ عَلَى نَهْرٍ مِنْ أَنْهَارِ الْمَشْرِقِ تُبْنَى عَلَيْهِ مَدِينَتَانِ ، يَشُقُّ النَّهْرُ بَيْنَهُمَا شَقًّا، فَإِذَا أَذِنَ اللَّهُ فِي زَوَالِ مُلْكِهِمْ وَانْقِطَاعِ دَوْلَتِهِمْ وَمُدَّتِهِمْ، بَعَثَ اللَّهُ عَلَى إِحْدَاهُمَا نَارًا لَيْلًا فَتُصْبِحُ سَوْدَاءَ مُظْلِمَةً وَقَدِ احْتَرَقَتْ، كَأَنَّهَا لَمْ تَكُنْ مَكَانَهَا، وَتُصْبِحُ صَاحِبَتُهَا مُتَعَجِّبَةً: كَيْفَ أَفْلَتَتْ؟ فَمَا هُوَ إِلَّا بَيَاضُ يَوْمِهَا ذَلِكَ، حَتَّى يَجْتَمِعَ فِيهَا كُلُّ جَبَّارٍ عَنِيدٍ مِنْهُمْ، ثُمَّ يَخْسِفُ اللَّهُ بِهَا وَبِهِمْ جَمِيعًا، فَذَلِكَ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿حم عسق﴾ يَعْنِي: عَزِيمَةٌ مِنَ اللَّهِ تَعَالَى وَفِتْنَةٌ وَقَضَاءٌ حُمّ: ﴿حُمَّ﴾ عَيْنٌ: يَعْنِي عَدْلًا مِنْهُ، سِينٌ: يَعْنِي سَيَكُونُ، ق: يَعْنِي واقع بِهَاتَيْنِ الْمَدِينَتَيْنِ .

وَأَغْرَبَ مِنْهُ مَا رَوَاهُ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو يَعْلَى الْمَوْصِلِيُّ فِي الْجُزْءِ الثَّانِي مِنْ مُسْنَدِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، وَعَنْ أَبِي ذَرٍّ عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ فِي ذَلِكَ، وَلَكِنَّ إِسْنَادَهُ ضَعِيفٌ جِدًّا وَمُنْقَطِعٌ، فَإِنَّهُ قَالَ:

حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو طَالِبٍ عَبْدُ الْجَبَّارِ بْنُ عَاصِمٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو عَبْدِ الْمَلِكِ الْحَسَنُ بْنُ يَحْيَى الخُشَني الدِّمَشْقِيُّ، عَنْ أَبِي مُعَاوِيَةَ قَالَ: صَعِدَ عُمَرُ بْنُ الْخَطَّابِ الْمِنْبَرَ فَقَالَ: أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ هَلْ سَمِعَ مِنْكُمْ أَحَدٌ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يُفَسِّرُ ﴿حم عسق﴾ ؟ فَوَثَبَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ فَقَالَ: أَنَا: قَالَ: " ﴿حم﴾ اسْمٌ مِنْ أَسْمَاءِ اللَّهِ تَعَالَى" قَالَ: فَعَيْنٌ؟ قَالَ: "عَايَنَ الْمُوَلُّونَ عَذَابَ يَوْمِ بَدْرٍ" قَالَ: فَسِينٌ؟ قال: "سيعلم الذين ظلموا أي منقلب يَنْقَلِبُونَ" قَالَ: فَقَافٌ؟ فَسَكَتَ فَقَامَ أَبُو ذَرٍّ، فَفَسَّرَ كَمَا قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا، وَقَالَ: قَافٌ: قَارِعَةٌ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ تَغْشَى النَّاسَ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿كَذَلِكَ يُوحِي إِلَيْكَ وَإِلَى الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِكَ اللَّهُ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ﴾ أَيْ: كَمَا أَنْزَلَ إِلَيْكَ هَذَا الْقُرْآنَ، كَذَلِكَ أَنْزَلَ الْكُتُبَ وَالصُّحُفَ عَلَى الْأَنْبِيَاءِ قَبْلَكَ. وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿اللَّهُ الْعَزِيزُ﴾ أَيْ: فِي انْتِقَامِهِ، ﴿الْحَكِيمُ﴾ فِي أَقْوَالِهِ وَأَفْعَالِهِ.

قَالَ: الْإِمَامُ مَالِكٌ -رَحِمَهُ اللَّهِ-عَنْ هِشَامِ بْنِ عُرْوَة عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ عَائِشَةَ: أَنَّ الْحَارِثَ بْنَ هِشَامٍ سَأَلَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، كَيْفَ يَأْتِيكَ الْوَحْيُ؟ فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "أَحْيَانًا يَأْتِينِي مِثْلَ صَلْصَلَةِ الجَرَس، وَهُوَ أَشَدُّهُ عَلَيّ فَيَفْصِمُ عَنِّي قَدْ وَعَيت مَا قَالَ. وَأَحْيَانًا يَأْتِينِي الْمَلَكُ رجُلا فَيُكَلِّمُنِي، فَأَعِي مَا يَقُولُ" قَالَتْ عَائِشَةُ فَلَقَدْ رَأَيْتُهُ يَنْزِلُ عَلَيْهِ الْوَحْيُ فِي الْيَوْمِ الشَّدِيدِ الْبَرْدِ، فَيَفْصِمُ عَنْهُ، وَإِنَّ جَبِينَهُ لِيَتَفَصَّدُ عَرَقًا.

أَخْرَجَاهُ فِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ، وَلَفْظُهُ لِلْبُخَارِيِّ .

وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ ابْنِ الْإِمَامِ أَحْمَدَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ عَامِرِ بْنِ صَالِحٍ، عَنْ هِشَامِ بْنِ عُرْوَةَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ عائشة، عَنِ الْحَارِثِ بْنِ هِشَامٍ؛ أَنَّهُ سَأَلَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ: كَيْفَ يَنْزِلُ عَلَيْكَ الْوَحْيُ؟ فَقَالَ: "مِثْلَ صَلْصَلَةِ الْجَرَسِ فيفصمُ عَنِّي وَقَدْ وعَيتُ مَا قَالَهُ" قَالَ: "وَهُوَ أَشَدُّهُ عَلَيَّ" قَالَ: "وَأَحْيَانًا يَأْتِينِي الْمَلَكُ فَيَتَمَثَّلُ لِي فَيُكَلِّمُنِي فَأَعِي مَا يَقُولُ" .

وَقَالَ: الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا قُتَيْبَةُ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ لَهِيعة، عَنْ يَزِيدَ بْنِ أَبِي حَبِيبٍ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ الْوَلِيدِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا، قَالَ: سَأَلْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقُلْتُ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، هَلْ تُحِسُّ بِالْوَحْيِ؟ فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: " أَسْمَعُ صَلَاصِلَ ثُمَّ أَسْكُتُ عِنْدَ ذَلِكَ، فَمَا مِنْ مَرَّةٍ يُوحَى إليَّ إِلَّا ظَنَنْتُ أَنَّ نَفْسِي تُقبَض" تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ أَحْمَدُ .

وَقَدْ ذَكَرْنَا كَيْفِيَّةَ إِتْيَانِ الْوَحْيِ إِلَى رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم في أَوَّلِ شَرْحِ الْبُخَارِيِّ، بِمَا أَغْنَى عَنْ إِعَادَتِهِ هَاهُنَا، وَلِلَّهِ الْحَمْدُ وَالْمِنَّةُ.

وَقَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿لَهُ مَا فِي السَّمَوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الأرْضِ﴾ أي: الْجَمِيعُ عَبِيدٌ لَهُ وَمِلْكٌ لَهُ، تَحْتَ قَهْرِهِ وَتَصْرِيفِهِ، ﴿وَهُوَ الْعَلِيُّ الْعَظِيمُ﴾ كَقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿الْكَبِيرُ الْمُتَعَالِ﴾ [الرَّعْدِ: ٩] ﴿وَهُوَ الْعَلِيُّ الْكَبِيرُ﴾ [سَبَأٍ: ٢٣] وَالْآيَاتُ فِي هَذَا كَثِيرَةٌ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿تَكَادُ السَّمَوَاتُ يَتَفَطَّرْنَ مِنْ فَوْقِهِنَّ﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ، وَكَعْبُ الْأَحْبَارِ: أَيْ فَرَقًا، مِنَ الْعَظَمَةِ ﴿وَالْمَلائِكَةُ يُسَبِّحُونَ بِحَمْدِ رَبِّهِمْ وَيَسْتَغْفِرُونَ لِمَنْ فِي الأرْضِ﴾ كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿الَّذِينَ يَحْمِلُونَ الْعَرْشَ وَمَنْ حَوْلَهُ يُسَبِّحُونَ بِحَمْدِ رَبِّهِمْ وَيُؤْمِنُونَ بِهِ وَيَسْتَغْفِرُونَ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا رَبَّنَا وَسِعْتَ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ رَحْمَةً وَعِلْمًا﴾ [غَافِرٍ:٧] .

## 42:7-8 — verses 7-8 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says: just as We revealed to the prophets before you, {We have revealed to you an Arabic Qur'an}, meaning clear, manifest, plain, {so that you may warn the Mother of Cities} which is Mecca, {and whoever is around it}, meaning the rest of the lands, east and west. Mecca was named "the Mother of Cities" because it is more noble than all other lands, on account of many proofs mentioned in their respective places. Among the most concise and clearest of these is what Imam Ahmad said:

[chain] Abu al-Yaman told us, Shu'ayb told us, from al-Zuhri, Abu Salamah ibn Abd al-Rahman informed us that Abdullah ibn Adi ibn al-Hamra al-Zuhri informed him that he heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say, while standing at al-Hazwarah in the market of Mecca: "By Allah, you are indeed the best of Allah's land and the most beloved of Allah's land to Allah, and had I not been driven out of you, I would not have left."

This is likewise the report of al-Tirmidhi, al-Nasa'i, and Ibn Majah, from the hadith of al-Zuhri, with the same wording. Al-Tirmidhi said: hasan sahih (sound and authentic).

His statement: {and warn of the Day of Gathering}, which is the Day of Resurrection, when Allah gathers the first and the last on a single plain.

His statement: {There is no doubt about it}, meaning there is no doubt about its occurrence, and that it will inevitably come to pass. His statement: {a party in Paradise and a party in the Blazing Fire}, is like His statement: {The Day He will gather you for the Day of Gathering, that is the Day of Mutual Loss and Gain} [al-Taghabun: 9], meaning the people of Paradise will cause the people of the Fire to lose out. And like His statement: {That is a Day for which mankind will be gathered, and that is a Day to be witnessed. And We do not delay it except for a term already fixed. On the Day it comes, no soul will speak except by His permission, and among them will be the wretched and the fortunate} [Hud: 103-105].

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Hashim ibn al-Qasim told us, Layth told us, Abu Qabil al-Ma'afiri told me, from Shufayy al-Asbahi, from Abdullah ibn Amr, may Allah be pleased with them both, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ came out to us with two books in his hand, and said: "Do you know what these two books are?" We said: "No, unless you tell us, O Messenger of Allah." He said, regarding the one in his right hand: "This is a book from the Lord of the worlds, containing the names of the people of Paradise, and the names of their fathers and their tribes, and then he summarized what comes after them, nothing will ever be added to them nor taken away from them." Then he said regarding the one in his left hand: "This is the book of the people of the Fire, with their names and the names of their fathers and their tribes, and then he summarized what comes after them, nothing will ever be added to them nor taken away from them." The companions of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Then for what purpose do we act, if this matter has already been settled?" The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Act rightly and draw near, for the companion of Paradise is sealed with the deeds of Paradise, whatever deed he may do, and the companion of the Fire is sealed with the deeds of the Fire, whatever deed he may do." Then he made a gesture with his hand and closed it, and said: "Your Lord, Mighty and Majestic, has finished with His servants." Then he made a gesture with his right hand and cast it forth, saying: "A party in Paradise," and cast forth with his left hand, saying: "and a party in the Blazing Fire."

This is likewise how al-Tirmidhi and al-Nasa'i both narrated it, from Qutaybah, from Layth ibn Sa'd and Bakr ibn Mudar, both of them from Abu Qabil, from Shufayy ibn Mati' al-Asbahi, from Abdullah ibn Amr, with the same content.

Al-Tirmidhi said: hasan sahih gharib (sound, authentic, singular in its chain).

Al-Baghawi related it in his tafsir through the route of Bishr ibn Bakr, from Sa'id ibn Uthman, from Abu al-Zahiriyyah, from Abdullah ibn Amr, from the Prophet ﷺ, mentioning it similarly. He has additions, among them: then he said: "A party in Paradise and a party in the Blazing Fire, this is justice from Allah, Mighty and Majestic."

Ibn Abi Hatim related it from his father, from Abdullah ibn Salih, the scribe of Layth, from Layth, with the same content.

Ibn Jarir related it from Yunus, from Ibn Wahb, from Amr ibn al-Harith, from Abu Qabil, from Shufayy, from a man among the companions, mentioning it likewise.

Then it was related from Yunus, from Ibn Wahb, from Amr ibn al-Harith and Hayawah ibn Shurayh, from Yahya ibn Abi Usayd, that Abu Firas told him that he heard Abdullah ibn Amr say: when Allah created Adam, He shook him as one shakes a provision bag, and brought forth from him all his offspring, and they came out like small worm-like creatures. He then gathered them into two handfuls, and said: "wretched and fortunate." Then He cast them down, then gathered them again, and said: "a party in Paradise, and a party in the Blazing Fire."

This mawquf (halted) report is closer to being correct, and Allah knows best.

Imam Ahmad, may Allah have mercy on him, said: [chain] Abd al-Samad told us, Hammad, that is, ibn Salamah, told us, al-Jurayri informed us, from Abu Nadrah, that a man among the companions of the Prophet ﷺ called Abu Abdullah, whose companions came to visit him while he was ill and he was weeping, they said to him: "What makes you weep? Did not the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say to you, 'Trim your mustache, then leave it be until you meet me'?" He said: "Yes, but I heard the Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, say: 'Allah took a handful with His right hand, and another with His other hand, and said: this is for this, and this is for this, and I do not care.' So I do not know in which of the two handfuls I am."

The hadiths about al-qadar (divine decree) in the sahih collections, the sunan, and the musnads are very numerous, among them the hadith of Ali, Ibn Mas'ud, A'ishah, and a great many others.

His statement: {And had Allah willed, He would have made them one nation}, meaning either upon guidance or upon misguidance, but the Exalted made distinctions among them, guiding whom He wills to the truth and leading astray from it whom He wills, and His is the wisdom and the conclusive argument. Therefore He said: {but He admits whom He wills into His mercy, and the wrongdoers have no protector and no helper}.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Yunus told me, Ibn Wahb informed us, Amr ibn al-Harith informed me, from Abu Suwayd, who was told by Ibn Hujayrah, that it reached him that Moses (peace be upon him) said: "O Lord, Your creation whom You created, You have made a party of them for Paradise and a party for the Fire, why not admit them all into Paradise?" He said: "O Moses, raise up your forearm's length." So he raised it. He said: "I have raised it." He said: "Raise it more." So he raised it, leaving nothing out. He said: "O Lord, I have raised it." He said: "Raise it more." He said: "I have raised it, except for that in which there is no good." He said: "Likewise, I admit all My creation into Paradise, except for that in which there is no good."

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ﴾ أَيْ: لَا شَكَّ فِي وُقُوعِهِ، وَأَنَّهُ كَائِنٌ لَا مَحَالَةَ. وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَرِيقٌ فِي الْجَنَّةِ وَفَرِيقٌ فِي السَّعِيرِ﴾ ، كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿يَوْمَ يَجْمَعُكُمْ لِيَوْمِ الْجَمْعِ ذَلِكَ يَوْمُ التَّغَابُنِ﴾ [التَّغَابُنِ:٩] أَيْ: يَغْبَن أَهْلُ الْجَنَّةِ أَهْلَ النَّارِ، وَكَقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿ذَلِكَ يَوْمٌ مَجْمُوعٌ لَهُ النَّاسُ وَذَلِكَ يَوْمٌ مَشْهُودٌ وَمَا نُؤَخِّرُهُ إِلا لأجَلٍ مَعْدُودٍ يَوْمَ يَأْتِ لَا تَكَلَّمُ نَفْسٌ إِلا بِإِذْنِهِ فَمِنْهُمْ شَقِيٌّ وَسَعِيدٌ﴾ [هُودٍ:١٠٣-١٠٥] .

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا هَاشِمُ بْنُ الْقَاسِمِ، حَدَّثَنَا لَيْث، حَدَّثَنِي أَبُو قَبِيلٍ الْمَعَافِرِيُّ، عَنْ شُفَيّ الْأَصْبَحِيِّ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو -رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا-قَالَ: خَرَجَ عَلَيْنَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ وَفِي يَدِهِ كِتَابَانِ، فَقَالَ: "أَتَدْرُونَ مَا هَذَانِ الْكِتَابَانِ؟ " قَالَ: قُلْنَا: لَا إِلَّا أَنْ تُخْبِرَنَا يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ قال للذي في يده اليمينى: "هَذَا كِتَابٌ مِنْ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ، بِأَسْمَاءِ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ وَأَسْمَاءِ آبَائِهِمْ وَقَبَائِلِهِمْ، ثُمَّ أَجْمَلَ عَلَى آخِرِهِمْ -لَا يُزَادُ فِيهِمْ وَلَا يُنْقَصُ مِنْهُمْ أَبَدًا" ثُمَّ قَالَ لِلَّذِي فِي يَسَارِهِ: "هَذَا كِتَابُ أَهْلِ النَّارِ بِأَسْمَائِهِمْ وَأَسْمَاءِ آبَائِهِمْ وَقَبَائِلِهِمْ، ثُمَّ أَجْمَلَ عَلَى آخِرِهِمْ -لَا يُزَادُ فِيهِمْ وَلَا يُنْقَصُ مِنْهُمْ أَبَدًا" فَقَالَ أَصْحَابُ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ: فَلِأَيِّ شَيْءٍ إذًا نعمل إن كان هذا أمر قَدْ فُرِغ مِنْهُ؟ فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "سَدِّدُوا وَقَارِبُوا، فَإِنَّ صَاحِبَ الْجَنَّةِ يُخْتَمُ لَهُ بِعَمَلِ الْجَنَّةِ ، وَإِنْ عَمِلَ أَيَّ عَمل، وَإِنَّ صَاحِبَ النَّارِ يُخْتَمُ لَهُ بِعَمَلِ النَّارِ ، وَإِنْ عَمِلَ أَيَّ عَمَلٍ" ثُمَّ قَالَ بِيَدِهِ فَقَبَضَهَا، ثُمَّ قَالَ: "فَرَغَ رَبُّكُمْ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ مِنَ الْعِبَادِ" ثُمَّ قَالَ بِالْيُمْنَى فَنَبَذَ بِهَا فَقَالَ: "فَرِيقٌ فِي الْجَنَّةِ"، وَنَبَذَ بِالْيُسْرَى فَقَالَ: "فَرِيقٌ فِي السَّعِيرِ"

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ وَالنَّسَائِيُّ جَمِيعًا، عَنْ قُتَيْبَةَ عَنِ اللَّيْثِ بْنِ سَعْدٍ وَبَكْرِ بْنِ مُضَرَ، كِلَاهُمَا عَنْ أَبِي قَبِيلٍ، عَنْ شُفَيّ بْنِ مَاتِعٍ الْأَصْبَحِيِّ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو، بِهِ .

وَقَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ غَرِيبٌ.

وَسَاقَهُ الْبَغَوِيُّ فِي تَفْسِيرِهِ مِنْ طَرِيقِ بِشْرِ بْنِ بَكْرٍ ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ عُثْمَانَ، عَنْ أَبِي الزَّاهِرِيَّةِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ فَذَكَرَهُ بِنَحْوِهِ. وَعِنْدَهُ زِيَادَاتٌ مِنْهَا: ثُمَّ قَالَ: "فَرِيقٌ فِي الْجَنَّةِ وَفَرِيقٌ فِي السَّعِيرِ، عَدْلٌ مِنَ اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ" .

وَرَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ عن أبيه، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ صَالِحٍ -كَاتِبِ اللَّيْثِ-عَنِ اللَّيْثِ، بِهِ.

وَرَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ عَنْ يُونُسَ، عَنِ ابْنِ وَهْبٍ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ الْحَارِثِ، عَنْ أَبِي قَبِيل، عَنْ شُفَيٍّ، عَنْ رَجُلٍ مِنَ الصَّحَابَةِ، فَذَكَرَهُ .

ثُمَّ رُوِيَ عَنْ يُونُسَ، عَنِ ابْنِ وَهْب، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ الْحَارِثِ وحَيْوَة بْنِ شُرَيْح، عَنْ يَحْيَى بْنِ أَبِي أُسَيْدٍ؛ أَنَّ أَبَا فِرَاسٍ حَدَّثَهُ: أَنَّهُ سَمِعَ عَبْدَ اللَّهِ بْنَ عَمْرٍو يَقُولُ: إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَمَّا خَلَقَ آدَمَ نَفَضَهُ نَفْضَ المزْوَد ، وَأَخْرَجَ مِنْهُ كُلَّ ذُرِّيَّتِهِ، فَخَرَجَ أَمْثَالَ النَّغَف، فَقَبَضَهُمْ قَبْضَتَيْنِ، ثُمَّ قَالَ: شَقِيٌّ وَسَعِيدٌ، ثُمَّ أَلْقَاهُمَا، ثُمَّ قَبَضَهُمَا فَقَالَ: فَرِيقٌ فِي الْجَنَّةِ، وَفَرِيقٌ فِي السَّعِيرِ .

## 42:9-12 — verses 9-12 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty is speaking in denunciation of the polytheists for their taking of gods besides Allah, and informing that He is the true Guardian to whom worship alone is due, for He is capable of bringing the dead to life and He has power over all things.

Then He said: {And whatever you differ in, its judgment is to Allah}, meaning: whatever matters you differ in, and this is general regarding all things, {its judgment is to Allah}, meaning: He is the Judge in it through His Book and the Sunnah of His Prophet ﷺ, as in His saying: {And if you dispute over anything, refer it to Allah and the Messenger} [al-Nisa: 59].

{That is Allah, my Lord}, meaning: the Judge over all things, {upon Him I rely and to Him I turn}, meaning: I return in all matters.

His saying: {Originator of the heavens and the earth}, meaning: the Creator of both of them and what is between them. {He has made for you mates from among yourselves}, meaning: from your own kind and form, as a favor and grace bestowed upon you, He made from your kind male and female. {And among the cattle mates}, meaning: and He created for you eight pairs of cattle.

His saying: {He multiplies you thereby}, meaning: He creates you thereby, that is, within that creation in this manner He continually multiplies you within it, males and females, creation after creation, generation after generation, offspring after offspring, from mankind and from the cattle.

Al-Baghawi, may Allah have mercy on him, said: {He multiplies you thereby}, meaning: in the womb. It is also said: in the belly. It is also said: in this mode of creation.

Mujahid said: offspring after offspring, from mankind and from the cattle.

It is also said that "fi" here carries the meaning of "bi", meaning: He multiplies you by means of it.

{There is nothing like unto Him}, meaning: there is nothing like the Creator of all these pairs, because He is al-Fard (the One), al-Samad (the Eternal Refuge, the One depended upon by all, free of need Himself, the Master toward whom all affairs are directed), who has no equal. {And He is the All-Hearing, the All-Seeing}.

His saying: {To Him belong the keys of the heavens and the earth}: its interpretation has already preceded in Surat al-Zumar, and the sum of it is that He is the Disposer and Judge over both of them. {He extends provision for whom He wills and restricts it}, meaning: He widens it for whom He wills and narrows it for whom He wills, and to Him belongs perfect wisdom and complete justice. {Indeed He is Knowing of all things}.

## 42:13-14 — verses 13-14 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says to this nation: {He has ordained for you of the religion what He enjoined upon Noah, and that which We have revealed to you}. He mentioned the first of the messengers after Adam, who is Noah (peace be upon him), and the last of them, who is Muhammad ﷺ. Then He mentioned, from among them, those of firm resolve (uli al-azm), who are Abraham, Moses, and Jesus son of Mary (peace be upon them). This verse gathered the mention of the five, just as the verse of "al-Ahzab" included them in His saying: {And when We took from the prophets their covenant, and from you, and from Noah, Abraham, Moses, and Jesus son of Mary} [al-Ahzab: 7].

The religion that all the messengers brought is the worship of God alone, without partner, as He said: {And We did not send before you any messenger except that We revealed to him that there is no god but Me, so worship Me} [al-Anbiya: 25]. And in the hadith: "We, the community of prophets, are brothers from different mothers; our religion is one," meaning that the shared element among them is the worship of God alone without partner, even though their laws (shara'i) and paths (manahij) differed, as in His saying: {For each of you We have made a law and a way} [al-Ma'idah: 48]. This is why He says here: {that you establish the religion and not be divided therein}, meaning God, glorified and exalted, enjoined upon all the prophets (peace be upon them) unity and togetherness, and forbade them division and disagreement.

His saying: {Great upon the polytheists is that to which you call them} means: it was hard for them and they rejected what you, O Muhammad, call them to, namely monotheism (tawhid).

Then He said: {God chooses for Himself whom He wills, and guides to Himself whoever turns to Him in repentance}, meaning He is the One who decrees guidance for whoever deserves it, and decrees misguidance for whoever chooses it over the path of right guidance. This is why He said: {And they did not become divided until after knowledge had come to them}, meaning their opposition to the truth only occurred after it had reached them and the proof had been established against them, and nothing drove them to that except transgression, obstinacy, and hostility.

Then God, Exalted, said: {And had it not been for a word that preceded from your Lord until an appointed term}, meaning: were it not for the word that had preceded from God, granting respite to His servants until the settling of their account on the Day of Return, He would have hastened the punishment for them swiftly in this world.

His saying: {And indeed, those who were made to inherit the Scripture after them} refers to the later generation that came after the first generation which had denied the truth, {are in disquieting doubt concerning it}, meaning they are not upon certainty regarding their affair, but rather they are merely imitators of their fathers and forebears, without proof or evidence, and they are in bewilderment concerning their affair, in disquieting doubt, and in far-reaching discord.

## 42:15 — verse 15

### Translation

This noble verse comprises ten independent statements, each of them separate from the one before it, [having] a ruling of its own. They said: there is no parallel to it except the Verse of the Throne (Ayat al-Kursi), for it too consists of ten sections like this one.

His saying, {So to that call}, means: so to that which We revealed to you of the religion which We enjoined upon all the messengers before you, the possessors of the great, followed laws, such as the resolute ones (ulu al-azm) and others, call the people.

His saying, {and be steadfast as you have been commanded}, means: be steadfast, you and whoever follows you, in the worship of Allah, just as Allah, Mighty and Majestic, commanded you.

His saying, {and do not follow their desires}, means: the desires of the polytheists in what they invented, falsified, and fabricated regarding the worship of idols.

His saying, {and say, I believe in whatever Book Allah has sent down}, means: I affirm as true all the Books sent down from heaven upon the prophets, we do not distinguish between any of them.

His saying, {and I have been commanded to act justly between you}, means: in judgment, just as Allah commanded me.

His saying, {Allah is our Lord and your Lord}, means: He is the One worshipped, there is no god besides Him. We acknowledge this by choice, and you, even if you do not do so by choice, then to Him bows down whoever is in the worlds, willingly and by choice.

His saying, {for us are our deeds and for you are your deeds}, means: we are free of you, as He, Exalted, said: {And if they deny you, then say, "For me is my deed and for you is your deed. You are free of what I do and I am free of what you do"} [Yunus: 41].

His saying, {there is no argument between us and you}, Mujahid said: meaning, there is no dispute. Al-Suddi said: that was before the revelation of the Verse of the Sword. This is well directed, for this verse is Meccan, while the Verse of the Sword came after the migration.

His saying, {Allah will gather between us}, means: on the Day of Resurrection, as in His saying: {Say, "Our Lord will gather us together, then He will judge between us in truth, and He is the Knowing Judge"} [Saba: 26].

His saying, {and to Him is the final return}, means: the place of return and the destination on the Day of Reckoning.

## 42:16-18 — verses 16-18 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, threatening those who turn away from the path of God those who believe in Him: {And those who dispute concerning God after having been answered}, meaning: they argue with the believers who responded to God and His Messenger, in order to turn them away from the path of guidance which they had taken. {Their argument is futile before their Lord}, meaning: false before God. {And upon them is wrath}, meaning: from Him. {And for them is severe punishment}, meaning: on the Day of Resurrection.

Ibn Abbas and Mujahid said: they argued with the believers after they had responded to God and His Messenger, in order to turn them away from guidance, hoping that the Age of Ignorance (jahiliyyah) would return.

Qatadah said: they are the Jews and Christians, who said to them: our religion is better than yours, our prophet came before your prophet, and we are better than you and more deserving of God than you. But they lied in that.

Then He says: {God is He who sent down the Book in truth}, meaning: the books sent down from Him upon His prophets, {and the balance}, which is: justice and fairness, as Mujahid and Qatadah said. This is like His saying: {We have already sent Our messengers with clear proofs and sent down with them the Book and the balance that the people may maintain justice} [al-Hadid: 25], and His saying: {And the heaven He raised and He set up the balance. That you not transgress within the balance. And establish weight in justice and do not make deficient the balance} [al-Rahman: 7-9].

His saying: {And what will make you know? Perhaps the Hour is near} contains encouragement toward it, warning against it, and inducement to detach from the worldly life.

His saying: {Those who do not believe in it seek to hasten it} means: they say: {When is this promise, if you should be truthful?} [Saba: 29]. They say this only out of denial, dismissal as far-fetched, disbelief, and stubborn opposition. {But those who believe are fearful of it}, meaning: fearful and apprehensive of its occurrence, {and know that it is the truth}, meaning: that it will inevitably come to be, so they are prepared for it and act for its sake.

It has been narrated through chains reaching the degree of tawatur (mass transmission), in the sound and good collections, in the Sunan works and the Musnads, and in some of its wordings, that a man asked the Messenger of God ﷺ in a loud voice, while he was on one of his journeys, calling out to him and saying: O Muhammad! The Prophet ﷺ answered him in a voice similar to his: "Here I am." The man said: When is the Hour? The Messenger of God ﷺ said to him: "Woe to you, it is indeed coming, so what have you prepared for it?" He said: love of God and His Messenger. He said: "You will be with whom you love."

His saying in the hadith: "A person is with whom he loves" is mutawatir (mass-transmitted) without doubt. The point is that he did not answer him regarding the timing of the Hour, but rather commanded him to prepare for it.

His saying: {Unquestionably, those who dispute concerning the Hour} meaning: they argue about its occurrence and deny its coming, {are in extreme error}, meaning: in manifest ignorance, because the One who created the heavens and the earth is capable of bringing the dead to life by an even greater and more fitting right, as He said: {And it is He who begins creation and then repeats it, and that is even easier for Him} [al-Rum: 27].

## 42:19-22 — verses 19-22 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, informing of His gentleness toward His creation in providing for all of them down to the last one, forgetting none of them. He is equal in providing for the righteous and the wicked, as in His saying, the Exalted: {And there is no creature on earth but that its provision is upon Allah, and He knows its dwelling place and its place of storage; all is in a clear register} [Hud: 6]. This has many parallels.

His saying: {He provides for whom He wills}, that is, He makes ample provision for whomever He wills, {and He is the Powerful, the Mighty}, that is, nothing overcomes Him.

Then He said: {Whoever desires the harvest of the Hereafter}, that is, the deeds of the Hereafter, {We increase for him in his harvest}, that is, We strengthen him and aid him in what he is pursuing, and We increase its growth for him, and We reward him for a good deed with ten times its like up to seven hundredfold, up to whatever Allah wills. {And whoever desires the harvest of this world, We give him thereof, but he will have no share in the Hereafter}, that is, whoever's striving is only to obtain something of this world, and he has no concern at all for the Hereafter, Allah deprives him of the Hereafter, and as for this world, if He wills He gives him something of it, and if He does not will it, he obtains neither this nor that. This striver, with this intention, ends up with a losing bargain in both this world and the Hereafter.

The proof of this is that this verse here is qualified by the verse in Surat "Subhan" (al-Isra), which is His saying, the Exalted: {Whoever desires the immediate life, We hasten for him therein what We will to whomever We intend. Then We have made for him Hell, which he will enter, disgraced and expelled. And whoever desires the Hereafter and strives for it as it should be strived for while he is a believer, those are the ones whose striving is accepted with gratitude. To all, We extend from the gift of your Lord, both these and those, and the gift of your Lord is not restricted. Look how We have preferred some of them over others, and the Hereafter is greater in degrees and greater in preference} [al-Isra: 18-21].

Al-Thawri said, from Mughirah, from Abu al-'Aliyah, from Ubayy ibn Ka'b (may Allah be pleased with him), who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Give this nation glad tidings of eminence, elevation, victory, and establishment in the land. Whoever among them does the deeds of the Hereafter for the sake of this world will have no share in the Hereafter."

His saying: {Or do they have partners who have decreed for them a religion which Allah has not authorized?}, that is, they do not follow what Allah has legislated for you of the straight religion, but rather they follow what their devils among the jinn and mankind have legislated for them, of prohibiting what they prohibited for them, such as al-bahirah, al-sa'ibah, al-wasilah, and al-ham, and permitting carrion, blood, and gambling, and other such misguidance and false ignorance which they had invented in their pre-Islamic ignorance (jahiliyyah), of permitting and prohibiting, false acts of worship, and corrupt sayings.

It is established in the authentic hadith that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "I saw 'Amr ibn Luhayy ibn Qam'ah dragging his intestines in the Fire," because he was the first to institute the practice of the sa'ibah (setting animals loose as an offering). This man was one of the kings of Khuza'ah, and he was the first to do these things, and he was the one who led Quraysh to worship idols. May Allah curse him and disgrace him. This is why the Exalted said: {And had it not been for a decisive word already given, it would have been settled between them}, that is, they would have been hastily punished, were it not for the respite previously decreed until the Day of Return. {And indeed, the wrongdoers will have a painful punishment}, that is, severe and agonizing, in Hell, and how evil is the destination.

Then the Exalted said: {You will see the wrongdoers fearful because of what they earned}, that is, in the open plains of the Resurrection, {and it must befall them}, that is, what they fear will inevitably befall them. This is their state on the Day of their Return, while they are in this fear and dread. {But those who believed and did righteous deeds will be in the gardens' meadows; they will have whatever they wish in the presence of their Lord}. How far is this from that! How far is one who is in the plains of Resurrection, in humiliation, disgrace, and certain fear because of his wrongdoing, from one who is in the meadows of the gardens, having whatever he wishes of foods, drinks, garments, dwellings, sights, spouses, and pleasures, in what no eye has seen, no ear has heard, and no human heart has conceived.

Al-Hasan ibn 'Arafah said: 'Umar ibn 'Abd al-Rahman al-Abbar narrated to us, Muhammad ibn Sa'd al-Ansari narrated to us, from Abu Tayyibah, who said: The drinking companions among the people of Paradise, a cloud shades them and says: "What shall I rain upon you?" He said: No one among the people calls for anything but that it is rained upon them, so much so that a speaker among them says: "Rain upon us young full-breasted maidens of equal age."

Ibn Jarir narrated it from al-Hasan ibn 'Arafah, with this wording.

This is why the Exalted said: {That is the great bounty}, that is, the tremendous triumph and the complete, all-encompassing, comprehensive, general blessing.

## 42:23-24 — verses 23-24 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, having mentioned the meadows of Paradise for His servants who believed and did righteous deeds: {That is what Allah gives as glad tidings to His servants who believe and do righteous deeds}, meaning: this is something that will certainly come to be for them, by virtue of Allah's glad tidings to them of it.

Regarding His saying: {Say: I do not ask of you any reward for it except love for close relatives}, meaning: say, O Muhammad, to these polytheists among the disbelievers of Quraysh: I do not ask of you, in return for this conveyance and this counsel to you, anything that you would not give me. Rather, I only ask of you that you restrain your harm from me and leave me to convey the messages of my Lord. If you will not support me, then at least do not harm me on account of the kinship between me and you.

Al-Bukhari said: Muhammad ibn Bashshar told us, Muhammad ibn Ja'far told us, Shu'bah told us, from Abd al-Malik ibn Maysarah, who said: I heard Tawus from Ibn Abbas, that he was asked about the saying of the Almighty: {except love for close relatives}, and Sa'id ibn Jubayr said: the kinship of the family of Muhammad. Ibn Abbas said: You have been hasty. There was no clan of Quraysh but that the Prophet ﷺ had kinship with them among its members. Then he said: except that you maintain the kinship that exists between me and you. Al-Bukhari transmitted this alone.

Imam Ahmad related it from Yahya al-Qattan, from Shu'bah, with the same wording. Likewise, Amir al-Sha'bi, al-Dahhak, Ali ibn Abi Talhah, al-Awfi, Yusuf ibn Mihran, and others narrated the same from Ibn Abbas. Mujahid, Ikrimah, Qatadah, al-Suddi, Abu Malik, Abd al-Rahman ibn Zayd ibn Aslam, and others held the same view.

The hafiz Abu al-Qasim al-Tabarani said: Hashim ibn Yazid al-Tabarani and Ja'far al-Qalanisi told us, both saying: Adam ibn Abi Iyas told us, Sharik told us, from Khusayf, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, from Ibn Abbas, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said to them: "I do not ask of you any reward for it, except that you love me for my own sake because of my kinship with you, and that you preserve the kinship that exists between me and you."

Imam Ahmad related, from Hasan ibn Musa: Qaza'ah, that is, ibn Suwayd, told us, and Ibn Abi Hatim from his father, from Muslim ibn Ibrahim, from Qaza'ah ibn Suwayd, from Ibn Abi Najih, from Mujahid, from Ibn Abbas: that the Prophet ﷺ said: "I do not ask of you any reward for the clear proofs and guidance I have brought you, except that you show love toward Allah, and that you draw near to Him through obedience to Him."

Likewise, Qatadah related the same from al-Hasan al-Basri.

This appears to be an explanation according to a second view, as though he is saying: {except love for close relatives}, meaning: except that you act with the obedience that draws you near to Allah.

A third view is what al-Bukhari and others reported, as a narration from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, whose meaning is that he said: the meaning of that is that you love me by way of my relatives, that is, that you treat them well and act kindly toward them.

Al-Suddi said, from Abu al-Daylam, who said: When Ali ibn al-Husayn was brought as a captive and made to stand on the steps of Damascus, a man from the people of Syria stood up and said: Praise be to Allah who killed you, uprooted you, and cut off the two horns of sedition. Ali ibn al-Husayn said to him: Have you read the Qur'an? He said: Yes. He said: Have you read the family of Ha-Mim? He said: I have read the Qur'an, but I have not read "the family of Ha-Mim." He said: Have you not read: {Say: I do not ask of you any reward for it except love for close relatives}? He said: And are you they? He said: Yes.

Abu Ishaq al-Sabi'i said: I asked Amr ibn Shu'ayb about the saying of the Almighty: {Say: I do not ask of you any reward for it except love for close relatives}, and he said: the kinship of the Prophet ﷺ. Ibn Jarir related both of these.

Then Ibn Jarir said: Abu Kurayb told us, Malik ibn Isma'il told us, Abd al-Salam told us, Yazid ibn Abi Ziyad told me, from Miqsam, from Ibn Abbas, who said: The Ansar said, "We did such and such and such and such," as though they were boasting. Ibn Abbas, or al-Abbas (Abd al-Salam was uncertain), said: "We have precedence over you." This reached the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, and he came to them in their gatherings and said: "O company of the Ansar, were you not lowly, and Allah gave you honor through me?" They said: "Indeed so, O Messenger of Allah." He said: "Were you not astray, and Allah guided you through me?" They said: "Indeed so, O Messenger of Allah." He said: "Will you not answer me?" They said: "What shall we say, O Messenger of Allah?" He said: "Will you not say: Did not your people drive you out, and we gave you shelter? Did they not call you a liar, and we believed you? Did they not abandon you, and we gave you victory?" He continued speaking until they fell to their knees and said: "Our wealth and whatever is in our hands belong to Allah and His Messenger." He said: Then this was revealed: {Say: I do not ask of you any reward for it except love for close relatives}.

Ibn Abi Hatim likewise related it, from Ali ibn al-Husayn, from Abd al-Mu'min ibn Ali, from Abd al-Salam, from Yazid ibn Abi Ziyad, who is weak, with his chain, in the same or similar wording.

In the two Sahihs, in the section on the distribution of the spoils of Hunayn, there is something close to this account, but it does not mention the revelation of this verse there. The claim that it was revealed in Medina requires scrutiny, since the surah is Meccan, and there does not appear to be any correspondence between this noble verse and that context. Allah knows best.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: Ali ibn al-Husayn told us, a man he named told us, Husayn al-Ashqar told us, from Qays, from al-A'mash, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, from Ibn Abbas, who said: When this verse was revealed: {Say: I do not ask of you any reward for it except love for close relatives}, they said: O Messenger of Allah, who are these whom Allah has commanded us to love? He said: "Fatimah and her children, peace be upon them."

This chain is weak; it contains an unidentified narrator who is not known, transmitted from an unreliable Shi'ite elder, who is Husayn al-Ashqar, and his report is not accepted on this matter. The claim that this verse was revealed in Medina is also unlikely, since it is Meccan, and at that time Fatimah had no children at all, for she was not married to Ali until after Badr, in the second year of the Hijrah.

The truth is that the verse is to be explained as the imam, the sage of this nation and interpreter of the Qur'an, Abdullah ibn Abbas, explained it, as al-Bukhari (may Allah have mercy on him) related from him. And this does not negate the recommendation to be mindful of the People of the House, and the command to treat them well, to honor and respect them, for they are from a pure lineage, from the most noble household to be found on the face of the earth in honor, standing, and lineage, especially when they follow the clear, sound, and manifest prophetic tradition, as their forebears did, such as al-Abbas and his sons, and Ali and the people of his household and his descendants, may Allah be pleased with them all.

It is established in the authentic hadith that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said in his sermon at Ghadir Khumm: "I am leaving behind among you two weighty things: the Book of Allah and my kindred (itrati), and they will never separate until they meet me at the Pool."

Imam Ahmad said: Yazid ibn Harun told us, Isma'il ibn Abi Khalid informed us, from Yazid ibn Abi Ziyad, from Abdullah ibn al-Harith, from al-Abbas ibn Abd al-Muttalib, who said: I said: O Messenger of Allah, when Quraysh meet one another they greet each other with good cheer, but when they meet us they meet us with faces we do not recognize. He said: The Prophet ﷺ became intensely angry and said: "By the One in whose hand is my soul, faith does not enter the heart of a man until he loves you for the sake of Allah and His Messenger."

Then Ahmad said: Jarir told us, from Yazid ibn Abi Ziyad, from Abdullah ibn al-Harith, from Abd al-Muttalib ibn Rabi'ah, who said: al-Abbas came to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and said: We go out and see Quraysh talking together, but when they see us they fall silent. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ became angry, and a vein between his eyes throbbed, then he said: "By Allah, faith does not enter the heart of a person until he loves you for the sake of Allah and for my kinship."

Al-Bukhari said: Abdullah ibn Abd al-Wahhab told us, Khalid told us, Shu'bah told us, from Waqid, who said: I heard my father narrating from Ibn Umar, from Abu Bakr al-Siddiq, may Allah be pleased with him, who said: Watch over Muhammad ﷺ through his household.

It is established in the authentic hadith that al-Siddiq said to Ali, may Allah be pleased with both of them: By Allah, it is dearer to me to maintain kinship with the relatives of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ than with my own relatives.

Umar ibn al-Khattab said to al-Abbas, may Allah be pleased with both of them: By Allah, your becoming Muslim on the day you did so was dearer to me than the becoming Muslim of al-Khattab, had he become Muslim, because your becoming Muslim was dearer to the Messenger of Allah than the becoming Muslim of al-Khattab would have been.

The condition of the two Shaykhs, may Allah be pleased with them, is what is obligatory upon every person to be like. This is why the two of them were the most excellent of the believers after the Prophets and Messengers, may Allah be pleased with them and with all the rest of the Companions altogether.

Imam Ahmad, may Allah have mercy on him, said: Isma'il ibn Ibrahim narrated to us, from Abu Hayyan al-Taymi, Yazid ibn Hayyan narrated to me, saying: I set out, along with Husayn ibn Maysarah and Umar ibn Muslim, to Zayd ibn Arqam. When we sat down with him, Husayn said to him: You have indeed encountered much good, O Zayd. You saw the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, you heard his speech, you fought alongside him, and you prayed with him. You have indeed seen much good, O Zayd. Tell us, O Zayd, what you heard from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. He said: O son of my brother, by Allah, my age has grown great, my time has become distant, and I have forgotten some of what I used to retain from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. So whatever I relate to you, accept it, and whatever I do not, do not press me for it. Then he said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ stood one day delivering a sermon among us, at a water source called Khumm, between Makkah and Madinah. He praised Allah and extolled Him, gave reminder and admonished, then said: "To proceed: O people, I am only a human being. The messenger of my Lord is about to come to me and I shall answer. I am leaving among you two weighty things: the first of them is the Book of Allah, in which is guidance and light, so hold fast to the Book of Allah and cling to it." He urged them strongly toward the Book of Allah and encouraged devotion to it, and said: "And the people of my household. I remind you of Allah regarding the people of my household. I remind you of Allah regarding the people of my household." Husayn said to him: And who are the people of his household, O Zayd? Are not his wives among the people of his household? He said: His wives are indeed among the people of his household, but the people of his household are those who were forbidden charity after him. He said: And who are they? He said: They are the family of Ali, the family of Uqayl, the family of Ja'far, and the family of al-Abbas. He said: Were all of these forbidden charity? He said: Yes.

This is how Muslim (in the Book of Virtues) and al-Nasa'i related it, through routes from Yazid ibn Hayyan, with the same content.

Abu Isa al-Tirmidhi said: Ali ibn al-Mundhir al-Kufi narrated to us, Muhammad ibn Fudayl narrated to us, al-A'mash narrated to us, from Atiyyah, from Abu Sa'id, and also al-A'mash, from Habib ibn Abi Thabit, from Zayd ibn Arqam, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "I am leaving among you that which, if you hold fast to it, you will never go astray after me. One of the two is greater than the other: the Book of Allah, a rope stretched from heaven to earth, and the other is my kinsfolk, the people of my household. They will never separate until they come together before me at the Pool. So watch how you conduct yourselves toward them after me."

Al-Tirmidhi alone related this, then said: This is a hasan gharib (sound, singularly transmitted) hadith.

Al-Tirmidhi also said: Nasr ibn Abd al-Rahman al-Kufi narrated to us, Zayd ibn al-Hasan narrated to us, from Ja'far ibn Muhammad, from his father, from Jabir ibn Abdullah, who said: I saw the Messenger of Allah ﷺ during his pilgrimage on the day of Arafah, upon his she-camel al-Qaswa, delivering a sermon, and I heard him say: "O people, I have left among you that which, if you hold to it, you will never go astray: the Book of Allah, and my kinsfolk, the people of my household."

Al-Tirmidhi alone related this as well, and said: hasan gharib. On this subject there are also reports from Abu Dharr, Abu Sa'id, Zayd ibn Arqam, and Hudhayfah ibn Asid.

Then al-Tirmidhi said: Abu Dawud Sulayman ibn al-Ash'ath narrated to us, Yahya ibn Ma'in narrated to us, Hisham ibn Yusuf narrated to us, from Abdullah ibn Sulayman al-Nawfali, from Muhammad ibn Ali ibn Abdullah ibn Abbas, from his father, from his grandfather Abdullah ibn Abbas, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Love Allah for what He nourishes you with of His favors, love me out of love for Allah, and love the people of my household out of love for me."

Then he said: hasan gharib, we know it only through this route.

We have already presented other hadiths regarding His statement, {Allah only wishes to remove from you the impurity, O people of the household, and to purify you with a thorough purification} [al-Ahzab: 33], in a manner that makes it unnecessary to repeat them here. To Allah belongs praise and grace.

The Hafiz Abu Ya'la said: Suwayd ibn Sa'id narrated to us, Mufaddal ibn Abdullah narrated to us, from Abu Ishaq, from Hanash, who said: I heard Abu Dharr, while he was gripping the ring of the door, saying: O people, whoever knows me knows me, and whoever does not recognize me, I am Abu Dharr. I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "The likeness of the people of my household among you is the likeness of the ark of Noah. Whoever entered it was saved, and whoever held back from it perished."

This, with this chain of transmission, is weak.

Regarding His statement, {And whoever earns a good deed, We increase for him therein a goodness}, that is: whoever does a good deed, {We increase for him therein a goodness}, meaning a reward and recompense, as in His statement, {Indeed, Allah does not wrong so much as the weight of an atom, and if there be a good deed, He multiplies it and gives from Himself a great reward} [al-Nisa: 40].

Some of the early scholars said: Among the reward for a good deed is the good deed that follows it, and among the recompense for a bad deed is the bad deed that follows it.

Regarding His statement, {Indeed Allah is Forgiving, Appreciative}, that is: He forgives much of the bad deeds, and multiplies the little of the good deeds. He conceals and forgives, and He multiplies, thus showing appreciation.

Regarding His statement, {Or do they say, "He has invented a lie about Allah"? But if Allah willed, He could seal your heart}, that is: if you had invented a lie about Him, as these ignorant ones claim, {He could seal your heart}, meaning He would have sealed your heart and stripped from you what He had given you of the Qur'an, as in His statement, {And if he had made up about Us some sayings, We would have seized him by the right hand, then We would have cut off his life vein, and not one of you could have held that off from him} [al-Haqqah: 44-47], meaning: We would have taken vengeance upon him with the most severe vengeance, and none of the people would have had the power to hold that off from him.

His statement, {And Allah erases falsehood}, is not connected to His statement {He could seal} in such a way as to be in the jussive mood. Rather it is in the nominative as an independent clause beginning a new sentence, as Ibn Jarir said. He said: The letter "waw" was dropped from its writing in the script of the authoritative copy of the Qur'an, just as it was dropped in His statement, {We will call the Zabaniyah} [al-Alaq: 18], and His statement, {And man calls for evil as he calls for good} [al-Isra: 11].

His statement, {and confirms the truth by His words}, is connected to {And Allah erases falsehood and confirms the truth}, meaning: He establishes it, affirms it, clarifies it, and makes it plain by His words, that is, by His proofs and evidences. {Indeed He is Knowing of what is in the breasts}, meaning: of what the innermost thoughts conceal and what the hidden depths enfold.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿قُلْ لَا أَسْأَلُكُمْ عَلَيْهِ أَجْرًا إِلا الْمَوَدَّةَ فِي الْقُرْبَى﴾ أَيْ: قُلْ يَا مُحَمَّدُ لِهَؤُلَاءِ الْمُشْرِكِينَ مِنْ كُفَّارِ قُرَيْشٍ: لَا أَسْأَلُكُمْ عَلَى هَذَا الْبَلَاغِ وَالنُّصْحِ لَكُمْ مَا لَا تُعْطُونِيهِ، وَإِنَّمَا أَطْلُبُ مِنْكُمْ أَنْ تَكُفُّوا شَرَّكُمْ عَنِّي وَتَذَرُونِي أُبَلِّغُ رِسَالَاتِ رَبِّي، إِنْ لَمْ تَنْصُرُونِي فَلَا تُؤْذُونِي بِمَا بَيْنِي وَبَيْنَكُمْ مِنَ الْقَرَابَةِ.

قَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ بَشَّارٍ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ جَعْفَرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا شُعْبَةُ، عَنْ عَبْدِ الْمَلِكِ بْنِ مَيْسَرَةَ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ طَاوُسًا عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: أَنَّهُ سُئِلَ عَنْ قَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِلا الْمَوَدَّةَ فِي الْقُرْبَى﴾ فَقَالَ سَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ: قُرْبَى آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ. فَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: عَجِلْتَ إِنَّ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ لَمْ يَكُنْ بَطْنٌ مِنْ قُرَيْشٍ إِلَّا كَانَ لَهُ فِيهِمْ قَرَابَةٌ، فَقَالَ: إِلَّا أَنْ تَصِلُوا مَا بَيْنِي وَبَيْنَكُمْ مِنَ الْقَرَابَةِ. انْفَرَدَ بِهِ الْبُخَارِيُّ .

وَرَوَاهُ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ، عَنْ يَحْيَى الْقَطَّانِ، عَنْ شُعْبَةَ بِهِ. وَهَكَذَا رَوَى عَامِرٌ الشَّعْبِيُّ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَعَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، والعَوْفي، وَيُوسُفُ بْنُ مِهْران وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، مِثْلَهُ. وَبِهِ قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَعِكْرِمَةُ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ، وَأَبُو مَالِكٍ، وَعَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ، وَغَيْرُهُمْ.

وَقَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو الْقَاسِمِ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ حَدَّثَنَا هَاشِمُ بْنُ يَزِيدَ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ وَجَعْفَرٌ الْقَلَانِسِيُّ قَالَا حدثنا آدَمُ بْنُ أَبِي إِيَاسٍ، حَدَّثَنَا شَرِيكٍ، عَنْ خُصَيف، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: قَالَ لَهُمْ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "لَا أَسْأَلُكُمْ عَلَيْهِ أَجْرًا إِلَّا أَنْ تَوَدّوني فِي نَفْسِي لِقَرَابَتِي مِنْكُمْ، وَتَحْفَظُوا الْقَرَابَةَ الَّتِي بَيْنِي وَبَيْنَكُمْ" .

وَرَوَى الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ، عَنْ حَسَنِ بْنِ مُوسَى: حَدَّثَنَا قَزَعَة يَعْنِي ابْنَ سُوَيد -وَابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ-عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ مُسْلِمِ بْنِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، عَنْ قَزَعة بْنِ سُوَيْدٍ-عَنِ ابْنِ أَبِي نَجِيح، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ؛ أَنَّ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ قَالَ: "لَا أَسْأَلُكُمْ عَلَى مَا آتَيْتُكُمْ مِنَ الْبَيِّنَاتِ وَالْهُدَى أَجْرًا، إِلَّا أَنْ تُوَادوا اللَّهَ، وَأَنْ تَقَرَّبُوا إِلَيْهِ بِطَاعَتِهِ" .

وَهَكَذَا رَوَى قَتَادَةُ عَنِ الْحَسَنِ الْبَصْرِيِّ، مِثْلَهُ.

وَهَذَا كَأَنَّهُ تَفْسِيرٌ بِقَوْلٍ ثَانٍ، كَأَنَّهُ يَقُولُ: ﴿إِلا الْمَوَدَّةَ فِي الْقُرْبَى﴾ أَيْ: إِلَّا أَنْ تَعْمَلُوا بِالطَّاعَةِ الَّتِي تُقَرِّبُكُمْ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ زُلْفَى.

وَقَوْلٌ ثَالِثٌ: وَهُوَ مَا حَكَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ وَغَيْرُهُ، رِوَايَةً عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ، مَا مَعْنَاهُ أَنَّهُ قَالَ: مَعْنَى ذَلِكَ أَنْ تَوَدُّونِي فِي قَرَابَتِي، أَيْ: تُحْسِنُوا إِلَيْهِمْ وَتَبَرُّوهُمْ.

وَقَالَ السُّدِّيُّ، عَنْ أَبِي الدَّيْلَمِ قَالَ: لَمَّا جِيءَ بِعَلِيِّ بْنِ الْحُسَيْنِ أَسِيرًا، فَأُقِيمَ عَلَى دَرَجِ دِمَشْقَ، قَامَ رَجُلٌ مِنْ أَهْلِ الشَّامِ فَقَالَ: الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ الَّذِي قَتَلَكُمْ وَاسْتَأْصَلَكُمْ، وَقَطَعَ قَرْنَيِ الْفِتْنَةِ. فَقَالَ لَهُ عَلِيُّ بْنُ الْحُسَيْنِ: أَقَرَأْتَ الْقُرْآنَ؟ قَالَ: نَعَمْ. قَالَ: أَقَرَأْتَ آلَ حم؟ قَالَ: قَرَأْتُ الْقُرْآنَ، وَلَمْ أَقْرَأْ آلَ حم. قَالَ: مَا قَرَأْتَ: ﴿قُلْ لَا أَسْأَلُكُمْ عَلَيْهِ أَجْرًا إِلا الْمَوَدَّةَ فِي الْقُرْبَى﴾ ؟ قَالَ: وَإِنَّكُمْ أَنْتُمْ هُمْ؟ قَالَ: نَعَمْ.

وَقَالَ: أَبُو إِسْحَاقَ السَّبِيعيّ: سَأَلْتُ عَمْرَو بْنَ شُعَيْبٍ عَنْ قَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿قُلْ لَا أَسْأَلُكُمْ عَلَيْهِ أَجْرًا إِلا الْمَوَدَّةَ فِي الْقُرْبَى﴾ فَقَالَ: قُرْبَى النَّبِيِّ ﷺ. رَوَاهُمَا ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ .

ثُمَّ قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو كُرَيْب، حَدَّثَنَا مَالِكُ بْنُ إِسْمَاعِيلَ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ السَّلَامِ، حَدَّثَنِي يَزِيدَ بْنِ أَبِي زِيَادٍ، عَنْ مِقْسَمٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: قَالَتِ الْأَنْصَارُ: فَعَلْنَا وَفَعَلْنَا، وَكَأَنَّهُمْ فَخَرُوا فَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ -أَوِ: الْعَبَّاسُ، شَكَّ عَبْدُ السَّلَامِ-: لَنَا الْفَضْلُ عَلَيْكُمْ. فَبَلَغَ ذَلِكَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَأَتَاهُمْ فِي مَجَالِسِهِمْ فَقَالَ: "يَا مَعْشَرَ الْأَنْصَارِ، أَلَمْ تَكُونُوا أَذِلَّةً فَأَعَزَّكُمُ اللَّهُ بِي؟ " قَالُوا: بَلَى، يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ. قَالَ: أَلَمْ تَكُونُوا ضُلَّالًا فَهَدَاكُمُ اللَّهُ بِي؟ " قَالُوا: بَلَى يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ قَالَ: "أَفَلَا تُجِيبُونِي؟ " قَالُوا: مَا نَقُولُ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ؟ قَالَ: "أَلَا تَقُولُونَ: أَلَمْ يخرجك قومك فآويناك؟ أو لم يكذبوك فصدقناك؟ أو لم يَخْذُلُوكَ فَنَصَرْنَاكَ"؟ قَالَ: فَمَا زَالَ يَقُولُ حَتَّى جَثَوْا عَلَى الرُّكَبِ، وَقَالُوا: أَمْوَالُنَا وَمَا فِي أَيْدِينَا لِلَّهِ وَلِرَسُولِهِ. قَالَ: فَنَزَلَتْ: ﴿قُلْ لَا أَسْأَلُكُمْ عَلَيْهِ أَجْرًا إِلا الْمَوَدَّةَ فِي الْقُرْبَى﴾ .

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، عَنْ عَلِيِّ بْنِ الْحُسَيْنِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ الْمُؤْمِنِ بْنِ عَلِيٍّ، عن عبد السلام، عن يَزِيدَ بْنِ أَبِي زِيَادٍ -وَهُوَ ضَعِيفٌ-بِإِسْنَادِهِ مِثْلَهُ، أَوْ قَرِيبًا مِنْهُ.

وَفِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ -فِي قَسْمِ غَنَائِمِ حُنَيْنٍ-قَرِيبٌ مِنْ هَذَا السِّيَاقِ، وَلَكِنْ لَيْسَ فِيهِ ذِكْرُ نُزُولِ هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ. وذكْرُ نُزُولِهَا فِي الْمَدِينَةِ فِيهِ نَظَرٌ؛ لِأَنَّ السُّورَةَ مَكِّيَّةٌ، وَلَيْسَ يَظْهَرُ بَيْنَ هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ الْكَرِيمَةِ وَبَيْنَ السِّيَاقِ مُنَاسَبَةٌ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا علي بن الحسين، حَدَّثَنَا رَجُلٌ سَمَّاهُ، حَدَّثَنَا حُسَيْنٌ الْأَشْقَرُ، عَنْ قَيْسٍ، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: لَمَّا نَزَلَتْ هَذِهِ الْآيَةُ: ﴿قُلْ لَا أَسْأَلُكُمْ عَلَيْهِ أَجْرًا إِلا الْمَوَدَّةَ فِي الْقُرْبَى﴾ قَالُوا: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، مَنْ هَؤُلَاءِ الَّذِينَ أَمَرَ اللَّهُ بِمَوَدَّتِهِمْ؟ قَالَ: "فَاطِمَةُ وَوَلَدُهَا، عَلَيْهِمُ السَّلَامُ" .

وَهَذَا إِسْنَادٌ ضَعِيفٌ، فِيهِ مُبْهَمٌ لَا يُعْرَفُ، عَنْ شَيْخٍ شِيعِيٍّ مُتَخَرّق ، وَهُوَ حُسَيْنٌ الْأَشْقَرُ، وَلَا يُقْبَلُ خَبَرُهُ فِي هَذَا الْمَحَلِّ. وَذِكْرُ نُزُولِ هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ فِي الْمَدِينَةِ بَعِيدٌ؛ فَإِنَّهَا مَكِّيَّةٌ وَلَمْ يَكُنْ إِذْ ذَاكَ لِفَاطِمَةَ أَوْلَادٌ بِالْكُلِّيَّةِ، فَإِنَّهَا لَمْ تَتَزَوَّجْ بَعْلِيٍّ إِلَّا بَعْدَ بَدْرٍ مِنَ السَّنَةِ الثانية من الهجرة.

والحق تفسير الآية بِمَا فَسَّرَهَا بِهِ الْإِمَامُ حَبرُ الْأُمَّةِ، وَتُرْجُمَانُ الْقُرْآنِ، عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، كَمَا رَوَاهُ عَنْهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ [رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ] وَلَا تُنْكَرُ الْوَصَاةُ بِأَهْلِ الْبَيْتِ، وَالْأَمْرُ بِالْإِحْسَانِ إِلَيْهِمْ، وَاحْتِرَامِهِمْ وَإِكْرَامِهِمْ، فَإِنَّهُمْ مِنْ ذُرِّيَّةٍ طَاهِرَةٍ، مِنْ أَشْرَفِ بَيْتٍ وُجِدَ عَلَى وَجْهِ الْأَرْضِ، فَخْرًا وَحَسَبًا وَنَسَبًا، وَلَا سِيَّمَا إِذَا كَانُوا مُتَّبِعِينَ لِلسُّنَّةِ النَّبَوِيَّةِ الصَّحِيحَةِ الْوَاضِحَةِ الْجَلِيَّةِ، كَمَا كَانَ عَلَيْهِ سَلَفُهُمْ، كَالْعَبَّاسِ وَبَنِيهِ، وَعَلِيٍّ وَأَهْلِ بَيْتِهِ وَذُرِّيَّتِهِ، رَضِيَ الله عنهم أجمعين.

و [قد ثَبَتَ] فِي الصَّحِيحِ: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ فِي خُطْبَتِهِ بغَدِير خُمّ: "إِنِّي تَارِكٌ فِيكُمُ الثَّقَلَيْنِ: كِتَابَ اللَّهِ وَعِتْرَتِي، وَإِنَّهُمَا لَمْ يَفْتَرِقَا حَتَّى يَرِدَا عَلَيَّ الْحَوْضَ" .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا يَزِيدُ بْنُ هَارُونَ، أَخْبَرَنَا إِسْمَاعِيلُ بْنُ أَبِي خَالِدٌ، عَنْ يَزِيدَ بْنِ أَبِي زِيَادٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ الْحَارِثِ ، عَنِ الْعَبَّاسِ بْنِ عَبَدِ الْمُطَّلِبِ قَالَ: قُلْتُ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، إِنَّ قُرَيْشًا إِذَا لَقِيَ بَعْضُهُمْ بَعْضًا لَقُوهُمْ بِبِشْرٍ حَسَنٍ، وَإِذَا لَقُونَا لَقُونَا بِوُجُوهٍ لَا نَعْرِفُهَا؟ قَالَ: فَغَضِبَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ غَضَبًا شَدِيدًا، وَقَالَ: "وَالَّذِي نَفْسِي بِيَدِهِ، لَا يَدْخُلُ قَلْبَ الرَّجُلِ الْإِيمَانُ حَتَّى يُحِبَّكُمْ لِلَّهِ وَلِرَسُولِهِ" .

ثُمَّ قَالَ أَحْمَدُ حَدَّثَنَا جَرِيرٌ، عَنْ يَزِيدَ بْنِ أَبِي زِيَادٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ الْحَارِثِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ الْمُطَّلِبِ بْنِ رَبِيعَةَ قَالَ: دَخَلَ الْعَبَّاسُ عَلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالَ: إِنَّا لَنَخْرُجُ فَنَرَى قُرَيْشًا تُحدث، فإذا رأونا سَكَتُوا. فَغَضِبَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ ودَرّ عِرْقُ بَيْنَ عَيْنِهِ ، ثُمَّ قَالَ: "وَاللَّهِ لَا يَدْخُلُ قَلْبَ امْرِئٍ إِيمَانٌ حَتَّى يُحِبَّكُمْ لِلَّهِ وَلِقَرَابَتِي" .

وَقَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ عَبْدِ الْوَهَّابِ، حَدَّثَنَا خَالِدٌ، حَدَّثَنَا شُعْبَةُ، عَنْ وَاقِدٍ قَالَ: سمعتُ أَبِي يُحَدِّثُ عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ، عَنْ أَبِي بَكْرٍ الصِّدِّيقِ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، قَالَ: ارْقُبُوا مُحَمَّدًا ﷺ فِي أَهْلِ بَيْتِهِ .

وَفِي الصَّحِيحِ: أَنَّ الصِّدِّيقَ قَالَ لَعَلِيٍّ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا: وَاللَّهِ لِقَرَابَةِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ أَحَبُّ إِلَيَّ أَنْ أَصِلَ مِنْ قَرَابَتِي .

وَقَالَ عُمَرُ بْنُ الْخَطَّابِ لِلْعَبَّاسِ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا: وَاللَّهِ لَإِسْلَامُكَ يَوْمَ أَسْلَمْتَ كَانَ أَحَبَّ إليَّ مِنْ إِسْلَامِ الْخَطَّابِ لَوْ أَسْلَمَ؛ لَأَنَّ إِسْلَامَكَ كَانَ أَحَبَّ إِلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ مِنْ إِسْلَامِ الْخَطَّابِ.

فَحَالُ الشَّيْخَيْنِ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا، هُوَ الْوَاجِبُ عَلَى كُلِّ أَحَدٍ أَنْ يَكُونَ كَذَلِكَ؛ وَلِهَذَا كَانَا أَفْضَلَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ بَعْدَ النَّبِيِّينَ وَالْمُرْسَلِينَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا، وَعَنْ سَائِرِ الصَّحَابَةِ أَجْمَعِينَ.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ، رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ: حَدَّثَنَا إِسْمَاعِيلُ بْنُ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، عَنْ أَبِي حَيّان التيمي، حدثني يزيد ابن حَيَّانَ قَالَ: انْطَلَقْتُ أَنَا وحُسَيْن بْنُ مَيْسَرة، وَعُمَرُ بْنُ مُسْلِمٍ إِلَى زَيْدِ بْنِ أَرْقَمَ، فَلَمَّا جَلَسْنَا إِلَيْهِ قَالَ لَهُ حُصَيْنٌ: لَقَدْ لقيتَ يَا زَيْدُ خَيْرًا كَثِيرًا، رَأَيْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ، وَسَمِعْتَ حَدِيثَهُ وَغَزَوْتَ مَعَهُ، وَصَلَّيْتَ مَعَهُ. لَقَدْ رَأَيْتَ يَا زَيْدُ خَيْرًا كَثِيرًا. حَدِّثْنَا يَا زَيْدُ مَا سَمِعْتُ مِنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ. فَقَالَ: يَا ابْنَ أَخِي، وَاللَّهِ كَبُرت سِنِّي، وَقَدِمَ عَهْدِي، وَنَسِيتُ بَعْضَ الَّذِي كُنْتُ أَعِي مِنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، فَمَا حَدَّثْتُكُمْ فَاقْبَلُوهُ، وَمَا لَا فَلَا تُكَلّفونيه. ثُمَّ قَالَ: قَامَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَوْمًا خَطِيبًا فِينَا، بِمَاءٍ يُدْعَى خُمّا -بَيْنَ مَكَّةَ وَالْمَدِينَةِ-فَحَمِدَ اللَّهَ وَأَثْنَى عَلَيْهِ، وَذَكَرَ وَوَعَظَ، ثُمَّ قَالَ: "أَمَّا بَعْدُ، أَلَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ، إِنَّمَا أَنَا بَشَرٌ يُوشِكُ أَنْ يَأْتِيَنِي رَسُولُ رَبِّي فَأُجِيبَ، وَإِنِّي تَارِكٌ فِيكُمُ الثَّقَلَيْنِ، أَوَّلُهُمَا: كِتَابُ اللَّهِ، فِيهِ الهدى والنور، فخذوا بكتاب الله واستمسكوا به" فَحَثَّ عَلَى كِتَابِ اللَّهِ وَرَغَّبَ فِيهِ، وَقَالَ: "وَأَهْلُ بَيْتِي أُذَكِّرُكُمُ اللَّهَ فِي أَهْلِ بَيْتِي أُذَكِّرُكُمُ اللَّهَ فِي أَهْلِ بَيْتِي" فَقَالَ لَهُ حُصَيْنٌ: وَمَنْ أَهْلُ بَيْتِهِ يَا زَيْدُ؟ أَلَيْسَ نِسَاؤُهُ مِنْ أَهْلِ بَيْتِهِ؟ قَالَ: إِنَّ نِسَاءَهُ من أهل بيته، ولكن أهل بيته من حُرم الصَّدَقَةَ بَعْدَهُ قَالَ: وَمَنْ هُمْ؟ قَالَ: هم آل علي، وآل عقيل، وآل جعفر، وَآلُ الْعَبَّاسِ، قَالَ: أَكُلُّ هَؤُلَاءِ حُرِمَ الصَّدَقَةَ؟ قال: نعم.

وهكذا رواه مسلم [في فضائل] وَالنَّسَائِيُّ مِنْ طُرُقٍ عَنْ يَزِيدَ بْنِ حَيّان به .

وَقَالَ أَبُو عِيسَى التِّرْمِذِيُّ حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ الْمُنْذِرِ الْكُوفِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ فُضَيْلٍ، حَدَّثَنَا الْأَعْمَشُ، عَنْ عَطِيَّةَ، عَنْ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ -وَالْأَعْمَشُ، عَنْ حَبِيبِ بْنِ أَبِي ثَابِتٍ، عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَرْقَمَ-قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ "إِنِّي تَارِكٌ فِيكُمْ مَا إِنْ تَمَسَّكْتُمْ بِهِ لَنْ تَضِلُّوا بَعْدِي، أَحَدُهُمَا أَعْظَمُ مِنَ الْآخَرِ: كِتَابُ اللَّهِ حَبْلٌ مَمْدُودٌ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ إِلَى الْأَرْضِ، وَالْآخَرُ عِتْرَتِي: أَهْلُ بَيْتِي، وَلَنْ يَتَفَرَّقا حَتَّى يَرِدَا عَلَيَّ الْحَوْضَ، فَانْظُرُوا كَيْفَ تَخْلُفُونِي فِيهِمَا"

تَفَرَّدَ بِرِوَايَتِهِ التِّرْمِذِيُّ ثُمَّ قَالَ: هَذَا حَدِيثٌ حَسَنٌ غَرِيبٌ.

وَقَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ أَيْضًا حَدَّثَنَا نَصْرُ بْنُ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ الْكُوفِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا زَيْدُ بْنُ الْحَسَنِ، عَنْ جَعْفَرِ بْنِ مُحَمَّدٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ جَابِرِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ قَالَ: رَأَيْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ فِي حَجَّتِهِ يَوْمَ عَرَفَةَ، وَهُوَ عَلَى نَاقَتِهِ الْقَصْوَاءِ يَخْطُبُ، فَسَمِعْتُهُ يَقُولُ: "يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ، إِنِّي تَرَكْتُ فِيكُمْ مَا إِنْ أَخَذْتُمْ بِهِ لَنْ تَضِلُّوا: كِتَابَ اللَّهِ، وَعِتْرَتِي: أَهْلَ بَيْتِي"

تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ التِّرْمِذِيُّ أَيْضًا ، وَقَالَ: حَسَنٌ غَرِيبٌ. وَفِي الْبَابِ عَنْ أَبِي ذَرٍّ، وَأَبِي سَعِيدٍ، وَزَيْدِ بْنِ أَرْقَمَ، وَحُذَيْفَةَ بْنِ أَسِيدٍ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو دَاوُدَ سُلَيْمَانُ بْنُ الْأَشْعَثِ، حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى بْنُ مَعِين، حَدَّثَنَا هِشَامُ بْنُ يُوسُفَ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ سُلَيْمَانَ النَّوْفَلِيِّ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عَلِيِّ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ جَدِّهِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "أَحِبُّوا اللَّهَ لِمَا يَغْذُوكُمْ مِنْ نِعَمِهِ، وَأَحِبُّونِي بِحُبِّ اللَّهِ، وَأَحِبُّوا أَهْلَ بَيْتِي بِحُبِّي"

ثُمَّ قَالَ حَسَنٌ غَرِيبٌ إِنَّمَا نَعْرِفُهُ مِنْ هَذَا الْوَجْهِ .

وَقَدْ أَوْرَدْنَا أَحَادِيثَ أُخَر عِنْدَ قَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِنَّمَا يُرِيدُ اللَّهُ لِيُذْهِبَ عَنْكُمُ الرِّجْسَ أَهْلَ الْبَيْتِ وَيُطَهِّرَكُمْ تَطْهِيرًا﴾ [الْأَحْزَابِ: ٣٣] ، بِمَا أَغْنَى عَنْ إِعَادَتِهَا هَاهُنَا، وَلِلَّهِ الْحَمْدُ وَالْمِنَّةُ.

وَقَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو يَعْلَى: حَدَّثَنَا سُوَيْدُ بْنُ سَعِيد، حَدَّثَنَا مُفَضَّلُ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ، عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ حَنَش قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ أَبَا ذَرٍّ وَهُوَ آخِذٌ بِحَلْقَةِ الْبَابِ يَقُولُ: يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ، مَنْ عَرَفَنِي فَقَدْ عَرَفَنِي، وَمَنْ أَنْكَرَنِي فَأَنَا أَبُو ذَرٍّ، سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "إِنَّمَا مَثَلُ أهل بيتي فيكم مَثَل سفينة نوح، مَنْ دَخَلَهَا نَجَا، وَمَنْ تَخَلَّفَ عَنْهَا هَلَكَ" .

هَذَا بِهَذَا الْإِسْنَادِ ضَعِيفٌ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَمَنْ يَقْتَرِفْ حَسَنَةً نزدْ لَهُ فِيهَا حُسْنًا﴾ أَيْ: وَمَنْ يَعْمَلُ حَسَنَةً ﴿نزدْ لَهُ فِيهَا حُسْنًا﴾ أَيْ: أَجْرًا وَثَوَابًا، كَقَوْلِهِ ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يَظْلِمُ مِثْقَالَ ذَرَّةٍ وَإِنْ تَكُ حَسَنَةً يُضَاعِفْهَا وَيُؤْتِ مِنْ لَدُنْهُ أَجْرًا عَظِيمًا﴾ [النِّسَاءِ:٤٠] .

وَقَالَ بَعْضُ السَّلَفِ: [إِنَّ] مِنْ ثَوَابِ الْحَسَنَةِ الْحَسَنَةَ بَعْدَهَا، وَمِنْ جَزَاءِ السَّيِّئَةِ (السَّيِّئَةَ) بَعْدَهَا.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنَّ اللَّهَ غَفُورٌ شَكُورٌ﴾ أَيْ: يَغْفِرُ الْكَثِيرَ مِنَ السَّيِّئَاتِ، وَيُكَثِّرُ الْقَلِيلَ مِنَ الْحَسَنَاتِ، فَيَسْتُرُ وَيَغْفِرُ، وَيُضَاعِفُ فَيَشْكُرُ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَيُحِقُّ الْحَقَّ بِكَلِمَاتِهِ﴾ مَعْطُوفٌ عَلَى ﴿وَيَمْحُ اللَّهُ الْبَاطِلَ وَيُحِقُّ الْحَقَّ﴾ أَيْ: يُحَقِّقُهُ وَيُثْبِتُهُ وَيُبَيِّنُهُ وَيُوَضِّحُهُ بِكَلِمَاتِهِ، أَيْ: بِحُجَجِهِ وَبَرَاهِينِهِ، ﴿إِنَّهُ عَلِيمٌ بِذَاتِ الصُّدُورِ﴾ أَيْ: بِمَا تُكِنُّهُ الضَّمَائِرُ، وَتَنْطَوِي عَلَيْهِ السَّرَائِرُ.

## 42:25-28 — verses 25-28 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, bestowing favor upon His servants by accepting their repentance to Him when they repent and turn back to Him: that it is part of His generosity and forbearance that He pardons, overlooks, conceals, and forgives, as in His saying: {And whoever does evil or wrongs himself, then seeks forgiveness from Allah, will find Allah Forgiving, Merciful} [al-Nisa: 110]. It is established in Sahih Muslim, may Allah have mercy on him, where he said: Muhammad ibn al-Sabbah and Zuhayr ibn Harb told us, they said: Umar ibn Yunus told us, Ikrimah ibn Ammar told us, Ishaq ibn Abi Talhah told us, Anas ibn Malik, who was his uncle, told me: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Allah is more joyful at the repentance of His servant when he repents to Him than one of you would be if his mount were in a barren land and it escaped from him, carrying his food and drink, so that he despaired of it, and he came to a tree and lay down in its shade, having despaired of his mount. Then while he was in that state, suddenly it was standing beside him. He took hold of its halter and then said, out of the intensity of his joy: 'O Allah, You are my servant and I am Your lord,' erring from the intensity of his joy."

This is also established in the Sahih, from a similar narration by Abdullah ibn Masud.

Abd al-Razzaq said, from Ma'mar, from al-Zuhri, concerning His saying: {And He is the One who accepts repentance from His servants}: that Abu Hurayrah said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Allah is more joyful at the repentance of His servant than one of you would be upon finding his lost animal in a place where he feared death by thirst."

Hammam ibn al-Harith said: Ibn Masud was asked about a man who commits fornication with a woman and then marries her. He said: There is no harm in it, and he recited: {And He is the One who accepts repentance from His servants} the verse. This was narrated by Ibn Jarir and Ibn Abi Hatim, from the hadith of Sharik the judge, from Ibrahim ibn Muhajir, from Ibrahim al-Nakha'i, from Hammam, and he mentioned it.

His saying: {and He pardons evil deeds} means: He accepts repentance in the future and pardons evil deeds of the past. {and He knows what you do} means: He knows all that you have done, made, and said, and despite this, He turns in acceptance toward whoever repents to Him.

His saying: {and He answers those who believe and do righteous deeds} al-Suddi said: meaning He answers them. Ibn Jarir said likewise: its meaning is that He answers the supplication for them, for themselves and for their companions and brothers. He related this from some of the grammarians, who made it like His saying: {so their Lord answered them} [Al Imran: 195].

Then he, and Ibn Abi Hatim, related from the hadith of al-A'mash, from Shaqiq ibn Salamah, from Salamah ibn Sabrah, who said: Mu'adh delivered a sermon to us in Syria and said: You are the believers, and you are the people of Paradise. By Allah, I hope that Allah will admit into Paradise those of the Persians and Romans whom you take captive, and that is because when one of you does a good deed for someone, meaning when one of them does a deed, that person says: "Well done, may Allah have mercy on you," "Well done, may Allah bless you," then he recited: {and He answers those who believe and do righteous deeds and increases them from His bounty}.

Ibn Jarir related from some of the grammarians that they took His saying {and He answers those who believe} like His saying {those who listen to the word} [al-Zumar: 18], meaning: they are the ones who respond to the truth and follow it, like His saying, Blessed and Exalted is He: {Only those who hear will respond, but as for the dead, Allah will resurrect them} [al-An'am: 36]. But the first meaning is more apparent, because of His saying: {and increases them from His bounty}, meaning: He answers their supplication and increases them beyond that. For this reason Ibn Abi Hatim said:

Ali ibn al-Husayn told us, Muhammad ibn al-Musaffa told us, Baqiyyah told us, Isma'il ibn Abdullah al-Kindi told us, al-A'mash told us, from Shaqiq, from Abdullah, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said concerning His saying {and increases them from His bounty}: "It is intercession for those for whom the Fire has become due, from among those to whom they had done good in the worldly life."

Qatadah said, from Ibrahim al-Nakha'i al-Lakhmi, concerning His saying: {and He answers those who believe and do righteous deeds}: he said, they are given the right to intercede for their brothers. {and increases them from His bounty}, he said: they are given the right to intercede for the brothers of their brothers.

His saying: {and the disbelievers, for them is a severe punishment.} Having mentioned the believers and the abundant reward due to them, He mentions the disbelievers and the severe, agonizing, painful punishment they will have before Him on the Day of Resurrection, the day of their return and reckoning.

His saying: {And if Allah were to spread out provision for His servants, they would transgress in the earth}, meaning: if He gave them provision beyond their need, that would drive them to transgression and tyranny against one another, out of insolence and excess.

Qatadah said: It used to be said: The best life is one that neither distracts you nor makes you tyrannical. Qatadah mentioned the hadith: "I only fear for you what Allah brings forth from the flower of the worldly life," and the questioner's question: "Does good bring about evil?" and the rest of the hadith.

His saying: {but He sends down in the measure that He wills; indeed He is, concerning His servants, All-Aware, All-Seeing}, meaning: rather He gives them provision according to what He chooses, of what contains their welfare, and He knows best about that, so He enriches whoever deserves richness, and impoverishes whoever deserves poverty. As it is stated in a reported hadith: "Indeed among My servants there are those whom nothing but wealth will keep upright; if I impoverished him, it would corrupt his religion for him. And indeed among My servants there are those whom nothing but poverty will keep upright; if I enriched him, it would corrupt his religion for him."

His saying: {And He is the One who sends down the rain after they had despaired}, meaning: after people had lost hope of rainfall, He sends it down upon them at the time of their need and poverty toward it, as in His saying: {though before it was sent down upon them, before that, they were indeed in despair} [al-Rum: 49].

His saying: {and spreads His mercy}, meaning: He extends it generally over the existence of the people of that region and that district.

Qatadah said: It was mentioned to us that a man said to Umar ibn al-Khattab: O Commander of the Believers, the rain has failed and the people have despaired. Umar, may Allah be pleased with him, said: You have been given rain, then he recited: {And He is the One who sends down the rain after they had despaired and spreads His mercy}.

{And He is the Guardian, the Praiseworthy}, meaning: He is the One who disposes of the affairs of His creation in what benefits them in their worldly life and their hereafter, and He is the One whose outcome is praiseworthy in all that He decrees and does.

## 42:29-31 — verses 29-31 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says: {And among His signs} that point to His magnificence, His tremendous power, and His overwhelming dominion, is {the creation of the heavens and the earth and what He has dispersed in them}, that is, what He has scattered in them, that is, in the heavens and the earth, {of moving creatures}. This encompasses the angels, the jinn, mankind, and all other living beings, in their differing forms, colors, languages, natures, kinds, and species. He has distributed them throughout the regions of the earth and the heavens, {and He}, despite all of this, {has power to gather them whenever He wills}, that is, on the Day of Resurrection He gathers the first and the last and all creatures together in one place, where the caller can be heard by all of them and the sight can reach all of them, and He judges among them with His just and true judgment.

Concerning His statement, {Whatever affliction befalls you is because of what your hands have earned}, meaning: whatever afflictions befall you, O people, are only on account of sins you have committed previously, {and He pardons much}, that is, of the sins, so He does not requite you for them but rather pardons them. {And if Allah were to seize mankind for what they have earned, He would not leave upon its surface any moving creature} [Fatir: 45]. And in the authentic hadith: "By the One in whose hand is my soul, no fatigue, no illness, no worry, and no grief befalls the believer except that Allah expiates through it some of his sins, even a thorn that pricks him."

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Ayyub said: I read in the book of Abu Qilaba, who said: When {So whoever does an atom's weight of good will see it, and whoever does an atom's weight of evil will see it} [al-Zalzalah: 7-8] was revealed, Abu Bakr was eating, and he stopped and said: O Messenger of Allah, will I really see what I have done of good and evil? He said: "Have you seen what you have disliked seeing? That is from the atom's weights of evil, while the atom's weights of good are stored up for you until you are given them on the Day of Resurrection." Abu Idris said: I see its confirmation in the Book of Allah: {Whatever affliction befalls you is because of what your hands have earned, and He pardons much}.

Then he narrated it by another route, from Abu Qilaba, from Anas, and he said: the first is more authentic.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, said: Shall I not inform you of the most excellent verse in the Book of Allah, Mighty and Majestic, which the Messenger of Allah ﷺ related to us? He said: {Whatever affliction befalls you is because of what your hands have earned, and He pardons much}. And I will explain it to you, O Ali: "Whatever affliction of illness, punishment, or trial befalls you in this world is because of what your hands have earned, and Allah the Exalted is too forbearing to double the punishment upon it in the Hereafter. And whatever Allah pardons in this world, Allah the Exalted is too generous to return to it after His pardon."

Imam Ahmad likewise narrated it from Marwan ibn Muawiyah and Abdah, from Abu Sukhayla, who said: Ali said... and he mentioned something similar, as a raised report (marfu').

Then Ibn Abi Hatim narrated something similar by another route as a halted report (mawquf), saying: [chain] Abu Juhayfah said: I entered upon Ali ibn Abi Talib, may Allah be pleased with him, and he said: Shall I not relate to you a hadith which every believer ought to bear in mind? He said: So we asked him, and he recited this verse: {Whatever affliction befalls you is because of what your hands have earned, and He pardons much}. He said: whatever Allah punishes with in this world, Allah is too forbearing to double the punishment for it on the Day of Resurrection, and whatever Allah pardons in this world, Allah is too generous to go back on His pardon on the Day of Resurrection.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Muawiyah, that is, ibn Abi Sufyan, may Allah be pleased with both of them, said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "There is nothing that befalls the believer in his body that harms him except that Allah expiates through it some of his sins."

Ahmad also said: [chain] Aishah said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "When the sins of a servant multiply and he has nothing with which to expiate them, Allah afflicts him with grief in order to expiate them."

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] al-Hasan, that is al-Basri, said concerning His statement {Whatever affliction befalls you is because of what your hands have earned, and He pardons much}: When it was revealed, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "By the One in whose hand is the soul of Muhammad, there is no scratch from a stick, no twitching of a vein, and no stumbling of a foot except on account of a sin, and what Allah pardons is greater."

He also said: [chain] Imran ibn Husayn, may Allah be pleased with him, said: One of his companions entered upon him while he had been afflicted in his body, and one of them said to him: We are distressed for you because of what we see in you. He said: Do not be distressed at what you see, for what you see is on account of a sin, and what Allah pardons is greater. Then he recited this verse: {Whatever affliction befalls you is because of what your hands have earned, and He pardons much}.

[He said:] And [chain] Abu al-Bilad said: I said to al-Ala ibn Badr: {Whatever affliction befalls you is because of what your hands have earned}, yet I lost my sight while I was a young boy? He said: That was because of the sins of your parents.

And [chain] al-Dahhak said: We do not know of anyone who memorized the Qur'an and then forgot it except on account of a sin. Then al-Dahhak recited: {Whatever affliction befalls you is because of what your hands have earned, and He pardons much}. Then al-Dahhak would say: And what affliction is greater than forgetting the Qur'an?

## 42:32-35 — verses 32-35 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: Among His signs that indicate His power and dominion is His subjugation of the sea so that ships may sail upon it by His command. These are the vessels that run through the sea like al-a'lam (the landmarks/mountains), that is, like mountains, as Mujahid, al-Hasan, al-Suddi, and al-Dahhak said, meaning: they are in the sea like mountains are on land.

{If He willed, He would still the wind}, meaning the wind that drives the ships. If He willed, He would still it so that the ships would not move, but would remain motionless, neither coming nor going, but standing still upon its back, that is, upon the surface of the water. {Indeed in that are signs for everyone who is very patient} that is, amid hardships, {and grateful}, meaning: indeed in His subjugation of the sea and His sending of the wind in the measure that they need for their travel, there are indications of His blessings, the Almighty, upon His creation. {For every steadfastly patient one} that is, amid hardships, {and grateful} in times of ease.

His saying: {Or He could destroy them for what they have earned}, meaning: and if He willed, He would destroy the ships and drown them because of the sins of those aboard them who are riding upon them, {but He pardons much}, meaning: of their sins. Had He seized them for all of their sins, He would have destroyed everyone who rode upon the sea.

Some of the scholars of tafsir said: the meaning of His saying {Or He could destroy them for what they have earned} is: if He willed, He would send a strong, violent wind that would seize the ships and turn them from their straight course, diverting them to the right or to the left, straying, not following any path nor heading toward any intended destination.

This view implies their destruction, and it is consistent with the first view, which is that the Almighty, if He willed, would still the wind so that it would stop, or He would strengthen it so that the ships would go astray, wander off course, and be destroyed. But out of His kindness and mercy, He sends it according to need, just as He sends rain in a sufficient measure. Had He sent it down in very great abundance, it would have demolished buildings, and had He sent very little, it would not have caused crops and fruits to grow. Indeed, to lands such as Egypt He sends a flowing stream from land other than it, because they have no need of rain, and had He sent rain down upon them, it would have demolished their buildings and brought down their walls.

His saying: {And that those who dispute concerning Our signs may know that they have no refuge}, meaning: they have no escape from Our punishment and Our vengeance, for they are utterly overpowered by Our might.

## 42:36-39 — verses 36-39 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, belittling the affair of worldly life and its adornment, and the flowers and fading pleasures within it, in His saying: {So whatever thing you have been given is but the enjoyment of the worldly life}, meaning: whatever you have obtained and gathered, do not be deceived by it, for it is only the enjoyment of the worldly life, which is a lowly, perishing, and inevitably vanishing abode. {But what is with Allah is better and more lasting}, meaning: the reward of Allah is better than this world, and it is everlasting, eternal. So do not put the perishing before the everlasting. For this reason He said: {for those who believe}, meaning: for those who are patient in giving up the pleasures of this world, {and put their trust in their Lord}, meaning: so that He may help them to be patient in performing the obligations and abandoning the forbidden things.

Then He said: {And those who avoid the major sins and immoralities}, and we have already discussed sin (al-ithm) and immoralities (al-fawahish) in Surat al-A'raf, {and when they are angered, they forgive}, meaning: their disposition, character, and nature call for pardoning and forgiving people; their disposition is not to seek revenge against people.

It has been established in the Sahih that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ never took revenge for himself, except when the sanctities of Allah were violated. And in another hadith: "He used to say to one of us at the time of reproach: 'What is wrong with him? May his forehead be rubbed with dust.'"

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Ibrahim said: The believers used to dislike being humiliated, and when they had the power, they would forgive.

His saying: {And those who have responded to their Lord}, meaning: they followed His messengers, obeyed His command, and avoided what He forbade, {and established prayer}, which is the greatest of the acts of worship of Allah, Mighty and Majestic, {and whose affair is [conducted through] consultation among themselves}, meaning: they do not settle a matter until they consult about it, so that they may support one another with their opinions in matters such as wars and similar affairs, as the Almighty said: {And consult them in the matter, and when you have decided, then rely upon Allah} [Al Imran: 159]. For this reason, he, upon him be peace, used to consult them in wars and similar matters, in order to please their hearts thereby.

Likewise, when death approached Umar ibn al-Khattab (may Allah be pleased with him) after he was stabbed, he made the matter after him a matter of consultation among six men: Uthman, Ali, Talha, al-Zubayr, Sa'd, and Abd al-Rahman ibn Awf, may Allah be pleased with them all. The opinion of all the Companions came together on putting Uthman forward over them, may Allah be pleased with them.

{And they spend out of what We have provided them}, meaning: through kindness to Allah's creation, beginning with those nearest to them, then those next nearest.

His saying: {And those who, when tyranny befalls them, defend themselves}, meaning: within them is the strength to seek redress against whoever wrongs them and transgresses against them. They are not incapable or lowly, but are able to take revenge against whoever tyrannizes them, even though, along with this, when they have the power, they forgive, just as Joseph (peace be upon him) said to his brothers: {No reproach will there be upon you today. Allah will forgive you, [and He is the most merciful of the merciful]} [Yusuf: 92], despite his ability to hold them to account and repay them for what they had done to him.

Likewise, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ pardoned those eighty men who came seeking him in the year of al-Hudaybiyyah, descending from the mountain of al-Tan'im. When he gained power over them, he showed them favor despite his ability to take revenge. Likewise was his pardon of Ghawrath ibn al-Harith, who intended to assassinate him (peace be upon him) when he drew his sword while the Prophet was asleep. He, upon him be peace, awoke while the sword was drawn in the man's hand. He rebuked him, so the man put the sword down, and the Messenger of Allah ﷺ took the sword from his hand, called his companions, then informed them of what had happened between himself and this man, and pardoned him.

Likewise he pardoned Labid ibn al-A'sam, who had bewitched him (peace be upon him); despite this, he did not confront him nor reproach him, even though he had the power over him.

Likewise was his pardon, peace be upon him, of the Jewish woman, Zaynab, sister of Marhab the Jew of Khaybar whom Mahmud ibn Maslamah had killed, who poisoned the shoulder of meat on the day of Khaybar. The shoulder informed him of this, so he summoned her and she confessed. He said: "What led you to do that?" She said: "I thought that if you were a prophet it would not harm you, and if you were not a prophet we would be rid of you." So he set her free, peace and blessings be upon him. But when Bishr ibn al-Bara died from it, he had her killed in retaliation for him.

The hadiths and reports on this subject are very numerous, and all praise is due to Allah.

## 42:40-43 — verses 40-43 (shared commentary)

### Translation

His statement, the Exalted: {The recompense of an evil deed is an evil deed like it}, is like His statement, the Exalted: {So whoever transgresses against you, transgress against him in the same measure that he transgressed against you} [al-Baqarah: 194], and like His statement: {And if you retaliate, then retaliate in the same measure as you were made to suffer, but if you endure patiently, that is best for those who are patient} [al-Nahl: 129]. He legislated justice, which is qisas (retaliatory equivalence), and He encouraged the more excellent way, which is pardon, as in His statement [the Exalted]: {And wounds are subject to retaliation, but whoever forgoes it as an act of charity, it will be an atonement for him} [al-Ma'idah: 45]. For this reason He says here: {But whoever pardons and reconciles, his reward is with Allah}, meaning: that will not be lost with Allah, as is confirmed in the hadith: "Allah does not increase a servant in anything through pardon except honor." And His statement: {Indeed He does not love the wrongdoers}, meaning the transgressors, that is, the one who initiates the evil deed.

[Some of them said: since there are three categories, one who wrongs himself, one who is moderate, and one who is foremost in good deeds, He mentioned the three categories in this verse. He mentioned the moderate one, who exacts only the measure of his right, in His statement: {The recompense of an evil deed is an evil deed like it}. Then He mentioned the one who is foremost, in His statement: {But whoever pardons and reconciles, his reward is with Allah}. Then He mentioned the wrongdoer in His statement: {Indeed He does not love the wrongdoers}. So He commanded justice, encouraged the more excellent way, and forbade wrongdoing.]

Then He says: {But whoever defends himself after being wronged, such ones, there is no blame upon them}, meaning: there is no sin upon them in defending themselves against the one who wronged them.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Ibn 'Awn said: I used to ask about self-defense (al-intisar), regarding {But whoever defends himself after being wronged, such ones, there is no blame upon them}. 'Ali ibn Zayd ibn Jud'an related to me, from Umm Muhammad, his father's wife, Ibn 'Awn said: they claimed that she used to visit the Mother of the Believers, 'A'ishah, she said: the Mother of the Believers said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ came in to us while Zaynab bint Jahsh was with us. He began doing something with his hand and did not notice her, so I gestured with my hand until I made him notice her, and he stopped. Zaynab came forward pressing against 'A'ishah, so he forbade her, but she refused to stop. So he said to 'A'ishah: "Insult her back," so she insulted her and overcame her. Zaynab went off and came to 'Ali and said: 'A'ishah has been speaking ill of you and doing such and such to you. Then Fatimah came, and he said to her: "She is your father's beloved, by the Lord of the Ka'bah." So she went away and said to 'Ali: I said to him such and such, and he said to me such and such. He said: and 'Ali came to the Prophet ﷺ and spoke to him about that.

This is how this account has come down, and 'Ali ibn Zayd ibn Jud'an frequently brings odd (munkar) reports in his narrations, and there is oddness in this one. The authentic hadith is different from this account, as al-Nasa'i and Ibn Majah narrated it from the hadith of Khalid ibn Salamah al-Fa'fa', from 'Abdullah al-Bahi, from 'Urwah, who said: 'A'ishah, may Allah be pleased with her, said: I did not know until Zaynab came in to me without permission while angry. Then she said to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ: "It is enough for you that the daughter of Abu Bakr has turned her little tongues against you," then she turned to face me, but I turned away from her, until the Prophet ﷺ said: "Go on, defend yourself." So I turned to face her until I saw her saliva had dried up in her mouth, she could not respond to me with anything. And I saw the Prophet ﷺ, his face beaming with joy. This is the wording of al-Nasa'i.

Al-Bazzar said: [chain] from Ibrahim, from al-Aswad, from 'A'ishah, may Allah be pleased with her, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Whoever invokes Allah's curse upon one who wronged him has indeed defended himself."

Al-Tirmidhi also narrated it from the hadith of Abu al-Ahwas, from Abu Hamzah, whose name was Maymun, then he said: "We do not know it except through his report, and he has been criticized regarding his memory."

And His statement: {The blame is only}, meaning: the constraint and the wrongdoing is only {upon those who wrong people and transgress in the land without right}, meaning: they initiate wrongdoing against people. As it has come in the authentic hadith: "The two who insult each other, whatever they say, the blame is upon the one who initiated it, so long as the wronged party does not exceed the bounds."

{Those will have a painful punishment}, meaning: severe and agonizing.

Abu Bakr ibn Abi Shaybah said: [chain] Muhammad ibn Wasi' said: I came to Makkah, and there was a watchtower over the trench, so I was seized and taken to Marwan ibn al-Muhallab, who was governor of Basrah. He said: What is your need, O Abu 'Abdullah? I said: My need, if you are able, is that you be as the brother of the Banu 'Adi said. He said: Who is the brother of Banu 'Adi? He said: al-'Ala' ibn Ziyad, he once appointed a friend of his to a post, and wrote to him: Now then, if you are able, do not spend any night except with your back light of burden, your stomach empty, and your hand clean of the blood and wealth of the Muslims, for if you do that, there will be no blame upon you: {The blame is only upon those who wrong people and transgress in the land without right; those will have a painful punishment}. He said: He has spoken truly, by Allah, and given sincere counsel. Then he said: What is your need, O Abu 'Abdullah? I said: My need is that you let me rejoin my family. He said: Yes. Ibn Abi Hatim narrated it.

Then, when He, the Exalted, had condemned wrongdoing and its people and had legislated retaliatory equivalence, He said, encouraging pardon and overlooking faults: {But whoever is patient and forgives}, meaning: bears the injury patiently and covers over the evil deed, {indeed that is among the matters of determination (`azm al-umur, resolute matters requiring firm resolve)}.

Sa'id ibn Jubayr said: [meaning] it is among the matters that are the rightful due of the things Allah has commanded, that is, among the praiseworthy matters and commendable deeds for which there is abundant reward and beautiful commendation.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] 'Abd al-Samad ibn Yazid, the servant of al-Fudayl ibn 'Iyad, said: I heard al-Fudayl ibn 'Iyad say: If a man comes to you complaining to you about another man, say to him: "O my brother, pardon him," for pardon is closer to piety (taqwa). If he says: My heart cannot bear pardon, but I will defend myself as Allah, Mighty and Majestic, has commanded me, then say to him: If you are capable of defending yourself properly, do so, otherwise return to the door of pardon, for it is a wide door. Whoever pardons and reconciles, his reward is with Allah. The one who pardons sleeps on his bed at night, while the one who seeks retaliation keeps turning matters over in his mind.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Sa'id ibn Abi Sa'id, from Abu Hurayrah, may Allah be pleased with him, that a man reviled Abu Bakr while the Prophet ﷺ was sitting there, and the Prophet ﷺ began to be amazed and smile. When the man persisted, Abu Bakr responded to some of what he said, and the Prophet ﷺ became angry and stood up. Abu Bakr caught up with him and said: O Messenger of Allah, he was reviling me while you sat there, but when I responded to some of what he said, you became angry and stood up! He said: "An angel was with you responding on your behalf, but when you responded to some of what he said, the devil appeared, and I was not going to sit with the devil." Then he said: "O Abu Bakr, three things, all of them true: there is no servant who is wronged with an injustice and overlooks it for the sake of Allah, except that Allah increases him in honor through it, and no man opens the door of giving, intending thereby to maintain ties, except that Allah increases him in abundance through it, and no man opens the door of asking, intending thereby abundance, except that Allah increases him in scarcity through it."

Abu Dawud likewise narrated it from 'Abd al-A'la ibn Hammad, from Sufyan ibn 'Uyaynah, he said: and Safwan ibn 'Isa also narrated it, both of them from Muhammad ibn 'Ajlan. And it was narrated through the route of al-Layth, from Sa'id al-Maqburi, from Bashir ibn al-Muharrar, from Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab, as a mursal (report with a missing link at the end) report.

This hadith is of the utmost excellence in meaning, and it explains the reason for his reviling the Siddiq (Abu Bakr).

[وَقَالَ بَعْضُهُمْ: لَمَّا كَانَتِ الْأَقْسَامُ ثَلَاثَةً: ظَالِمٌ لِنَفْسِهِ، وَمُقْتَصِدٌ، وَسَابِقٌ بِالْخَيْرَاتِ، ذَكَرَ الْأَقْسَامَ الثَّلَاثَةَ فِي هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ فَذَكَرَ الْمُقْتَصِدَ وَهُوَ الَّذِي يُفِيضُ بِقَدْرِ حَقِّهِ لِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَجَزَاءُ سَيِّئَةٍ سَيِّئَةٌ مِثْلُهَا﴾ ، ثُمَّ ذَكَرَ السَّابِقَ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿فَمَنْ عَفَا وَأَصْلَحَ فَأَجْرُهُ عَلَى اللَّهِ﴾ ثُمَّ ذَكَرَ الظَّالِمَ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿إِنَّهُ لَا يُحِبُّ الظَّالِمِينَ﴾ فَأَمَرَ بِالْعَدْلِ،وَنَدَبَ إِلَى الْفَضْلِ، وَنَهَى مِنَ الظُّلْمِ] .

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿وَلَمَنِ انْتَصَرَ بَعْدَ ظُلْمِهِ فَأُولَئِكَ مَا عَلَيْهِمْ مِنْ سَبِيلٍ﴾ أَيْ: لَيْسَ عَلَيْهِمْ جُنَاحٌ فِي الِانْتِصَارِ مِمَّنْ ظَلَمَهُمْ.

قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ بَزيع حَدَّثَنَا مُعَاذُ بْنُ مُعَاذٍ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ عَوْن قَالَ: كُنْتُ أَسْأَلُ عَنِ الِانْتِصَارِ: ﴿وَلَمَنِ انْتَصَرَ بَعْدَ ظُلْمِهِ فَأُولَئِكَ مَا عَلَيْهِمْ مِنْ سَبِيلٍ﴾ فَحَدَّثَنِي عَلِيُّ بْنُ زَيْدِ بْنِ جُدْعَانَ عَنْ أُمِّ مُحَمَّدٍ -امْرَأَةِ أَبِيهِ-قَالَ ابْنُ عَوْنٍ: زَعَمُوا أَنَّهَا كَانَتْ تَدْخُلُ عَلَى أُمِّ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ عَائِشَةَ -قَالَتْ: قَالَتْ أُمُّ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ: دَخَلَ عَلَيْنَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ، وَعِنْدَنَا زَيْنَبُ بِنْتُ جَحْشٍ، فَجَعَلَ يَصْنَعُ بِيَدِهِ شَيْئًا فَلَمْ يَفْطِن لَهَا، فَقُلْتُ بِيَدِهِ حَتَّى فَطَّنته لَهَا، فَأَمْسَكَ. وَأَقْبَلَتْ زَيْنَبُ تُقْحِمُ لِعَائِشَةَ، فَنَهَاهَا، فَأَبَتْ أَنْ تَنْتَهِيَ. فَقَالَ لِعَائِشَةَ: "سُبّيها" فَسَبَّتْهَا فَغَلَبَتْهَا، وَانْطَلَقَتْ زَيْنَبُ فَأَتَتْ عَلِيًّا فَقَالَتْ: إِنَّ عَائِشَةَ تَقَعُ بِكُمْ، وَتَفْعَلُ بِكُمْ. فَجَاءَتْ فَاطِمَةُ فَقَالَ لَهَا "إِنَّهَا حِبَّةُ أَبِيكِ وَرَبِّ الْكَعْبَةِ" فَانْصَرَفَتْ، وَقَالَتْ لِعَلِيٍّ: إِنِّي قُلْتُ لَهُ كَذَا وَكَذَا، فَقَالَ لِي كَذَا وَكَذَا. قَالَ: وَجَاءَ عَلِيٌّ إِلَى النَّبِيِّ ﷺ فَكَلَّمَهُ فِي ذَلِكَ .

هَكَذَا وَرَدَ هَذَا السِّيَاقُ، وَعَلِيُّ بْنُ زَيْدِ بْنِ جُدْعَانَ يَأْتِي فِي رِوَايَاتِهِ بِالْمُنْكَرَاتِ غَالِبًا، وَهَذَا فِيهِ نَكَارَةٌ، وَالْحَدِيثُ الصَّحِيحُ خِلَافُ هَذَا السِّيَاقِ، كَمَا رَوَاهُ النَّسَائِيُّ وَابْنُ مَاجَهْ مِنْ حَدِيثِ خَالِدِ بْنِ سَلَمَةَ الْفَأْفَاءِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ البَهِيّ، عَنْ عُرْوَةَ قَالَ: قَالَتْ عَائِشَةُ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهَا: مَا علمتُ حَتَّى دَخَلَتْ عليَّ زَيْنَبُ بِغَيْرِ إِذَنٍ وَهِيَ غَضْبَى، ثُمَّ قَالَتْ لِرَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ: حَسْبُكَ إِذَا قَلَبَتْ لَكَ ابْنَةُ أَبِي بَكْرٍ ذُرَيّعَتَيهَا ثُمَّ أَقْبَلَتْ عَلَيَّ فَأَعْرَضْتُ عَنْهَا، حَتَّى قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: "دُونَكِ فَانْتَصِرِي" فَأَقْبَلْتُ عَلَيْهَا حَتَّى رَأَيْتُهَا وَقَدْ يَبِسَ رِيقُهَا فِي فَمِهَا، مَا تَرُدُّ عَلَيَّ شَيْئًا. فَرَأَيْتُ النَّبِيَّ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عليه وسلم يتهلل وجهه. وهذا لفظ النَّسَائِيِّ .

وَقَالَ الْبَزَّارُ: حَدَّثَنَا يُوسُفُ بْنُ مُوسَى، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو غَسَّانَ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو الْأَحْوَصِ عَنْ أَبِي حَمْزَةَ، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، عَنِ الْأَسْوَدِ ، عَنْ عَائِشَةَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهَا، قَالَتْ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "مَنْ دَعَا عَلَى مَنْ ظَلَمَهُ فَقَدِ انْتَصَرَ".

وَرَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي الْأَحْوَصِ، عَنْ أَبِي حَمْزَةَ -وَاسْمُهُ مَيْمُونٌ-ثُمَّ قَالَ: "لَا نَعْرِفُهُ إِلَّا مِنْ حَدِيثِهِ، وَقَدْ تَكَلَّمَ فِيهِ مِنْ قِبَلِ حِفْظِهِ" .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنَّمَا السَّبِيلُ﴾ أَيْ: إِنَّمَا الْحَرَجُ وَالْعَنَتُ ﴿عَلَى الَّذِينَ يَظْلِمُونَ النَّاسَ وَيَبْغُونَ فِي الأرْضِ بِغَيْرِ الْحَقِّ﴾ أَيْ: يَبْدَؤُونَ النَّاسَ بِالظُّلْمِ. كَمَا جَاءَ فِي الْحَدِيثِ الصَّحِيحِ: "الْمُسْتَبَّانُ مَا قَالَا فَعَلَى الْبَادِئِ مَا لَمْ يَعْتَد الْمَظْلُومُ".

﴿أُولَئِكَ لَهُمْ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ﴾ أَيْ: شَدِيدٌ مُوجِعٌ.

قَالَ أَبُو بَكْرِ بْنُ أَبِي شَيْبَةَ: حَدَّثَنَا الْحَسَنُ بْنُ مُوسَى، حَدَّثَنَا سَعِيدُ بْنُ زَيْدٍ -أَخُو حَمَّادِ بْنِ زَيْدٍ-حَدَّثَنَا عُثْمَانُ الشَّحَّامُ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ وَاسِعٍ قَالَ: قَدِمْتُ مَكَّةَ فَإِذَا عَلَى الْخَنْدَقِ مَنْظَرَة، فَأُخِذْتُ فَانْطُلِقَ بِي إِلَى مَرْوَانَ بْنِ الْمُهَلَّبِ، وَهُوَ أَمِيرٌ عَلَى الْبَصْرَةِ، فَقَالَ: حَاجَتَكَ يَا أَبَا عَبْدِ اللَّهِ. قُلْتُ حَاجَتِي إِنِ اسْتَطَعْتَ أَنْ تَكُونَ كَمَا قَالَ أَخُو بَنِي عَدِيٍّ. قَالَ: وَمَنْ أَخُو بَنِي عَدِيٍّ؟ قَالَ: الْعَلَاءُ بْنُ زِيَادٍ، اسْتَعْمَلَ صَدِيقًا لَهُ مَرَّةً عَلَى عَمَلٍ، فَكَتَبَ إِلَيْهِ: أَمَّا بَعْدُ فَإِنِ اسْتَطَعْتَ أَلَّا تَبِيتَ إِلَّا وَظَهْرُكَ خَفِيفٌ، وَبَطْنُكَ خَمِيصٌ، وَكَفُّكَ نَقِيَّةٌ مِنْ دِمَاءِ الْمُسْلِمِينَ وَأَمْوَالِهِمْ، فَإِنَّكَ إِذَا فَعَلْتَ ذَلِكَ لَمْ يَكُنْ عَلَيْكَ سَبِيلٌ، ﴿إِنَّمَا السَّبِيلُ عَلَى الَّذِينَ يَظْلِمُونَ النَّاسَ وَيَبْغُونَ فِي الأرْضِ بِغَيْرِ الْحَقِّ أُولَئِكَ لَهُمْ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ﴾ فَقَالَ صَدَقَ وَاللَّهِ وَنَصَحَ ثُمَّ قَالَ: مَا حَاجَتُكَ يَا أَبَا عَبْدِ اللَّهِ؟ قُلْتُ: حَاجَتِي أَنْ تُلْحِقَنِي بِأَهْلِي. قَالَ: نَعَمْ رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ .

ثُمَّ إِنَّهُ تَعَالَى لَمَّا ذَمَّ الظُّلْمَ وَأَهْلَهُ وَشَرَّعَ الْقَصَاصَ، قَالَ نَادِبًا إِلَى الْعَفْوِ وَالصَّفْحِ: ﴿وَلَمَنْ صَبَرَ وَغَفَرَ﴾ أَيْ: صَبَرَ عَلَى الْأَذَى وَسَتَرَ السَّيِّئَةَ، ﴿إِنَّ ذَلِكَ لَمِنْ عَزْمِ الأمُورِ﴾

قَالَ سَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ: [يَعْنِي] لَمِنْ حَقِّ الْأُمُورِ الَّتِي أَمَرَ اللَّهُ بِهَا، أَيْ: لَمِنَ الْأُمُورِ الْمَشْكُورَةِ وَالْأَفْعَالِ الْحَمِيدَةِ الَّتِي عَلَيْهَا ثَوَابٌ جَزِيلٌ وَثَنَاءٌ جَمِيلٌ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا عِمْرَانُ بْنُ مُوسَى الطَّرْسُوسِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الصَّمَدِ بْنُ يَزِيدَ -خَادِمُ الْفُضَيْلِ بْنِ عِيَاضٍ-قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ الْفُضَيْلَ بْنَ عِيَاضٍ يقول إذا أتاك رجل يَشْكُو إِلَيْكَ رَجُلًا فَقُلْ: "يَا أَخِي، اعْفُ عَنْهُ". فَإِنَّ الْعَفْوَ أَقْرَبُ لِلتَّقْوَى، فَإِنْ قَالَ: لَا يَحْتَمِلُ قَلْبِي الْعَفْوَ، وَلَكِنْ أَنْتَصِرُ كَمَا أَمَرَنِي اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ. فَقُلْ لَهُ إِنْ كُنْتُ تُحْسِنُ أَنْ تَنْتَصِرَ وَإِلَّا فَارْجِعْ إِلَى بَابِ الْعَفْوِ، فَإِنَّهُ بَابٌ وَاسِعٌ، فَإِنَّهُ مَنْ عَفَا وَأَصْلَحَ فَأَجْرُهُ عَلَى اللَّهِ، وَصَاحِبُ الْعَفْوِ يَنَامُ عَلَى فِرَاشِهِ بِاللَّيْلِ، وَصَاحِبُ الِانْتِصَارِ يُقَلِّبُ الْأُمُورَ .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى -يَعْنِي ابْنَ سَعِيدٍ الْقَطَّانَ-عَنِ ابْنِ عَجْلان، حَدَّثَنَا سَعِيدُ بْنُ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ أَنَّ رَجُلًا شَتَمَ أَبَا بَكْرٍ وَالنَّبِيُّ ﷺ جَالِسٌ، فَجَعَلَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يَعْجَبُ وَيَتَبَسَّمُ، فَلَمَّا أَكْثَرَ رَدَّ عَلَيْهِ بَعْضَ قَوْلِهِ، فَغَضِبَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ وَقَامَ، فَلَحِقَهُ أَبُو بَكْرٍ فَقَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ إِنَّهُ كَانَ يَشْتُمُنِي وَأَنْتَ جَالِسٌ، فَلَمَّا رَدَدْتُ عَلَيْهِ بَعْضَ قَوْلِهِ غَضِبْتَ وَقُمْتَ! قَالَ: "إِنَّهُ كَانَ مَعَكَ مَلَكٌ يَرُدُّ عَنْكَ، فَلَمَّا رَدَدْتَ عَلَيْهِ بَعْضَ قَوْلِهِ حَضَرَ الشَّيْطَانُ، فَلَمْ أَكُنْ لِأَقْعُدَ مَعَ الشَّيْطَانِ". ثُمَّ قَالَ: "يَا أَبَا بَكْرٍ، ثَلَاثٌ كُلُّهُنَّ حَقٌّ، مَا مِنْ عَبْدٍ ظُلم بِمَظْلَمَةٍ فَيُغْضِي عَنْهَا لِلَّهِ، إِلَّا أَعَزَّ اللَّهُ بِهَا نَصْرَه، وَمَا فَتَحَ رَجُلٌ بَابَ عَطِيَّةٍ يُرِيدُ بِهَا صِلَةً، إِلَّا زَادَهُ اللَّهُ بِهَا كَثْرَةً، وَمَا فَتَحَ رَجُلٌ بَابَ مَسْأَلَةٍ يُرِيدُ بِهَا كَثْرَةً، إِلَّا زَادَهُ اللَّهُ بِهَا قِلَّةً"

وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ أَبُو دَاوُدَ، عَنْ عَبْدِ الْأَعْلَى بْنِ حَمَّادٍ، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ بْنِ عُيَيْنَةَ -قَالَ: وَرَوَاهُ صَفْوَانُ بْنُ عِيسَى، كِلَاهُمَا عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عَجْلان وَرَوَاهُ مِنْ طَرِيقِ اللَّيْثِ، عَنْ سَعِيدٍ المَقْبُرِي، عَنْ بَشِيرِ بْنِ الْمُحَرِّرِ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ الْمُسَيَّبِ مُرْسَلًا .

وَهَذَا الْحَدِيثُ فِي غَايَةِ الْحُسْنِ فِي الْمَعْنَى، وَهُوَ سببُ سَبِّهِ لِلصِّدِّيقِ .

## 42:44-46 — verses 44-46 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, informing about His noble Self, that whatever He wills comes to be and nothing can repel it, and whatever He does not will does not come to be and nothing can bring it into existence, and that whoever He guides, none can lead astray, and whoever He leads astray, none can guide, as He said: {And whoever He leads astray, you will never find for him a guiding protector} [al-Kahf: 17]

Then He says, informing about the wrongdoers, who are those who associate partners with Allah, {When they see the punishment}, that is, on the Day of Resurrection they will wish to return to the worldly life, {they will say, "Is there any way of return?"}, as He said [the Exalted]: {And if you could but see when they are made to stand before the Fire, and they say, "Oh, would that we could be returned and not deny the signs of our Lord, and be among the believers." Rather, what they used to conceal before has become apparent to them. And even if they were returned, they would return to that which they were forbidden, and indeed they are liars} [al-An'am: 27, 28].

His saying: {And you will see them being exposed to it}, that is, to the Fire, {humbled from disgrace}, meaning the disgrace that has overtaken them because of the disobedience toward Allah which they had committed beforehand, {looking with a furtive glance}. Mujahid said: it means with lowliness, that is, they look at it stealthily out of fear of it, while what they fear will inevitably befall them, and it will be even greater than what is in their souls. May Allah shelter us from that.

## 42:47-48 — verses 47-48 (shared commentary)

### Translation

When the Exalted mentioned what will occur on the Day of Resurrection of terrors and momentous, dreadful matters, He warned against it and commanded preparation for it, saying: {Respond to your Lord before there comes a Day for which there will be no averting from Allah}, meaning: when He commands it to be, it happens like the twinkling of an eye, and there is no one to repel it or prevent it.

His saying: {You will have no refuge that Day, and no denial} means: you will have no fortress in which to shelter yourselves, nor any place that will conceal you and in which you might hide yourselves, so as to be hidden from His sight, Blessed and Exalted is He. Rather, He encompasses you with His knowledge, His sight, and His power, so there is no refuge from Him except to Him. {The man will say on that Day, "Where is the escape?" No! There is no refuge. To your Lord that Day is the final place of settlement} [al-Qiyamah: 10-12].

His saying: {But if they turn away}, meaning the polytheists, {then We have not sent you as a guardian over them}, meaning: you are not a controller over them. The Exalted said: {Their guidance is not upon you, but Allah guides whom He wills} [al-Baqarah: 272], and the Exalted said: {Upon you is only the conveyance, and upon Us is the reckoning} [al-Ra'd: 40], and He said here: {Upon you is only the conveyance}, meaning: We have only charged you with conveying to them the message of Allah addressed to them.

Then the Exalted said: {And indeed, when We let man taste mercy from Us, he rejoices in it}, meaning: when ease and blessing befall him, he rejoices in that. {And if evil befalls them}, meaning people, {evil} meaning drought, affliction, trial, and hardship, {then indeed man is ungrateful}, meaning: he denies the blessing that preceded, and knows nothing but the present moment. If a blessing befalls him he becomes insolent and reckless, and if a trial befalls him he despairs and gives up hope, just as the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said [to the women]: "O company of women, give charity, for I have seen that you are the majority of the people of the Fire." A woman said: "Why is that, O Messenger of Allah?" He said: "Because you complain excessively and are ungrateful to your husbands. If one were to treat one of you well for a lifetime and then withheld it for a single day, she would say: 'I have never seen any good from you at all.'" This is the condition of most people, except for those whom Allah has guided and inspired with right guidance, and who are among those who believed and did righteous deeds. The believer, as the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "If good fortune befalls him, he gives thanks, and that is good for him; and if harm befalls him, he is patient, and that is good for him. And that is not granted to anyone except the believer."

## 42:49-50 — verses 49-50 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He, exalted, informs that He is the Creator of the heavens and the earth and their Owner, and the One who disposes of them as He wills; that whatever He wills comes to be, and whatever He does not will does not come to be; that He gives to whom He wills and withholds from whom He wills, and there is no one who can withhold what He has given, and no one who can give what He has withheld; and that He creates what He wills. {He grants to whom He wills daughters}, meaning He provides him with daughters only. Al-Baghawi said: among them was Lot (peace be upon him). {And He grants to whom He wills sons}, meaning He provides him with sons only. Al-Baghawi said: like Abraham the Intimate Friend (peace be upon him), no daughter was born to him. {Or He combines for them males and females}, meaning He gives to whomever He wills among people both kinds together, male and female, that is, from this one and that one. Al-Baghawi said: like Muhammad ﷺ. {And He makes whom He wills barren}, meaning no child is born to him. Al-Baghawi said: like John and Jesus (peace be upon them both). Thus He made people into four categories: among them are those to whom He gives daughters, those to whom He gives sons, those to whom He gives both kinds, male and female, and those from whom He withholds both, making him barren, without offspring, unable to have children. {Indeed He is All-Knowing}, meaning of who deserves each of these categories, {All-Powerful}, meaning over whomever He wills, regarding the variation among people in this matter.

This subject resembles His saying, exalted, concerning Jesus: {And that We might make him a sign for mankind} [Maryam: 21], meaning a proof for them of His power, exalted and sanctified is He, in that He created creation in four categories. Adam (peace be upon him) was created from dust, neither from a male nor a female. Eve (peace be upon her) was created from a male without a female. The rest of creation, apart from Jesus (peace be upon him), comes from a male and a female. And Jesus (peace be upon him) came from a female without a male. Thus the proof was completed through the creation of Jesus son of Mary (peace be upon them both). This is why He said: {And that We might make him a sign for mankind}. This subject concerns the fathers, while the first subject concerns the sons, and each of the two consists of four categories. So glory be to the All-Knowing, the All-Powerful.

## 42:51-53 — verses 51-53 (shared commentary)

### Translation

These are the modes of revelation with respect to the presence of Allah, Exalted and Glorified is He. It is that He, exalted, sometimes casts something into the heart of the Prophet ﷺ such that he has no doubt whatsoever that it is from Allah, Exalted and Glorified is He, as has come in the Sahih of Ibn Hibban, from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, that he said: "The Holy Spirit breathed into my heart: no soul shall die until it has completed its provision and its appointed term, so fear Allah and be moderate in seeking."

And His saying: {or from behind a veil}, as He spoke to Musa (peace be upon him), for he asked to see Him after having been spoken to, and was veiled from that sight.

In the Sahih, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, said to Jabir ibn Abdullah: "Allah has never spoken to anyone except from behind a veil, but He spoke to your father face to face," the hadith. His father had been killed on the day of Uhud, but this was in the realm of the barzakh (intermediate realm), whereas the verse concerns only this worldly life.

And His saying: {or that He sends a messenger who reveals, by His permission, what He wills}, as when He sends down Jibril (peace be upon him) and other angels besides him upon the prophets (peace be upon them). {Indeed, He is Most High, Most Wise}, meaning He is High, Knowing, Aware, Wise.

And His saying: {And thus have We revealed to you a spirit from Our command}, meaning the Qur'an. {You did not know what the Book was, nor what faith was}, meaning according to the detail that has been legislated for you in the Qur'an. {But We have made it}, that is, the Qur'an, {a light by which We guide whom We will of Our servants}, as in His saying: {Say, it is for those who believe a guidance and a healing, but those who do not believe, in their ears is deafness, and it is upon them blindness. Those are being called from a distant place} [Fussilat: 44].

And His saying: {And indeed you}, meaning O Muhammad, {surely guide to a straight path}, which is the upright character. Then He explains it by His saying: {The path of Allah}, meaning His legislation which Allah has commanded, {to whom belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth}, meaning He is their Lord and their Owner, the One who disposes of them, the Judge whose judgment none can overturn. {Unquestionably, to Allah do all matters return}, meaning that matters return to Him, and He decides and rules concerning them.

End of the tafsir of Surat Ha Mim, al-Shura. All praise is due to Allah, Lord of the worlds.
