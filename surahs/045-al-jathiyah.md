## 45:1-5 — verses 1-5 (shared commentary)

### Translation

# Tafsir of Surat al-Jathiyah

It is Meccan.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

The Almighty guides His creation to reflect upon His signs and blessings, and upon His tremendous power by which He created the heavens and the earth, and what is within them of creatures differing in kind and species: angels, jinn, and mankind, beasts, birds, wild animals, predators, and insects, and the varied kinds found in the sea, and the alternation of night and day succeeding one another continuously without pause, this one with its darkness and that one with its light, and what Allah sends down from the clouds of rain at the time of need for it, calling it "provision" (rizq) because provision comes about through it: {and gave life thereby to the earth after its death}, that is, after it had been barren, with no plant life or anything in it.

His saying: {and the shifting of the winds}, that is, southerly and northerly, westerly and easterly, sea winds and land winds, night winds and day winds. Some of them bring rain, some bring pollination, some are sustenance for living beings, and some are barren, producing nothing.

He said first {signs for the believers}, then {who are certain}, then {who use reason}, and this is a progression from a noble state to one that is even nobler and higher than it. These verses are similar to the verse of "al-Baqarah," namely His saying: {Indeed, in the creation of the heavens and the earth, and the alternation of night and day, and the ships that sail through the sea with what benefits people, and what Allah sends down from the sky of water, giving life thereby to the earth after its death, and dispersing therein every kind of moving creature, and the shifting of the winds, and the clouds subjected between the sky and the earth, are signs for a people who use reason} [al-Baqarah: 164]. Ibn Abi Hatim has related here, from Wahb ibn Munabbih, a long report, gharib (singular/rare), concerning the creation of man from the four humors.

## 45:6-11 — verses 6-11 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: These are the signs of Allah, meaning the Qur'an with what it contains of proofs and clear evidences, {We recite it to you in truth}, that is, containing truth from truth. So if they do not believe in it and do not submit to it, then in what discourse after Allah and His signs will they believe?!

Then He said: {Woe to every sinful liar} that is, a liar in his speech, one given to false oaths, contemptible, sinful in his deed and his word, disbelieving in the signs of Allah. Therefore He said: {He hears the signs of Allah recited to him}, that is, read to him, {then persists} that is, in his disbelief and denial out of arrogance and stubbornness, {as if he had not heard them}, that is, as if he had never heard them at all, {so give him tidings of a painful punishment}, meaning: inform him that with Allah on the Day of Resurrection there awaits him a painful, agonizing punishment.

{And when he learns anything of Our signs, he takes them in mockery}, that is, when he memorizes something of the Qur'an he disbelieves in it and takes it as an object of ridicule and mockery. {For such there is a humiliating punishment}, that is, in return for his having held the Qur'an in contempt and mocked it. For this reason Muslim related in his Sahih, from Ibn Umar, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ forbade that the Qur'an be taken traveling into enemy territory, for fear that the enemy might get hold of it.

Then He explained the punishment that will befall him on the Day of his Return, saying: {Before them is Hell}, that is, everyone characterized by that description will end up in Hell on the Day of Resurrection. {And nothing of what they earned will avail them}, that is, neither their wealth nor their children will benefit them. {Nor what they took besides Allah as protectors}, that is, nor will the gods they worshipped besides Allah avail them anything at all. {And for them is a great punishment}.

Then the Almighty said: {This is guidance}, meaning the Qur'an. {And those who disbelieve in the signs of their Lord, for them is a punishment of painful wrath}, which is the agonizing, painful torment.

## 45:12-15 — verses 12-15 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He, exalted, mentions His favors upon His servants in what He subjected to them of the sea: {so that the ships may sail}, meaning the vessels sail upon it by His command, exalted, for it is He who commanded the sea to carry them, {and that you may seek of His bounty}, meaning in trade and earnings, {and that you may give thanks}, meaning for the attainment of the benefits brought to you from distant regions and far horizons.

Then He, exalted, said: {And He has subjected to you whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth}, meaning the stars, the mountains, the seas, the rivers, and everything you benefit from, that is, all of it is from His bounty, His kindness, and His grace. Therefore He said: {all, from Him}, meaning from Him alone, He having no partner in that, just as He, exalted, said: {And whatever blessing you have is from Allah; then when affliction touches you, to Him you cry out} [al-Nahl: 53].

Ibn Jarir reported through the route of al-Awfi, from Ibn Abbas, concerning His saying: {And He has subjected to you whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth, all, from Him}, that everything is from Allah, and that name contains within it one of His names, so that is all from Him, and no disputants may dispute Him in it, and one who is certain knows that it is so.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Abu Arakah said: A man asked Abdullah ibn Amr, saying: From what were creatures created? He said: From light and fire, darkness and moist earth (al-thara). He said: Go to Ibn Abbas and ask him. So he went to him and said the same thing to him. He said: Go back to him and ask him: From what was all of that created? So he returned to him and asked him, and he recited: {And He has subjected to you whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth, all, from Him}. This is a gharib (singular) report, and there is something objectionable (nakarah) in it.

{Indeed, in that are signs for a people who reflect.}

Concerning His saying: {Say to those who believe that they should forgive those who do not expect the days of Allah}, meaning that they should pardon them and bear the harm from them. This was at the beginning of Islam, when they were commanded to be patient with the harm of the polytheists and the People of the Book, so that this might serve to reconcile their hearts. Then, when they persisted in obstinacy, Allah prescribed for the believers combat and jihad. This is what has been reported from Ibn Abbas and Qatadah.

Mujahid said, [concerning His saying] {do not expect the days of Allah}: they do not care about the favors of Allah.

Concerning His saying: {that He may recompense a people for what they used to earn}, meaning that if they pardon them in this world, Allah will indeed recompense them for their evil deeds in the Hereafter. Therefore He said: {Whoever does righteousness, it is for himself, and whoever does evil, it is against himself; then to your Lord you will be returned}, meaning you will return to Him on the Day of Resurrection, and your deeds will be presented [before Him], and He will recompense you for your deeds, the good of them and the evil of them.

## 45:16-20 — verses 16-20 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He, exalted is He, mentions what He bestowed upon the Children of Israel by sending down the Scriptures to them, sending the messengers to them, and establishing dominion among them. This is why He said: {And We had certainly given the Children of Israel the Scripture and judgment and prophethood, and We provided them with good things} that is, from foods and drinks, {and We preferred them over the worlds} that is, in their own time.

{And We gave them clear signs of the matter} that is, decisive proofs, evidences, and conclusive demonstrations. The proofs were thus established against them, then after that they differed, after the proof had been established. This differing among them was merely transgression of some against others. {Indeed your Lord}, O Muhammad, {will judge between them on the Day of Resurrection concerning that over which they used to differ} that is, He will decide between them by His just judgment. In this there is a warning to this nation (ummah) against following their path and pursuing their course. This is why He said: {Then We put you on a clear path (shari'ah) of the matter, so follow it} that is, follow what has been revealed to you from your Lord, there is no god but He, and turn away from the polytheists. And He said here: {and do not follow the desires of those who do not know. Indeed, they will never avail you anything against Allah. And indeed, the wrongdoers are allies of one another} that is, and what good will their alliance with one another do for them, for it will only increase them in loss, ruin, and destruction. {And Allah is the ally of the righteous (muttaqin)}. He, exalted is He, brings them out of the darknesses into the light, while those who disbelieve, their allies are the false gods (taghut) who bring them out of the light into the darknesses.

Then He said: {This is a source of insight for the people} meaning the Qur'an, {and guidance and mercy for a people who are certain in faith}.

## 45:21-23 — verses 21-23 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: the believers and the disbelievers are not equal, just as He said: {Not equal are the companions of the Fire and the companions of Paradise. The companions of Paradise are the successful} [al-Hashr: 20]. And He says here: {Or do those who have committed evil deeds think}, meaning: who did them and earned them, {that We will make them like those who have believed and done righteous deeds, equal in their life and their death?} meaning: that We should make them equal to them in this world and the Hereafter! {Evil is what they judge}, meaning: evil is what they have assumed about Us and about Our justice, that We would make the righteous and the wicked equal in the Hereafter and in this world.

The hafiz Abu Ya'la said: [chain] from Abu Dharr, may Allah be pleased with him, who said: Indeed Allah built His religion upon four pillars. Whoever endures upon them but does not act by them will meet Allah while being among the fasiqin (transgressors). It was said: What are they, O Abu Dharr? He said: That one surrenders what Allah has made lawful to Allah, what Allah has made unlawful to Allah, the command of Allah to Allah, and the prohibition of Allah to Allah. None can be trusted with them except Allah.

Abu al-Qasim ﷺ said: "Just as grapes cannot be harvested from thorns, likewise the wicked cannot attain the ranks of the righteous."

This is a gharib (singular) hadith from this route. Muhammad ibn Ishaq mentioned in the book "al-Sira" that they found a stone in Mecca, in the foundation of the Ka'ba, upon which was written: "Do you commit evil deeds and yet hope for good ones? Indeed, just as grapes cannot be harvested from thorns."

Al-Tabarani narrated, from the hadith of Shu'ba, from 'Amr ibn Murra, from Abu al-Duha, from Masruq, that Tamim al-Dari stood one night until morning, repeating this verse: {Or do those who have committed evil deeds think that We will make them like those who have believed and done righteous deeds}. This is why the Almighty said: {Evil is what they judge}.

And He said: {And Allah created the heavens and the earth in truth}, meaning: with justice, {and so that every soul may be recompensed for what it has earned, and they will not be wronged}.

Then He, the Almighty, said: {Have you seen he who has taken his own desire as his god?} meaning: he only follows the command of his desire. Whatever he regards as good, he does; whatever he regards as evil, he leaves. This may be used as evidence against the Mu'tazila in their doctrine of rational good and evil (al-tahsin wa'l-taqbih al-'aqliyyayn).

It is reported from Malik, in what has been transmitted from him regarding tafsir: he does not desire anything except that he worships it.

As for His saying: {and Allah has sent him astray with knowledge}, it carries two possible meanings:

One: Allah led him astray because of His knowledge that he deserved that. The other: Allah led him astray after knowledge had reached him and the proof had been established against him. The second necessarily implies the first, but the reverse is not true.

{and sealed his hearing and his heart, and placed over his sight a covering}, meaning: he does not hear what would benefit him, does not comprehend anything by which he might be guided, and does not see any proof by which he might be illumined. This is why He said: {then who will guide him after Allah? Will you not then take heed?}, like His saying: {Whomever Allah sends astray, there is no guide for him, and He leaves them wandering blindly in their transgression} [al-A'raf: 186].

## 45:24-26 — verses 24-26 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty informs about the statement of the Dahriyyah (materialists who deny the afterlife, attributing all to time) among the disbelievers, and those who agreed with them among the polytheists of the Arabs in denying the return to life: {And they said: There is nothing but our life in this world; we die and we live}

That is: there is nothing except this abode. A people die and others live, and there is no return and no resurrection. This is said by the polytheists of the Arabs who deny the return to life, and it is said by the theistic philosophers among them, who deny the origination and the return, and it is said by the cyclical materialist philosophers who deny the Maker and believe that every thirty-six thousand years everything returns to what it was. They claimed that this has repeated itself an infinite number of times, so they defied reason and denied the revealed text. For this reason they said: {and nothing destroys us but time} God the Almighty said: {And they have no knowledge of that; they only conjecture}, meaning: they imagine and fancy.

As for the hadith which the two authors of the Sahih, Abu Dawud, and al-Nasa'i reported, from the narration of Sufyan ibn Uyaynah, from al-Zuhri, from Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab, from Abu Hurayrah, may God be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "God the Almighty says: The son of Adam harms Me; he curses time (al-dahr), and I am time (al-dahr), in My hand is the affair, I turn over its night and its day." And in another narration: "Do not curse time, for God is time (al-dahr)."

Ibn Jarir related it with a very gharib (singular) wording, saying: Abu Kurayb narrated to us, Sufyan ibn Uyaynah narrated to us, from al-Zuhri, from Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab, from Abu Hurayrah, may God be pleased with him, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "The people of the Jahiliyyah (pre-Islamic ignorance) used to say: only the night and the day destroy us, and it is what destroys us, causes us to die and gives us life. So God said in His Book: {And they said: There is nothing but our life in this world; we die and we live, and nothing destroys us but time}." He said: "And they used to curse time, so God, Mighty and Majestic, said: The son of Adam harms Me; he curses time, and I am time, in My hand is the affair, I turn over the night and the day."

Ibn Abi Hatim likewise related it, from Ahmad ibn Mansur, from Shurayh ibn al-Nu'man, from Ibn Uyaynah, similarly. Then it was related from Yunus, from Ibn Wahb, from al-Zuhri, from Abu Salamah, from Abu Hurayrah: I heard the Messenger of God ﷺ say: "God the Almighty said: The son of Adam curses time, and I am time, in My hand are the night and the day."

The two authors of the Sahih and al-Nasa'i reported it from the hadith of Yunus ibn Zayd, with the same chain.

Muhammad ibn Ishaq said, from al-'Ala' ibn Abd al-Rahman, from his father, from Abu Hurayrah, may God be pleased with him, that the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "God says: My servant sought a loan from Me but did not give Me anything, and My servant cursed Me, saying: Oh, the disappointment of time (dahr)! And I am time (al-dahr)."

Al-Shafi'i, Abu Ubaydah, and other imams besides them said, in explaining his statement, upon him be prayers and peace: "Do not curse time, for God is time": the Arabs in their Jahiliyyah, when misfortune, affliction, or calamity befell them, would say: O disappointment of time! They would attribute those events to time and curse it, whereas the actual doer of them was God, Mighty and Majestic. It was as though they were thereby cursing God, Mighty and Majestic, because He is in reality the doer of that. For this reason cursing time was forbidden in this sense, because God is the time that they mean and to which they attribute those events.

This is the best that has been said in explaining it, and it is what is meant, and God knows best. Ibn Hazm and those among the Zahiriyyah who followed his approach erred in counting al-Dahr (time) among the Beautiful Names, basing this on this hadith.

And His statement, the Almighty: {And when Our clear signs are recited to them}, that is, when proof is presented to them and the truth is made clear to them, namely that God is able to restore bodies after their disintegration and dispersal, {their only argument was to say: Bring back our forefathers, if you are truthful}, meaning: bring them back to life if what you say is true.

God the Almighty said: {Say: God gives you life}, meaning: just as you observe that He brings you out from non-existence into existence, {How can you disbelieve in God when you were dead and He gave you life, then He will cause you to die, then He will give you life again} [al-Baqarah: 28], meaning: the One who was able to originate is able to restore, by a path all the more fitting and appropriate. {And He is the One who originates creation, then repeats it, and that is easier for Him} [al-Rum: 27], {Then He will gather you to the Day of Resurrection, about which there is no doubt}, meaning: He will only gather you for the Day of Resurrection, He will not restore you in this world so that you would say: {Bring back our forefathers, if you are truthful}. {The day He will gather you for the Day of Gathering} [al-Taghabun: 9], {For what Day has it been deferred? For the Day of Judgment} [al-Mursalat: 12-13], {And We only delay it for a fixed term} [Hud: 104]. And here He said: {Then He will gather you to the Day of Resurrection, about which there is no doubt}, meaning: no doubt about it, {but most people do not know}, meaning: and for this reason they deny the return to life and consider the raising of bodies to be far-fetched. God the Almighty said: {Indeed, they see it as far off, but We see it as near} [al-Ma'arij: 6-7], meaning: they see its occurrence as far off, while the believers see it as easy and near.

## 45:27-29 — verses 27-29 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty informs that He is the Owner of the heavens and the earth, the Ruler over them in this world and the next. Therefore He said: {And on the day the Hour arises} that is, the Day of Resurrection, {the falsifiers will lose} meaning the disbelievers in Allah, those who deny the clear signs and manifest proofs that He sent down upon His messengers.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: Sufyan al-Thawri came to Medina and heard al-Mu'afiri speaking something that made people laugh. He said to him: "O elder, do you not know that Allah has a day on which the falsifiers will lose?" He said: This continued to be known about al-Mu'afiri until he met Allah, Mighty and Majestic. Ibn Abi Hatim mentioned this.

Then He said: {And you will see every nation kneeling} that is, upon its knees from the severity and enormity of the event. It is said that this occurs when Hell is brought forth, for it will let out a roar such that no one will remain except that he falls to his knees, even Ibrahim the Friend (peace be upon him), who will say: "Myself, myself, myself, I ask You for nothing today except my own soul." Even Isa (peace be upon him) will say: "I ask You for nothing today except my own soul, I do not ask You today even for Maryam who bore me."

Mujahid, Ka'b al-Ahbar, and al-Hasan al-Basri said: {every nation kneeling} means upon their knees. Ikrimah said: {kneeling} means set apart in its own group, not upon the knees. The first view is preferable.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "It is as though I see you kneeling upon the mounds short of Hell."

Isma'il ibn Rafi' al-Madini said, from Muhammad ibn Ka'b, from Abu Hurayrah, may Allah be pleased with him, in marfu' (elevated) form, in the hadith of the Trumpet: "The people will be separated and the nations will kneel, and this is what Allah says: {And you will see every nation kneeling, every nation being called to its record}."

In this there is a combination of the two views, and there is no contradiction between them, and Allah knows best.

His saying: {every nation being called to its record} means the record of its deeds, as in His saying: {And the record will be placed, and the prophets and witnesses will be brought} [al-Zumar: 69]. Therefore He said: {Today you will be recompensed for what you used to do} that is, you will be repaid for your deeds, the good of them and the evil of them, as in His saying: {Man will be informed that Day of what he sent forth and what he left behind. Rather, man, against himself, will be a witness, even if he presents his excuses} [al-Qiyamah: 13-15].

Then He said: {This is Our record, speaking about you in truth} that is, it will present all your deeds without addition or omission, as in His saying: {And the record will be placed, and you will see the criminals fearful of what is in it, and they will say: Oh, woe to us! What is this record that leaves nothing small or great except that it has enumerated it? And they will find what they did present, and your Lord does not wrong anyone} [al-Kahf: 49].

His saying: {Indeed, We used to have transcribed what you used to do} means: We used to command the recording angels to write down your deeds against you.

Ibn Abbas and others said: The angels write the deeds of the servants, then they ascend with them to heaven, where they meet the angels who are in charge of the register of deeds, comparing what is in their hands with what has been brought forth for them from the Preserved Tablet on every Night of Decree (Laylat al-Qadr), from what Allah wrote in pre-eternity concerning the servants before He created them. Not a letter is added and not a letter is removed. Then he recited: {Indeed, We used to have transcribed what you used to do}.

## 45:30-37 — verses 30-37 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty informs about His judgment concerning His creation on the Day of Resurrection, saying: {As for those who believed and did righteous deeds}, meaning: their hearts believed and their limbs performed righteous deeds, that is, deeds that are sincere and in accordance with the sacred law, {their Lord will admit them into His mercy}, which is Paradise, as is established in the sahih that Allah said to Paradise: "You are My mercy, by you I show mercy to whomever I will."

{That is the manifest triumph}, meaning: the clear, evident one.

Then He says: {But as for those who disbelieved: were not My verses recited to you, and you were arrogant}, meaning: this will be said to them as a rebuke and reproach: were not the verses of the Most Merciful recited to you, and you were too arrogant to follow them, and you turned away when you heard them, {and you were a criminal people}, meaning: in your deeds, along with the denial that your hearts contained?

{And when it was said, "Indeed the promise of Allah is true, and the Hour, there is no doubt about it,"} meaning: when the believers said that to you, {you said, "We do not know what the Hour is,} meaning: we do not recognize it, {we only assume, as a mere assumption,} meaning: we merely imagine its occurrence as an imagining, that is, a weak, unlikely one. Hence He said: {and we are not convinced}, meaning: not certain. Allah the Almighty says: {And the evil consequences of what they did will appear to them}, meaning: the punishment for their evil deeds will become manifest to them, {and there will surround them}, meaning: it will encompass them, {that which they used to mock}, meaning: the punishment and the exemplary chastisement. {And it will be said, "Today We forget you}, meaning: We will treat you as one who has forgotten treats another, in the fire of Hell, {just as you forgot the meeting of this Day of yours,}" meaning: so you did not work for it because you did not believe in it, {and your abode is the Fire, and you have no helpers.}

It is established in the sahih that Allah the Almighty says to some of His servants on the Day of Resurrection: "Did I not give you a wife? Did I not honor you? Did I not subject to you horses and camels, and let you be a leader and one who takes his rightful share?" He will say: "Yes indeed, my Lord." Then He will say: "Did you then think that you would meet Me?" He will say: "No." Then Allah the Almighty will say: "Then today I will forget you just as you forgot Me."

Allah the Almighty says: {That is because you took the verses of Allah in mockery}, meaning: We only repaid you with this recompense because you took Allah's proofs against you as an object of derision, mocking and ridiculing them, {and the life of this world deluded you}, meaning: it deceived you, so you settled comfortably into it, and thus you became among the losers. Hence He said: {So today they will not be brought out of it}, meaning: out of the Fire, {nor will they be allowed to make amends}, meaning: no plea for their satisfaction will be sought from them; rather they will be punished without reckoning and without reproach, just as a group of the believers will enter Paradise without punishment and without reckoning.

Then, having mentioned His judgment concerning the believers and the disbelievers, He says: {So praise be to Allah, Lord of the heavens and Lord of the earth}, meaning: the Owner of both of them and of all that is in them. Hence He said: {Lord of all the worlds}.

Then He says: {And to Him belongs greatness in the heavens and the earth}. Mujahid said: it means dominion (sultan). That is: He is the Magnificent, the Glorified, before whom everything is submissive and in need of Him. It has come in the sahih hadith: "Allah the Almighty says: Magnificence is My lower garment (izar) and greatness (kibriya) is My upper garment (rida'), so whoever contends with Me for either of them, I will settle him in My Fire." Muslim narrated it through the hadith of al-A'mash, from Abu Ishaq, from al-Agharr Abu Muslim, from Abu Hurayrah and Abu Sa'id, may Allah be pleased with both of them, from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, in similar wording.

And His saying: {And He is the Mighty}, meaning: He who cannot be overcome nor resisted, {the Wise}, in His words and His deeds, His legislation and His decree. Exalted and sanctified is He, there is no god but Him.

End of the tafsir of Surat al-Jathiyah, and to Allah belongs all praise and grace.

يُخْبِرُ تَعَالَى عَنْ حُكْمِهِ فِي خَلْقِهِ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ، فَقَالَ: ﴿فَأَمَّا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ﴾ أَيْ: آمَنَتْ قُلُوبُهُمْ وَعَمِلَتْ جَوَارِحُهُمُ الْأَعْمَالَ الصَّالِحَاتِ ، وَهِيَ الْخَالِصَةُ الْمُوَافِقَةُ لِلشَّرْعِ، ﴿فَيُدْخِلُهُمْ رَبُّهُمْ فِي رَحْمَتِهِ﴾ ، وَهِيَ الْجَنَّةُ، كَمَا ثَبَتَ فِي الصَّحِيحِ أَنَّ اللَّهَ قَالَ لِلْجَنَّةِ: "أَنْتِ رَحْمَتِي، أَرْحَمُ بِكِ مَنْ أَشَاءُ" .

﴿ذَلِكَ هُوَ الْفَوْزُ الْمُبِينُ﴾ أَيِ: الْبَيِّنُ الْوَاضِحُ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿وَأَمَّا الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا أَفَلَمْ تَكُنْ آيَاتِي تُتْلَى عَلَيْكُمْ فَاسْتَكْبَرْتُمْ﴾ أَيْ: يُقَالُ لَهُمْ ذَلِكَ تَقْرِيعًا وَتَوْبِيخًا: أَمَا قُرِئَتْ عَلَيْكُمْ آيَاتُ الرَّحْمَنِ فَاسْتَكْبَرْتُمْ عَنِ اتِّبَاعِهَا، وَأَعْرَضْتُمْ عِنْدَ سَمَاعِهَا، ﴿وَكُنْتُمْ قَوْمًا مُجْرِمِينَ﴾ أَيْ: فِي أَفْعَالِكُمْ، مَعَ مَا اشْتَمَلَتْ عَلَيْهِ قُلُوبُكُمْ مِنَ التَّكْذِيبِ؟

﴿وَإِذَا قِيلَ إِنَّ وَعْدَ اللَّهِ حَقٌّ وَالسَّاعَةُ لَا رَيْبَ فِيهَا﴾ أَيْ: إِذَا قَالَ لَكُمُ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ ذَلِكَ، ﴿قُلْتُمْ مَا نَدْرِي مَا السَّاعَةُ﴾ أَيْ: لَا نَعْرِفُهَا، ﴿إِنْ نَظُنُّ إِلا ظَنًّا﴾ أَيْ: إِنْ نَتَوَهَّمُ وُقُوعَهَا إِلَّا تَوَهُّمًا، أَيْ مَرْجُوحًا ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿وَمَا نَحْنُ بِمُسْتَيْقِنِينَ﴾ أَيْ: بِمُتَحَقِّقِينَ، قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَبَدَا لَهُمْ سَيِّئَاتُ مَا عَمِلُوا﴾ أَيْ: وَظَهَرَ لَهُمْ عُقُوبَةُ أَعْمَالِهِمُ السَّيِّئَةِ، ﴿وَحَاقَ بِهِمْ﴾ أَيْ: أَحَاطَ بِهِمْ ﴿مَا كَانُوا بِهِ يَسْتَهْزِئُونَ﴾ أَيْ: مِنَ الْعَذَابِ وَالنَّكَالِ، ﴿وَقِيلَ الْيَوْمَ نَنْسَاكُمْ﴾ أَيْ: نُعَامِلُكُمْ مُعَامَلَةَ النَّاسِي لَكُمْ فِي نَارِ جَهَنَّمَ ﴿كَمَا نَسِيتُمْ لِقَاءَ يَوْمِكُمْ هَذَا﴾ أَيْ: فَلَمْ تَعْمَلُوا لَهُ لِأَنَّكُمْ لَمْ تُصَدِّقُوا بِهِ، ﴿وَمَأْوَاكُمُ النَّارُ وَمَا لَكُمْ مِنْ نَاصِرِينَ﴾

وَقَدْ ثَبَتَ فِي الصَّحِيحِ أَنَّ اللَّهَ تَعَالَى يَقُولُ لِبَعْضِ الْعَبِيدِ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ: "أَلَمْ أُزَوِّجْكَ؟ أَلَمْ أُكْرِمْكَ؟ أَلَمْ أُسَخِّرْ لَكَ الْخَيْلَ وَالْإِبِلَ، وَأَذَرْكَ تَرْأَسُ وتَرْبَع؟ فَيَقُولُ: بَلَى، يَا رَبِّ. فَيَقُولُ: أَفَظَنَنْتَ أَنَّكَ مُلاقيّ؟ فَيَقُولُ: لَا. فَيَقُولُ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: فَالْيَوْمَ أَنْسَاكَ كَمَا نَسِيتَنِي" .

قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿ذَلِكُمْ بِأَنَّكُمُ اتَّخَذْتُمْ آيَاتِ اللَّهِ هُزُوًا﴾ أَيْ: إِنَّمَا جَازَيْنَاكُمْ هَذَا الْجَزَاءَ لِأَنَّكُمُ اتَّخَذْتُمْ حُجَجَ اللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ سُخْرِيًّا، تَسْخَرُونَ وَتَسْتَهْزِئُونَ بِهَا، ﴿وَغَرَّتْكُمُ الْحَيَاةُ الدُّنْيَا﴾ أَيْ: خَدَعَتْكُمْ فَاطْمَأْنَنْتُمْ إِلَيْهَا، فَأَصْبَحْتُمْ مِنَ الْخَاسِرِينَ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿فَالْيَوْمَ لَا يُخْرَجُونَ مِنْهَا﴾ أَيْ: مِنَ النَّارِ ﴿وَلا هُمْ يُسْتَعْتَبُونَ﴾ أَيْ: لَا يُطْلَبُ مِنْهُمُ الْعُتْبَى، بَلْ يُعَذَّبُونَ بِغَيْرِ حِسَابٍ وَلَا عِتَابٍ، كَمَا تَدْخُلُ طَائِفَةٌ مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ الْجَنَّةَ بِغَيْرِ عَذَابٍ وَلَا حِسَابٍ.

ثُمَّ لَمَّا ذَكَرَ حُكْمَهُ فِي الْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَالْكَافِرِينَ قَالَ: ﴿فَلِلَّهِ الْحَمْدُ رَبِّ السمواتِ وَرَبِّ الأرْضِ﴾ أَيِ: الْمَالِكِ لَهُمَا وَمَا فِيهِمَا؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ﴾ .

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿وَلَهُ الْكِبْرِيَاءُ فِي السمواتِ وَالأرْضِ﴾ قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: يَعْنِي السُّلْطَانَ. أَيْ: هُوَ الْعَظِيمُ الْمُمَجَّدُ، الَّذِي كَلُّ شَيْءٍ خَاضِعٌ لَدَيْهِ فَقِيرٌ إِلَيْهِ. وَقَدْ وَرَدَ فِي الْحَدِيثِ الصَّحِيحِ: "يَقُولُ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى الْعَظَمَةُ إِزَارِي وَالْكِبْرِيَاءُ رِدَائِي، فَمَنْ نَازَعَنِي وَاحِدًا مِنْهُمَا أَسْكَنْتُهُ نَارِي". وَرَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ مِنْ حَدِيثِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ، عَنِ الْأَغَرِّ أَبِي مُسْلِمٍ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ وَأَبِي سَعِيدٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا، عَنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، بِنَحْوِهِ .
