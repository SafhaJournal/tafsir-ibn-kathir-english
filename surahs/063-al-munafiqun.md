## 63:1-4 — verses 1-4 (shared commentary)

### Translation

# Tafsir of Surat al-Munafiqun

It is Medinan.

In the name of Allah, the Most Merciful, the Most Compassionate.

The Exalted says, informing about the hypocrites: they only utter the profession of Islam when they come to the Prophet ﷺ, but in the inner reality of the matter they are not so, rather the opposite of that. Hence He says: {When the hypocrites come to you, they say, "We bear witness that you are indeed the Messenger of Allah"}, meaning: when they are present before you they confront you with this and display it to you, though they are not as they claim. For this reason He interjected a clause affirming that he is indeed the Messenger of Allah, saying: {Allah, and Allah knows that you are indeed His Messenger}.

Then He said: {and Allah bears witness that the hypocrites are indeed liars}, meaning: in what they reported, even though it corresponded to outward reality, because they did not believe in the truth of what they were saying nor in its sincerity. For this reason He declared them liars in relation to their actual belief.

His saying: {They have taken their oaths as a shield, and thus have averted people from the way of Allah}, meaning: they guarded themselves against people by means of false oaths and sinful swearing, so that they would be believed in what they said. Thus whoever did not know the true nature of their affair was deceived by them, believing them to be Muslims, and might even imitate what they did and believe what they said, while their actual state was that inwardly they spared no effort in corrupting Islam and its people. A great deal of harm therefore befell many people through this. Hence the Exalted says: {and so they have averted [people] from the way of Allah; indeed, evil is what they used to do}. For this reason al-Dahhak ibn Muzahim used to read it as "they have taken their iman (belief/faith) as a shield," meaning: their outward profession of faith as a shield, that is, a precaution by which they protected themselves from being killed. The majority read it as {aymanahum}, the plural of yamin (oath).

[His saying]: {That is because they believed, then disbelieved, so their hearts were sealed, and they do not understand}, meaning: hypocrisy was decreed for them because they turned back from belief to disbelief, exchanging guidance for error. {So their hearts were sealed, and they do not understand}, meaning: no guidance reaches their hearts, and no good penetrates them, so they neither comprehend nor find guidance.

{And when you see them, their bodies please you, and if they speak, you listen to their speech}, meaning: they had handsome forms and were possessed of eloquence and fluent tongues, so that whoever heard them would incline to listen to their speech because of their eloquence. Yet along with this they were at the extreme of weakness, feebleness, terror, panic, and cowardice. Hence He says: {They think every shout is against them}, meaning: whenever any event, occurrence, or fear arose, they believed, out of their cowardice, that it was descending upon them, as the Exalted says: {being niggardly toward you. Then when fear comes, you see them looking at you, their eyes rolling like one being overcome by death. But when the fear departs, they lash you with sharp tongues, being niggardly of good. Such ones have not believed, so Allah has rendered their deeds void, and that is easy for Allah} [al-Ahzab: 19]. They are but hollow clouds, mere forms devoid of substance. Hence He says: {They are the enemy, so beware of them. May Allah curse them; how are they deluded away [from the truth]?}, meaning: how are they turned away from guidance toward error?

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] ... Sa'id ibn Abi Sa'id al-Maqburi, from his father, from Abu Hurayrah, may Allah be pleased with him, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "The hypocrites have signs by which they are known: their greeting is a curse, their food is plunder, their spoils are misappropriated gains, they do not approach the mosques except in avoidance, and they do not come to the prayer except lagging behind, being arrogant, neither befriending nor being befriended; they are like dry timber by night, and clamorous by day." Yazid said once: "noisy by day."

## 63:5-8 — verses 5-8 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, informing us of the hypocrites, upon whom be the curses of Allah, that they, {when it is said to them, "Come, the Messenger of Allah will seek forgiveness for you," they turn their heads away}, meaning: they turned away and averted themselves from what was said to them, out of arrogance toward that and out of contempt for what was said to them. This is why He said: {and you see them turning away while they are arrogant}. Then He recompensed them for that, saying: {It is the same for them whether you ask forgiveness for them or do not ask forgiveness for them; Allah will never forgive them; indeed, Allah does not guide the transgressing people}, just as He said in Surat Bara'ah (Repentance), and the discussion of that, along with the citation of the hadiths reported there, has already preceded.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: my father narrated to us, Ibn Abi Umar al-Adani narrated to us, saying: Sufyan said regarding {they turn their heads away}, Ibn Abi Umar said: Sufyan turned his face to his right and glanced sideways with his eye, then said: "They are like this."

More than one of the early scholars have mentioned that this entire passage was revealed concerning Abdullah ibn Ubayy ibn Salul, as we shall relate shortly, if Allah wills. And in Him is trust, and upon Him is reliance.

Muhammad ibn Ishaq said in his Sirah: when the Messenger of Allah ﷺ arrived in Medina, meaning upon his return from Uhud, and Abdullah ibn Ubayy ibn Salul, as Ibn Shihab al-Zuhri narrated to me, had a standing position that he would take up every Friday without objection, as an honor to himself and to his people, for he was a man of standing among them. When the Prophet ﷺ sat down on Friday to address the people, he would stand up and say: "O people, this is the Messenger of Allah ﷺ among you; Allah has honored you through him and given you might through him, so support him and assist him, and listen to him and obey him." Then he would sit down. This continued until the day of Uhud, when he did what he did, meaning his withdrawal with a third of the army, and when the people returned he stood to do as he had been accustomed to doing. The Muslims seized his garment from every side and said: "Sit down, O enemy of Allah, you are not fit for that, after you have done what you did." So he went out, stepping over the necks of the people, saying: "By Allah, it is as though I had said something monstrous, merely because I stood up to strengthen his affair." Then some men of the Ansar met him at the door of the mosque and said: "Woe to you, what is the matter with you?" He said: "I stood up to strengthen his affair, and men from among his companions leapt upon me, pulling at me and rebuking me, as though I had said something monstrous merely because I stood up to strengthen his affair." They said: "Woe to you, go back so that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ may seek forgiveness for you." He said: "By Allah, I do not wish him to seek forgiveness for me."

Qatadah and al-Suddi said: this verse was revealed concerning Abdullah ibn Ubayy. That was because a young man, a relative of his, went to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and told him a report about him, concerning a serious matter. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ then summoned him, and he swore by Allah and disavowed that. The Ansar turned against that young man, blaming him and reproaching him, and Allah revealed concerning him what you hear. It was said to the enemy of Allah: "Why do you not go to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ?" And he began turning his head away, meaning: "I will not do that."

Ibn Abi Hatim said: my father narrated to us, Abu al-Rabi' al-Zahrani narrated to us, Hammad ibn Zayd narrated to us, Ayyub narrated to us, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr: that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, whenever he made camp somewhere, would not set out again until he had prayed there. When the expedition of Tabuk took place, word reached him that Abdullah ibn Ubayy ibn Salul had said: {The more honorable among us will certainly expel the more abased}, so he set out before making camp at the end of the day. It was said to Abdullah ibn Ubayy: "Go to the Prophet ﷺ so that he may seek forgiveness for you." Then Allah revealed: {When the hypocrites come to you} up to His saying: {and when it is said to them, "Come, the Messenger of Allah will seek forgiveness for you," they turn their heads away}.

This is a sound chain of transmission reaching Sa'id ibn Jubayr. As for his statement that this occurred during the expedition of Tabuk, there is doubt about it; indeed it is not sound, for Abdullah ibn Ubayy ibn Salul was not among those who went out on the expedition of Tabuk, but rather turned back with a group of the army. What is well known among the scholars of military campaigns and biography is that this occurred during the expedition of al-Muraysi', which is the expedition against Banu al-Mustaliq.

Yunus ibn Bukayr said, from Ibn Ishaq: Muhammad ibn Yahya ibn Hibban, Abdullah ibn Abi Bakr, and Asim ibn Umar ibn Qatadah narrated to me, concerning the story of Banu al-Mustaliq: while the Messenger of Allah was encamped there, Jahjah ibn Sa'id al-Ghifari, who was a hired worker for Umar ibn al-Khattab, and Sinan ibn Wabr fought with one another at the watering place. Ibn Ishaq said: Muhammad ibn Yahya ibn Hibban narrated to me, saying: the two crowded each other at the water and came to blows, and Sinan called out: "O company of the Ansar!" and al-Jahjah called out: "O company of the Muhajirin!" Zayd ibn Arqam and a group of the Ansar were with Abdullah ibn Ubayy at the time, and when he heard this he said: "They have risen against us in our own land. By Allah, our situation with these vagabonds of the Quraysh is just as the saying goes: 'Fatten your dog and it will eat you.' By Allah, if we return to Medina, the more honorable among us will certainly expel the more abased." Then he turned to those of his people who were present and said: "This is what you have done to yourselves: you have let them settle in your lands and you have shared your wealth with them. By Allah, if you had withheld from them, they would have moved away from your lands to somewhere else." Zayd ibn Arqam heard this and went with it to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, who was then a young lad, and Umar ibn al-Khattab, may Allah be pleased with him, was with him, and he informed him of the report. Umar, may Allah be pleased with him, said: "O Messenger of Allah, command Abbad ibn Bishr to strike off his head." The Prophet ﷺ said: "And how would it be, O Umar, if people were to say that Muhammad kills his own companions? No, rather call out, O Umar, for departure."

When it reached Abdullah ibn Ubayy that this had reached the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, he came to him and made his excuses, and swore by Allah that he had not said what Zayd ibn Arqam had reported against him, for he held a position of standing among his people. They said: "O Messenger of Allah, perhaps this young man was mistaken and did not correctly retain what the man said."

The Messenger of Allah ﷺ set out journeying at midday during an hour in which he did not normally travel. Usayd ibn al-Hudayr met him and greeted him with the greeting due to prophethood, then said: "By Allah, you have set out at an unusual hour in which you were not accustomed to travel." The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Has it not reached you what your companion Ibn Ubayy has said? He claims that when he arrives in Medina the more honorable will expel the more abased from it." He said: "But you, O Messenger of Allah, are the honorable one, and he is the abased one." Then he said: "O Messenger of Allah, deal gently with him, for by Allah, Allah has brought you to us at a time when we were stringing beads to crown him, and he sees that you have snatched away a kingdom from him."

The Messenger of Allah ﷺ then traveled on with the people until evening came, and through that night until they reached morning, and through the following day until the heat of midmorning grew intense. Then he made camp with the people in order to distract them from the talk that had occurred, and no sooner had the people felt the touch of the earth than they slept. And Surat al-Munafiqun (The Hypocrites) was revealed.

The hafiz Abu Bakr al-Bayhaqi said: Abu Abdullah al-Hafiz informed us, Abu Bakr ibn Ishaq informed us, Bishr ibn Musa informed us, al-Humaydi narrated to us, Sufyan narrated to us, Amr ibn Dinar narrated to us, [I heard] Jabir ibn Abdullah say: We were with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ on an expedition, and a man from the Muhajirin struck a man from the Ansar with the back of his hand. The Ansari man called out: "O company of the Ansar!" and the Muhajiri man called out: "O company of the Muhajirin!" The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "What is this call to pre-Islamic partisanship? Leave it, for it is foul." Abdullah ibn Ubayy ibn Salul, when this had occurred, said: "By Allah, if we return to Medina, the more honorable among us will certainly expel the more abased." Jabir said: at the time the Messenger of Allah ﷺ arrived in Medina, the Ansar were more numerous than the Muhajirin there, but the Muhajirin grew more numerous afterward. Umar said: "Let me strike off the neck of this hypocrite." The Prophet ﷺ said: "Leave him be, lest people say that Muhammad kills his own companions."

This was also related by Imam Ahmad, from Husayn ibn Muhammad al-Marwazi, from Sufyan ibn Uyaynah. It was also related by al-Bukhari, from al-Humaydi, and by Muslim, from Abu Bakr ibn Abi Shaybah and others, from Sufyan, with similar wording.

Imam Ahmad said: Muhammad ibn Ja'far narrated to us, Shu'bah narrated to us, from al-Hakam, from Muhammad ibn Ka'b al-Qurazi, from Zayd ibn Arqam, who said: I was with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ on the expedition of Tabuk, and Abdullah ibn Ubayy said: "If we return to Medina, the more honorable among us will certainly expel the more abased." He said: so I went to the Prophet ﷺ and informed him, and Abdullah ibn Ubayy swore that there was nothing to it. He said: my people then blamed me and said: "What did you mean by doing this?" He said: so I went off and slept, sorrowful and grieved. He said: then Allah's Prophet ﷺ sent for me and said: "Indeed Allah has revealed your excuse and confirmed you as truthful."

He said, "Then this verse was revealed: {It is they who say, 'Do not spend on those who are with the Messenger of Allah until they disperse'}, up to His saying: {If we return to Madinah, the mightier will surely expel therefrom the meaner.}"

Al-Bukhari related it at this verse from Adam ibn Abi Iyas, from Shu'bah, then said, "Ibn Abi Za'idah said, from al-A'mash, from 'Amr, from Ibn Abi Layla, from Zayd, from the Prophet ﷺ." Al-Tirmidhi and al-Nasa'i also related it at this verse from the hadith of Shu'bah, with the same chain.

Another route from Zayd: Imam Ahmad, may Allah have mercy on him, said, [chain] Yahya ibn Adam and Yahya ibn Abi Bukayr said, Isra'il narrated to us, from Abu Ishaq, who said: I heard Zayd ibn Arqam, and Ibn Abi Bukayr said "from Zayd ibn Arqam," say: I went out with my uncle on a military expedition, and I heard 'Abdullah ibn Ubayy ibn Salul saying to his companions, "Do not spend on those who are with the Messenger of Allah, and if we return to Madinah, the mightier will surely expel therefrom the meaner." I mentioned that to my uncle, and my uncle mentioned it to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ sent for me, and I told him. He then sent for 'Abdullah ibn Ubayy ibn Salul and his companions, and they swore they had not said it. So the Messenger of Allah ﷺ took me to be a liar and believed them, and a grief befell me such as had never befallen me before, and I sat in the house. My uncle said, "You have brought about nothing but that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ disbelieved you and became angry with you." He said: until Allah sent down {When the hypocrites come to you}. He said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ sent for me and recited it to me, then said, "Indeed Allah has confirmed you as truthful."

Ahmad also said, [chain] Hasan ibn Musa narrated to us, Zuhayr narrated to us, Abu Ishaq narrated to us: that he heard Zayd ibn Arqam say: We went out with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ on a journey, and the people suffered hardship. 'Abdullah ibn Ubayy said to his companions, "Do not spend on those who are with the Messenger of Allah until they disperse from around him," and he said, "If we return to Madinah, the mightier will surely expel therefrom the meaner." I came to the Prophet ﷺ and informed him of that. He sent for 'Abdullah ibn Ubayy and questioned him, and he swore emphatically that he had not done it. They said, "Zayd has lied, O Messenger of Allah." What they said settled into my heart with distress, until Allah sent down confirmation of my truthfulness: {When the hypocrites come to you}. He said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ called them to seek forgiveness for them, but they turned their heads away. As for His saying, {as if they were propped-up beams}, he said: they were men of the most handsome appearance.

Al-Bukhari, Muslim, and al-Nasa'i related it from the hadith of Zuhayr. Al-Bukhari also related it, and al-Tirmidhi, from the hadith of Isra'il, both from Abu Ishaq 'Amr ibn 'Abdullah al-Sabi'i al-Hamdani al-Kufi, from Zayd, with the same content.

Another route from Zayd: Abu 'Isa al-Tirmidhi said, [chain] 'Abd ibn Humayd narrated to us, 'Ubaydullah ibn Musa narrated to us, from Isra'il, from al-Suddi, from Abu Sa'd al-Azdi, who said: Zayd ibn Arqam narrated to us, saying: We went on a military expedition with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, and there were with us some Bedouin. We used to race to the water, and the Bedouin would outrun us; a Bedouin would outrun his companions, fill the trough, place stones around it, and put a leather sheet over it until his companions arrived. A man from the Ansar came to the Bedouin and loosened the rein of his she-camel so it could drink, but the Bedouin refused to let it. So the Ansari pulled out a stone and the water overflowed. The Bedouin lifted a piece of wood and struck the Ansari's head with it, wounding it. The Ansari went to 'Abdullah ibn Ubayy, the head of the hypocrites, and informed him, for he was one of his companions. 'Abdullah ibn Ubayy grew angry and said, "Do not spend on those who are with the Messenger of Allah until they disperse from around him," meaning the Bedouin, for they used to attend the Messenger of Allah ﷺ at mealtime. 'Abdullah said to his companions, "When they disperse from around Muhammad, bring Muhammad food, so that he and those with him may eat," then he said to his companions, "When you return to Madinah, let the mightier expel from it the meaner." Zayd said: I was riding behind my uncle, and I heard 'Abdullah, so I told my uncle. He went and informed the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, who sent for him. He swore and denied it. Zayd said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ believed him and disbelieved me. My uncle came to me and said, "You have brought about nothing but that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ became angry with you and the Muslims disbelieved you." A grief befell me such as had never befallen anyone before. While I was traveling with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ on a journey, my head nodding from grief, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ came to me, twisted my ear, and smiled in my face, and nothing could have pleased me more, not even eternal life in this world. Then Abu Bakr caught up with me and said, "What did the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say to you?" I said, "The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said nothing to me, except that he twisted my ear and smiled in my face." He said, "Rejoice." Then 'Umar caught up with me and I said to him the same as I had said to Abu Bakr. When morning came, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ recited Surat al-Munafiqun.

Al-Tirmidhi alone related this, and said, "This is a hasan sahih hadith (good and authentic)." Al-Hafiz al-Bayhaqi likewise related it from al-Hakim, from Abu al-'Abbas Muhammad ibn Ahmad al-Mahbubi, from Sa'id ibn Mas'ud, from 'Ubaydullah ibn Musa, with the same content, and he added after his saying "Surat al-Munafiqun": {When the hypocrites come to you, they say, "We bear witness that you are indeed the Messenger"}, up to His saying: {It is they who say, "Do not spend on those who are with the Messenger of Allah until they disperse"}, up to His saying: {the mightier will surely expel therefrom the meaner.}

'Abdullah ibn Lahi'ah related, from Abu al-Aswad, 'Urwah ibn al-Zubayr, in the Maghazi, and Musa ibn 'Uqbah likewise mentioned this story in the same account in his Maghazi, but they made the one who reported the speech of 'Abdullah ibn Ubayy ibn Salul to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ to be Aws ibn Arqam, from Banu al-Harith ibn al-Khazraj. Perhaps he was a different reporter, or this is a corruption arising from hearing. And Allah knows best.

Ibn Abi Hatim, may Allah have mercy on him, said: [chain] Muhammad ibn 'Aziz al-Ayli narrated to us, Salamah narrated to us, 'Uqayl narrated to me, Muhammad ibn Muslim informed me, that 'Urwah ibn al-Zubayr and 'Amr ibn Thabit al-Ansari informed him: that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ waged the expedition of al-Muraysi', which is the one in which the Messenger of Allah ﷺ demolished the idol Manat, the tyrant idol which stood between Qafa al-Mushallal and the sea. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ sent Khalid ibn al-Walid, who destroyed Manat. Two men fought during that expedition of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, one of them from the Muhajirun and the other from Bahz, who were allies of the Ansar. The man from the Muhajirun overcame the Bahzi, so the Bahzi cried out, "O company of the Ansar!" and men from the Ansar came to his aid. The Muhajir cried out, "O company of the Muhajirun!" and men from the Muhajirun came to his aid, until there was some fighting between those men of the Muhajirun and those men of the Ansar. Then they were separated, and every hypocrite, or man in whose heart was disease, turned back to 'Abdullah ibn Ubayy ibn Salul and said, "You used to be hoped for and defended, but now you neither harm nor benefit. The Jalabib have banded together against us," for they used to call every recent emigrant "al-Jalabib." 'Abdullah ibn Ubayy, the enemy of Allah, said, "By Allah, if we return to Madinah, the mightier will surely expel from it the meaner." Malik ibn al-Dukhshum, who was one of the hypocrites, said, "Did I not tell you, do not spend on those who are with the Messenger of Allah until they disperse?" 'Umar ibn al-Khattab heard of this, and came walking until he reached the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, and said, "O Messenger of Allah, give me leave concerning this man who has caused corruption among the people, that I may strike his neck," meaning by this 'Abdullah ibn Ubayy. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said to 'Umar, "Would you kill him if I ordered you to kill him?" 'Umar said, "Yes, by Allah, if you ordered me to kill him I would surely strike his neck." The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "Sit down." Then Usayd ibn Hudayr came forward, one of the Ansar, from Banu 'Abd al-Ashhal, until he came to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and said, "O Messenger of Allah, give me leave concerning this man who has caused corruption among the people, that I may strike his neck." The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "Would you kill him if I ordered you to kill him?" He said, "Yes, by Allah, if you ordered me to kill him, I would strike him with the sword beneath the earrings of his ears." The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "Sit down." Then the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "Announce departure." So he traveled with the people through the heat of midday, journeying that day and night and the next day until the day grew long, then he halted.

Then he made the people march at midday journey for the same distance, and he reached Medina at dawn on the third day, having traveled from behind al-Mushallal. When the Messenger of God ﷺ arrived at Medina, he sent for Umar and summoned him, and the Messenger of God said to him: "O Umar, would you have killed him if I had ordered you to kill him?" Umar said: Yes. So the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "By God, if you had killed him that day, you would have enraged men who, if I ordered them today to kill him, would carry it out, and people would say that I had fallen upon my companions and killed them by execution." And God, Mighty and Majestic, revealed: {They are the ones who say, "Do not spend on those who are with the Messenger of God until they disperse"} up to His saying: {If we return to Medina, the most honorable among them will surely expel the most abased} the verse.

This is a gharib (singular) narration, and it contains precious details found nowhere else.

Muhammad ibn Ishaq ibn Yasar said: Asim ibn Umar ibn Qatada told me that Abdullah ibn Ubayy's son, Abdullah, meaning when news of what had happened concerning his father reached him, came to the Messenger of God ﷺ and said: O Messenger of God, it has reached me that you intend to kill Abdullah ibn Ubayy for what has reached you about him. If you intend to do so, then command me to do it, and I will bring you his head. By God, the Khazraj know well that none among them was more dutiful to his father than I. I fear that you will command someone other than me to kill him, and then my own soul will not let me see the killer of Abdullah ibn Ubayy walking among the people, so that I would kill him, and thus kill a believer for a disbeliever, and enter the Fire. The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Rather, we shall treat him gently and keep good company with him as long as he remains with us."

Ikrimah, Ibn Zayd, and others mentioned that when the people returned to Medina, this Abdullah ibn Abdullah stood at the gate of Medina, drew his sword, and the people began passing by him. When his father, Abdullah ibn Ubayy, came, his son said to him: Stay back. His father said: What is wrong with you? Woe to you! He said: By God, you shall not pass from here until the Messenger of God ﷺ gives you permission, for he is the honorable one and you are the abased one. When the Messenger of God ﷺ arrived, for he used to travel at the rear of the column, Abdullah ibn Ubayy complained to him about his son. His son Abdullah said: By God, O Messenger of God, he shall not enter until you give him permission. So the Messenger of God ﷺ gave him permission, and his son said: Now that the Messenger of God ﷺ has given you permission, pass now.

Abu Bakr Abdullah ibn al-Zubayr said in his Musnad: [chain] Sufyan ibn Uyaynah told us, Abu Harun al-Madani told us, saying: Abdullah ibn Abdullah ibn Ubayy ibn Salul said to his father: By God, you shall never enter Medina until you say, "The Messenger of God ﷺ is the most honorable and I am the most abased." He said: And he came to the Prophet ﷺ and said: O Messenger of God, it has reached me that you intend to kill my father. By Him who sent you with the truth, I have never looked closely at his face out of awe of him. If you wish that I bring you his head, I will surely bring it to you, for I hate to see the killer of my father...

وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ وَالسُّدِّيُّ: أُنْزِلَتْ هَذِهِ الْآيَةُ فِي عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ أُبَيٍّ، وَذَلِكَ أَنَّ غُلَامًا مِنْ قَرَابَتِهِ انْطَلَقَ إلى رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم فَحَدَّثَهُ بِحَدِيثٍ عَنْهُ وَأَمْرٍ شَدِيدٍ، فَدَعَاهُ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ، فَإِذَا هُوَ يَحْلِفُ بِاللَّهِ وَيَتَبَرَّأُ مِنْ ذَلِكَ، وَأَقْبَلَتِ الْأَنْصَارُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ الْغُلَامِ فَلَامُوهُ وعَذَموه وَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ فِيهِ مَا تَسْمَعُونَ، وَقِيلَ لِعَدُوِّ اللَّهِ: لَوْ أَتَيْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ؟ فَجَعَلَ يَلْوِي رَأْسَهُ، أَيْ: لَسْتُ فَاعِلًا 

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو الرَّبِيعِ الزَّهْرَانِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا حَمَّادُ بْنُ زَيْدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَيُّوبُ، عَنِ سَعِيدِ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ كَانَ إِذَا نَزَلَ مَنْزِلًا لَمْ يَرْتَحِلْ حَتَّى يُصَلِّيَ فِيهِ، فَلَمَّا كَانَتْ غَزْوَةُ تَبُوكَ بَلَغَهُ أنَّ عبدَ الله ابن أُبَيِّ بْنِ سَلُولٍ قَالَ: ﴿لَيُخْرِجَنَّ الأعَزُّ مِنْهَا الأذَلَّ﴾ فَارْتَحَلَ قَبْلَ أَنْ يَنْزِلَ آخِرَ النَّهَارِ، وَقِيلَ لِعَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ أُبَيٍّ: ائْتِ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ حَتَّى يَسْتَغْفِرَ لَكَ. فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ: ﴿إِذَا جَاءَكَ الْمُنَافِقُونَ﴾ إِلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَإِذَا قِيلَ لَهُمْ تَعَالَوْا يَسْتَغْفِرْ لَكُمْ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ لَوَّوْا رُءُوسَهُمْ﴾

وَهَذَا إِسْنَادٌ صَحِيحٌ إِلَى سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ. وَقَوْلُهُ: إِنَّ ذَلِكَ كَانَ فِي غَزْوَةِ تَبُوكَ، فِيهِ نَظَرٌ، بَلْ لَيْسَ بِجَيِّدٍ؛ فَإِنَّ عَبْدَ اللَّهِ بْنَ أُبَيِّ بْنِ سَلُولٍ لَمْ يَكُنْ مِمَّنْ خَرَجَ فِي غَزْوَةِ تَبُوكَ، بَلْ رَجَعَ بِطَائِفَةٍ مِنَ الْجَيْشِ. وَإِنَّمَا الْمَشْهُورُ عِنْدَ أَصْحَابِ الْمَغَازِي وَالسِّيَرِ أَنَّ ذَلِكَ كَانَ فِي غَزْوَةِ المُرَيسيع، وَهِيَ غَزْوَةُ بَنِي المصطلق.

قَالَ يُونُسُ بْنُ بُكَيْر، عَنِ ابْنِ إِسْحَاقَ: حَدَّثَنِي مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ يَحْيَى بْنِ حِبَّانَ، وَعَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ أَبِي بَكْرٍ، وَعَاصِمِ بْنِ عُمَر بْنِ قَتَادَةَ، فِي قِصَّةِ بَنِي الْمُصْطَلِقِ: فَبَيْنَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ مُقِيمٌ هُنَاكَ، اقْتَتَلَ عَلَى الْمَاءِ جَهجاه بْنُ سَعِيدٍ الْغِفَارِيُّ -وَكَانَ أَجِيرًا-لِعُمَرَ بْنِ الْخَطَّابِ، وَسِنَانُ بْنُ وَبْر قَالَ ابْنُ إِسْحَاقَ: فَحَدَّثَنِي مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ يَحْيَى بْنِ حِبَّانَ قَالَ: ازْدَحَمَا عَلَى الْمَاءِ فَاقْتَتَلَا فَقَالَ سِنَانٌ: يَا مَعْشَرَ الْأَنْصَارِ. وَقَالَ الْجَهْجَاهُ: يَا مَعْشَرَ الْمُهَاجِرِينَ -وَزَيْدُ بْنُ أَرْقَمَ وَنَفَرٌ مِنَ الْأَنْصَارِ عِنْدَ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ أُبَيٍّ-فَلَمَّا سَمِعَهَا قَالَ: قَدْ ثاورُونا فِي بِلَادِنَا. وَاللَّهِ مَا مثلُنا وَجَلَابِيبُ قُرَيْشٍ هَذِهِ إِلَّا كَمَا قَالَ الْقَائِلُ: "سَمن كَلْبَكَ يَأْكُلْكَ". وَاللَّهِ لَئِنْ رَجَعْنَا إِلَى الْمَدِينَةِ لَيُخْرِجَنَّ الْأَعَزُّ مِنْهَا الْأَذَلَّ. ثُمَّ أَقْبَلُ عَلَى مَنْ عِنْدَهُ مِنْ قَوْمِهِ وَقَالَ: هَذَا مَا صَنَعْتُمْ بِأَنْفُسِكُمْ، أَحْلَلْتُمُوهُمْ بِلَادَكُمْ، وَقَاسَمْتُمُوهُمْ أَمْوَالَكُمْ، أَمَا وَاللَّهِ لَوْ كَفَفْتُمْ عَنْهُمْ لَتَحَوَّلُوا عَنْكُمْ فِي بِلَادِكُمْ إِلَى غَيْرِهَا. فَسَمِعَهَا زَيْدُ ابن أَرْقَمَ، فَذَهَبَ بِهَا إِلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ وَهُوَ غُلَيّمٌ -وَعِنْدَهُ عُمَرُ بْنُ الْخَطَّابِ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ-فَأَخْبَرَهُ الْخَبَرَ، فَقَالَ عُمَرُ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ مُرْ عَبّاد بْنَ بشرْ فَلْيَضْرِبْ عُنُقَهُ. فَقَالَ ﷺ: "فَكَيْفَ إِذَا تَحَدَّثَ النَّاسُ -يَا عُمَرُ-أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا يَقْتُلُ أَصْحَابَهُ؟ لَا وَلَكِنْ نَادِ يَا عُمَرُ فِي الرَّحِيلِ".

فَلَمَّا بَلَغَ عَبْدَ اللَّهِ بْنَ أُبَيٍّ أَنَّ ذَلِكَ قَدْ بَلَغَ رسولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ، أَتَاهُ فَاعْتَذَرَ إِلَيْهِ، وَحَلَفَ بِاللَّهِ مَا قَالَ مَا قَالَ عَلَيْهِ زَيْدُ بْنُ أَرْقَمَ -وَكَانَ عِنْدَ قَوْمِهِ بِمَكَانٍ-فَقَالُوا: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، عَسَى أَنْ يَكُونَ هَذَا الْغُلَامُ أَوْهَمَ وَلَمْ يُثْبِتْ مَا قَالَ الرَّجُلُ.

وَرَاحَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ مُهجرًا فِي سَاعَةٍ كَانَ لَا يَرُوحُ فِيهَا، فَلَقِيَهُ أُسَيْدُ بْنُ الْحُضَيْرِ فَسَلَّمَ عَلَيْهِ بِتَحِيَّةِ النُّبُوَّةِ، ثُمَّ قَالَ: وَاللَّهِ لَقَدْ رُحتَ فِي سَاعَةٍ مُنكَرَة مَا كُنْتَ تَرُوحُ فِيهَا. فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "أما بَلَغَكَ مَا قَالَ صَاحِبُكَ ابْنُ أُبَيٍّ؟. زَعَمَ أَنَّهُ إِذَا قَدِمَ الْمَدِينَةَ سَيُخْرِجُ الْأَعَزُّ مِنْهَا الْأَذَلَّ". قَالَ: فَأَنْتَ -يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ-العزيزُ وَهُوَ الذَّلِيلُ. ثُمَّ قَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ارْفُقْ بِهِ فَوَاللَّهِ لَقَدْ جَاءَ اللَّهُ بِكَ وَإِنَّا لَنَنْظِمُ لَهُ الخَرزَ لِنُتَوّجه، فَإِنَّهُ لَيَرَى أَنْ قَدِ استلبتَه مُلْكًا.

فَسَارَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ بِالنَّاسِ حَتَّى أَمْسَوْا، لَيْلَتَهُ حَتَّى أَصْبَحُوا، وصَدرَ يَوْمَهُ حَتَّى اشْتَدَّ الضُّحَى. ثُمَّ نَزَلَ بِالنَّاسِ لِيَشْغَلَهُمْ عَمَّا كَانَ مِنَ الْحَدِيثِ، فَلَمْ يَأْمَنِ النَّاسُ أَنْ وَجَدُوا مَس الْأَرْضِ فَنَامُوا، وَنَزَلَتْ سُورَةُ الْمُنَافِقِينَ 

وَقَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو بَكْرٍ الْبَيْهَقِيُّ: أَخْبَرَنَا أَبُو عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الْحَافِظُ، أَخْبَرَنَا أَبُو بَكْرِ بْنُ إِسْحَاقَ، أَخْبَرَنَا بِشْرُ بْنُ مُوسَى، حَدَّثَنَا الحُميدي، حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، حَدَّثَنَا عَمْرُو بْنُ دِينَارٍ، سَمِعْتُ جَابِرَ بْنَ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ يَقُولُ: كُنَّا مَعَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فِي غَزَاة فكَسَعَ رجلٌ مِنَ الْمُهَاجِرِينَ رَجُلًا مِنَ الْأَنْصَارِ، فَقَالَ الْأَنْصَارِيُّ: يَالَلْأَنْصَارِ. وَقَالَ الْمُهَاجِرِيُّ: يَا لَلْمُهَاجِرِينَ. فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "مَا بَالُ دَعْوَى الْجَاهِلِيَّةِ؟ دَعُوهَا فَإِنَّهَا مُنْتِنَةٌ". وَقَالَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ أُبَيِّ بْنِ سَلُولَ -وَقَدْ فعلوها-: والله لئن رجعنا إلى المدينة ليخرجن الأعز منها الأذل. قال جَابِرٌ: وَكَانَ الْأَنْصَارُ بِالْمَدِينَةِ أَكْثَرَ مِنَ الْمُهَاجِرِينَ حِينَ قَدِمَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ ثُمَّ كَثُرَ الْمُهَاجِرُونَ بَعْدَ ذَلِكَ، فَقَالَ عُمَرُ: دَعْنِي أَضْرِبْ عُنُقَ هَذَا الْمُنَافِقِ. فَقَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: "دَعْهُ؛ لَا يَتَحَدَّثُ النَّاسُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا يَقْتُلُ أَصْحَابَهُ" 

وَرَوَاهُ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ عَنْ حُسَيْنِ بْنِ مُحَمَّدٍ الْمَرْوَزِيِّ، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ بْنِ عُيَيْنَةَ وَرَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ عَنِ الْحُمَيْدِيِّ، وَمُسْلِمٌ عَنْ أَبِي بَكْرِ بْنِ أَبِي شَيْبَةَ وَغَيْرِهِ، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ، بِهِ نَحْوَهُ 

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ جَعْفَرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا شُعْبَةُ، عَنِ الْحَكَمِ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ كَعْبٍ الْقُرَظِيِّ، عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَرْقَمَ قَالَ: كُنْتُ مَعَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فِي غَزْوَةِ تَبُوكَ، فَقَالَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ أُبَيٍّ: لَئِنْ رَجَعْنَا إِلَى الْمَدِينَةِ لَيُخْرِجَنَّ الْأَعَزُّ مِنْهَا الْأَذَلَّ. قَالَ: فَأَتَيْتُ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ فَأَخْبَرْتُهُ، قَالَ: فَحَلَفَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ أُبَيٍّ أَنَّهُ لَمْ يَكُنْ شَيْءٌ مِنْ ذَلِكَ. قَالَ: فَلَامَنِي قَوْمِي وَقَالُوا: مَا أردتَ إِلَى هَذَا؟ قَالَ: فَانْطَلَقْتُ فنمتُ كَئِيبًا حَزينا، قَالَ: فَأَرْسَلَ إِلَى نَبِيَّ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالَ: "إِنَّ اللَّهَ قَدْ أَنْزَلَ عُذركَ وصَدَّقك". قَالَ: فَنَزَلَتْ هَذِهِ الْآيَةُ ﴿هُمُ الَّذِينَ يَقُولُونَ لَا تُنْفِقُوا عَلَى مَنْ عِنْدَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ حَتَّى يَنْفَضُّوا﴾ حَتَّى بَلَغَ: ﴿لَئِنْ رَجَعْنَا إِلَى الْمَدِينَةِ لَيُخْرِجَنَّ الأعَزُّ مِنْهَا الأذَلَّ﴾

وَرَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ عِنْدَ هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ، عَنْ آدَمَ بْنِ أَبِي إِيَاسٍ، عَنْ شُعْبَةَ ثُمَّ قَالَ: "وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي زَائِدَةَ، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ عَمْرٍو، عَنِ ابْنِ أَبِي لَيْلَى، عَنْ زَيْدٍ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ وَرَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ وَالنَّسَائِيُّ عِنْدَهَا أَيْضًا مِنْ حَدِيثِ شُعْبَةَ، بِهِ 

طَرِيقٌ أُخْرَى عَنْ زَيْدٍ: قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ، رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ، حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى بْنُ آدَمَ، وَيَحْيَى بْنُ أَبِي بُكَير قَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا إِسْرَائِيلُ، عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ زَيْدَ بْنَ أَرْقَمَ -وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي بُكَير عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَرْقَمَ-قَالَ: خَرَجْتُ مَعَ عَمِّي فِي غَزَاةٍ، فَسَمِعْتُ عَبْدَ اللَّهِ بْنَ أُبَيِّ بْنِ سَلُولٍ يَقُولُ لِأَصْحَابِهِ: لَا تُنْفِقُوا عَلَى مَنْ عِنْدِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ، وَلَئِنْ رَجَعْنَا إِلَى الْمَدِينَةِ لَيُخْرِجَنَّ الْأَعَزُّ مِنْهَا الْأَذَلَّ. فَذَكَرْتُ ذَلِكَ لِعَمِّي فَذَكَرَهُ عَمِّي لِرَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَأَرْسَلَ إِلَيَّ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَحَدَّثْتُهُ فَأَرْسَلَ إِلَى عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ أُبَيِّ بْنِ سَلُولٍ وَأَصْحَابِهِ فَحَلَفُوا مَا قَالُوا: فكَذبني رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ وصَدَّقه، فَأَصَابَنِي هَمٌ لَمْ يُصِبْنِي مِثْلُهُ قَطُّ، وَجَلَسْتُ فِي الْبَيْتِ، فَقَالَ عَمِّي: مَا أَرَدْتَ إِلَّا أَنْ كَذَّبَكَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ وَمَقَتَكَ. قَالَ: حَتَّى أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ: ﴿إِذَا جَاءَكَ الْمُنَافِقُونَ﴾ قَالَ: فَبَعَثَ إليَّ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَرَأَهَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ عَلَيَّ، ثُمَّ قَالَ: "إِنَّ اللَّهَ قَدْ صَدَّقَكَ" 

ثُمَّ قَالَ أَحْمَدُ أَيْضًا: حَدَّثَنَا حَسَنُ بْنُ مُوسَى، حَدَّثَنَا زُهَيْرُ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو إسحاق: أنه سمع زيد ابن أَرْقَمَ يَقُولُ: خَرَجْنَا مَعَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فِي سَفَرٍ، فَأَصَابَ النَّاسَ شدةٌ، فَقَالَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ أُبَيٍّ لِأَصْحَابِهِ: لا تنفقوا على من عند رسول الله حَتَّى يَنْفَضُّوا مِنْ حَوْلِهِ. وَقَالَ: لَئِنْ رَجَعْنَا إِلَى الْمَدِينَةِ لَيُخْرِجَنَّ الْأَعَزُّ مِنْهَا الْأَذَلَّ. فَأَتَيْتُ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ فَأَخْبَرْتُهُ بِذَلِكَ، فَأَرْسَلَ إِلَى عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ أُبَيٍّ فَسَأَلَهُ، فَاجْتَهَدَ يمينَه مَا فَعَلَ. فَقَالُوا: كَذَبَ زَيْدٌ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ. فَوَقَعَ فِي نَفْسِي مَا قَالُوا، حَتَّى أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ تَصْدِيقِي: ﴿إِذَا جَاءَكَ الْمُنَافِقُونَ﴾ قَالَ: وَدَعَاهُمْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ لِيَسْتَغْفِرَ لهم، فلووا رؤوسهم. وَقَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿كَأَنَّهُمْ خُشُبٌ مُسَنَّدَةٌ﴾ قَالَ: كَانُوا رِجَالًا أَجْمَلَ شَيْءٍ.

وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ وَمُسْلِمٌ وَالنَّسَائِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ زُهَيْرٍ وَرَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ أَيْضًا وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ إِسْرَائِيلَ، كِلَاهُمَا عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ عَمْرِو بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ السَّبيعيّ الْهَمْدَانِيِّ الْكُوفِيِّ، عَنْ زَيْدٍ، بِهِ .

طَرِيقٌ أُخْرَى عَنْ زَيْدٍ: قَالَ أَبُو عِيسَى التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ بْنُ حُمَيد، حَدَّثَنَا عُبَيْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ مُوسَى، عَنْ إِسْرَائِيلَ، عَنِ السُّدِّيِّ، عَنْ أَبِي سَعْدٍ الْأَزْدِيِّ قَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا زَيْدُ بْنُ أَرْقَمَ قَالَ: غَزَوْنَا مَعَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ وَكَانَ مَعَنَا أُنَاسٌ مِنَ الْأَعْرَابِ، فَكُنَّا نَبتَدرُ الْمَاءَ، وَكَانَ الْأَعْرَابُ يَسْبِقُونَنَا يَسْبِقُ الْأَعْرَابِيُّ أَصْحَابَهُ يَمْلَأُ الْحَوْضَ، وَيَجْعَلُ حَوْلَهُ حِجَارَةً، وَيَجْعَلُ النَّطْعَ عَلَيْهِ حَتَّى يَجِيءَ أَصْحَابُهُ. قَالَ: فَأَتَى رَجُلٌ مِنَ الْأَنْصَارِ الْأَعْرَابِيَّ، فَأَرْخَى زِمَامَ نَاقَتِهِ لِتَشْرَبَ، فَأَبَى أَنْ يَدَعَهُ، فَانْتَزَعَ حَجَرًا فَفَاضَ الْمَاءُ، فَرَفَعَ الْأَعْرَابِيُّ خَشَبَةً، فَضَرَبَ بِهَا رَأْسَ الْأَنْصَارِيِّ فَشَجَّهُ، فَأَتَى عبدَ اللَّهِ بْنُ أُبَيٍّ رأسَ الْمُنَافِقِينَ فَأَخْبَرَهُ -وَكَانَ مِنْ أَصْحَابِهِ-فَغَضِبَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ أُبَيٍّ، ثُمَّ قَالَ: لَا تُنْفِقُوا عَلَى مَنْ عِنْدَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ حَتَّى يَنْفَضُّوا مِنْ حَوْلِهِ -يَعْنِي الْأَعْرَابَ-وَكَانُوا يَحْضُرُونَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ عِنْدَ الطَّعَامِ. فَقَالَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ لِأَصْحَابِهِ: إِذَا انْفَضُّوا مِنْ عِنْدِ مُحَمَّدٍ فَائْتُوا مُحَمَّدًا بِالطَّعَامِ، فَلْيَأْكُلْ هُوَ وَمَنْ عِنْدَهُ، ثُمَّ قَالَ لِأَصْحَابِهِ: إِذَا رَجَعْتُمْ إِلَى الْمَدِينَةِ فَلْيُخْرِجِ الْأَعَزُّ مِنْهَا الْأَذَلَّ. قَالَ زَيْدٌ: وَأَنَا رِدْفُ عَمِّي، فَسَمِعْتُ عَبْدَ اللَّهِ فَأَخْبَرْتُ عَمِّي، فَانْطَلَقَ فَأَخْبَرَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ، فَأَرْسَلَ إِلَيْهِ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ، فَحَلَفَ وَجَحَدَ، قَالَ: فَصَدَّقَهُ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ وَكَذَّبَنِي، فَجَاءَ إِلَيَّ عَمِّي فَقَالَ: مَا أَرَدْتَ إِلَّا أَنَّ مَقَتَكَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ وكذبكَ الْمُسْلِمُونَ. فَوَقَعَ عَلَيَّ مِنَ الْغَمِّ مَا لَمْ يَقَعْ عَلَى أَحَدٍ قَطُّ، فَبَيْنَمَا أَنَا أَسِيرُ مَعَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فِي سَفَرٍ وَقَدْ خَفَقْتُ بِرَأْسِي مِنَ الْهَمِّ، إِذْ أَتَانِي رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَعَرَك أُذُنِي، وَضَحِكَ فِي وَجْهِي، فَمَا كَانَ يَسُرُّنِي أَنَّ لِي بِهَا الْخُلْدَ فِي الدُّنْيَا، ثُمَّ إِنَّ أَبَا بَكْرٍ لَحِقَنِي وَقَالَ: مَا قَالَ لَكَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ قُلْتُ: مَا قَالَ لِي رَسُولُ اللَّهِ شَيْئًا، غَيْرَ أَنْ عَرَكَ أُذُنِي وَضَحِكَ فِي وَجْهِي. فَقَالَ: أَبْشِرْ. ثُمَّ لَحِقَنِي عُمَرُ فَقُلْتُ لَهُ مِثْلَ قَوْلِي لِأَبِي بَكْرٍ. فَلَمَّا أَنْ أَصْبَحْنَا قَرَأَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ سُورَةَ الْمُنَافِقِينَ.

انْفَرَدَ بِإِخْرَاجِهِ التِّرْمِذِيُّ وَقَالَ: هَذَا حَدِيثٌ حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ. وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ الحافظ البيهقي عن الْحَاكِمِ عَنْ أَبِي الْعَبَّاسِ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ أَحْمَدَ الْمَحْبُوبِيِّ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ مَسْعُودٍ، عَنْ عُبَيْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ مُوسَى، بِهِ وَزَادَ بَعْدَ قَوْلِهِ "سُورَةُ الْمُنَافِقِينَ" ﴿إِذَا جَاءَكَ الْمُنَافِقُونَ قَالُوا نَشْهَدُ إِنَّكَ لَرَسُولُ﴾ حَتَّى بَلَغَ: ﴿هُمُ الَّذِينَ يَقُولُونَ لا تُنْفِقُوا عَلَى مَنْ عِنْدَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ حَتَّى يَنْفَضُّوا﴾ حَتَّى بَلَغَ: ﴿لَيُخْرِجَنَّ الأعَزُّ مِنْهَا الأذَلَّ﴾

وَقَدْ رَوَى عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ لَهِيعَةَ، عَنْ أَبِي الْأُسُودِ، عُروَةَ بْنِ الزُّبَيْرِ فِي الْمَغَازِي -وَكَذَا ذَكَرَ مُوسَى بْنُ عُقْبَةَ فِي مَغَازِيهِ أَيْضًا هَذِهِ الْقِصَّةَ بِهَذَا السِّيَاقِ، وَلَكِنْ جَعَلَا الَّذِي بَلّغ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ كَلَامُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ أُبَيِّ بْنِ سَلُولٍ إِنَّمَا هُوَ أَوْسُ بْنُ أَرْقَمَ، مِنْ بَنِي الْحَارِثِ بْنِ الْخَزْرَجِ. فَلَعَلَّهُ مُبَلِّغٌ آخَرُ، أَوْ تَصْحِيفٌ مِنْ جِهَةِ السَّمْعِ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

وَقَدْ قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عَزِيزٍ الْأَيْلِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا سَلَامَةُ، حَدَّثَنِي عُقَيْلٌ، أَخْبَرَنِي مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ مُسْلِمٍ، أَنَّ عُرْوَةَ بْنَ الزُّبَيْرِ وَعَمْرَو بْنَ ثَابِتٍ الْأَنْصَارِيَّ أَخْبَرَاهُ: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ غَزَا غَزْوَةَ الْمُرَيْسِيعِ، وَهِيَ الَّتِي هَدَمَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فِيهَا مَنَاةَ الطَّاغِيَةَ الَّتِي كَانَتْ بَيْنَ قَفَا المُشَلّل وَبَيْنَ الْبَحْرِ، فَبَعَثَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ خَالِدَ بْنَ الْوَلِيدِ فَكَسَرَ مَنَاةَ، فَاقْتَتَلَ رَجُلَانِ فِي غَزْوَةِ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ تِلْكَ، أَحَدُهُمَا مِنَ الْمُهَاجِرِينَ، وَالْآخَرُ مَنْ بَهْز، وَهُمْ حُلَفَاءُ الْأَنْصَارِ، فَاسْتَعْلَى الرَّجُلُ الَّذِي مِنَ الْمُهَاجِرِينَ عَلَى الْبَهْزِيِّ، فَقَالَ الْبَهْزِيُّ: يَا مَعْشَرَ الْأَنْصَارِ، عَدُوُّ اللَّهِ: [وَاللَّهِ] لَئِنْ رَجَعْنَا إِلَى الْمَدِينَةِ لَيُخْرِجَنَّ الْأَعَزُّ مِنْهَا الْأَذَلَّ. قَالَ مَالِكُ بْنُ الدخْشُم -وَكَانَ مِنَ الْمُنَافِقِينَ-: أَوَلَمْ أَقُلْ لَكُمْ لَا تُنْفِقُوا عَلَى مَنْ عِنْدَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ حَتَّى يَنْفَضُّوا. فَسَمِعَ بِذَلِكَ عمرُ بْنُ الْخَطَّابِ، فَأَقْبَلَ يَمْشِي حَتَّى جَاءَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، ائْذَنْ لِي فَصبح بِالْمَدِينَةِ فِي ثَلَاثٍ سَارَهَا مِنْ قَفَا المُشلَّل فَلَمَّا قَدِمَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ الْمَدِينَةَ أَرْسَلَ إِلَى عُمَرَ فَدَعَاهُ، فَقَالَ لَهُ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ: "أَيْ ْعمر، أَكُنْتَ اللَّهِ حَتَّى يَنْفَضُّوا﴾ إِلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿لَئِنْ رَجَعْنَا إلَى الْمَدِينَةِ [لَيُخْرِجَنَّ الأعَزُّ مِنْهَا الأذَل] ﴾ الْآيَةَ.

## 63:9-11 — verses 9-11 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, commanding His believing servants to remember Him abundantly, and forbidding them from letting wealth and children distract them from that, and informing them that whoever becomes absorbed in the enjoyment of worldly life and its adornment, away from what he was created for, namely obedience to his Lord and remembrance of Him, then he is among the losers, those who lose their own souls and their families on the Day of Resurrection. Then He urged them to spend in obedience to Him, saying: {And spend from what We have provided you before death comes to one of you and he says, "My Lord, if only You would delay me for a short term, I would give charity and be among the righteous."} So every negligent person regrets at the moment of death, and asks for a longer span of time, even if only a little, seeking to make amends and to make up for what he has missed. But it is too late. What was, was, and what is coming will come, each according to the degree of his negligence. As for the disbelievers, it is as the Exalted [Allah] said: {And warn the people of a Day when the punishment will come to them, and those who did wrong will say, "Our Lord, delay us for a short term; we will answer Your call and follow the messengers." Had you not sworn before that there would be no end for you?} [Ibrahim: 44]. And the Exalted said: {Until, when death comes to one of them, he says, "My Lord, send me back, that I may do righteous deeds in that which I left behind." No! It is only a word he speaks, and behind them is a barrier until the Day they are resurrected.} [al-Mu'minun: 99-100]

Then the Exalted said: {And Allah will never delay a soul when its term has come, and Allah is well acquainted with what you do.} That is, He does not grant respite to anyone after his appointed term has arrived, and He is most knowing and most aware of who would be truthful in his statement and his request, as opposed to one who, if returned, would go back to something worse than what he was upon. This is why He said: {and Allah is well acquainted with what you do.}

Abu Isa al-Tirmidhi said: [chain] Ibn Abbas said: whoever has wealth sufficient to perform the pilgrimage to the House of his Lord, or upon whom zakat becomes obligatory because of it, and he does not do so, will ask to be sent back at the time of death. A man said: "O Ibn Abbas, fear Allah, for it is only the disbelievers who ask to be sent back." Ibn Abbas said: "I will recite to you a Qur'an concerning that: {O you who believe, let not your wealth and your children divert you from the remembrance of Allah. And whoever does that, then those are the losers. And spend from what We have provided you before death comes to one of you and he says, "My Lord, if only You would delay me for a short term, I would give charity [and be among the righteous. And Allah will never delay a soul when its term has come], and Allah is well acquainted with what you do."} The man said: "What makes zakat obligatory?" He said: "When wealth reaches two hundred [dirhams] or more." He said: "What makes the pilgrimage obligatory?" He said: "Provisions and a mount."

Then he said: [chain] from Ibn Abbas, from the Prophet ﷺ, with similar wording.

Then he said: Sufyan ibn Uyaynah and others narrated it from Abu Janab, from Ibn al-Dahhak, from Ibn Abbas, as his own statement, and this is more correct. And he weakened Abu Janab al-Kalbi.

I say: the narration of al-Dahhak from Ibn Abbas contains a break in the chain, and Allah knows best.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Abu al-Darda, may Allah be pleased with him, said: we mentioned in the presence of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ the matter of increase in lifespan, and he said: "Indeed Allah does not delay a soul when its term has come. Rather, the increase in lifespan is that Allah grants the servant righteous offspring who pray for him, so that their prayer reaches him in his grave."

The end of the commentary on Surat al-Munafiqun. To Allah belongs all praise and grace.

يَقُولُ تَعَالَى آمِرًا لِعِبَادِهِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ بِكَثْرَةِ ذِكْرِهِ وَنَاهِيًا لَهُمْ عَنْ أَنْ تَشْغَلَهُمُ الْأَمْوَالُ وَالْأَوْلَادُ عَنْ ذَلِكَ وَمُخْبِرًا لَهُمْ بِأَنَّهُ مَنِ التَهَى بِمَتَاعِ الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا وَزِينَتِهَا عَمَّا خُلِقَ لَهُ مِنْ طَاعَةِ رَبِّهِ وَذِكْرِهِ، فَإِنَّهُ مِنَ الْخَاسِرِينَ الَّذِينَ يَخْسَرُونَ أَنْفُسَهُمْ وَأَهْلِيهِمْ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ، ثُمَّ حَثَّهُمْ عَلَى الْإِنْفَاقِ فِي طَاعَتِهِ فَقَالَ: ﴿وَأَنْفِقُوا مِنْ مَا رَزَقْنَاكُمْ مِنْ قَبْلِ أَنْ يَأْتِيَ أَحَدَكُمُ الْمَوْتُ فَيَقُولَ رَبِّ لَوْلا أَخَّرْتَنِي إِلَى أَجَلٍ قَرِيبٍ فَأَصَّدَّقَ وَأَكُنْ مِنَ الصَّالِحِينَ﴾ فَكُلُّ مُفَرِّط يَنْدَمُ عِنْدَ الِاحْتِضَارِ، وَيَسْأَلُ طُولَ الْمُدَّةِ وَلَوْ شَيْئًا يَسِيرًا، يَسْتَعْتِبُ وَيَسْتَدْرِكُ مَا فَاتَهُ، وَهَيْهَاتَ! كَانَ مَا كَانَ، وَأَتَى مَا هُوَ آتٍ، وَكُلٌّ بِحَسَبِ تَفْرِيطِهِ، أَمَّا الْكُفَّارُ فَكَمَا قَالَ [اللَّهُ] تَعَالَى: ﴿وَأَنْذِرِ النَّاسَ يَوْمَ يَأْتِيهِمُ الْعَذَابُ فَيَقُولُ الَّذِينَ ظَلَمُوا رَبَّنَا أَخِّرْنَا إِلَى أَجَلٍ قَرِيبٍ نُجِبْ دَعْوَتَكَ وَنَتَّبِعِ الرُّسُلَ أَوَلَمْ تَكُونُوا أَقْسَمْتُمْ مِنْ قَبْلُ مَا لَكُمْ مِنْ زَوَالٍ﴾ [إِبْرَاهِيمَ: ٤٤] وَقَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿حَتَّى إِذَا جَاءَ أَحَدَهُمُ الْمَوْتُ قَالَ رَبِّ ارْجِعُونِ لَعَلِّي أَعْمَلُ صَالِحًا فِيمَا تَرَكْتُ كَلا إِنَّهَا كَلِمَةٌ هُوَ قَائِلُهَا وَمِنْ وَرَائِهِمْ بَرْزَخٌ إِلَى يَوْمِ يُبْعَثُونَ﴾ [الْمُؤْمِنُونَ: ٩٩، ١٠٠]

ثُمَّ قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَلَنْ يُؤَخِّرَ اللَّهُ نَفْسًا إِذَا جَاءَ أَجَلُهَا وَاللَّهُ خَبِيرٌ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ﴾ أَيْ: لَا يَنْظُرُ أَحَدًا بَعْدَ حُلُولِ أَجْلِهِ، وَهُوَ أَعْلَمُ وَأَخْبَرُ بِمَنْ يَكُونُ صَادِقًا فِي قَوْلِهِ وَسُؤَالِهِ مِمَّنْ لَوْ رُدّ لَعَادَ إِلَى شَرٍّ مِمَّا كَانَ عَلَيْهِ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿وَاللَّهُ خَبِيرٌ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ﴾ وَقَالَ أَبُو عِيسَى التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ بْنُ حُمَيْدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا جَعْفَرُ بْنُ عَوْنٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو جَنَاب الْكَلْبِيَّ، عَنِ الضَّحَّاكِ بْنِ مُزاحم، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: مَنْ كَانَ لَهُ مَالٌ يُبَلِّغُهُ حَجَّ بَيْتِ رَبِّهِ، أَوْ تَجِبُ عَلَيْهِ فِيهِ زكاةٌ، فَلَمْ يَفْعَلْ، سَأَلَ الرَّجْعَةَ عِنْدَ الْمَوْتِ. فَقَالَ رَجُلٌ: يَا ابْنَ عَبَّاسٍ، اتَّقِ اللَّهَ، فَإِنَّمَا يَسْأَلُ الرجعة الكفار. فقال سأتلوا عَلَيْكَ بِذَلِكَ قُرْآنًا: ﴿يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَا تُلْهِكُمْ أَمْوَالُكُمْ وَلا أَوْلادُكُمْ عَنْ ذِكْرِ اللَّهِ وَمَنْ يَفْعَلْ ذَلِكَ فَأُولَئِكَ هُمُ الْخَاسِرُونَ وَأَنْفِقُوا مِنْ مَا رَزَقْنَاكُمْ مِنْ قَبْلِ أَنْ يَأْتِيَ أَحَدَكُمُ الْمَوْتُ فَيَقُولَ رَبِّ لَوْلا أَخَّرْتَنِي إِلَى أَجَلٍ قَرِيبٍ فَأَصَّدَّقَ [وَأَكُنْ مِنَ الصَّالِحِينَ وَلَنْ يُؤَخِّرَ اللَّهُ نَفْسًا إِذَا جَاءَ أَجَلُهَا] وَاللَّهُ خَبِيرٌ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ﴾ قَالَ: فَمَا يُوجِبُ الزَّكَاةَ؟ قَالَ: إِذَا بَلَغَ الْمَالُ مِائَتَيْنِ فَصَاعِدًا. قَالَ: فَمَا يُوجِبُ الْحَجَّ؟ قَالَ: الزَّادُ وَالْبَعِيرُ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ بْنُ حُميَد، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، عَنِ الثَّوْرِيِّ، عَنْ يَحْيَى بْنِ أَبِي حَيَّة -وَهُوَ أَبُو جَنَابٍ الْكَلْبِيُّ-عَنِ الضَّحَّاكِ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ، بِنَحْوِهِ 

ثُمَّ قَالَ: وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ سُفْيَانُ بْنُ عُيَيْنَةَ وَغَيْرُهُ، عَنْ أَبِي جَنَاب، عَنِ ابْنِ الضَّحَّاكِ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، مِنْ قَوْلِهِ. وَهُوَ أَصَحُّ، وضعَّف أَبَا جَنَابٍ الكلبي.

قُلْتُ: رِوَايَةُ الضَّحَّاكِ عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ فِيهَا انْقِطَاعٌ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ نُفَيل، حَدَّثَنَا سُلَيْمَانُ بْنُ عَطَاءٍ، عَنْ مَسْلَمَةَ الْجُهَنِيِّ، عَنْ عَمِّهِ -يَعْنِي أَبَا مَشْجَعَةَ بْنِ رِبْعِي-عَنْ أَبِي الدَّرْدَاءِ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، قَالَ: ذَكَرْنَا عِنْدَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ الزِّيَادَةَ فِي الْعُمْرِ فَقَالَ: " إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يُؤَخِّرُ نَفْسًا إِذَا جَاءَ أَجْلُهَا، وَإِنَّمَا الزِّيَادَةُ فِي الْعُمْرِ أَنْ يَرْزُقَ اللَّهُ العبدَ ذُرية صَالِحَةً يَدْعُونَ لَهُ، فَيَلْحَقُهُ دُعَاؤُهُمْ فِي قَبْرِهِ" 

آخِرُ تفسير سورة "المنافقون" ولله الحمد والمنة
