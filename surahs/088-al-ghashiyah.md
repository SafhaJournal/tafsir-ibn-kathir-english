## 88:1-7 — verses 1-7 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Tafsir of Surat al-Ghashiyah

And it is Meccan.

It has already preceded, on the authority of al-Nu'man ibn Bashir, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to recite {Glorify the name of your Lord, the Most High} and al-Ghashiyah in the prayer of the two Eids and on the day of Jumu'ah (Friday).

Imam Malik said, on the authority of Damrah ibn Sa'id, from 'Ubaydullah ibn 'Abdullah: that al-Dahhak ibn Qays asked al-Nu'man ibn Bashir: "With what did the Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to recite in Jumu'ah along with Surat al-Jumu'ah?" He said: "{Has there come to you the report of the Overwhelming Event}."

Abu Dawud narrated it from al-Qa'nabi, and al-Nasa'i from Qutaybah, both of them from Malik, with it. And Muslim and Ibn Majah narrated it from the hadith of Sufyan ibn 'Uyaynah, from Damrah ibn Sa'id, with it.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful

* *

Al-Ghashiyah (the Overwhelming Event, an enveloping calamity) is one of the names of the Day of Resurrection. Ibn 'Abbas, Qatadah, and Ibn Zayd said this, because it overwhelms the people and encompasses them all. Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] ... from 'Amr ibn Maymun, who said: The Prophet ﷺ passed by a woman reciting {Has there come to you the report of the Overwhelming Event?}, so he stood listening and said: "Yes, it has come to me."

* *

His saying, {Faces, that Day, will be humbled}, meaning: abased. Qatadah said this. Ibn 'Abbas said: they will be humbled and their deeds will not benefit them.

* *

His saying, {Working, exhausted}, meaning: it had done much work, and had toiled in it, and on the Day of Resurrection it will burn in a scorching fire.

Al-Hafiz Abu Bakr al-Barqani said: [chain] ... Abu 'Imran al-Jawni said: 'Umar ibn al-Khattab, may Allah be pleased with him, passed by a monk's cell. He said: so he called out to him, "O monk! O monk!" and he looked out. He said: 'Umar began to look at him and weep. So it was said to him: "O Commander of the Believers, what makes you weep at this?" He said: "I remembered the saying of Allah, Mighty and Majestic, in His Book: {Working, exhausted, it will burn in a scorching fire}, and that is what made me weep."

Al-Bukhari said: Ibn 'Abbas said: {Working, exhausted} refers to the Christians.

And from 'Ikrimah and al-Suddi: {Working} in this world through acts of disobedience, {exhausted} in the Fire through torment and shackles.

Ibn 'Abbas, al-Hasan, and Qatadah said: {It will burn in a scorching fire}, meaning: hot, intensely hot. {It will be given to drink from a boiling spring}, meaning: its heat and boiling have reached their utmost. Ibn 'Abbas, Mujahid, al-Hasan, and al-Suddi said this.

* *

His saying, {They will have no food except from dari' (a bitter, thorny plant)}, 'Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, on the authority of Ibn 'Abbas: it is a tree from the Fire.

Sa'id ibn Jubayr said: it is al-Zaqqum (the bitter tree of Hellfire). And from him also: that it is stones.

Ibn 'Abbas, Mujahid, 'Ikrimah, Abu al-Jawza', and Qatadah said: it is al-shibriq (a bitter desert plant). Qatadah said: the Quraysh call it al-shibriq in the spring, and al-dari' in the summer. 'Ikrimah said: it is a thorny plant that lies flat upon the ground.

Al-Bukhari said: Mujahid said: al-dari' is a plant called al-shibriq. The people of the Hijaz call it al-dari' when it dries up, and it is poison.

Ma'mar said, on the authority of Qatadah: {except from dari'}, it is al-shibriq; when it dries up it is called al-dari'.

Sa'id said, on the authority of Qatadah: {They will have no food except from dari'}, it is among the worst of foods, the most repulsive of it and the vilest of it.

* *

His saying, {It neither nourishes nor avails against hunger}, meaning: no purpose is achieved by it, and no harm is averted by it.

## 88:8-16 — verses 8-16 (shared commentary)

### Translation

When He mentioned the condition of the wretched, He followed it with mention of the fortunate ones, so He said: {Faces, that Day} that is, the Day of Resurrection, {will be radiant} that is, the bliss will be recognized upon them. This was attained by them only through their striving.

Sufyan said: {with its striving well pleased} it has become pleased with its deed.

* *

His saying: {in an elevated Garden} that is, lofty and resplendent, secure within its chambers, {you will not hear therein vain talk} that is, no idle word will be heard in the Garden in which they dwell. As He said: {They will not hear therein vain talk, only "peace"} [Maryam: 62], and He said: {No vain talk therein, nor sin} [al-Tur: 23], and He said: {They will not hear therein vain talk nor sin, only the saying, "peace, peace"} [al-Waqi'ah: 25, 26].

{Therein is a flowing spring} that is, running freely. This is an indefinite noun in an affirmative context, and what is meant by it is not a single spring; rather this is a generic term, meaning: therein are flowing springs.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: it was read out to al-Rabi' ibn Sulayman: [chain] ... from Abu Hurayrah, who said: The Prophet ﷺ said: "The rivers of Paradise burst forth from beneath hills, or: from beneath mountains, of musk."

{Therein are raised couches} that is, elevated and soft, richly spread with furnishings, high in structure, upon which are the wide eyed houris. They said: when the wali (friend and close servant) of Allah wishes to sit upon those elevated couches, they lower themselves for him. {And cups set in place}, meaning: drinking vessels prepared and set ready for whichever of their possessors desires them. {And cushions arranged in rows}. Ibn 'Abbas said: al-namariq are cushions. 'Ikrimah, Qatadah, al-Dahhak, al-Suddi, al-Thawri, and others said likewise.

* *

And His saying: {and carpets spread about}. Ibn 'Abbas said: al-zarabi are carpets. Al-Dahhak and more than one other said likewise.

The meaning of mabthuthah (spread about) is: here and there, for whoever wishes to sit upon them.

We mention here this hadith which Abu Bakr ibn Abi Dawud narrated: [chain] ... Kurayb told me that he heard Usamah ibn Zayd say: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Is there not one who girds himself up (mushammir), ready to strive, for Paradise? For Paradise has no equal in worth. By the Lord of the Ka'bah, it is a light that gleams, a fragrant plant that sways, a lofty palace, a flowing river, ripe fruit, a beautiful and lovely wife, abundant garments, and an everlasting station in a sound abode, with fruit and greenery, joy and blessing, in a lofty and resplendent quarter." They said: Yes, O Messenger of Allah, we are those who gird ourselves up for it. He said: "Say: if Allah wills." The people said: if Allah wills.

Ibn Majah narrated it from al-'Abbas ibn 'Uthman al-Dimashqi, from al-Walid ibn Muslim, from Muhammad ibn Muhajir, with the same chain and wording.

﴿فِيهَا عَيْنٌ جَارِيَةٌ﴾ أَيْ: سَارِحَةٌ. وَهَذِهِ نَكِرَةٌ فِي سِيَاقِ الْإِثْبَاتِ، وَلَيْسَ الْمُرَادُ بِهَا عَيْنًا وَاحِدَةً، وَإِنَّمَا هَذَا جِنْسٌ، يَعْنِي: فِيهَا عُيُونٌ جَارِيَاتٌ.

## 88:17-26 — verses 17-26 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, commanding His servants to look into His creatures which point to His power and His greatness: {Will they not then look at the camel, how it was created?} For it is a wondrous creation and its structure is strange, for it is at the utmost of strength and toughness, and yet with that it yields to the heavy load and is led along by the weak driver, and it is eaten, its hair is put to use, and its milk is drunk. They were alerted to this because the animals of the Arabs were mostly camels. Shurayh the judge used to say: Go out with us so that we may look at the camel, how it was created, and at the sky, how it was raised. That is, how God, Mighty and Majestic, raised it above the earth with this tremendous raising, as the Exalted said: {Have they not looked at the sky above them, how We constructed it and adorned it, and it has no rifts?} [Qaf: 6]

{And at the mountains, how they were set up?} That is: they were made set up, standing, firm, and immovable, so that the earth would not shake with its people, and He placed in them the benefits and the minerals that He placed.

{And at the earth, how it was spread out?} That is: how it was laid out, extended, and made level. So He alerted the Bedouin to draw evidence from what he witnesses of his camel that he is riding, and the sky that is above his head, and the mountain that faces him, and the earth that is beneath him, pointing to the power of the Creator and Maker of all that, and that He is the Great Lord, the Creator, the Disposer, the Owner, and that He is the God besides whom none deserves worship. It was in this manner that Dimam swore in his questioning of the Messenger of God ﷺ, as Imam Ahmad narrated, where he said:

Hashim ibn al-Qasim narrated to us, Sulayman ibn al-Mughirah narrated to us, from Thabit, from Anas, who said: We were forbidden to ask the Messenger of God ﷺ about anything, so it used to please us when a sensible man from the people of the desert would come and ask him while we listened. So a man from the people of the desert came and said: O Muhammad, your messenger came to us and claimed to us that you claim that God sent you. He said: He spoke the truth. He said: Then who created the sky? He said: God. He said: Then who created the earth? He said: God. He said: Then who set up these mountains and placed in them what He placed? He said: God. He said: Then by the One who created the sky and the earth and set up these mountains, did God send you? He said: Yes. He said: And your messenger claimed that we owe five prayers in our day and our night. He said: He spoke the truth. He said: By the One who sent you, did God command you with this? He said: Yes. He said: And your messenger claimed that we owe zakat on our wealth. He said: He spoke the truth. He said: By the One who sent you, did God command you with this? He said: Yes. He said: And your messenger claimed that we owe the pilgrimage to the House upon whoever is able to find a way to it. He said: He spoke the truth. He said, then he turned to leave, saying: By the One who sent you with the truth, I will not add anything to them nor take anything away from them. So the Prophet ﷺ said: If he has spoken the truth, he will surely enter Paradise.

Muslim narrated it from Amr al-Naqid, from Abu al-Nadr Hashim ibn al-Qasim, with it. Al-Bukhari mentioned it as a mu'allaq report (a report cited with part of its chain of narration dropped). Al-Tirmidhi and al-Nasa'i narrated it from the hadith of Sulayman ibn al-Mughirah, with it. Imam Ahmad, al-Bukhari, Abu Dawud, al-Nasa'i, and Ibn Majah narrated it from the hadith of al-Layth ibn Sa'd, from Sa'id al-Maqburi, from Sharik ibn Abd Allah ibn Abi Namir, from Anas, with it in full, and he said at its end: I am Dimam ibn Tha'labah, the brother of Banu Sa'd ibn Bakr.

Al-Hafiz Abu Ya'la said: Ishaq narrated to us, Abd Allah ibn Ja'far narrated to us, Abd Allah ibn Dinar narrated to me, from Ibn Umar, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ used to often tell of a woman in the Jahiliyyah (the pre-Islamic era) atop a mountain, with a son of hers with her grazing sheep. Her son said to her: O mother, who created you? She said: God. He said: Then who created my father? She said: God. He said: Then who created me? She said: God. He said: Then who created the sky? She said: God. He said: Then who created the earth? She said: God. He said: Then who created the mountain? She said: God. He said: Then who created these sheep? She said: God. He said: Indeed, I hear that God has a great matter. And he threw himself off the mountain and was torn to pieces.

Ibn Umar said: The Messenger of God ﷺ often used to tell us this.

Ibn Dinar said: Ibn Umar often used to tell us this.

In its chain of narration there is weakness, and this Abd Allah ibn Ja'far is al-Madini, whom his own son, the Imam Ali ibn al-Madini, and others declared weak.

His saying: {So remind, you are only a reminder. You are not a controller over them} That is: so remind, O Muhammad, the people of what you were sent with to them, for upon you is only the conveying, and upon Us is the reckoning. It is for this that He said: {You are not a controller over them}. Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, and others besides them said: You are not a tyrant over them.

Ibn Zayd said: You are not the one who compels them to faith.

Imam Ahmad said: Waki' narrated to us, from Sufyan, from Abu al-Zubayr, from Jabir, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: I have been commanded to fight the people until they say, There is no god but God. So when they say it, they have protected from me their blood and their wealth, except by its right, and their reckoning is upon God, Mighty and Majestic. Then he recited: {So remind, you are only a reminder. You are not a controller over them.}

It was likewise narrated by Muslim in the Book of Faith, and by al-Tirmidhi and al-Nasa'i in the two Books of Tafsir from their two Sunan (hadith collections arranged by chapters of law), from the hadith of Sufyan ibn Sa'id al-Thawri, with it, with this addition. And this hadith is brought out in the two Sahihs (Sahih al-Bukhari and Sahih Muslim) from the narration of Abu Hurayrah, without the mention of this verse.

* *

His saying: {except whoever turns away and disbelieves} That is: he turns away from acting with his limbs, and disbelieves in the truth with his heart and his tongue. This is like His saying: {So he did not believe nor did he pray, but he denied and turned away} [al-Qiyamah: 31, 32]. It is for this that He said: {Then God will punish him with the greatest punishment}. Imam Ahmad said:

Qutaybah narrated to us, Layth narrated to us, from Sa'id ibn Abi Hilal, from Ali ibn Khalid, that Abu Umamah al-Bahili passed by Khalid ibn Yazid ibn Mu'awiyah, and he asked him about the gentlest word he had heard from the Messenger of God ﷺ. He said: I heard the Messenger of God ﷺ say: Indeed, all of you will enter Paradise, except whoever bolts away from God as the camel bolts away from its owners.

Imam Ahmad alone brought this out. And this Ali ibn Khalid, Ibn Abi Hatim mentioned him from his father, and did not add to what is here: He narrated from Abu Umamah, and Sa'id ibn Abi Hilal narrated from him.

* *

His saying: {Indeed, to Us is their return} That is: their coming back and their turning back. {Then indeed, upon Us is their reckoning} That is: We reckon them for their deeds and repay them for them, if good then good, and if evil then evil.

The end of the tafsir of Surat al-Ghashiyah, and to God belongs all praise and grace.

حَدَّثَنَا هَاشِمُ بْنُ الْقَاسِمِ، حَدَّثَنَا سُلَيْمَانُ بْنُ الْمُغِيرَةِ، عَنْ ثَابِتٍ، عَنْ أَنَسٍ قَالَ: كُنَّا نُهِينَا أَنْ نَسْأَلَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ عَنْ شَيْءٍ، فَكَانَ يُعْجِبُنَا أَنْ يَجِيءَ الرَّجُلُ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْبَادِيَةِ الْعَاقِلُ فَيَسْأَلُهُ وَنَحْنُ نَسْمَعُ، فَجَاءَ رَجُلٌ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْبَادِيَةِ فَقَالَ: يَا مُحَمَّدُ، إِنَّهُ أَتَانَا رسولُك فزعَم لَنَا أَنَّكَ تَزعُم أَنَّ اللَّهَ أَرْسَلَكَ. قَالَ: "صَدَقَ". قَالَ: فَمَنْ خَلَقَ السَّمَاءَ؟ قَالَ: "اللَّهُ". قَالَ: فَمَنْ خَلَقَ الْأَرْضَ؟ قَالَ: "اللَّهُ". قَالَ: فَمَنْ نَصَبَ هَذِهِ الْجِبَالَ وَجَعَلَ فِيهَا مَا جَعَلَ؟ قَالَ: "اللَّهُ". قَالَ: فَبِالَّذِي خَلَقَ السَّمَاءَ وَالْأَرْضَ وَنَصَبَ هَذِهِ الْجِبَالَ، آللهُ أَرْسَلَكَ؟ قَالَ: "نَعَمْ". قَالَ: وَزَعَمَ رسولُك أَنَّ عَلَيْنَا خَمْسَ صَلَوَاتٍ فِي يَوْمِنَا وَلَيْلَتِنَا. قَالَ: "صَدَقَ". قَالَ: فَبِالَّذِي أَرْسَلَكَ، آللَّهُ أَمَرَكَ بِهَذَا؟ قَالَ: "نَعَمْ". قَالَ: وَزَعَمَ رَسُولُكَ أَنَّ عَلَيْنَا زَكَاةً فِي أَمْوَالِنَا؟ قَالَ: "صَدَقَ". قَالَ: فَبِالَّذِي أَرْسَلَكَ، آللَّهُ أَمَرَكَ بِهَذَا؟. قَالَ: "نَعَمْ". قَالَ: وَزَعَمَ رَسُولُكَ أَنَّ عَلَيْنَا حَجّ الْبَيْتِ مَنِ اسْتَطَاعَ إِلَيْهِ سَبِيلًا. قَالَ: "صَدَقَ". قَالَ: ثُمَّ وَلَّى فَقَالَ: وَالَّذِي بَعَثَكَ بِالْحَقِّ لَا أَزِيدُ عَلَيْهِنَّ وَلَا أَنْقُصُ مِنْهُنَّ شَيْئًا. فَقَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: "إِنَّ صَدَقَ ليدخُلَنّ الْجَنَّةَ".

وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ، عَنْ عَمْرٍو النَّاقِدِ، عَنْ أَبِي النَّضْرِ هَاشِمِ بْنِ الْقَاسِمِ، بِهِ وعَلّقه الْبُخَارِيُّ، وَرَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ وَالنَّسَائِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ سُلَيْمَانَ بْنِ الْمُغِيرَةِ بِهِ وَرَوَاهُ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ وَالْبُخَارِيُّ وَأَبُو دَاوُدَ وَالنَّسَائِيُّ وَابْنُ مَاجَهْ مِنْ حَدِيثِ اللَّيْثِ بْنِ سَعْدٍ، عَنْ سَعِيدٍ الْمَقْبُرِيِّ، عن شريك ابن عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ أَبِي نَمِرٍ، عَنْ أَنَسٍ، بِهِ بِطُولِهِ وَقَالَ فِي آخِرِهِ: "وَأَنَا ضِمَامُ بْنُ ثَعْلَبَةَ أَخُو بَنِي سَعْدِ بْنِ بَكْرٍ".

وَقَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو يَعْلَى: حَدَّثَنَا إِسْحَاقُ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ جَعْفَرٍ، حَدَّثَنِي عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ دِينَارٍ عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ قَالَ: كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ كَثِيرًا مَا كَانَ يُحَدِّثُ عَنِ امْرَأَةٍ فِي الْجَاهِلِيَّةِ عَلَى رَأْسِ جَبَلٍ، مَعَهَا ابْنٌ لَهَا تَرْعَى غَنَمًا، فَقَالَ لَهَا ابْنُهَا: يَا أُمَّهْ، مَنْ خَلَقَكِ؟ قَالَتِ: اللَّهُ. قَالَ: فَمَنْ خَلَقَ أَبِي؟ قَالَتِ: اللَّهُ. قَالَ: فَمَنْ خَلَقَنِي؟ قَالَتِ: اللَّهُ. قَالَ: فَمَنْ خَلَقَ السَّمَاءَ؟ قَالَتِ: اللَّهُ. قَالَ: فَمَنْ خَلَقَ الْأَرْضَ؟ قَالَتِ: اللَّهُ. قَالَ: فَمَنْ خَلَقَ الْجَبَلَ؟ قَالَتِ: اللَّهُ. قَالَ: فَمَنْ خَلَقَ هَذِهِ الْغَنَمَ؟ قَالَتِ: اللَّهُ. قَالَ: إِنِّي لَأَسْمَعُ لِلَّهِ شَأْنًا. وَأَلْقَى نَفْسَهُ مِنَ الْجَبَلِ فَتَقَطَّعَ.

قَالَ ابْنُ عُمَرَ: كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ كَثِيرًا مَا يُحَدِّثُنَا هَذَا.

قَالَ ابْنُ دِينَارٍ: كَانَ ابْنُ عُمَرَ كَثِيرًا مَا يُحَدِّثُنَا بِهَذَا .

فِي إِسْنَادِهِ ضَعْفٌ، وَعَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ جَعْفَرٍ هَذَا هُوَ الْمَدِينِيُّ، ضَعّفه وَلَدُهُ الْإِمَامُ عَلِيُّ بْنُ الْمَدِينِيِّ وَغَيْرُهُ.

وَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿فَذَكِّرْ إِنَّمَا أَنْتَ مُذَكِّرٌ. لَسْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ بِمُسَيْطِرٍ﴾ أَيْ: فَذَكِّرْ-يَا مُحَمَّدُ-النَّاسَ بِمَا أُرْسِلْتَ بِهِ إِلَيْهِمْ، فَإِنَّمَا عَلَيْكَ الْبَلَاغُ وَعَلَيْنَا الْحِسَابُ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿لَسْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ بِمُسَيْطِرٍ﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَغَيْرُهُمَا: لَسْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ بِجَبَّارٍ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ زَيْدٍ: لَسْتَ بِالَّذِي تُكْرِهُهُمْ عَلَى الْإِيمَانِ.

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا وَكِيع، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ، عَنْ أَبِي الزُّبَيْرِ، عَنْ جَابِرٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "أُمِرْتُ أَنْ أُقَاتِلَ النَّاسَ حَتَّى يَقُولُوا: لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ، فَإِذَا قَالُوهَا عَصَمُوا مِنِّي دِمَاءَهُمْ وَأَمْوَالَهُمْ إِلَّا بِحَقِّهَا، وَحِسَابُهُمْ عَلَى اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ". ثُمَّ قَرَأَ: ﴿فَذَكِّرْ إِنَّمَا أَنْتَ مُذَكِّرٌ لَسْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ بِمُسَيْطِرٍ﴾

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ فِي كِتَابِ "الْإِيمَانِ"، وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ وَالنَّسَائِيُّ فِي كِتَابَيِ التَّفْسِيرِ" مِنْ سُنَنَيْهِمَا، مِنْ حَدِيثِ سُفْيَانَ بْنِ سَعِيدٍ الثَّوْرِيِّ، بِهِ بِهَذِهِ الزِّيَادَةِ وَهَذَا الْحَدِيثُ مُخَرَّجٌ فِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ مِنْ رِوَايَةِ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، بِدُونِ ذِكْرِ هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِلا مَنْ تَوَلَّى وَكَفَرَ﴾ أَيْ: تَوَلَّى عَنِ الْعَمَلِ بِأَرْكَانِهِ، وَكَفَرَ بِالْحَقِّ بِجِنَانِهِ وَلِسَانِهِ. وَهَذِهِ كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿فَلا صَدَّقَ وَلا صَلَّى وَلَكِنْ كَذَّبَ وَتَوَلَّى﴾ [الْقِيَامَةِ: ٣١، ٣٢] وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿فَيُعَذِّبُهُ اللَّهُ الْعَذَابَ الأكْبَرَ﴾ قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ:

حَدَّثَنَا قُتَيْبَةُ، حَدَّثَنَا لَيْثٌ، عَنِ سَعِيدِ بْنِ أَبِي هِلَالٍ، عَنْ عَلِيِّ بْنِ خَالِدٍ أَنَّ أَبَا أُمَامَةَ الْبَاهِلِيَّ مَرَّ عَلَى خَالِدِ بْنِ يَزِيدَ بْنِ مُعَاوِيَةَ، فَسَأَلَهُ عَنْ أَلْيَنِ كَلِمَةٍ سَمِعَهَا مِنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، فَقَالَ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم يَقُولُ: "أَلَا كُلُّكُمْ يَدْخُلُ الْجَنَّةَ، إِلَّا مَنْ شَرَد عَلَى اللَّهِ شَراد الْبَعِيرِ عَلَى أَهْلِهِ".

تَفَرَّدَ بِإِخْرَاجِهِ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ وَعَلِيُّ بْنُ خَالِدٍ هَذَا ذَكَرَهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ عَنْ أَبِيهِ، وَلَمْ يَزِدْ عَلَى مَا هَاهُنَا: "رَوَى عَنْ أَبِي أُمَامَةَ، وَعَنْهُ سَعِيدُ بْنُ أَبِي هِلَالٍ" .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنَّ إِلَيْنَا إِيَابَهُمْ﴾ أَيْ: مَرْجِعَهُمْ وَمُنْقَلَبَهُمْ ﴿ثُمَّ إِنَّ عَلَيْنَا حِسَابَهُمْ﴾ أَيْ: نَحْنُ نُحَاسِبُهُمْ عَلَى أَعْمَالِهِمْ وَنُجَازِيهِمْ بِهَا، إِنْ خَيْرًا فَخَيْرٌ، وَإِنْ شَرًا فَشَرٌّ.

آخِرُ تَفْسِيرِ سُورَةِ "الْغَاشِيَةِ" ولله الحمد والمنة.
