### Translation

# Tafsir of Surat al-Qamar

It is Meccan.

It has already been mentioned in the hadith of Abu Waqid that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to recite Surah Qaf and Surah al-Qamar (Iqtarabat) on the two Eids, the Adha and the Fitr, and he used to recite them in large gatherings because of what they contain regarding the mention of promise and threat, the beginning of creation and its return, the affirmation of divine oneness and the establishment of prophethood, and other great purposes.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful

The Exalted informs us of the nearness of the Hour and the emptying and passing away of this world, as He, the Exalted, said: {The command of Allah has come, so do not seek to hasten it [Glorified is He]} [al-Nahl: 1], and He said: {The reckoning for mankind has drawn near, while they are in heedlessness turning away} [al-Anbiya: 1]. Hadiths have been reported concerning this. The Hafiz Abu Bakr al-Bazzar said:

[chain] Anas reported that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ addressed his companions one day when the sun was about to set, with only a small remnant of it left, and he said: "By the One in whose hand is my soul, what remains of this world compared to what has already passed of it is only like what remains of this day of yours compared to what has already passed of it, and we see nothing of the sun now but a little."

I say: this hadith turns upon Khalaf ibn Musa ibn Khalaf al-'Ammi, from his father. Ibn Hibban mentioned him among the trustworthy narrators (al-thiqat) and said: he sometimes erred.

Another hadith that supports and explains the one before it. Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Ibn Umar said: We were sitting with the Prophet ﷺ while the sun was over Qu'ayqi'an after the afternoon prayer, and he said: "Your lifespans compared to the lifespans of those who came before are only like what remains of the day compared to what has already passed of it."

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Sahl ibn Sa'd said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "I was sent and the Hour is like this," and he gestured with his index and middle fingers.

Both al-Bukhari and Muslim reported it from the hadith of Abu Hazim, Salamah ibn Dinar.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Wahb al-Sawa'i said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "I was sent, and the Hour is like this in relation to this, it nearly outstripped it." Al-A'mash joined together the index and middle fingers.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Isma'il ibn Ubayd Allah said: Anas ibn Malik came to al-Walid ibn Abd al-Malik, who asked him: What did you hear from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ concerning the Hour? He said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "You and the Hour are like these two," referring to his two fingers.

Ahmad, may Allah have mercy on him, was alone in reporting this. A corroborating witness for this is also found in the Sahih, among the names of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ: that he is al-Hashir, the one after whose footsteps the people will be gathered.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Khalid ibn Umayr said: Utbah ibn Ghazwan delivered a sermon to us, Bahz said: and before this he had said, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ delivered a sermon to us, he said: he praised Allah and extolled Him, then said: "To proceed: indeed this world has announced its departure and has turned away swiftly, and nothing remains of it except a residue like the residue at the bottom of a vessel which its owner drains out. You are indeed moving on from it to an abode that has no end, so move on with the best of what is in your possession, for it has been mentioned to us that a stone is thrown from the edge of Hell and falls into it for seventy years without reaching its bottom. By Allah, you will surely fill it! Are you astonished? By Allah, it has been mentioned to us that the distance between the two leaves of the gate of Paradise is a journey of forty years, and there will surely come upon it a day when it is packed tight with crowding," and he mentioned the rest of the hadith. Muslim alone reported it.

Abu Ja'far ibn Jarir said: [chain] Abu Abd al-Rahman al-Sulami said: We settled in al-Mada'in, and we were a parasang's distance from it. Friday came, and my father attended and I attended with him, and Hudhayfah delivered a sermon to us and said: Indeed Allah says: {The Hour has drawn near and the moon has split}. Indeed the Hour has drawn near, and indeed the moon has split, and indeed this world has announced its departure. Indeed today is the training ground, and tomorrow is the race. I said to my father: Will the people race tomorrow? He said: My son, you are indeed ignorant, it is only a race of deeds.

Then the next Friday came, and we attended, and Hudhayfah delivered a sermon and said: Indeed Allah, Mighty and Majestic, says: {The Hour has drawn near and the moon has split}. Indeed this world has announced its departure. Indeed today is the training ground and tomorrow is the race. Indeed the finish line is the Fire, and the winner is the one who reaches Paradise first.

His statement, {and the moon has split}: this occurred during the time of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, as has been established in mutawatir (mass-transmitted) hadiths with sound chains. It has been established in the Sahih from Ibn Mas'ud that he said: "Five have already passed: the Romans, the smoke, the binding punishment, the seizing, and the moon." This is a matter agreed upon among the scholars, namely that the splitting of the moon occurred during the time of the Prophet ﷺ and that it was one of the dazzling miracles.

Mention of the hadiths reported concerning this:

The narration of Anas ibn Malik:

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Anas ibn Malik said: The people of Mecca asked the Prophet ﷺ for a sign, so the moon split at Mecca twice, and he said: {The Hour has drawn near and the moon has split}.

Muslim also reported it, from Muhammad ibn Rafi', from Abd al-Razzaq.

Al-Bukhari said: [chain] Anas ibn Malik reported that the people of Mecca asked the Messenger of Allah ﷺ to show them a sign, so he showed them the moon split into two halves, until they saw Hira' between them.

Both al-Bukhari and Muslim also reported it from the hadith of Yunus ibn Muhammad al-Mu'addib, from Shayban, from Qatadah. Muslim also reported it from the hadith of Abu Dawud al-Tayalisi, Yahya al-Qattan, and others, from Shu'bah, from Qatadah, with the same content.

The narration of Jubayr ibn Mut'im, may Allah be pleased with him:

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Jubayr ibn Mut'im said: The moon split during the time of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and it became two portions, one portion over this mountain and one portion over that mountain. So they said: Muhammad has bewitched us. Then they said: If he has bewitched us, he cannot bewitch all the people together.

Imam Ahmad was alone in reporting it by this route, and al-Bayhaqi transmitted it with a chain in "al-Dala'il" through the route of Muhammad ibn Kathir, from his brother Sulayman ibn Kathir, from Husayn ibn Abd al-Rahman, [with the same content]. Ibn Jarir likewise reported it from the hadith of Muhammad ibn Fudayl and others, from Husayn, with the same content. Al-Bayhaqi also reported it through the route of Ibrahim ibn Tahman and Hushaym, both from Husayn, from Jubayr ibn Muhammad ibn Jubayr ibn Mut'im, from his father, from his grandfather, and he mentioned it.

The narration of Abdullah ibn Abbas, may Allah be pleased with both of them:

Al-Bukhari said: [chain] Ibn Abbas said: The moon split during the time of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ.

Al-Bukhari and Muslim also reported it from the hadith of Bakr ibn Mudar, from Ja'far ibn Rabi'ah, from 'Irak [ibn Malik], with the same content, similarly.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Ibn Abbas said regarding His statement: {The Hour has drawn near and the moon has split. And if they see a sign they turn away and say, this is a continuing magic}: this has already occurred, it was before the migration (hijrah). The moon split until they saw its two halves.

Al-'Awfi also reported something similar from Ibn Abbas.

Al-Tabarani said: [chain] Ibn Abbas said: The moon was eclipsed during the time of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, so they said: The moon has been bewitched. Then this was revealed: {The Hour has drawn near and the moon has split} up to His statement {a continuing magic}.

The report of Abdullah ibn Umar:

The Hafiz Abu Bakr al-Bayhaqi said: Abu Abdullah al-Hafiz and Abu Bakr Ahmad ibn al-Hasan al-Qadi informed us, they said: Abu al-Abbas al-Asamm narrated to us, Al-Abbas ibn Muhammad al-Duri narrated to us, Wahb ibn Jarir narrated to us, from Shu'bah, from al-A'mash, from Mujahid, from Abdullah ibn Umar, concerning the words of the Almighty {The Hour has drawn near and the moon has split}, he said: this happened during the time of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. It split into two pieces, one piece on the near side of the mountain and one piece behind the mountain, and the Prophet ﷺ said: "O Allah, bear witness."

Thus it was narrated by Muslim and al-Tirmidhi, through various chains from Shu'bah, from al-A'mash, from Mujahid, with the same content. Muslim said it is like the report of Mujahid from Abu Ma'mar from Ibn Mas'ud. Al-Tirmidhi said: hasan sahih (sound and authentic).

The report of Abdullah ibn Mas'ud:

Imam Ahmad said: Sufyan narrated to us, from Ibn Abi Najih, from Mujahid, from Abu Ma'mar, from Ibn Mas'ud, who said: the moon split during the time of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ into two pieces, until they looked at it, and the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Bear witness."

Thus it was narrated by al-Bukhari and Muslim, from the hadith of Sufyan ibn Uyaynah, with the same content. They both also related it from the hadith of al-A'mash, from Ibrahim, from Abu Ma'mar Abdullah ibn Sakhbarah, from Ibn Mas'ud, with the same content.

Ibn Jarir said: Isa ibn Uthman ibn Isa al-Ramli narrated to me, my uncle Yahya ibn Isa narrated to us, from al-A'mash, from Ibrahim, from a man, from Abdullah, who said: we were with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ at Mina when the moon split, and one part went behind the mountain, and the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Bear witness, bear witness."

Al-Bukhari said: Abu al-Duha said, from Masruq, from Abdullah: "at Mecca."

Abu Dawud al-Tayalisi said: Abu Awanah narrated to us, from al-Mughirah, from Abu al-Duha, from Masruq, from Abdullah ibn Mas'ud, who said: the moon split during the time of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, and the Quraysh said: "This is the magic of the son of Abu Kabshah." He said: they said: "Watch for what the travelers bring you, for Muhammad cannot cast a spell on all the people." He said: then the travelers came and said the same thing.

Al-Bayhaqi said: Abu Abdullah al-Hafiz informed us, Abu al-Abbas Muhammad ibn Ya'qub informed us, al-Abbas ibn Muhammad al-Duri narrated to us, Sa'id ibn Sulayman narrated to us, Hushaym narrated to us, Mughirah narrated to us, from Abu al-Duha, from Masruq, from Abdullah, who said: the moon split at Mecca until it became two pieces, and the disbelievers of Quraysh, the people of Mecca, said: "This is magic that the son of Abu Kabshah has cast upon you. Watch the travelers, for if they saw what you saw, then he has spoken the truth, and if they did not see anything like what you saw, then it is magic he has cast upon you." He said: so the travelers were asked, and they had come from every direction, and they said: "We saw it."

Ibn Jarir narrated it from the hadith of al-Mughirah, with the same content, and he added: then Allah, mighty and majestic, revealed {The Hour has drawn near and the moon has split}. Then Ibn Jarir said: Ya'qub ibn Ibrahim narrated to me, Ibn Ulayyah narrated to us, Ayyub informed us, from Muhammad, that is Ibn Sirin, who said: I was informed that Ibn Mas'ud, may Allah be pleased with him, used to say: "Indeed the moon split."

Ibn Jarir also said: Muhammad ibn Imarah narrated to me, Amr ibn Hammad narrated to us, Asbat narrated to us, from Simak, from Ibrahim, from al-Aswad, from Abdullah, who said: "Indeed I saw the mountain through the gap of the moon when it split."

Imam Ahmad narrated it from Mu'ammal, from Isra'il, from Simak, from Ibrahim, from al-Aswad, from Abdullah, who said: the moon split during the time of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, until I saw the mountain between the two gaps of the moon.

Layth said, from Mujahid: the moon split during the time of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and became two pieces, and the Prophet ﷺ said to Abu Bakr: "Bear witness, O Abu Bakr." And the polytheists said: "The moon has been bewitched, so that it split."

And His saying: {And if they see a sign} that is, a proof, an argument, and a demonstration, {they turn away} that is, they do not submit to it, but rather turn away from it and cast it behind their backs, {and say, 'a continuous magic'} that is, they say: "this which we have witnessed of the proofs is magic by which we have been bewitched."

The meaning of {mustamirr (continuous/passing away)} is: passing away. This was said by Mujahid, Qatadah, and others, meaning: false, vanishing, having no permanence.

{And they denied and followed their own desires} that is, they denied the truth when it came to them, and followed what their own opinions and desires, born of their ignorance and feeble-mindedness, commanded them to do.

And His saying: {And every matter is settled} Qatadah said its meaning is: that good befalls the people of good, and evil befalls the people of evil.

Ibn Jurayj said: settled with its people. Mujahid said: {And every matter is settled} that is, on the Day of Resurrection.

Al-Suddi said: {mustaqirr (settled)} that is, occurring/coming to pass.

And His saying: {And there has certainly come to them of the accounts} that is, of the reports concerning the stories of the nations who denied the messengers, and what befell them of punishment, retribution, and torment, which is recited to them in this Qur'an, {that which contains a deterrent} that is, that which contains a warning for them against polytheism and persisting in denial.

And His saying: {far-reaching wisdom} that is, in His guidance of whomever He guides and His leading astray of whomever He leads astray, {but of what benefit are the warnings} meaning: of what benefit are the warnings to one upon whom Allah has decreed wretchedness and upon whose heart He has sealed? Who then can guide him besides Allah? This verse is like His saying, the Almighty: {Say, "With Allah is the far-reaching argument, and had He willed He would have guided you all"} [al-An'am: 149], and likewise His saying, the Almighty: {And of what benefit are the signs and warnings to a people who do not believe} [Yunus: 101].

تَفْسِيرُ سُورَةِ الْقَمَرِ 

وَهِيَ مَكِّيَّةٌ.

قَدْ تَقَدَّمَ فِي حَدِيثِ أَبِي وَاقِدٍ : أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ كَانَ يَقْرَأُ بِقَافٍ، وَاقْتَرَبَتِ السَّاعَةُ، فِي الْأَضْحَى والفِطْر، وَكَانَ يَقْرَأُ بِهِمَا فِي الْمَحَافِلِ الْكِبَارِ، لِاشْتِمَالِهِمَا عَلَى ذِكْرِ الْوَعْدِ وَالْوَعِيدِ وَبَدْءِ الْخَلْقِ وَإِعَادَتِهِ، وَالتَّوْحِيدِ وَإِثْبَاتِ النُّبُوَّاتِ، وَغَيْرِ ذَلِكَ مِنَ الْمَقَاصِدِ الْعَظِيمَةِ.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

* *

يُخْبِرُ تَعَالَى عَنِ اقْتِرَابِ السَّاعَةِ وَفَرَاغِ الدُّنْيَا وَانْقِضَائِهَا. كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿أَتَى أَمْرُ اللَّهِ فَلا تَسْتَعْجِلُوهُ [سُبْحَانَهُ] ﴾ [النَّحْلِ: ١] ، وَقَالَ: ﴿اقْتَرَبَ لِلنَّاسِ حِسَابُهُمْ وَهُمْ فِي غَفْلَةٍ مُعْرِضُونَ﴾ [الْأَنْبِيَاءِ: ١] وَقَدْ وَرَدَتِ الْأَحَادِيثُ بِذَلِكَ، قَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو بَكْرٍ الْبَزَّارُ:

حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ الْمُثَنَّى وَعَمْرُو بْنُ عَلِيٍّ قَالَا حَدَّثَنَا خَلَفُ بْنُ مُوسَى، حَدَّثَنِي أَبِي، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ، عَنْ أَنَسٍ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ خَطَبَ أَصْحَابَهُ ذَاتَ يَوْمٍ، وَقَدْ كَادَتِ الشَّمْسُ أَنْ تَغْرُبَ فَلَمْ يَبْقَ مِنْهَا إِلَّا شِفٌّ يَسِيرٌ، فَقَالَ: "وَالَّذِي نَفْسِي بِيَدِهِ مَا بَقِيَ مِنَ الدُّنْيَا فِيمَا مَضَى مِنْهَا إِلَّا كَمَا بَقِيَ مِنْ يَوْمِكُمْ هَذَا فِيمَا مَضَى مِنْهُ، وَمَا نَرَى مِنَ الشَّمْسِ إِلَّا يَسِيرًا" .

قُلْتُ: هَذَا حَدِيثٌ مَدَارُهُ عَلَى خَلَفِ بْنِ مُوسَى بْنِ خَلَفٍ العَمِّيّ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ. وَقَدْ ذَكَرَهُ ابْنُ حِبَّان فِي الثِّقَاتِ، وَقَالَ: رُبَّمَا أَخْطَأَ.

حَدِيثٌ آخَرُ يُعَضِّدُ الَّذِي قَبْلَهُ وَيُفَسِّرُهُ، قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا الْفَضْلُ بْنُ دُكَيْن، حَدَّثَنَا شَرِيكٌ، حَدَّثَنَا سَلَمَةُ بْنُ كُهَيْل، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ قَالَ: كُنَّا جُلُوسًا عِنْدَ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ وَالشَّمْسُ عَلَى قُعَيْقِعان بَعْدَ الْعَصْرِ، فَقَالَ: "مَا أَعْمَارُكُمْ فِي أَعْمَارِ مَنْ مَضَى إِلَّا كَمَا بقي من النهار فيما مضى" .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا حُسَيْنٌ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ مُطَرِّف، عَنْ أَبِي حَازِمٍ، عَنْ سَهْلِ بْنِ سَعْدٍ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "بُعِثتُ وَالسَّاعَةُ هَكَذَا". وَأَشَارَ بِإِصْبَعَيْهِ: السَّبَّابَةِ وَالْوُسْطَى.

أَخْرَجَاهُ مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي حَازِمٍ سَلَمَةَ بْنِ دِينَارٍ .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عُبَيد، حَدَّثَنَا الْأَعْمَشُ، عَنْ أَبِي خَالِدٍ، عَنْ وَهْبٍ السَّوَائي قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم: "بُعِثْتُ أَنَا وَالسَّاعَةُ كَهَذِهِ مِنْ هَذِهِ إِنْ كَادَتْ لَتَسْبِقُهَا " وَجَمَعَ الْأَعْمَشُ بَيْنَ السَّبَّابَةِ وَالْوُسْطَى .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو الْمُغِيرَةِ، حَدَّثَنَا الْأَوْزَاعِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا إِسْمَاعِيلُ بْنُ عُبَيْدِ اللَّهِ، قَالَ: قَدِمَ أَنَسُ بْنُ مَالِكٍ عَلَى الْوَلِيدِ بْنِ عَبْدِ الْمَلِكِ فَسَأَلَهُ: مَاذَا سَمِعْتُ مِنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَذْكُرُ بِهِ السَّاعَةَ؟ فَقَالَ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "أَنْتُمْ وَالسَّاعَةُ كَهَاتَيْنِ".

تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ أَحْمَدُ، رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ . وَشَاهِدُ ذَلِكَ أَيْضًا فِي الصَّحِيحِ فِي أَسْمَاءِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ: أَنَّهُ الْحَاشِرُ الَّذِي يُحْشَرُ النَّاسُ عَلَى قَدَمَيْهِ.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا بَهْزُ بْنُ أَسَدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا سُلَيْمَانُ بْنُ الْمُغِيرَةِ، حَدَّثَنَا حُمَيْدُ بْنُ هِلَالٍ، عَنْ خَالِدِ بْنٍ عُمَيْرٍ قَالَ: خَطَبَ عُتْبَةُ بْنُ غَزْوَان -قَالَ بَهْزٌ: وَقَالَ قَبْلَ هَذِهِ الْمَرَّةِ-خَطَبَنَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: فَحَمِدَ اللَّهَ وَأَثْنَى عَلَيْهِ، ثُمَّ قَالَ: "أَمَّا بَعْدُ، فَإِنَّ الدُّنْيَا قَدْ آذَنَتْ بصَرْمٍ وَوَلَّتْ حَذَّاءَ، وَلَمْ يَبْقَ مِنْهَا إِلَّا صُبَابة كَصُبَابَةِ الْإِنَاءِ يَتَصَابُّهَا صَاحِبُهَا، وَإِنَّكُمْ مُنْتَقِلُونَ مِنْهَا إِلَى دَارٍ لَا زَوَالَ لَهَا، فَانْتَقِلُوا بِخَيْرِ مَا بِحَضْرَتِكُمْ، فَإِنَّهُ قَدْ ذُكِرَ لَنَا أَنَّ الْحَجَرَ يُلقَى مِنْ شَفِيرِ جَهَنَّمَ فَيَهْوِي فِيهَا سَبْعِينَ عَامًا مَا يُدْرِكُ لَهَا قَعْرًا، وَاللَّهِ لَتَمْلَؤُنَّهُ، أَفَعَجِبْتُمْ! وَاللَّهِ لَقَدْ ذُكِرَ لَنَا أَنَّ مَا بَيْنَ مِصْرَاعَي الْجَنَّةِ مَسِيرَةُ أَرْبَعِينَ عَامًا، وَلَيَأْتِيَنَّ عَلَيْهِ يَوْمٌ وَهُوَ كَظِيظُ الزِّحَامِ" وَذَكَرَ تَمَامَ الْحَدِيثِ، انْفَرَدَ بِهِ مُسْلِمٌ .

وَقَالَ أَبُو جَعْفَرِ بْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنِي يَعْقُوبُ، حَدَّثَنِي ابْنُ عُلَيَّةَ، أَخْبَرَنَا عَطَاءُ بْنُ السَّائِبِ، عَنْ أَبِي عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ السُّلَمي قَالَ: نَزَلْنَا الْمَدَائِنَ فَكُنَّا مِنْهَا عَلَى فَرْسَخ، فَجَاءَتِ الْجُمُعَةُ، فَحَضَرَ أَبِي وَحَضَرْتُ مَعَهُ فَخَطَبَنَا حُذَيْفَةُ فَقَالَ: أَلَا إِنَّ اللَّهَ يَقُولُ: ﴿اقْتَرَبَتِ السَّاعَةُ وَانْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ﴾ ، أَلَا وَإِنَّ السَّاعَةَ قَدِ اقْتَرَبَتْ، أَلَا وَإِنَّ الْقَمَرَ قَدِ انْشَقَّ، أَلَا وَإِنَّ الدُّنْيَا قَدْ آذَنَتْ بِفِرَاقٍ، أَلَا وَإِنَّ الْيَوْمَ الْمِضْمَارُ، وَغَدًا السِّبَاقُ، فَقُلْتُ لِأَبِي: أَيَسْتَبِقُ النَّاسُ غَدًا؟ فَقَالَ: يَا بُنَيَّ إِنَّكَ لجاهل، إنما هو السباق بالأعمال.

ثُمَّ جَاءَتِ الْجُمُعَةُ الْأُخْرَى فَحَضَرْنَا فَخَطَبَ حُذَيْفَةُ، فَقَالَ: أَلَا إِنَّ اللَّهَ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ يَقُولُ: ﴿اقْتَرَبَتِ السَّاعَةُ وَانْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ﴾ ، أَلَا وَإِنَّ الدُّنْيَا قَدْ آذَنَتْ بِفِرَاقٍ، أَلَا وَإِنَّ الْيَوْمَ الْمِضْمَارُ وَغَدًا السِّبَاقُ، أَلَا وَإِنَّ الْغَايَةَ النَّارُ، وَالسَّابِقُ مَنْ سَبَقَ إِلَى الْجَنَّةِ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَانْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ﴾ : قَدْ كَانَ هَذَا فِي زَمَانِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، كَمَا ثَبَتَ ذَلِكَ فِي الْأَحَادِيثِ الْمُتَوَاتِرَةِ بِالْأَسَانِيدِ الصَّحِيحَةِ. وَقَدْ ثَبَتَ فِي الصَّحِيحِ عَنِ ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ أَنَّهُ قَالَ: "خَمْسٌ قَدْ مَضَيْنَ: الرُّومُ، وَالدُّخَانُ، وَاللِّزَامُ، وَالْبَطْشَةُ، وَالْقَمَرُ" . وَهَذَا أَمْرٌ مُتَّفَقٌ عَلَيْهِ بَيْنَ الْعُلَمَاءِ أَيِ انْشِقَاقُ الْقَمَرِ قَدْ وَقَعَ فِي زَمَانِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ وَأَنَّهُ كَانَ إِحْدَى الْمُعْجِزَاتِ الْبَاهِرَاتِ.

ذِكْرُ الْأَحَادِيثِ الْوَارِدَةِ فِي ذَلِكَ:

رِوَايَةُ أَنَسِ بْنِ مَالِكٍ:

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، حَدَّثَنَا مَعْمَر، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ، عَنْ أَنَسِ بْنِ مَالِكٍ قَالَ: سَأَلَ أَهْلُ مَكَّةَ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ آيَةً، فَانْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ بِمَكَّةَ مَرَّتَيْنِ، فَقَالَ: ﴿اقْتَرَبَتِ السَّاعَةُ وَانْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ﴾ .

وَرَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ رَافِعٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ الرَّزَّاقِ .

وَقَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ: حَدَّثَنِي عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ عَبْدِ الْوَهَّابِ، حَدَّثَنَا بِشْرُ بْنُ الْمُفَضَّلِ، حَدَّثَنَا سَعِيدُ بْنُ أَبِي عَرُوبة، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ، عن أنس بن مالك؛ أَنَّ أَهْلَ مَكَّةَ سَأَلُوا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ أَنْ يُرِيَهُمْ آيَةً، فَأَرَاهُمُ الْقَمَرَ شِقَّين، حَتَّى رَأَوْا حِرَاء بَيْنَهُمَا .

وَأَخْرَجَاهُ أَيْضًا مِنْ حَدِيثِ يُونُسَ بْنِ مُحَمَّدٍ الْمُؤَدِّبِ، عَنْ شَيْبَانَ، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ . وَرَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ أَيْضًا مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي دَاوُدَ الطَّيَالِسِيِّ، وَيَحْيَى الْقَطَّانِ، وَغَيْرِهِمَا، عَنْ شُعْبَةَ، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ، بِهِ .

رِوَايَةُ جُبَيْرِ بْنِ مُطْعِمٍ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ:

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ كَثِيرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا سليمان بن كثير، عن حصين بن عبد الرَّحْمَنِ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرِ بْنِ مُطْعِمٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، قَالَ: انْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ عَلَى عَهْدِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَصَارَ فِرْقَتَيْنِ: فِرْقَةٌ عَلَى هَذَا الْجَبَلِ، وَفِرْقَةٌ عَلَى هَذَا الْجَبَلِ، فَقَالُوا: سَحَرَنَا مُحَمَّدٌ. فَقَالُوا: إِنْ كَانَ سَحَرَنَا فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَسْتَطِيعُ أَنْ يَسْحَرَ النَّاسَ كُلَّهُمْ.

تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ مِنْ هَذَا الْوَجْهِ، وَأَسْنَدَهُ الْبَيْهَقِيُّ فِي "الدَّلَائِلِ" مِنْ طريق محمد بن كثير، عَنْ أَخِيهِ سُلَيْمَانَ بْنِ كَثِيرٍ، عَنْ حُصَيْنِ بْنِ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ، [بِهِ] . وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ مِنْ حَدِيثِ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ فُضَيْلٍ وَغَيْرِهِ، عَنْ حُصَيْنٍ، بِهِ . وَرَوَاهُ الْبَيْهَقِيُّ أَيْضًا مِنْ طَرِيقِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ بْنِ طَهْمَان وهُشَيْم، كِلَاهُمَا عَنْ حُصَين عَنْ جُبَيْرِ بْنِ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرِ بْنِ مُطْعِمٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ جَدِّهِ فَذَكَرَهُ .

رِوَايَةُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَبَّاسٍ [رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا] 

قَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى بْنُ بُكَيْرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا بَكْرٌ، عَنْ جَعْفَرٍ، عَنْ عِرَاك بْنِ مَالِكٍ، عَنْ عُبَيْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عُتْبَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: انْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ فِي زَمَانِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ .

وَرَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ أَيْضًا وَمُسْلِمٌ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ بَكْرِ بْنِ مُضَرَ، عَنْ جَعْفَرِ بْنِ رَبِيعَةَ، عَنْ عِرَاك [بْنِ مَالِكٍ] ، بِهِ مِثْلَهُ .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ مُثَنَّى، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الْأَعْلَى، حَدَّثَنَا دَاوُدُ بْنُ أَبِي هِنْدَ، عَنْ عَلِيِّ بْنِ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿اقْتَرَبَتِ السَّاعَةُ وَانْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ. وَإِنْ يَرَوْا آيَةً يُعْرِضُوا وَيَقُولُوا سِحْرٌ مُسْتَمِرٌّ﴾ قَالَ: قَدْ مَضَى ذَلِكَ، كَانَ قَبْلَ الْهِجْرَةِ، انْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ حَتَّى رَأَوْا شِقَّيْهِ.

وَرَوَى العَوْفي، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ نَحْوَ هَذَا.

وَقَالَ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ عَمْرٍو الْبَزَّارُ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ يَحْيَى القُطَعِي، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ بَكْرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنِ جُرَيْجٍ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ دِينَارٍ، عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: كُسِفَ الْقَمَرُ عَلَى عَهْدِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالُوا: سُحِر الْقَمَرُ. فَنَزَلَتْ: ﴿اقْتَرَبَتِ السَّاعَةُ وَانْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ﴾ إِلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿مُسْتَمِرٌّ﴾ .

رِوَايَةُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عُمَرَ:

قَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو بَكْرٍ الْبَيْهَقِيُّ: أَخْبَرَنَا أَبُو عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الْحَافِظُ وَأَبُو بَكْرٍ أَحْمَدُ بْنُ الْحَسَنِ الْقَاضِي قَالَا حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو الْعَبَّاسِ الْأَصَمُّ، حَدَّثَنَا الْعَبَّاسُ بْنُ مُحَمَّدٍ الدُّورِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا وَهْبُ بْنُ جَرِيرٍ، عَنْ شُعْبَةَ، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عُمَرَ فِي قَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿اقْتَرَبَتِ السَّاعَةُ وَانْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ﴾ قَالَ: وَقَدْ كَانَ ذَلِكَ عَلَى عَهْدِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم انْشَقَّ فِلْقَتَين: فِلْقَة مِنْ دُونِ الْجَبَلِ، وَفِلْقَةٌ مِنْ خَلْفِ الْجَبَلِ، فَقَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: "اللَّهُمَّ اشهد".

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ، وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ، مِنْ طُرُقٍ عَنْ شُعْبَةَ، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ، بِهِ . قَالَ مُسْلِمٌ كَرِوَايَةِ مُجَاهِدٍ عَنْ أَبِي مَعْمَرٍ عَنِ ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ. وَقَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ.

رِوَايَةُ عبد الله ابن مَسْعُودٍ:

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، عَنِ ابْنِ أَبِي نَجِيح، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ، عَنْ أَبِي مَعْمَر، عَنِ ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ قَالَ: انْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ عَلَى عَهْدِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ شِقَّيْنِ حَتَّى نَظَرُوا إِلَيْهِ، فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ "اشْهَدُوا".

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ وَمُسْلِمٌ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ سُفْيَانَ بْنِ عُيَيْنَةَ، بِهِ . وَأَخْرَجَاهُ مِنْ حَدِيثِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، عَنْ أَبِي مَعْمَرٍ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ سَخْبَرَة، عَنِ ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ، بِهِ .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنِي عِيسَى بْنُ عُثْمَانَ بْنِ عِيسَى الرَّمْلِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عَمِّي يَحْيَى بْنُ عِيسَى، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، عَنْ رَجُلٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهُ، قَالَ: كُنَّا مَعَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ بِمِنَى فَانْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ، فَأَخَذَتْ فِرْقَةٌ خَلْفَ الْجَبَلِ، فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "اشْهَدُوا، اشْهَدُوا" .

قَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ: وَقَالَ أَبُو الضُّحَى، عَنْ مَسْرُوقٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ: بِمَكَّةَ .

وَقَالَ أَبُو دَاوُدَ الطَّيَالِسِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو عَوَانَةَ، عَنِ الْمُغِيرَةِ، عَنْ أَبِي الضُّحَى، عَنْ مَسْرُوقٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ مَسْعُودٍ، قَالَ: انْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ عَلَى عَهْدِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، فَقَالَتْ قُرَيْشٌ: هَذَا سِحْرُ ابْنِ أَبِي كَبْشَةَ. قَالَ: فَقَالُوا: انْظُرُوا مَا يَأْتِيكُمْ بِهِ السّفَّار، فَإِنَّ مُحَمَّدًا لَا يَسْتَطِيعُ أَنْ يَسْحَرَ النَّاسَ كُلَّهُمْ. قَالَ: فَجَاءَ السّفَّار فَقَالُوا: ذَلِكَ .

وَقَالَ الْبَيْهَقِيُّ: أَخْبَرَنَا أَبُو عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الْحَافِظُ، أَخْبَرْنَا أَبُو الْعَبَّاسِ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ يَعْقُوبَ، حَدَّثَنَا الْعَبَّاسُ بْنُ مُحَمَّدٍ الدَّوْري، حَدَّثَنَا سَعِيدُ بْنُ سُلَيْمَانَ، حَدَّثَنَا هُشَيْم، حَدَّثَنَا مُغِيرَةُ، عَنْ أَبِي الضُّحَى، عَنْ مَسْرُوقٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ، قَالَ: انْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ بِمَكَّةَ حَتَّى صَارَ فِرْقَتَيْنِ، فَقَالَ كُفَّارُ قُرَيْشٍ أَهْلُ مَكَّةَ: هَذَا سِحْرٌ سَحَرَكُمْ بِهِ ابْنُ أَبِي كَبْشَة، انْظُرُوا السُّفَّارَ، فَإِنْ كَانُوا رَأَوْا مَا رَأَيْتُمْ فَقَدْ صَدَقَ، وَإِنْ كَانُوا لَمْ يَرَوْا مِثْلَ مَا رَأَيْتُمْ فَهُوَ سِحْرٌ سَحَرَكُمْ بِهِ. قَالَ: فَسُئِلَ السُّفَّارُ، قَالَ: وَقَدِمُوا مِنْ كُلِّ وُجْهَةٍ، فَقَالُوا: رَأَيْنَاهُ.

رَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ مِنْ حَدِيثِ الْمُغِيرَةِ، بِهِ . وَزَادَ: فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ: ﴿اقْتَرَبَتِ السَّاعَةُ وَانْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ﴾ . ثُمَّ قَالَ ابْنُ جرير: حَدَّثَنِي يَعْقُوبُ بْنُ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ عُلَيَّة، أَخْبَرَنَا أَيُّوبُ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدٍ -هُوَ ابْنُ سِيرِينَ-قَالَ: نُبِّئْتُ أَنَّ ابْنَ مَسْعُودٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، كَانَ يَقُولُ: لَقَدِ انْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ أَيْضًا: حَدَّثَنِي مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عِمَارَةَ، حَدَّثَنَا عَمْرُو بْنُ حَمَّادٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَسْبَاطٌ، عَنْ سِمَاكٍ، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، عَنِ الْأَسْوَدِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ قَالَ: لَقَدْ رَأَيْتُ الْجَبَلَ مِنْ فَرْج الْقَمَرِ حِينَ انْشَقَّ.

وَرَوَاهُ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ عَنْ مُؤَمَّل، عَنْ إِسْرَائِيلَ، عَنْ سِمَاك، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، عَنِ الْأَسْوَدِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ، قَالَ: انْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ عَلَى عَهْدِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، حَتَّى رَأَيْتُ الْجَبَلَ مِنْ بَيْنِ فُرْجَتَيِ الْقَمَرِ .

وَقَالَ لَيْثٌ عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ: انْشَقَّ الْقَمَرُ عَلَى عَهْدِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَصَارَ فِرْقَتَيْنِ، فَقَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ لِأَبِي بَكْرٍ: "اشْهَدْ يَا أَبَا بَكْرٍ". فَقَالَ الْمُشْرِكُونَ: سُحِر الْقَمَرُ حَتَّى انْشَقَّ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِنْ يَرَوْا آيَةً﴾ أَيْ: دَلِيلًا وَحُجَّةً وَبُرْهَانًا ﴿يُعْرِضُوا﴾ أَيْ: لَا يَنْقَادُونَ لَهُ، بَلْ يُعْرِضُونَ عَنْهُ وَيَتْرُكُونَهُ وَرَاءَ ظُهُورِهِمْ، ﴿وَيَقُولُوا سِحْرٌ مُسْتَمِرٌّ﴾ أَيْ: وَيَقُولُونَ: هَذَا الَّذِي شَاهَدْنَاهُ مِنَ الْحُجَجِ، سحرٌ سُحِرْنَا بِهِ.

وَمَعْنَى ﴿مُسْتَمِرٌّ﴾ أَيْ: ذَاهِبٌ. قَالَهُ مُجَاهِدٌ، وقَتَادَةُ، وَغَيْرُهُمَا، أَيْ: بَاطِلٌ مُضْمَحِلٌّ، لَا دَوَامَ لَهُ.

﴿وَكَذَّبُوا وَاتَّبَعُوا أَهْوَاءَهُمْ﴾ أَيْ: كَذَّبُوا بِالْحَقِّ إِذْ جَاءَهُمْ، وَاتَّبَعُوا مَا أَمَرَتْهُمْ بِهِ آرَاؤُهُمْ وَأَهْوَاؤُهُمْ مِنْ جَهْلِهِمْ وَسَخَافَةِ عَقْلِهِمْ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَكُلُّ أَمْرٍ مُسْتَقِرٌّ﴾ قَالَ قَتَادَةُ: مَعْنَاهُ: أَنَّ الْخَيْرَ وَاقِعٌ بِأَهْلِ الْخَيْرِ، وَالشَّرَّ وَاقِعٌ بِأَهْلِ الشَّرِّ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جُرَيْجٍ: مُسْتَقِرٌّ بِأَهْلِهِ. وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: ﴿وَكُلُّ أَمْرٍ مُسْتَقِرٌّ﴾ أَيْ: يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ.

وَقَالَ السُّدِّيُّ: ﴿مُسْتَقِرٌّ﴾ أَيْ: وَاقِعٌ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَلَقَدْ جَاءَهُمْ مِنَ الأنْبَاءِ﴾ أَيْ: مِنَ الْأَخْبَارِ عَنْ قِصَصِ الْأُمَمِ الْمُكَذِّبِينَ بِالرُّسُلِ، وَمَا حَلَّ بِهِمْ مِنَ الْعِقَابِ وَالنَّكَالِ وَالْعَذَابِ، مِمَّا يُتْلَى عَلَيْهِمْ فِي هَذَا الْقُرْآنِ، ﴿مَا فِيهِ مُزْدَجَرٌ﴾ أَيْ: مَا فِيهِ وَاعِظٌ لَهُمْ عَنِ الشِّرْكِ وَالتَّمَادِي عَلَى التَّكْذِيبِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿حِكْمَةٌ بَالِغَةٌ﴾ أَيْ: فِي هِدَايَتِهِ تَعَالَى لِمَنْ هَدَاهُ وَإِضْلَالِهِ لِمَنْ أَضَلَّهُ، ﴿فَمَا تُغْنِ النُّذُرُ﴾ يَعْنِي : أَيُّ شَيْءٍ تُغْنِي النُّذُرُ عَمَّنْ كَتَبَ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ الشَّقَاوَةَ، وَخَتَمَ عَلَى قَلْبِهِ؟ فَمَنِ الَّذِي يَهْدِيهِ مِنْ بَعْدِ اللَّهِ؟ وَهَذِهِ الْآيَةُ كَقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿قُلْ فَلِلَّهِ الْحُجَّةُ الْبَالِغَةُ فَلَوْ شَاءَ لَهَدَاكُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ﴾ [الْأَنْعَامِ: ١٤٩] ، وَكَذَا قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَمَا تُغْنِي الآيَاتُ وَالنُّذُرُ عَنْ قَوْمٍ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ﴾ [يونس: ١٠١] .

## 54:6-8 — verses 6-8 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: Turn away, O Muhammad, from these people who, when they see a sign, turn aside and say, "This is persistent magic." Turn away from them and wait for them, {The Day the caller calls to a thing forbidding} meaning: to a thing rejected and horrible, which is the standing place of reckoning and what it contains of trial, indeed of quakings and terrors. {Their eyes humbled} meaning: their eyes abased, {they will come out of the graves} that is, the tombs, {as if they were locusts spread abroad} meaning: as if they, in their spreading out and the speed of their movement toward the standing place of reckoning in response to the caller, were {locusts spread abroad} across the horizons. Therefore He said: {Racing ahead} meaning: hastening {toward the caller}, not opposing and not lagging behind. {The disbelievers will say, this is a difficult day} meaning: a day of intense terror, scowling and grim. {That, that Day, is a difficult day, not easy for the disbelievers} [al-Muddaththir: 9, 10].

## 54:9-17 — verses 9-17 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: {Denied} before your people, O Muhammad, {the people of Nuh denied Our servant}, that is, they openly declared disbelief in him and accused him of madness. {And they said, "A madman," and he was rebuked (izduajira)}. Mujahid said: {and he was rebuked} means he was driven away as if by madness. And it is said: {and he was rebuked} means they reprimanded him, restrained him, and threatened him: {If you do not desist, O Nuh, you will surely be among those who are stoned} [al-Shu'ara: 116]. This was said by Ibn Zayd, and this interpretation is sound and well directed.

{So he called upon his Lord, "Indeed I am overpowered, so help."} That is: I am too weak against these people and against confronting them, so You Yourself grant help to Your religion. The Almighty said: {So We opened the gates of heaven with pouring water}. Al-Suddi said: it means abundant water. {And We caused the earth to burst forth in springs}, meaning that springs gushed forth from every region of the earth, even the ovens which are places of fire burst forth as springs. {And the waters met}, meaning the water from the sky and the water from the earth, {upon a matter already decreed}, meaning a matter predetermined.

Ibn Jurayj said, on the authority of Ibn Abbas: {So We opened the gates of heaven with pouring water}, meaning abundant water. The sky did not rain before that day nor after it, and not from clouds; the gates of heaven were opened with water without any cloud on that day. So the two waters met upon a matter already decreed.

Ibn Abi Hatim reported that Ibn al-Kawwa asked Ali about the Milky Way, and he said: it is the fissure (sharaj) of the sky, and from it the sky was opened with pouring water.

{And We carried him upon a thing of planks and dusur}. Ibn Abbas, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, al-Qurazi, Qatadah, and Ibn Zayd said: these are the nails, and Ibn Jarir preferred this view. He said: its singular is disar, and it is also said dasir, just as one says habik and hibak, with the plural hubuk.

Mujahid said: the dusur are the ribs of the ship. Ikrimah and al-Hasan said: it is its front which strikes against the waves.

Al-Dahhak said: the dusur are its edge and its base.

Al-Awfi reported from Ibn Abbas: it is its breast (kalkal).

His statement: {Sailing before Our eyes}, meaning by Our command, in Our sight, under Our protection and Our guardianship, {a reward for one who had been denied}, meaning a reward for them against their disbelief in Allah, and vindication for Nuh (peace be upon him).

His statement: {And We left it as a sign}. Qatadah said: Allah preserved the ship of Nuh until the first generations of this nation encountered it. But the apparent meaning is that what is intended is the genus of ships in general, as in the Almighty's statement: {And a sign for them is that We carried their offspring in the laden ship, and We created for them the like of it in which they ride} [Ya-Sin: 41-42]. And He said: {Indeed, when the water overflowed, We carried you in the sailing vessel, that We might make it for you a reminder, and that a retaining ear might retain it} [al-Haqqah: 11-12]. For this reason He says here: {So is there any who will remember?}, meaning: is there anyone who will take heed and be admonished?

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Ibn Mas'ud said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ recited to me {fa-hal min muddakir}, and a man said, "O Abu Abd al-Rahman, is it muddakir or mudhdhakkir?" He said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ recited it to me as {muddakir}.

Al-Bukhari likewise reported this: [chain] Abdullah said: I recited before the Prophet ﷺ {fa-hal min mudhdhakir}, and the Prophet ﷺ said, {fa-hal min muddakir}.

Al-Bukhari also reported through the hadith of Shu'bah, from Abu Ishaq, from al-Aswad, from Abdullah, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to recite {fa-hal min muddakir}.

He also said: [chain] Abu Ishaq heard a man asking al-Aswad, "Is it muddakir or mudhdhakkir?" He said: I heard Abdullah recite {fa-hal min muddakir}, and he said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ recite it {fa-hal min muddakir}, with a dal.

Muslim and the compilers of the Sunan, except Ibn Majah, also transmitted this hadith through the narration of Abu Ishaq.

His statement: {So how were My punishment and My warnings?}, meaning: how was My punishment for those who disbelieved in Me and denied My messengers and did not take heed of what My warnings brought, and how did I grant vindication to them and take vengeance on their behalf.

{And We have certainly made the Qur'an easy for remembrance}, meaning: We made its wording easy, and We made its meaning easy for whoever seeks it, so that people may remember. As He said: {A Book which We have sent down to you, blessed, that they may reflect upon its verses and that those of understanding may remember} [Sad: 29], and the Almighty said: {We have only made it easy in your tongue that you may give good tidings therewith to the righteous and warn therewith a hostile people} [Maryam: 97].

Mujahid said: {And We have certainly made the Qur'an easy for remembrance} means: We made its recitation easy.

Al-Suddi said: We made its recitation easy upon the tongues.

Al-Dahhak reported from Ibn Abbas: were it not that Allah made it easy upon the tongues of human beings, none among creation would be able to speak the speech of Allah, Mighty and Majestic.

I say: among the aspects of Allah's making the recitation of the Qur'an easy for people is what was mentioned previously from the Prophet ﷺ, that he said: "Indeed this Qur'an was revealed upon seven ahruf (modes/readings)." We have already presented the hadith with its various chains and wordings in a manner sufficient enough that there is no need to repeat it here. Praise and grace belong to Allah.

His statement: {So is there any who will remember?}, meaning: is there anyone who will take heed of this Qur'an, whose memorization and meaning Allah has made easy?

Muhammad ibn Ka'b al-Qurazi said: is there anyone who will be restrained from sins?

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Matar, that is al-Warraq, said regarding the Almighty's statement {fa-hal min muddakir}: is there any seeker of knowledge who will be given aid toward it?

Al-Bukhari likewise cited this in the definitive form (jazm) from Matar al-Warraq, and Ibn Jarir transmitted it likewise, and something similar was reported from Qatadah.

## 54:18-22 — verses 18-22 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, informing about 'Ad, the people of Hud (peace be upon him): that they too denied their messenger, just as the people of Noah had done, and that He, the Exalted, sent {upon them a furious wind}, that is, the cold wind severe in coldness, {on a day of misfortune}, meaning, misfortune upon them. This was said by al-Dahhak, Qatadah, and al-Suddi. {Continuous}, its misfortune and destruction continued upon them, because it was a day in which their worldly punishment was joined to the punishment of the Hereafter.

His saying, {plucking out men as if they were trunks of uprooted palm trees}: this was because the wind would come upon one of them and lift him up until it made him disappear from sight, then it would turn him upside down onto the crown of his head, so he would fall to the ground and his head would be smashed, leaving him a headless corpse. This is why He said: {as if they were trunks of uprooted palm trees. Then how were My punishment and My warnings? And We have indeed made the Qur'an easy for remembrance, so is there any who will remember?}

## 54:23-32 — verses 23-32 (shared commentary)

### Translation

This is a report about Thamud, that they denied their messenger Salih. {So they said, "Shall we follow a single human being from among ourselves? Indeed we would then be in error and madness."} They are saying: we would truly be disappointed and lost if all of us surrendered our leadership to a single one of us!

Then they expressed astonishment that revelation should be cast upon him specifically, to the exclusion of themselves, and then they charged him with lying, saying: {"Rather, he is a lying, insolent one,"} meaning one who exceeds the bounds of falsehood. God Most High said: {"They will know tomorrow who is the lying, insolent one."} This is a severe threat to them and a firm warning.

Then He, Most High, said: {"Indeed We are sending the she-camel as a trial for them,"} meaning as a test for them. God brought forth for them a great she-camel, ten months pregnant, from a solid rock, exactly as they had requested, so that it would be God's proof against them regarding the truthfulness of Salih, peace be upon him, in what he had brought to them.

Then He said, commanding His servant and messenger Salih: {"So watch them and be patient,"} meaning: wait for what their affair will come to, and be patient with them, for the outcome will be yours and the victory will be yours, in this world and the next. {"And inform them that the water is to be divided between them,"} meaning a day for them and a day for the she-camel, as in His saying: {He said, "This is a she-camel; she has a drink and you have a drink, on a known day."} [al-Shu'ara: 155]

Regarding His saying: {"Each drink shall be attended,"} Mujahid said: when she was absent they attended to the water, and when she came they attended to the milk.

Then God, Most High, said: {"So they called their companion, and he took it upon himself and hamstrung her."} The commentators said: he was the one who hamstrung the she-camel, and his name was Qudar ibn Salif, and he was the most wretched of his people, as in His saying: {when the most wretched of them arose} [al-Shams: 12]. {"So he took it upon himself,"} meaning he dared.

{"And hamstrung her. So how was My punishment and My warning?"} meaning: so I punished them, and how was My punishment [of them] for their disbelief in Me and their denial of My messenger? {"Indeed We sent upon them a single blast, and they became like the withered stubble of a pen-builder,"} meaning they perished to the last of them, none of them remaining, and they were extinguished and stilled just as dried-up crops and plants become still. This was said by more than one of the commentators. Al-Suddi said that "al-muhtazir" refers to pasture in the desert when it dries up, burns, and is scattered by the wind.

Ibn Zayd said: the Arabs used to make an enclosure for camels and livestock out of dried thorns, and this is what is meant by His saying: {"like the withered stubble of a pen-builder."}

Sa'id ibn Jubayr said: {"like the withered stubble of a pen-builder"} refers to the crumbling dust of a wall. This is a gharib (unusual) view, and the first is stronger. And God knows best.

## 54:33-40 — verses 33-40 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, informing about the people of Lot, how they denied their messenger and opposed him, and committed the abomination of approaching males, which was the indecency that no one among the worlds had ever preceded them in. For this reason God destroyed them with a destruction He had not inflicted on any other nation. He, the Almighty, commanded Gabriel (peace be upon him), who lifted up their cities until he reached with them the horizon of the sky, then turned them upside down upon them and released them, and they were pursued by stones of baked clay, layered one upon another. This is why He says here: {We sent upon them a hasib (a violent wind carrying stones)}, that is, the stones, {except the family of Lot, whom We saved before dawn}, meaning they went out in the last part of the night and were thus saved from what struck their people. None of his people believed in Lot, not a single man, not even his wife; what befell her people befell her too. The Prophet of God, Lot, and his daughters went out from among them safely, untouched by any harm. This is why the Almighty says: {Thus do We reward whoever is grateful. And he had already warned them of Our seizure}, meaning that before the punishment descended upon them, he had already warned them of God's might and His punishment, but they paid no heed to it and did not incline their ears to it. Rather, they doubted it and disputed about it. {And they indeed sought to seduce him away from his guests}, and that was on the night when the angels came to him, Gabriel, Michael, and Israfil, in the form of handsome beardless youths, as a trial from God for them. Lot (peace be upon him) hosted them as guests, and his wicked old wife sent word to her people, informing them of Lot's guests. They came rushing toward him from every direction, so Lot shut the door against them. They set about trying to break the door; this was in the evening, and Lot (peace be upon him) kept defending and repelling them to protect his guests, saying to them: {These are my daughters}, meaning their wives (i.e., women available to them), {if you must act} [al-Hijr: 71]. {They said, "You certainly know that we have no right to your daughters,"} meaning, we have no desire for them, {"and indeed you well know what we want"} [Hud: 79]. When the situation grew intense and they refused anything but entry, Gabriel (peace be upon him) came out against them and struck their eyes with the tip of his wing, and their eyes were obliterated. It is said that their eyes sank into their faces.

And it is also said that no eyes remained for them at all, so they turned back on their heels, feeling their way along the walls, and threatening Lot (peace be upon him) until morning.

God, the Almighty, says: {And there came upon them early in the morning a settled punishment}, meaning there was no escape for them from it and no release from it. {So taste My punishment and My warnings. And We have indeed made the Qur'an easy for remembrance, so is there any who will remember?}

## 54:41-46 — verses 41-46 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, informing about Pharaoh and his people, that the Messenger of Allah Moses and his brother Aaron came to them with glad tidings if they believed, and with warning if they disbelieved, and He supported the two of them with mighty miracles and numerous signs, but they rejected all of it, so Allah seized them with the seizure of one who is Mighty and All-Powerful, meaning: Allah destroyed them and left of them neither one to report, nor a trace of an eye, nor any remnant.

Then He said: {Are your disbelievers} that is, O polytheists among the disbelievers of the Quraysh {better than those?} meaning: better than those mentioned previously, those who were destroyed because of their rejection of the messengers and their disbelief in the scriptures: are you better or are they? {Or do you have some immunity in the scriptures?} that is, do you have with you an immunity from Allah that no punishment or exemplary chastisement will befall you?

Then He said, informing about them: {Or do they say, "We are a group, victorious together"?} meaning: they believe that they support one another, and that their gathering together will be sufficient to protect them against whoever intends them harm. Allah the Almighty said: {The gathering will be defeated, and they will turn their backs in flight} meaning: their unity will be scattered and they will be overcome.

Al-Bukhari said: Ishaq narrated to us, Khalid narrated to us, from Khalid, and he also said: Muhammad narrated to us, Affan ibn Muslim narrated to us, from Wuhayb, from Khalid, from Ikrimah, from Ibn Abbas: that the Prophet ﷺ said, while he was in a small tent of his on the day of Badr: "I beseech You by Your covenant and Your promise. O Allah, if You wish, You will never be worshipped after this day." So Abu Bakr, may Allah be pleased with him, took hold of his hand and said: "That is enough for you, O Messenger of Allah! You have pressed your Lord insistently." So he went out, leaping about in his coat of mail, saying: {The gathering will be defeated, and they will turn their backs in flight. Rather, the Hour is their appointed time, and the Hour is more calamitous and more bitter.}

Al-Bukhari and al-Nasa'i likewise narrated this in more than one place, from the hadith of Khalid, who is Mihran al-Hadhdha', with this same wording.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] My father narrated to us, Abu al-Rabi' al-Zahrani narrated to us, Hammad narrated to us from Ayyub, from Ikrimah, who said: When {The gathering will be defeated, and they will turn their backs in flight} was revealed, Umar said: "Which gathering will be defeated? Which gathering will be overcome?" Umar said: "Then when the day of Badr came, I saw the Messenger of Allah ﷺ leaping about in his coat of mail, saying: {The gathering will be defeated, and they will turn their backs in flight}, and I knew its interpretation that day."

Al-Bukhari said: [chain] Ibrahim ibn Musa narrated to us, Hisham ibn Yusuf narrated to us, that Ibn Jurayj informed them: Yusuf ibn Mahek informed me, saying: "I was with Aisha, mother of the believers, and she said: 'It was revealed to Muhammad ﷺ in Mecca, and I was a young girl playing: {Rather, the Hour is their appointed time, and the Hour is more calamitous and more bitter}.'" Thus he narrated it here in an abridged form, and he narrated it at length in "The Virtues of the Qur'an," and Muslim did not include it.

## 54:47-55 — verses 47-55 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty informs us that the criminals are in error away from the truth, and in suˁur (blazing torments) from the doubts and confusion of opinions in which they find themselves. This includes everyone characterized by that, whether disbeliever or innovator, from all the sects.

Then He said: {The Day they are dragged into the Fire upon their faces}, meaning: just as they were in blazing torment, doubt, and vacillation, that condition brought them to the Fire, and just as they were people of error, they are dragged into it upon their faces, not knowing where they are going. And it is said to them in rebuke and reproach: {Taste the touch of Saqar}.

Regarding His saying: {Indeed, We have created everything according to a measure (qadar)}, this is like His saying: {And He created everything and determined it with precise determination} [al-Furqan: 2], and like His saying: {Glorify the name of your Lord, the Most High, who created and proportioned, and who determined and guided} [al-Aˁla: 1-3], meaning: He determined a measure and guided the creation toward it. For this reason, the leading scholars of the Sunnah use this noble verse as evidence for affirming Allah's prior decree (qadar) over His creation, which is His knowledge of things before their coming into being and His writing of them before their creation. With this verse and similar verses, and with the authentic hadiths conveying the same meaning, they refuted the Qadariyyah sect, who emerged in the later era of the Companions. We have discussed this matter in detail, along with the hadiths reported concerning it, in the commentary on "The Book of Faith" from "Sahih al-Bukhari," may Allah have mercy on him. Let us mention here the hadiths connected to this noble verse:

Ahmad said: [chain] Abu Hurayrah said: The polytheists of Quraysh came to the Prophet ﷺ disputing with him about al-qadar (divine decree), so it was revealed: {The Day they are dragged into the Fire upon their faces, taste the touch of Saqar. Indeed, We have created everything according to a measure}.

It was likewise narrated by Muslim, al-Tirmidhi, and Ibn Majah, from the hadith of Wakiˁ, from Sufyan al-Thawri, with the same chain.

Al-Bazzar said: [chain] from his grandfather, who said: These verses were not revealed except concerning the people of qadar: {Indeed, the criminals are in error and blazing torment. The Day they are dragged into the Fire upon their faces, taste the touch of Saqar. Indeed, We have created everything according to a measure}.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] from the Prophet ﷺ that he recited this verse: {Taste the touch of Saqar. Indeed, We have created everything according to a measure}, and said: "It was revealed concerning people from my nation who will appear at the end of time, who will deny Allah's decree."

He also narrated: [chain] Ataʼ ibn Abi Rabah said: I came to Ibn Abbas while he was drawing water from Zamzam, and the lower part of his garment had become wet, and I said to him: There has been talk about al-qadar. He said: Have they indeed done that? I said: Yes. He said: By Allah, this verse was revealed concerning no one but them: {Taste the touch of Saqar. Indeed, We have created everything according to a measure}. They are the worst of this nation, so do not visit their sick nor pray over their dead. If I saw one of them, I would gouge out his eyes with these two fingers of mine.

Imam Ahmad narrated it by another route, and in it there is a marfuˁ (raised, i.e. attributed directly to the Prophet) version, saying:

[chain] Abdullah ibn Abbas said: It was said to him that a man had come to them denying al-qadar, so he said: Point me to him, and he was blind. They said: What will you do with him, O Abu Abbas? He said: By the One in whose hand is my soul, if I could get hold of him I would bite his nose until I severed it, and if his neck fell into my hand I would crush it, for I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "It is as if I can see the women of Banu Fihr circling around al-Khazraj, their buttocks swaying, as polytheists; this is the first shirk (polytheism/associating partners with Allah) of this nation. By the One in whose hand is my soul, their evil opinion will lead them until they take Allah out of having decreed good, just as they took Him out of having decreed evil."

Then Ahmad narrated it from Abu al-Mughirah, from al-Awzaˁi, from al-ˁAlaʼ ibn al-Hajjaj, from Muhammad ibn ˁUbayd, mentioning the like of it. Neither of the two Sahihs recorded it.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Nafiˁ said: Ibn Umar had a friend from the people of Syria with whom he corresponded, and Abdullah ibn Umar wrote to him: It has reached me that you have spoken about something concerning al-qadar, so beware of writing to me, for I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "There will be, among my nation, peoples who deny al-qadar."

Abu Dawud narrated it from Ahmad ibn Hanbal, with the same chain.

Ahmad said: [chain] that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Every nation has its Magians, and the Magians of my nation are those who say there is no qadar. If they fall sick, do not visit them, and if they die, do not attend their funerals."

None among the authors of the six books narrated it by this route.

Ahmad said: [chain] Ibn Umar said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "There will be transformation (into animals) in this nation; indeed that will be among those who deny al-qadar and among the zindiqs (heretics)."

Al-Tirmidhi and Ibn Majah narrated it from the hadith of Abu Sakhr Humayd ibn Ziyad, with the same chain. Al-Tirmidhi said: Hasan sahih gharib (a sound, authentic, singular report).

Ahmad said: [chain] Tawus al-Yamani said: I heard Ibn Umar say: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Everything is by a decree, even incapacity and competence."

Muslim narrated it alone, from the hadith of Malik.

In the authentic hadith: "Seek help from Allah and do not be incapable; if something befalls you, say: Allah decreed it and did what He willed. And do not say: If only I had done such and such, it would have been thus, for indeed 'if only' opens the door to the work of Satan."

In the hadith of Ibn Abbas: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said to him: "Know that if the whole nation gathered together to benefit you with something, they could not benefit you with anything except what Allah had already written for you, and if they gathered together to harm you with something, they could not harm you with anything except what Allah had already written against you. The pens have dried and the pages have been folded."

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Al-Walid ibn Ubadah said: I went to see Ubadah while he was ill, and death seemed apparent in him, and I said: O my father, advise me and exert yourself for my sake. He said: Sit me up. When they had sat him up, he said: O my son, you will not taste the taste of faith, nor attain the true reality of knowledge of Allah, until you believe in al-qadar, its good and its evil. I said: O my father, how can I know what is the good of al-qadar and what is its evil? He said: Know that what missed you was never going to strike you, and what struck you was never going to miss you. O my son, I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "Indeed, the first thing Allah created was the Pen. Then He said to it: Write. So it flowed in that very hour with all that would come to be until the Day of Resurrection." O my son, if you die without holding to that belief, you will enter the Fire.

Al-Tirmidhi narrated it from Yahya ibn Musa al-Balkhi, from Abu Dawud al-Tayalisi, from Abd al-Wahid ibn Sulaym, from Ataʼ ibn Abi Rabah, from al-Walid ibn Ubadah, from his father, with the same chain. He said: Hasan sahih gharib.

Sufyan al-Thawri said: [chain] Ali ibn Abi Talib said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "No servant truly believes until he believes in four things: he bears witness that there is no god but Allah, and that I am the Messenger of Allah who has sent me with the truth; he believes in death; he believes in resurrection after death; and he believes in al-qadar, its good and its evil."

Al-Tirmidhi likewise narrated it from the hadith of al-Nadr ibn Shumayl, from Shuˁbah, from Mansur, with the same chain. He also narrated it from the hadith of Abu Dawud al-Tayalisi, from Shuˁbah, from Mansur, from Ribˁi, from Ali, mentioning it, and said: "This is, in my view, more authentic." Ibn Majah likewise narrated it from the hadith of Sharik, from Mansur, from Ribˁi, from Ali, with the same chain.

It is established in Sahih Muslim, in the narration of Abdullah ibn Wahb and others, from Abu Hani' al-Khawlani, from Abu Abd al-Rahman al-Hubuli, from Abdullah ibn Amr, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Allah wrote down the measures (maqadir) of created beings fifty thousand years before He created the heavens and the earth." Ibn Wahb added: {and His Throne was upon the water} [Hud: 7]. Al-Tirmidhi also narrated it and said: hasan sahih gharib (a sound, authentic, singular report).

His saying: {Our command is but a single word, like the blink of an eye}. This is a report about the effectiveness of His will in His creation, just as He reported the effectiveness of His decree (qadar) among them, saying: {Our command is but a single word}, meaning: We only command a thing once; We have no need to reinforce it with a second command. That which We command comes into being and exists like the blink of an eye, without delay of even the twinkling of an eye. How well one of the poets put it:

When Allah wills a matter, He simply
says to it: "Be," a single word, and it is.

His saying: {And We had certainly destroyed your kind} means: your likes and your predecessors among the past nations who denied the messengers. {So is there any who will remember?} means: is there anyone who will take heed from how Allah disgraced those people and decreed for them punishment, as He said: {And a barrier was placed between them and what they desired, just as was done with their kind before} [Saba: 54].

His saying: {And everything they did is in the records (al-zubur)} means: it is written against them in the books that are in the hands of the angels (peace be upon them). {And every small and great thing} of their deeds {is inscribed (mustatar)} means: it is gathered against them and written in their scrolls, leaving out neither a small nor a great deed without recording it.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] from A'ishah, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to say: "O A'ishah, beware of belittled sins, for they have a seeker from Allah."

Al-Nasa'i and Ibn Majah also narrated it, through the route of Sa'id ibn Muslim ibn Banak al-Madani, whom Ahmad, Ibn Ma'in, Abu Hatim and others considered trustworthy.

The hafiz Ibn Asakir narrated it in the biographical entry on this Sa'id ibn Muslim through another route, and then Sa'id said: I related this hadith to Amir ibn Hisham, and he said to me: Woe to you, O Sa'id ibn Muslim! Sulayman ibn al-Mughirah told me that he once committed a sin and considered it insignificant, so someone came to him in his sleep and said to him: O Sulayman:

Do not belittle any sin as small,
for tomorrow the small will become great.

The small sin, however long ago it occurred,
is with the Deity inscribed in full record.

So restrain your desire from idleness, do not be
hard to lead, and gird yourself with resolve.

Indeed the lover, when he loves his God,
his heart takes flight and he is inspired with reflection.

So ask your God for guidance with sincere intent,
for your Lord suffices as a Guide and a Helper.

His saying: {Indeed the righteous will be in gardens and rivers} means: the opposite of what the wretched are in, of misguidance, blazing fire, and being dragged upon their faces into the Fire, along with rebuke, reproach, and threat.

His saying: {In a seat of truth} means: in the abode of Allah's honor, His good pleasure, His bounty, His favor, His generosity, and His kindness. {With a Sovereign, Perfect in Ability} means: with the Mighty King, the Creator of all things and their Determiner, and He has full power over whatever they seek and desire.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] from Abdullah ibn Amr, tracing it to the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "Those who act justly will be with Allah on the Day of Resurrection upon pulpits of light, on the right hand of the Most Merciful, and both His hands are right hands: those who are just in their rule, their families, and whatever they are entrusted with."

Muslim and al-Nasa'i alone narrated it, from the hadith of Sufyan ibn Uyaynah, with a similar chain.

This is the end of the tafsir of Surat "Iqtarabat" (al-Qamar). To Allah belongs all praise and grace, and by Him is success and protection granted.

يُخْبِرُنَا تَعَالَى عَنِ الْمُجْرِمِينَ أَنَّهُمْ فِي ضَلَالٍ عَنِ الْحَقِّ، وسُعُر مِمَّا هُمْ فِيهِ مِنَ الشُّكُوكِ وَالِاضْطِرَابِ فِي الْآرَاءِ، وَهَذَا يَشْمَلُ كُلَّ مَنِ اتَّصَفَ بِذَلِكَ مِنْ كَافِرٍ وَمُبْتَدِعٍ مِنْ سَائِرِ الْفِرَقِ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿يَوْمَ يُسْحَبُونَ فِي النَّارِ عَلَى وُجُوهِهِمْ﴾ أَيْ: كَمَا كَانُوا فِي سُعُر وَشَكٍّ وَتَرَدُّدٍ أَوْرَثَهُمْ ذَلِكَ النَّارَ، وَكَمَا كَانُوا ضُلَّالًا سُحبوا فِيهَا عَلَى وُجُوهِهِمْ، لَا يَدْرُونَ أَيْنَ يَذْهَبُونَ، وَيُقَالُ لَهُمْ تَقْرِيعًا وَتَوْبِيخًا: ﴿ذُوقُوا مَسَّ سَقَرَ﴾ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنَّا كُلَّ شَيْءٍ خَلَقْنَاهُ بِقَدَرٍ﴾ ، كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَخَلَقَ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ فَقَدَّرَهُ تَقْدِيرًا﴾ [الْفُرْقَانِ:٢] وَكَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿سَبِّحِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ الأعْلَى. الَّذِي خَلَقَ فَسَوَّى. وَالَّذِي قَدَّرَ فَهَدَى﴾ [الْأَعْلَى:١-٣] أَيْ: قَدَّرَ قَدَرًا، وَهَدَى الْخَلَائِقَ إِلَيْهِ؛ وَلِهَذَا يَسْتَدِلُّ بِهَذِهِ الْآيَةِ الْكَرِيمَةِ أئمةُ السُّنَّةِ عَلَى إِثْبَاتِ قَدَر اللَّهِ السَّابِقِ لِخَلْقِهِ، وَهُوَ عِلْمُهُ الْأَشْيَاءَ قَبْلَ كَوْنِهَا وَكِتَابَتُهُ لَهَا قَبْلَ بُرْئِهَا، وَرَدُّوا بِهَذِهِ الْآيَةِ وَبِمَا شَاكَلَهَا مِنَ الْآيَاتِ، وَمَا وَرَدَ فِي مَعْنَاهَا مِنَ الْأَحَادِيثِ الثَّابِتَاتِ عَلَى الفرْقة القَدرية الَّذِينَ نَبَغُوا فِي أَوَاخِرِ عَصْرِ الصَّحَابَةِ. وَقَدْ تَكَلَّمْنَا عَلَى هَذَا الْمَقَامِ مُفَصَّلًا وَمَا وَرَدَ فِيهِ مِنَ الْأَحَادِيثِ فِي شَرْحِ "كِتَابِ الْإِيمَانِ" مِنْ "صَحِيحِ الْبُخَارِيِّ" رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ، وَلْنَذْكُرْ هَاهُنَا الْأَحَادِيثَ الْمُتَعَلِّقَةَ بِهَذِهِ الْآيَةِ الْكَرِيمَةِ:

قَالَ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا وَكِيع، حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ الثَّوْرِيُّ، عَنْ زِيَادِ بْنِ إِسْمَاعِيلَ السَّهْمِيِّ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عَبَّادِ بْنِ جَعْفَرٍ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيرَة قَالَ: جَاءَ مُشْرِكُو قُرَيْشٍ إِلَى النَّبِيِّ ﷺ يُخَاصِمُونَهُ فِي الْقَدَرِ، فَنَزَلَتْ: ﴿يَوْمَ يُسْحَبُونَ فِي النَّارِ عَلَى وُجُوهِهِمْ ذُوقُوا مَسَّ سَقَرَ. إِنَّا كُلَّ شَيْءٍ خَلَقْنَاهُ بِقَدَرٍ﴾ .

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ وَابْنُ مَاجَهْ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ وَكِيعٍ، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ الثَّوْرِيِّ، بِهِ .

وَقَالَ الْبَزَّارُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَمْرُو بْنُ عَلِيٍّ، حَدَّثَنَا الضَّحَّاكُ بْنُ مَخْلَد، حَدَّثَنَا يُونُسُ بْنُ الْحَارِثِ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ شُعَيْبٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ جده قَالَ: مَا نَزَلَتْ هَذِهِ الْآيَاتُ: ﴿إِنَّ الْمُجْرِمِينَ فِي ضَلالٍ وَسُعُرٍ. يَوْمَ يُسْحَبُونَ فِي النَّارِ عَلَى وُجُوهِهِمْ ذُوقُوا مَسَّ سَقَرَ. إِنَّا كُلَّ شَيْءٍ خَلَقْنَاهُ بِقَدَرٍ﴾ ، إِلَّا فِي أَهْلِ الْقَدَرِ .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا سَهْلُ بْنُ صَالِحٍ الْأَنْطَاكِيُّ، حَدَّثَنِي قُرَّة بْنُ حَبِيبٍ، عَنْ كِنَانَةَ حَدَّثَنَا جَرِيرُ بْنُ حَازِمٍ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ عَمْرِو بْنِ جَعْدَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ زُرَارة، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ أَنَّهُ تَلَا هَذِهِ الْآيَةَ: ﴿ذُوقُوا مَسَّ سَقَرَ. إِنَّا كُلَّ شَيْءٍ خَلَقْنَاهُ بِقَدَرٍ﴾ ، قَالَ: "نَزَلَتْ فِي أُنَاسٍ مِنْ أُمَّتِي يَكُونُونَ فِي آخِرِ الزَّمَانِ يُكَذِّبُونَ بِقَدَرِ اللَّهِ" .

وَحَدَّثَنَا الْحَسَنُ بْنُ عَرَفَةَ، حَدَّثَنَا مَرْوان بْنُ شُجَاعٍ الجزَري، عَنْ عَبْدِ الْمَلِكِ بْنِ جُرَيْج، عَنْ عَطَاءِ بْنِ أَبِي رَبَاح، قَالَ: أَتَيْتُ ابْنَ عَبَّاسٍ وَهُوَ يَنزع مِنْ زَمْزَمَ، وَقَدِ ابْتَلَّتْ أَسَافِلُ ثِيَابِهِ، فَقُلْتُ لَهُ: قَدْ تُكُلّم فِي الْقَدَرِ. فَقَالَ: أَوَ [قَدْ] فَعَلُوهَا؟ قُلْتُ: نَعَمْ. قَالَ: فَوَاللَّهِ مَا نَزَلَتْ هَذِهِ الْآيَةُ إِلَّا فِيهِمْ: ﴿ذُوقُوا مَسَّ سَقَرَ. إِنَّا كُلَّ شَيْءٍ خَلَقْنَاهُ بِقَدَرٍ﴾ ، أُولَئِكَ شِرَارُ هَذِهِ الْأُمَّةِ، فَلَا تَعُودُوا مَرْضَاهُمْ وَلَا تُصَلّوا عَلَى مَوْتَاهُمْ، إِنْ رَأَيْتُ أَحَدًا مِنْهُمْ فَقَأْتُ عَيْنَيْهِ بِأُصْبُعَيَّ هَاتَيْنِ.

وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ مِنْ وَجْهٍ آخَرَ، وَفِيهِ مَرْفُوعٌ، فَقَالَ:

حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو الْمُغِيرَةِ، حَدَّثَنَا الْأَوْزَاعِيُّ، عَنْ بَعْضِ إِخْوَتِهِ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عُبَيد الْمَكِّيِّ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، قَالَ: قِيلَ لَهُ: إِنَّ رَجُلًا قَدِمَ عَلَيْنَا يُكَذّب بِالْقَدَرِ فَقَالَ: دُلُّونِي عَلَيْهِ -وَهُوَ أَعْمَى-قَالُوا: وَمَا تَصْنَعُ بِهِ يَا أَبَا عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: وَالَّذِي نَفْسِي بِيَدِهِ لَئِنِ اسْتَمْكَنْتُ مِنْهُ لأعضَّنّ أَنْفَهُ حَتَّى أَقْطَعَهُ، وَلَئِنْ وَقَعَتْ رَقَبَتُهُ فِي يَدِي لَأَدُقَّنَّهَا؛ فَإِنِّي سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "كَأَنِّي بِنِسَاءِ بَنِي فِهْر يَطُفْنَ بِالْخَزْرَجِ، تَصْطَفِقُ أَلَيَاتُهُنَّ مُشْرِكَاتٍ، هَذَا أَوَّلُ شِرْكِ هَذِهِ الْأُمَّةِ، وَالَّذِي نَفْسِي بِيَدِهِ لَيَنْتَهِيَنَّ بِهِمْ سُوءُ رَأْيِهِمْ حَتَّى يُخْرِجُوا اللَّهَ مِنْ أَنْ يَكُونَ قَدّر خَيْرًا، كَمَا أَخْرَجُوهُ مِنْ أَنْ يَكُونَ قَدَّرَ شَرًّا" .

ثُمَّ رَوَاهُ أَحْمَدُ عَنْ أَبِي الْمُغِيرَةِ، عَنِ الْأَوْزَاعِيِّ، عَنِ الْعَلَاءِ بْنِ الْحَجَّاجِ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بن عبيد، فذكر مثله . لم يخرجوه.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ يَزِيدَ، حَدَّثَنَا سَعِيدُ بْنُ أَبِي أَيُّوبَ، حَدَّثَنِي أَبُو صَخْرٍ، عَنْ نَافِعٍ قَالَ: كَانَ لِابْنِ عُمَرَ صَدِيقٌ مِنْ أَهْلِ الشَّامِ يُكَاتِبُهُ ، فَكَتَبَ إِلَيْهِ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ عُمَرَ: إِنَّهُ بَلَغَنِي أَنَّكَ تَكَلَّمْتَ فِي شَيْءٍ مِنَ الْقَدَرِ، فَإِيَّاكَ أَنْ تَكْتُبَ إِلَيَّ، فَإِنِّي سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "سَيَكُونُ فِي أُمَّتِي أَقْوَامٌ يُكَذِّبُونَ بِالْقَدَرِ".

رَوَاهُ أَبُو دَاوُدَ، عَنْ أَحْمَدَ بْنِ حَنْبَلٍ، بِهِ .

وَقَالَ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا أَنَسُ بْنُ عِيَاضٍ، حَدَّثَنَا عُمَرُ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ مَوْلَى غُفْرَة، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عُمَرَ؛ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "لِكُلِّ أُمَّةٍ مَجُوسٌ، وَمَجُوسُ أُمَّتِي الَّذِينَ يَقُولُونَ: لَا قَدَرَ. إِنْ مَرِضُوا فَلَا تَعُودُوهُمْ، وَإِنْ مَاتُوا فَلَا تَشْهَدُوهُمْ" .

لَمْ يُخْرِجْهُ أَحَدٌ مِنْ أَصْحَابِ الْكُتُبِ السِّتَّةِ مِنْ هَذَا الْوَجْهِ.

وَقَالَ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا قُتَيْبَةُ، حَدَّثَنَا رِشْدِين، عَنْ أَبِي صَخْرٍ حُمَيد بْنِ زِيَادٍ، عَنْ نَافِعٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَرَ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم يقول: "سَيَكُونُ فِي هَذِهِ الْأُمَّةِ مَسْخٌ، أَلَا وَذَاكَ فِي الْمُكَذِّبِينَ بِالْقَدَرِ وَالزِّنْدِيقِيَّةِ".

وَرَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ وَابْنُ مَاجَهْ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي صَخْرٍ حُمَيْدِ بْنِ زِيَادٍ، بِهِ . وَقَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ غَرِيبٌ.

وَقَالَ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا إِسْحَاقُ بْنُ الطَّبَّاعِ، أَخْبَرَنِي مَالِكٌ، عَنْ زِيَادِ بْنِ سَعْدٍ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ مُسْلِمٍ، عَنْ طَاوُسٍ الْيَمَانِيِّ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ ابْنِ عُمَرَ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى الله عليه وسلم: "كل شيء بقدرن حَتَّى الْعَجْزُ وَالْكَيْسُ".

وَرَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ مُنْفَرِدًا بِهِ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ مَالِكٍ .

وَفِي الْحَدِيثِ الصَّحِيحِ: "اسْتَعِنْ بِاللَّهِ وَلَا تَعْجِزْ، فَإِنْ أَصَابَكَ أَمْرٌ فَقُلْ: قَدَّرُ اللَّهُ وَمَا شَاءَ فَعَلَ، وَلَا تَقُلْ: لَوْ أَنِّي فَعَلْتُ لَكَانَ كَذَا، فَإِنَّ لَوْ تَفْتَحُ عَمَلَ الشَّيْطَانِ" .

وَفِي حَدِيثِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عليه وسلم قَالَ لَهُ: "وَاعْلَمْ أَنَّ الْأُمَّةَ لَوِ اجْتَمَعُوا عَلَى أَنْ يَنْفَعُوكَ بِشَيْءٍ، لَمْ يَكْتُبْهُ اللَّهُ لَكَ، لَمْ يَنْفَعُوكَ، وَلَوِ اجْتَمَعُوا عَلَى أَنْ يَضُرُّوكَ بِشَيْءٍ، لَمْ يَكْتُبْهُ اللَّهُ عَلَيْكَ، لَمْ يضروك. جفّت الأقلام وطويت الصحف" .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا الْحَسَنُ بْنُ سَوَّار، حَدَّثَنَا اللَّيْثُ ، عَنْ مُعَاوِيَةَ، عَنْ أَيُّوبَ بْنِ زِيَادٍ، حَدَّثَنِي عُبَادَةُ بْنُ الْوَلِيدِ بْنِ عُبَادَةَ، حَدَّثَنِي أَبِي قَالَ: دَخَلْتُ عَلَى عُبَادَةَ وَهُوَ مَرِيضٌ أَتَخَايَلَ فِيهِ الْمَوْتَ، فَقُلْتُ: يَا أَبَتَاهُ، أَوْصِنِي وَاجْتَهِدْ لِي. فَقَالَ: أَجْلِسُونِي. فَلَمَّا أَجْلَسُوهُ قَالَ: يَا بُنَيَّ، إِنَّكَ لَمَّا تَطْعَمْ طَعْمَ الْإِيمَانِ، وَلَمْ تَبْلُغْ حَقَّ حَقِيقَةِ الْعِلْمِ بِاللَّهِ، حَتَّى تُؤْمِنَ بِالْقَدَرِ خَيْرِهِ وَشَرِّهِ. قُلْتُ: يَا أَبَتَاهُ، وَكَيْفَ لِي أَنْ أَعْلَمَ مَا خَيْرُ الْقَدَرِ وَشَرُّهُ؟ قَالَ: تَعْلَمُ أَنَّ مَا أَخْطَأَكَ لَمْ يَكُنْ لِيُصِيبَكَ، وَمَا أَصَابَكَ لَمْ يَكُنْ لِيُخْطِئَكَ. يَا بُنَيَّ، إِنِّي سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "إِنَّ أَوَّلَ مَا خَلَقَ اللَّهُ الْقَلَمُ. ثُمَّ قَالَ لَهُ: اكْتُبْ. فَجَرَى فِي تِلْكَ السَّاعَةِ بِمَا هُوَ كَائِنٌ إِلَى يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ" يَا بُنَيَّ، إِنْ متَّ ولستَ عَلَى ذَلِكَ دَخَلْتَ النَّارَ .

وَرَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ عَنْ يَحْيَى بْنِ مُوسَى البَلْخِي، عَنْ أَبِي دَاوُدَ الطَّيَالِسِيِّ، عَنْ عَبْدِ الْوَاحِدِ بْنِ سُلَيْمٍ، عَنْ عَطَاءِ بْنِ أَبِي رَبَاحٍ، عَنِ الْوَلِيدِ بْنِ عُبَادَةَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، بِهِ. وَقَالَ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ غَرِيبٌ .

وَقَالَ سُفْيَانُ الثَّوْرِيُّ، عَنْ مَنْصُورٍ، عَنْ رِبْعِي بْنِ خِرَاش، عَنْ رَجُلٍ، عَنْ عَلِيِّ بْنِ أَبِي طَالِبٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم: "لا يُؤْمِنُ عَبْدٌ حَتَّى يُؤْمِنَ بِأَرْبَعٍ: يَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ، وَأَنِّي رَسُولُ اللَّهِ، بَعَثَنِي بِالْحَقِّ، وَيُؤْمِنُ بِالْمَوْتِ، وَيُؤْمِنُ بِالْبَعْثِ بَعْدَ الْمَوْتِ، وَيُؤْمِنُ بِالْقَدَرِ خَيْرِهِ وَشَرِّهِ".

وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ النَّضْرِ بْنِ شُمَيْل، عَنْ شُعْبَةَ، عَنْ مَنْصُورٍ، بِهِ . وَرَوَاهُ مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي دَاوُدَ الطَّيَالِسِيِّ، عَنْ شُعْبَةَ، عَنْ مَنْصُورٍ، عَنْ رِبْعِيٍّ، عَنْ عَلِيٍّ، فَذَكَرَهُ وَقَالَ: "هَذَا عِنْدِي أَصَحُّ". وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ ابْنُ مَاجَهْ مِنْ حَدِيثِ شَرِيكٍ، عَنْ مَنْصُورٍ، عَنْ رِبْعِيٍّ، عَنْ عَلِيٍّ، بِهِ .

وَقَدْ ثَبَتَ فِي صَحِيحِ مُسْلِمٍ مِنْ رِوَايَةِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ وَهْبٍ وَغَيْرِهِ، عَنْ أَبِي هَانِئٍ الْخَوْلَانِيِّ، عَنْ أَبِي عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ الحُبُلي، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو، قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ اللَّهَ كَتَبَ مَقَادِيرَ الْخَلَائِقِ قَبْلَ أَنْ يَخْلُقَ السَّمَوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ بِخَمْسِينَ أَلْفَ سَنَةٍ" زَادَ ابْنُ وَهْبٍ: ﴿وَكَانَ عَرْشُهُ عَلَى الْمَاءِ﴾ [هُودٍ: ٧] . وَرَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ وَقَالَ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ غَرِيبٌ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَمَا أَمْرُنَا إِلا وَاحِدَةٌ كَلَمْحٍ بِالْبَصَرِ﴾ . وَهُوَ إِخْبَارٌ عَنْ نفوذ مشيئته في خلقه كما أخبر بِنُفُوذِ قَدَرِهِ فِيهِمْ، فَقَالَ: ﴿وَمَا أَمْرُنَا إِلا وَاحِدَةٌ﴾ أَيْ: إِنَّمَا نَأْمُرُ بِالشَّيْءِ مَرَّةً وَاحِدَةً، لَا نَحْتَاجُ إِلَى تَأْكِيدٍ بِثَانِيَةٍ، فَيَكُونُ ذَلِكَ الَّذِي نَأْمُرُ بِهِ حَاصِلًا مَوْجُودًا كَلَمْحِ الْبَصَرِ ، لَا يَتَأَخَّرُ طَرْفَةَ عَيْنٍ، وَمَا أَحْسَنَ مَا قَالَ بَعْضُ الشُّعَرَاءِ:

إذَا مَا أرَادَ اللَّهُ أمْرًا فَإِنَّما ... يقُولُ لهُ: كُنْ، قَوَلةً فَيَكُونُ ...

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَلَقَدْ أَهْلَكْنَا أَشْيَاعَكُمْ﴾ يَعْنِي: أَمْثَالَكُمْ وَسَلَفَكُمْ مِنَ الْأُمَمِ السَّابِقَةِ الْمُكَذِّبِينَ بِالرُّسُلِ، ﴿فَهَلْ مِنْ مُدَّكِرٍ﴾ أَيْ: فَهَلْ مِنْ مُتَّعِظٍ بِمَا أَخْزَى اللَّهُ أُولَئِكَ، وَقَدَّرَ لَهُمْ مِنَ الْعَذَابِ، كَمَا قَالَ: ﴿وَحِيلَ بَيْنَهُمْ وَبَيْنَ مَا يَشْتَهُونَ كَمَا فُعِلَ بِأَشْيَاعِهِمْ مِنْ قَبْلُ﴾ [سَبَأٍ: ٥٤] .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَكُلُّ شَيْءٍ فَعَلُوهُ فِي الزُّبُرِ﴾ أَيْ: مَكْتُوبٌ عَلَيْهِمْ فِي الْكُتُبِ الَّتِي بِأَيْدِي الْمَلَائِكَةِ عَلَيْهِمُ السَّلَامُ، ﴿وَكُلُّ صَغِيرٍ وَكَبِيرٍ﴾ أَيْ: مِنْ أَعْمَالِهِمْ ﴿مُسْتَطَرٌ﴾ أَيْ: مَجْمُوعٌ عَلَيْهِمْ، وَمُسَطَّرٌ فِي صَحَائِفِهِمْ، لَا يُغَادِرُ صَغِيرَةً وَلَا كَبِيرَةً إِلَّا أَحْصَاهَا.

وَقَدْ قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو عَامِرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا سَعِيدُ بْنُ مُسْلِمِ بْنِ بَانَكَ: سَمِعْتُ عَامِرَ بْنَ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ الزُّبَيْرِ، حَدَّثَنِي عَوْفُ بْنُ الْحَارِثِ -وَهُوَ ابْنُ أَخِي عَائِشَةَ لِأُمِّهَا-عَنْ عَائِشَةَ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ كَانَ يَقُولُ: "يَا عَائِشَةُ، إِيَّاكِ ومُحَقِّرات الذُّنُوبِ، فَإِنَّ لَهَا مِنَ اللَّهِ طَالِبًا".

وَرَوَاهُ النَّسَائِيُّ وَابْنُ مَاجَهْ، مِنْ طَرِيقِ سَعِيدِ بْنِ مُسْلِمِ بْنِ بَانَكَ الْمَدَنِيِّ . وَثَّقَهُ أَحْمَدُ، وَابْنُ مَعِينٍ، وَأَبُو حَاتِمٍ، وَغَيْرُهُمْ.

وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ الْحَافِظُ ابْنُ عَسَاكِرَ فِي تَرْجَمَةِ سَعِيدِ بْنِ مُسْلِمٍ هَذَا مِنْ وَجْهٍ آخَرَ ، ثُمَّ قَالَ سَعِيدٌ: فَحَدَّثْتُ بِهَذَا الْحَدِيثِ عَامِرَ بْنَ هِشَامٍ فَقَالَ لِي: وَيْحَكَ يَا سَعِيدُ بْنَ مُسْلِمٍ! لَقَدْ حَدَّثَنِي سُلَيْمَانُ بْنُ الْمُغِيرَةِ أَنَّهُ عَمِلَ ذَنْبًا فَاسْتَصْغَرَهُ، فَأَتَاهُ آتٍ فِي مَنَامِهِ فَقَالَ لَهُ: يَا سُلَيْمَانُ:

لَا تَحْقِرنَّ مِنَ الذنوبِ صَغِيرا ... إِنَّ الصَّغير غَدًا يَعُودُ كَبِيرَا ...

إِنَّ الصَّغِيرَ وَلَوْ تَقَادَمَ عَهْدُهُ ... عِنْدَ الْإِلَهِ مُسَطَّرٌ تَسْطِيرَا ...

فَازْجُرْ هَوَاكَ عَنِ الْبَطَالَةِ لَا تَكُنْ ... صَعْبَ الْقِيَادِ وَشَمِّرَنْ تَشْمِيرَا ...

إِنَّ المُحِبَّ إِذَا أَحَبَّ إلههُ ... طَارَ الْفُؤَادُ وأُلْهِم التَّفْكِيرَا ...

فَاسْأَلْ هِدَايَتَكَ الْإِلَهَ بِنِيَّة ... فَكَفَى بِرَبّكَ هَادِيًا وَنَصِيرَا وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنَّ الْمُتَّقِينَ فِي جَنَّاتٍ وَنَهَرٍ﴾ أَيْ: بِعَكْسِ مَا الْأَشْقِيَاءُ فِيهِ مِنَ الضَّلَالِ والسُّعر وَالسَّحْبِ فِي النَّارِ عَلَى وُجُوهِهِمْ، مَعَ التَّوْبِيخِ وَالتَّقْرِيعِ وَالتَّهْدِيدِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فِي مَقْعَدِ صِدْقٍ﴾ أَيْ: فِي دَارِ كَرَامَةِ اللَّهِ وَرِضْوَانِهِ وَفَضْلِهِ، وَامْتِنَانِهِ وَجُودِهِ وَإِحْسَانِهِ، ﴿عِنْدَ مَلِيكٍ مُقْتَدِرٍ﴾ أَيْ: عِنْدَ الْمَلِكِ الْعَظِيمِ الْخَالِقِ لِلْأَشْيَاءِ كُلِّهَا وَمُقَدِّرِهَا، وَهُوَ مُقْتَدِرٌ عَلَى مَا يَشَاءُ مِمَّا يَطْلُبُونَ وَيُرِيدُونَ؛ وَقَدْ قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ:

حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، عَنِ عَمْرِو بْنِ دِينَارٍ، عَنِ عَمْرِو بْنِ أَوْسٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو -يَبلُغُ بِهِ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ-قال: "المقسطون عِنْدَ اللَّهِ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ عَلَى مَنَابِرَ مِنْ نُورٍ، عَنْ يَمِينِ الرَّحْمَنِ، وَكِلْتَا يَدَيْهِ يَمِينٌ: الَّذِينَ يَعْدِلُونَ فِي حُكْمِهِمْ وَأَهْلِيهِمْ وَمَا وُلُّوا".

انْفَرَدَ بِإِخْرَاجِهِ مُسْلِمٌ وَالنَّسَائِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ سُفْيَانَ بْنِ عُيَيْنَةَ، بِإِسْنَادِهِ مِثْلَهُ .

آخِرُ تَفْسِيرِ سُورَةِ "اقْتَرَبَتْ"، وَلِلَّهِ الْحَمْدُ وَالْمِنَّةُ وَبِهِ التَّوْفِيقُ وَالْعِصْمَةُ
