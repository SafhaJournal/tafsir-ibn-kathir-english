## 89:1-14 — verses 1-14 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Tafsir of Surah al-Fajr (the chapter of the Dawn)

It is Meccan.

An-Nasa'i said: [chain] Jabir said: Mu'adh led a prayer, and a man came and prayed with him, but he prolonged it, so the man prayed in a corner of the mosque and then left. That reached Mu'adh, so he said, "A hypocrite!" That was mentioned to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, so he asked the young man, and he said, "O Messenger of Allah, I came to pray with him, but he prolonged the prayer for me, so I left and prayed in a corner of the mosque, for I had my nadih (a camel used to draw water for irrigation) tied up." The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "Are you a fattan (one who causes people trial and drives them away), O Mu'adh? Where are you from {Glorify the name of your Lord, the Most High}, {By the sun and its morning brightness}, {By the dawn}, {By the night when it covers}?"

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

* *

As for al-fajr (the dawn), it is well known: it is the subh (dawn/morning). This was said by Ali, Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, Ikrimah, and al-Suddi. And it is related from Masruq, Mujahid, and Muhammad ibn Ka'b that what is meant by it is specifically the dawn of the Day of Sacrifice (yawm al-nahr), which is the conclusion of the ten nights.

And it is said that what is meant by it is the prayer that is performed at that time, as Ikrimah said.

And it is said that what is meant by it is the whole day. This is a report from Ibn Abbas.

As for the ten nights: what is meant by them is the ten [days] of Dhul-Hijjah, as Ibn Abbas, Ibn al-Zubayr, Mujahid, and more than one of the salaf (early generations) and khalaf (later generations) have said. It is established in Sahih al-Bukhari, from Ibn Abbas, as a marfu' report (one attributed to the Prophet ﷺ): "There are no days in which righteous deeds are more beloved to Allah than these days" - meaning the ten days of Dhul-Hijjah - they said, "Not even jihad in the path of Allah?" He said, "Not even jihad in the path of Allah, except for a man who goes out with his life and his wealth and then does not return with any of that."

And it is said that what is meant by that is the first ten [days] of Muharram. Abu Ja'far Ibn Jarir related this without attributing it to anyone. Abu Kudaynah reported, from Qabus ibn Abi Zibyan, from his father, from Ibn Abbas, concerning {and ten nights}: he said it is the first ten [nights] of Ramadan.

The correct view is the first one. Imam Ahmad said:

[chain] Jabir, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "The ten is the ten of al-Adha (the Sacrifice), the witr (odd) is the Day of Arafah, and the shaf' (even) is the Day of Sacrifice."

An-Nasa'i related it from Muhammad ibn Rafi' and Abdah ibn Abdullah, each of them from Zayd ibn al-Hubab, with the same chain. Ibn Jarir and Ibn Abi Hatim also related it from the hadith of Zayd ibn al-Hubab, with the same chain. This is a chain whose narrators are not objectionable, but in my view there is nakarah (a suspicious irregularity) in the matn (textual content) being raised [to the Prophet ﷺ]. And Allah knows best.

* *

Regarding His saying {By the even and the odd}: it has already come in this hadith that the odd is the Day of Arafah, since it is the ninth [day], and that the even is the Day of Sacrifice, since it is the tenth [day]. Ibn Abbas, Ikrimah, and al-Dahhak also said this.

A second view: Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Wasil ibn al-Sa'ib said: I asked Ata about His saying {By the even and the odd}. I said, "Is it our prayer, this witr of ours?" He said, "No, rather the even is the Day of Arafah, and the odd is the night of al-Adha (the Sacrifice)."

A third view: Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Abu Sa'id ibn Awf, who narrated to him at Mecca, said: I heard Abdullah ibn al-Zubayr delivering a sermon to the people, and a man stood up to him and said, "O Commander of the Faithful, tell me about the even and the odd." He said, "The even is the saying of Allah, Mighty and Majestic is He, {And whoever hastens in two days, there is no sin upon him}, and the odd is His saying, {and whoever delays, there is no sin upon him}" [al-Baqarah: 203].

Ibn Jurayj said: Muhammad ibn al-Murtafi' informed me that he heard Ibn al-Zubayr say, "The even is the middle day of the days of Tashriq (the three days following the Day of Sacrifice), and the odd is the last day of the days of Tashriq."

And in the two Sahihs, from the report of Abu Hurayrah, from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ: "Allah has ninety-nine names, one hundred less one; whoever enumerates them enters Paradise, and He is witr (one/odd) and loves the witr."

A fourth view: al-Hasan al-Basri and Zayd ibn Aslam said: creation, all of it, is even and odd; He, exalted is He, swore by His creation. This is also a report from Mujahid, though the well-known view from him is the first one.

Al-Awfi said, from Ibn Abbas, concerning {By the even and the odd}: he said, "Allah is the One and Only Odd, and you are even." And it is said: the even is the dawn prayer, and the odd is the sunset prayer.

A fifth view: Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Mujahid, concerning {By the even and the odd}: he said, "The even is the pair (al-zawj), and the odd is Allah, Mighty and Majestic."

Abu Abdullah said, from Mujahid: Allah is the odd, and His creation is the even, the male and the female.

Ibn Abi Najih said, from Mujahid, concerning His saying {By the even and the odd}: everything Allah created is even/paired: the heaven and the earth, the land and the sea, the jinn and mankind, the sun and the moon, and the like of that. Mujahid, in this, followed the same direction as what they mentioned concerning His saying, exalted is He, {And of everything We created pairs, that you might remember} [al-Dhariyat: 49], meaning: so that you may know that the Creator of the pairs is One.

A sixth view: Qatadah said, from al-Hasan: {By the even and the odd} is number; some of it is even and some of it is odd.

A seventh view, concerning this noble verse, was related by Ibn Abi Hatim and Ibn Jarir through the route of Ibn Jurayj. Ibn Jarir then said: And a report has been related from the Prophet ﷺ that supports the view we mentioned from Abu al-Zubayr: [chain] Jabir, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "The even is the two days, and the odd is the third day."

This is how this report has come with this wording, and it differs from the wording that preceded it in the narration of Ahmad, al-Nasa'i, and Ibn Abi Hatim, and what he himself related as well. And Allah knows best.

Abu al-Aliyah, al-Rabi' ibn Anas, and others said: it is the prayer; some of it is even, like the four-unit and two-unit prayers, and some of it is odd, like the sunset prayer, for it is three units, and it is the odd of the daytime [prayers]. And likewise the witr prayer at the end of the night vigil (tahajjud) of the night.

Abd al-Razzaq said, from Ma'mar, from Qatadah, from Imran ibn Husayn, concerning {By the even and the odd}: he said, "It is the obligatory prayer; some of it is even and some of it is odd." This is munqati' (a report with a broken chain) and mawquf (a report halted at a Companion, not attributed to the Prophet), and its wording is specific to the obligatory prayer. It has also been related with a connected chain, elevated to the Prophet ﷺ, and its wording is general. Imam Ahmad said:

[chain] Qatadah, from Imran ibn Isam, that a shaykh from the people of Basra narrated to him, from Imran ibn Husayn: that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was asked about the even and the odd, and he said, "It is the prayer; some of it is even, and some of it is odd."

This is how it appears in the Musnad, and Ibn Jarir related it likewise from Bundar, from Affan, and from Abu Kurayb, from Ubaydullah ibn Musa, both of them from Hammam - who is Ibn Yahya - from Qatadah, from Imran ibn Isam, from a shaykh, from Imran ibn Husayn. Abu Isa al-Tirmidhi likewise related it, from Amr ibn Ali, from Ibn Mahdi and Abu Dawud, both of them from Hammam, from Qatadah, from Imran ibn Isam, from a man from the people of Basra, from Imran ibn Husayn, with the same content. He then said: "Gharib (singular in its transmission); we do not know it except from the hadith of Qatadah." Khalid ibn Qays also related it from Qatadah.

It has also been related from Imran ibn Isam, from Imran himself. And Allah knows best.

I say: Ibn Abi Hatim also related it: [chain] Qatadah, from Imran ibn Isam al-Duba'i - a shaykh from the people of Basra - from Imran ibn Husayn, from the Prophet ﷺ, and he mentioned it. This is how I saw it in his tafsir, where he made the Basran shaykh to be Imran ibn Isam al-Duba'i himself.

Ibn Jarir likewise related it: [chain] Qatadah, from Imran ibn Isam, from Imran ibn Husayn, from the Prophet ﷺ, concerning the even and the odd: he said, "It is the prayer; some of it is even, and some of it is odd."

Thus he omitted mention of the unnamed shaykh, and Imran ibn Isam al-Duba'i, Abu Umarah al-Basri, imam of the mosque of Banu Duba'i and father of Abu Jamrah Nasr ibn Imran al-Duba'i, transmitted it alone.

Qatadah, his son Abu Jamrah, al-Muthanna ibn Sa'id, and Abu al-Tayyah Yazid ibn Humayd narrated from him. Ibn Hibban mentioned him in Kitab al-Thiqat (The Book of the Trustworthy), and Khalifah ibn Khayyat mentioned him among the Tabi'in (Successors, the generation after the Companions) from the people of Basrah. He was noble, distinguished, and favored with al-Hajjaj ibn Yusuf, then al-Hajjaj killed him on the day of al-Zawiyah, in the year eighty-three, for his going out with Ibn al-Ash'ath. He has nothing with al-Tirmidhi except this one hadith. In my view, that it be reported as mawquf (a halted report, attributed only to the Companion) upon 'Imran ibn Husayn is more fitting. And Allah knows best.

Ibn Jarir did not decide firmly on any of these sayings concerning the even and the odd.

* *

His saying, {By the night when it journeys on}: al-'Awfi said, on the authority of Ibn 'Abbas: meaning, when it goes away.

'Abdullah ibn al-Zubayr said: {By the night when it journeys on}, until one part of it drives away another.

Mujahid, Abu al-'Aliyah, Qatadah, and Malik, on the authority of Zayd ibn Aslam and Ibn Zayd, said: {By the night when it journeys on}, meaning when it travels.

This can be carried on what Ibn 'Abbas said, namely that it means it goes away. It is also possible that what is meant by "when it travels" is that it comes, that is, it advances. It may be said that this is more fitting, because it stands opposite His saying {By the dawn}, for the dawn is the advancing of the day and the retreating of the night. So if His saying {By the night when it journeys on} is carried on its advancing, it becomes an oath by the advancing of the night and the retreating of the day, and conversely by the dawn, as in His saying {By the night when it grows dark and the dawn when it breathes} [al-Takwir: 17, 18]. Likewise al-Dahhak said: {[By the night] when it journeys on}, meaning it flows on.

'Ikrimah said: {By the night when it journeys on} means the night of Jam' (the gathering, i.e. Muzdalifah). Narrated by Ibn Jarir and Ibn Abi Hatim.

Then Ibn Abi Hatim said: Ahmad ibn 'Isam narrated to us, Abu 'Amir narrated to us, Kathir ibn 'Abdullah ibn 'Amr narrated to us, he said: I heard Muhammad ibn Ka'b al-Qurazi saying regarding His saying {By the night when it journeys on}: he said: travel by night, O traveler, and do not appear except at Jam'.

* *

His saying, {Is there in that an oath for one who has hijr?}, meaning: for one who has 'aql (intellect), lubb (understanding), and hija [and religion]. The intellect is only called hijr because it restrains a person from engaging in what does not befit him of deeds and words. From this comes hijr al-bayt, because it prevents the one circling the House from clinging to its northern wall. From this too comes Hijr al-Yamamah, and one says "the judge placed hajr upon so-and-so" when he prevents him from disposing of his property, {and they will say, forbidden, utterly forbidden} [al-Furqan: 22]. All of this belongs to one category and a closely related meaning. This oath is by the times of worship, and by worship itself of hajj, prayer, and other kinds of acts of nearness by which His servants who are God-fearing, obedient to Him, fearful of Him, humble before Him, and reverent before His noble Face draw near to Him.

When He had mentioned these people, their worship, and their obedience, He said after that: {Have you not seen how your Lord dealt with 'Ad?} These were rebellious, insolent tyrants, who went out from obedience to Him, denying His messengers and rejecting His books. So the Exalted mentioned how He destroyed them and annihilated them and made them tales and lessons, saying: {Have you not seen how your Lord dealt with 'Ad, Iram, possessor of lofty pillars?} These were 'Ad the First, the descendants of 'Ad ibn Iram ibn 'Aws ibn Sam ibn Nuh, as Ibn Ishaq said. They were the ones to whom Allah sent His messenger Hud (peace be upon him), but they denied him and opposed him, so Allah saved him from among them, along with those who believed with him, and destroyed them with a furious, raging wind, {which He imposed upon them for seven nights and eight days in succession, so that you would see the people therein fallen prostrate, as if they were hollow trunks of palm trees. Then do you see any remnant of them?} [al-Haqqah: 7, 8]. Allah has mentioned their story in the Qur'an in more than one place, so that believers might take heed from their fall.

His saying, the Exalted, {Iram, possessor of lofty pillars}, is 'atf bayan (an appositive added for further clarification), an additional identification of them.

* *

His saying, {possessor of lofty pillars}: because they used to dwell in tents woven of hair that were raised up with strong pillars, and they were the most powerful people of their time in build and the strongest of them in force. For this reason Hud reminded them of that blessing and guided them to employ it in obedience to their Lord who created them, saying: {And remember when He made you successors after the people of Nuh and increased you amply in stature; so remember the favors of Allah, that you might succeed} [al-A'raf: 69]. And the Exalted said: {As for 'Ad, they were arrogant in the land without right and said, who is mightier than us in strength? Did they not see that Allah, who created them, is mightier than them in strength?} [Fussilat: 15]. And He said here: {the like of which had not been created in the lands}, meaning: the tribe, the like of which had not been created in their lands, on account of their strength, their might, and the greatness of their build.

Mujahid said: Iram was an ancient nation, meaning 'Ad the First, just as Qatadah ibn Di'amah and al-Suddi said: Iram was the seat of the kingdom of 'Ad. This is a good, sound, strong saying.

Mujahid, Qatadah, and al-Kalbi said regarding His saying, {possessor of lofty pillars}: they were people of the tent pole, who did not settle in one place.

Al-'Awfi said, on the authority of Ibn 'Abbas: they were only called {possessor of lofty pillars} because of their great height.

Ibn Jarir chose the first saying and rejected the second, and he was correct.

* *

His saying, {the like of which had not been created in the lands}: Ibn Zayd returned the pronoun to the pillars, because of their loftiness, and said: they built pillars in al-Ahqaf the like of which had not been created in the lands. As for Qatadah and Ibn Jarir, they returned the pronoun to the tribe, meaning: the like of that tribe had not been created in the lands, that is, in their time. This saying is the correct one, and the saying of Ibn Zayd and those who followed his view is weak, because had that been intended, He would have said "the like of which had not been made in the lands," but instead He said {had not been created in the lands}.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: my father narrated to us, Abu Salih the scribe of al-Layth narrated to us, Mu'awiyah ibn Salih narrated to us, from someone who narrated to him, from al-Miqdam, from the Prophet ﷺ, that he mentioned Iram, possessor of lofty pillars, and said: "A man among them would come to a rock and carry it upon the settlement, and so destroy them."

Then Ibn Abi Hatim said: 'Ali ibn al-Husayn narrated to us, Abu al-Tahir narrated to us, Anas ibn 'Iyad narrated to us, from Thawr ibn Zayd al-Dili, who said: I read a document, and he named the place where he read it: I am Shaddad ibn 'Ad. I am the one who raised the pillars. I am the one who bound with my forearm the sight of a single man. I am the one who buried a treasure at a depth of seven cubits, which none shall bring out except the nation of Muhammad ﷺ.

I say: on every one of these sayings, whether the pillars were buildings they constructed, or the poles of their tents as nomads, or a weapon with which they fought, or the height of each one of them, they were a tribe and a nation among the nations, and they are the ones mentioned in the Qur'an in more than one place, coupled with Thamud as here. And Allah knows best. As for whoever claims that what is meant by His saying, {Iram, possessor of lofty pillars}, is a city, either Damascus, as was narrated from Sa'id ibn al-Musayyib and 'Ikrimah, or Alexandria, as was narrated from al-Qurazi, or some other city, there is doubt in this. For how would the speech cohere on this view: {Have you not seen how your Lord dealt with 'Ad, Iram, possessor of lofty pillars?}, if that were made a badal (substitute) or an 'atf bayan? For the speech would not be coherent in that case. Rather, what is meant is only the report of the destruction of the tribe named 'Ad and what Allah brought down upon them of His punishment which cannot be turned back, not that what is meant is a report about a city or a region.

I have only drawn attention to this so that one not be deceived by much of what a group of exegetes have mentioned concerning this verse: the mention of a city called {Iram, possessor of lofty pillars}, built of bricks of gold and silver, with its palaces, its houses, and its orchards, whose pebbles are said to be pearls and jewels, whose dust is musk pellets, whose rivers flow freely, whose fruits fall of themselves, whose houses have no one dwelling in them, and whose wall and gates turn yellow, with none in it to call and none to answer. And that it moves about: sometimes it is in the land of al-Sham, sometimes in Yemen, sometimes in Iraq, and sometimes in other lands besides these. All of this is among the khurafat (fables) of the Isra'iliyyat (Israelite narrations), fabricated by some of their zanadiqah (heretics), so that they might test with it the minds of the ignorant among the people, to see whether they would believe them in all of that.

Al-Tha'labi and others mentioned that a man from the bedouins (namely 'Abd Allah ibn Qilabah), in the time of Mu'awiyah, went out searching for some camels of his that had strayed. While he was wandering about in search of them, he came upon a great city having a wall and gates. He entered it and found within it something close to what we have already mentioned of the description of the golden city spoken of earlier. He then returned and informed the people, so they went with him to the place he had described, but they saw nothing.

Ibn Abi Hatim mentioned the story of {Iram, possessor of pillars} here at very great length, but this account's chain of transmission is not sound. Even if it were sound back to that bedouin, he may well have invented it, or he may have been afflicted with a kind of delusion and derangement, so that he believed it had an actual reality outside himself, when it did not. This is a matter whose unsoundness can be stated with certainty. It resembles what many of the ignorant, the greedy, and the schemers report concerning the existence of hidden hoards beneath the earth, containing heaps of gold and silver, varieties of gems, rubies, and pearls, and the great elixir, but with barriers upon them preventing anyone from reaching them or taking from them. So they scheme against the wealth of the rich, the weak, and the foolish, consuming it wrongfully by spending it on fumigants and drugs and similar ravings, all the while mocking them. What can be stated with certainty is that in the earth there are buried hoards from the pre-Islamic era and from the Islamic era, and many treasures; whoever comes upon any of them is able to make use of it. But as for it being in the manner they claim, that is falsehood, fabrication, and slander, and nothing of what they say about it has been established as sound except through their own report, or the report of those who took it from them. God, glorified and exalted, is the Guide to what is correct.

As for Ibn Jarir's statement, it is possible that what is meant by His saying {Iram} is a tribe or a town that 'Ad used to inhabit, and that is why it is not inflected as a diptote, for what is meant by the context is only the reporting about the tribe. This is why He says after it: {and Thamud, who carved out the rock in the valley}, meaning: they cut into the rock in the valley. Ibn 'Abbas said: they hewed it and hollowed it out. Mujahid, Qatadah, al-Dahhak, and Ibn Zayd said likewise. From this comes the expression "mujtabi al-nimar" (those who slit open the striped cloaks), said when they tore them open; and "ijtaba al-thawb" means: when he cut the garment open. From this root also comes "al-jayb" (the opening of a garment, the pocket). God, exalted is He, said: {and you carve out houses from the mountains, exulting} [al-Shu'ara: 149]. Ibn Jarir and Ibn Abi Hatim cited here the poet's line:

Indeed, everything, save God, perishes,
just as a tribe of Shanif and Marid once perished...

They struck against every solid, towering rock,
with strong hands, powerful of forearm.

Ibn Ishaq said: they were Arabs, and their dwelling place was in Wadi al-Qura. We have already related the story of "'Ad" in full detail in Surat al-A'raf, in a manner that makes repeating it here unnecessary.

* * *

Regarding His saying {and Pharaoh, possessor of the stakes}: al-'Awfi said, on the authority of Ibn 'Abbas: the stakes are the soldiers who secured his rule for him. It is also said: Pharaoh used to peg people's hands and feet with stakes of iron, hanging them by them. Mujahid said likewise: he used to peg people with stakes. Sa'id ibn Jubayr, al-Hasan, and al-Suddi said the same. Al-Suddi said: he would tie a man down, each of his limbs to a stake, then let loose upon him a great boulder that would crush him.

Qatadah said: it has reached us that he had lookout-platforms and play-structures, made of stakes and ropes, beneath which performances were put on for him.

Thabit al-Bunani said, on the authority of Abu Rafi': Pharaoh was called {possessor of the stakes} because he drove four stakes into the ground for his wife, then placed upon her back a great millstone until she died.

* * *

Regarding His saying {those who transgressed in the lands and increased corruption therein}, meaning: they rebelled, grew insolent, and ranged through the earth working corruption and harm upon the people. {So your Lord poured upon them a scourge of punishment}, meaning: He sent down upon them a torment from the sky, and brought upon them a punishment that nothing can turn back from a criminal people.

* * *

Regarding His saying {Indeed your Lord is ever watchful}: Ibn 'Abbas said: He hears and He sees. That is: He watches over His creation in what they do, and He recompenses each one for his striving in this world and the next. All of creation will be presented before Him, and He will judge among them with His justice, meeting each one with what he deserves. He is exalted above injustice and wrongdoing.

Ibn Abi Hatim mentioned here a very gharib (rare, singularly-transmitted) hadith, one whose chain and soundness are open to question. He said: our father narrated to us, Ahmad ibn Abi al-Hawari narrated to us, Yunus al-Hadhdha' narrated to us, from Abu Hamzah al-Baysani, from Mu'adh ibn Jabal, who said: the Messenger of God ﷺ said, "O Mu'adh, indeed the believer is, before the truth, a captive. O Mu'adh, indeed the believer's fright does not settle, nor does his unease feel secure, until he has left the bridge of Hell behind his back. O Mu'adh, indeed the believer is restrained by the Qur'an from many of his desires, and from perishing through them, by the permission of God, Mighty and Majestic. So the Qur'an is his guide, fear is his path, longing is his mount, prayer is his refuge, fasting is his shield, charity is his ransom, truthfulness is his commander, and modesty is his minister; and his Lord, Mighty and Majestic, stands watchful behind all of that."

Ibn Abi Hatim said: Yunus al-Hadhdha' and Abu Hamzah are both majhul (unidentified narrators), and Abu Hamzah's report from Mu'adh is mursal (missing the link at the Companion's level). Had it been attributed only to Abu Hamzah himself, it would have been hasan (a sound, acceptable report); that is, had it been his own words rather than a report from the Prophet ﷺ, it would have been good.

Then Ibn Abi Hatim said: our father narrated to us, Safwan ibn Salih narrated to us, al-Walid ibn Muslim narrated to us, from Safwan ibn 'Amr, from Ayfa' ibn 'Abd al-Kula'i, that he heard him admonishing the people, saying: Hell has seven bridges, he said, and the Sirat (the bridge spanning Hell) runs over them. He said: the creatures are held back at the first bridge, and it is said, {Stop them, for they are to be questioned} [al-Saffat: 24]. He said: they are called to account for the prayer and questioned about it. He said: whoever is to perish perishes there, and whoever is to be saved is saved. Then when they reach the second bridge, they are called to account for the trust, how they discharged it and how they betrayed it. He said: whoever is to perish perishes, and whoever is to be saved is saved. Then when they reach the third bridge, they are asked about the tie of kinship, how they maintained it and how they severed it. He said: whoever is to perish perishes, and whoever is to be saved is saved. He said: and the tie of kinship on that day hangs down toward the plunge into Hell, saying, "O God, whoever maintained me, maintain him, and whoever severed me, sever him." He said: and it is this that God, Mighty and Majestic, refers to when He says: {Indeed your Lord is ever watchful}.

Thus he related this report, without mentioning it in full.

تَفْسِيرُ سُورَةِ الْفَجْرِ

وَهِيَ مَكِّيَّةٌ.

قَالَ النَّسَائِيُّ: أَخْبَرَنَا عَبْدُ الْوَهَّابِ بْنُ الْحَكَمِ، أَخْبَرَنِي يَحْيَى بْنُ سَعِيدٍ، عَنْ سُلَيْمَانَ، عَنْ مُحَارِبِ بْنِ دِثار وَأَبِي صَالِحٍ، عَنْ جَابِرٍ قَالَ: صَلَّى مُعَاذٌ صَلَاةً، فَجَاءَ رَجُلٌ فَصَلَّى مَعَهُ فطَول، فَصَلَّى فِي نَاحِيَةِ الْمَسْجِدِ ثُمَّ انْصَرَفَ، فَبَلَغَ ذَلِكَ مُعَاذًا فَقَالَ: مُنَافِقٌ. فَذُكِرَ ذَلِكَ لِرَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَسَأَلَ الْفَتَى، فَقَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، جِئْتُ أُصَلِّي مَعَهُ فَطَوّل عَلَيّ، فَانْصَرَفْتُ وصليتُ فِي نَاحِيَةِ الْمَسْجِدِ، فَعَلَّقْتُ نَاضِحِي. فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "أفَتَّان يَا مُعَاذُ؟ أَيْنَ أَنْتَ مِن ﴿سَبِّحِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ الأعْلَى﴾ ﴿وَالشَّمْسِ وَضُحَاهَا﴾ ﴿وَالْفَجْرِ﴾ ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَغْشَى﴾ .

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

* *

أَمَّا الْفَجْرُ فَمَعْرُوفٌ، وَهُوَ: الصُّبْحُ. قَالَهُ عَلَيٌّ، وَابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَعِكْرِمَةُ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ. وَعَنْ مَسْرُوقٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٍ، وَمُحَمَّدِ بْنِ كَعْبٍ: الْمُرَادُ بِهِ فَجْرُ يَوْمِ النَّحْرِ خَاصَّةً، وَهُوَ خَاتِمَةُ اللَّيَالِي الْعَشْرِ.

وَقِيلَ: الْمُرَادُ بِذَلِكَ الصَّلَاةُ الَّتِي تُفْعَلُ عِنْدَهُ، كَمَا قَالَهُ عِكْرِمَةُ.

وَقِيلَ: الْمُرَادُ بِهِ جَمِيعُ النَّهَارِ. وَهُوَ رِوَايَةٌ عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ.

وَاللَّيَالِي الْعَشْرُ: الْمُرَادُ بِهَا عَشَرُ ذِي الْحِجَّةِ. كَمَا قَالَهُ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَابْنُ الزُّبَيْرِ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ مِنَ السَّلَفِ وَالْخَلَفِ. وَقَدْ ثَبَتَ فِي صَحِيحِ الْبُخَارِيِّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ مَرْفُوعًا: "مَا مِنْ أَيَّامٍ الْعَمَلُ الصَّالِحُ أَحَبُّ إِلَى اللَّهِ فِيهِنَّ مِنْ هَذِهِ الْأَيَّامِ" -يَعْنِي عَشَرَ ذِي الْحِجَّةِ -قَالُوا: وَلَا الْجِهَادُ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ؟ قَالَ: "وَلَا الْجِهَادُ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ، إِلَّا رَجُلًا خَرَجَ بِنَفْسِهِ وَمَالِهِ، ثُمَّ لَمْ يَرْجِعُ مِنْ ذَلِكَ بِشَيْءٍ" .

وَقِيلَ: الْمُرَادُ بِذَلِكَ الْعَشْرُ الْأَوَّلُ مِنَ الْمُحَرَّمِ، حكاه أبو جعفر ابن جَرِيرٍ وَلَمْ يَعْزُهُ إِلَى أَحَدٍ وَقَدْ رَوَى أَبُو كُدَيْنة، عَنْ قَابُوسُ بْنُ أَبِي ظِبْيان، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿وَلَيَالٍ عَشْرٍ﴾ قَالَ: هُوَ الْعَشْرُ الْأَوَّلُ مِنْ رَمَضَانَ.

وَالصَّحِيحُ الْقَوْلُ الْأَوَّلُ؛ قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ:

حَدَّثَنَا زَيْدُ بْنُ الْحُبَابِ، حَدَّثَنَا عَيَّاش بْنُ عُقْبَةَ، حَدَّثَنِي خَير بْنُ نُعَيم، عَنْ أَبِي الزُّبَيْرِ، عَنْ جَابِرٍ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ قَالَ: "إِنَّ الْعَشْرَ عَشْرُ الْأَضْحَى، وَالْوَتْرُ يَوْمُ عَرَفَةَ، وَالشَّفْعُ يَوْمُ النَّحْرِ".

وَرَوَاهُ النَّسَائِيُّ عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ رَافِعٍ وَعَبْدَةَ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ، كُلٌّ مِنْهُمَا عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ الْحُبَابِ، بِهِ وَرَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ وَابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ زَيْدِ بْنِ الْحُبَابِ، بِهِ وَهَذَا إِسْنَادٌ رِجَالُهُ لَا بَأْسَ بِهِمْ، وَعِنْدِي أَنَّ الْمَتْنَ فِي رَفْعِهِ نَكَارَةٌ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَالشَّفْعِ وَالْوَتْرِ﴾ قَدْ تَقَدَّمَ فِي هَذَا الْحَدِيثِ أَنَّ الْوَتْرَ يَوْمُ عَرَفَةَ، لِكَوْنِهِ التَّاسِعَ، وَأَنَّ الشَّفْعَ يَوْمُ النَّحْرِ لِكَوْنِهِ الْعَاشِرَ. وَقَالَهُ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَعِكْرِمَةُ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ أَيْضًا.

قَوْلٌ ثَانٍ: وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو سَعِيدٍ الْأَشَجُّ، حَدَّثَنِي عقبة بن خالد، عن واصل ابن السَّائِبِ قَالَ: سَأَلْتُ عَطَاءً عَنْ قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَالشَّفْعِ وَالْوَتْرِ﴾ قلتُ: صَلَاتُنَا وِتْرَنَا هَذَا؟ قَالَ: لَا وَلَكِنَّ الشَّفْعَ يَوْمُ عَرَفَةَ، وَالْوَتْرَ لَيْلَةُ الْأَضْحَى.

قَوْلٌ ثَالِثٌ: قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عَامِرِ بْنِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ الْأَصْبَهَانِيُّ، حَدَّثَنِي أَبِي، عَنِ النُّعْمَانِ -يَعْنِي ابْنَ عَبْدِ السَّلَامِ-عَنْ أَبِي سَعِيدِ بْنِ عَوْفٍ، حَدَّثَنِي بِمَكَّةَ قال: سمعتُ عبد الله ابن الزُّبَيْرِ يَخْطُبُ النَّاسَ، فَقَامَ إِلَيْهِ رجلٌ فَقَالَ: يَا أَمِيرَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ، أَخْبِرْنِي عَنِ الشَّفْعِ وَالْوَتْرِ. فَقَالَ: الشَّفْعُ قَوْلُ اللَّهِ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ: ﴿فَمَنْ تَعَجَّلَ فِي يَوْمَيْنِ فَلا إِثْمَ عَلَيْهِ﴾ وَالْوَتْرُ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَمَنْ تَأَخَّرَ فَلا إِثْمَ عَلَيْهِ﴾ [الْبَقَرَةِ: ٢٠٣] .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جُرَيْجٍ: أَخْبَرَنِي مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ الْمُرْتَفِعِ أَنَّهُ سَمِعَ ابْنَ الزُّبَيْرِ يَقُولُ: الشَّفْعُ أَوْسَطُ أَيَّامِ التَّشْرِيقِ، وَالْوَتْرُ آخِرُ أَيَّامِ التَّشْرِيقِ.

وَفِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ مِنْ رِوَايَةِ أَبِي هُرَيرة، عَنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنْ لِلَّهِ تِسْعَةً وَتِسْعِينَ اسْمًا، مِائَةٌ إِلَّا وَاحِدًا، مَنْ أَحْصَاهَا دَخَلَ الْجَنَّةَ، وهو وتر يحب الوتر" .

قَوْلٌ رَابِعٌ: قَالَ الْحَسَنُ الْبَصْرِيُّ، وَزَيْدُ بْنُ أَسْلَمَ: الْخَلْقُ كُلُّهُمْ شَفْعٌ، وَوِتْرٌ، أَقْسَمَ تَعَالَى بِخَلْقِهِ. وَهُوَ رِوَايَةٌ عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ، وَالْمَشْهُورُ عَنْهُ الأول.

وَقَالَ العَوفي، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿وَالشَّفْعِ وَالْوَتْرِ﴾ قَالَ: اللَّهُ وِتْرٌ وَاحِدٌ، وَأَنْتُمْ شَفْعٌ. وَيُقَالُ: الشَّفْعُ صَلَاةُ الْغَدَاةِ، وَالْوَتْرُ: صَلَاةُ الْمَغْرِبِ.

قَوْلٌ خَامِسٌ: قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو سَعِيدٍ الْأَشَجُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عُبَيْدُ بْنُ مُوسَى، عَنْ إِسْرَائِيلَ، عَنْ أَبِي يَحْيَى، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ: ﴿وَالشَّفْعِ وَالْوَتْرِ﴾ قَالَ: الشَّفْعُ الزَّوْجُ، وَالْوَتْرُ: اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ.

وَقَالَ أَبُو عَبْدِ اللَّهِ، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ: اللَّهُ الْوَتْرُ، وَخَلْقُهُ الشَّفْعُ، الذَّكَرُ وَالْأُنْثَى.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي نَجِيح، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَالشَّفْعِ وَالْوَتْرِ﴾ كُلُّ شَيْءٍ خَلَقَهُ اللَّهُ شَفْعٌ، السَّمَاءُ وَالْأَرْضُ، وَالْبَرُّ وَالْبَحْرُ، وَالْجِنُّ وَالْإِنْسُ، وَالشَّمْسُ وَالْقَمَرُ، وَنَحْوُ هَذَا. وَنَحَا مُجَاهِدٌ فِي هَذَا مَا ذَكَرُوهُ فِي قَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَمِنْ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ خَلَقْنَا زَوْجَيْنِ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَذَكَّرُونَ﴾ [الذَّارِيَاتِ:٤٩] أَيْ: لِتَعْلَمُوا أَنَّ خَالِقَ الْأَزْوَاجِ وَاحِدٌ.

قَوْلٌ سَادِسٌ: قَالَ قَتَادَةُ، عَنِ الْحَسَنِ: ﴿وَالشَّفْعِ وَالْوَتْرِ﴾ هُوَ الْعَدَدُ، مِنْهُ شَفْعٌ وَمِنْهُ وَتْرٌ.

قَوْلٌ سَابِعٌ: فِي الْآيَةِ الْكَرِيمَةِ رَوَاهُ ابنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ وابنُ جَرير مِنْ طَرِيقِ ابْنِ جُرَيْجٍ، ثُمَّ قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: وَرُوي عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ خَبَرٌ يُؤَيِّدُ الْقَوْلَ الَّذِي ذَكَرْنَا عَنْ أَبِي الزُّبَيْرِ: حَدَّثَنِي عَبد اللَّهِ بْنُ أَبِي زِيَادٍ الْقَطَوَانِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا زَيْدُ بْنُ الْحُبَابِ، أَخْبَرَنِي عَيَّاشُ بْنُ عُقْبَةَ، حَدَّثَنِي خَيْرُ بْنُ نُعَيم، عَنْ أَبِي الزُّبَيْرِ، عَنْ جَابِرٍ: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عليه وسلم قال: "الشَّفْعُ الْيَوْمَانِ، وَالْوَتَرُ الْيَوْمُ الثَّالِثُ" .

هَكَذَا وَرَدَ هَذَا الْخَبَرُ بِهَذَا اللَّفْظِ، وَهُوَ مُخَالِفٌ لِمَا تَقَدَّمَ مِنَ اللَّفْظِ فِي رِوَايَةِ أَحْمَدَ وَالنَّسَائِيِّ وَابْنِ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، وَمَا رَوَاهُ هُوَ أَيْضًا، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

قَالَ أَبُو الْعَالِيَةِ، وَالرَّبِيعُ بْنُ أَنَسٍ، وَغَيْرُهُمَا: هِيَ الصَّلَاةُ، مِنْهَا شَفْعٌ كَالرُّبَاعِيَّةِ وَالثُّنَائِيَّةِ، وَمِنْهَا وَتْرٌ كَالْمَغْرِبِ، فَإِنَّهَا ثَلَاثٌ، وَهِيَ وَتْرُ النَّهَارِ. وَكَذَلِكَ صَلَاةُ الْوِتْرِ فِي آخِرِ التَّهَجُّدِ مِنَ اللَّيْلِ.

وَقَدْ قَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، عَنْ مَعْمَر، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ، عَنْ عِمْرَانَ بْنِ حُصَيْنٍ: ﴿وَالشَّفْعِ وَالْوَتْرِ﴾ قَالَ: هِيَ الصَّلَاةُ الْمَكْتُوبَةُ، مِنْهَا شَفْعٌ وَمِنْهَا وَتْرٌ. وَهَذَا مُنْقَطِعٌ وَمَوْقُوفٌ، وَلَفْظُهُ خَاصٌّ بِالْمَكْتُوبَةِ. وَقَدْ رُوِيَ مُتَّصِلًا مَرْفُوعًا إِلَى النَّبِيِّ ﷺ وَلَفْظُهُ عَامٌّ، قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ:

حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو دَاوُدَ -هُوَ الطَّيَالِسِيُّ-حَدَّثَنَا هَمَّامٌ، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ، عَنْ عِمْرَانَ بْنِ عِصَامٍ: أَنَّ شَيْخًا حَدَّثَهُ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْبَصْرَةِ، عَنْ عِمْرَانِ بْنِ حُصَيْنٍ: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ سُئِل عَنِ الشَّفْعِ وَالْوَتْرِ، فَقَالَ: "هِيَ الصَّلَاةُ، بَعْضُهَا شَفْعٌ، وَبَعْضُهَا وَتْرٌ" .

هَكَذَا وَقَعَ فِي الْمُسْنَدِ، وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ عَنْ بُنْدَار، عَنِ عَفَّانَ وَعَنْ أَبِي كُرَيْب، عَنْ عُبَيْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ مُوسَى، كِلَاهُمَا عَنْ هَمَّامٍ -وَهُوَ ابْنُ يَحْيَى-عَنْ قَتَادَةَ، عَنْ عِمْرَانَ بْنِ عِصَامٍ، عن شَيْخٍ، عَنْ عِمْرَانَ بْنِ حُصَيْنٍ وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ أَبُو عِيسَى التِّرْمِذِيُّ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ عَلِيٍّ، عَنِ ابْنِ مَهْدِيّ وَأَبِي دَاوُدَ، كِلَاهُمَا عَنْ همام، عن قتادة، عن عمران بن عصام، عَنْ رَجُلٍ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْبَصْرَةِ، عَنْ عِمْرَانَ بْنِ حُصَيْنٍ، بِهِ. ثُمَّ قَالَ: غَرِيبٌ، لَا نَعْرِفُهُ إِلَّا مِنْ حَدِيثِ قَتَادَةَ، وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ خَالِدُ بْنُ قَيْسٍ أَيْضًا عَنْ قَتَادَةَ .

وَقَدْ رُوِيَ عَنْ عِمْرَانَ بْنِ عِصَامٍ، عَنْ عِمْرَانَ نَفْسِهِ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

قُلْتُ: وَرَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ سِنَانٍ الْوَاسِطِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا يَزِيدُ بْنُ هَارُونَ، أَخْبَرَنَا هَمَّامُ عَنْ قَتَادَةَ، عَنْ عِمْرَانَ بْنِ عِصَامٍ الضُّبَعِيِّ -شَيْخٌ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْبَصْرَةِ-عَنْ عِمْرَانَ بْنِ حُصَيْنٍ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ فَذَكَرَهُ، هَكَذَا رَأَيْتُهُ فِي تَفْسِيرِهِ، فَجَعَلَ الشَّيْخَ الْبَصْرِيَّ هُوَ عِمْرَانَ بْنَ عِصَامٍ [الضُّبَعِيَّ] .

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا نَصْرُ بْنُ عَلِيٍّ، حَدَّثَنِي أَبِي، حَدَّثَنِي خَالِدُ بْنُ قَيْسٍ، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ، عَنْ عِمْرَانَ بْنِ عِصَامٍ، عَنْ عِمْرَانَ بْنِ حُصَيْنٍ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ فِي الشَّفْعِ وَالْوَتْرِ قَالَ: "هِيَ الصَّلَاةُ مِنْهَا شَفْعٌ، وَمِنْهَا وَتْرٌ" .

فَأَسْقَطَ ذِكْرَ الشَّيْخِ الْمُبْهَمِ، وَتَفَرَّدَ بِهِ عِمْرَانُ بْنُ عِصَامٍ الضُّبَعِيُّ أَبُو عُمَارَةَ الْبَصْرِيُّ، إِمَامُ مَسْجِدِ بَنِي ضُبَيعة وَهُوَ وَالِدُ أَبِي جَمْرَة نَصْرِ بْنِ عِمْرَانَ الضُّبَعِيُّ. رَوَى عَنْهُ قَتَادَةُ، وَابْنُهُ أَبُو جَمْرَةَ وَالْمُثَنَّى بْنُ سَعِيدٍ، وَأَبُو التَّيَّاحِ يَزِيدُ بْنُ حُمَيْدٍ. وَذَكَرَهُ ابْنُ حبَّان فِي كِتَابِ الثِّقَاتِ وَذَكَرَهُ خَلِيفَةُ ابن خَيَاط فِي التَّابِعِينَ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْبَصْرَةِ، وَكَانَ شَرِيفًا نَبِيلًا حَظِيًّا عِنْدَ الْحَجَّاجِ بْنِ يُوسُفَ، ثُمَّ قَتَلَهُ يَوْمَ الزَّاوِيَةِ سَنَةَ ثَلَاثٍ وَثَمَانِينَ لِخُرُوجِهِ مَعَ ابْنِ الْأَشْعَثِ، وَلَيْسَ لَهُ عِنْدَ التِّرْمِذِيِّ سِوَى هَذَا الْحَدِيثِ الْوَاحِدِ. وَعِنْدِي أَنَّ وَقْفَهُ عَلَى عِمْرَانَ بْنِ حُصَيْنٍ أَشْبَهُ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

وَلَمْ يَجْزِمِ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ بِشَيْءٍ مِنْ هَذِهِ الْأَقْوَالِ فِي الشَّفْعِ وَالْوَتْرِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَسْرِ﴾ قَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: أَيْ إِذَا ذَهَبَ.

وَقَالَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ الزُّبَيْرِ: ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَسْرِ﴾ حَتَّى يُذْهِبَ بَعْضُهُ بَعْضًا.

وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَأَبُو الْعَالِيَةِ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَمَالِكٌ، عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ وَابْنِ زَيْدٍ: ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَسْرِ﴾ إِذَا سَارَ.

وَهَذَا يُمْكِنُ حَمْلُهُ عَلَى مَا قَالَهُ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، أَيْ: ذَهَبَ. ويحتمل أن يكون المراد إذا سار، أَيْ: أَقْبَلَ. وَقَدْ يُقَالُ: إِنَّ هَذَا أَنْسَبُ؛ لِأَنَّهُ فِي مُقَابَلَةِ قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَالْفَجْرِ﴾ فَإِنَّ الْفَجْرَ هُوَ إِقْبَالُ النَّهَارِ وَإِدْبَارُ اللَّيْلِ، فَإِذَا حُمِلَ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَسْرِ﴾ عَلَى إِقْبَالِهِ كَانَ قَسَمًا بِإِقْبَالِ اللَّيْلِ وَإِدْبَارِ النَّهَارِ، وَبِالْعَكْسِ، كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا عَسْعَسَ وَالصُّبْحِ إِذَا تَنَفَّسَ﴾ [التَّكْوِيرِ:١٧، ١٨] . وَكَذَا قَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ: ﴿ [وَاللَّيْلِ] إِذَا يَسْرِ﴾ أَيْ: يَجْرِي.

وَقَالَ عِكْرِمَةُ: ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَسْرِ﴾ يَعْنِي: لَيْلَةَ جَمْع. رَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، وَابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ عِصَامٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو عَامِرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا كَثِيرُ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنُ عَمْرٍو قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ مُحَمَّدَ بْنَ كَعْبٍ الْقُرَظِيَّ، يَقُولُ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَسْرِ﴾ قَالَ: اسْرِ يَا سَارِ وَلَا تَبِينَ إِلَّا بجَمْع.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿هَلْ فِي ذَلِكَ قَسَمٌ لِذِي حِجْرٍ﴾ أَيْ: لِذِي عَقْلٍ وَلُبٍّ وَحِجَا [وَدِينٍ] وَإِنَّمَا سُمِّيَ الْعَقْلُ حجْرًا لِأَنَّهُ يَمْنَعُ الْإِنْسَانَ مِنْ تَعَاطِي مَا لَا يَلِيقُ بِهِ مِنَ الْأَفْعَالِ وَالْأَقْوَالِ، وَمِنْهُ حجْرُ الْبَيْتِ لِأَنَّهُ يَمْنَعُ الطَّائِفَ مِنَ اللُّصُوقِ بِجِدَارِهِ الشَّامِيِّ. وَمِنْهُ حِجْرُ الْيَمَامَةِ، وحَجَرَ الْحَاكِمُ عَلَى فُلَانٍ: إِذَا مَنَعَهُ التَّصَرُّفَ، ﴿وَيَقُولُونَ حِجْرًا مَحْجُورًا﴾ [الْفُرْقَانِ:٢٢] ، كُلُّ هَذَا مِنْ قَبِيلٍ وَاحِدٍ، وَمَعْنًى مُتَقَارِبٍ، وَهَذَا الْقَسَمُ هُوَ بِأَوْقَاتِ الْعِبَادَةِ، وَبِنَفْسِ الْعِبَادَةِ مِنْ حَجٍّ وَصَلَاةٍ وَغَيْرِ ذَلِكَ مِنْ أَنْوَاعِ الْقُرَبِ الَّتِي يَتَقَرَّبُ بِهَا [إِلَيْهِ عِبَادُهُ] الْمُتَّقُونَ الْمُطِيعُونَ لَهُ، الْخَائِفُونَ مِنْهُ، الْمُتَوَاضِعُونَ لَدَيْهِ، الْخَاشِعُونَ لِوَجْهِهِ الْكَرِيمِ.

وَلَمَّا ذَكَرَ هَؤُلَاءِ وَعِبَادَتَهُمْ وَطَاعَتَهُمْ قَالَ بَعْدَهُ: ﴿أَلَمْ تَرَ كَيْفَ فَعَلَ رَبُّكَ بِعَادٍ﴾ وَهَؤُلَاءِ كَانُوا مُتَمَرِّدِينَ عُتَاةً جَبَّارِينَ، خَارِجِينَ عَنْ طَاعَتِهِ مُكَذِّبِينَ لِرُسُلِهِ، جَاحِدِينَ لِكُتُبِهِ. فَذَكَرَ تَعَالَى كَيْفَ أَهْلَكَهُمْ وَدَمَّرَهُمْ، وَجَعَلَهُمْ أَحَادِيثَ وعبَرا، فَقَالَ: ﴿أَلَمْ تَرَ كَيْفَ فَعَلَ رَبُّكَ بِعَادٍ إِرَمَ ذَاتِ الْعِمَادِ﴾ وَهَؤُلَاءِ عَادٌ الْأَوْلَى، وَهُمْ أَوْلَادُ عَادِ بْنِ إِرَمَ بْنِ عَوص بْنِ سَامَ بْنِ نُوحٍ، قَالَهُ ابْنُ إِسْحَاقَ وَهُمُ الَّذِينَ بَعَثَ اللَّهُ فِيهِمْ رَسُولَهُ هُودًا، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ، فَكَذَّبُوهُ وَخَالَفُوهُ، فَأَنْجَاهُ اللَّهُ مِنْ بَيْنِ أَظْهُرِهِمْ وَمَنْ آمَنُ مَعَهُ مِنْهُمْ، وَأَهْلَكَهُمْ بِرِيحٍ صَرْصَرٍ عَاتِيَةٍ، ﴿سَخَّرَهَا عَلَيْهِمْ سَبْعَ لَيَالٍ وَثَمَانِيَةَ أَيَّامٍ حُسُومًا فَتَرَى الْقَوْمَ فِيهَا صَرْعَى كَأَنَّهُمْ أَعْجَازُ نَخْلٍ خَاوِيَةٍ فَهَلْ تَرَى لَهُمْ مِنْ بَاقِيَةٍ﴾ [الْحَاقَّةِ:٧، ٨] وَقَدْ ذَكَرَ اللَّهُ قِصَّتَهُمْ فِي الْقُرْآنِ فِي غَيْرِ مَا مَوْضِعٍ، لِيَعْتَبِرَ بِمَصْرَعِهِمُ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ.

فَقَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِرَمَ ذَاتِ الْعِمَادِ﴾ عَطْفُ بَيَانٍ؛ زِيَادَةُ تَعْرِيفٍ بِهِمْ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿ذَاتِ الْعِمَادِ﴾ لِأَنَّهُمْ كَانُوا يَسْكُنُونَ بُيُوتَ الشِّعر الَّتِي تُرْفَعُ بِالْأَعْمِدَةِ الشِّدَادِ، وَقَدْ كَانُوا أَشَدَّ النَّاسِ فِي زَمَانِهِمْ خِلْقَةً وَأَقْوَاهُمْ بَطْشًا، وَلِهَذَا ذكَّرهم هُودٌ بِتِلْكَ النِّعْمَةِ وَأَرْشَدَهُمْ إِلَى أَنْ يَسْتَعْمِلُوهَا فِي طَاعَةِ رَبِّهِمُ الَّذِي خَلَقَهُمْ، فَقَالَ: ﴿وَاذْكُرُوا إِذْ جَعَلَكُمْ خُلَفَاءَ مِنْ بَعْدِ قَوْمِ نُوحٍ وَزَادَكُمْ فِي الْخَلْقِ بَسْطَةً فَاذْكُرُوا آلاءَ اللَّهِ [لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ] ﴾ [الأعراف:٦٩] . وقال تعالى: ﴿فَأَمَّا عَادٌ فَاسْتَكْبَرُوا فِي الأرْضِ بِغَيْرِ الْحَقِّ وَقَالُوا مَنْ أَشَدُّ مِنَّا قُوَّةً أَوَلَمْ يَرَوْا أَنَّ اللَّهَ الَّذِي خَلَقَهُمْ هُوَ أَشَدُّ مِنْهُمْ قُوَّةً﴾ [فُصِّلَتْ:١٥] ، وَقَالَ هَاهُنَا: ﴿الَّتِي لَمْ يُخْلَقْ مِثْلُهَا فِي الْبِلادِ﴾ أَيِ: الْقَبِيلَةُ الَّتِي لَمْ يَخْلُقْ مِثْلَهَا فِي بِلَادِهِمْ، لِقُوَّتِهِمْ وَشِدَّتِهِمْ وَعِظَمِ تَرْكِيبِهِمْ.

قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: إِرَمَ: أُمَّةٌ قَدِيمَةٌ. يَعْنِي: عَادًا الْأُولَى، كَمَا قَالَ قَتَادَةُ بْنُ دِعَامَةَ، والسُّدِّيُّ: إِنَّ إِرَمَ بَيْتُ مَمْلَكَةِ عَادٍ. وَهَذَا قَوْلٌ حَسَنٌ جَيِّدٌ قَوِيٌّ.

وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالْكَلْبِيُّ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿ذَاتِ الْعِمَادِ﴾ كَانُوا أَهْلَ عَمُودٍ لَا يُقِيمُونَ.

وَقَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: إِنَّمَا قِيلَ لَهُمْ: ﴿ذَاتِ الْعِمَادِ﴾ لِطُولِهِمْ.

وَاخْتَارَ الْأَوَّلَ ابنُ جَرِيرٍ، وَرَدَّ الثَّانِي فَأَصَابَ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿الَّتِي لَمْ يُخْلَقْ مِثْلُهَا فِي الْبِلادِ﴾ أَعَادَ ابْنُ زَيْدٍ الضميرَ عَلَى الْعِمَادِ؛ لِارْتِفَاعِهَا، وَقَالَ: بَنَوْا عُمُدا بِالْأَحْقَافِ لَمْ يُخْلَقْ مِثْلُهَا فِي الْبِلَادِ. وَأَمَّا قَتَادَةُ وَابْنُ جَرِيرٍ فَأَعَادَ الضَّمِيرَ عَلَى الْقَبِيلَةِ، أَيْ: لَمْ يَخْلُقْ مِثْلَ تِلْكَ الْقَبِيلَةِ فِي الْبِلَادِ، يَعْنِي فِي زَمَانِهِمْ. وَهَذَا الْقَوْلُ هُوَ الصَّوَابُ، وَقَوْلُ ابْنِ زَيْدٍ وَمَنْ ذَهَبَ مَذْهَبَهُ ضَعِيفٌ؛ لِأَنَّهُ لَوْ كَانَ أَرَادَ ذَلِكَ لَقَالَ: الَّتِي لَمْ يُعْمَلْ مِثْلُهَا فِي الْبِلَادِ، وَإِنَّمَا قَالَ: ﴿لَمْ يُخْلَقْ مِثْلُهَا فِي الْبِلادِ﴾

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو صَالِحٍ كَاتِبُ اللَّيْثِ، حَدَّثَنَا مُعَاوِيَةُ بْنُ صَالِحٍ، عَمَّنْ حَدَّثَهُ، عَنِ الْمِقْدَامِ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ أَنَّهُ ذَكَرَ إِرَمَ ذَاتِ الْعِمَادِ فَقَالَ: "كَانَ الرَّجُلُ مِنْهُمْ يَأْتِي عَلَى صَخْرَةٍ فَيَحْمِلُهَا عَلَى الْحَيِّ فَيُهْلِكُهُمْ" .

ثُمَّ قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ الْحُسَيْنِ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو الطَّاهِرِ، حَدَّثَنَا أَنَسُ بْنُ عِيَاضٍ، عَنْ ثَوْرِ بْنِ زَيْدٍ الدِّيلِيِّ. قَالَ: قَرَأْتُ كِتَابًا -قَدْ سَمَّى حَيْثُ قَرَأَهُ-: أَنَا شَدَّادُ بْنُ عَادٍ، وَأَنَا الَّذِي رَفَعْتُ الْعِمَادَ، وَأَنَا الَّذِي شَدَدْتُ بِذِرَاعِي نَظَرَ وَاحِدٍ، وَأَنَا الَّذِي كَنَزْتُ كَنْزًا عَلَى سَبْعَةِ أَذْرُعٍ، لَا يُخْرِجُهُ إِلَّا أُمَّةُ مُحَمَّدٍ ﷺ.

قُلْتُ: فَعَلَى كُلِّ قَوْلٍ سَوَاءٌ كَانَتِ الْعِمَادُ أَبْنِيَةً بَنَوْهَا، أَوْ أَعْمِدَةَ بُيُوتِهِمْ لِلْبَدْوِ، أَوْ سِلَاحًا يُقَاتِلُونَ بِهِ، أَوْ طُولَ الْوَاحِدِ مِنْهُمْ -فَهُمْ قَبِيلَةٌ وَأُمَّةٌ مِنَ الْأُمَمِ، وَهُمُ الْمَذْكُورُونَ فِي الْقُرْآنِ فِي غَيْرِ مَا مَوْضِعٍ، الْمَقْرُونُونَ بِثَمُودَ كَمَا هَاهُنَا، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ. وَمَنْ زَعَمَ أَنَّ الْمُرَادَ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿إِرَمَ ذَاتِ الْعِمَادِ﴾ مَدِينَةٌ إِمَّا دِمَشْقُ، كَمَا رُوِيَ عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ الْمُسَيَّبِ وَعِكْرِمَةَ، أَوِ إِسْكَنْدَرِيَّةُ كَمَا رُوي عَنِ القُرَظي أَوْ غَيْرُهُمَا، فَفِيهِ نَظَرٌ، فَإِنَّهُ كَيْفَ يَلْتَئِمُ الْكَلَامُ عَلَى هَذَا: ﴿أَلَمْ تَرَ كَيْفَ فَعَلَ رَبُّكَ بِعَادٍ إِرَمَ ذَاتِ الْعِمَادِ﴾ إِنْ جَعَلَ ذَلِكَ بَدَلًا أَوْ عَطْفَ بَيَانٍ، فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَتَّسِقُ الْكَلَامُ حِينَئِذٍ. ثُمَّ الْمُرَادُ إِنَّمَا هُوَ الْإِخْبَارُ عَنْ إِهْلَاكِ الْقَبِيلَةِ الْمُسَمَّاةِ بِعَادٍ، وَمَا أَحَلَّ اللَّهُ بِهِمْ مِنْ بَأْسِهِ الَّذِي لَا يُرَد، لَا أَنَّ المراد الإخبار عن مدينة أو إقليم.

وَإِنَّمَا نَبَّهْتُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ لِئَلَّا يُغْتَرَّ بِكَثِيرٍ مِمَّا ذَكَرَهُ جَمَاعَةٌ مِنَ الْمُفَسِّرِينَ عِنْدَ هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ، مِنْ ذِكْرِ مَدِينَةٍ يُقَالُ لَهَا: ﴿إِرَمَ ذَاتِ الْعِمَادِ﴾ مَبْنِيَّةٍ بِلَبِنِ الذَّهَبِ وَالْفِضَّةِ، قُصُورُهَا وَدَوْرُهَا وَبَسَاتِينُهَا، وَإِنَّ حَصْبَاءَهَا لَآلِئٌ وَجَوَاهِرُ، وَتُرَابُهَا بَنَادِقُ الْمِسْكِ، وَأَنْهَارُهَا سَارِحَةٌ، وَثِمَارُهَا سَاقِطَةٌ، وَدُورُهَا لَا أَنِيسَ بِهَا، وَسُورُهَا وَأَبْوَابُهَا تَصْفَرُّ، لَيْسَ بِهَا دَاعٍ وَلَا مُجِيبٍ. وَأَنَّهَا تَنْتَقِلُ فَتَارَةٌ تَكُونُ بِأَرْضِ الشَّامِ، وَتَارَةٌ بِالْيَمَنِ، وَتَارَةٌ بِالْعِرَاقِ، وَتَارَةٌ بِغَيْرِ ذَلِكَ مِنَ الْبِلَادِ -فَإِنَّ هَذَا كُلَّهُ مِنْ خُرَافَاتِ الْإِسْرَائِيلِيِّينَ، مِنْ وَضْعِ بَعْضِ زَنَادِقَتِهِمْ، لِيَخْتَبِرُوا بِذَلِكَ عُقُولَ الْجَهَلَةِ مِنَ النَّاسِ أَنْ تُصَدِّقَهُمْ فِي جَمِيعِ ذَلِكَ.

وَذَكَرَ الثَّعْلَبِيُّ وَغَيْرُهُ أَنَّ رَجُلًا مِنَ الْأَعْرَابِ -وَهُوَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ قِلابة-فِي زَمَانِ مُعَاوِيَةَ ذَهَبَ فِي طَلَبِ أَبَاعِرَ لَهُ شَرَدَتْ، فَبَيْنَمَا هُوَ يَتِيهُ فِي ابْتِغَائِهَا، إِذْ طَلَعَ عَلَى مَدِينَةٍ عَظِيمَةٍ لَهَا سُورٌ وَأَبْوَابٌ، فَدَخَلَهَا فَوَجَدَ فِيهَا قَرِيبًا مِمَّا ذَكَرْنَاهُ مِنْ صِفَاتِ الْمَدِينَةِ الذَّهَبِيَّةِ الَّتِي تَقَدَّمَ ذِكْرُهَا، وَأَنَّهُ رَجَعَ فَأَخْبَرَ النَّاسَ، فَذَهَبُوا مَعَهُ إِلَى الْمَكَانِ الَّذِي قَالَ فَلَمْ يَرَوْا شَيْئًا.

وَقَدْ ذَكَرَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ قِصَّةَ ﴿إِرَمَ ذَاتِ الْعِمَادِ﴾ هَاهُنَا مُطَوَّلَةً جِدًّا، فَهَذِهِ الْحِكَايَةُ لَيْسَ يَصِحُّ إِسْنَادُهَا، وَلَوْ صَحَّ إِلَى ذَلِكَ الْأَعْرَابِيِّ فَقَدْ يَكُونُ اخْتَلَقَ ذَلِكَ، أَوْ أَنَّهُ أَصَابَهُ نَوْعٌ مِنَ الهَوَس وَالْخَبَالِ فَاعْتَقَدَ أَنَّ ذَلِكَ لَهُ حَقِيقَةٌ فِي الْخَارِجِ، وَلَيْسَ كَذَلِكَ. وَهَذَا مِمَّا يُقْطَعُ بِعَدَمِ صِحَّتِهِ. وَهَذَا قَرِيبٌ مِمَّا يُخْبِرُ بِهِ كَثِيرٌ مِنَ الْجَهَلَةِ وَالطَّامِعِينَ وَالْمُتَحَيِّلِينَ، مِنْ وُجُودِ مَطَالِبَ تَحْتَ الْأَرْضِ، فِيهَا قَنَاطِيرُ الذَّهَبِ وَالْفِضَّةِ، وَأَلْوَانِ الْجَوَاهِرِ وَالْيَوَاقِيتِ وَاللَّآلِئِ وَالْإِكْسِيرِ الْكَبِيرِ، لَكِنْ عَلَيْهَا مَوَانِعُ تَمْنَعُ مِنَ الْوُصُولِ إِلَيْهَا وَالْأَخْذِ مِنْهَا، فَيَحْتَالُونَ عَلَى أَمْوَالِ الْأَغْنِيَاءِ وَالضَّعَفَةِ وَالسُّفَهَاءِ، فَيَأْكُلُونَهَا بِالْبَاطِلِ فِي صَرْفِهَا فِي بَخَاخِيرَ وَعَقَاقِيرَ، وَنَحْوِ ذَلِكَ مِنَ الْهَذَيَانَاتِ، ويَطْنزون بِهِمْ. وَالَّذِي يُجْزَمُ بِهِ أَنَّ فِي الْأَرْضِ دَفَائِنَ جَاهِلِيَّةً وَإِسْلَامِيَّةً وَكُنُوزًا كَثِيرَةً، مَنْ ظَفَرَ بِشَيْءٍ مِنْهَا أَمْكَنَهُ تَحْوِيلُهُ فَأَمَّا عَلَى الصِّفَةِ الَّتِي زَعَمُوهَا فَكَذِبٌ وَافْتِرَاءٌ وَبُهْتٌ، وَلَمْ يَصِحَّ فِي ذَلِكَ شَيْءٌ مِمَّا يَقُولُونَ إِلَّا عَنْ نَقْلِهِمْ أَوْ نَقْلِ مَنْ أَخَذَ عَنْهُمْ، وَاللَّهُ سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَى الْهَادِي لِلصَّوَابِ.

وقولُ ابْنِ جَرِيرٍ: يُحْتَمَلُ أَنْ يَكُونَ الْمُرَادُ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿إِرَمَ﴾ قَبِيلَةً أَوْ بَلْدَةً كَانَتْ عَادٌ تَسْكُنُهَا فَلِذَلِكَ لَمْ تُصرَف فِيهِ نَظَرٌ؛ لِأَنَّ الْمُرَادَ مِنَ السِّيَاقِ إِنَّمَا هُوَ الْإِخْبَارُ عَنِ الْقَبِيلَةِ، وَلِهَذَا قَالَ بَعْدَهُ: ﴿وَثَمُودَ الَّذِينَ جَابُوا الصَّخْرَ بِالْوَادِ﴾ يَعْنِي: يَقْطَعُونَ الصَّخْرَ بِالْوَادِي. قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: يَنْحِتُونَهَا وَيَخْرِقُونَهَا. وَكَذَا قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَابْنُ زَيْدٍ. وَمِنْهُ يُقَالُ: "مُجْتَابِي النِّمَارَ". إِذَا خَرَقُوهَا، وَاجْتَابَ الثَّوْبَ: إِذَا فَتَحَهُ. وَمِنْهُ الْجَيْبُ أَيْضًا. وَقَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَتَنْحِتُونَ مِنَ الْجِبَالِ بُيُوتًا فَارِهِينَ﴾ [الشُّعَرَاءِ:١٤٩] . وَأَنْشَدَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ وَابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ هَاهُنَا قَوْلَ الشَّاعِرِ: أَلَا كُلّ شَيْءٍ -مَا خَلا اللَّهَ-بائدٌ ... كَما بَاد حَيٌّ مِنْ شَنِيفٍ وَمَارِدِ ...

هُم ضَرَبُوا فِي كُلِّ صَمَّاء صَعدة ... بِأَيْدٍ شِدَاد أَيَّدَاتِ السَّواعد 

وَقَالَ ابْنُ إِسْحَاقَ: كَانُوا عَرَبًا، وَكَانَ مَنْزِلُهُمْ بِوَادِي الْقُرَى. وَقَدْ ذَكَرْنَا قِصَّةَ "عَادٍ" مُسْتَقْصَاةً فِي سُورَةِ "الْأَعْرَافِ" بِمَا أَغْنَى عَنْ إِعَادَتِهِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَفِرْعَوْنَ ذِي الأوْتَادِ﴾ قَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: الْأَوْتَادُ: الْجُنُودُ الَّذِينَ يَشُدُّونَ لَهُ أَمْرَهُ. وَيُقَالُ: كَانَ فِرْعَوْنُ يُوتِدُ أَيْدِيَهُمْ وَأَرْجُلَهُمْ فِي أَوْتَادٍ مِنْ حَديد يُعَلِّقُهُمْ بِهَا. وَكَذَا قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: كَانَ يُوتِدُ النَّاسَ بِالْأَوْتَادِ. وَهَكَذَا قَالَ سَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ، وَالْحَسَنُ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ. قَالَ السُّدِّيُّ: كَانَ يَرْبِطُ الرَّجُلَ، كُلُّ قَائِمَةٍ مِنْ قَوَائِمِهِ فِي وَتَدٍ ثُمَّ يُرْسِلُ عَلَيْهِ صَخْرَةً عَظِيمَةً فَتَشْدَخُهُ .

وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: بَلَغَنَا أَنَّهُ كَانَتْ لَهُ مَطَالٌّ وَمَلَاعِبٌ، يَلْعَبُ لَهُ تَحْتَهَا، مِنْ أَوْتَادٍ وَحِبَالٍ.

وَقَالَ ثَابِتٌ الْبُنَانِيُّ، عَنْ أَبِي رَافِعٍ: قِيلَ لِفِرْعَوْنَ ﴿ذِي الأوْتَادِ﴾ ؛ لِأَنَّهُ ضَرَبَ لِامْرَأَتِهِ أَرْبَعَةَ أَوْتَادٍ، ثُمَّ جَعَلَ عَلَى ظَهْرِهَا رَحًى عَظِيمَةً حَتَّى مَاتَتْ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿الَّذِينَ طَغَوْا فِي الْبِلادِ فَأَكْثَرُوا فِيهَا الْفَسَادَ﴾ أَيْ: تَمَرَّدُوا وَعَتَوْا وَعَاثُوا فِي الْأَرْضِ بِالْإِفْسَادِ وَالْأَذِيَّةِ لِلنَّاسِ، ﴿فَصَبَّ عَلَيْهِمْ رَبُّكَ سَوْطَ عَذَابٍ﴾ أَيْ: أَنْزَلَ عَلَيْهِمْ رِجْزًا مِنَ السَّمَاءِ، وَأَحَلَّ بِهِمْ عُقُوبَةً لَا يَرُدُّهَا عَنِ الْقَوْمِ الْمُجْرِمِينَ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنَّ رَبَّكَ لَبِالْمِرْصَادِ﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: يَسْمَعُ وَيَرَى. يَعْنِي: يَرْصُدُ خَلْقَهُ فِيمَا يَعْمَلُونَ، وَيُجَازِي كُلًّا بِسَعْيِهِ فِي الدُّنْيَا وَالْأُخْرَى، وَسَيُعْرَضُ الْخَلَائِقُ كُلُّهُمْ عَلَيْهِ، فَيَحْكُمُ فِيهِمْ بِعَدْلِهِ، وَيُقَابِلُ كُلًّا بِمَا يَسْتَحِقُّهُ. وَهُوَ الْمُنَزَّهُ عَنِ الظُّلْمِ وَالْجَوْرِ.

وَقَدْ ذَكَرَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ هَاهُنَا حَدِيثًا غَرِيبًا جِدًّا -وَفِي إِسْنَادِهِ نَظَرٌ وَفِي صِحَّتِهِ-فَقَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ أَبِي الْحَوَارِيِّ، حَدَّثَنَا يُونُسُ الْحَذَّاءُ، عَنْ أَبِي حَمْزَةَ الْبَيْسَانِيِّ، عَنْ مُعَاذِ بْنِ جَبَلٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "يَا مُعَاذُ، إِنَّ الْمُؤْمِنَ لَدَى الْحَقِّ أَسِيرٌ. يَا مُعَاذُ، إِنَّ الْمُؤْمِنَ لَا يَسْكُنُ رَوْعُهُ وَلَا يَأْمَنُ اضْطِرَابُهُ حَتَّى يُخَلَّف جِسْرَ جَهَنَّمَ خَلْفَ ظَهْرِهِ. يَا مُعَاذُ، إِنَّ الْمُؤْمِنَ قَيَّدَهُ الْقُرْآنُ عَنْ كَثِيرٍ مِنْ شَهَوَاتِهِ، وَعَنْ أَنْ يَهْلَكَ فِيهَا هُوَ بِإِذْنِ اللَّهِ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، فَالْقُرْآنُ دَلِيلُهُ، وَالْخَوْفُ مَحَجَّتُهُ، وَالشَّوْقُ مَطِيَّتُهُ، وَالصَّلَاةُ كَهْفُهُ، وَالصَّوْمُ جَنَّتُهُ، وَالصَّدَقَةُ فِكَاكُهُ، وَالصِّدْقُ أَمِيرُهُ، وَالْحَيَاءُ وَزِيرُهُ، وَرَبُّهُ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، مِنْ وَرَاءِ ذَلِكَ كُلِّهِ بِالْمِرْصَادِ" .

قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: يُونُسُ الْحَذَّاءُ وَأَبُو حَمْزَةَ مَجْهُولَانِ، وَأَبُو حَمْزَةَ عَنْ مُعَاذٍ مُرْسَلٌ. وَلَوْ كَانَ عَنْ أَبِي حَمْزَةَ لَكَانَ حَسَنًا. أَيْ: لَوْ كَانَ مِنْ كَلَامِهِ لَكَانَ حَسَنًا. ثُمَّ قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حاتم: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا صَفْوَانُ بْنُ صَالِحٍ، حَدَّثَنَا الْوَلِيدُ بْنُ مُسْلِمٍ، عَنْ صَفْوَانَ بْنِ عَمْرٍو، عَنْ أَيْفَعَ بْنِ عَبْدٍ الْكُلَاعِيِّ: أَنَّهُ سَمِعَهُ وَهُوَ يَعِظُ النَّاسَ يَقُولُ: إِنَّ لِجَهَنَّمَ سَبْعَ قَنَاطِرَ -قَالَ: وَالصِّرَاطُ عَلَيْهِنَّ، قَالَ: فَيَحْبِسُ الْخَلَائِقَ عِنْدَ الْقَنْطَرَةِ الْأُولَى، فَيَقُولُ: ﴿وَقِفُوهُمْ إِنَّهُمْ مَسْئُولُونَ﴾ [الصَّافَّاتِ:٢٤] ، قَالَ: فَيُحَاسَبُونَ عَلَى الصَّلَاةِ ويُسألون عَنْهَا، قَالَ: فَيَهْلِكُ فِيهَا مَنْ هَلَكَ، وَيَنْجُو مَنْ نَجَا، فَإِذَا بَلَغُوا الْقَنْطَرَةَ الثَّانِيَةَ حُوسبوا عَلَى الْأَمَانَةِ كَيْفَ أَدَّوْهَا، وَكَيْفَ خَانُوهَا؟ قَالَ: فَيَهْلِكُ مَنْ هَلَكَ وَيَنْجُو مَنْ نَجَا. فَإِذَا بَلَغُوا الْقَنْطَرَةَ الثَّالِثَةَ سُئلوا عَنِ الرَّحِمِ كَيْفَ وَصَلُوها وَكَيْفَ قَطَعُوهَا؟ قَالَ: فَيَهْلِكُ مَنْ هَلَكَ وَيَنْجُو مَنْ نَجَا. قَالَ: وَالرَّحِمُ يَوْمَئِذٍ مُتَدَلِّيَةٌ إِلَى الهُويّ فِي جَهَنَّمَ تَقُولُ: اللَّهُمَّ مَنْ وَصَلَنِي فَصِلْه، وَمَنْ قَطَعَنِي فَاقْطَعْهُ. قَالَ: وَهِيَ الَّتِي يَقُولُ اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ: ﴿إِنَّ رَبَّكَ لَبِالْمِرْصَادِ﴾ .

هَكَذَا أَوْرَدَ هَذَا الْأَثَرَ، وَلَمْ يَذْكُرْ تَمَامَهُ.

## 89:15-20 — verses 15-20 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, disapproving of man's belief when Allah expands provision for him in order to test him by that, and he believes that this is honor from Allah for him, when it is not so. Rather, it is a trial and a test. As the Exalted said: {Do they think that what We extend to them of wealth and children, We hasten to them thereby the good things? Rather, they do not perceive.} [al-Mu'minun: 55, 56]. Likewise, on the other side, when He tries him and tests him and restricts provision upon him, he believes that this is humiliation from Allah for him. Allah said: {No indeed}, meaning: the matter is not as he claimed, neither in this nor in that. For Allah gives wealth to whom He loves and to whom He does not love, and He restricts it upon whom He loves and upon whom He does not love. Rather, what the matter turns upon in that is obedience to Allah in each of the two states: if he is wealthy, that he give thanks to Allah for that, and if he is poor, that he be patient.

* *

His saying, {Rather, you do not honor the orphan}, contains a command to honor him, as has come in the hadith that [chain] Abu Hurayrah reported, from the Prophet ﷺ: "The best house among the Muslims is a house in which there is an orphan who is treated well, and the worst house among the Muslims is a house in which there is an orphan who is treated badly." Then he said, with his finger: "I and the guardian of the orphan will be in Paradise like this."

Abu Dawud said: [chain] Sahl ibn Sa'd reported that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "I and the guardian of the orphan will be in Paradise like these two," and he joined his two fingers together: the middle finger and the one next to the thumb.

{And you do not urge one another to feed the poor}, meaning: they do not enjoin kindness toward the poor and the destitute, nor does one of them urge another to do so. {And you consume the inheritance}, meaning: the inherited wealth, {devouring it entirely}, meaning: from whatever direction it comes to them, whether lawful or unlawful. {And you love wealth with immense love}, meaning: abundantly. Some of them added: excessively.

## 89:21-30 — verses 21-30 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Most High informs of the great terrors that will occur on the Day of Resurrection, so He said: {No indeed}, that is, truly, {when the earth is crushed, pounded and pounded}, that is, the earth and the mountains are trampled down, leveled, and made even, and creation rises from their graves to their Lord, {and your Lord comes}, meaning: to render the final judgment between His creation, and that is after they seek intercession from Him through the master of the children of Adam absolutely, Muhammad ﷺ, after they have asked the possessors of firm resolve (uli al-'azm) among the messengers, one after another, and each of them says: I am not the one for that, until the turn reaches Muhammad ﷺ, and he says: "I am for it, I am for it." So he goes and intercedes with Allah that He come to render the final judgment, and Allah grants him intercession in that, and this is the first of the intercessions, and it is the Praised Station (al-maqam al-mahmud) as its explanation preceded in Surat "Subhan" [al-Isra]. Then the Lord, Most High, comes to render the final judgment as He wills, and the angels come before Him rank upon rank.

* *

And His saying, {and Hell is brought that Day}: Imam Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj said in his Sahih: [chain] ... Abdullah, that is, Ibn Mas'ud, said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Hell will be brought that Day, having seventy thousand reins, with every rein seventy thousand angels dragging it."

Al-Tirmidhi likewise narrated it [chain] ... from Umar ibn Hafs, with the same chain. He also narrated it [chain] ... from al-Ala' ibn Khalid, from Shaqiq ibn Salamah (that is, Abu Wa'il), from Abdullah ibn Mas'ud, as his own statement, not raising it to the Prophet ﷺ (that is, as a mawquf, halted report, rather than a marfu', raised, one). Ibn Jarir likewise narrated it [chain] ... from Abdullah, as his own statement.

* *

And His saying, {That Day man will remember}, that is, his deeds and what he had put forth in the earlier and later part of his life, {but how will the remembrance benefit him?}, that is, how will remembering avail him? {He will say: Would that I had sent ahead for my life}, meaning: he regrets the sins he had committed, if he was disobedient, and he wishes he had increased in acts of obedience, if he was obedient, as Imam Ahmad ibn Hanbal said:

[chain] ... Muhammad ibn Abi 'Umayrah, who was one of the Companions of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, said: If a servant were to fall upon his face from the day he was born until he died of old age, in obedience to Allah, he would still regard it as trifling on the Day of Resurrection, and would wish that he could be sent back to the world so that he might increase in reward and recompense.

Bahir ibn Sa'd narrated it from Khalid ibn Ma'dan, from Utbah ibn Abd, from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ.

Allah, Most High, said: {so on that Day none will punish as He punishes}, that is, none is more severe in punishment than Allah's punishment of one who disobeyed Him, {and none will bind as He binds}, that is, none seizes and binds more severely than the Zabaniyah (the angels who drag the wicked into Hell) do to one who disbelieved in his Lord, Mighty and Majestic. This concerns the wrongdoers and the criminals among creation. As for the pure, tranquil soul, which is at rest, firmly settled, revolving with the truth, it will be told: {O tranquil soul, return to your Lord}, that is, to His nearness, His reward, and what He has prepared for His servants in His Paradise, {well pleased}, that is, within itself, {pleasing}, that is, it has become pleased with Allah, and He has become pleased with it and has satisfied it, {so enter among My servants}, that is, among their number, {and enter My Paradise}. This is said to it at the time of death, and also on the Day of Resurrection, just as the angels give the believer glad tidings at the time of his death and when he rises from his grave, and so it is here.

The exegetes then differed as to concerning whom this verse was revealed. Al-Dahhak narrated from Ibn Abbas: it was revealed concerning Uthman ibn Affan. And from Buraydah ibn al-Husayb: it was revealed concerning Hamzah ibn Abd al-Muttalib, may Allah be pleased with him.

Al-'Awfi said, from Ibn Abbas: it will be said to the tranquil souls on the Day of Resurrection: {O tranquil soul, return to your Lord}, meaning: your companion, that is, its body which it used to inhabit in the world, {well pleased and pleasing}.

It is narrated from him that he used to recite it: "so enter into My servant, and enter My Paradise." Ikrimah and al-Kalbi likewise said this, and Ibn Jarir preferred it, but it is gharib (an unusual, singular reading), and the apparent reading is the first, because of His saying: {then they are returned to Allah, their true Master} [al-An'am 6:62], {and that our return is to Allah} [Ghafir 40:43], that is, to His judgment and to standing before Him.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] ... Ibn Abbas, concerning His saying {O tranquil soul, return to your Lord, well pleased and pleasing}, said: it was revealed while Abu Bakr was sitting, and he said: "O Messenger of Allah, how excellent is this!" So he ﷺ said: "Indeed, this will be said to you."

Then he said: [chain] ... Sa'id ibn Jubayr said: I recited before the Prophet ﷺ: {O tranquil soul, return to your Lord, well pleased and pleasing}, and Abu Bakr, may Allah be pleased with him, said: "This is indeed excellent." So the Prophet ﷺ said to him: "Indeed, the angel will say this to you at death."

Ibn Jarir likewise narrated it from Abu Kurayb, from Ibn Yaman, with the same chain. And this is a hasan mursal (a sound report with a chain that omits the Companion) report.

Ibn Abi Hatim then said: [chain] ... Sa'id ibn Jubayr said: Ibn Abbas died at al-Ta'if, and a bird such as had never been seen in its form came and entered his bier, and was then never seen leaving it. When he was buried, this verse was recited at the edge of the grave, and it was not known who recited it: {O tranquil soul, return to your Lord, well pleased and pleasing, so enter among My servants and enter My Paradise}.

Al-Tabarani narrated it from Abdullah ibn Ahmad, from his father, from Marwan ibn Shuja', from Salim ibn Ajlan al-Aftas, with the same chain, and he mentioned it.

The Hafiz Muhammad ibn al-Mundhir al-Harawi, known as Shakkar, mentioned in the book "al-Aja'ib" (The Wonders), with his chain of transmission, from Qubath ibn Razin, Abu Hashim, who said: I was taken captive in the lands of the Byzantines (al-Rum), and the king gathered us and presented his religion to us, on condition that whoever refused would have his neck struck. Three of them apostatized, and the fourth came forward and refused, so his neck was struck, and his head was thrown into a river there. It sank into the water, then floated to the surface of the water, and looked at those three and said: "O so-and-so, O so-and-so, O so-and-so," calling them by their names, "Allah, Most High, has said in His Book: {O tranquil soul, return to your Lord, well pleased and pleasing, so enter among My servants and enter My Paradise}," then it sank into the water. [He said:] The Christians nearly embraced Islam, and the king's throne collapsed, and those three returned to Islam. He said: Then the ransom came from the Caliph Abu Ja'far al-Mansur, and he freed us.

The Hafiz Ibn Asakir narrated, in the biographical entry of Rawahah bint Abi Amr al-Awza'i, from her father: Sulayman ibn Habib al-Muharibi related to me, Abu Umamah related to me: that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said to a man: "Say: O Allah, I ask You for a soul tranquil in You, that believes in meeting You, is content with Your decree, and is satisfied with Your gift."

He then narrated from Abu Sulayman ibn Zabr that he said: this hadith of Rawahah is one of a kind, unmatched.

The end of the tafsir of Surat al-Fajr. And praise, and grace, belong to Allah.

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ الدَّارِمِيِّ، عَنْ عُمَرَ بْنِ حَفْصٍ، بِهِ وَرَوَاهُ أَيْضًا عَنْ عَبْدِ بْنِ حُمَيد، عَنْ أَبِي عَامِرٍ، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ الثَّوْرِيِّ، عَنِ العلاء بن خالد، عن شقيق ابن سَلَمَةَ -وَهُوَ أَبُو وَائِلٍ-عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ مَسْعُودٍ، قَوْلُهُ وَلَمْ يَرْفَعْهُ وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، عَنِ الْحَسَنِ بْنِ عَرَفَةَ، عَنْ مَروان بْنِ مُعَاوِيَةَ الْفَزَارِيِّ، عَنِ الْعَلَاءِ بْنِ خَالِدٍ، عَنْ شَقِيقٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ، قَوْلُهُ .

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿يَوْمَئِذٍ يَتَذَكَّرُ الإنْسَانُ﴾ أَيْ: عَمَلَهُ وَمَا كَانَ أَسْلَفَهُ فِي قَدِيمِ دَهْرِهِ وَحَدِيثِهِ، ﴿وَأَنَّى لَهُ الذِّكْرَى﴾ أَيْ: وَكَيْفَ تَنْفَعُهُ الذِّكْرَى؟ ﴿يَقُولُ يَا لَيْتَنِي قَدَّمْتُ لِحَيَاتِي﴾ يَعْنِي: يَنْدَمُ عَلَى مَا كَانَ سَلَفَ مِنْهُ مِنَ الْمَعَاصِي -إِنْ كَانَ عَاصِيًا-وَيَوَدُّ لَوْ كَانَ ازْدَادَ مِنَ الطَّاعَاتِ -إِنْ كَانَ طَائِعًا-كَمَا قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ بْنُ حَنْبَلٍ:

حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ إِسْحَاقَ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ -يَعْنِي ابْنَ الْمُبَارَكِ-حَدَّثَنَا ثَوْرُ بْنُ يَزِيدَ، عَنْ خَالِدِ بْنِ مَعْدَانَ، عَنْ جُبَيْرِ بْنِ نُفَيْرٍ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ أَبِي عَمِيرة -وَكَانَ مِنْ أَصْحَابِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ-قَالَ: لَوْ أَنَّ عَبْدًا خَرَّ عَلَى وَجْهِهِ مِنْ يَوْمِ وُلِدَ إِلَى أَنْ يَمُوتَ هَرمًا فِي طَاعَةِ اللَّهِ، لَحَقِرَه يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ، ولودَّ أَنَّهُ يُرَدَّ إِلَى الدُّنْيَا كَيْمَا يَزْدَادُ مِنَ الْأَجْرِ وَالثَّوَابِ.

وَرَوَاهُ بَحِيرُ بنُ سَعد، عَنْ خَالِدِ بْنِ مَعْدَانَ، عَنْ عُتْبَةَ بْنِ عَبْدٍ، عَنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ .

قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَيَوْمَئِذٍ لَا يُعَذِّبُ عَذَابَهُ أَحَدٌ﴾ أَيْ: لَيْسَ أَحَدٌ أَشَدَّ عَذَابًا مِنْ تَعْذِيبِ اللَّهِ مَنْ عَصَاهُ، ﴿وَلا يُوثِقُ وَثَاقَهُ أَحَدٌ﴾ أَيْ: وَلَيْسَ أَحَدٌ أَشَدَّ قَبْضًا وَوَثْقًا مِنَ الزَّبَانِيَةِ لِمَنْ كَفَرَ بِرَبِّهِمْ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، هَذَا فِي حَقِّ الْمُجْرِمِينَ مِنَ الْخَلَائِقِ وَالظَّالِمِينَ فَأَمَّا النَّفْسُ الزَّكِيَّةُ الْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ وَهِيَ السَّاكِنَةُ الثَّابِتَةُ الدَّائِرَةُ مَعَ الْحَقِّ فَيُقَالُ لَهَا: ﴿يَا أَيَّتُهَا النَّفْسُ الْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ ارْجِعِي إِلَى رَبِّكِ﴾ أَيْ: إِلَى جِوَارِهِ وَثَوَابِهِ وَمَا أَعَدَّ لِعِبَادِهِ فِي جَنَّتِهِ، ﴿رَاضِيَةً﴾ أَيْ: فِي نَفْسِهَا ﴿مَرْضِيَّةً﴾ أَيْ: قَدْ رَضِيَتْ عَنِ اللَّهِ وَرَضِيَ عَنْهَا وَأَرْضَاهَا، ﴿فَادْخُلِي فِي عِبَادِي﴾ أَيْ: فِي جُمْلَتِهِمْ، ﴿وَادْخُلِي جَنَّتِي﴾ وَهَذَا يُقَالُ لَهَا عِنْدَ الِاحْتِضَارِ، وَفِي يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ أَيْضًا، كَمَا أَنَّ الْمَلَائِكَةَ يُبَشِّرُونَ الْمُؤْمِنَ عِنْدَ احْتِضَارِهِ وَعِنْدَ قِيَامِهِ مِنْ قَبْرِهِ، وَكَذَلِكَ هَاهُنَا.

ثُمَّ اخْتَلَفَ الْمُفَسِّرُونَ فَيَمَنْ نَزَلَتْ هَذِهِ الْآيَةُ، فَرَوَى الضَّحَّاكُ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: نَزَلَتْ فِي عُثْمَانَ بْنِ عَفَّانَ. وَعَنْ بُرَيدة بْنِ الْحَصِيبِ: نَزَلَتْ فِي حَمْزَةَ بْنِ عَبْدِ الْمُطَّلِبِ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ.

وَقَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: يُقَالُ لِلْأَرْوَاحِ الْمُطْمَئِنَّةِ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ: ﴿يَا أَيَّتُهَا النَّفْسُ الْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ ارْجِعِي إِلَى رَبِّكِ﴾ يَعْنِي: صَاحِبُكِ، وَهُوَ بَدَنُهَا الَّذِي كَانَتْ تُعَمِّرُهُ فِي الدُّنْيَا، ﴿رَاضِيَةً مَرْضِيَّةً﴾

وَرُوِيَ عَنْهُ أَنَّهُ كَانَ يَقرؤها: "فَادْخُلِي فِي عَبْدِي وَادْخُلِي جَنَّتِي". وَكَذَا قَالَ عِكْرِمَةُ وَالْكَلْبِيُّ، وَاخْتَارَهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، وَهُوَ غَرِيبٌ، وَالظَّاهِرُ الْأَوَّلُ؛ لِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿ثُمَّ رُدُّوا إِلَى اللَّهِ مَوْلاهُمُ الْحَقِّ﴾ [الْأَنْعَامِ:٦٢] ﴿وَأَنَّ مَرَدَّنَا إِلَى اللَّهِ﴾ [غَافِرٍ:٤٣] أَيْ: إِلَى حُكْمِهِ وَالْوُقُوفِ بَيْنَ يَدَيْهِ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ الْحُسَيْنِ، حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الدَّشْتَكِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ أَشْعَثَ، عَنْ جَعْفَرٍ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ في قَوْلِهِ: ﴿يَا أَيَّتُهَا النَّفْسُ الْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ ارْجِعِي إِلَى رَبِّكِ رَاضِيَةً مَرْضِيَّةً﴾ قَالَ: نَزَلَتْ وَأَبُو بَكْرٍ جَالِسٌ، فَقَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، مَا أَحْسَنَ هَذَا. فَقَالَ: "أَمَا إِنَّهُ سَيُقَالُ لَكَ هَذَا" .

ثُمَّ قَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو سَعِيدٍ الْأَشَجُّ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ يَمَانٍ، عَنْ أَشْعَثَ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ قَالَ: قَرَأْتُ عِنْدَ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ: ﴿يَا أَيَّتُهَا النَّفْسُ الْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ ارْجِعِي إِلَى رَبِّكِ رَاضِيَةً مَرْضِيَّةً﴾ فَقَالَ أَبُو بَكْرٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ: إِنْ هَذَا حَسَنٌ. فَقَالَ لَهُ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: "أَمَا إِنَّ الْمَلَكَ سَيَقُولُ لَكَ هَذَا عِنْدَ الْمَوْتِ".

وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، عَنْ أَبِي كُرَيْب، عَنْ ابْنِ يَمَانٍ، بِهِ. وَهَذَا مُرْسَلٌ حَسَنٌ .

ثُمَّ قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: وَحَدَّثَنَا الْحَسَنُ بْنُ عَرَفَةَ، حَدَّثَنَا مَرْوان بْنُ شُجَاعٍ الْجَزَرِيُّ، عَنْ سَالِمٍ الْأَفْطَسِ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ قَالَ: مَاتَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ بِالطَّائِفِ، فَجَاءَ طَيْرٌ لَمْ يُرَ عَلَى خَلْقه فَدَخَلَ نَعْشَهُ، ثُمَّ لَمْ يُرَ خَارِجًا مِنْهُ فَلَمَّا دُفِنَ تُليت هَذِهِ الْآيَةُ عَلَى شَفِيرِ الْقَبْرِ، مَا يُدْرَى مَنْ تَلَاهَا: ﴿يَا أَيَّتُهَا النَّفْسُ الْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ ارْجِعِي إِلَى رَبِّكِ رَاضِيَةً مَرْضِيَّةً فَادْخُلِي فِي عِبَادِي وَادْخُلِي جَنَّتِي﴾ .

رَوَاهُ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ أَحْمَدَ عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ مَرْوان بْنِ شُجَاعٍ، عَنْ سَالِمِ بْنِ عَجْلَانَ الْأَفْطَسِ، بِهِ فَذَكَرَهُ .

وَقَدْ ذَكَرَ الْحَافِظُ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ الْمُنْذِرِ الْهَرَوِيُّ -الْمَعْرُوفُ بشكِّر-فِي كِتَابِ "الْعَجَائِبِ" بِسَنَدِهِ عَنْ قُبَاث بْنِ رَزِينٍ أَبِي هَاشِمٍ قَالَ: أسرتُ فِي بِلَادِ الرُّومِ، فَجَمَعَنَا الْمَلِكُ وعَرَض عَلَيْنَا دِينَهُ، عَلَى أَنَّ مَنِ امْتَنَعَ ضُرِبَتْ عُنُقُهُ. فَارْتَدَّ ثَلَاثَةٌ، وَجَاءَ الرَّابِعُ فَامْتَنَعَ، فَضُرِبَتْ عُنُقُهُ، وَأُلْقِيَ رَأْسُهُ فِي نَهْرٍ هُنَاكَ، فَرَسَبَ فِي الْمَاءِ ثُمَّ طَفَا عَلَى وَجْهِ الْمَاءِ، وَنَظَرَ إِلَى أُولَئِكَ الثَّلَاثَةِ فَقَالَ: يَا فُلَانُ، وَيَا فُلَانُ، وَيَا فُلَانُ -يُنَادِيهِمْ بِأَسْمَائِهِمْ-قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى فِي كِتَابِهِ: ﴿يَا أَيَّتُهَا النَّفْسُ الْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ ارْجِعِي إِلَى رَبِّكِ رَاضِيَةً مَرْضِيَّةً فَادْخُلِي فِي عِبَادِي وَادْخُلِي جَنَّتِي﴾ ثُمَّ غَاصَ فِي الْمَاءِ، [قَالَ] فَكَادَتِ النَّصَارَى أَنْ يُسْلِمُوا، وَوَقَعَ سَرِيرُ الْمَلِكِ، وَرَجَعَ أُولَئِكَ الثَّلَاثَةُ إِلَى الْإِسْلَامِ. قَالَ: وَجَاءَ الْفِدَاءُ مِنْ عِنْدِ الْخَلِيفَةِ أَبِي جَعْفَرٍ الْمَنْصُورِ فَخَلَّصَنَا.

وَرَوَى الْحَافِظُ ابْنُ عَسَاكِرَ فِي تَرْجَمَةِ رَوَاحَةَ بِنْتِ أَبِي عَمْرٍو الْأَوْزَاعِيِّ، عَنْ أَبِيهَا: حَدَّثَنِي سُلَيْمَانُ بْنُ حَبِيبٍ الْمُحَارِبِيُّ، حَدَّثَنِي أَبُو أُمَامَةَ: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ لِرَجُلٍ: "قُلِ اللَّهُمَّ، إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ نَفْسًا بِكَ مُطَمْئِنَةً، تُؤْمِنُ بِلِقَائِكَ، وَتَرْضَى بِقَضَائِكَ، وَتَقْنَعُ بِعَطَائِكَ" 

ثُمَّ رَوَى عَنْ أَبِي سُلَيْمَانَ بْنِ زَبْر أَنَّهُ قَالَ: حَدِيثُ رَوَاحَةَ هَذَا وَاحِدُ أُمِّهِ.

آخِرُ تَفْسِيرِ سُورَةِ "الفجر" ولله الحمد [والمنة] .
