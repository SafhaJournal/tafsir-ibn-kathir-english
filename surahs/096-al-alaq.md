## 96:1-5 — verses 1-5 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Tafsir of Surat Iqra

And it is the first thing that was revealed of the Qur'an.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful

* *

Imam Ahmad said: Abd al-Razzaq narrated to us, Ma'mar narrated to us, from al-Zuhri, from Urwah, from Aisha, who said: The first thing with which the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was inaugurated of revelation was the true vision in sleep. He would not see a vision except that it came like the breaking of dawn. Then seclusion was made beloved to him, so he would go to Hira and engage there in tahannuth (devotional worship) for a number of nights, taking provisions for that, then he would return to Khadijah, who would provide him with provisions for the like of that, until the truth came upon him suddenly while he was in the cave of Hira. The angel came to him there and said: "Read!" The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "I said: I am not a reciter." He said: "So he seized me and pressed me until exertion overtook me, then he released me and said: Read! I said: I am not a reciter. So he pressed me a second time until exertion overtook me, then he released me and said: Read! I said: I am not a reciter. So he pressed me a third time until exertion overtook me, then he released me and said: {Read in the name of your Lord who created} until he reached {what he did not know}." She said: So he returned with it, his body trembling, until he entered upon Khadijah and said: "Wrap me, wrap me!" So they wrapped him until the fright left him. Then he said: "O Khadijah, what is wrong with me?" And he told her the news, and said: "I fear for myself." She said to him: "No, rejoice, for by Allah, Allah will never disgrace you. Indeed you uphold the ties of kinship, you speak the truth, you bear the burden of the weak, you give hospitality to the guest, and you help against the calamities that befall in the cause of truth." Then Khadijah set off with him until she brought him to Waraqah ibn Nawfal ibn Asad ibn Abd al-Uzza ibn Qusayy (who was the cousin of Khadijah, the son of her father's brother; he was a man who had embraced Christianity in the Jahiliyyah [the pre-Islamic age of ignorance]; he used to write the Arabic script, and had written from the Gospel in Arabic what Allah willed to be written; he was a very old man who had gone blind). Khadijah said: "O cousin, listen to your nephew." Waraqah said: "O my nephew, what do you see?" So the Messenger of Allah ﷺ told him what he had seen, and Waraqah said: "This is the Namus (the angel of revelation) who was sent down upon Musa (peace be upon him). I wish I were a young man in it, that I might be alive when your people expel you." The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Will they expel me?" Waraqah said: "Yes. No man has ever come with what you have brought except that he was treated with hostility, and if your day should reach me, I will support you with a mighty support." Then it was not long before Waraqah died, and the revelation paused for a period, until the Messenger of Allah ﷺ grieved, as has reached us, a grief so intense that he went out several times intending to throw himself down from the summits of towering mountains. But every time he reached the top of a mountain in order to throw himself from it, Gabriel would appear to him and say: "O Muhammad, you are truly the Messenger of Allah." At that his agitation would settle and his soul would find peace, so he would return. Then when the pause of revelation grew long upon him, he would go out again to do the like of that, and when he reached the top of the mountain, Gabriel would appear to him and say to him the like of that.

This hadith is recorded in the Sahihayn (the two Sahihs, that is, the collections of al-Bukhari and Muslim) from the report of al-Zuhri. We have spoken about this hadith in terms of its chain, its text, and its meanings, exhaustively, at the beginning of our commentary on al-Bukhari. Whoever wants it, it is there, thoroughly set out. To Allah belongs praise and grace.

So the first thing revealed of the Qur'an is these noble, blessed verses. They are the first mercy with which Allah showed mercy to His servants, and the first favor with which Allah favored them. In them is an alert to the beginning of the creation of man from a 'alaqah (clinging clot), and that it is part of His generosity, Exalted is He, that He taught man what he did not know, so He honored him and ennobled him with knowledge, which is the very distinction by which the father of mankind, Adam (peace be upon him), was set apart above the angels. Knowledge is sometimes in the minds, sometimes on the tongue, and sometimes in writing by the fingers: mental, verbal, and written. The written necessarily entails the other two, but not the reverse. That is why He said: {Read, and your Lord is the Most Generous, who taught by the pen, taught man what he did not know.} And in the athar (a report from a Companion or Successor): "Bind knowledge by writing." And in it also: "Whoever acts upon what he knows, Allah will grant him knowledge of what he did not know."

## 96:6-19 — verses 6-19 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Allah, exalted is He, informs about man that he is possessed of joy, insolence, arrogance, and transgression when he sees himself grown self-sufficient and his wealth increased. Then He threatened him, warned him, and admonished him, saying: {Indeed, to your Lord is the return}, meaning: to Allah is the final destination and the return, and He will call you to account concerning your wealth: from where did you gather it, and on what did you spend it?

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Abdullah said: "Two are insatiable, never satisfied: the seeker of knowledge and the seeker of this world, and they are not equal. As for the seeker of knowledge, he increases in the pleasure of the Most Merciful, while the seeker of this world persists further in transgression." He said: Then Abdullah recited: {Indeed, man transgresses when he sees himself self-sufficient}, and said concerning the other: {It is only those among His servants who have knowledge that fear Allah} [Fatir: 28].

This has been reported as marfu' (a report attributed directly to the Prophet ﷺ): "Two are insatiable, never satisfied: the seeker of knowledge and the seeker of this world."

Then Allah, exalted is He, said: {Have you seen the one who forbids a servant when he prays}. It was revealed concerning Abu Jahl, may Allah curse him, who threatened the Prophet ﷺ over his praying at the House. Allah, exalted is He, first admonished him with that which is best, saying: {Have you considered: if he is upon guidance}, meaning: what then do you think, if this one whom you forbid is upon the straight path in his conduct, or {enjoins taqwa (piety, God-consciousness)} in his speech, while you rebuke him and threaten him over his prayer? Therefore He said: {Does he not know that Allah sees}, meaning: does this forbidder not know, regarding this one who is rightly guided, that Allah sees him and hears his speech, and will requite him for his deed with the fullest recompense.

Then Allah, exalted is He, said, threatening and warning: {No! If he does not desist}, meaning: if he does not turn back from the discord and obstinacy he persists in, {We will surely seize him by the forelock}, meaning: We will surely blacken it on the Day of Resurrection.

Then He said: {A lying, sinful forelock}, meaning: the forelock of Abu Jahl, lying in its speech, sinful in its deeds.

{So let him call his nadi (assembly, gathering of his people)}, meaning: his people and his kinsfolk, that is, let him call them so that he may seek their aid, {We will call the zabaniyah}, who are the angels of punishment, so that it may be known who prevails: our party or his party.

Al-Bukhari said: [chain] Ibn Abbas said: Abu Jahl said, "If I see Muhammad praying at the Kaaba, I will surely trample on his neck." This reached the Prophet ﷺ, and he said: "If he did that, the angels would have seized him." Then he said: Amr ibn Khalid corroborated him, from Ubaydullah, that is, Ibn Amr, from Abd al-Karim.

Likewise al-Tirmidhi and al-Nasa'i related it in their tafsirs by way of Abd al-Razzaq, with the same content, and likewise Ibn Jarir related it from Abu Kurayb, from Zakariyya ibn Adi, from Ubaydullah ibn Amr, with the same content.

Ahmad, al-Tirmidhi, and Ibn Jarir related, and this is his wording, [chain] Ibn Abbas said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ was praying at the Maqam (the Station of Ibrahim) when Abu Jahl ibn Hisham passed by him and said, "O Muhammad, did I not forbid you from this?" and he threatened him. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ spoke sternly to him and rebuked him, so he said, "O Muhammad, with what do you threaten me? By Allah, I am the greatest in nadi (assembly, following) in this valley!" So Allah revealed: {So let him call his nadi, We will call the zabaniyah}. Ibn Abbas said: "Had he called his nadi, the angels of punishment would have seized him at once." Al-Tirmidhi said: hasan sahih (a sound and authentic hadith).

Imam Ahmad also said: [chain] Ibn Abbas said: Abu Jahl said, "If I see the Messenger of Allah praying at the Kaaba, I will surely come to him until I trample on his neck." He said: So he (the Messenger of Allah) said: "Had he done so, the angels would have seized him openly. And had the Jews wished for death, they would have died and seen their seats in the Fire. And had those who make mubahalah (mutual imprecation before Allah) with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ gone out to do so, they would have returned finding neither wealth nor family."

Ibn Jarir also said: [chain] Ibn Abbas said: Abu Jahl said, "If Muhammad returns to praying at the Maqam, I will surely kill him." So Allah, mighty and majestic is He, revealed: {Read in the name of your Lord who created [He created man from a clinging clot]} until He reached this verse: {We will surely seize him by the forelock, a lying, sinful forelock. So let him call his nadi, We will call the zabaniyah}. Then the Prophet ﷺ came and prayed. It was said to Abu Jahl, "What prevents you?" He said, "What is between me and him has turned black with troops." Ibn Abbas said: By Allah, had he moved, the angels would have seized him while the people were looking on at him.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Abu Hurayrah said: Abu Jahl said, "Does Muhammad grind his face into the dust among you?" They said, "Yes." He said, "By al-Lat and al-Uzza, if I see him praying like that, I will surely trample on his neck and grind his face into the dust." So he came to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ while he was praying, in order to trample on his neck. He said: Then nothing struck them with surprise except that he was recoiling on his heels and warding off with his hands. It was said to him, "What is wrong with you?" He said, "Between me and him is a trench of fire, and a terror, and wings." The Messenger of Allah said: "Had he drawn near me, the angels would have snatched him limb by limb." He said: And Allah revealed, though I do not know whether this is within the hadith of Abu Hurayrah or not: {No! Indeed, man transgresses} to the end of the surah.

Ahmad ibn Hanbal, Muslim, al-Nasa'i, and Ibn Abi Hatim related it from the hadith of Mu'tamir ibn Sulayman, with the same content.

* * *

His saying: {No! Do not obey him}, meaning: O Muhammad, do not obey him in what he forbids you from, of persisting in worship and increasing it. Pray wherever you wish and do not concern yourself with him, for Allah is your protector and your helper, and He safeguards you from the people. {And prostrate and draw near}, as is established in the Sahih, with Muslim, [chain] Abu Hurayrah reported that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "The servant is nearest to his Lord while he is prostrating, so increase supplication therein."

It has also preceded that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to prostrate during {When the sky is split open} and {Read in the name of your Lord who created}.

This is the end of the tafsir of Surah "Iqra" (Read).

يُخْبِرُ تَعَالَى عَنِ الْإِنْسَانِ أَنَّهُ ذُو فَرَحٍ وَأَشَرٍ وَبَطَرٍ وَطُغْيَانٍ، إِذَا رَأَى نَفْسَهُ قَدِ اسْتَغْنَى وَكَثُرَ مَالُهُ. ثُمَّ تَهدده وَتَوَعَّدَهُ وَوَعَظَهُ فَقَالَ: ﴿إِنَّ إِلَى رَبِّكَ الرُّجْعَى﴾ أَيْ: إِلَى اللَّهِ الْمَصِيرُ وَالْمَرْجِعُ، وَسَيُحَاسِبُكَ عَلَى مَالِكِ: مِنْ أَيْنَ جَمَعْتَهُ؟ وَفِيمَ صَرَفْتَهُ؟

قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا زَيْدُ بْنُ إِسْمَاعِيلَ الصَّائِغُ، حَدَّثَنَا جَعْفَرُ بْنُ عَوْنٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو عُمَيس، عَنْ عَوْنٍ قَالَ: قَالَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ: مَنهومان لَا يَشْبَعَانِ، صَاحِبُ العلم وصاحب الدنيا، ولا يستويان، فَأَمَّا صَاحِبُ الْعِلْمِ فَيَزْدَادُ رِضَا الرَّحْمَنِ، وَأَمَّا صَاحِبُ الدُّنْيَا فَيَتَمَادَى فِي الطُّغْيَانِ. قَالَ ثُمَّ قَرَأَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ: ﴿إِنَّ الإنْسَانَ لَيَطْغَى أَنْ رَآهُ اسْتَغْنَى﴾ وَقَالَ لِلْآخَرِ: ﴿إِنَّمَا يَخْشَى اللَّهَ مِنْ عِبَادِهِ الْعُلَمَاءُ﴾ [فَاطِرٍ: ٢٨] .

وَقَدْ رُوي هَذَا مَرْفُوعًا إِلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "مَنْهُومَانِ لَا يَشْبَعَانِ: طَالِبُ عِلْمٍ، وَطَالِبُ دُنْيَا" .

ثُمَّ قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿أَرَأَيْتَ الَّذِي يَنْهَى عَبْدًا إِذَا صَلَّى﴾ نَزَلَتْ فِي أَبِي جَهْلٍ، لَعَنَهُ اللَّهُ، تَوَعَّدَ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ عَلَى الصَّلَاةِ عِنْدَ الْبَيْتِ، فَوَعَظَهُ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى بِالَّتِي هِيَ أَحْسَنُ أَوَّلًا فَقَالَ: ﴿أَرَأَيْتَ إِنْ كَانَ عَلَى الْهُدَى﴾ أَيْ: فَمَا ظَنُّكَ إِنْ كَانَ هَذَا الَّذِي تَنْهَاهُ عَلَى الطَّرِيقِ الْمُسْتَقِيمَةِ فِي فِعْلِهِ، أَوْ ﴿أَمَرَ بِالتَّقْوَى﴾ بِقَوْلِهِ، وَأَنْتَ تَزْجُرُهُ وَتَتَوَعَّدُهُ عَلَى صِلَاتِهِ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿أَلَمْ يَعْلَمْ بِأَنَّ اللَّهَ يَرَى﴾ أَيْ: أَمَا عَلِمَ هَذَا النَّاهِي لِهَذَا الْمُهْتَدِي أَنَّ اللَّهَ يَرَاهُ وَيَسْمَعُ كَلَامَهُ، وَسَيُجَازِيهِ عَلَى فِعْلِهِ أَتَمَّ الْجَزَاءِ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ تَعَالَى مُتَوَعِّدًا وَمُتَهَدِّدًا: ﴿كَلا لَئِنْ لَمْ يَنْتَهِ﴾ أَيْ: لَئِنْ لَمْ يَرْجِعْ عَمَّا هُوَ فِيهِ مِنَ الشِّقَاقِ وَالْعِنَادِ ﴿لَنَسْفَعَنْ بِالنَّاصِيَةِ﴾ أَيْ: لنَسمَنَّها سَوَادًا يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿نَاصِيَةٍ كَاذِبَةٍ خَاطِئَةٍ﴾ يَعْنِي: نَاصِيَةَ أَبِي جَهْلٍ كَاذِبَةً فِي مَقَالِهَا خَاطِئَةً فِي فعَالها.

﴿فَلْيَدْعُ نَادِيَهُ﴾ أَيْ: قَوْمَهُ وَعَشِيرَتَهُ، أَيْ: لِيَدَعُهُمْ يَسْتَنْصِرُ بِهِمْ، ﴿سَنَدْعُ الزَّبَانِيَةَ﴾ وَهُمْ مَلَائِكَةُ الْعَذَابِ، حَتَّى يَعْلَمَ مَنْ يغلبُ: أحزبُنا أَوْ حِزْبُهُ.

قَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، عَنْ مَعْمَر، عَنْ عَبْدِ الْكَرِيمِ الجَزَري، عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: قَالَ أَبُو جَهْلٍ: لَئِنْ رَأَيْتُ مُحَمَّدًا يُصَلِّي عِنْدَ الْكَعْبَةِ لَأَطَأَنَّ عَلَى عُنُقه. فبَلغَ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ، فَقَالَ: "لَئِنْ فَعَلَهُ لَأَخَذَتْهُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ". ثُمَّ قَالَ: تَابَعَهُ عَمْرُو بْنُ خَالِدٍ، عَنْ عُبَيْدِ اللَّهِ -يَعْنِي ابْنَ عَمْرٍو-عَنْ عَبْدِ الْكَرِيمِ .

وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ وَالنَّسَائِيُّ فِي تَفْسِيرِهِمَا مِنْ طَرِيقِ عَبْدِ الرَّزَّاقِ، بِهِ وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، عَنْ أَبِي كُرَيْب، عَنْ زَكَرِيَّا بْنِ عَدِيّ، عَنْ عُبَيْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو، بِهِ .

وَرَوَى أَحْمَدُ، وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ وَابْنُ جَرِيرٍ -وَهَذَا لَفْظُهُ-من طريق داود بن أبي هند، عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ يُصَلِّي عِنْدَ الْمَقَامِ فَمَرَّ بِهِ أَبُو جَهْلِ بْنُ هِشَامٍ فَقَالَ: يَا مُحَمَّدُ، أَلَمْ أَنْهَكَ عَنْ هَذَا؟ -وَتَوعَّده-فَأَغْلَظَ لَهُ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ وانتهره، فقال: يا محمد، بِأَيِّ شَيْءٍ تُهَدِّدُنِي؟ أَمَا وَاللَّهِ إِنِّي لَأَكْثَرُ هَذَا الْوَادِي نَادِيًا! فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ: ﴿فَلْيَدْعُ نَادِيَهُ سَنَدْعُ الزَّبَانِيَةَ﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: لَوْ دَعَا نَادِيَهُ لَأَخَذَتْهُ مَلَائِكَةُ الْعَذَابِ مِنْ سَاعَتِهِ وَقَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ أَيْضًا: حَدَّثَنَا إِسْمَاعِيلُ بْنُ زَيْدٍ أَبُو يَزِيدَ، حَدَّثَنَا فُرَات، عَنْ عَبْدِ الْكَرِيمِ، عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: قَالَ أَبُو جَهْلٍ: لَئِنْ رَأَيْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ يُصَلِّي عِنْدَ الْكَعْبَةِ لَآتِيَنَّهُ حَتَّى أَطَأَ عَلَى عُنُقِهِ. قَالَ: فَقَالَ: "لَوْ فَعَلَ لَأَخَذَتْهُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ عِيَانًا، وَلَوْ أَنَّ الْيَهُودَ تَمَنَّوا الْمَوْتَ لَمَاتُوا وَرَأَوْا مَقَاعِدَهُمْ مِنَ النَّارِ، وَلَوْ خَرَجَ الَّذِينَ يُبَاهلون رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ لَرَجَعُوا لَا يَجِدُونَ مَالًا وَلَا أَهْلًا" .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ أَيْضًا: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ حُمَيْدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى بْنُ وَاضِحٍ، أَخْبَرَنَا يُونُسُ بْنُ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ، عَنِ الْوَلِيدِ بْنِ الْعَيْزَارِ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: قَالَ أَبُو جَهْلٍ: لَئِنْ عَادَ مُحَمَّدٌ يُصَلِّي عِنْدَ الْمَقَامِ لَأَقْتُلَنَّهُ. فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ: ﴿اقْرَأْ بِاسْمِ رَبِّكَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ [خَلَقَ الإنْسَانَ مِنْ عَلَقٍ] ﴾ حَتَّى بَلَغَ هَذِهِ الْآيَةَ: ﴿لَنَسْفَعَنْ بِالنَّاصِيَةِ نَاصِيَةٍ كَاذِبَةٍ خَاطِئَةٍ فَلْيَدْعُ نَادِيَهُ سَنَدْعُ الزَّبَانِيَةَ﴾ فَجَاءَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ فَصَلَّى فَقِيلَ: مَا يَمْنَعُكَ؟ قَالَ: قَدِ اسْوَدَّ مَا بَيْنِي وَبَيْنَهُ مِنَ الْكَتَائِبِ. قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: وَاللَّهِ لَوْ تَحَرَّكَ لَأَخَذَتْهُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ وَالنَّاسُ يَنْظُرُونَ إِلَيْهِ .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ عَبْدِ الْأَعْلَى، حَدَّثَنَا الْمُعْتَمِرُ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، حَدَّثَنَا نُعَيْمُ بْنُ أَبِي هِنْدٍ، عَنْ أَبِي حَازِمٍ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيرة قَالَ: قَالَ أَبُو جَهْلٍ: هَلْ يعفِّر مُحَمَّدٌ وَجْهَهُ بَيْنَ أَظْهُرِكُمْ؟ قَالُوا: نَعَمْ. قَالَ: فَقَالَ: وَاللَّاتِ وَالْعُزَّى لَئِنْ رَأَيْتُهُ يُصَلِّي كَذَلِكَ لَأَطَأَنَّ عَلَى رَقَبَتِهِ ولأعفِّرن وَجْهَهُ فِي التُّرَابِ، فَأَتَى رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ وَهُوَ يُصَلي لِيَطَأَ عَلَى رَقَبَتِهِ، قَالَ: فَمَا فَجأهم مِنْهُ إِلَّا وَهُوَ يَنْكِصُ عَلَى عَقِبَيْهِ وَيَتَّقِي بِيَدَيْهِ، قَالَ: فَقِيلَ لَهُ: مَا لَكَ؟ فَقَالَ: إِنَّ بَيْنِي وَبَيْنَهُ خَنْدقا مِنْ نَارٍ وهَولا وَأَجْنِحَةً. قَالَ: فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ: "لَوْ دَنَا مِنِّي لَاخْتَطَفَتْهُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ عُضْوًا عُضْوًا". قَالَ: وَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ -لَا أَدْرِي فِي حَدِيثِ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ أَمْ لَا-: ﴿كَلا إِنَّ الإنْسَانَ لَيَطْغَى﴾ إِلَى آخِرِ السُّورَةِ.

وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ أَحْمَدُ بْنُ حَنْبَلٍ، وَمُسْلِمٌ، وَالنَّسَائِيُّ، وَابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ مُعْتَمِرِ بْنِ سُلَيْمَانَ، بِهِ .

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿كَلا لَا تُطِعْهُ﴾ يَعْنِي: يَا مُحَمَّدُ، لَا تُطِعْهُ فِيمَا يَنْهَاكَ عَنْهُ مِنَ الْمُدَاوَمَةِ عَلَى الْعِبَادَةِ وَكَثْرَتِهَا، وصلِّ حَيْثُ شِئْتَ وَلَا تُبَالِهِ؛ فَإِنَّ اللَّهَ حَافِظُكَ وَنَاصِرُكَ، وَهُوَ يَعْصِمُكَ مِنَ النَّاسِ، ﴿وَاسْجُدْ وَاقْتَرِبْ﴾ كَمَا ثَبَتَ فِي الصَّحِيحِ -عِنْدَ مُسْلِمٍ-مِنْ طَرِيقِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ وَهْبٍ، عن عَمْرِو بْنِ الْحَارِثِ، عَنْ عُمَارَةَ بْنِ غَزِيَّةَ، عَنْ سُمَيّ، عَنْ أَبِي صَالِحٍ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "أَقْرَبُ مَا يَكُونُ الْعَبْدُ مِنْ رَبِّهِ وَهُوَ سَاجِدٌ، فَأَكْثِرُوا الدُّعَاءَ" .

وَتَقَدَّمَ أَيْضًا: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم كَانَ يَسْجُدُ فِي: ﴿إِذَا السَّمَاءُ انْشَقَّتْ﴾ وَ ﴿اقْرَأْ بِاسْمِ رَبِّكَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ﴾

آخِرُ تَفْسِيرِ سورة "اقرأ" .
