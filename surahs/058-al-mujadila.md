## 58:1 — verse 1

### Translation

Tafsir of Surat al-Mujadalah

It is Medinan.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Abu Muawiyah told us, al-A'mash told us, from Tamim ibn Salamah, from Urwah, from Aishah, who said: Praise be to Allah whose hearing encompasses all sounds. The woman who disputed came to the Prophet ﷺ speaking to him while I was in a corner of the house, not hearing what she was saying, so Allah, Mighty and Majestic, revealed: {Allah has indeed heard the speech of the one who disputes with you concerning her husband} to the end of the verse.

Al-Bukhari related this in the Book of Monotheism as a suspended report (ta'liq), saying: Al-A'mash said, from Tamim ibn Salamah, from Urwah, from Aishah, and mentioned it. Al-Nasa'i, Ibn Majah, Ibn Abi Hatim, and Ibn Jarir also transmitted it through other channels from al-A'mash with the same content.

In a version reported by Ibn Abi Hatim from al-A'mash, from Tamim ibn Salamah, from Urwah, from Aishah, she said: Blessed is He whose hearing contains everything. I could hear the speech of Khawlah bint Tha'labah, though part of it was hidden from me, while she was complaining about her husband to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, saying: "O Messenger of Allah, he consumed my youth and I bore his children until, when I grew old in years and could no longer bear children, he pronounced zihar (a form of divorce oath) against me. O Allah, I complain to You." She said: She had not yet departed when Gabriel descended with this verse: {Allah has indeed heard the speech of the one who disputes with you concerning her husband}. And it was said that her husband was Aws ibn al-Samit.

Ibn Lahi'ah said, from Abu al-Aswad, from Urwah: He is Aws ibn al-Samit. Aws was a man afflicted with a touch of madness (lamam), and when this affliction seized him and grew intense, he would pronounce zihar against his wife, and when it passed he would say nothing. So she came to the Messenger of Allah seeking a ruling concerning this and complaining to Allah, and Allah revealed: {Allah has indeed heard the speech of the one who disputes with you concerning her husband and complains to Allah} the verse.

Hisham ibn Urwah likewise related from his father that a man was afflicted with lamam, and mentioned something similar.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] My father told us, Musa ibn Isma'il Abu Salamah told us, Jarir, that is ibn Hazim, told us, saying: I heard Abu Yazid relate, saying: A woman met Umar, called Khawlah bint Tha'labah, while he was traveling with the people. She asked him to stop, and he stopped for her, drew near to her, inclined his head toward her, and placed his hands on her shoulders until she finished what she needed and then departed. A man said to him: "O Commander of the Believers, you kept the notables of Quraysh waiting for this old woman?!" He said: "Woe to you! Do you know who this is?" He said: "No." He said: "This is a woman whose complaint Allah heard from above seven heavens. This is Khawlah bint Tha'labah. By Allah, if she had not left me until nightfall, I would not have left her until she finished what she needed, except when a prayer time came, in which case I would pray it and then return to her until she finished what she needed."

This report has a break (munqati') between Abu Yazid and Umar ibn al-Khattab. It has also been related through another channel. Ibn Abi Hatim also said: [chain] Al-Mundhir ibn Shadhan told us, Ya'la told us, Zakariyya told us, from Amir, who said: The woman who disputed concerning her husband was Khawlah bint al-Samit, and her mother was Mu'adhah, the one concerning whom Allah revealed: {And do not compel your slave girls to prostitution if they desire chastity} [al-Nur: 33].

The correct statement is: Khawlah, the wife of Aws ibn al-Samit.

## 58:2-4 — verses 2-4 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Imam Ahmad said: Sa'd ibn Ibrahim and Ya'qub narrated to us, they said: our father narrated to us, Muhammad ibn Ishaq narrated to us, Ma'mar ibn Abdullah ibn Handhala narrated to me, from Ibn Abdullah ibn Salam, from Khuwaylah bint Tha'labah, who said: it was concerning me, by God, and concerning Aws ibn al-Samit, that God revealed the opening of Surat al-Mujadilah. She said: I was married to him, and he was an old man whose temperament had become bad. She said: One day he came in to me, and I answered him back about something, and he became angry and said, "You are to me like my mother's back." She said: Then he went out and sat for a while in the gathering place of his people, then he came back in to me, and behold, he wanted me for himself. She said: I said, "No, by Him in whose hand is the soul of Khuwaylah, you shall not have access to me, having said what you said, until God and His Messenger give judgment concerning us." She said: He then lunged at me, and I resisted him, and I overcame him as a woman overcomes a weak old man, and I threw him off me. She said: Then I went out to one of my neighbors and borrowed clothes from her, then I went out until I came to the Messenger of God ﷺ. I sat before him and mentioned to him what I had encountered from him, and I began to complain to him of the bad treatment I met from him. She said: The Messenger of God ﷺ kept saying, "O Khuwaylah, your cousin is an old man, so fear God concerning him." She said: By God, I had not yet left when the Qur'an was revealed concerning me. The Messenger of God ﷺ was overcome by the state that used to come over him, then it was lifted from him, and he said to me, "O Khuwaylah, God has revealed something concerning you and your companion." Then he recited to me: {God has heard the statement of the one who disputes with you concerning her husband and complains to God, and God hears your exchange. Indeed, God is All-Hearing, All-Seeing} up to His saying: {and for the disbelievers is a painful punishment}. She said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said to me, "Command him to free a slave." I said, "O Messenger of God, he has nothing with which to free a slave." He said, "Then let him fast two consecutive months." I said, "By God, he is an old man, he cannot fast at all." He said, "Then let him feed sixty poor people a wasq (a measure) of dates." I said, "O Messenger of God, he has none of that." She said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said, "Then we shall help him with a araq (basket) of dates." I said, "O Messenger of God, and I shall help him with another araq." He said, "You have done well and rightly. Go and give it as charity on his behalf, then treat your cousin well." She said: So I did.

Abu Dawud narrated it in the Book of Divorce in his Sunan through two chains, from Muhammad ibn Ishaq ibn Yasar, with the same content, and in his version she is called Khawlah bint Tha'labah. She is also said to be Khawlah bint Malik ibn Tha'labah. And it may be given in the diminutive form, so she is called Khuwaylah. There is no contradiction between these statements; the matter is close in any case. God knows best.

This is the sound account of the reason for the revelation of the opening of this surah. As for the hadith of Salamah ibn Sakhr, it does not indicate that it was the reason for the revelation, but rather it is an account of acting upon what God revealed in this surah, of freeing a slave, fasting, or feeding, as Imam Ahmad said:

Yazid ibn Harun narrated to us, Muhammad ibn Ishaq informed us, from Muhammad ibn Amr ibn Ata, from Sulayman ibn Yasar, from Salamah ibn Sakhr al-Ansari, who said: I was a man who had been given, in relations with women, what no other man had been given. When Ramadan came, I declared dhihar upon my wife until the end of Ramadan, fearing that I might do something with her one night and then continue in that until daybreak came upon me while I was unable to stop myself. Then one night, while she was serving me, something of her became uncovered to me, and I lunged upon her. When morning came, I went out to my people and told them my story, and I said, "Come with me to the Prophet ﷺ so I can inform him of my matter." They said, "No, by God, we will not do that. We fear that the Messenger of God ﷺ will reveal, or say, concerning us a statement whose disgrace will remain upon us forever. Rather, go yourself and do as you see fit." He said: So I went out until I came to the Prophet ﷺ and told him my story. He said to me, "Are you the one who did that?" I said, "I am the one who did that." He said, "Are you the one who did that?" I said, "I am the one who did that." He said, "Are you the one who did that?" I said, "Yes, here I am, so carry out upon me the judgment of God Most High, for I shall bear it patiently." He said, "Free a slave." He said: I struck the side of my neck with my hand and said, "No, by the One who sent you with the truth, I own nothing besides it (my own neck, meaning I have no slave)." He said, "Then fast two months." I said, "O Messenger of God, did what befell me befall me except because of fasting (i.e. it happened at night during a fast)?" He said, "Then give charity." I said, "By the One who sent you with the truth, we spent this very night of ours hungry, having no evening meal." He said, "Go to the collector of charity of Banu Zurayq and tell him to hand it over to you. Feed sixty poor people from it, a wasq of dates, then use the rest to help yourself and your family." He said: So I returned to my people and said, "I found with you narrowness and poor counsel, but I found with the Messenger of God ﷺ generosity and blessing. He has ordered your charity to be given to me, so hand it over to me." So they handed it over to me.

Abu Dawud and Ibn Majah narrated it likewise, and al-Tirmidhi narrated it in abridged form and graded it hasan (good).

The apparent sense of the narration is that this story occurred after the story of Aws ibn al-Samit and his wife Khuwaylah bint Tha'labah, as is indicated by the wording of both accounts upon reflection.

Khasif said, from Mujahid, from Ibn Abbas: The first to declare dhihar against his wife was Aws ibn al-Samit, brother of Ubadah ibn al-Samit, and his wife was Khawlah bint Tha'labah ibn Malik. When he declared dhihar against her, she feared that it would count as divorce, so she came to the Messenger of God ﷺ and said, "O Messenger of God, Aws has declared dhihar against me, and if we separate we will be ruined, for I have borne children from him and I have a long history with him." And she complained of that and wept, and nothing had yet come down concerning it. Then God revealed: {God has heard the statement of the one who disputes with you concerning her husband and complains to God} up to His saying: {and for the disbelievers is a painful punishment}. So the Messenger of God ﷺ called him and said, "Can you afford a slave to free?" He said, "No, by God, O Messenger of God, I cannot afford one." He said: So the Messenger of God ﷺ gathered enough for him, until a slave was freed on his behalf, and then he took his wife back. Ibn Jarir narrated it.

For this reason Ibn Abbas and the majority went to what we have stated. God knows best.

As for His saying, Exalted is He: {Those among you who declare dhihar against their wives}, the origin of dhihar (declaring one's wife forbidden by likening her to one's mother's back) is derived from al-dhahr (the back). This is because in the Age of Ignorance, when a man declared dhihar against his wife, he would say to her, "You are to me like my mother's back." Then in the Sacred Law, dhihar came to apply to all parts of the body by analogy to the back, and dhihar in the Age of Ignorance was counted as divorce. So God granted this community a concession and made for it an expiation, and did not make it a divorce as they used to hold in their pre-Islamic practice. Thus said more than one of the Salaf (early generations).

Ibn Jarir said: Abu Kurayb narrated to us, Ubaydullah ibn Musa narrated to us, from Abu Hamzah, from Ikrimah, from Ibn Abbas, who said: In the Age of Ignorance, when a man said to his wife, "You are to me like my mother's back," she became forbidden to him. The first to declare dhihar in Islam was Aws, and he was married to a cousin of his called Khuwaylah bint Tha'labah. He declared dhihar against her, and he was struck with regret, and said, "I see that you have indeed become forbidden to me." And she said the same to him. He said, "Then go to the Messenger of God ﷺ." So she went to the Messenger of God and found with him a hairdresser combing his hair. He said, "O Khuwaylah, we have not been commanded anything concerning your matter." Then God revealed to His Messenger ﷺ, and he said, "O Khuwaylah, rejoice." She said, "Good news?" He said: Then he recited to her: {God has heard the statement of the one who disputes with you concerning her husband and complains to God, and God hears your exchange} up to His saying: {And those who declare dhihar against their wives and then go back on what they said, then the freeing of a slave before they touch each other}.

She said, "And what slave do we have? By God, he finds no slave except me." He said: {But whoever does not find one, then a fast of two consecutive months}. She said, "By God, were it not that he drinks three times a day, he would lose his sight!" He said: {But whoever is unable, then the feeding of sixty poor people}.

She said: "How so? It is nothing but a meal followed by another like it!" He said: So he called for half a wasq, thirty sa'; a wasq being sixty sa', and said: "Let him feed sixty poor persons, and let him return to you." This is a good and strong chain of transmission, and a gharib (singular) wording. Something similar has been related from Abu al-'Aliyah, for Ibn Abi Hatim said:

[chain] Muhammad ibn Abd al-Rahman al-Harawi told us, 'Ali ibn 'Asim told us, from Dawud ibn Abi Hind, from Abu al-'Aliyah, who said: Khawlah bint Dulayj was married to a man of the Ansar who was blind, poor, and ill-tempered. In the marriage practice of the Jahiliyyah (pre-Islamic era), when a man wished to divorce his wife, he would say: "You are to me like my mother's back." She had one child or two children by him. One day she argued with him about something, and he said: "You are to me like my mother's back." So she gathered her garments about her and went in to the Prophet ﷺ, who was in the house of 'A'ishah, and 'A'ishah was washing one side of his head. She came to him with her child, and said: "O Messenger of Allah, my husband is blind, poor, has nothing, and is ill-tempered. I argued with him about something, and he became angry and said: 'You are to me like my mother's back,' though he did not intend divorce by it. I have one child or two children by him." He said: "I know of nothing except that she has become forbidden to him." She said: "I complain to Allah of what has befallen me, and of the father of my child." He said: Then 'A'ishah moved and began washing the other side of his head, and the woman moved along with her, saying: "O Messenger of Allah, my husband is blind, poor, ill-tempered, and I have one child or two children by him. I argued with him about something and he became angry and said: 'You are to me like my mother's back,' and he did not intend divorce by it!" She said: He raised his head to me and said: "I know of nothing except that you have become forbidden to him." She said: "I complain to Allah of what has befallen me and of the father of my child." He said: 'A'ishah saw that the face of the Prophet ﷺ had changed, so she said to her: "Behind you, behind you!" So she withdrew. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ remained in that state of receiving revelation as long as Allah willed, and when the revelation ceased he said: "O 'A'ishah, where is the woman?" So she called her, and the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said to her: "Go and bring me your husband." So she went off hurrying and brought him. And indeed he was, as she had said, blind, poor, and ill-tempered. The Prophet ﷺ said: "I seek refuge in Allah, the All-Hearing, the All-Knowing. In the name of Allah, the Most Merciful, the Most Compassionate: {Allah has indeed heard the speech of the one who disputes with you concerning her husband [and complains to Allah]}" up to His saying: {And those who pronounce zihar (declaring one's wife forbidden like a mother) against their wives and then go back on what they said, [let them free a slave]}. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Can you find a slave to free before you touch her?" He said: "No." He said: "Can you fast two consecutive months?" He said: "By the One who sent you with truth, if I go without eating for two or three times, my eyesight nearly fails." He said: "Then can you feed sixty poor persons?" He said: "Not unless you help me." So the Messenger of Allah ﷺ helped him, and said: "Feed sixty poor persons." He said: And Allah changed the divorce, making it zihar instead.

Ibn Jarir related it, from Ibn al-Muthanna, from Abd al-A'la, from Dawud: "I heard Abu al-'Aliyah," and he mentioned something similar, more briefly than this wording.

Sa'id ibn Jubayr said: "Ila' (oath of abstention) and zihar were among the forms of divorce in the Jahiliyyah. Allah then set a time limit of four months for ila', and made expiation obligatory for zihar." Ibn Abi Hatim related it similarly.

Imam Malik used the phrase {of you} as evidence that the disbeliever is not included in this verse, since the address is to the believers. The majority responded that this expression occurs as a matter of the general/typical case, so it carries no restrictive implication. The majority used the phrase {of their wives} as evidence that zihar cannot apply to a slave woman, and that she is not included in this address.

Concerning His saying, {They are not their mothers; their mothers are none but those who gave birth to them}: that is, a woman does not become the man's mother by his saying "You are to me like my mother" or "like my mother" or "like my mother's back" or anything similar to that; she does not become his mother by that. Rather, his mother is the one who gave birth to him. Hence He said: {And indeed they utter an evil word and a falsehood}, that is, foul, false speech. {And indeed Allah is Ever-Pardoning, Oft-Forgiving}, that is, for what occurred among you in the state of Jahiliyyah. Likewise also for what results from a slip of the tongue, when the speaker did not intend it, as related by Abu Dawud: that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ heard a man saying to his wife, "O my sister," and he said: "Is she your sister?" This was a disapproval, but he did not thereby forbid her to him merely on that basis, because he had not intended it. Had he intended it, she would have become forbidden to him, since, according to the correct view, there is no difference between the mother and any of the other unmarriageable female relatives such as a sister, paternal aunt, maternal aunt, and the like.

Concerning His saying, {And those who pronounce zihar against their wives and then go back on what they said}: the predecessors and the leading scholars differed over what is meant by "then go back on what they said." Some said: the "going back" means returning to the utterance of zihar and repeating it. This view is invalid, and it is the position chosen by Ibn Hazm and the view of Dawud, and Abu 'Umar ibn Abd al-Barr related it from Bukayr ibn al-Ashajj, al-Farra', and a faction of the theologians.

Al-Shafi'i said: it means that he keeps her after the zihar for a period during which he could have divorced her, but does not divorce her.

Ahmad ibn Hanbal said: it means that he returns to intercourse or resolves upon it, so she does not become lawful for him until he performs this expiation. It has been related from Malik that it is resolving upon intercourse or upon keeping her, and it has also been related from him that it is intercourse itself.

Abu Hanifah said: it means that he returns to zihar after it has been forbidden and after the practice of the Jahiliyyah concerning it has been abolished. So whenever a man pronounces zihar against his wife, he has thereby made her forbidden to himself with a prohibition that can only be lifted by the expiation. This is the view held by his companions and by al-Layth ibn Sa'd.

Ibn Lahi'ah said: 'Ata' told me, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, concerning {then go back on what they said}: it means they intend to return to the intercourse which they had forbidden to themselves.

Al-Hasan al-Basri said: it means intercourse in the private parts specifically. He did not consider it objectionable for there to be contact short of that before expiation.

'Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn 'Abbas, concerning {before the two of them touch each other}: "touching" means intercourse. Likewise said 'Ata', al-Zuhri, Qatadah, and Muqatil ibn Hayyan.

Al-Zuhri said: he may not kiss her nor touch her until he performs the expiation.

The compilers of the Sunan related, from the hadith of 'Ikrimah, from Ibn 'Abbas, that a man said: "O Messenger of Allah, I pronounced zihar against my wife, then I had intercourse with her before I made expiation." He said: "What led you to do that, may Allah have mercy on you?" He said: "I saw her anklet in the moonlight." He said: "Then do not approach her until you do what Allah, mighty and majestic, has commanded you."

Al-Tirmidhi said: "Hasan gharib sahih (a sound, singular, authentic hadith)." Abu Dawud and al-Nasa'i related it from the hadith of 'Ikrimah as mursal (a report missing a link at the end of the chain). Al-Nasa'i said: "And this is more likely to be correct."

Concerning His saying, {then the freeing of a slave}: that is, the manumission of a whole slave before the two of them touch each other. Here the "slave" is unrestricted, not qualified by faith, whereas in the expiation for killing it is qualified by faith. Al-Shafi'i, may Allah have mercy on him, therefore applied what is unrestricted here to what is restricted there, on the grounds that the obligation is the same, namely the freeing of a slave. He supported this with what he related from Malik, by his chain, from Mu'awiyah ibn al-Hakam al-Sulami, in the story of the black slave girl, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Free her, for she is a believer." Ahmad related it in his Musnad, and Muslim in his Sahih.

The Hafiz Abu Bakr al-Bazzar said:

[chain] Yusuf ibn Musa told us, Abdullah ibn Numayr told us, from Isma'il ibn Muslim, from 'Amr ibn Dinar, from Tawus, from Ibn 'Abbas, who said: A man came to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and said: "I pronounced zihar against my wife, then I had intercourse with her before making expiation." The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Did Allah not say, {before the two of them touch each other}?" He said: "She appealed to me." He said: "Refrain until you make expiation."

Al-Bazzar then said: "Nothing better than this has been related from Ibn 'Abbas. Isma'il ibn Muslim has been criticized, though a large number of scholars related from him." A point of jurisprudence in it is that he commanded him with only a single expiation.

Concerning His saying, {That is what you are admonished with}: that is, you are restrained by it. {And Allah is All-Aware of what you do}: that is, He is aware of what will set you right, knowing of your conditions.

Concerning His saying, {And whoever does not find [a slave] then a fast of two consecutive months before the two of them touch each other, and whoever is unable, then the feeding of sixty poor persons}...

The hadiths conveying this in sequence have already been mentioned earlier, as is established in the two Sahihs regarding the story of the man who had intercourse with his wife during Ramadan.

{That is so that you may believe in Allah and His Messenger} means: We legislated this for this purpose.

His saying: {And these are the limits of Allah} means: His prohibitions, so do not violate them.

His saying: {And for the disbelievers there is a painful punishment} means: those who did not believe and did not commit themselves to the rulings of this Shari'ah. Do not suppose that they are safe from affliction. Not at all, the matter is not as they claim. Rather, for them there is a painful punishment, that is, in this world and in the Hereafter.

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ أَبُو دَاوُدَ، وَابْنُ مَاجَهْ، وَاخْتَصَرَهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ وحَسَّنه 

وَظَاهِرُ السِّيَاقِ: أَنَّ هَذِهِ الْقِصَّةَ كَانَتْ بَعْدَ قِصَّةِ أَوْسِ بْنِ الصَّامِتِ وَزَوْجَتِهِ خُويَلة بِنْتِ ثَعْلَبَةَ، كَمَا دَلَّ عَلَيْهِ سِيَاقُ تِلْكَ وَهَذِهِ بَعْدَ التَّأَمُّلِ.

قَالَ خَصيف، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ، عَنِ بن عَبَّاسٍ: أَوَّلُ مَنْ ظَاهَرَ مِنِ امْرَأَتِهِ أَوْسُ بْنُ الصَّامِتِ، أَخُو عُبَادَةَ بْنِ الصَّامِتِ، وَامْرَأَتُهُ خَوْلَةُ بِنْتُ ثَعْلَبَةَ بْنِ مَالِكٍ، فَلَمَّا ظَاهَرَ مِنْهَا خَشِيت أَنْ يَكُونَ ذَلِكَ طَلَاقًا، فَأَتَتْ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالَتْ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، إِنَّ أَوْسًا ظَاهَرَ مِنِّي، وَإِنَّا إِنِ افْتَرَقْنَا هَلَكْنَا، وَقَدْ نَثَرتُ بَطْنِي مِنْهُ، وقَدمتْ صُحْبَتَهُ. وَهِيَ تَشْكُو ذَلِكَ وَتَبْكِي، وَلَمْ يَكُنْ جَاءَ فِي ذَلِكَ شَيْءٌ. فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ: ﴿قَدْ سَمِعَ اللَّهُ قَوْلَ الَّتِي تُجَادِلُكَ فِي زَوْجِهَا وَتَشْتَكِي إِلَى اللَّهِ﴾ إِلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَلِلْكَافِرِينَ عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ﴾ فَدَعَاهُ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالَ: "أَتَقْدِرُ عَلَى رَقَبَةٍ تُعْتِقُهَا؟ ". قَالَ: لَا وَاللَّهِ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ مَا أَقْدِرُ عَلَيْهَا؟ قَالَ: فَجَمَعَ لَهُ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ، حَتَّى أَعْتَقَ عنه، ثم راجع أهله رواه بن جَرِيرٍ 

وَلِهَذَا ذَهَبَ ابنُ عَبَّاسٍ وَالْأَكْثَرُونَ إِلَى مَا قُلْنَاهُ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

فَقَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿الَّذِينَ يُظَاهِرُونَ مِنْكُمْ مِنْ نِسَائِهِمْ﴾ أَصْلُ الظِّهَارِ مُشْتَقٌّ مِنَ الظَّهْرِ، وَذَلِكَ أَنَّ الْجَاهِلِيَّةَ كَانُوا إِذَا تَظَاهَرَ أَحَدٌ مِنِ امْرَأَتِهِ قَالَ لَهَا: أَنْتِ عليَّ كَظَهْرِ أُمِّي، ثُمَّ فِي الشَّرْعِ كَانَ الظِّهَارُ فِي سَائِرِ الْأَعْضَاءِ قِيَاسًا عَلَى الظَّهْرِ، وَكَانَ الظِّهَارُ عِنْدَ الْجَاهِلِيَّةِ طَلَاقًا، فَأَرْخَصَ اللَّهُ لِهَذِهِ الْأُمَّةِ وَجَعَلَ فِيهِ كَفَّارَةً، وَلَمْ يَجْعَلْهُ طَلَاقًا كَمَا كَانُوا يَعْتَمِدُونَهُ فِي جَاهِلِيَّتِهِمْ. هَكَذَا قَالَ غَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ مِنَ السَّلَفِ.

قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو كُرَيْب، حَدَّثَنَا عُبَيْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ مُوسَى، عَنْ أَبِي حَمْزَةَ، عَنْ عِكْرمة، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: كَانَ الرَّجُلُ إِذَا قَالَ لِامْرَأَتِهِ فِي الْجَاهِلِيَّةِ: أَنْتِ عليَّ كَظَهْرِ أُمِّي، حُرِّمت عَلَيْهِ، فَكَانَ أَوَّلُ مَنْ ظَاهَرَ في الإسلام أوس، وَكَانَ تَحْتَهُ ابْنَةُ عَمٍّ لَهُ يُقَالُ لَهَا: "خُوَيْلَةُ بِنْتُ ثَعْلَبَةَ . فَظَاهَرَ مِنْهَا، فَأُسْقِطَ فِي يَدَيْهِ، وَقَالَ: مَا أَرَاكِ إِلَّا قَدْ حَرُمت عَلَيَّ. وَقَالَتْ لَهُ مِثْلَ ذَلِكَ، قَالَ: فَانْطَلِقِي إلى رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم. فَأَتَتْ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ فَوَجَدَتْ عِنْدَهُ مَاشِطَةً تُمَشِّطُ رأسه، فقال: "ياخويلة، مَا أُمِرْنَا فِي أَمْرِكِ بِشَيْءٍ فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ عَلَى رَسُولِهِ ﷺ، فَقَالَ: " يَا خُوَيْلَةُ، أَبْشِرِي" قَالَتْ: خَيْرًا. قَالَ فَقَرَأَ عَلَيْهَا: ﴿قَدْ سَمِعَ اللَّهُ قَوْلَ الَّتِي تُجَادِلُكَ فِي زَوْجِهَا وَتَشْتَكِي إِلَى اللَّهِ وَاللَّهُ يَسْمَعُ تَحَاوُرَكُمَا﴾ إِلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَالَّذِينَ يُظَاهِرُونَ مِنْ نِسَائِهِمْ ثُمَّ يَعُودُونَ لِمَا قَالُوا فَتَحْرِيرُ رَقَبَةٍ مِنْ قَبْلِ أَنْ يَتَمَاسَّا﴾

قَالَتْ: وَأَيُّ رَقَبَةٍ لَنَا؟ وَاللَّهِ مَا يَجِدُ رَقَبَةً غَيْرِي. قَالَ: ﴿فَمَنْ لَمْ يَجِدْ فَصِيَامُ شَهْرَيْنِ مُتَتَابِعَيْنِ﴾ قَالَتْ: وَاللَّهِ لَوْلَا أَنَّهُ يَشْرَبُ فِي الْيَوْمِ ثَلَاثَ مَرَّاتٍ لَذَهَبَ بَصَرُهُ! قَالَ: ﴿فَمَنْ لَمْ يَسْتَطِعْ فَإِطْعَامُ سِتِّينَ مِسْكِينًا﴾ قَالَتْ: مِنْ أَيْنَ؟ مَا هِيَ إِلَّا أَكْلَةٌ إِلَى مِثْلِهَا! قَالَ: فَدَعَا بِشَطْرِ وَسْق-ثَلَاثِينَ صَاعًا، وَالْوَسْقُ: سِتُّونَ صَاعًا-فَقَالَ: "لِيُطْعِمْ سِتِّينَ مِسْكِينًا وَلْيُرَاجِعْكِ" وَهَذَا إِسْنَادٌ جَيِّدٌ قَوِيٌّ، وَسِيَاقٌ غَرِيبٌ، وَقَدْ رُوِيَ عَنْ أَبِي الْعَالِيَةِ نَحْوُ هَذَا، فَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ:

حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ الْهَرَوِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عَلَىُّ بْنُ عَاصِمٍ، عَنْ دَاوُدَ بْنِ أَبِي هِنْدٍ، عَنْ أَبِي الْعَالِيَةِ قَالَ: كَانَتْ خَوْلَةُ بِنْتُ دُلَيج تَحْتَ رَجُلٍ مِنَ الْأَنْصَارِ، وَكَانَ ضَرِيرَ الْبَصَرِ فَقِيرًا سَيِّئَ الْخُلُقِ، وَكَانَ طَلَاقُ أَهْلِ الْجَاهِلِيَّةِ إِذَا أَرَادَ الرَّجُلُ أَنْ يُطَلِّقَ امْرَأَتَهُ، قَالَ: "أَنْتِ عليَّ كَظَهْرِ أُمِّي". وَكَانَ لَهَا مِنْهُ عَيِّلٌ أَوْ عَيِّلَانِ، فَنَازَعَتْهُ يَوْمًا فِي شَيْءٍ فَقَالَ: "أَنْتِ عليَّ كَظَهْرِ أُمِّي". فَاحْتَمَلَتْ عَلَيْهَا ثِيَابَهَا حَتَّى دَخَلَتْ عَلَى النَّبِيَّ ﷺ. وَهُوَ فِي بَيْتِ عَائِشَةَ، وَعَائِشَةُ تَغْسِلُ شِقَّ رَأْسِهِ، فَقَدِمَتْ عَلَيْهِ وَمَعَهَا عَيّلها، فَقَالَتْ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، إِنَّ زَوْجِي ضَرِيرُ الْبَصَرِ، فَقِيرٌ لَا شَيْءَ لَهُ سَيِّئُ الخُلُق، وَإِنِّي نَازَعْتُهُ فِي شَيْءٍ فَغَضِبَ، فَقَالَ: "أَنْتِ عليَّ كَظَهْرِ أُمِّي"، وَلَمْ يُرِدْ بِهِ الطَّلَاقَ، وَلِي مِنْهُ عَيِّلٌ أَوْ عَيِّلَانِ، فَقَالَ: "مَا أَعْلَمُكِ إِلَّا قَدْ حَرُمت عَلَيْهِ" فَقَالَتْ: أَشْكُو إِلَى اللَّهِ مَا نَزَلَ بِي وَأَبَا صَبِيِّي. قَالَ: وَدَارَتْ عَائِشَةُ فَغَسَلَتْ شِقَّ رَأْسِهِ الْآخَرَ، فَدَارَتْ مَعَهَا، فَقَالَتْ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، زَوْجِي ضَرِيرُ الْبَصَرِ، فَقِيرٌ سَيِّئُ الْخُلُقِ، وَإِنَّ لِي مِنْهُ عَيِّلا أَوْ عَيِّلَيْنِ، وَإِنِّي نَازَعْتُهُ فِي شَيْءٍ فَغَضِبَ، وَقَالَ: "أَنْتِ عليَّ كَظَهْرِ أُمِّي"، وَلَمْ يُرِدْ بِهِ الطَّلَاقَ! قَالَتْ: فَرَفَعَ إِلَيَّ رَأَسَهُ وَقَالَ: "مَا أَعْلَمُكِ إِلَّا قَدْ حُرِّمْتِ عَلَيْهِ". فَقَالَتْ: أَشْكُو إِلَى اللَّهِ مَا نَزَلَ بِي وَأَبَا صَبِيِّي؟ قَالَ: وَرَأَتْ عَائِشَةُ وَجْهَ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ تَغَيَّر، فَقَالَتْ لَهَا: "وَرَاءَكِ وَرَاءَكِ؟ " فَتَنَحَّتْ، فَمَكَثَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ فِي غَشَيَانِهِ ذَلِكَ مَا شَاءَ اللَّهُ، فَلَمَّا انْقَطَعَ الْوَحْيُ قَالَ: "يَا عَائِشَةُ، أَيْنَ الْمَرْأَةُ" فَدَعَتْهَا، فَقَالَ لَهَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "اذْهَبِي فَأْتِنِي بِزَوْجِكِ". فَانْطَلَقَتْ تَسْعَى فَجَاءَتْ بِهِ. فَإِذَا هُوَ-[كَمَا قَالَتْ] -ضَرِيرُ الْبَصَرِ، فَقِيرٌ سَيِّئُ الْخُلُقِ. فَقَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: "أَسْتَعِيذُ بِاللَّهِ السَّمِيعِ الْعَلِيمِ، بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ ﴿قَدْ سَمِعَ اللَّهُ قَوْلَ الَّتِي تُجَادِلُكَ فِي زَوْجِهَا [وَتَشْتَكِي إِلَى اللَّهِ] ﴾ إِلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَالَّذِينَ يُظَاهِرُونَ مِنْ نِسَائِهِمْ ثُمَّ يَعُودُونَ لِمَا قَالُوا [فَتَحْرِيرُ رَقَبَةٍ] ﴾ قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: "أَتَجِدُ رَقَبَةً تُعْتِقُهَا مِنْ قَبْلِ أَنْ تَمَسَّهَا؟ ". قَالَ: لَا. قَالَ: "أَتَسْتَطِيعُ أَنْ تَصُومَ شَهْرَيْنِ مُتَتَابِعَيْنِ؟ ". قَالَ: وَالَّذِي بَعَثَكَ بِالْحَقِّ، إِنِّي إِذَا لَمْ آكُلِ الْمَرَّتَيْنِ وَالثَّلَاثَ يَكَادُ أَنْ يَعْشُوَ بَصَرِي. قَالَ: "أَفَتَسْتَطِيعُ أَنْ تُطْعِمَ سِتِّينَ مِسْكِينًا؟ ". قَالَ: لَا إِلَّا [أَنْ] تُعِينَنِي. قَالَ: فَأَعَانَهُ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ فقال: "أطعم ستين مسكينا". قال: وحَوّل اللَّهُ الطَّلَاقَ، فَجَعَلَهُ ظِهَارًا.

وَرَوَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ الْمُثَنَّى، عَنْ عَبْدِ الْأَعْلَى، عَنْ دَاوُدَ، سَمِعْتُ أَبَا الْعَالِيَةِ، فَذَكَرَ نَحْوَهُ، بأخصر من هذا السياق 

وقال سعيد ابن جُبَيْرٍ: كَانَ الْإِيلَاءُ وَالظِّهَارُ مِنْ طَلَاقِ الْجَاهِلِيَّةِ، فَوَقَّتَ اللَّهُ الْإِيلَاءَ أَرْبَعَةَ أَشْهُرٍ، وَجَعَلَ فِي الظهار الكفارة. رواه بن أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، بِنَحْوِهِ.

وَقَدِ اسْتَدَلَّ الْإِمَامُ مَالِكٌ عَلَى أَنَّ الْكَافِرَ لَا يَدْخُلُ فِي هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿مِنْكُمْ﴾ فَالْخِطَابُ لِلْمُؤْمِنِينَ، وَأَجَابَ الْجُمْهُورُ بِأَنَّ هَذَا خَرَجَ مَخْرَجَ الْغَالِبِ فَلَا مَفْهُومَ لَهُ، وَاسْتَدَلَّ الْجُمْهُورُ عَلَيْهِ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿مِنْ نِسَائِهِمْ﴾ عَلَى أَنَّ الْأَمَةَ لَا ظِهَارَ مِنْهَا، وَلَا تَدْخُلُ فِي هَذَا الْخِطَابِ.

## 58:5-7 — verses 5-7 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty informs us about those who opposed God and His Messenger and defied His law: {They were disgraced as those before them were disgraced}, meaning they were humiliated, cursed, and shamed, just as was done to those like them among earlier peoples. {And We have sent down clear signs}, meaning clear and manifest, which none opposes or defies except a disbelieving, immoral, and arrogant person. {And for the disbelievers is a humiliating punishment}, meaning in return for their arrogant refusal to follow God's law, submit to it, and be humble before it.

Then He said: {On the Day when God will resurrect them all together}, and that is the Day of Resurrection, when God will gather the first and the last on one plain, {and inform them of what they did}, meaning He will tell them of the good and evil they used to do. {God has kept count of it while they forgot it}, meaning God recorded it and preserved it against them, while they themselves had forgotten what they used to do. {And God is witness over all things}, meaning nothing is absent from Him, nothing is hidden from Him, and He forgets nothing.

Then the Almighty said, informing of the encompassing nature of His knowledge over His creation, His awareness of them, His hearing of their speech, and His seeing their location wherever they are and wherever they may be: {Do you not see that God knows what is in the heavens and what is on the earth? There is no secret conversation among three}

meaning no secret talk among three people, {but that He is their fourth, nor among five but that He is their sixth, nor fewer than that nor more but that He is with them wherever they are}, meaning He is aware of them, He hears their speech, their secrets, and their private conversations. His angels too, along with that, write down what they converse about secretly, alongside God's knowledge and hearing of them, as He said: {Do they not know that God knows their secret and their private conversation, and that God is the Knower of the unseen} [al-Tawbah: 78], and He said: {Or do they think that We do not hear their secret and their private talk? Yes indeed, and Our messengers are with them, recording} [al-Zukhruf: 80]. For this reason, more than one scholar has related a consensus that what is meant by this verse is the accompaniment of God's knowledge, and there is no doubt that this is what is intended. But His hearing too, along with His knowledge, encompasses them, and His sight penetrates them. He, glory be to Him, is fully aware of His creation; nothing of their affairs is ever absent from Him.

Then He said: {Then He will inform them of what they did on the Day of Resurrection. Indeed, God is Knowing of all things.} Imam Ahmad said: He opened the verse with knowledge and closed it with knowledge.

## 58:8-10 — verses 8-10 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Ibn Abi Najih said, from Mujahid, [concerning His saying] {Have you not seen those who were forbidden secret talk}: "the Jews." Muqatil ibn Hayyan said likewise, and added: there was a truce between the Prophet ﷺ and the Jews, and whenever a man from among the companions of the Prophet ﷺ passed by them, they would sit and whisper together among themselves, until the believer would think that they were whispering about killing him, or about something the believer would dislike. When the believer saw that, he feared them and abandoned his path past them. So the Prophet ﷺ forbade them from such secret talk, but they did not desist and returned to it. So Allah revealed: {Have you not seen those who were forbidden secret talk, then they return to what they were forbidden}.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Rubayh ibn Abd al-Rahman ibn Abi Sa'id al-Khudri, from his father, from his grandfather, who said: We used to take turns staying overnight with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, in case some matter arose at night or some need occurred to him. One night the number of those keeping watch and those seeking reward grew large, so that we became gathered groups conversing. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ came out to us and said: "What is this secret talk? Were you not forbidden from secret talk?" We said: "We repent to Allah, O Messenger of Allah. We were speaking of the Antichrist (al-Masih), out of fear of him." He said: "Shall I not tell you of what is, in my view, more to be feared for you than him?" We said: "Yes indeed, O Messenger of Allah." He said: "Hidden shirk, that a man rises to perform an act for the sake of another man's presence." This is a gharib (singular) chain, and it contains some weak narrators.

Concerning His saying: {and they converse secretly in sin, transgression, and disobedience to the Messenger}, meaning: they speak among themselves of sin, which pertains specifically to themselves, and transgression, which relates to others, and among this is disobedience to the Messenger and opposition to him. They persist in this and enjoin it upon one another.

Concerning His saying: {And when they come to you, they greet you with that with which Allah does not greet you}, Ibn Abi Hatim said:

[chain] from Masruq, from Aishah, who said: some Jews entered upon me while the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was present, and they said: "Al-sam (death) be upon you, O Abu al-Qasim." Aishah said: "And upon you be death and [the curse]." She said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "O Aishah, Allah does not love foul speech nor indulgence in it." I said: "Do you not hear what they are saying, 'al-sam be upon you'?" The Messenger of Allah said: "Did you not hear me say, 'and upon you'?" So Allah revealed: {And when they come to you, they greet you with that with which Allah does not greet you}.

In another report in the Sahih, she said to them: "Upon you be death and disgrace and the curse," and the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Indeed our prayer against them is answered, but their prayer against us is not answered."

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] from Anas ibn Malik: that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was sitting with his companions when a Jew came to them and greeted them with salam, and they returned the greeting. The Prophet of Allah ﷺ said: "Do you know what he said?" They said: "He greeted us with salam, O Messenger of Allah." He said: "Rather he said, 'sam (death) be upon you,' meaning: may you be afflicted regarding your religion." The Messenger of Allah said: "Return his greeting to him." So they returned it to him. The Prophet of Allah said: "Did you say, 'sam be upon you'?" He said: "Yes." The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "When one of the People of the Book greets you, then say: 'Alayka (upon you),'" meaning, upon you be what you said.

The original of Anas's hadith is recorded in the Sahih, and this hadith is in the Sahih from Aishah, in similar wording.

Concerning His saying: {and they say to themselves, "Why does Allah not punish us for what we say?"}, meaning: they do this and they say what they twist of speech while feigning a greeting of peace, when it is in reality abuse concealed within. Yet along with this they say to themselves: "If this man were truly a prophet, Allah would have punished us for what we say to him secretly, since Allah knows what we conceal. So if he were truly a prophet, Allah would hasten to punish us in this world." So Allah, exalted is He, said: {Sufficient for them is Hell}, meaning: Hell suffices as their punishment in the afterlife, {into which they will burn, and how evil is that destination}.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] from Abd Allah ibn Amr: that the Jews used to say to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, "sam be upon you," then say to themselves: {Why does Allah not punish us for what we say}? So this verse was revealed: {And when they come to you, they greet you with that with which Allah does not greet you, and they say to themselves, "Why does Allah not punish us for what we say?" Sufficient for them is Hell, into which they will burn, and how evil is that destination}. A hasan (sound) chain, though the two [Bukhari and Muslim] did not include it.

Al-Awfi said, from Ibn Abbas, concerning {And when they come to you, they greet you with that with which Allah does not greet you}: he said: the hypocrites used to say to the Messenger of Allah, when they greeted him, "sam be upon you." Allah said: {Sufficient for them is Hell, into which they will burn, and how evil is that destination}.

Then Allah said, instructing His believing servants not to be like the disbelievers and the hypocrites: {O you who believe, when you converse secretly, do not converse in sin, transgression, and disobedience to the Messenger}, meaning: as the ignorant among the disbelieving People of the Book converse secretly, along with those hypocrites who aided them in their misguidance. {But converse secretly in righteousness and piety, and fear Allah, to whom you will be gathered}, meaning: so He will inform you of all your deeds and words which He has recorded against you, and He will requite you for them.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] from Safwan ibn Muhriz, who said: I was holding the hand of Ibn Umar when a man approached him and said: "How did you hear the Messenger of Allah ﷺ speak about secret converse on the Day of Resurrection?" He said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "Indeed Allah brings the believer near, places His covering over him, and shields him from the people, and He makes him acknowledge his sins, saying to him: 'Do you recognize such a sin? Do you recognize such a sin? Do you recognize such a sin?' until, when He has made him acknowledge his sins and the man thinks within himself that he is ruined, He says: 'I concealed these for you in the world, and I forgive them for you today.' Then he is given the record of his good deeds. As for the disbelievers and the hypocrites, the witnesses will proclaim: 'These are the ones who lied about their Lord. Indeed, the curse of Allah is upon the wrongdoers.'"

Both [Bukhari and Muslim] recorded it in the two Sahihs, from the hadith of Qatadah.

Then He, exalted is He, said: {Secret talk is only from Satan, so that he may grieve those who believe, but it will not harm them at all except by the permission of Allah, and upon Allah let the believers rely}, meaning: this secret whispering, which is confiding quietly, in cases where a believer suspects some evil from it, {is from Satan, so that he may grieve those who believe}, meaning: this only issues from those who whisper together through Satan's temptation and adornment, {so that he may grieve those who believe}, meaning: to cause them distress, but that will not harm them at all except by Allah's permission. Whoever senses anything of that should seek refuge with Allah and rely upon Allah, for then nothing will harm him except by Allah's permission.

The Sunnah has come forbidding secret conversation where it causes distress to a believer, as Imam Ahmad said: [chain] from Abd Allah ibn Mas'ud, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "When you are three, let not two of you converse secretly apart from their companion, for that will grieve him." Both [Bukhari and Muslim] recorded it from the hadith of al-A'mash.

Abd al-Razzaq said: [chain] from Ibn Umar, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "When you are three, let not two converse secretly apart from the third except with his permission, for that will grieve him." Muslim alone recorded it, from Abu al-Rabi' and Abu Kamil, both from Hammad ibn Zayd, from Ayyub, with this wording.

## 58:11 — verse 11

### Translation

The Almighty says, disciplining His believing servants and commanding them that they be kind to one another in gatherings: {O you who believe, when it is said to you, "Make room in the gatherings"} it is also read {in the gathering} {then make room, and God will make room for you}. This is because the reward corresponds to the type of deed, as has come in the authentic hadith: "Whoever builds a mosque for God's sake, God builds for him a house in Paradise," and in another hadith: "Whoever eases a hardship for someone in difficulty, God eases things for him in this world and the next, [and whoever conceals the faults of a Muslim, God will conceal his faults in this world and the next], and God is in the aid of the servant so long as the servant is in the aid of his brother." There are many similar examples of this. This is why He said: {then make room, and God will make room for you}.

Qatadah said: this verse was revealed concerning the gatherings of remembrance (majalis al-dhikr). This was because when they saw one of their companions approaching, they would be reluctant to give up their seats in the gathering by the Messenger of God ﷺ, so God commanded them to make room for one another.

Muqatil ibn Hayyan said: this verse was revealed on a Friday. The Messenger of God ﷺ was that day on the suffah (raised platform), and the place was cramped. He used to honor the people of Badr among the Emigrants and the Helpers. Some men of Badr came, and others had already taken the seats in the gathering ahead of them. So they stood facing the Messenger of God ﷺ and said: "Peace be upon you, O Prophet, and the mercy of God and His blessings." The Prophet ﷺ returned their greeting. Then they greeted the people after that, and the people returned their greeting. They remained standing on their feet, waiting for room to be made for them, but no one made room. The Prophet ﷺ recognized what was making them stand there, but no room was made for them, and this was hard on the Prophet ﷺ. So he said to those around him from the Emigrants and Helpers who were not among the people of Badr: "Get up, so-and-so, and you, so-and-so." He kept making them stand up, in the same number as the men of Badr among the Emigrants and Helpers who were standing before him, until they could sit. This was hard on those who were made to get up from their places, and the Prophet ﷺ recognized the displeasure on their faces. So the hypocrites said: "Do you not claim that this companion of yours deals justly among people? By God, we have never before seen him act justly toward these men. Some people had taken their seats and loved being near their Prophet, yet he made them stand up and seated instead those who had been slow in coming to him." It has reached us that the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "May God have mercy on a man who makes room for his brother." After that, they began to get up quickly, and the people made room for their brothers. This verse was revealed on that Friday. Reported by Ibn Abi Hatim.

Imam Ahmad and al-Shafi'i said: [chain] Sufyan, from Ayyub, from Nafi', from Ibn Umar, that the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "A man should not make another man get up from his place in order to sit in it himself, but rather make room and open up space."

Both [Bukhari and Muslim] narrated it in the two Sahihs from the hadith of Nafi', with the same wording.

Al-Shafi'i said: [chain] Abd al-Majid, from Ibn Jurayj, who said: Sulayman ibn Musa said, from Jabir ibn Abdullah, that the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "None of you should make his brother get up on the day of Friday, but rather let him say: 'Make room.'" This meets the standard of the Sunan collections, though they did not include it.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Abd al-Malik ibn Amr, [chain] Fulayh, from Ayyub, from Abd al-Rahman ibn [Abi] Sa'sa'ah, from Ya'qub ibn Abi Ya'qub, from Abu Hurayrah, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "A man should not make another man get up from his place and then sit in it, but rather make room, and God will make room for you."

He also related it from Surayj ibn Yunus and Yunus ibn Muhammad al-Mu'addib, from Fulayh, with the same chain, and its wording was: "A man should not stand up for another man from his place, but rather make room, and God will make room for you." Ahmad alone narrated it with this wording.

The jurists have differed over the permissibility of standing up for someone arriving, with several positions. Some permitted it, citing the hadith: "Rise for your master." Others prohibited it, citing the hadith: "Whoever loves that men stand up for him should be prepared to take his seat in the Fire." Others made a distinction, saying: it is permissible when someone returns from a journey, and for a ruler in the place of his authority, as indicated by the story of Sa'd ibn Mu'adh. When the Prophet ﷺ brought him forward to judge the case of the Banu Qurayzah and saw him approaching, he said to the Muslims: "Rise for your master." This was only so that his ruling would carry more weight and be more effective. God knows best. But as for making this a habitual practice, it is one of the customs of the non-Arabs (foreign peoples). It has come in the Sunan collections that there was no one more beloved to them than the Messenger of God ﷺ, yet when he came, they would not stand up for him, because they knew he disliked that.

In the hadith related in the Sunan: the Messenger of God ﷺ used to sit wherever he ended up in the gathering, but wherever he sat became the head (front) of that gathering. The Companions, may God be pleased with them, used to sit around him according to their rank. Abu Bakr (al-Siddiq) would sit on his right, Umar on his left, and before him usually sat Uthman and Ali, because they were among those who wrote down the revelation, and he used to instruct them to do so, as Muslim narrated from the hadith of al-A'mash, from Umarah ibn Umayr, from Abu Ma'mar, from Abu Mas'ud, that the Messenger of God ﷺ used to say: "Let those among you of sound judgment and understanding be nearest to me, then those next to them, then those next to them." This was only so that they might understand what he said ﷺ. This is why he commanded that group to stand up so that the men of Badr who had arrived could sit, either because those others had been remiss regarding the rights of the men of Badr, or so that the men of Badr could take their share of knowledge as those before them had taken theirs, or as a lesson in bringing forward the most excellent to the front.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Waki', from al-A'mash, from Umarah ibn Umayr al-Taymi, from Abu Ma'mar, from Abu Mas'ud, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ used to touch our shoulders during prayer and say: "Stand in straight rows and do not differ, or your hearts will differ. Let those among you of sound judgment and understanding be nearest to me, then those next to them, then those next to them." Abu Mas'ud said: today you are more given to disagreement.

Muslim and the compilers of the Sunan, except al-Tirmidhi, narrated it similarly through various routes from al-A'mash, with the same chain.

If this was his command to them regarding prayer, that the people of sound reason should be nearest to him, then the learned, then this applies with even greater reason outside of prayer.

Abu Dawud related from the hadith of Mu'awiyah ibn Salih, from Abu al-Zahiriyyah, from Kathir ibn Murrah, from Abdullah ibn Umar, that the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Straighten the rows, align the shoulders, close the gaps, be gentle in the hands of your brothers, and do not leave openings for Satan. Whoever joins a row, God joins him, and whoever breaks a row, God breaks him off." This is why Ubay ibn Ka'b, the master of the reciters, whenever he reached the first row, would pull out from it a man who was of ordinary rank among the people, and would take his place in the front row himself, citing this hadith as evidence: "Let those among you of sound judgment and understanding be nearest to me." As for Abdullah ibn Umar, he would never sit in the place from which its occupant had risen for him, acting according to the implication of the hadith he narrated which we mentioned earlier. Let us limit ourselves to this much as an example connected to this verse, for a fuller treatment would require a different place. In the authentic hadith: while the Messenger of God ﷺ was sitting, three men approached. One of them found an opening in the circle and entered it. Another sat behind the people. The third turned away and left. The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Shall I not inform you about the three? As for the first, he sought shelter with God, so God sheltered him. As for the second, he showed modesty, so God showed him consideration. As for the third, he turned away, so God turned away from him."

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Attab ibn Ziyad, [chain] Abdullah, [chain] Usamah ibn Zayd, from Amr ibn Shu'ayb, from his father, from Abdullah ibn Amr, that the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "It is not lawful for a man to separate two people except with their permission."

Abu Dawud and al-Tirmidhi related it from the hadith of Usamah ibn Zayd al-Laythi, with the same chain, and al-Tirmidhi rated it as hasan (sound).

It has been related from Ibn Abbas, al-Hasan al-Basri, and others that they said regarding the Almighty's words {when it is said to you, "Make room in the gathering," then make room}: this refers to gatherings of war. They said the meaning of His words {and when it is said, "Rise up," then rise up} is: rise up for battle.

Qatadah said: {and when it is said, "Rise up," then rise up} means: when you are called to something good, respond to it.

Muqatil [ibn Hayyan] said: when you are called to prayer, get up for it.

Abd al-Rahman ibn Zayd ibn Aslam said: when they were with the Prophet ﷺ in his house and wanted to leave, each of them would love to be the last one to leave his presence. This was sometimes difficult for him, peace be upon him, since he might have some need to attend to. So they were commanded that when they were told to leave, they should leave, as in His words: {and if you are told, "Go back," then go back} [al-Nur: 28].

His saying: {God will raise those among you who believe and those who are given knowledge in ranks, and God is aware of what you do} means: do not think that if one of you makes room for his brother when he approaches, or if he is told to leave and he leaves, that this is a diminishment of his standing. Rather, it is an elevation and a distinction in God's sight, and God Almighty does not let that go to waste for him. Rather He rewards him for it in this world and the next. For whoever humbles himself for the sake of God's command, God raises his rank and spreads his good name. This is why He said: {God will raise those among you who believe and those who are given knowledge in ranks, and God is aware of what you do}, meaning: aware of who deserves this and who does not.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Abu Kamil, [chain] Ibrahim, [chain] Ibn Shihab, from Abu al-Tufayl Amir ibn Wathilah, that Nafi' ibn Abd al-Harith met Umar ibn al-Khattab at Usfan. Umar had appointed him over Mecca. Umar said to him: "Whom have you appointed as deputy over the people of the valley?" He said: "I have appointed Ibn Abza over them." Umar said: "And who is Ibn Abza?" He replied: "A man from among our freed slaves (mawali)." Umar [ibn al-Khattab] said: "You have appointed a freed slave over them?" He said: "O Commander of the Faithful, he is a reciter of the Book of God, learned in the laws of inheritance, and a judge." Umar, may God be pleased with him, said: "Indeed, your Prophet ﷺ has said: 'God raises some people by means of this Book and lowers others by means of it.'"

Muslim narrated it likewise through more than one route from al-Zuhri, with the same chain. I have mentioned the merit of knowledge and its people, and the hadiths reported concerning that, exhaustively in the commentary on "The Book of Knowledge" from Sahih al-Bukhari. Praise and thanks be to God.

وَقَدِ اخْتَلَفَ الْفُقَهَاءُ فِي جَوَازِ الْقِيَامِ لِلْوَارِدِ إِذَا جَاءَ عَلَى أَقْوَالٍ: فَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ رَخَّصَ فِي ذَلِكَ مُحْتَجًّا بِحَدِيثِ: "قُومُوا إِلَى سَيِّدِكُمْ" وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ مَنَعَ مِنْ ذَلِكَ مُحْتَجًّا بِحَدِيثِ: "مَنْ أحَبَّ أَنْ يَتَمثَّلَ لَهُ الرِّجَالُ قِيَامًا فَلْيَتبوَّأ مَقْعَدَه مِنَ النَّارِ" وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ فَصَّلَ فَقَالَ: يَجُوزُ عِنْدَ الْقُدُومِ مِنْ سَفَرٍ، وَلِلْحَاكِمِ فِي مَحَلِّ وِلَايَتِهِ، كَمَا دَلَّ عَلَيْهِ قِصَّةُ سَعْدِ بْنِ مُعَاذٍ، فَإِنَّهُ لِمَا اسْتَقْدَمَهُ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ حاكمًا فِي بَنِي قُرَيْظَةَ فَرَآهُ مُقْبِلًا قَالَ لِلْمُسْلِمِينَ: "قُومُوا إِلَى سَيِّدِكُمْ". وَمَا ذَاكَ إِلَّا لِيَكُونَ أَنْفَذَ لِحُكْمِهِ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ. فَأَمَّا اتِّخَاذُهُ دَيْدَنًا فَإِنَّهُ مِنْ شِعَارِ الْعَجَمِ. وَقَدْ جَاءَ فِي السُّنَنِ أَنَّهُ لَمْ يَكُنْ شَخْصٌ أَحَبَّ إِلَيْهِمْ مِنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، وَكَانَ إِذَا جَاءَ لَا يَقُومُونَ لَهُ، لِمَا يَعْلَمُونَ مِنْ كَرَاهَتِهِ لِذَلِكَ 

وَفِي الْحَدِيثِ الْمَرْوِيِّ فِي السُّنَنِ: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ كَانَ يَجْلِسُ حَيْثُ انْتَهَى بِهِ الْمَجْلِسُ، وَلَكِنْ حَيْثُ يَجْلِسُ يَكُونُ صَدْرُ ذَلِكَ الْمَجْلِسِ، وَكَانَ الصَّحَابَةُ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ، يَجْلِسُونَ مِنْهُ عَلَى مَرَاتِبِهِمْ، فَالصِّدِّيقُ يُجْلِسُهُ عَنْ يَمِينِهِ، وَعُمَرُ عَنْ يَسَارِهِ، وَبَيْنَ يَدَيْهِ غَالِبًا عُثْمَانُ وَعَلِيٌّ؛ لِأَنَّهُمَا كَانَا مِمَّنْ يَكْتُبُ الْوَحْيَ، وَكَانَ يَأْمُرُهُمَا بِذَلِكَ، كَمَا رَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ مِنْ حَدِيثِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ عُمَارَةَ بْنِ عُمَيْرٍ، عَنْ أَبِي مَعْمَر، عَنْ أَبِي مَسْعُودٍ، أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ كَانَ يَقُولُ: "لِيَليني منكم أولوا الْأَحْلَامِ والنُّهَى، ثُمَّ الَّذِينَ يَلُونَهُمْ، ثُمَّ الَّذِينَ يَلُونَهُمْ" وَمَا ذَاكَ إِلَّا لِيَعْقِلُوا عَنْهُ مَا يَقُولُهُ، صَلَوَاتُ اللَّهِ وَسَلَامُهُ عَلَيْهِ؛ وَلِهَذَا أَمَرَ أُولَئِكَ النَّفَرَ بِالْقِيَامِ لِيَجْلِسَ الَّذِينَ وَرَدُوا مَنْ أَهْلِ بَدْرٍ، إِمَّا لِتَقْصِيرِ أُولَئِكَ فِي حَقِّ الْبَدْرِيِّينَ، أَوْ لِيَأْخُذَ الْبَدْرِيُّونَ مِنَ الْعِلْمِ بِنَصِيبِهِمْ، كَمَا أَخَذَ أُولَئِكَ قَبْلَهُمْ، أَوْ تَعْلِيمًا بِتَقْدِيمِ الْأَفَاضِلِ إِلَى الْأَمَامِ.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا وَكِيع، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ عُمَارة بْنِ عُمَيْرٍ التَّيْمِيِّ عَنْ أَبِي مَعْمَرٍ، عَنْ أَبِي مَسْعُودٍ قَالَ: كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَمْسَحُ مَنَاكِبَنَا فِي الصَّلَاةِ وَيَقُولُ: "اسْتَوُوا وَلَا تَخْتَلِفُوا فَتَخْتَلِفَ قُلُوبُكُمْ، لِيَلِيَنِي مِنْكُمْ أُولُو الْأَحْلَامِ والنُّهى، ثُمَّ الَّذِينَ يَلُونَهُمْ، ثُمَّ الَّذِينَ يَلُونَهُمْ". قَالَ أَبُو مَسْعُودٍ فَأَنْتُمُ الْيَوْمَ أَشَدُّ اخْتِلَافًا.

وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ وَأَهْلُ السُّنَنِ، إِلَّا التِّرْمِذِيَّ، مِنْ طُرُقٍ عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، بِهِ 

وَإِذَا كَانَ هَذَا أَمْرَهُ لَهُمْ فِي الصَّلَاةِ أَنْ يَلِيَهُ الْعُقَلَاءُ ثُمَّ الْعُلَمَاءُ، فَبِطْرِيقِ الْأَوْلَى أَنْ يَكُونَ ذَلِكَ فِي غَيْرِ الصَّلَاةِ.

وَرَوَى أَبُو دَاوُدَ مِنْ حَدِيثِ مُعَاوِيَةُ بْنُ صَالِحٍ، عَنْ أَبِي الزَّاهِرِيَّةِ، عَنْ كَثِيرِ بْنِ مُرَّةَ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عُمَرَ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "أَقِيمُوا الصُّفُوفَ، وحَاذُوا بَيْنَ الْمَنَاكِبِ، وسُدّوا الْخَلَلَ، ولِينُوا بِأَيْدِي إِخْوَانِكُمْ، وَلَا تَذَروا فُرُجَاتٍ لِلشَّيْطَانِ، وَمَنْ وَصَل صَفًّا وَصَلَهُ اللَّهُ، وَمَنْ قَطَعَ صَفًّا قَطَعَهُ الله" وَلِهَذَا كَانَ أُبَيُّ بْنُ كَعْبٍ -سَيِّدُ الْقُرَّاءِ-إِذَا انْتَهَى إِلَى الصَّفِّ الْأَوَّلِ انْتَزَعَ مِنْهُ رَجُلًا يَكُونُ مِنْ أَفْنَاءِ النَّاسِ، وَيَدْخُلُ هُوَ فِي الصَّفِّ الْمُقَدَّمِ، وَيَحْتَجُّ بِهَذَا الْحَدِيثِ: "لِيَلِيَنِي مِنْكُمْ أُولُو الْأَحْلَامِ وَالنُّهَى". وَأَمَّا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ عُمَرَ فَكَانَ لَا يَجْلِسُ فِي الْمَكَانِ الَّذِي يَقُومُ لَهُ صَاحِبُهُ عَنْهُ، عَمَلًا بِمُقْتَضَى مَا تَقَدَّمَ مِنْ رِوَايَتِهِ الْحَدِيثَ الَّذِي أَوْرَدْنَاهُ. وَلْنَقْتَصِرْ عَلَى هَذَا الْمِقْدَارِ مِنَ الْأُنْمُوذَجِ الْمُتَعَلِّقِ بِهَذِهِ الْآيَةِ، وَإِلَّا فَبَسْطُهُ يَحْتَاجُ إِلَى غَيْرِ هَذَا الْمَوْضِعِ، وَفِي الْحَدِيثِ الصَّحِيحِ: بَيْنَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ جَالِسٌ، إِذْ أَقْبَلَ ثَلَاثَةُ نَفَرٍ، فَأَمَّا أَحَدُهُمْ فَوَجَدَ فُرْجَةً فِي الْحَلْقَةِ فَدَخَلَ فِيهَا، وَأَمَّا الْآخَرُ فَجَلَسَ وَرَاءَ النَّاسِ، وَأَدْبَرَ الثَّالِثُ ذَاهِبًا. فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "ألا أنبئكم بِخَبَرِ الثَّلَاثَةِ، أَمَّا الْأَوَّلُ فَآوَى إِلَى اللَّهِ فَآوَاهُ اللَّهُ، وَأَمَّا الثَّانِي فَاسْتَحْيَا فَاسْتَحْيَا اللَّهُ مِنْهُ، وَأَمَّا الثَّالِثُ فَأَعْرَضَ فَأَعْرَضَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ" 

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَتَّاب بْنُ زِيَادٍ، أَخْبَرَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ، أَخْبَرَنَا أُسَامَةُ بْنُ زَيْدٍ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ شُعَيْبٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "لَا يَحِلُّ لِرَجُلٍ أَنْ يُفَرِّقَ بَيْنَ اثْنَيْنِ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِمَا".

وَرَوَاهُ أَبُو دَاوُدَ وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ أُسَامَةَ بْنِ زَيْدٍ اللَّيْثِيِّ، بِهِ وَحَسَّنَهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ.

وَقَدْ رُوي عن بن عَبَّاسٍ، وَالْحَسَنِ الْبَصْرِيِّ وَغَيْرِهِمَا أَنَّهُمْ قَالُوا فِي قَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِذَا قِيلَ لَكُمْ تَفَسَّحُوا فِي الْمَجْلِسِ فَافْسَحُوا﴾ يَعْنِي: فِي مَجَالِسِ الْحَرْبِ، قَالُوا: وَمَعْنَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَإِذَا قِيلَ انْشُزُوا فَانْشُزُوا﴾ أَيِ: انْهَضُوا لِلْقِتَالِ.

وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: ﴿وَإِذَا قِيلَ انْشُزُوا فَانْشُزُوا﴾ أَيْ: إِذَا دُعِيتُمْ إِلَى خَيْرٍ فَأَجِيبُوا.

وَقَالَ مُقَاتِلُ [بْنُ حَيَّانَ] إِذَا دُعِيتُمْ إِلَى الصَّلَاةِ فَارْتَفِعُوا إِلَيْهَا.

وَقَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ: كَانُوا إِذَا كَانُوا عِنْدَ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ فِي بَيْتِهِ فَأَرَادُوا الِانْصِرَافَ أَحَبَّ كُلٌّ مِنْهُمْ أَنْ يَكُونَ هُوَ آخِرَهُمْ خُرُوجًا مِنْ عِنْدِهِ، فَرُبَّمَا يَشُقُّ ذَلِكَ عَلَيْهِ-عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ-وَقَدْ تَكُونُ لَهُ الْحَاجَةُ، فَأُمِرُوا أَنَّهُمْ إِذَا أُمِرُوا بِالِانْصِرَافِ أَنْ يَنْصَرِفُوا، كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَإِنْ قِيلَ لَكُمُ ارْجِعُوا فَارْجِعُوا﴾ [النُّورِ: ٢٨] 

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿يَرْفَعِ اللَّهُ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا مِنْكُمْ وَالَّذِينَ أُوتُوا الْعِلْمَ دَرَجَاتٍ وَاللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌ﴾ أَيْ: لَا تَعْتَقِدُوا أَنَّهُ إِذَا فَسَح أَحَدٌ مِنْكُمْ لِأَخِيهِ إِذَا أَقْبَلَ، أَوْ إِذَا أُمِرَ بِالْخُرُوجِ فَخَرَجَ، أَنْ يَكُونَ ذَلِكَ نَقْصًا فِي حَقِّهِ، بَلْ هُوَ رِفْعَةٌ وَمَزِيَّةٌ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ، وَاللَّهُ تَعَالَى لَا يُضَيِّعُ ذَلِكَ لَهُ، بَلْ يَجْزِيهِ بِهَا فِي الدُّنْيَا وَالْآخِرَةِ، فَإِنَّ مَنْ تَوَاضَعَ لِأَمْرِ اللَّهِ رَفَع اللَّهُ قَدْرَهُ، ونَشَر ذِكْرَهُ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿يَرْفَعِ اللَّهُ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا مِنْكُمْ وَالَّذِينَ أُوتُوا الْعِلْمَ دَرَجَاتٍ وَاللَّهُ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ خَبِيرٌ﴾

أَيْ: خَبِيرٌ بِمَنْ يَسْتَحِقُّ ذَلِكَ وَبِمَنْ لَا يَسْتَحِقُّهُ.

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو كَامِلٍ، حَدَّثَنَا إِبْرَاهِيمُ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ شِهَابٍ، عَنْ أَبِي الطُّفَيْلِ عَامِرِ بْنِ وَاثِلَةَ، أَنَّ نَافِعَ بْنَ عَبْدِ الْحَارِثِ لَقِيَ عُمَرَ بْنَ الْخَطَّابِ بِعُسْفَانَ، وَكَانَ عُمَرُ اسْتَعْمَلَهُ عَلَى مَكَّةَ، فَقَالَ لَهُ عُمَرُ: مَنِ اسْتَخْلَفْتَ عَلَى أَهْلِ الْوَادِي؟ قَالَ: اسْتَخْلَفْتُ عَلَيْهِمُ ابْنَ أَبْزَى. قَالَ: وَمَا ابْنُ أَبْزَى؟ فَقَالَ: رَجُلٌ مِنْ مَوَالِينَا. فَقَالَ عُمَرُ [بْنُ الْخَطَّابِ] اسْتَخْلَفْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ مَوْلًى؟. فَقَالَ: يَا أَمِيرَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ، إِنَّهُ قَارِئٌ لِكِتَابِ اللَّهِ، عَالِمٌ بِالْفَرَائِضِ، قَاضٍ. فَقَالَ عُمَرُ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ: أَمَا إِنَّ نَبِيَّكُمْ ﷺ قَدْ قَالَ: "إِنَّ اللَّهَ يَرْفَعُ بِهَذَا الْكِتَابِ قَوْمًا وَيَضَعُ بِهِ آخَرِينَ" 

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ مِنْ غَيْرِ وَجْهٍ، عَنِ الزُّهْرِيِّ، بِهِ وَقَدْ ذَكَرْتُ فَضْلَ الْعِلْمِ وَأَهْلِهِ وَمَا وَرَدَ فِي ذَلِكَ مِنَ الْأَحَادِيثِ مُسْتَقْصَاةً فِي شَرْحِ "كِتَابِ الْعِلْمِ" مِنْ صَحِيحِ الْبُخَارِيِّ، وَلِلَّهِ الْحَمْدُ وَالْمِنَّةُ.

## 58:12-13 — verses 12-13 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty is commanding His believing servants that when one of them wants to hold a private conversation with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, that is, to speak confidentially with him about a matter between them, he should present before that a charity that purifies him, cleanses him, and qualifies him to be fit for this station. This is why He said: {That is better for you and purer}.

Then He said: {But if you find not} that is, except one who is unable to do so because he possesses nothing, {then indeed, Allah is Forgiving, Merciful}. So He commanded it only for one who is able to do it.

Then He said: {Have you feared that you would not offer before your private consultation charities?} that is, did you fear the continuation of this ruling upon you, of the obligation of charity before conversing privately with the Messenger, {But when you did not do so and Allah forgave you, then establish prayer and give zakah and obey Allah and His Messenger. And Allah is Acquainted with what you do}.

So the obligation of that was abrogated for them.

It has been said that no one acted upon this verse before its abrogation except Ali ibn Abi Talib, may Allah be pleased with him.

Ibn Abi Najih said, from Mujahid, who said: they were forbidden from conversing privately with the Prophet ﷺ until they gave charity, and no one held such a private conversation with him except Ali ibn Abi Talib, who presented a dinar as charity that he gave, then he spoke privately with the Prophet ﷺ and asked him about ten matters, then the concession was revealed.

Layth ibn Abi Sulaym said, from Mujahid: Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, said: There is a verse in the Book of Allah, mighty and majestic is He, that no one acted upon before me, and no one will act upon after me. I had a dinar, and I exchanged it for ten dirhams, so whenever I spoke privately with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ I would give a dirham in charity. Then it was abrogated, and no one acted upon it before me, and no one will act upon it after me. Then he recited this verse: {O you who have believed, when you privately consult the Messenger, present before your consultation a charity} to the end of the verse.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] from Ali ibn Alqamah al-Anmari, from Ali [ibn Abi Talib], may Allah be pleased with him, who said: The Prophet ﷺ said, "What do you think, a dinar?" He said: They would not be able to bear it. He said, "Half a dinar?" He said: They would not be able to bear it. He said, "What do you think?" He said: A grain of barley's weight. The Prophet ﷺ said to him, "You are frugal indeed." Ali said: Because of me Allah lightened this burden for this nation. And His saying: {[O you who have believed] when you privately consult the Messenger, present before your consultation a charity}, then {Have you feared that you would not offer before your private consultation charities?} was revealed.

Al-Tirmidhi also related it [chain] from Ali ibn Alqamah al-Anmari, from Ali ibn Abi Talib, who said: When {O you who have believed, when you privately consult the Messenger, present before your consultation a charity} [to its end] was revealed, the Prophet ﷺ said to me, "What do you think, a dinar?" I said: They would not be able to bear it. And he mentioned the hadith in full, similarly. Then he said: "This is a hasan gharib (good, singular) hadith; we know it only from this route." Then he said: The meaning of his saying "a grain of barley" is the weight of a grain of barley in gold.

Abu Ya'la also related it [chain], through the same route.

Al-Awfi said, from Ibn Abbas, regarding His saying: {O you who have believed, when you privately consult the Messenger, present before your consultation a charity} up to {then indeed, Allah is Forgiving, Merciful}: the Muslims used to present charity before their private consultation, but when zakah was revealed, this was abrogated.

Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn Abbas, regarding His saying: {present before your consultation a charity}: this was because the Muslims multiplied their questions to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ until it became burdensome for him, so Allah wished to lighten the burden upon His Prophet, peace be upon him. When He said that, many people held back and refrained from asking questions. So Allah revealed after this: {Have you feared that you would not offer before your private consultation a charity? But when you did not do so and Allah forgave you, then establish prayer and give zakah}. Thus Allah made things easier for them and did not restrict them.

Ikrimah and al-Hasan al-Basri said regarding His saying: {present before your consultation a charity}: it was abrogated by the verse after it, {Have you feared that you would not offer before your private consultation a charity?} to its end.

Sa'id [ibn Abi Arubah] said, from Qatadah and Muqatil ibn Hayyan: the people asked the Messenger of Allah ﷺ so persistently that they wearied him with their questions, so Allah restrained them by this verse. So if a man among them had a need to bring to the Prophet of Allah ﷺ, he could not fulfill it until he presented before it a charity, and this became difficult for them. So Allah revealed the concession after that: {But if you find not, then indeed, Allah is Forgiving, Merciful}.

Ma'mar said, from Qatadah, regarding {When you privately consult the Messenger, present before your consultation a charity}: it was abrogated; it lasted only an hour of a day. Abd al-Razzaq related likewise: [chain] from Mujahid, who said: Ali said: No one acted upon it besides me until it was abrogated, and I think he said: it lasted only an hour.

## 58:14-19 — verses 14-19 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, censoring the hypocrites for their inner allegiance to the disbelievers, while in reality they are neither with them nor with the believers, as the Almighty said: {Wavering between that, belonging neither to these nor to those. Whoever Allah leaves astray, you will never find for him a way} [al-Nisa: 143]. And here He says: {Have you not seen those who took as allies a people with whom Allah is angry?} meaning the Jews, whom the hypocrites used to support and secretly ally themselves with. Then He said: {They are neither of you nor of them}, meaning: these hypocrites are, in truth, neither of you, O believers, nor of those whom they allied with, namely the Jews.

Then He said: {And they swear to falsehood while they know [it is false]}, meaning: the hypocrites swear to falsehood while knowing that they are lying in what they swear, and this is al-yamin al-ghamus (the deliberate false oath that plunges one into sin), especially given the like of their accursed condition, we seek refuge with Allah from it. For when they met those who believed, they said, "We believe," and when they came to the Messenger they swore by Allah to him that they were believers, while knowing all along that they were lying in what they swore, because they did not believe the truth of what they said, even though it might correspond outwardly to the fact. For this reason Allah testified to their lying in their oaths and their testimony regarding that.

Then He said: {Allah has prepared for them a severe punishment. Indeed, evil is what they used to do}, meaning: Allah has set in store for them, because of this conduct, a painful punishment for their evil deeds, namely their allegiance to and support of the disbelievers, and their hostility toward and deceit of the believers. For this reason the Almighty said: {They took their oaths as a shield, and so turned [people] away from the path of Allah}, meaning: they outwardly displayed faith while inwardly harboring disbelief, and protected themselves through false oaths. So many who did not know the reality of their affair thought them truthful and were deceived by them, and thus by this means some people were turned away from the path of Allah. {So for them is a humiliating punishment}, meaning: in requital for what they made cheap and trivial, namely swearing by the Name of Allah the Great in false, perjurious oaths.

Then He said: {Their wealth and their children will never avail them anything against Allah}, meaning: that will never repel from them any calamity when it comes to them. {Those are the companions of the Fire, abiding therein forever}.

Then He said: {On the Day when Allah will resurrect them all together}, meaning: He will gather them on the Day of Resurrection, every last one of them, leaving none of them behind. {And they will swear to Him just as they swear to you, and they think that they are upon something}, meaning: they will swear by Allah, Mighty and Majestic, that they were upon guidance and rectitude, just as they used to swear to people in the worldly life, because whoever lives upon something dies upon it and is resurrected upon it. And they believe that this will benefit them with Allah just as it used to benefit them with people, such that outward judgments were applied to them accordingly. For this reason He said: {And they think that they are upon something}, meaning: that their swearing that oath to their Lord, Mighty and Majestic, [will benefit them].

Then He said, censoring their supposition: {Unquestionably, it is they who are the liars}, thus confirming the report about them with certainty of their lying.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] my father told us, Ibn Nufayl told us, Zuhayr told us, from Simak ibn Harb, Sa'id ibn Jubayr told me, that Ibn Abbas told him: that the Prophet ﷺ was in the shade of one of his chambers, with a group of Muslims beside him, and the shade had begun to shrink away from them. He said: "A man will come to you who looks with the eyes of a devil, and when he comes to you, do not speak to him." Then a blue-eyed man came, and the Messenger of Allah ﷺ called him and spoke with him, and he said: "Why do you and so-and-so and so-and-so curse me?" naming a group of men by their names. He said: the man then went and called them, and they swore to him and made excuses to him. He said: then Allah, Mighty and Majestic, revealed: {And they will swear to Him just as they swear to you, and they think that they are upon something. Unquestionably, it is they who are the liars}.

And likewise Imam Ahmad narrated it through two routes from Simak, with the same content. And Ibn Jarir narrated it from Muhammad ibn al-Muthanna, from Ghundar, from Shu'bah, from Simak, similarly. He also reported it through the hadith of Sufyan al-Thawri, from Simak, similarly. The chain is good, but neither of the two [Bukhari and Muslim] recorded it.

The condition of these people is as Allah the Almighty informed us about the polytheists, where He says: {Then their only argument will be that they say, "By Allah, our Lord, we were not polytheists." Look how they lied against themselves, and lost is what they used to invent} [al-An'am: 23, 24].

Then He said: {Satan has overcome them and made them forget the remembrance of Allah}, meaning: Satan has taken hold of their hearts to the point that he made them forget to remember Allah, Mighty and Majestic, and this is what he does to whoever he overcomes. For this reason Abu Dawud said:

[chain] Ahmad ibn Yunus told us, Za'idah told us, al-Sa'ib ibn Hubaysh told us, from Ma'dan ibn Abi Talhah al-Ya'muri, from Abu al-Darda: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "There are not three people in a town or in the desert among whom prayer is not established, but that Satan has overcome them. So hold fast to the congregation, for indeed the wolf eats the one who strays apart." Za'idah said: al-Sa'ib said: meaning the congregational prayer.

Then the Almighty said: {Those are the party of Satan}, meaning: those over whom Satan has gained mastery and made them forget the remembrance of Allah. Then the Almighty said: {Unquestionably, it is the party of Satan who are the losers}.

## 58:20-22 — verses 20-22 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, informing about the disbelievers who are obstinate opponents contending with God and His Messenger, meaning those who are on one side while the Sacred Law is on another side, that is, those who diverge from the truth and oppose it, being in one direction while guidance is in another. {Those are among the most abased} means among the wretched ones, those cast far away and driven off from what is right, the most abased in this world and the Hereafter.

{God has decreed: I shall surely be victorious, I and My messengers} means: He has already judged and inscribed in His first Book, and decreed by a decree that cannot be opposed, resisted, or altered, that victory belongs to Him, to His Book, to His messengers, and to His believing servants, in this world and the Hereafter, and that the final outcome belongs to those who are mindful of Him, as the Almighty says: {Indeed We shall surely help Our messengers and those who believe, in the life of this world and on the Day when the witnesses will stand, the Day when the excuses of the wrongdoers will not benefit them; theirs is the curse and theirs is the evil abode} [Ghafir: 51, 52]. And He says here: {God has decreed: I shall surely be victorious, I and My messengers. Indeed God is Strong, Mighty} meaning: the Strong, the Mighty has decreed that He is the one who overcomes His enemies. This is a fixed decree and a settled matter, that the final outcome and the victory belong to the believers in this world and the Hereafter.

Then the Almighty says: {You will not find a people who believe in God and the Last Day showing love to those who oppose God and His Messenger, even if they were their fathers, their sons, their brothers, or their clan} meaning: they do not show love to those who oppose God, even if they are among their closest relatives, as the Almighty says: {The believers should not take the disbelievers as allies rather than the believers. Whoever does that has no connection with God at all, except when you fear them out of caution. And God warns you of Himself} [Al Imran: 28], the verse; and the Almighty says: {Say: If your fathers, your sons, your brothers, your spouses, your clan, wealth you have acquired, trade whose decline you fear, and dwellings you find pleasing are dearer to you than God and His Messenger and striving in His way, then wait until God brings His command. And God does not guide the wrongdoing people} [al-Tawbah: 24].

Sa'id ibn 'Abd al-'Aziz and others said: this verse, {You will not find a people who believe in God and the Last Day...} to its end, was revealed concerning Abu 'Ubaydah 'Amir ibn 'Abdullah ibn al-Jarrah, when he killed his father on the day of Badr. This is why 'Umar ibn al-Khattab, may God be pleased with him, said, when the matter of succession after him was made a matter of consultation among those six, may God be pleased with them: "Had Abu 'Ubaydah been alive, I would have appointed him as successor."

It has also been said regarding His statement, {even if they were their fathers}, that it was revealed about Abu 'Ubaydah who killed his father on the day of Badr; {or their sons} about al-Siddiq (Abu Bakr), who that day intended to kill his son 'Abd al-Rahman; {or their brothers} about Mus'ab ibn 'Umayr, who killed his brother 'Ubayd ibn 'Umayr that day; {or their clan} about 'Umar, who also killed a relative of his that day, and about Hamzah, 'Ali, and 'Ubaydah ibn al-Harith, who killed 'Utbah, Shaybah, and al-Walid ibn 'Utbah that day. And God knows best.

I say: of this same kind is the incident when the Messenger of God ﷺ consulted the Muslims about the captives of Badr. Al-Siddiq advised that they be ransomed, so that what was taken from them would become a strength for the Muslims, since they were paternal cousins and kinsmen, and perhaps God would guide them. But 'Umar said: "I do not see it as he sees it, O Messenger of God. Give me power over so-and-so," a relative of 'Umar's, "so that I may kill him, and give 'Ali power over 'Aqil, and give so-and-so power over so-and-so, so that God may know that there is no leniency in our hearts toward the polytheists" ... the story in full.

His statement, {Those, He has inscribed faith in their hearts and strengthened them with a spirit from Him} means: whoever is characterized by not showing love to those who oppose God and His Messenger, even if it were his father or brother, this is one in whose heart God has inscribed faith, meaning: He has decreed happiness for him and established it firmly in his heart, and adorned faith in his inner insight.

Al-Suddi said: {He has inscribed faith in their hearts} means He placed faith in their hearts.

Ibn 'Abbas said: {and strengthened them with a spirit from Him} means: He empowered them.

His statement, {and He will admit them into gardens beneath which rivers flow, abiding therein forever. God is pleased with them and they are pleased with Him}, all of this has been explained previously more than once.

In His statement, {God is pleased with them and they are pleased with Him}, there is a wondrous subtlety: since they became displeased with their relatives and clans for the sake of God, God compensated them with His pleasure with them, and made them pleased with Him through what He gave them of lasting bliss, tremendous triumph, and all-encompassing favor.

His statement, {Those are the party of God. Indeed, it is the party of God who are the successful ones} means: these are the party of God, that is, the servants of God and the people of His honor.

His statement, {Indeed, it is the party of God who are the successful ones} is a proclamation exalting their success, happiness, and victory in this world and the Hereafter, in contrast to what He said about the others, that they are the party of Satan. He then says: {Indeed, it is the party of Satan who are the losers}.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] al-Fadl ibn 'Anbasah, from a man he named, said to be 'Abd al-Hamid ibn Sulayman, this part having been detached from my written source, from al-Dhayyal ibn 'Abbad, who said: Abu Hazim al-A'raj wrote to al-Zuhri: "Know that standing before people is of two kinds. There is a standing which God grants at the hands of His close friends for the sake of His close friends, and such people are of little renown, hidden in their persons. Their description has indeed come on the tongue of the Messenger of God ﷺ: 'Indeed God loves the hidden ones, the God-fearing, the innocent, those who when absent are not missed, and when present are not called upon; their hearts are lamps of guidance, they emerge from every dark, blackening trial.'" These are the close friends of God, the Almighty, of whom God says: {Those are the party of God. Indeed, it is the party of God who are the successful ones}.

Nu'aym ibn Hammad said: [chain] Muhammad ibn Thawr, from Yunus, from al-Hasan, who said: the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "O God, do not let any depraved person or transgressor have any favor or benefaction from me, for I found in what You revealed to me: {You will not find a people who believe in God and the Last Day showing love to those who oppose God and His Messenger}." Sufyan said: they consider it to have been revealed concerning one who associates closely with those in power. It was also related by Abu Ahmad al-'Askari.

يَقُولُ تَعَالَى مُخْبِرًا عَنِ الْكُفَّارِ الْمُعَانِدِينَ الْمُحَادِّينَ لِلَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِ، يَعْنِي: الَّذِينَ هُمْ فِي حَدٍّ وَالشَّرْعُ فِي حَدٍّ، أَيْ: مُجَانِبُونَ لِلْحَقِّ مُشَاقُّونَ لَهُ، هُمْ فِي نَاحِيَةٍ وَالْهُدَى فِي نَاحِيَةٍ، ﴿أُولَئِكَ فِي الأذَلِّينَ﴾ أَيْ: فِي الْأَشْقِيَاءِ الْمُبْعَدِينَ الْمَطْرُودِينَ عَنِ الصَّوَابِ، الْأَذَلِّينَ فِي الدُّنْيَا وَالْآخِرَةِ.

﴿كَتَبَ اللَّهُ لأغْلِبَنَّ أَنَا وَرُسُلِي﴾ أَيْ: قَدْ حَكَمَ وَكَتَبَ فِي كِتَابِهِ الْأَوَّلِ وقَدَره الَّذِي لَا يُخالَف وَلَا يُمانع، وَلَا يُبَدَّلُ، بِأَنَّ النُّصْرَةَ لَهُ وَلِكِتَابِهِ وَرُسُلِهِ وَعِبَادِهِ المؤمنين في الدنيا والآخرة، وأن العاقبة لِلْمُتَّقِينَ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِنَّا لَنَنْصُرُ رُسُلَنَا وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا فِي الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا وَيَوْمَ يَقُومُ الأشْهَادُ يَوْمَ لَا يَنْفَعُ الظَّالِمِينَ مَعْذِرَتُهُمْ وَلَهُمُ اللَّعْنَةُ وَلَهُمْ سُوءُ الدَّارِ﴾ [غَافِرٍ: ٥١، ٥٢] وَقَالَ هَا هُنَا ﴿كَتَبَ اللَّهُ لأغْلِبَنَّ أَنَا وَرُسُلِي إِنَّ اللَّهَ قَوِيٌّ عَزِيزٌ﴾ أَيْ: كَتَبَ الْقَوِيُّ الْعَزِيزُ أَنَّهُ الْغَالِبُ لِأَعْدَائِهِ. وَهَذَا قَدْرٌ مُحْكَمٌ وَأَمْرٌ مُبْرَمٌ، أَنَّ الْعَاقِبَةَ وَالنُّصْرَةَ لِلْمُؤْمِنِينَ فِي الدُّنْيَا وَالْآخِرَةِ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿لَا تَجِدُ قَوْمًا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِاللَّهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الآخِرِ يُوَادُّونَ مَنْ حَادَّ اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ وَلَوْ كَانُوا آبَاءَهُمْ أَوْ أَبْنَاءَهُمْ أَوْ إِخْوَانَهُمْ أَوْ عَشِيرَتَهُمْ﴾ أَيْ: لَا يُوَادُّونَ الْمُحَادِّينَ وَلَوْ كَانُوا مِنَ الْأَقْرَبِينَ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿لَا يَتَّخِذِ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ الْكَافِرِينَ أَوْلِيَاءَ مِنْ دُونِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَمَنْ يَفْعَلْ ذَلِكَ فَلَيْسَ مِنَ اللَّهِ فِي شَيْءٍ إِلا أَنْ تَتَّقُوا مِنْهُمْ تُقَاةً وَيُحَذِّرُكُمُ اللَّهُ نَفْسَهُ﴾ [آلِ عِمْرَانَ: ٢٨] الْآيَةَ، وَقَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿قُلْ إِنْ كَانَ آبَاؤُكُمْ وَأَبْنَاؤُكُمْ وَإِخْوَانُكُمْ وَأَزْوَاجُكُمْ وَعَشِيرَتُكُمْ وَأَمْوَالٌ اقْتَرَفْتُمُوهَا وَتِجَارَةٌ تَخْشَوْنَ كَسَادَهَا وَمَسَاكِنُ تَرْضَوْنَهَا أَحَبَّ إِلَيْكُمْ مِنَ اللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِ وَجِهَادٍ فِي سَبِيلِهِ فَتَرَبَّصُوا حَتَّى يَأْتِيَ اللَّهُ بِأَمْرِهِ وَاللَّهُ لَا يَهْدِي الْقَوْمَ الْفَاسِقِينَ﴾ [التَّوْبَةِ: ٢٤]

وَقَدْ قَالَ سَعِيدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ الْعَزِيزِ وَغَيْرُهُ: أُنْزِلَتْ هَذِهِ الْآيَةَ ﴿لَا تَجِدُ قَوْمًا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِاللَّهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الآخِرِ﴾ إِلَى آخِرِهَا فِي أَبِي عُبَيْدَةَ عَامِرِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ الْجَرَّاحِ، حِينَ قَتَلَ أَبَاهُ يَوْمَ بَدْرٍ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ عُمَرُ بْنُ الْخَطَّابِ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، حِينَ جُعِلَ الْأَمْرُ شُورَى بَعْدَهُ فِي أُولَئِكَ السِّتَّةِ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ: "وَلَوْ كَانَ أَبُو عُبَيْدَةَ حَيًّا لَاسْتَخْلَفْتُهُ".

وَقِيلَ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَلَوْ كَانُوا آبَاءَهُمْ﴾ نَزَلَتْ فِي أَبِي عُبَيْدَةَ قَتَلَ أَبَاهُ يَوْمَ بَدْرٍ ﴿أَوْ أَبْنَاءَهُمْ﴾ فِي الصِّدِّيقِ، هَمَّ يَوْمَئِذٍ بِقَتْلِ ابْنِهِ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ، ﴿أَوْ إِخْوَانَهُمْ﴾ فِي مُصْعَبِ بْنِ عُمَيْرٍ، قَتَلَ أَخَاهُ عُبَيْدَ بْنَ عُمَيْرٍ يَوْمَئِذٍ ﴿أَوْ عَشِيرَتَهُمْ﴾ فِي عُمَرَ، قَتَلَ قَرِيبًا لَهُ يَوْمَئِذٍ أَيْضًا، وَفِي حَمْزَةَ وَعَلِيٍّ وَعُبَيْدَةَ بْنِ الْحَارِثِ، قَتَلُوا عُتْبَةَ وَشَيْبَةَ وَالْوَلِيدَ بْنَ عُتْبَةَ يَوْمَئِذٍ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

قُلْتُ: وَمِنْ هَذَا الْقَبِيلِ حِينَ اسْتَشَارَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ الْمُسْلِمِينَ فِي أُسَارَى بَدْرٍ، فَأَشَارَ الصِّدِّيقُ بِأَنْ يُفَادُوا، فَيَكُونُ مَا يُؤْخَذُ مِنْهُمْ قُوَّةً لِلْمُسْلِمِينَ، وَهُمْ بَنُو الْعَمِّ وَالْعَشِيرَةِ، وَلَعَلَّ اللَّهَ أَنْ يَهْدِيَهُمْ. وَقَالَ عُمَرُ: لَا أَرَى مَا رَأَى يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، هَلْ تُمَكِّنِّي مِنْ فُلَانٍ-قَرِيبٍ لِعُمَرَ-فَأَقْتُلَهُ، وَتُمَكِّنُ عَلَيًّا مِنْ عَقِيلٍ، وَتُمَكِّنُ فُلَانًا مِنْ فُلَانٍ، لِيَعْلَمَ اللَّهُ أَنَّهُ لَيْسَتْ فِي قُلُوبِنَا هَوَادَةٌ لِلْمُشْرِكِينَ ... الْقِصَّةَ بِكَامِلِهَا.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿أُولَئِكَ كَتَبَ فِي قُلُوبِهِمُ الإيمَانَ وَأَيَّدَهُمْ بِرُوحٍ مِنْهُ﴾ أَيْ: مَنِ اتَّصَفَ بِأَنَّهُ لَا يُوَادُّ مَنْ حَادَ اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ وَلَوْ كَانَ أَبَاهُ أَوْ أَخَاهُ، فَهَذَا مِمَّنْ كَتَبَ اللَّهُ فِي قَلْبِهِ الْإِيمَانَ، أَيْ: كَتَبَ لَهُ السَّعَادَةَ وَقَرَّرَهَا فِي قلبه وزين الإيمان في بصيرته.

وَقَالَ السُّدِّيُّ: ﴿كَتَبَ فِي قُلُوبِهِمُ الإيمَانَ﴾ جَعَلَ فِي قُلُوبِهِمُ الْإِيمَانَ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿وَأَيَّدَهُمْ بِرُوحٍ مِنْهُ﴾ أَيْ: قَوَّاهُمْ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَيُدْخِلُهُمْ جَنَّاتٍ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهَا الأنْهَارُ خَالِدِينَ فِيهَا رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ وَرَضُوا عَنْهُ﴾ كُلُّ هَذَا تَقَدَّمَ تَفْسِيرُهُ غَيْرَ مَرَّةٍ.

وَفِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ وَرَضُوا عَنْهُ﴾ سِرٌّ بَدِيعٌ، وَهُوَ أَنَّهُ لَمَّا سَخِطُوا عَلَى الْقَرَائِبِ وَالْعَشَائِرِ فِي اللَّهِ عَوَّضَهُمُ اللَّهُ بِالرِّضَا عَنْهُمْ، وَأَرْضَاهُمْ عَنْهُ بِمَا أَعْطَاهُمْ مِنَ النَّعِيمِ الْمُقِيمِ، وَالْفَوْزِ الْعَظِيمِ، وَالْفَضْلِ الْعَمِيمِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿أُولَئِكَ حِزْبُ اللَّهِ أَلا إِنَّ حِزْبَ اللَّهِ هُمُ الْمُفْلِحُونَ﴾ أَيْ: هَؤُلَاءِ حزبُ اللَّهِ، أَيْ: عِبَادُ اللَّهِ وَأَهْلُ كَرَامَتِهِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿أَلا إِنَّ حِزْبَ اللَّهِ هُمُ الْمُفْلِحُونَ﴾ تَنْوِيهٌ بِفَلَاحِهِمْ وَسَعَادَتِهِمْ وَنَصْرِهِمْ فِي الدُّنْيَا وَالْآخِرَةِ، فِي مُقَابَلَةِ مَا أَخْبَرَ عَنْ أُولَئِكَ بِأَنَّهُمْ حِزْبُ الشَّيْطَانِ. ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿أَلا إِنَّ حِزْبَ الشَّيْطَانِ هُمُ الْخَاسِرُونَ﴾

وقد قال بن أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا هَارُونُ بْنُ حُمَيْدٍ الْوَاسِطِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا الْفَضْلُ بْنُ عَنْبَسة، عَنْ رَجُلٍ قَدْ سَمَّاهُ-يُقَالُ هُوَ عَبْدُ الْحَمِيدِ بْنُ سُلَيْمَانَ، انْقَطَعَ مِنْ كِتَابِي-عَنِ الذَيَّال بْنِ عَبَّادٍ قَالَ: كَتَبَ أَبُو حَازِمٍ الْأَعْرَجُ إِلَى الزُّهْرِيِّ: اعْلَمْ أَنَّ الْجَاهَ جَاهَانَ، جَاهٌ يُجْرِيهِ اللَّهُ عَلَى أَيْدِي أَوْلِيَائِهِ لِأَوْلِيَائِهِ، وَأَنَّهُمُ الْخَامِلُ ذِكْرُهُمُ، الْخَفِيَّةُ شُخُوصُهُمْ، وَلَقَدْ جَاءَتْ صِفَتُهُمْ عَلَى لِسَانِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم. "إن اللَّهَ يُحِبُّ الْأَخْفِيَاءَ الْأَتْقِيَاءَ الْأَبْرِيَاءَ، الَّذِينَ إِذَا غَابُوا لَمْ يُفتَقَدوا، وَإِذَا حَضَرُوا لَمْ يُدْعَوا، قُلُوبُهُمْ مَصَابِيحُ الْهُدَى، يَخْرُجُونَ مِنْ كُلِّ فِتْنَةٍ سَوْدَاءَ مُظْلِمَةٍ" فَهَؤُلَاءِ أَوْلِيَاءُ اللَّهِ تَعَالَى الَّذِينَ قَالَ اللَّهُ: ﴿أُولَئِكَ حِزْبُ اللَّهِ أَلا إِنَّ حِزْبَ اللَّهِ هُمُ الْمُفْلِحُونَ﴾

وَقَالَ نُعَيم بْنُ حَمّاد: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ ثَوْرٍ، عَنْ يُونُسَ، عَنِ الْحَسَنِ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "اللَّهُمَّ، لَا تَجْعَلْ لِفَاجِرٍ وَلَا لِفَاسِقٍ عِنْدِي يَدًا وَلَا نِعْمَةً، فَإِنِّي وَجَدْتُ فِيمَا أَوْحَيْتَهُ إِلَيَّ: ﴿لَا تَجِدُ قَوْمًا يُؤْمِنُونَ بِاللَّهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الآخِرِ يُوَادُّونَ مَنْ حَادَّ اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ﴾ قَالَ سُفْيَانُ: يَرَوْنَ أَنَّهَا نَزَلَتْ فِيمَنْ يُخَالِطُ السُّلْطَانَ. وَرَوَاهُ أَبُو أَحْمَدَ الْعَسْكَرِيُّ.
