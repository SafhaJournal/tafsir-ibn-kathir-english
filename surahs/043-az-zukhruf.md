## 43:1-8 — verses 1-8 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Tafsir of Surat al-Zukhruf

It is Meccan.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

The Most High says: {Ha Mim. By the Clear Book} that is, the clear, plain book, luminous in both meanings and wording, because it was sent down in the language of the Arabs, which is the most eloquent of languages for communication among people. This is why He said: {Indeed, We have made it} that is, We sent it down {a Qur'an in Arabic} that is, in the language of the Arabs, eloquent and clear, {that you might understand} that is, that you might comprehend it and reflect upon it, as He said: {in a clear Arabic tongue} [al-Shu'ara: 195].

The Most High's saying: {And indeed it is, in the Mother of the Book with Us, truly exalted, full of wisdom} clarifies its honor in the highest assembly, so that the people of the earth might honor it, revere it, and obey it. So the Most High says: {And indeed it} that is, the Qur'an {is in the Mother of the Book} that is, the Preserved Tablet, as said by Ibn Abbas and Mujahid, {with Us} that is, in Our presence, as said by Qatadah and others, {truly exalted} that is, possessing tremendous standing, honor, and excellence, as said by Qatadah, {full of wisdom} that is, made firm and free from confusion and deviation.

All of this is a pointing out of its honor and excellence, as He said: {Indeed it is a noble Qur'an. In a well-guarded Book. None touch it except the purified. A revelation from the Lord of the worlds} [al-Waqi'ah: 77-80], and as He said: {Nay, indeed it is a reminder. So whoever wills may remember it. In honored pages, exalted, purified, in the hands of scribes, noble and virtuous} [Abasa: 11-16]. This is why the scholars, may Allah have mercy on them, derived from these two verses that a person in a state of ritual impurity (muhdith) may not touch the Mus'haf, as is reported in the hadith if it is authentic, because the angels revere the copies of the Qur'an in the highest assembly, so the people of the earth are even more deserving and worthy of doing so, since it was sent down upon them and its address is directed to them. They are more entitled to meet it with honor and reverence, and to submit to it with acceptance and surrender, in accordance with His saying: {And indeed it is, in the Mother of the Book with Us, truly exalted, full of wisdom}.

His saying: {Then shall We turn away the Reminder from you completely because you are a transgressing people?} The commentators differed regarding its meaning. It was said that its meaning is: do you suppose that We would overlook you and not punish you, when you have not done what you were commanded to do? This was said by Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, Abu Salih, and al-Suddi, and Ibn Jarir preferred it.

Qatadah said regarding His saying: {Then shall We turn away the Reminder from you completely}: By Allah, if this Qur'an had been lifted away when the early generations of this ummah rejected it, they would have perished. But Allah returned to them with His favor and mercy, and repeated it to them and called them to it for twenty years, or whatever period Allah willed of that.

Qatadah's statement is extremely subtle in meaning, and its gist is that he is saying: the Most High, out of His kindness and mercy toward His creation, does not abandon calling them to good and to the wise Reminder, which is the Qur'an, even though they may be transgressors turning away from it. Rather, He commanded it so that whoever was destined for guidance might be guided, and so that the proof might be established against whoever's wretchedness had been decreed.

Then the Most High said, consoling His Prophet ﷺ regarding the denial of those of his people who denied him, and commanding him to be patient with them: {And how many a prophet did We send among the former peoples} that is, among the communities of the former peoples, {and no prophet came to them except that they used to mock him} that is, they would deny him and ridicule him.

His saying: {So We destroyed those who were stronger than them in might} that is, We destroyed those who denied the messengers, and they were indeed stronger in might than these people who deny you, O Muhammad. Like His saying: {Have they not traveled through the land and seen how was the end of those before them? They were more numerous than them and greater in strength} [Ghafir: 82]. And there are many verses to this effect.

His saying: {And the example of the former peoples has passed} Mujahid said: their way (sunnah). Qatadah said: their punishment. Others said: their lesson, that is, We made them a lesson for those who came after them among the deniers, so that what befell them might befall these later ones as well, as in His saying at the end of this surah: {So We made them a precedent and an example for later peoples} [al-Zukhruf: 56]. And as in His saying: {The way of Allah which has already occurred with His servants} [Ghafir: 85], and His saying: {And you will never find in the way of Allah any change} [al-Ahzab: 62].

## 43:9-14 — verses 9-14 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: If you ask, O Muhammad, these polytheists who associate others with Allah and worship other than Him alongside Him: {Who created the heavens and the earth, they will surely say: The Mighty, the All-Knowing created them}, meaning: they will surely acknowledge that the Creator of that is Allah alone, without partner, and yet despite this they worship other than Him among idols and rivals.

Then He says: {Who made the earth a cradle for you}, meaning: a bed, a fixed resting place, upon which they walk, stand, sleep, and go about their business, even though it was created upon the surge of water, but He anchored it with mountains so that it would not sway this way or that. {And made for you therein paths}, meaning: routes between the mountains and the valleys, {that you may be guided}, meaning: in your travel from one land to another, from one region to another, and from one territory to another.

{And He who sent down water from the sky in due measure}, meaning: in an amount sufficient for your crops and your fruits and your drinking, for yourselves and for your livestock. And His saying: {And We revived thereby a dead land}, meaning: a land that was dead, but when the water came to it, it stirred and swelled and produced every delightful kind of growth.

Then, by the revival of the earth, He draws attention to the revival of bodies on the Day of Resurrection after their death, saying: {Thus will you be brought forth}.

Then He says: {And He who created all the pairs}, meaning: from what the earth produces of all its various kinds, of plants, crops, fruits, and flowers, and other than that, that is, from animals of differing species and kinds, {and made for you of ships}, meaning: vessels, {and of livestock those which you ride}, meaning: He tamed them for you, subjected them, and made them easy for you, that you may eat their meat, drink their milk, and ride upon their backs. Therefore He said: {That you may settle yourselves upon their backs}, meaning: that you may settle firmly and comfortably {upon their backs}, meaning: upon the backs of this kind, {and then remember the favor of your Lord}, meaning: in what He has subjected for you, {when you are settled thereon, and say: Glory be to Him who has subjected this to us, and we could not have done it ourselves}, meaning: we could not have overpowered it. Had Allah not subjected this to us, we would not have been able to do it.

Ibn Abbas, Qatadah, al-Suddi, and Ibn Zayd said: {muqrinin} means: capable.

{And indeed, to our Lord we are returning}, meaning: we will surely come back to Him after our death, and to Him is our greatest journey. This is of the type where He draws attention by means of worldly travel to the travel of the Hereafter, just as He drew attention by means of worldly provision to the provision of the Hereafter in His saying: {And take provision, for indeed the best provision is piety} [al-Baqarah: 197], and by means of worldly clothing to that of the Hereafter in His saying, the Almighty: {and feathers (i.e., adornment), but the clothing of piety, that is better. That is from the signs of Allah} [al-A'raf: 26].

Mention of the hadiths reported regarding riding a mount:

The hadith of the Commander of the Faithful, Ali ibn Abi Talib, may Allah be pleased with him:

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Ali ibn Rabi'ah said: I saw Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, brought a mount. When he placed his foot in the stirrup he said: "In the name of Allah." When he was settled upon it he said: "Praise be to Allah," {Glory be to Him who has subjected this to us, and we could not have done it ourselves, and indeed, to our Lord we are returning}. Then he praised Allah three times, and said "Allahu Akbar" three times, then said: "Glory be to You, there is no god but You, I have wronged myself, so forgive me." Then he laughed, and I said to him: "What made you laugh, O Commander of the Faithful?" He said: "I saw the Messenger of Allah ﷺ do as I did, then he laughed." I said: "What made you laugh, O Messenger of Allah?" He said: "The Lord is amazed at His servant when he says: 'My Lord, forgive me,' and He says: 'My servant knows that none forgives sins except Me.'"

It was likewise reported by Abu Dawud, al-Tirmidhi, and al-Nasa'i, from the hadith of Abu al-Ahwas, and al-Nasa'i added: and Mansur, from Abu Ishaq al-Sabi'i, from Ali ibn Rabi'ah al-Asadi al-Walibi, with the same content. Al-Tirmidhi said: hasan sahih (good and authentic).

Abd al-Rahman ibn Mahdi said, from Shu'bah: I said to Abu Ishaq al-Sabi'i, "From whom did you hear this hadith?" He said: "From Yunus ibn Khabbab." So I met Yunus ibn Khabbab and said: "From whom did you hear it?" He said: "From a man who heard it from Ali ibn Rabi'ah." Some of them reported it from Yunus ibn Khabbab, from Shaqiq ibn Uqbah al-Asadi, from Ali ibn Rabi'ah al-Walibi, with the same content.

The hadith of Abdullah ibn Abbas, may Allah be pleased with both of them:

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ seated him behind him on his mount, and when he was settled upon it the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said "Allahu Akbar" three times, and praised Allah three times, and declared His oneness once. Then he leaned back upon him and laughed, then turned toward him and said: "There is no Muslim man who rides a mount and does as I have done, except that Allah, Mighty and Majestic, turns toward him and laughs at him as I have laughed at you." Ahmad alone reported it.

The hadith of Abdullah ibn Umar, may Allah be pleased with both of them:

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] that the Prophet ﷺ, when he mounted his riding beast, would say "Allahu Akbar" three times, then say: {Glory be to Him who has subjected this to us, and we could not have done it ourselves, and indeed, to our Lord we are returning}. Then he would say: "O Allah, I ask You in this journey of mine for righteousness and piety, and for deeds that please You. O Allah, make this journey easy for us and fold up for us its distance. O Allah, You are the Companion on the journey and the Guardian of the family left behind. O Allah, accompany us on our journey and take care of our family in our absence." And when he returned to his family he would say: "We return, repentant, if Allah wills, worshipping, praising our Lord."

It was likewise reported by Muslim, Abu Dawud, and al-Nasa'i, from the hadith of Ibn Jurayj, and by al-Tirmidhi from the hadith of Hammad ibn Salamah, both of them from Abu al-Zubayr, with the same content.

Another hadith:

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Abu Las al-Khuza'i said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ mounted us on some camels from the camels of charity for the Hajj. We said: "O Messenger of Allah, we do not think it right that you should mount us on these!" He said: "There is no camel but a devil rides upon its hump, so mention the name of Allah over it when you ride it as I command you, then subdue it for your own use, for it is Allah, Mighty and Majestic, who truly carries you."

Abu Las's name is Muhammad ibn al-Aswad ibn Khalaf.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿الَّذِي جَعَلَ لَكُمُ الأرْضَ مَهْدًا﴾ أَيْ: فِرَاشًا قَرَارًا ثَابِتَةً، يَسِيرُونَ عَلَيْهَا وَيَقُومُونَ وَيَنَامُونَ وَيَنْصَرِفُونَ، مَعَ أَنَّهَا مَخْلُوقَةٌ عَلَى تَيَّارِ الْمَاءِ، لَكِنَّهُ أَرْسَاهَا بِالْجِبَالِ لِئَلَّا تَمِيدَ هَكَذَا وَلَا هَكَذَا، ﴿وَجَعَلَ لَكُمْ فِيهَا سُبُلا﴾ أَيْ: طُرُقًا بَيْنَ الْجِبَالِ وَالْأَوْدِيَةِ ﴿لَعَلَّكُمْ تَهْتَدُونَ﴾ أَيْ: فِي سَيْرِكُمْ مِنْ بلد إلى بلد، وَقُطْرٍ إِلَى قُطْرٍ، وَإِقْلِيمٍ إِلَى إِقْلِيمٍ.

﴿وَالَّذِي نزلَ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ مَاءً بِقَدَرٍ﴾ أَيْ: بِحَسَبِ الْكِفَايَةِ لِزُرُوعِكُمْ وَثِمَارِكُمْ وَشُرْبِكُمْ، لِأَنْفُسِكُمْ وَلِأَنْعَامِكُمْ. وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَأَنْشَرْنَا بِهِ بَلْدَةً مَيْتًا﴾ أَيْ: أَرْضًا مَيْتَةً، فَلَمَّا جَاءَهَا الْمَاءُ اهْتَزَّتْ وَرَبَتْ، وَأَنْبَتَتْ مِنْ كُلِّ زَوْجٍ بَهِيجٍ.

ثُمَّ نَبَّهَ بِإِحْيَاءِ الْأَرْضِ عَلَى إِحْيَاءِ الْأَجْسَادِ يَوْمَ الْمَعَادِ بَعْدَ مَوْتِهَا، فَقَالَ: ﴿كَذَلِكَ تُخْرَجُونَ﴾

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿وَالَّذِي خَلَقَ الأزْوَاجَ كُلَّهَا﴾ أَيْ: مِمَّا تُنْبِتُ الْأَرْضُ مِنْ سَائِرِ الْأَصْنَافِ، مِنْ نَبَاتٍ وَزُرُوعٍ وَثِمَارٍ وَأَزَاهِيرَ، وَغَيْرِ ذَلِكَ [أَيْ] مِنَ الْحَيَوَانَاتِ عَلَى اخْتِلَافِ أَجْنَاسِهَا وَأَصْنَافِهَا، ﴿وَجَعَلَ لَكُمْ مِنَ الْفُلْكِ﴾ أَيِ: السُّفُنِ ﴿وَالأنْعَامِ مَا تَرْكَبُونَ﴾ أَيْ: ذَلَّلَهَا لَكُمْ وَسَخَّرَهَا وَيَسَّرَهَا لِأَكْلِكُمْ لُحُومَهَا، وَشُرْبِكُمْ أَلْبَانَهَا وَرُكُوبِكُمْ ظُهُورَهَا؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿لِتَسْتَوُوا عَلَى ظُهُورِهِ﴾ أَيْ: لِتَسْتَوُوا مُتَمَكِّنِينَ مُرْتَفِقِينَ ﴿عَلَى ظُهُورِهِ﴾ أَيْ: عَلَى ظُهُورِ هَذَا الْجِنْسِ، ﴿ثُمَّ تَذْكُرُوا نِعْمَةَ رَبِّكُمْ﴾ أَيْ: فِيمَا سَخَّرَ لَكُمْ ﴿إِذَا اسْتَوَيْتُمْ عَلَيْهِ وَتَقُولُوا سُبْحَانَ الَّذِي سَخَّرَ لَنَا هَذَا وَمَا كُنَّا لَهُ مُقْرِنِينَ﴾ أَيْ: مُقَاوِمِينَ. وَلَوْلَا تَسْخِيرُ اللَّهِ لَنَا هَذَا مَا قَدَرْنَا عَلَيْهِ.

قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ وَابْنُ زَيْدٍ: ﴿مُقْرِنِينَ﴾ أَيْ: مُطِيقِينَ. 

﴿وَإِنَّا إِلَى رَبِّنَا لَمُنْقَلِبُونَ﴾ أَيْ: لَصَائِرُونَ إِلَيْهِ بَعْدَ مَمَاتِنَا، وَإِلَيْهِ سَيْرُنَا الْأَكْبَرُ. وَهَذَا مِنْ بَابِ التَّنْبِيهِ بِسَيْرِ الدُّنْيَا عَلَى سَيْرِ الْآخِرَةِ، كَمَا نَبَّهَ بِالزَّادِ الدُّنْيَوِيِّ عَلَى [الزَّادِ] الْأُخْرَوِيِّ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَتَزَوَّدُوا فَإِنَّ خَيْرَ الزَّادِ التَّقْوَى﴾ [الْبَقَرَةِ: ١٩٧] وَبِاللِّبَاسِ الدُّنْيَوِيِّ عَلَى الْأُخْرَوِيِّ فِي قَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَرِيشًا وَلِبَاسُ التَّقْوَى ذَلِكَ خَيْرٌ [ذَلِكَ مِنْ آيَاتِ اللَّهِ] ﴾ [الْأَعْرَافِ: ٢٦] .

ذِكْرُ الْأَحَادِيثِ الْوَارِدَةِ عِنْدَ رُكُوبِ الدَّابَّةِ:

حَدِيثُ أَمِيرِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ عَلِيِّ بْنِ أَبِي طَالِبٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ:

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا يَزِيدُ، حَدَّثَنَا شَرِيكُ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ، عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ عَلِيِّ بْنِ رَبِيعَةَ قَالَ: رَأَيْتُ عَلِيًّا، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، أُتِيَ بِدَابَّةٍ، فَلَمَّا وَضَعَ رِجْلَهُ فِي الرِّكاب قَالَ: بِاسْمِ اللَّهِ. فَلَمَّا اسْتَوَى عَلَيْهَا قَالَ: الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ، ﴿سُبْحَانَ الَّذِي سَخَّرَ لَنَا هَذَا وَمَا كُنَّا لَهُ مُقْرِنِينَ. وَإِنَّا إِلَى رَبِّنَا لَمُنْقَلِبُونَ﴾ ثُمَّ حَمِدَ اللَّهَ ثَلَاثًا، وَكَبَّرَ ثَلَاثًا، ثُمَّ قَالَ: سُبْحَانَكَ، لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ، قَدْ ظَلَمْتُ نَفْسِي فَاغْفِرْ لِي. ثُمَّ ضَحِكَ فَقُلْتُ لَهُ: مِنْ أَيْ شَيْءٍ ضَحِكْتَ يَا أَمِيرَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ؟ فَقَالَ: رأيتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ صَنَعَ كَمَا صَنَعْتُ ، ثُمَّ ضَحِكَ. فَقُلْتُ: مِمَّ ضَحِكْتَ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ؟ فَقَالَ: "يَعْجَبُ الرَّبُّ مِنْ عَبْدِهِ إِذَا قَالَ: رَبِّ اغْفِرْ لِي. وَيَقُولُ: عَلِمَ عَبْدِي أَنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ غَيْرِي".

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ أَبُو دَاوُدَ، وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ، وَالنَّسَائِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ أَبِي الْأَحْوَصِ -زَادَ النَّسَائِيُّ: وَمَنْصُورٌ-عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ السَّبِيعي، عَنْ عَلِيِّ بْنِ رَبِيعَةَ الْأَسَدِيِّ الْوَالِبِيِّ، بِهِ . وَقَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ.

وَقَدْ قَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ مَهْدِيّ، عَنْ شُعْبَةَ: قُلْتُ لِأَبِي إِسْحَاقَ السَّبِيعي: مِمَّنْ سَمِعْتَ هَذَا الْحَدِيثَ؟ قَالَ: مِنْ يُونُسَ بْنِ خَبَّابٍ. فَلَقِيتُ يُونُسَ بْنَ خَبَّابٍ فَقُلْتُ: مِمَّنْ سَمِعْتَهُ؟ فَقَالَ: مِنْ رَجُلٍ سَمِعَهُ مِنْ عَلِيِّ بْنِ رَبِيعَةَ. وَرَوَاهُ بَعْضُهُمْ عَنْ يُونُسَ بْنِ خَبَّابٍ، عَنْ شَقِيقِ بْنِ عُقْبَةَ الْأَسَدِيِّ، عَنْ عَلِيِّ بْنِ رَبِيعَةَ الْوَالِبِيِّ، بِهِ. 

حَدِيثُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا:

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو الْمُغِيرَةِ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو بَكْرِ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ، عَنْ عَلِيِّ بْنِ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَبَّاسٍ؛ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ أَرْدَفَهُ عَلَى دَابَّتِهِ، فَلَمَّا اسْتَوَى عَلَيْهَا كَبَّرَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ ثَلَاثًا، وَحَمِدَ ثَلَاثًا، وَهَلَّلَ اللَّهَ وَاحِدَةً. ثُمَّ اسْتَلْقَى عَلَيْهِ فَضَحِكَ، ثُمَّ أَقْبَلَ عَلَيْهِ فَقَالَ: "مَا مِنِ امْرِئٍ مُسْلِمٍ يَرْكَبُ دَابَّةً فَيَصْنَعُ كَمَا صَنَعْتُ، إِلَّا أَقْبَلَ اللَّهُ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، عَلَيْهِ، فَضَحِكَ إِلَيْهِ كَمَا ضَحِكْتُ إِلَيْكَ". تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ أَحْمَدُ. 

حَدِيثُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عُمَرَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا:

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو كَامِلٍ حَدَّثَنَا حَمَّادٌ بْنُ سَلَمَةَ، عَنْ أَبِي الزُّبَيْرِ، عَنْ عَلِيِّ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الْبَارِقِيِّ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عُمَرَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عنهما؛ أَنَّ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ كَانَ إِذَا رَكِبَ رَاحِلَتَهُ كَبَّرَ ثَلَاثًا، ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿سُبْحَانَ الَّذِي سَخَّرَ لَنَا هَذَا وَمَا كُنَّا لَهُ مُقْرِنِينَ. وَإِنَّا إِلَى رَبِّنَا لَمُنْقَلِبُونَ﴾ . ثُمَّ يَقُولُ: "اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ فِي سَفَرِي هَذَا الْبِرَّ وَالتَّقْوَى، وَمِنَ الْعَمَلِ مَا تَرْضَى. اللَّهُمَّ، هَوِّنْ عَلَيْنَا السَّفَرَ وَاطْوِ لَنَا الْبَعِيدَ. اللَّهُمَّ، أَنْتَ الصَّاحِبُ فِي السَّفَرِ، وَالْخَلِيفَةُ فِي الْأَهْلِ. اللَّهُمَّ، اصْحَبْنَا فِي سَفَرِنَا، وَاخْلُفْنَا فِي أَهْلِنَا". وَكَانَ إِذَا رَجَعَ إِلَى أَهْلِهِ قَالَ: "آيِبُونَ تَائِبُونَ إِنْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ، عَابِدُونَ، لِرَبِّنَا حَامِدُونَ".

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ وَأَبُو دَاوُدَ وَالنَّسَائِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ ابْنِ جُرَيْجٍ، وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ مِنْ حَدِيثِ حَمَّادِ بْنِ سَلَمَةَ، كِلَاهُمَا عَنْ أَبِي الزُّبَيْرِ، بِهِ. 

حَدِيثٌ آخَرُ:

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عُبَيْدٍ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ إبراهيم، عن عَمْرِو بْنِ الْحَكَمِ بْنِ ثَوْبَانَ ، عَنْ أَبِي لَاسٍ الْخُزَاعِيِّ قَالَ: حَمَلَنَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ عَلَى إِبِلٍ مِنْ إِبِلِ الصَّدَقَةِ إِلَى الْحَجِّ. فَقُلْنَا: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، مَا نَرَى أَنْ تَحَمِلَنَا هَذِهِ! فَقَالَ: "مَا مِنْ بَعِيرٍ إِلَّا فِي ذُرْوَتِهِ شَيْطَانٌ، فَاذْكُرُوا اسْمَ اللَّهِ عَلَيْهَا إِذَا رَكِبْتُمُوهَا كَمَا آمُرُكُمْ ، ثُمَّ امْتَهِنُوهَا لِأَنْفُسِكُمْ، فَإِنَّمَا يَحْمِلُ اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ".

## 43:15-20 — verses 15-20 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, informing about the polytheists concerning what they fabricated and falsely claimed in assigning some of their livestock to their false gods and some to Allah, as Allah mentioned about them in Surat al-An'am, in His saying: {And they assign to Allah a share of what He has produced of crops and livestock, and they say, "This is for Allah," by their claim, "and this is for our partners." But what is for their partners does not reach Allah, while what is for Allah reaches their partners. Evil is that which they judge} [al-An'am: 136]. Likewise they assigned to Him, of the two categories of daughters and sons, the lesser and more inferior of the two, which is daughters, as the Almighty said: {Is the male for you and for Him the female? That, then, is an unjust division} [al-Najm: 21-22]. And He says here: {And they assign to Him a portion of His servants. Indeed, man is clearly ungrateful}

Then He says: {Or has He taken, from what He creates, daughters, and He has chosen you for sons?} This is a rejection of them, the utmost rejection. Then He mentions the completion of the rejection, saying: {And when one of them is given good news of that which he attributes to the Most Merciful, his face becomes dark and he suppresses grief}. That is: when one of them is given the news of what they have assigned to Allah, namely daughters, he shows the utmost aversion to it, gloom overtakes him from the badness of what he has been told, and he hides himself from the people out of shame from it. The Almighty is saying: how then can you show aversion to this yourselves, while you attribute it to Allah, Mighty and Majestic?

Then He says: {Then is one brought up in ornaments while being, in dispute, unclear?} That is: the woman is deficient, and her deficiency is completed by the wearing of ornaments from the time she is a child, and when she disputes she has no clear expression, but is rather incapable and inarticulate. Is one who is like this to be attributed to the presence of Allah, Mighty and Majestic?! For the female is deficient both outwardly and inwardly, in form and in meaning. So the deficiency of her outward appearance and form is completed by the wearing of ornaments and the like, in order to compensate for what deficiency is in her, as one of the Arab poets said:

"Ornament is nothing but an adornment for a deficiency, completing beauty when beauty falls short... but when beauty is already abundant, as is your beauty, it has no need to be embellished..."

As for the deficiency of her inner meaning, she is weak, incapable of gaining victory when victory is sought, having no clear expression and no resolve, as one of the Arabs said when given the good news of a daughter: "She is not a good child: her support is by weeping, and her devotion is theft."

His saying: {And they make the angels, who are servants of the Most Merciful, females} that is, they held this belief about them. The Almighty then rejects their statement, saying: {Did they witness their creation?} That is, did they witness it, when Allah created them as females? {Their testimony will be recorded} that is, regarding that, {and they will be questioned} about that on the Day of Resurrection. This is a severe threat and a firm warning.

{And they say, "If the Most Merciful had willed, we would not have worshipped them"} that is: if Allah had wanted, He would have come between us and the worship of these idols, which are in the forms of the angels who are the daughters of Allah, for He knows of this and He confirms us in it. They thus combined many types of error together:

The first: their assigning to Allah a child, exalted, sanctified, and far removed is He above that, greatly exalted.

The second: their claim that He chose daughters over sons, so they made the angels, who are servants of the Most Merciful, females.

The third: their worship of them despite all of this, without any proof, evidence, or permission from Allah, Mighty and Majestic, but rather by mere opinion and desire, and blind imitation of forebears, elders, and fathers, and floundering in the utter ignorance of the Jahiliyyah.

The fourth: their argument based on decree (qadar) for this [while proof can only be established by revealed law], and they have shown great ignorance in this argument, for the Almighty has rejected this from them in the strongest terms. Ever since He sent the messengers and revealed the books, He has commanded worship of Him alone without partner, and forbidden the worship of anything besides Him. He [the Almighty] said: {And We certainly sent into every nation a messenger, saying, "Worship Allah and avoid the Taghut (false god)." And among them were those whom Allah guided, and among them were those upon whom error was justly decreed. So travel through the earth and observe how was the end of the deniers} [al-Nahl: 36]. And the Almighty said: {And ask those We sent before you of Our messengers, "Did We appoint besides the Most Merciful deities to be worshipped?"} [al-Zukhruf: 45].

And He said in this verse, after mentioning this argument of theirs: {They have no knowledge of that} that is, of the truth of what they said and argued, {they only conjecture} that is, they lie and fabricate speech.

Mujahid said regarding His saying: {They have no knowledge of that; they only conjecture}, that is: they do not know Allah's power over that.

## 43:21-25 — verses 21-25 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, denouncing the polytheists for worshiping other than Allah without proof, evidence, or argument: {Or have We given them a Book before it} that is, before their shirk (associating partners with Allah), {so they are holding fast to it} that is, to what they are upon. Meaning: the matter is not so. This is like His saying: {Or have We sent down upon them an authority that speaks of what they used to associate as partners} [al-Rum: 35], meaning: that did not happen.

Then He said: {Rather they said, indeed we found our fathers upon a way, and indeed we are guided upon their footsteps} that is, they have no basis for what they are upon of shirk except imitation of their fathers and forefathers, in that they were upon a "ummah" (way/religion), and what is meant by it here is religion, as also in His saying: {Indeed this, your ummah, is one ummah} [al-Anbiya: 92].

Their saying: {and indeed we are upon their footsteps} that is, behind them, {guided}, is a claim made by them without evidence.

Then the Exalted made clear that this statement of theirs had already been preceded by their likes and counterparts among the past nations who rejected the messengers, their hearts resembling one another, so they said the like of their statement: {Thus, no messenger came to those before them except that they said, a magician or a madman. Did they enjoin it upon one another? Rather they are a transgressing people} [al-Dhariyat: 52, 53]. And likewise He said here: {And thus We did not send before you any warner into a city except that its affluent ones said, indeed we found our fathers upon a way, and indeed we are following in their footsteps}

Then the Exalted said: {Say}, that is, O Muhammad, to these polytheists: {Even if I brought you something more guided than what you found your fathers upon? They said, indeed we are disbelievers in what you have been sent with} that is, even if they knew and were certain of the soundness of what you brought them, they would not have submitted to it, due to their evil intent and their arrogant resistance to the truth and to its people.

Allah the Exalted said: {So We took vengeance upon them} that is, upon the nations that disbelieved, with various kinds of punishment, as He detailed in their stories, {so look how was the end of those who denied} that is, how they perished and were destroyed, and how Allah saved the believers.

## 43:26-35 — verses 26-35 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, informing about His servant, messenger, and intimate friend, the leader of the upright monotheists (hunafa) and the father of the prophets who were sent after him, the one to whom Quraysh trace their lineage and their creed: that he disavowed himself from his father and his people in their worship of idols, saying: {Indeed, I am free of what you worship, except Him who created me, for He will guide me. And he made it a word remaining among his descendants} That is, this word, which is the worship of God alone without partner, and the casting off of all idols besides Him, namely "there is no god but God." That is, He made it perpetual among his progeny, so that whoever among the descendants of Ibrahim (peace be upon him) whom God guided would follow him in it. {that they might return} That is, return to it.

Ikrimah, Mujahid, al-Dahhak, Qatadah, al-Suddi, and others said concerning His saying, {And he made it a word remaining among his descendants}: it means "there is no god but God," and there will always be among his descendants someone who says it. Something similar has been related from Ibn Abbas.

Ibn Zayd said: it is the word of Islam. This goes back to what the group said.

Then the Exalted says: {Rather, I gave enjoyment to these} meaning the polytheists, {and their fathers} that is, their lifespans were prolonged in their misguidance, {until the truth came to them and a clear messenger} that is, one clear in his message and his warning.

{And when the truth came to them, they said: this is magic, and indeed we disbelieve in it} That is, they acted with obstinate pride, opposed it, and rejected it outright and with contempt, out of disbelief, envy, and transgression. {And they said} as if objecting to what the Exalted and Sanctified had revealed: {Why was this Qur'an not sent down upon a great man from the two towns?} That is, why was this Qur'an not sent down upon a man great and important in their eyes, from the two towns? Meaning Makkah and al-Ta'if. This was said by Ibn Abbas, Ikrimah, Muhammad ibn Ka'b al-Qurazi, Qatadah, al-Suddi, and Ibn Zayd.

More than one of them mentioned that by this they meant al-Walid ibn al-Mughirah and Urwah ibn Mas'ud al-Thaqafi.

Malik reported from Zayd ibn Aslam, al-Dahhak, and al-Suddi: they meant al-Walid ibn al-Mughirah and Mas'ud ibn Amr al-Thaqafi.

From Mujahid: Umayr ibn Amr ibn Mas'ud al-Thaqafi. And also from him: that they meant al-Walid ibn al-Mughirah and Habib ibn Amr ibn Umayr al-Thaqafi.

From Mujahid: they meant Utbah ibn Rabi'ah in Makkah, and Ibn Abd Yalil in al-Ta'if.

Al-Suddi said: by this they meant al-Walid ibn al-Mughirah and Kinanah ibn Amr ibn Umayr al-Thaqafi.

The apparent meaning is that what they intended was simply a great man, from whichever of the two towns he might be.

God the Exalted says, responding to them regarding this objection: {Do they distribute the mercy of your Lord?} That is, the matter is not entrusted to them, but rather to God, mighty and majestic, and God knows best where to place His messages. He does not send them down except upon the purest of creation in heart and soul, the noblest of them in lineage, and the most untainted of them in origin.

Then the Exalted says, clarifying that He has apportioned differently among His creation in what He has given them of wealth, provisions, minds, understanding, and other apparent and hidden capacities: {We have apportioned among them their livelihood in the life of this world, and We have raised some of them above others in degrees}

Regarding His saying, {that some of them may take others in service}: it was said that its meaning is that some of them make use of others in labor, because of the need of this one for that one, and that one for this one. This was said by al-Suddi and others.

Qatadah and al-Dahhak said: that some of them may own others. This goes back to the first meaning.

Then He says: {And the mercy of your Lord is better than what they accumulate} That is, God's mercy toward His creation is better for them than what is in their hands of wealth and the enjoyments of worldly life.

Then the Exalted says: {And were it not that mankind would become one community} That is, were it not that many ignorant people would believe that Our giving them wealth is proof of Our love for those to whom We gave it, and thus they would unite in disbelief for the sake of wealth, this is the meaning given by Ibn Abbas, al-Hasan, Qatadah, al-Suddi, and others, {We would have made for those who disbelieve in the Most Merciful, for their houses, roofs of silver, and stairways [upon which they ascend]} that is, ladders and steps of silver, as said by Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, Qatadah, al-Suddi, Ibn Zayd, and others. {upon which they ascend} that is, they go up.

{And for their houses, doors} that is, locks upon their doors, {and couches upon which they recline} that is, all of that would be of silver, {and ornament} that is, and gold, as said by Ibn Abbas, Qatadah, al-Suddi, and Ibn Zayd. Then He says: {And all of that is only the enjoyment of the life of this world} That is, that is only from this perishing, vanishing world, insignificant in the sight of God the Exalted. That is, He hastens for them, on account of the good deeds they perform in this world, food and drink, so that when they arrive at the Hereafter they will have no good deed with God for which He might reward them, as has come in the sound hadith. And in another hadith it has come: "If this world weighed with God the wing of a mosquito, He would not have given a disbeliever a drink of water from it." Al-Baghawi related this with a chain through Zakariyya ibn Manzur, from Abu Hazim, from Sahl ibn Sa'd, from the Prophet ﷺ, and mentioned it. Al-Tabarani related it through the path of Zam'ah ibn Salih, from Abu Hazim, from Sahl ibn Sa'd, from the Prophet ﷺ: "If this world were equal to the wing of a mosquito, He would not have given a disbeliever anything from it."

Then He says: {And the Hereafter with your Lord is for the God-fearing} That is, it belongs to them exclusively, and no one shares it with them besides them. This is why, when Umar ibn al-Khattab said to the Messenger of God ﷺ, when he had climbed up to him in that upper chamber after he had sworn an oath (ila) against his wives, and Umar saw him lying on a rough mat that had left marks on his side, his eyes began to weep and he said: "O Messenger of God, this is how Khosrow and Caesar live in what they have, while you are the chosen one of God among His creation." The Messenger of God ﷺ had been reclining, so he sat up and said: "Are you in doubt, O son of al-Khattab?" Then he said: "Those are a people whose good things were hastened for them in the life of this world." And in another narration: "Are you not satisfied that this world be theirs and the Hereafter be ours?"

And in the two Sahihs and elsewhere: that the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Do not drink from vessels of gold and silver, and do not eat from their dishes, for they are theirs in this world and ours in the Hereafter." God the Exalted only granted them dominion in this world because of its insignificance, as al-Tirmidhi and Ibn Majah related, through the path of Abu Hazim, from Sahl ibn Sa'd, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "If this world weighed with God the wing of a mosquito, He would never have given a disbeliever a drink of water from it." Al-Tirmidhi said: hasan sahih (good and sound).

## 43:36-45 — verses 36-45 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: {And whoever turns a blind eye}, meaning: whoever feigns blindness, becomes heedless, and turns away, {from the remembrance of the Merciful}. Al-'asha in the eye means weakness of its sight, and what is meant here is the blindness of insight. {We appoint for him a devil who becomes his intimate companion}, like His saying: {And whoever opposes the Messenger after guidance has become clear to him, and follows a path other than that of the believers, We will let him continue in what he has chosen and land him in Hell, and evil it is as a destination} [al-Nisa: 115], and like His saying: {So when they deviated, Allah caused their hearts to deviate} [al-Saff: 5], and like His saying: {And We appointed for them companions who made attractive to them what was before them and what was behind them, and the word came to pass against them along with nations that had passed before them of jinn and men; indeed they were losers} [Fussilat: 25]. This is why He says here: {And indeed they, the devils, avert them from the path while they think that they are rightly guided. Until, when he comes to Us}, meaning: this person who neglected guidance, We appoint for him from the devils one who misleads him and guides him to the path of the Blazing Fire. So when he arrives before Allah on the Day of Resurrection, feeling burdened by the devil who was assigned to him, {he says: Oh, would that between me and you were the distance of the two easts! What an evil companion!} [meaning: what an evil companion you were to me in the worldly life]. Some read it as "hatta idha ja'ana" (until when they both come to Us), meaning the companion and the one accompanied.

Abd al-Razzaq said: Ma'mar informed us, from Sa'id al-Jurayri, who said: It has reached us that when the disbeliever is raised from his grave on the Day of Resurrection, a devil seizes his hand and does not part from him until Allah brings the two of them to the Fire. That is when he says: {Oh, would that between me and you were the distance of the two easts! What an evil companion!}

What is meant by "the two easts" here is what lies between the east and the west. It is used here by way of overriding predominance (taghlib), just as one says "the two moons" (for sun and moon), "the two Umars" (for Abu Bakr and Umar), "the two parents," and "the two difficulties." This was stated by Ibn Jarir and others.

[Since sharing in a calamity in this worldly life brings comfort to the one who shares it with another, as al-Khansa said weeping over her brother:

"Were it not for the many weepers around me over their dead, I would have killed myself...

They do not weep for one like my brother, but I console myself for him through shared mourning..."

Allah cut off this consolation between the people of the Fire, so that they find in it no mutual comfort, no consolation, and no relief.]

Then the Almighty says: {And it will not benefit you that Day, since you wronged, that you are sharing together in the punishment}, meaning: your gathering together in the Fire and your sharing together in the painful punishment will not avail you anything.

* *

His saying: {So can you cause the deaf to hear, or guide the blind and he who is in clear error?}, meaning: that is not up to you. Upon you is only the conveying of the message, and their guidance is not your responsibility. Rather, Allah guides whom He wills and leads astray whom He wills, and He is the just Judge in that.

Then He says: {And whether We take you away, We will surely take retribution upon them}, meaning: We will inevitably exact retribution against them and punish them, even if you were to depart. {Or We show you that which We promised them, indeed We have full power over them}, meaning: We are capable of this and of that. Allah did not take His Messenger's soul until He had gladdened his eye with what He did to his enemies, given him authority over their forelocks, and granted him possession of what their strongholds contained. This is the meaning of what al-Suddi said, and Ibn Jarir preferred it.

Ibn Jarir said: Ibn Abd al-A'la told us, Ibn Thawr told us, from Ma'mar, who said: Qatadah recited: {And whether We take you away, We will surely take retribution upon them}, and said: The Prophet ﷺ departed while the retribution remained. Allah did not show His Prophet ﷺ anything he disliked concerning his nation until he passed away, and there was never a prophet but that he saw punishment inflicted upon his nation, except your Prophet ﷺ. He said: And it has been mentioned to us that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was shown what would befall his nation after him, and he was never again seen laughing openly until Allah, Mighty and Majestic, took his soul.

Something similar was mentioned from the report of Sa'id ibn Abi Arubah, from Qatadah. Then Ibn Jarir also reported something similar from al-Hasan.

And in the hadith: "The stars are a safeguard for the sky; when the stars go, what was promised comes to the sky. And I am a safeguard for my companions; when I go, what was promised comes to my companions."

Then the Almighty says: {So hold fast to that which has been revealed to you; indeed you are upon a straight path}, meaning: hold to the Qur'an revealed upon your heart, for it is the truth, and what it guides to is the truth that leads to the straight path of Allah, which leads to the Gardens of Delight and everlasting, abiding good.

Then He says: {And indeed it is a remembrance for you and for your people}. It was said: its meaning is "a source of honor for you and for your people," as stated by Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, Qatadah, al-Suddi, and Ibn Zayd. Ibn Jarir preferred this and did not relate any other view.

Al-Baghawi cited here the hadith of al-Zuhri, from Muhammad ibn Jubayr ibn Mut'im, from Mu'awiyah, who said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "Indeed this matter belongs to the Quraysh; none disputes it with them but that Allah casts him down upon his face, so long as they uphold the religion." Reported by al-Bukhari.

And [it was said] its meaning is: that it is an honor for them in that it was revealed in their language, so they are the most capable of people in understanding it, and it is therefore fitting that they be the most upright of people in adhering to it and the most active in acting according to its requirements. And such indeed were the best of them, their choicest, from among the elite of the foremost early emigrants and those who resembled and followed them.

And it was said the meaning of {And indeed it is a remembrance for you and for your people} is: it is a reminder for you and for your people, and singling them out for mention does not exclude others besides them, like His saying: {We have certainly sent down to you a Book in which is your remembrance; then will you not use reason?} [al-Anbiya: 10], and like His saying: {And warn your closest kindred} [al-Shu'ara: 214].

{And you will be questioned}, meaning: about this Qur'an and how you were in acting upon it and responding to it.

* *

His saying: {And ask those We sent before you of Our messengers: did We appoint besides the Merciful gods to be worshipped?}, meaning: all of the messengers called to what you have called the people to, namely the worship of Allah alone without partner, and they forbade the worship of idols and rivals, as in His saying: {And We certainly sent into every nation a messenger, saying: Worship Allah and avoid false gods} [al-Nahl: 36]. Mujahid said: in the recitation of Abdullah ibn Mas'ud it reads: "And ask those to whom We sent Our messengers before you." Qatadah, al-Dahhak, and al-Suddi related this similarly from Ibn Mas'ud. This appears to be an explanatory gloss rather than an actual recitation, and Allah knows best.

Abd al-Rahman ibn Zayd ibn Aslam said: Ask them on the Night of the Ascension (al-Isra), for the prophets were gathered for him. Ibn Jarir preferred the first view, and Allah knows best.

وَالْمُرَادُ بِالْمَشْرِقَيْنِ هُنَا هُوَ مَا بَيْنَ الْمَشْرِقِ وَالْمَغْرِبِ. وَإِنَّمَا اسْتُعْمِلَ هَاهُنَا تَغْلِيبًا، كَمَا يُقَالُ الْقَمَرَانِ، وَالْعُمَرَانُ، وَالْأَبَوَانِ، [وَالْعُسْرَانِ] . قَالَهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ وَغَيْرُهُ.

[وَلَمَّا كَانَ الِاشْتِرَاكُ فِي الْمُصِيبَةِ فِي الدُّنْيَا يَحْصُلُ بِهِ تَسْلِيَةٌ لِمَنْ شَارَكَهُ فِي مُصِيبَتِهِ، كَمَا قَالَتِ الْخَنْسَاءُ تَبْكِي أَخَاهَا:

ولَوْلا كثرةُ الْبَاكِينَ حَوْلي ... عَلَى قَتْلاهم لقتلتُ نَفْسي ...

وَمَا يَبْكُون مثلَ أَخِي وَلَكِنْ ... أُسَلِّي النفسَ عَنْهُ بالتأسِّي ...

قَطَعَ اللَّهُ بِذَلِكَ بَيْنَ أَهْلِ النَّارِ، فلا يحصل لهم بذلك تأسي وَتَسْلِيَةٌ وَلَا تَخْفِيفٌ] . 

ثُمَّ قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَلَنْ يَنْفَعَكُمُ الْيَوْمَ إِذْ ظَلَمْتُمْ أَنَّكُمْ فِي الْعَذَابِ مُشْتَرِكُونَ﴾ أَيْ: لَا يُغْنِي عَنْكُمُ اجْتِمَاعُكُمْ فِي النَّارِ وَاشْتِرَاكُكُمْ فِي الْعَذَابِ الْأَلِيمِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿أَفَأَنْتَ تُسْمِعُ الصُّمَّ أَوْ تَهْدِي الْعُمْيَ وَمَنْ كَانَ فِي ضَلالٍ مُبِينٍ﴾ أَيْ: لَيْسَ ذَلِكَ إِلَيْكَ، إِنَّمَا عَلَيْكَ الْبَلَاغُ، وَلَيْسَ عَلَيْكَ هُدَاهُمْ، وَلَكِنَّ اللَّهَ يَهْدِي مَنْ يَشَاءُ، ويضل من يشاء، وهو الحكم العدل في ذلك.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿فَإِمَّا نَذْهَبَنَّ بِكَ فَإِنَّا مِنْهُمْ مُنْتَقِمُونَ﴾ أَيْ: لَا بُدَّ أَنْ نَنْتَقِمَ مِنْهُمْ وَنُعَاقِبَهُمْ، وَلَوْ ذَهَبْتَ أَنْتَ، ﴿أَوْ نُرِيَنَّكَ الَّذِي وَعَدْنَاهُمْ فَإِنَّا عَلَيْهِمْ مُقْتَدِرُونَ﴾ أَيْ: نَحْنُ قَادِرُونَ عَلَى هَذَا وَعَلَى هَذَا. وَلَمْ يَقْبِضِ اللَّهُ رَسُولَهُ حَتَّى أَقَرَّ عَيْنَهُ مِنْ أَعْدَائِهِ، وَحَكَّمَهُ فِي نَوَاصِيهِمْ، وَمَلَّكَهُ مَا تَضَمَّنَتْهُ صَيَاصِيهِمْ. هَذَا مَعْنَى قَوْلِ السُّدِّيُّ، وَاخْتَارَهُ ابْنِ جَرِيرٍ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ عَبْدِ الْأَعْلَى، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ ثَوْرٍ، عَنْ مَعْمَرٍ قَالَ: تَلَا قَتَادَةُ: ﴿فَإِمَّا نَذْهَبَنَّ بِكَ فَإِنَّا مِنْهُمْ مُنْتَقِمُونَ﴾ فَقَالَ: ذَهَبَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ وَبَقِيَتِ النِّقْمَةُ، وَلَمْ يُرِ اللَّهُ نَبِيَّهُ ﷺ فِي أُمَّتِهِ شَيْئًا يَكْرَهُهُ، حَتَّى مَضَى ، وَلَمْ يَكُنْ نَبِيٌّ قَطُّ إِلَّا وَرَأَى الْعُقُوبَةَ فِي أُمَّتِهِ، إِلَّا نَبِيَّكُمْ ﷺ. قَالَ: وذُكر لَنَا أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ أرِيَ مَا يُصِيبُ أُمَّتَهُ مِنْ بَعْدِهِ، فَمَا رُئِي ضَاحِكًا مُنْبَسِطًا حَتَّى قَبَضَهُ اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ. 

وَذُكِرَ مِنْ رِوَايَةِ سَعِيدِ بْنِ أَبِي عَرُوبَةَ، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ نَحْوَهُ. ثُمَّ رَوَى ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ عَنِ الْحَسَنِ نَحْوَ ذَلِكَ أَيْضًا.

وَفِي الْحَدِيثِ: "النُّجُومُ أَمَنَةٌ لِلسَّمَاءِ، فَإِذَا ذَهَبَتِ النُّجُومُ أَتَى السَّمَاءَ مَا تُوعَدُ، وَأَنَا أمَنَة لِأَصْحَابِي، فَإِذَا ذَهَبْتُ أَتَى أَصْحَابِي مَا يُوعَدُونَ". 

ثُمَّ قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَاسْتَمْسِكْ بِالَّذِي أُوحِيَ إِلَيْكَ إِنَّكَ عَلَى صِرَاطٍ مُسْتَقِيمٍ﴾ أَيْ: خُذْ بِالْقُرْآنِ الْمُنَزَّلِ عَلَى قَلْبِكَ، فَإِنَّهُ هُوَ الْحَقُّ، وَمَا يَهْدِي إِلَيْهِ هُوَ الْحَقُّ الْمُفْضِي إِلَى صِرَاطِ اللَّهِ الْمُسْتَقِيمِ، الْمُوصِلِ إِلَى جَنَّاتِ النَّعِيمِ، وَالْخَيْرِ الدَّائِمِ الْمُقِيمِ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿وَإِنَّهُ لَذِكْرٌ لَكَ وَلِقَوْمِكَ﴾ قِيلَ: مَعْنَاهُ لَشَرَفٌ لَكَ وَلِقَوْمِكَ، قَالَهُ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ، وَابْنُ زَيْدٍ. وَاخْتَارَهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، وَلَمْ يَحْكِ سِوَاهُ.

وَأَوْرَدَ الْبَغَوِيُّ هَاهُنَا حَدِيثَ الزُّهْرِيِّ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرِ بْنِ مُطْعِمٍ، عَنْ مُعَاوِيَةَ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "إِنَّ هَذَا الْأَمْرَ فِي قُرَيْشٍ لَا يُنَازِعُهُمْ فِيهِ أَحَدٌ إِلَّا أكَبَّه اللَّهُ عَلَى وَجْهِهِ مَا أَقَامُوا الدِّينَ". رَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ. 

وَ [قِيلَ] مَعْنَاهُ: أَنَّهُ شَرَفٌ لَهُمْ مِنْ حَيْثُ إِنَّهُ أُنْزِلَ بَلُغَتِهِمْ، فَهُمْ أَفْهَمُ النَّاسِ لَهُ، فَيَنْبَغِي أَنْ يَكُونُوا أَقْوَمَ النَّاسِ بِهِ وَأَعْمَلَهُمْ بِمُقْتَضَاهُ، وَهَكَذَا كَانَ خِيَارُهُمْ وَصَفْوَتُهُمْ مِنَ الخُلَّص مِنَ الْمُهَاجِرِينَ السَّابِقِينَ الْأَوَّلِينَ، وَمَنْ شَابَهَهُمْ وَتَابَعَهُمْ.

وَقِيلَ: مَعْنَاهُ: ﴿وَإِنَّهُ لَذِكْرٌ لَكَ وَلِقَوْمِكَ﴾ أَيْ: لَتَذْكِيرٌ لَكَ وَلِقَوْمِكَ، وَتَخْصِيصُهُمْ بِالذِّكْرِ لَا يَنْفِي مَنْ سِوَاهُمْ، كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿لَقَدْ أَنزلْنَا إِلَيْكُمْ كِتَابًا فِيهِ ذِكْرُكُمْ أَفَلا تَعْقِلُونَ﴾ [الأنبياء: ١٠] ، وكقوله: ﴿وَأَنْذِرْ عَشِيرَتَكَ الأقْرَبِينَ﴾ [الشعراء: ٢١٤] .

﴿وَسَوْفَ تُسْأَلُونَ﴾ أَيْ: عَنْ هَذَا الْقُرْآنِ وَكَيْفَ كُنْتُمْ فِي الْعَمَلِ بِهِ وَالِاسْتِجَابَةِ لَهُ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَاسْأَلْ مَنْ أَرْسَلْنَا مِنْ قَبْلِكَ مِنْ رُسُلِنَا أَجَعَلْنَا مِنْ دُونِ الرَّحْمَنِ آلِهَةً يُعْبَدُونَ﴾ ؟ أَيْ: جَمِيعُ الرُّسُلِ دَعَوْا إِلَى مَا دَعَوْتَ النَّاسَ إِلَيْهِ مِنْ عِبَادَةِ اللَّهِ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ، وَنَهَوْا عَنْ عِبَادَةِ الْأَصْنَامِ وَالْأَنْدَادِ، كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَلَقَدْ بَعَثْنَا فِي كُلِّ أُمَّةٍ رَسُولا أَنِ اُعْبُدُوا اللَّهَ وَاجْتَنِبُوا الطَّاغُوتَ﴾ [النَّحْلِ: ٣٦] . قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: فِي قِرَاءَةِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ مَسْعُودٍ: "وَاسْأَلِ الَّذِينَ أَرْسَلْنَا إِلَيْهِمْ قَبْلَكَ رُسُلَنَا". وَهَكَذَا حَكَاهُ قَتَادَةُ وَالضَّحَّاكُ وَالسُّدِّيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ. وَهَذَا كَأَنَّهُ تَفْسِيرٌ لَا تِلَاوَةٌ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

وَقَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ: وَاسْأَلْهُمْ لَيْلَةَ الْإِسْرَاءِ، فَإِنَّ الْأَنْبِيَاءَ جُمِعوا لَهُ. وَاخْتَارَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ الْأَوَّلَ، [وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ] .

## 43:46-50 — verses 46-50 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, informing about His servant and messenger Moses, peace be upon him, that He sent him to Pharaoh and his court of commanders, ministers, leaders, followers and subjects, from the Copts and the Children of Israel, calling them to worship God alone with no partner, and forbidding them from worshipping anything besides Him. He sent with him great signs, such as his hand and his staff, and what He sent along with him of the flood, the locusts, the lice, the frogs, and the blood, and the diminishment of crops, lives, and fruits. Despite all of this, they were too arrogant to follow these signs and submit to them; they denied them, mocked them, and laughed at the one who brought them.

{And We show them no sign except that it is greater than its sister sign} Yet despite this, they did not turn back from their error and misguidance, their ignorance and corruption. Every time a sign of these signs came to them, they would plead humbly to Moses, peace be upon him, and would soften their words toward him, saying: {O sorcerer}, meaning: O learned one, as Ibn Jarir said. The learned men of their time were the sorcerers, and sorcery was not something blameworthy among them in their time. So this was not said as a way of belittling him, because their state was one of desperate need for him, which does not fit with insult; rather it was, in their reckoning, a term of exaltation. Every single time, they would promise Moses, peace be upon him, that if he removed this affliction from them they would believe and would send the Children of Israel with him. And every single time they would break the pledge they had made. This is like His statement, the Exalted:

{So We sent upon them the flood, the locusts, the lice, the frogs, and the blood as detailed signs, but they were too arrogant and were a people of criminals. And when the punishment fell upon them, they said, "O Moses, call upon your Lord for us by what He has promised you. If you remove the punishment from us, we will surely believe you and will surely send with you the Children of Israel." But when We removed the punishment from them until a term which they were to reach, behold, they broke their word} [al-A'raf: 133-135].

## 43:51-56 — verses 51-56 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, informing about Pharaoh, his rebellion, his tyranny, his disbelief, and his obstinacy: that he gathered his people and called out among them, boasting and taking pride in the dominion of Egypt and his control over it: {"Is not the kingdom of Egypt mine, and these rivers flowing beneath me?"} Qatadah said: they had gardens and rivers of water. {"Do you not then see?"} That is: do you not see the greatness and dominion I am in, meaning: while Moses and his followers are poor and weak. This is like His saying: {"So he gathered [his people] and called out. He said: I am your lord, the most high. So Allah seized him with the punishment of the last and the first"} [al-Nazi'at: 23-25].

Regarding His saying: {"Or am I better than this one who is despicable?"} al-Suddi said: he means, rather I am better than this one who is despicable. Some grammarians of Basra likewise said that "am" here carries the meaning of "rather." This is supported by what al-Farra' related from some of the reciters, that one of them recited it as: "Am I not better than this one who is despicable?" Ibn Jarir said: if this reading were established, its meaning would be sound and clear, but it goes against the reading of the major cities, for they recited it: {"Or am I better than this one who is despicable?"} as an interrogative.

I say: in any case, Pharaoh, may the curse be upon him, meant that he was better than Moses (peace be upon him). He lied in this statement of his with a plain and clear lie, so upon him be the successive curses of Allah until the Day of Resurrection.

By his saying {despicable} he meant, as Sufyan said: contemptible. Qatadah and al-Suddi said: it means weak. Ibn Jarir said: it means he has no dominion, no authority, and no wealth.

{"And he can hardly make himself clear"} means: he can hardly express his speech clearly, for he is inarticulate and tongue-tied.

Al-Suddi said: {"And he can hardly make himself clear"} means: he can hardly make himself understood. Qatadah, al-Suddi, and Ibn Jarir said it means: weak of tongue. Sufyan said: it means there was something in his tongue from the live coal, from when he put it in his mouth as a small child.

What Pharaoh, may Allah curse him, said here is a lie and a fabrication. What drove him to this was only disbelief and obstinacy, for he looked upon Moses (peace be upon him) with a disbelieving, wretched eye. Moses (peace be upon him) possessed such majesty, greatness, and radiance in his form that he dazzled the eyes of those endowed with sight and understanding. His saying {despicable} is a lie. Rather, it was Pharaoh himself who was despicable and contemptible in creation, character, and religion, while Moses (peace be upon him) was the noble, eminent, truthful, righteous, and rightly-guided one. His saying {"And he can hardly make himself clear"} is likewise a fabrication, for even though something had affected his tongue in his childhood because of that live coal, he had asked Allah, Mighty and Majestic, to loosen the knot from his tongue so that people would understand his speech, and Allah answered him in that, as in His saying: {"He said: You have been granted your request, O Moses"} [Ta Ha: 26]. Even supposing that something remained which he had not asked to have removed, as al-Hasan al-Basri said, he had only asked for the removal of what prevented conveying the message and being understood. Natural traits which are not the doing of the servant are not something for which he is blamed or reproached. Pharaoh, though he understood and possessed reason, knew this well; he only intended to sway his subjects, for they were ignorant and foolish. This is like His saying: {"Why then have not bracelets of gold been bestowed upon him"}, meaning the ornaments placed on the hands, as Ibn Abbas, Qatadah, and others said, {"or angels come with him in conjunction"}, meaning surrounding him in service to him and bearing witness to his truthfulness. He looked to outward appearance and did not understand the inner, spiritual reality which is more manifest than what he looked to, had he but known. This is why the Exalted said: {"So he belittled his people and they obeyed him"}, meaning he made light of their reason, calling them to misguidance, and they responded to him, {"Indeed they were a wicked people."}

Allah the Exalted says: {"So when they angered Us, We took vengeance upon them and drowned them all together."} Ali ibn Abi Talhah reported from Ibn Abbas: {angered Us} means enraged Us.

Al-Dahhak reported from him: made Us angry. Ibn Abbas said likewise, as did Mujahid, Ikrimah, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, Muhammad ibn Ka'b al-Qurazi, Qatadah, al-Suddi, and other exegetes.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] from Uqbah ibn Amir, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "When you see Allah, Mighty and Majestic, giving a servant whatever he wishes while he persists in his acts of disobedience, that is only a gradual seizing (istidraj) from Him." Then he recited: {"So when they angered Us, We took vengeance upon them"}.

[chain] Tariq ibn Shihab said: I was with Abdullah when sudden death was mentioned in his presence, and he said: it is a relief for the believer and a source of grief for the disbeliever. Then he recited: {"So when they angered Us, We took vengeance upon them"}.

Umar ibn Abd al-Aziz, may Allah be pleased with him, said: I found that vengeance comes together with heedlessness, meaning His saying: {"So when they angered Us, We took vengeance upon them and drowned them all together."}

Regarding His saying: {"So We made them a precedent and an example for those who come after"}: Abu Mijlaz said: {a precedent} for the like of whoever acts as they acted.

He and Mujahid said: {and an example} means a lesson for those who come after them.

قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَلَمَّا آسَفُونَا انْتَقَمْنَا مِنْهُمْ فَأَغْرَقْنَاهُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ﴾ ، قَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿آسَفُونَا﴾ أَسْخَطُونَا.

وَقَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ، عَنْهُ: أَغْضَبُونَا. وَهَكَذَا قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ أَيْضًا، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَعِكْرِمَةُ، وَسَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ، وَمُحَمَّدُ بْنُ كَعْبٍ الْقُرَظِيُّ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ، وَغَيْرُهُمْ مِنَ الْمُفَسِّرِينَ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو عُبَيْدِ اللَّهِ ابْنِ أَخِي ابْنِ وَهْبٍ، حَدَّثَنَا عَمِّي، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ لَهِيعَةَ، عَنْ عُقْبَةَ بْنِ مُسْلِمٍ التُّجِيبِيِّ عَنْ عُقْبَةَ بْنِ عَامِرٍ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "إِذَا رَأَيْتَ اللَّهَ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ يُعْطِي الْعَبْدَ مَا شَاءَ، وَهُوَ مُقِيمٌ عَلَى مَعَاصِيهِ، فَإِنَّمَا ذَلِكَ اسْتِدْرَاجٌ مِنْهُ لَهُ" ثُمَّ تَلَا ﴿فَلَمَّا آسَفُونَا انْتَقَمْنَا مِنْهُمْ﴾ .

وَحَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى بْنُ عَبْدِ الْحَمِيدِ الحِمَّاني، حَدَّثَنَا قَيْسُ بْنُ الرَّبِيعِ، عَنْ قَيْسِ بْنِ مُسْلِمٍ ، عَنْ طَارِقِ بْنِ شِهَابٍ قَالَ: كُنْتُ عِنْدَ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ فَذُكِرَ عِنْدَهُ مَوْتُ الْفَجْأَةِ، فَقَالَ: تَخْفِيفٌ عَلَى الْمُؤْمِنِ، وَحَسْرَةٌ عَلَى الْكَافِرِ. ثُمَّ قَرَأَ: ﴿فَلَمَّا آسَفُونَا انْتَقَمْنَا مِنْهُمْ﴾ .

## 43:57-65 — verses 57-65 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, informing about the obstinacy of Quraysh in their unbelief and their deliberate insistence on stubbornness and disputation: {And when the son of Mary is presented as an example, behold, your people cry out in derision.} More than one of the scholars, from Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, Ikrimah, al-Dahhak, and al-Suddi, said: "yasiddun" means they laugh, that is, they were amazed by it.

Qatadah said: they show alarm and laugh. Ibrahim al-Nakha'i said: they turn away.

The reason for this was what Muhammad ibn Ishaq mentioned in the Sirah, where he said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ, as it has reached me, sat one day with al-Walid ibn al-Mughirah in the mosque. Then al-Nadr ibn al-Harith came and sat with them, and in the gathering were several men of Quraysh. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ spoke, and al-Nadr ibn al-Harith opposed him, so the Messenger of Allah ﷺ spoke to him until he silenced him. Then he recited to him and to them: {Indeed, you and what you worship besides Allah are the firewood of Hell; you will come to it} [al-Anbiya: 98] and the following verses. Then the Messenger of Allah ﷺ got up, and Abdullah ibn al-Zib'ara al-Tamimi came forward and sat down. Al-Walid ibn al-Mughirah said to him: "By Allah, al-Nadr ibn al-Harith could not stand up to the son of Abd al-Muttalib, whether he tried or not. Muhammad has claimed that we and what we worship of these gods of ours are the firewood of Hell." Abdullah ibn al-Zib'ara said: "By Allah, had I found him I would have refuted him. Ask Muhammad: is everything that is worshipped besides Allah in Hell along with whoever worshipped it? For we worship the angels, the Jews worship Ezra, and the Christians worship the Messiah, Jesus son of Mary." Al-Walid and those present in the gathering were amazed at what Abdullah ibn al-Zib'ara said, and they thought that he had produced an argument and won the dispute. This was mentioned to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, and he said: "Everyone who loved to be worshipped besides Allah is with whoever worshipped him, for indeed they only worship Satan and whoever commanded them to worship him." Then Allah, Mighty and Majestic, revealed: {Indeed, those for whom the best has preceded from Us, those will be removed far from it} [al-Anbiya: 101], meaning Jesus, Ezra, and whoever was worshipped along with them from among the scholars and monks who lived in obedience to Allah, Mighty and Majestic, but whom the people of misguidance who worshipped them took as lords besides Allah. And it was revealed concerning what they mention, that they worship the angels and claim they are the daughters of Allah: {And they say, "The Most Merciful has taken a son." Exalted is He! Rather, they are honored servants} and the following verses [al-Anbiya: 26]. And it was revealed concerning what is mentioned of the matter of Jesus and that he is worshipped besides Allah, and the amazement of al-Walid and those present at his argument and disputation: {And when the son of Mary is presented as an example, behold, your people cry out in derision}, that is, they turn away from what you commanded them regarding this, from his statement. Then He mentioned Jesus and said: {He was not but a servant upon whom We bestowed favor, and We made him an example for the Children of Israel. And if We willed, We could have made angels from among you succeeding [one another] on the earth. And indeed, he is [a sign for] knowledge of the Hour}, meaning: what I placed at his hands of signs, of raising the dead and healing the sick, that alone suffices as proof of knowledge of the Hour. He says: {so be not in doubt of it, and follow Me. This is a straight path.}

Ibn Jarir mentioned, from the report of al-Awfi, from Ibn Abbas, regarding His statement: {And when the son of Mary is presented as an example, behold, your people cry out in derision}, he said: this means Quraysh, when it was said to them: {Indeed, you and what you worship besides Allah are the firewood of Hell; you will come to it} [al-Anbiya: 98] to the end of the verses, Quraysh said to him: "What about the son of Mary?" He said: "That one is a servant of Allah and His messenger." They said: "By Allah, this man only wants us to take him as a lord, just as the Christians took Jesus son of Mary as a lord." So Allah the Exalted said: {They did not present it to you except as argument. Rather, they are a quarrelsome people.}

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Hashim ibn al-Qasim, Shayban, Asim ibn Abi al-Najud, Abu Razin, Abu Yahya, freedman of Ibn Uqayl al-Ansari, said: Ibn Abbas said: "I know of a verse from the Qur'an that no man has ever asked me about. I do not know whether people already know it and so have not asked about it, or whether they have not noticed it and so have not asked about it." He said: then he began telling us something, and when he got up we blamed ourselves for not having asked him about it. I said: "I will take charge of it tomorrow when he comes." So when the next day came, I said: "O Ibn Abbas, yesterday you mentioned that there is a verse of the Qur'an that no man has ever asked you about, and you do not know whether people already know it or have not noticed it." I said: "Tell me about it and about the verses you recited before it." He said: "Yes. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said to Quraysh: 'O assembly of Quraysh, there is no one worshipped besides Allah in whom there is any good.' Quraysh knew that the Christians worship Jesus son of Mary, and wondered what he would say about Muhammad. So they said: 'O Muhammad, do you not claim that Jesus was a prophet and a righteous servant among the servants of Allah? If you are truthful, then their gods are as you describe.'" He said: so Allah revealed: {And when the son of Mary is presented as an example, behold, your people cry out in derision}. I said: "What does 'yasiddun' mean?" He said: "They laugh." {And indeed, he is [a sign for] knowledge of the Hour}, he said: this refers to the descent of Jesus son of Mary before the Resurrection.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Muhammad ibn Ya'qub al-Dimashqi, Adam, Shayban, Asim ibn Abi al-Najud, Abu Ahmad, freedman of the Ansar, from Ibn Abbas, said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "O assembly of Quraysh, there is no one worshipped besides Allah in whom there is any good." They said to him: "Do you not claim that Jesus was a prophet and a righteous servant among the servants of Allah? Yet he was worshipped besides Allah." So Allah, Mighty and Majestic, revealed: {And when the son of Mary is presented as an example, behold, your people cry out in derision}.

Mujahid said regarding His statement {And when the son of Mary is presented as an example, behold, your people cry out in derision}: Quraysh said: "Muhammad only wants us to worship him as the people of Jesus worshipped Jesus." Qatadah said something similar to this.

Concerning His statement: {And they say, "Are our gods better, or is he?"}, Qatadah said: they mean, "Our gods are better than he is." Qatadah also said: Ibn Mas'ud recited it: "And they say, 'Are our gods better, or is this one?'" meaning Muhammad ﷺ.

Concerning His statement: {They did not present it to you except as argument}, that is, as mere disputation, while they knew that it did not actually apply to the verse, because that verse concerns things without intellect, namely His statement: {Indeed, you and what you worship besides Allah are the firewood of Hell} [al-Anbiya: 98]. Moreover, it was addressing Quraysh, and they only worshipped idols and rivals, they did not worship the Messiah so as to bring him into the matter. It is therefore established that their statement was mere disputation on their part, and they themselves did not believe it to be sound.

Imam Ahmad, may Allah have mercy on him, said: [chain] Ibn Numayr, Hajjaj ibn Dinar, from Abu Ghalib, from Abu Umamah, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "No people went astray after having been upon guidance, except that they were given disputation." Then he recited this verse: {They did not present it to you except as argument. Rather, they are a quarrelsome people.}

This was narrated by al-Tirmidhi, Ibn Majah, and Ibn Jarir, from the hadith of Hajjaj ibn Dinar, with this chain. Al-Tirmidhi then said: "Hasan sahih; we do not know it except from his hadith." Thus he said.

It has also been narrated from another route from Abu Umamah with an addition. Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Humayd ibn Ayyash al-Ramli, Mu'ammal, Hammad, Ibn Makhzum, from al-Qasim Abu Abd al-Rahman al-Shami, from Abu Umamah, Hammad said: "I do not know whether he attributed it [to the Prophet] or not," he said: "No nation ever went astray after its prophet except that the first sign of its going astray was denial of divine decree (al-qadar), and no nation ever went astray after its prophet except that they were given disputation." Then he recited: {They did not present it to you except as argument. Rather, they are a quarrelsome people.}

Ibn Jarir also said: [chain] Abu Kurayb, Ahmad ibn Abd al-Rahman, from Abbad ibn Abbad, from Ja'far, from al-Qasim, from Abu Umamah, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ came out to the people while they were disputing about the Qur'an, and he became extremely angry, as if vinegar had been poured upon his face. Then he said: "Do not strike parts of the Book of Allah against one another, for no people ever went astray except that they were given disputation." Then he recited: {They did not present it to you except as argument. Rather, they are a quarrelsome people.}

Concerning His statement: {He was not but a servant upon whom We bestowed favor}, meaning Jesus, peace be upon him: he was nothing but a servant among the servants of Allah, upon whom Allah bestowed favor through prophethood and messengership. {And We made him an example for the Children of Israel}, that is, a proof, an evidence, and a demonstration of Our power to do whatever We will.

His statement, {And if We willed, We could have made among you} that is, in place of you {angels succeeding one another on the earth}: al-Suddi said, they would succeed you upon it. Ibn Abbas and Qatadah said, they would succeed one another, just as you succeed one another. This latter statement necessarily implies the first. Mujahid said: they would populate the earth in your place.

His statement, {And indeed, he is knowledge of the Hour}: the interpretation of Ibn Ishaq was mentioned earlier, that what is meant by this is what Jesus (peace be upon him) was sent with, of raising the dead, healing the one born blind and the leper, and other such ailments. There is question about this. Further still from correctness is what Qatadah related from al-Hasan al-Basri and Sa'id ibn Jubayr, namely that the pronoun in {And indeed, he} refers back to the Qur'an. Rather, the correct view is that it refers back to Jesus (peace be upon him), for the context is speaking of him. Then what is meant by that is his descent before the Day of Resurrection, as the Blessed and Exalted said: {And there is none among the People of the Book but that he will believe in him before his death} that is, before the death of Jesus, upon him be prayer and peace, then {and on the Day of Resurrection he will be a witness against them} [al-Nisa: 159]. This meaning is supported by the other recitation: "wa innahu la'alamun lil-sa'ah" (and indeed he is a sign for the Hour), that is, a mark and a proof of the occurrence of the Hour. Mujahid said: {And indeed, he is knowledge of the Hour} means, a sign of the Hour is the emergence of Jesus son of Mary before the Day of Resurrection. This has likewise been related from Abu Hurayrah (may Allah be pleased with him), Ibn Abbas, Abu al-Aliyah, Abu Malik, Ikrimah, al-Hasan, Qatadah, al-Dahhak, and others.

The hadiths from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ are numerous and mutually corroborating (tawatur) that he informed of the descent of Jesus son of Mary (peace be upon him) before the Day of Resurrection, as a just leader (Imam) and a fair judge.

His statement, {So do not doubt it} that is, do not be in doubt about it, for it is something that will occur and come to pass without fail, {and follow me} that is, in what I inform you of, {this is a straight path. And let not Satan avert you} that is, from following the truth, {indeed he is to you a clear enemy. And when Jesus came with the clear proofs he said, I have come to you with wisdom} that is, with prophethood, {and to make clear to you some of that over which you differ}.

Ibn Jarir said: meaning, of religious matters, not worldly ones. What he said here is good and sound. He then rejected the view of those who claimed that "some" here carries the meaning of "all," and who cited as evidence the verse of the poet Labid: "One who abandons places when he is not pleased with them, or until death seizes some of the souls." They interpreted this to mean that he intended all souls. Ibn Jarir said: rather he meant only his own soul, and expressed it using the word "some." What he said is plausible.

His statement, {So fear Allah} that is, [in] what He has commanded you, {and obey me}, in what I have brought to you, {indeed Allah, He is my Lord and your Lord, so worship Him. This is a straight path} that is, I and you are servants to Him, in need of Him, sharing together in worshipping Him alone, with no partner for Him. {This is a straight path} that is, this which I have brought to you is the straight path, and it is the worship of the Lord, Mighty and Majestic, alone.

His statement, {But the factions among them differed} that is, the groups differed and became sects concerning him. Among them are those who acknowledge that he is a servant of Allah and His messenger, which is the truth, and among them are those who claim that he is the son of Allah, and among them are those who say that he is Allah, exalted be Allah far above what they say in great transcendence. Therefore He said: {So woe to those who wronged, from the punishment of a painful day}.

لِرَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالَ: "كُلُّ مَنْ أَحَبَّ أَنْ يُعْبَدَ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ، فَهُوَ مَعَ مَنْ عَبَدَهُ، فَإِنَّهُمْ إِنَّمَا يَعْبُدُونَ الشَّيْطَانَ وَمَنْ أَمَرَهُمْ بِعِبَادَتِهِ" فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ: ﴿إِنَّ الَّذِينَ سَبَقَتْ لَهُمْ مِنَّا الْحُسْنَى أُولَئِكَ عَنْهَا مُبْعَدُونَ﴾ [الْأَنْبِيَاءِ: ١٠١] أَيْ: عِيسَى وَعُزَيْرٌ وَمَنْ عُبِدَ مَعَهُمَا مِنَ الْأَحْبَارِ وَالرُّهْبَانِ الَّذِينَ مَضَوْا عَلَى طَاعَةِ اللَّهِ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، فَاتَّخَذَهُمْ مَنْ يَعْبُدُهُمْ مِنْ أَهْلِ الضَّلَالَةِ أَرْبَابًا مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ. وَنَزَلَ فِيمَا يَذْكُرُونَ أَنَّهُمْ يَعْبُدُونَ الْمَلَائِكَةَ وَأَنَّهُمْ بَنَاتُ اللَّهِ: ﴿وَقَالُوا اتَّخَذَ الرَّحْمَنُ وَلَدًا سُبْحَانَهُ بَلْ عِبَادٌ مُكْرَمُونَ﴾ الآيات [الأنبياء: ٢٦] ، ونزل فِيمَا يُذْكَرُ مَنْ أَمْرِ عِيسَى وَأَنَّهُ يُعْبَدُ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ. وَعَجَبِ الْوَلِيدِ وَمَنْ حَضَرَهُ مِنْ حُجَّتِهِ وَخُصُومَتِهِ: ﴿وَلَمَّا ضُرِبَ ابْنُ مَرْيَمَ مَثَلا إِذَا قَوْمُكَ مِنْهُ يَصِدُّونَ﴾ أَيْ: يَصِدُّونَ عَنْ أَمْرِكَ بِذَلِكَ مِنْ قَوْلِهِ. ثُمَّ ذَكَرَ عِيسَى فَقَالَ: ﴿إِنْ هُوَ إِلا عَبْدٌ أَنْعَمْنَا عَلَيْهِ وَجَعَلْنَاهُ مَثَلا لِبَنِي إِسْرَائِيلَ. وَلَوْ نَشَاءُ لَجَعَلْنَا مِنْكُمْ مَلائِكَةً فِي الأرْضِ يَخْلُفُونَ. وَإِنَّهُ لَعِلْمٌ لِلسَّاعَةِ﴾ أَيْ: مَا وَضَعْتُ عَلَى يَدَيْهِ مِنَ الْآيَاتِ مِنْ إِحْيَاءِ الْمَوْتَى وَإِبْرَاءِ الْأَسْقَامِ، فَكَفَى بِهِ دَلِيلًا عَلَى عِلْمِ السَّاعَةِ، يَقُولُ: ﴿فَلا تَمْتَرُنَّ بِهَا وَاتَّبِعُونِ هَذَا صِرَاطٌ مُسْتَقِيمٌ﴾ .

وَذَكَرَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ مِنْ رِوَايَةِ العَوفي، عَنْ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَوْلَهُ: ﴿وَلَمَّا ضُرِبَ ابْنُ مَرْيَمَ مَثَلا إِذَا قَوْمُكَ مِنْهُ يَصِدُّونَ﴾ قَالَ: يَعْنِي قُرَيْشًا، لَمَّا قِيلَ لَهُمْ: ﴿إِنَّكُمْ وَمَا تَعْبُدُونَ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ حَصَبُ جَهَنَّمَ أَنْتُمْ لَهَا وَارِدُونَ﴾ [الْأَنْبِيَاءِ: ٩٨] إِلَى آخِرِ الْآيَاتِ، فَقَالَتْ لَهُ قُرَيْشٌ: فَمَا ابْنُ مَرْيَمَ؟ قَالَ: "ذَاكَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ وَرَسُولُهُ". فَقَالُوا: وَاللَّهِ مَا يُرِيدُ هَذَا إِلَّا أَنْ نَتَّخِذَهُ رَبًّا، كَمَا اتَّخَذَتِ النَّصَارَى عِيسَى ابْنَ مَرْيَمَ رَبًّا، فَقَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى ﴿مَا ضَرَبُوهُ لَكَ إِلا جَدَلا بَلْ هُمْ قَوْمٌ خَصِمُونَ﴾ .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا هَاشِمُ بْنُ الْقَاسِمِ، حَدَّثَنَا شَيْبَانَ، عَنْ عَاصِمِ بْنِ أَبِي النَّجُود، عَنْ أَبِي رَزِين، عَنْ أَبِي يَحْيَى -مَوْلَى ابْنِ عُقَيْلٍ الْأَنْصَارِيِّ-قَالَ: قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: لَقَدْ عَلِمْتُ آيَةً مِنَ الْقُرْآنِ مَا سَأَلَنِي عَنْهَا رَجُلٌ قَطُّ، فَمَا أَدْرِي أَعَلِمَهَا النَّاسُ فَلَمْ يَسْأَلُوا عَنْهَا، أَمْ لَمْ يَفْطِنُوا لَهَا فَيَسْأَلُوا عَنْهَا. قَالَ: ثُمَّ طَفِقَ يُحَدِّثُنَا، فَلَمَّا قَامَ تَلَاوَمْنَا أَلَّا نَكُونَ سَأَلْنَاهُ عَنْهَا. فَقُلْتُ: أَنَا لَهَا إِذَا رَاحَ غَدًا. فَلَمَّا رَاحَ الْغَدُ قُلْتُ: يَا ابْنَ عَبَّاسٍ، ذَكَرْتَ أَمْسِ أَنَّ آيَةً مِنَ الْقُرْآنِ لَمْ يَسْأَلْكَ عَنْهَا رَجُلٌ قَطُّ، فَلَا تَدْرِي أَعَلِمَهَا النَّاسُ أَمْ لَمْ يَفْطِنُوا لَهَا؟ فَقُلْتُ: أَخْبِرْنِي عَنْهَا وَعَنِ اللَّاتِي قَرَأْتَ قَبْلَهَا. قَالَ: نَعَمْ، أن رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم قَالَ لِقُرَيْشٍ: "يَا مَعْشَرَ قُرَيْشٍ، إِنَّهُ لَيْسَ أَحَدٌ يُعْبَدُ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ فِيهِ خَيْرٌ"، وَقَدْ عَلِمَتْ قُرَيْشٌ أَنَّ النَّصَارَى تَعْبُدُ عِيسَى ابْنَ مَرْيَمَ، وَمَا تَقُولُ فِي مُحَمَّدٍ، فَقَالُوا: يَا مُحَمَّدُ، أَلَسْتَ تَزْعُمُ أَنَّ عِيسَى كَانَ نَبِيًّا وَعَبْدًا مِنْ عِبَادِ اللَّهِ صَالِحًا، فَإِنْ كُنْتَ صَادِقًا كَانَ آلِهَتُهُمْ كَمَا تَقُولُونَ؟ قَالَ: فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ: ﴿وَلَمَّا ضُرِبَ ابْنُ مَرْيَمَ مَثَلا إِذَا قَوْمُكَ مِنْهُ يَصِدُّونَ﴾ . قُلْتُ: مَا يَصِدون؟ قَالَ: يَضْحَكُونَ، ﴿وَإِنَّهُ لَعِلْمٌ لِلسَّاعَةِ﴾ قَالَ: هُوَ خُرُوجُ عِيسَى ابْنِ مَرْيَمَ قَبْلَ الْقِيَامَةِ. 

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ يَعْقُوبَ الدِّمَشْقِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا آدَمُ، حَدَّثَنَا شَيْبَانَ، عَنْ عَاصِمِ بْنِ أَبِي النَّجُود، عَنْ أَبِي أَحْمَدَ مَوْلَى الْأَنْصَارِ ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "يَا مَعْشَرَ قُرَيْشٍ، إِنَّهُ لَيْسَ أَحَدٌ يُعْبَدُ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ فِيهِ خَيْرٌ". فَقَالُوا لَهُ: أَلَسْتَ تَزْعُمُ أَنَّ عِيسَى كَانَ نَبِيًّا وَعَبْدًا مِنْ عِبَادِ اللَّهِ صَالِحًا، فَقَدْ كَانَ يُعبد مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ؟ فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ: ﴿وَلَمَّا ضُرِبَ ابْنُ مَرْيَمَ مَثَلا إِذَا قَوْمُكَ مِنْهُ يَصِدُّونَ﴾ .

وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَلَمَّا ضُرِبَ ابْنُ مَرْيَمَ مَثَلا إِذَا قَوْمُكَ مِنْهُ يَصِدُّونَ﴾ : قَالَتْ قُرَيْشٌ: إِنَّمَا يُرِيدُ مُحَمَّدٌ أَنْ نَعْبُدَهُ كَمَا عَبَدَ قَوْمُ عِيسَى عِيسَى. وَنَحْوَ هَذَا قَالَ قَتَادَةُ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَقَالُوا أَآلِهَتُنَا خَيْرٌ أَمْ هُوَ﴾ : قَالَ قَتَادَةُ: يَقُولُونَ: آلِهَتُنَا خَيْرٌ مِنْهُ. وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: قَرَأَ ابْنُ مَسْعُودٍ: "وَقَالُوا أَآلِهَتُنَا خَيْرٌ أَمْ هَذَا"، يَعْنُونَ مُحَمَّدًا ﷺ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿مَا ضَرَبُوهُ لَكَ إِلا جَدَلا﴾ أَيْ: مِرَاءً، وَهُمْ يَعْلَمُونَ أَنَّهُ لَيْسَ بِوَارِدٍ عَلَى الْآيَةِ؛ لِأَنَّهَا لِمَا لَا يَعْقِلُ، وَهِيَ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنَّكُمْ وَمَا تَعْبُدُونَ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ حَصَبُ جَهَنَّمَ﴾ [الْأَنْبِيَاءِ: ٩٨] . ثُمَّ هِيَ خُطَّابٌ لِقُرَيْشٍ، وَهُمْ إِنَّمَا كَانُوا يَعْبُدُونَ الْأَصْنَامَ وَالْأَنْدَادَ، وَلَمْ يَكُونُوا يَعْبُدُونَ الْمَسِيحَ حَتَّى يُورِدُوهُ، فَتَعَيَّنَ أَنَّ مَقَالَتَهُمْ إِنَّمَا كَانَتْ جَدَلًا مِنْهُمْ، لَيْسُوا يَعْتَقِدُونَ صِحَّتَهَا.

وَقَدْ قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ، رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ نُمَيْرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا حَجَّاجُ بْنُ دِينَارٍ، عَنْ أَبِي غَالِبٍ، عَنْ أَبِي أُمَامَةَ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "مَا ضَلَّ قَوْمٌ بَعْدَ هُدًى كَانُوا عَلَيْهِ، إِلَّا أُورِثُوا الْجَدَلَ"، ثُمَّ تَلَا هَذِهِ الْآيَةَ: ﴿مَا ضَرَبُوهُ لَكَ إِلا جَدَلا بَلْ هُمْ قَوْمٌ خَصِمُونَ﴾ .

وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ، وَابْنُ مَاجَهْ، وَابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ حَجَّاجِ بْنِ دِينَارٍ، بِهِ . ثُمَّ قَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ لَا نَعْرِفُهُ إِلَّا مِنْ حَدِيثِهِ كَذَا قَالَ.

وَقَدْ رُوِيَ مِنْ وَجْهٍ آخَرَ عَنْ أَبِي أُمَامَةَ بِزِيَادَةٍ فَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا حُمَيْدُ بْنُ عَيَّاشٍ الرَّمْلِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا مؤمَّل، حَدَّثَنَا حَمَّادٌ، أَخْبَرَنَا ابْنُ مَخْزُومٍ، عَنِ الْقَاسِمِ أَبِي عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ الشَّامِيِّ، عَنْ أَبِي أُمَامَةَ -قَالَ حَمَّادٌ: لَا أَدْرِي رَفَعَهُ أَمْ لَا؟ -قَالَ: مَا ضَلَّتْ أُمَّةٌ بَعْدَ نَبِيِّهَا إِلَّا كَانَ أَوَّلَ ضَلَالِهَا التَّكْذِيبُ بِالْقَدَرِ، وَمَا ضَلَّتْ أُمَّةٌ بَعْدَ نَبِيِّهَا إِلَّا أُعْطُوا الْجَدَلَ، ثُمَّ قَرَأَ: ﴿مَا ضَرَبُوهُ لَكَ إِلا جَدَلا بَلْ هُمْ قَوْمٌ خَصِمُونَ﴾ 

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ أَيْضًا: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو كُرَيْبٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَحْمَدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ، عَنْ عَبَّادِ بْنِ عَبَّادٍ، عَنْ جَعْفَرٍ، عَنِ الْقَاسِمِ ، عَنْ أَبِي أُمَامَةَ قَالَ: إِنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ خَرَجَ عَلَى النَّاسِ وَهُمْ يَتَنَازَعُونَ فِي الْقُرْآنِ، فَغَضِبَ غَضَبًا شَدِيدًا حَتَّى كَأَنَّمَا صُبَّ عَلَى وَجْهِهِ الْخَلُّ، ثُمَّ قَالَ: "لَا تَضْرِبُوا كِتَابَ اللَّهِ بَعْضَهُ بِبَعْضٍ، فَإِنَّهُ مَا ضَلَّ قَوْمٌ قَطُّ إِلَّا أُوتُوا الْجَدَلَ"، ثُمَّ تَلَا ﴿مَا ضَرَبُوهُ لَكَ إِلا جَدَلا بَلْ هُمْ قَوْمٌ خَصِمُونَ﴾

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿إِنْ هُوَ إِلا عَبْدٌ أَنْعَمْنَا عَلَيْهِ﴾ يَعْنِي: عِيسَى، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ، مَا هُوَ إِلَّا عَبْدٌ [مِنْ عِبَادِ اللَّهِ] أَنْعَمَ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ بِالنُّبُوَّةِ وَالرِّسَالَةِ، ﴿وَجَعَلْنَاهُ مَثَلا لِبَنِي إِسْرَائِيلَ﴾ أَيْ: دَلَالَةً وَحُجَّةً وَبُرْهَانًا عَلَى قُدْرَتِنَا عَلَى مَا نَشَاءُ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَلَوْ نَشَاءُ لَجَعَلْنَا مِنْكُمْ﴾ أَيْ: بَدَلَكُمْ ﴿مَلائِكَةً فِي الأرْضِ يَخْلُفُونَ﴾ ، قَالَ السُّدِّيُّ: يَخْلُفُونَكَ فِيهَا. وَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَقَتَادَةُ: يَخْلُفُ بَعْضُهُمْ بَعْضًا، كَمَا يَخْلُفُ بَعْضُكُمْ بَعْضًا. وَهَذَا الْقَوْلُ يَسْتَلْزِمُ الْأَوَّلَ. وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: يُعَمِّرُونَ الْأَرْضَ بَدَلَكُمْ.

## 43:66-73 — verses 66-73 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Most High says: are these polytheists who reject the messengers waiting for anything except {the Hour, that it should come upon them suddenly while they do not perceive}? That is: it is certainly coming and will occur, and these people are heedless of it, unprepared [for it]. So when it comes, it will come only while they do not perceive it, and then they will regret with the utmost regret, at a time when regret will not benefit them nor ward it off from them.

Regarding His saying: {Close friends on that Day will be enemies to one another, except the righteous}, that is: every friendship and companionship not for the sake of Allah will turn on the Day of Resurrection into enmity, except what was for the sake of Allah, Mighty and Majestic, for that remains enduring as He endures. This is like what Ibrahim (peace be upon him) said to his people: {You have only taken idols besides Allah out of affection among yourselves in the worldly life; then on the Day of Resurrection you will disbelieve in one another and curse one another, and your refuge will be the Fire, and you will have no helpers} [al-Ankabut: 25].

Abd al-Razzaq said: Israil informed us, from Abu Ishaq, from al-Harith, from Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, regarding {Close friends on that Day will be enemies to one another, except the righteous}: he said, two believing close friends, and two disbelieving close friends. One of the believers dies and is given glad tidings of Paradise, and he remembers his close friend, so he says: "O Allah, so-and-so was my close friend, he used to command me to obey You and obey Your Messenger, and he used to command me to good and forbid me from evil, and he informed me that I would meet You. O Allah, do not let him go astray after me until You show him what You have shown me, and be pleased with him as You have been pleased with me." Then it is said to him: "Go, for if you knew what he has with Me, you would laugh much and weep little." He said: then the other one dies, and their souls are gathered together, and it is said: "Let each of you praise his companion." So each of them says to his companion: "What a good brother, what a good companion, what a good close friend." And when one of the two disbelievers dies and is given glad tidings of the Fire, he remembers his close friend and says: "O Allah, so-and-so, my close friend, used to command me to disobey You and disobey Your Messenger, and he used to command me to evil and forbid me from good, and he told me that I would not meet You. O Allah, do not guide him after me until You show him what You have shown me, and be angry with him as You have been angry with me." He said: then the other disbeliever dies, and their souls are brought together, and it is said: "Let each of you praise his companion." So each of them says to his companion: "What an evil brother, what an evil companion, what an evil close friend." Ibn Abi Hatim narrated it.

Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, and Qatadah said: every close friendship becomes enmity on the Day of Resurrection, except that of the righteous.

The hafiz Ibn Asakir narrated, in the biographical entry on Hisham ibn Ahmad, from Hisham ibn Abd Allah ibn Kathir: [chain] Abu Ja'far Muhammad ibn al-Khadir in al-Raqqah, from Mu'afa: [chain] Hakim ibn Nafi', from al-A'mash, from Abu Salih, from Abu Hurayrah, may Allah be pleased with him, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "If two men love one another for the sake of Allah, one of them in the east and the other in the west, Allah will bring them together on the Day of Resurrection, saying: 'This is the one you loved for My sake.'"

Regarding His saying: {O My servants, there is no fear upon you today, nor will you grieve}, then He gives them glad tidings.

He says: {those who believed in Our signs and were Muslims (submitters)}, that is: their hearts and their inner selves believed, and their limbs and outward selves submitted to the law of Allah.

Al-Mu'tamir ibn Sulayman said, from his father: when the Day of Resurrection comes, when people are resurrected there will remain no one among them but is terrified, and a caller calls out: {O My servants, there is no fear upon you today, nor will you grieve}, and all the people hope for it. He said: then follows: {those who believed in Our signs and were Muslims}, he said: so the people despair of it, except the believers. {Enter Paradise}, that is, it is said to them: enter Paradise, {you and your spouses}, that is, your counterparts, {being made joyful}, that is, enjoying bliss and happiness. Its explanation has already preceded in Surat al-Rum.

{Trays of gold will be passed around them}, that is, bowls, vessels for food, {and cups}, which are vessels for drink, that is, of gold, having neither spouts nor handles, {and therein is whatever souls desire}, and some read it "whatever souls crave", {and what delights the eyes}, that is, good in taste and smell and beautiful in appearance.

Abd al-Razzaq said: Ma'mar informed us, Isma'il ibn Abi Sa'id informed me, from Ikrimah, the freedman of Ibn Abbas, who informed him that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "The lowest of the people of Paradise in rank, and the lowest of them in degree, is a man after whom no one else will enter Paradise. His sight will be extended a hundred years' journey, over palaces of gold and tents of pearl, with not a hand-span of space that is not filled and cultivated. Seventy thousand golden trays will be brought to him morning and evening, no tray among them but has in it a flavor not found in another, his craving at the end of it being like his craving at the beginning of it. If all the people of the earth were to come down upon him, what he has been given would suffice for them, without any of what he has been given being diminished at all."

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Ali ibn al-Husayn ibn al-Junayd, [chain] Amr ibn Sawad al-Sarhi, [chain] Abd Allah ibn Wahb, from Ibn Lahi'ah, from Uqayl ibn Khalid, from al-Hasan, from Abu Hurayrah: that Abu Umamah, may Allah be pleased with him, related that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ told them, mentioning Paradise, and said: "By the One in whose hand is the soul of Muhammad, one of you will take a morsel and put it in his mouth, then another food will come to his mind, and the food that is in his mouth will change into what he craved." Then he recited: {and therein is whatever souls crave and what delights the eyes, and you will abide therein forever}.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Hasan, that is, ibn Musa, [chain] Sukayn ibn Abd al-Aziz, [chain] al-Ash'ath al-Darir, from Shahr ibn Hawshab, from Abu Hurayrah, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "The lowest of the people of Paradise in rank has seven levels, and he is on the sixth, above him being the seventh. He has three hundred servants, and three hundred trays are brought to him morning and evening, and I only know that he said, of gold, in every tray a flavor not found in another, and he craves it at the end just as he craved it at the beginning. And of drinks, three hundred vessels, in every vessel a flavor not found in another, and he craves it at the end just as he craved it at the beginning. And he says: 'O my Lord, if You would permit me, I would feed the people of Paradise and give them drink, without anything I have being diminished.' And he has of the wide-eyed maidens seventy-two spouses, besides his wives from the worldly life, and each one of them, her sitting place occupies the space of a mile of land."

{and you will abide therein} that is, in Paradise, {forever}, that is, you will not leave it nor seek any change from it. Then it is said to them, as a favor and a bestowal of grace: {And that is the Paradise which you have been made to inherit for what you used to do}, that is: your righteous deeds were a cause for Allah's mercy to encompass you, for no one's deeds alone admit him into Paradise, but rather it is by the grace of Allah and His mercy.

The differing ranks are only according to the degree of righteous works performed.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] al-Fadl ibn Shadhan al-Muqri, [chain] Yusuf ibn Ya'qub, that is, al-Saffar, [chain] Abu Bakr ibn Ayyash, from al-A'mash, from Abu Salih, from Abu Hurayrah, may Allah be pleased with him, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Every one of the people of the Fire sees his place in Paradise as a source of regret, and he says: {If only Allah had guided me, I would have been among the righteous} [al-Zumar: 57]. And every one of the people of Paradise sees his place in the Fire, and he says: {And we would not have been guided if Allah had not guided us} [al-A'raf: 43], so that it may be a cause of gratitude for him." He said: and the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "There is no one but has a place in Paradise and a place in the Fire. The disbeliever inherits the believer's place in the Fire, and the believer inherits the disbeliever's place in Paradise." That is the meaning of His saying: {And that is the Paradise which you have been made to inherit for what you used to do}.

Regarding His saying: {For you therein is abundant fruit, of which you will eat}, that is, of every kind, {of which you will eat}, that is, whatever you choose and desire. And when [Allah, the Most High] mentioned food and drink, He mentioned after it the fruit, so that this blessing and happy state might be complete.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿الأخِلاءُ يَوْمَئِذٍ بَعْضُهُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّ إِلا الْمُتَّقِينَ﴾ أَيْ: كُلُّ صَدَاقَةٍ وَصَحَابَةٍ لِغَيْرِ اللَّهِ فَإِنَّهَا تَنْقَلِبُ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ عَدَاوَةً إِلَّا مَا كَانَ لِلَّهِ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، فَإِنَّهُ دَائِمٌ بِدَوَامِهِ. وَهَذَا كَمَا قَالَ إِبْرَاهِيمُ، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ، لِقَوْمِهِ: ﴿إِنَّمَا اتَّخَذْتُمْ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ أَوْثَانًا مَوَدَّةَ بَيْنِكُمْ فِي الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا ثُمَّ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ يَكْفُرُ بَعْضُكُمْ بِبَعْضٍ وَيَلْعَنُ بَعْضُكُمْ بَعْضًا وَمَأْوَاكُمُ النَّارُ وَمَا لَكُمْ مِنْ نَاصِرِينَ﴾ [الْعَنْكَبُوتِ:٢٥] .

وَقَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ: أَخْبَرَنَا إِسْرَائِيلُ، عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ، عَنِ الْحَارِثِ ، عَنْ عَلِيٍّ، رضي الله عَنْهُ: ﴿الأخِلاءُ يَوْمَئِذٍ بَعْضُهُمْ لِبَعْضٍ عَدُوٌّ إِلا الْمُتَّقِينَ﴾ قَالَ: خَلِيلَانِ مُؤْمِنَانِ، وَخَلِيلَانِ كَافِرَانِ، فَتُوُفِّيَ أَحَدُ الْمُؤْمِنَيْنِ وَبُشِّرَ بِالْجَنَّةِ فَذَكَرَ خَلِيلَهُ، فَقَالَ: اللَّهُمَّ، إِنْ فُلَانًا خَلِيلِي كَانَ يَأْمُرُنِي بِطَاعَتِكَ وَطَاعَةِ رَسُولِكَ، وَيَأْمُرُنِي بِالْخَيْرِ وَيَنْهَانِي عَنِ الشَّرِّ، وَيُنْبِئُنِي أَنِّي مُلَاقِيكَ، اللَّهُمَّ فَلَا تُضِلَّهُ بَعْدِي حَتَّى تُرِيَهُ مِثْلَ مَا أَرَيْتَنِي، وَتَرْضَى عَنْهُ كَمَا رَضِيتَ عَنِّي. فَيُقَالُ لَهُ: اذْهَبْ فَلَوْ تَعْلَمُ مَا لَهُ عِنْدِي لَضَحِكْتَ كَثِيرًا وَبَكَيْتَ قَلِيلًا. قَالَ: ثُمَّ يَمُوتُ الْآخَرُ، فَتَجْتَمِعُ أَرْوَاحُهُمَا، فَيُقَالُ: لِيُثْنِ أَحَدُكُمَا عَلَى صَاحِبِهِ، فَيَقُولُ كُلُّ وَاحِدٍ مِنْهُمَا لِصَاحِبِهِ: نِعْمَ الْأَخُ، وَنِعْمَ الصَّاحِبُ، وَنِعْمَ الْخَلِيلُ. وَإِذَا مَاتَ أَحَدُ الْكَافِرَيْنِ، وَبُشِّرَ بِالنَّارِ ذَكَرَ خَلِيلَهُ فَيَقُولُ: اللَّهُمَّ إِنَّ خَلِيلِي فُلَانًا كَانَ يَأْمُرُنِي بِمَعْصِيَتِكَ وَمَعْصِيَةِ رَسُولِكَ، وَيَأْمُرُنِي بِالشَّرِّ وَيَنْهَانِي عَنِ الْخَيْرِ، وَيُخْبِرُنِي أَنِّي غَيْرُ مُلَاقِيكَ، اللَّهُمَّ فَلَا تَهْدِهِ بَعْدِي حَتَّى تُرِيَهُ مِثْلَ مَا أَرَيْتَنِي، وَتَسْخَطَ عَلَيْهِ كَمَا سَخِطْتَ عَلَيَّ. قَالَ: فَيَمُوتُ الْكَافِرُ الْآخَرُ، فَيُجْمَعُ بَيْنَ أَرْوَاحِهِمَا فَيُقَالُ: لِيُثْنِ كُلُّ وَاحِدٍ مِنْكُمَا عَلَى صَاحِبِهِ. فَيَقُولُ كُلُّ وَاحِدٍ مِنْهُمَا لِصَاحِبِهِ: بِئْسَ الْأَخُ، وَبِئْسَ الصَّاحِبُ، وَبِئْسَ الْخَلِيلُ. رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَقَتَادَةُ: صَارَتْ كُلُّ خِلَّةٍ عَدَاوَةً يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ إِلَّا الْمُتَّقِينَ.

وَرَوَى الْحَافِظُ ابْنُ عَسَاكِرَ -فِي تَرْجَمَةِ هِشَامِ بْنِ أَحْمَدَ-عَنْ هِشَامِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ كَثِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو جَعْفَرٍ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ الْخَضِرِ بِالرِّقَّةِ، عَنْ مُعَافًى: حَدَّثَنَا حَكِيمُ بْنُ نَافِعٍ، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ أَبِي صَالِحٍ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "لَوْ أَنَّ رَجُلَيْنِ تَحَابَّا فِي اللَّهِ، أَحَدُهُمَا بِالْمُشْرِقِ وَالْآخَرُ بِالْمَغْرِبِ، لَجَمَعَ اللَّهُ بَيْنَهُمَا يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ، يَقُولُ: هَذَا الَّذِي أَحْبَبْتَهُ فِيَّ" .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿يَا عِبَادِ لَا خَوْفٌ عَلَيْكُمُ الْيَوْمَ وَلا أَنْتُمْ تَحْزَنُونَ﴾ ثُمَّ بَشَّرَهُمْ.

فَقَالَ: ﴿الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا بِآيَاتِنَا وَكَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ﴾ أَيْ: آمَنَتْ قُلُوبُهُمْ وَبَوَاطِنُهُمْ، وَانْقَادَتْ لِشَرْعِ اللَّهِ جَوَارِحُهُمْ وَظَوَاهِرُهُمْ.

قَالَ الْمُعْتَمِرُ بْنُ سُلَيْمَانَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ: إِذَا كَانَ يَوْمُ الْقِيَامَةِ فَإِنَّ النَّاسَ حِينَ يُبْعَثُونَ لَا يَبْقَى أَحَدٌ مِنْهُمْ إلا فزع، فينادي مناد: ﴿يَا عِبَادِ لَا خَوْفٌ عَلَيْكُمُ الْيَوْمَ وَلا أَنْتُمْ تَحْزَنُونَ﴾ فَيَرْجُوهَا النَّاسُ كُلُّهُمْ، قَالَ: فيتبعُها: ﴿الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا بِآيَاتِنَا وَكَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ﴾ ، قَالَ: فَيَيْأَسُ النَّاسُ مِنْهَا غَيْرَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ. ﴿ادْخُلُوا الْجَنَّةَ﴾ أَيْ: يُقَالُ لَهُمْ: ادْخُلُوا الْجَنَّةَ ﴿أَنْتُمْ وَأَزْوَاجُكُمْ﴾ أَيْ: نُظَرَاؤُكُمْ ﴿تُحْبَرُونَ﴾ أَيْ: تَنْعَمُونَ وَتَسْعَدُونَ، وَقَدْ تَقَدَّمَ تَفْسِيرُهَا فِي سُورَةِ الرُّومِ.

﴿يُطَافُ عَلَيْهِمْ بِصِحَافٍ مِنْ ذَهَبٍ﴾ أَيْ: زَبَادِيِّ آنِيَةِ الطَّعَامِ، ﴿وَأَكْوَابٍ﴾ وَهِيَ: آنِيَةُ الشَّرَابِ، أَيْ: مِنْ ذَهَبٍ لَا خَرَاطِيمَ لَهَا وَلَا عُرَى، ﴿وَفِيهَا مَا تَشْهِي الأنْفُسُ﴾ -وَقَرَأَ بعضهم: "تشتهيه الْأَنْفُسُ"- ﴿وَتَلَذُّ الأعْيُنُ﴾ أَيْ: طِيبَ الطَّعْمِ وَالرِّيحِ وَحُسْنَ الْمَنْظَرِ.

قَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ: أَخْبَرَنَا مَعْمَر، أَخْبَرَنِي إِسْمَاعِيلُ بْنُ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ ، عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ -مَوْلَى ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ-أَخْبَرَهُ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "إِنَّ أَدْنَى أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ مَنْزِلَةً وَأَسْفَلَهُمْ دَرَجَةً لَرَجُلٌ لَا يَدْخُلُ الْجَنَّةَ بَعْدَهُ أَحَدٌ، يُفْسَحُ لَهُ فِي بَصَرِهِ مَسِيرَةَ مِائَةِ عَامٍ فِي قُصُورٍ مِنْ ذَهَبٍ، وَخِيَامٍ مِنْ لُؤْلُؤٍ، لَيْسَ فِيهَا مَوْضِعُ شِبْرٍ إِلَّا مَعْمُورٌ يُغَدَّى عَلَيْهِ وَيُرَاحُ بِسَبْعِينَ أَلْفَ صَحْفَةٍ مِنْ ذَهَبٍ، لَيْسَ فِيهَا صَحْفَةٌ إِلَّا فِيهَا لَوْنٌ لَيْسَ فِي الْأُخْرَى، مِثْلُهُ شَهْوَتُهُ فِي آخِرِهَا كَشَهْوَتِهِ فِي أَوَّلِهَا، لَوْ نَزَلَ بِهِ جَمِيعُ أَهْلِ الْأَرْضِ لَوَسِعَ عَلَيْهِمْ مِمَّا أُعْطِيَ، لَا يَنْقُصُ ذَلِكَ مِمَّا أُوتِيَ شَيْئًا" .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ الْحُسَيْنِ بْنِ الْجُنَيْدِ، حَدَّثَنَا عَمْرُو بْنُ سَوَادٍ السَّرْحِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ وَهْبٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ لَهِيعَةَ، عَنْ عُقَيْلِ بْنِ خَالِدٍ، عَنِ الْحَسَنِ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ: أَنَّ أَبَا أُمَامَةَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، حَدَّثَ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ حَدَّثَهُمْ -وَذَكَرَ الْجَنَّةَ-فَقَالَ: "وَالَّذِي نَفْسُ مُحَمَّدٍ بِيَدِهِ، لَيَأْخُذَنَّ أَحَدُكُمُ اللُّقْمَةَ فَيَجْعَلُهَا فِي فِيهِ، ثُمَّ يَخْطُرُ عَلَى بَالِهِ طَعَامٌ آخَرُ، فَيَتَحَوَّلُ الطَّعَامُ الَّذِي فِي فِيهِ عَلَى الَّذِي اشْتَهَى" ثُمَّ قَرَأَ: ﴿وَفِيهَا مَا تَشْتَهِيهِ الأنْفُسُ وَتَلَذُّ الأعْيُنُ وَأَنْتُمْ فِيهَا خَالِدُونَ﴾ .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا حَسَنٌ -هُوَ ابْنُ مُوسَى-حَدَّثَنَا سُكَيْن بْنُ عَبْدِ الْعَزِيزِ، حَدَّثَنَا الْأَشْعَثُ الضَّرِيرُ، عَنْ شَهْرِ بْنِ حَوْشَب، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ أَدْنَى أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ مَنْزِلَةً إِنَّ لَهُ لَسَبْعَ دَرَجَاتٍ، وَهُوَ عَلَى السَّادِسَةِ وَفَوْقَهُ السَّابِعَةُ، وَإِنَّ لَهُ ثلثمائة خادم، ويغدى عليه ويراح كل يوم بثلثمائة صَحْفَةٍ -وَلَا أَعْلَمُهُ إِلَّا قَالَ: مِنْ ذَهَبٍ-فِي كُلِّ صَحْفَةٍ لَوْنٌ لَيْسَ فِي الْأُخْرَى، وَإِنَّهُ لَيَلَذُّ أَوَّلَهُ كَمَا يَلَذُّ آخِرَهُ، وَمِنَ الْأَشْرِبَةِ ثَلَاثَمِائَةِ إِنَاءٍ، فِي كُلِّ إِنَاءٍ لَوْنٌ لَيْسَ فِي الْآخَرِ، وَإِنَّهُ لَيَلَذُّ أَوَّلَهُ كَمَا يَلَذُّ آخِرَهُ، وَإِنَّهُ يَقُولُ: يَا رَبِّ، لَوْ أَذِنْتَ لِي لَأَطْعَمْتُ أَهْلَ الْجَنَّةِ وَسَقَيْتُهُمْ، لَمْ يَنْقُصْ مِمَّا عِنْدِي شَيْءٌ، وَإِنَّ لَهُ مِنَ الْحُورِ الْعِينِ لَاثْنَيْنِ وَسَبْعِينَ زَوْجَةً، سِوَى أَزْوَاجِهِ مِنَ الدُّنْيَا، وَإِنَّ الْوَاحِدَةَ مِنْهُنَّ لَيَأْخُذُ مَقْعَدُهَا قَدْرَ مَيْلٍ مِنَ الْأَرْضِ" .

﴿وَأَنْتُمْ فِيهَا﴾ أَيْ: فِي الْجَنَّةِ ﴿خَالِدُونَ﴾ أَيْ: لَا تَخْرُجُونَ مِنْهَا وَلَا تَبْغُونَ عَنْهَا حِوَلًا. ثُمَّ قِيلَ لَهُمْ عَلَى وَجْهِ التَّفَضُّلِ وَالِامْتِنَانِ: ﴿وَتِلْكَ الْجَنَّةُ الَّتِي أُورِثْتُمُوهَا بِمَا كُنْتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ﴾ أَيْ: أَعْمَالُكُمُ الصَّالِحَةُ كَانَتْ سَبَبًا لِشُمُولِ رَحْمَةِ اللَّهِ إِيَّاكُمْ، فَإِنَّهُ لَا يُدْخِلُ أَحَدًا عَمَلُهُ الْجَنَّةَ، وَلَكِنْ بِفَضْلٍ من الله ورحمته.

وَإِنَّمَا الدَّرَجَاتُ تَفَاوُتُهَا بِحَسَبِ عَمَلِ الصَّالِحَاتِ.

## 43:74-80 — verses 74-80 (shared commentary)

### Translation

When He, exalted is He, mentioned the state of the fortunate, He followed it with mention of the wretched, saying: {Indeed, the criminals are in the punishment of Hell, abiding eternally. It will not be lightened for them} that is, not for a single moment, {and they, therein, are in despair} that is, despairing of all good. {And We did not wrong them, but they were the wrongdoers} that is, through their evil deeds, after proofs had been established against them and messengers had been sent to them, yet they denied and disobeyed, so they were repaid for that with a fitting recompense, and your Lord is not unjust to His servants.

{And they will call out, "O Malik!"} who is the keeper of the Fire.

Al-Bukhari said: [chain] Sufyan ibn Uyaynah, from Amr ibn Ata, from Safwan ibn Ya'la, from his father, who said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ recite upon the pulpit: {And they will call out, "O Malik, let your Lord put an end to us"}

that is, let Him seize our souls and give us rest from what we are in. For indeed they are as He, exalted is He, said: {It is not decreed for them that they die, nor is the punishment lightened for them} [Fatir: 36]. And He said: {And it will be avoided by the most wretched one, who enters the greatest Fire, then neither dies therein nor lives} [al-A'la: 11-13]. So when they asked to die, Malik answered them: {He said, "Indeed, you will remain."} Ibn Abbas said: he waited a thousand years, then said: "Indeed, you will remain." Narrated by Ibn Abi Hatim.

That is: there is no exit for you from it, nor any way for you to turn away from it.

Then He mentioned the cause of their wretchedness, which was their opposition to the truth and their obstinate resistance to it, saying: {We had certainly brought you the truth} that is, We made it clear to you, explained it, and elucidated it, {but most of you, to the truth, were averse} that is, but your natures would not accept it nor turn toward it; rather, they submitted to falsehood and exalted it, and turned away from the truth and refused it, and hated its people. So blame yourselves and feel regret, though regret will not benefit you where you are.

Then He, exalted is He, said: {Or have they devised a plan? But indeed, We are devising a plan} Mujahid said: they intended an evil scheme, so We schemed against them.

What Mujahid said is like what He, exalted is He, said: {And they schemed a scheme, and We schemed a scheme, while they perceived not} [al-Naml: 50]. This is because the polytheists used to resort to tricks in order to repel the truth with falsehood, through stratagems and schemes they employed, so Allah schemed against them and turned the consequence of that back upon them. Therefore He said: {Or do they think that We do not hear their secrets and their private conversations?} that is, their secret and their open speech. {Yes, and Our messengers are with them recording} that is, We know what they are upon, and the angels, too, are recording their deeds, the small of them and the great.

## 43:81-89 — verses 81-89 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: {Say} O Muhammad: {If the Most Merciful had a son, then I would be the first of the worshippers}, meaning: if this were supposed to be the case, I would worship on that basis, because I am a servant among His servants, obedient to everything He commands me, having no arrogance nor refusal to worship Him. So if it were supposed to be the case, this would happen, but this is impossible with respect to Him, the Almighty. A conditional clause does not necessarily entail that the thing occurs, nor even that it is possible, as the Almighty said: {If Allah had willed to take a son, He would have chosen from what He creates whatever He willed. Glory be to Him. He is Allah, the One, the Subduer} [al-Zumar: 4].

Some of the commentators said regarding His statement {then I would be the first of the worshippers}, meaning: the first of those who refuse and disdain. Among them was Sufyan al-Thawri, and al-Bukhari related this, saying: it is also said that {the first of al-'abidin} means "the deniers," from the verb "'abida ya'budu."

Ibn Jarir mentioned as supporting evidence for this view what he related from Yunus ibn 'Abd al-A'la, from Ibn Wahb: Ibn Abi Dhi'b told me, from Abu Qusayt, from Ba'jah ibn Zayd al-Juhani, that a woman from among them went to her husband, who was also a man from among them, and she gave birth to a child by him after six months. Her husband mentioned this to 'Uthman ibn 'Affan, may Allah be pleased with him, who ordered that she be stoned. Then 'Ali ibn Abi Talib, may Allah be pleased with him, came to him and said: "Allah says in His Book: {and his bearing and weaning take thirty months} [al-Ahqaf: 15], and He said: {and his weaning is in two years} [Luqman: 14]." He said: By Allah, 'Uthman, may Allah be pleased with him, did not "'abada" (refuse/disdain) to send to her that she be brought back. Yunus said: Ibn Wahb said: "'abada" means "he refrained out of disdain."

And the poet said:

Whenever the one who has affection wishes, he severs ties with his close friend, and he disdains him unjustly, inevitably.

There is a difficulty with this view, for how can it be reconciled with the conditional structure, so that its meaning would be: "if this were the case, then I would refuse it"? This is problematic; let it be reflected upon. Unless it is said that "in" (if) here is not conditional but rather negating, as 'Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn 'Abbas, regarding His statement {Say: if the Most Merciful had a son}: he says, meaning: the Most Merciful does not have a son, so I am the first of those who bear witness to that.

Qatadah said: it is an expression from the speech of the Arabs: {Say: if the Most Merciful had a son, then I would be the first of the worshippers}, meaning: that is, this is not the case, so it ought not to be.

Abu Sakhr said: {Say: if the Most Merciful had a son, then I would be the first of the worshippers}, meaning: I am the first to worship Him on the basis that He has no son, and the first to affirm His oneness. Similarly said 'Abd al-Rahman ibn Zayd ibn Aslam.

Mujahid said: {then I would be the first of the worshippers}, meaning: the first to worship Him and affirm His oneness, and to reject you.

Al-Bukhari said: {then I would be the first of al-'abidin} means "the disdainers." These are two dialectal forms: a man may be described as "'abid" or "'abid."

The first interpretation is closer to being correct, on the basis that it is a conditional clause with its consequence, though it remains an impossibility.

Al-Suddi said, regarding His statement {Say: if the Most Merciful had a son, then I would be the first of the worshippers}: he means, if He had a son, I would be the first to worship him, on the basis that He has a son, but He has no son. This is the view Ibn Jarir chose, and he rejected the view of those who claimed that "in" is negating.

Therefore He said: {Glory be to the Lord of the heavens and the earth, the Lord of the Throne, above what they attribute to Him}, meaning: exalted, sanctified, and free is the Creator of all things from having a son, for He is Singular, One, al-Samad (the Self-Sufficient Master sought by all in their needs, free of all need Himself), having no equal and no peer, so He has no son.

His statement {So leave them to wallow} means: in their ignorance and misguidance, {and play} in their worldly life, {until they meet their Day which they are promised}, that is, the Day of Resurrection, meaning: they will come to know how their end, their outcome, and their state will be on that Day.

His statement {And He is the One who is a god in heaven and a god on earth} means: He is the God of whoever is in heaven and the God of whoever is on earth; the inhabitants of both worship Him, and all of them are submissive before Him, humbled in His presence. {And He is the Wise, the Knowing}.

This verse is like His statement: {And He is Allah in the heavens and on the earth; He knows your secret and your open speech, and He knows what you earn} [al-An'am: 3], meaning: He is the one called upon as Allah in the heavens and the earth.

{And Blessed is the One to whom belongs the dominion of the heavens and the earth and what is between them}, meaning: He is their Creator, their Owner, and the One who disposes of them without any opposition or hindrance. So glorified and exalted is He above having a son. And "tabaraka" (blessed) means: freedom from defects and deficiencies is firmly established for Him, because He is the High, the Magnificent Lord, the Owner of all things, in whose hand is the reins of all matters, to undo or establish. {And with Him is the knowledge of the Hour}, meaning: none but He can reveal it at its appointed time. {And to Him you will be returned}, meaning: He will repay each one for his deeds, good for good and evil for evil.

Then the Almighty said: {And those whom they call upon besides Him do not possess the power of intercession}, meaning: the idols and false gods, {intercession}, meaning: they are not capable of interceding for them, {except those who testify to the truth, and they know}. This is a discontinuous exception, meaning: but whoever testifies to the truth with insight and knowledge, his intercession will indeed benefit, with Him, by His permission granted to that person.

Then He said: {And if you asked them who created them, they would surely say: Allah. How then are they deluded away?}, meaning: if you asked these polytheists who worship, alongside Allah, someone other than Him, {who created them, they would surely say: Allah}, meaning: they acknowledge that He is the Creator of all things, alone, having no partner in that, and yet, despite this, they worship besides Him others who possess nothing and have no power over anything. So they are, in this, at the extreme of ignorance, foolishness, and feebleness of mind. Therefore He said: {How then are they deluded away?}

His statement {And His saying: "O my Lord, indeed these are a people who do not believe"} means: and Muhammad said his saying, that is, he complained to his Lord of his complaint against his people who had rejected him, saying: O my Lord, these are indeed a people who do not believe, just as the Almighty informed in another verse: {And the Messenger said: O my Lord, indeed my people have taken this Qur'an as a thing to be shunned} [al-Furqan: 30]. This is what we have said, and it is the meaning of the statement of Ibn Mas'ud, Mujahid, and Qatadah, and Ibn Jarir explained it accordingly.

Al-Bukhari said: 'Abdullah, meaning Ibn Mas'ud, recited it as: "wa qala al-rasulu ya Rabb" (and the Messenger said: O my Lord).

Mujahid said regarding His statement {And His saying: "O my Lord, indeed these are a people who do not believe"}: Allah confirmed and made good the statement of Muhammad.

Qatadah said: it is the statement of your Prophet ﷺ, complaining of his people to his Lord, Mighty and Majestic.

Then Ibn Jarir related, regarding {And His saying, "O my Lord"}, two readings. One is with the accusative case, which has two explanations: one, that it is connected to His statement {We hear their secret talk and their private conversation} [al-Zukhruf: 80]; and the other, that a verb is understood, "and he said his saying." The second reading is with the genitive case, "wa qilihi," connected to His statement {And with Him is the knowledge of the Hour}, its meaning being: "and the knowledge of his saying."

His statement {So turn away from them}, meaning: the polytheists, {and say: Peace}, meaning: do not answer them with the like of the evil speech with which they address you, but rather conciliate them and turn away from them in deed and word. {For they will come to know}, this is a threat from Him, the Almighty, against them. Therefore He brought down upon them His wrath which cannot be turned back, and He exalted His religion and His word, and thereafter He ordained struggle and combat, until people entered into the religion of Allah in multitudes, and Islam spread throughout the east and the west.

End of the commentary on Surat al-Zukhruf.

يَقُولُ تَعَالَى: ﴿قُلْ﴾ يَا مُحَمَّدُ: ﴿إِنْ كَانَ لِلرَّحْمَنِ وَلَدٌ فَأَنَا أَوَّلُ الْعَابِدِينَ﴾ أَيْ: لَوْ فُرِضَ هذا لعبدته عَلَى ذَلِكَ لِأَنِّي عَبْدٌ مِنْ عَبِيدِهِ، مُطِيعٌ لِجَمِيعِ مَا يَأْمُرُنِي بِهِ، لَيْسَ عِنْدِي اسْتِكْبَارٌ وَلَا إِبَاءٌ عَنْ عِبَادَتِهِ، فَلَوْ فُرِضَ كَانَ هَذَا، وَلَكِنْ هَذَا مُمْتَنِعٌ فِي حَقِّهِ تَعَالَى، وَالشَّرْطُ لَا يَلْزَمُ مِنْهُ الْوُقُوعُ وَلَا الْجَوَازُ أَيْضًا، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿لَوْ أَرَادَ اللَّهُ أَنْ يَتَّخِذَ وَلَدًا لاصْطَفَى مِمَّا يَخْلُقُ مَا يَشَاءُ سُبْحَانَهُ هُوَ اللَّهُ الْوَاحِدُ الْقَهَّارُ﴾ [الزُّمَرِ: ٤] .

[وَ] قَالَ بَعْضُ الْمُفَسِّرِينَ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿فَأَنَا أَوَّلُ الْعَابِدِينَ﴾ أَيِ: الْآنِفِينَ. وَمِنْهُمْ سُفْيَانُ الثَّوْرِيُّ، وَالْبُخَارِيُّ حَكَاهُ فَقَالَ: وَيُقَالُ: ﴿أَوَّلُ الْعَابِدِينَ﴾ الْجَاحِدِينَ، مِنْ عَبِدَ يَعْبَدُ.

وَذَكَرَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ لِهَذَا الْقَوْلِ مِنَ الشَّوَاهِدِ مَا رَوَاهُ عَنْ يُونُسَ بْنِ عَبْدِ الْأَعْلَى، عَنِ ابْنِ وَهْبٍ، حَدَّثَنِي ابْنُ أَبِي ذِئْبٍ، عَنْ أَبِي قُسَيْط ، عَنْ بَعَجة بْنِ زَيْدٍ الْجُهَنِيِّ؛ أَنَّ امْرَأَةً مِنْهُمْ دَخَلَتْ عَلَى زَوْجِهَا -وَهُوَ رَجُلٌ مِنْهُمْ أَيْضًا-فَوَلَدَتْ لَهُ فِي سِتَّةِ أَشْهُرٍ، فَذَكَرَ ذَلِكَ زَوْجُهَا لِعُثْمَانَ بْنِ عَفَّانَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، فَأَمَرَ بِهَا أَنْ تُرْجَمَ، فَدَخَلَ عَلَيْهِ عَلِيُّ بْنِ أَبِي طَالِبٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، فقال: إِنَّ اللَّهَ يَقُولُ فِي كِتَابِهِ: ﴿وَحَمْلُهُ وَفِصَالُهُ ثَلاثُونَ شَهْرًا﴾ [الْأَحْقَافِ: ١٥] ، وَقَالَ ﴿وَفِصَالُهُ فِي عَامَيْنِ﴾ [لُقْمَانَ: ١٤] ، قَالَ: فَوَاللَّهِ مَا عَبَدَ عُثْمَانُ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، أَنْ بَعْثَ إِلَيْهَا: تُرَدُّ -قَالَ يُونُسُ: قَالَ ابْنُ وَهْبٍ: عَبَدَ: اسْتَنْكَفَ. 

[وَ] قَالَ الشَّاعِرُ:

مَتَى مَا يَشَأ ذُو الوُدِّ يصْرِمْ خَليله ... ويَعْبَدُ عَلَيه لَا مِحَالَة ظَالمًا 

وَهَذَا الْقَوْلُ فِيهِ نَظَرٌ؛ لِأَنَّهُ كَيْفَ يَلْتَئِمُ مَعَ الشَّرْطِ فَيَكُونَ تَقْدِيرُهُ: إِنْ كَانَ هَذَا فَأَنَا مُمْتَنِعٌ مِنْهُ؟ هَذَا فِيهِ نَظَرٌ، فَلْيُتَأَمَّلْ. اللَّهُمَّ إِلَّا أَنْ يُقَالَ: "إِنْ" لَيْسَتْ شَرْطًا، وَإِنَّمَا هِيَ نَافِيَةٌ كَمَا قَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿قُلْ إِنْ كَانَ لِلرَّحْمَنِ وَلَدٌ﴾ ، يَقُولُ: لَمْ يَكُنْ لِلرَّحْمَنِ وَلَدٌ فَأَنَا أَوَّلُ الشَّاهِدِينَ.

وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: هِيَ كَلِمَةٌ مِنْ كَلَامِ الْعَرَبِ: ﴿قُلْ إِنْ كَانَ لِلرَّحْمَنِ وَلَدٌ فَأَنَا أَوَّلُ الْعَابِدِينَ﴾ أَيْ: إِنْ ذَلِكَ لَمْ يَكُنْ فَلَا يَنْبَغِي.

وَقَالَ أَبُو صَخْرٍ: ﴿قُلْ إِنْ كَانَ لِلرَّحْمَنِ وَلَدٌ فَأَنَا أَوَّلُ الْعَابِدِينَ﴾ أَيْ: فَأَنَا أَوَّلُ مَنْ عَبَدَهُ بِأَنْ لَا وَلَدَ لَهُ، وَأَوَّلُ مَنْ وَحَّدَهُ. وَكَذَا قَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ.

وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: ﴿فَأَنَا أَوَّلُ الْعَابِدِينَ﴾ أَيْ: أَوَّلُ مَنْ عَبَدَهُ وَوَحَّدَهُ وَكَذَّبَكُمْ.

وَقَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ: ﴿فَأَنَا أَوَّلُ الْعَابِدِينَ﴾ الْآنِفِينَ. وَهُمَا لُغَتَانِ، رَجُلٌ عَابِدٌ وَعَبِدٌ .

وَالْأَوَّلُ أَقْرَبُ عَلَى أَنَّهُ شَرْطٌ وَجَزَاءٌ، وَلَكِنْ هُوَ مُمْتَنِعٌ.

وَقَالَ السُّدِّيُّ [فِي قَوْلِهِ] ﴿قُلْ إِنْ كَانَ لِلرَّحْمَنِ وَلَدٌ فَأَنَا أَوَّلُ الْعَابِدِينَ﴾ يَقُولُ: لَوْ كَانَ لَهُ وَلَدٌ كُنْتُ أَوَّلَ مَنْ عَبَدَهُ، بِأَنَّ لَهُ وَلَدًا، لَكِنْ لَا وَلَدَ لَهُ. وَهُوَ اخْتِيَارُ ابْنِ جَرِيرٍ، وَرَدَّ قَوْلَ مَنْ زَعَمَ أَنَّ "إِنْ" نَافِيَةٌ.

وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿سُبْحَانَ رَبِّ السَّمَوَاتِ وَالأرْضِ رَبِّ الْعَرْشِ عَمَّا يَصِفُونَ﴾ أَيْ: تَعَالَى وَتَقَدَّسَ وَتَنَزَّهَ خَالِقُ الْأَشْيَاءِ عَنْ أَنْ يَكُونَ لَهُ وَلَدٌ، فَإِنَّهُ فَرْدٌ أَحَدٌ صَمَدٌ، لَا نَظِيرَ لَهُ وَلَا كُفْءَ لَهُ، فَلَا وَلَدَ لَهُ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَذَرْهُمْ يَخُوضُوا﴾ أَيْ: فِي جَهْلِهِمْ وَضَلَالِهِمْ ﴿وَيَلْعَبُوا﴾ فِي دُنْيَاهُمْ ﴿حَتَّى يُلاقُوا يَوْمَهُمُ الَّذِي يُوعَدُونَ﴾ وَهُوَ يَوْمُ الْقِيَامَةِ، أَيْ: فَسَوْفَ يَعْلَمُونَ كَيْفَ يَكُونُ مَصِيرُهُمْ، وَمَآلُهُمْ، وَحَالُهُمْ فِي ذَلِكَ الْيَوْمِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَهُوَ الَّذِي فِي السَّمَاءِ إِلَهٌ وَفِي الأرْضِ إِلَهٌ﴾ أَيْ: هُوَ إِلَهُ مَنْ فِي السَّمَاءِ، وَإِلَهُ مَنْ فِي الْأَرْضِ، يَعْبُدُهُ أَهْلُهُمَا، وَكُلُّهُمْ خَاضِعُونَ لَهُ، أَذِلَّاءُ بَيْنَ يَدَيْهِ، ﴿وَهُوَ الْحَكِيمُ الْعَلِيمُ﴾

وَهَذِهِ الْآيَةُ كَقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَهُوَ اللَّهُ فِي السَّمَوَاتِ وَفِي الأرْضِ يَعْلَمُ سِرَّكُمْ وَجَهْرَكُمْ وَيَعْلَمُ مَا تَكْسِبُونَ﴾ [الْأَنْعَامِ: ٣] أَيْ: هُوَ الْمَدْعُوُّ اللَّهُ فِي السَّمَوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ.

﴿وَتَبَارَكَ الَّذِي لَهُ مُلْكُ السَّمَوَاتِ وَالأرْضِ وَمَا بَيْنَهُمَا﴾ أَيْ: هُوَ خَالِقُهُمَا وَمَالِكُهُمَا وَالْمُتَصَرِّفُ فِيهِمَا، بِلَا مُدَافَعَةٍ وَلَا مُمَانَعَةٍ، فَسُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَى عَنِ الْوَلَدِ، وَتَبَارَكَ: أَيِ اسْتَقَرَّ لَهُ السَّلَامَةُ مِنَ الْعُيُوبِ وَالنَّقَائِصِ؛ لِأَنَّهُ الرَّبُّ الْعَلِيُّ الْعَظِيمُ، الْمَالِكُ لِلْأَشْيَاءِ، الَّذِي بِيَدِهِ أَزِمَّةُ الْأُمُورِ نَقْضًا وَإِبْرَامًا، ﴿وَعِنْدَهُ عِلْمُ السَّاعَةِ﴾ أَيْ: لَا يُجَلِّيهَا لِوَقْتِهَا إِلَّا هُوَ، ﴿وَإِلَيْهِ تُرْجَعُونَ﴾ أَيْ: فَيُجَازِي كُلًّا بِعَمَلِهِ، إِنْ خَيْرًا فَخَيْرٌ، وَإِنْ شَرًّا فَشَرٌّ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَلا يَمْلِكُ الَّذِينَ يَدْعُونَ مِنْ دُونِهِ﴾ أَيْ: مِنَ الْأَصْنَامِ وَالْأَوْثَانِ ﴿الشَّفَاعَةَ﴾ أَيْ: لَا يَقْدِرُونَ عَلَى الشَّفَاعَةِ لَهُمْ، ﴿إِلا مَنْ شَهِدَ بِالْحَقِّ وَهُمْ يَعْلَمُونَ﴾ هَذَا اسْتِثْنَاءٌ مُنْقَطِعٌ، أَيْ: لَكِنْ مَنْ شَهِدَ بِالْحَقِّ عَلَى بَصِيرَةٍ وَعِلْمٍ، فَإِنَّهُ تَنْفَعُ شَفَاعَتُهُ عِنْدَهُ بِإِذْنِهِ لَهُ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿وَلَئِنْ سَأَلْتَهُمْ مَنْ خَلَقَهُمْ لَيَقُولُنَّ اللَّهُ فَأَنَّى يُؤْفَكُونَ﴾ أَيْ: وَلَئِنْ سَأَلْتَ هَؤُلَاءِ الْمُشْرِكِينَ بِاللَّهِ الْعَابِدِينَ مَعَهُ غَيْرَهُ ﴿مَنْ خَلَقَهُمْ لَيَقُولُنَّ اللَّهُ﴾ أَيْ: هُمْ يَعْتَرِفُونَ أَنَّهُ الْخَالِقُ لِلْأَشْيَاءِ جَمِيعِهَا، وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ فِي ذَلِكَ، وَمَعَ هَذَا يَعْبُدُونَ مَعَهُ غَيْرَهُ، مِمَّنْ لَا يَمْلِكُ شَيْئًا وَلَا يَقْدِرُ على شيء، فهم في ذَلِكَ فِي غَايَةِ الْجَهْلِ وَالسَّفَاهَةِ وَسَخَافَةِ الْعَقْلِ؛ ولهذا قال: ﴿فَأَنَّى يُؤْفَكُونَ﴾

وقوله: ﴿وَقِيلِهِ يَا رَبِّ إِنَّ هَؤُلاءِ قَوْمٌ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ﴾ أَيْ: وَقَالَ: مُحَمَّدٌ: قِيلُهُ، أَيْ: شَكَا إِلَى رَبِّهِ شَكْوَاهُ مِنْ قَوْمِهِ الَّذِينَ كَذَّبُوهُ، فَقَالَ: يَا رَبِّ، إِنَّ هَؤُلَاءِ قَوْمٌ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ، كَمَا أَخْبَرَ تَعَالَى فِي الْآيَةِ الْأُخْرَى: ﴿وَقَالَ الرَّسُولُ يَا رَبِّ إِنَّ قَوْمِي اتَّخَذُوا هَذَا الْقُرْآنَ مَهْجُورًا﴾ [الْفُرْقَانِ: ٣٠] وَهَذَا الَّذِي قُلْنَاهُ هُوَ [مَعْنَى] قَوْلِ ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٍ، وَقَتَادَةَ، وَعَلَيْهِ فَسَّرَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ .

قَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ: وَقَرَأَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ -يَعْنِي ابْنَ مَسْعُودٍ-: "وَقَالَ الرَّسُولُ يَا رَبِّ". 

وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ في قوله: ﴿وَقِيلِهِ يَا رَبِّ إِنَّ هَؤُلاءِ قَوْمٌ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ﴾ ، قَالَ: فَأَبَرَّ اللَّهُ قَوْلَ مُحَمَّدٍ.

وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: هُوَ قَوْلُ نَبِيِّكُمْ ﷺ يَشْكُو قَوْمَهُ إِلَى رَبِّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ.

ثُمَّ حكى ابن جرير في قوله: ﴿وَقِيلِهِ يَا رَبِّ﴾ قِرَاءَتَيْنِ، إِحْدَاهُمَا النَّصْبُ، وَلَهَا تَوْجِيهَانِ: أَحَدُهُمَا أَنَّهُ مَعْطُوفٌ عَلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿نَسْمَعُ سِرَّهُمْ وَنَجْوَاهُمْ﴾ [الزُّخْرُفِ: ٨٠] وَالثَّانِي: أَنْ يُقَدَّرَ فِعْلٌ، وَقَالَ: قيلَه. وَالثَّانِيَةُ: الْخَفْضُ، وقيلِهِ، عَطْفًا عَلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَعِنْدَهُ عِلْمُ السَّاعَةِ﴾ تَقْدِيرُهُ: وعِلم قِيلِهِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَاصْفَحْ عَنْهُمْ﴾ أَيِ: الْمُشْرِكِينَ، ﴿وَقُلْ سَلامٌ﴾ أَيْ: لَا تُجَاوِبْهُمْ بِمِثْلِ مَا يُخَاطِبُونَكَ بِهِ مِنَ الْكَلَامِ السَّيِّئِ، وَلَكِنْ تَأَلَّفْهُمْ وَاصْفَحْ عَنْهُمْ فِعْلًا وَقَوْلًا ﴿فَسَوْفَ يَعْلَمُونَ ﴾ ، هَذَا تَهْدِيدٌ مِنْهُ تَعَالَى لَهُمْ، وَلِهَذَا أَحَلَّ بِهِمْ بَأْسَهُ الَّذِي لَا يُرَدُّ، وَأَعْلَى دِينَهُ وَكَلِمَتَهُ، وَشَرَعَ بَعْدَ ذَلِكَ الْجِهَادَ وَالْجِلَادَ، حَتَّى دَخَلَ النَّاسُ فِي دِينِ اللَّهِ أَفْوَاجًا، وَانْتَشَرَ الْإِسْلَامُ فِي الْمَشَارِقِ وَالْمَغَارِبِ.
