## 47:1-3 — verses 1-3 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Commentary on Surat al-Qital (Muhammad)

[It is Medinan]

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful

The Exalted says: {Those who disbelieve}, that is, in the signs of Allah, {and hinder} others {from the path of Allah, He has made their deeds go astray}, meaning He has nullified them and made them vanish, and He has not granted them any reward or recompense, similar to His saying: {And We shall turn to whatever deeds they did and make them scattered dust} [al-Furqan: 23].

Then He says: {And those who believe and do righteous deeds}, meaning their hearts and inner selves believed, and their limbs, their inward and outward parts, submitted, {and believe in what has been sent down to Muhammad}, this is a joining of the specific to the general, and it is a proof that this is a condition for the validity of faith after his mission, may the prayers and peace of Allah be upon him.

His saying: {and it is the truth from their Lord} is a fine parenthetical clause. This is why He then said: {He has removed from them their sins and set right their state}. Ibn Abbas said: meaning their affair. Mujahid said: their condition. Qatadah and Ibn Zayd said: their state. All these are close in meaning. It has come in the hadith about responding to the sneezer: "May Allah guide you and set right your bal (state/condition)."

Then the Exalted says: {That is because those who disbelieve followed falsehood}, meaning: We only nullified the deeds of the disbelievers, and overlooked the sins of the righteous, and set right their affairs, because those who disbelieved followed falsehood, that is, they chose falsehood over truth, {while those who believe followed the truth from their Lord. Thus does Allah set forth for people their parables}, meaning He makes clear to them the outcome of their deeds, and what they will come to in their return to Him.

## 47:4-9 — verses 4-9 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, guiding the believers to what they should rely upon in their wars against the polytheists: {So when you meet those who disbelieve, strike the necks} meaning: when you confront them, mow them down with the swords, {until when you have subdued them fully, then bind fast} meaning: you have destroyed them by killing, {then bind fast} the fetters of the captives whom you take prisoner. Then, after the war has ended and the battle has concluded, you are given a choice regarding their affair: if you wish, you may show favor to them and release your captives without charge, and if you wish, you may ransom them for wealth that you take from them and share among yourselves.

It appears that this verse was revealed after the Battle of Badr, for God, glorified is He, rebuked the believers for taking too many captives that day in order to obtain ransom from them, and for reducing the killing that day, saying: {It is not for a prophet to take captives until he has thoroughly subdued the land. You desire the goods of this world, while God desires the Hereafter. God is Mighty, Wise. Had it not been for a decree from God that preceded, a great punishment would have befallen you for what you took} [al-Anfal: 67, 68].

Some scholars have then claimed that this verse, which gives the choice between ransoming the captive and showing favor to him, has been abrogated by His statement: {So when the sacred months have passed, kill the polytheists wherever you find them [and seize them, and besiege them, and lie in wait for them at every place of ambush]} [al-Tawbah: 5]. This was narrated by al-Awfi from Ibn Abbas, and it was also said by Qatadah, al-Dahhak, al-Suddi, and Ibn Jurayj.

Others, who are the majority, said: it is not abrogated.

Then some of them said: the imam is only given the choice between showing favor to the captive and ransoming him, and it is not permissible for him to kill him.

Others among them said: rather he may kill him if he wishes, on account of the hadith of the Prophet's ﷺ killing of al-Nadr ibn al-Harith and Uqbah ibn Abi Mu'ayt from among the captives of Badr. And Thumamah ibn Uthal said to the Messenger of God ﷺ, when he said to him, "What have you to say, O Thumamah?" He replied: "If you kill, you will kill one who has taken blood; if you show favor, you will show favor to one who is grateful; and if you want wealth, ask and you will be given of it whatever you wish."

Al-Shafi'i, may God have mercy on him, added further, saying: the imam has the choice between killing him, or showing favor to him, or ransoming him, or enslaving him as well. This matter is settled in detail in the science of legal branches (al-furu'), and we have provided evidence for that in our book "al-Ahkam." Praise and grace belong to God.

Regarding His statement: {until war lays down its burdens}, Mujahid said: until Jesus son of Mary (peace be upon him) descends. It seems he derived this from the saying of the Prophet ﷺ: "A group from my nation will continue to prevail upon the truth until the last of them fights the Dajjal."

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] that Salamah ibn Nufayl informed them that he came to the Messenger of God ﷺ and said: "I have released the horses, laid down the weapons, and war has laid down its burdens, and I have said: 'there is no more fighting.'" The Prophet ﷺ said to him: "Now has fighting come. A group from my nation will continue to prevail over the people; God will cause the hearts of certain peoples to swerve, and they will fight them, and God will grant them provision from them, until the command of God comes while they are in that state. Indeed, the heartland of the believers is al-Sham, and good is tied to the forelocks of horses until the Day of Resurrection."

Thus it was also narrated by al-Nasa'i through two routes, from Jubayr ibn Nufayr, from Salamah ibn Nufayl al-Sakuni, with the same content.

Abu al-Qasim al-Baghawi said: [chain] from al-Nawwas ibn Sam'an, who said: when a conquest was granted to the Messenger of God ﷺ, they said: "O Messenger of God, the horses have been released, the weapons have been laid down, and war has laid down its burdens." They said: "there is no more fighting." He said: "They have lied. Now has fighting come. God will continue to raise up the hearts of a people who will fight them, and He will grant them provision from them, until the command of God comes while they are in that state, and the heartland of the Muslims is in al-Sham."

Thus it was also narrated by the hadith master Abu Ya'la al-Mawsili from Dawud ibn Rushayd, with the same content. What is preserved is that it is from the narration of Salamah ibn Nufayl, as mentioned before. This strengthens the view that there is no abrogation, as though this ruling was legislated for war to continue until no war remains.

Qatadah said: {until war lays down its burdens} means until no polytheism remains. This is like His statement: {And fight them until there is no more persecution and religion belongs entirely to God} [al-Baqarah: 193]. Then some of them said: {until war lays down its burdens} means the burdens of the warriors, that is, the polytheists, by their repenting to God, mighty and exalted is He. And it was said: the burdens of its people, meaning that they exert their full effort in obedience to God, mighty and exalted is He.

Regarding His statement: {That is so. And had God willed, He could have taken vengeance upon them} meaning: this is so, and had God willed, He could have taken revenge upon the disbelievers with a punishment and chastisement from Himself, {but [He ordained it] that He might test some of you by means of others} meaning: rather He legislated for you jihad and the fighting of enemies in order to test you and examine your affairs. As He mentioned the wisdom of legislating jihad in the surahs of "Al Imran" and "Bara'ah" (al-Tawbah), in His statement: {Or did you think that you would enter Paradise while God has not yet known those among you who strive, and known those who are steadfast?} [Al Imran: 142].

And He said in Surat Bara'ah: {Fight them; God will punish them by your hands and disgrace them, and give you victory over them, and heal the breasts of a believing people, and remove the rage of their hearts. And God turns in mercy to whom He wills, and God is Knowing, Wise} [al-Tawbah: 14, 15].

Then, since it is the nature of fighting that many believers are killed, He said: {And those who are killed in the way of God, He will never let their deeds go astray} meaning: He will never cause them to be lost, rather He will multiply them, increase them, and double them. And among them are those whose deeds continue to be credited to them throughout the length of their barzakh (intermediate state), as reported in the hadith narrated by Imam Ahmad in his Musnad, where he said:

[chain] from Qays al-Judhami, a man who was a companion of the Prophet, who said: the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "The martyr is granted six qualities at the first drop of his blood: every sin is forgiven for him, he is shown his seat in Paradise, he is married to the wide-eyed maidens (al-hur al-'in), he is protected from the greatest terror and from the punishment of the grave, and he is adorned with the garment of faith." Ahmad, may God have mercy on him, transmitted this alone.

Another hadith: Ahmad also said: [chain] from al-Miqdam ibn Ma'dikarib al-Kindi, who said: the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "The martyr has six qualities with God: he is forgiven at the first gush of his blood, he is shown his seat in Paradise, he is adorned with the garment of faith, he is married to the wide-eyed maidens, he is protected from the punishment of the grave, he is safe from the greatest terror, and a crown of dignity is placed upon his head, one ruby of which is better than this world and everything in it, and he is married to seventy-two wives from the wide-eyed maidens, and he is granted intercession for seventy people from among his relatives."

This was transmitted by al-Tirmidhi, and Ibn Majah judged it authentic.

In Sahih Muslim, from Abdullah ibn Amr and from Abu Qatadah: the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Everything is forgiven for the martyr except debt." This has been narrated from a group of the companions. Abu al-Darda said: the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "The martyr is granted intercession for seventy of his household." Abu Dawud transmitted this. The hadiths on the merit of the martyr are extremely numerous.

Regarding His statement: {He will guide them}, meaning: to Paradise, as in His statement: {Indeed, those who believe and do righteous deeds, their Lord guides them by their faith; rivers flow beneath them in the gardens of bliss} [Yunus: 9].

Regarding His statement: {and set right their state}, meaning: their affair and their condition, {and admit them into Paradise, which He has made known to them}, meaning: He has made them acquainted with it and guided them to it.

Mujahid said: its people find their way to their houses and dwellings, and to wherever God has allotted to them of it, without erring, as though they had dwelt there since they were created, needing no one to guide them to it. Malik narrated something similar from Ibn Zayd ibn Aslam.

Muhammad ibn Ka'b said: they will recognize their houses when they enter Paradise, just as you recognize your houses when you leave the Friday prayer.

Muqatil ibn Hayyan said: it has reached us that the angel who was charged with recording his deeds in this world walks before him in Paradise, and the son of Adam follows him until he reaches the farthest dwelling that belongs to him, and the angel acquaints him with everything God has given him in Paradise. When he reaches the farthest point of his dwelling in Paradise, he enters his abode and to his wives, and the angel departs from him. Ibn Abi Hatim, may God have mercy on him, recorded these reports.

An authentic hadith has also come regarding this, narrated by al-Bukhari, from Qatadah, from Abu al-Mutawakkil al-Naji, from Abu Sa'id al-Khudri (may God be pleased with him): that the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "When the believers are delivered from the Fire, they will be held back at a bridge between Paradise and the Fire, settling accounts for wrongs that were between them in this world, until, when they have been purified and cleansed, they are permitted to enter Paradise. By Him in whose hand is my soul, indeed one of them will find his dwelling in Paradise more clearly guided to than his dwelling was in this world."

Then the Exalted says: {O you who believe, if you support God, He will support you and make your feet firm}, like His statement: {And God will surely support whoever supports Him} [al-Hajj: 40], for the reward is of the same kind as the deed. This is why He said: {and make your feet firm}, as it has come in the hadith: "Whoever conveys to a person of authority a need of one who cannot convey it himself, God will make his foot firm upon the Path (al-Sirat) on the Day of Resurrection."

Then the Exalted says: {But as for those who disbelieve, then misery is for them}, the opposite of the firmness granted to the feet of the believers who support God and His Messenger ﷺ. It has been established in the hadith from the Messenger of God ﷺ that he said: "Wretched is the slave of the dinar, wretched is the slave of the dirham, wretched is the slave of the fine cloak" (and in another version: "wretched is the slave of the striped cloak") "he is wretched and overturned, and when he is pricked by a thorn, may he not be relieved," meaning: may God not heal him.

Regarding His statement: {and He has made their deeds go astray}, meaning: He has nullified them and rendered them void. This is why He said: {That is because they hated what God has sent down}, meaning: they do not want it and do not love it, {so He nullified their deeds}.

ثُمَّ قَالَ بَعْضُهُمْ: إِنَّمَا الْإِمَامُ مُخَيَّر بَيْنَ الْمَنِّ عَلَى الْأَسِيرِ وَمُفَادَاتِهِ فَقَطْ، وَلَا يَجُوزُ لَهُ قَتْلُهُ.

وَقَالَ آخَرُونَ مِنْهُمْ: بَلْ لَهُ أَنْ يَقْتُلَهُ إِنْ شَاءَ، لِحَدِيثِ قَتْلِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ النَّضْرَ بْنَ الْحَارِثِ وَعُقْبَةَ بْنَ أَبِي مُعَيط مِنْ أُسَارَى بَدْرٍ، وَقَالَ ثُمَامَةُ بْنُ أَثَالٍ لِرَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ حِينَ قَالَ لَهُ: "مَا عِنْدَكَ يَا ثُمَامَةُ؟ " فَقَالَ: إِنْ تَقْتَلْ تَقْتُلْ ذَا دَمٍ، وَإِنْ تَمْنُنْ تَمْنُنْ عَلَى شَاكِرٍ، وَإِنْ كُنْتَ تُرِيدُ الْمَالَ فَسَلْ تُعطَ مِنْهُ مَا شِئْتَ .

وَزَادَ الشَّافِعِيُّ، رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ، فَقَالَ: الْإِمَامُ مُخَيَّرٌ بَيْنَ قَتْلِهِ أَوِ الْمَنِّ عَلَيْهِ، أَوْ مُفَادَاتِهِ أَوِ اسْتِرْقَاقِهِ أَيْضًا. وَهَذِهِ الْمَسْأَلَةُ مُحَرّرة فِي عِلْمِ الْفُرُوعِ، وَقَدْ دَلَّلْنَا عَلَى ذَلِكَ فِي كِتَابِنَا "الْأَحْكَامِ"، وَلِلَّهِ الْحَمْدُ وَالْمِنَّةُ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿حَتَّى تَضَعَ الْحَرْبُ أَوْزَارَهَا﴾ قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: حَتَّى يَنْزِلَ عيسى ابن مريم [عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ] . وَكَأَنَّهُ أَخَذَهُ مِنْ قَوْلِهِ ﷺ: "لَا تَزَالُ طَائِفَةٌ مِنْ أُمَّتِي ظَاهِرِينَ عَلَى الْحَقِّ حَتَّى يُقَاتِلَ آخِرُهُمُ الدَّجَّالَ" .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا الْحَكَمُ بْنُ نَافِعٍ، حَدَّثَنَا إِسْمَاعِيلُ بْنُ عَيَّاشٍ، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ بْنِ سُلَيْمَانَ، عَنِ الْوَلِيدِ بْنِ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ الجُرَشي ، عَنْ جُبَير بْنِ نُفيَر؛ أَنَّ سَلَمَةَ بْنَ نُفيَل أَخْبَرَهُمْ: أَنَّهُ أَتَى رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالَ: إِنِّي سَيَّبْتُ الْخَيْلَ، وَأَلْقَيْتُ السِّلَاحَ، وَوَضَعَتِ الْحَرْبُ أَوْزَارَهَا، وَقُلْتُ: "لَا قِتَالَ" فَقَالَ لَهُ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: "الْآنَ جَاءَ الْقِتَالُ، لَا تَزَالُ طَائِفَةٌ مِنْ أُمَّتِي ظَاهِرِينَ عَلَى النَّاسِ يُزيغ اللَّهُ قُلُوبَ أَقْوَامٍ فَيُقَاتِلُونَهُمْ: وَيَرْزُقُهُمُ اللَّهُ مِنْهُمْ، حَتَّى يَأْتِيَ أَمْرُ اللَّهِ وَهُمْ عَلَى ذَلِكَ. أَلَا إِنَّ عُقْرَ دَارِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ الشَّامُ، والخيلُ مَعْقُودٌ فِي نَوَاصِيهَا الْخَيْرُ إِلَى يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ".

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ النَّسَائِيُّ مِنْ طَرِيقَيْنِ، عَنْ جُبَيْر بْنِ نُفَير، عَنْ سَلَمَةَ بْنِ نُفَيْل السَّكُونِيِّ، بِهِ .

وَقَالَ أَبُو الْقَاسِمِ الْبَغَوِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا دَاوُدُ بْنُ رُشَيْد، حَدَّثَنَا الْوَلِيدُ بْنُ مُسْلِمٍ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ مُهَاجِرٍ، عَنِ الْوَلِيدِ بْنِ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ الجُرَشي، عَنْ جُبَيْرِ بْنِ نُفَير، عَنِ النَّوَّاسِ بْنِ سَمْعَانَ قَالَ: لَمَّا فُتِحَ عَلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَتْح فَقَالُوا: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، سُيِّبَتِ الْخَيْلُ، وَوُضِعَتِ السِّلَاحُ، وَوَضَعَتِ الْحَرْبُ أَوْزَارَهَا، قَالُوا: لَا قِتَالَ، قَالَ: "كَذَبُوا، الْآنَ، جَاءَ الْقِتَالُ، لَا يَزَالُ اللَّهُ يُرَفِّع قُلُوبَ قَوْمٍ يُقَاتِلُونَهُمْ، فَيَرْزَقُهُمْ مِنْهُمْ، حَتَّى يَأْتِيَ أَمْرُ اللَّهِ وَهُمْ عَلَى ذَلِكَ، وعُقْر دَارِ الْمُسْلِمِينَ بِالشَّامِ".

وَهَكَذَا رَوَاهُ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو يَعْلَى الْمَوْصِلِيُّ عَنْ دَاوُدَ بْنِ رُشَيْد، بِهِ . وَالْمَحْفُوظُ أَنَّهُ مِنْ رِوَايَةِ سَلَمَةَ بْنِ نُفَيْل كَمَا تَقَدَّمَ. وَهَذَا يُقَوِّي الْقَوْلَ بِعَدَمِ النَّسْخِ، كَأَنَّهُ شَرَّعَ هَذَا الْحُكْمَ فِي الْحَرْبِ إِلَى أَلَّا يَبْقَى حَرْبٌ.

وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: ﴿حَتَّى تَضَعَ الْحَرْبُ أَوْزَارَهَا﴾ حَتَّى لَا يَبْقَى شِرْكٌ. وَهَذَا كَقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَقَاتِلُوهُمْ حَتَّى لَا تَكُونَ فِتْنَةٌ وَيَكُونَ الدِّينُ لِلَّهِ﴾ [الْبَقَرَةِ: ١٩٣] . ثُمَّ قَالَ بَعْضُهُمْ: ﴿حَتَّى تَضَعَ الْحَرْبُ أَوْزَارَهَا﴾ أَيْ: أَوْزَارَ الْمُحَارِبِينَ، وَهُمُ الْمُشْرِكُونَ، بِأَنْ يَتُوبُوا إِلَى اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ. وَقِيلَ: أَوْزَارُ أَهْلِهَا بِأَنْ يَبْذُلُوا الْوُسْعَ فِي طَاعَةِ اللَّهِ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿ذَلِكَ وَلَوْ يَشَاءُ اللَّهُ لانْتَصَرَ مِنْهُمْ﴾ أَيْ: هَذَا وَلَوْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ لَانْتَقَمَ مِنَ الْكَافِرِينَ بِعُقُوبَةٍ ونَكَال مِنْ عِنْدِهِ، ﴿وَلَكِنْ لِيَبْلُوَ بَعْضَكُمْ بِبَعْضٍ﴾ أَيْ: وَلَكِنْ شَرَعَ لَكُمُ الْجِهَادَ وَقِتَالَ الْأَعْدَاءِ لِيَخْتَبِرَكُمْ، وَيَبْلُوَ أَخْبَارَكُمْ. كَمَا ذَكَرَ حِكْمَتَهُ فِي شَرْعِيَّةِ الْجِهَادِ فِي سُورَتَيْ "آلِ عِمْرَانَ" وَ "بَرَاءَةَ" فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿أَمْ حَسِبْتُمْ أَنْ تَدْخُلُوا الْجَنَّةَ وَلَمَّا يَعْلَمِ اللَّهُ الَّذِينَ جَاهَدُوا مِنْكُمْ وَيَعْلَمَ الصَّابِرِينَ﴾ [آلِ عمران: ١٤٢] .

وَقَالَ فِي سُورَةِ بَرَاءَةَ: ﴿قَاتِلُوهُمْ يُعَذِّبْهُمُ اللَّهُ بِأَيْدِيكُمْ وَيُخْزِهِمْ وَيَنْصُرْكُمْ عَلَيْهِمْ وَيَشْفِ صُدُورَ قَوْمٍ مُؤْمِنِينَ وَيُذْهِبْ غَيْظَ قُلُوبِهِمْ وَيَتُوبُ اللَّهُ عَلَى مَنْ يَشَاءُ وَاللَّهُ عَلِيمٌ حَكِيمٌ﴾ [التَّوْبَةِ: ١٤، ١٥] .

ثُمَّ لَمَّا كَانَ مِنْ شَأْنِ الْقِتَالِ أَنْ يُقتل كثيرٌ مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ، قَالَ: ﴿وَالَّذِينَ قُتِلُوا فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ فَلَنْ يُضِلَّ أَعْمَالَهُمْ﴾ أَيْ: لَنْ يُذْهِبَهَا بَلْ يُكَثِّرُهَا وَيُنَمِّيهَا وَيُضَاعِفُهَا. وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَجْرِي عَلَيْهِ عَمَلُهُ فِي طُولِ بَرْزَخه، كَمَا وَرَدَ بِذَلِكَ الْحَدِيثِ الَّذِي رَوَاهُ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ فِي مُسْنَدِهِ، حَيْثُ قَالَ:

حَدَّثَنَا زَيْدُ بْنُ يَحْيَى الدِّمَشْقِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ ثَوْبَانَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ مَكْحُولٍ، عَنْ كَثِيرِ بْنِ مُرّة ، عَنْ قَيْسٍ الْجُذَامِيِّ -رَجُلٍ كَانَتْ لَهُ صُحْبَةٌ-قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم: "يُعْطَى الشَّهِيدُ سِتُّ خِصَالٍ عِنْدَ أَوَّلِ قَطْرَةٍ مِنْ دَمِهِ: يُكَفر عَنْهُ كُلُّ خَطِيئَةٍ، وَيَرَى مَقْعَدَهُ مِنَ الْجَنَّةِ، وَيُزَوَّجُ مِنَ الْحُورِ الْعِينِ، وَيُؤَمَّنُ مِنَ الْفَزَعِ الْأَكْبَرِ، وَمِنْ عَذَابِ الْقَبْرِ، وَيُحَلَّى حُلَّة الْإِيمَانِ" . تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ أَحْمَدُ رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ.

حَدِيثٌ آخَرُ: قَالَ أَحْمَدُ أَيْضًا: حَدَّثَنَا الْحَكَمُ بْنُ نَافِعٍ، حَدَّثَنَا إِسْمَاعِيلُ بْنُ عَيَّاشٍ، عن بَحِير ابن سَعِيدٍ، عَنْ خَالِدِ بْنِ مَعْدان، عَنِ الْمِقْدَامِ بْنِ معد يكرب الْكِنْدِيِّ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ لِلشَّهِيدِ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ سِتَّ خِصَالٍ: أَنْ يُغْفَرَ لَهُ فِي أَوَّلِ دَفْعَة مِنْ دَمِهِ، وَيَرَى مَقْعَدَهُ مِنَ الْجَنَّةِ، وَيُحَلَّى حُلَّة الْإِيمَانِ، وَيُزَوَّجَ مِنَ الْحُورِ الْعِينِ، وَيُجَارَ مِنْ عَذَابِ الْقَبْرِ، ويَأمَن مِنَ الْفَزَعِ الْأَكْبَرِ، وَيُوضَعَ عَلَى رَأْسِهِ تَاجُ الْوَقَارِ، الْيَاقُوتَةُ مِنْهُ خَيْرٌ مِنَ الدُّنْيَا وَمَا فِيهَا، وَيُزَوَّجَ اثْنَتَيْنِ وَسَبْعِينَ زَوْجَةً مِنَ الْحُورِ الْعِينِ، ويُشَفَّع فِي سَبْعِينَ إِنْسَانًا مِنْ أَقَارِبِهِ".

وَقَدْ أَخْرَجَهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ وَصَحَّحَهُ ابْنُ مَاجَهْ .

وَفِي صَحِيحِ مُسْلِمٍ عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرو، وَعَنْ أَبِي قَتَادَةَ؛ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم قَالَ: "يُغفر لِلشَّهِيدِ كُلُّ شَيْءٍ إِلَّا الدَّيْن" . وَرُوِيَ مِنْ حَدِيثِ جَمَاعَةٍ مِنَ الصَّحَابَةِ، وَقَالَ أَبُو الدَّرْدَاءِ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "يُشَفَّعُ الشَّهِيدُ فِي سَبْعِينَ مِنْ أَهْلِ بَيْتِهِ". وَرَوَاهُ أَبُو دَاوُدَ . وَالْأَحَادِيثُ فِي فَضْلِ الشَّهِيدِ كَثِيرَةٌ جِدًّا.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿سَيَهْدِيهِمْ﴾ أَيْ: إِلَى الْجَنَّةِ، كَقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِنَّ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ يَهْدِيهِمْ رَبُّهُمْ بِإِيمَانِهِمْ تَجْرِي مِنْ تَحْتِهِمُ الأنْهَارُ فِي جَنَّاتِ النَّعِيمِ﴾ [يونس: ٩] .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَيُصْلِحُ بَالَهُمْ﴾ أَيْ: أَمْرَهُمْ وَحَالَهُمْ، ﴿وَيُدْخِلُهُمُ الْجَنَّةَ عَرَّفَهَا لَهُمْ﴾ أَيْ: عَرَّفَهُمْ بِهَا وَهَدَاهُمْ إِلَيْهَا.

قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: يَهْتَدِي أَهْلُهَا إِلَى بُيُوتِهِمْ وَمَسَاكِنِهِمْ، وَحَيْثُ قَسَمَ اللَّهُ لَهُمْ مِنْهَا، لَا يُخْطِئُونَ كَأَنَّهُمْ سَاكِنُوهَا مُنْذُ خُلِقُوا، لَا يَسْتَدِلُّونَ عَلَيْهَا أَحَدًا. وَرَوَى مَالِكٌ عَنِ ابْنِ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ نَحْوَ هَذَا.

وَقَالَ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ كَعْبٍ: يَعْرِفُونَ بُيُوتَهُمْ إِذَا دَخَلُوا الْجَنَّةَ، كَمَا تَعْرِفُونَ بُيُوتَكُمْ إِذَا انْصَرَفْتُمْ مِنَ الْجُمُعَةِ.

## 47:10-13 — verses 10-13 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: {Have they not travelled} meaning: those who associate partners with Allah and disbelieve their Messenger, {in the land and seen how was the end of those before them? Allah destroyed them} meaning: He punished them for their denial and disbelief, that is: He saved the believers from among them. Therefore He said: {and for the disbelievers are the likes of it}.

Then He said: {That is because Allah is the protector of those who believe, and because the disbelievers have no protector}. It is for this reason that when Abu Sufyan Sakhr ibn Harb, the leader of the polytheists, asked on the day of Uhud about the Prophet ﷺ, and about Abu Bakr and Umar, and received no answer, he said: "As for these, they have perished." Umar ibn al-Khattab answered him, saying: "You have lied, O enemy of Allah. Rather, Allah has kept alive for you what will grieve you, and those whom you have counted are all alive." Abu Sufyan said: "A day for the day of Badr, and war is turns of fortune. You will find mutilation which I did not order and which did not displease me." Then he went off chanting: "Rise up, Hubal! Rise up, Hubal!" The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Will you not answer him?" They said: "O Messenger of Allah, what shall we say?" He said: "Say: Allah is higher and more majestic." Then Abu Sufyan said: "We have al-Uzza, and you have no Uzza." He said: "Will you not answer him?" They said: "What shall we say, O Messenger of Allah?" He said: "Say: Allah is our protector, and you have no protector."

Then He, the Almighty, said: {Indeed Allah admits those who believe and do righteous deeds into gardens beneath which rivers flow} that is, on the Day of Resurrection, {while those who disbelieve enjoy themselves and eat as cattle eat} that is, in their worldly life, they enjoy themselves in it and eat from it as cattle eat, gorging and gnawing, having no concern except for that. It is for this reason that it is established in the sound hadith: "The believer eats with one intestine, and the disbeliever eats with seven intestines."

Then He said: {and the Fire will be their abode} that is, on the day of their reckoning.

His saying: {And how many a town, stronger in might than your town which expelled you} meaning Mecca, {We destroyed them, and they had no helper}. This is a severe threat and a firm warning to the people of Mecca for their denial of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, who is the chief of the messengers and the seal of the prophets. If Allah, Mighty and Majestic, destroyed the nations before them who denied the messengers because of their denial, nations who were stronger in might than these, then what did these people suppose Allah would do with them in this world and the next? For if He lifted punishment from many of them in this world for the blessing of the presence of the Messenger, the prophet of mercy, then the punishment is stored up in full for those who disbelieve in him in their return to Him: {The punishment will be doubled for them. They were unable to hear, and they did not see} [Hud: 20].

His saying: {from your town which expelled you} means: those who expelled you from among them.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: my father mentioned, [chain] from Ibn Abbas: that when the Prophet ﷺ left Mecca for the cave, I think he said, he turned toward Mecca and said: "You are the most beloved of Allah's lands to Allah, and you are the most beloved of Allah's lands to me. Had the polytheists not expelled me, I would not have left you." So the most hostile of enemies is one who transgresses against Allah in His sanctuary, or kills someone who did not kill him, or kills for the old blood feuds of the Age of Ignorance. Then Allah sent down upon His Prophet ﷺ: {And how many a town, stronger in might than your town which expelled you, We destroyed them, and they had no helper}.

## 47:14-15 — verses 14-15 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He says: {Is he who is upon clear evidence from his Lord}, that is, upon insight and certainty in the matter of Allah and His religion, by virtue of what Allah has sent down in His Book of guidance and knowledge, and by virtue of the sound natural disposition (fitra) upon which Allah formed him, {like one for whom the evil of his deeds has been made attractive, and they have followed their own desires}, meaning this one is not like that one. It is like His saying: {Is he who knows that what has been sent down to you from your Lord is the truth like one who is blind?} [al-Ra'd: 19], and like His saying: {Not equal are the companions of the Fire and the companions of Paradise. The companions of Paradise, they are the triumphant} [al-Hashr: 20].

Then He says: {The description of Paradise which the righteous have been promised}. Ikrimah said: {The description of Paradise}, that is, its characterization: {therein are rivers of water not stagnant}. Ibn Abbas, al-Hasan, and Qatadah said: meaning not changed. Qatadah, al-Dahhak, and Ata al-Khurasani said: not foul-smelling. The Arabs say "asina al-ma'" (the water became asin) when its smell changes.

In a marfu' (elevated) hadith reported by Ibn Abi Hatim: {not stagnant} means the clear water in which there is no murkiness.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Masruq said: Abdullah said: The rivers of Paradise burst forth from a mountain of musk.

{and rivers of milk whose taste does not change}, that is, rather it is at the utmost degree of whiteness, sweetness, and richness. In a marfu' hadith: "It did not come out from the udders of livestock."

{and rivers of wine, delightful to those who drink}, that is, it is not foul in taste and smell like the wine of this world, rather it is beautiful in appearance, taste, smell, and effect. {No headache is in it, nor are they intoxicated thereby} [al-Saffat: 47], {They will not be given headache therefrom, nor will they be intoxicated} [al-Waqi'ah: 19], {white, delightful to those who drink} [al-Saffat: 46]. In a marfu' hadith: "Men did not press it with their feet."

His saying {and rivers of purified honey}, that is, it is at the utmost degree of purity, and beauty of color, taste, and smell. In a marfu' hadith: "It did not come out from the bellies of bees."

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Hakim ibn Mu'awiyah, from his father, who said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "In Paradise there is a sea of milk, a sea of water, a sea of honey, and a sea of wine. Then the rivers split off from them afterward."

Al-Tirmidhi related it in "The Description of Paradise," from Muhammad ibn Bashar, from Yazid ibn Harun, from Sa'id ibn Iyas al-Jurayri, with the same chain, and he said: hasan sahih.

Abu Bakr ibn Mardawayh said: [chain] Abu Bakr ibn Abdullah ibn Qays, from his father, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "These rivers gush forth from the Garden of Eden in a hollow, then they split afterward into rivers."

And in the Sahih: "When you ask Allah, ask Him for al-Firdaws, for it is the middle of Paradise and the highest part of Paradise, and from it the rivers of Paradise burst forth, and above it is the Throne of the Merciful."

The hafiz Abu al-Qasim al-Tabarani said: [chain] Laqit ibn Amir went as a delegate to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. He said: I said, O Messenger of Allah, upon what shall we look out in Paradise? He said: "Upon rivers of purified honey, and rivers of wine in which there is no headache nor regret, and rivers of milk whose taste does not change, and water not stagnant, and fruit. By the life of your God, what you do not know and what is better than the like of it, and purified spouses." I said: O Messenger of Allah, will we have therein righteous wives? He said: "The righteous women are for the righteous men. You will enjoy them just as you enjoyed pleasures in this world, and they will enjoy you, except that there will be no procreation."

Abu Bakr Abdullah ibn Muhammad ibn Abi al-Dunya said: [chain] Anas ibn Malik said: Perhaps you think that the rivers of Paradise flow in a trench in the ground. By Allah, they flow coursing freely over the surface of the ground, their banks are domes of pearl, and their soil is fragrant musk.

Abu Bakr ibn Mardawayh related it from the hadith of Mahdi ibn Hakim, from Yazid ibn Harun, with the same chain, as marfu'.

His saying: {and for them therein are all kinds of fruits}, is like His saying: {They will call therein for every fruit, in security} [al-Dukhan: 55], and His saying: {In both of them are two kinds of every fruit} [al-Rahman: 52].

His saying: {and forgiveness from their Lord}, that is, along with all of that.

His saying: {like one who abides eternally in the Fire}, that is, are these whose station in Paradise we have mentioned like one who abides eternally in the Fire? These are not like those, that is, one who is in the elevated ranks (darajat) is not like one who is in the depths (darakat). {and are given to drink scalding water}, that is, hot with an intensity of heat that cannot be endured. {which cuts up their intestines}, that is, it cuts up what is in their bellies of intestines and internal organs. We seek refuge in Allah from that.

## 47:16-19 — verses 16-19 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, informing us about the hypocrites in their dullness and lack of understanding, how they used to sit with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and listen to his speech but understand nothing from it. So when they left his presence {they said to those who had been given knowledge}, meaning from the Companions: {What did he say just now?}, meaning just at this moment, not comprehending what was said and not caring about it.

Allah the Almighty says: {Those are the ones upon whose hearts Allah has set a seal, and they have followed their desires}, meaning they have neither sound understanding nor sound intention.

Then He says: {But those who accept guidance, He increases them in guidance}, meaning those who sought guidance, Allah granted them success toward it, guided them to it, established them upon it, and increased them in it. {And gave them their taqwa (God-consciousness)}, meaning He inspired them with their right guidance.

As for His saying: {So do they wait for anything except that the Hour should come upon them suddenly?}, meaning while they are heedless of it, {for its signs have already come}, meaning the indications of its approach, as in His saying: {This is a warner among the former warners; the imminent [Hour] has drawn near} [al-Najm: 56-57], and as in His saying: {The Hour has drawn near and the moon has split} [al-Qamar: 1], and His saying: {The command of Allah has come, so do not seek to hasten it} [al-Nahl: 1], and His saying: {The reckoning for mankind has drawn near, while they turn away in heedlessness} [al-Anbiya: 1]. So the sending of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ is among the signs of the Hour, for he is the seal of the messengers by whom Allah completed the religion and established the proof against all the worlds. He ﷺ, informed of the indications and signs of the Hour, and clarified and explained that with what no prophet before him had been given, as is elaborated in its place.

Al-Hasan al-Basri said: The sending of Muhammad ﷺ is among the signs of the Hour. And it is as he said. For this reason it has come among his names, peace be upon him, that he is the Prophet of Repentance, the Prophet of Battle, al-Hashir (the gatherer) before whose feet the people will be gathered, and al-Aqib (the last) after whom there is no prophet.

Al-Bukhari said: [chain] Sahl ibn Sa'd said: I saw the Messenger of Allah ﷺ gesture with his two fingers like this, the middle finger and the one next to it: "I have been sent, and the Hour, like these two."

Then the Almighty says: {So how will they benefit from their reminder when it comes to them?}, meaning how can the disbelievers be reminded when the Resurrection comes to them, when that will not benefit them, as in His saying: {That Day man will remember, but how will remembrance benefit him?} [al-Fajr: 23], and {And they will say, "We believe in it," but how can they reach it from a place far away?} [Saba: 52].

As for His saying: {So know that there is no god but Allah}, this is an informing that there is no god but Allah, and it cannot be that He is merely commanding the knowledge of that alone. For this reason He conjoined to it His saying: {And ask forgiveness for your sin and for the believing men and believing women}. In the sahih it is reported that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to say: "O Allah, forgive me my error and my ignorance, and my excess in my affair, and what You know better of than I. O Allah, forgive me my jesting and my seriousness, my error and my intent, and all of that is within me." And in the sahih it is reported that he used to say at the end of the prayer: "O Allah, forgive me what I have put forward and what I have delayed, what I have concealed and what I have made known, and what I have committed in excess, and what You know better of than I. You are my God, there is no god but You." And in the sahih he said: "O people, repent to your Lord, for I ask forgiveness of Allah and repent to Him more than seventy times in a day."

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Abdullah ibn Sarjis said: I came to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and ate with him from his food. I said, "May Allah forgive you, O Messenger of Allah." I said, "Shall I ask forgiveness for you?" He said, "Yes, and for you," and he recited: {And ask forgiveness for your sin and for the believing men and believing women}. Then I looked at the protruding bone of his right shoulder, or his left shoulder (Shu'bah was uncertain which), and it was like the shape of a collection [of flesh] with warts upon it.

Reported by Muslim, al-Tirmidhi, al-Nasa'i, Ibn Jarir, and Ibn Abi Hatim, through various chains, from Asim al-Ahwal, with this content.

And in another hadith reported by Abu Ya'la: [chain] from Abu Bakr al-Siddiq, may Allah be pleased with him, from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ that he said: "Hold fast to 'there is no god but Allah' and to seeking forgiveness, and multiply them both, for Iblis said: 'I have destroyed mankind with sins, and they have destroyed me with "there is no god but Allah" and seeking forgiveness. So when I saw that, I began to destroy them with their desires, so that they think they are rightly guided.'"

And in the narrated report: "Iblis said: By Your might and majesty, I will not cease to lead them astray as long as their souls remain in their bodies. So Allah, mighty and majestic, said: By My might and majesty, I will not cease to forgive them as long as they seek My forgiveness."

The hadiths on the merit of seeking forgiveness are very numerous.

As for His saying: {And Allah knows your movement and your resting place}, meaning He knows your activity during your day and your place of settling during your night, as in His saying: {And it is He who takes your souls by night and knows what you have committed by day} [al-An'am: 60], and as in His saying: {And there is no creature on earth but that upon Allah is its provision, and He knows its resting place and its place of storage; all is in a clear register} [Hud: 6]. This view was held by Ibn Jurayj, and it is the choice of Ibn Jarir. And from Ibn Abbas: your movement is in this world, and your resting place is in the Hereafter.

Al-Suddi said: your movement is in this world, and your resting place is in your graves.

The first view is more fitting and more apparent, and Allah knows best.

## 47:20-23 — verses 20-23 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, informing about the believers, that they had longed for the legislating of jihad. But when God, Mighty and Majestic, made it obligatory and commanded it, many people flinched from it, as in His saying: {Have you not seen those who were told, "Restrain your hands, establish prayer, and give zakat," but when fighting was prescribed for them, behold, a faction among them feared men as they should fear God, or with even greater fear, and they said, "Our Lord, why have You prescribed fighting for us? If only You had delayed us for a little while." Say: "The enjoyment of this world is little, and the Hereafter is better for whoever fears God, and you will not be wronged in the slightest."} [al-Nisa: 77].

And here He says: {And those who believe say, "Why has a surah not been sent down?"} meaning, one containing the ruling concerning fighting. This is why He says: {But when a decisive surah is sent down and fighting is mentioned in it, you see those in whose hearts is disease looking at you with the look of one about to faint from death,} meaning, from their terror, fright, and cowardice at meeting the enemy. Then He says, encouraging them: {Then more suitable for them would be obedience and proper speech,} meaning, it would have been more befitting for them to listen and obey, that is, in the present situation. {Then when the matter is determined,} meaning, when the situation becomes serious and fighting is at hand, {if only they had been truthful to God,} meaning, sincere in their intention toward Him, {it would have been better for them.}

His saying: {So do you perhaps, if you turn away,} meaning, from jihad, and flinch from it, {cause corruption in the land and sever your ties of kinship?}

meaning, would you return to what you were in during the utter ignorance of the Jahiliyyah, shedding blood and severing kinship ties. This is why He says: {Those are the ones whom God has cursed, so He made them deaf and blinded their eyes.} This is a prohibition against causing corruption in the land in general, and against severing ties of kinship in particular. Indeed, God, Exalted, has commanded setting things right in the land and maintaining ties of kinship, which means doing good to relatives in word and deed and by spending wealth on them. Sound and good hadiths have come regarding this from the Messenger of God ﷺ, through numerous chains and in many forms.

Al-Bukhari said: [chain] Abu Hurayrah reported from the Prophet ﷺ that he said: "God created creation, and when He finished it, the womb (al-rahim, kinship) stood up and took hold of the waist-wrap of the Merciful, Mighty and Majestic. He said: 'Enough!' It said: 'This is the station of one seeking refuge in You from being severed.' He said: 'Would it not please you that I maintain ties with whoever maintains ties with you, and sever ties with whoever severs ties with you?' It said: 'Yes indeed.' He said: 'Then that is granted to you.'" Abu Hurayrah said: "Recite, if you wish: {So do you perhaps, if you turn away, cause corruption in the land and sever your ties of kinship?}"

Al-Bukhari then narrated it through two other chains from Muawiyah ibn Abi Muzarrid, with the same wording. The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Recite, if you wish: {So do you perhaps, if you turn away, cause corruption in the land and sever your ties of kinship?}" Muslim also narrated it from the hadith of Muawiyah ibn Abi Muzarrid, with the same wording.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Abu Bakrah said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "There is no sin more deserving that God hasten its punishment in this world, along with what is stored up for its doer in the Hereafter, than transgression and severing ties of kinship."

Narrated by Abu Dawud, al-Tirmidhi, and Ibn Majah, from the hadith of Ismail, who is Ibn Ulayyah, with the same wording. Al-Tirmidhi said: This is a sound hadith.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] from Thawban, from the Messenger of God ﷺ, who said: "Whoever wishes to be pleased with a long life and increase in provision, let him maintain ties of kinship." Ahmad alone narrated this, and it has a corroborating witness in the sound collections.

Ahmad also said: [chain] from Amr ibn Shuayb, from his father, from his grandfather, who said: A man came to the Messenger of God ﷺ and said: "O Messenger of God, I have relatives with whom I maintain ties but they sever them, I forgive them but they wrong me, and I do good to them but they do evil to me. Should I repay them in kind?" He said: "No, for then you would all be left to yourselves. Rather, be generous and excel, and maintain ties with them, for as long as you do that, a supporter from God, Mighty and Majestic, will remain with you."

This is narrated uniquely through this route, and it has a corroborating witness through another route.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] from Abdullah ibn Amr, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Kinship is suspended from the Throne, and the one who maintains ties is not the one who merely repays in kind, but rather the one who maintains ties is the one who, when his kinship ties are severed, joins them anew." Narrated by al-Bukhari.

Ahmad said: [chain] from Abdullah ibn Amr, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Kinship will be set down on the Day of Resurrection, having a hook like the hook of a spindle, speaking with an eloquent and fluent tongue, joining with whoever joined it and cutting off whoever cut it off."

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] from Abdullah ibn Amr, tracing it to the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "The merciful are shown mercy by the Merciful. Show mercy to those on earth and the One in heaven will show mercy to you. Kinship is a branch suspended from the Merciful (al-Rahman): whoever maintains it, He maintains ties with him, and whoever severs it, He cuts him off."

This was narrated by Abu Dawud and al-Tirmidhi, from the hadith of Sufyan ibn Uyaynah, from Amr ibn Dinar, with the same wording. This is the one narrated in the chain known for its primacy (tasalsul al-awwaliyyah). Al-Tirmidhi said: A good, sound hadith.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Ibrahim ibn Abdullah ibn Qariz reported that his father told him: He entered upon Abd al-Rahman ibn Awf while he was ill, and Abd al-Rahman said to him: "May kinship maintain ties with you," for the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "God, Mighty and Majestic, said: 'I am al-Rahman (the Merciful). I created kinship (al-rahim) and derived for it a name from My own name. Whoever maintains it, I maintain ties with him, and whoever cuts it off, I cut him off and sever him,' or he said: 'whoever severs it, I sever him.'"

This is narrated uniquely through this route. Ahmad also narrated it from the hadith of al-Zuhri, from Abu Salamah, from al-Raddad, or Abu al-Raddad, from Abd al-Rahman ibn Awf, with the same wording. Abu Dawud and al-Tirmidhi narrated it from the report of Abu Salamah, from his father. The hadiths on this subject are numerous.

Al-Tabarani said: [chain] from Salman, who said: The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Souls are troops mustered together; those among them who recognize one another become friends, and those among them who do not recognize one another become at odds."

And with the same chain, the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "When speech becomes apparent while deeds are hidden away, when tongues are in agreement while hearts are filled with mutual hatred, and every kinsman severs his ties of kinship, then at that point God curses them, making them deaf and blinding their eyes."

يَقُولُ تَعَالَى مُخْبِرًا عَنِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ أَنَّهُمْ تَمَنَّوْا شَرْعِيَّةَ الْجِهَادِ، فَلَمَّا فَرَضَهُ اللَّهُ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ ، وَأَمَرَ بِهِ نَكَلَ عَنْهُ كَثِيرٌ مِنَ النَّاسِ، كَقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿أَلَمْ تَرَ إِلَى الَّذِينَ قِيلَ لَهُمْ كُفُّوا أَيْدِيَكُمْ وَأَقِيمُوا الصَّلاةَ وَآتُوا الزَّكَاةَ فَلَمَّا كُتِبَ عَلَيْهِمُ الْقِتَالُ إِذَا فَرِيقٌ مِنْهُمْ يَخْشَوْنَ النَّاسَ كَخَشْيَةِ اللَّهِ أَوْ أَشَدَّ خَشْيَةً وَقَالُوا رَبَّنَا لِمَ كَتَبْتَ عَلَيْنَا الْقِتَالَ لَوْلا أَخَّرْتَنَا إِلَى أَجَلٍ قَرِيبٍ قُلْ مَتَاعُ الدُّنْيَا قَلِيلٌ وَالآخِرَةُ خَيْرٌ لِمَنِ اتَّقَى وَلا تُظْلَمُونَ فَتِيلا﴾ [النساء:٧٧] .

وقال ها هنا: ﴿وَيَقُولُ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَوْلا نزلَتْ سُورَةٌ﴾ أَيْ: مُشْتَمِلَةٌ عَلَى حُكْم الْقِتَالِ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿فَإِذَا أُنزلَتْ سُورَةٌ مُحْكَمَةٌ وَذُكِرَ فِيهَا الْقِتَالُ رَأَيْتَ الَّذِينَ فِي قُلُوبِهِمْ مَرَضٌ يَنْظُرُونَ إِلَيْكَ نَظَرَ الْمَغْشِيِّ عَلَيْهِ مِنَ الْمَوْتِ﴾ أَيْ: مِنْ فَزَعِهِمْ وَرُعْبِهِمْ وَجُبْنِهِمْ مِنْ لِقَاءِ الْأَعْدَاءِ. ثُمَّ قَالَ مُشَجِّعًا لَهُمْ: ﴿فَأَوْلَى لَهُمْ طَاعَةٌ وَقَوْلٌ مَعْرُوفٌ﴾ أَيْ: وَكَانَ الْأَوْلَى بِهِمْ أَنْ يَسْمَعُوا وَيُطِيعُوا، أَيْ: فِي الْحَالَةِ الرَّاهِنَةِ، ﴿فَإِذَا عَزَمَ الأمْرُ﴾ أَيْ: جَدَّ الْحَالُ، وَحَضَرَ الْقِتَالُ، ﴿فَلَوْ صَدَقُوا اللَّهَ﴾ أَيْ: أَخْلَصُوا لَهُ النِّيَّةَ، ﴿لَكَانَ خَيْرًا لَهُمْ﴾

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَهَلْ عَسَيْتُمْ إِنْ تَوَلَّيْتُمْ﴾ أَيْ: عَنِ الْجِهَادِ وَنَكَلْتُمْ عَنْهُ، ﴿أَنْ تُفْسِدُوا فِي الأرْضِ وَتُقَطِّعُوا أَرْحَامَكُمْ﴾

أَيْ: تَعُودُوا إِلَى مَا كُنْتُمْ فِيهِ مِنَ الْجَاهِلِيَّةِ الْجَهْلَاءِ، تَسْفِكُونَ الدِّمَاءَ وَتُقَطِّعُونَ الْأَرْحَامَ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿أُولَئِكَ الَّذِينَ لَعَنَهُمُ اللَّهُ فَأَصَمَّهُمْ وَأَعْمَى أَبْصَارَهُمْ﴾ وَهَذَا نَهْيٌ عَنِ الْإِفْسَادِ فِي الْأَرْضِ عُمُومًا، وَعَنْ قَطْعِ الْأَرْحَامِ خُصُوصًا، بَلْ قَدْ أَمَرَ [اللَّهُ] تَعَالَى بِالْإِصْلَاحِ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَصِلَةِ الْأَرْحَامِ، وَهُوَ الْإِحْسَانُ إِلَى الْأَقَارِبِ فِي الْمَقَالِ وَالْأَفْعَالِ وَبَذْلِ الْأَمْوَالِ. وَقَدْ وَرَدَتِ الْأَحَادِيثُ الصِّحَاحُ وَالْحِسَانُ بِذَلِكَ عَنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، مِنْ طُرُقٍ عَدِيدَةٍ، وَوُجُوهٍ كَثِيرَةٍ.

قَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ: حَدَّثَنَا خَالِدُ بْنُ مَخْلَد، حَدَّثَنَا سُلَيْمَانُ، حَدَّثَنِي مُعَاوِيَةَ بْنِ أَبِي مُزَرّد، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ يَسَارٍ ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ قَالَ: "خَلَقَ اللَّهُ الْخَلْقَ، فَلَمَّا فَرَغَ مِنْهُ قَامَتِ الرَّحِمُ فَأَخَذَتْ بِحَقْوِ الرَّحْمَنِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، فَقَالَ: مَهْ! فَقَالَتْ: هَذَا مَقَامُ الْعَائِذِ بِكَ مِنَ الْقَطِيعَةِ. فَقَالَ: أَلَا تَرْضَيْنَ أَنْ أَصِلَ مَنْ وَصَلَكَ وَأَقْطَعَ مَنْ قَطَعَكِ؟ قَالَتْ: بَلَى. قَالَ: فَذَاكَ . قال أبو هريرة: اقرؤوا إِنْ شِئْتُمْ: ﴿فَهَلْ عَسَيْتُمْ إِنْ تَوَلَّيْتُمْ أَنْ تُفْسِدُوا فِي الأرْضِ وَتُقَطِّعُوا أَرْحَامَكُمْ﴾ .

ثُمَّ رَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ مِنْ طَرِيقَيْنِ آخَرَيْنِ، عَنْ مُعَاوِيَةَ بْنِ أَبِي مُزَرِّدٍ، بِهِ. قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "اقرؤوا إِنْ شِئْتُمْ: ﴿فَهَلْ عَسَيْتُمْ إِنْ تَوَلَّيْتُمْ أَنْ تُفْسِدُوا فِي الأرْضِ وَتُقَطِّعُوا أَرْحَامَكُمْ﴾ وَرَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ مِنْ حَدِيثِ مُعَاوِيَةَ بْنِ أَبِي مُزَرِّدٍ، بِهِ .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا إِسْمَاعِيلُ أَخْبَرَنَا عُيَيْنَةَ بْنِ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ جَوْشَنٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ أَبِي بَكَرَةَ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "مَا مِنْ ذَنْبٍ أَحْرَى أَنْ يُعَجِّلَ اللَّهُ عُقُوبَتَهُ فِي الدُّنْيَا، مَعَ مَا يُدَّخَرُ لِصَاحِبِهِ فِي الْآخِرَةِ، مِنَ الْبَغْيِ وَقَطِيعَةِ الرَّحِمِ".

رَوَاهُ أَبُو دَاوُدَ وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ وَابْنُ مَاجَهْ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ إِسْمَاعِيلَ -هُوَ ابْنُ عُلَية-بِهِ . وَقَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: هَذَا حَدِيثٌ صَحِيحٌ.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ بَكْرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا مَيْمُونٌ أَبُو مُحَمَّدٍ الْمَرْئِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عَبَّادٍ الْمَخْزُومِيُّ، عَنْ ثَوْبَانَ، عَنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ قال: "من سَرَّهُ النِّساء فِي الْأَجَلِ، وَالزِّيَادَةُ فِي الرِّزْقِ، فَلْيَصِلْ رَحِمَهُ" . تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ أَحْمَدُ، وَلَهُ شَاهِدٌ في الصحيح.

وَقَالَ أَحْمَدُ أَيْضًا: حَدَّثَنَا يَزِيدُ بْنُ هَارُونَ، حَدَّثَنَا حَجَّاجِ بْنِ أَرْطَاةَ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ شُعَيْبٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ جَدِّهِ قَالَ: جَاءَ رَجُلٌ إِلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ إِنَّ لِي ذَوِي أَرْحَامٍ، أَصِلُ وَيَقْطَعُونَ، وَأَعْفُو وَيَظْلِمُونَ، وَأُحْسِنُ وَيُسِيئُونَ، أَفَأُكَافِئُهُمْ؟ قَالَ: "لَا إِذَنْ تُتْرَكُونَ جَمِيعًا، وَلَكِنْ جُدْ بِالْفَضْلِ وَصِلْهُمْ؛ فَإِنَّهُ لَنْ يَزَالَ مَعَكَ ظَهِيرٌ مِنَ اللَّهِ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، مَا كُنْتَ عَلَى ذَلِكَ" .

تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ مِنْ هَذَا الْوَجْهِ، وَلَهُ شَاهِدٌ مِنْ وَجْهٍ آخَرَ.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا يَعْلَى، حَدَّثَنَا فِطْر، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم: "إِنَّ الرَّحِمَ مُعَلَّقَةٌ بِالْعَرْشِ، وَلَيْسَ الْوَاصِلُ بِالْمُكَافِئِ، وَلَكِنَّ الْوَاصِلَ الَّذِي إِذَا قُطِعَتْ رَحِمُهُ وَصَلَهَا"، رَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ .

وَقَالَ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا بَهْزٌ، حَدَّثَنَا حَمَّادُ بْنُ سَلَمَةَ، أَخْبَرَنَا قَتَادَةُ، عَنْ أَبِي ثُمَامَةَ الثَّقَفِيِّ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "تُوضَعُ الرَّحِمُ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ لَهَا حُجْنَة كَحُجْنَةِ الْمِغْزَلِ، تَتَكَلَّمُ بِلِسَانٍ طُلَق ذُلَق، فَتَصِلُ مَنْ وَصَلَهَا وَتَقْطَعُ مَنْ قَطَعَهَا" .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، حَدَّثَنَا عَمْرٌو، عَنْ أَبِي قَابُوسَ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَمْرٍو -يَبْلُغُ بِهِ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ-قَالَ: "الرَّاحِمُونَ يَرْحَمُهُمُ الرَّحْمَنُ، ارْحَمُوا أَهْلَ الْأَرْضِ يَرْحَمُكُمْ أَهْلُ السَّمَاءِ، وَالرَّحِمُ شُجْنَة مِنَ الرَّحْمَنِ، مَنْ وَصَلَهَا وَصَلَتْهُ، وَمَنْ قَطَعَهَا بَتَّتْهُ".

وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ أَبُو دَاوُدَ وَالتِّرْمِذِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ سُفْيَانَ بْنِ عُيَيْنَةَ، عَنْ عَمْرٍو بْنِ دِينَارٍ، بِهِ . وَهَذَا هُوَ الَّذِي يُرْوَى بِتَسَلْسُلِ الْأَوَّلِيَّةِ ، وَقَالَ التِّرْمِذِيُّ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا يَزِيدُ بْنُ هَارُونَ، حَدَّثَنَا هِشَامٌ الدَّسْتَوائي، عَنْ يَحْيَى بْنِ أَبِي كَثِيرٍ، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ قَارِظٍ؛ أَنَّ أَبَاهُ حَدَّثَهُ: أَنَّهُ دَخَلَ عَلَى عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ عَوْفٍ وَهُوَ مَرِيضٌ، فَقَالَ لَهُ عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ: وَصَلَتْكَ رَحمٌ، أن رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم قَالَ: "قَالَ اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ: أَنَا الرَّحْمَنُ، خَلَقْتُ الرَّحِمَ وَشَقَقْتُ لَهَا مِنَ اسْمِي، فَمَنْ يَصِلْهَا أَصِلْهُ، وَمَنْ يَقْطَعْهَا أَقْطَعْهُ فَأَبُتَّهُ -أَوْ قَالَ: مَنْ يَبُتَّهَا أَبُتَّهُ".

## 47:24-28 — verses 24-28 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, commanding reflection upon the Qur'an and understanding of it, and forbidding turning away from it: {Do they not reflect upon the Qur'an, or are there locks upon hearts?} meaning: rather, there are locks upon hearts, so they are sealed shut, and nothing of its meanings reaches them.

Ibn Jarir said: Bishr narrated to us, he said: Yazid narrated to us, he said: Sa'id narrated to us, he said: Hammad ibn Zayd narrated to us, Hisham ibn Urwah narrated to us, from his father, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ recited one day: {Do they not reflect upon the Qur'an, or are there locks upon hearts?} A young man from the people of Yemen said: Rather, there are locks upon them, until Allah, Mighty and Majestic, opens them or relieves them. That young man remained in Umar's mind, may Allah be pleased with him, until he became caliph, and he sought his help.

Then the Almighty says: {Indeed, those who turned back on their heels} meaning: they departed from faith and returned to disbelief, {after guidance had become clear to them, Satan enticed them} meaning: he made that appealing and attractive to them, {and prolonged hope for them} meaning: he deceived them and beguiled them, {that is because they said to those who hated what Allah has sent down, We will obey you in part of the matter} meaning: they colluded with them and gave them sincere support inwardly upon falsehood. This is the way of the hypocrites: they show the opposite of what they conceal. Therefore Allah, Mighty and Majestic, said: {And Allah knows their secrets} meaning: He knows what they conceal and what they hide, Allah is aware of it and knowing of it, just as He said: {And Allah records what they plot by night} [al-Nisa: 81].

Then He says: {So how will it be when the angels take them in death, striking their faces and their backs?} meaning: what will be their state when the angels come to seize their souls, and the souls resist within their bodies, and the angels extract them with violence, force, and striking, just as He said: {And if you could see when the angels take the souls of those who disbelieved, striking their faces and their backs} the verse [al-Anfal: 50]. And He said: {And if you could see when the wrongdoers are in the throes of death, and the angels extend their hands} meaning: with striking, {saying: Discharge your souls! Today you will be recompensed with the punishment of humiliation for what you used to say against Allah other than the truth, and for being arrogant toward His signs} [al-An'am: 93]. Therefore He said here: {That is because they followed what angered Allah and disliked His pleasure, so He rendered their deeds worthless.}

## 47:29-31 — verses 29-31 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: {Or do those in whose hearts is disease think that Allah will never bring forth their malice?} That is: did the hypocrites believe that Allah would not expose their affair to His believing servants? Rather, He would make their affair clear and manifest until those possessed of insight would comprehend them. The Almighty revealed concerning this Surat Bara'ah, in which He made plain their disgraceful deeds and the actions they relied upon that indicated their hypocrisy. For this reason it was called al-Fadihah (the Exposer). Al-Adghan is the plural of dighn, which is what lies within the souls of envy and rancor toward Islam, its people, and those who stand in support of it.

His saying: {And if We willed, We could show them to you, and you would know them by their marks} means: the Almighty says, if We willed, O Muhammad, We could show you their persons, so that you would recognize them by sight. But the Almighty did not do this with all the hypocrites, as a concealment from Him over His creation, so that matters would be carried upon apparent soundness, and the hidden things referred back to the One who knows them. {But you will surely know them by the tone of their speech}, that is: by what becomes apparent in their speech indicating their intentions. The listener understands from which of the two parties the speaker belongs by the meanings and drift of his speech, and this is what is meant by "the tone of speech." As the Commander of the Faithful, Uthman ibn Affan, may Allah be pleased with him, said: no one conceals a secret except that Allah reveals it upon the surface of his face and in the slips of his tongue. And in the hadith: "No one conceals a secret except that Allah clothes him in its garment. If it is good, then good; and if it is evil, then evil." We have already mentioned what may be used as evidence for a man's hypocrisy, and we have spoken about hypocrisy of action and hypocrisy of belief at the beginning of "Sharh al-Bukhari," in a manner that makes repeating it here unnecessary. It has been reported in hadith the naming of a group of the hypocrites specifically. Imam Ahmad said:

[chain] Abu Mas'ud Uqbah ibn Amr, may Allah be pleased with him, said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ delivered a sermon to us. He praised Allah and extolled Him, then said: "Among you are hypocrites, so whomever I name, let him stand up." Then he said: "Stand up, so-and-so. Stand up, so-and-so. Stand up, so-and-so," until he had named thirty-six men. Then he said: "Indeed among you, or from you, so fear Allah." He said: Umar then passed by a veiled man among those who had been named, whom he recognized, and said: "What is wrong with you?" So he told him what the Messenger of Allah ﷺ had said. Umar said: "May you be cast far away for the rest of the day."

His saying: {And We will surely test you} means: We will surely try you through commands and prohibitions, {until We know those among you who strive and those who are patient, and We will test your accounts}. There is no doubt or uncertainty implied in Allah's foreknowledge of what is to occur, in the sense that it will actually happen. Rather what is meant is: until We know its occurrence. This is why Ibn Abbas says regarding similar cases: "except that We may know," meaning: that We may see.

## 47:32-35 — verses 32-35 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty informs about those who disbelieved, turned others away from the path of Allah, opposed the Messenger and defied him, and apostatized from faith after guidance had become clear to them: that such a person will never harm Allah in the least, but will only harm himself and cause himself loss on the Day of his return. Allah will render his deeds void, and He will not reward him for any of the good he had previously done, which he then followed up with his apostasy, not even to the weight of a gnat's worth of good. Rather, He will nullify it and obliterate it entirely, just as good deeds erase bad deeds.

The Imam Muhammad ibn Nasr al-Marwazi said in the Book of Prayer: [chain] Abu al-'Aliyah said: The companions of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to think that no sin could harm one who said "there is no god but Allah," just as no deed could benefit one who committed shirk. Then this was revealed: {Obey Allah and obey the Messenger, and do not render your deeds void} so they feared that sin might nullify good deeds.

It is then related through the path of 'Abdullah ibn al-Mubarak: [chain] Ibn 'Umar said: We, the companions of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, used to think that every good deed was accepted, until this was revealed: {Obey Allah and obey the Messenger, and do not render your deeds void}. So we said, "What is it that nullifies our deeds?" We said: it must be the grave sins that incur punishment and the shameful acts, until this was revealed: {Indeed, Allah does not forgive that partners be associated with Him, but He forgives what is less than that for whom He wills} [al-Nisa: 48]. When this was revealed, we ceased speaking on the matter, and we came to fear for whoever committed grave sins and shameful acts, while hoping for whoever had not committed them.

Then the Almighty commanded His believing servants to obey Him and obey His Messenger, which is their happiness in this world and the next, and forbade them from apostasy, which nullifies deeds. Hence He said: {and do not render your deeds void}, meaning through apostasy. And hence He said afterward: {Indeed, those who disbelieved and turned others away from the path of Allah, then died as disbelievers, Allah will never forgive them}, similar to His saying {Indeed, Allah does not forgive that partners be associated with Him, but He forgives what is less than that for whom He wills}.

Then He said to His believing servants: {So do not weaken}, meaning do not become feeble before the enemies, {and call for peace}, meaning a truce and reconciliation, and a cessation of fighting between yourselves and the disbelievers, while you are in a state of strength and superior numbers and equipment. Hence He said: {So do not weaken and call for peace while you are superior}, meaning while you hold superiority over your enemy. But if the disbelievers possess strength and numbers relative to the totality of the Muslims, and the leader (Imam) sees benefit in making a treaty and truce, then he may do so, just as the Messenger of Allah ﷺ did when the disbelievers of Quraysh barred him from Makkah and called him to reconciliation and a cessation of war between them for ten years, and he agreed to that.

His saying, {And Allah is with you}, contains a great glad tiding of support and victory over the enemies. {And He will never deprive you of your deeds}, meaning He will never nullify them, render them void, or strip you of them. Rather, He will pay you their reward in full and will not diminish anything from them.

## 47:36-38 — verses 36-38 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, belittling the matter of this world and diminishing its importance: {The life of this world is only play and diversion}, meaning: that is what it amounts to, except for what is done in it for the sake of Allah, Mighty and Majestic. Therefore He said: {And if you believe and fear Allah, He will give you your rewards and will not ask you for your wealth}, meaning: He is self-sufficient, free of need of you, and asks nothing of you. He has only made charity from your wealth obligatory upon you as a means of support for your poor brothers, so that its benefit may return to you and its reward come back to you.

Then He said: {If He should ask you for it and press you, you would withhold}, meaning: if He were to press you hard, you would be stingy: {and He would bring out your malice}.

Qatadah said: "Allah knew that in bringing out wealth lies the bringing out of malice." Qatadah spoke the truth, for wealth is beloved, and it is only spent on what is more beloved to the person than it is.

His saying: {Here you are, those called upon to spend in the way of Allah, but among you are those who withhold}, meaning: they do not respond to that call. {And whoever withholds only withholds from himself}, meaning: he only diminishes himself of reward, and the consequence of that returns upon him alone. {And Allah is the Self-Sufficient (al-Ghani)}, meaning: free of need of all besides Him, while everything else is ever in need of Him. Therefore He said: {and you are the ones in need (al-fuqara)}, meaning: in your very essence, in need of Him. So His description as al-Ghani (the Self-Sufficient) is an inseparable attribute of His, and the description of creation as being in need (al-fuqara) is an inseparable attribute of theirs, that is, they can never be free of it.

His saying: {And if you turn away}, meaning: from obedience to Him and following His law, {He will replace you with another people, then they will not be like you}, meaning: rather they will be a people who listen and obey Him and His commands.

Ibn Abi Hatim and Ibn Jarir said: [chain] from Abu Hurairah, may Allah be pleased with him, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ recited this verse: {And if you turn away, He will replace you with another people, then they will not be like you}. They said: O Messenger of Allah, who are these people such that if we turn away, He will replace us with them, and then they will not be like us? He said: he struck his hand upon the shoulder of Salman al-Farisi and said: "This one and his people. Even if faith were suspended at the Pleiades, men from among the Persians would attain it." This hadith is uniquely narrated by Muslim ibn Khalid al-Zanji, and it has been transmitted from him by more than one narrator. Some of the imams have raised criticism concerning him. And Allah knows best.

يَقُولُ تَعَالَى تَحْقِيرًا لِأَمْرِ الدُّنْيَا وَتَهْوِينًا لِشَأْنِهَا: ﴿إِنَّمَا الْحَيَاةُ الدُّنْيَا لَعِبٌ وَلَهْوٌ﴾ أَيْ: حَاصِلُهَا ذَلِكَ إِلَّا مَا كَانَ مِنْهَا لِلَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿وَإِنْ تُؤْمِنُوا وَتَتَّقُوا يُؤْتِكُمْ أُجُورَكُمْ وَلا يَسْأَلْكُمْ أَمْوَالَكُمْ﴾ أَيْ: هُوَ غَنِيٌّ عَنْكُمْ لَا يَطْلُبُ مِنْكُمْ شَيْئًا، وَإِنَّمَا فَرَضَ عَلَيْكُمُ الصَّدَقَاتِ مِنَ الْأَمْوَالِ مُوَاسَاةً لإخوانكم الْفُقَرَاءِ، لِيَعُودَ نَفْعُ ذَلِكَ عَلَيْكُمْ، وَيَرْجِعَ ثَوَابُهُ إِلَيْكُمْ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿إِنْ يَسْأَلْكُمُوهَا فَيُحْفِكُمْ تَبْخَلُوا﴾ أَيْ: يُحْرِجُكُمْ تَبْخَلُوا: ﴿وَيُخْرِجْ أَضْغَانَكُمْ﴾

قَالَ قَتَادَةُ: "قَدْ عَلِمَ اللَّهُ أَنَّ فِي إِخْرَاجِ الْأَمْوَالِ إِخْرَاجُ الْأَضْغَانِ". وَصَدَقَ قَتَادَةُ فَإِنَّ الْمَالَ مَحْبُوبٌ، وَلَا يُصْرَفُ إِلَّا فِيمَا هُوَ أَحَبُّ إِلَى الشخص منه.

وقوله: ﴿هَاأَنْتُمْ هَؤُلاءِ تُدْعَوْنَ لِتُنْفِقُوا فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ فَمِنْكُمْ مَنْ يَبْخَلُ﴾ أَيْ: لَا يُجِيبُ إِلَى ذَلِكَ ﴿وَمَنْ يَبْخَلْ فَإِنَّمَا يَبْخَلُ عَنْ نَفْسِهِ﴾ أَيْ: إِنَّمَا نَقَصَ نَفْسَهُ مِنَ الْأَجْرِ، وَإِنَّمَا يَعُودُ وَبَالُ ذَلِكَ عَلَيْهِ، ﴿وَاللَّهُ الْغَنِيُّ﴾ أَيْ: عَنْ كُلِّ مَا سِوَاهُ، وَكُلُّ شَيْءٍ فَقِيرٌ إِلَيْهِ دَائِمًا؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿وَأَنْتُمُ الْفُقَرَاءُ﴾ أَيْ: بِالذَّاتِ إِلَيْهِ. فَوَصْفُهُ بِالْغِنَى وَصْفٌ لَازِمٌ لَهُ، وَوَصْفُ الْخَلْقِ بِالْفَقْرِ وَصْفٌ لَازِمٌ لَهُمْ، [أَيْ] لَا يَنْفَكُّونَ عَنْهُ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِنْ تَتَوَلَّوْا﴾ أَيْ: عَنْ طَاعَتِهِ وَاتِّبَاعِ شَرْعِهِ ﴿يَسْتَبْدِلْ قَوْمًا غَيْرَكُمْ ثُمَّ لَا يَكُونُوا أَمْثَالَكُمْ﴾ أَيْ: وَلَكِنْ يَكُونُونَ سَامِعِينَ مُطِيعِينَ لَهُ وَلِأَوَامِرِهِ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، وَابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا يُونُسُ بْنُ عَبْدِ الْأَعْلَى، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ وَهْبٍ، أَخْبَرَنِي مُسْلِمُ بْنُ خَالِدٍ، عَنْ الْعَلَاءِ بْنِ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ [رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ] أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ تَلَا هَذِهِ الْآيَةَ: ﴿وَإِنْ تَتَوَلَّوْا يَسْتَبْدِلْ قَوْمًا غَيْرَكُمْ ثُمَّ لَا يَكُونُوا أَمْثَالَكُمْ﴾ ، قَالُوا: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ مَنْ هَؤُلَاءِ الَّذِينَ إِنْ تَوَلَّيْنَا اسْتَبْدَلَ بِنَا ثُمَّ لَا يَكُونُوا أَمْثَالَنَا؟ قَالَ: فَضَرَبَ بِيَدِهِ عَلَى كَتِفِ سَلْمَانَ الْفَارِسِيِّ ثُمَّ قَالَ: "هَذَا وَقَوْمُهُ، وَلَوْ كَانَ الدِّينُ عِنْدَ الثُّرَيَّا لَتَنَاوَلَهُ رِجَالٌ مِنَ الْفُرْسِ" تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ مُسْلِمُ بْنُ خَالِدٍ الزِّنْجِيُّ، وَرَوَاهُ عَنْهُ غَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ، وَقَدْ تَكَلَّمَ فِيهِ بَعْضُ الْأَئِمَّةِ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.
