## 79:1-14 — verses 1-14 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Tafsir of Surat an-Nazi'at

It is Meccan.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful

* *

Ibn Mas'ud, Ibn 'Abbas, Masruq, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, Abu Salih, Abu al-Duha, and al-Suddi said: {By those who pull out violently} refers to the angels, meaning at the time when the souls of the children of Adam are pulled out. Some of them, the angel takes their soul with violence, so it is overwhelmed in its extraction, and some of them, the angel takes their soul with ease, as though it were released from him with liveliness. That is His saying: {and those who draw out gently}. This was said by Ibn 'Abbas.

And from Ibn 'Abbas: {those who pull out} are the souls of the disbelievers; they are pulled out, then drawn out, then plunged into the Fire. Narrated by Ibn Abi Hatim.

Mujahid said: {By those who pull out violently} means death. Al-Hasan and Qatadah said: {By those who pull out violently, and those who draw out gently} refers to the stars.

'Ata' ibn Abi Rabah said regarding His saying {those who pull out} and {those who draw out}: they are the bows in battle. The correct view is the first one, and it is held by the majority.

As for His saying {and those who swim swiftly}, Ibn Mas'ud said it refers to the angels. The same has been narrated from 'Ali, Mujahid, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, and Abu Salih.

And from Mujahid: {and those who swim swiftly} means death. Qatadah said it refers to the stars. 'Ata' ibn Abi Rabah said it refers to the ships.

* *

As for His saying {and those who race ahead swiftly}, it has been narrated from 'Ali, Masruq, Mujahid, Abu Salih, and al-Hasan al-Basri that it means the angels. Al-Hasan said: they raced ahead to faith and to affirming it. And from Mujahid: death. Qatadah said it refers to the stars, and 'Ata' said it refers to the horses in the path of Allah.

* *

As for His saying {and those who direct the affair}, 'Ali, Mujahid, 'Ata', Abu Salih, al-Hasan, Qatadah, al-Rabi' ibn Anas, and al-Suddi said it refers to the angels. Al-Hasan added: they direct the affair from the heaven to the earth, meaning by the command of their Lord, Mighty and Majestic is He. They did not differ concerning this. Ibn Jarir did not decide conclusively on the intended meaning in any of these, except that regarding {and those who direct the affair} he related that it means the angels, and he neither affirmed nor denied it.

* *

As for His saying {The Day the quaking one quakes, followed by the succeeding one}, Ibn 'Abbas said: they are the first and second blowing [of the Trumpet]. Mujahid, al-Hasan, Qatadah, al-Dahhak, and more than one other said likewise.

And from Mujahid: as for the first, which is His saying {The Day the quaking one quakes}, it is like His saying, exalted is His majesty, {The Day the earth and the mountains will quake} [al-Muzzammil: 14]. And the second, which is the succeeding one, it is like His saying {and the earth and the mountains are lifted up and crushed with a single crushing} [al-Haqqah: 14].

Imam Ahmad said: Waki' related to us, Sufyan related to us, from 'Abdullah ibn Muhammad ibn 'Aqil, from al-Tufayl ibn Ubayy ibn Ka'b, from his father, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "The quaking one has come, followed by the succeeding one; death has come with what is in it." A man said: O Messenger of Allah, what do you think if I make all of my prayer for you? He said: "Then Allah will suffice you regarding what concerns you of your worldly life and your Hereafter."

Al-Tirmidhi, Ibn Jarir, and Ibn Abi Hatim narrated it from the hadith of Sufyan al-Thawri, with a chain like it, and the wording of al-Tirmidhi and Ibn Abi Hatim is: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ, when a third of the night had passed, would stand and say: "O people, remember Allah. The quaking one has come, followed by the succeeding one; death has come with what is in it."

* *

As for His saying {Hearts, that Day, will be trembling}, Ibn 'Abbas said: meaning fearful. Mujahid and Qatadah said likewise.

{Their eyes humbled}, meaning the eyes of their owners. It was attributed to the hearts only because of the close association, meaning: humbled and abject because of the terrors they have witnessed.

* *

As for His saying {They say: "Will we indeed be returned to al-hafirah"?}, meaning: the polytheists of Quraysh and those who held their view in denying the resurrection considered it far-fetched that the raising would occur after they had gone to al-hafirah, that is, the graves, as Mujahid said, and after their bodies had disintegrated and their bones had crumbled and rotted. That is why they said: {Are we indeed to become decayed bones?} It was also recited as "crumbling" (nakhirah).

Ibn 'Abbas, Mujahid, and Qatadah said: meaning decayed. Ibn 'Abbas said: that is the bone when it has decayed and the wind has entered into it. {They say: "That, then, would be a losing return."}

And from Ibn 'Abbas, Muhammad ibn Ka'b, 'Ikrimah, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, Abu Malik, al-Suddi, and Qatadah: al-hafirah is life after death. Ibn Zayd said: al-hafirah is the Fire. How numerous are its names! It is the Fire, al-Jahim, Saqar, Jahannam, al-Hawiyah, al-Hafirah, Laza, and al-Hutamah.

As for their saying {That, then, would be a losing return}, Muhammad ibn Ka'b said: Quraysh said, "If Allah brings us back to life after we die, we will surely be losers."

Allah, exalted is He, said: {It will only be a single blast, and behold, they are upon al-sahirah}, meaning: it is only a command from Allah with no repetition in it and no need for reiteration, and behold, the people are standing, looking on. That is: He, exalted is He, will command Israfil, and he will blow into the Horn the blast of resurrection, and behold, the first and the last are standing before the Lord, Mighty and Majestic is He, looking on, as He said: {The Day He will call you and you will respond with His praise, and you will think that you had remained but a little} [al-Isra': 52], and He, exalted is He, said: {And Our command is but one, like the twinkling of an eye} [al-Qamar: 50], and He, exalted is He, said: {And the matter of the Hour is only like the twinkling of an eye, or even nearer} [al-Nahl: 77].

Mujahid said: {It will only be a single blast} means a single shout.

Ibrahim al-Taymi said: The severest anger the Lord will ever have upon His creation is on the day He resurrects them.

Al-Hasan al-Basri said: a blast of wrath. Abu Malik and al-Rabi' ibn Anas said: a single blast is the final blowing [of the Horn].

* *

As for His saying {and behold, they are upon al-sahirah}, Ibn 'Abbas said: al-sahirah is the whole earth. Sa'id ibn Jubayr, Qatadah, and Abu Salih said likewise.

'Ikrimah, al-Hasan, al-Dahhak, and Ibn Zayd said: al-sahirah is the surface of the earth.

Mujahid said: they were beneath it, then they were brought out to its surface. He said: al-sahirah is the level ground.

Al-Thawri said: al-sahirah is the land of Syria. 'Uthman ibn Abi al-'Atikah said: al-sahirah is the land of Jerusalem. Wahb ibn Munabbih said: al-sahirah is a mountain beside Jerusalem. Qatadah also said: al-sahirah is Hell.

These are all strange views, and the correct view is that it is the earth, its uppermost surface.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: 'Ali ibn al-Husayn related to us, Khazar ibn al-Mubarak, the righteous shaykh, related to us, Bishr ibn al-Sariyy related to us, Mus'ab ibn Thabit related to us, from Abu Hazim, from Sahl ibn Sa'd al-Sa'idi, regarding {and behold, they are upon al-sahirah}, he said: a white, dust-colored, barren land, like a clean loaf of bread.

Al-Rabi' ibn Anas said, regarding {and behold, they are upon al-sahirah}: Allah, Mighty and Majestic is He, says: {The Day the earth will be changed into another earth, and the heavens as well, and they will come forth before Allah, the One, the Subjugator} [Ibrahim: 48], and He says: {And they ask you about the mountains, so say: My Lord will blast them completely away, and leave it a level, barren plain, in which you will see no crookedness or unevenness} [Ta Ha: 105-106]. And He said: {And the Day We shall set the mountains moving, and you will see the earth exposed} [al-Kahf: 47]: that is, the earth upon which the mountains rest will be exposed, and it is not counted as part of this earth. It is an earth upon which no sin has been committed, and upon which no blood has been shed.

تَفْسِيرُ سُورَةِ النَّازِعَاتِ

وَهِيَ مَكِّيَّةٌ.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

* *

قَالَ ابْنُ مَسْعُودٍ وَابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمَسْرُوقٌ، وَسَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ، وَأَبُو صَالِحٍ، وَأَبُو الضُّحَى، والسُّدي: ﴿وَالنَّازِعَاتِ غَرْقًا﴾ الْمَلَائِكَةُ، يَعْنُونَ حِينَ تُنْزَعُ أَرْوَاحُ بَنِي آدَمَ، فَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ تَأْخُذُ رُوحَهُ بعُنف فَتُغرق فِي نَزْعِهَا، وَ [مِنْهُمْ] مَنْ تَأْخُذُ رُوحَهُ بِسُهُولَةٍ وَكَأَنَّمَا حَلَّته مِنْ نَشَاطٍ، وَهُوَ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَالنَّاشِطَاتِ نَشْطًا﴾ قَالَهُ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ.

وَعَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿وَالنَّازِعَاتِ﴾ هِيَ أَنْفُسُ الْكُفَّارِ، تُنزع ثُمَّ تُنشَط، ثُمَّ تَغْرَقُ فِي النَّارِ. رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ.

وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: ﴿وَالنَّازِعَاتِ غَرْقًا﴾ الْمَوْتُ.وَقَالَ الْحَسَنُ، وَقَتَادَةُ: ﴿وَالنَّازِعَاتِ غَرْقًا وَالنَّاشِطَاتِ نَشْطًا﴾ هِيَ النُّجُومُ.

وَقَالَ عَطَاءُ بنُ أَبِي رَباح فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَالنَّازِعَاتِ﴾ وَ ﴿النَّاشِطَاتِ﴾ هِيَ الْقِسِيُّ فِي الْقِتَالِ. وَالصَّحِيحُ الْأَوَّلُ، وَعَلَيْهِ الْأَكْثَرُونَ.

وَأَمَّا قَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَالسَّابِحَاتِ سَبْحًا﴾ فَقَالَ ابْنُ مَسْعُودٍ: هِيَ الْمَلَائِكَةُ. ورُوي عَنْ عَلِيٍّ، وَمُجَاهِدٍ، وَسَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَير، وَأَبِي صَالِحٍ مثلُ ذَلِكَ.

وَعَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ: ﴿وَالسَّابِحَاتِ سَبْحًا﴾ الْمَوْتُ.وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: هِيَ النُّجُومُ. وَقَالَ عَطَاءُ بْنُ أَبِي رَبَاحٍ: هِيَ السُّفُنُ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَالسَّابِقَاتِ سَبْقًا﴾ رُوي عَنْ عَلِيٍّ، وَمَسْرُوقٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٍ، وَأَبِي صَالِحٍ، وَالْحَسَنِ الْبَصْرِيِّ: يَعْنِي الْمَلَائِكَةَ؛ قَالَ الْحَسَنُ: سَبَقَتْ إِلَى الْإِيمَانِ وَالتَّصْدِيقِ بِهِ. وَعَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ: الْمَوْتُ. وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: هِيَ النُّجُومُ وَقَالَ عَطَاءٌ: هِيَ الْخَيْلُ فِي سبيل الله.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَالْمُدَبِّرَاتِ أَمْرًا﴾ قَالَ عَلِيٌّ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَعَطَاءٌ، وَأَبُو صَالِحٍ، وَالْحَسَنُ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالرَّبِيعُ بْنُ أَنَسٍ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ: هِيَ الْمَلَائِكَةُ-زَادَ الْحَسَنُ: تُدَبِّرُ الْأَمْرَ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ إِلَى الْأَرْضِ. يَعْنِي: بِأَمْرِ رَبِّهَا عَزَّ وَجَلَّ. وَلَمْ يَخْتَلِفُوا فِي هَذَا، وَلَمْ يَقْطَعِ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ بِالْمُرَادِ فِي شَيْءٍ مِنْ ذَلِكَ، إِلَّا أَنَّهُ حَكَى فِي ﴿فَالْمُدَبِّرَاتِ أَمْرًا﴾ أَنَّهَا الْمَلَائِكَةُ، وَلَا أَثْبَتَ وَلَا نَفَى.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿يَوْمَ تَرْجُفُ الرَّاجِفَةُ تَتْبَعُهَا الرَّادِفَةُ﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ هَمًّا النَّفْخَتَانِ الْأُولَى وَالثَّانِيَةُ. وَهَكَذَا قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَالْحَسَنُ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ.

وَعَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ: أَمَّا الْأُولَى-وَهِيَ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿يَوْمَ تَرْجُفُ الرَّاجِفَةُ﴾ -فَكَقَوْلِهِ جَلَّتْ عَظَمَتُهُ: ﴿يَوْمَ تَرْجُفُ الأرْضُ وَالْجِبَالُ﴾ [الْمُزَّمِّلِ: ١٤] ، وَالثَّانِيَةُ-وَهِيَ الرَّادِفَةُ-فَهِيَ كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَحُمِلَتِ الأرْضُ وَالْجِبَالُ فَدُكَّتَا دَكَّةً وَاحِدَةً﴾ [الْحَاقَّةِ: ١٤] .

وَقَدْ قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ حَدَّثَنَا وَكِيع، حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عَقِيلٍ، عَنِ الطُّفَيْلِ بْنِ أُبَيِّ بْنِ كَعْبٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "جَاءَتِ الرَّاجِفَةُ، تَتْبَعُهَا الرَّادِفَةُ، جَاءَ الْمَوْتُ بِمَا فِيهِ". فَقَالَ رَجُلٌ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، أَرَأَيْتَ إِنْ جَعَلْتُ صَلَاتَيْ كُلَّهَا عَلَيْكَ؟ قَالَ: "إِذًا يَكْفِيكَ اللَّهُ مَا أهَمَّك مِنْ دُنْيَاكَ وَآخِرَتَكَ".

وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ، وَابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، وَابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ سُفْيَانِ الثَّوْرِيِّ، بِإِسْنَادِهِ مِثْلِهِ وَلَفْظُ التِّرْمِذِيِّ وَابْنِ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ إِذَا ذَهَبَ ثُلْثُ اللَّيْلِ قَامَ فَقَالَ: " يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ اذْكُرُوا اللَّهَ جَاءَتِ الرَّاجِفَةُ تَتْبَعُهَا الرَّادِفَةُ جَاءَ الْمَوْتُ بِمَا فِيهِ".

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿قُلُوبٌ يَوْمَئِذٍ وَاجِفَةٌ﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: يَعْنِي خَائِفَةٌ. وَكَذَا قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَقَتَادَةُ.

﴿أَبْصَارُهَا خَاشِعَةٌ﴾ أَيْ: أَبْصَارُ أَصْحَابِهَا. وَإِنَّمَا أُضِيفَ إِلَيْهَا؛ لِلْمُلَابَسَةِ، أَيْ: ذَلِيلَةٌ حَقِيرَةٌ؛ مِمَّا عَايَنَتْ مِنَ الْأَهْوَالِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿يَقُولُونَ أَئِنَّا لَمَرْدُودُونَ فِي الْحَافِرَةِ﴾ ؟ يَعْنِي: مُشْرِكِي قُرَيْشٍ وَمَنْ قَالَ بِقَوْلِهِمْ فِي إِنْكَارِ الْمَعَادِ، يَسْتَبْعِدُونَ وقوعَ الْبَعْثِ بَعْدَ الْمَصِيرِ إِلَى الْحَافِرَةِ، وَهِيَ الْقُبُورُ، قَالَهُ مُجَاهِدٌ. وَبَعْدَ تَمَزُّقِ أَجْسَادِهِمْ وَتَفَتُّتِ عِظَامِهِمْ وَنَخُورِهَا؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالُوا: ﴿أَئِذَا كُنَّا عِظَامًا نَخِرَةً﴾ ؟ وَقُرِئَ: "نَاخِرَةً".

وَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَقَتَادَةُ: أَيُّ بَالِيَةً. قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: وَهُوَ الْعَظَمُ إِذَا بَلِيَ ودَخَلت

الرِّيحُ فِيهِ. ﴿قَالُوا تِلْكَ إِذًا كَرَّةٌ خَاسِرَةٌ﴾

وَعَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُحَمَّدِ بْنِ كَعْبٍ، وَعِكْرِمَةَ، وَسَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ، وَأَبِي مَالِكٍ، وَالسُّدِّيِّ، وَقَتَادَةَ: الْحَافِرَةُ: الْحَيَاةُ بَعْدَ الْمَوْتِ. وَقَالَ ابْنُ زَيْدٍ: الْحَافِرَةُ: النَّارُ. وَمَا أَكْثَرُ أَسْمَائِهَا! هِيَ النَّارُ، وَالْجَحِيمُ، وَسَقَرُ، وَجَهَنَّمُ، وَالْهَاوِيَةُ، وَالْحَافِرَةُ، وَلَظَى، والحُطَمة.

وَأَمَّا قَوْلُهُمْ: ﴿تِلْكَ إِذًا كَرَّةٌ خَاسِرَةٌ﴾ فَقَالَ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ كَعْبٍ: قَالَتْ قُرَيْشٌ: لَئِنْ أَحْيَانَا اللَّهُ بعد أن نموت لنخسرن.

قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَإِنَّمَا هِيَ زَجْرَةٌ وَاحِدَةٌ فَإِذَا هُمْ بِالسَّاهِرَةِ﴾ أَيْ: فَإِنَّمَا هُوَ أَمْرٌ مِنَ اللَّهِ لَا مَثْنَوِيَّةَ فِيهِ وَلَا تَأْكِيدَ، فَإِذَا النَّاسُ قِيَامٌ يَنْظُرُونَ، وَهُوَ أَنْ يَأْمُرَ تَعَالَى إسرافيلَ فَيُنْفَخُ فِي الصُّورِ نفخَة الْبَعْثِ، فَإِذَا الْأَوَّلُونَ وَالْآخَرُونَ قيامٌ بَيْنَ يَدَي الرَّبِّ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ يَنْظُرُونَ، كَمَا قَالَ: ﴿يَوْمَ يَدْعُوكُمْ فَتَسْتَجِيبُونَ بِحَمْدِهِ وَتَظُنُّونَ إِنْ لَبِثْتُمْ إِلا قَلِيلا﴾ [الْإِسْرَاءِ: ٥٢] وَقَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَمَا أَمْرُنَا إِلا وَاحِدَةٌ كَلَمْحٍ بِالْبَصَرِ﴾ [الْقَمَرِ: ٥٠] وَقَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَمَا أَمْرُ السَّاعَةِ إِلا كَلَمْحِ الْبَصَرِ أَوْ هُوَ أَقْرَبُ﴾ [النَّحْلِ: ٧٧] .

قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: ﴿فَإِنَّمَا هِيَ زَجْرَةٌ وَاحِدَةٌ﴾ صَيْحَةٌ وَاحِدَةٌ.

وَقَالَ إِبْرَاهِيمُ التَّيْمِيُّ: أَشُدُّ مَا يَكُونُ الرَّبُّ غَضَبًا عَلَى خَلْقِهِ يَوْمَ يَبْعَثُهُمْ.

وَقَالَ الْحَسَنُ الْبَصْرِيُّ: زَجْرَةٌ مِنَ الْغَضَبِ. وَقَالَ أَبُو مَالِكٍ، وَالرَّبِيعُ بْنُ أَنَسٍ: زَجْرَةٌ وَاحِدَةٌ: هِيَ النَّفْخَةُ الْآخِرَةُ.

وَقَالَ الرَّبِيعُ بْنُ أَنَسٍ: ﴿فَإِذَا هُمْ بِالسَّاهِرَةِ﴾ وَيَقُولُ اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ: ﴿يَوْمَ تُبَدَّلُ الأرْضُ غَيْرَ الأرْضِ وَالسَّمَاوَاتُ وَبَرَزُوا لِلَّهِ الْوَاحِدِ الْقَهَّارِ﴾ [إِبْرَاهِيمَ: ٤٨] ، وَيَقُولُ: ﴿وَيَسْأَلُونَكَ عَنِ الْجِبَالِ فَقُلْ يَنْسِفُهَا رَبِّي نَسْفًا فَيَذَرُهَا قَاعًا صَفْصَفًا لَا تَرَى فِيهَا عِوَجًا وَلا أَمْتًا﴾ [طه: ١٠٥، ١٠٦] . وقال: ﴿وَيَوْمَ نُسَيِّرُ الْجِبَالَ وَتَرَى الأرْضَ بَارِزَةً﴾ [الْكَهْفِ: ٤٧] : وَبَرَزَتِ الْأَرْضُ الَّتِي عَلَيْهَا الْجِبَالُ، وَهِيَ لَا تُعَدُّ مِنْ هَذِهِ الْأَرْضِ، وَهِيَ أَرْضٌ لَمْ يَعْمَلْ عَلَيْهَا خَطِيئَةٌ، وَلِمَ يَهرَاق عَلَيْهَا دَمٌ.

## 79:15-26 — verses 15-26 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Allah, the Exalted, informs His Messenger Muhammad ﷺ about His servant and messenger Musa (peace be upon him), that He sent him to Fir'awn, and supported him with miracles, yet despite this he persisted in his disbelief and tyranny, until Allah seized him with the seizure of One Mighty and Powerful. Such is likewise the end of whoever opposes you and denies what you have brought; therefore He said at the end of the story: {Indeed, in that is a lesson for whoever fears}.

His saying, {Has there come to you the story of Musa?}: that is, have you heard news of him? {When his Lord called him}: that is, He spoke to him by calling out, {in the sacred valley}: that is, the purified one, {Tuwa}, which is the name of the valley according to the correct view, as was mentioned previously in Surat Ta Ha. So He said to him: {Go to Fir'awn, indeed he has transgressed}: that is, he acted with arrogance, rebellion, and insolence. {And say, would you [be willing] to purify yourself}: that is, say to him, would you respond to a way and a path by which you would be purified, meaning: submit and obey. {And I will guide you to your Lord}: that is, I will direct you to the worship of your Lord, {so that you would fear [Him]}: that is, so that your heart would become submissive to Him, obedient, fearful, after having been hard, wicked, and far from good. {So he showed him the greatest sign}: meaning, Musa presented to him, along with this call to the truth, a strong proof and a clear evidence of the truthfulness of what he had brought him from Allah. {But he denied and disobeyed}: that is, he denied the truth and opposed what he had been commanded to obey. The upshot of it is that his heart disbelieved, so he was not affected by Musa either inwardly or outwardly, and his knowledge that what he had brought was true did not necessitate that he was a believer in it, because recognition (ma'rifah) is the knowledge of the heart, while faith (iman) is its action, which is submission to the truth and humbling oneself before it.

* *

His saying, {Then he turned his back, striving}: that is, in opposing the truth with falsehood, which was his gathering of the sorcerers so that they might confront what Musa (peace be upon him), had brought of the dazzling miracle. {So he assembled and called out}: that is, among his people, {and said, I am your lord, the most high.}

Ibn 'Abbas and Mujahid said: Fir'awn said this statement forty years after his saying, {I do not know of any god for you other than myself} [al-Qasas: 38].

Allah, the Exalted, said: {So Allah seized him with the exemplary punishment of the last and the first}: that is, Allah took vengeance upon him with a vengeance that He made into a lesson and an exemplary punishment for those like him among the rebellious in this world, {and on the Day of Resurrection, evil is the gift that is given} [Hud: 99], as He, the Exalted, said: {And We made them leaders calling to the Fire, and on the Day of Resurrection they will not be helped} [al-Qasas: 41]. This is the correct view regarding the meaning of the verse, that what is meant by His saying, {the exemplary punishment of the last and the first}, is this world and the Hereafter. It has also been said that what is meant by that is his first statement and his second statement. And it has been said: his disbelief and his disobedience. But the correct view, about which there is no doubt, is the first.

* *

His saying, {Indeed, in that is a lesson for whoever fears}: that is, for whoever takes heed and is deterred.

## 79:27-33 — verses 27-33 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, arguing against those who deny the resurrection concerning the return of creation after its first creation: {Are you}, O people, {harder to create, or the heaven}? Meaning: rather the heaven is harder to create than you, as the Exalted said: {The creation of the heavens and the earth is greater than the creation of mankind} [Ghafir: 57], and He said: {Is not He who created the heavens and the earth able to create the like of them? Yes indeed, and He is the Supreme Creator, the All-Knowing} [Ya-Sin: 81]. So His saying {He built it} is explained by His saying {He raised its height, then made it even}, meaning: He made it lofty in structure, far from perishing, level in its regions, crowned with the stars on the dark night.

* *

His saying {And He darkened its night and brought forth its forenoon brightness} means: He made its night dark, intensely black, and its day luminous, radiant, bright, and clear.

Ibn Abbas said: {He darkened its night} means He made it dark. Mujahid, Ikrimah, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, and many others said likewise.

{And brought forth its forenoon brightness} means: He illuminated its day.

* *

His saying {And the earth, after that, He spread it out} is explained by His saying {He brought forth from it its water and its pasture}. It has already preceded in Surat Ha Mim al-Sajdah [Fussilat] that the earth was created before the heaven, but it was only spread out after the creation of the heaven, in the sense that He brought out what was in it potentially into actuality. This is the meaning of the statement of Ibn Abbas and more than one other, and Ibn Jarir chose it.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] from Ibn Abbas: {He spread it out}, and its spreading out was that He brought forth from it the water and the pasture, split open in it the rivers, and placed in it the mountains, the sands, the paths, and the hills. That is His saying {And the earth, after that, He spread it out}. The establishing of that has already preceded there.

* *

His saying {And the mountains, He anchored them firmly} means: He fixed them, made them firm, and secured them in their places, and He is the All-Wise, the All-Knowing, the Most Kind to His creation, the Most Merciful.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] from Anas ibn Malik, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "When Allah created the earth, it began to shake, so He created the mountains and cast them upon it, and it became still. The angels marveled at the creation of the mountains and said: O Lord, is there anything among Your creation harder than the mountains? He said: Yes, iron. They said: O Lord, is there anything among Your creation harder than iron? He said: Yes, fire. They said: O Lord, is there anything among Your creation harder than fire? He said: Yes, water. They said: O Lord, is there anything among Your creation harder than water? He said: Yes, the wind. They said: O Lord, is there anything among Your creation harder than the wind? He said: Yes, the son of Adam, who gives charity with his right hand and conceals it from his left."

Abu Ja'far ibn Jarir said: [chain] from Ali, who said: When Allah created the earth, it bucked and said: Will You create upon me Adam and his offspring, who will cast their filth upon me and commit sins upon me? So Allah anchored it with the mountains, some of which you see and some of which you do not see. And the first settling of the earth was like the flesh of a slaughtered camel when it is slaughtered, its flesh still quivering. Gharib (singular).

* *

His saying {As provision for you and your grazing livestock} means: He spread out the earth, made its springs gush forth, brought out what was hidden within it, made its rivers flow, caused its crops, trees, and fruits to grow, and made its mountains firm, so that it would be settled for its inhabitants and its settling would be secure. All of that as provision for His creation and for what they need of the grazing livestock which they eat and ride during the time they need them in this abode, until the appointed time comes to an end and the term expires.

## 79:34-46 — verses 34-46 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Allah, exalted is He, says: {But when there comes the great, overwhelming calamity}, and it is the Day of Resurrection. This is what Ibn Abbas said. It was given this name because it overwhelms every terrifying and dreadful matter, just as He, exalted is He, said: {And the Hour is more calamitous and more bitter} [al-Qamar: 46].

{The Day when man will remember what he strove for}, meaning: at that time the son of Adam will remember all of his deeds, the good of it and the evil of it, just as He said: {On that Day man will remember, but how will the remembrance benefit him?} [al-Fajr: 23].

{And the Hellfire will be brought into view for whoever sees}, meaning: it will be made apparent to those who look, so that the people will see it with their own eyes. {As for whoever transgressed}, meaning: rebelled and was insolent, {and preferred the life of this world}, meaning: put it before the matter of his religion and his Hereafter, {then indeed the Hellfire is the abode}, meaning: his destination will be to the Hellfire, his food will be from zaqqum (a bitter tree that grows in Hellfire), and his drink will be from boiling water. {But as for whoever feared the standing before his Lord and restrained the soul from base desire}

meaning: he feared standing before Allah, Mighty and Majestic is He, and feared Allah's judgment concerning him, and he restrained his soul from its base desire and turned it back to obedience to its Master, {then indeed Paradise is the abode}, meaning: his return, his destination, and his final resort will be to the spacious Paradise.

Then He, exalted is He, said: {They ask you about the Hour: when will it take place? What business is it of yours to mention it? To your Lord is its finality}, meaning: knowledge of it does not belong to you, nor to anyone among creation, rather its return and its reference is to Allah, Mighty and Majestic is He, for He is the One who knows its time precisely. {It weighs heavily in the heavens and the earth; it will not come to you except suddenly. They ask you as if you were well informed about it. Say: its knowledge is only with Allah} [al-A'raf: 187]. And He said here: {To your Lord is its finality}. And it is for this reason that when Jibril (peace be upon him) asked the Messenger of Allah ﷺ about the time of the Hour, he said: "The one asked about it is no more knowledgeable of it than the one asking."

* *

And His saying {You are only the warner of whoever fears it}, meaning: I only sent you to warn the people and caution them against Allah's might and His punishment. So whoever feared Allah and feared His standing and His threat followed you, and so he succeeded and prospered, while failure and loss are upon whoever denied you and opposed you.

* *

And His saying {It will be as if, on the day they see it, they had not remained except for an evening or its forenoon}, meaning: when they rise from their graves to the place of gathering, they will find the duration of the life of this world short, until it will be to them as though it had been an evening of a day or the forenoon of a day.

Juwaybir said, from al-Dahhak, from Ibn Abbas, regarding {It will be as if, on the day they see it, they had not remained except for an evening or its forenoon}: as for the evening, it is what is between noon and sunset, and {or its forenoon} is what is between sunrise and midday.

And Qatadah said: such is the time of this world in the eyes of the people when they behold the Hereafter with their own eyes.

وَقَوْلُهُ ﴿إِنَّمَا أَنْتَ مُنْذِرُ مَنْ يَخْشَاهَا﴾ أَيْ: إِنَّمَا بَعَثْتُكَ لِتُنْذِرَ النَّاسَ وَتُحَذِّرَهُمْ مِنْ بَأْسِ اللَّهِ وَعَذَابِهِ ، فَمَنْ خَشِيَ اللَّهَ وَخَافَ مَقَامَهُ وَوَعِيدَهُ، اتَّبَعَكَ فَأَفْلَحَ وَأَنْجَحَ، وَالْخَيْبَةُ وَالْخَسَارُ عَلَى مَنْ كَذَّبَكَ وَخَالَفَكَ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿كَأَنَّهُمْ يَوْمَ يَرَوْنَهَا لَمْ يَلْبَثُوا إِلا عَشِيَّةً أَوْ ضُحَاهَا﴾ أَيْ: إِذَا قَامُوا مِنْ قُبُورِهِمْ إِلَى الْمَحْشَرِ يَسْتَقْصِرُونَ مُدّة الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا، حَتَّى كَأَنَّهَا عِنْدَهُمْ كَانَتْ عَشِيَّةً مِنْ يَوْمٍ أَوْ ضُحى مِنْ يَوْمٍ.

قَالَ جُويْبر، عَنِ الضِّحَاكِ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿كَأَنَّهُمْ يَوْمَ يَرَوْنَهَا لَمْ يَلْبَثُوا إِلا عَشِيَّةً أَوْ ضُحَاهَا﴾ أَمَّا عَشِيَّة: فَمَا بَيْنَ الظَّهْرِ إِلَى غُرُوبِ الشَّمْسِ، ﴿أَوْ ضُحَاهَا﴾ مَا بَيْنَ طُلُوعِ الشَّمْسِ إِلَى نِصْفِ النَّهَارِ.

وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: وَقْتُ الدُّنْيَا فِي أَعْيُنِ الْقَوْمِ حِينَ عَايَنُوا الْآخِرَةَ.
