## 72:1-7 — verses 1-7 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Tafsir of Surah al-Jinn

It is Meccan.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

The Exalted says, commanding His Messenger ﷺ to inform his people that the jinn listened to the Qur'an, believed in it, affirmed it as true, and submitted to it. He, the Exalted, said: {Say: it has been revealed to me that a group of the jinn listened and said, indeed we heard a wondrous Qur'an that guides to right conduct}, meaning: to correctness and success, {so we believed in it and will never associate anyone with our Lord}. This passage resembles His saying, the Exalted: {And when We directed toward you a group of the jinn, listening to the Qur'an} [al-Ahqaf: 29]. We have already presented the hadiths reported concerning this in a manner that makes repeating them here unnecessary.

Concerning His statement: {and that exalted is the jadd of our Lord}, Ali ibn Abi Talha reported from Ibn Abbas regarding the Exalted's statement {jadd of our Lord} that it means: His action, His command, and His power.

Al-Dahhak reported from Ibn Abbas: the jadd of Allah is His bounties, His power, and His favor upon His creation.

It was reported from Mujahid and Ikrimah: the majesty of our Lord. Qatadah said: exalted is His majesty, His greatness, and His command. Al-Suddi said: exalted is the command of our Lord. From Abu al-Darda, and also Mujahid and Ibn Jurayj: exalted is the mention of Him. Said ibn Jubayr said: {exalted is the jadd of our Lord} means: exalted is our Lord.

As for what Ibn Abi Hatim reported: [chain] Muhammad ibn Abdullah ibn Yazid al-Muqri told us, Sufyan told us, from Amr, from Ata, from Ibn Abbas, who said: al-jadd means father. If the jinn had known that among mankind there is a father figure, they would not have said, "exalted is the jadd of our Lord."

This chain is sound, but I do not understand the meaning of this statement. Perhaps something has fallen out of the text. Allah knows best.

Concerning His statement: {He has not taken a companion nor a son}, meaning: exalted is He above taking a companion or children. That is, the jinn said, upon submitting and believing in the Qur'an: transcendent is the Lord, exalted is His majesty and His greatness, above taking a companion or a son.

Then they said: {and that the foolish one among us used to say extravagant falsehood about Allah}. Mujahid, Ikrimah, Qatadah, and al-Suddi said: by {the foolish one among us} they mean Iblis. Regarding {shatatan (extravagant falsehood)}, al-Suddi reported from Abu Malik: it means injustice. Ibn Zayd said: great wrongdoing.

It is possible that what is meant by their statement {the foolish one among us} is a generic term for anyone who claims that Allah has a companion or a son. This is why they said: {and that the foolish one among us used to say}, meaning: before his conversion, {extravagant falsehood about Allah}, meaning: falsehood and fabrication; {and we had thought that mankind and the jinn would never speak a lie against Allah}, meaning: we did not think that mankind and the jinn would collude together in lying about Allah by attributing a companion and a son to Him. But when we heard this Qur'an and believed in it, we knew that they had been lying about Allah in that regard.

Concerning His statement: {and that there were men among mankind who sought refuge with men among the jinn, so they increased them in burden}, meaning: we used to think that we had superiority over mankind because they used to seek refuge with us. That is, when they descended into a valley or a desolate place among the wilderness and elsewhere, as was the custom of the Arabs in their pre-Islamic ignorance, they would seek refuge with the mighty one of that place among the jinn, so that he would not afflict them with something harmful, just as one of them would enter the territory of his enemies under the protection, guarantee, and safe conduct of a great man. When the jinn saw that mankind sought refuge with them out of fear of them, {so they increased them in burden}, meaning: fear, terror, and dread, until they remained even more fearful of them and sought refuge with them even more, as Qatadah said: {so they increased them in burden} means: sin, and the jinn's boldness against them increased because of that.

Al-Thawri reported from Mansur, from Ibrahim: {so they increased them in burden} means: the jinn's audacity against them increased.

Al-Suddi said: a man would go out with his family and come to a piece of land and settle in it, and he would say: I seek refuge with the master of this valley among the jinn, so that no harm befalls me, my wealth, my children, or my livestock therein. He said: when they sought refuge with them instead of with Allah, the jinn would burden them with harm because of that.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Abu Sa'id Yahya ibn Sa'id al-Qattan told us, Wahb ibn Jarir told us, my father told us, al-Zubayr ibn al-Khirrit told us, from Ikrimah, who said: the jinn used to fear mankind just as mankind feared them, or even more so. When people descended into a valley, the jinn would flee, and the chief of the people would say: we seek refuge with the master of the people of this valley.

The jinn then said: we see them fearing us just as we fear them. So they drew near to the humans and afflicted them with derangement and madness. That is the meaning of Allah's statement: {and that there were men among mankind who sought refuge with men among the jinn, so they increased them in burden}.

Abu al-Aliyah, al-Rabi, and Zayd ibn Aslam said: {rahaqan (burden)} means: fear. Al-Awfi reported from Ibn Abbas: {so they increased them in burden} means: sin. Qatadah said likewise. Mujahid said: it increased the disbelievers in transgression.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] my father told us, Farwah ibn al-Mughra al-Kindi told us, al-Qasim ibn Malik, meaning al-Muzani, told us, from Abd al-Rahman ibn Ishaq, from his father, from Kardam ibn Abi al-Sa'ib al-Ansari, who said: I went out with my father from Medina on some business, and this was at the very beginning of when the Messenger of Allah ﷺ began mentioning his message in Mecca. Nightfall brought us to shelter with a shepherd of sheep. When it was midnight, a wolf came and seized a lamb from the flock. The shepherd jumped up and said: O guardian of the valley, protect your neighbor. Then a caller whom we could not see called out, saying: O Sirhan, release it. The lamb came running back until it entered among the flock, without a single bite mark on it. And Allah, the Exalted, revealed to His Messenger in Mecca: {and that there were men among mankind who sought refuge with men among the jinn, so they increased them in burden}.

He then said: it was reported similarly from Ubayd ibn Umayr, Mujahid, Abu al-Aliyah, al-Hasan, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, and Ibrahim al-Nakha'i.

It is possible that this wolf which seized the lamb, which is the offspring of a sheep, was itself a jinn, in order to terrify the human and make him afraid, and then it returned the lamb to him when he sought refuge with it, so as to lead him astray, humiliate him, and draw him out of his religion. Allah knows best.

Concerning His statement: {and that they thought, just as you thought, that Allah would never resurrect anyone}, meaning: that Allah would never send a messenger after this period. This was said by al-Kalbi and Ibn Jarir.

### Source (Arabic) — review against this

تَفْسِيرُ سُورَةِ الْجِنِّ

وَهِيَ مَكِّيَّةٌ.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

* *

يَقُولُ تَعَالَى آمِرًا رَسُولَهُ ﷺ أَنْ يُخْبَرَ قَوْمَهُ: أَنَّ الْجِنَّ اسْتَمَعُوا الْقُرْآنَ فَآمَنُوا بِهِ وَصَدَّقُوهُ وَانْقَادُوا لَهُ، فَقَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿قُلْ أُوحِيَ إِلَيَّ أَنَّهُ اسْتَمَعَ نَفَرٌ مِنَ الْجِنِّ فَقَالُوا إِنَّا سَمِعْنَا قُرْآنًا عَجَبًا يَهْدِي إِلَى الرُّشْدِ﴾ أَيْ: إِلَى السَّدَادِ وَالنَّجَاحِ، ﴿فَآمَنَّا بِهِ وَلَنْ نُشْرِكَ بِرَبِّنَا أَحَدًا﴾ وَهَذَا الْمَقَامُ شَبِيهٌ بِقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَإِذْ صَرَفْنَا إِلَيْكَ نَفَرًا مِنَ الْجِنِّ يَسْتَمِعُونَ الْقُرْآنَ﴾ [الْأَحْقَافِ: ٢٩] وَقَدْ قَدَّمْنَا الْأَحَادِيثَ الْوَارِدَةَ فِي ذَلِكَ بِمَا أَغْنَى عَنْ إِعَادَتِهِ هَاهُنَا.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَأَنَّهُ تَعَالَى جَدُّ رَبِّنَا﴾ قَالَ عَلِيُّ بْنُ أَبِي طَلْحَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿جَدُّ رَبِّنَا﴾ أَيْ: فِعْلُهُ وَأَمْرُهُ وَقُدْرَتُهُ.

وَقَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: جَدُّ اللَّهِ: آلَاؤُهُ وَقُدْرَتُهُ وَنِعْمَتُهُ عَلَى خَلْقِهِ.

وَرُوِيَ عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ وَعِكْرِمَةَ: جَلَالُ رَبِّنَا. وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: تَعَالَى جَلَالُهُ وَعَظَمَتُهُ وَأَمْرُهُ. وَقَالَ السُّدِّيُّ: تَعَالَى أَمْرُ رَبِّنَا. وَعَنْ أَبِي الدَّرْدَاءِ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ أَيْضًا وَابْنُ جُرَيْجٍ: تَعَالَى ذِكْرُهُ. وَقَالَ سَعِيدُ بْنُ جُبَيْرٍ: ﴿تَعَالَى جَدُّ رَبِّنَا﴾ أَيْ: تَعَالَى رَبُّنَا.

فَأَمَّا مَا رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ يَزِيدَ الْمُقْرِئُ حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، عَنْ عَمْرٍو، عَنْ عَطَاءٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: الْجَدُّ: أَبٌ. وَلَوْ عَلِمَتِ الْجِنُّ أَنَّ فِي الْإِنْسِ جِدًّا مَا قَالُوا: تَعَالَى جَدّ رَبِّنَا.

فَهَذَا إِسْنَادٌ جَيِّدٌ، وَلَكِنْ لَسْتُ أَفْهَمُ مَا مَعْنَى هَذَا الْكَلَامِ؛ وَلَعَلَّهُ قَدْ سَقَطَ شَيْءٌ، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ. وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿مَا اتَّخَذَ صَاحِبَةً وَلا وَلَدًا﴾ أَيْ: تَعَالَى عَنِ اتِّخَاذِ الصَّاحِبَةِ وَالْأَوْلَادِ، أَيْ: قالت الْجِنُّ: تَنَزَّهَ الرَّبُّ تَعَالَى جَلَالُهُ وَعَظَمَتُهُ، حِينَ أَسْلَمُوا وَآمَنُوا بِالْقُرْآنِ، عَنِ اتِّخَاذِ الصَّاحِبَةِ وَالْوَلَدِ.

ثُمَّ قَالُوا: ﴿وَأَنَّهُ كَانَ يَقُولُ سَفِيهُنَا عَلَى اللَّهِ شَطَطًا﴾ قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَعِكْرِمَةُ، وَقَتَادَةُ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ: ﴿سَفِيهُنَا﴾ يَعْنُونَ: إِبْلِيسَ، ﴿شَطَطًا﴾ قَالَ السُّدِّي، عَنْ أَبِي مَالِكٍ: ﴿شَطَطًا﴾ أَيْ: جَوْرًا. وَقَالَ ابْنُ زَيْدٍ: ظُلْمًا كَبِيرًا.

وَيُحْتَمَلُ أَنْ يَكُونَ الْمُرَادُ بِقَوْلِهِمْ: ﴿سَفِيهُنَا﴾ اسْمَ جِنْسٍ لِكُلِّ مَنْ زَعَمَ أَنَّ لِلَّهِ صَاحِبَةً أَوْ وَلَدًا. وَلِهَذَا قَالُوا: ﴿وَأَنَّهُ كَانَ يَقُولُ سَفِيهُنَا﴾ أَيْ: قَبْلَ إِسْلَامِهِ ﴿عَلَى اللَّهِ شَطَطًا﴾ أَيْ: بَاطِلًا وَزُورًا؛ ﴿وَأَنَّا ظَنَنَّا أَنْ لَنْ تَقُولَ الإنْسُ وَالْجِنُّ عَلَى اللَّهِ كَذِبًا﴾ أَيْ: مَا حَسِبْنَا أَنَّ الْإِنْسَ وَالْجِنَّ يَتَمَالَئُونَ عَلَى الْكَذِبِ عَلَى اللَّهِ فِي نِسْبَةِ الصَّاحِبَةِ وَالْوَلَدِ إِلَيْهِ. فَلَمَّا سَمِعْنَا هَذَا الْقُرْآنَ وَآمَنَّا بِهِ، عَلِمْنَا أَنَّهُمْ كَانُوا يَكْذِبُونَ عَلَى اللَّهِ فِي ذَلِكَ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَأَنَّهُ كَانَ رِجَالٌ مِنَ الإنْسِ يَعُوذُونَ بِرِجَالٍ مِنَ الْجِنِّ فَزَادُوهُمْ رَهَقًا﴾ أَيْ: كُنَّا نَرَى أَنَّ لَنَا فَضْلًا عَلَى الْإِنْسِ؛ لِأَنَّهُمْ كَانُوا يَعُوذُونَ بِنَا، أَيْ: إِذَا نَزَلُوا وَادِيًا أَوْ مَكَانًا مُوحِشًا مِنَ الْبَرَارِي وَغَيْرِهَا كَمَا كَانَ عَادَةُ الْعَرَبِ فِي جَاهِلِيَّتِهَا. يَعُوذُونَ بِعَظِيمِ ذَلِكَ الْمَكَانِ مِنَ الْجَانِّ، أَنْ يُصِيبَهُمْ بِشَيْءٍ يَسُوؤُهُمْ كَمَا كَانَ أَحَدُهُمْ يَدْخُلُ بِلَادَ أَعْدَائِهِ فِي جِوَارِ رَجُلٍ كَبِيرٍ وَذِمَامِهِ وَخَفَارَتِهِ، فَلَمَّا رَأَتِ الْجِنُّ أَنَّ الْإِنْسَ يَعُوذُونَ بِهِمْ مِنْ خَوْفِهِمْ مِنْهُمْ، ﴿فَزَادُوهُمْ رَهَقًا﴾ أَيْ: خَوْفًا وَإِرْهَابًا وَذُعْرًا، حَتَّى تَبْقَوْا أَشَدَّ مِنْهُمْ مَخَافَةً وَأَكْثَرَ تَعَوُّذًا بِهِمْ، كَمَا قَالَ قَتَادَةُ: ﴿فَزَادُوهُمْ رَهَقًا﴾ أَيْ: إِثْمًا، وَازْدَادَتِ الْجِنُّ عَلَيْهِمْ بِذَلِكَ جَرَاءَةً.

وَقَالَ الثَّوْرِيُّ، عَنْ مَنْصُورٍ عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ: ﴿فَزَادُوهُمْ رَهَقًا﴾ أَيِ: ازْدَادَتِ الْجِنُّ عَلَيْهِمْ جُرْأَةً.

وَقَالَ السُّدِّيُّ: كَانَ الرَّجُلُ يَخْرُجُ بِأَهْلِهِ فَيَأْتِي الْأَرْضَ فَيَنْزِلُهَا فَيَقُولُ: أَعُوذُ بِسَيِّدِ هَذَا الْوَادِي مِنَ الْجِنِّ أَنْ أضَرّ أَنَا فِيهِ أَوْ مَالِي أَوْ وَلَدِي أَوْ مَاشِيَتِي، قَالَ: فَإِذَا عَاذَ بِهِمْ مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ، رَهقَتهم الْجِنُّ الْأَذَى عِنْدَ ذَلِكَ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو سعيد يحيى بْنُ سَعِيدٍ الْقَطَّانُ، حَدَّثَنَا وَهْبُ بْنُ جَرِيرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا الزُّبَيْرُ بْنُ الخرِّيت، عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ قَالَ: كَانَ الْجِنُّ يَفْرَقُون مِنَ الْإِنْسِ كَمَا يفرَق الْإِنْسُ مِنْهُمْ أَوْ أَشَدَّ، وَكَانَ الْإِنْسُ إِذَا نَزَلُوا وَادِيًا هَرَبَ الْجِنُّ، فَيَقُولُ سَيِّدُ الْقَوْمِ: نَعُوذُ بِسَيِّدِ أَهْلِ هَذَا الْوَادِي.

فَقَالَ الْجِنُّ: نَرَاهُمْ يَفْرَقُونَ مِنَّا كَمَا نَفْرَقُ مِنْهُمْ. فَدَنَوْا مِنَ الْإِنْسِ فَأَصَابُوهُمْ بِالْخَبَلِ وَالْجُنُونِ، فَذَلِكَ قَوْلُ اللَّهِ: ﴿وَأَنَّهُ كَانَ رِجَالٌ مِنَ الإنْسِ يَعُوذُونَ بِرِجَالٍ مِنَ الْجِنِّ فَزَادُوهُمْ رَهَقًا﴾

وَقَالَ أَبُو الْعَالِيَةِ، وَالرَّبِيعُ، وَزَيْدُ بْنُ أَسْلَمَ: ﴿رَهَقًا﴾ أَيْ: خَوْفًا. وَقَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: ﴿فَزَادُوهُمْ رَهَقًا﴾ أَيْ: إِثْمًا. وَكَذَا قَالَ قتادة. وقال مجاهد: زاد الكفار طغيانا.

## 72:8-10 — verses 8-10 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He, exalted, informs about the jinn at the time when God sent His Messenger Muhammad ﷺ and revealed to him the Qur'an. Part of His preservation of it was that the sky was filled with intense guards, and it was protected in all its regions, and the devils were driven away from the places where they used to sit before that, so that they would not steal anything from the Qur'an and cast it upon the tongues of soothsayers, causing the matter to become confused and mixed up, so that no one would know who was truthful. This is part of God's kindness toward His creation, His mercy toward His servants, and His preservation of His Mighty Book. It is for this that the jinn said: {And indeed we sought to reach the heaven, but we found it filled with intense guards and shooting stars. And indeed we used to sit in places therein to listen, but whoever listens now will find a shooting star lying in wait for him.} That is: whoever seeks to steal a hearing today will find a shooting star lying in wait for him, which he cannot pass or overstep, but rather it will annihilate and destroy him. {And indeed we do not know whether evil is intended for those on earth, or whether their Lord intends for them right guidance.} That is: we do not know about this matter which has occurred in the sky, whether evil is intended for those on earth, or whether their Lord intends for them right guidance. This reflects their propriety in expression, in that they attributed evil to no specific doer, while they ascribed good directly to God, mighty and majestic. It has been reported in the authentic hadith: "and evil is not attributed to You."

Now, stars had been shot down before this, but not frequently, only occasionally, from time to time. This is as in the hadith of Ibn Abbas: "While we were sitting with the Messenger of God ﷺ, a star suddenly shot down and lit up. He said: 'What did you used to say about this?' We said: 'We used to say a great one has been born, or a great one has died.' He said: 'It is not like that; rather, when God decrees a matter in heaven...'" and he mentioned the rest of the hadith. We have already related it in full in Surat Saba. This was the reason that drove them to seek out the cause of this, so they went about striking through the easts and wests of the earth, until they found the Messenger of God ﷺ reciting with his companions in prayer. They then knew that this was the one for whose sake the sky had been guarded. So some of them believed, while those who remained persisted in their rebellion, as the hadith of Ibn Abbas mentioned previously in connection with His statement in Surat al-Ahqaf: {And when We directed to you a company of the jinn, listening to the Qur'an} (the verse, 29).

There is no doubt that when this event occurred, namely the increase of shooting stars in the sky and their being cast down, it terrified both mankind and the jinn, disturbed them, and alarmed them. They thought that this signified the destruction of the world, as al-Suddi said: the sky used not to be guarded except when there was on earth a prophet or a manifest religion of God. So the devils, before Muhammad ﷺ, had taken up seats in the lowest heaven, listening to whatever matter occurred in the sky. When God sent Muhammad as a prophet, they were struck down with stars one night, and the people of Ta'if were terrified by this. They said: "The people of heaven have perished," when they saw the intensity of the fire in the sky and the scattering of the shooting stars. So they began freeing their slaves and setting loose their livestock. Then Abd Yalil ibn Amr ibn Umayr said to them: "Woe to you, people of Ta'if! Hold back from your wealth, and look at the positions of the stars. If you see them fixed in their places, then the people of heaven have not perished; this is only because of Ibn Abi Kabsha," meaning Muhammad ﷺ, "but if you do not see them, then the people of heaven have indeed perished." So they looked and saw them in place, and they held back from their wealth.

The devils were terrified that night, and they went to Iblis and told him what had happened to them. He said: "Bring me a handful of soil from every land, so that I may smell it." So they brought it to him, and he smelled it and said: "Your master is in Mecca." So he sent seven of the jinn of Nasibin, and they came to Mecca and found the Messenger of God ﷺ standing in prayer in the Sacred Mosque, reciting the Qur'an. They drew near to him out of eagerness for the Qur'an, until their chests nearly touched him. Then they submitted to Islam. God, exalted, then revealed their affair to His Prophet ﷺ. We have discussed this section exhaustively at the beginning of the account of the Prophetic mission in the extended Book of the Sira (biography). And God knows best, and to God belongs all praise and grace.

## 72:11-17 — verses 11-17 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He is speaking, informing about the jinn: that they said, speaking about themselves, {And among us are the righteous, and among us are less than that}, meaning other than that, {We were of different ways, diverging paths}, meaning multiple and differing paths and scattered opinions.

Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, and several others said: {We were of different ways, diverging paths} means: among us are the believer and among us is the disbeliever.

Ahmad ibn Sulayman al-Najjad said in his Amali: [chain] Al-A'mash said: A jinn used to visit us in the evening, so I said to him: "What food is most beloved to you?" He said: "Rice." He said: So we brought it to them, and I would see the morsels being lifted while I saw no one. So I said: "Do you have among you these differing inclinations that we have?" He said: "Yes." I said: "What about the Rafidah among you?" He said: "They are the worst of us." I presented this chain of transmission to our teacher, the hafiz Abu al-Hajjaj al-Mizzi, and he said: "This is a sound chain going back to al-A'mash."

The hafiz Ibn Asakir mentioned, in the biographical entry on al-Abbas ibn Ahmad al-Dimashqi, that he said: I heard some of the jinn, while I was in a dwelling of mine at night, reciting:

Hearts that love has worn away until their paths became attached, in every west and east...

Wandering in love of Allah, and Allah is their Lord, attached to Allah apart from all created things.

And His saying: {And that we thought that we could never outmatch Allah upon the earth, nor could we ever escape Him by flight}, meaning: we know that the power of Allah rules over us and that we cannot escape Him on the earth, even if we went to the utmost lengths in fleeing, for He has power over us that none of us can escape.

{And that when we heard the guidance, we believed in it}. They are boasting of this, and it is indeed something to boast of for them, a lofty honor and a fine attribute.

Their saying: {So whoever believes in his Lord shall fear no loss and no burden}. Ibn Abbas, Qatadah, and others said: he shall not fear that any of his good deeds will be diminished, nor that he will be made to bear sins other than his own, just as He, may He be exalted, said: {So he will fear no injustice and no burden} [Taha: 112].

{And among us are the Muslims (those submitting), and among us are the Qasitun}, meaning: among us is the Muslim and among us is the Qasit, which is one who deviates and turns away from the truth, as opposed to al-Muqsit, which is the just one. {And whoever submits, then those have sought out right guidance for themselves}, meaning: they sought salvation for themselves.

{But as for the Qasitun, they will be firewood for Hell}, meaning: fuel by which it is kindled.

His saying: {And that if they had remained upright upon the path, We would have given them abundant water to drink, so that We might test them therein}. The commentators differed over the meaning of this into two opinions.

The first: that if the Qasitun had remained upright upon the path of Islam, turned to it and persisted upon it, {We would have given them abundant water to drink}, meaning plentiful water, and what is meant by this is abundance of provision, as in His saying: {And if only they had upheld the Torah and the Gospel and what was sent down to them from their Lord, they would have eaten from above them and from beneath their feet} [al-Ma'idah: 66], and as in His saying: {And if only the people of the towns had believed and been mindful of Allah, We would have opened upon them blessings from the heaven and the earth} [al-A'raf: 96]. According to this, the meaning of His saying {so that We might test them therein} would be: so that We might try them, as Malik said, on the authority of Zayd ibn Aslam: {so that We might test them} means so that We might put them to trial, distinguishing whoever persists upon guidance from whoever reverts to error.

Mention of those who held this view: al-Awfi reported from Ibn Abbas: {And that if they had remained upright upon the path}, meaning by uprightness: obedience. Mujahid said: {And that if they had remained upright upon the path} means Islam. Sa'id ibn Jubayr, Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab, Ata', al-Suddi, and Muhammad ibn Ka'b al-Qurazi said likewise.

Qatadah said: {And that if they had remained upright upon the path} means: had they all believed, We would have granted them abundance in this worldly life.

Mujahid said: {And that if they had remained upright upon the path}, meaning the path of truth. Al-Dahhak said likewise, citing as evidence the two verses we have mentioned. All of these, or most of them, said regarding {so that We might test them therein} that it means: so that We might try them by means of it.

Muqatil said: it was revealed concerning the disbelievers of Quraysh when rain was withheld from them for seven years.

The second opinion: {And that if they had remained upright upon the path}, that is, the path of error, {We would have given them abundant water to drink}, meaning: We would have expanded provision for them as a means of gradual enticement (istidraj), just as He said: {So when they forgot what they had been reminded of, We opened upon them the gates of all things, until, when they rejoiced in what they had been given, We seized them suddenly, and thereupon they were in utter despair} [al-An'am: 44], and as in His saying: {Do they think that what We extend to them of wealth and children means that We hasten to them good things? Rather, they do not perceive} [al-Mu'minun: 55-56]. This is the view of Abu Mijlaz, Lahiq ibn Humayd, for regarding His saying {And that if they had remained upright upon the path} he said: meaning the path of error. Ibn Jarir and Ibn Abi Hatim reported this, and al-Baghawi related it from al-Rabi' ibn Anas, Zayd ibn Aslam, al-Kalbi, and Ibn Kaysan. This view has merit and is supported by His saying {so that We might test them therein}.

His saying: {And whoever turns away from the remembrance of his Lord, He will lead him into a climbing, wearisome punishment}, meaning: a punishment that is arduous, severe, distressing, and painful.

Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, Ikrimah, Qatadah, and Ibn Zayd said: {a climbing, wearisome punishment} means: hardship with no ease accompanying it.

From Ibn Abbas: a mountain in Hell. From Sa'id ibn Jubayr: a well within it.

## 72:18-24 — verses 18-24 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, commanding His servants to affirm His oneness in the sphere of worship, and that no one else is to be called upon with Him, nor is any partner to be ascribed to Him, as Qatadah said concerning His statement: {And that the mosques are for Allah, so do not call upon anyone else along with Allah}, he said: The Jews and the Christians, when they entered their churches and synagogues, would ascribe partners to Allah, so Allah commanded His Prophet ﷺ that they should affirm His oneness alone.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: Ali bin al-Husayn mentioned, [chain] Isma'il the son of the daughter of al-Suddi, a man he named informed us, from al-Suddi, from Abu Malik (or Abu Salih), from Ibn Abbas concerning His statement: {And that the mosques are for Allah, so do not call upon anyone else along with Allah}, he said: On the day this verse was revealed, there was no mosque on earth except the Sacred Mosque and the mosque of Iliya, that is, Jerusalem (Bayt al-Maqdis).

Al-A'mash said: The jinn said, "O Messenger of Allah, permit us to attend the prayers with you in your mosque." So Allah revealed: {And that the mosques are for Allah, so do not call upon anyone else along with Allah}, meaning: pray, and do not mix with the people.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Ibn Humayd, Mihran, Sufyan, from Isma'il bin Abi Khalid, from Mahmud, from Sa'id bin Jubayr, concerning {And that the mosques are for Allah}, he said: The jinn said to the Prophet of Allah ﷺ: "How can we come to the mosque while we are far away [from you]? And how can we attend the prayer while we are far from you?" So it was revealed: {And that the mosques are for Allah, so do not call upon anyone else along with Allah}.

Sufyan said, from Khusayf, from Ikrimah: It was revealed concerning all mosques.

Sa'id bin Jubayr said: It was revealed concerning the limbs of prostration, meaning: they belong to Allah, so do not prostrate with them to anyone other than Him. In connection with this view they mentioned the authentic hadith, narrated by Abdullah bin Tawus, from his father, from Ibn Abbas, may Allah be pleased with them both, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "I have been commanded to prostrate on seven bones: on the forehead," and he pointed with his hands to his nose, "and the hands, and the knees, and the tips of the feet."

Concerning His statement: {And that when the servant of Allah stood calling upon Him, they almost became a compressed crowd around him}, al-Awfi said, from Ibn Abbas: When they heard the Prophet ﷺ reciting the Qur'an, they almost mounted upon him out of eagerness, when they heard him reciting the Qur'an, and they drew near to him, and he was not aware of them, until the Messenger came to him and began to recite to him: {Say: it has been revealed to me that a group of the jinn listened} listening to the Qur'an.

This is one view, and it is also narrated from al-Zubayr bin al-Awwam, may Allah be pleased with him.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Muhammad bin Ma'mar, Abu Muslim, from Abu Awanah, from Abu Bishr, from Sa'id bin Jubayr, from Ibn Abbas, who said: The jinn said to their people, concerning {And that when the servant of Allah stood calling upon Him, they almost became a compressed crowd around him}, he said: When they saw him praying and his companions bowing when he bowed and prostrating when he prostrated, they said, marveling at the obedience of his companions to him, then they said to their people: {When the servant of Allah stood calling upon Him, they almost became a compressed crowd around him}.

This is a second view, also narrated from Sa'id bin Jubayr.

Al-Hasan said: When the Messenger of Allah ﷺ stood saying "There is no god but Allah" and calling the people to their Lord, the Arabs nearly all gathered together against him in a compressed crowd.

Qatadah said concerning His statement: {And that when the servant of Allah stood calling upon Him, they almost became a compressed crowd around him}, he said: The humans and the jinn banded together against this matter in order to extinguish it, but Allah refused to do anything but grant him victory, cause his cause to prevail, and make him triumph over whoever opposed him.

This is a third view, narrated from Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, and Sa'id bin Jubayr, and it is the view of Ibn Zayd, and the preference of Ibn Jarir, and it is the clearer view because of what follows it: {Say: I only call upon my Lord, and I do not ascribe any partner to Him}, meaning: the Messenger said to them, when they harmed him, opposed him, denied him, and banded together against him in order to nullify the truth he had brought, and united in enmity toward him: {I only call upon my Lord}, meaning: I only worship my Lord alone, He has no partner, and I seek refuge in Him and place my trust in Him, {and I do not ascribe any partner to Him}.

Concerning His statement: {Say: I possess no power to harm you nor to guide you rightly}, meaning: I am only a human being like you, to whom revelation is sent, and a servant among the servants of Allah, I have no authority whatsoever over your guidance or your going astray, rather the return in all of that is to Allah, Mighty and Majestic.

Then He informs, on his behalf, that no one can shelter him from Allah, meaning: if I were to disobey Him, no one would be able to save me from His punishment, {and I will never find, besides Him, any refuge}. Mujahid, Qatadah, and al-Suddi said: meaning no shelter. Qatadah also said, concerning {Say: none can shelter me from Allah, and I will never find, besides Him, any refuge}: meaning no helper and no shelter. And in another report: no protector and no place of retreat.

Concerning the statement of the Exalted: {except a proclamation from Allah and His messages}, some of them said: it is an exception from His statement {I possess no power to harm you nor to guide you rightly, except a proclamation}. And it is possible that it is an exception from His statement {none can shelter me from Allah}, meaning: nothing shelters me from Him and delivers me except my conveying the message which He has obligated me to deliver, as the Exalted said: {O Messenger, convey what has been revealed to you from your Lord, and if you do not, then you have not conveyed His message. And Allah will protect you from the people} [al-Ma'idah: 67].

Concerning His statement: {And whoever disobeys Allah and His Messenger, then indeed for him is the fire of Hell, abiding therein forever}, meaning: I only convey to you the message of Allah, and whoever disobeys after that, then his recompense for that is the fire of Hell, abiding therein forever, meaning there is no turning away from it for them, nor any exit from it for them.

Concerning His statement: {until, when they see what they are promised, then they will know who is weaker in helper and fewer in number}, meaning: until these polytheists among the jinn and mankind see what they are threatened with on the Day of Resurrection, they will know on that day who is weaker in helper and fewer in number: they, or the believers who affirm the oneness of Allah, Mighty and Majestic. That is: rather the polytheists have no helper at all, and they are fewer in number than the soldiers of Allah, Mighty and Majestic.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَأَنَّهُ لَمَّا قَامَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ يَدْعُوهُ كَادُوا يَكُونُونَ عَلَيْهِ لِبَدًا﴾ قَالَ الْعَوْفِيُّ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ يَقُولُ: لَمَّا سَمِعُوا النَّبِيَّ ﷺ يَتْلُو الْقُرْآنَ كَادُوا يَرْكَبُونَهُ؛ مِنَ الْحِرْصِ، لَمَّا سَمِعُوهُ يَتْلُو الْقُرْآنَ، وَدَنَوْا مِنْهُ فَلَمْ يَعْلَمْ بِهِمْ حَتَّى أَتَاهُ الرَّسُولُ فَجَعَلَ يُقْرِئُهُ: ﴿قُلْ أُوحِيَ إِلَيَّ أَنَّهُ اسْتَمَعَ نَفَرٌ مِنَ الْجِنِّ﴾ يَسْتَمِعُونَ الْقُرْآنَ.

هَذَا قَوْلٌ، وَهُوَ مَرْوِيٌّ عَنِ الزُّبَيْرِ بْنِ الْعَوَّامِ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنِي مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ مَعْمَرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو مُسْلِمٍ، عَنْ أبي عَوَانة، عن أبي بشر، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: قَالَ الْجِنُّ لِقَوْمِهِمْ: ﴿وَأَنَّهُ لَمَّا قَامَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ يَدْعُوهُ كَادُوا يَكُونُونَ عَلَيْهِ لِبَدًا﴾ قَالَ: لَمَّا رَأَوْهُ يُصَلِّي وَأَصْحَابُهُ، يَرْكَعُونَ بِرُكُوعِهِ وَيَسْجُدُونَ بِسُجُودِهِ، قَالُوا: عَجِبُوا مِنْ طَوَاعِيَةِ أَصْحَابِهِ لَهُ، قَالَ: فَقَالُوا لِقَوْمِهِمْ: ﴿لَمَّا قَامَ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ يَدْعُوهُ كَادُوا يَكُونُونَ عَلَيْهِ لِبَدًا﴾

وَقَالَ الْحَسَنُ: لَمَّا قَامَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ" وَيَدْعُو النَّاسَ إِلَى رَبِّهِمْ، كَادَتِ الْعَرَبُ تَلْبُدُ عَلَيْهِ جَمِيعًا.

هَذَا قَوْلٌ ثَالِثٌ، وَهُوَ مَرْوِيٌّ عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٍ، وَسَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ، وَقَوْلُ ابْنِ زَيْدٍ، وَاخْتِيَارُ ابْنِ جَرِيرٍ، وَهُوَ الْأَظْهَرُ لِقَوْلِهِ بَعْدَهُ: ﴿قُلْ إِنَّمَا أَدْعُو رَبِّي وَلا أُشْرِكُ بِهِ أَحَدًا﴾ أَيْ: قَالَ لَهُمُ الرَّسُولُ-لَمَّا آذَوْهُ وَخَالَفُوهُ وَكَذَّبُوهُ وَتَظَاهَرُوا عَلَيْهِ، لِيُبْطِلُوا مَا جَاءَ بِهِ مِنَ الْحَقِّ وَاجْتَمَعُوا عَلَى عَدَاوَتِهِ: ﴿إِنَّمَا أَدْعُو رَبِّي﴾ أَيْ: إِنَّمَا أَعْبُدُ رَبِّي وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ، وَأَسْتَجِيرُ بِهِ وَأَتَوَكَّلُ عَلَيْهِ، ﴿وَلا أُشْرِكُ بِهِ أَحَدًا﴾

## 72:25-28 — verses 25-28 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, commanding His Messenger ﷺ to say to the people: that he has no knowledge of the time of the Hour, and does not know whether its time is near or far. {Say: I do not know whether that which you are promised is near, or whether my Lord will set for it a term} that is, a long duration.

In this noble verse there is proof that the hadith which many of the ignorant circulate, that he, peace be upon him, will not complete a thousand years under the earth, is a lie with no basis, and we have not seen it in any book. He ﷺ used to be asked about the time of the Hour and would not answer concerning it. When Gabriel appeared to him in the form of a bedouin, among what he asked him was that he said: O Muhammad, tell me about the Hour. He said: "The one asked about it is no more knowledgeable than the one asking." And when that bedouin called out to him in a loud voice, saying: O Muhammad, when is the Hour? He said: "Woe to you. It is coming, so what have you prepared for it?" He said: I have not prepared for it much prayer or fasting, but I love Allah and His Messenger. He said: "Then you are with whomever you love." Anas said: The Muslims did not rejoice at anything as they rejoiced at this hadith.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] ... from Abu Sa'id al-Khudri, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "O children of Adam, if you have reason, then count yourselves among the dead. By the One in whose hand is my soul, that which you are promised is surely coming."

Abu Dawud said at the end of the "Book of Tribulations": [chain] ... from Abu Tha'labah al-Khushani, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Allah will never fail this nation for half a day." Abu Dawud alone transmitted it. Then Abu Dawud said:

[chain] ... from Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas, from the Prophet ﷺ, that he said: "I truly hope that my nation will not fail before its Lord such that He delays them half a day." It was said to Sa'd: How much is half a day? He said: Five hundred years. Abu Dawud alone transmitted it.

His saying: {Knower of the unseen, He does not disclose His unseen to anyone, except to a messenger whom He has approved} this is like His saying, Exalted is He: {And they encompass nothing of His knowledge except what He wills} [al-Baqarah: 255]. Thus He says here likewise: that He knows the unseen and the manifest, and that none of His creation attains anything of His knowledge except what He, Exalted is He, causes them to attain. For this reason He said: {He does not disclose His unseen to anyone, except to a messenger whom He has approved}, and this includes both the angelic messenger and the human messenger.

Then He said: {Then indeed He sends before him and behind him watchers} that is, He singles him out with additional successive angels who guard him by Allah's command, and who accompany him regarding what he carries of Allah's revelation. For this reason He said: {That He may know that they have indeed conveyed the messages of their Lord, and He has encompassed what is with them, and He has counted everything in number}.

The commentators differed regarding the pronoun in His saying {That He may know}, as to whom it refers. It was said that it refers to the Prophet ﷺ.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] ... from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, concerning His saying: {Knower of the unseen, He does not disclose His unseen to anyone, except to a messenger whom He has approved. Then indeed He sends before him and behind him watchers}, he said: four guardians among the angels together with Gabriel. {That he may know}, meaning Muhammad ﷺ, {that they have indeed conveyed the messages of their Lord, and He has encompassed what is with them, and He has counted everything in number}.

Ibn Abi Hatim narrated it from the hadith of Ya'qub al-Qummi with the same chain. Likewise it was narrated by al-Dahhak, al-Suddi, and Yazid ibn Abi Habib.

Abd al-Razzaq said, from Ma'mar, from Qatadah, concerning {That he may know that they have indeed conveyed the messages of their Lord}: he said: that the Prophet of Allah may know that the messengers have indeed conveyed on behalf of Allah, and that the angels have preserved it and defended it. Likewise it was narrated by Sa'id ibn Abi Arubah, from Qatadah. Ibn Jarir preferred this view.

Other than this was also said, as narrated by al-Awfi from Ibn Abbas concerning His saying: {except to a messenger whom He has approved. Then indeed He sends before him and behind him watchers}, he said: these are successive angels who guard the Prophet from Satan, until what he has been sent with becomes clear to them, and that is when He says, that the people of shirk may know that they have indeed conveyed the messages of their Lord.

Similarly, Ibn Abi Najih said, from Mujahid, concerning {That he may know that they have indeed conveyed the messages of their Lord}: he said: that whoever denied the messengers may know that they have indeed conveyed the messages of their Lord. There is reservation regarding this.

Al-Baghawi said: Ya'qub read it as "li-yu'lama" (with damma), meaning: that the people may know that the messengers have indeed conveyed their message.

It is also possible that the pronoun refers to Allah, Mighty and Majestic, and this is an opinion related by Ibn al-Jawzi in "Zad al-Masir." The meaning in that case would be: that He preserves His messengers by means of His angels so that they may be able to deliver His messages, and He preserves what has been made clear to them of the revelation, so that it may be known that they have indeed conveyed the messages of their Lord. This would be like His saying: {And We did not make the direction you used to face except that We might know who would follow the Messenger from who would turn on his heels} [al-Baqarah: 143], and like His saying: {And Allah will surely know those who believe, and He will surely know the hypocrites} [al-Ankabut: 11], and similar verses, together with the knowledge that He, Exalted is He, certainly knows all things before they occur, without any doubt. For this reason He said after this: {and He has encompassed what is with them, and He has counted everything in number}.

وَقَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ، عَنْ مَعْمَر عَنْ قَتَادَةَ: ﴿لِيَعْلَمَ أَنْ قَدْ أَبْلَغُوا رِسَالاتِ رَبِّهِمْ﴾ قَالَ: لِيَعْلَمَ نَبِيُّ اللَّهِ أَنَّ الرُّسُلَ قَدْ بَلَّغَتْ عَنِ اللَّهِ، وَأَنَّ الْمَلَائِكَةَ حَفِظَتْهَا وَدَفَعَتْ عَنْهَا. وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ سَعِيدُ بْنُ أَبِي عَرُوبَةَ، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ. وَاخْتَارَهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ.

وَقِيلَ غَيْرُ ذَلِكَ، كَمَا رَوَاهُ الْعَوْفِيُّ عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿إِلا مَنِ ارْتَضَى مِنْ رَسُولٍ فَإِنَّهُ يَسْلُكُ مِنْ بَيْنِ يَدَيْهِ وَمِنْ خَلْفِهِ رَصَدًا﴾ قَالَ: هِيَ مُعَقِّبَاتٌ مِنَ الْمَلَائِكَةِ يَحْفَظُونَ النَّبِيَّ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ، حَتَّى يَتَبَيَّنَ الَّذِي أُرْسِلَ بِهِ إِلَيْهِمْ، وَذَلِكَ حِينَ يَقُولُ، لِيَعْلَمَ أَهْلُ الشِّرْكِ أَنْ قَدْ أَبْلَغُوا رِسَالَاتِ رَبِّهِمْ.

وَكَذَا قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي نَجِيحٍ، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ: ﴿لِيَعْلَمَ أَنْ قَدْ أَبْلَغُوا رِسَالاتِ رَبِّهِمْ﴾ قَالَ: لِيَعْلَمَ مَنْ كَذَّبَ الرُّسُلَ أَنْ قَدْ أَبْلَغُوا رِسَالَاتِ رَبِّهِمْ. وَفِي هَذَا نَظَرٌ.

وَقَالَ الْبَغَوِيُّ: قَرَأَ يَعْقُوبُ: "ليُعلَم" بِالضَّمِّ، أَيْ: لِيَعْلَمَ النَّاسُ أَنَّ الرُّسُلَ بُلّغوا.

وَيُحْتَمَلُ أَنْ يَكُونَ الضَّمِيرُ عَائِدًا إِلَى اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، وَهُوَ قَوْلٌ حَكَاهُ ابْنُ الْجَوْزِيِّ فِي "زَادِ الْمَسِيرِ" وَيَكُونُ الْمَعْنَى فِي ذَلِكَ: أَنَّهُ يَحْفَظُ رُسُلَهُ بِمَلَائِكَتِهِ لِيَتَمَكَّنُوا مِنْ أَدَاءِ رِسَالَاتِهِ، وَيَحْفَظُ مَا بُيِّن إِلَيْهِمْ مِنَ الْوَحْيِ؛ لِيَعْلَمَ أَنَّ قَدْ أَبْلَغُوا رِسَالَاتِ رَبِّهِمْ، وَيَكُونُ ذَلِكَ كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَمَا جَعَلْنَا الْقِبْلَةَ الَّتِي كُنْتَ عَلَيْهَا إِلا لِنَعْلَمَ مَنْ يَتَّبِعُ الرَّسُولَ مِمَّنْ يَنْقَلِبُ عَلَى عَقِبَيْهِ﴾ [الْبَقَرَةِ: ١٤٣] وَكَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَلَيَعْلَمَنَّ اللَّهُ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَلَيَعْلَمَنَّ الْمُنَافِقِينَ﴾ [الْعَنْكَبُوتِ: ١١] إِلَى أَمْثَالِ ذَلِكَ، مَعَ الْعِلْمِ بِأَنَّهُ تَعَالَى يَعْلَمُ الْأَشْيَاءَ قَبْلَ كَوْنِهَا قَطْعًا لَا مَحَالَةَ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ بَعْدَ هَذَا: ﴿وَأَحَاطَ بِمَا لَدَيْهِمْ وَأَحْصَى كُلَّ شَيْءٍ عَدَدًا﴾ .
