## 15:1-3 — verses 1-3 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Tafsir of Surat al-Hijr

It is Meccan.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

Discussion of the disjointed letters has already preceded at the beginnings of the surahs.

His statement: {Perhaps those who disbelieved will wish that they had been Muslims} is a report about them that they will regret the disbelief they were in, and will wish they had been with the Muslims in the worldly abode.

Al-Suddi related in his tafsir, with his well-known chain, from Ibn Abbas, Ibn Mas'ud, and others among the Companions: that the disbelievers, when they were exposed to the Fire, wished they had been Muslims.

It is also said: what is meant is that every disbeliever, at the moment of his death, wishes he had been a believer.

It is also said: this is a report about the Day of Resurrection, as in His statement, Exalted is He: {And if you could but see when they are made to stand before the Fire and say, "Oh, would that we could be sent back and not deny the signs of our Lord and be among the believers"} [al-An'am: 27].

Sufyan al-Thawri said, from Salama ibn Kuhayl, from Abu al-Za'ra', from Abdullah, concerning His statement: {Perhaps those who disbelieved will wish that they had been Muslims}, he said: this concerns the "Jahannamiyyin" (people of Hell) when they see them coming out of the Fire.

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] Ibn Abi Farwa al-Abdi related that Ibn Abbas and Anas ibn Malik used to interpret this verse, {Perhaps those who disbelieved will wish that they had been Muslims}, as referring to the day when Allah detains the people of sins among the Muslims together with the polytheists in the Fire. He said: the polytheists say to them, "What benefit was there for you in what you used to worship in this world?" He said: so Allah becomes angry on their behalf, out of the excellence of His mercy, and brings them out. That is when He says: {Perhaps those who disbelieved will wish that they had been Muslims}.

Abd al-Razzaq said: [chain] from Ibrahim, from Khusayf, from Mujahid, both of them said: the people of the Fire say to the monotheists, "What benefit was your faith to you?" When they say that, He says: "Bring out whoever has in his heart the weight of an atom [of faith]." He said: at that point comes His statement: {Perhaps those who disbelieved will wish that they had been Muslims}.

The same has been related from al-Dahhak, Qatada, Abu al-Aliya, and others. Regarding this, marfu' (elevated) hadiths have also come. Al-Hafiz Abu al-Qasim al-Tabarani said:

[chain] Muhammad ibn Mansur al-Tusi told us, Salih ibn Ishaq al-Jahbadh (Yahya ibn Ma'in directed me to him) told us, Mu'arrif ibn Wasil told us, from Ya'qub ibn Abi Nubata, from Abd al-Rahman al-Aghar, from Anas ibn Malik, may Allah be pleased with him, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Indeed some people among the people of 'there is no god but Allah' will enter the Fire because of their sins, and the people of al-Lat and al-Uzza will say to them: 'What benefit was your saying "there is no god but Allah" to you, while you are with us in the Fire?' So Allah becomes angry on their behalf and brings them out, casting them into the River of Life, and they recover from their burns just as the moon recovers from its eclipse, and they enter Paradise, and they are named therein 'the Jahannamiyyin'." A man said: "O Anas, did you hear this from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ?" Anas said: "I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: 'Whoever deliberately lies about me, let him take his seat in the Fire.' Yes, I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say this."

Then al-Tabarani said: al-Jahbadh alone narrated it.

The second hadith: Al-Tabarani also said: [chain] Khalid ibn Nafi' al-Ash'ari, from Sa'id ibn Abi Burda, from his father, from Abu Musa, may Allah be pleased with him, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "When the people of the Fire gather in the Fire, along with them whomever Allah wills from the people of the qibla, the disbelievers will say to the Muslims: 'Were you not Muslims?' They will say: 'Yes indeed.' They will say: 'Then what benefit was Islam to you? For you have ended up with us in the Fire!' They will say: 'We had sins for which we were seized.' Allah hears what they say, so He commands that whoever is in the Fire from the people of the qibla be brought out, and they are brought out. When the remaining disbelievers see that, they say: 'Would that we had been Muslims, so that we might come out as they came out.'" He said: then the Messenger of Allah ﷺ recited, "I seek refuge in Allah from Satan the accursed," {Alif, Lam, Ra. These are the verses of the Book and a clear Qur'an. Perhaps those who disbelieved will wish that they had been Muslims}.

Ibn Abi Hatim related it as well, from the hadith of Khalid ibn Nafi', with the same chain, adding in it: "In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful" in place of the seeking of refuge.

The third hadith: Al-Tabarani also said: [chain] Musa ibn Harun told us, Ishaq ibn Rahwayh told us, he said: I said to Abu Usama: did Abu Rawq (whose name is Atiyya ibn al-Harith) narrate to you: Salih ibn Abi Tarif told me, he said: I asked Abu Sa'id al-Khudri, and I said to him: did you hear the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say, concerning this verse, {Perhaps those who disbelieved will wish that they had been Muslims}? He said: Yes, I heard him say: "Allah brings out people from among the believers from the Fire after He has taken His vengeance upon them," and he said: "When Allah had made them enter the Fire together with the polytheists, the polytheists said to them: 'You used to claim that you were the allies of Allah in this world, so what is the matter with you being with us in the Fire?' When Allah hears that from them, He permits intercession for them, so the angels intercede, and the prophets intercede, and the believers intercede, until they come out by Allah's permission. When the polytheists see that, they say: 'Would that we had been like them, so that intercession might reach us and we might come out with them.'" He said: "That is the statement of Allah: {Perhaps those who disbelieved will wish that they had been Muslims}. They are named in Paradise 'the Jahannamiyyin' because of a blackness on their faces, so they say: 'O our Lord, remove this name from us,' and He commands them, so they bathe in a river of Paradise, and that name is removed from them." Abu Usama confirmed it and said: Yes.

The fourth hadith: Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Miskin Abu Fatima told us, al-Yaman ibn Yazid told me, from Muhammad ibn Himyar, from Muhammad ibn Ali, from his father, from his grandfather, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Among them are those whom the Fire seizes up to their knees, and among them are those whom the Fire seizes up to their waist, and among them are those whom the Fire seizes up to their neck, according to the measure of their sins and deeds. Among them are those who remain in it for a month and then come out of it, and among them are those who remain in it for a year and then come out of it, and the longest of them remaining in it is for a period equal to the duration of this world from the day it was created until it perishes. When Allah wills that they come out of it, the Jews and the Christians and whoever is in the Fire from the people of religions and idols say to those in the Fire from the people of monotheism: 'You believed in Allah, His Books, and His Messengers, yet we and you are today equally in the Fire!' So Allah becomes angry on their behalf with an anger He has not shown for anything before, and He brings them out to a spring in Paradise." And that is His statement: {Perhaps those who disbelieved will wish that they had been Muslims}.

His statement: {Leave them to eat and enjoy themselves} is a severe threat to them and a firm warning, like His statement, Exalted is He: {Say, "Enjoy yourselves, for indeed your destination is the Fire"} [Ibrahim: 30], and His statement: {Eat and enjoy yourselves a little; indeed you are criminals} [al-Mursalat: 46]. For this reason He said: {and let hope divert them}, that is, from repentance and turning back to Allah. {for they will come to know}, that is, the outcome of their affair.

### Source (Arabic) — review against this

تَفْسِيرُ سُورَةِ الْحِجْرِ

وَهِيَ مَكِّيَّةٌ.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

* *

قَدْ تَقَدَّمَ الْكَلَامُ عَلَى الْحُرُوفِ الْمُقَطَّعَةِ فِي أَوَائِلِ السُّورِ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿رُبَمَا يَوَدُّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَوْ كَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ﴾ إِخْبَارٌ عَنْهُمْ أَنَّهُمْ سَيَنْدَمُونَ عَلَى مَا كَانُوا فِيهِ مِنَ الْكُفْرِ، وَيَتَمَنَّوْنَ لَوْ كَانُوا مَعَ الْمُسْلِمِينَ فِي الدَّارِ الدُّنْيَا. 

وَنَقَلَ السُّدِّيّ فِي تَفْسِيرِهِ بِسَنَدِهِ الْمَشْهُورِ عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ، وَابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ، وَغَيْرِهِمَا مِنَ الصَّحَابَةِ: أَنَّ الْكُفَّارَ لَمَّا عُرضوا عَلَى النَّارِ، تَمَنَّوْا أَنْ لَوْ كَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ.

وَقِيلَ: الْمُرَادُ أَنَّ كُلَّ كَافِرٍ يَوَدُّ عِنْدَ احْتِضَارِهِ أَنْ لَوْ كَانَ مُؤْمِنًا.

وَقِيلَ: هَذَا إِخْبَارٌ عَنْ يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ، كَمَا فِي قَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَلَوْ تَرَى إِذْ وُقِفُوا عَلَى النَّارِ فَقَالُوا يَا لَيْتَنَا نُرَدُّ وَلا نُكَذِّبَ بِآيَاتِ رَبِّنَا وَنَكُونَ مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ﴾ [الْأَنْعَامِ: ٢٧]

وَقَالَ سُفْيَانُ الثَّوْرِيُّ: عَنْ سَلَمَةَ بْنِ كُهَيْل، عَنْ أَبِي الزَّعْرَاءِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿رُبَمَا يَوَدُّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَوْ كَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ﴾ قَالَ: هَذَا فِي الجُهنَمين إِذْ رَأَوْهُمْ يَخْرُجُونَ مِنَ النَّارِ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا الْمُثَنَّى، حَدَّثَنَا مُسْلِمٌ، حَدَّثَنَا الْقَاسِمُ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ أَبِي فَرْوة العَبْدي؛ أَنَّ ابْنَ عَبَّاسٍ وَأَنَسَ بْنَ مَالِكٍ كَانَا يَتَأَوَّلَانِ هَذِهِ الآية: ﴿رُبَمَا يَوَدُّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَوْ كَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ﴾ يَتَأَوَّلَانِهَا: يَوْمَ يَحْبِسُ اللَّهُ أَهْلَ الْخَطَايَا مِنَ الْمُسْلِمِينَ مَعَ الْمُشْرِكِينَ فِي النَّارِ. قَالَ: فَيَقُولُ لَهُمُ الْمُشْرِكُونَ: مَا أَغْنَى عَنْكُمْ مَا كُنْتُمْ تَعْبُدُونَ فِي الدُّنْيَا. قَالَ: فَيَغْضَبُ اللَّهُ لَهُمْ بِفَضْلِ رَحْمَتِهِ، فَيُخْرِجُهُمْ، فَذَلِكَ حِينَ يَقُولُ: ﴿رُبَمَا يَوَدُّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَوْ كَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ﴾

وَقَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ: أَخْبَرَنَا الثَّوْرِيُّ، عَنْ حَمَّادٍ، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، عَنْ خُصَيْفٍ، عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ قَالَا يَقُولُ أَهْلُ النَّارِ لِلْمُوَحِّدِينَ: مَا أَغْنَى عَنْكُمْ إِيمَانُكُمْ؟ فَإِذَا قَالُوا ذَلِكَ. قَالَ: أَخْرِجُوا مَنْ كَانَ فِي قَلْبِهِ مِثْقَالُ ذَرَّةٍ. قَالَ: فَعِنْدَ ذَلِكَ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿ [رُبَمَا] يَوَدُّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَوْ كَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ﴾ 

وَهَكَذَا رُوِيَ عَنِ الضَّحَّاكِ، وَقَتَادَةَ، وَأَبِي الْعَالِيَةِ، وَغَيْرِهِمْ. وَقَدْ وَرَدَ فِي ذَلِكَ أَحَادِيثُ مَرْفُوعَةٌ، فَقَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو الْقَاسِمِ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ.

حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ الْعَبَّاسِ، هُوَ الْأَخْرَمُ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ مَنْصُورٍ الطُّوسِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا صَالِحُ بْنُ إِسْحَاقَ الْجَهْبَذُ دَلَّنِي عَلَيْهِ يَحْيَى بْنُ مَعِينٍ حَدَّثَنَا مُعَرّف بْنُ وَاصِلٍ، عَنْ يَعْقُوبَ بْنِ أَبِي نُبَاتَةَ عَنْ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ الْأَغَرِّ، عَنْ أَنَسِ بْنِ مَالِكٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ نَاسًا مِنْ أَهْلٍ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ يَدْخُلُونَ النَّارَ بِذُنُوبِهِمْ، فَيَقُولُ لَهُمْ أَهْلُ اللَّاتِ وَالْعُزَّى: مَا أَغْنَى عَنْكُمْ قَوْلُكُمْ: لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَأَنْتُمْ مَعَنَا فِي النَّارِ؟. فَيَغْضَبُ اللَّهُ لَهُمْ، فَيُخْرِجُهُمْ، فَيُلْقِيهِمْ فِي نَهْرِ الْحَيَاةِ، فَيَبْرَءُونَ مِنْ حَرْقِهِمْ كَمَا يَبْرَأُ الْقَمَرُ مِنْ خُسُوفِهِ، فَيَدْخُلُونَ الْجَنَّةَ، ويسمَّون فِيهَا الْجَهَنَّمِيِّينَ" فَقَالَ رَجُلٌ: يَا أَنَسُ، أَنْتَ سمعتَ هَذَا مِنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ؟ فَقَالَ أَنَسٌ: سمعتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "مِنْ كَذَبَ عَلِيَّ مُتَعَمَّدًا، فَلْيَتَبَوَّأْ مَقْعَدَهُ مِنَ النَّارِ". نَعَمْ، أَنَا سَمِعْتُ رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم يقول هَذَا.

ثُمَّ قَالَ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ: تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ الْجَهْبَذُ 

الْحَدِيثُ الثَّانِي: وَقَالَ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ أَيْضًا: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ أَحْمَدَ بْنِ حَنْبَلٍ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو الشَّعْثَاءِ عَلِيُّ بْنُ حَسَنٍ الْوَاسِطِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا خَالِدُ بْنُ نَافِعٍ الْأَشْعَرِيُّ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ أَبِي بُرْدَةَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ أَبِي مُوسَى، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِذَا اجْتَمَعَ أَهْلُ النَّارِ فِي النَّارِ، وَمَعَهُمْ مَنْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْقِبْلَةِ، قَالَ الْكُفَّارُ لِلْمُسْلِمِينَ: أَلَمْ تَكُونُوا مُسْلِمِينَ؟ قَالُوا: بَلَى. قَالُوا: فَمَا أَغْنَى عَنْكُمُ الْإِسْلَامُ! فَقَدْ صِرْتُمْ مَعَنَا فِي النَّارِ؟ قَالُوا: كَانَتْ لَنَا ذُنُوبٌ فَأُخِذْنَا بِهَا. فَسَمِعَ اللَّهُ مَا قَالُوا، فَأَمَرَ بِمَنْ كَانَ فِي النَّارِ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْقِبْلَةِ فَأُخْرِجُوا، فَلَمَّا رَأَى ذَلِكَ مَنْ بَقِيَ مِنَ الْكُفَّارِ قَالُوا: يَا لَيْتَنَا كُنَّا مُسْلِمِينَ فَنَخْرُجَ كَمَا خَرَجُوا". قَالَ: ثُمَّ قَرَأَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: أَعُوذُ بِاللَّهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيمِ، ﴿الر تِلْكَ آيَاتُ الْكِتَابِ وَقُرْآنٍ مُبِين رُبَمَا يَوَدُّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَوْ كَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ﴾ 

وَرَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ خَالِدِ بْنِ نَافِعٍ، بِهِ، وَزَادَ فِيهِ: (بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ) ، عِوَضَ الِاسْتِعَاذَةِ.

الْحَدِيثُ الثَّالِثُ: وَقَالَ الطَّبَرَانِيُّ أَيْضًا: حَدَّثَنَا مُوسَى بْنُ هَارُونَ، حَدَّثَنَا إِسْحَاقُ بْنُ رَاهَوَيْهِ قَالَ: قُلْتُ لِأَبِي أُسَامَةَ: أَحَدَّثَكُمْ أَبُو رَوْقٍ -وَاسْمُهُ عَطِيَّةُ بْنُ الْحَارِثِ-: حَدَّثَنِي صَالِحُ بْنُ أَبِي طَرِيفٍ قَالَ: سَأَلْتُ أَبَا سَعِيدٍ الْخُدْرِيَّ فَقُلْتُ لَهُ: هَلْ سَمِعْتَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ فِي هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ: ﴿رُبَمَا يَوَدُّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَوْ كَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ﴾ ؟ قَالَ: نَعَمْ، سَمِعْتُهُ يَقُولُ: "يُخرج اللَّهُ نَاسًا من المؤمنين من النار بعد ما يَأْخُذُ نِقْمَتَهُ مِنْهُمْ"، وَقَالَ: "لَمَّا أَدْخَلَهُمُ اللَّهُ النَّارَ مَعَ الْمُشْرِكِينَ قَالَ لَهُمُ الْمُشْرِكُونَ: تَزْعُمُونَ أَنَّكُمْ أَوْلِيَاءُ اللَّهِ فِي الدُّنْيَا، فَمَا بَالُكُمْ مَعَنَا فِي النَّارِ؟ فَإِذَا سَمِعَ اللَّهُ ذَلِكَ مِنْهُمْ، أَذِنَ فِي الشَّفَاعَةِ لَهُمْ فَتَشْفَعُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ وَالنَّبِيُّونَ، وَيَشْفَعُ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ، حَتَّى يَخْرُجُوا بِإِذْنِ اللَّهِ، فَإِذَا رَأَى الْمُشْرِكُونَ ذَلِكَ، قَالُوا: يَا لَيْتَنَا كُنَّا مِثْلَهُمْ، فَتُدْرِكَنَا الشَّفَاعَةُ، فَنَخْرُجُ مَعَهُمْ". قَالَ: "فَذَلِكَ قَوْلُ اللَّهِ: ﴿رُبَمَا يَوَدُّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَوْ كَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ﴾ فَيُسَمَّوْنَ فِي الْجَنَّةِ الجُهَنَّمِيِّين مِنْ أَجْلِ سَواد فِي وُجُوهِهِمْ، فَيَقُولُونَ: يَا رَبِّ، أَذْهِبْ عَنَّا هَذَا الِاسْمَ، فَيَأْمُرُهُمْ فَيَغْتَسِلُونَ فِي نَهْرِ الْجَنَّةِ، فَيَذْهَبُ ذَلِكَ الِاسْمُ عَنْهُمْ"، فَأَقَرَّ بِهِ أَبُو أُسَامَةَ، وَقَالَ: نَعَمْ. 

الْحَدِيثُ الرَّابِعُ وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ الْحُسَيْنِ، حَدَّثَنَا الْعَبَّاسُ بْنُ الْوَلِيدِ النَّرْسِيُّ حَدَّثَنَا مِسْكِينٌ أَبُو فَاطِمَةَ، حَدَّثَنِي الْيَمَانُ بْنُ يَزِيدَ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ حِمْير عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عَلِيٍّ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ جَدِّهِ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ الله صلى الله عليه وسلم: "مِنْهُمْ مَنْ تَأْخُذُهُ النَّارُ إِلَى رُكْبَتَيْهِ، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ تَأْخُذُهُ النَّارُ إِلَى حُجْزَتِهِ، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ تَأْخُذُهُ النَّارُ إِلَى عنُقه، عَلَى قَدْرِ ذُنُوبِهِمْ وَأَعْمَالِهِمْ، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَمْكُثُ فِيهَا شَهْرًا ثُمَّ يَخْرُجُ مِنْهَا، وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَمْكُثُ فِيهَا سَنَةً ثُمَّ يَخْرُجُ مِنْهَا، وَأَطْوَلُهُمْ فِيهَا مُكْثًا بِقَدْرِ الدُّنْيَا مُنْذُ يَوْمِ خُلِقَتْ إِلَى أَنْ تَفْنَى، فَإِذَا أَرَادَ اللَّهُ أَنْ يَخْرُجُوا مِنْهَا قَالَتِ الْيَهُودُ وَالنَّصَارَى وَمَنْ فِي النَّارِ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْأَدْيَانِ وَالْأَوْثَانِ، لِمَنْ فِي النَّارِ مِنْ أَهْلِ التَّوْحِيدِ: آمَنْتُمْ بِاللَّهِ وَكُتُبِهِ وَرُسُلِهِ، فَنَحْنُ وَأَنْتُمُ الْيَوْمَ فِي النَّارِ سَوَاءً، فَيَغْضَبُ اللَّهُ لَهُمْ غَضَبًا لَمْ يَغْضَبْهُ لِشَيْءٍ فِيمَا مَضَى، فَيُخْرِجُهُمْ إِلَى عَيْنٍ فِي الْجَنَّةِ، وَهُوَ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿رُبَمَا يَوَدُّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَوْ كَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ﴾ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿ذَرْهُمْ يَأْكُلُوا وَيَتَمَتَّعُوا﴾ تَهْدِيدٌ لَهُمْ شَدِيدٌ، وَوَعِيدٌ أَكِيدٌ، كَقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿قُلْ تَمَتَّعُوا فَإِنَّ مَصِيرَكُمْ إِلَى النَّارِ﴾ [إِبْرَاهِيمَ: ٣٠] وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿كُلُوا وَتَمَتَّعُوا قَلِيلا إِنَّكُمْ مُجْرِمُونَ﴾ [الْمُرْسَلَاتِ: ٤٦] وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿وَيُلْهِهِمُ الأمَلُ﴾ أَيْ: عَنِ التَّوْبَةِ وَالْإِنَابَةِ، ﴿فَسَوْفَ يَعْلَمُونَ﴾ أَيْ: عَاقِبَةَ أَمْرِهِمْ.

## 15:4-5 — verses 4-5 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He, exalted, says: He never destroys a town except after the proof has been established against it and its appointed term has come to an end, and He does not delay a nation whose destruction has come due from its appointed time, nor do they come before their term. This is a warning to the people of Makkah, and guidance for them to turn away from the shirk (polytheism), obstinacy, and deviation from truth in which they were engaged, that which makes them deserving of destruction.

## 15:6-9 — verses 6-9 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty informs of their disbelief, their insolence, and their stubbornness in their statement: {O you upon whom the Reminder has been sent down}, meaning, the one who claims that, {indeed you are truly mad}, meaning, in your calling us to follow you and abandon that upon which we found our forefathers. {Why do you not} meaning, why do you not {bring us the angels}, meaning, who would bear witness for you to the truth of what you have brought, {if you are among the truthful}. This is like the statement of Pharaoh: {Then why were bracelets of gold not cast upon him, or angels came with him in accompaniment?} [al-Zukhruf: 53], and {Those who do not expect the meeting with Us say, Why were angels not sent down upon us, or why do we not see our Lord? They have certainly been arrogant within themselves and gone beyond all bounds in insolence. The Day they see the angels, no good tidings will there be that day for the criminals, and they will say, Refuge is forbidden, all forbidden} [al-Furqan: 21, 22].

And likewise He says in this verse: {We do not send down the angels except with truth, and if We did, they would not then be reprieved}.

Mujahid said concerning His statement, {We do not send down the angels except with truth}: with the message and with punishment.

Then the Almighty establishes that it is He who sent down the Reminder, which is the Qur'an, and that He is the one who preserves it from alteration and change.

Some of them referred the pronoun in His statement, {and indeed We are its guardians}, back to the Prophet ﷺ, as in His statement, {and Allah will protect you from the people} [al-Ma'idah: 67]. But the first meaning is more fitting, and it is what the apparent context indicates. [And Allah knows best.]

## 15:10-13 — verses 10-13 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, consoling His Messenger ﷺ regarding the denial of those who denied him among the disbelievers of Quraysh: that He sent messengers before him to the past nations, and that no messenger came to any nation without them denying him and mocking him.

Then He informs that He caused denial to run its course in the hearts of the criminals who were obstinate and arrogantly refused to follow guidance.

Anas and al-Hasan al-Basri said: {Thus We make it run through the hearts of the criminals} meaning: shirk (associating partners with God).

His saying: {And the way of the former peoples has already passed} means: it is already known what the Almighty did to those who denied His messengers, of destruction and ruin, and how God saved the prophets and their followers in this world and the next.

## 15:14-15 — verses 14-15 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He, the Exalted, informs us of the strength of their disbelief, their stubbornness, and their obstinate resistance to the truth: that even if He opened for them a gate from the sky, and they went on ascending through it, they would not believe in that, but would rather say: {Our eyes have been dazzled (sukkirat absaruna)}

Mujahid, Ibn Kathir, and al-Dahhak said: "Our eyes have been sealed shut."

Qatadah reported from Ibn Abbas: "Our eyes have been seized."

Al-Awfi reported from Ibn Abbas: "It has been made to appear confused to us, we have simply been bewitched."

Al-Kalbi said: "Our eyes have gone blind."

Ibn Zayd said: {Our eyes have been dazzled}, meaning like the drunkard (al-sakran) who does not comprehend.

## 15:16-20 — verses 16-20 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted mentions His creation of the sky in its loftiness, and what He adorned it with of piercing stars, for whoever contemplates it and repeats his gaze upon it will see in it wonders and dazzling signs at which his sight grows bewildered. This is why Mujahid and Qatadah said: the buruj (constellations/towers) here mean the stars.

I say: this is like His statement, the Exalted: {Blessed is He who placed in the sky towers, and placed therein a lamp and a luminous moon} [al-Furqan: 61]. Some of them said: the buruj are the stations (mansions) of the sun and moon.

Atiyyah al-Awfi said: the buruj here are the palaces of the guards.

He made the shooting stars (shuhub) guards for it against the rebellious devils, so that they would not listen in on the highest assembly. So whoever among them rebelled and advanced to eavesdrop, a {clear shooting flame} would come to him and destroy him. Sometimes he might have already cast down the word he heard, before the flame overtook him, to the one below him, and that other one would take it and bring it to his ally, as has come explicitly stated in the authentic report. As al-Bukhari said in his commentary on this verse:

[chain] Ali ibn Abdullah told us, Sufyan told us, from Amr, from Ikrimah, from Abu Hurairah, tracing it back to the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "When Allah decrees a matter in the heaven, the angels beat their wings in submission to His word, as if it were a chain being dragged upon a smooth rock." Ali said, and someone else said: a smooth rock, that sound penetrates through them. Then when fear is lifted from their hearts, they say: "What did your Lord say?" They reply: "That which He said is the truth, and He is the Most High, the Most Great." Then those who eavesdrop hear it, the eavesdroppers, one above another like this, and Sufyan illustrated with his hand, spreading the fingers of his right hand, placing some above others. So sometimes the flame catches the listener before he can cast the word down to his companion, and burns him, and sometimes it does not catch him until he casts it down to the one next to him, who is below him, until they cast it down to the earth. And sometimes Sufyan said: until it reaches the earth, and is cast into the mouth of the sorcerer, or the soothsayer, who then lies along with it a hundred lies, so that people say: "Did he not tell us on such and such a day that such and such would happen, and we found it true?" because of the one word that was heard from the sky.

Then the Exalted mentions His creation of the earth, His stretching it out, widening it and spreading it flat, and what He placed in it of firmly-anchored mountains, valleys, lands and sands, and what He caused to grow in it of crops and fruits in due proportion to one another.

Ibn Abbas said regarding {of everything weighed (mawzun)}: meaning, known. Sa'id ibn Jubair, Ikrimah, Abu Malik, Mujahid, al-Hakam ibn Utaybah, al-Hasan ibn Muhammad, Abu Salih, and Qatadah said likewise.

Some of them said: it means apportioned according to a measure.

Ibn Zayd said: of everything that is weighed and apportioned according to a measure. Ibn Zayd also said: that which the people of the markets weigh.

As for His statement: {And We placed for you therein means of livelihood, and that for which you are not providers}, the Exalted mentions that He distributed for them in the earth various kinds of means and livelihoods, ma'ayish being the plural of ma'ishah (means of living).

As for His statement: {and that for which you are not providers}, Mujahid said: this refers to beasts and livestock.

Ibn Jarir said: they are the male slaves, female slaves, beasts, and livestock.

The point is that the Exalted reminds them of His favor upon them in what He facilitated for them of means of earning a livelihood, various causes, and kinds of sustenance, and in what He subjected for them of the beasts they ride and the livestock they eat, and the male and female slaves they employ. Their provision rests upon their Creator, not upon them; for them is the benefit, while provision rests upon Allah, the Exalted.

## 15:21-25 — verses 21-25 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He, exalted is He, informs that He is the Owner of everything, and that everything is easy and effortless for Him, and that with Him are the storehouses of all things, of every kind. {And We do not send it down except in a known measure}, as He wills and as He intends, and because of the far-reaching wisdom He has in that, and mercy toward His servants, not as something obligatory upon Him, rather He has written mercy upon Himself.

Yazid ibn Abi Ziyad said, from Abu Juhayfa, from Abdullah: There is no year that has more rain than another year, but Allah distributes it wherever He wills, a year here and a year there. Then he recited: {And there is not a thing but that with Us are its storehouses, and We do not send it down except in a known measure}. Ibn Jarir related it.

He also said: Al-Qasim related to us, al-Hasan related to us, Hushaym related to us, Isma'il ibn Salim informed us, from al-Hakam ibn Utaybah, concerning His saying: {And We do not send it down except in a known measure}, he said: No year has more rain than another, nor less, but rather some people are given rain while others are deprived of it, and sometimes it falls in the sea. He said: And it has reached us that more angels descend with the rain than the number of the offspring of Iblis and the offspring of Adam, counting every drop, where it falls and what it makes grow.

Al-Bazzar said: Dawud, that is Ibn Bakr al-Tustari, related to us, Habban ibn Aghlab ibn Tamim related to us, my father related to me, from Hisham, from Muhammad ibn Sirin, from Abu Hurayrah, may Allah be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "The storehouses of Allah are speech; when He wills a thing, He says to it: Be, and it is."

Then he said: Only Aghlab relates it, and he was not strong; more than one of the earlier scholars narrated from him, but only his son narrated this from him.

His saying: {And We sent the winds fertilizing} means: they fertilize the clouds so that they yield water, and they fertilize the trees so that their leaves and calyxes open.

These "winds" are mentioned here in the plural form, so that from them comes production, unlike the barren wind, which He mentions in the singular and describes as barren, meaning devoid of production, since production only occurs from two things or more.

Al-A'mash said, from al-Minhal ibn Amr, from Qays ibn al-Sakan, from Abdullah ibn Mas'ud, concerning His saying: {And We sent the winds fertilizing}, he said: The winds are sent, and they carry water from the sky, then they stir the clouds until they yield milk as a milch-camel yields milk.

Ibn Abbas, Ibrahim al-Nakha'i, and Qatadah said likewise.

Al-Dahhak said: Allah sends it upon the clouds, and it fertilizes them, so they fill with water.

Ubayd ibn Umayr al-Laythi said: Allah sends the bringer of good news, and it sweeps the earth thoroughly, then Allah sends the stirrer, and it stirs up the clouds, then Allah sends the uniter, and it unites the clouds, then Allah sends the fertilizing ones, and they fertilize the trees. Then he recited: {And We sent the winds fertilizing}.

Ibn Jarir related, from the hadith of Ubays ibn Maymun, from Abu al-Muhazzim, from Abu Hurayrah, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "The south wind is from Paradise, and it is the fertilizing wind, which Allah mentions in His Book, and in it are benefits for the people." This is a weak chain of transmission.

The imam Abu Bakr Abdullah ibn al-Zubayr al-Humaydi said in his Musnad: Sufyan related to us, Amr ibn Dinar related to us, Yazid ibn Ju'dubah al-Laythi informed me: that he heard Abdullah ibn Mikhraq narrating from Abu Dharr, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Allah created in Paradise a wind, one after another, seven years apart, and below it is a closed gate, and the wind only reaches you through that gate. If it were opened, it would sweep away everything between the heaven and the earth. It is called al-Azyab with Allah, and it is the south wind among you."

His saying: {So We gave it to you to drink} means: We sent it down for you sweet, so that you are able to drink from it, whereas if We willed, We could have made it bitter and salty. Allah draws attention to this in the other verse, in Surat al-Waqi'ah, where He says: {Have you seen the water that you drink? Is it you who sent it down from the rain clouds, or are We the senders? If We willed, We could make it bitter and salty, so why are you not grateful?} [al-Waqi'ah: 68-70]. And in His saying: {It is He who sent down from the sky water; from it is drink for you, and from it are trees in which you pasture your cattle} [al-Naml: 10].

His saying: {And you are not the ones who store it} Sufyan al-Thawri said: meaning, "you are not the ones who withhold it."

It is also possible that the meaning is: you are not the ones who preserve it, rather We send it down and preserve it for you, and We make it into flowing springs and wellsprings in the earth. If He, exalted, had willed, He could have made it sink away and disappear, but out of His mercy He sent it down, made it sweet, and preserved it in springs, wells, rivers, and other places, so that it would remain for them throughout the year, for them to drink and to water their livestock, their crops, and their fruits.

His saying: {And indeed, it is We who give life and cause death} is a report about His power, exalted is He, over the origination of creation and its restoration, and that it is He who brought creation to life from nonexistence, then causes them to die, then resurrects them all for the Day of Gathering.

He informs that He, exalted, inherits the earth and whoever is upon it, and to Him they return.

Then He said, informing of the completeness of His knowledge of them, the first of them and the last of them: {And We have already known the preceding among you, and We have already known those who are yet to come}.

Ibn Abbas, may Allah be pleased with both of them, said: "those who preceded" means everyone who has perished since the time of Adam (peace be upon him), and "those who come later" means whoever is alive now and whoever will come until the Day of Resurrection.

Something similar was related from Ikrimah, Mujahid, al-Dahhak, Qatadah, Muhammad ibn Ka'b, al-Sha'bi, and others, and this is the choice of Ibn Jarir, may Allah have mercy on him.

Ibn Jarir said: Muhammad ibn Abd al-A'la related to us, al-Mu'tamir ibn Sulayman related to us, from his father, from a man, from Marwan ibn al-Hakam, who said: There were people who used to hang back in the prayer rows because of the women, so Allah sent down: {And We have already known the preceding among you, and We have already known those who are yet to come}.

A very gharib (singular) hadith has come regarding this. Ibn Jarir said:

Muhammad ibn Musa al-Harashi related to me, Nuh ibn Qays related to us, Amr ibn Malik related to us, from Abu al-Jawza, from Ibn Abbas, may Allah be pleased with both of them, who said: A woman used to pray behind the Messenger of Allah ﷺ. Ibn Abbas said: No, by Allah, I never saw the like of her. Some of the Muslims, when they prayed, would move forward, meaning so as not to see her, while others would hang back, so that when they prostrated they would look at her from beneath their arms! So Allah sent down: {And We have already known the preceding among you, and We have already known those who are yet to come}.

Ahmad and Ibn Abi Hatim in his Tafsir related it likewise, as did al-Tirmidhi and al-Nasa'i in the book of Tafsir in their two Sunan works, and Ibn Majah, through various routes from Nuh ibn Qays al-Hudani. Ahmad and Abu Dawud and others authenticated him, though it was related from Ibn Ma'in that he weakened him; Muslim and the authors of the Sunan works have included his narrations.

This hadith contains severe objectionable content (nakarah). Abd al-Razzaq related it, from Ja'far ibn Sulayman, from Amr ibn Malik, who is al-Nukri, that he heard Abu al-Jawza saying, concerning His saying: {And We have already known the preceding among you}, referring to the rows in prayer, {and those who are yet to come}. It appears that this is only the statement of Abu al-Jawza himself, with no mention of Ibn Abbas in it. Al-Tirmidhi said: This is more likely to be correct than the narration of Nuh ibn Qays, and Allah knows best.

Likewise, Ibn Jarir related from Muhammad ibn Abi Ma'shar, from his father: that he heard Awn ibn Abdullah discussing with Muhammad ibn Ka'b concerning His saying: {And We have already known the preceding among you, and We have already known those who are yet to come}, that it refers to the rows of prayer. Muhammad ibn Ka'b said: It is not like that. {And We have already known the preceding among you} refers to the dead and those who were killed, and {those who are yet to come} refers to those who will be created afterward, {and indeed your Lord, He will gather them; indeed He is Wise, Knowing}. Awn ibn Abdullah said: May Allah grant you success and reward you with good.

يُخْبِرُ، تَعَالَى، أَنَّهُ مَالِكُ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ، وَأَنَّ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ سَهْلٌ عليه، يسير لديه، وأن عنده خزائن الْأَشْيَاءِ مِنْ جَمِيعِ الصُّنُوفِ، ﴿وَمَا نُنزلُهُ إِلا بِقَدَرٍ مَعْلُومٍ﴾ كَمَا يَشَاءُ وَكَمَا يُرِيدُ، وَلِمَا لَهُ فِي ذَلِكَ مِنَ الْحِكْمَةِ الْبَالِغَةِ، وَالرَّحْمَةِ بِعِبَادِهِ، لَا عَلَى [وَجْهِ] الْوُجُوبِ، بَلْ هُوَ كَتَبَ عَلَى نَفْسِهِ الرَّحْمَةَ.

وَقَالَ أَيْضًا: حَدَّثَنَا الْقَاسِمُ، حَدَّثَنَا الْحَسَنُ حَدَّثَنَا هُشَيْم، أَخْبَرَنَا إِسْمَاعِيلُ بْنُ سَالِمٍ، عَنِ الْحَكَمِ بْنِ عُتَيْبَة فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَمَا نُنزلُهُ إِلا بِقَدَرٍ مَعْلُومٍ﴾ قَالَ: مَا عَامٌ بِأَكْثَرَ مَطَرًا مِنْ عَامٍ وَلَا أَقَلَّ، وَلَكِنَّهُ يُمطر قَوْمٌ وَيُحْرَمُ آخَرُونَ وَرُبَّمَا كَانَ فِي الْبَحْرِ. قَالَ: وَبَلَغَنَا أَنَّهُ يَنْزِلُ مَعَ الْمَطَرِ مِنَ الْمَلَائِكَةِ أَكْثَرُ مِنْ عَدَدِ وَلَدِ إِبْلِيسَ وَوُلِدِ آدَمَ، يُحصُون كُلَّ قَطْرَةٍ حَيْثُ تَقَعُ وَمَا تُنْبِتُ 

وَقَالَ الْبَزَّارُ: حَدَّثَنَا دَاوُدُ -وَهُوَ ابْنُ بَكْرٍ التُّسْتُري -حَدَّثَنَا حبَّان بْنُ أَغْلَبَ بْنِ تَمِيمٍ، حَدَّثَنِي أَبِي، عَنْ هِشَامٍ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ سِيرِينَ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "خَزَائِنُ اللَّهِ الْكَلَامُ، فَإِذَا أَرَادَ شَيْئًا قَالَ لَهُ: كُنْ، فَكَانَ" 

ثُمَّ قَالَ: لَا يَرْوِيهِ إِلَّا أَغْلَبُ، وَلَمْ يَكُنْ بِالْقَوِيِّ، وَقَدْ حَدَّثَ عَنْهُ غَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ مِنَ الْمُتَقَدِّمِينَ، وَلَمْ يَرْوِهِ عَنْهُ إِلَّا ابْنُهُ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَأَرْسَلْنَا الرِّيَاحَ لَوَاقِحَ﴾ أَيْ: تُلَقِّحُ السَّحَابَ فَتُدِرُّ مَاءً، وَتُلَقِّحُ الشَّجَرَ فَتَتَفَتَّحُ عَنْ أَوْرَاقِهَا وَأَكْمَامِهَا.

هَذِهِ "الرِّيَاحُ" ذَكَرَهَا بِصِيغَةِ الْجَمْعِ، لِيَكُونَ مِنْهَا الْإِنْتَاجُ، بِخِلَافِ الرِّيحِ الْعَقِيمِ فَإِنَّهُ أَفْرَدَهَا، وَوَصَفَهَا بِالْعَقِيمِ، وَهُوَ عَدَمُ الْإِنْتَاجِ؛ لِأَنَّهُ لَا يَكُونُ إِلَّا مِنْ شَيْئَيْنِ فَصَاعِدًا.

وَقَالَ الْأَعْمَشُ، عَنِ المِنْهَال بْنِ عَمْرٍو، عَنْ قَيْسِ بْنِ السَّكَنِ، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ مَسْعُودٍ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَأَرْسَلْنَا الرِّيَاحَ لَوَاقِحَ﴾ قَالَ: تُرْسَلُ الرِّيَاحُ، فَتَحْمِلُ الْمَاءَ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ، ثُمَّ تَمْرى السَّحَابَ، حَتَّى تُدِرَّ كَمَا تَدر اللَّقحَة.

وَكَذَا قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَإِبْرَاهِيمُ النَّخَعِيُّ، وَقَتَادَةُ.

وَقَالَ الضَّحَّاكُ: يَبْعَثُهَا اللَّهُ عَلَى السَّحَابِ، فتُلقحه، فَيَمْتَلِئُ ماء.

وَقَالَ عُبَيْد بْنُ عُمَير اللَّيْثِيُّ: يَبْعَثُ اللَّهُ المُبشرّة فتَقمُّ الأرض قَمًّا ثم بعث اللَّهُ الْمُثِيرَةَ فَتُثِيرُ السَّحَابَ، ثُمَّ يَبْعَثُ اللَّهُ الْمُؤَلِّفَةَ فَتُؤَلِّفُ السَّحَابَ، ثُمَّ يَبْعَثُ اللَّهُ اللَّوَاقِحَ فَتُلَقِّحُ الشَّجَرَ، ثُمَّ تَلَا ﴿وَأَرْسَلْنَا الرِّيَاحَ لَوَاقِحَ﴾

وَقَدْ رَوَى ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ عُبَيْس بْنِ مَيْمُونٍ، عَنْ أَبِي المُهَزَّم، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ قَالَ: "الرِّيحُ الْجَنُوبُ مِنَ الْجَنَّةِ، وَهِيَ [الرِّيحُ اللَّوَاقِحُ، وَهِيَ الَّتِي] ذَكَرَ اللَّهُ فِي كِتَابِهِ، وَفِيهَا مَنَافِعُ لِلنَّاسِ" وَهَذَا إِسْنَادٌ ضَعِيفٌ.

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَبُو بَكْرٍ عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ الزُّبَيْرِ الحُمَيدي فِي مَسْنَدِهِ: حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، حَدَّثَنَا عَمْرُو بْنُ دِينَارٍ، أَخْبَرَنِي يَزِيدُ بْنُ جُعْدبة اللَّيْثِيُّ: أَنَّهُ سَمِعَ عَبْدَ اللَّهِ بْنَ مِخْراق، يُحَدِّثُ عَنْ أَبِي ذَرٍّ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ اللَّهَ خَلَقَ فِي الْجَنَّةِ رِيحًا بَعْدَ الرِّيحِ بِسَبْعِ سِنِينَ، وَإِنَّ مِنْ دُونِهَا بَابًا مُغْلَقًا، وَإِنَّمَا يَأْتِيكُمُ الرِّيحُ مِنْ ذَلِكَ الْبَابِ، وَلَوْ فُتِحَ لَأَذْرَتْ مَا بَيْنَ السَّمَاءِ وَالْأَرْضِ مِنْ شَيْءٍ، وَهِيَ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ الأزَيبُ، وَهِيَ فِيكُمُ الْجَنُوبُ" 

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَأَسْقَيْنَاكُمُوهُ﴾ أَيْ: أَنْزَلْنَاهُ لَكُمْ عَذْبًا يُمكنكم أَنْ تَشْرَبُوا مِنْهُ، وَلَوْ نَشَاءُ لَجَعَلْنَاهُ أُجَاجًا. كَمَا يُنَبِّهُ اللَّهُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ فِي الْآيَةِ الْأُخْرَى فِي سُورَةِ "الْوَاقِعَةِ"، وَهُوَ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿أَفَرَأَيْتُمُ الْمَاءَ الَّذِي تَشْرَبُونَ أَأَنْتُمْ أَنزلْتُمُوهُ مِنَ الْمُزْنِ أَمْ نَحْنُ الْمُنزلُونَ لَوْ نَشَاءُ جَعَلْنَاهُ أُجَاجًا فَلَوْلا تَشْكُرُونَ﴾ [الْوَاقِعَةِ: ٦٨-٧٠] وَفِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿هُوَ الَّذِي أَنزلَ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ مَاءً لَكُمْ مِنْهُ شَرَابٌ وَمِنْهُ شَجَرٌ فِيهِ تُسِيمُونَ﴾ [النَّمْلِ: ١٠]

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَمَا أَنْتُمْ لَهُ بِخَازِنِينَ﴾ قَالَ سُفْيَانُ الثَّوْرِيُّ: بِمَانِعِينَ.

وَيُحْتَمَلُ أَنَّ الْمُرَادَ: وَمَا أَنْتُمْ لَهُ بِحَافِظِينَ، بَلْ نَحْنُ نُنَزِّلُهُ وَنَحْفَظُهُ عَلَيْكُمْ، وَنَجْعَلُهُ مَعِينًا وَيَنَابِيعَ فِي الْأَرْضِ، وَلَوْ شَاءَ تَعَالَى لَأَغَارَهُ وَذَهَبَ بِهِ، وَلَكِنْ مِنْ رَحْمَتِهِ أَنْزَلَهُ وَجَعْلِهِ عَذْبًا، وَحَفِظَهُ فِي الْعُيُونِ وَالْآبَارِ وَالْأَنْهَارِ وَغَيْرِ ذَلِكَ. لِيَبْقَى لَهُمْ فِي طُولِ السَّنَةِ، يَشْرَبُونَ وَيَسْقُونَ أَنْعَامَهُمْ وَزُرُوعَهُمْ وَثِمَارَهُمْ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِنَّا لَنَحْنُ نُحْيِي وَنُمِيتُ﴾ إِخْبَارٌ عَنْ قُدْرَتِهِ تَعَالَى عَلَى بَدْءِ الْخَلْقِ وَإِعَادَتِهِ، وَأَنَّهُ هُوَ الَّذِي أَحْيَا الْخَلْقَ مِنَ الْعَدَمِ، ثُمَّ يُمِيتُهُمْ ثُمَّ يَبْعَثُهُمْ كُلَّهُمْ لِيَوْمِ الْجَمْعِ.

وَأَخْبَرَ أَنَّهُ، تَعَالَى، يَرِثُ الْأَرْضَ وَمَنْ عَلَيْهَا وَإِلَيْهِ يَرْجِعُونَ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ مُخْبِرًا عَنْ تَمَامِ عِلْمِهِ بِهِمْ، أَوَّلِهِمْ وَآخِرِهِمْ: ﴿وَلَقَدْ عَلِمْنَا الْمُسْتَقْدِمِينَ مِنْكُمْ وَلَقَدْ عَلِمْنَا الْمُسْتَأْخِرِينَ﴾

قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا الْمُسْتَقْدِمُونَ: كُلُّ مَنْ هَلَكَ مِنْ لَدُنْ آدَمَ، عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ، وَالْمُسْتَأْخِرُونَ: مَنْ هُوَ حَيٌّ وَمَنْ سَيَأْتِي إِلَى يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ.

وَرُوِيَ نَحْوُهُ عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ، وَمُجَاهِدٍ، وَالضَّحَّاكِ، وَقَتَادَةَ، وَمُحَمَّدِ بْنِ كَعْبٍ، وَالشَّعْبِيِّ، وَغَيْرِهِمْ. وَهُوَ اخْتِيَارُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ، رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ 

وَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ الْأَعْلَى، حَدَّثَنَا الْمُعْتَمِرُ بْنُ سُلَيْمَانَ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ رَجُلٍ عَنْ مَرْوان بْنِ الْحَكَمِ أَنَّهُ قَالَ: كَانَ أُنَاسٌ يَسْتَأْخِرُونَ فِي الصُّفُوفِ مِنْ أَجْلِ النِّسَاءِ فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ: ﴿وَلَقَدْ عَلِمْنَا الْمُسْتَقْدِمِينَ مِنْكُمْ وَلَقَدْ عَلِمْنَا الْمُسْتَأْخِرِينَ﴾ 

وَقَدْ وَرَدَ فِي هَذَا حَدِيثٌ غَرِيبٌ جِدًّا، فَقَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ:

## 15:26-27 — verses 26-27 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, and Qatadah said: what is meant by al-salsal (dried clay) here is dry earth.

The apparent meaning is that it is like His saying, exalted is He: {He created man from salsal like pottery, and He created the jinn from a smokeless flame of fire} [al-Rahman: 14-15].

And from Mujahid also: al-salsal is the putrid.

Explaining the verse by means of the verse is more fitting.

His saying: {from hama' masnun} means: the salsal is from hama', which is clay. And al-masnun means smooth, as the poet said: "then I turned aside with her toward the green dome, walking upon marmar masnun," meaning smooth, polished.

For this reason it is reported from Ibn Abbas that he said: it is moist earth. And from Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, and al-Dahhak also: that al-hama' al-masnun is the putrid. And it is said: what is meant by al-masnun here is that which is poured out.

His saying: {and the jinn We created before} means: before man, {from the fire of al-samum}. Ibn Abbas said: it is the samum (scorching wind) that kills.

Some of them said: al-samum is by night and by day. And among them is one who said: al-samum is by night, and al-harur (the scorching heat) is by day.

Abu Dawud al-Tayalisi said: Shu'bah narrated to us, from Abu Ishaq, who said: I entered upon Amr al-Asamm to visit him, and he said: Shall I not tell you a report I heard from Abdullah ibn Mas'ud, who said: this samum is one part out of seventy parts of the samum from which the jinn were created. Then he recited: {and the jinn We created before, from the fire of al-samum}.

And from Ibn Abbas: that the jinn were created from the flame of fire, and in another report: from the finest part of the fire.

And from Amr ibn Dinar: from the fire of the sun. And it has been transmitted in the authentic collection: "the angels were created from light, and the jinn were created from a smokeless flame of fire, and the sons of Adam were created from what has been described to you." The purpose of the verse is to draw attention to the nobility of Adam, peace be upon him, the goodness of his origin, and the purity of his source of creation.

## 15:28-33 — verses 28-33 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He, exalted, mentions how He drew attention to Adam among His angels before He created him, and how He honored him by commanding the angels to prostrate to him. And He mentions how Iblis, his enemy, held back from prostrating to him, alone among all the angels, out of envy, disbelief, obstinacy, arrogance, and pride in falsehood. For this reason he said: {I would not prostrate to a human being You created from sounding clay, from black mud molded into shape}, just as He said in another verse: {I am better than him, You created me from fire and created him from clay} [al-A'raf: 12], and His saying: {Do You see this one whom You have honored above me? If You delay me until the Day of Resurrection, I will surely lead his descendants astray, except a few} [al-Isra: 62].

Ibn Jarir has related here a gharib (singular) and remarkable report, from the hadith of Shabib ibn Bishr, from Ikrimah, from Ibn Abbas, who said: When Allah created the angels, He said, "I am going to create a human being from clay, and when I have shaped him, prostrate to him." They said, "We will not do it." So He sent fire upon them and it burned them. Then He created other angels and said to them the same thing, [and they said, "We will not do it." So He sent fire upon them and it burned them. Then He created yet other angels and said, "I am going to create a human being from clay, and when I have created him, prostrate to him," but they refused, so He sent fire upon them and it burned them. Then He created angels and said, "I am going to create a human being from clay, and when I have created him, prostrate to him"], and they said, "We have heard and we obey," except Iblis, who was among the first disbelievers.

There is considerable doubt about the soundness of this being attributed to him (Ibn Abbas), and it appears most likely to be an Isra'ili (Israelite-derived) report. And Allah knows best.

## 15:34-38 — verses 34-38 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He says, commanding Iblis with a cosmic command that cannot be opposed or resisted, to go forth from the rank he had held among the exalted assembly (al-mala' al-a'la), and that he is {rajim}, meaning stoned, cursed and cast away. He was granted that as a gradual leading on (istidraj) and a granting of respite. So when the respite was confirmed for him, may Allah disfigure him:

## 15:39-44 — verses 39-44 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, informing us about Iblis and his rebellion and insolence, that he said to the Lord: {Because You have led me astray}. Some of them said: he swore by God's having led him astray.

I say: it is also possible that it means "because of the fact that You led me astray and caused me to go astray, {I will surely make [it] fair-seeming to them}," meaning to the offspring of Adam (peace be upon him), {on earth}, that is, "I will make sins beloved to them and make them desire them, and I will incite them toward them and drive them into them relentlessly," {and I will surely lead them all astray}, meaning "just as You led me astray, and I have sworn to do so, {all of them, except Your servants among them who are sincerely devoted (mukhlasin)}," just as He said: {Do You see this one whom You have honored above me? If You grant me respite until the Day of Resurrection, I will surely entice his offspring, all except a few} [al-Isra: 62].

God the Exalted said to him, threatening and warning: {This is a straight path incumbent upon Me}, meaning "your return, all of you, is to Me, and I shall repay you for your deeds. If good, then good; and if evil, then evil," just as He said: {Indeed your Lord is ever watchful} [al-Fajr: 14].

It was also said: it means the path of truth, whose return is to God the Exalted, and to Him it leads and terminates. This was said by Mujahid, al-Hasan, and Qatadah, just as He said: {And upon God is the direction of the [right] way} [al-Nahl: 9].

Qays ibn 'Ubad, Muhammad ibn Sirin, and Qatadah read it as "hadha siratun 'aliyyun mustaqim" (this is an elevated, straight path), like His saying: {And indeed it is, in the Mother of the Book, with Us, truly elevated (or wise) and full of wisdom} [al-Zukhruf: 4], meaning "exalted." But the well-known reading is the first one.

As for His saying, {Indeed, over My servants you have no authority}, meaning "those for whom I have decreed guidance, you have no path over them, and no way to reach them," {except those who follow you from among those who are astray}, this is a discontinuous exception (istithna munqati').

Ibn Jarir related here, through the hadith of 'Abdullah ibn al-Mubarak, from 'Abdullah ibn Mawhab, who said: Yazid ibn Qusayt narrated to us, saying: The prophets used to have mosques (places of prayer) located outside their towns, and when a prophet wanted to seek knowledge from his Lord about something, he would go out to his place of prayer and pray as much as God had ordained for him, then ask about what had occurred to him. So while a prophet was in his place of prayer, the enemy of God, meaning Iblis, came and sat between him and the direction of prayer. The prophet said: "I seek refuge in God from the accursed Satan." [The enemy of God said: "Do you see the one from whom you seek refuge? He is right here." The prophet said again: "I seek refuge in God from the accursed Satan."] He repeated this three times. Then the enemy of God said: "Tell me, by what do you escape from me?" The prophet said: "Rather, tell me, by what do you overcome the son of Adam?" This was repeated twice, and each of them took a pledge from the other. The prophet said: "Indeed God the Exalted says: {Indeed, over My servants you have no authority except those who follow you from among those who are astray}." The enemy of God said: "I heard this before you were born." The prophet said: "And God says: {And if an incitement from Satan should provoke you, then seek refuge in God; indeed He is All-Hearing, All-Knowing} [al-A'raf: 200], and by God, I have never sensed your presence without seeking refuge in God from you." The enemy of God said: "You have spoken truly, by this you escape from me." The prophet said: "Tell me, by what do you overcome the son of Adam?" He said: "I take hold of him at the moment of anger and desire."

As for His saying, {And indeed, Hell is truly their appointed meeting place, all of them}, meaning Hell is the appointed place for all who follow Iblis, just as He said about the Qur'an: {And whoever among the factions disbelieves in it, the Fire is his appointed place} [Hud: 17].

Then He informs us that Hell has seven gates: {For each gate among them is an assigned portion}, meaning that for each of its gates a portion of the followers of Iblis has been decreed, who will enter it, having no escape from it, may God protect us from it, and each one enters through a gate according to his deeds, and settles at a level (darak) corresponding to his conduct.

Isma'il ibn 'Ulayyah and Shu'bah, both from Abu Harun al-Ghanawi, from Hittan ibn 'Abdullah, said: I heard 'Ali ibn Abi Talib delivering a sermon, saying: "The gates of Hell are like this," Abu Harun said, meaning layers, one above the other.

Isra'il said, from Abu Ishaq, from Hubayrah ibn Yarim, from 'Ali, may God be pleased with him, who said: "The gates of Hell are seven, one above the other. The first fills up, then the second, then the third, until they are all filled."

'Ikrimah said: {seven gates} means seven layers.

Ibn Jurayj said: {seven gates}: the first of them is Jahannam, then Lada, then al-Hutamah, then Sa'ir, then Saqar, then al-Jahim, then al-Hawiyah.

Al-Dahhak related something similar from Ibn 'Abbas. And likewise it was related from al-A'mash, similarly.

Qatadah said: {It has seven gates, for each gate among them is an assigned portion}, and by God, these are dwellings according to their deeds. Ibn Jarir related these [reports].

Juwaybir said, from al-Dahhak: {It has seven gates, for each gate among them is an assigned portion}, he said: a gate for the Jews, a gate for the Christians, a gate for the Sabians, a gate for the Magians, a gate for those who associated partners with God, meaning the disbelievers among the Arabs, a gate for the hypocrites, and a gate for the people of monotheism (tawhid). So for the people of tawhid there is hope, but for the others there is never any hope.

Al-Tirmidhi said: 'Abd ibn Humayd narrated to us, 'Uthman ibn 'Umar narrated to us, from Malik ibn Mighwal, from Junayd, from Ibn 'Umar, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "Hell has seven gates: one of its gates is for whoever draws the sword against my nation," or he said "against the nation of Muhammad."

Then he [al-Tirmidhi] said: we do not know it except through the hadith of Malik ibn Mighwal.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: my father narrated to us, 'Abbas ibn al-Walid al-Khallal narrated to us, Zayd, meaning ibn Yahya, narrated to us, Sa'id ibn Bashir narrated to us, from Qatadah, from Abu Nadrah, from Samurah ibn Jundab, from the Prophet ﷺ, concerning His saying {for each gate among them is an assigned portion}: "Among the people of the Fire there are those whom the Fire seizes up to their ankles, and among them are those whom the Fire seizes up to their waist-wrap, and among them are those whom the Fire seizes up to their collarbones, dwellings according to their deeds. That is the meaning of His saying: {for each gate among them is an assigned portion}."

## 15:45-50 — verses 45-50 (shared commentary)

### Translation

When the Exalted mentioned the state of the people of the Fire, He followed it with mention of the people of Paradise, and that they are amid gardens and springs.

His saying, {Enter it in peace}, means: safe from afflictions, greeted with peace, {secure} from every fear and terror, and not fearing expulsion, nor termination, nor annihilation.

His saying, {And We shall remove whatever rancor is in their breasts; brothers, on couches facing one another}. Al-Qasim reported from Abu Umamah, who said: the people of Paradise enter Paradise carrying whatever hatred and grudges were in their breasts in this world, until, when they meet and face one another, Allah removes from their breasts whatever rancor was there in this world. Then he recited: {And We shall remove whatever rancor is in their breasts}.

This is how it appears in this report, but al-Qasim ibn Abd al-Rahman, in his transmission from Abu Umamah, is weak (da'if).

Sunayd reported in his tafsir: [chain] Ibn Fadalah, from Luqman, from Abu Umamah, who said: no believer enters Paradise until Allah removes whatever rancor is in his breast, until He removes from it something like the ferocity of a predatory beast.

This agrees with what is in the Sahih, from the report of Qatadah: [chain] Abu al-Mutawakkil al-Naji, that Abu Sa'id al-Khudri narrated to them that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "The believers will be delivered from the Fire, and they will be held back on a bridge between Paradise and the Fire, and retribution will be exacted between them for wrongs that occurred among them in this world, until, when they have been purified and cleansed, they will be permitted to enter Paradise."

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] al-Hasan, from Yazid ibn Harun, from Hisham, from Muhammad, that is Ibn Sirin, who said: al-Ashtar sought permission to enter upon Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, while a son of Talhah was with him. Ali kept him waiting, then admitted him. When he entered, he said: "I can see that you only kept me waiting because of this man." Ali said: "Indeed." He said: "I can see that if a son of Uthman had been with you, you would have kept me waiting too." Ali said: "Indeed, I hope that I and Uthman will be among those of whom Allah Most High said: {And We shall remove whatever rancor is in their breasts; brothers, on couches facing one another}."

And [chain] al-Hasan told us: Abu Mu'awiyah al-Darir, from Abu Malik al-Ashja'i, from Abu Habibah, a client (mawla) of Talhah, who said: Imran ibn Talhah entered upon Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, after he had finished with the people of the Camel (battle). Ali welcomed him and said: "I hope that Allah will place me and your father among those of whom Allah said: {And We shall remove whatever rancor is in their breasts; brothers, on couches facing one another}." He said: Two men were sitting at the edge of the carpet, and they said: "Allah is more just than that. You killed them yesterday, and now you claim to be brothers!" Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, said: "Get up, you two, to the farthest and most remote of lands! Who else would it be, if not I and Talhah?" Abu Mu'awiyah mentioned the hadith at length.

Waki' reported, from Aban ibn Abd Allah al-Bajali, from Nu'aym ibn Abi Hind, from Rib'i ibn Khirash, from Ali, something similar, and in it he said: A man from Hamdan stood up and said: "Allah is more just than that, O Commander of the Faithful." He said: Ali shouted at him such a shout that I thought the palace shook from it. Then he said: "If it is not us, then who is it?"

Sa'id ibn Masruq said, from Abu Talhah, and he mentioned it, in it: al-Harith al-A'war said that, so Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, rose against him and struck him on the head with something he had in his hand, and said: "Then who are they, O one-eyed man, if not us?"

Sufyan al-Thawri said, from Mansur, from Ibrahim, who said: Ibn Jurmuz, the killer of al-Zubayr, came seeking permission to enter upon Ali, may Allah be pleased with him. Ali kept him barred for a long time, then admitted him. The man said to him: "As for the people of trial, you treat them harshly." Ali said: "Dust in your mouth! I hope that I, Talhah, and al-Zubayr will be among those of whom Allah said: {And We shall remove whatever rancor is in their breasts; brothers, on couches facing one another}."

Likewise al-Thawri reported, from Ja'far ibn Muhammad, from his father, from Ali, something similar.

Sufyan ibn Uyaynah said, from Isra'il, from Abu Musa, who heard al-Hasan al-Basri say: Ali said: "By Allah, it was concerning us, the people of Badr, that this verse was revealed: {And We shall remove whatever rancor is in their breasts; brothers, on couches facing one another}."

Kathir al-Nawwa' said: I entered upon Abu Ja'far Muhammad ibn Ali and said: "My ally is your ally, my peace is your peace, my enemy is your enemy, and my war is your war. I ask you by Allah: do you disavow Abu Bakr and Umar?" He said: "{Then indeed I would have gone astray and I would not be among the rightly guided}" [al-An'am: 56]. Hold both of them in love, O Kathir, and whatever befalls you because of that is upon my own neck. Then he recited this verse: {brothers, on couches facing one another}, and said: "Abu Bakr, Umar, and Ali, may Allah be pleased with all of them."

Al-Thawri said, from a man, from Abu Salih, concerning His saying {brothers, on couches facing one another}: he said: they are ten: Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, Ali, Talhah, al-Zubayr, Abd al-Rahman ibn Awf, Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas, Sa'id ibn Zayd, and Abd Allah ibn Mas'ud, may Allah be pleased with all of them.

Concerning His saying {facing one another}, Mujahid said: none of them looks at the back of another's neck.

There is a raised (marfu') hadith on this. Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Yahya ibn Abdak al-Qazwini, from Hassan ibn Hassan, from Ibrahim ibn Bishr, from Yahya ibn Ma'in, from Ibrahim al-Qurashi, from Sa'id ibn Shurahbil, from Zayd ibn Abi Awfa, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ came out to us and recited this verse: {brothers, on couches facing one another}, for the sake of Allah, looking at one another.

His saying {No weariness shall touch them therein} means: no hardship or harm, as it comes in the two Sahihs: "Allah has commanded me to give Khadijah glad tidings of a house in Paradise made of reeds (qasab), in which there is neither clamor nor weariness."

His saying {and they shall not be expelled from it} is as it comes in the hadith: "It will be said: O people of Paradise, you shall have health and never be sick again, you shall have life and never die again, you shall have youth and never grow old again, and you shall have permanent residence and never depart again." And Allah Most High said: {abiding therein forever, not desiring any change from it} [al-Kahf: 108].

His saying {Inform My servants that I am indeed the Forgiving, the Merciful, and that My punishment is indeed the painful punishment} means: inform, O Muhammad, My servants that I possess mercy and I possess painful retribution.

Mention has already preceded of a verse similar to this noble verse, and it points to the two stations of hope and fear. Concerning the occasion of its revelation, it is mentioned what Musa ibn Ubaydah reported, from Mus'ab ibn Thabit, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ passed by some of his companions who were laughing, and he said: "Remember Paradise, and remember the Fire." Then this was revealed: {Inform My servants that I am indeed the Forgiving, the Merciful, and that My punishment is indeed the painful punishment}. Ibn Abi Hatim reported it, and it is a mursal (a report missing a link at the end of its chain, here lacking a Companion).

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] al-Muthanna, from Ishaq, from Ibn al-Makki, from Ibn al-Mubarak, from Mus'ab ibn Thabit, from Asim ibn Ubayd Allah, from Ibn Abi Rabah, from a man among the companions of the Prophet ﷺ, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ appeared to us from the gate through which the Banu Shaybah enter, and said: "Do I not see you laughing?" Then he turned away, and when he reached the Stone (the Black Stone), he came back to us walking backwards, and said: "When I went out, Gabriel, upon him be peace, came to me and said: 'O Muhammad, Allah says: Why do you make My servants despair? {Inform My servants that I am indeed the Forgiving, the Merciful, and that My punishment is indeed the painful punishment}.'"

Sa'id said, from Qatadah, concerning His saying {Inform My servants that I am indeed the Forgiving, the Merciful}: he said: it has reached us that the Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, said: "If the servant knew the extent of Allah's pardon, he would not restrain himself from what is forbidden, and if he knew the extent of His punishment, he would exhaust himself in grief."

## 15:51-56 — verses 51-56 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says: inform them, O Muhammad, about the story of {the guests of Ibrahim}. "Guest" (dayf) is used for both the singular and the plural, like "visitor" (zawr) and "traveler" (sufr). And how {they entered upon him and said, "Peace," he said, "Indeed, we are fearful of you"}, meaning: afraid.

He mentioned the reason for his fear of them: when he saw that their hands did not reach what he had presented to them as hospitality, which was the fattened, roasted calf.

{They said, "Do not be afraid"}, meaning: do not fear, {and they gave him glad tidings of a knowing boy} [al-Dhariyat: 28], and this was Ishaq (peace be upon him), as was mentioned previously in Surat Hud.

Then he said, in astonishment at his own old age and that of his wife, and seeking confirmation of the promise: {"Do you give me glad tidings though old age has come upon me? So of what do you give me glad tidings?"} So they answered him, affirming what they had announced to him, as confirmation and glad tidings upon glad tidings: {They said, "We have given you glad tidings in truth, so do not be among the despairing."} Some of them recited it as "al-qanitin" (with a different vowel pattern but the same meaning). So he answered them that he was not despairing, but rather he was hoping from Allah for the child, even though he had grown old and his wife had grown old in years, for he knew of Allah's power and mercy something far greater than that.

## 15:57-60 — verses 57-60 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Most High relates, reporting about Ibrahim, peace be upon him, that when the fear had left him and the good tidings had come to him, he began to ask them about what they had come for. So they said: {We have been sent to a criminal people} meaning the people of Lot. And they informed him that they would save the family of Lot from among them, except his wife, for she would be among those destroyed. This is why they said: {Except his wife; we have decreed that she is indeed among those who remain behind} meaning those who remain and are destroyed.

## 15:61-64 — verses 61-64 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty informs about Lot (peace be upon him) when the angels came to him in the form of handsome young men, and they entered his house. He said: {Indeed, you are a people unknown to me. They said: Rather, we have brought you that concerning which they used to be in doubt} meaning: their punishment, their destruction, and their ruin, which they used to doubt would befall them and descend upon their courtyard. {And we have come to you with the truth}, as the Almighty said: {We do not send down the angels except with truth} [al-Hijr: 8].

## 15:65-66 — verses 65-66 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted mentions concerning the angels that they commanded him to travel by night with his family after part of the night had passed, and that Lot, peace be upon him, should walk behind them, so that he could better guard and protect them.

This is indeed how the Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to walk during military expeditions, for he would be at the rear guard, urging on the weak and carrying those who fell behind.

As for His saying, {and let none of you look back}, meaning: when you hear the cry befalling the people, do not turn to look at them, but leave them to what has befallen them of punishment and retribution. {And proceed where you are commanded}, as if there were someone with them guiding them along the way.

{And We decreed for him that matter}, meaning: We informed him beforehand of this, {that the last remnant of these people would be cut off by the morning}, that is, at the time of morning, as He says in another verse: {Indeed their appointed time is the morning. Is not the morning near?} [Hud: 81]

## 15:67-72 — verses 67-72 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty informs about the coming of the people of Lot when they learned of his guests and the comeliness of their faces, and that they came rejoicing and delighted at them: {He said, "Indeed, these are my guests, so do not disgrace me. Fear Allah and do not shame me."}

He said this to them only before he knew that they were messengers of Allah, as stated in the context of Surat Hud. Here, however, mention of them being messengers of Allah has already preceded, and the mention of the coming of his people and his disputation with them is joined to it by "and." But the conjunction "wa" does not require sequence, especially when there is evidence indicating otherwise. They answered him saying: {"Have we not forbidden you from [hosting] the people [of the world]?"} that is, did we not forbid you from hosting anyone? So he directed them to their wives, and to what their Lord had created for them of the lawful private parts among them. The discussion of this matter has already preceded, which makes repeating it unnecessary.

All of this while they were heedless of what was intended for them, of the calamity that had already encompassed them, and of what punishment, firmly settled, would overtake them by morning. Therefore the Almighty said to His Prophet ﷺ: {By your life, indeed they were wandering blindly in their intoxication.} The Almighty swore by the life of His Prophet ﷺ, and in this is a tremendous honor, an elevated station, and a broad standing.

Amr ibn Malik al-Nukri said, from Abu al-Jawza, from Ibn Abbas, that he said: Allah did not create, bring into being, or originate a soul more honored before Him than Muhammad ﷺ, and I have not heard of Allah swearing by the life of anyone other than him. Allah the Almighty said: {By your life, indeed they were wandering blindly in their intoxication} [meaning: by your life, your lifetime, and your remaining in this world, "indeed they were wandering blindly in their intoxication"]. Narrated by Ibn Jarir.

Qatadah said: {in their intoxication} means: in their misguidance, {wandering blindly} means: playing about.

Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn Abbas: {By your life} means by your living, {indeed they were wandering blindly in their intoxication}, he said: they were bewildered.

## 15:73-77 — verses 73-77 (shared commentary)

### Translation

He, exalted, says: {Then the Cry seized them}, that is, what came upon them from the crashing sound at sunrise, that is, at its rising, and that was together with the raising of their lands up to the vault of the sky, then their overturning, making their upper part their lower part, and sending down upon them stones of baked clay (sijjil). The discussion of sijjil has already preceded in [the surah of] Hud, with sufficient explanation there.

His saying: {Indeed in that are signs for al-mutawassimin (those who discern by physical marks)} means that the traces of this vengeance are apparent upon those lands for whoever reflects upon that and discerns it with the eye of his sight and his insight. As Mujahid said concerning His saying {li'l-mutawassimin}: he said, those who possess physiognomic insight (al-mutafarrisin).

From Ibn Abbas and al-Dahhak: for those who look. Qatadah said: for those who take heed. Malik said, on the authority of some of the people of Medina: {li'l-mutawassimin} means for those who reflect carefully.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] from Abu Sa'id, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "Beware of the discernment (firasa) of the believer, for he sees by the light of Allah." Then the Prophet ﷺ recited: {Indeed in that are signs for al-mutawassimin}.

Al-Tirmidhi and Ibn Jarir both narrated it from the hadith of Amr ibn Qays al-Mula'i, and al-Tirmidhi said: we do not know it except from this route.

Ibn Jarir also said: [chain] from Ibn Umar, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "Beware of the discernment of the believer, for indeed the believer sees by the light of Allah."

Ibn Jarir said: [chain] from Thawban, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "Beware of the discernment of the believer, for he sees by the light of Allah and speaks by the enabling grace (tawfiq) of Allah."

He also said: [chain] from Anas ibn Malik, who said: the Prophet ﷺ said, "Indeed Allah has servants who recognize people through tawassum (discerning insight)."

Al-Hafiz Abu Bakr al-Bazzar narrated it: [chain] Abu Bishr, who is called Ibn al-Muzalliq, and he said: he was reliable (thiqa), from Thabit, from Anas, who said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "Indeed Allah has servants who recognize people through tawassum."

His saying: {And indeed it is on a road that remains} means that the town of Sadum (Sodom), which was struck by what struck it of literal and figurative overturning and the pelting of stones, until it became a foul, noxious lake, lies on a well-trodden road whose paths remain in constant use to this day. As He, exalted, said: {And indeed you pass by them in the morning, and by night. Then will you not use reason?} [al-Saffat: 137-138]

Mujahid and al-Dahhak said concerning {And indeed it is on a road that remains}: he said, a marked road. Qatadah said: a clear road. Qatadah also said: in one single tract of land.

Al-Suddi said: with a clear register (kitab mubin), meaning like His saying: {And everything We have enumerated in a clear register (imam mubin)} [Ya-Sin: 12]. But the meaning here is not what he said, and Allah knows best.

His saying: {Indeed in that is a sign for the believers} means that what We did to the people of Lut of destruction and ruin, and Our saving of Lut and his family, is a clear and evident proof for those who believe in Allah and His messengers.

## 15:78-79 — verses 78-79 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Companions of the Aykah (the Thicket) were the people of Shu'ayb (peace be upon him).

Al-Dahhak, Qatadah, and others said: al-Aykah means the intertwined trees.

Their wrongdoing consisted of their associating partners with Allah, their waylaying of the road, and their giving short measure and weight. So Allah took vengeance upon them through the Scream, the Earthquake, and the punishment of the Day of the Overshadowing Cloud. They had lived close to the people of Lut, coming after them in time and adjacent to them in place. It is for this reason that Allah, Exalted is He, said: {And indeed, both of them are on a clear road} meaning a plain, evident path.

Ibn 'Abbas, Mujahid, and al-Dahhak said: an open road. It is for this reason that when Shu'ayb warned his people, he said in his warning to them: {And the people of Lut are not far from you}

## 15:80-84 — verses 80-84 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The People of al-Hijr (the Rock) are Thamud, who denied their prophet Salih (peace be upon him). Whoever denies one messenger has in fact denied all the messengers; this is why the denial of "the messengers" is attributed to them in general terms.

The Most High mentions that He gave them signs that pointed to the truth of what Salih brought them, such as the she-camel that Allah brought forth for them, by Salih's supplication, from a solid rock. She would roam freely in their land, having her turn to drink, and they having their turn to drink on a known day. But when they transgressed and hamstrung her, he said to them: {Enjoy yourselves in your homes for three days. That is a promise that will not be proven false} [Hud: 65]. And the Most High said: {As for Thamud, We guided them, but they preferred blindness over guidance} [Fussilat: 17].

The Most High also mentions that they {used to carve out houses from the mountains, feeling secure}, that is, without any fear or need driving them to it, but rather out of insolence, wantonness, and idle amusement, as can still be seen from what they did with their houses in the valley of al-Hijr. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ passed by that place on his way to Tabuk, and he covered his head and hastened his mount, saying to his companions: "Do not enter the houses of the people who were punished, unless you do so weeping. And if you cannot weep, then do not enter at all, for fear that what befell them might befall you as well."

Regarding His saying: {So the Shout seized them in the morning}, that is, at the time of morning on the fourth day, {and what they used to earn availed them nothing}, meaning that what they used to derive benefit from, their crops and fruits, for the sake of which they had begrudged the she-camel her share of water until they hamstrung her so that she would not restrict their access to water, none of that wealth could ward anything off from them, nor did it benefit them in the least when the command of your Lord came upon them.

## 15:85-86 — verses 85-86 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: {And We did not create the heavens and the earth and what is between them except in truth}, meaning: with justice; {that He may recompense those who do evil for what they have done and recompense those who do good with the best reward} [al-Najm: 31]. And the Almighty says: {And We did not create the heaven and the earth and what is between them in vain. That is the assumption of those who disbelieve, so woe to those who disbelieve from the Fire} [Sad: 27]. And He says: {Did you then think that We created you in vain and that you would not be returned to Us? So exalted is Allah, the True King, there is no god but He, Lord of the Noble Throne} [al-Mu'minun: 115-116].

Then He informed His Prophet ﷺ of the establishment of the Hour, and that it will inevitably come to pass. Then He commanded him to show gracious forbearance toward the polytheists, in the face of their harming him and their denial of what he had brought them, as the Almighty says: {So forgive them and say, "Peace," for they are going to know} [al-Zukhruf: 89].

Mujahid, Qatadah, and others said: this was before fighting was ordained. And it is as they said, for this surah is Meccan, and fighting was only legislated after the Hijrah.

His statement: {Indeed, your Lord is the Creator, the All-Knowing} is an affirmation of the resurrection, and that He, the Almighty, is capable of establishing the Hour, for He is the Creator whom nothing renders incapable of creating what He wills, and He is the All-Knowing of what has decomposed of bodies and scattered throughout all the regions of the earth, as the Almighty says: {Is not He who created the heavens and the earth able to create the like of them? Yes indeed, and He is the Creator, the All-Knowing. His command, when He intends a thing, is only to say to it, "Be," and it is. So exalted is He in whose hand is the dominion of all things, and to Him you will be returned} [Ya-Sin: 81-83].

## 15:87-88 — verses 87-88 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says to His Prophet: just as We have given you the great Qur'an, do not look toward this world and its adornment, and what We have granted its people to enjoy of fleeting bloom in order to try them with it. Do not envy them for what they possess, and do not let yourself waste away in sorrow over their denial of you and their opposition to your religion. {And lower your wing to those who follow you among the believers} [al-Shu'ara: 215], meaning: be gentle and softhearted toward them, as the Almighty said: {There has certainly come to you a Messenger from among yourselves. Grievous to him is what you suffer; he is concerned over you and to the believers is kind and merciful} [al-Tawbah: 128].

There has been disagreement over what "the seven oft-repeated" (al-sab' al-mathani) refers to.

Ibn Mas'ud, Ibn 'Umar, Ibn 'Abbas, Mujahid, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, al-Dahhak, and others said: they are the seven long surahs, meaning: al-Baqarah, Aal 'Imran, al-Nisa', al-Ma'idah, al-An'am, al-A'raf, and Yunus. This was stated explicitly by Ibn 'Abbas and Sa'id ibn Jubayr.

Sa'id said: in them the obligatory duties, the legal punishments, the narratives, and the rulings are made clear.

Ibn 'Abbas said: in them the parables, the reports, and the lessons are made clear.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Sufyan said: "al-mathani" means the paired ones: al-Baqarah, Aal 'Imran, al-Nisa', al-Ma'idah, al-An'am, al-A'raf, and al-Anfal and Bara'ah counted as one surah.

Ibn 'Abbas said: no one was given them except the Prophet ﷺ, while Musa (peace be upon him) was given two of them. Narrated by Hushaym, from al-Hajjaj, from al-Walid ibn al-'Ayzar, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, from him.

Al-A'mash said, from Muslim al-Batin, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, from Ibn 'Abbas, who said: the Prophet ﷺ was given seven of the long mathani, and Musa (peace be upon him) was given six, but when he threw down the tablets two were lifted away and four remained.

Mujahid said: they are the seven long surahs. And it is also said: it is the great Qur'an itself.

Khasif reported, from Ziyad ibn Abi Maryam, concerning the saying of the Almighty {seven of the mathani}, that it means: I have given you seven parts: I command, I forbid, I give glad tidings, I warn, I strike parables, I enumerate blessings, and I inform you of the tidings of the Qur'an. Narrated by Ibn Jarir and Ibn Abi Hatim.

The second opinion: that it is al-Fatihah, which consists of seven verses. This was narrated from 'Umar, 'Ali, Ibn Mas'ud, and Ibn 'Abbas. Ibn 'Abbas said: the basmalah is the seventh verse, and God has singled you out with it. This was also said by Ibrahim al-Nakha'i, 'Abdullah ibn 'Ubayd ibn 'Umayr, Ibn Abi Mulaykah, Shahr ibn Hawshab, al-Hasan al-Basri, and Mujahid.

Qatadah said: it was mentioned to us that they are the Opening of the Book (Fatihat al-Kitab), and that they are repeated in every recitation. In one report: in every prescribed or voluntary rak'ah.

Ibn Jarir chose this view, citing the hadiths reported concerning it, which we have already presented in the section on the virtues of Surat al-Fatihah at the beginning of the tafsir. Praise be to God.

Al-Bukhari, may God have mercy on him, cited two hadiths here:

The first: [chain] Abu Sa'id ibn al-Mu'alla said: the Prophet ﷺ passed by me while I was praying, and he called me, but I did not go to him until I had finished my prayer. Then I went to him, and he said: "What kept you from coming to me?" I said: I was praying. He said: "Did God not say: {O you who believe, respond to God and to the Messenger when he calls you} [al-Anfal: 24]? Shall I not teach you the greatest surah in the Qur'an before I leave the mosque?" The Prophet ﷺ then made to leave, and I reminded him, so he said: "{Praise be to God, Lord of all the worlds} [al-Fatihah: 2], it is the seven oft-repeated verses and the great Qur'an which I have been given."

The second: [chain] Abu Hurayrah, may God be pleased with him, said: the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "The Mother of the Qur'an is the seven oft-repeated verses and the great Qur'an."

This is an explicit text that al-Fatihah is the seven mathani and the great Qur'an. But this does not preclude describing the other seven long surahs with the same term, since that quality is present in them as well, just as it does not preclude describing the Qur'an in its entirety with that term either, as the Almighty said: {God has sent down the best of speech, a Book consistent, oft-repeated (mathani)} [al-Zumar: 23]. So it is mathani from one angle, and consistent (mutashabih) from another angle, and it is also the great Qur'an. This is similar to when he, peace be upon him, was asked about the mosque that was founded upon piety, and he pointed to his own mosque, while the verse had actually been revealed concerning the mosque of Quba. There is no contradiction here, for mentioning one thing does not negate mentioning something else when they share that same quality. And God knows best.

Regarding His saying: {Do not extend your eyes toward what We have given certain classes of them to enjoy}, meaning: be content with what God has given you of the great Qur'an, in place of the enjoyment and fleeting bloom that they possess.

From this, Ibn 'Uyaynah went on to interpret the authentic hadith, "He is not one of us who does not chant the Qur'an melodiously (yataghanna)," to mean that one becomes self-sufficient through it in place of anything else. This is a sound interpretation, but it is not what is actually intended by the hadith, as was mentioned earlier at the beginning of the tafsir.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: it was mentioned from Waki' ibn al-Jarrah, [chain] from Abu Rafi', a companion of the Prophet ﷺ, who said: the Prophet ﷺ hosted a guest, but the Prophet ﷺ had nothing with which to prepare food for him. So he sent to a Jewish man, saying: Muhammad, the Messenger of God, says to you: lend me some flour until the new moon of Rajab. The man said: No, not without a pledge. So I went to the Prophet ﷺ and informed him, and he said: "By God, I am indeed trustworthy to those in the heavens and trustworthy to those on the earth, and if he lends to me or sells to me, I will surely repay him." When I left his presence, this verse was revealed: {Do not extend your eyes toward what We have given certain classes of them to enjoy, the bloom of the life of this world} to the end of the verse [Ta Ha: 131], as if consoling him regarding this world.

Al-'Awfi said, from Ibn 'Abbas, concerning {Do not extend your eyes}: he said: a man is forbidden from coveting the wealth of his companion.

Mujahid said, concerning {what We have given certain classes of them to enjoy}: they are the wealthy.

## 15:89-93 — verses 89-93 (shared commentary)

### Translation

God Most High commands His Prophet ﷺ, to say to the people that he is {the manifest warner}, clear in his warning, a warner to the people of a painful punishment that would befall them for denying him, just as it befell the nations before them who denied their messengers, and the punishment and vengeance that God sent down upon them.

His saying, {al-muqtasimin} (those who swore mutual oaths) means those who bound themselves by mutual oaths, that is, they swore together to oppose the prophets, to deny them, and to harm them, just as God Most High said, relating of the people of Salih (peace be upon him), that they: {said, "Swear to one another by God that we will attack him and his family by night"} [al-Naml: 49], meaning: we will kill them at night. Mujahid said: taqasamu means they swore mutual oaths.

{And they swore by God with their most earnest oaths that God would not resurrect one who dies} [al-Nahl: 38]. {Did you not swear before that there would be no change of state for you?} [Ibrahim: 44]. {Are these the ones concerning whom you swore that God would not grant them any mercy?} [al-A'raf: 49]. It is as though they never denied anything without swearing an oath about it, so they were named "the swearers of mutual oaths" (al-muqtasimin).

Abd al-Rahman ibn Zayd ibn Aslam said: al-muqtasimun are the companions of Salih, those who swore to one another by God that they would attack him and his family.

In the two Sahihs, from Abu Musa [al-Ash'ari], from the Prophet ﷺ, he said: "My likeness and the likeness of that with which God sent me is like the likeness of a man who came to his people and said: O my people, I have seen the army with my own eyes, and I am the naked warner, so flee, flee! A group of his people obeyed him and set out at night, departing at their ease, and they were saved. But another group among them disbelieved him and remained in their place, and the army came upon them in the morning and destroyed them and wiped them out. That is the likeness of one who obeys me and follows what I have brought, and the likeness of one who disobeys me and denies the truth that I have brought."

His saying, {those who have made the Qur'an into pieces (idin)} means: they divided up their revealed scriptures, believing in part and disbelieving in part.

Al-Bukhari said: Ya'qub ibn Ibrahim narrated to us, Hushaym narrated to us, Abu Bishr informed us, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr, from Ibn Abbas, concerning {they made the Qur'an into pieces}: he said, they are the People of the Book, they divided it into parts, believing in some of it and disbelieving in some of it.

Ubaydullah ibn Musa narrated to us, from al-A'mash, from Abu Zabyan, from Ibn Abbas, concerning {just as We sent down upon al-muqtasimin}: he said, they believed in part and disbelieved in part, the Jews and the Christians.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: it has been related from Mujahid, Ikrimah, Sa'id ibn Jubayr, al-Hasan, and al-Dahhak, the like of that.

Al-Hakam ibn Aban said, from Ikrimah, from Ibn Abbas, concerning {they made the Qur'an into pieces}: he said, it means sorcery. Ikrimah said: al-'adh, in the language of Quraysh, means sorcery; they would say of a sorceress, "she is al-'adihah."

Mujahid said: they broke it into parts, some of them saying "sorcery," others saying "soothsaying," and others saying "tales of the ancients."

Ata' said: some of them said "sorcerer," some said "madman," and some said "soothsayer." That is what is meant by al-idin. The same has been related from al-Dahhak and others.

Muhammad ibn Ishaq said, from Muhammad ibn Abi Muhammad, from Sa'id or Ikrimah, from Ibn Abbas: that al-Walid ibn al-Mughirah gathered around him a group of Quraysh, and he was a man of standing among them, and the pilgrimage season had come. He said to them: O assembly of Quraysh, this season has arrived, and the delegations of the Arabs will be coming to you during it, and they have heard about the matter of this companion of yours. So agree upon a single opinion about him and do not differ, lest some of you contradict others and your statements refute one another. They said: and you, O Abu Abd Shams, speak and lay out for us an opinion that we can adopt. He said: rather, you speak so that I may hear. They said: we will say "soothsayer." He said: he is not a soothsayer. They said: then we will say "madman." He said: he is not a madman! They said: then we will say "poet." He said: he is not a poet! They said: then we will say "sorcerer." He said: he is not a sorcerer! They said: then what shall we say? He said: by God, his speech has a sweetness to it, and there is nothing you could say about this but that it would be known to be false. And the closest thing you could say is that you say: he is a sorcerer. So they dispersed from him upon that, and God sent down concerning them: {those who have made the Qur'an into pieces}, that is, into different categories. {So by your Lord, We will surely question them all together about what they used to do}, meaning that small group of men who said that concerning the Messenger of God.

Atiyyah al-Awfi said, from Ibn Umar, concerning His saying {We will surely question them all together about what they used to do}: he said, about "there is no god but God."

Abd al-Razzaq said: al-Thawri informed us, from Layth, that is, Ibn Abi Sulaym, from Mujahid, concerning His saying {We will surely question them all together about what they used to do}: he said, about "there is no god but God."

Al-Tirmidhi, Abu Ya'la al-Mawsili, Ibn Jarir, and Ibn Abi Hatim have related, from the hadith of Sharik al-Qadi, from Layth ibn Abi Sulaym, from Bashir ibn Nahik, from Anas, from the Prophet ﷺ, concerning {so by your Lord, We will surely question them all together}: [he said] about "there is no god but God."

Ibn Idris related it from Layth, from Bashir, from Anas, as a mawquf (halted report).

Ibn Jarir said: Ahmad narrated to us, Abu Ahmad narrated to us, Sharik narrated to us, from Hilal, from Abdullah ibn Ukaym, who said: Abdullah, that is, Ibn Mas'ud, said: By Him besides whom there is no god, there is not one of you but that God will be alone with him on the Day of Resurrection, just as one of you is alone with the moon on the night it is full. And He will say: O son of Adam, what deceived you concerning Me, about Me? O son of Adam, what did you do with what you knew? O son of Adam, how did you answer the messengers?

Abu Ja'far said, from al-Rabi', from Abu al-'Aliyah: he said, all servants will be questioned about two matters on the Day of Resurrection: about what they used to worship, and how they answered the messengers.

Ibn Uyaynah said: about your deeds, and about your possessions.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: my father narrated to us, Ahmad ibn Abi al-Hawari narrated to us, Yunus al-Haddad narrated to us, from Abu Hamzah al-Shaybani, from Mu'adh ibn Jabal, who said: the Messenger of God ﷺ said to me: "O Mu'adh, the believer will indeed be questioned on the Day of Resurrection about all his efforts, even the kohl of his eyes and the crumbling of clay between his two fingers, so let me not find you on the Day of Resurrection with anyone happier than you with what God has given you."

Ali ibn Abi Talhah said, from Ibn Abbas, concerning {so by your Lord, We will surely question them all together about what they used to do}, then He said {so on that day, no man or jinn will be asked about his sin} [al-Rahman: 39]: he said, He will not ask them, "did you do such and such?" for He knows that better than they do, but rather He will say: "why did you do such and such?"

## 15:94-99 — verses 94-99 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says, commanding His Messenger ﷺ, to convey what He had sent him with, to carry it out, and to declare it openly, which is confronting the polytheists with it directly, as Ibn 'Abbas said regarding {So proclaim openly what you are commanded}: meaning, carry it out. And in another report: do what you are commanded.

Mujahid said: it means reciting the Qur'an aloud in prayer.

Abu 'Ubaydah reported from 'Abdullah ibn Mas'ud: The Prophet ﷺ remained concealing himself until {So proclaim openly what you are commanded} was revealed, whereupon he and his companions went out openly.

Regarding His saying, {And turn away from the polytheists. Indeed, We have sufficed you against the mockers}: meaning, convey what has been revealed to you from your Lord, and do not pay heed to the polytheists who wish to turn you away from the signs of God. {They wish that you would compromise, so they would compromise} [al-Qalam: 9]. Do not fear them, for God is sufficient for you against them and will protect you from them, as He, the Exalted, said: {O Messenger, convey what has been revealed to you from your Lord, and if you do not, then you have not conveyed His message. And God will protect you from the people} [al-Ma'idah: 67].

The hafiz Abu Bakr al-Bazzar said: [chain] Yazid ibn Dirham said: I heard Anas say concerning this verse, {Indeed, We have sufficed you against the mockers, those who set up another god with God}:

He said: The Messenger of God ﷺ passed by, and some of them made a mocking gesture at him. Then Gabriel came, I think he said, and made a gesture at them, and it struck their bodies like the mark of a stab wound, until they died.

Muhammad ibn Ishaq said, as related to me by Yazid ibn Ruman, from 'Urwah ibn al-Zubayr: The chief mockers were five men of standing and nobility among their people, from the Banu Asad ibn 'Abd al-'Uzza ibn Qusayy: al-Aswad ibn al-Muttalib, Abu Zam'ah, against whom the Messenger of God ﷺ, as I have been informed, had prayed because of the harm and mockery that reached him from him, saying: "O God, blind his sight and bereave him of his children." And from Banu Zuhrah: al-Aswad ibn 'Abd Yaghuth ibn Wahb ibn 'Abd Manaf ibn Zuhrah. And from Banu Makhzum: al-Walid ibn al-Mughirah ibn 'Abdullah ibn 'Umar ibn Makhzum. And from Banu Sahm ibn 'Amr ibn Husays ibn Ka'b ibn Lu'ayy: al-'As ibn Wa'il ibn Hisham ibn Sa'id ibn Sa'd. And from Khuza'ah: al-Harith ibn al-Tulatilah ibn 'Amr ibn al-Harith ibn 'Abd 'Amr ibn Malkan. When they persisted in evil and multiplied their mockery of the Messenger of God ﷺ, God the Exalted revealed: {So proclaim openly what you are commanded, and turn away from the polytheists. Indeed, We have sufficed you against the mockers} up to His saying, {then they will come to know}.

Ibn Ishaq said: Yazid ibn Ruman related, from 'Urwah ibn al-Zubayr or another of the scholars, that Gabriel came to the Messenger of God ﷺ while he was circling the Ka'bah. He stood, and the Messenger of God ﷺ stood beside him. Al-Aswad ibn al-Muttalib passed by him, and he threw a green leaf at his face, and he went blind. Then al-Aswad ibn 'Abd Yaghuth passed by him, and he pointed at his belly, so his belly became dropsical, and he died of the swelling from it. Then al-Walid ibn al-Mughirah passed by him, and he pointed to the mark of a wound at the lower part of his ankle, which had struck him two years earlier while he was dragging his lower garment. This was because he had passed by a man of Khuza'ah who was feathering his arrows, and one of the arrows caught in his garment and scratched his leg, a scratch that was nothing in itself, but it reopened and killed him. Then al-'As ibn Wa'il passed by him, and he pointed to the sole of his foot. He set out on a donkey of his heading toward al-Ta'if, and the donkey knelt down upon a thorny plant, and a thorn from it entered the sole of his foot and killed him. Then al-Harith ibn al-Tulatilah passed by him, and he pointed to his head, and he blew pus from his nose, and it killed him.

Muhammad ibn Ishaq said: [chain] from Ibn 'Abbas, who said: Their chief was al-Walid ibn al-Mughirah, and he was the one who gathered them together.

It has likewise been related from Sa'id ibn Jubayr and 'Ikrimah, similar to the account of Muhammad ibn Ishaq from Yazid, from 'Urwah, at length, except that Sa'id says: al-Harith ibn Ghaytalah, while 'Ikrimah says: al-Harith ibn Qays.

Al-Zuhri said: In truth, he is al-Harith ibn Qays, and his mother was Ghaytalah.

It has likewise been related from Mujahid, Miqsam, Qatadah, and others, that they were five men.

Al-Sha'bi said: They were seven.

But the well-known account is the first one.

Regarding His saying, {those who set up another god with God, then they will come to know}: this is a stern warning and a firm threat against whoever sets up another object of worship alongside God.

Regarding His saying, {And We already know that your chest tightens because of what they say. So exalt with praise of your Lord and be among those who prostrate}: meaning, We indeed know, O Muhammad, that you experience constriction and tightness of chest because of the harm they cause you. So let that not trouble you, and let it not turn you away from conveying the message of God. Put your trust in God, for He is sufficient for you and will support you against them. So occupy yourself with the remembrance of God, with praising Him, exalting Him, and worshiping Him, which is prayer. This is why He said, {and be among those who prostrate}, as is stated in the hadith related by Imam Ahmad:

[chain] Nu'aym ibn Hammar heard the Messenger of God ﷺ say: "God said: O son of Adam, do not fail to perform four units of prayer at the beginning of the day; I will suffice you for the rest of it..."

Abu Dawud also related it from the hadith of Makhul, from Kathir ibn Murrah, in similar wording.

This is why, whenever a matter weighed heavily on the Messenger of God ﷺ, he would pray.

Regarding His saying, {And worship your Lord until certainty comes to you}: al-Bukhari said: Salim said: it means death.

This Salim is Salim ibn 'Abdullah ibn 'Umar, as Ibn Jarir said: [chain] from Salim ibn 'Abdullah regarding {And worship your Lord until certainty comes to you}, he said: it means death.

This is likewise what Mujahid, al-Hasan, Qatadah, 'Abd al-Rahman ibn Zayd ibn Aslam, and others said.

The evidence for this is God's statement informing of the people of the Fire, that they said: {We were not among those who prayed, nor did we feed the poor, and we used to indulge with those who indulged, and we used to deny the Day of Judgment, until certainty came to us} [al-Muddaththir: 43-47].

It is established in the authentic collection, from the hadith of al-Zuhri, from Kharijah ibn Zayd ibn Thabit, from Umm al-'Ala', a woman of the Ansar, that when the Messenger of God ﷺ came in upon 'Uthman ibn Maz'un, who had died, she said: I said, "God's mercy be upon you, Abu al-Sa'ib. I bear witness for you that God has honored you." The Messenger of God ﷺ said: "How do you know that God has honored him?" I said, "By my father and mother, O Messenger of God, then who?" He said: "As for him, certainty has come to him, and I truly hope good for him."

It may be deduced from this noble verse, His saying {And worship your Lord until certainty comes to you}, that worship such as prayer and the like remains obligatory upon a person as long as his reason remains intact, so he should pray according to his condition, as is established in Sahih al-Bukhari, from 'Imran ibn Husayn, may God be pleased with him and his father, that the Messenger of God ﷺ said: "Pray standing; if you are unable, then sitting; if you are unable, then on your side."

It may also be deduced from it a refutation of those heretics who hold that certainty here means knowledge (ma'rifah), such that once one of them attains this knowledge, according to them, the obligation of religious duty falls away from him. This is disbelief, error, and ignorance, for the prophets, peace be upon them, and their companions were the most knowledgeable of people about God and the most aware of His rights, His attributes, and what is due to Him of exaltation, and yet along with this they were the most devoted of people in worship, and the most constant of people in performing good deeds until the moment of death. Rather, what is meant by certainty here is death, as we have already stated. Praise and gratitude belong to God, and praise belongs to God for guidance. In Him alone is help sought and upon Him alone is reliance placed, and He is the One asked to cause us to die in the most complete and best of states, for He is generous and noble.

God is sufficient for us, and He is the best of guardians.

قَالَ: مَرَّ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ، فَغَمَزَهُ بَعْضُهُمْ، فَجَاءَ جِبْرِيلُ -أَحْسَبُهُ قَالَ: فَغَمَزَهُمْ فَوَقَعَ فِي أَجْسَادِهِمْ -كَهَيْئَةِ الطَّعْنَةِ حَتَّى مَاتُوا 

وَقَالَ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ إِسْحَاقَ: كَانَ عُظَمَاءُ الْمُسْتَهْزِئِينَ -كَمَا حَدَّثَنِي يَزِيدَ بْنِ رُومَانَ، عَنْ عُرْوَةَ بْنِ الزُّبَيْرِ -خَمْسَةَ نَفَرٍ، كَانُوا ذَوِي أَسْنَانٍ وَشَرَفٍ فِي قَوْمِهِمْ، مِنْ بَنِي أَسَدِ بْنِ عَبْدِ الْعُزَّى بْنِ قُصي: الْأَسْوَدُ بْنُ الْمُطَّلِبِ أَبُو زَمْعَةَ، كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ -فِيمَا بَلَغَنِي -قَدْ دَعَا عَلَيْهِ، لِمَا كَانَ يَبْلُغُهُ مِنْ أَذَاهُ وَاسْتِهْزَائِهِ [بِهِ] فَقَالَ: اللَّهُمَّ، أَعْمِ بَصَرَهُ، وَأَثْكِلْهُ وَلَدَهُ. وَمِنْ بَنِي زُهْرَةَ: الْأَسْوَدُ بْنُ عَبْدِ يَغُوثَ بْنِ وَهْبِ بْنِ عَبْدِ مَنَافِ بْنِ زُهرة. وَمِنْ بَنِي مَخْزُومٍ: الْوَلِيدَ بْنَ الْمُغِيرَةِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عُمَر بْنِ مَخْزُومٍ. وَمِنْ بَنِي سَهْمِ بْنِ عَمْرِو بْنِ هُصيص بْنِ كَعْبِ بْنِ لُؤَيٍّ: الْعَاصُ بْنُ وَائِلِ بْنِ هِشَامِ بْنِ سَعِيدِ بْنِ سَعْدٍ. وَمِنْ خُزَاعَةَ: الْحَارِثُ بْنُ الطُّلَاطِلَةِ بْنِ عَمْرِو بْنِ الْحَارِثِ بْنِ عَبْدِ عَمْرِو بْنِ مَلْكَانَ -فَلَمَّا تَمَادَوْا فِي الشَّرِّ وَأَكْثَرُوا بِرَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ الِاسْتِهْزَاءَ، أَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَاصْدَعْ بِمَا تُؤْمَرُ وَأَعْرِضْ عَنِ الْمُشْرِكِينَ إِنَّا كَفَيْنَاكَ الْمُسْتَهْزِئِينَ﴾ إِلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿فَسَوْفَ يَعْلَمُونَ﴾

وَقَالَ ابْنُ إسحاق: فحدث يَزِيدَ بْنِ رُومَانَ، عَنْ عُرْوَةَ بْنِ الزُّبَيْرِ، أَوْ غَيْرِهِ مِنَ الْعُلَمَاءِ، أَنَّ جِبْرِيلَ أَتَى رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ وهو يَطُوفُ بِالْبَيْتِ، فَقَامَ وَقَامَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ إِلَى جَنْبِهِ، فَمَرَّ بِهِ الْأَسْوَدُ [ابْنُ الْمُطَّلِبِ فَرَمَى فِي وَجْهِهِ بِوَرَقَةٍ خَضْرَاءَ، فَعَمِيَ، وَمَرَّ بِهِ الْأَسْوَدُ] بْنُ عَبْدِ يَغُوثَ، فَأَشَارَ إِلَى بَطْنِهِ، فَاسْتَسْقَى بَطْنَهُ، فَمَاتَ مِنْهُ حَبَنَا، وَمَرَّ بِهِ الْوَلِيدُ بْنُ الْمُغِيرَةِ، فَأَشَارَ إِلَى أَثَرِ جُرح بِأَسْفَلِ كَعْبِ رِجْلِهِ -كَانَ أَصَابَهُ قَبْلَ ذَلِكَ بِسَنَتَيْنِ وَهُوَ يَجُرُّ إِزَارَهُ، وَذَلِكَ أَنَّهُ مَرَّ بِرَجُلٍ مِنْ خُزَاعَةَ يَرِيشُ نَبْلًا لَهُ، فَتَعَلَّقَ سَهْمٌ مِنْ نَبْلِهِ بِإِزَارِهِ، فَخَدَشَ رِجْلَهُ ذَلِكَ الْخَدْشَ، وَلَيْسَ بِشَيْءٍ، فَانْتَقَضَ بِهِ فَقَتَلَهُ. وَمَرَّ بِهِ الْعَاصُ بْنُ وَائِلٍ، فَأَشَارَ إِلَى أَخْمَصِ قَدَمِهِ، فَخَرَجَ عَلَى حِمَارٍ لَهُ يُرِيدُ الطَّائِفَ، فَرَبَضَ عَلَى شِبْرِقَةٍ فَدَخَلَتْ فِي أَخْمَصِ رِجْلِهِ مِنْهَا شَوْكَةٌ فَقَتَلَتْهُ. وَمَرَّ بِهِ الْحَارِثُ بْنُ الطُّلَاطِلَةِ، فَأَشَارَ إِلَى رَأْسِهِ، فَامْتَخَطَ قَيْحًا، فَقَتَلَهُ 

قَالَ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ إِسْحَاقَ: حَدَّثَنِي مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ أَبِي مُحَمَّدٍ، عَنْ رَجُلٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ قَالَ: كَانَ رأسُهم الْوَلِيدَ بْنَ الْمُغِيرَةِ، وَهُوَ الَّذِي جَمَعَهُمْ.

وَهَكَذَا رُوِيَ عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ جُبَيْرٍ وَعِكْرِمَةَ، نَحْوُ سِيَاقِ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ يَزِيدَ، عَنْ عُرْوَةَ، بِطُولِهِ، إِلَّا أَنَّ سَعِيدًا يَقُولُ: الْحَارِثُ بْنُ غَيْطَلَةَ. وَعِكْرِمَةُ يَقُولُ: الْحَارِثُ بْنُ قَيْسٍ.

قَالَ الزُّهْرِيُّ: وَصِدْقًا، هُوَ الْحَارِثُ بْنُ قَيْسٍ، وَأُمُّهُ غَيْطَلَةُ.

وَكَذَا رُوِيَ عَنْ مُجَاهِدٍ، وَمِقْسَمٍ، وقتادة، وغير واحد، أنهم كانوا خمسة.

وَقَالَ الشَّعْبِيُّ: كَانُوا سَبْعَةً.

وَالْمَشْهُورُ الْأَوَّلُ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿الَّذِينَ يَجْعَلُونَ مَعَ اللَّهِ إِلَهًا آخَرَ فَسَوْفَ يَعْلَمُونَ﴾ تَهْدِيدٌ شَدِيدٌ، وَوَعِيدٌ أَكِيدٌ، لِمَنْ جَعَلَ مَعَ اللَّهِ مَعْبُودًا آخَرَ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَلَقَدْ نَعْلَمُ أَنَّكَ يَضِيقُ صَدْرُكَ بِمَا يَقُولُونَ فَسَبِّحْ بِحَمْدِ رَبِّكَ وَكُنْ مِنَ السَّاجِدِينَ﴾ أَيْ: وَإِنَّا لَنَعْلَمُ يَا مُحَمَّدُ أَنَّكَ يَحْصُلُ لَكَ مِنْ أَذَاهُمْ لَكَ انْقِبَاضٌ وَضِيقُ صَدْرٍ. فَلَا يَهِيدَنَّكَ ذَلِكَ، وَلَا يَثْنِيَنَّكَ عَنْ إِبْلَاغِكَ رِسَالَةَ اللَّهِ، وَتَوَكَّلْ عَلَى اللَّهِ فَإِنَّهُ كَافِيكَ وَنَاصِرُكَ عَلَيْهِمْ، فَاشْتَغِلْ بِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ وَتَحْمِيدِهِ وَتَسْبِيحِهِ وَعِبَادَتِهِ الَّتِي هِيَ الصَّلَاةُ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿وَكُنْ مِنَ السَّاجِدِينَ﴾ كَمَا جَاءَ فِي الْحَدِيثِ الَّذِي رَوَاهُ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ:

حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ مَهْدِي، حَدَّثَنَا مُعَاوِيَةُ بْنُ صَالِحٍ، عَنْ أَبِي الزَّاهِرِيَّةِ، عَنْ كَثِيرِ بْنِ مُرَّة، عَنْ نُعَيْمِ بْنِ هَمَّار أَنَّهُ سَمِعَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم يَقُولُ: "قَالَ اللَّهُ: يَا ابْنَ آدَمَ، لَا تَعْجِزْ عَنْ أَرْبَعِ رَكَعَاتٍ مِنْ أَوَّلِ النَّهَارِ أَكفِكَ آخِرَهُ..

وَرَوَاهُ أَبُو دَاوُدَ مِنْ حَدِيثِ مَكْحُولٍ، عَنْ كَثِيرِ بْنِ مُرَّةَ، بِنَحْوِهِ 

وَلِهَذَا كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ إِذَا حَزبه أَمْرٌ صلَّى.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَاعْبُدْ رَبَّكَ حَتَّى يَأْتِيَكَ الْيَقِينُ﴾ قَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ: قَالَ سَالِمٌ: الْمَوْتُ 

وَسَالِمٌ هَذَا هُوَ: سَالِمُ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عُمَرَ، كَمَا قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ:

حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ بَشَّارٍ، حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى بْنُ سَعِيدٍ، عَنْ سُفْيَانَ، حَدَّثَنِي طَارِقُ بْنُ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ، عَنْ سَالِمِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ: ﴿وَاعْبُدْ رَبَّكَ حَتَّى يَأْتِيَكَ الْيَقِينُ﴾ قَالَ: الْمَوْتُ 

وَهَكَذَا قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وَالْحَسَنُ، وَقَتَادَةَ، وَعَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ، وَغَيْرُهُ 

وَالدَّلِيلُ عَلَى ذَلِكَ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى إِخْبَارًا عَنْ أَهْلِ النَّارِ أَنَّهُمْ قَالُوا: ﴿لَمْ نَكُ مِنَ الْمُصَلِّينَ وَلَمْ نَكُ نُطْعِمُ الْمِسْكِينَ وَكُنَّا نَخُوضُ مَعَ الْخَائِضِينَ وَكُنَّا نُكَذِّبُ بِيَوْمِ الدِّينِ حَتَّى أَتَانَا الْيَقِينُ﴾ [الْمُدَّثِّرِ: ٤٣-٤٧]

وَفِي الصَّحِيحِ مِنْ حَدِيثِ الزُّهْرِيِّ، عَنْ خَارِجَةَ بْنِ زَيْدِ بْنِ ثَابِتٍ، عَنْ أُمِّ الْعَلَاءِ -امْرَأَةٍ مِنَ الْأَنْصَارِ -أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ لَمَّا دَخَلَ عَلَى عُثْمَانَ بْنِ مَظْعُونٍ -وَقَدْ مَاتَ -قُلْتُ: رَحْمَةُ اللَّهِ عَلَيْكَ أَبَا السَّائِبِ، فَشَهَادَتِي عَلَيْكَ لَقَدْ أَكْرَمَكَ اللَّهِ. فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "وَمَا يُدْرِيكِ أَنَّ الله أكرمه؟ " فَقُلْتُ: بِأَبِي وَأُمِّي يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، فَمَنْ؟ فَقَالَ: "أَمَّا هُوَ فَقَدْ جَاءَهُ الْيَقِينُ، وَإِنِّي لَأَرْجُو لَهُ الْخَيْرَ" 

وَيُسْتَدَلُّ مِنْ هَذِهِ الْآيَةِ الْكَرِيمَةِ وَهِيَ قَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَاعْبُدْ رَبَّكَ حَتَّى يَأْتِيَكَ الْيَقِينُ﴾ -عَلَى أَنَّ الْعِبَادَةَ كَالصَّلَاةِ وَنَحْوِهَا وَاجِبَةٌ عَلَى الْإِنْسَانِ مَا دَامَ عَقْلُهُ ثَابِتًا فَيُصَلِّي بِحَسَبِ حَالِهِ، كَمَا ثَبَتَ فِي صَحِيحِ الْبُخَارِيِّ، عَنْ عِمْرَانَ بْنِ حُصَيْنٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا، أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "صَلِّ قَائِمًا، فَإِنْ لَمْ تَسْتَطِعْ فَقَاعِدًا، فَإِنْ لَمْ تَسْتَطِعْ فَعَلَى جَنْب" 

وَيُسْتَدَلُّ بِهَا عَلَى تَخْطِئَةِ مَنْ ذَهَبَ مِنَ الْمَلَاحِدَةِ إِلَى أَنَّ الْمُرَادَ بِالْيَقِينِ الْمُعْرِفَةُ، فَمَتَى وَصَلَ أَحَدُهُمْ إِلَى الْمَعْرِفَةِ سَقَطَ عَنْهُ التَّكْلِيفُ عِنْدَهُمْ. وَهَذَا كُفْرٌ وَضَلَالٌ وَجَهْلٌ، فَإِنَّ الْأَنْبِيَاءَ، عَلَيْهِمُ السَّلَامُ، كَانُوا هُمْ وَأَصْحَابُهُمْ أَعْلَمَ النَّاسِ بِاللَّهِ وَأَعْرَفَهُمْ بِحُقُوقِهِ وَصِفَاتِهِ، وَمَا يَسْتَحِقُّ مِنَ التَّعْظِيمِ، وَكَانُوا مَعَ هَذَا أَعْبَدَ النَّاسِ وَأَكْثَرَ النَّاسِ عِبَادَةً وَمُوَاظَبَةً عَلَى فِعْلِ الْخَيْرَاتِ إِلَى حِينِ الْوَفَاةِ. وَإِنَّمَا الْمُرَادُ بِالْيَقِينِ هَاهُنَا الْمَوْتُ، كَمَا قَدَّمْنَاهُ. وَلِلَّهِ الْحَمْدُ وَالْمِنَّةُ، وَالْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ عَلَى الْهِدَايَةِ، وعليه الاستعانة والتوكل، وهو المسؤول أَنْ يَتَوَفَّانَا عَلَى أَكْمَلِ الْأَحْوَالِ وَأَحْسَنِهَا [فَإِنَّهُ جواد كريم]
