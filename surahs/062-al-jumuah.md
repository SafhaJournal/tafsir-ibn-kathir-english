## 62:1-4 — verses 1-4 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Tafsir of Surat al-Jumu'ah

It is Medinan.

From Ibn Abbas and Abu Hurairah: that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to recite in the Friday prayer Surat al-Jumu'ah and al-Munafiqun. Muslim related it in his Sahih.

In the name of Allah, the Most Merciful, the Most Compassionate.

The Exalted informs that whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth glorifies Him, that is, all created things, both those that speak and those that are inanimate, as He said: {And there is not a thing except that it glorifies Him with praise} [al-Isra: 44]

Then He said: {The Sovereign, the Holy} meaning, He is the Owner of the heavens and the earth, disposing of them by His decree. He is {al-Quddus (the Holy)}, meaning, the One free of all deficiencies, described by attributes of perfection. {The Mighty, the Wise}, whose commentary has already been given more than once.

His statement, Exalted is He: {It is He who has sent among the unlettered ones a messenger from among themselves}. The unlettered ones (al-ummiyyun) are the Arabs, as the Exalted said: {And say to those who were given the Scripture and to the unlettered ones, "Have you submitted yourselves [to Allah]?" And if they submit, they are rightly guided; but if they turn away, then upon you is only the [duty of] notification. And Allah is Seeing of His servants} [Al Imran: 20]. The singling out of the unlettered ones by mention does not exclude others besides them, but the favor bestowed upon them is more emphatic and stronger, as in His statement: {And indeed, it is a remembrance for you and your people} [al-Zukhruf: 44], while it is also a remembrance for others who take remembrance by it. Likewise His statement: {And warn your close relatives} [al-Shu'ara: 214]. This and similar verses do not contradict His statement, Exalted is He: {Say, "O mankind, indeed I am the Messenger of Allah to you all"} [al-A'raf: 158], nor His statement: {That I may warn you with it and whomever it reaches} [al-An'am: 19], nor His statement informing about the Qur'an: {And whoever disbelieves in it among the factions, the Fire is his promised place} [Hud: 17], along with other verses indicating the universality of his mission, may the prayers and peace of Allah be upon him, to all of creation, the red among them and the black among them. We have already presented the commentary on this in Surat al-An'am, with the verses and authentic hadiths. To Allah belongs praise and favor.

This verse is the fulfillment of Allah's response to His close friend Ibrahim, when he prayed for the people of Makkah that Allah would raise up among them a messenger from among themselves who would recite to them His verses, purify them, and teach them the Book and wisdom. So Allah, glorified and exalted is He, and to Him belongs praise and favor, sent him during a period of dearth of messengers and obscurity of paths, when the need for him had become acute, and when Allah had shown wrath toward the people of the earth, Arab and non-Arab alike, except remnants of the People of the Book, that is, a very small number, among those who held fast to what Allah had sent Isa son of Maryam (peace be upon him) with. For this reason the Exalted said: {It is He who has sent among the unlettered ones a messenger from among themselves reciting to them His verses and purifying them and teaching them the Book and wisdom, although they were before in clear error}. This is because the Arabs had formerly held to the religion of Ibrahim (the close friend of Allah, peace be upon him), but then they altered it, changed it, overturned it, and opposed it, exchanging monotheism for polytheism and certainty for doubt, and they invented things that Allah had not permitted. Likewise the People of the Two Books had altered their books, distorted them, changed them, and interpreted them wrongly. So Allah sent Muhammad, the prayers and peace of Allah be upon him, with a magnificent, complete, and comprehensive legislation for all of creation, containing their guidance and the clarification of all that they need regarding the affairs of their worldly life and their afterlife, calling them to what draws them near to Paradise and to Allah's pleasure with them, and forbidding them from what draws them near to the Fire and to Allah's wrath. It is a judge, deciding between all doubts, uncertainties, and misgivings in matters of fundamental principles and subsidiary branches. Allah the Exalted, to Him belongs praise and favor, gathered for him all the excellent qualities of those who came before him, and gave him what He had not given to anyone among those who came before, and will not give to anyone among those who come after. So may the prayers and peace of Allah be upon him always, until the Day of Judgment.

His statement: {And [also for] others of them who have not yet joined them. And He is the Mighty, the Wise}. The Imam Abu Abdullah al-Bukhari, may Allah have mercy on him, said:

[chain] ... Abu Hurairah, may Allah be pleased with him, said: We were sitting with the Prophet ﷺ when Surat al-Jumu'ah was revealed to him: {And others of them who have not yet joined them}. They said, "Who are they, O Messenger of Allah?" He did not respond to them until he was asked three times, and among us was Salman al-Farisi. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ placed his hand upon Salman and then said, "If faith were at the Pleiades, men, or a man, from among these would attain it."

Muslim, al-Tirmidhi, al-Nasa'i, Ibn Abi Hatim, and Ibn Jarir related it through various routes from Thawr ibn Zayd al-Dili, from Salim Abu al-Ghayth, from Abu Hurairah, with this wording.

In this hadith there is evidence that this surah is Medinan, and evidence for the universality of his ﷺ mission to all of mankind, because he explained His statement {and others of them} as referring to Persia. For this reason he wrote his letters to Persia, to Rome, and to other nations, calling them to Allah, Mighty and Exalted, and to follow what he had brought. For this reason Mujahid and others said regarding His statement, {And [also for] others of them who have not yet joined them}: they are the non-Arabs, and everyone who believed in the Prophet ﷺ from among the non-Arabs.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] ... Sahl ibn Sa'd al-Sa'idi said: the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "Indeed among the loins of the loins of the loins of men [from among my companions there are men] and women from my nation who will enter Paradise without being brought to account." Then he recited: {And [also for] others of them who have not yet joined them}, meaning: those remaining from the nation of Muhammad ﷺ.

His statement: {And He is the Mighty, the Wise}, meaning: possessor of might and wisdom in His legislation and His decree.

His statement: {That is the bounty of Allah, which He gives to whom He wills, and Allah is the possessor of great bounty}, meaning: what Allah gave Muhammad ﷺ of the magnificent prophethood, and what He singled out for his nation by his ﷺ mission to them.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَآخَرِينَ مِنْهُمْ لَمَّا يَلْحَقُوا بِهِمْ وَهُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ﴾ قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَبُو عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الْبُخَارِيُّ رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ.

حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الْعَزِيزِ بْنُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ، حَدَّثَنَا سُلَيْمَانُ بْنُ بِلَالٍ، عَنْ ثَوْرٍ، عَنْ أَبِي الغَيث، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، قَالَ: كُنَّا جُلُوسًا عِنْدَ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ فَأُنْزِلَتْ عَلَيْهِ سُورَةِ الْجُمُعَةِ: ﴿وَآخَرِينَ مِنْهُمْ لَمَّا يَلْحَقُوا بِهِمْ﴾ قَالُوا: مَنْ هُمْ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ؟ فَلَمْ يُرَاجِعْهُمْ حَتَّى سُئِلَ ثَلَاثًا، وَفِينَا سَلْمَانُ الْفَارِسِيُّ، فَوَضَعَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَدَهُ عَلَى سَلْمَانَ ثُمَّ قَالَ: "لَوْ كَانَ الْإِيمَانُ عِنْدَ الثُّرَيَّا لَنَالَهُ رِجَالٌ -أَوْ: رَجُلٌ-مِنْ هَؤُلَاءِ".

## 62:5-8 — verses 5-8 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says, censuring the Jews who were given the Torah and entrusted with acting upon it, but they did not act upon it. Their example in this is like the example of the donkey carrying volumes, that is, like the example of the donkey when it is loaded with books it does not know what is in them. It carries them as a physical burden but does not know what is upon it. Likewise are these people in their carrying of the Book which they were given: they preserved its wording but did not understand it, nor did they act according to what it required. Rather, they interpreted it wrongly, distorted it, and altered it. So they are in a worse state than donkeys, because the donkey has no understanding at all, while these people have understanding but did not use it. This is why He said in another verse: {Those are like cattle, rather they are more astray. Those are the heedless} [al-A'raf: 179], and He said here: {Evil is the example of the people who denied the signs of Allah, and Allah does not guide the wrongdoing people}.

Imam Ahmad, may Allah have mercy on him, said: [chain] Ibn Numayr, from Mujalid, from al-Sha'bi, from Ibn 'Abbas, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Whoever speaks on Friday while the imam is delivering the sermon is like the example of the donkey carrying volumes, and the one who says to him 'be silent' has no Friday prayer (of reward)."

Then the Almighty says: {Say, O you who are Jews, if you claim that you are the allies of Allah to the exclusion of other people, then wish for death, if you are truthful}, that is, if you claim that you are upon guidance and that Muhammad and his companions are upon misguidance, then pray for death upon whichever of the two parties is astray, {if you are truthful} in what you claim.

Allah the Almighty says: {But they will never wish for it, because of what their hands have put forth}, that is, because of the disbelief, wrongdoing, and immorality they know to be theirs. {And Allah is All-Knowing of the wrongdoers}. We have already discussed this mutual imprecation (mubahala) with the Jews in Surat al-Baqarah, where the Almighty says: {Say, if the abode of the Hereafter with Allah is exclusively for you, to the exclusion of other people, then wish for death, if you are truthful. But they will never wish for it, because of what their hands have put forth, and Allah is All-Knowing of the wrongdoers. And you will surely find them the most attached of people to life, even more than those who associate partners with Allah. Each one of them wishes he could be granted a life of a thousand years, but it would not remove him in the least from the punishment that he should be granted such a life. And Allah is Seeing of what they do} [al-Baqarah: 94-96]. We have discussed this previously there and explained that what is meant is that they should pray against whichever party is astray, whether from among themselves or their opponents, just as the mutual imprecation with the Christians came previously in Al 'Imran: {Then whoever disputes with you concerning him after the knowledge has come to you, say, come let us call our sons and your sons, our women and your women, ourselves and yourselves, then let us invoke earnestly and place the curse of Allah upon the liars} [Al 'Imran: 61], and the mutual imprecation with the polytheists in Surat Maryam: {Say, whoever is in error, let the Most Merciful extend for him an extension} [Maryam: 75].

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Isma'il ibn Yazid al-Raqqi Abu Yazid, told us Furat, from 'Abd al-Karim ibn Malik al-Jazari, from 'Ikrimah, from Ibn 'Abbas, who said: Abu Jahl, may Allah curse him, said: "If I see Muhammad at the Ka'bah, I will surely go to him until I tread on his neck." He said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Had he done so, the angels would have seized him openly. And had the Jews wished for death, they would have died and seen their seats in the Fire. And had those who wish to engage in mutual imprecation with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ gone out to do so, they would have returned finding neither wealth nor family."

Narrated by al-Bukhari, al-Tirmidhi, and al-Nasa'i, from the hadith of 'Abd al-Razzaq from Ma'mar, from 'Abd al-Karim, [with the same chain]. Al-Bukhari said: "'Amr ibn Khalid followed him in this, from 'Ubayd Allah ibn 'Amr, from 'Abd al-Karim." Al-Nasa'i also narrated it from 'Abd al-Rahman ibn 'Abd Allah al-Halabi, from 'Ubayd Allah ibn 'Amr al-Raqqi, with the same chain, in fuller form.

As for the Almighty's saying: {Say, indeed the death from which you flee will surely meet you, then you will be returned to the Knower of the unseen and the seen, and He will inform you of what you used to do}, this is like His saying in Surat al-Nisa': {Wherever you may be, death will overtake you, even if you should be in towers of lofty construction} [al-Nisa': 78].

And in the Mu'jam of al-Tabarani, from the hadith of Mu'adh Muhammad ibn Muhammad al-Hudhali, from Yunus, from al-Hasan, from Samurah, as a raised report (marfu'): "The example of one who flees from death is like the example of the fox pursued by the earth over a debt. It comes running until, when it becomes exhausted and out of breath, it enters its den. The earth says to it: 'O fox, my debt.' So it lets out a burst of wind from fright, and it continues like that until its neck is severed and it dies."

## 62:9-10 — verses 9-10 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Jumu'ah is called Jumu'ah because it is derived from al-jam' (gathering), for the people of Islam gather in it once every week at the great houses of worship, and in it all of creation was completed, for it is the sixth of the six days in which Allah created the heavens and the earth. In it Adam was created, in it he was made to enter Paradise, and in it he was expelled from it. In it the Hour will occur. And in it there is an hour in which no believing servant coincides while asking Allah for something good except that He gives it to him, as has been established by sound hadiths.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Salman said: Abu al-Qasim ﷺ said, "O Salman, what is the day of Jumu'ah?" I said: Allah and His Messenger know best. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "It is a day on which your two parents (or: your father) were gathered."

Something similar to this has been narrated from Abu Hurayrah, as his own words, and Allah knows best.

In the ancient language it used to be called Yawm al-'Arubah. It has been established that the nations before us were commanded to observe it but went astray from it. The Jews chose Saturday, on which no creation took place, and the Christians chose Sunday, on which creation began, while Allah chose for this nation the day of Jumu'ah, on which Allah completed creation, as al-Bukhari and Muslim recorded from the hadith of 'Abd al-Razzaq, from Ma'mar, from Hammam ibn Munabbih, who said: This is what Abu Hurayrah narrated to us, saying: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "We are the last, but we will be foremost on the Day of Resurrection, though they were given the Scripture before us. This is their day which Allah made obligatory upon them, but they differed concerning it, so Allah guided us to it. So the people follow us in it: the Jews tomorrow, and the Christians the day after tomorrow." This is the wording of al-Bukhari.

In a wording of Muslim: "Allah led astray those who were before us, so the Jews had Saturday and the Christians had Sunday. Then Allah brought us, and Allah guided us to the day of Jumu'ah, so He made it Jumu'ah, Saturday, and Sunday. And likewise they will follow us on the Day of Resurrection. We are the last of the people of this world and the first on the Day of Resurrection, the first to be judged among before all other creatures."

Allah commanded the believers to gather for His worship on the day of Jumu'ah, saying: {O you who believe, when the call is made for prayer on the day of Jumu'ah, hasten to the remembrance of Allah}, meaning: aim for it, proceed toward it, and give it importance in your going to it. What is meant by "hastening" (sa'y) here is not fast walking, but rather giving it importance, as in His statement, Most High: {And whoever desires the Hereafter and strives for it as it should be striven for, while he is a believer} [al-Isra: 19]. 'Umar ibn al-Khattab and Ibn Mas'ud, may Allah be pleased with both of them, used to recite it: "Then proceed to the remembrance of Allah." As for fast walking to the prayer, it has been forbidden, as reported by al-Bukhari and Muslim in the two Sahihs, from Abu Hurayrah, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said: "When you hear the iqamah, walk to the prayer, and let there be calmness and dignity upon you, and do not hurry. Whatever you catch, pray, and whatever you miss, complete it." This is the wording of al-Bukhari.

From Abu Qatadah, who said: While we were praying with the Prophet ﷺ, he heard the commotion of some men. When he finished praying, he said: "What is the matter with you?" They said: We hurried to the prayer. He said: "Do not do that. When you come to the prayer, walk, and let there be calmness upon you. Whatever you catch, pray, and whatever you miss, complete it." Both al-Bukhari and Muslim recorded it.

'Abd al-Razzaq said: [chain] Abu Hurayrah, may Allah be pleased with him, said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "When the prayer is called, do not come to it hurrying, but come to it walking, and let there be calmness and dignity upon you. Whatever you catch, pray, and whatever you miss, complete it."

Al-Tirmidhi narrated it from the hadith of 'Abd al-Razzaq likewise, and also recorded it through the route of Yazid ibn Zurai', from Ma'mar, from al-Zuhri, from Abu Salamah, from Abu Hurayrah, with similar wording.

Al-Hasan said: By Allah, it is not about hastening on foot. They were only forbidden from coming to the prayer except with calmness and dignity upon them, but rather it is with the hearts, the intention, and humility.

Qatadah said concerning His statement {then hasten to the remembrance of Allah}: it means that you should strive with your heart and your action, and it also refers to walking to it. He used to interpret His statement, Most High: {Then when he reached the age of striving (sa'y) with him} [al-Saffat: 102] as meaning: walking with him. Something similar has been narrated from Muhammad ibn Ka'b, Zayd ibn Aslam, and others.

It is recommended for whoever comes to Jumu'ah to perform ghusl before coming to it, as established in the two Sahihs from 'Abd Allah ibn 'Umar, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "When one of you comes to Jumu'ah, let him perform ghusl." And both of them also recorded from Abu Sa'id, may Allah be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "The ghusl on the day of Jumu'ah is obligatory upon every person who has reached puberty."

From Abu Hurayrah, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "It is a right of Allah upon every Muslim that he perform ghusl once every seven days, washing his head and his body." Narrated by Muslim.

From Jabir, may Allah be pleased with him, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Upon every Muslim man, once every seven days, is the ghusl of a day, and that is the day of Jumu'ah." Narrated by Ahmad, al-Nasa'i, and Ibn Hibban.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Aws ibn Aws al-Thaqafi said: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "Whoever washes and performs ghusl on the day of Jumu'ah, goes early and is among the first to arrive, walks and does not ride, draws near to the imam and listens attentively and does not engage in idle talk, will have for every step the reward of a year's fasting and standing in prayer."

This hadith has various chains and wordings, and it was recorded by the compilers of the four Sunan, and al-Tirmidhi graded it hasan (sound but with a slightly weaker chain).

From Abu Hurayrah, may Allah be pleased with him, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Whoever performs ghusl on the day of Jumu'ah like the ghusl of major ritual impurity, then goes early, it is as if he offered a camel as a sacrifice. Whoever goes in the second hour, it is as if he offered a cow. Whoever goes in the third hour, it is as if he offered a horned ram. Whoever goes in the fourth hour, it is as if he offered a chicken. Whoever goes in the fifth hour, it is as if he offered an egg. Then when the imam comes out, the angels are present listening to the remembrance." Both al-Bukhari and Muslim recorded it.

It is recommended for him to wear his best clothes, to perfume himself, to use the tooth-stick (siwak), and to cleanse and purify himself. In the previously mentioned hadith of Abu Sa'id: "The ghusl on the day of Jumu'ah is obligatory upon every person who has reached puberty, as is the use of the siwak, and touching some of the perfume of his household, if he has any."

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Abu Ayyub al-Ansari: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "Whoever performs ghusl on the day of Jumu'ah, and touches some of the perfume of his household if he has any, and wears his best clothes, then goes out until he comes to the mosque, and performs a voluntary prayer if he wishes, and does not harm anyone, then remains silent when his imam comes out until he finishes the prayer, it will be an expiation for what is between it and the next Jumu'ah."

In the Sunan of Abu Dawud and Ibn Majah, from 'Abd Allah ibn Salam, may Allah be pleased with him, that he heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say upon the pulpit: "What would it harm any of you if he bought two garments for the day of Jumu'ah, besides his two garments of work?" And from 'A'ishah, may Allah be pleased with her: that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ delivered a sermon to the people on the day of Jumu'ah, and he saw upon them garments of coarse striped cloth, so he said: "What would it harm any of you, if he found the means, to take two garments for his Jumu'ah besides his two garments of work?" Narrated by Ibn Majah.

And His statement, Most High: {When the call is made for prayer}

What is meant by this call is the second call, which used to be made before the Messenger of Allah ﷺ when he came out and sat on the pulpit, for the adhan would then be given in front of him. This is what is meant. As for the first call, which the Commander of the Faithful, Uthman ibn Affan, may Allah be pleased with him, added, it was only because of the increase in the number of people, as al-Bukhari, may Allah have mercy on him, narrated when he said: [chain] al-Sa'ib ibn Yazid said: The call on the day of Friday, at its beginning, used to be when the imam sat on the pulpit, in the time of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and Abu Bakr and Umar. Then when it was the time of Uthman, [after a while], and the people increased in number, he added the second call at al-Zawra, meaning that the adhan was given at the house called al-Zawra, which was the highest house in Medina, near the mosque.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Makhul reported that the call on the day of Friday used to be a single adhan, given when the imam came out, and then the prayer would be established. That was the call after which buying and selling became forbidden once it was called. So Uthman, may Allah be pleased with him, ordered that a call be given before the imam came out, so that the people would gather.

Only free men are commanded to attend the Friday prayer, not women, slaves, or children. The traveler and the sick person are excused, as is the caretaker of the sick person, and similar excuses, as is established in the books of jurisprudential branches.

His saying, {and leave off trading}, means: hasten to the remembrance of Allah and leave off trading when the call to prayer is given. For this reason the scholars, may Allah be pleased with them, agreed unanimously that trading is forbidden after the second call. They differed, however, as to whether a transaction conducted at that time is valid or not, holding two views. The apparent sense of the verse is that it is not valid, as is established in its proper place. And Allah knows best.

His saying, {That is better for you, if you only knew}, means: your leaving off trading and your turning toward the remembrance of Allah and toward the prayer is better for you, that is, in this world and the next, if you only knew.

His saying, {And when the prayer is concluded}, meaning when it is finished, {then disperse throughout the land and seek from the bounty of Allah}. Since He had restricted their dealings after the call and commanded them to gather, He then permitted them, after the prayer was finished, to disperse throughout the land and to seek from the bounty of Allah. This is as Irak ibn Malik, may Allah be pleased with him, used to do: when he had prayed the Friday prayer, he would leave and stand at the door of the mosque and say: "O Allah, I have answered Your call, I have prayed Your obligatory prayer, and I have dispersed as You commanded me, so provide for me from Your bounty, for You are the best of providers." Narrated by Ibn Abi Hatim.

It is reported from some of the early generations that he said: "Whoever buys and sells on the day of Friday after the prayer, Allah will bless him seventy times over," because of the saying of Allah Most High: {And when the prayer is concluded, then disperse throughout the land and seek from the bounty of Allah}.

His saying, {And remember Allah much, that you may prosper}, means: while you are buying and selling, taking and giving, remember Allah much, and do not let worldly life distract you from what will benefit you in the Hereafter. For this reason it is reported in the hadith: "Whoever enters any of the marketplaces and says: 'There is no god but Allah, alone, without partner, His is the dominion and His is the praise, and He has power over all things,' a thousand thousand good deeds are recorded for him, and a thousand thousand bad deeds are erased from him."

Mujahid said: "A servant is not among those who remember Allah much until he remembers Allah standing, sitting, and lying down."

وَقَدْ أَمَرَ اللَّهُ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ بِالِاجْتِمَاعِ لِعِبَادَتِهِ يَوْمَ الْجُمُعَةِ، فَقَالَ: ﴿يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا نُودِيَ لِلصَّلاةِ مِنْ يَوْمِ الْجُمُعَةِ فَاسْعَوْا إِلَى ذِكْرِ اللَّهِ﴾ أَيِ: اقْصِدُوا وَاعْمَدُوا وَاهْتَمُّوا فِي مَسيركم إِلَيْهَا، وَلَيْسَ الْمُرَادُ بالسعي هاهنا الْمَشْيُ السَّرِيعُ، وَإِنَّمَا هُوَ الِاهْتِمَامُ بِهَا، كَقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَمَنْ أَرَادَ الآخِرَةَ وَسَعَى لَهَا سَعْيَهَا وَهُوَ مُؤْمِنٌ﴾ [الْإِسْرَاءِ: ١٩] وَكَانَ عُمَرُ بْنُ الْخَطَّابِ وَابْنُ مَسْعُودٍ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا يَقْرَآنِهَا: "فَامْضُوا إِلَى ذِكْرِ اللَّهِ". فَأَمَّا الْمَشْيُ السَّرِيعُ إِلَى الصَّلَاةِ فَقَدْ نُهِيَ عَنْهُ، لِمَا أَخْرَجَاهُ فِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيرة، عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ قَالَ: "إِذَا سَمِعْتُمُ الْإِقَامَةَ فَامْشُوا إِلَى الصَّلَاةِ، وَعَلَيْكُمُ السَّكِينَةُ وَالْوَقَارُ، وَلَا تُسرِعوا، فَمَا أَدْرَكْتُمْ فصَلُّوا، وَمَا فَاتَكُمْ فَأَتِمُّوا". لَفَظُ الْبُخَارِيِّ 

وَعَنْ أَبِي قَتَادَةَ قَالَ: بَيْنَمَا نَحْنُ نُصَلي مَعَ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ إِذْ سَمِعَ جَلَبة رِجَالٍ، فَلَمَّا صَلَّى قَالَ: "مَا شَأْنُكُمْ؟ ". قَالُوا: اسْتَعْجَلْنَا إِلَى الصَّلَاةِ. قَالَ: "فَلَا تَفْعَلُوا، إِذَا أَتَيْتُمُ الصَّلَاةَ فَامْشُوا وَعَلَيْكُمْ بِالسَّكِينَةِ فَمَا أَدْرَكْتُمْ فَصَلُّوا وَمَا فَاتَكُمْ فَأَتِمُّوا". أَخْرَجَاهُ 

وَقَالَ عَبْدُ الرَّزَّاقِ: أَخْبَرَنَا مَعْمَر، عَنِ الزُّهْرِيِّ، عَنْ سَعِيدِ بْنِ الْمُسَيَّبِ عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم: "إذا أُقِيمَتِ الصَّلَاةُ فَلَا تَأْتُوهَا تَسْعَوْنَ، وَلَكِنِ ائْتُوهَا تَمْشُونَ، وَعَلَيْكُمُ السَّكِينَةُ وَالْوَقَارُ، فَمَا أَدْرَكْتُمْ فَصَلُّوا، وَمَا فَاتَكُمْ فَأَتِمُّوا".

رَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ عَبْدِ الرَّزَّاقِ كَذَلِكَ وَأَخْرَجَهُ مِنْ طَرِيقِ يَزِيدَ بْنِ زُرَيع، عَنْ مَعْمَرٌ، عَنِ الزُّهْرِيِّ، عَنْ أَبِي سَلَمَةَ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ، بِمَثَلِهِ 

قَالَ الْحَسَنُ أَمَا وَاللَّهِ مَا هُوَ بِالسَّعْيِ عَلَى الْأَقْدَامِ، وَلَقَدْ نُهُوا أَنْ يَأْتُوا الصَّلَاةَ إِلَّا وَعَلَيْهِمُ السَّكِينَةُ وَالْوَقَارُ، وَلَكِنْ بِالْقُلُوبِ وَالنِّيَّةِ وَالْخُشُوعِ.

وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿فَاسْعَوْا إِلَى ذِكْرِ اللَّهِ﴾ يَعْنِي: أَنْ تَسْعَى بِقَلْبِكَ وَعَمَلِكَ، وَهُوَ الْمَشْيُ إِلَيْهَا، وَكَانَ يتأولُ قَوْلَهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَلَمَّا بَلَغَ مَعَهُ السَّعْيَ﴾ [الصَّافَّاتِ: ١٠٢] أَيِ: الْمَشْيَ مَعَهُ. رُوِيَ عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ كَعْبٍ، وَزَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ، وَغَيْرِهِمَا نَحْوُ ذَلِكَ.

ويستحَب لِمَنْ جَاءَ الْجُمُعَةَ أَنْ يَغْتَسِلَ قَبْلَ مَجِيئِهِ إِلَيْهَا، لِمَا ثَبَتَ فِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عُمَر أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "إِذَا جَاءَ أحدُكم الجمعةَ فَلْيغتسل" وَلَهُمَا عَنْ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ "غُسلُ يَوْمِ الْجُمُعَةِ وَاجِبٌ عَلَى كُلِّ مُحتَلِم" 

وَعَنْ أَبِي هُرَيرة، قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "حَقٌّ لِلَّهِ عَلَى كُلِّ مُسْلِمٍ أَنْ يَغْتَسِلَ فِي كُلِّ سَبْعَةِ أَيَّامٍ، يَغْسِلُ رَأْسَهُ وَجَسَدَهُ". رَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ 

وَعَنْ جَابِرٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم: "على كُلِّ رَجُلٍ مُسْلِمٍ فِي كُلِّ سَبْعَةِ أَيَّامٍ غُسْلُ يَوْمٍ، وَهُوَ يَوْمُ الْجُمُعَةِ". رَوَاهُ أَحْمَدُ، وَالنَّسَائِيُّ، وَابْنُ حِبَّانَ 

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا يَحْيَى بْنُ آدَمَ، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ الْمُبَارَكِ، عَنِ الْأَوْزَاعِيِّ، عَنْ حَسَّانِ بْنِ عَطِيَّةَ، عَنْ أَبِي الْأَشْعَثِ الصَّنْعَانِيِّ، عَنْ أَوْسِ بْنِ أَوْسٍ الثَّقَفِيِّ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "مَنْ غَسَّل وَاغْتَسَلَ يَوْمَ الْجُمُعَةِ، وَبَكَّرَ وَابْتَكَرَ، وَمَشَى وَلَمْ يَرْكَبْ، وَدَنَا مِنَ الْإِمَامِ وَاسْتَمَعَ وَلَمْ يَلْغُ كَانَ لَهُ بِكُلِّ خطوة أجر سنة، صِيَامِهَا وَقِيَامِهَا".

وَهَذَا الْحَدِيثُ لَهُ طُرُقٌ وَأَلْفَاظٌ، وَقَدْ أَخْرَجَهُ أَهْلُ السُّنَنِ الْأَرْبَعَةِ وحَسَّنَهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ 

وَعَنْ أَبِي هُرَيرة، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، أَنَّ رَسُولَ الله صلى الله عليه وسلم قال: "مَنِ اغْتَسَلَ يَوْمَ الْجُمُعَةِ غُسلَ الْجَنَابَةِ، ثُمَّ رَاحَ فَكَأَنَّمَا قَرَّبَ بَدَنَةً، وَمَنْ رَاحَ فِي السَّاعَةِ الثَّانِيَةِ فَكَأَنَّمَا قَرَّبَ بَقَرَةً، وَمَنْ رَاحَ فِي السَّاعَةِ الثَّالِثَةِ فَكَأَنَّمَا قَرَّبَ كَبْشًا أَقْرَنَ، وَمَنْ رَاحَ فِي السَّاعَةِ الرَّابِعَةِ فَكَأَنَّمَا قَرَّبَ دَجَاجَةً، وَمَنْ رَاحَ فِي السَّاعَةِ الْخَامِسَةِ فَكَأَنَّمَا قَرَّبَ بَيْضَةً، فَإِذَا خَرَجَ الْإِمَامُ حَضَرَتِ الْمَلَائِكَةُ يَسْتَمِعُونَ الذِّكْرَ" أَخْرَجَاهُ 

وَيُسْتَحَبُّ لَهُ أَنْ يَلْبَسَ أَحْسَنَ ثِيَابِهِ، وَيَتَطَيَّبَ وَيَتَسَوَّكَ، وَيَتَنَظَّفَ وَيَتَطَهَّرَ. وَفِي حَدِيثِ أَبِي سَعِيدٍ الْمُتَقَدِّمِ: "غسلُ يَوْمِ الْجُمُعَةِ وَاجِبٌ عَلَى كُلِّ مُحْتَلِمٍ، والسواكُ، وَأَنْ يَمَس مَنْ طِيبِ أَهْلِهِ".

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا يَعْقُوبُ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، عَنِ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ إِسْحَاقَ، حَدَّثَنِي مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ إِبْرَاهِيمَ التَّيْمِيُّ، عَنْ عِمْرَانَ بْنِ أَبِي يَحْيَى، عَنْ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ كَعْبِ بْنِ مَالِكٍ، عَنْ أَبِي أَيُّوبَ الْأَنْصَارِيِّ: سمعت رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم يَقُولُ: "مَنِ اغْتَسَلَ يَوْمَ الْجُمُعَةِ ومَس مِنْ طِيبِ أَهْلِهِ -إِنْ كَانَ عِنْدَهُ-وَلَبِسَ مِنْ أَحْسَنِ ثِيَابِهِ، ثُمَّ خَرَجَ حَتَّى يَأْتِيَ الْمَسْجِدَ فَيَرْكَعَ -إِنْ بَدَا لَهُ-وَلَمْ يُؤذ أَحَدًا، ثُمَّ أَنْصَتَ إِذَا خَرَجَ إِمَامُهُ حَتَّى يُصَلِّيَ، كَانَتْ كَفَّارَةً لِمَا بَيْنَهَا وَبَيْنَ الْجُمُعَةِ الْأُخْرَى" 

وَفِي سُنَنِ أَبِي دَاوُدَ وَابْنِ مَاجَهْ، عَنْ عَبْدَ اللَّهِ بْنَ سَلَامٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، أَنَّهُ سَمِعَ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ عَلَى الْمِنْبَرِ: "مَا عَلَى أَحَدِكُمْ لَوِ اشْتَرَى ثَوْبَيْنِ لِيَوْمِ الْجُمُعَةِ سِوَى ثَوْبَيْ مِهْنَته" وَعَنْ عَائِشَةَ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهَا: أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ خَطَبَ النَّاسَ يَوْمَ الْجُمُعَةِ، فَرَأَى عَلَيْهِمْ ثِيَابَ النِّمَارِ، فَقَالَ: "مَا عَلَى أَحَدِكُمْ إِنْ وَجَدَ سَعَة أَنْ يَتَّخِذَ ثَوْبَيْنِ لَجُمُعَتِهِ، سِوَى ثَوْبَيْ مِهْنَتِهِ". رَوَاهُ ابْنُ مَاجَهْ 

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿إِذَا نُودِيَ لِلصَّلاةِ﴾ الْمُرَادُ بِهَذَا النِّدَاءِ هُوَ النِّدَاءُ الثَّانِي الَّذِي كَانَ يُفْعَلُ بَيْنَ يَدَيْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ إِذَا خَرَجَ فَجَلَسَ عَلَى الْمِنْبَرِ، فَإِنَّهُ كَانَ حِينَئِذٍ يُؤَذَّنُ بَيْنَ يَدَيْهِ، فَهَذَا هُوَ الْمُرَادُ، فَأَمَّا النِّدَاءُ الْأَوَّلُ الَّذِي زَادَهُ أَمِيرِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ عُثْمَانَ بْنِ عَفَّانَ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، فَإِنَّمَا كَانَ هَذَا لِكَثْرَةِ النَّاسِ، كَمَا رَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ رَحِمَهُ اللَّهُ حَيْثُ قَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا آدَمُ -هُوَ ابْنُ أَبِي إِيَاسٍ-حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ أَبِي ذِئْبٍ، عَنِ الزُّهْرِيِّ، عَنِ السَّائِبِ بْنِ يَزِيدَ قَالَ: كَانَ النِّدَاءُ يَوْمَ الْجُمُعَةِ أولهُ إِذَا جَلَسَ الْإِمَامُ عَلَى الْمِنْبَرِ عَلَى عَهْدِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ وَأَبِي بَكْرٍ وَعُمَرَ، فَلَمَّا كَانَ عُثْمَانُ [بَعْدَ زَمَنٍ] وَكَثُرَ النَّاسُ، زَادَ النِّدَاءَ الثَّانِيَ عَلَى الزَّوْرَاءِ يَعْنِي: يُؤَذَّنُ بِهِ عَلَى الدَّارِ الَّتِي تُسَمَّى بِالزَّوْرَاءِ، وَكَانَتْ أَرْفَعَ دَارٍ بِالْمَدِينَةِ، بِقُرْبِ الْمَسْجِدِ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبِي، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو نُعَيْمٍ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ رَاشِدٍ الْمَكْحُولِيُّ، عَنْ مَكْحُولٍ: أَنَّ النِّدَاءَ كَانَ فِي يَوْمِ الْجُمُعَةِ مُؤَذَّنٌ وَاحِدٌ حِينَ يَخْرُجُ الْإِمَامُ، ثُمَّ تُقَامُ الصَّلَاةُ، وَذَلِكَ النِّدَاءُ الَّذِي يَحْرُمُ عنده البيع الشراء إِذَا نُودِيَ بِهِ، فَأَمَرَ عُثْمَانُ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، أَنْ يُنَادَى قَبْلَ خُرُوجِ الْإِمَامِ حَتَّى يَجْتَمِعَ النَّاسُ.

وَإِنَّمَا يُؤْمَرُ بِحُضُورِ الْجُمُعَةِ [الرِّجَالُ] الْأَحْرَارُ دُونَ النِّسَاءِ وَالْعَبِيدِ وَالصِّبْيَانِ، وَيُعْذَرُ الْمُسَافِرُ وَالْمَرِيضُ، وقَيّم الْمَرِيضِ، وَمَا أَشْبَهَ ذَلِكَ مِنَ الْأَعْذَارِ، كَمَا هُوَ مُقَرَّرٌ فِي كُتُبِ الْفُرُوعِ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿ذَلِكُمْ خَيْرٌ لَكُمْ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ﴾ أَيْ: تَرْكُكُمُ الْبَيْعَ وَإِقْبَالُكُمْ إِلَى ذِكْرِ اللَّهِ وَإِلَى الصَّلَاةِ خيرٌ لَكُمْ، أَيْ: فِي الدُّنْيَا وَالْآخِرَةِ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَإِذَا قُضِيَتِ الصَّلاةُ﴾ أَيْ: فُرغ مِنْهَا، ﴿فَانْتَشِرُوا فِي الأرْضِ وَابْتَغُوا مِنْ فَضْلِ اللَّهِ﴾ لَمَّا حَجَر عَلَيْهِمْ فِي التَّصَرُّفِ بَعْدَ النِّدَاءِ وَأَمَرَهُمْ بِالِاجْتِمَاعِ، أَذِنَ لَهُمْ بَعْدَ الْفَرَاغِ فِي الِانْتِشَارِ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَالِابْتِغَاءِ مِنْ فَضْلِ اللَّهِ. كَمَا كَانَ عرَاك بْنُ مَالِكٍ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ إِذَا صَلَّى الْجُمُعَةَ انْصَرَفَ فَوَقَفَ عَلَى بَابِ الْمَسْجِدِ، فَقَالَ: اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أجبتُ دعوتَك، وصليتُ فريضتك، وانتشرت كما أمرتني، فَارْزُقْنِي مِنْ فَضْلِكَ، وَأَنْتَ خَيْرُ الرَّازِقِينَ. رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ.

وَرُوِي عَنْ بَعْضِ السَّلَفِ أَنَّهُ قَالَ: مَنْ بَاعَ وَاشْتَرَى فِي يَوْمِ الْجُمُعَةِ بَعْدَ الصَّلَاةِ، بَارَكَ اللَّهُ لَهُ سَبْعِينَ مَرَّةً، لِقَوْلِ اللَّهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَإِذَا قُضِيَتِ الصَّلاةُ فَانْتَشِرُوا فِي الأرْضِ وَابْتَغُوا مِنْ فَضْلِ اللَّهِ﴾

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَاذْكُرُوا اللَّهَ كَثِيرًا لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ﴾ أَيْ: حَالَ بَيْعِكُمْ وَشِرَائِكُمْ، وَأَخْذِكُمْ وعَطَائكم، اذْكُرُوا اللَّهَ ذِكْرًا كَثِيرًا، وَلَا تَشْغَلْكُمُ الدُّنْيَا عَنِ الَّذِي يَنْفَعُكُمْ فِي الدَّارِ الْآخِرَةِ؛ وَلِهَذَا جَاءَ فِي الْحَدِيثِ: "مَنْ دَخَلَ سُوقًا مِنَ الْأَسْوَاقِ فَقَالَ: لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ، وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ، لَهُ الْمُلْكُ وَلَهُ الْحَمْدُ، وَهُوَ عَلَى كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ كُتبت لَهُ ألفُ أَلْفِ حَسنة، ومُحي عَنْهُ ألفُ أَلْفِ سَيئة" 

وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: لَا يَكُونُ الْعَبْدُ مِنَ الذَّاكِرِينَ اللَّهَ كَثِيرًا، حَتَّى يَذْكُرَ اللَّهَ قَائِمًا وَقَاعِدًا وَمُضْطَجِعًا.

## 62:11 — verse 11

### Translation

The Blessed and Exalted rebukes them for what had occurred, namely turning away from the sermon on Friday toward the merchandise that had arrived in Medina that day. He, Exalted is He, says: {And when they see merchandise or amusement, they scatter off toward it and leave you standing}, meaning: standing on the pulpit delivering the sermon. This is how more than one of the Successors mentioned it, among them Abu al-Aliyah, al-Hasan, Zayd ibn Aslam, and Qatadah.

Muqatil ibn Hayyan claimed that the merchandise belonged to Dihyah ibn Khalifah before he had accepted Islam, and that it had drums accompanying it, so they went out to it and left the Messenger of Allah ﷺ standing on the pulpit, except for a few of them. The report has been confirmed as sound regarding this. Imam Ahmad said:

[chain] Jabir said: A caravan arrived in Medina while the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was delivering the sermon, so the people went out, and only twelve men remained. Then this was revealed: {And when they see merchandise or amusement, they scatter off toward it}.

Both of them (al-Bukhari and Muslim) narrated it in the two Sahihs, from the hadith of Salim, with this wording.

Al-Hafiz Abu Ya'la said: [chain] Jabir ibn Abdullah said: While the Prophet ﷺ was delivering the Friday sermon, a caravan arrived in Medina, and the companions of the Messenger of Allah ﷺ rushed toward it until no one remained with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ except twelve men. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ then said: "By the One in whose hand is my soul, if you had all followed one after another until none of you remained, the valley would have flowed with fire upon you." And this verse was revealed: {And when they see merchandise or amusement, they scatter off toward it and leave you standing}. He said: among the twelve who remained steadfast with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ were Abu Bakr and Umar, may Allah be pleased with both of them.

In His saying {and leave you standing} there is evidence that the imam delivers the Friday sermon while standing. Muslim narrated in his Sahih, from Jabir ibn Samurah, who said: The Prophet ﷺ used to deliver two sermons, sitting between them, reciting the Qur'an and admonishing the people.

However, there is something here that should be known, namely: it has been said that this incident occurred when the Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to perform the prayer on Friday before the sermon, as Abu Dawud narrated in the Book of al-Marasil (disconnected reports): [chain] Muqatil ibn Hayyan said: "The Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to pray the Friday prayer before the sermon, just as with the two festival prayers, until one day, when the Prophet ﷺ was delivering the sermon after having already prayed the Friday prayer, a man entered and said: 'Dihyah ibn Khalifah has arrived with merchandise,' meaning that they then scattered off, and only a small group remained with him."

As for His saying: {Say, what is with Allah}, meaning: the reward that is with Allah in the Hereafter, {is better than amusement and than merchandise, and Allah is the best of providers}, meaning: for the one who places his trust in Him and seeks provision at its proper time.

يُعَاتِبُ تَبَارَكَ وَتَعَالَى عَلَى مَا كَانَ وَقَعَ مِنْ الِانْصِرَافِ عَنِ الْخُطْبَةِ يَوْمَ الْجُمُعَةِ إِلَى التِّجَارَةِ الَّتِي قَدِمَتِ الْمَدِينَةَ يَوْمَئِذٍ، فَقَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَإِذَا رَأَوْا تِجَارَةً أَوْ لَهْوًا انْفَضُّوا إِلَيْهَا وَتَرَكُوكَ قَائِمًا﴾ أَيْ: عَلَى الْمِنْبَرِ تَخْطُبُ. هَكَذَا ذَكَرَهُ غَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ مِنَ التَّابِعِينَ، مِنْهُمْ: أَبُو الْعَالِيَةِ، وَالْحَسَنُ، وَزَيْدُ بْنُ أَسْلَمَ، وقَتَادَةُ.

حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ إِدْرِيسَ، عَنْ حُصَين، عَنْ سَالِمِ بْنِ أَبِي الْجَعْدِ، عَنْ جَابِرٍ قَالَ: قَدمَت عيرٌ الْمَدِينَةَ، وَرَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَخْطُبُ، فَخَرَجَ النَّاسُ وَبَقِيَ اثْنَا عَشَرَ رَجُلًا فَنَزَلَتْ: ﴿وَإِذَا رَأَوْا تِجَارَةً أَوْ لَهْوًا انْفَضُّوا إِلَيْهَا﴾

أَخْرَجَاهُ فِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ، مِنْ حَدِيثِ سَالِمٍ، بِهِ 

وَقَالَ الْحَافِظُ أَبُو يَعْلَى: حَدَّثَنَا زَكَرِيَّا بْنُ يَحْيَى، حَدَّثَنَا هُشَيم، عَنْ حُصَين، عَنْ سَالِمِ بْنِ أَبِي الْجَعْدِ وَأَبِي سُفْيَانَ، عَنْ جَابِرِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ قَالَ: بَيْنَمَا النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يَخْطُبُ يَوْمَ الْجُمُعَةِ، فَقَدِمَتْ عيرٌ إِلَى الْمَدِينَةِ، فَابْتَدَرَهَا أصحابُ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، حَتَّى لَمْ يَبْقَ مَعَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ إِلَّا اثْنَا عَشَرَ رَجُلًا فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "وَالَّذِي نَفْسِي بِيَدِهِ، لَوْ تَتَابَعْتُمْ حَتَّى لَمْ يَبْقَ مِنْكُمْ أَحَدٌ، لَسَالَ بِكُمُ الْوَادِي نَارًا" وَنَزَلَتْ هَذِهِ الْآيَةُ: ﴿وَإِذَا رَأَوْا تِجَارَةً أَوْ لَهْوًا انْفَضُّوا إِلَيْهَا وَتَرَكُوكَ قَائِمًا﴾ وَقَالَ: كَانَ فِي الِاثْنَيْ عَشَرَ الَّذِينَ ثَبَتُوا مَعَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ: أَبُو بَكْرٍ، وَعُمَرُ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمَا .

وَفِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَتَرَكُوكَ قَائِمًا﴾ دَلِيلٌ عَلَى أَنَّ الْإِمَامَ يَخْطُبُ يَوْمَ الْجُمُعَةِ قَائِمًا. وَقَدْ رَوَى مُسْلِمٌ فِي صَحِيحِهِ عَنْ جَابِرِ بْنِ سَمُرَة قَالَ: كَانَتْ لِلنَّبِيِّ ﷺ خُطْبَتَانِ يَجْلِسُ بينهما، يقرأ القرآن ويذكر الناس.

ولكن هاهنا شَيْءٌ يَنْبَغِي أَنْ يُعلَم وَهُوَ: أَنَّ هَذِهِ الْقِصَّةَ قَدْ قِيلَ: إِنَّهَا كَانَتْ لَمَّا كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ يُقَدِّمُ الصَّلَاةَ يَوْمَ الْجُمُعَةِ عَلَى الْخُطْبَةِ، كَمَا رَوَاهُ أَبُو دَاوُدَ فِي كِتَابِ الْمَرَاسِيلِ: حَدَّثَنَا مَحْمُودُ بْنُ خَالِدٍ، عَنِ الْوَلِيدِ، أَخْبَرَنِي أَبُو مُعَاذٍ بُكَير بْنُ مَعروف، أَنَّهُ سَمِعَ مُقَاتل بْنَ حَيَّان يَقُولُ: "كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ يُصَلِّي يَوْمَ الْجُمُعَةِ قَبْلَ الْخُطْبَةِ مِثْلَ الْعِيدَيْنِ، حَتَّى إِذَا كَانَ يَوْمٌ وَالنَّبِيُّ ﷺ يَخْطُبُ، وَقَدْ صَلَّى الْجُمُعَةَ، فَدَخَلَ رَجُلٌ فَقَالَ: إِنَّ دحيةَ بْنَ خَلِيفَةَ قَدْ قدمَ بِتِجَارَةٍ يَعْنِي: فَانْفَضُّوا، وَلَمْ يَبْقَ مَعَهُ إِلَّا نَفَرٌ يَسِيرٌ.
