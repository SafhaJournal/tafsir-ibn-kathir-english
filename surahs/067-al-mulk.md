## 67:1-5 — verses 1-5 (shared commentary)

### Translation

This is a gharib (singular) hadith, and Ibrahim is weak. Something similar has already come before in Surat Ya-Sin. Abd ibn Humayd narrated this hadith in his Musnad in a more expanded form, saying:

[chain] Ibrahim ibn al-Hakam, from his father, from Ikrimah, from Ibn Abbas, that he said to a man: Shall I not present you with a gift, a hadith you will rejoice in? He said: Yes indeed. He said: Recite {Blessed is He in Whose hand is the dominion}, and teach it to your family, all your children, the children of your household, and your neighbors, for it is al-Munjiyah (the deliverer) and al-Mujadilah (the disputer); it disputes, or contends, on the Day of Resurrection before its Lord on behalf of the one who recites it, and seeks that he be delivered from the punishment of the Fire, and it delivers its companion by it from the punishment of the grave. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "I would love for it to be in the heart of every person of my ummah."

The Hafiz Ibn Asakir narrated in his History, in the biography of Ahmad ibn Nasr ibn Ziyad, Abu Abd Allah al-Qurashi al-Naysaburi, the Qur'an reciter, the ascetic, the jurist, one of the trustworthy narrators from whom al-Bukhari and Muslim narrated, though not in the two Sahihs; al-Tirmidhi, Ibn Majah, and Ibn Khuzaymah narrated from him, and it was under him that others studied jurisprudence following the school of Abu Ubayd ibn Harbawayh, along with many others besides them. He traced with his chain from his hadith, from Furat ibn al-Sa'ib, from al-Zuhri, from Anas ibn Malik, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "A man among those who came before you died, and he had nothing of the Book of Allah with him except {Tabaraka}. When he was placed in his grave, the angel came to him, and the surah rose up before him. The angel said to it: You are part of the Book of Allah, and I dislike causing you distress, but I have no power to bring harm or benefit to you, to him, or to myself. If you intend anything regarding him, then go to the Lord, Blessed and Exalted is He, and intercede for him. So it goes to the Lord and says: O Lord, so-and-so devoted himself to me among all of Your Book; he learned me and recited me. Will You then burn him with the Fire and punish him while I am within him? If You are going to do that to him, then erase me from Your Book. He says: Do I not see that you are angry? It says: I have every right to be angry. He says: Go, for I have granted him to you, and I have accepted your intercession for him. Then it comes, and the angel departs, leaving dejected, having gained nothing from him. Then it comes and places its mouth upon his mouth, saying: Welcome to this mouth, for it often recited me; and welcome to this chest, for it often held me; and welcome to these two feet, for they often stood for my sake. And it keeps him company in his grave for fear of loneliness befalling him." He said: When the Messenger of Allah ﷺ related this, there remained no young or old, free man or slave, except that he learned it, and the Messenger of Allah ﷺ named it al-Munjiyah (the deliverer).

I say: this is a very objectionable (munkar) hadith, and this Furat ibn al-Sa'ib was declared weak by Imam Ahmad, Yahya ibn Ma'in, al-Bukhari, Abu Hatim, al-Daraqutni, and more than one other. Ibn Asakir also mentioned it through another route, from al-Zuhri, as his own statement, in abridged form. Al-Bayhaqi narrated in his book "Ithbat Adhab al-Qabr" (Establishing the Punishment of the Grave), from Ibn Mas'ud, both as a mawquf (halted report) and marfu (elevated report), something that corroborates this, and we have recorded it in the Book of Funerals in al-Ahkam al-Kubra. Praise be to Allah.

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

He, Exalted is He, glorifies His noble Self and informs that in His hand is the dominion, meaning: He is the One who disposes of all creation as He wills; none can reverse His judgment, and He is not questioned about what He does, due to His overwhelming power, His wisdom, and His justice. Therefore He said: {and He is over all things competent}.

Then He said: {Who created death and life}. Those who held that death is an existent thing, since it is created, cited this verse as evidence. The meaning of the verse is that He brought creatures into being out of nonexistence, in order to try them and test which of them is best in deed, as He said: {How can you disbelieve in Allah when you were dead and He gave you life} [al-Baqarah: 28]. He named the first state, which is nonexistence, death, and He named this coming into being life. Therefore He said: {then He causes you to die, then He gives you life} [al-Baqarah: 28].

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Abu Zur'ah, from Safwan, from al-Walid, from Khulayd, from Qatadah, regarding His saying {Who created death and life}, he said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to say: "Allah has humbled the children of Adam through death, and made this world a home of life then a home of death, and made the Hereafter a home of recompense then a home of permanence."

Ma'mar also narrated it from Qatadah.

As for His saying {to test you as to which of you is best in deed}, meaning: best in deed, as Muhammad ibn Ajlan said, He did not say "most in deed."

Then He said: {and He is the Almighty, the Forgiving}, meaning: He is the Mighty, the Great, the One of unassailable majesty, and along with that He is Forgiving toward whoever turns to Him in repentance and returns to Him after having disobeyed Him and opposed His command. Though He, Exalted is He, is Almighty, He nevertheless forgives, has mercy, pardons, and overlooks.

Then He said: {Who created seven heavens in layers}, meaning: one layer after another. Are they connected to one another, in the sense of being stacked one above another, or are they separated, with empty space between them? There are two views on this, the more correct of which is the second, as indicated by the hadith of the Night Journey (al-Isra) and other reports.

As for His saying {You do not see in the creation of the Most Merciful any inconsistency}, meaning: rather it is harmonious and uniform, containing no discrepancy, no discord, no contradiction, no deficiency, no defect, and no flaw. Therefore He said: {So return your vision, do you see any fissure?}, meaning: look at the sky and reflect upon it, do you see in it any defect, deficiency, flaw, or fissure?

Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, al-Dahhak, al-Thawri, and others said regarding His saying {So return your vision, do you see any fissure?}: meaning, cracks.

Al-Suddi said: {Do you see any fissure?} means: any openings. Ibn Abbas said in another report: {any fissure} means: any weakening. Qatadah said: {Do you see any fissure?} meaning: do you see any flaw, O son of Adam?

As for His saying {Then return your vision twice again}, he said: twice more. {Your vision will return to you humbled}, Ibn Abbas said: meaning, abased. Mujahid and Qatadah said: lowly.

{while it is worn out}, Ibn Abbas said: meaning, it is exhausted. Mujahid, Qatadah, and al-Suddi said: al-hasir means worn out from fatigue.

The meaning of the verse is: if you were to repeat your gaze, however many times you repeated it, it would return to you, meaning your vision would come back to you, {humbled}, unable to see any defect or flaw, {while it is worn out}, meaning exhausted, having given out from fatigue due to the repeated looking, and it perceives no deficiency.

Having negated any deficiency in its creation, He then clarified its perfection and adornment, saying: {And We have certainly adorned the nearest heaven with lamps}, which are the stars placed within it, both the moving planets and the fixed stars.

As for His saying {and We have made them missiles against the devils}, the pronoun in {and We have made them} refers to the general category of lamps, not to their specific selves, for it is not the stars in the sky themselves that are thrown, but rather meteors beneath them, which may draw upon them, and Allah knows best.

As for His saying {and We have prepared for them the punishment of the Blaze}, meaning: We have decreed this disgrace for the devils in this world, and We have prepared for them the punishment of the Blaze in the Hereafter, as He said at the beginning of al-Saffat: {Indeed, We have adorned the nearest heaven with an adornment of stars, and as protection against every rebellious devil. They cannot listen to the exalted assembly, and they are pelted from every side, repelled, and for them is a perpetual punishment, except one who snatches a fragment, and he is pursued by a piercing flame} [al-Saffat: 6-10].

Qatadah said: These stars were created for three purposes: Allah created them as an adornment for the sky, as missiles against the devils, and as signs by which guidance is found. Whoever interprets them as anything other than that has spoken according to his own opinion, has erred regarding his due share, has wasted his portion, and has taken upon himself something of which he has no knowledge. Narrated by Ibn Jarir and Ibn Abi Hatim.

### Source (Arabic) — review against this

هَذَا حَدِيثٌ غَرِيبٌ، وَإِبْرَاهِيمُ ضَعِيفٌ، وَقَدْ تَقَدَّمَ مِثْلُهُ فِي سُورَةِ "يس" وَقَدْ رَوَى هَذَا الْحَدِيثَ عَبْدُ بْنُ حُمَيد فِي مَسْنَدِهِ بِأَبْسَطِ مِنْ هَذَا، فَقَالَ:

حَدَّثَنَا إِبْرَاهِيمَ بْنِ الْحَكَمِ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ أَنَّهُ قَالَ لِرَجُلٍ: أَلَا أُتْحِفُكَ بِحَدِيثٍ تَفْرَحُ بِهِ؟ قَالَ: بَلَى. قَالَ اقْرَأْ: " تَبَارَكَ الَّذِي بِيَدِهِ الْمُلْكُ " وَعَلِّمْهَا أَهْلَكَ وَجَمِيعَ وَلَدِكَ وَصِبْيَانَ بَيْتِكَ وَجِيرَانَكَ، فَإِنَّهَا الْمُنْجِيَةُ وَالْمُجَادِلَةُ، تُجَادِلُ -أَوْ تُخَاصِمُ-يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ عِنْدَ رَبِّهَا لِقَارِئِهَا، وَتَطْلُبُ لَهُ أَنْ [يُنْجِيَهُ] مِنْ عَذَابِ النَّارِ، وَيُنْجِي بِهَا صَاحِبَهَا مِنْ عَذَابِ الْقَبْرِ؛ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "لوددتُ أَنَّهَا فِي قَلْبِ كُلِّ إِنْسَانٍ مِنْ أُمَّتِي" .

وَقَدْ رَوَى الْحَافِظُ ابْنُ عَسَاكِرَ فِي تَارِيخِهِ، فِي تَرْجَمَةِ أَحْمَدَ بْنِ نَصْرِ بْنِ زِيَادٍ، أَبِي عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الْقُرَشِيِّ النَّيْسَابُورِيِّ الْمُقْرِئِ الزَّاهِدِ الْفَقِيهِ، أَحَدِ الثِّقَاتِ الَّذِينَ رَوَى عَنْهُمُ الْبُخَارِيُّ وَمُسْلِمٌ، وَلَكِنْ فِي غَيْرِ الصَّحِيحَيْنِ، وَرَوَى عَنْهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ وَابْنُ مَاجَهْ وَابْنُ خُزَيْمَةَ. وَعَلَيْهِ تَفَقَّهَ فِي مَذْهَبِ أَبِي عُبَيد بْنِ حَرْبَويه، وَخَلْقٌ سِوَاهُمْ، سَاقَ بِسَنَدِهِ مِنْ حَدِيثِهِ عَنْ فُرَاتِ بْنِ السَّائِبِ، عَنِ الزُّهْرِيِّ، عَنْ أَنَسِ بْنِ مَالِكٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ رَجُلًا مِمَّنْ كَانَ قَبْلَكُمْ مَاتَ، وَلَيْسَ مَعَهُ شَيْءٌ مِنْ كِتَابِ اللَّهِ إِلَّا " تَبَارَكَ "، فَلَمَّا وُضِعَ فِي حُفْرَتِهِ أَتَاهُ المَلَك فَثَارَتِ السُّورَةُ فِي وَجْهِهِ، فَقَالَ لَهَا: إِنَّكِ مِنْ كِتَابِ اللَّهِ، وَأَنَا أَكْرَهُ مُسَاءَتَكِ، وَإِنِّي لَا أَمْلِكُ لَكِ وَلَا لَهُ وَلَا لِنَفْسِي ضَرًّا وَلَا نَفْعًا، فَإِنْ أَرَدْتِ هَذَا بِهِ فَانْطَلِقِي إِلَى الرَّبِّ تَبَارَكَ وَتَعَالَى فَاشْفَعِي لَهُ. فَتَنْطَلِقُ إِلَى الرَّبِّ فَتَقُولُ: يَا رَبِّ، إِنَّ فُلَانًا عَمَد إليَّ مِنْ بَيْنِ كِتَابِكَ فتَعَلَّمني وَتَلَانِي أَفَتُحَرِّقُهُ أَنْتَ بِالنَّارِ وَتُعَذِّبُهُ وَأَنَا فِي جَوْفِهِ؟ فَإِنْ كُنْتَ فَاعِلًا ذَاكَ بِهِ فَامْحُنِي مِنْ كِتَابِكَ. فَيَقُولُ: أَلَا أَرَاكِ غَضِبْتِ؟ فَتَقُولُ: وحُقّ لِي أَنْ أَغْضَبَ. فَيَقُولُ: اذْهَبِي فَقَدْ وَهَبْتُهُ لَكِ، وشَفّعتك فِيهِ. قَالَ: فَتَجِيءُ فَيَخْرُجُ الْمَلَكُ، فَيَخْرُجُ كَاسِفَ الْبَالِ لَمْ يَحْلَ مِنْهُ بِشَيْءٍ. قَالَ: فَتَجِيءُ فَتَضَعُ فَاهَا عَلَى فِيهِ، فَتَقُولُ مَرْحَبًا بِهَذَا الْفَمِ، فَرُبَّمَا تَلَانِي، وَمَرْحَبًا بِهَذَا الصَّدْرِ، فَرُبَّمَا وَعَانِي، وَمَرْحَبًا بِهَاتَيْنِ الْقَدَمَيْنِ، فَرُبَّمَا قَامَتَا بِي. وَتُؤْنِسُهُ فِي قَبْرِهِ مَخَافَةَ الْوَحْشَةِ عَلَيْهِ". قَالَ: فَلَمَّا حَدّث بِهَذَا رسولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ لَمْ يَبْقَ صَغِيرٌ وَلَا كَبِيرٌ وَلَا حُرّ وَلَا عَبْدٌ، إِلَّا تَعَلَّمَهَا، وَسَمَّاهَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ الْمُنْجِيَةَ .

قُلْتُ: وَهَذَا حَدِيثٌ مُنْكَرٌ جِدًّا، وَفُرَاتُ بْنُ السَّائِبِ هَذَا ضَعَّفَهُ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ، وَيَحْيَى بْنُ مَعِينٍ، وَالْبُخَارِيُّ، وَأَبُو حَاتِمٍ، وَالدَّارَقُطْنِيُّ وَغَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ. وَقَدْ ذَكَرَهُ ابْنُ عَسَاكِرَ مِنْ وَجْهٍ آخَرَ، عَنِ الزُّهْرِيِّ، مِنْ قَوْلِهِ مُخْتَصَرًا. وَرَوَى الْبَيْهَقِيُّ فِي كِتَابِ "إِثْبَاتِ عَذَابِ الْقَبْرِ" عَنِ ابْنِ مَسْعُودٍ مَوْقُوفًا وَمَرْفُوعًا مَا يَشْهَدُ لِهَذَا وَقَدْ كَتَبْنَاهُ فِي كِتَابِ الْجَنَائِزِ من الأحكام الكبرى، ولله الحمد .

يُمَجِّدُ تَعَالَى نَفْسَهُ الْكَرِيمَةَ، وَيُخْبِرُ أَنَّهُ بِيَدِهِ الْمُلْكُ، أَيْ: هُوَ الْمُتَصَرِّفُ فِي جَمِيعِ الْمَخْلُوقَاتِ بِمَا يَشَاءُ لَا مُعَقِّبَ لِحُكْمِهِ، وَلَا يُسْأَلُ عَمَّا يَفْعَلُ لِقَهْرِهِ وَحِكْمَتِهِ وَعَدْلِهِ. وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿وَهُوَ عَلَى كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ﴾

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿الَّذِي خَلَقَ الْمَوْتَ وَالْحَيَاةَ﴾ وَاسْتَدَلَّ بِهَذِهِ الْآيَةِ مَنْ قَالَ: إِنَّ الْمَوْتَ أَمْرٌ وُجُودِيٌّ لِأَنَّهُ مَخْلُوقٌ. وَمَعْنَى الْآيَةِ: أَنَّهُ أَوْجَدَ الْخَلَائِقَ مِنَ الْعَدَمِ، لِيَبْلُوَهُمْ وَيَخْتَبِرَهُمْ أَيُّهُمْ أَحْسَنُ عَمَلًا؟ كَمَا قَالَ: ﴿كَيْفَ تَكْفُرُونَ بِاللَّهِ وَكُنْتُمْ أَمْوَاتًا فَأَحْيَاكُمْ﴾ [الْبَقَرَةِ: ٢٨] فَسَمَّى الْحَالَ الْأَوَّلَ -وَهُوَ الْعَدَمُ-مَوْتًا، وَسَمَّى هَذِهِ النَّشْأَةَ حَيَاةً. وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿ثُمَّ يُمِيتُكُمْ ثُمَّ يُحْيِيكُمْ﴾ [الْبَقَرَةِ: ٢٨] .

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو زُرْعَة، حَدَّثَنَا صَفْوَانُ، حَدَّثَنَا الْوَلِيدُ، حَدَّثَنَا خُلَيْد، عَنْ قَتَادَةَ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿الَّذِي خَلَقَ الْمَوْتَ وَالْحَيَاةَ﴾ قَالَ: كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم يقول: "إِنَّ اللَّهَ أَذَلَّ بَنِي آدَمَ بِالْمَوْتِ، وَجَعَلَ الدُّنْيَا دَارَ حَيَاةٍ ثُمَّ دَارَ مَوْتٍ، وَجَعَلَ الْآخِرَةَ دَارَ جَزَاءٍ ثُمَّ دَارَ بَقَاءٍ".

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿لِيَبْلُوَكُمْ أَيُّكُمْ أَحْسَنُ عَمَلا﴾ أَيْ: خَيْرٌ عَمَلًا كَمَا قَالَ مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ عَجْلان: وَلَمْ يَقُلْ أَكْثَرُ عَمَلًا.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿وَهُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْغَفُورُ﴾ أَيْ: هُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْعَظِيمُ الْمَنِيعُ الْجَنَابِ، وَهُوَ مَعَ ذَلِكَ غَفُورٌ لِمَنْ تَابَ إِلَيْهِ وَأَنَابَ، بَعْدَمَا عَصَاهُ وَخَالَفَ أَمْرَهُ، وَإِنْ كَانَ تَعَالَى عَزِيزًا، هُوَ مَعَ ذَلِكَ يَغْفِرُ وَيَرْحَمُ وَيَصْفَحُ وَيَتَجَاوَزُ.

ثُمَّ قَالَ: ﴿الَّذِي خَلَقَ سَبْعَ سَمَاوَاتٍ طِبَاقًا﴾ أَيْ: طَبَقَةً بَعْدَ طَبَقَةٍ، وَهَلْ هُنَّ مُتَوَاصِلَاتٌ بِمَعْنَى أَنَّهُنَّ عَلَوِيَّاتٌ بَعْضُهُمْ عَلَى بَعْضٍ، أَوْ مُتَفَاصِلَاتٌ بَيْنَهُنَّ خَلَاءٌ؟ فِيهِ قَوْلَانِ، أَصَحُّهُمَا الثَّانِي، كَمَا دَلَّ على ذلك حديث الإسراء وغيره.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿مَا تَرَى فِي خَلْقِ الرَّحْمَنِ مِنْ تَفَاوُتٍ﴾ أَيْ: بَلْ هُوَ مُصْطَحِبٌ مُسْتَوٍ، لَيْسَ فِيهِ اخْتِلَافٌ وَلَا تَنَافُرٌ وَلَا مُخَالَفَةٌ، وَلَا نَقْصٌ وَلَا عَيْبٌ وَلَا خَلَلٌ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿فَارْجِعِ الْبَصَرَ هَلْ تَرَى مِنْ فُطُورٍ﴾ أي: انْظُرْ إِلَى السَّمَاءِ فَتَأَمَّلْهَا، هَلْ تَرَى فِيهَا عَيْبًا أَوْ نَقْصًا أَوْ خَلَلًا؛ أَوْ فُطُورًا؟.

قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ، وَالثَّوْرِيُّ، وَغَيْرُهُمْ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿فَارْجِعِ الْبَصَرَ هَلْ تَرَى مِنْ فُطُورٍ﴾ أَيْ: شُقُوقٍ.

وَقَالَ السُّدِّيُّ: ﴿هَلْ تَرَى مِنْ فُطُورٍ﴾ أَيْ: مِنْ خُروق. وَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ فِي رِوَايَةٍ: ﴿مِنْ فُطُورٍ﴾ أَيْ: مِنْ وُهِيّ وَقَالَ قَتَادَةُ: ﴿هَلْ تَرَى مِنْ فُطُورٍ﴾ أَيْ: هَلْ تَرَى خَلَلا يَا ابْنَ آدَمَ؟.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿ثُمَّ ارْجِعِ الْبَصَرَ كَرَّتَيْنِ﴾ قَالَ: مَرَّتَيْنِ. ﴿يَنْقَلِبْ إِلَيْكَ الْبَصَرُ خَاسِئًا﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: ذَلِيلًا؟ وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وقَتَادَةُ: صَاغِرًا.

﴿وَهُوَ حَسِيرٌ﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: يَعْنِي: وَهُوَ كَلِيلٌ. وَقَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ، وقَتَادَةُ، وَالسُّدِّيُّ: الْحَسِيرُ: الْمُنْقَطِعُ مِنَ الْإِعْيَاءِ.

وَمَعْنَى الْآيَةِ: إِنَّكَ لَوْ كَرَّرْتَ الْبَصَرَ، مَهْمَا كَرَّرْتَ، لَانْقَلَبَ إِلَيْكَ، أَيْ: لَرَجَعَ إِلَيْكَ الْبَصَرُ، ﴿خَاسِئًا﴾ عَنْ أَنْ يَرَى عَيْبًا أَوْ خَلَلًا ﴿وَهُوَ حَسِيرٌ﴾ أَيْ: كَلِيلٌ قَدِ انْقَطَعَ مِنَ الْإِعْيَاءِ مِنْ كَثْرَةِ التَّكَرُّرِ، وَلَا يَرَى نَقْصًا.

وَلَمَّا نَفَى عَنْهَا فِي خَلْقِهَا النَّقْصَ بَيَّنَ كَمَالَهَا وَزِينَتَهَا فَقَالَ: ﴿وَلَقَدْ زَيَّنَّا السَّمَاءَ الدُّنْيَا بِمَصَابِيحَ﴾ وَهِيَ الْكَوَاكِبُ الَّتِي وُضِعَتْ فِيهَا مِنَ السَّيَّارَاتِ وَالثَّوَابِتِ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَجَعَلْنَاهَا رُجُومًا لِلشَّيَاطِينِ﴾ عَادَ الضَّمِيرُ فِي قَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَجَعَلْنَاهَا﴾ عَلَى جِنْسِ الْمَصَابِيحِ لَا عَلَى عَيْنِهَا؛ لِأَنَّهُ لَا يَرْمِي بِالْكَوَاكِبِ الَّتِي فِي السَّمَاءِ، بَلْ بِشُهُبٍ مِنْ دُونِهَا، وَقَدْ تَكُونُ مُسْتَمَدَّةً مِنْهَا، وَاللَّهُ أَعْلَمُ.

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَأَعْتَدْنَا لَهُمْ عَذَابَ السَّعِيرِ﴾ أَيْ: جَعَلْنَا لِلشَّيَاطِينِ هَذَا الْخِزْيَ فِي الدُّنْيَا، وَأَعْتَدْنَا لَهُمْ عَذَابَ السَّعِيرِ فِي الْأُخْرَى، كَمَا قَالَ: فِي أَوَّلِ الصَّافَّاتِ: ﴿إِنَّا زَيَّنَّا السَّمَاءَ الدُّنْيَا بِزِينَةٍ الْكَوَاكِبِ وَحِفْظًا مِنْ كُلِّ شَيْطَانٍ مَارِدٍ لَا يَسَّمَّعُونَ إِلَى الْمَلإ الأعْلَى وَيُقْذَفُونَ مِنْ كُلِّ جَانِبٍ دُحُورًا وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ وَاصِبٌ إِلا مَنْ خَطِفَ الْخَطْفَةَ فَأَتْبَعَهُ شِهَابٌ ثَاقِبٌ﴾ [الصَّافَّاتِ: ٦ -١٠] .

قَالَ قَتَادَةُ: إِنَّمَا خُلِقَتْ هَذِهِ النُّجُومُ لِثَلَاثِ خِصَالٍ: خَلَقَهَا اللَّهُ زِينَةً لِلسَّمَاءِ، وَرُجُومًا لِلشَّيَاطِينِ، وَعَلَامَاتٍ يُهْتَدَى بِهَا، فَمَنْ تَأَوَّلَ فِيهَا غَيْرَ ذَلِكَ فَقَدْ قَالَ بِرَأْيِهِ وَأَخْطَأَ حَظَّهُ، وَأَضَاعَ نَصِيبَهُ، وَتَكَلَّفَ مَا لَا عِلْمَ لَهُ بِهِ. رَوَاهُ ابْنُ جرير، وابن أبي حاتم.

## 67:6-11 — verses 6-11 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: {and} We have prepared {for those who disbelieved in their Lord the punishment of Hell, and evil is the destination}, meaning: evil is the final abode and place of return. {When they are thrown into it, they hear its roaring} Ibn Jarir said: meaning the shrieking sound.

{While it boils up} al-Thawri said: it boils with them just as a small quantity of grain boils in a large amount of water.

His statement: {It almost bursts apart from rage} means: some of it is about to separate from other parts, out of the intensity of its rage and fury against them, {every time a group is thrown into it, its keepers ask them: "Did no warner come to you?" They say: "Yes indeed, a warner did come to us, but we denied him and said: 'God has not sent down anything, you are only in great error.'"} The Almighty is mentioning His justice toward His creation, and that He does not punish anyone except after the proof has been established against him and a messenger has been sent to him, as He said: {And We would not punish until We sent a messenger} [al-Isra: 15]. And the Almighty said: {Until, when they reach it, its gates are opened, and its keepers say to them: "Did not messengers come to you from among yourselves, reciting to you the verses of your Lord and warning you of the meeting of this Day of yours?" They will say: "Yes indeed," but the word of punishment against the disbelievers had come into effect} [al-Zumar: 71]. Thus they turned upon themselves in blame, and regretted at a time when regret would not benefit them, saying: {If only we had listened or reasoned, we would not have been among the companions of the blazing Fire}, meaning: had we possessed minds that we could have made use of, or had we listened to the truth that God sent down, we would not have remained in the disbelief in God and the delusion we were in. But we had no understanding by which we could grasp what the messengers brought, nor did we have reason to guide us to follow them. God the Almighty said: {So they will confess their sin, so away with the companions of the blazing Fire}.

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] Muhammad ibn Ja'far told us, Shu'bah told us, from Amr ibn Murrah, from Abu al-Bakhtari al-Ta'i, who said: someone who heard it from the Messenger of God ﷺ informed me that he said: "People will never be destroyed until they have exhausted every excuse for themselves." And in another hadith: "No one enters the Fire except that he knows the Fire is more deserving of him than Paradise."

## 67:12-15 — verses 12-15 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Most High is telling us about one who fears the standing of his Lord in what is between himself and his Lord when he is absent from people, so that he restrains himself from disobedience and performs acts of obedience where no one sees him except Allah, that for him there is forgiveness and a great reward. That is, He expiates his sins for him and rewards him with abundant recompense, as is established in the two Sahihs: "Seven whom Allah will shade in the shade of His Throne on the Day when there is no shade but His shade," and among them he mentioned: "a man whom a woman of position and beauty invited [to unlawful relations] and he said, 'I fear Allah,' and a man who gave charity and concealed it, such that his left hand did not know what his right hand spent."

Al-Hafiz Abu Bakr al-Bazzar said in his Musnad: [chain] Anas said: They said, "O Messenger of Allah, we are in one state with you, but when we part from you we are in another." He said, "How are you and your Lord?" They said, "Allah is our Lord in secret and in open." He said, "That is not hypocrisy."

We know of no one who narrated it from Thabit except al-Harith ibn Ubayd.

Then the Most High said, alerting that He is aware of inner thoughts and secrets: {And conceal your speech or proclaim it; indeed He is Knowing of that which is in the breasts} meaning, of what occurs to the hearts.

{Does He who created not know?} meaning, does the Creator not know? And it is said its meaning is: does Allah not know His creation? The first is more fitting, on account of His saying: {And He is the Subtle, the Aware}.

Then He mentioned His favor upon His creation in subjecting the earth to them and making it tractable for them, by making it stable and settled, not stretching nor shaking, through the mountains He placed in it, the springs He caused to gush forth in it, the paths He laid through it, and the benefits and places for crops and fruits He prepared in it. He said: {He it is who made the earth tractable for you, so walk among its slopes} meaning, so travel wherever you wish among its regions, and move about in its provinces and its far reaches in the various pursuits of livelihood and trade, and know that your effort avails you nothing except that Allah make it easy for you. Therefore He said: {and eat of His provision}. So striving through means does not contradict reliance upon Allah (tawakkul), as Imam Ahmad said:

[chain] Umar ibn al-Khattab said that he heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "If you were to rely upon Allah with the true reliance due to Him, He would provide for you as He provides for the birds: they go out in the morning hungry and return in the evening full."

Al-Tirmidhi, al-Nasa'i, and Ibn Majah narrated it from the hadith of Ibn Hubayrah, and al-Tirmidhi said: hasan sahih (good and authentic). So he affirmed for the birds a going out in the evening and a morning departure in seeking provision, along with their reliance upon Allah, Mighty and Majestic, and He is the One who subjects, who sets in motion, who ordains causes. {And to Him is the resurrection} meaning, the return on the Day of Resurrection.

Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, Qatadah, and al-Suddi said: {its slopes} means its extremities, its passes, and its regions. Ibn Abbas and Qatadah also said: {its slopes} means the mountains.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] Bashir ibn Ka'b recited this verse: {so walk among its slopes} and said to a slave-mother of his: "If you know what its slopes are, you are free." She said: "They are the mountains." So he asked Abu al-Darda, and he said: "They are the mountains."

## 67:16-19 — verses 16-19 (shared commentary)

### Translation

This too is part of His gentleness and mercy toward His creation, that He is able to punish them because of the disbelief of some of them in Him and their worship of others alongside Him, yet despite this He is forbearing and overlooks, and He grants respite and does not hasten, as He said: {And if Allah were to seize mankind for what they have earned, He would not leave upon its surface any creature. But He defers them to a specified term, and when their term comes, indeed Allah is Ever Seeing of His servants} [Fatir: 45].

And He said here: {Do you feel secure that He who is in heaven will not cause the earth to swallow you and suddenly it should sway?} meaning: it goes back and forth and shakes violently, {Or do you feel secure that He who is in heaven will not send against you a storm of stones?} meaning: a wind carrying pebbles that would crush you, as He said: {Do you then feel secure that He will not cause a side of the land to swallow you or send against you a storm of stones, and then you would not find for yourselves any guardian?} [al-Isra: 68]. And thus He threatens them here with His statement: {Then you will know how [severe] was My warning} meaning: how My warning is, and the fate of whoever turned away from it and denied it.

Then He said: {And those before them had already denied} meaning: from the past nations and bygone generations, {then how was My rejection?} meaning: how was My condemnation of them and My punishment of them? That is: great, severe, and painful.

Then the Exalted said: {Do they not see the birds above them, spreading their wings and folding them?} meaning: at times they spread their wings flat in the air, and at times one wing gathers while the other spreads, {None holds them up} meaning: in the air, {except the Most Merciful} meaning: by what He has subjected for them of the air, out of His mercy and gentleness, {Indeed, He is Seeing of all things} meaning: of what sets right every one of His creatures. This is like His statement: {Do they not see the birds subjected in the atmosphere of the sky? None holds them up except Allah. Indeed in that are signs for a people who believe} [al-Nahl: 79].

## 67:20-27 — verses 20-27 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Exalted says to the polytheists who worshipped other than Him, seeking help and provision from them, censuring them for what they believed and informing them that they will not attain what they had hoped for. He says: {Or who is this that is an army for you to help you besides the Merciful?} meaning: you have no guardian or protector besides Him, nor any helper for you other than Him. Therefore He says: {The disbelievers are in nothing but delusion.}

Then He says: {Or who is this that will provide for you if He withholds His provision?} That is: who is there who, if Allah were to cut off His provision from you, would provide for you after Him? Meaning: no one gives, withholds, creates, provides, and helps except Allah, Mighty and Majestic, alone, without partner. That is, and they know this, yet despite this they worship other than Him. Therefore He says: {Nay, they persist} meaning: they continue in their transgression, their falsehood, and their misguidance, {in obstinacy and aversion}, meaning: in defiance and arrogance, turning away in aversion from the truth. That is, they do not listen to it nor do they follow it.

Then He says: {Is he who walks fallen upon his face more guided, or he who walks upright on a straight path?} This is a parable that Allah has set forth for the believer and the disbeliever. The disbeliever's likeness in his condition is that of one who walks fallen upon his face, that is, walking bent over, not upright, upon his face, meaning: he does not know where he is going nor how he is proceeding. Rather, he is lost, bewildered, astray. Is this one more guided, {or he who walks upright}, meaning: with an erect stature, {on a straight path}, meaning: on a clear, evident way, being himself upright and his path being straight? This is their likeness in this world, and thus they will be in the Hereafter as well. The believer will be gathered walking upright on a straight path leading him to the spacious Garden, while the disbeliever will be gathered walking on his face toward the Fire of Hell. {Gather those who did wrong and their peers and what they used to worship besides Allah, and guide them to the path of the Blazing Fire, and stop them, for they are to be questioned. What is the matter with you that you do not help one another? Nay, on this Day they are surrendering.}

Imam Ahmad, may Allah have mercy on him, said: [chain] Ibn Numayr told us, Isma'il told us, from Nufay', who said: I heard Anas ibn Malik say: It was asked, "O Messenger of Allah, how will people be gathered upon their faces?" He ﷺ said: "Is not the One who made them walk upon their feet able to make them walk upon their faces?"

This hadith is recorded in the two Sahihs by the route of [Yunus ibn Muhammad, from Shayban, from Qatadah, from Anas], with similar wording.

And His saying: {Say, He is the One who brought you into being}, that is: He originated your creation after you were not a thing worth mentioning, {and made for you hearing, sight, and hearts}, that is, intellects and perception, {yet little do you give thanks}, meaning: how little you make use of these faculties with which Allah has favored you, in obeying Him, following His commands, and abandoning what He has forbidden.

{Say, He is the One who spread you throughout the earth}, that is: He scattered and dispersed you across the regions and expanses of the earth, despite the differences in your tongues, your languages, your colors, your adornments, your forms, and your appearances, {and to Him you will be gathered}, that is: you will be gathered together after this dispersal and scattering. He will gather you as He dispersed you, and He will bring you back as He originated you.

Then He says, informing about the disbelievers who deny the Return and consider its occurrence far-fetched: {And they say, "When is this promise, if you are truthful?"} That is: when will this thing occur, which you tell us will happen, this gathering together after this dispersal? {Say, "The knowledge is only with Allah"}, meaning: no one knows the precise time of that except Allah, Mighty and Majestic. But He has commanded me to inform you that this will certainly come to pass and occur without doubt, so beware of it. {I am only a clear warner}, meaning: my duty is only to deliver the message, and I have indeed delivered it to you.

Allah the Exalted says: {But when they see it approaching, the faces of those who disbelieved will be distressed}, that is: when the Hour is established and the disbelievers witness it, and they see that the matter was indeed near, for whatever is coming is coming, even if its time is long, then when what they had denied came to pass, it distressed them, because of what they know awaits them there of evil. That is, it engulfed them, and there came to them from Allah's command what had never occurred to their minds or their reckoning. {And there appeared to them from Allah what they had not taken into account, and there appeared to them the evils of what they had earned, and what they used to mock overtook them} [al-Zumar: 47-48]. Therefore it will be said to them by way of reproach and rebuke: {This is what you used to call for}, meaning: what you used to demand hastily.

## 67:28-30 — verses 28-30 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Almighty says: {Say} O Muhammad, to these polytheists who associate partners with Allah and deny His blessings: {Do you see, if Allah destroys me and those with me or has mercy upon us, then who will shelter the disbelievers from a painful punishment?} That is: save yourselves, for there is no rescuer for you from Allah except repentance, turning back to Him, and returning to His religion. It will not benefit you that the punishment and retribution you wish upon us should occur, for whether Allah punishes us or has mercy upon us, there is no escape for you from His retribution and painful punishment which will befall you.

Then He said: {Say, He is the Most Merciful. We have believed in Him and upon Him we have relied} That is: we have believed in the Lord of the worlds, the Most Merciful, the Bestower of Mercy, and upon Him we have relied in all our affairs, just as He said: {So worship Him and rely upon Him} [Hud: 123]. For this reason He said: {Then you will know who is in manifest error} That is: which of us, us or you, and for whom the final outcome will be, in this world and the Hereafter.

Then He said: {Say, do you see, if your water were to sink} That is: to go down into the earth, downward, so that it cannot be reached by sharp axes nor by strong arms. And "the sinking one" (al-gha'ir) is the opposite of "the springing one" (al-nabi'). For this reason He said: {Then who will bring you flowing water?} That is: springing, flowing, running upon the surface of the earth. None is able to do that except Allah, Mighty and Majestic. So it is from His bounty and generosity that He caused springs of water to flow forth for you and made them run through all the regions of the earth, according to what His servants need, whether little or much. So to Allah belongs all praise and grace.

[End of the tafsir of Surat "Tabarak," and to Allah belongs all praise]
