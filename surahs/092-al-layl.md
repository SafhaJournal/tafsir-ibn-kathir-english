## 92:1-11 — verses 1-11 (shared commentary)

### Translation

The Tafsir of Surah al-Layl

It is Makkan.

His ﷺ statement to Mu'adh has already been mentioned: "Why did you not pray with {Glorify the name of your Lord, the Most High}, {By the sun and its morning brightness}, {By the night when it covers}?"

In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful

* *

Imam Ahmad said: [chain] from 'Alqamah: that he came to Syria and entered the mosque of Damascus, and prayed two rak'ahs in it, and said: "O Allah, grant me a righteous companion." He said: So he sat with Abu al-Darda', and Abu al-Darda' said to him: "Where are you from?" He said: "From the people of Kufah." He said: "How did you hear Ibn Umm 'Abd recite: {By the night when it covers, and the day when it appears in brightness}?" 'Alqamah said: "{and the male and the female}." Abu al-Darda' said: "I indeed heard it from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, but these people have not ceased until they made me doubt." Then he said: "Was there not among you the companion of the cushion, and the keeper of the secret that no one else knows, and the one who was granted protection from Satan by the tongue of the Prophet ﷺ?"

Al-Bukhari related it here, and Muslim, via the route of al-A'mash, from Ibrahim, who said: The companions of 'Abdullah came to Abu al-Darda', so he sought them out and found them, and said: "Which of you recites to me the recitation of 'Abdullah?" They said: "All of us." He said: "Which of you has the best memory?" So they pointed to 'Alqamah, and he said: "How did you hear him recite: {By the night when it covers}?" He said: "{and the male and the female}." He said: "I bear witness that I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ recite it in this way, while these people want me to recite: {And that which created the male and the female}. By Allah, I will not follow them."

This is the wording of al-Bukhari. Thus did Ibn Mas'ud and Abu al-Darda' recite it, and Abu al-Darda' raised it as marfu' (attributed directly to the Prophet ﷺ). As for the majority, they recited it just as it is fixed in al-Mushaf al-Imam (the master codex of 'Uthman) established throughout all the regions: {And that which created the male and the female}.

Allah, exalted is He, swore by {the night when it covers}, that is, when it covers creation with its darkness, {and the day when it appears in brightness}, that is, with its light and radiance, {and that which created the male and the female}, as in His saying: {And We created you in pairs} [al-Naba': 8], and as in His saying: {And of everything We created pairs} [al-Dhariyat: 49].

Since the oath was by these opposing things, that which was sworn about was likewise opposing in nature. This is why He said: {Indeed, your efforts are diverse}, that is, the deeds that the servants have earned are likewise opposing and differing: some do good and some do evil.

Allah, exalted is He, said: {As for the one who gives and is God-fearing}, that is, he gives what he was commanded to disburse, and fears Allah in his affairs, {and believes in al-husna (the best)}, meaning: in the recompense for that. This is what Qatadah said. Khasif said: the reward. Ibn 'Abbas, Mujahid, 'Ikrimah, Abu Salih, and Zayd ibn Aslam said: {and believes in al-husna} means: in al-khalaf (the compensation given in return). Abu 'Abd al-Rahman al-Sulami and al-Dahhak said: {and believes in al-husna} means: in "there is no god but Allah." In one report from 'Ikrimah: {and believes in al-husna} means: in what Allah has bestowed upon him. In one report from Zayd ibn Aslam: {and believes in al-husna}, he said: prayer, zakah, and fasting. And on another occasion he said: the charity of al-Fitr.

Ibn Abi Hatim said: [chain] from Ubayy ibn Ka'b, who said: "I asked the Messenger of Allah ﷺ about al-husna. He said: 'Al-husna is Paradise.'"

* *

As for His saying: {We will ease him toward ease}, Ibn 'Abbas said: meaning, toward good. Zayd ibn Aslam said: meaning, toward Paradise.

Some of the salaf (early generations) said: Among the rewards of a good deed is the good deed that follows it, and among the recompense of an evil deed is the evil deed that follows it. This is why Allah, exalted is He, said: {But as for he who withholds}, that is, withholds what he has, {and considers himself self-sufficient}. 'Ikrimah said, on the authority of Ibn 'Abbas: that is, he withholds his wealth and considers himself in no need of his Lord, mighty and majestic is He. Ibn Abi Hatim related it.

{and denies al-husna}, that is, denies the recompense in the abode of the Hereafter.

{We will ease him toward hardship}, that is, toward the path of evil, just as Allah, exalted is He, said: {And We turn about their hearts and their sight just as they did not believe in it the first time, and We leave them wandering blindly in their transgression} [al-An'am: 11]. The verses to this effect are many, indicating that Allah, mighty and majestic is He, rewards the one who intends good with success (tawfiq) toward it, and the one who intends evil with abandonment (khidhlan). All of that is by a decreed measure (qadar), and the hadiths indicating this meaning are many:

The report of Abu Bakr al-Siddiq, may Allah be pleased with him: Imam Ahmad said: [chain] from Talha ibn 'Abdullah ibn 'Abd al-Rahman ibn Abi Bakr al-Siddiq, from his father, who said: "I heard my father mention that his father heard Abu Bakr saying: 'I said to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ: O Messenger of Allah, do we act according to what has already been decreed, or according to a matter newly begun?' He said: 'Rather, according to a matter that has already been decreed.'"

He said: "Then what is the point of deeds, O Messenger of Allah?" He said: "Everyone is eased toward that for which he was created."

The report of 'Ali, may Allah be pleased with him: al-Bukhari said: [chain] from 'Ali ibn Abi Talib, who said: "We were with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ at Baqi' al-Gharqad (a cemetery in Madinah) at a funeral, and he said: 'There is not one of you but that his seat in Paradise and his seat in the Fire have already been written.' They said: 'O Messenger of Allah, shall we not then rely [on that and abandon deeds]?' He said: 'Act, for everyone is eased toward that for which he was created.'" He said: Then he recited: {As for the one who gives and is God-fearing and believes in al-husna, We will ease him toward ease} up to His saying: {toward hardship}.

He likewise related it via the route of Shu'bah and Waki', from al-A'mash, with similar wording. Then he related it [chain] from 'Ali ibn Abi Talib, may Allah be pleased with him: "We were at a funeral at Baqi' al-Gharqad, and the Messenger of Allah ﷺ came and sat, and we sat around him. He had a short stick with him, and he lowered his head and began scratching the ground with his stick. Then he said: 'There is not one of you, or he said: there is no soul that is born, but that its place in Paradise and the Fire has been written, and that it has been written as wretched or blessed.'" A man said: "O Messenger of Allah, shall we not then rely [on that] and abandon deeds? For whoever among us is of the people of felicity will end up among the people of felicity, and whoever among us is of the people of wretchedness will end up among the people of wretchedness?" He said: "As for the people of felicity, they are eased toward the deeds of the people of felicity, and as for the people of wretchedness, they are eased toward the deeds of the people of wretchedness." Then he recited: {As for the one who gives and is God-fearing and believes in al-husna, We will ease him toward ease}, the verse.

The rest of al-Jama'ah (the group, i.e., the other compilers of the hadith collections) related it, through various routes, from Sa'd ibn 'Ubaydah, with it.

The report of 'Abdullah ibn 'Umar: Imam Ahmad said: [chain] from 'Asim ibn 'Ubaydillah, who said: "I heard Salim ibn 'Abdullah narrating from Ibn 'Umar: 'Umar said: 'O Messenger of Allah, tell me about what we do: is it in a matter already decreed, or newly begun, or newly invented?' He said: 'In what has already been decreed. So act, O son of al-Khattab, for indeed everyone is eased. As for the one who is among the people of felicity, he acts for felicity, and as for the one who is among the people of wretchedness, he acts for wretchedness.'"

Al-Tirmidhi related it in [the chapter on] al-Qadar, from Bundar, from Ibn Mahdi, with it, and he said: "Hasan sahih (a sound, authentic hadith)."

Another hadith, from the report of Jabir: Ibn Jarir said: [chain] from Jabir ibn 'Abdullah, that he said: "O Messenger of Allah, do we act for a matter already decreed, or for a matter we newly begin?" He said: "For a matter already decreed." Suraqah said: "Then what is the point of deeds?" The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Every doer is eased toward his deed."

Muslim related it from Abu al-Tahir, from Ibn Wahb, with it.

Another hadith: Ibn Jarir said: [chain] from Bashir ibn Ka'b al-'Adawi, who said: Two young men asked the Prophet ﷺ, saying: "O Messenger of Allah, do we act according to that for which the pens have dried and the decreed measures have run their course, or in something newly begun?" He said: "Rather, according to that for which the pens have dried and the decreed measures have run their course."

The two of them said, "Then what is the point of deeds, then?" He said, "Work, for every worker is enabled toward the deed for which he was created." The two of them said, "Now we shall strive and work."

The report of Abu al-Darda: Imam Ahmad said, [chain] from Abu al-Darda, who said: They said, "O Messenger of Allah ﷺ, tell us about what we do: is it a matter already settled, or something we are undertaking anew?" He said, "Rather, it is a matter already settled." They said, "Then what is the point of deeds, O Messenger of Allah ﷺ?" He said, "Every person is prepared for that for which he was created."

Ahmad alone related it by this route.

Another hadith: Ibn Jarir said, [chain] from Abu al-Darda, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said, "There is no day whose sun sets but that two angels are at its two sides, calling out in a voice that all of Allah's creation hears except al-thaqalayn (the two weighty ones, i.e., mankind and jinn): 'O Allah, grant the one who spends a replacement, and grant the one who withholds ruin.'" And Allah revealed the Qur'an concerning that: {As for the one who gives and is God-fearing and believes in the best reward, We shall ease him toward ease. But as for the one who withholds, considers himself self-sufficient, and denies the best reward, We shall ease him toward hardship}.

Ibn Abi Hatim also related it, from his father, from Ibn Abi Kabshah, with a chain like it.

Another hadith: Ibn Abi Hatim said, [chain] from Ikrimah, from Ibn Abbas: that a man had palm trees, among them a palm tree whose branch overhung into the house of a righteous, poor man with dependents. Whenever the man came and entered the house and took the fruit from his palm tree, some fruit would fall and the poor man's children would take it, so he would climb down from his palm tree and snatch the fruit from their hands, and if one of them had already put the fruit in his mouth, he would put his finger into the boy's throat and pull the fruit out of his throat. So the poor man complained of that to the Prophet ﷺ and told him what he suffered from the owner of the palm tree. The Prophet ﷺ said to him, "Go." And the Prophet ﷺ met the owner of the palm tree and said to him, "Give me your palm tree whose branch overhangs into so-and-so's house, and you shall have in exchange for it a palm tree in Paradise." He said to him, "I have indeed given it, but I am fond of its fruit, and I have many palm trees, none of which has fruit more pleasing to me than its fruit." So the Prophet ﷺ went away, and a man who had heard the conversation between the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and the owner of the palm tree followed him. The man said, "O Messenger of Allah, if I take the palm tree so that it becomes mine, and I give it, will you give me for it what you offered him: a palm tree in Paradise?" He said, "Yes." Then the man met the owner of the palm tree, both of them having palm trees, and said to him, "I tell you that Muhammad has offered me a palm tree in Paradise for my leaning palm tree in so-and-so's house, and I said to him, 'I have given it, but I am fond of its fruit.'" The man fell silent at that, then said to him, "Do you think, then, you would sell it?" He said, "No, unless I were given something for it, and I do not think I would be given it." He said, "And what would satisfy you for it?" He said, "Forty palm trees." The man said, "You have said something extraordinary! Your palm tree, and you ask forty palm trees for it?!" Then the two of them fell silent, and he began speaking of something else, then said, "I will give you forty palm trees." He said, "Bring me witnesses, if you are truthful." So he called for some people and summoned them, and said, "Bear witness that I have given him, from my own palm trees, forty palm trees for his palm tree whose branch overhangs into the house of so-and-so son of so-and-so." Then he said, "What do you say?" The owner of the palm tree said, "I am satisfied." Then afterward he said, "There is no binding sale between me and you so long as we have not parted." He said to him, "Allah has granted you release, but I am not foolish, having given you forty palm trees for your leaning palm tree." The owner of the palm tree said, "I am satisfied, provided you give me the forty as I wish." He said, "You are to give them to me already grown on the trunk (i.e., mature bearing trees, not young shoots)." Then he paused for a while, then said, "They are yours, already grown on the trunk," and he set up witnesses for him and counted out to him forty palm trees already grown on the trunk, and the two of them parted. Then the man went to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ and said, "O Messenger of Allah, the leaning palm tree in so-and-so's house has become mine, so it is yours." So the Messenger of Allah ﷺ went to the man who owned the house and said to him, "The palm tree is yours and your family's."

Ikrimah said: Ibn Abbas said: So Allah, Mighty and Majestic, revealed {By the night when it covers} up to His saying {As for the one who gives and is God-fearing and believes in the best reward, We shall ease him toward ease. But as for the one who withholds, considers himself self-sufficient, and denies the best reward, We shall ease him toward hardship} to the end of the surah.

Thus Ibn Abi Hatim related it, and it is a hadith that is extremely gharib (singular, rare in its chain of transmission).

Ibn Jarir said: And it is mentioned that this verse was revealed concerning Abu Bakr al-Siddiq, may Allah be pleased with him. [chain] from Amir ibn Abdullah ibn al-Zubayr, who said: Abu Bakr used to emancipate slaves for the sake of Islam in Makkah, freeing old women and women whenever they embraced Islam. His father said to him, "O my son, I see you freeing weak people. If only you would free strong men who could stand with you, protect you, and defend you!" He said, "O my father, I only desire," he thinks he said, "what is with Allah." He said: Some of the people of my household told me that this verse was revealed concerning him: {As for the one who gives and is God-fearing and believes in the best reward, We shall ease him toward ease}.

* *

And His saying, {And what will his wealth avail him when he perishes}: Mujahid said, that is, when he dies. Abu Salih said, and Malik reported from Zayd ibn Aslam: when he falls into the Fire.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

* *

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا يَزِيدُ بْنُ هَارُونَ، حَدَّثَنَا شُعْبَةُ، عَنْ مُغِيرَةَ، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، عَنْ عَلْقَمَةَ: أَنَّهُ قَدِمَ الشَّامَ فَدَخَلَ مَسْجِدَ دِمَشْقَ، فَصَلَّى فِيهِ رَكْعَتَيْنِ وَقَالَ: اللَّهُمَّ، ارْزُقْنِي جَلِيسًا صَالِحًا. قَالَ: فَجَلَسَ إِلَى أَبِي الدَّرْدَاءِ، فَقَالَ لَهُ أَبُو الدَّرْدَاءِ: مِمَّنْ أَنْتَ؟ قَالَ: مِنْ أَهْلِ الْكُوفَةِ. قَالَ: كَيْفَ سَمِعْتَ ابْنَ أُمِّ عَبْدٍ يَقْرَأُ: ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَغْشَى وَالنَّهَارِ إِذَا تَجَلَّى﴾ ؟ قَالَ عَلْقَمَةُ: "وَالذَّكَرِ وَالْأُنْثَى". فَقَالَ أَبُو الدَّرْدَاءِ: لَقَدْ سَمِعْتُهَا مِنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ، فَمَا زَالَ هَؤُلَاءِ حَتَّى شَكَّكُونِي. ثُمَّ قَالَ: ثُمَّ أَلَمْ يَكُنْ فِيكُمْ صَاحِبُ الْوِسَادِ وَصَاحِبُ السِّرِّ الَّذِي لَا يَعْلَمُهُ أَحَدٌ غَيْرُهُ، وَالَّذِي أُجِيرَ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ عَلَى لِسَانِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺ؟ .

وَقَدْ رَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ هَاهُنَا وَمُسْلِمٌ، مِنْ طَرِيقِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ قَالَ: قَدِمَ أَصْحَابُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ عَلَى أَبِي الدَّرْدَاءِ، فَطَلَبَهُمْ فَوَجَدَهُمْ، فَقَالَ: أَيُّكُمْ يَقْرَأُ عَلَيَّ قِرَاءَةَ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ؟ قَالُوا: كُلُّنَا، قَالَ: أَيُّكُمْ أَحْفَظُ؟ فَأَشَارُوا إِلَى عَلْقَمَةَ، فَقَالَ: كَيْفَ سَمِعْتَهُ يَقْرَأُ: ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَغْشَى﴾ ؟ قَالَ: "وَالذَّكَرِ وَالْأُنْثَى". قَالَ: أَشْهَدُ إِنِّي سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقْرَأُ هَكَذَا، وَهَؤُلَاءِ يُرِيدُونِي أَنْ أَقْرَأَ: ﴿وَمَا خَلَقَ الذَّكَرَ وَالأنْثَى﴾ وَاللَّهِ لَا أُتَابِعُهُمْ .

هَذَا لَفْظُ الْبُخَارِيِّ: هَكَذَا قَرَأَ ذَلِكَ ابْنُ مَسْعُودٍ، وَأَبُو الدَّرْدَاءِ -وَرَفَعَهُ أَبُو الدَّرْدَاءِ-وَأَمَّا الْجُمْهُورُ فَقَرَأُوا ذَلِكَ كَمَا هُوَ مُثبَت فِي الْمُصْحَفِ الْإِمَامُ الْعُثْمَانِيُّ فِي سائر الآفاق: ﴿وَمَا خَلَقَ الذَّكَرَ وَالأنْثَى﴾

فَأَقْسَمَ تَعَالَى بِـ ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَغْشَى﴾ أَيْ: إِذَا غَشيَ الخليقةَ بِظَلَامِهِ، ﴿وَالنَّهَارِ إِذَا تَجَلَّى﴾ أَيْ: بِضِيَائِهِ وَإِشْرَاقِهِ، ﴿وَمَا خَلَقَ الذَّكَرَ وَالأنْثَى﴾ كَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَخَلَقْنَاكُمْ أَزْوَاجًا﴾ [النَّبَأِ: ٨] ، وَكَقَوْلِهِ: ﴿وَمِنْ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ خَلَقْنَا زَوْجَيْنِ﴾ [الذَّارِيَاتِ: ٤٩] .

وَلَمَّا كَانَ الْقَسَمُ بِهَذِهِ الْأَشْيَاءِ الْمُتَضَادَّةِ كَانَ الْقَسَمُ عَلَيْهِ أَيْضًا مُتَضَادًّا؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿إِنَّ سَعْيَكُمْ لَشَتَّى﴾ أَيْ: أَعْمَالُ الْعِبَادِ الَّتِي اكْتَسَبُوهَا مُتَضَادَّةٌ أَيْضًا وَمُتَخَالِفَةٌ، فَمِنْ فَاعِلٍ خَيْرًا وَمِنْ فَاعِلٍ شَرًّا.

قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿فَأَمَّا مَنْ أَعْطَى وَاتَّقَى﴾ أَيْ: أَعْطَى مَا أُمِرَ بِإِخْرَاجِهِ، وَاتَّقَى اللَّهَ فِي أُمُورِهِ، ﴿وَصَدَّقَ بِالْحُسْنَى﴾ أَيْ: بِالْمُجَازَاةِ عَلَى ذَلِكَ -قَالَهُ قَتَادَةُ، وَقَالَ خَصِيف: بِالثَّوَابِ. وَقَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ، وَمُجَاهِدٌ، وَعِكْرِمَةُ، وَأَبُو صَالِحٍ، وَزَيْدُ بْنُ أَسْلَمَ: ﴿وَصَدَّقَ بِالْحُسْنَى﴾ أَيْ: بِالْخَلَفِ. وَقَالَ أَبُو عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ السُّلَمِيُّ، وَالضَّحَّاكُ: ﴿وَصَدَّقَ بِالْحُسْنَى﴾ أَيْ: بِلَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ. وَفِي رِوَايَةٍ عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ: ﴿وَصَدَّقَ بِالْحُسْنَى﴾ أَيْ: بِمَا أَنْعَمَ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ. وَفِي رِوَايَةٍ عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ: ﴿وَصَدَّقَ بِالْحُسْنَى﴾ قَالَ: الصَّلَاةُ وَالزَّكَاةُ وَالصَّوْمُ. وَقَالَ مَرَّةٍ: وَصَدَقَةُ الْفِطْرِ.

وَقَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو زُرْعَة، حَدَّثَنَا صَفْوَانُ بْنُ صَالِحٍ الدِّمَشْقِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا الْوَلِيدُ بْنُ مُسْلِمٍ، حَدَّثَنَا زُهَير بْنُ مُحَمَّدٍ، حَدَّثَنِي مَن سَمِع أَبَا الْعَالِيَةِ الرِّيَاحِيَّ يُحدث عَنْ أُبَيِّ بْنِ كَعْبٍ قَالَ: سَأَلْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وسلم عَنِ الْحُسْنَى قَالَ: "الْحُسْنَى: الْجَنَّةُ" .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَسَنُيَسِّرُهُ لِلْيُسْرَى﴾ قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: يَعْنِي لِلْخَيْرِ. وَقَالَ زَيْدُ بْنُ أَسْلَمَ: يَعْنِي لِلْجَنَّةِ.

وَقَالَ بَعْضُ السَّلَفِ: مِنْ ثَوَابِ الحسنةُ الْحَسَنَةَ بَعْدَهَا، وَمِنْ جَزَاءِ السَّيِّئَةِ السيئةُ بَعْدَهَا؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَأَمَّا مَنْ بَخِلَ﴾ أَيْ: بِمَا عِنْدَهُ، ﴿وَاسْتَغْنَى﴾ قَالَ عِكْرِمَةُ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ: أَيْ بَخِلَ بِمَالِهِ، وَاسْتَغْنَى عَنْ رَبِّهِ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ. رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ.

﴿وَكَذَّبَ بِالْحُسْنَى﴾ أَيْ: بِالْجَزَاءِ فِي الدَّارِ الْآخِرَةِ.

﴿فَسَنُيَسِّرُهُ لِلْعُسْرَى﴾ أَيْ: لِطَرِيقِ الشَّرِّ، كَمَا قَالَ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَنُقَلِّبُ أَفْئِدَتَهُمْ وَأَبْصَارَهُمْ كَمَا لَمْ يُؤْمِنُوا بِهِ أَوَّلَ مَرَّةٍ وَنَذَرُهُمْ فِي طُغْيَانِهِمْ يَعْمَهُونَ﴾ [الْأَنْعَامِ: ١١] ، وَالْآيَاتُ فِي هَذَا الْمَعْنَى كَثِيرَةٌ دَالَّةٌ عَلَى أَنَّ اللَّهَ، عَزَّ وَجَلَّ، يُجازي مَنْ قَصَدَ الْخَيْرَ بِالتَّوْفِيقِ لَهُ، وَمَنْ قَصَدَ الشَّرَّ بِالْخِذْلَانِ. وَكُلُّ ذَلِكَ بِقَدَرٍ مُقدّر، وَالْأَحَادِيثُ الدَّالَّةُ عَلَى هَذَا الْمَعْنَى كَثِيرَةٌ:

رِوَايَةُ أَبِي بَكْرٍ الصِّدِيقِ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ: قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَلِيُّ بْنُ عَيَّاش، حَدَّثَنِي الْعَطَّافُ بْنُ خَالِدٍ، حَدَّثَنِي رَجُلٌ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْبَصْرَةِ، عَنْ طَلْحَةَ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ أَبِي بَكْرٍ الصِّدِّيقِ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ قَالَ: سَمِعْتُ أَبِي يَذْكُرُ أَنَّ أَبَاهُ سَمِعَ أَبَا بَكْرٍ وَهُوَ يَقُولُ: قُلْتُ لِرَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، أَنَعْمَلُ عَلَى مَا فُرِغَ مِنْهُ أَوْ عَلَى أَمْرٍ مُؤْتَنِفٍ؟ قَالَ: "بَلْ عَلَى أمر قد فُرغ منه".

قَالَ: فَفِيمَ العملُ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ؟ قَالَ: "كُلٌّ مُيَسَّرٌ لِمَا خُلِقَ لَهُ" .

رِوَايَةُ عَلِيٍّ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ: قَالَ الْبُخَارِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو نُعَيْمٍ: حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، عَنْ سَعْدِ بْنِ عُبَيْدَةَ، عَنْ أَبِي عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ السُّلَمِيِّ، عَنْ عَلِيِّ بْنِ أَبِي طَالِبٍ قَالَ: كُنَّا مَعَ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فِي بَقِيع الغَرْقَد فِي جِنَازَةٍ، فَقَالَ: "مَا مِنْكُمْ مِنْ أَحَدٍ إِلَّا وَقَدْ كُتب مَقْعَدُهُ مِنَ الْجَنَّةِ وَمَقْعَدُهُ مِنَ النَّارِ". فَقَالُوا: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، أَفَلَا نَتَّكِلُ؟ فَقَالَ: "اعْمَلُوا، فَكُلٌّ مُيَسَّرٌ لِمَا خُلِقَ لَهُ". قَالَ: ثُمَّ قَرَأَ: ﴿فَأَمَّا مَنْ أَعْطَى وَاتَّقَى وَصَدَّقَ بِالْحُسْنَى فَسَنُيَسِّرُهُ لِلْيُسْرَى﴾ إِلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿لِلْعُسْرَى﴾ .

وَكَذَا رَوَاهُ مِنْ طَرِيقِ شُعْبَةَ ووَكِيع، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشِ، بِنَحْوِهِ ثُمَّ رَوَاهُ عَنْ عُثْمَانَ بْنِ أَبِي شَيْبَةَ، عَنْ جَرِيرٍ، عَنْ مَنْصُورٍ، عَنْ سَعْدِ بْنِ عُبَيْدَةَ عَنْ أَبِي عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ، عَنْ عَلِيِّ بْنِ أَبِي طَالِبٍ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ: كُنَّا فِي جِنَازَةٍ فِي بَقِيعِ الْغَرْقَدِ، فَأَتَى رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَعَدَ وَقَعَدْنَا حَوْلَهُ، وَمَعَهُ مخْصَرَةٌ فَنَكَسَ فَجَعَلَ ينكُت بِمِخْصَرَتِهِ، ثُمَّ قَالَ: "مَا مِنْكُمْ مِنْ أَحَدٍ -أَوْ: مَا مِنْ نَفْسٍ مَنْفُوسَةٍ إِلَّا كُتِبَ مَكَانُهَا مِنَ الْجَنَّةِ وَالنَّارِ، وَإِلَّا قَدْ كُتِبَتْ شَقِيَّةٌ أَوْ سَعِيدَةٌ". فَقَالَ رَجُلٌ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، أَفَلَا نَتَّكِلُ وَنَدَعُ الْعَمَلَ؟ فَمَنْ كَانَ مِنَّا مَنْ أَهْلِ السَّعَادَةِ فَسَيَصِيرُ إِلَى أَهْلِ السَّعَادَةِ، وَمَنْ كَانَ مِنَّا مَنْ أَهْلِ الشَّقَاءِ فَسَيَصِيرُ إِلَى أَهْلِ الشَّقَاءِ؟ فَقَالَ: "أَمَّا أَهْلُ السَّعَادَةِ فَيُيَسَّرُونَ لِعَمَلِ أَهْلِ السَّعَادَةِ، وَأَمَّا أَهْلُ الشَّقَاءِ فَيُيَسَّرُونَ إِلَى عَمَلِ أَهْلِ الشَّقَاءِ". ثُمَّ قَرَأَ: ﴿فَأَمَّا مَنْ أَعْطَى وَاتَّقَى وَصَدَّقَ بِالْحُسْنَى فَسَنُيَسِّرُهُ لِلْيُسْرَى﴾ الْآيَةَ .

وَقَدْ أَخْرَجَهُ بَقِيَّةُ الْجَمَاعَةِ، مِنْ طُرُقٍ، عَنْ سَعْدِ بْنِ عُبَيْدَةَ، بِهِ .

رِوَايَةُ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ عُمَرَ: وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ، حَدَّثَنَا شُعْبَةُ عَنْ عَاصِمِ بْنِ عُبَيْدِ اللَّهِ قَالَ: سمعتُ سَالِمَ بنَ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ يُحدث عَنِ ابْنِ عُمَر: قَالَ: قَالَ عُمَرُ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، أَرَأَيْتَ مَا نَعْمَلُ فِيهِ؟ أَفِي أَمْرٍ قَدْ فُرغ أَوْ مُبْتَدَأٍ أَوْ مُبْتَدَعٍ؟ قَالَ: " فِيمَا قَدْ فُرغَ مِنْهُ، فَاعْمَلْ يَا ابْنَ الْخَطَّابِ، فَإِنَّ كُلا مُيَسَّر، أَمَّا مَنْ كَانَ مِنْ أَهْلِ السَّعَادَةِ فَإِنَّهُ يَعْمَلُ لِلسَّعَادَةِ، وَأَمَّا مَنْ كَانَ مِنْ أَهْلِ الشَّقَاءِ فَإِنَّهُ يَعْمَلُ لِلشَّقَاءِ".

وَرَوَاهُ التِّرْمِذِيُّ فِي الْقَدْرِ، عَنْ بُنْدَارٍ، عَنِ ابْنِ مَهْدِي، بِهِ وَقَالَ: حَسَنٌ صَحِيحٌ.

حَدِيثٌ آخَرُ مِنْ رِوَايَةِ جَابِرٍ: قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنِي يُونُسُ، أَخْبَرَنَا ابْنُ وَهْبٍ، أخبرني عمرو ابن الْحَارِثِ، عَنْ أَبِي الزُّبَيْرِ، عَنْ جَابِرِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ أَنَّهُ قَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، أنعمل لأمر قد فرغ مِنْهُ، أَوْ لِأَمْرٍ نَسْتَأْنِفُهُ؟ فَقَالَ: "لِأَمْرٍ قَدْ فُرِغَ مِنْهُ". فَقَالَ سُرَاقَةُ: فَفِيمَ الْعَمَلُ إِذًا؟ فَقَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "كُلُ عَامِلٍ مُيَسَّر لِعَمَلِهِ".

وَرَوَاهُ مُسْلِمٌ عَنْ أَبِي الطَّاهِرِ، عَنِ ابْنِ وَهْبٍ، بِهِ .

حَدِيثٌ آخَرُ: قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنِي يُونُسُ، حَدَّثَنَا سُفْيَانُ، عَنْ عَمْرِو بْنِ دِينَارٍ، عَنِ طَلْقِ ابن حَبِيبٍ، عَنْ بَشِيرِ بْنِ كَعْبٍ الْعَدَوِيِّ قَالَ: سَأَلَ غُلَامَانِ شَابَّانِ النَّبِيَّ ﷺ فَقَالَا يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، أَنَعْمَلُ فِيمَا جَفَّت بِهِ الْأَقْلَامُ وجَرَتْ بِهِ الْمَقَادِيرُ، أَوْ فِي شَيْءٍ يُسْتَأْنَفُ؟ فَقَالَ: "بَلْ فِيمَا جَفَّتْ بِهِ الْأَقْلَامُ، وَجَرَتْ بِهِ الْمَقَادِيرُ". قَالَا فَفِيمَ الْعَمَلُ إِذًا؟ قَالَ: "اعْمَلُوا فَكُلُ عَامِلٍ مُيَسَّرٌ لِعَمَلِهِ الَّذِي خُلِقَ لَهُ". قَالَا فَالْآنَ نَجِدُّ وَنَعْمَلُ .

رِوَايَةُ أَبِي الدَّرْدَاءِ: قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا هَيْثَم بْنُ خَارِجَةَ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو الرَّبِيعِ سُلَيْمَانُ بْنُ عُتْبَةَ السُّلَمِيُّ، عَنْ يُونُسَ بْنِ مَيْسَرَةَ بْنِ حَلْبس، عَنْ أَبِي إِدْرِيسَ، عَنْ أَبِي الدَّرْدَاءِ قَالَ: قَالُوا: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، أَرَأَيْتَ مَا نَعْمَلُ، أَمْرٌ قَدْ فُرغ مِنْهُ أَمْ شَيْءٌ نَسْتَأْنِفُهُ؟ قَالَ: "بَلْ أَمْرٌ قَدْ فُرِغَ مِنْهُ". قَالُوا: فَكَيْفَ بِالْعَمَلِ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ؟ قَالَ: "كُلُّ امْرِئٍ مُهَيَّأٌ لِمَا خُلِقَ لَهُ" .

تَفَرَّدَ بِهِ أَحْمَدُ مِنْ هَذَا الْوَجْهِ.

حَدِيثٌ آخَرُ: قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: حَدَّثَنِي الْحَسَنُ بْنُ سَلَمَةَ بْنِ أَبِي كَبْشَة، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الْمَلِكِ بْنُ عَمْرٍو، حَدَّثَنَا عَبَّادُ بْنُ رَاشِدٍ، عَنْ قَتَادَةُ، حَدَّثَنِي خُلَيد الْعَصَرِيُّ، عَنْ أَبِي الدَّرْدَاءِ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "مَا مِنْ يَوْمٍ غَرَبَتْ فِيهِ شَمْسُهُ إِلَّا وبجَنْبَتَيْهَا مَلَكَانِ يُنَادِيَانِ بِصَوْتٍ يَسْمَعُهُ خَلْقُ اللَّهِ كُلُّهُمْ إِلَّا الثَّقَلَيْنِ: اللَّهُمَّ أَعْطِ مُنْفِقًا خَلَفًا، وَأَعْطِ مُمْسِكًا تَلَفًا". وَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ فِي ذَلِكَ الْقُرْآنَ: ﴿فَأَمَّا مَنْ أَعْطَى وَاتَّقَى وَصَدَّقَ بِالْحُسْنَى فَسَنُيَسِّرُهُ لِلْيُسْرَى وَأَمَّا مَنْ بَخِلَ وَاسْتَغْنَى وَكَذَّبَ بِالْحُسْنَى فَسَنُيَسِّرُهُ لِلْعُسْرَى﴾ .

وَرَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، عَنْ أَبِيهِ، عَنْ ابْنِ أَبِي كَبْشَةَ، بِإِسْنَادِهِ مِثْلِهِ.

حَدِيثٌ آخَرُ: قَالَ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ: حَدَّثَنِي أَبُو عَبْدِ اللَّهِ الطِّهْرَانِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا حَفْصُ بْنُ عُمَر العَدَاني، حَدَّثَنَا الْحَكَمُ بْنُ أَبَانٍ عَنْ عِكْرِمَةَ، عَنِ ابْنِ عَبَّاسٍ؛ أَنَّ رَجُلًا كَانَ لَهُ نَخْلٌ، وَمِنْهَا نَخْلَةٌ فَرْعُهَا إِلَى دَارِ رَجُلٍ صَالِحٍ فَقِيرٍ ذِي عِيَالٍ، فَإِذَا جَاءَ الرَّجُلُ فَدَخَلَ دَارَهُ وَأَخَذَ الثَّمَرَ مَنْ نَخْلَتِهِ، فَتَسْقُطُ الثَّمَرَةُ فَيَأْخُذُهَا صِبْيَانُ الْفَقِيرِ فَنَزَلَ مِنْ نَخْلَتِهِ فَنزع الثَّمَرَةَ مِنْ أيديهم، وإن أدخل أحدهم الثَّمَرَةَ فِي فَمِهِ أَدْخَلَ أُصْبُعَهُ فِي حَلْقِ الْغُلَامِ وَنَزَعَ الثَّمَرَةَ مَنْ حَلْقِهِ. فَشَكَا ذَلِكَ الرجلُ إِلَى النَّبِيِّ ﷺ، وَأَخْبَرَهُ بِمَا هُوَ فِيهِ مِنْ صَاحِبِ النَّخْلَةِ، فَقَالَ لَهُ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: "اذْهَبْ". وَلَقِيَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ صاحب النخلة، فَقَالَ لَهُ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: "أَعْطِنِي نَخْلَتَكَ الَّتِي فَرْعُهَا فِي دَارِ فُلَانٍ وَلَكَ بِهَا نَخْلَةٌ فِي الْجَنَّةِ" فَقَالَ لَهُ: لَقَدْ أَعْطَيْتُ، وَلَكِنْ يُعْجِبُنِي ثَمَرُهَا، وَإِنَّ لِي لَنَخْلًا كَثِيرًا مَا فِيهَا نَخْلَةٌ أَعْجَبُ إِلَيَّ ثَمَرَةً مِنْ ثَمَرِهَا. فَذَهَبَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ فَتَبِعَهُ رَجُلٌ كَانَ يَسْمَعُ الْكَلَامَ مِنْ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ وَمِنْ صَاحِبِ النَّخْلَةِ. فَقَالَ الرَّجُلُ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، إِنْ أَنَا أَخَذْتُ النَّخْلَةَ فَصَارَتْ لِي النَّخْلَةُ فَأَعْطَيْتُهَا أَتُعْطِينِي بِهَا مَا أَعْطَيْتَهُ بِهَا نَخْلَةً فِي الْجَنَّةِ؟ قَالَ: "نَعَمْ". ثُمَّ إِنَّ الرَّجُلَ لَقِيَ صَاحِبَ النَّخْلَةِ، وَلِكِلَاهُمَا نَخْلٌ، فَقَالَ لَهُ: أُخْبِرُكَ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا، [قَدْ] أَعْطَانِي بِنَخْلَتِي الْمَائِلَةِ فِي دَارِ فُلَانٍ نَخْلَةً فِي الْجَنَّةِ، فَقُلْتُ، لَهُ: قَدْ أعطيتُ وَلَكِنْ يُعْجِبُنِي ثَمَرُهَا. فَسَكَتَ عَنْهُ الرجلُ، فَقَالَ لَهُ: أتُراك إِذَا بِعْتَهَا؟ قَالَ: لَا إِلَّا أَنْ أُعْطَى بِهَا شَيْئًا، وَلَا أَظُنُّنِي أُعْطَاهُ. قَالَ: وَمَا مُنَاكَ بِهَا ؟ قَالَ: أَرْبَعُونَ نَخْلَةً. فَقَالَ الرَّجُلُ: لَقَدْ جئتَ بِأَمْرٍ عَظِيمٍ، نَخْلَتُكَ تَطْلُبُ بِهَا أَرْبَعِينَ نَخْلَةً؟! ثُمَّ سَكَتَا وَأَنْشَأَ فِي كَلَامٍ [آخَرَ] ثُمَّ قَالَ: أَنَا أَعْطَيْتُكَ أَرْبَعِينَ نَخْلَةً، فَقَالَ: أَشْهِدْ لِي إِنْ كُنْتَ صَادِقًا. فَأَمَرَ بِأُنَاسٍ فَدَعَاهُمْ فَقَالَ: اشْهَدُوا أَنِّي قَدْ أَعْطَيْتُهُ مِنْ نَخْلِي أَرْبَعِينَ نَخْلَةً بِنَخْلَتِهِ الَّتِي فَرْعُهَا فِي دَارِ فُلَانِ ابْنِ فُلَانٍ. ثُمَّ قَالَ: مَا تَقَوُّلُ؟ فَقَالَ صَاحِبُ النَّخْلَةِ: قَدْ رَضِيتُ. ثُمَّ قَالَ بعدُ: لَيْسَ بَيْنِي وَبَيْنَكَ بَيْعٌ لَمْ نَفْتَرِقْ قَالَ لَهُ: قَدْ أَقَالَكَ اللَّهُ، وَلَسْتُ بِأَحْمَقَ حِينَ أَعْطَيْتُكَ أَرْبَعِينَ نَخْلَةً بِنَخْلَتِكَ الْمَائِلَةِ. فَقَالَ صَاحِبُ النَّخْلَةِ: قَدْ رضيتُ عَلَى أَنْ تُعْطِيَنِي الْأَرْبَعِينَ عَلَى مَا أُرِيدُ. قَالَ: تُعْطِينِيهَا عَلَى سَاقٍ. ثُمَّ مَكَثَ سَاعَةً، ثُمَّ قَالَ: هِيَ لَكَ عَلَى سَاقٍ وَأَوْقَفَ لَهُ شُهُودًا وَعَدَّ لَهُ أَرْبَعِينَ نَخْلَةً عَلَى سَاقٍ، فَتَفَرَّقَا، فَذَهَبَ الرَّجُلُ إِلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ ﷺ فَقَالَ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، إِنَّ النَّخْلَةَ الْمَائِلَةَ فِي دَارِ فُلَانٍ قَدْ صَارَتْ لِي، فَهِيَ لَكَ. فَذَهَبَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ إِلَى الرَّجُلِ صَاحِبِ الدَّارِ فَقَالَ لَهُ: "النَّخْلَةُ لَكَ وَلِعِيَالِكَ". قَالَ عِكْرِمَةُ: قَالَ ابْنُ عَبَّاسٍ: فَأَنْزَلَ اللَّهُ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ: ﴿وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَغْشَى﴾ إِلَى قَوْلِهِ: ﴿فَأَمَّا مَنْ أَعْطَى وَاتَّقَى وَصَدَّقَ بِالْحُسْنَى فَسَنُيَسِّرُهُ لِلْيُسْرَى وَأَمَّا مَنْ بَخِلَ وَاسْتَغْنَى وَكَذَّبَ بِالْحُسْنَى فَسَنُيَسِّرُهُ لِلْعُسْرَى﴾ إِلَى آخِرِ السُّورَةِ .

هَكَذَا رَوَاهُ ابْنُ أَبِي حَاتِمٍ، وَهُوَ حَدِيثٌ غَرِيبٌ جِدًّا.

قَالَ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ: وَذُكِرَ أَنَّ هَذِهِ الْآيَةَ نَزَلَتْ فِي أَبِي بَكْرٍ الصِّدِّيقِ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عنه: حدثني هارون ابن إِدْرِيسَ الْأَصَمُّ، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنُ مُحَمَّدٍ الْمُحَارِبِيُّ، حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ إِسْحَاقَ، عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ ابن عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ بْنِ أَبِي بَكْرٍ الصِّدِّيقِ، عَنْ عَامِرِ بْنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ الزُّبَيْرِ قَالَ: كَانَ أَبُو بَكْرٍ يُعْتِقُ عَلَى الْإِسْلَامِ بِمَكَّةَ، فَكَانَ يُعْتِقُ عَجَائِزَ وَنِسَاءً إِذَا أَسْلَمْنَ، فَقَالَ لَهُ أَبُوهُ: أَيْ بُنَيَّ، أَرَاكَ تَعْتِقُ أُنَاسًا ضُعَفَاءَ، فَلَوْ أَنَّكَ تَعْتِقُ رِجَالًا جُلَداء يَقُومُونَ مَعَكَ وَيَمْنَعُونَكَ وَيَدْفَعُونَ عَنْكَ؟! فَقَالَ: أيْ أبَت، إِنَّمَا أُرِيدُ -أَظُنُّهُ قَالَ -مَا عِنْدَ اللَّهِ: قَالَ: فَحَدَّثَنِي بعض أهل بيتي أن هذه الآية أُنْزِلَتْ فِيهِ: ﴿فَأَمَّا مَنْ أَعْطَى وَاتَّقَى وَصَدَّقَ بِالْحُسْنَى فَسَنُيَسِّرُهُ لِلْيُسْرَى﴾ .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَمَا يُغْنِي عَنْهُ مَالُهُ إِذَا تَرَدَّى﴾ قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: أَيْ إِذَا مَاتَ. وَقَالَ أَبُو صَالِحٍ، وَمَالِكٌ عَنْ زَيْدِ بْنِ أَسْلَمَ: إِذَا تَرَدَّى فِي النَّارِ.

## 92:12-21 — verses 12-21 (shared commentary)

### Translation

Qatadah said, regarding {Indeed, upon Us is the guidance}: meaning, We make clear the lawful and the unlawful. Others said: whoever follows the path of guidance reaches Allah. And he likened it to His saying, the Exalted: {And upon Allah is the direction of the way} [al-Nahl: 9]. Ibn Jarir related this.

* *

And His saying: {And indeed, to Us belong the Hereafter and the first (life)}: meaning, all of it is Our possession, and I am the One who disposes over both of them.

* *

And His saying: {So I have warned you of a Fire that blazes}: Mujahid said: meaning, it flares up.

Imam Ahmad said: Muhammad ibn Ja'far narrated to us, Shu'bah narrated to us, from Simak ibn Harb: I heard al-Nu'man ibn Bashir delivering a sermon, saying: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ delivering a sermon, saying: "I warn you of the Fire, [I have warned you of the Fire, I have warned you of the Fire], until if a man were in the marketplace he would hear it from where I am standing now." He said: until a khamisah (a marked woolen cloak) that was upon his shoulder fell down to his feet.

And Imam Ahmad said: Muhammad ibn Ja'far narrated to us, Shu'bah narrated to us, Abu Ishaq narrated to me: I heard al-Nu'man ibn Bashir delivering a sermon, saying: I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say: "Indeed, the lightest in punishment among the people of the Fire on the Day of Resurrection is a man beneath the arch of whose two feet two live embers are placed, from which his brain boils."

Al-Bukhari related it.

And Muslim said: Abu Bakr ibn Abi Shaybah narrated to us, Abu Usamah narrated to us, from al-A'mash, from Abu Ishaq, from al-Nu'man ibn Bashir, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Indeed, the lightest in punishment among the people of the Fire is one who has two sandals and two straps of fire, from which his brain boils just as a cooking pot boils. He does not think that anyone's punishment is more severe than his, yet his is the lightest of all their punishments."

* *

And His saying: {None will burn therein except the most wretched}: meaning, none will enter it with an entry that encompasses him from every side except the most wretched. Then He explained it, saying: {who denies}, meaning, with his heart, {and turns away}, meaning, from acting with his limbs and members.

Imam Ahmad said: Hasan ibn Musa narrated to us, Ibn Lahi'ah narrated to us, Abd Rabbih ibn Sa'id narrated to us, from al-Maqburi, from Abu Hurayrah, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "None enters the Fire except a wretched one." It was said: And who is the wretched one? He said: "The one who does not act in obedience, and does not abandon disobedience for the sake of Allah."

And Imam Ahmad said: Yunus and Surayj narrated to us, they said: Fulayh narrated to us, from Hilal ibn Ali, from Ata' ibn Yasar, from Abu Hurayrah, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "All of my nation will enter Paradise on the Day of Resurrection, except whoever refuses." They said: And who refuses, O Messenger of Allah? He said: "Whoever obeys me enters Paradise, and whoever disobeys me has refused."

Al-Bukhari related it from Muhammad ibn Sinan, from Fulayh, with the same chain.

* *

And His saying: {And it will be kept away from the most righteous}: meaning, the pious, the pure, the most righteous one will be moved away from the Fire.

Then He explained it by His saying: {who gives his wealth to purify himself}: meaning, he spends his wealth in obedience to his Lord, in order to purify himself, his wealth, and what Allah has granted him of religion and worldly good.

{And no one has for him any favor to be repaid}: meaning, his giving is not in repayment of someone who did him a kindness, such that he gives in exchange for that. Rather, what drove him to that was only {seeking the Face of his Lord, the Most High}: meaning, out of longing that he might attain the sight of Him in the Hereafter, in the meadows of the gardens of Paradise. Allah, the Exalted, said: {And he is going to be pleased}: meaning, whoever is characterized by these qualities will indeed be pleased.

More than one of the exegetes have mentioned that these verses were revealed concerning Abu Bakr al-Siddiq, may Allah be pleased with him, to the point that some of them related that there is consensus (ijma) among the exegetes on that. There is no doubt that he is included within them, and that he is the most deserving of the ummah (community) of their general application, for their wording is a wording of generality, namely His saying, the Exalted: {And it will be kept away from the most righteous, who gives his wealth to purify himself, and no one has for him any favor to be repaid}. But he is the foremost of the ummah and the one who preceded them in all of these qualities and all the other praiseworthy qualities. For he was truthful (siddiq), pious, noble, generous, one who spent freely of his wealth in obedience to his Master and in support of the Messenger of Allah. How many dirhams and dinars did he give away seeking the Face of his generous Lord, while no one among the people had any favor owed to him that he needed to repay. Rather, his bounty and kindness fell upon the chiefs and leaders of the various tribes. That is why Urwah ibn Mas'ud (the chief of Thaqif) said to him on the day of the truce of al-Hudaybiyah: "By Allah, were it not for a favor of yours that is upon me, which I have not repaid you for, I would have answered you." For al-Siddiq had spoken harshly to him in that exchange. So if this was his standing with the chiefs of the Arabs and the leaders of the tribes, then how much more so with those beneath them? That is why He said: {And no one has for him any favor to be repaid, except seeking the Face of his Lord, the Most High, and he is going to be pleased}. And it is related in the two Sahihs that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "Whoever spends a pair of anything in the way of Allah, the keepers of Paradise will call out to him: 'O servant of Allah, this is good.'" Abu Bakr said: "O Messenger of Allah, there is no hardship upon one who is called from it, but will anyone be called from all of its gates?" He said: "Yes, and I hope that you will be among them."

This is the end of the tafsir of Surat al-Layl, and to Allah belongs all praise and grace.

قَالَ قَتَادَةُ: ﴿إِنَّ عَلَيْنَا لَلْهُدَى﴾ أَيْ: نُبَيِّنُ الحلالَ والحرامَ. وَقَالَ غَيْرُهُ: مَنْ سَلك طَرِيقَ الْهُدَى وَصَل إِلَى اللَّهِ. وَجَعَلَهُ كَقَوْلِهِ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَعَلَى اللَّهِ قَصْدُ السَّبِيلِ﴾ [النَّحْلِ: ٩] . حَكَاهُ ابْنُ جَرِيرٍ.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَإِنَّ لَنَا لَلآخِرَةَ وَالأولَى﴾ أَيِ: الْجَمِيعُ مِلْكُنَا وَأَنَا الْمُتَصَرِّفُ فِيهِمَا.

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿فَأَنْذَرْتُكُمْ نَارًا تَلَظَّى﴾ قَالَ مُجَاهِدٌ: أَيْ تَوَهَّجُ.

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ جَعْفَرٍ، حدثنا شعبة، عن سِماك بْنِ حَرْبٍ، سمعتُ النُّعْمَانَ بْنَ بَشِيرٍ يَخْطُبُ يَقُولُ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَخْطُبُ يَقُولُ: "أُنْذِرُكُمُ النَّارَ [أَنْذَرْتُكُمُ النَّارَ، أَنْذَرْتُكُمُ النَّارَ] حَتَّى لَوْ أَنَّ رَجُلًا كَانَ بِالسُّوقِ لَسَمِعَهُ مِنْ مَقَامِي هَذَا. قَالَ: حَتَّى وَقَعَتْ خَميصة كَانَتْ عَلَى عَاتِقِهِ عِنْدَ رِجْلَيْهِ .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا مُحَمَّدُ بْنُ جَعْفَرٍ، حَدَّثَنَا شُعْبَةُ، حَدَّثَنِي أَبُو إِسْحَاقَ: سَمِعْتُ النعمان ابن بَشِيرٍ يَخْطُبُ وَيَقُولُ: سَمِعْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ يَقُولُ: "إِنَّ أَهْوَنَ أَهْلِ النَّارِ عَذَابًا يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ رجلٌ تُوضَعُ فِي أَخْمَصِ قَدَمَيْهِ جَمْرَتَانِ يَغْلِي مِنْهَا دِمَاغُهُ".

رَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ 

وَقَالَ مُسْلِمٌ: حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو بَكْرِ بْنُ أَبِي شَيْبَةَ، حَدَّثَنَا أَبُو أُسَامَةَ، عَنِ الْأَعْمَشُ، عَنْ أَبِي إِسْحَاقَ، عَنِ النُّعْمَانِ بْنِ بَشِيرٍ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "إِنَّ أَهْوَنَ أَهْلِ النَّارِ عَذَابًا مَنْ لَهُ نَعْلَانِ وَشِرَاكَانِ مِنْ نَارٍ يَغلي مِنْهُمَا دِمَاغُهُ كَمَا يَغْلي المِرْجَل، مَا يَرَى أَنَّ أَحَدًا أَشَدَّ مِنْهُ عَذَابًا، وَإِنَّهُ لَأَهْوَنُهُمْ عَذَابًا" .

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿لَا يَصْلاهَا إِلا الأشْقَى﴾ أَيْ: لَا يَدْخُلُهَا دُخُولًا يُحِيطُ بِهِ مِنْ جَمِيعِ جَوَانِبِهِ إِلَّا الْأَشْقَى. ثُمَّ فَسَّرَهُ فَقَالَ: ﴿الَّذِي كَذَّبَ﴾ أَيْ: بِقَلْبِهِ، ﴿وَتَوَلَّى﴾ أَيْ: عَنِ الْعَمَلِ بِجَوَارِحِهِ وأركانه.

قَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا حَسَنُ بْنُ مُوسَى، حَدَّثَنَا ابْنُ لَهِيعة، حَدَّثَنَا عَبْدُ رَبِّهِ بْنُ سَعِيدٍ، عَنِ الْمَقْبُرِيِّ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "لَا يَدْخُلُ النَّارَ إِلَّا شَقِيٌّ". قِيلَ: وَمَنِ الشَّقِيُّ؟ قَالَ: "الَّذِي لَا يَعْمَلُ بِطَاعَةٍ، وَلَا يَتْرُكُ لِلَّهِ مَعْصِيَةً" .

وَقَالَ الْإِمَامُ أَحْمَدُ: حَدَّثَنَا يُونُسُ وسُريج قَالَا حَدَّثَنَا فُلَيح، عَنْ هِلَالِ بْنِ عَلِيٍّ، عَنْ عَطَاءِ بْنِ يَسَارٍ، عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيرة قَالَ: قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ: "كُلُّ أُمَّتِي تَدْخُلُ الْجَنَّةَ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ إِلَّا مَنْ أَبَى". قَالُوا: وَمَنْ يَأْبَى يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ؟ قَالَ: "مَنْ أَطَاعَنِي دَخَلَ الْجَنَّةَ، وَمَنْ عَصَانِي فَقَدْ أَبَى".

وَرَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ عَنْ مُحَمَّدِ بْنِ سِنَانٍ، عَنْ فُلَيح، بِهِ 

* *

وَقَوْلُهُ: ﴿وَسَيُجَنَّبُهَا الأتْقَى﴾ أَيْ: وسَيُزَحزح عَنِ النَّارِ التَّقِيُّ النَّقِيُّ الْأَتْقَى.

ثُمَّ فَسَّرَهُ بِقَوْلِهِ: ﴿الَّذِي يُؤْتِي مَالَهُ يَتَزَكَّى﴾ أَيْ: يَصْرِفُ مَالَهُ فِي طَاعَةِ رَبِّهِ؛ لِيُزَكِّيَ نَفْسَهُ وَمَالَهُ وَمَا وَهَبَهُ اللَّهُ مِنْ دِينٍ وَدُنْيَا.

﴿وَمَا لأحَدٍ عِنْدَهُ مِنْ نِعْمَةٍ تُجْزَى﴾ أَيْ: لَيْسَ بَذْله حَالَهُ فِي مُكَافَأَةِ مَنْ أَسْدَى إِلَيْهِ مَعْرُوفًا، فَهُوَ يُعْطِي فِي مُقَابَلَةِ ذَلِكَ، وَإِنَّمَا دَفَعَهُ ذَلِكَ ﴿ابْتِغَاءَ وَجْهِ رَبِّهِ الأعْلَى﴾ أَيْ: طَمَعًا فِي أَنْ يَحْصُلَ لَهُ رُؤْيَتُهُ فِي الدَّارِ الْآخِرَةِ فِي رَوْضَاتِ الْجَنَّاتِ، قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَلَسَوْفَ يَرْضَى﴾ أَيْ: وَلَسَوْفَ يَرْضَى مَنِ اتَّصَفَ بِهَذِهِ الصِّفَاتِ.

وَقَدْ ذَكَرَ غَيْرُ وَاحِدٍ مِنَ الْمُفَسِّرِينَ أَنَّ هَذِهِ الْآيَاتِ نَزَلَتْ فِي أَبِي بَكْرٍ الصِّدِّيقِ، رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ، حَتَّى إِنَّ بَعْضَهُمْ حَكَى الْإِجْمَاعَ مِنَ الْمُفَسِّرِينَ عَلَى ذَلِكَ. وَلَا شَكَّ أَنَّهُ دَاخِلٌ فِيهَا، وَأَوْلَى الْأُمَّةِ بِعُمُومِهَا، فَإِنَّ لَفْظَهَا لَفْظُ الْعُمُومِ، وَهُوَ قَوْلُهُ تَعَالَى: ﴿وَسَيُجَنَّبُهَا الأتْقَى الَّذِي يُؤْتِي مَالَهُ يَتَزَكَّى وَمَا لأحَدٍ عِنْدَهُ مِنْ نِعْمَةٍ تُجْزَى﴾ وَلَكِنَّهُ مُقَدَّمُ الْأُمَّةِ وَسَابِقُهُمْ فِي جَمِيعِ هَذِهِ الْأَوْصَافِ وَسَائِرِ الْأَوْصَافِ الْحَمِيدَةِ؛ فَإِنَّهُ كَانَ صِدِّيقًا تَقِيًا كَرِيمًا جَوَادًا بَذَّالًا لِأَمْوَالِهِ فِي طَاعَةِ مَوْلَاهُ، وَنُصْرَةِ رَسُولِ اللَّهِ، فَكَمْ مِنْ دَرَاهِمَ وَدَنَانِيرَ بَذَلَهَا ابْتِغَاءَ وَجْهِ رَبِّهِ الْكَرِيمِ، وَلَمْ يَكُنْ لِأَحَدٍ مِنَ النَّاسِ عِنْدَهُ منّةٌ يَحْتَاجُ إِلَى أَنْ يُكَافِئَهُ بِهَا، وَلَكِنْ كَانَ فَضْلُهُ وَإِحْسَانُهُ عَلَى السَّادَاتِ وَالرُّؤَسَاءِ مِنْ سَائِرِ الْقَبَائِلِ؛ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ لَهُ عُرْوَةُ بْنُ مَسْعُودٍ -وَهُوَ سَيِّدُ ثَقِيفٍ، يَوْمَ صُلْحِ الْحُدَيْبِيَةِ-: أَمَا وَاللَّهِ لَوْلَا يَدٌ لَكَ كَانَتْ عِنْدِي لَمْ أَجْزِكَ بِهَا لَأَجَبْتُكَ. وَكَانَ الصِّدِّيقُ قَدْ أَغْلَظَ لَهُ فِي الْمَقَالَةِ، فَإِذَا كَانَ هَذَا حَالُهُ مَعَ سَادَاتِ الْعَرَبِ وَرُؤَسَاءِ الْقَبَائِلِ، فَكَيْفَ بِمَنْ عَدَاهُمْ؟ وَلِهَذَا قَالَ: ﴿وَمَا لأحَدٍ عِنْدَهُ مِنْ نِعْمَةٍ تُجْزَى إِلا ابْتِغَاءَ وَجْهِ رَبِّهِ الأعْلَى وَلَسَوْفَ يَرْضَى﴾ وَفِي الصَّحِيحَيْنِ أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ ﷺ قَالَ: "مَنْ أَنْفَقَ زَوْجَيْنِ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ دَعَته خَزَنَةُ الْجَنَّةِ: يَا عَبْدَ اللَّهِ، هَذَا خَيْرٌ"، فَقَالَ أَبُو بَكْرٍ: يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ، مَا عَلَى مَنْ يُدعى مِنْهَا ضَرُورَةٌ فَهَلْ يُدْعَى مِنْهَا كُلِّهَا أَحَدٌ؟ قَالَ: "نَعَمْ، وَأَرْجُو أَنْ تَكُونَ مِنْهُمْ" .
